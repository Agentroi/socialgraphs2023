<p><b>Isaiah Philip Camacho</b> (born 1981), known professionally as <b>Isaiah Toothtaker</b>, is an American rapper, tattoo artist, and designer.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He is the co-founder of the hip hop collective Machina Muerte.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span></h2>
<p>Isaiah Toothtaker was born Isaiah Philip Camacho in 1981.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is of <a href="/wiki/Mexicans" title="Mexicans">Mexican</a> and <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> heritage.<sup id="cite_ref-TheBoombox_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheBoombox-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Tucson,_Arizona" title="Tucson, Arizona">Tucson, Arizona</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> At the age of 11, he was kicked out of home.<sup id="cite_ref-TheBoombox_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheBoombox-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> At age 15, he started rapping.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He learned tattooing from the then-president of the local <a href="/wiki/Hells_Angels" title="Hells Angels">Hells Angels</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, he released a solo album, <i>Illuminati Thug Mafia</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, he released a <a href="/wiki/The_Hood_Internet" title="The Hood Internet">the Hood Internet</a>-produced collaborative EP with <a href="/wiki/Max_B" title="Max B">Max B</a>, titled <i>Toothy Wavy</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> a <a href="/wiki/Harry_Fraud" title="Harry Fraud">Harry Fraud</a>-produced collaborative EP with Rapewolf, titled <i>Rob Zombie</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and a <a href="/wiki/Sixtoo" title="Sixtoo">Sixtoo</a>-produced solo EP, titled <i>Sea Punk Funk</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, he released a solo album, <i>Illmatic 2</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, he published a book of emoji art, titled <i>That's Not Relevant</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, <i><a href="/wiki/Tucson_Weekly" title="Tucson Weekly">Tucson Weekly</a></i> took down their 2013 interview with him in response to behavioral allegations.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> investigated the charges and claims of sexual assault against him in a 2022 article.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
