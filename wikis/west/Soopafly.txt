<p><b>Priest Joseph Brooks</b>, (known professionally as <b>Soopafly</b> born December 8, 1972) is a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> from <a href="/wiki/Long_Beach,_California" title="Long Beach, California">Long Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Tha_Dogg_Pound" title="Tha Dogg Pound">DPGC</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soopafly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The oldest of three children, Brooks grew up in Long Beach. He was given a keyboard by his father after being inspired by the piano players he saw in church.<sup id="cite_ref-Deep_In_His_Own_Hustle_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Deep_In_His_Own_Hustle-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soopafly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During his teenage years, Brooks met rappers <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>. Proving himself with his keyboard skills, Soopafly was assigned his first piece of work for <a href="/wiki/Death_Row_Records" title="Death Row Records">Death Row Records</a> on the soundtrack <i><a href="/wiki/Murder_Was_The_Case" class="mw-redirect" title="Murder Was The Case">Murder Was The Case</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Deep_In_His_Own_Hustle_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Deep_In_His_Own_Hustle-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He played keyboards for Dr. Dre and <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a> on the track "<a href="/wiki/Natural_Born_Killaz" title="Natural Born Killaz">Natural Born Killaz</a>" and for <a href="/wiki/Jodeci" title="Jodeci">Jodeci</a> on the track "Come Up To My Room", as well as his first production credit with "Who Got Some Gangsta Shit" featuring Snoop Dogg and Tha Dogg Pound. In 1995, he produced "Sooo Much Style" for <a href="/wiki/Tha_Dogg_Pound" title="Tha Dogg Pound">Tha Dogg Pound</a> on their debut album "Dogg Food". He also played the keyboards on eight other songs throughout. Then in 1996, he was featured on the soundtrack to the movie <i><a href="/wiki/A_Thin_Line_Between_Love_and_Hate" title="A Thin Line Between Love and Hate">A Thin Line Between Love and Hate</a></i> on the song "I Don't Hang." A remix would later follow.
</p><p>Soopafly was further introduced to mainstream listeners on Snoop Dogg's second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Doggfather" title="Tha Doggfather">Tha Doggfather</a></i>. He produced the songs "Freestyle Conversation" and "You Thought", appearing alongside veteran rapper <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too Short</a>. Since then, he has been featured primarily on albums by local artists such as <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daz_Dillinger" title="Daz Dillinger">Daz Dillinger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warren_G" title="Warren G">Warren G</a>, <a href="/wiki/RBX" title="RBX">RBX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bad_Azz_(rapper)" title="Bad Azz (rapper)">Bad Azz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tha_Eastsidaz" title="Tha Eastsidaz">Tha Eastsidaz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> To date, he has released three solo albums.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Westurn_Union">Westurn Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soopafly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Westurn Union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2006, the rap group called <a href="/wiki/Westurn_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="Westurn Union">Westurn Union</a> was formed. It features Soopafly, Damani and Bad Lucc. They made their first appearance on <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Blue_Carpet_Treatment" title="Tha Blue Carpet Treatment">Tha Blue Carpet Treatment</a></i> and released a street single titled "I Don't Think So",<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> produced by <a href="/wiki/Rick_Rock" title="Rick Rock">Rick Rock</a>. In April 2008, a mixtape was released titled <i>House Shoe Musik, Vol. 1</i> and was hosted by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Crazy_Toones" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Crazy Toones">DJ Crazy Toones</a>. After inking a deal with <a href="/wiki/Koch_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Koch Records">Koch Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doggystyle_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Doggystyle Records">Doggystyle Records</a>, the official debut album <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg_Presents:_Dubb_Union" title="Snoop Dogg Presents: Dubb Union">Snoop Dogg Presents: Dubb Union</a> was released on September 2, 2008. Prior to the release, the group and the labels were approached by the company <a href="/wiki/Western_Union" title="Western Union">Western Union</a>, threatening legal action if the name was not changed. Though there is a difference between Westurn and Western, the labels did not want to spend money dealing with a lawsuit,  the name of the group was changed to <a href="/wiki/Dubb_Union" title="Dubb Union">Dubb Union</a>.
</p>
