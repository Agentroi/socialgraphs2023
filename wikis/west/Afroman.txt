<p><b>Joseph Edgar Foreman</b> (born July 28, 1974), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Afroman</b>, is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, political candidate, and comedian. He is best known for his 2000 single "<a href="/wiki/Because_I_Got_High" title="Because I Got High">Because I Got High</a>", and its follow-up, the 2001 single "<a href="/wiki/Crazy_Rap" title="Crazy Rap">Crazy Rap</a>". Both songs were featured on his album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Times" title="The Good Times">The Good Times</a></i> (2001). Afroman was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Grammy" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy">Grammy</a> award a year later.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Afroman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Afroman was born on July 28, 1974, in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He initially grew up in the state of Mississippi before moving back to <a href="/wiki/South_Los_Angeles" title="South Los Angeles">South-Central Los Angeles</a> at an early age. He later lived in the <a href="/wiki/High_Desert_(California)" title="High Desert (California)">High Desert</a> <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a> city of <a href="/wiki/Palmdale,_California" title="Palmdale, California">Palmdale</a>, briefly in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas, Nevada</a>, and then <a href="/wiki/Hattiesburg,_Mississippi#Notable_people" title="Hattiesburg, Mississippi">Hattiesburg, Mississippi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Afroman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first song Afroman wrote was entitled "Hairy Carrie". He stated it was written during his time in middle school and was a "<a href="/wiki/Diss_(music)" title="Diss (music)">diss song</a>" about a student known for her cruel harassment of fellow students concerning their appearances.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His musical career began in the eighth grade when he began recording homemade songs and selling them to his classmates.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He later recalled: "The first tape I made was about my eighth-grade teacher. She got me kicked out of school for <a href="/wiki/Sagging_(fashion)" title="Sagging (fashion)">sagging my pants</a>, which was a big deal back then. So I wrote this song about her and it sold about 400 copies: it was selling to teachers, students, just about everybody. And I realized that, even though I wasn't at school, my song was at school, so in a way I was still there. All these people would come by my house just to give me comments about how cool they thought the song was."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Foreman also performed in his church at a young age, playing both the drums and guitar.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1998, Afroman released his first album, <i>My Fro-losophy</i>, which was described as a "flop". The following year, he released <i>Sell Your Dope</i> and relocated to <a href="/wiki/Hattiesburg,_Mississippi" title="Hattiesburg, Mississippi">Hattiesburg, Mississippi</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> where he met drummer Jody Stallone, keyboardist/bassist Darrell Havard, and producer Tim Ramenofsky (a.k.a. Headfridge). Afroman attributes his musical success in part to his relocation. In a 2023 interview with DJ Vlad, he stated Midwesterners and Southerners were more willing than people in LA to experiment with their taste in music.
</p><p>Ramenofsky produced and released Afroman's album <i><a href="/wiki/Because_I_Got_High_(album)" title="Because I Got High (album)">Because I Got High</a></i> in 2000 on <a href="/wiki/T-Bones_Records" title="T-Bones Records">T-Bones Records</a>; it was distributed primarily through local concerts and the file-sharing service <a href="/wiki/Napster" title="Napster">Napster</a> before <a href="/wiki/Because_I_Got_High" title="Because I Got High">its title track</a> was played on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Howard_Stern_Show" title="The Howard Stern Show">The Howard Stern Show</a></i>. The song humorously describes how <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">cannabis</a> use is degrading the narrator's quality of life.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In late 2001, the song became a hit and was featured in the 2000s films <i><a href="/wiki/Jay_and_Silent_Bob_Strike_Back" title="Jay and Silent Bob Strike Back">Jay and Silent Bob Strike Back</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Perfect_Score" title="The Perfect Score">The Perfect Score</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Disturbia_(film)" title="Disturbia (film)">Disturbia</a></i>. "Because I Got High" was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Solo_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Solo Performance">Grammy Award for Best Rap Solo Performance</a> in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the single's success, Afroman joined the lineup of <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a>'s fall festival "Smoke Out" with the <a href="/wiki/Deftones" title="Deftones">Deftones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, and others.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After this, <a href="/wiki/Universal_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Records">Universal Records</a> signed Afroman to a six-album deal, and Universal released <i>The Good Times</i> in 2001. <i>The Good Times</i> was a compilation of Afroman's first two albums and also featured new songs.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Afroman started releasing his music independently and mostly through the Internet in 2004,<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and that year, he recorded <i>Jobe Bells</i>, which <a href="/wiki/Satirized" class="mw-redirect" title="Satirized">satirized</a> traditional <a href="/wiki/Christmas_music" title="Christmas music">Christmas songs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Afroman was part of the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Gathering_of_the_Juggalos" title="Gathering of the Juggalos">Gathering of the Juggalos</a> lineup.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2014, Afroman released a remix of his hit song "Because I Got High" in order to highlight the usefulness of cannabis as part of the effort to legalize its sale across the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Afroman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Afroman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2003, Afroman declared himself a <a href="/wiki/Christian" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian">Christian</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In January 2023, he released an hour-long video on YouTube titled "How To Be Happy In An Unhappy World - Sermon By Afroman" detailing his experiences and understanding on life's issues and how to overcome tribulations through faith in <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">the word of</a> God.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Assault_case">Assault case</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Afroman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Assault case">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 17, 2015, Afroman was midway through his performance at a live music venue in <a href="/wiki/Biloxi,_Mississippi" title="Biloxi, Mississippi">Biloxi, Mississippi</a>, when a woman walked on stage. While dancing and holding a drink, she approached Afroman from behind. Afroman punched her in the face and was eventually escorted offstage by security. He was taken into custody, charged with assault, and released on bond shortly thereafter.
</p><p>There were reportedly 12 to 15 security guards on duty to manage a crowd in excess of 500 people. According to Afroman's representative, he had punched the woman as an involuntary reaction to the invasion of his personal space. Afroman also stated that he mistook her for another audience member who had been heckling him throughout the performance. He publicly apologized and sought assistance with anger management.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Raid_on_Ohio_home">Raid on Ohio home</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Afroman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Raid on Ohio home">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2022, the <a href="/wiki/Adams_County,_Ohio" title="Adams County, Ohio">Adams County, Ohio</a> Sheriff's department searched Afroman's home on suspicion of kidnapping and drug trafficking. Nothing was found during the search, and according to Afroman he "had nothing more in his house than the ends of a few blunts and unused pipes made for him by fans." No charges were presented against Afroman. <sup id="cite_ref-NPR_RAID_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPR_RAID-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Afroman, who was in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> when his neighbors informed him of the raid, posted about the experience on social media, and criticized the officers for raiding his home and causing damage to his property.<sup id="cite_ref-raid_tmz_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raid_tmz-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He also posted about a previous burglary on his home where he was threatened with arrest by the sheriff's department if he kept checking about the progress of the case and was told they did not have time to provide him with an update.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The search of Afroman's home damaged his door, external gate, and security system wiring, which cost over $20,000 to repair. During the raid officers also seized over $5,000 of cash from his home. The cash was later returned to him, but with $400 missing.<sup id="cite_ref-NPR_RAID_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPR_RAID-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>He recorded a trio of songs criticizing the raid, titled "Will You Help Me Repair My Door","Lemon Pound Cake" (also the name of his album, set to the tune of <i><a href="/wiki/Under_the_Boardwalk" title="Under the Boardwalk">Under the Boardwalk</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/The_Drifters" title="The Drifters">The Drifters</a>) and "Why You Disconnecting My Video Camera", and released three music videos composed primarily of security camera recordings of the raid, to his <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> account, on December 29, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 14, 2023, seven police officers from the Adams County Sheriff's office sued Afroman, alleging that his use of their personas from the video of the raid was an invasion of the officers' privacy.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2024_presidential_candidacy">2024 presidential candidacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Afroman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2024 presidential candidacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 20, 2022, Afroman announced his candidacy in the <a href="/wiki/2024_United_States_presidential_election" title="2024 United States presidential election">2024 presidential election</a>. His campaign manager, Jason Savage, announced his candidacy on his behalf, citing inflation, the housing market, law enforcement corruption, and legalizing marijuana as key issues of his campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
