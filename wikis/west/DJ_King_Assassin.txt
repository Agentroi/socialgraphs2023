<div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Website</th><td class="infobox-data"><span class="url"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://djkingassass.in">Official website</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Craig Venegas Alvarado</b> (born January 15, 1971), known by his stage name <b>DJ King Assassin</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a>, producer, and <a href="/wiki/Music_engineer" class="mw-redirect" title="Music engineer">engineer</a> from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles, California</a>.
</p><p>Venegas became influential in the west coast <a href="/wiki/Hip-hop_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip-hop music">hip-hop music</a> scene after having helped pioneer records for 1990s era rap moguls such as <a href="/wiki/2Pac" class="mw-redirect" title="2Pac">2Pac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice-T" title="Ice-T">Ice-T</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Venegas started producing at an early age, growing up in the <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> <a href="/wiki/Bay_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Bay Area">Bay Area</a>. Starting in the 1980s, Venegas influenced the west coast hip-hop music culture by creating sounds for rappers in the Bay Area &amp; L.A California.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He connected with former <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records" title="Ruthless Records">Ruthless Records</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/Kokane" title="Kokane">Kokane</a> and charted on billboard with Dr Kokastien and The Legend Continues.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Throughout his career, Venegas engineered over 300 titles for recording artists signed to <a href="/wiki/Universal_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Records">Universal Records</a>, <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a>, <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam">Def Jam</a>, including several of which reached the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Billboard charts</a>, respectively. In 2014, he peaked at #7 on the Billboard charts in the US following the release of a mixtape with fellow industry partner <a href="/wiki/Kokane" title="Kokane">Kokane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> For several consecutive years, Venegas has been awarded DJ of the Year for his lengthy music contribution in the West Coast Hip Hop Awards.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_King_Assassin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Venegas spent most of his early life in California traveling from San Jose, Vallejo, East Palo Alto and Sacramento, where he then relocated back to his native California lifestyle in the city of Los Angeles. As a kid, he won his school's spelling bee at Hillsdale Elementary and later went on to the district of Franklin Mckinley's spelling bee. He also learned to play drums in elementary school, and in eighth grade, how to make music. From that time, he became interested in music as well as breakdancing, in which he won trophies at local fairs and contests throughout the state of California.
</p><p>Venegas' influences include long-time friend and collaborator <a href="/wiki/2Pac" class="mw-redirect" title="2Pac">2Pac</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a>, as he has grown up in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_King_Assassin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s-2000s:_Career_beginnings">1990s-2000s: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_King_Assassin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1990s-2000s: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Venegas moved where originally his parents are from, in Southern California, and began his career as a record disc jockey, in which he later produced for recording artists signed to Russell Simmons record label Def Jam. In the early part of his career, Venegas produced for several projects from <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a> at Kitchen Sync and Echo Sound studios. It was there where, Venegas, a rising producer, teamed up with Tupac Shakur in which he later established a close relationship with the West Coast rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While Tupac Shakur worked on "Me Against The World", Venegas began producing music with Shakur, later witnessing the creation and scratching on original version of "<a href="/wiki/Dear_Mama" title="Dear Mama">Dear Mama</a>", one of Shakur's most successful hip-hop records. Venegas, thereafter, produced, mixed and mastered an inordinate number of records for hip-hop recording artists in the west coast of the US, influencing the unique funk sound established by rappers like Eazy-E and 2Pac.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010-2015:_The_King_Assassin_Show_.26_Mixtapes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010-2015:_The_King_Assassin_Show_&amp;_Mixtapes">2010-2015: <i>The King Assassin Show</i> &amp; Mixtapes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_King_Assassin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010-2015: The King Assassin Show &amp; Mixtapes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Venegas started his own internet syndicated radio show, The King Assassin Show, which featured appearances from recording artists and other notable figures like Freeway Rick Ross, for example.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Throughout the first half of the 2010s, Venegas released numerous mixtapes, some of which featured unreleased music from Tupac Shakur, and others in collaboration with west coast rappers.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.94present:_Original_Dear_Mama_Recording_.26_Makaveli_Film"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016—present:_Original_Dear_Mama_Recording_&amp;_Makaveli_Film">2016—present: Original <i>Dear Mama</i> Recording &amp; <i>Makaveli</i> Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_King_Assassin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2016—present: Original Dear Mama Recording &amp; Makaveli Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In May 2016, following the passing of Afeni Shakur, Tupac Shakur's birth mother, Venegas was featured on <a href="/wiki/TMZ" title="TMZ">TMZ</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> magazine where he spoke on his affiliation and relationship with the Shakur estate and the release of the original version of Tupac Shakur's classic record "<a href="/wiki/Dear_Mama" title="Dear Mama">Dear Mama</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, Venegas announced he will be producing and directing a film loosely based on Tupac Shakur's personal life and music career. The film is expected to be shot in California, and acting cast has not been released yet.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, following the passing of Tupac Shakur's mother, Alvarado released a rare recording of 'Dear Mama' in honor of her death, which was later picked up by numerous music news outlets including TMZ, XXL and Complex.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guinness_book_of_world_records">Guinness book of world records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_King_Assassin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Guinness book of world records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Assassin holds the Guinness Book Of World Records for recording the longest hip-hop song in the history followed by <i>Rappers Delight</i> entitled <i>State Of Emergency</i> which features mostly platinum and legendary artists and to be released in 2020.
</p>
