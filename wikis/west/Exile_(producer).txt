<p><b>Aleksander Thomas Manfredi</b>, better known by his stage name <b>Exile</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an American <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a> and occasional <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>. Aside from his solo career, he is a member of the musical groups <a href="/wiki/Emanon" title="Emanon">Emanon</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Aloe_Blacc" title="Aloe Blacc">Aloe Blacc</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Blu_%26_Exile" title="Blu &amp; Exile">Blu &amp; Exile</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Exile_(producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Exile's first record appearances were as a member of the hip hop duo Emanon with the rapper Aloe Blacc. They released various mixtapes beginning in 1995 with "Stretch Marx" before releasing their first album, <i>Anon &amp; On</i>. After 2002, Exile went on to release one more Emanon album, <i>The Waiting Room</i> (2005),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> a solo album, <i>Dirty Science</i> (2006),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and another collaborative effort with the rapper <a href="/wiki/Blu_(rapper)" title="Blu (rapper)">Blu</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Below_the_Heavens" title="Below the Heavens">Below the Heavens</a></i> (2007).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Exile released two more solo albums, <i>Radio</i> in 2009<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and <i>4TRK Mind</i> in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 2013 saw the release of his instrumental album, <i>Zip Disks &amp; Floppies</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Exile released his second collaborative album with Blu, <i>Give Me My Flowers While I Can Still Smell Them</i> in 2012. Exile and Blu released their third collaborative album, <i>Miles</i>, in 2020. <i>Miles</i> was ranked #4 on music critic Anthony Fantano's 50 Best Albums of 2020 list.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_and_influences">Style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Exile_(producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Exile is known for "coarsely chopped beats" that give off "laid back soulful vibes".<sup id="cite_ref-style_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-style-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He says his influences are the contemporary producers <a href="/wiki/J_Dilla" title="J Dilla">J Dilla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jon_Brion" title="Jon Brion">Jon Brion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Madlib" title="Madlib">Madlib</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-influence_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-influence-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
