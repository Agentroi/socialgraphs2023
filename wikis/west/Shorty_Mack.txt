<p><b>Derrelle Owens</b> (born June 9, 1981), better known by his stage name <b>Shorty Mack</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>. He is most significantly known by his 1996 single, "Hard To Tame Me", and his self-titled debut album, both released under <a href="/wiki/JVC" title="JVC">JVC</a>/Vertex Music. He has since made appearances on numerous recordings by <a href="/wiki/Ray_J" title="Ray J">Ray J</a>, including the Knockout remix of "<a href="/wiki/Formal_Invite" title="Formal Invite">Formal Invite</a>" and the Smokin' Trees version of "<a href="/wiki/What_I_Need_(Ray_J_song)" title="What I Need (Ray J song)">What I Need</a>". He also played the role of "Nyce" on the sitcom <a href="/wiki/Moesha" title="Moesha">Moesha</a>. Owens co-created <a href="/wiki/Knockout_Entertainment" title="Knockout Entertainment">Knockout Entertainment</a>. A full-length album, tentatively titled <i>Shorty Mack</i>, was expected to be released in 2012.
</p>
