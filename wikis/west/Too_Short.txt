<p><b>Todd Anthony Shaw</b> (born April 28, 1966),<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-starpulse1_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-starpulse1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-allmusic1_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> better known by the stage name <b>Too Short</b> (stylized as <b>Too $hort</b>), is an American rapper and record producer. He became famous in the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast hip hop</a> scene in the late 1980s, with lyrics often based on <a href="/wiki/Pimping" class="mw-redirect" title="Pimping">pimping</a> and <a href="/wiki/Promiscuity" title="Promiscuity">promiscuity</a>, but also drug culture and street survival.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This is respectively exemplified in his most popular songs "<a href="/wiki/Blow_the_Whistle_(song)" title="Blow the Whistle (song)">Blow the Whistle</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/The_Ghetto_(Too_Short_song)" title="The Ghetto (Too Short song)">The Ghetto</a>".
</p><p>A pioneer of <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast hip hop</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-starpulse1_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-starpulse1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-allmusic1_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Shaw began recording in 1983, cultivating a large following in his native <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland</a>. In 1987, his fourth album <i><a href="/wiki/Born_to_Mack" title="Born to Mack">Born to Mack</a></i> attracted the attention of <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>, who signed him and distributed the album nationally. His subsequent 1988 album <i><a href="/wiki/Life_Is...Too_Short" title="Life Is...Too Short">Life Is...Too Short</a></i> was highly successful, going double <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification#List_of_certifications" title="RIAA certification">platinum</a>, and he remained prominent into the 1990s.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Too_Short&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shaw was born and raised in Los Angeles. In 1980, he and his family moved to Oakland. He was a drummer in the band at <a href="/wiki/Fremont_High_School_(Oakland,_California)" title="Fremont High School (Oakland, California)">Fremont High School</a> in Oakland.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Too_Short&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the mid-1980s, Shaw along with a high school friend Freddy B, produced custom songs (called "special requests") locally on cassette for people in Oakland and the Bay Area. In 1983, Too Short started his solo career and released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Stop_Rappin%27" class="mw-redirect" title="Don&#39;t Stop Rappin&#39;">Don't Stop Rappin'</a></i> on Oakland record label 75 Girls Records And Tapes which, along with four 12" releases, featured raw, simple drum beats from a <a href="/wiki/LinnDrum" title="LinnDrum">LinnDrum</a> <a href="/wiki/Drum_machine" title="Drum machine">drum machine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  This was also one of the first hip hop records to use the word "<a href="/wiki/Bitch_(insult)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bitch (insult)">bitch</a>" – a word which became one of the rapper's trademarks and was the focus of subsequent raps such as "Ain't Nothin' but a Word to Me".
</p><p>In the early 1990s, his self-produced beats came from mostly a <a href="/wiki/TR-808" class="mw-redirect" title="TR-808">TR-808</a> and from mid-to-late 2000s, a <a href="/wiki/TR-909" class="mw-redirect" title="TR-909">TR-909</a> was used. In 1985, Too Short and Freddie B. formed the label Dangerous Music to regionally distribute his music, and with others formed rap group <a href="/wiki/The_Dangerous_Crew" title="The Dangerous Crew">the Dangerous Crew</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-amg_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-amg-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Dangerous Music became Short Records, and then Up All Nite Records. With his 1988 release, <i><a href="/wiki/Life_Is...Too_Short" title="Life Is...Too Short">Life Is...Too Short</a></i>, he began using replayed established <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a> riffs (rather than samples) with his beats.
</p><p>Subsequent work was primarily collaborative, including work with <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scarface_(rapper)" title="Scarface (rapper)">Scarface</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pimp_C" title="Pimp C">Pimp C</a>. One of his notable collaborations during this period was on the track "The World Is Filled..." on the Notorious B.I.G. album <i><a href="/wiki/Life_After_Death" title="Life After Death">Life After Death</a></i>; he comes in on the third verse after Diddy and Biggie. Being featured on the album introduced him to a wider audience as well, due to his typical style contrasting greatly with the Mafioso theme of the album. He also appeared on TWDY's hit single "Player's Holiday" from their 1999 debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Derty_Werk" title="Derty Werk">Derty Werk</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Priority_Records" title="Priority Records">Priority Records</a> compilation <i>Nuthin but a Gangsta Party</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> After these appearances, he began working on his eleventh album, <i><a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Stay_Away" title="Can&#39;t Stay Away">Can't Stay Away</a></i>.  The album included guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/8Ball_%26_MJG" title="8Ball &amp; MJG">8Ball &amp; MJG</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Sean Combs</a>, <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daz_Dillinger" title="Daz Dillinger">Daz Dillinger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Jon" title="Lil Jon">Lil Jon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soopafly" title="Soopafly">Soopafly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scarface_(rapper)" title="Scarface (rapper)">Scarface</a> and <a href="/wiki/B-Legit" title="B-Legit">B-Legit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mtv.com_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtv.com-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Too Short relocated to <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> in 1994, but he did not begin working with a more diverse variety of Southern artists until 2000, when he collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Jon" title="Lil Jon">Lil Jon</a>. With the 1999 release of <i><a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Stay_Away" title="Can&#39;t Stay Away">Can't Stay Away</a></i>, Too Short came out of retirement, continuing his sexually explicit, relaxed style of rap.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> New albums released 2000–2003 were <i><a href="/wiki/You_Nasty" title="You Nasty">You Nasty</a></i> (2000), <i><a href="/wiki/Chase_the_Cat" title="Chase the Cat">Chase the Cat</a></i> (2001), <i><a href="/wiki/What%27s_My_Favorite_Word%3F" title="What&#39;s My Favorite Word?">What's My Favorite Word?</a></i> (2002), and <i><a href="/wiki/Married_to_the_Game" title="Married to the Game">Married to the Game</a></i> (2003).  These albums all charted fairly well, as they all were in the top 71 of the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, but they did not do as well as Too Short's earlier 1990s releases, as none of them reached the top 10.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For his next album, <i><a href="/wiki/Blow_the_Whistle_(album)" title="Blow the Whistle (album)">Blow the Whistle</a></i> (2006), Too Short now took advantage of the new <a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">hyphy</a> rap music that was emerging out of his original home base in Oakland. This saw somewhat of a resurgence for Too Short as it peaked at No. 14 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, much better than each of his previous three releases.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  However, his subsequent releases, such as 2007's <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Off_the_Stage" class="mw-redirect" title="Get Off the Stage">Get Off the Stage</a></i>, have not been as successful. On October 7, 2008, Too Short was honored by VH1 at the fifth annual "Hip-Hop Honors" along with <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/De_La_Soul" title="De La Soul">De La Soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Naughty_By_Nature" class="mw-redirect" title="Naughty By Nature">Naughty By Nature</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Too Short recorded for <a href="/wiki/Daz_Dillinger" title="Daz Dillinger">Daz Dillinger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Jon" title="Lil Jon">Lil Jon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soopafly" title="Soopafly">Soopafly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scarface_(rapper)" title="Scarface (rapper)">Scarface</a> and <a href="/wiki/B-Legit" title="B-Legit">B-Legit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mtv.com_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtv.com-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, the rapper was featured on <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/On_My_Level" title="On My Level">On My Level</a>". He also collaborated in <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>'s 2011 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Doggumentary" title="Doggumentary">Doggumentary</a></i> in the song "Take U Home" and on the <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> song "<a href="/wiki/First_Date_(50_Cent_song)" title="First Date (50 Cent song)">First Date</a>". In 2012 Too Short along with <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a> released two collaboration albums on the same day titled <i><a href="/wiki/History:_Mob_Music" title="History: Mob Music">History: Mob Music</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/History:_Function_Music" title="History: Function Music">History: Function Music</a></i>. Both charted in the top 100 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> albums chart. Too Short has said the best verse he has ever recorded is a verse for a song on <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s <i>Detox</i> called "Man's Best Friend (Pussy)".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, it was announced he would collaborate with <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a> on a song "Jewels n' Drugs" for her upcoming album <i><a href="/wiki/Artpop" title="Artpop">Artpop</a></i>. Also featured on the song are <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Twista" title="Twista">Twista</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, Too Short was featured on actor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tavion_Tate_Guice&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tavion Tate Guice (page does not exist)">Tavion Tate Guice</a>'s song "Oakland Brookfield Dance".
</p><p>In 2016, Too Short was featured on the song "Cochino" by Argentine-American rapper <a href="/wiki/Dumbfoundead" title="Dumbfoundead">Dumbfoundead</a>. The song is the seventh track off of Dumbfoundead's fifth studio album <i>We Might Die</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Six years after his last release, Too Short released his 20th album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pimp_Tape" title="The Pimp Tape">The Pimp Tape</a></i> on November 9, 2018. On December 20, 2019, he released his 21st album <i>The Vault</i>. On December 18, 2020, Too Short and <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a> released their collaboration album <i>Ain't Gone Do It and Terms &amp; Conditions</i>.
</p>

</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Up_All_Nite_Records">Up All Nite Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Too_Short&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Up All Nite Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Too Short runs his own record label—Up All Nite Records.  Artists on the label include <a href="/wiki/The_Pack_(group)" title="The Pack (group)">the Pack</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Dolla Will, Boo Ski, Li'l J &amp; Boi Payton, FX.  In addition to creating Up All Nite Records, Too Short has been a mentor at Youth UpRising, a group serving at-risk youths for several years.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Filmography">Filmography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Too_Short&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Filmography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Too Short played the role of Lew-Loc in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Menace_II_Society" title="Menace II Society">Menace II Society</a></i>. Too Short has also worked in the <a href="/wiki/Adult_film_industry" class="mw-redirect" title="Adult film industry">adult film industry</a>, with the 2003 film <i>Get In Where You Fit In</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Too Short was an interviewee in <i><a href="/wiki/American_Pimp" title="American Pimp">American Pimp</a>.</i> Too Short starred in and performed the music for <i>America's Sexiest Girls</i> 2003. Too Short has also appeared in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Game_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Game (U.S. TV series)">The Game</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Too Short made a cameo appearance in Jay-Z's video for the hit single, "Big Pimpin'". Too Short was in VH1's Rock Doc, "Planet Rock". Too Short made a cameo in the feature film <i>Stop Pepper Palmer</i> with <a href="/wiki/Scott_Schwartz" title="Scott Schwartz">Scott Schwartz</a>.
</p><p>Too Short released two straight-to-DVD films as well, 2001's <i>Too Short Uncensored</i>, produced by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jarrod_Donoman&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jarrod Donoman (page does not exist)">Jarrod Donoman</a> and Terrell Taylor, the DVD Live Nationwide, and 2003's <i>Too Short Presents....Titty City</i>, again produced by Taylor and partner Co Garrett.
</p><p>Too Short was also featured in a 2009 episode of the E! show <a href="/wiki/Kendra_(TV_series)" title="Kendra (TV series)"><i>Kendra</i></a> starring Kendra Wilkinson.
</p>
