<p><b>Keith A. Moore</b> (born December 15, 1985) is a <a href="/wiki/Nerdcore" title="Nerdcore">nerdcore</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>, better known by the <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Beefy</b>, from the <a href="/wiki/Tri-Cities,_Washington" title="Tri-Cities, Washington">Tri-Cities, Washington</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beefy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2005,  Moore produced two independent EPs, <i>The Whitesican EP</i> and <i>nerd</i>. He created animated music videos for the songs "Whitesican" and "David's Sister" (both from <i>The Whitesican EP</i>), which gave him notoriety after they appeared on <a href="/wiki/Albino_Blacksheep" title="Albino Blacksheep">Albino Blacksheep</a>. In 2006 Moore, in conjunction with Nerdy South Records, released his first full-length album, <i>Tube Technology</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In August 2008, he released his album <i>Rolling Doubles</i> as a digital download through Amazon and iTunes<sup id="cite_ref-beefynessdotcom_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-beefynessdotcom-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Moore contributed to the Rhyme Torrents compilation, contributing both music and original cover artwork.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He can also be found in two documentaries on the Nerdcore movement – <i>Nerdcore Rising</i>, covering <a href="/wiki/MC_Frontalot" title="MC Frontalot">MC Frontalot</a> on his first national tour,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and the <i>Nerdcore For Life</i> documentary which covers other nerdcore artists.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He has been interviewed on NPR,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ka_Leo_O_Hawaii" class="mw-redirect" title="Ka Leo O Hawaii">Ka Leo O Hawaii</a>,
<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and has also been mentioned more than once in the blogs at G4tv.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He was also ranked number 5 in the Hipster, Please poll of the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20080408131845/http://www.hipsterplease.com/2007/04/top-10-nerdcore-artists-of-all-time.html">top 10 nerdcore hip-hop artists</a>.
</p><p>Moore is also a frequent collaborator with Doctor Popular (aka <a href="/w/index.php?title=Drown_Radio&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Drown Radio (page does not exist)">Drown Radio</a>), MC Router, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shael_Riley&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Shael Riley (page does not exist)">Shael Riley</a>, tanner4105 and DJ Snyder. In 2007 he took part in The Mediocre Tour with fellow nerdcore artists <a href="/wiki/MC_Router" title="MC Router">MC Router</a> and Doctor Popular, performing shows in Washington, Oregon, and California. Moore has done shows with notable nerdcore artists <a href="/wiki/Mc_chris" class="mw-redirect" title="Mc chris">mc chris</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Frontalot" title="MC Frontalot">MC Frontalot</a>, Schaffer the Darklord, <a href="/wiki/YTCracker" title="YTCracker">YTCracker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Optimus_Rhyme" title="Optimus Rhyme">Optimus Rhyme</a>, and <a href="/wiki/MC_Lars" title="MC Lars">MC Lars</a>.
</p><p>In December 2007, <a href="/wiki/The_Grammar_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="The Grammar Club">The Grammar Club</a> released their debut EP <i>Bremelanotide</i>. The Grammar Club are a six-piece band featuring Beefy, along with Shael Riley, Glenn Case, Sean "Ailsean" Stone, tanner4105, and Ty Guenley.
</p><p>In February 2009, Beefy attended "Glitched: The Dutch Nerdcore Event,"<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> the first major nerdcore event to be held outside the United States. It was held in Club Panama in Amsterdam and featured the film "Nerdcore for Life"<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> as well as performances from his fellow rappers <a href="/wiki/MC_Router" title="MC Router">MC Router</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Lars" title="MC Lars">MC Lars</a>, and <a href="/wiki/YTCracker" title="YTCracker">YTCracker</a>. In July 2009, Beefy performed at <a href="/wiki/Nerdapalooza" title="Nerdapalooza">Nerdapalooza</a>, the largest nerdcore hip hop event in North America.
</p>
