<p><b>Amanda Ingrid Seales</b> (born July 1, 1981), formerly known by the stage name <b>Amanda Diva</b>, is an American comedian and actress.  From 2017-2021, she starred in the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Insecure_(TV_series)" title="Insecure (TV series)">Insecure</a></i>. In 2019, <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> released her first stand-up comedy special <i>I Be Knowin</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Then, in 2020, Seales launched <i>Smart Funny &amp; Black</i>, a comedy gameshow that showcases Black culture, history, and experience.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Seales was also one of the co-hosts of the syndicated daytime talk show, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Real_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Real (TV series)">The Real</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-test_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-test-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amanda_Seales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amanda Seales was born in Inglewood, CA on July 1, 1981. Her mother was born and raised in Mt. Moritz, <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">Grenada</a>. Her father is African-American. As a result, both she and her mother are dual citizens of the US and <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">Grenada</a>. She moved to <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando, Florida</a>, in 1989, where she later attended <a href="/wiki/Dr._Phillips_High_School" title="Dr. Phillips High School">Dr. Phillips High School</a>. She graduated from <a href="/wiki/State_University_of_New_York_at_Purchase" title="State University of New York at Purchase">SUNY-Purchase</a>, then acquired a master's degree in <a href="/wiki/African-American_studies" class="mw-redirect" title="African-American studies">African-American studies</a> with a concentration in <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">Hip hop</a> from <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-seales_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seales-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amanda_Seales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television">Film and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amanda_Seales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Seales's first film was a minor role as Katy in the 1993 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Cop_and_a_Half" title="Cop and a Half">Cop and a Half</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The next year Seales was featured on the <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/My_Brother_and_Me" title="My Brother and Me">My Brother and Me</a></i> (1994) as Deonne Wilburn. In 2002 she appeared on <a href="/wiki/Russell_Simmons" title="Russell Simmons">Russell Simmons</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Def_Poetry_Jam" title="Def Poetry Jam">Def Poetry Jam</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Seales became publicly known as "<a href="/wiki/VJ_(media_personality)" class="mw-redirect" title="VJ (media personality)">VJ</a> Amanda Diva" on <a href="/wiki/MTV2" title="MTV2">MTV2</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Sucker_Free" title="Sucker Free">Sucker Free</a> Countdown</i> on Sundays.<sup id="cite_ref-seales_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seales-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2016 she appeared in a recurring role on the HBO series <i><a href="/wiki/Insecure_(TV_series)" title="Insecure (TV series)">Insecure</a>,</i> as Tiffany DuBois.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Also in 2016, Seales began hosting her own show on <a href="/wiki/TruTV" title="TruTV">truTV</a> called <i>Greatest Ever</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 26, 2019, <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> debuted her first stand-up comedy special <i>I Be Knowin'.</i><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Bring_the_Funny" title="Bring the Funny">Bring the Funny</a></i> is a comedy competition series that premiered on July 9, 2019, on NBC.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Seales hosts, alongside judges <a href="/wiki/Kenan_Thompson" title="Kenan Thompson">Kenan Thompson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chrissy_Teigen" title="Chrissy Teigen">Chrissy Teigen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Foxworthy" title="Jeff Foxworthy">Jeff Foxworthy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Throughout 2019, Seales served as a frequent guest host for the syndicated daytime talk show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Real_(talk_show)" title="The Real (talk show)">The Real</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On January 6, 2020, Seales was promoted from guest co-host to permanent co-host.<sup id="cite_ref-Petski_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Petski-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> She departed the series after six months of co-hosting, citing her dissatisfaction with the inability to openly speak on recent social issues as a reason.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In September 2020, Seales accused <i>The Real</i> of stealing ideas from her show <i>Smart Funny &amp; Black</i>, saying <i>The Real</i>'s segment "Black Lives Matter University" featured a logo that closely resembled <i>Smart Funny &amp; Black</i>'s emblem.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2020, Seales hosted the <a href="/wiki/BET_Awards_2020" title="BET Awards 2020">BET Awards 2020</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2022, Seales was cast in the three-part documentary series <i><a href="/wiki/Everything%27s_Gonna_Be_All_White" title="Everything&#39;s Gonna Be All White">Everything's Gonna Be All White</a></i>, airing on <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amanda_Seales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, Seales replaced <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Stewart" class="mw-redirect" title="Natalie Stewart">Natalie Stewart</a> of the musical duo <a href="/wiki/Floetry" title="Floetry">Floetry</a> on tour with <a href="/wiki/Marsha_Ambrosius" title="Marsha Ambrosius">Marsha Ambrosius</a>, and in December of that same year Seales (as Amanda Diva) released her first <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP) <i>Life Experience</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In 2008, she was featured on the song "Manwomanboogie" on <a href="/wiki/Q-Tip_(musician)" title="Q-Tip (musician)">Q-Tip</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Grammy" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy">Grammy</a>-nominated album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Renaissance_(Q-Tip_album)" title="The Renaissance (Q-Tip album)">The Renaissance</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On March 3, 2009, Seales released <i>Spandex, Rhymes &amp; Soul</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Podcast">Podcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amanda_Seales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Podcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Seales hosts a weekly podcast titled <i>Small Doses</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Petski_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Petski-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_Show">Game Show</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amanda_Seales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Game Show">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She created and hosts the touring variety game show, <i>Smart Funny &amp; Black</i>, where her love of games and music, was used to serve as a safe space for the Black voice and celebrates the many contributions Black Americans have made to history and pop culture and more.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Activism">Activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amanda_Seales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, Seales teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Truth_(anti-tobacco_campaign)" title="Truth (anti-tobacco campaign)">Truth</a>, to start an <a href="/wiki/Tobacco_control" title="Tobacco control">anti-smoking campaign</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2014, Seales appeared in a segment on CNN alongside Steve Santagati, hosted by <a href="/wiki/Fredricka_Whitfield" title="Fredricka Whitfield">Fredricka Whitfield</a>, discussing the video <i><a href="/wiki/10_Hours_of_Walking_in_NYC_as_a_Woman" title="10 Hours of Walking in NYC as a Woman">10 Hours of Walking in NYC as a Woman</a></i>. During the segment, Seales said that men catcalling women is not complimentary and that women do not like being catcalled, while Santagati went on to defend men catcalling women, saying that women should take catcalling as a compliment and not abuse.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 11, 2017, during a livestream on <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a>'s YouTube channel, an exchange took place between Seales and <a href="/wiki/Caitlyn_Jenner" title="Caitlyn Jenner">Caitlyn Jenner</a>, with Seales saying that Jenner benefits from <a href="/wiki/White_privilege" title="White privilege">white privilege</a>. Towards the end of the exchange, Seales told Jenner "This country is here for you. This country ain’t here for me in the same way, sis".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Seales has attributed this exchange to her becoming an activist.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio">Radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amanda_Seales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 18, 2022, Seales began co-hosting a new weekly radio show, <i>Amanda Seales’ Smart Funny &amp; Black Radio</i> on SiriusXM, alongside Taj Rani and JeremiahLikeTheBible, featuring topics such as games, celebrity interviews and <a href="/wiki/African-American_culture" title="African-American culture">African-American culture</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amanda_Seales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2019, Seales was involved in a controversy where many people mistakenly reported that she had accused former NFL player and neurosurgeon resident <a href="/wiki/Myron_Rolle" title="Myron Rolle">Myron Rolle</a> of sexually harassing several women. She made several Instagram videos alleging he was sexually inappropriate, and was sharing that she had heard those allegations from other women. Multiple people who were spreading that story have since admitted that Seales did not make those allegations herself.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the weeks preceding <a href="/wiki/Rodney_Reed" title="Rodney Reed">Rodney Reed</a>'s November 20, 2019, execution date, Seales publicly urged Texas Governor <a href="/wiki/Greg_Abbott" title="Greg Abbott">Greg Abbott</a> to exonerate Reed or stay his execution. She later rescinded her support after learning more about his history.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2020, Seales drew controversy while on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Real_(talk_show)" title="The Real (talk show)">The Real</a></i> when defending <a href="/wiki/Jussie_Smollett" title="Jussie Smollett">Jussie Smollett</a> after he was arrested for <a href="/wiki/Jussie_Smollett_hate_crime_hoax" title="Jussie Smollett hate crime hoax">faking a hate crime</a>. While describing the accusations as alleged, she went on to defend the actor saying, "Even if it was a hoax for the sake of bringing attention to this, then I’m like, that’s low-key noble".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> In 2022, Smollett was found guilty of orchestrating a fake hate crime and sentenced to 150 days in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2021, Seales was criticized for portraying an <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Kappa_Alpha" title="Alpha Kappa Alpha">Alpha Kappa Alpha</a>, or "AKA," on the series <i><a href="/wiki/Insecure_(TV_series)" title="Insecure (TV series)">Insecure</a></i>. Real-life members of the sorority responded negatively to Seales' character wearing Alpha Kappa Alpha colors.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Seales responded to the criticism in an Instagram video defending her portrayal.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> It was later reported that the sorority plans to take legal action against Seales.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
