<p><b>Adam Aminé Daniel</b> (born April 18, 1994) is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He first gained notability for his commercial debut <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Caroline_(Amin%C3%A9_song)" title="Caroline (Aminé song)">Caroline</a>", which peaked at number 11 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-hot100_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hot100-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Aminé released his debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Good_for_You_(album)" title="Good for You (album)">Good for You</a></i>, on July 28, 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-GFYAM_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GFYAM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Limbo_(Amin%C3%A9_album)" title="Limbo (Aminé album)">Limbo</a></i>, on August 7, 2020.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amin%C3%A9_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aminé is the son of an <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethiopian">Ethiopian</a> father and a <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Eritrea" title="Demographics of Eritrea">Eritrean</a> mother.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:02_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Introducing_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Introducing-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Aminé's mother worked at a post office and his father was a teacher and translator part-time.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Aminé was born and raised in the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Portland" class="mw-redirect" title="Northeast Portland">Northeast Portland</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Woodlawn,_Portland,_Oregon" title="Woodlawn, Portland, Oregon">Woodlawn</a>. He grew up speaking <a href="/wiki/Amharic" title="Amharic">Amharic</a> at home.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aminé graduated from <a href="/wiki/Benson_Polytechnic_High_School" title="Benson Polytechnic High School">Benson Polytechnic High School</a>, and attended <a href="/wiki/Portland_State_University" title="Portland State University">Portland State University</a> to study <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">marketing</a> before dropping out to focus on his music career full time.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Aminé  worked as an <a href="/wiki/Intern" class="mw-redirect" title="Intern">intern</a> at the hip-hop publication <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amin%C3%A9_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.932015:_Early_career_and_career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2015:_Early_career_and_career_beginnings">2014–2015: Early career and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amin%C3%A9_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2014–2015: Early career and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 17, 2014, Aminé began his music career after releasing his debut <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, <i>Odyssey to Me</i>. On September 4, 2014, Aminé also went on to release his debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a>, titled <i>En Vogue</i>. On August 31, 2015, Aminé followed suit with his second mixtape titled, <i>Calling Brío</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Introducing_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Introducing-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932017:_Breakthrough_with_.22Caroline.22_and_Good_for_You"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2017:_Breakthrough_with_&quot;Caroline&quot;_and_Good_for_You">2016–2017: Breakthrough with "Caroline" and <i>Good for You</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amin%C3%A9_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2016–2017: Breakthrough with &quot;Caroline&quot; and Good for You">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Caroline_(Amin%C3%A9_song)" title="Caroline (Aminé song)">Caroline (Aminé song)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Good_for_You_(album)" title="Good for You (album)">Good for You (album)</a></div>
<p>On March 9, 2016, Aminé released his debut <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>, titled "<a href="/wiki/Caroline_(Amin%C3%A9_song)" title="Caroline (Aminé song)">Caroline</a>". Aminé went on to release his self-directed music video for "Caroline" through his <a href="/wiki/Vevo" title="Vevo">Vevo</a> channel on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> on June 1, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The song debuted at number 96 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart and would later rise to number 11.<sup id="cite_ref-hot100_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hot100-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In June 2017, the song was certified three-times platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2016, Aminé partnered with <a href="/wiki/Republic_Records" title="Republic Records">Republic Records</a>. Aminé went on to release his second single under Republic Records, titled "Baba" on November 4, 2016, following the success of "Caroline".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On November 15, 2016, Aminé performed  "Caroline" on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Jimmy_Fallon" title="The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 9, 2017, Aminé released a new single titled, "REDMERCEDES".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The music video for the song was released on April 7, 2017, through his Vevo channel on YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The song had been praised for being influenced by early 2000s rap, such as songs by <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The official <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remix</a> of the song features vocals from Missy Elliott and <a href="/wiki/AJ_Tracey" title="AJ Tracey">AJ Tracey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The remix was released to <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> on May 26, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Aminé went on to release another single on the same day titled, "Heebiejeebies" which features vocals from <a href="/wiki/Kehlani" title="Kehlani">Kehlani</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aminé was named as one of the ten members of <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL's</a></i> "2017 Freshman Class" on June 13, 2017, alongside rappers <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a>, <a href="/wiki/PnB_Rock" title="PnB Rock">PnB Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Playboi_Carti" title="Playboi Carti">Playboi Carti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ugly_God" title="Ugly God">Ugly God</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyle_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kyle (rapper)">Kyle</a>, <a href="/wiki/MadeinTYO" title="MadeinTYO">MadeinTYO</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kamaiyah" title="Kamaiyah">Kamaiyah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kap_G" title="Kap G">Kap G</a>, and <a href="/wiki/XXXTentacion" title="XXXTentacion">XXXTentacion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Aminé went on to release the single "Turf" three days later.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 22, 2017, Aminé announced and revealed the album's title and cover of his debut studio album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Good_for_You_(album)" title="Good for You (album)">Good for You</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Leading up to the release of the album, Aminé released the singles "Blinds" and "Wedding Crashers" featuring vocals from rapper <a href="/wiki/Offset_(rapper)" title="Offset (rapper)">Offset</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> <i>Good for You</i> was released on July 28, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-GFYAM_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GFYAM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The album debuted at number 31 on the US <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a></i> chart with first-week sales of 13,000 copies first week.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Following the release of <i>Good for You</i>, Aminé released the single "Squeeze" on October 21, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2018.E2.80.932020:_OnePointFive_and_Limbo"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–2020:_OnePointFive_and_Limbo">2018–2020: <i>OnePointFive</i> and <i>Limbo</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amin%C3%A9_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2018–2020: OnePointFive and Limbo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/OnePointFive" title="OnePointFive">OnePointFive</a> and <a href="/wiki/Limbo_(Amin%C3%A9_album)" title="Limbo (Aminé album)">Limbo (Aminé album)</a></div>
<p>On January 16, 2018, Aminé was featured on <a href="/wiki/Rejjie_Snow" title="Rejjie Snow">Rejjie Snow</a>'s single titled "Egyptian Luvr".<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Aminé later released the single "Campfire" featuring <a href="/wiki/Injury_Reserve" title="Injury Reserve">Injury Reserve</a> on April 6.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> After teasing a 2018 project release, the project, titled <i><a href="/wiki/OnePointFive" title="OnePointFive">OnePointFive</a></i>, was announced on August 14, 2018, alongside the cover art, tracklist and its release date. The album was released to the public on August 15.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> He was later a part of the <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man:_Into_the_Spider-Verse_(soundtrack)" title="Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (soundtrack)">soundtrack</a> for the 2018 movie, <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man:_Into_the_Spider-Verse" title="Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse">Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse</a></i>, with the song, "Invincible" on December 14.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 26, 2020, Aminé released the single, "Shimmy".<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> On May 29, 2020, Aminé released the single "Riri".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> On July 6, 2020, Aminé released the single "Compensating" featuring <a href="/wiki/Young_Thug" title="Young Thug">Young Thug</a>, whilst announcing and revealing the cover art and release date for his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Limbo_(Amin%C3%A9_album)" title="Limbo (Aminé album)">Limbo</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On August 7, 2020, <i>Limbo</i> was released.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2021.E2.80.93present:_TwoPointFive"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2021–present:_TwoPointFive">2021–present: <i>TwoPointFive</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amin%C3%A9_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2021–present: TwoPointFive">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 25, 2021, Aminé released the single, called "Charmander".<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On November 3, Aminé announced on Instagram and Twitter that his mixtape, titled TwoPointFive, would release the following day on Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The following day Aminé would reveal the tracklist and cover art for the mixtape<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On November 5, TwoPointFive was released.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 7, 2023, Aminé and <a href="/wiki/Kaytranada" title="Kaytranada">Kaytranada</a> released "4eva" featuring <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, the first single from their <a href="/wiki/Kaytramin%C3%A9" title="Kaytraminé">joint album</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amin%C3%A9_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aminé currently lives in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Aminé has listed <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino</a> as his favorite director;<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>   he wants to branch out into film directing  and fashion.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aminé had a romantic relationship with singer <a href="/wiki/Kehlani" title="Kehlani">Kehlani</a>, which he mentions in his 2017 song "DR. WHOEVER".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_views">Political views</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amin%C3%A9_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Political views">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Aminé is an open critic of former <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">U.S. President</a> <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> During Aminé's television debut on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Jimmy_Fallon" title="The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a></i> on November 15, 2016, Aminé performed a remixed version of the song "Caroline". At the end of the performance, Aminé performed a verse dedicated to the results of the <a href="/wiki/2016_United_States_presidential_election" title="2016 United States presidential election">2016 United States presidential election</a> saying the lines:
</p>
<blockquote><p><a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">9/11</a>, a day that we never forgetting, 11/9, a day that we all regrettin', If my president is Trump then it's relevant enough to talk 'bout it on TV and not give a... I'm black, and I'm proud my skin is brown and I'm loud, everybody love it when a rapper tells some lies well that ain't me, homie, I guess that's a surprise America wanna act all happy and holy but deep down inside they're like <a href="/wiki/Brad_Pitt" title="Brad Pitt">Brad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Jolie" title="Angelina Jolie">Jolie</a>, Caroline divine and I won't get specific, Club Banana the illest and it's too terrific, You can never make America great again, all you ever did was make this country hate again.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>When speaking about Trump's immigration policy during the 2016 presidential campaign, Aminé said, "My parents are immigrants to this country, they came to this country for a better opportunity just like everyone else."<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
