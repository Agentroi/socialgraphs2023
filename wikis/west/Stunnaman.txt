<p><b>The Pack</b> was a hip hop group formed in <a href="/wiki/Berkeley,_California" title="Berkeley, California">Berkeley, California</a> in 2004. The group consisted of <a href="/wiki/Lil_B" title="Lil B">Lil B</a> (Brandon McCartney), Young L (Lloyd Omadhebo), Stunnaman (Keith Jenkins), and Lil Uno (Damonte Johnson). They all recorded together for over a year at Young L's home studio where they released their first two mixtapes <i><a href="/wiki/Wolfpack_Muzik_Vol._1" class="mw-redirect" title="Wolfpack Muzik Vol. 1">Wolfpack Muzik Vol. 1</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Wolfpack_Muzik_Vol._2" class="mw-redirect" title="Wolfpack Muzik Vol. 2">Wolfpack Muzik Vol. 2</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Pack expanded their fanbase and eventually got signed to a major label, when <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too Short</a> signed The Pack to his <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>-distributed label Up All Nite Records. The Pack is usually known for their skateboard raps and the sexually explicit content of many of their songs. The group is best known for the track "Vans". The Pack has also collaborated with musicians such as <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Boy" title="Soulja Boy">Soulja Boy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dev_(singer)" title="Dev (singer)">Dev</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Cataracs" title="The Cataracs">The Cataracs</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Pack_(group)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Skateboards_2_Scrapers_EP_and_Based_Boys_.282006.E2.80.932008.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Skateboards_2_Scrapers_EP_and_Based_Boys_(2006–2008)"><i>Skateboards 2 Scrapers</i> EP and <i>Based Boys</i> (2006–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Pack_(group)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Skateboards 2 Scrapers EP and Based Boys (2006–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Soon after, The Pack created 2006's sneaker anthem, "Vans" and released it via their MySpace page. The song was heard by veteran Oakland rapper <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too Short</a> who then signed them to his Up All Nite Records imprint via <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>. The Pack's hit song "Vans" was number 23 on the top 30 hip hop charts, played on 65 hip hop stations nationwide on 35 pop stations nationwide. It was also listed at No.5 on <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s "Best songs of 2006".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The song featured a chorus of "Got my Vans on, but they look like sneakers; (You) wearing coke whites but my Vans look cleaner." The music video for "Vans" aired on <a href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Entertainment Television">BET</a>, but <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>, and <a href="/wiki/MTV_Jams" class="mw-redirect" title="MTV Jams">MTV Jams</a>, refused to show the video because it promoted a consumer product. However, MTV aired an edited version of the video, in which the word "Vans" was censored throughout the song.<sup id="cite_ref-Music_Filter_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Music_Filter-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The Pack released a seven track EP in 2006 entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Skateboards_2_Scrapers" title="Skateboards 2 Scrapers">Skateboards 2 Scrapers</a></i>, which included hits such as "Vans" and "I'm Shinin".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, The Pack put out their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Based_Boys" title="Based Boys">Based Boys</a></i>, which included the single "In My Car".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In early 2008, it was announced that The Pack was dropped from <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>/<a href="/wiki/Zomba_Label_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Zomba Label Group">Zomba Label Group</a> due to their album's lackluster sales. The Pack recently signed with Los Angeles-based Indie Pop which is home to other artists such as <a href="/wiki/The_Cataracs" title="The Cataracs">The Cataracs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dev_(singer)" title="Dev (singer)">Dev</a>, and Bobby Brackins. In November 2010, Lil B and Young L were featured on the cover of the publication The FADER, in its 71st issue.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Wolfpack_Party_.282009.E2.80.932010.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Wolfpack_Party_(2009–2010)"><i>Wolfpack Party</i> (2009–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Pack_(group)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wolfpack Party (2009–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Pack began recording again throughout 2009. They released the mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pack_Is_Back" title="The Pack Is Back">The Pack Is Back</a></i> in December 2009, their first set of material in a year.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
Their second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Wolfpack_Party" title="Wolfpack Party">Wolfpack Party</a></i>, was released on August 24, 2010<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and includes the group's newest single "Wolfpack Party," produced by <a href="/wiki/The_Cataracs" title="The Cataracs">The Cataracs</a>.
</p>
