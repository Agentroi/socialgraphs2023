<p><b>Tramayne Rayel Thompson</b> (born October 22, 1979),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known by his stage name <b>Shade Sheist</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood, California</a>. He began his career in 2000 by contributing the single "<a href="/wiki/Where_I_Wanna_Be_(Shade_Sheist_song)" title="Where I Wanna Be (Shade Sheist song)">Where I Wanna Be</a>" to a compilation executive produced by himself and local producer <a href="/wiki/Damizza" title="Damizza">Damizza</a>. After writing and recording tracks for other artists, television, film and video game soundtracks, Shade Sheist released his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Informal_Introduction" title="Informal Introduction">Informal Introduction</a></i>, featuring "Where I Wanna Be" in 2002 under <a href="/wiki/Universal_Records_(1995)" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Records (1995)">Universal Records</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shade_Sheist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thompson was born in <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood, California</a> and grew up in both Inglewood and neighboring city <a href="/wiki/South_Los_Angeles" title="South Los Angeles">South Central Los Angeles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He started writing music around the age of 10 and formed a performing group with his best friends that won first and second place prizes at local talent shows produced by <a href="/wiki/American_Society_of_Composers,_Authors_and_Publishers" title="American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers">ASCAP</a>. He worked with producers <a href="/wiki/Johnny_%22J%22" title="Johnny &quot;J&quot;">Johnny "J"</a>, Howie Hersh and <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_King" title="Ronnie King">Ronnie King</a> on his early recordings.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shade_Sheist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shade Sheist performed from 1994 to 1999 as Shady Montage, signed to <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Records" title="Hollywood Records">Hollywood Records</a>. Two singles he released under this moniker, "Shake You Down" and "Leaving You Again" were well received and aired on major market radio stations nationally. At one point during this period he became label mates and recorded with <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As a teen, he appeared in several music videos including <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">2Pac</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/So_Many_Tears" title="So Many Tears">So Many Tears</a>" and <a href="/wiki/South_Circle" title="South Circle">South Circle</a>'s "Attitudes". Shade Sheist graduated from <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Hamilton_High_School_(Los_Angeles)" title="Alexander Hamilton High School (Los Angeles)">Alexander Hamilton High School</a> in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He first performed commercially as Shade Sheist in 2000 on the song "If You Were My Bitch" from the compilation <i>Irv Gotti Presents: The Murderers</i>. That year, he also appeared on the song "It's Your Life" from <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>'s triple Platinum-selling album <i><a href="/wiki/Rule_3:36" title="Rule 3:36">Rule 3:36</a></i>. Additionally, Shade Sheist met <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>-based producer <a href="/wiki/Damizza" title="Damizza">Damizza</a>, who worked as senior director for artist relations at Los Angeles <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> station <a href="/wiki/KPWR" title="KPWR">Power 106</a> and released a various-artists compilation titled <i>Where I Wanna Be</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Shade Sheist, <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a> performed the title single, which peaked at No. 60 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> chart but at No. 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Songs" title="Hot Rap Songs">Hot Rap Singles</a>. Shade Sheist and <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a> collaborated again on the track "Cali Diseaz" on the soundtrack to the 2001 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fast_and_the_Furious_(2001_film)" title="The Fast and the Furious (2001 film)">The Fast and the Furious</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Damizza recorded the track "Bad News" with Shade Sheist and N.U.N.E. for the soundtrack to the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> television drama <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Angel_(American_TV_series)" title="Dark Angel (American TV series)">Dark Angel</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2002, Shade Sheist released his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Informal_Introduction" title="Informal Introduction">Informal Introduction</a></i> through a deal between <a href="/wiki/MCA_Records" title="MCA Records">MCA Records</a> and his own Baby Ree label. <i>Informal Introduction</i> featured production by <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a>, Damizza and <a href="/wiki/KayGee" title="KayGee">KayGee</a> of <a href="/wiki/Naughty_by_Nature" title="Naughty by Nature">Naughty by Nature</a>. The singles included "Money Owners", "Wake Up", and "John Doe". The "Where I Wanna Be" music video featured cameos from N.U.N.E., <a href="/wiki/TQ_(singer)" title="TQ (singer)">TQ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irv_Gotti" title="Irv Gotti">Irv Gotti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_D.O.C." title="The D.O.C.">The D.O.C.</a> The "Money Owners" music video featured a special appearance from <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, who worked in the studio room next door to Shade Sheist during the recording of the <i>Informal Introduction</i> album. Shade Sheist stated that Dr. Dre was a big help during the creation of his debut album.
</p><p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> asserted that Power 106 played Shade Sheist's singles more than any other <a href="/wiki/Urban_contemporary" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban contemporary">urban</a> radio station in the U.S. and reported that Peter Hart of <a href="/wiki/Fairness_%26_Accuracy_in_Reporting" title="Fairness &amp; Accuracy in Reporting">Fairness &amp; Accuracy in Reporting</a> brought up <a href="/wiki/Conflict_of_interest" title="Conflict of interest">conflict of interest</a> issues given that <a href="/wiki/Emmis_Communications" title="Emmis Communications">Emmis Communications</a> funded both the radio station and Shade Sheist's label Baby Ree.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Power 106 decided to disclose its ties to Shade Sheist after the <i>LA Times</i> brought up the issue.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Emmis president Rick Cummings stated that his station played Shade Sheist's music based on listener tastes.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Shade Sheist returned in 2004 with the single "What Would You Do" featuring <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a>. The track appeared on Shade Sheist's second album <i>Before the Waitin' Before the Hatin'</i> which originally released in 2005 and re-released in late 2006 with new tracks included.
</p><p>In 2008, Shade Sheist and N.U.N.E. set off on an international tour that would cover two years and several continents.
</p><p>In 2010, Shade Sheist released a new album with N.U.N.E., <i>Movin Units</i> which was his first original studio release in four years. The album featured the single "Movin Like a Boss" which spawned several international remixes.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 1, 2013, Shade Sheist released a brand-new seven-track EP titled <i>Blackops: Lite</i>, which featured himself on production as well as vocals. The EP served as a preview of his upcoming full-length LP of the same title. Shade Sheist simultaneously unveiled his Gfunkisforever label with the release of the EP.
</p>
