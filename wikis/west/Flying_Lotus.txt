<p><b>Steven D. Bingley-Ellison</b> (born October 7, 1983), known by his stage name <b>Flying Lotus</b> or sometimes <b>FlyLo</b>, is an American record producer, <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Filmmaker" class="mw-redirect" title="Filmmaker">filmmaker</a> and rapper from Los Angeles. He is also the founder of the record label <a href="/wiki/Brainfeeder" title="Brainfeeder">Brainfeeder</a>.
</p><p>Flying Lotus has released seven studio albums‍&#8212;‌<i><a href="/wiki/1983_(album)" title="1983 (album)">1983</a></i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_(Flying_Lotus_album)" title="Los Angeles (Flying Lotus album)">Los Angeles</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/Cosmogramma" title="Cosmogramma">Cosmogramma</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Until_the_Quiet_Comes" title="Until the Quiet Comes">Until the Quiet Comes</a></i> (2012), <i><a href="/wiki/You%27re_Dead!" title="You&#39;re Dead!">You're Dead!</a></i> (2014), <i><a href="/wiki/Flamagra" title="Flamagra">Flamagra</a></i> (2019) and <i><a href="/wiki/Yasuke_(Flying_Lotus_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Yasuke (Flying Lotus album)">Yasuke</a></i> (2021)‍&#8212;‌to critical acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He has produced much of the <a href="/wiki/Bumper_music" title="Bumper music">bumper music</a> on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">Adult Swim</a> programming block.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He also contributed remixes for fellow Plug Research artists including <a href="/wiki/Mia_Doi_Todd" title="Mia Doi Todd">Mia Doi Todd</a>.
</p><p>In 2012, Flying Lotus began rapping under the persona <b>Captain Murphy</b>, based on the <i><a href="/wiki/Sealab_2021" title="Sealab 2021">Sealab 2021</a></i> character of the same name. Ellison kept this fact a secret for several months, finally revealing his identity several weeks after the release of his first rap mixtape: <i><a href="/wiki/Duality_(mixtape)" title="Duality (mixtape)">Duality</a></i> (2012).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flying_Lotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Flying Lotus was born Steven Ellison on October 7, 1983, in Los Angeles, California.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He is the grandnephew of the late <a href="/wiki/Jazz_pianist" class="mw-redirect" title="Jazz pianist">jazz pianist</a> <a href="/wiki/Alice_Coltrane" title="Alice Coltrane">Alice Coltrane</a>,  whose husband was <a href="/wiki/Saxophonist" class="mw-redirect" title="Saxophonist">saxophonist</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Coltrane" title="John Coltrane">John Coltrane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, he is the grandson of singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_McLeod" title="Marilyn McLeod">Marilyn McLeod</a>, who is notable for having written <a href="/wiki/Diana_Ross" title="Diana Ross">Diana Ross</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Love_Hangover" title="Love Hangover">Love Hangover</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Freda_Payne" title="Freda Payne">Freda Payne</a>'s "I Get High (On Your Memory)" and is <a href="/wiki/Alice_Coltrane" title="Alice Coltrane">Alice Coltrane</a>'s sister.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> His great aunt Alice has been called by one writer "the biggest influence on Ellison's music".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> A DNA test revealed that Ellison is a descendant of the <a href="/wiki/Tikar_people" title="Tikar people">Tikar people</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ellison attended <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Film_School" title="Los Angeles Film School">Los Angeles Film School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Art_University" title="Academy of Art University">Academy of Art University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flying_Lotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.932007:_Adult_Swim.2C_1983_and_Warp_Records"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–2007:_Adult_Swim,_1983_and_Warp_Records">2006–2007: Adult Swim, <i>1983</i> and Warp Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flying_Lotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006–2007: Adult Swim, 1983 and Warp Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, while at his mother's house, Ellison saw an advertisement on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">Adult Swim</a> programming block asking for song submissions. He sent some in, under the name Flying Lotus (a moniker inspired by <a href="/wiki/Lucid_dreaming" class="mw-redirect" title="Lucid dreaming">lucid dreaming</a>) and was accepted.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Around this time, he was <a href="/wiki/Intern" class="mw-redirect" title="Intern">interning</a> at the pioneering hip hop label <a href="/wiki/Stones_Throw_Records" title="Stones Throw Records">Stones Throw Records</a>. He released his debut studio album <a href="/wiki/1983_(album)" title="1983 (album)"><i>1983</i></a> on October 3, 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, Ellison participated in that year's annual <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Music_Academy" title="Red Bull Music Academy">Red Bull Music Academy</a>, which took place in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne,_Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne, Australia">Melbourne, Australia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, he announced on CSU-Fullerton's Titan Radio that he signed with <a href="/wiki/Warp_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warp Records">Warp Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He released his debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a>, the six-track <i><a href="/wiki/Reset_(Flying_Lotus_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Reset (Flying Lotus album)">Reset</a></i> on October 1, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Soon afterwards, he became one of the label's cornerstone artists and released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_(Flying_Lotus_album)" title="Los Angeles (Flying Lotus album)">Los Angeles</a></i> on June 10, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.932009:_Los_Angeles_and_Brainfeeder"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–2009:_Los_Angeles_and_Brainfeeder">2008–2009: <i>Los Angeles</i> and Brainfeeder</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flying_Lotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2008–2009: Los Angeles and Brainfeeder">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2008 Flying Lotus released <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_(Flying_Lotus_album)" title="Los Angeles (Flying Lotus album)">Los Angeles</a></i>, a 17-track album dedicated to his home city. Telling Quietus on the subject of <a href="/wiki/J_Dilla" title="J Dilla">J Dilla</a>'s influence on his music: "I love Dilla and who knows where this beat thing would be without him."
</p><p>2008 saw Ellison enter a stage of hyper-productivity, as he followed up the Los Angeles record with collaborative tracks with <a href="/wiki/Samiyam" title="Samiyam">Samiyam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gonjasufi" title="Gonjasufi">Gonjasufi</a>, a limited white label run of remixes ("<a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_(Flying_Lotus_album)" title="Los Angeles (Flying Lotus album)">Camel</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Donuts_(album)" title="Donuts (album)">Lightworks</a>", <a href="/wiki/R2-D2" title="R2-D2">R2-D2</a> sound effects, "<a href="/wiki/Madvillainy" title="Madvillainy">Shadows of Tomorrow</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Promiscuous_(song)" title="Promiscuous (song)">Promiscuous</a>") called Shhh!, and a series of LA inspired EPs. Each of the La EPs features remixes and unreleased tracks from his Los Angeles Album. The third in that series (titled L.A. EP 3 X 3), marked a new atmospheric style in his sound. The same year, Flying Lotus also remixed "Reckoner" from <i><a href="/wiki/In_Rainbows" title="In Rainbows">In Rainbows</a>,</i> an album by <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">Radiohead</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932011:_Cosmogramma_and_collaborations"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2011:_Cosmogramma_and_collaborations">2010–2011: <i>Cosmogramma</i> and collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flying_Lotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010–2011: Cosmogramma and collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Cosmogramma" title="Cosmogramma">Cosmogramma</a></i>, was released in the UK on May 3, 2010, and in the US on May 4, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In January 2011, <i>Cosmogramma</i> won in the Dance/Electronica Album category in the 10th Annual <a href="/wiki/Independent_Music_Awards_(IMAs)" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent Music Awards (IMAs)">Independent Music Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The multi-award-winning <a href="/wiki/Cosmogramma" title="Cosmogramma">Cosmogramma</a> was a hard-hitting <a href="/wiki/Afrofuturism" title="Afrofuturism">afrofuturistic</a> shrine to soul, hip-hop, jazz and IDM but, with more emphasis on a lyrical message than ever before. The album was accompanied by live instrumentation (<a href="/wiki/Thundercat_(musician)" title="Thundercat (musician)">Thundercat</a> on bass, <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Atwood-Ferguson" title="Miguel Atwood-Ferguson">Miguel Atwood-Ferguson</a> on strings, Rebekah Raff on harp) and live vocalists (<a href="/wiki/Thom_Yorke" title="Thom Yorke">Thom Yorke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laura_Darlington" class="mw-redirect" title="Laura Darlington">Laura Darlington</a>) – all picked to help communicate the spiritual musical lineage of Ellison's family (<a href="/wiki/Ravi_Coltrane" title="Ravi Coltrane">Ravi Coltrane</a>, himself, played tenor sax). Stephen Bruner, aka Thundercat, who is featured extensively on Cosmogramma, would later become a large part of future albums by Flying Lotus.
</p><p>In 2010, Flying Lotus collaborated with the <a href="/wiki/Ann_Arbor_Film_Festival" title="Ann Arbor Film Festival">Ann Arbor Film Festival</a> in the performance of a live scoring of the 1962 <a href="/wiki/Avant-garde" title="Avant-garde">avant-garde</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Heaven_and_Earth_Magic" title="Heaven and Earth Magic">Heaven and Earth Magic</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> In a post-viewing interview with the audience, Flying Lotus said that he was unsure whether or not a recording of the performance (or a recreation of it) would be publicly released, but he would be enthusiastic towards similar projects in the future. He was chosen by <a href="/wiki/Battles_(band)" title="Battles (band)">Battles</a> to perform at the <a href="/wiki/All_Tomorrow%27s_Parties_(music_festival)" class="mw-redirect" title="All Tomorrow&#39;s Parties (music festival)">ATP Nightmare Before Christmas</a> festival that it co-curated in December 2011 in Minehead, England, UK.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2010, Flying Lotus released "<a href="/wiki/Pattern%2BGrid_World" title="Pattern+Grid World">Pattern+Grid World</a>", an 8 track EP featuring Thundercat on bass &amp; art by Theo Ellsworth. The Track <i>Camera Day</i> was used in the <a href="/wiki/Killer_Mike" title="Killer Mike">Killer Mike</a> song <i>Swimming</i>, which was released as part of the <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">Adult Swim</a> Singles Series.
</p><p>In January 2011, Flying Lotus won the 10th Annual Independent Music Awards for his video "MmmHmm" in the Short-Form Video category.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was reported in 2011 that Flying Lotus would be collaborating with R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a> on new material for her next album,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> and planned to remix one of Radiohead's songs from <i><a href="/wiki/The_King_of_Limbs" title="The King of Limbs">The King of Limbs</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932013:_Until_the_Quiet_Comes_and_Captain_Murphy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2013:_Until_the_Quiet_Comes_and_Captain_Murphy">2012–2013: <i>Until the Quiet Comes</i> and Captain Murphy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flying_Lotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2012–2013: Until the Quiet Comes and Captain Murphy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Flying_Lotus,_Bonnaroo_2012_(7373612680).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Flying_Lotus%2C_Bonnaroo_2012_%287373612680%29.jpg/220px-Flying_Lotus%2C_Bonnaroo_2012_%287373612680%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Flying_Lotus%2C_Bonnaroo_2012_%287373612680%29.jpg/330px-Flying_Lotus%2C_Bonnaroo_2012_%287373612680%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Flying_Lotus%2C_Bonnaroo_2012_%287373612680%29.jpg/440px-Flying_Lotus%2C_Bonnaroo_2012_%287373612680%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3846" data-file-height="2539" /></a><figcaption>Flying Lotus performing at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Bonnaroo_Music_Festival" title="2012 Bonnaroo Music Festival">2012 Bonnaroo Music Festival</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>With an appreciation for hip hop, but no established work, artists like <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a> (also aficionados of Adult Swim), <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Mob" title="ASAP Mob">A$AP Mob</a>, <a href="/wiki/SpaceGhostPurrp" title="SpaceGhostPurrp">Spaceghostpurrp</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shabazz_Palaces" title="Shabazz Palaces">Shabazz Palaces</a> inspired him to directly participate, and he began production work for Odd Future's <a href="/wiki/Hodgy_Beats" class="mw-redirect" title="Hodgy Beats">Hodgy Beats</a>' <a href="/wiki/Untitled_Hodgy_Beats_EP" class="mw-redirect" title="Untitled Hodgy Beats EP">Untitled EP</a>. This led to the moment in which he decided to produce more in the genre.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Throughout the summer of 2012, Captain Murphy began to make more appearances, dressed in Luchadore Ensemble.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In August 2012, Flying Lotus announced a multimedia project with filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Miwa_Matreyek" title="Miwa Matreyek">Miwa Matreyek</a>, which is to be titled <i>The Mapping of Countries Yet to Come</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Flying Lotus produced rapper <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>'s song "SDS'" for his album <i><a href="/wiki/Watching_Movies_with_the_Sound_Off" title="Watching Movies with the Sound Off">Watching Movies with the Sound Off</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> He also created a song for the <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time" title="Adventure Time">Adventure Time</a></i> entitled "About that time//A glitch is a glitch".<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Behind closed doors, though, the work for his fourth album <i><a href="/wiki/Until_the_Quiet_Comes" title="Until the Quiet Comes">Until the Quiet Comes</a></i> was pretty much done. A year prior, Ellison had worked with Ann Arbor Festival to live score a surreal and <a href="/wiki/Avant-garde_music" title="Avant-garde music">avant-garde</a> 50s animation Heaven and Earth Magic, and a fascination with dreamlike states had continued into this record. Until The Quiet Comes continued his creative relationship with Radiohead's <a href="/wiki/Thom_Yorke" title="Thom Yorke">Thom Yorke</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Jonny_Greenwood" title="Jonny Greenwood">Jonny Greenwood</a>, Niki Randa, <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laura_Darlington" class="mw-redirect" title="Laura Darlington">Laura Darlington</a> and, of course, Thundercat, and was cinematically captured in a short film by filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Kahlil_Joseph_(filmmaker)" title="Kahlil Joseph (filmmaker)">Kahlil Joseph</a>.
</p><p>In late 2012, less than two months after the release of his last record, a website started circulating online: www.captainmurphy.xxx. Press speculated who the rapper was, with guessing being centered around the Odd Future Crew. The site hosted <i>Duality</i>, a 34-minute short film mixtape, that comprised both new material and tracks that had been leaking all summer.
</p><p>In April 2013, Flying Lotus announced that he has his own radio station called FlyLo FM in the <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a></i>, saying there would be "a ton of new songs and information, including rapping a new Captain Murphy song produced by <a href="/wiki/Hudson_Mohawke" title="Hudson Mohawke">Hudson Mohawke</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The radio station includes songs from <a href="/wiki/Clams_Casino_(musician)" title="Clams Casino (musician)">Clams Casino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aphex_Twin" title="Aphex Twin">Aphex Twin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hudson_Mohawke" title="Hudson Mohawke">Hudson Mohawke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dabrye" title="Dabrye">Dabrye</a>, Thundercat, <a href="/wiki/Machinedrum" title="Machinedrum">Machinedrum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Rashad" title="DJ Rashad">DJ Rashad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Simon_Neale" title="Simon Neale">Shadow Child</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> In November 2014, Flying Lotus collaborated with <a href="/wiki/MF_DOOM" class="mw-redirect" title="MF DOOM">MF DOOM</a> on the track "Masquatch" for the reissued copy of the video game.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> The two collaborated again on the track "Lunch Break" for an updated version of <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_Online" title="Grand Theft Auto Online">Grand Theft Auto Online</a></i> in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 17, 2013, Flying Lotus announced on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> that he had been nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Video_Music_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Video Music Awards">VMA</a> for the song "Tiny Tortures".<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.932015:_You.27re_Dead.21"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2015:_You're_Dead!">2014–2015: <i>You're Dead!</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flying_Lotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2014–2015: You&#039;re Dead!">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 15, 2014, Flying Lotus posted on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> a photo of vinyl test pressings labeled "LP #5".<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On July 22, he announced that his fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/You%27re_Dead!" title="You&#39;re Dead!">You're Dead!</a></i>, would be released in the UK on October 6, 2014, and in the US on October 7, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The album features guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Herbie_Hancock" title="Herbie Hancock">Herbie Hancock</a>.
</p><p>On August 15, 2014, Flying Lotus released a new song called "Cosplay" with his alias Captain Murphy.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 30, 2014, Flying Lotus scored the short film <i>A Portrait of Noomi Rapace</i>, starring actress <a href="/wiki/Noomi_Rapace" title="Noomi Rapace">Noomi Rapace</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Aitor_Throup" title="Aitor Throup">Aitor Throup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the summer of 2015, Flying Lotus appeared at many summer music festivals including the <a href="/wiki/Bonnaroo_Music_and_Arts_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Bonnaroo Music and Arts Festival">Bonnaroo Music and Arts Festival</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Governors_Ball_Music_Festival" title="Governors Ball Music Festival">Governors Ball Music Festival</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Glastonbury_Festival" title="Glastonbury Festival">Glastonbury Festival</a>. His performances received critical acclaim.
</p><p>He appeared alongside Thundercat on Lamar's album <i><a href="/wiki/To_Pimp_a_Butterfly" title="To Pimp a Butterfly">To Pimp a Butterfly</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> In July 2015, Flying Lotus, made his national television debut on <i><a href="/wiki/Why%3F_with_Hannibal_Buress" title="Why? with Hannibal Buress">Why? with Hannibal Buress</a></i>, as the show's in-studio <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lotus received two <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nominations at the <a href="/wiki/58th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="58th Annual Grammy Awards">58th ceremony</a>: <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Dance_Recording" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording">Best Dance Recording</a> for his song "<a href="/wiki/Never_Catch_Me" title="Never Catch Me">Never Catch Me</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Album_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Album of the Year">Album of the Year</a> for his credits as producer on Lamar's <i>To Pimp a Butterfly.</i><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932018:_Kuso"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2018:_Kuso">2016–2018: <i>Kuso</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flying_Lotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2016–2018: Kuso">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 5, 2016, Ellison announced the start of Brainfeeder film division.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Shortly after, at the Sundance NEXT festival, he premiered a short film titled <i>Royal</i>. It was later revealed to be part of his feature film directorial debut, <i>Kuso</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Kuso_(film)" title="Kuso (film)">Kuso</a></i> would star <a href="/wiki/Hannibal_Buress" title="Hannibal Buress">Hannibal Buress</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tim_Heidecker" title="Tim Heidecker">Tim Heidecker</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Firth_(animator)" title="David Firth (animator)">David Firth</a> among others.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> <i>Kuso</i> featured new music from Ellison himself under both the Flying Lotus and Captain Murphy names, <a href="/wiki/Aphex_Twin" title="Aphex Twin">Aphex Twin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busdriver" title="Busdriver">Busdriver</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Thundercat_(musician)" title="Thundercat (musician)">Thundercat</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> <i>Kuso</i> premiered at the Sundance Film Festival in 2017. On June 6, 2017, Ellison announced that <i>Kuso</i> would be released via the horror video streaming service <a href="/wiki/Shudder_(streaming_service)" title="Shudder (streaming service)">Shudder</a>, on July 21, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> <i>Kuso</i> was also confirmed a theatrical release in New York City and Los Angeles. Addressing the responsibility of the wide release of <i>Kuso</i>, on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>, Ellison praised Shudder, explaining he found it to be a suitable home for the film's release.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 2, 2017, Ellison released a music video for a new song of his, "Post Requisite", and is "currently finishing his next studio album on Warp".<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> He also wrote the soundtrack for the 2017 short film <i><a href="/wiki/Blade_Runner_Black_Out_2022" title="Blade Runner Black Out 2022">Blade Runner Black Out 2022</a></i> and has contributed music to the 2019 <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Carole_%26_Tuesday" title="Carole &amp; Tuesday">Carole &amp; Tuesday</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2019:_Flamagra">2019: <i>Flamagra</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flying_Lotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2019: Flamagra">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 17, 2019, Ellison announced his sixth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Flamagra" title="Flamagra">Flamagra</a></i>, released via Warp on May 24, 2019. The first single off the album, "Fire Is Coming" featuring <a href="/wiki/David_Lynch" title="David Lynch">David Lynch</a>, was released on the same day.<sup id="cite_ref-flmgr_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flmgr-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> On May 12, 2019, a second single, "More", was released alongside a music video directed by <a href="/wiki/Shinichir%C5%8D_Watanabe" title="Shinichirō Watanabe">Shinichirō Watanabe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> <i>Flamagra (Instrumentals)</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> an instrumental version of the original LP, was released in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2021:_Yasuke">2021: <i>Yasuke</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flying_Lotus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2021: Yasuke">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2021, Flying Lotus released <i>Yasuke</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> his score for the Netflix series <a href="/wiki/Yasuke_(TV_series)" title="Yasuke (TV series)">Yasuke</a> and his "full-length anime scoring debut" after a long history of contributing to other animation and film projects.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>On June 16, 2022, Ellison released two singles, "You Don't Know" and "The Room", both featuring Devin Tracy.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
