<p><b>Stefan Kendal Gordy</b> (born September 3, 1975), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Redfoo</b>, is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, record producer, and DJ best known for being part of the musical duo <a href="/wiki/LMFAO" title="LMFAO">LMFAO</a>, and for their hit songs "<a href="/wiki/Party_Rock_Anthem" title="Party Rock Anthem">Party Rock Anthem</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Sexy_and_I_Know_It" title="Sexy and I Know It">Sexy and I Know It</a>". LMFAO released two studio albums, "<a href="/wiki/Party_Rock" title="Party Rock">Party Rock</a>" in 2009, and "<a href="/wiki/Sorry_for_Party_Rocking" title="Sorry for Party Rocking">Sorry for Party Rocking</a>" in 2011. Redfoo released his debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Party_Rock_Mansion" title="Party Rock Mansion">Party Rock Mansion</a></i> in 2016.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redfoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gordy was born on September 3, 1975, to <a href="/wiki/Berry_Gordy" title="Berry Gordy">Berry Gordy Jr.</a>, the founder of the <a href="/wiki/Motown" title="Motown">Motown</a> record label,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and writer-producer Nancy Leiviska. His maternal grandfather was <a href="/wiki/Finns" title="Finns">Finnish</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His half-brother is recording artist <a href="/wiki/Rockwell_(musician)" title="Rockwell (musician)">Rockwell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He attended junior high school with <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a> and <a href="/wiki/GoonRock" title="GoonRock">GoonRock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Gordy graduated from <a href="/wiki/Palisades_Charter_High_School" title="Palisades Charter High School">Palisades Charter High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Palisades,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Palisades, California">Pacific Palisades</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  Redfoo is a former <a href="/wiki/Day_trader" class="mw-redirect" title="Day trader">day trader</a> and has been featured on business television <a href="/wiki/CNBC" title="CNBC">CNBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_Money" title="Mad Money">Mad Money</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Jim_Cramer" title="Jim Cramer">Jim Cramer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redfoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1994.E2.80.932005:_Balance_Beam"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1994–2005:_Balance_Beam">1994–2005: <i>Balance Beam</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redfoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1994–2005: Balance Beam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1994, Redfoo scored his first major production credit for the song "<a href="/wiki/Back_in_the_Day_(Ahmad_song)" title="Back in the Day (Ahmad song)">Back in the Day</a>" by Los Angeles rapper <a href="/wiki/Ahmad_(rapper)" title="Ahmad (rapper)">Ahmad</a> and co-produced seven other tracks on Ahmad's <a href="/wiki/Ahmad_(album)" title="Ahmad (album)">self-titled debut album</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Murphy_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Murphy-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 1996, he signed with Bubonic Records, and he worked in collaboration with the rapper Dre' Kroon for an album together.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The album, <i>Balance Beam</i>, was released on October 10, 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Redfoo and Kroon released two singles: "Life Is a Game Of Chess"<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and "The Freshest".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 1999, he collaborated on "Duet" with <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas" class="mw-redirect" title="The Black Eyed Peas">The Black Eyed Peas</a>, from their album <i><a href="/wiki/Behind_the_Front" title="Behind the Front">Behind the Front</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He also produced <i>Focused Daily</i> for Defari.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In 2004, <a href="/wiki/Figgkidd" title="Figgkidd">Figgkidd</a> released "I Gotta Know" as single which featured vocals from Redfoo and <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The song peaked at number 50 in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.9312:_Breakthrough_with_LMFAO"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–12:_Breakthrough_with_LMFAO">2006–12: Breakthrough with LMFAO</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redfoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006–12: Breakthrough with LMFAO">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:LMFAO_Presented_by_Debit_Mastercard_(6430209423).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/LMFAO_Presented_by_Debit_Mastercard_%286430209423%29.jpg/140px-LMFAO_Presented_by_Debit_Mastercard_%286430209423%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="140" height="210" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/LMFAO_Presented_by_Debit_Mastercard_%286430209423%29.jpg/210px-LMFAO_Presented_by_Debit_Mastercard_%286430209423%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/LMFAO_Presented_by_Debit_Mastercard_%286430209423%29.jpg/280px-LMFAO_Presented_by_Debit_Mastercard_%286430209423%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="550" data-file-height="825" /></a><figcaption>Redfoo during the <a href="/wiki/LMFAO" title="LMFAO">LMFAO</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sorry_for_Party_Rocking_Tour" title="Sorry for Party Rocking Tour">Sorry for Party Rocking Tour</a>, in 2011</figcaption></figure>
<p>He formed the electro-pop band LMFAO with his nephew <a href="/wiki/Sky_Blu_(rapper)" title="Sky Blu (rapper)">Sky Blu</a> in 2006. The duo started building a local buzz through their shows and radio play.<sup id="cite_ref-hitquarters_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hitquarters-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Once they had recorded some demos, Redfoo's friend <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a> introduced them to <a href="/wiki/Interscope" class="mw-redirect" title="Interscope">Interscope</a> head <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Iovine" title="Jimmy Iovine">Jimmy Iovine</a>, who gave the green light for them to be signed to Interscope/will.i.am Music.<sup id="cite_ref-hitquarters_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hitquarters-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> LMFAO released the debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Party_Rock" title="Party Rock">Party Rock</a></i> on July 7, 2009. The duo then recorded their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Sorry_for_Party_Rocking" title="Sorry for Party Rocking">Sorry for Party Rocking</a></i>, late in 2010 and released it on June 17, 2011, in the U.S. The first single taken from the album, "<a href="/wiki/Party_Rock_Anthem" title="Party Rock Anthem">Party Rock Anthem</a>", was released on January 1, 2011. The song is so far the most successful of their career, peaking at number one in the United States,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Canada, the UK and over ten other countries, as well as being in the top ten in many others. The third single, "<a href="/wiki/Sexy_and_I_Know_It" title="Sexy and I Know It">Sexy and I Know It</a>", was released on October 3, 2011, and reached number one on the iTunes charts worldwide and number one on the Australian and Canadian Hot 100.
</p><p>Also in 2011, LMFAO embarked on the world tour <a href="/wiki/Sorry_for_Party_Rocking_Tour" title="Sorry for Party Rocking Tour">Sorry for Party Rocking Tour</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-asiantour_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-asiantour-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On February 5, 2012, the group appeared with <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVI" title="Super Bowl XLVI">Super Bowl XLVI</a> during the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Super_Bowl_halftime_shows" title="List of Super Bowl halftime shows">Bridgestone Halftime Show</a>. Also in 2012, Redfoo was sued by a previous management company for $7 million, claiming breach of contract.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On September 21, 2012, the duo released a statement announcing their hiatus. Redfoo said, "I feel like we've been doing this for so long, five or six years", and that he and Sky Blu would not be performing together anytime soon as they would be taking their careers in different directions.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.9315:_Television_and_solo_releases"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–15:_Television_and_solo_releases">2012–15: Television and solo releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redfoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–15: Television and solo releases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Concierto_de_LMFAO_en_Barcelona_(3).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Concierto_de_LMFAO_en_Barcelona_%283%29.jpg/220px-Concierto_de_LMFAO_en_Barcelona_%283%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Concierto_de_LMFAO_en_Barcelona_%283%29.jpg/330px-Concierto_de_LMFAO_en_Barcelona_%283%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Concierto_de_LMFAO_en_Barcelona_%283%29.jpg/440px-Concierto_de_LMFAO_en_Barcelona_%283%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1050" data-file-height="701" /></a><figcaption>Redfoo performing in 2012</figcaption></figure>
<p>In December 2012, Redfoo released his first solo single "<a href="/wiki/Bring_Out_the_Bottles" title="Bring Out the Bottles">Bring Out the Bottles</a>" and appeared in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Vegas" title="Last Vegas">Last Vegas</a></i>. On April 21, 2013, Redfoo was confirmed as <a href="/wiki/Guy_Sebastian" title="Guy Sebastian">Guy Sebastian</a>'s replacement for the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(Australia_season_5)" class="mw-redirect" title="The X Factor (Australia season 5)">fifth season</a> of the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(Australian_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (Australian TV series)">Australian version of <i>The X Factor</i></a>, a singing talent television show, joining <a href="/wiki/Ronan_Keating" title="Ronan Keating">Ronan Keating</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Bassingthwaighte" title="Natalie Bassingthwaighte">Natalie Bassingthwaighte</a> and fellow debuting judge <a href="/wiki/Dannii_Minogue" title="Dannii Minogue">Dannii Minogue</a>, who replaced <a href="/wiki/Mel_B" title="Mel B">Mel B</a>. Because of commitments to <i>The X Factor</i>, he relocated to <a href="/wiki/Sydney,_Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Sydney, Australia">Sydney, Australia</a> in 2013. In September, Redfoo released a new hit single titled "Let's Get Ridiculous", which he premiered on <i>The X Factor Australia</i>, performing live during the third live show. The single debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">ARIA Singles Chart</a> and was certified four times platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Australian Recording Industry Association">Australian Recording Industry Association</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On October 19, 2013, Redfoo released the pilot episode of his new comedy web series, <i>Behind the Speedo</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> A second episode was released on March 25, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, Redfoo returned for the sixth season of <i>The X Factor Australia</i>. In June 2014, he released a new single titled "Where The Baes At" with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eric_D-lux&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Eric D-lux (page does not exist)">Eric D-lux</a> and Rio. In August 2014, Redfoo released a new single titled "<a href="/wiki/New_Thang" title="New Thang">New Thang</a>"<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and peaked at number three in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-AUS_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AUS-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The promotional single "Like Ya Just Don't Care" was released on September 2, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The single is part of his solo debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Party_Rock_Mansion" title="Party Rock Mansion">Party Rock Mansion</a></i>, but under the title "Keep Shining", the first track on the album. In October 2014, Redfoo was featured on <a href="/wiki/Play-N-Skillz" title="Play-N-Skillz">Play-N-Skillz</a> song and video "<a href="/wiki/Literally_I_Can%27t" title="Literally I Can&#39;t">Literally I Can't</a>" along with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Jon" title="Lil Jon">Lil Jon</a> and Enertia McFly. The song attracted widespread criticism for being misogynistic.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On February 24, 2015, Redfoo was announced as one of the celebrities who will compete on the <a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._season_20)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 20)">20th season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. TV series)">Dancing with the Stars</a></i>. He was partnered with professional dancer <a href="/wiki/Emma_Slater" title="Emma Slater">Emma Slater</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> He had been a guest judge on the <a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._season_18)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 18)">18th season</a> of the show, and is the first guest judge ever to return as a contestant. He and Slater were the first couple eliminated from the competition on March 23, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> On May 1, 2015, Redfoo announced that he would not be returning for the seventh season of <i>The X Factor Australia</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.93present:_Party_Rock_Mansion"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–present:_Party_Rock_Mansion">2016–present: <i>Party Rock Mansion</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redfoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2016–present: Party Rock Mansion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Redfoo announced his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Party_Rock_Mansion" title="Party Rock Mansion">Party Rock Mansion</a></i>, near the end of December 2015. He released a promotional single, "Lights Out", on January 15, 2016. The album was released on March 18, 2016, receiving mixed reviews from music critics, as well as being a commercial failure, with only 144 copies sold throughout Australia in its first week of release.
</p><p>He has since released the singles "Brand New Day" and "Sock It to Ya" in 2017, and "Everything I Need" in 2018.
</p><p>In 2020, his previously released single "New Thang" went viral on <a href="/wiki/TikTok" title="TikTok">TikTok</a>, being used in many videos and gaining newfound traction.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2023, after being six years retired, came back with his new track "Long Live Party Rock" along with Dainjazone, which he released on his official YouTube channel on April 20.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redfoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Redfoo began dating former number one<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> ranked tennis player <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Azarenka" title="Victoria Azarenka">Victoria Azarenka</a> in 2012, but the couple split in 2014. He attended the <a href="/wiki/2012_US_Open_(tennis)" title="2012 US Open (tennis)">2012 US Open</a>, <a href="/wiki/2013_Australian_Open" title="2013 Australian Open">2013 Australian Open</a>, <a href="/wiki/2013_Wimbledon_Championships" title="2013 Wimbledon Championships">2013 Wimbledon Championships</a> and <a href="/wiki/2014_Australian_Open" title="2014 Australian Open">2014 Australian Open</a> as a member of Azarenka's player box.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Tennis_player" class="mw-redirect" title="Tennis player">tennis player</a>, he attempted to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2013_US_Open_(tennis)" title="2013 US Open (tennis)">2013 US Open</a> as a wild-card entry. He entered the USTA Northern California Sectional qualifying tournament in June and was knocked out 6–1, 6–2 in his first match.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Since 2018, he has been dating Jasmine Alkouri, a chef and road manager.
</p><p>Redfoo, through his line of Party Rock clothing, sponsored the <a href="/wiki/Red_Rock_Pro_Open" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Rock Pro Open">Red Rock Pro Open</a>, an <a href="/wiki/ITF_Women%27s_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="ITF Women&#39;s Circuit">ITF Women's Circuit</a> tournament held in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, from 2012 to 2013. Tyler Weeks, a tennis pro at the <a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_of_Las_Vegas" title="Cosmopolitan of Las Vegas">Cosmopolitan of Las Vegas</a>, mentioned to Foo that his tournament needed a new sponsor after giving him a lesson, and Foo agreed for Party Rock clothing to be the new sponsor. The event is usually held at the end of September.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Redfoo started coding in 2017 and has since hosted a three-day hackathon at his home.<sup id="cite_ref-code_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-code-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>  He is also vegan since 2017.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
