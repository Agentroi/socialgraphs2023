<p><b>Aaron Smith</b>, better known as <b>Shwayze</b> (born May 29, 1985), is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>.  His first single "<a href="/wiki/Buzzin%27_(Shwayze_song)" title="Buzzin&#39; (Shwayze song)">Buzzin'</a>" peaked at No. 46 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His second single "<a href="/wiki/Corona_and_Lime_(song)" title="Corona and Lime (song)">Corona and Lime</a>," reached No. 23 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 in the US<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and No. 3 in the US iTunes Store. Shwayze's <a href="/wiki/Shwayze_(album)" title="Shwayze (album)">self-titled debut album</a>, which Cisco Adler co-wrote, was released on August 19, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Self-Titled_album_.282008.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Self-Titled_album_(2008)"><i>Self-Titled</i> album (2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Self-Titled album (2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After recording several tracks with <a href="/wiki/Cisco_Adler" title="Cisco Adler">Cisco Adler</a>, the duo was signed to <a href="/wiki/Geffen_Records" title="Geffen Records">Geffen</a> and a self-titled album was produced. Adler helped with songwriting, production, and backup vocals. "<a href="/wiki/Buzzin%27_(Shwayze_song)" title="Buzzin&#39; (Shwayze song)">Buzzin'</a>" was released on August 19, 2008, as the first single off the album, which was entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Shwayze_(album)" title="Shwayze (album)">Shwayze</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The single "<a href="/wiki/Corona_and_Lime_(song)" title="Corona and Lime (song)">Corona and Lime</a>" soon followed. "Buzzin'" shot up the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, reaching No. 46; "Corona and Lime" reached No. 23. The album debuted at No. 10 on the Billboard and sold 47,000 copies in its first week while receiving mixed reviews from critics.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Despite being all but ignored in the hip hop community, <i>Buzzin'</i> gained its popularity in the pop world. It was featured in <a href="/wiki/Pontiac_(automobile)" title="Pontiac (automobile)">Pontiac</a> commercials. The misadventures of Shwayze and Cisco Adler appeared to marketers as a successful advertising campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Shwayze and Adler were the focus of a <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">reality television</a> series on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>, <i> <a href="/wiki/Buzzin%27_(TV_series)" title="Buzzin&#39; (TV series)">Buzzin'</a></i>, which debuted on July 23, 2008.
</p>
<h3><span id="Let_It_Beat_.282009.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Let_It_Beat_(2009)"><i>Let It Beat</i> (2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Let It Beat (2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Their second studio album, <i>Let It Beat</i> was initially announced on February 4, 2009. The album's first single, "Get U Home", was released on June 28, 2009. After the lack of success from the reality TV series, <i>Buzzin'</i> that accompanied the first album, Shwayze was hopeful that he would gain greater music credibility. Rather than being famous for "Buzzin'", both Shwayze and Cisco Adler wanted to become more of a career band.
</p><p>Adler was quoted saying he wanted to make the sound "bigger" on <i>Let It Beat</i> than it had been on <i>Shwayze</i>, their debut album. In an attempt to change the sound, the first single off the album, "Get U Home," has more of a rhythmic club feeling. "Crazy For You," a collaboration with the <a href="/wiki/Ric_Ocasek" title="Ric Ocasek">Ric Ocasek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Darryl_Jenifer" title="Darryl Jenifer">Darryl Jenifer</a>, sounds more like a rock track, while "Heart and Soul," with <a href="/wiki/Roy_Bittan" title="Roy Bittan">Roy Bittan</a>, is a ballad. <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> is also featured on "Livin' It Up" and the Knux is on "Dirty Little Girl".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Music_mix_tapes_.282008.E2.80.932010.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_mix_tapes_(2008–2010)">Music mix tapes (2008–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music mix tapes (2008–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shwayze and Cisco Adler released a mixtape, <i>Rich Girls</i>, on November 24, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2010, Adler released a mixtape, <i>Super California Lipstick Sexy Magic Dope Shit</i>. Shwayze appeared on three tracks, "California Girl," "High, High, High" and "More Than A Fan." On September 7, 2010, as Aaron Smith, Shwayze released <i>Love Stoned</i>, a free mixtape. Shwayze promoted the mixtape throughout the summer.
</p><p>Shwayze's song "Get U Home" was featured in the horror films, <i><a href="/wiki/Sorority_Row" title="Sorority Row">Sorority Row</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Piranha_3D" title="Piranha 3D">Piranha 3D</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2010, Shwayze was featured in a sketch on <i><a href="/wiki/Nick_Swardson%27s_Pretend_Time" title="Nick Swardson&#39;s Pretend Time">Nick Swardson's Pretend Time</a></i>.
</p>
<h3><span id="The_W.27s_free_mini-album_.282011.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_W's_free_mini-album_(2011)"><i>The W's</i> free mini-album (2011)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The W&#039;s free mini-album (2011)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2011, Cisco Adler and Shwayze announced a new free "miniature album" <i>The W's</i>.  The title is a reference to the song "High Together" from Shwayze's first album in which he says "I live by the W's: Weed, and Women". More info on the mini album was released during "Cisco's House", a weekly webcast from Cisco Adler on Justin.TV.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Name_clarification">Name clarification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Name clarification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The name <i>Shwayze</i> had, up until August 30, 2011, been the name of the duo Aaron Smith and Cisco Adler. However, after some confusion and a change of attitude, the duo decided that <i>Shwayze</i> should refer to Smith, and the duo would be known as Shwayze &amp; Cisco Adler.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> This change in name was the precursor to the release of their third album <i>Island in the Sun.</i> The album was the first on their new label Bananabeat Records which was distributed by the Orchard.
</p>
<h3><span id="Island_in_the_Sun_.282011.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Island_in_the_Sun_(2011)"><i>Island in the Sun</i> (2011)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Island in the Sun (2011)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shwayze and Cisco planned to release their third studio album, <i>Island in the Sun</i> in mid-2011. The album's single, "You Could Be My Girl" hits a similar vibe as several songs from their debut album, Shwayze, unlike "Let It Beat", which had a more club, mainstream vibe. The album was released on September 13, 2011, it included features from <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, Sophie Stern, Nikko Gray and Sky Blu of <a href="/wiki/LMFAO_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="LMFAO (group)">LMFAO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Leading up to the album, the duo established the <a href="/wiki/Web_series" title="Web series">internet show</a> <i>Cisco's House</i>, as well as a weekly "Island on Web Concept", which they used to release new tracks. "Butterfly" and "You Could Be My Girl" were debuted on this special.
</p><p>Adler was quoted saying that he hopes Bananabeat Records will launch a "lifestyle-based movement" that will form partnerships with <a href="/wiki/Oakley,_Inc." title="Oakley, Inc.">Oakley</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Era_Cap_Company" title="New Era Cap Company">New Era</a>. He cited <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Buffett" title="Jimmy Buffett">Jimmy Buffett</a>'s empire and business model as an inspiration.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Shwayze has also worked with his Bananabeat label-mate, rapper Chris Young.
</p>
<h3><span id="Shwayzed_and_Confused_EP_.282012.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Shwayzed_and_Confused_EP_(2012)"><i>Shwayzed and Confused</i> EP (2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Shwayzed and Confused EP (2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shwayze released his first EP album titled, <i>Shwayzed and Confused</i> as a play on the <a href="/wiki/Cannabis" title="Cannabis">cannabis</a> movie <a href="/wiki/Dazed_and_Confused_(film)" title="Dazed and Confused (film)">Dazed and Confused</a>. It features 5 tracks including "West Coast Party" and "Love is Overrated".
</p>
<h3><span id="Shwayze_Summer_.282013.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Shwayze_Summer_(2013)"><i>Shwayze Summer</i> (2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Shwayze Summer (2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The fourth studio album, <i>Shwayze Summer</i> was released on July 14, 2013. The first single for the album was "Love is Overrated" produced by Paul Couture. This is Shwayze's first full-length debut album as a <b>solo artist</b> without his previous singer Cisco Adler. Shwayze stated in an interview that they both needed a musical break from each other, but they are still on good terms. The album contains 12 tracks and features The Cataracs, Tayyib Ali, Devin K, Paul Couture, Ferrari Snowday, Marco and Austin Paul.
</p>
<h3><span id="King_of_the_Summer_EP_.282015.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="King_of_the_Summer_EP_(2015)"><i>King of the Summer</i> EP (2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: King of the Summer EP (2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shwayze released his second EP, <i>King of the Summer</i>, on July 17, 2015.  The title track off the album was written by Shwayze and produced by Paul Couture.  The EP consists of 7 tracks and features Paul Couture, Atrel, Wildcard, and Jodi Valentin.
</p>
<h3><span id="Beach_Boy_.282019.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Beach_Boy_(2019)"><i>Beach Boy</i> (2019)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Beach Boy (2019)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The fifth studio album, <i>Beach Boy</i> was released on July 19, 2019. It features 9 tracks and three singles "Perfect Wave" featuring Cisco Adler, "Beach Boy" featuring Paul Couture, and "God Gives" featuring Thev Case.
</p>
<h3><span id="Surf_Trap_.282020.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Surf_Trap_(2020)"><i>Surf Trap</i> (2020)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Surf Trap (2020)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 17, 2020, Shwayze collaborated with producer Paul Couture with the release of a sixth studio album, <i>Surf Trap</i>, which featured 12 tracks. Special guests included reggae rock band HIRIE featured on "Play God", Cisco Adler on "Stuck" and Fxckyeah featured on "Adios".
</p>
<h3><span id="Shwayze_SZN_.282022.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Shwayze_SZN_(2022)"><i>Shwayze SZN</i> (2022)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Shwayze SZN (2022)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 2, 2022, Shwayze released his seventh studio album, <i>Shway SZN</i>, and his first with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ineffable_Records&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ineffable Records (page does not exist)">Ineffable Records</a>. The album has a very strong <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reggae_rock" title="Reggae rock">reggae rock</a>-vibe, especially with the five singles released leading up to the album's date. Jared Watson of <a href="/wiki/Dirty_Heads" title="Dirty Heads">Dirty Heads</a> is featured on "Too Late", <a href="/wiki/The_Elovaters" title="The Elovaters">The Elovaters</a> featured on "Gone With The Wind", The Hip Abduction featured on "Planes", <a href="/wiki/Pepper_(band)" title="Pepper (band)">Pepper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slightly_Stoopid" title="Slightly Stoopid">Slightly Stoopid</a> featured on "Tides", as well as,  <a href="/wiki/Garrett_Nash" title="Garrett Nash">Garrett Nash</a> featured on "Breathe In".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to the singles, the album includes 12 tracks that feature more guest appearances, including Rome of <a href="/wiki/Sublime_with_Rome" title="Sublime with Rome">Sublime with Rome</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Claire_Wright_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Claire Wright (singer) (page does not exist)">Claire Wright</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_Dume&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Little Dume (page does not exist)">Little Dume</a>. Shwayze describes the album as "Feel good, Cali reggae with a splash of Nashville!" <i>Shwayze SZN</i> is also being "considered" for a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nomination for "Best Reggae Album" at the 65th Grammy Awards in 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shwayze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In June 2010, Shwayze's girlfriend, model Shelby Keeton, announced that she was three months pregnant.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> She gave birth to a baby boy, whom the couple named Hendrix after the <a href="/wiki/Jimi_Hendrix" title="Jimi Hendrix">guitarist</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Shwayze and Keeton married on the beach in <a href="/wiki/Kauai,_Hawaii" class="mw-redirect" title="Kauai, Hawaii">Kauai, Hawaii</a>, on February 13, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The couple divorced in 2013.
</p>
