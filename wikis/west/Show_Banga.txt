					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American rapper</div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; font-size: 125%;"><div class="">Show Banga</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">William Lassiter</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Also known as</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Show Banga aka Showy aka Showyformayor</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data">October 3rd</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Origin</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a> Fillmore District</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%26B" class="mw-redirect" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">hyphy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rap" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap">Rap</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role">Rapper, songwriter</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">2010–present</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data">Cam Publishing, <a href="/wiki/Empire_Distribution" title="Empire Distribution">EMPIRE Distribution</a>, HomeTeam Entertainment</td></tr><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-header" style="background-color: #b0c4de"></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Website</th><td class="infobox-data"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://showy4mayor.com">Showy4Mayor.com</a></td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Show Banga</b> is a rapper and songwriter from <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>. Currently signed to Cam Publishing <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://www.facebook.com/CamPublishing/">[1]</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-snaFacebook_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snaFacebook-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> he was releasing singles as early as 2011 on HomeTeam Entertainment, and in 2013 he released the deluxe edition of his album <i>Don't Stop Votin'.</i> Show Banga's single "Milli," which was produced by Dave-O of <a href="/wiki/The_A-Team" title="The A-Team">The A-Team</a>, was released in 2014.  According to <i>Rehab Online Magazine</i>, "Show Banga arguably has one of the most rare [rap] flows in the Bay Area, with 'I Been That' being a contender for the rapper’s breakout cut. However, the bass-packed track 'Milli' is pretty damn hard."<sup id="cite_ref-snfMilli_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snfMilli-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In April 2015, he released <i>Mayor 4 Life</i> on EMPIRE Recordings, with guest artists such as <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_John" class="mw-redirect" title="Kool John">Kool John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sage_The_Gemini" class="mw-redirect" title="Sage The Gemini">Sage The Gemini</a>, and <a href="/wiki/P-Lo" class="mw-redirect" title="P-Lo">P-Lo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-snbthizzler_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snbthizzler-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Show Banga has performed at festivals such as <a href="/wiki/SXSW" class="mw-redirect" title="SXSW">SXSW</a>, and was on to tour with <a href="/wiki/Iamsu!" title="Iamsu!">Iamsu!</a> in spring 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-sncsxsw_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sncsxsw-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On August 12, 2016, he released his technical sophomore album, <i>Showtime 2</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-New_album_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_album-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Now, under new management, he's been churning out singles during the 2020 pandemic such as, "That Filthy" (Feat. Ronski) <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://music.apple.com/us/album/that-filthy-single/1523564121">[2]</a>, "Exotic" <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://music.apple.com/us/album/exotic-single/1533489379">[3]</a>, "Smoke" (Feat. DrillkidMari) <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://music.apple.com/us/album/smoke-feat-lil-yee-single/1566438457">[4]</a>, &amp; "Double Seal" (Prod. by Clayton William) <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://music.apple.com/us/album/double-seal-single/1576441265">[5]</a>. He capped off 2021 dropping a new 5 track EP "Almost Time" with features from Nef the Pharaoh, Stunnaman02, &amp; Drillkidmari <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://music.apple.com/us/album/almost-time-ep/1590245605">[6]</a>. As of 2022, Show Banga has been in the studio working on his follow up album "Showtime 3" due to release late 2022. He will also be releasing his own cannabis strain "Mr. Bag a Bitch" this summer in collaboration with Bay Area cannabis brand "Camabis"<a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://www.shopcamabis.com/">[7]</a> .
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Show_Banga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Show Banga was born in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>, where he was raised in the <a href="/wiki/Fillmore_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Fillmore District">Fillmore District</a>. By the age of seven he was rapping, and by high school he had decided to pursue music as a career.<sup id="cite_ref-snaFacebook_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snaFacebook-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Early on he rapped in the local group Go - Gettaz, which developed a local following and went on to tour locally before disbanding.<sup id="cite_ref-snaFacebook_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snaFacebook-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> By 2011 Show Banga was releasing his own solo material, as well as appearing on the Team BackPack <a href="/wiki/Cyphers" title="Cyphers">cyphers</a> in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-fnoisey_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fnoisey-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Singles.2C_Don.27t_Stop_Votin_.282013.E2.80.932014.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles,_Don't_Stop_Votin_(2013–2014)">Singles, <i>Don't Stop Votin</i> (2013–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Show_Banga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Singles, Don&#039;t Stop Votin (2013–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As early as 2013 he was releasing solo material on HomeTeam Entertainment, and singles that year included "No Losses" in May and "Do My Own Thang" in June, which featured <a href="/wiki/Iamsu!" title="Iamsu!">Iamsu!</a>, CJ and <a href="/wiki/P-Lo" class="mw-redirect" title="P-Lo">P-Lo</a>. In October 2013, he released the deluxe edition of his album <i>Don't Stop Votin',</i> also on HomeTeam. He increased his singles output in 2014, and continued to appear in episodes of the <i>Road to A3C</i> cypher series, which is produced by TeamBackpack.<sup id="cite_ref-sneFredo_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sneFredo-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Also in 2014, he appeared as a guest artist on tracks such as "Panoramic" by <a href="/wiki/Sage_the_Gemini" title="Sage the Gemini">Sage the Gemini</a><sup id="cite_ref-sncsxsw_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sncsxsw-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id=".22I_Been_That.22_.282014.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="&quot;I_Been_That&quot;_(2014)">"I Been That" (2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Show_Banga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: &quot;I Been That&quot; (2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1066479718"><table class="infobox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin:0 0 1.5em 1.5em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="font-size:115%">External video</th></tr><tr><td colspan="2" class="infobox-full-data" style="text-align: left"><span typeof="mw:File"><span><img alt="video icon" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Nuvola_apps_kaboodle.svg/16px-Nuvola_apps_kaboodle.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Nuvola_apps_kaboodle.svg/24px-Nuvola_apps_kaboodle.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Nuvola_apps_kaboodle.svg/32px-Nuvola_apps_kaboodle.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="128" data-file-height="128" /></span></span> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=csf8zny0z_I">"I Been That" Music Video</a> on <a href="/wiki/VEVO" class="mw-redirect" title="VEVO">VEVO</a> (Nov 17, 2014)</td></tr><tr><td colspan="2" class="infobox-full-data" style="text-align: left"><span typeof="mw:File"><span><img alt="video icon" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Nuvola_apps_kaboodle.svg/16px-Nuvola_apps_kaboodle.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Nuvola_apps_kaboodle.svg/24px-Nuvola_apps_kaboodle.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Nuvola_apps_kaboodle.svg/32px-Nuvola_apps_kaboodle.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="128" data-file-height="128" /></span></span> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fyWjVn8bSp0">"Aww Shit" ft. <p>In late 2014, Show Banga's single “I Been That," which featured <a href="/wiki/Sage_the_Gemini" title="Sage the Gemini">Sage the Gemini</a>, was included in the <i>Roof Vol. 1</i> mix put out by the music publication <a href="/wiki/Noisey" class="mw-redirect" title="Noisey">Noisey</a>. The mix featured artists in the Bay Area rap scene. The publication wrote that "Showy’s been a ninja for a few years, catching our attention with 'Shots Fired' in 2011 and becoming a fan favorite on the TeamBackPack cyphers around the same time. Although Show Banga hasn’t had that one mega hit yet, we know it’s coming, and 'I Been That' is another step closer. 'I Been That' deploys a proven formula popularized by <a href="/wiki/Clyde_Carson" title="Clyde Carson">Clyde Carson</a>'s "Slow Down" and <a href="/wiki/Sage_The_Gemini" class="mw-redirect" title="Sage The Gemini">Sage The Gemini</a>'s '<a href="/wiki/Gas_Pedal" title="Gas Pedal">Gas Pedal</a>' in that it uses a similar driving bass line..."<sup id="cite_ref-fnoisey_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fnoisey-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Guest_and_solo_projects_.282014.E2.80.932015.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Guest_and_solo_projects_(2014–2015)">Guest and solo projects (2014–2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Show_Banga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Guest and solo projects (2014–2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"I Been That" was placed in local radio rotation,<sup id="cite_ref-sncsxsw_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sncsxsw-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and was shortly after followed by Show Banga's new single "Milli," which was produced by Dave-O of <a href="/wiki/The_A-Team" title="The A-Team">The A-Team</a>. According to <i>Rehab Online Magazine</i> in November 2014, "Show Banga arguably has one of the most rare [rap] flows in the Bay Area, with 'I Been That' being a contender for the rapper’s breakout cut. However, the bass-packed track 'Milli' is pretty damn hard."<sup id="cite_ref-snfMilli_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snfMilli-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was featured along with <a href="/wiki/J._Stalin" title="J. Stalin">J. Stalin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kool_John" class="mw-redirect" title="Kool John">Kool John</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Rayven_Justice" title="Rayven Justice">Rayven Justice</a> track in February 2015, also releasing a number of solo singles around the same time.<sup id="cite_ref-sndJohn_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sndJohn-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On March 21, 2015, he performed at <a href="/wiki/SXSW" class="mw-redirect" title="SXSW">SXSW</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-sncsxsw_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sncsxsw-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and afterwards announced that he'll be opening for <a href="/wiki/Iamsu!" title="Iamsu!">Iamsu!</a> on Iamsu!'s spring <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eyes_On_Me_(Iamsu!_tour)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Eyes On Me (Iamsu! tour) (page does not exist)">Eyes On Me</a></i> tour.<sup id="cite_ref-sncsxsw_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sncsxsw-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 7, 2015, he released his full-length album <i>Mayor 4 Life</i> on <a href="/w/index.php?title=EMPIRE_Recordings&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="EMPIRE Recordings (page does not exist)">EMPIRE Recordings</a>, as well as on the HomeTeam and Mo Betta labels. Among the guest artists on the album were <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, iamsu!, Kool John, Jay Ant, Sage The Gemini, <a href="/wiki/P-Lo" class="mw-redirect" title="P-Lo">P-Lo</a>, and Kidd Kidd, with singles such as "Aww Shit" featuring Sage the Gemini and <a href="/wiki/P-Lo" class="mw-redirect" title="P-Lo">P-Lo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-snbthizzler_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snbthizzler-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Show Banga has released several official music videos, also appearing as a collaborator on videos such as "How U Love That," also featuring Iamsu! and Smoovie Baby." The video debuted in <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex Magazine">Complex Magazine</a></i> in April 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-sngU_Luv_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sngU_Luv-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On August 12, 2016, he released his technical sophomore album, Showtime 2, whose title paid tribute to his father a hustler and businessman from the Filmore District.<sup id="cite_ref-New_album_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_album-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
