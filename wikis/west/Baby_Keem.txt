<p><b>Hykeem Jamaal Carter Jr.</b> (born October 22, 2000), known professionally as <b>Baby Keem</b>, is an American rapper and record producer.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Raised in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, he gained recognition in the late 2010s with his contributions to <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Panther_(soundtrack)" title="Black Panther (soundtrack)">Black Panther: The Album</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Redemption_(Jay_Rock_album)" title="Redemption (Jay Rock album)">Redemption</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Crash_Talk" title="Crash Talk">Crash Talk</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King:_The_Gift" title="The Lion King: The Gift">The Lion King: The Gift</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He rose to prominence in 2019 through his <a href="/wiki/Sleeper_hit" title="Sleeper hit">sleeper hit</a> single "<a href="/wiki/Orange_Soda_(song)" title="Orange Soda (song)">Orange Soda</a>".
</p><p>After signing with <a href="/wiki/PGLang" title="PGLang">PGLang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> in 2020, Keem released his debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Melodic_Blue" title="The Melodic Blue">The Melodic Blue</a></i> (2021), which was met with critical and commercial success. It was certified <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_certification" title="Music recording certification">platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA), and its final single, "<a href="/wiki/Family_Ties_(song)" title="Family Ties (song)">Family Ties</a>", won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance">Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance</a>. His collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Donda" title="Donda">Donda</a></i> (2021) and his cousin <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Morale_%26_the_Big_Steppers" title="Mr. Morale &amp; the Big Steppers">Mr. Morale &amp; the Big Steppers</a></i> (2022) earned him two nominations for the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Album_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Album of the Year">Grammy Award for Album of the Year</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baby_Keem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Carter was born in <a href="/wiki/Carson,_California" title="Carson, California">Carson, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Carter spent his early life, as young as four years old, in music studios watching his aunts and uncles <a href="/wiki/Burn_CD" class="mw-redirect" title="Burn CD">burn CDs</a> using <a href="/wiki/LimeWire" title="LimeWire">LimeWire</a>. He later recalled memories of listening to American rapper <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s album "<a href="/wiki/808s_%26_Heartbreak" title="808s &amp; Heartbreak">808s &amp; Heartbreak</a>" on the radio while living in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a> during winter 2009. This sparked Carter’s interest for music even more, and he began researching music in 2010. Carter looked up to artists such as West, <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> and <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, Carter started pursuing his own musical career.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Carter made <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> accounts "AllMightyModz" and "HykeezyHD", posting <a href="/wiki/Minecraft_(franchise)" title="Minecraft (franchise)">Minecraft</a> Survival minigames gameplay in the early 2010s from his Minecraft account "hykeemc".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Carter frequently <a href="/wiki/Skype" title="Skype">Skype</a>-called his fans, who played along with him.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Today, Carter no longer posts, but his YouTube still links to his <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> account.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baby_Keem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Keem worked with American record producer <a href="/wiki/Cardo_(record_producer)" title="Cardo (record producer)">Cardo</a>, on his two solo mixtapes, <i>The Sound of a Bad Habit</i> (2018) and <i>Die For My Bitch</i> (2019).
</p><p>In March 2020, Keem was featured in the visual mission statement for <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dave_Free" title="Dave Free">Dave Free</a>'s company, <a href="/wiki/PGLang" title="PGLang">pgLang</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In August 2020, it was revealed Baby Keem was included on <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s 2020 <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)#Annual_Freshman_Class_List" title="XXL (magazine)">Annual Freshman Class</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 18, 2020, Keem returned with the release of two new songs, "Hooligan", and "Sons &amp; Critics Freestyle".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He then released "No Sense", on March 5, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On April 30, Keem released "<a href="/wiki/Durag_Activity" title="Durag Activity">Durag Activity</a>" with fellow American rapper <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The song was followed by another collaboration with Keem's cousin Kendrick Lamar, titled "<a href="/wiki/Family_Ties_(song)" title="Family Ties (song)">Family Ties</a>", which was released on August 27, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> These songs are featured on Keem's debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Melodic_Blue" title="The Melodic Blue">The Melodic Blue</a></i>, which was released on September 10, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The album also included the single "Issues", which Keem performed on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Jimmy_Fallon" title="The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a></i>, a collaboration with American singer <a href="/wiki/Don_Toliver" title="Don Toliver">Don Toliver</a> titled "Cocoa", as well as previously teased tracks "Vent" and "<a href="/wiki/Range_Brothers" title="Range Brothers">Range Brothers</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-Issues_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Issues-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Keem also made an appearance on <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s tenth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Donda" title="Donda">Donda</a></i>, being on the song "<a href="/wiki/Praise_God" title="Praise God">Praise God</a>", alongside Travis Scott.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 3, 2022, Keem won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Best_Rap_Performance" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Rap Performance">Best Rap Performance</a> for "Family Ties", along with Kendrick Lamar.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On October 28, 2022, Keem released the deluxe edition of <i>The Melodic Blue.</i><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On May 30, 2023, Keem and Lamar released a surprise single and music video for "<a href="/wiki/The_Hillbillies" title="The Hillbillies">The Hillbillies</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baby_Keem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baby_Keem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In a 2019 interview, Keem cited American musician <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a>, as a major influence: "I was inspired by Cudi's cadences and shit like that. Kid Cudi's one of my favorite artists."<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a 2021 interview with <i>Billboard</i>, Keem reportedly identified as part of the <a href="/wiki/Mike_Will_Made_It" title="Mike Will Made It">Mike WiLL Made-It</a> and <a href="/wiki/Metro_Boomin" title="Metro Boomin">Metro Boomin</a> generation, saying he derives inspiration from the two producers, as well as from <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
