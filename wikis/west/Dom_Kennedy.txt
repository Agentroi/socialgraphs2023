<p><b>Dominic Ross Hunn</b> (born August 22, 1984),<sup id="cite_ref-Zwalkk_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zwalkk-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Dom Kennedy</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Leimert_Park,_Los_Angeles" title="Leimert Park, Los Angeles">Leimert Park, Los Angeles</a>, California.<sup id="cite_ref-Gipson,_Brooklyne_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gipson,_Brooklyne-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Since 2008, Kennedy has released 5 <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent</a> mixtapes, most notably his 2010 critically acclaimed mixtape <i>From the Westside with Love</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His first <a href="/wiki/Commercialism" title="Commercialism">commercial</a> studio album <i>From the Westside with Love II</i> was released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> June 28, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Roy_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roy-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Despite it being his iTunes debut, FTWSWL2 received "a top 10 spot on Hip Hop/Rap albums chart during its release week"<sup id="cite_ref-Meara,_Paul_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Meara,_Paul-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and was Kennedy's first album associated with The OpM Company, Kennedy's self-established <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>. His song "My Type of Party" was ranked by <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> at #43 in the magazine's Best 50 Songs of 2012 list.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dom_Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dom Kennedy was born on August 22, 1984, in Los Angeles, California. In an interview with music media outlet IMFlashy, Kennedy describes his childhood growing up as "normal".<sup id="cite_ref-Zwalkk_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zwalkk-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> After his parents' divorce, Kennedy moved with his mother to <a href="/wiki/Leimert_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Leimert Park">Leimert Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Graham,_Nadine_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Graham,_Nadine-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy played baseball to get out of the inner city to stay safe and out of trouble. He also played basketball everyday from the age of five to the age of 16.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Upon graduating from Santa Monica high school,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy enrolled in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica_College" title="Santa Monica College">Santa Monica College</a>, a two-year junior college in California, majoring in business management. In an interview with Dr. Josh Hamilton in October 2012, Dom gave his reasoning on why he entered college and what his plan B would be.
</p><p>"I mainly focused on business management while I was there, not really thinking about music per se at first, but just on life. You know, like damn if I wasn't rapping or doing anything with music that was kind of where I saw myself in the world. I always hoped I could be an entrepreneur, you know I guess, but thats the aspirations of many people but I just felt like if I was going to learn about anything I might as well learn about structures of corporations, or at least find out everyone's job."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dom_Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Working with his cousin Jason Madison,<sup id="cite_ref-Graham,_Nadine_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Graham,_Nadine-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy released his debut mixtape album <i>25th Hour</i> in 2008, instantly producing a buzz throughout the south side of L.A. In particular, the song "Watermelon Sundae" was widely played on local radio stations and performed live throughout the area, bringing more attention to Kennedy's music ambitions. Around this time, Kennedy's cousin Madison was a film student at <a href="/wiki/Loyola_Marymount_University" title="Loyola Marymount University">Loyola Marymount University</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Graham,_Nadine_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Graham,_Nadine-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> sparking the beginning importance of visuals in Kennedy's later projects.
</p><p>After his <i>25th Hour</i> debut, Kennedy released 2 additional mixtapes in 2009; <i>Best After Bobby</i> (the title referring to "Bobby" <a href="/wiki/Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Robert F. Kennedy">Robert F. Kennedy</a>), and <i>Future Street/Drug Sounds</i>. For the <i>Best After Bobby</i> mixtape, Kennedy collaborated with West Coast heavy hitter DJ Sour Milk and the Los Angeles Leakers, producing an instant buzz in underground circles across the country.<sup id="cite_ref-3rd_Eye_Drops_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3rd_Eye_Drops-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Within the first hours of dropping the mixtape, Kennedy amassed over 10,000 downloads,<sup id="cite_ref-3rd_Eye_Drops_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3rd_Eye_Drops-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> officially solidifying his name among some of the top rappers in L.A.
</p><p>However, with the release of <i>From the Westside With Love</i> in 2010, Kennedy became a major hit throughout the underground hip hop scene in L.A. Gaining over 100,000 internet downloads,<sup id="cite_ref-South_by_Southwest_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-South_by_Southwest-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> With the success of <i>From the Westside With Love</i>, Kennedy became a major player on the independent circuit, traveling for performances throughout and outside the country; including a show in <a href="/wiki/Djibouti" title="Djibouti">Djibouti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gipson,_Brooklyne_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gipson,_Brooklyne-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy also began releasing an extensive amount of visual material at this time, including <i>From the Westside With Love</i> music videos for songs such as "1997", "Locals Only", and "The 4 Heartbeats".
</p><p>In early 2011, Kennedy released his 5th studio mixtape <i>The Original Dom Kennedy</i>, a tape meant to reflect Kennedy's individuality and sound as an artist. In an interview with <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> News reporter James Lacsina, Kennedy described the project as something that was a testament to himself in the rap game as well as the city he proudly represents:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>With <i>The Original Dom Kennedy</i>, it was a time for me to take a stand in music. The way we grew up and our lifestyle, being kids from the inner city of Los Angeles. I just felt like that was missing.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8202;<cite>Dom Kennedy, from an interview with Brooklyn Gipsone</cite></div></blockquote>
<p>Kennedy also took a personal stance in the mixtape against major hip hop DJ and producer <a href="/wiki/Funkmaster_Flex" title="Funkmaster Flex">Funkmaster Flex</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gipson,_Brooklyne_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gipson,_Brooklyne-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On his song "The Homies", Kennedy takes shots at Flex's disparaging remarks regarding the late California rapper <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>: "Shit I heard Funk Flex say that Pac ain't shit/ and I hope when you see him that he slap your mouth/"
In 2011, Kennedy performed at premier <a href="/wiki/Film_festival" title="Film festival">film festival</a> <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a>'s Music Matters Show in <a href="/wiki/Albuquerque,_New_Mexico" title="Albuquerque, New Mexico">Albuquerque, New Mexico</a>. The show was hosted by cable entertainment network <a href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Entertainment Television">Black Entertainment Television</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Dom_Kennedy_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dom_Kennedy-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Both his performance and that of <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>'s were featured online by major, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop music</a> magazine <a href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Source (magazine)">The Source (magazine)</a>. Kennedy performed his first show outside of the U.S. also in 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Djibouti" title="Djibouti">Djibouti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hip_Hop_South_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hip_Hop_South-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the release of <i>From the Westside With Love II</i>, Kennedy headlined at The Key Club in <a href="/wiki/West_Hollywood,_California" title="West Hollywood, California">West Hollywood, California</a>, alongside fellow West Coast rappers Overdoz, Epic Twelve, and Black Cobain.<sup id="cite_ref-Key_Club_Hollywood_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Key_Club_Hollywood-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Marketed as the show on "7.1.11", the event was sold out, prompting another show the following week, marketed as the "7.7.11" show.<sup id="cite_ref-Key_Club_Hollywood_2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Key_Club_Hollywood_2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Kennedy traveled throughout Europe and the United States for his Yellow Album World Tour. The tour started in London in October and wrapped up in San Francisco in December.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2013, <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> attempted to sign Dom Kennedy, however he chose to remain independent. His second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Home_Safely" title="Get Home Safely">Get Home Safely</a></i> was released through his The Other People's Money Company record label on October 15, 2013. The album featured guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Skeme" title="Skeme">Skeme</a>, <a href="/wiki/Krondon" title="Krondon">Krondon</a>, <a href="/wiki/TeeFLii" class="mw-redirect" title="TeeFLii">TeeFLii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a>. <i>Get Home Safely</i> debuted at number 29 in its first week, selling more than 10,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 2, 2015, Dom Kennedy released his third studio album entitled, <i>By Dom Kennedy</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>  The album peaked at number 23 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> selling 9,000 copies the first week. On December 23, 2016, Dom Kennedy released his fourth studio album entitled, <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Is_Not_For_Sale,_Vol._1" title="Los Angeles Is Not For Sale, Vol. 1">Los Angeles Is Not For Sale, Vol. 1</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dom Kennedy teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Hit-Boy" title="Hit-Boy">Hit-Boy</a> in order to create their own Hip-Hop group entitled Half-A-Mil. They would go on to release three self-titled EP's that dropped sporadically throughout late 2016 into mid 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Then later officially released their debut album <i>Courtesy Of Half-A-Mil</i> on November 24, 2017. In 2019, Kennedy was featured in <a href="/wiki/Kehlani" title="Kehlani">Kehlani</a>'s song "Nunya" from her mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/While_We_Wait" title="While We Wait">While We Wait</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He also appeared in the song's music video, which was released on February 20, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Additional_Information">Additional Information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dom_Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Additional Information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2014 Dom Kennedy signed Los-Angeles based rap group, Warm Brew to his independent-label OPM.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dom_Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kennedy is often heard proclaiming his hometown rep one liner "Leimert Park what's cool". As a kid, Kennedy describes himself as being highly influenced by artists such as "<a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a>, <a href="/wiki/2Pac" class="mw-redirect" title="2Pac">2Pac</a>, and <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>"<sup id="cite_ref-Zwalkk_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zwalkk-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> through the fact that by simply listening to their music, he could feel a sense of where they were from. With his music, Kennedy does the same thing, bringing the feel of the inner city streets of L.A. to anyone willing to listen. In an interview with Hip Hop DX, Kennedy talks about the influence behind the L.A. sound in his music:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942"><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>If anything, and I can only speak for myself, I think that I’m trying to do it like <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a> did it, or <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a> did it, but this is just my way of doing it therefore I guess it’s natural. But I’m not trying to do anything, you know, different or to say, ‘This is new.’... I set out to do it the way they did it, just in my own way.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8202;<cite>Dom Kennedy, from an interview with Nadine Graham<sup id="cite_ref-Graham,_Nadine_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Graham,_Nadine-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></cite></div></blockquote>
<p>Kennedy has rapped over a wide range of past and present hip hop beats in his career, consistently bringing the element of L.A. and <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast hip hop</a> to his music.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Such beats include <a href="/wiki/Big_Poppa" title="Big Poppa">Big Poppa</a> on the track "Notorious Dom" <i>(25th Hour)</i> and <a href="/wiki/Best_I_Ever_Had_(Drake_song)" title="Best I Ever Had (Drake song)">Best I Ever Had</a> on the track "Best You Never Had" <i>(Best After Bobby)</i>. South by Southwest reports major music executive John Monopoly as saying "He is going to get signed".<sup id="cite_ref-South_by_Southwest_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-South_by_Southwest-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_recognition">Musical style and recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dom_Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Musical style and recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, Kennedy was recognized as one of the leading artist in a new wave of <a href="/wiki/West_coast_hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="West coast hip hop">West coast hip hop</a> artist.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Dom Kennedy is known for his laid back flow, wordplay and unique storytelling.
</p>
