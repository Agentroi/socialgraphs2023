<p><b>Jeffrey James Logan</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born May 7, 1978),<sup id="cite_ref-birthday_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birthday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Jel</b>, is an American hip hop producer and rapper. He is co-founder of the record label <a href="/wiki/Anticon" title="Anticon">Anticon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He has been a member of Presage,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Themselves" title="Themselves">Themselves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Subtle_(band)" title="Subtle (band)">Subtle</a>, and <a href="/wiki/13_%26_God" title="13 &amp; God">13 &amp; God</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jel_(music_producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jel released his first solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/10_Seconds_(album)" title="10 Seconds (album)">10 Seconds</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/Mush_Records" title="Mush Records">Mush Records</a> in 2002. The album is titled after the limited sampling time of <a href="/wiki/E-mu_SP-1200" title="E-mu SP-1200">E-mu SP-1200</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It features contributions from <a href="/wiki/Dax_Pierson" title="Dax Pierson">Dax Pierson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Odd_Nosdam" title="Odd Nosdam">Odd Nosdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doseone" title="Doseone">Doseone</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alias_(musician)" title="Alias (musician)">Alias</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His second solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Soft_Money_(album)" title="Soft Money (album)">Soft Money</a></i>, was released on <a href="/wiki/Anticon" title="Anticon">Anticon</a> in 2006. The album features contributions from <a href="/wiki/Dosh_(musician)" title="Dosh (musician)">Dosh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wise_Intelligent" title="Wise Intelligent">Wise Intelligent</a> of <a href="/wiki/Poor_Righteous_Teachers" title="Poor Righteous Teachers">Poor Righteous Teachers</a>, and Stefanie Böhm of <a href="/wiki/Ms._John_Soda" title="Ms. John Soda">Ms. John Soda</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He released his third solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Pass" title="Late Pass">Late Pass</a></i>, on Anticon in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_and_influences">Style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jel_(music_producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jel is primarily a producer. He is known for his use of <a href="/wiki/E-mu_SP-1200" title="E-mu SP-1200">SP-1200</a> and <a href="/wiki/Music_Production_Center#MPC2000XL" class="mw-redirect" title="Music Production Center">MPC2000XL</a> to create drum beats with little or no sequencing like playing the drums live via the sampler pads.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He has produced numerous tracks for underground hip hop artists such as <a href="/wiki/Deep_Puddle_Dynamics" title="Deep Puddle Dynamics">Deep Puddle Dynamics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere_(music_group)" title="Atmosphere (music group)">Atmosphere</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sole_(hip_hop_artist)" title="Sole (hip hop artist)">Sole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sage_Francis" title="Sage Francis">Sage Francis</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Serengeti_(rapper)" title="Serengeti (rapper)">Serengeti</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jel is also a rapper. He provided vocals on <i>Soft Money</i><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and <i>Late Pass</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In a 2006 interview with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Skinny_(magazine)" title="The Skinny (magazine)">The Skinny</a></i>, he said: "What inspires me lyrically is television conversations and dope MCs."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
