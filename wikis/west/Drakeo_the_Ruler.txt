<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drakeo_the_Ruler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Caldwell was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/South_Los_Angeles" title="South Los Angeles">South Central Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> by a single mother. He attended <a href="/wiki/Washington_Preparatory_High_School" title="Washington Preparatory High School">Washington High School</a> in nearby <a href="/wiki/Westmont,_California" title="Westmont, California">Westmont</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LAT_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAT-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drakeo_the_Ruler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After recording a handful of mixtapes with much more of a <a href="/wiki/Trap_music" title="Trap music">trap</a> influence than the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast hip hop</a> he became known for, he was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Mustard_(record_producer)" title="Mustard (record producer)">DJ Mustard</a>, who remixed his song "Mr. Get Dough". The song, which premiered on <a href="/wiki/WorldStarHipHop" title="WorldStarHipHop">WorldStarHipHop</a> in April 2015, became his breakout song,<sup id="cite_ref-LAT_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAT-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and has amassed over 6.2 million <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> views as of August 2019. Six months later, in October, Drakeo released his official debut mixtape, <i>I Am Mr. Mosely</i>, as his first project under DJ Mustard's 10 Summers label.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He released his second project, <i>I Am Mr. Mosely 2</i>, on July 21, 2016, with features including <a href="/wiki/Mozzy" title="Mozzy">Mozzy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skeme" title="Skeme">Skeme</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Philthy_Rich" title="Philthy Rich">Philthy Rich</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He followed this up with the release of <i>So Cold I Do Em</i> in December.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After his jail release in November 2017, he recorded a 16-track mixtape, <i>Cold Devil</i>, in 10 days, and released it the following month.<sup id="cite_ref-stereo_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stereo-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Paul A. Thompson of <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork</a></i> described it as "the most compelling album of the Los Angeles emcee's career", adding that his "avant deadpan and impressionistic relationship to the beat" is "icy and unforgettable".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Similarly, Grant Rindner of <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_Networks" title="Complex Networks">Complex</a></i> called it "one of the most impressive California rap projects in years".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He released music videos for "Flu Flamming",<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> "Big Banc Uchies",<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and "Out the Slums" within a month.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> "Flu Flamming" was also notably remixed by <a href="/wiki/Lil_Yachty" title="Lil Yachty">Lil Yachty</a>, and "Big Banc Uchies" by <a href="/wiki/Shy_Glizzy" title="Shy Glizzy">Shy Glizzy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The music video for "Roll Bounce" was released in September, with Drakeo in jail awaiting trial.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The project, <i><a href="/wiki/Thank_You_for_Using_GTL" title="Thank You for Using GTL">Thank You for Using GTL</a></i>, was recorded from phone calls from jail,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and released in June 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-PF_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PF-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
His ninth mixtape, <i>The Truth Hurts</i>, was released on February 24, 2021. The mixtape contains notable guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Don_Toliver" title="Don Toliver">Don Toliver</a> on the song <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q0P0D_EPjAI">"Dawn Tolliver</a>", and <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a> on the song "<a href="/wiki/Talk_to_Me_(Drakeo_the_Ruler_song)" title="Talk to Me (Drakeo the Ruler song)">Talk to Me</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-Truth_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Truth-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drakeo_the_Ruler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2017, Caldwell was arrested by the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Police_Department" title="Los Angeles Police Department">LAPD</a> when they raided a condo where he regularly shot music videos. He was subsequently held at <a href="/wiki/Men%27s_Central_Jail" title="Men&#39;s Central Jail">Men's Central Jail</a> after being charged with unlawful possession of a firearm by a felon.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He was released in November.<sup id="cite_ref-stereo_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stereo-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2018, he was arrested again, this time charged with first-degree murder, attempted murder and conspiracy to commit murder. The charges stemmed from a December 2016 shooting in <a href="/wiki/Carson,_California" title="Carson, California">Carson, California</a>, where one person was killed and two were injured.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He was facing life in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Simultaneously, members of his Stinc Team collective, including his brother Ralfy the Plug, were arrested in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> on a variety of charges.<sup id="cite_ref-WeissInterview_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WeissInterview-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 25, 2019, he was acquitted of his murder and attempted murder charges in a <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton</a> courthouse.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> However, the district attorney decided to refile charges of criminal gang conspiracy and shooting from a motor vehicle in August, two counts that resulted in a hung jury during his initial trial.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> His trial date was set for August 3, 2020. While incarcerated, he recorded <i><a href="/wiki/Thank_You_for_Using_GTL" title="Thank You for Using GTL">Thank You for Using GTL</a></i>, which <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork</a></i> called "likely the greatest rap album ever recorded from jail".<sup id="cite_ref-PF_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PF-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He was released from jail in November 2020 following three years of incarceration after he accepted a deal from the District Attorney's office in which he pled guilty to shooting from a vehicle.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drakeo_the_Ruler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Caldwell was stabbed backstage at around 8:30<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>p.m. on December 18, 2021, during the Once Upon a Time in LA festival. Initial eyewitness accounts reported that he was stabbed in the neck during an altercation; later, his mother Darrylene Corniel revealed in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> that Caldwell, his brother, and their entourage were attacked by "around 40, 60 men" in masks around the time of <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>'s arrival to the venue, and that Caldwell had been stabbed in the neck.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Paramedics arrived at the scene at around 8:40<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>p.m. and transported Caldwell to a nearby hospital in critical condition.<sup id="cite_ref-LATimes20211219_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes20211219-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, who was co-headlining along with <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, YG, and <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>, cancelled their  performance once they were made aware of the situation. The event itself was cancelled shortly afterward.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Caldwell was pronounced dead as a result of his stab wounds at around midnight on December 19, 2021, roughly four hours after the initial altercation.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> As of December 21, 2021, the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Police_Department" title="Los Angeles Police Department">Los Angeles Police Department</a> is investigating his death as a <a href="/wiki/Homicide" title="Homicide">homicide</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> He was interred at <a href="/wiki/Forest_Lawn_Memorial_Park_(Hollywood_Hills)" title="Forest Lawn Memorial Park (Hollywood Hills)">Forest Lawn Memorial Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Hills" title="Hollywood Hills">Hollywood Hills</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath_and_reactions">Aftermath and reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Drakeo_the_Ruler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Aftermath and reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the news of his death, Corniel stated she would be suing <a href="/wiki/Live_Nation_Entertainment" title="Live Nation Entertainment">Live Nation</a> in regards to Caldwell's murder, citing negligence from the venue and lax security measures from the staff.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In January 2023, a <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_County_Superior_Court" title="Los Angeles County Superior Court">Los Angeles County Superior Court</a> judge denied a motion by Live Nation to dismiss the lawsuit, allowing it to move forward.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Snoop Dogg posted a lengthy message to his <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> expressing his condolences to Caldwell's family, and that he had been in his dressing room preparing to perform when he was informed of the incident. He closed the statement with "I'm praying for peace in hip hop."<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> also called for peace in the music industry following the violent deaths of Caldwell and rapper <a href="/wiki/Young_Dolph" title="Young Dolph">Young Dolph</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
