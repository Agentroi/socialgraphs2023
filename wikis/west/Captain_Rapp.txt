<p><b>Captain Rapp</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> of <b>Larry Earl Glenn</b>, an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>/<a href="/wiki/Post-disco" title="Post-disco">post-disco</a> musician, producer and <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_Rap" class="mw-redirect" title="West Coast Rap">West Coast Rap</a> pioneer.<sup id="cite_ref-AMGBi_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMGBi-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is best known for his politically conscious song "<a class="mw-selflink-fragment" href="#Singles">Bad Times (I Can't Stand It)</a>", which was a <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast</a> response to <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_Flash" title="Grandmaster Flash">Grandmaster Flash</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/The_Message_(Grandmaster_Flash_and_the_Furious_Five_song)" title="The Message (Grandmaster Flash and the Furious Five song)">The Message</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-salary_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-salary-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-message_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-message-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_Rapp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Glenn's musical career started in 1981 when he was signed to a small <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">indie label</a> called <a href="/wiki/Rappers_Rapp_Records" title="Rappers Rapp Records">Rappers Rapp Records</a>. His first record, party-oriented, "Gigolo Rapp" was a minor hit on the East Coast yet the record failed in his home state.<sup id="cite_ref-AMGBi_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMGBi-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1983, his most successful single "Bad Times" came out on Saturn Records and reached number 23 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> <a href="/wiki/Club_Play_Singles" class="mw-redirect" title="Club Play Singles">Dance Charts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Allmusic_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The single was arranged and performed by emerging <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">Contemporary R&amp;B</a> moguls <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Jam_and_Terry_Lewis" title="Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis">Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AMGBi_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMGBi-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> with Rich Cason.
</p><p>In 1992, Glenn recorded a sequel to his previous hit single, titled "Bad Times, Part 2: The Continuance."<sup id="cite_ref-AMGBi_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMGBi-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Themes">Themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_Rapp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"Bad Times" lyrically touches sensitive topics, including <a href="/wiki/Unemployment" title="Unemployment">unemployment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Child_sexual_abuse" title="Child sexual abuse">child sexual abuse</a>, <a href="/wiki/AIDS" class="mw-redirect" title="AIDS">AIDS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salvadoran_Civil_War" title="Salvadoran Civil War">Salvadoran Civil War</a> and even <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_war" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuclear war">nuclear war</a>, in contrast to uptempo <a href="/wiki/Synth-funk" class="mw-redirect" title="Synth-funk">synth-funk</a> melody and soulful vocals.<sup id="cite_ref-future_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-future-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song is a West Coast variant of "<a href="/wiki/The_Message_(Grandmaster_Flash_and_the_Furious_Five_song)" title="The Message (Grandmaster Flash and the Furious Five song)">The Message</a>" whereas the title is lampooning a name of the most sampled song in <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip-hop</a> history, "<a href="/wiki/Good_Times_(Chic_song)" title="Good Times (Chic song)">Good Times</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Chic_(band)" title="Chic (band)">Chic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-salary_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-salary-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-message_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-message-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
