<p><b>Tyrone William Griffin Jr.</b> (born April 13, 1982),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Ty Dolla Sign</b> (stylized as <b>Ty Dolla $ign</b> or <b>Ty$</b>), is an American singer, songwriter, multi-instrumentalist, and record producer. He first gained recognition for his feature on rapper <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>'s 2010 single "<a href="/wiki/Toot_It_and_Boot_It" title="Toot It and Boot It">Toot It and Boot It</a>". In 2012, he signed to <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>, and in 2013 signed with rapper <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Gang_Entertainment" title="Taylor Gang Entertainment">Taylor Gang Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He's since released three studio albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Free_TC" title="Free TC">Free TC</a></i> (2015), <i><a href="/wiki/Beach_House_3" title="Beach House 3">Beach House 3</a></i> (2017), and <i><a href="/wiki/Featuring_Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Featuring Ty Dolla Sign">Featuring Ty Dolla Sign</a></i> (2020), the latter of which reached number 4 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.
</p><p>Ty Dolla Sign is known for his songs "<a href="/wiki/Paranoid_(Ty_Dolla_Sign_song)" title="Paranoid (Ty Dolla Sign song)">Paranoid</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a>), "<a href="/wiki/Or_Nah_(Ty_Dolla_Sign_song)" title="Or Nah (Ty Dolla Sign song)">Or Nah</a>" (featuring Wiz Khalifa and <a href="/wiki/Mustard_(record_producer)" title="Mustard (record producer)">DJ Mustard</a>), and "<a href="/wiki/Blas%C3%A9_(song)" title="Blasé (song)">Blasé</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Rae_Sremmurd" title="Rae Sremmurd">Rae Sremmurd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>), as well his various features on singles such as "<a href="/wiki/Work_from_Home_(song)" title="Work from Home (song)">Work from Home</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Harmony" title="Fifth Harmony">Fifth Harmony</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Swalla" title="Swalla">Swalla</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Jason_Derulo" title="Jason Derulo">Jason Derulo</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Hot_Girl_Summer" title="Hot Girl Summer">Hot Girl Summer</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Megan_Thee_Stallion" title="Megan Thee Stallion">Megan Thee Stallion</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/Psycho_(Post_Malone_song)" title="Psycho (Post Malone song)">Psycho</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Post_Malone" title="Post Malone">Post Malone</a>, which was his first track to hit number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and is certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">Diamond</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Along with his recording career, Ty Dolla Sign is also a songwriter and record producer for other artists, with credits on singles including "<a href="/wiki/Loyal_(Chris_Brown_song)" title="Loyal (Chris Brown song)">Loyal</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Post_to_Be" title="Post to Be">Post to Be</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Omarion" title="Omarion">Omarion</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/FourFiveSeconds" title="FourFiveSeconds">FourFiveSeconds</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ty_Dolla_Sign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ty Dolla Sign was born Tyrone William Griffin Jr. in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BMIDATA_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BMIDATA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He is the son of musician Tyrone Griffin, a member of the band <a href="/wiki/Lakeside_(band)" title="Lakeside (band)">Lakeside</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He has said that through his father's involvement in Lakeside, he met singers like <a href="/wiki/Earth,_Wind_%26_Fire" title="Earth, Wind &amp; Fire">Earth, Wind &amp; Fire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a> as a child, which led to his love and interest for <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul music</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MalcolmMusic_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MalcolmMusic-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Growing up in <a href="/wiki/South_Los_Angeles" title="South Los Angeles">South Central Los Angeles</a>, Ty Dolla Sign was a <a href="/wiki/Gang" title="Gang">gang member</a>, representing the <a href="/wiki/Bloods" title="Bloods">Bloods</a>, while his brother was a <a href="/wiki/Crips" title="Crips">Crip</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ty_Dolla_Sign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2004.E2.80.932011:_Career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2004–2011:_Career_beginnings">2004–2011: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ty_Dolla_Sign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2004–2011: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ty Dolla Sign began his music career by learning how to play the <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass guitar</a> and later learned how to play drums, guitars, keyboards, and the <a href="/wiki/Akai_MPC" title="Akai MPC">MPC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Ty Dolla Sign and his writing partner Kory began working together after meeting in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-Ukiomogbe_2020_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ukiomogbe_2020-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> They were signed to a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Venus_Brown" title="Venus Brown">Venus Brown</a> and Buddah Brown Entertainment, subsequently releasing a <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, titled <i>Raw &amp; Bangin Mixtape Vol 2</i>. The duo also made <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> on <a href="/wiki/Album#Studio" title="Album">studio albums</a> by other artists such as <a href="/wiki/Sa-Ra" title="Sa-Ra">Sa-Ra Creative Partners</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Milk" title="Black Milk">Black Milk</a>. They also sang on songs such as "U", which was featured on the latter 2007's recording <i><a href="/wiki/Popular_Demand" title="Popular Demand">Popular Demand</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Eventually, however, the partners had a falling out related to these ventures.
</p><p>After the dispute, Ty Dolla Sign would go on to collaborate with <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>-based rapper <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Ty Dolla Sign's initial claim to fame was with the success of YG's single "<a href="/wiki/Toot_It_and_Boot_It" title="Toot It and Boot It">Toot It and Boot It</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> a song he both wrote and produced with YG.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He then became affiliated with YG's group <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)#4Hunnid_Records" title="YG (rapper)">Pushaz Ink</a>. In 2011, Ty Dolla Sign released his first solo song, titled "All Star" (featuring Joe Moses), produced by Fuego. Ty Dolla Sign also released his first mixtape <i>House on the Hill</i>. Then he released "My Cabana" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Jeezy" title="Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a>), which was ranked on <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_Networks#History" title="Complex Networks">Complex</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s Best 50 Songs of 2012 list at number 23.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Ty Dolla Sign co-founded the production team D.R.U.G.S. alongside longtime friends and fellow music producers Chordz 3D and G Casso, the latter of which was later murdered. Other producers later joined D.R.U.G.S., including Nate 3D, Buddah Shampoo, Fuego, <a href="/wiki/Mustard_(record_producer)" title="Mustard (record producer)">DJ Mustard</a>, and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Dahi" title="DJ Dahi">DJ Dahi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932014:_Beach_House_mixtapes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2014:_Beach_House_mixtapes">2012–2014: <i>Beach House</i> mixtapes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ty_Dolla_Sign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012–2014: Beach House mixtapes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:TyDolla2013_(cropped).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/TyDolla2013_%28cropped%29.jpg/190px-TyDolla2013_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="190" height="263" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/TyDolla2013_%28cropped%29.jpg/285px-TyDolla2013_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/TyDolla2013_%28cropped%29.jpg/380px-TyDolla2013_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2381" data-file-height="3299" /></a><figcaption>Ty Dolla Sign in November 2013.</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2012, Ty Dolla Sign signed a <a href="/wiki/Recording_contract" title="Recording contract">recording contract</a> with <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> After signing with Atlantic, he released his first solo mixtape, titled <i>Beach House</i>, on October 1, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He followed with the mixtape's sequel, <i>Beach House 2</i>, on July 1, 2013. <i>Beach House 2</i> features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too Short</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kirko_Bangz" title="Kirko Bangz">Kirko Bangz</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The following day, it was revealed that Ty Dolla Sign signed a deal with Khalifa's <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Gang_Entertainment" title="Taylor Gang Entertainment">Taylor Gang Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In mid-2013, he toured with Khalifa and <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a>, among others, on the Under the Influence of Music 2 concert tour.<sup id="cite_ref-theboombox1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theboombox1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 10, 2013, Ty Dolla Sign released a single, titled "<a href="/wiki/Paranoid_(Ty_Dolla_Sign_song)" title="Paranoid (Ty Dolla Sign song)">Paranoid</a>", which features a verse from <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a> and production from DJ Mustard. The song was later announced to be the first single off his upcoming <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP). On October 22, 2013, the Ethan Lader-directed <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for "Paranoid", premiered via <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Revolt_(TV_network)" title="Revolt (TV network)">Revolt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-thesource1_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thesource1-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The single became highly popular, peaking at number 29 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and eventually being <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA).<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 7, 2014, Ty Dolla Sign released his second single for his debut EP, titled "<a href="/wiki/Or_Nah_(Ty_Dolla_Sign_song)" title="Or Nah (Ty Dolla Sign song)">Or Nah</a>" (featuring Wiz Khalifa and DJ Mustard).<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The song's production was handled by DJ Mustard and it contains a <a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">sample</a> of <a href="/wiki/Trillville" title="Trillville">Trillville</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Some_Cut" title="Some Cut">Some Cut</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The music video was filmed during January 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The single was <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remixed</a> featuring vocals from Canadian singer <a href="/wiki/The_Weeknd" title="The Weeknd">the Weeknd</a> and was later certified triple platinum by the RIAA.
</p><p>On January 21, 2014, Ty Dolla Sign released his <a href="/wiki/Record_label#Major_labels" title="Record label">major label</a> debut project, <i><a href="/wiki/Beach_House_EP" title="Beach House EP">Beach House EP</a></i>. The EP features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Casey_Veggies" title="Casey Veggies">Casey Veggies</a>, Wiz Khalifa, <a href="/wiki/Twista" title="Twista">Twista</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Rock" title="Jay Rock">Jay Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trey_Songz" title="Trey Songz">Trey Songz</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fredo_Santana" title="Fredo Santana">Fredo Santana</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">production</a> was primarily handled by Ty Dolla Sign himself, along with DJ Mustard,<sup id="cite_ref-XXL_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-allhiphop1_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allhiphop1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cardo_(record_producer)" title="Cardo (record producer)">Cardo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Young_Chop" title="Young Chop">Young Chop</a>, among others. He stated that the EP would feature a new sound from him, and that it would be on a whole other level compared to his mixtapes.<sup id="cite_ref-allhiphop1_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allhiphop1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Ty Dolla Sign described the EP as being a preview for his debut studio album, at the time due to be released during 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The remix to "Paranoid", featuring Trey Songz, French Montana, and DJ Mustard, was also included on the EP.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.932016:_Free_TC_and_Campaign"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2016:_Free_TC_and_Campaign">2014–2016: <i>Free TC</i> and <i>Campaign</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ty_Dolla_Sign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2014–2016: Free TC and Campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On February 23, 2014, Ty Dolla Sign revealed that his debut studio album would be titled <i><a href="/wiki/Free_TC" title="Free TC">Free TC</a></i>, and would be released during the third quarter of 2014. He told <a href="/wiki/Revolt_(TV_network)" title="Revolt (TV network)">Revolt</a>, that he had already completed eight songs for the album and that he would be going on <a href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">tour</a>, namely Under the Influence 3, with Wiz Khalifa during 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He was chosen for the 2014 <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)#Annual_Freshman_Class_list" title="XXL (magazine)">Freshman Class</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> In May 2014, Ty Dolla Sign told <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fader" title="The Fader">The Fader</a></i>, that he would release a mixtape, titled <i>Sign Language</i>, sometime during the next few months, which would then be followed by <i>Free TC</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-thefader_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> In August 2014, Ty Dolla Sign revealed that <a href="/wiki/Jeremih" title="Jeremih">Jeremih</a>, Wiz Khalifa, YG, <a href="/wiki/Yo_Gotti" title="Yo Gotti">Yo Gotti</a>, and Jay 305, will be appearing on <i>Sign Language</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> On August 24, 2014, which happens to be the same day he released his mixtape <i>Sign Language</i>, Ty Dolla Sign told <i><a href="/wiki/HotNewHipHop" title="HotNewHipHop">HotNewHipHop</a></i>, that he has worked with <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a> on a track from her upcoming album.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While working on his debut album, Ty Dolla Sign appeared on <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Fan_of_a_Fan:_The_Album" title="Fan of a Fan: The Album">Fan of a Fan: The Album</a></i>, on the second track "Nothin' Like Me", as well as <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Play_No_Games" title="Play No Games">Play No Games</a>" off of <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Sky_Paradise" title="Dark Sky Paradise">Dark Sky Paradise</a></i>. On May 26, 2015, Ty Dolla Sign released the first single from <i>Free TC</i>, titled "Only Right", which features TeeCee4800, Joe Moses, and YG, and was produced by <a href="/wiki/Mustard_(record_producer)" title="Mustard (record producer)">DJ Mustard</a>. On June 26, 2015, he released the album's second single, titled "<a href="/wiki/Blas%C3%A9_(song)" title="Blasé (song)">Blasé</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rae_Sremmurd" title="Rae Sremmurd">Rae Sremmurd</a>). "Blasé" would go on to be certified platinum by the RIAA. Ty Dolla Sign's album pre-order as well as "When I See Ya" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Fetty_Wap" title="Fetty Wap">Fetty Wap</a>), became available on September 11, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> The album's third single, "<a href="/wiki/Saved_(Ty_Dolla_Sign_song)" title="Saved (Ty Dolla Sign song)">Saved</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>), went on to be certified gold by the RIAA.
</p><p>Ty Dolla Sign revealed that on October 13, 2015, exactly one month before his album came out, he would release a new mixtape <i>Airplane Mode</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> <i>Free TC</i> was released on November 13, 2015, via Atlantic Records.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The album debuted at number 14 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, with 31,000 <a href="/wiki/Album-equivalent_unit" title="Album-equivalent unit">equivalent album units</a> and first-week sales of 22,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> In February 2016, <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Harmony" title="Fifth Harmony">Fifth Harmony</a> released the single "<a href="/wiki/Work_from_Home_(song)" title="Work from Home (song)">Work from Home</a>" featuring Ty Dolla Sign, which has gone on to peak at number 4 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> In June 2016, Ty Dolla Sign, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, Wiz Khalifa, <a href="/wiki/Imagine_Dragons" title="Imagine Dragons">Imagine Dragons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Logic_(rapper)" title="Logic (rapper)">Logic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/X_Ambassadors" title="X Ambassadors">X Ambassadors</a> released the collaboration single "<a href="/wiki/Sucker_for_Pain" title="Sucker for Pain">Sucker for Pain</a>" for the motion picture <a href="/wiki/Suicide_Squad_(soundtrack)" title="Suicide Squad (soundtrack)">soundtrack</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Suicide_Squad_(2016_film)" title="Suicide Squad (2016 film)">Suicide Squad</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> In August 2016, Ty Dolla Sign and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Jonas" title="Nick Jonas">Nick Jonas</a> performed the song "<a href="/wiki/Bacon_(song)" title="Bacon (song)">Bacon</a>" at the <a href="/wiki/2016_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2016 MTV Video Music Awards">2016 MTV Video Music Awards</a>, where Ty Dolla Sign also won <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award_for_Best_Collaboration" title="MTV Video Music Award for Best Collaboration">Best Collaboration</a> for "Work from Home" with Fifth Harmony.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> On September 23, 2016, Ty Dolla Sign released a project titled <i><a href="/wiki/Campaign_(mixtape)" title="Campaign (mixtape)">Campaign</a></i>, which peaked at number 28 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932018:_Beach_House_3_and_MihTy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2018:_Beach_House_3_and_MihTy">2016–2018: <i>Beach House 3</i> and <i>MihTy</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ty_Dolla_Sign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2016–2018: Beach House 3 and MihTy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 19, 2016, Ty Dolla Sign announced that his next project following <i><a href="/wiki/Campaign_(mixtape)" title="Campaign (mixtape)">Campaign</a></i> would be <i><a href="/wiki/Beach_House_3" title="Beach House 3">Beach House 3</a></i> and that this installment would be an album rather than a mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The lead single "<a href="/wiki/Love_U_Better" title="Love U Better">Love U Better</a>" (featuring Lil Wayne and <a href="/wiki/The-Dream" title="The-Dream">The-Dream</a>) was released July 10, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> The second single "<a href="/wiki/So_Am_I_(Ty_Dolla_Sign_song)" title="So Am I (Ty Dolla Sign song)">So Am I</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Damian_Marley" title="Damian Marley">Damian Marley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skrillex" title="Skrillex">Skrillex</a>) was released on September 1, 2017. The album was released on October 27, 2017, and debuted at number 11 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2018, Ty Dolla Sign was featured on <a href="/wiki/Post_Malone" title="Post Malone">Post Malone</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Psycho_(Post_Malone_song)" title="Psycho (Post Malone song)">Psycho</a>", which peaked at number one on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> In May 2018, he was featured on <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Accelerate_(Christina_Aguilera_song)" title="Accelerate (Christina Aguilera song)">Accelerate</a>" along with <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>, the first single from Aguilera's eighth album <i><a href="/wiki/Liberation_(Christina_Aguilera_album)" title="Liberation (Christina Aguilera album)">Liberation</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> On June 1, he was featured on "<a href="/wiki/All_Mine_(Kanye_West_song)" title="All Mine (Kanye West song)">All Mine</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> on West's album <i><a href="/wiki/Ye_(album)" title="Ye (album)">Ye</a></i>. On June 8, he was featured on "<a href="/wiki/Freeee_(Ghost_Town,_Pt._2)" title="Freeee (Ghost Town, Pt. 2)">Freeee (Ghost Town, Pt. 2)</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Kids_See_Ghosts" title="Kids See Ghosts">Kids See Ghosts</a>, on their <a href="/wiki/Kids_See_Ghosts_(album)" title="Kids See Ghosts (album)">self-titled album</a>. On June 14, he was featured on <a href="/wiki/Bhad_Bhabie" title="Bhad Bhabie">Bhad Bhabie</a>'s single "Trust Me". On June 16, he was featured on "Boss" on <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>'s joint album <i><a href="/wiki/Everything_Is_Love" title="Everything Is Love">Everything Is Love</a></i>. In 2018, he was featured on <a href="/wiki/Tinashe" title="Tinashe">Tinashe</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Me_So_Bad" title="Me So Bad">Me So Bad</a>" with French Montana, which had moderate success in Japan, Australia, and New Zealand. He was also featured on <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>'s album, <i><a href="/wiki/Scorpion_(Drake_album)" title="Scorpion (Drake album)">Scorpion</a></i>, on the song "After Dark". He also featured on <a href="/wiki/Lecrae" title="Lecrae">Lecrae</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/All_Things_Work_Together" title="All Things Work Together">All Things Work Together</a></i> on the song "<a href="/wiki/Blessings_(Lecrae_song)" title="Blessings (Lecrae song)">Blessings</a>".
</p><p>On October 26, 2018, Ty Dolla Sign and Jeremih released a collaborative album under the joint pseudonym MihTy (a <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" class="mw-redirect" title="Portmanteau">portmanteau</a> of both the artists' names) through <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> and Atlantic Records, their respective labels. It was entitled <i><a href="/wiki/MihTy" title="MihTy">MihTy</a></i>, and features guest appearances from French Montana, Chris Brown, and Wiz Khalifa. The duo had released several singles throughout the fall season of 2018 in anticipation of the album's release and performed "The Light" on <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i> in the week leading up to the album's debut. The project also contained the hit single "Goin Thru Some Thangs" which reached #15 on the <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music" title="Apple Music">Apple Music</a> top charts.
</p>
<h3><span id="2019.E2.80.93present:_Featuring_Ty_Dolla_Sign_and_Cheers_to_the_Best_Memories"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–present:_Featuring_Ty_Dolla_Sign_and_Cheers_to_the_Best_Memories">2019–present: <i>Featuring Ty Dolla Sign</i> and <i>Cheers to the Best Memories</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ty_Dolla_Sign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2019–present: Featuring Ty Dolla Sign and Cheers to the Best Memories">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ty Dolla Sign released an album preview in February 2019 (via <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>) featuring a new song produced by <a href="/wiki/D.A._Got_That_Dope" title="D.A. Got That Dope">D.A. Got That Dope</a> along with a verse from Tyga. On May 20, 2019, Ty Dolla Sign released the single "Purple Emoji" (featuring <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>) as the intended first single. On August 9, 2019, he released another single titled "Hottest in the City" (featuring Juicy J and <a href="/wiki/Project_Pat" title="Project Pat">Project Pat</a>). He later announced that his third studio album was originally called <i>Tyrone</i> (named from his birth name) and was set to release fall 2019. However, he stated "I had a meeting one time with Kanye, and played him the album, and he was like, 'Bro nah, you need to do what you do. Add more bass, add more drums, add more, the real shit, that's what no one else is doing. Be the best you, bring that shit all the way up to the forefront...That conversation definitely inspired me, and made me go back and go crazy with the live instruments".<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 1, 2020, Ty Dolla Sign released "<a href="/wiki/Ego_Death_(song)" title="Ego Death (song)">Ego Death</a>" (featuring Kanye West, <a href="/wiki/FKA_Twigs" title="FKA Twigs">FKA Twigs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Skrillex" title="Skrillex">Skrillex</a>) as the official lead single to his third studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> On July 3, 2020, Ty Dolla Sign announced to <a href="/wiki/Big_Boy_(radio_host)" title="Big Boy (radio host)">Big Boy</a> that his third studio album is called <i>Dream House</i> and would be released "sooner than you think".<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> The second single "<a href="/wiki/Expensive_(song)" title="Expensive (song)">Expensive</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>) was released on August 28, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> On October 14, Ty Dolla Sign revealed that he changed the album's title to <i><a href="/wiki/Featuring_Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Featuring Ty Dolla Sign">Featuring Ty Dolla Sign</a></i>, a reference to his work on other artists' projects and songs since his last release. He said the album title came from his gift of collaborating, and features many of his industry friends.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Featuring Ty Dolla Sign</i> was released on October 23, 2020. The album features artists including <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a>, Post Malone, Kanye West, <a href="/wiki/Anderson_.Paak" title="Anderson .Paak">Anderson .Paak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quavo" title="Quavo">Quavo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Durk" title="Lil Durk">Lil Durk</a>, Nicki Minaj, Big Sean, <a href="/wiki/Jhen%C3%A9_Aiko" title="Jhené Aiko">Jhené Aiko</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kehlani" title="Kehlani">Kehlani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>, <a href="/wiki/Musiq_Soulchild" title="Musiq Soulchild">Musiq Soulchild</a>, FKA Twigs, and Skrillex.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Ty Dolla Sign was also featured on the song "Safety Net" with <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Grande" title="Ariana Grande">Ariana Grande</a> for her sixth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Positions_(album)" title="Positions (album)">Positions</a></i> on October 30, 2020.
</p><p>On August 11, 2021, Ty Dolla Sign and Canadian R&amp;B duo <a href="/wiki/Dvsn" title="Dvsn">Dvsn</a> posted the artwork from a forthcoming collaborative album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Cheers_to_the_Best_Memories" title="Cheers to the Best Memories">Cheers to the Best Memories</a></i> to their respective Instagram pages, captioned with the release date of August 20, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> The album featured <a href="/wiki/Rauw_Alejandro" title="Rauw Alejandro">Rauw Alejandro</a> and Ty Dolla Sign's longtime associate YG, as well as a posthumous guest appearance from <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>, with whom Ty Dolla Sign had collaborated in the past.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> The release preceded Ty Dolla Sign's appearances in the following two weeks on "Junya pt 2" from Kanye West's <i><a href="/wiki/Donda" title="Donda">Donda</a></i> and "Get Along Better" from Drake's <i><a href="/wiki/Certified_Lover_Boy" title="Certified Lover Boy">Certified Lover Boy</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ty_Dolla_Sign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ty_Dolla_Sign_2018_March.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Ty_Dolla_Sign_2018_March.jpg/170px-Ty_Dolla_Sign_2018_March.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="238" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Ty_Dolla_Sign_2018_March.jpg/255px-Ty_Dolla_Sign_2018_March.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Ty_Dolla_Sign_2018_March.jpg/340px-Ty_Dolla_Sign_2018_March.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3298" data-file-height="4620" /></a><figcaption>Ty Dolla Sign performing in March 2018.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Ty Dolla Sign's sound is a combination of <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">contemporary R&amp;B</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> elements. When asked if he was a rapper, he stated, "People would call it rap, but I really don't feel like I rap. There's so many great rappers, like if we had to <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">battle</a> or some shit, they would just cream me. I don't really consider myself a rapper, I just happen to have <a href="/wiki/Bar_(music)" title="Bar (music)">bars</a>, or something like that. I still sing man, I'm a singer, ya feel me?"<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He cited <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a> and Chris Brown as main influences for his musical style.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> When asked about his influences, Ty Dolla Sign responded, "<a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">2Pac</a> is my favorite artist of all time. I liked <a href="/wiki/Slum_Village" title="Slum Village">Slum Village</a> a lot back then, like <a href="/wiki/J_Dilla" title="J Dilla">J Dilla</a>, he's one of my favorite producers and artists. I'm like a hip-hop head type of dude. <a href="/wiki/Mos_Def" title="Mos Def">Mos Def</a>, <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, that whole <a href="/wiki/Rawkus_Records" title="Rawkus Records">Rawkus Records</a> scene, that's my type of shit." He continued, "I also like cats like <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>. Just like his production, he's kind of like the same thing as me. He sings, he produces, he <a href="/wiki/Multi-instrumentalist" title="Multi-instrumentalist">plays all types of instruments</a>. That's what I'm after. I wouldn't wear that type of clothes or wear my hair like that. [Laughs.] But as far as artistry, that's the same type of dude I am."<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> In an interview he revealed that <a href="/wiki/Kim_Burrell" title="Kim Burrell">Kim Burrell</a> was his absolute favorite vocalist, and that <a href="/wiki/Brandy_Norwood" title="Brandy Norwood">Brandy</a> was right there with her.<sup id="cite_ref-MalcolmMusic_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MalcolmMusic-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ty_Dolla_Sign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ty Dolla Sign has a daughter named Jailynn Crystal.<sup id="cite_ref-thefader_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He was in a relationship with Fifth Harmony member <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Jauregui" title="Lauren Jauregui">Lauren Jauregui</a> from 2017 to 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 10, 2018, it was revealed that Ty Dolla Sign was facing up to fifteen years in jail in relation to <a href="/wiki/Cocaine" title="Cocaine">cocaine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">marijuana</a> possession charges.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> These charges resulted from a traffic stop in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, where in a car with multiple people, Ty Dolla Sign was the only one charged with the drugs due to him being in close proximity to them.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> Later, all charges were dropped upon completion of a drug prevention program.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, Ty Dolla Sign formally endorsed the <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West_2020_presidential_campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Kanye West 2020 presidential campaign">presidential campaign</a> of fellow rapper and frequent collaborator Kanye West.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
