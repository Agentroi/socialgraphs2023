<p><b>Sydney Loren Bennett</b> (born April 23, 1992),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Syd</b> (formerly <b>Syd tha Kyd</b>), is an American musician and singer from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, California. She initially gained recognition as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Alternative_hip_hop" title="Alternative hip hop">alternative hip hop</a> collective <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a>, and went on to found her own band <a href="/wiki/The_Internet_(band)" title="The Internet (band)">The Internet</a>, in 2011. Bennett released her 2017 debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Fin_(Syd_album)" title="Fin (Syd album)">Fin</a></i>, followed by the EP <i><a href="/wiki/Always_Never_Home" title="Always Never Home">Always Never Home</a></i>. She released her second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Broken_Hearts_Club_(album)" title="Broken Hearts Club (album)">Broken Hearts Club</a></i>, in 2022.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syd_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Growing up in a musical family influenced Bennett's interest in music. Her mother once aspired to be a DJ and her uncle is <a href="/wiki/Mikey_Bennett" title="Mikey Bennett">Mikey Bennett</a>, an internationally popular <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> producer and studio owner from <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MassApeal_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MassApeal-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As she explained, "I began wishing I could take credit for some of my favorite songs. That was when I started to make my own – I only began singing on my own songs when I really started writing."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> When Bennett was 14, she built a small music studio in her home and worked on sound engineering before getting into production.<sup id="cite_ref-MassApeal_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MassApeal-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Her brother is <a href="/wiki/Travis_%22Taco%22_Bennett" class="mw-redirect" title="Travis &quot;Taco&quot; Bennett">Travis "Taco" Bennett</a>.
</p><p>For the first half of her high school years, Syd attended <a href="/wiki/Palisades_Charter_High_School" title="Palisades Charter High School">Palisades Charter High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MassApeal_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MassApeal-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> She felt left out and had few friends at Palisades and moved to the Hamilton Music Academy, which she considered a more open-minded school.<sup id="cite_ref-MassApeal_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MassApeal-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Syd_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Syd began making music while she was still living with her parents.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Syd's stage name was given to her by her older brother, Ty, as a kid. After growing out of it, she reclaimed the name when she joined <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Most of the group's original songs were recorded in Syd's house, also known as "The Trap". In 2014, Syd opened for <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> at Wembley Stadium as part of the Odd Future collective.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 13, 2017, Syd's debut solo single "All About Me" was released. It was produced by <a href="/wiki/The_Internet_(band)" title="The Internet (band)">the Internet</a> cohort <a href="/wiki/Steve_Lacy_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Steve Lacy (musician)">Steve Lacy</a>. On January 24, 2017, her second solo single "Body" was released in anticipation for her album in collaboration with Columbia Records, <i><a href="/wiki/Fin_(Syd_album)" title="Fin (Syd album)">Fin</a></i>, which was released on February 3, 2017. On May 18, 2017, Syd starred together with Korean R&amp;B artist <a href="/wiki/Dean_(South_Korean_singer)" title="Dean (South Korean singer)">Dean</a> in his music video for their collaboration "Love".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 7, 2017, Syd released <i><a href="/wiki/Always_Never_Home" title="Always Never Home">Always Never Home</a></i>, a three-track EP. It was the follow-up to her debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Fin_(Syd_album)" title="Fin (Syd album)">Fin</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Syd was featured on <a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert" title="Lil Uzi Vert">Lil Uzi Vert</a>'s second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Eternal_Atake" title="Eternal Atake">Eternal Atake</a></i> on the song "Urgency", which was released on March 6, 2020. She was featured on the song "When Love's Around" on <a href="/wiki/Zayn_Malik" title="Zayn Malik">Zayn</a>'s third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Nobody_Is_Listening" title="Nobody Is Listening">Nobody Is Listening</a></i>, which was released on January 15, 2021.
</p><p>On February 12, 2021, Syd released a single titled "Missing Out" followed by "Fast Car" on July 16, 2021, and "Right Track" on August 10, 2021. Syd's single "Cybah" featuring <a href="/wiki/Lucky_Daye" title="Lucky Daye">Lucky Daye</a> was released on March 18, 2022. She followed this release with the announcement of her 2022 Broken Hearts Club Tour with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Destin_Conrad&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Destin Conrad (page does not exist)">Destin Conrad</a> as the opener.
</p><p>Syd was credited on "Plastic Off the Sofa," the eighth track on <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>'s seventh studio album, <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_(Beyonc%C3%A9_album)" title="Renaissance (Beyoncé album)"><i>Renaissance</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Syd also appeared on <a href="/wiki/Duckwrth" title="Duckwrth">Duckwrth</a>'s single "Ce Soir", which later appeared on his third EP <i>Chrome Bull</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 8, 2022, Syd released her second album, <i>Broken Hearts Club.</i> The project began as a collection of love songs, but was completed after Syd's relationship came to an end, culminating in a mix of tracks about love and heartbreak.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
