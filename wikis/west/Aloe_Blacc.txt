<p><b>Egbert Nathaniel Dawkins III</b> (born January 7, 1979),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known professionally by his stage name <b>Aloe Blacc</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/æ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;bad&#39;">æ</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="/oʊ/: &#39;o&#39; in &#39;code&#39;">oʊ</span></span><span class="wrap"> </span><span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;b&#39; in &#39;buy&#39;">b</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="/æ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;bad&#39;">æ</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span></span>/</a></span></span>), is an American singer, songwriter and rapper. He is best known for his singles "<a href="/wiki/I_Need_a_Dollar" title="I Need a Dollar">I Need a Dollar</a>", "<a href="/wiki/The_Man_(Aloe_Blacc_song)" title="The Man (Aloe Blacc song)">The Man</a>", which topped the charts in the United Kingdom, and for writing and performing vocals on <a href="/wiki/Avicii" title="Avicii">Avicii</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Wake_Me_Up_(Avicii_song)" title="Wake Me Up (Avicii song)">Wake Me Up</a>", which topped the charts in 22 countries, including Australia and the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-RollingStone_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RollingStone-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MTV_Wake_Me_Up_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV_Wake_Me_Up-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Aside from his solo career, Blacc is also a member of hip hop duo <a href="/wiki/Emanon" title="Emanon">Emanon</a>, alongside American record producer <a href="/wiki/Exile_(producer)" title="Exile (producer)">Exile</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aloe_Blacc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blacc was born Egbert Nathaniel Dawkins III to <a href="/wiki/Panamanian" class="mw-redirect" title="Panamanian">Panamanian</a> parents in Southern California's <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_California" title="Orange County, California">Orange County</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Growing up in <a href="/wiki/Laguna_Hills,_California" title="Laguna Hills, California">Laguna Hills</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Bose_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bose-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> he began playing a rented trumpet in third grade. When it made more sense to buy the instrument, Blacc had what he later described as a "very specific moment" in his evolution as a musician. "It forced me to be serious about it. I couldn't just do it to get out of the class room," he said in a 2010 interview.  His exposure to <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a> in fourth grade was equally significant. "It wasn't too far off from the trumpet moment...I had a hip hop moment and a musician moment."<sup id="cite_ref-Hip_Hop_DX_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hip_Hop_DX-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A Renaissance and Trustee Scholar at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a>, Blacc majored in linguistics and psychology and graduated in 2001. He worked briefly in the corporate sector for <a href="/wiki/Ernst_%26_Young" title="Ernst &amp; Young">Ernst &amp; Young</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bose_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bose-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LA_Times_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA_Times-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aloe_Blacc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1995.E2.80.932002:_Early_career"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1995–2002:_Early_career">1995–2002: Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aloe_Blacc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1995–2002: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1995, Blacc teamed with hip hop producer Exile and formed <a href="/wiki/Emanon" title="Emanon">Emanon</a> -- 'no name' backwards—which was inspired by the title of the <a href="/wiki/Dizzy_Gillespie" title="Dizzy Gillespie">Dizzy Gillespie</a> song "Emanon".<sup id="cite_ref-Hip_Hop_DX_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hip_Hop_DX-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> With break-beat loops and jazz samples, Emanon became a mainstay of the indie rap underground, and released their first mixtape in 1996, followed by the EP <i>Acid 9</i> in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-LA_Times_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA_Times-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> They subsequently released six projects, the demo album <i>Imaginary Friends</i> (1996), a compilation  <i>Steps Through Time</i> (2001) the albums <i>The Waiting Room</i> (2005) and <i>Dystopia</i> (2016) and EPs <i>Acid 9</i> (1998) and <i>Anon &amp; On (2002)</i> respectively. A fourth album, <i>Bird's Eye View</i>, was recorded but shelved, it is unknown if the material was reworked into their 2016 reunion album <i>Dystopia</i>. While Emanon was Blacc's primary project during this time period, he additionally toured and recorded with the members of the collective Lootpack and worked with the French jazz group Jazz Liberatorz.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Emanon appeared as contestants on a 1998 episode of <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cut_(MTV)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Cut (MTV)">The Cut</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2003.E2.80.932009:_Shine_Through"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2003–2009:_Shine_Through">2003–2009: <i>Shine Through</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aloe_Blacc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2003–2009: Shine Through">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Shine_Through" title="Shine Through">Shine Through</a></div>
<p>Blacc launched his career as a solo artist in 2003, releasing two EPs, and signing to Stone's Throw Records in 2006, after label head Chris Manak (known as <a href="/wiki/Peanut_Butter_Wolf" title="Peanut Butter Wolf">Peanut Butter Wolf</a>) heard Blacc and immediately offered him a contract for the 2006 full-length <i>Shine Through</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-LA_Times_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA_Times-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> By then, he had become more focused on songwriting, a change inspired in part by his social consciousness. "I was uncomfortable with the state of hip hop being largely about the expression of ego. I wondered how I could be more crafty at writing songs in the form of a rap that actually expressed more than ego, style and finesse," he stated. "I figured I would educate myself to learn more about songwriting and apply that later to hip hop."<sup id="cite_ref-Hip_Hop_DX_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hip_Hop_DX-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Shine Through</i> was Blacc's first full-length album and was released in 2006. It received significant media attention in the U.S. and abroad. <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" class="mw-redirect" title="Pitchfork Media">Pitchfork</a></i> wrote that <i>Shine Through</i> had "flashes of keen musical interpolation" and signaled "some sort of greatness",<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/NPR" title="NPR">NPR</a> named the track "Nascimento" as song of the day, and <i><a href="/wiki/Absolute_Punk" class="mw-redirect" title="Absolute Punk">Absolute Punk</a></i> noted that <i>Shine Through</i> flows beautifully from one track to the next, infusing old-school funk and soul with a modern essence that makes it incredibly unique <sup id="cite_ref-Song_of_the_Day_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Song_of_the_Day-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
Also in 2009, while working on his second album, Blacc toured Europe and the United States with Emanon, and collaborated with the Japanese hip hop producer <a href="/wiki/Cradle_Orchestra" title="Cradle Orchestra">Cradle</a> on a project called Bee.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, Blacc attended the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Music_Academy" title="Red Bull Music Academy">Red Bull Music Academy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932011:_Good_Things"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2011:_Good_Things">2010–2011: <i>Good Things</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aloe_Blacc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010–2011: Good Things">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Good_Things_(Aloe_Blacc_album)" title="Good Things (Aloe Blacc album)">Good Things (Aloe Blacc album)</a></div>
<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:North_Sea_Jazz_Festival_2012_-_ALOE_BLACC.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/North_Sea_Jazz_Festival_2012_-_ALOE_BLACC.jpg/250px-North_Sea_Jazz_Festival_2012_-_ALOE_BLACC.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="145" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/North_Sea_Jazz_Festival_2012_-_ALOE_BLACC.jpg/375px-North_Sea_Jazz_Festival_2012_-_ALOE_BLACC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/North_Sea_Jazz_Festival_2012_-_ALOE_BLACC.jpg/500px-North_Sea_Jazz_Festival_2012_-_ALOE_BLACC.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="592" /></a><figcaption>Aloe Blacc with Randal Fisher (saxophone); Chris Bautista (trumpet) live at the NSJ Festival, Rotterdam, July 8, 2012</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2010, Blacc released <i>Good Things</i> on Stones Throw Records. A commercial success, <i>Good Things</i> was certified gold in the UK, France, Germany and Australia, among other countries, and ultimately hit double platinum sales. The single "<a href="/wiki/I_Need_A_Dollar" class="mw-redirect" title="I Need A Dollar">I Need A Dollar</a>", which was used as the theme song to the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Make_It_in_America" title="How to Make It in America">How To Make It in America</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-EW_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EW-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> reached 1 million in sales in 2013; two additional singles, "Loving You Is Killing Me" and "Green Lights" became European hits as well.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <i>Good Things</i> was praised by the media, receiving positive reviews in the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-EW_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EW-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Shortly after the release of the record, Blacc signed with <a href="/wiki/Simon_Fuller" title="Simon Fuller">Simon Fuller</a>'s XIX Management.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, Blacc contributed a track to the album <i>Red Hot + Rio 2</i>, a follow-up to the 1996 <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_%2B_Rio" title="Red Hot + Rio">Red Hot + Rio</a></i>, with all proceeds donated to raise money and awareness to fight AIDS/HIV and related health and social issues. That summer, Blacc performed on the main stages at major festivals such as <a href="/wiki/Glastonbury" title="Glastonbury">Glastonbury</a> and The <a href="/wiki/Falls_Festival" title="Falls Festival">Falls Festival</a> in Australia with his primary touring band The Grand Scheme. In 2012, he appeared at the renowned <a href="/wiki/North_Sea_Jazz" class="mw-redirect" title="North Sea Jazz">North Sea Jazz</a> festival in <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a> (<a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Osheaga" class="mw-redirect" title="Osheaga">Osheaga</a> (<a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.93present:_Major_label_debut_Lift_Your_Spirit"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–present:_Major_label_debut_Lift_Your_Spirit">2012–present: Major label debut <i>Lift Your Spirit</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aloe_Blacc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2012–present: Major label debut Lift Your Spirit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lift_Your_Spirit" title="Lift Your Spirit">Lift Your Spirit</a></div>
<p>In 2012, Blacc joined a music project, <a href="/wiki/Roseaux_(band)" title="Roseaux (band)">Roseaux</a> established by Emile Omar. Other musicians include Alex Finkin and Clement Petit. The band released its debut self-titled album <i>Roseaux</i>, with all eleven tracks featuring the vocals of Aloe Blacc. The album reached #92 in the French Albums Chart. The debut single, "More Than Material", peaked at #80 on the French singles chart.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Aloe Blacc was introduced to Swedish DJ <a href="/wiki/Avicii" title="Avicii">Avicii</a> by <a href="/wiki/Linkin_Park" title="Linkin Park">Linkin Park</a>'s co-vocalist <a href="/wiki/Mike_Shinoda" title="Mike Shinoda">Mike Shinoda</a> who was a good friend of Avicii. As a result, in 2013, Blacc co-wrote the song  "<a href="/wiki/Wake_Me_Up_(Avicii_song)" title="Wake Me Up (Avicii song)">Wake Me Up</a>" with Avicii.<sup id="cite_ref-RollingStone_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RollingStone-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> With Blacc on vocals, the song reached #1 in 103 countries and became the fastest selling single in the UK, selling 267,000 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He also collaborated with Avicii on a track called "Liar Liar" that featured <a href="/wiki/Blondfire" title="Blondfire">Blondfire</a> as well. In September of that year, Blacc signed with <a href="/wiki/Interscope" class="mw-redirect" title="Interscope">Interscope</a> Records<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and released an EP, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Wake_Me_Up_EP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wake Me Up EP">Wake Me Up EP</a></i>. The five-track EP included the songs "Love is the Answer", "Can You Do This", and "Ticking Bomb", as well as an acoustic version of "Wake Me Up".<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> "Ticking Bomb" was used as the background music for the new <i><a href="/wiki/Battlefield_4" title="Battlefield 4">Battlefield 4</a></i> game TV advert, and "The Man" as background music for the <a href="/wiki/Beats_Electronics" title="Beats Electronics">Beats by Dr. Dre</a> commercials featuring <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Garnett" title="Kevin Garnett">Kevin Garnett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colin_Kaepernick" title="Colin Kaepernick">Colin Kaepernick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Sherman_(American_football)" title="Richard Sherman (American football)">Richard Sherman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cesc_F%C3%A0bregas" title="Cesc Fàbregas">Cesc Fàbregas</a>. "Ticking Bomb" is also the theme for the WWE Pay-Per-View Payback.
</p><p>In October 2013, Blacc released the music video for the track "<a href="/wiki/Wake_Me_Up_(EP)" title="Wake Me Up (EP)">Wake Me Up</a>". Blacc collaborated with the immigrant rights group <a href="/wiki/National_Day_Laborer_Organizing_Network" title="National Day Laborer Organizing Network">National Day Laborer Organizing Network</a> and the ABC* Foundation's Healing Power of Music Initiative. The director was <a href="/wiki/Alex_Rivera" title="Alex Rivera">Alex Rivera</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The cast were immigrant activists: Hareth Andrade Ayala (a <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a> leader in the immigrant youth movement working to stop her own father's deportation), Agustin Chiprez Alvarez (a Los Angeles day laborer), and Margarita Reyes who was deported with her mother as a child despite being born in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Blacc's Interscope/XIX debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Lift_Your_Spirit" title="Lift Your Spirit">Lift Your Spirit</a></i>, was released in November 2013, and featured production by <a href="/wiki/Pharrell" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharrell">Pharrell</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khalil" title="DJ Khalil">DJ Khalil</a>, and songwriter Harold Lilly, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> In the UK, he helped promote his album by performing tracks from it on <i><a href="/wiki/Later..._with_Jools_Holland" title="Later... with Jools Holland">Later... with Jools Holland</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Aloe_Blacc_@_Sir_Stewart_Bovell_Park_(7_1_12)_(6693047439).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Aloe_Blacc_%40_Sir_Stewart_Bovell_Park_%287_1_12%29_%286693047439%29.jpg/170px-Aloe_Blacc_%40_Sir_Stewart_Bovell_Park_%287_1_12%29_%286693047439%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="256" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Aloe_Blacc_%40_Sir_Stewart_Bovell_Park_%287_1_12%29_%286693047439%29.jpg/255px-Aloe_Blacc_%40_Sir_Stewart_Bovell_Park_%287_1_12%29_%286693047439%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Aloe_Blacc_%40_Sir_Stewart_Bovell_Park_%287_1_12%29_%286693047439%29.jpg/340px-Aloe_Blacc_%40_Sir_Stewart_Bovell_Park_%287_1_12%29_%286693047439%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="452" data-file-height="680" /></a><figcaption>Aloe Blacc in 2012</figcaption></figure>
<p>Released in May 2014, Blacc features on the song "The World is Ours" on the album <i><a href="/wiki/One_Love,_One_Rhythm_%E2%80%93_The_2014_FIFA_World_Cup_Official_Album" title="One Love, One Rhythm – The 2014 FIFA World Cup Official Album">One Love, One Rhythm</a></i>, a compilation album released for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> which was held in Brazil in June and July 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was announced that Blacc would be the guest advisor for <a href="/wiki/Adam_Levine" title="Adam Levine">Adam Levine</a>'s team on <a href="/wiki/The_Voice_(U.S._season_6)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Voice (U.S. season 6)">Season 6</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Voice (U.S. TV series)">The Voice</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aloe performed "America the Beautiful" to open <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_31" title="WrestleMania 31">WrestleMania 31</a> at the Levi's stadium in California.
</p><p>On May 5, 2015, a sneak peek of <a href="/wiki/Owl_City" title="Owl City">Owl City</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Verge_(song)" title="Verge (song)">Verge</a>" featuring Aloe Blacc aired on <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>'s "Draft Academy". The song was released on May 14, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2016, Blacc announced his contribution to the film <i><a href="/wiki/Race_(2016_film)" title="Race (2016 film)">Race</a></i>, a song called "Let the Games Begin".
</p><p>On June 19, 2016, Blacc sang the national anthem and performed during the halftime show at the NBA finals, in which the Cleveland Cavaliers played the Golden State Warriors.
</p><p>In 2016, Exile and Aloe Blacc released their first album as Emanon since 2005, with the release of Dystopia. The album features production by Exile and Aloe, and guest rappers <a href="/wiki/Blu_(rapper)" title="Blu (rapper)">Blu</a> and Cashus King.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Blacc provided vocals for the <a href="/wiki/Ti%C3%ABsto" title="Tiësto">Tiësto</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Carry_You_Home_(Ti%C3%ABsto_song)" title="Carry You Home (Tiësto song)">Carry You Home</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 20, 2018, Blacc performed "Wake Me Up" with <a href="/wiki/Ti%C3%ABsto" title="Tiësto">Tiësto</a> as part of a tribute to Avicii at <a href="/wiki/Electric_Daisy_Carnival" title="Electric Daisy Carnival">Electric Daisy Carnival</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A posthumous song with Avicii, titled "<a href="/wiki/SOS_(Avicii_song)" title="SOS (Avicii song)">SOS</a>" was released on April 10.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The following month on May 16, Blacc was featured on a song with <a href="/wiki/Gryffin" title="Gryffin">Gryffin</a> titled "Hurt People" and finally on December 5, 2019, Blacc performed Wake Me Up and SOS at the Avicii Tribute Concert, held at <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>'s Friends Arena.
</p><p>In 2020, Blacc competed on season 4 of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Masked_Singer_(American_TV_series)" title="The Masked Singer (American TV series)">The Masked Singer</a></i> as the "Mushroom" and finished in second place behind <a href="/wiki/LeAnn_Rimes" title="LeAnn Rimes">LeAnn Rimes</a> as the "Sun".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aloe_Blacc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blacc is actively involved in <a href="/wiki/Malaria_No_More" title="Malaria No More">Malaria No More</a>. The charity's mission is to end malaria deaths, through "engaging leaders, rallying the public, and delivering life-saving tools and education to families across Africa."<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aloe_Blacc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aloe Blacc is married to Mexican-Australian rapper <a href="/wiki/Maya_Jupiter" title="Maya Jupiter">Maya Jupiter</a>. In 2013, they had their first child, a daughter, Mandela.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> In January 2016, they had their second child, a son.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
