<p><b>Simmie Sims III</b> (born September 10, 1993), known professionally as <b>Buddy</b>, is an American rapper, singer, dancer and actor. Previously on the <a href="/wiki/I_Am_Other" title="I Am Other">I Am Other</a> label, he is now signed to Cool Lil Company and <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>. He is also a member of the hip-hop supergroup&#160;Zoink Gang, with <a href="/wiki/JID" title="JID">JID</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smino" title="Smino">Smino</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guapdad_4000" title="Guapdad 4000">Guapdad 4000</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buddy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Introduced to gospel and soul by his father, a preacher and choir director, he began his performance career at age seven by starring in plays and musicals including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wiz" title="The Wiz">The Wiz</a></i>, <i>Oliver With a Twist</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Dreamgirls" title="Dreamgirls">Dreamgirls</a></i> at Amazing Grace Conservatory. "I grew up as a full-fledged entertainer rather than just somebody trying to rap about stuff," he has said about that experience.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> When the school became too expensive he withdrew, later attending a high school performing arts program in <a href="/wiki/Long_Beach,_California" title="Long Beach, California">Long Beach, California</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buddy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932015:_Career_beginnings_and_signing_to_Star_Trak"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2015:_Career_beginnings_and_signing_to_Star_Trak">2009–2015: Career beginnings and signing to Star Trak</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buddy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009–2015: Career beginnings and signing to Star Trak">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a teenager he began recording his own material. In 2009, he got a break when TV music supervisor Scott Vener showed <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a> an early effort by the 15-year-old Buddy. Impressed, Pharrell began to work with the teenager, and signed him to his <a href="/wiki/I_Am_Other" title="I Am Other">I Am Other</a> creative collective and record label.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> When Buddy was 18, his "Awesome Awesome" was produced by <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">The Neptunes</a> and Pharrell made an appearance in the video.
</p><p>On March 28, 2012 he released the single "Staircases" produced by Pharrell and featuring <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, continuing to work with Pharrell, he released his debut mixtape, <i>Idle Time</i>, which featured Kendrick Lamar, <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Gibbs" title="Freddie Gibbs">Freddie Gibbs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robin_Thicke" title="Robin Thicke">Robin Thicke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sir_Michael_Rocks" title="Sir Michael Rocks">Sir Michael Rocks</a>, and others, with production from Pharrell Williams, <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Inglish" title="Chuck Inglish">Chuck Inglish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boi-1da" title="Boi-1da">Boi-1da</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cardo" title="Cardo">Cardo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blaq" class="mw-redirect" title="Blaq">Blaq</a>, and Polyester among others.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He then embarked on a series of collaborations with various artists, including <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a>, <a href="/wiki/BJ_The_Chicago_Kid" class="mw-redirect" title="BJ The Chicago Kid">BJ The Chicago Kid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chance_the_Rapper" title="Chance the Rapper">Chance the Rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/A$AP_Rocky" class="mw-redirect" title="A$AP Rocky">A$AP Rocky</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.9317:_Ocean_.26_Montana_and_Magnolia"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–17:_Ocean_&amp;_Montana_and_Magnolia">2016–17: <i>Ocean &amp; Montana</i> and <i>Magnolia</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buddy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2016–17: Ocean &amp; Montana and Magnolia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2016 he put out his own single, "Shine," produced by <a href="/wiki/Mike_%26_Keys" title="Mike &amp; Keys">Mike &amp; Keys</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khalil" title="DJ Khalil">DJ Khalil</a>. It was premiered by hip-hop/R&amp;B tastemaker Carl Chery, with a video directed by Andy Hines featuring a cameo from <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a>. The song garnered over 2.7 million streams on <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a>, 12 million on <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music" title="Apple Music">Apple Music</a>, and nearly half a million views on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Buddy described the single as "a ghetto hymn acknowledging inevitable death while showing gratitude for abundant life."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On August 17, he released his second EP <i>Magnolia</i> which included features from Boogie, Wiz Khalifa, and Kent Jamz.
</p><p>His EP <i>Ocean &amp; Montana</i>, produced by the Haitian-Canadian DJ and producer <a href="/wiki/Kaytranada" title="Kaytranada">Kaytranada</a>, was released May 19, 2017 by Cool LIL Company. It featured the single "Find Me." The EP's title refers to the intersection in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica,_California" title="Santa Monica, California">Santa Monica</a> where he lived, sought inspiration, and began collaborating with <a href="/wiki/Kaytranada" title="Kaytranada">Kaytranada</a> after leaving his parents' home.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2018.E2.80.93present:_Harlan_.26_Alondra"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–present:_Harlan_&amp;_Alondra">2018–present: <i>Harlan &amp; Alondra</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buddy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2018–present: Harlan &amp; Alondra">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He released his debut full-length album <i><a href="/wiki/Harlan_%26_Alondra" title="Harlan &amp; Alondra">Harlan &amp; Alondra</a></i> on Cool Lil Company/<a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> on July 20, 2018. It includes the singles "Black" feat. <a href="/wiki/A$AP_Ferg" class="mw-redirect" title="A$AP Ferg">A$AP Ferg</a>, "Trouble on Central," "Hey Up There" feat. <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_$ign" class="mw-redirect" title="Ty Dolla $ign">Ty Dolla $ign</a>, and "Trippin'" feat. <a href="/wiki/Khalid_(singer)" title="Khalid (singer)">Khalid</a>. His aim, he has said, was "to paint a well-rounded portrait of my home city", <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.
</p><p>In spring 2018 he joined <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Badass</a> on the Amerikkkana Tour.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> His summer 2018 tour with A$AP Ferg included performances at <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a> festival.
</p><p>On April 17, 2020, he released a collaboration album with Kent Jamz, titled <i>Janktape Vol. 1</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
