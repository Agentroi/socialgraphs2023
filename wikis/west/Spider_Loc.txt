<p><b>Curtis Williams</b>  (born January 19, 1976), more commonly known as <b>Spider Loc</b>, is an American rapper and actor. He was formerly signed to <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>'s <a href="/wiki/G-Unit_Records" title="G-Unit Records">G-Unit Records</a>.
</p>

<p>Spider Loc was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Suge_Knight" title="Suge Knight">Suge Knight</a> and tagged along with <a href="/wiki/Death_Row_Records" title="Death Row Records">Death Row Records</a>, he later appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Death_Row_Records" title="Death Row Records">Death Row Records</a> released soundtrack to the movie <a href="/wiki/Dysfunktional_Family_(soundtrack)" title="Dysfunktional Family (soundtrack)">Dysfunktional Family soundtrack</a>, and also was affiliated with <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a>'s Against Tha Grain record label under the name "Spider", but never signed an agreement. In September 2004 Spider Loc met rapper <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a> in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> and performed for him. A week later in Los Angeles, Young Buck introduced him to 50 Cent on the set of the "<a href="/wiki/Shorty_Wanna_Ride" title="Shorty Wanna Ride">Shorty Wanna Ride</a>" video and he performed a freestyle rap. After four months the rapper signed a record deal with <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>.
</p><p>Spider Loc has since been featured on many G-Unit mixtapes such as <i>G-Unit Radio Part 18 - Rags to Riches</i> and has also released his own mixtape G-Unit West title <i>Bangadoshish</i> with artists Young Buck, <a href="/wiki/Big_Syke" title="Big Syke">Big Syke</a> (from Thug Life), 50 Cent, and <a href="/wiki/Olivia_(singer)" title="Olivia (singer)">Olivia</a>.
</p><p>In September 2007, he released a street album, <i><a href="/wiki/West_Kept_Secret:_The_Prequel" class="mw-redirect" title="West Kept Secret: The Prequel">West Kept Secret: The Prequel</a></i> and the following June released a follow-up project <i><a href="/wiki/Da_1_U_Luv_2_Hate" title="Da 1 U Luv 2 Hate">Da 1 U Luv 2 Hate</a></i>.
</p><p>In an interview with HipHopDX released March 27, 2011, Spider Loc confirmed that he is still signed to G-Unit and did not move to EMI.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2011 Spider stated that G-Unit West was back and up and running with the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Slim_da_Mobster" class="mw-redirect" title="Slim da Mobster">Slim da Mobster</a>.
</p><p>Spider Loc was dropped from G-Unit in late 2011.
</p><p>On June 8, 2017, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.datwav.com/remember-g-unit-signee-spider-loc-well-wheel-fortune-video/">Spider Loc appeared on <i>Wheel of Fortune</i></a>. During the episode, he stated he is currently employed as a security officer and is the father of 6 kids.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He also runs his own record label, BAYMAAC Records.
</p><p>In 2022 he launched a podcast entitled "Factz Ova Feelinz" on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Alonso&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alex Alonso (page does not exist)">Alex Alonso</a>'s LAnd media Network.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spider_Loc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2005, while Spider Loc and friends were engaged in a fight with rapper <a href="/wiki/Yukmouth" title="Yukmouth">Yukmouth</a> and his companions in a Los Angeles nightclub, one of Spider Loc's friends stole jewelry from Yukmouth, which was later <a href="/wiki/Ransom" title="Ransom">ransomed</a> by its owner, Yukmouth.<sup id="cite_ref-interview_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-interview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Yukmouth has said that the two have made up.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2008, Spider Loc, 40 Glocc, Village Boo and a large amount of <a href="/wiki/Crips" title="Crips">Crips</a> are seen on a <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> video taunting <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birdman_(rapper)" title="Birdman (rapper)">Birdman</a> and Slim of <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money Records</a>. Lil Wayne was leaving his "Get Money" video shoot in Los Angeles when his two SUV's were blocked off by surrounding cars on a one way street.  Spider Loc is seen walking around the cars and banging on the windows.

</p>
