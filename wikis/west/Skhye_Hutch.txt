<p><b>Derrick Hutchins, Jr.</b>, better known as his stage name <b>Skhye Hutch</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> record producer from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. The two-time Grammy nominee attended <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Palisades_High_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Palisades High School">Pacific Palisades High School</a> and graduated from the Musicians Institution in 2004.
</p><p>He began working with the production R&amp;B duo <a href="/wiki/The_Underdogs_(duo)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Underdogs (duo)">The Underdogs</a>. During this time, he met Rob Knox, now a member of <a href="/wiki/The_Y%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="The Y&#39;s">The Y's</a>. During his apprenticeship he met <a href="/wiki/1500_or_Nothin%27" title="1500 or Nothin&#39;">1500 or Nothin'</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyrese_Gibson" title="Tyrese Gibson">Tyrese</a>, Siege Monstracity and <a href="/wiki/Curtiss_King" title="Curtiss King">Curtiss King</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skhye_Hutch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2007, Skhye signed his first development deal with Mad Heaven as a producer and artist for the group Reign Major. The group released the album <i>Growing Pains</i>, on which all but one song was produced by Skhye.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Digi%2BPhonics" title="Digi+Phonics">Digi+Phonics</a> affiliate was introduced to <a href="/wiki/Carson,_California" title="Carson, California">Carson</a>-based independent record label <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a> (TDE) recording artists <a href="/wiki/Black_Hippy" title="Black Hippy">Black Hippy</a> by Grammy Winning producer <a href="/wiki/Tae_Beast" class="mw-redirect" title="Tae Beast">Tae Beast</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Section_80" class="mw-redirect" title="Section 80">Section 80</a> mix-tape era.
</p><p>In 2010, Skhye Hutch produced "Space Bars" by <a href="/wiki/Curtiss_King" title="Curtiss King">Curtiss King</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul" title="Ab-Soul">Ab-Soul</a> &amp; Young Rook.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>2012 proved to be a banner year for Skhye as he began to work with TDE artists on solo projects. Hutch produced three hip hop tracks for Ab-Soul's debut album <i>Control System</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The highly successful “calm and stoic song” single "ILLuminate". He also produced the album tracks "Empathy" featuring Alori Joh, Javonte and "Beautiful Death" featuring TDE Vice President Punch and <a href="/wiki/Ash_Riser" title="Ash Riser">Ash Riser</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Building on his relationships, he landed a single on <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>’s 2012 highly anticipated, <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Kid_Maad_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Good Kid Maad City">Good Kid M.A.A.D. City</a></i> producing <i>Dying of Thirst</i>. The album was nominated at the <a href="/wiki/56th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="56th Annual Grammy Awards">56th Annual Grammy Awards</a> for five awards including <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Album_of_the_Year" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Album of the Year">Album of The Year</a>, <a href="/wiki/Best_Rap_Album" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Rap Album">Best Rap Album</a>, <a href="/wiki/Best_New_Artist" class="mw-redirect" title="Best New Artist">Best New Artist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Best_Rap/Sung_Collaboration" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Rap/Sung Collaboration">Best Rap/Sung Collaboration</a> and <a href="/wiki/Best_Rap_Performance" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Rap Performance">Best Rap Performance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Linking back with Curtiss King for one of the “most highly anticipated independent releases from the west coast.”, <i>Atychiphobia</i>, Hutch produced "The Bottom" featuring Brooke Taylor.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Venturing into co-producing, Skhye Hutch and Tae Beast formulated the music production company WeirrdPeople in 2013. The production duo worked with J-GO on the <i>Mr.Goins II</i> album producing tracks Underdog, Kush &amp; 2k, Down and Catch Me.
</p><p>In 2014, he rejoined Ab-Soul on the <i><a href="/wiki/These_Days..." class="mw-redirect" title="These Days...">These Days...</a></i> album producing "Feeling Us" featuring Jay Rock. The year continued with a feature on iTunes as the best hip hop album of the year, <i>Under Pressure</i> by <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> &amp; Visionary Music Group rapper <a href="/wiki/Logic_(rapper)" title="Logic (rapper)">Logic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The song "Growing Pains III" was co-produced by Tae Beast, 6ix, and Skhye Hutch, and was released in October 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Reaching out to the <a href="/wiki/Bronx" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronx">Bronx</a> emerging artist <a href="/wiki/Euro_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Euro League">Euro League</a>, Hutch delivered “an atmospheric beat” on the single "Gold Chains In a Dungeon" featuring Doley Barnays off the <i>Euro Trip: Continuum</i> EP of the New York collective ReeLife.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In addition, Skhye produced "Eat Sleep and Die"<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> on <a href="/wiki/MC_Jin" title="MC Jin">MC Jin</a>'s album, <i>XIV:LIX</i>.
</p><p>In 2015, he branched out to work with the DMV native <a href="/wiki/Jay_IDK" class="mw-redirect" title="Jay IDK">Jay IDK</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> where the two collaborated on the tracks "Dirty Scale" and "God Said Trap" with additional production from Gamebrand, which serves as the second official leak from the highly anticipated <i>SubTrap LP</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He then produced for Jay Rock's first album in over 4 years, <a href="/wiki/90059_(album)" class="mw-redirect" title="90059 (album)">90059</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Loop_(music)" title="Loop (music)">loopy</a> beat<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> on "Easy Bake" featuring Kendrick Lamar &amp; <a href="/wiki/SZA_(singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="SZA (singer)">SZA</a> premiered on <a href="/wiki/Sirius_XM" title="Sirius XM">Sirius XM</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Shade_45" title="Shade 45">Shade 45</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Continuing to reach out to emerging artists, Skhye Hutch joined the <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood</a> rapper Shawn Chrys<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> on "NO FCKS" released on Timbaland's Imprint label.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He also produced ‘<i>Flicker</i>’ by Ash Riser.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
