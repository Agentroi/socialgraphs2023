<p><b>Oscar Jackson Jr.</b> (born October 29, 1967), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Paris</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, known for his highly charged <a href="/wiki/Political" class="mw-redirect" title="Political">political</a> and <a href="/wiki/Socially" class="mw-redirect" title="Socially">socially</a> conscious lyrics.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusicbio_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicbio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Black_Panthers" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Panthers">Black Panthers</a>, he was once a member of the <a href="/wiki/Nation_of_Islam" title="Nation of Islam">Nation of Islam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SFNews_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFNews-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paris_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paris_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Paris became known in the US in 1990 with the hit single "The Devil Made Me Do It" and <a href="/wiki/The_Devil_Made_Me_Do_It" title="The Devil Made Me Do It">album of the same name</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> after earning a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor&#39;s degree">bachelor's degree</a> in <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economics</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Davis" title="University of California, Davis">University of California, Davis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SFNews_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFNews-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Originally released on <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Boy_Records" title="Tommy Boy Records">Tommy Boy Records</a>, his first video was banned by <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Sleeping_with_the_Enemy_(album)" title="Sleeping with the Enemy (album)">Sleeping with the Enemy</a></i>, was ready for release in 1992, Paris was dropped from now-defunct <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Boy_Records" title="Tommy Boy Records">Tommy Boy Records</a> (since rechristened Tommy Boy Entertainment in 2002) and distributor <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>, owned by <a href="/wiki/Time_Warner" class="mw-redirect" title="Time Warner">Time Warner</a>, when the parent company discovered its incendiary content, which included fantasy revenge killings of then-<a href="/wiki/George_H._W._Bush" title="George H. W. Bush">President Bush</a> and racist police officers. Also problematic was the album's insert, which featured the artist waiting behind a tree, holding a <a href="/wiki/TEC-9" title="TEC-9">TEC-9</a>, as the president was waving to the crowd. Paris eventually released the LP himself on his newly formed <a href="/wiki/Scarface_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Scarface Records">Scarface Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Also in 1992, Paris contributed to <a href="/wiki/Industrial_music" title="Industrial music">industrial music</a> band <a href="/wiki/Consolidated_(band)" title="Consolidated (band)">Consolidated</a>'s 1992 album <i><a href="/wiki/Play_More_Music" title="Play More Music">Play More Music</a></i> with the track "Guerrillas in the Mist."
</p><p>Paris signed a major artist and distribution deal with <a href="/wiki/Priority_Records" title="Priority Records">Priority Records</a> for himself and Scarface Records in 1993 and released his third LP, <i><a href="/wiki/Guerrilla_Funk" title="Guerrilla Funk">Guerrilla Funk</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and several then up-and-coming groups, most notably <a href="/wiki/The_Conscious_Daughters" title="The Conscious Daughters">The Conscious Daughters</a>. Paris and Priority formally severed their business relationship due to creative differences in 1995, and in 1997, Paris signed a one-off deal with now-defunct Whirling Records (distributed by <a href="/wiki/Rykodisc" title="Rykodisc">Rykodisc</a>) for the release of his fourth LP, <i><a href="/wiki/Unleashed_(Paris_album)" title="Unleashed (Paris album)">Unleashed</a></i>, which was released in small numbers with little promotion. The album contained some very explicitly violent and racially charged verses, and while less overtly political as earlier efforts, the lyrics explicitly describe racial violence when confronted with the same. Also released in Europe with a different track list, the album featuring songs from 1994, 1996 and 1998, some of which were more in the style of his earlier releases. Becoming increasingly dissatisfied with the <a href="/wiki/Music_industry" title="Music industry">music industry</a>, Paris retired from recording and worked as a <a href="/wiki/Stockbroker" title="Stockbroker">stockbroker</a>, cementing his personal wealth allowing him to independently finance the next stage of his musical endeavors.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paris_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2003, Paris returned with the album, <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Jihad_(Paris_album)" title="Sonic Jihad (Paris album)">Sonic Jihad</a></i>. The album continued his tradition of <a href="/wiki/History_of_controversial_album_art" class="mw-redirect" title="History of controversial album art">controversial cover art</a>, evoking images of the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a>, it depicts a plane flying toward the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a>. Its content is equally radical and covers many topics, including the <a href="/wiki/War_on_Terrorism" class="mw-redirect" title="War on Terrorism">war on terror</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Iraq_War" title="Iraq War">war in Iraq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Police_brutality" title="Police brutality">police brutality</a>, <a href="/wiki/Race_and_crime_in_the_United_States#Terminology" title="Race and crime in the United States">black-on-black violence</a>, conditions in inner-city communities, the <a href="/wiki/Illuminati" title="Illuminati">Illuminati</a>, and <a href="/wiki/State-sponsored_terrorism" title="State-sponsored terrorism">state-sponsored terrorism</a>. Other <a href="/wiki/Political_hip_hop" title="Political hip hop">politically minded hip hop</a> artists were featured on the album, including <a href="/wiki/Kam_(rapper)" title="Kam (rapper)">Kam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dead_prez" class="mw-redirect" title="Dead prez">dead prez</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Enemy (band)">Public Enemy</a>. <i>Sonic Jihad</i> was the catalyst for the creation of Paris' new label and web site, Guerrilla Funk Recordings, a home for projects and material of such notable acts as dead prez and Public Enemy.
</p><p>Paris' anti-war anthem "What Would You Do?" (from <i>Sonic Jihad</i>) accuses<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/9/11_conspiracy_theories" title="9/11 conspiracy theories">the Bush Administration [of playing] a key role in orchestrating the terror attacks of September 11</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> saying, <i>"Ain't no terror threat, unless approval rating's slumpin'. So I'ma say it for the record we the ones that planned it, ain't no other country took a part or had they hand in it".</i>  The song is featured on the 2004 compilation album <i>Peace Not War, Vol. 2</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On the 2003 <a href="/wiki/Anybody_Killa" title="Anybody Killa">Anybody Killa</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/Hatchet_Warrior" title="Hatchet Warrior">Hatchet Warrior</a></i>, Paris appeared with <a href="/wiki/Monoxide_Child" title="Monoxide Child">Monoxide</a> on a track entitled "Ghetto Neighbor."
</p><p>In 2005, Paris completed a project with <a href="/wiki/Public_Enemy" title="Public Enemy">Public Enemy</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rebirth_of_a_Nation" title="Rebirth of a Nation">Rebirth of a Nation</a></i>, the title both a reference to the 1915 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Birth_of_a_Nation" title="The Birth of a Nation">The Birth of a Nation</a></i>, controversial for its glorification of the <a href="/wiki/Ku_Klux_Klan" title="Ku Klux Klan">Ku Klux Klan</a> and its racist portrayal of African Americans, and PE's 1988 album <i><a href="/wiki/It_Takes_a_Nation_of_Millions_to_Hold_Us_Back" title="It Takes a Nation of Millions to Hold Us Back">It Takes a Nation of Millions to Hold Us Back</a></i>.  Although <a href="/wiki/Chuck_D" title="Chuck D">Chuck D</a> was the primary vocal performer, Paris penned and produced the bulk of the album, which also featured <a href="/wiki/N.W.A" title="N.W.A">N.W.A</a>'s <a href="/wiki/MC_Ren" title="MC Ren">MC Ren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Immortal_Technique" title="Immortal Technique">Immortal Technique</a>, Kam, <a href="/wiki/Dead_Prez" title="Dead Prez">dead prez</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Conscious_Daughters" title="The Conscious Daughters">The Conscious Daughters</a>.
</p><p>In 2006, Paris also released <i>Paris presents Hard Truth Soldiers, Vol. 1</i>, the first in a Paris-produced compilation series that touched on subjects ranging from war and <a href="/wiki/Police_brutality" title="Police brutality">police brutality</a> to black-on-black crime and <a href="/wiki/Domestic_violence" title="Domestic violence">domestic violence</a>. It showcased contributions from Public Enemy, <a href="/wiki/The_Coup" title="The Coup">the Coup</a>, dead prez, Paris, T-K.A.S.H., Kam, The Conscious Daughters, <a href="/wiki/Mystic_(singer)" title="Mystic (singer)">Mystic</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Ren" title="MC Ren">MC Ren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sun_Rise_Above" title="Sun Rise Above">Sun Rise Above</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Stop_the_Violence_Movement" class="mw-redirect" title="Stop the Violence Movement">Stop the Violence Movement</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tray_Deee" class="mw-redirect" title="Tray Deee">Tray Deee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soopafly" title="Soopafly">Soopafly</a>, <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>, Defari, Daz, <a href="/wiki/J-Ro" class="mw-redirect" title="J-Ro">J-Ro</a>, <a href="/wiki/RBX" title="RBX">RBX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bad_Azz_(rapper)" title="Bad Azz (rapper)">Bad Azz</a>, <a href="/wiki/WC_(rapper)" title="WC (rapper)">WC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dilated_Peoples" title="Dilated Peoples">Dilated Peoples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Minister" title="Mac Minister">Mac Minister</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mack_10" title="Mack 10">Mack 10</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evidence_(musician)" title="Evidence (musician)">Evidence</a>, Defari, <a href="/wiki/Everlast_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Everlast (musician)">Everlast</a>, and <a href="/wiki/B-Real" title="B-Real">B-Real</a>), among others.
</p><p>In addition, Paris introduced the world to former <a href="/wiki/The_Coup" title="The Coup">Coup</a>-member T-K.A.S.H. later that year, releasing his debut offering, <i>Turf War Syndrome</i>, and handling production duties as well.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2007, Paris released the follow-up companion piece to <i>Rebirth of a Nation</i>, the Public Enemy collaboration <i>Remix of a Nation</i>, and began laying the framework for the film division of Guerrilla Funk Recordings.
</p><p>Paris inked a distribution deal with <a href="/wiki/Fontana_Distribution" title="Fontana Distribution">Fontana</a>/<a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal</a> for Guerrilla Funk (with movies through <a href="/wiki/Vivendi" title="Vivendi">Vivendi</a>) in late 2007, and released his next album, <i><a href="/wiki/Acid_Reflex" title="Acid Reflex">Acid Reflex</a></i>, featuring <a href="/wiki/Chuck_D" title="Chuck D">Chuck D</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Clinton_(funk_musician)" title="George Clinton (funk musician)">George Clinton</a>, in 2008.
</p><p>In 2009, Paris released <i>Paris Presents: Hard Truth Soldiers, Vol. 2</i>, the sophomore project from T-K.A.S.H., <i>Brains All Over The Streets</i>, and the latest offering from The Conscious Daughters, <i>The Nutcracker Suite</i>.
</p><p>Paris not only writes and produces songs, but also publishes information about ways to improve <a href="/wiki/Inner_city" title="Inner city">inner-city communities</a>. Numerous articles can be found on various subjects in the Thought Box section of Guerrilla Funk's website.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paris_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, Paris was featured in the musical documentary, <i><a href="/wiki/Sounds_Like_A_Revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="Sounds Like A Revolution">Sounds Like a Revolution</a></i>, and in 2011 was featured on the <a href="/wiki/Insane_Clown_Posse" title="Insane Clown Posse">Insane Clown Posse</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Featuring_Freshness" title="Featuring Freshness">Featuring Freshness</a></i> LP. He performed at that year's <a href="/wiki/Gathering_of_the_Juggalos" title="Gathering of the Juggalos">Gathering of the Juggalos</a>, and later at the Amsterdam Hiphopfestival in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>.
</p><p>Paris' production and original material has been featured in various Hollywood offerings, including <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Freeman" title="Morgan Freeman">Morgan Freeman</a>'s <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Prom_Night_in_Mississippi" title="Prom Night in Mississippi">Prom Night in Mississippi</a></i> in 2009,  <i><a href="/wiki/End_of_Watch" title="End of Watch">End of Watch</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Jake_Gyllenhaal" title="Jake Gyllenhaal">Jake Gyllenhaal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Pe%C3%B1a" title="Michael Peña">Michael Peña</a> in 2012, and 2014's <i><a href="/wiki/Kill_the_Messenger_(2014_film)" title="Kill the Messenger (2014 film)">Kill the Messenger</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Renner" title="Jeremy Renner">Jeremy Renner</a>.
</p><p>In November 2014, he released a video single, "Night of the Long Knives," which called for an increased <a href="/wiki/Black-brown_unity" class="mw-redirect" title="Black-brown unity">unity in the black and brown communities</a> and the adoption of the <a href="/wiki/Eye_for_an_eye" title="Eye for an eye">eye-for-an-eye</a> philosophy toward racist police aggression and brutality in the wake of the tragedy in <a href="/wiki/Ferguson,_Missouri#Michael_Brown_shooting" title="Ferguson, Missouri">Ferguson, Missouri</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lists_of_killings_by_law_enforcement_officers_in_the_United_States" title="Lists of killings by law enforcement officers in the United States">other high-profile shooting deaths</a>.
</p><p>In August 2015, Paris released his second video single, "Buck, Buck, Pass," a commentary on <a href="/wiki/Gun_violence" title="Gun violence">gun violence</a>, its often ignored racial implications and its after effects.
</p><p>His album <i>Pistol Politics</i> was released on September 11, 2015. It was named one of the Best Hip-Hop albums of 2015 by <a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)"><i>SPIN</i> magazine</a><sup id="cite_ref-SPIN_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPIN-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and received an "A−" rating by <i><a href="/wiki/Vice_(magazine)" title="Vice (magazine)">VICE</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Paris' third single and video, "Hard Truth Soldier (Redux)" was released in November 2015.
</p><p>Paris became an official contributor to <a href="/wiki/Vice_Media" title="Vice Media">Vice Media</a> in June 2016, beginning his ongoing relationship with that outlet to provide political and entertainment insights on current events from an urban perspective.
</p><p>In March 2016, EA Sports released <i><a href="/wiki/EA_Sports_UFC_2" title="EA Sports UFC 2">UFC2</a></i>, the second installment of its popular <a href="/wiki/UFC" class="mw-redirect" title="UFC">UFC</a> <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">mixed martial arts</a> fighting video game franchise, featuring music produced by Paris.
</p><p>On September 5, 2016, Paris performed at Hiero Day in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a> - hip-hop collective Hieroglyphics' annual block party celebrating Bay Area indie music, food and culture. In October 2016, Guerrilla Funk released videos for "Lethal Warning Shot" and "Power," the 4th and 5th singles from Paris' acclaimed <i>Pistol Politics</i> release.
</p><p>Paris rounded out 2016 with contributions to Level 33 Entertainment's mystery/suspense thriller, <i><a href="/wiki/Blood_in_the_Water_(2016_film)" title="Blood in the Water (2016 film)">Blood in the Water</a></i>.
</p><p>2018 found Paris' productions included in several movie and television offerings, including <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Shorty_(TV_series)" title="Get Shorty (TV series)">Get Shorty</a></i> on the Epix network, <i><a href="/wiki/Champaign_ILL" title="Champaign ILL">Champaign ILL</a></i> on YouTube Premium, and Netflix's <i><a href="/wiki/Flint_Town" title="Flint Town">Flint Town</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Hip-Hop_Evolution" title="Hip-Hop Evolution">Hip Hop Evolution</a></i>, the latter of which featured an in-depth interview by Paris.
</p><p>In 2019, Paris' music has been featured in USA network's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Purge_(TV_series)" title="The Purge (TV series)">The Purge</a></i>, HBO's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Deuce_(TV_series)" title="The Deuce (TV series)">The Deuce</a></i>, CBS' <i><a href="/wiki/The_Unicorn_(TV_series)" title="The Unicorn (TV series)">The Unicorn</a></i>, multiple episodes of CBS Sports Network's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jim_Rome_Show" title="The Jim Rome Show">The Jim Rome Show</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Between_Two_Ferns:_The_Movie" title="Between Two Ferns: The Movie">Between Two Ferns: The Movie</a></i> on Netflix, starring <a href="/wiki/Zach_Galifianakis" title="Zach Galifianakis">Zach Galifianakis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matthew_McConaughey" title="Matthew McConaughey">Matthew McConaughey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keanu_Reeves" title="Keanu Reeves">Keanu Reeves</a> and <a href="/wiki/Will_Ferrell" title="Will Ferrell">Will Ferrell</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2020s">2020s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paris_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2020s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Netflix's <i><a href="/wiki/All_Day_and_a_Night" title="All Day and a Night">All Day and a Night</a></i> started off 2020 with featured music by Paris, starring <a href="/wiki/Ashton_Sanders" title="Ashton Sanders">Ashton Sanders</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Wright" title="Jeffrey Wright">Jeffrey Wright</a>, Isaiah John and <a href="/wiki/Regina_Taylor" title="Regina Taylor">Regina Taylor</a>, among others.
</p><p>In August of that year, "Baby Man Hands" was released - a scathing indictment of <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>'s presidency and the first single and video from Paris' album, <i>Safe Space Invader.</i>
</p><p>On September 11, <i>Safe Space Invader's</i> second single and video, "Nobody Move" was released, a stylistic homage to Black Panther Party artist <a href="/wiki/Emory_Douglas" title="Emory Douglas">Emory Douglas</a> that calls out police brutality, gentrification and more. <i>Safe Space Invader</i> was released on September 25, 2020. On October 23, 2020, <i>Safe Space Invader's</i> third single and video, "Turned the Key," was released, addressing the hard topics of gentrification, income inequality &amp; housing affordability.  Its fourth single and video, "Walk Like a Panther," was released on December 2 of that same year.
</p><p>In 2021, Paris' music was featured in FX network's <i><a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_Uncovered" title="Hip Hop Uncovered">Hip Hop Uncovered</a>,</i> Netflix's Notorious B.I.G. biopic, <i><a href="/wiki/Biggie:_I_Got_a_Story_to_Tell" title="Biggie: I Got a Story to Tell">Biggie: I've Got a Story to Tell</a>,</i> NBC's <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Rock" title="Young Rock">Young Rock</a>,</i> and in <i><a href="/wiki/Blindspotting_(TV_series)" title="Blindspotting (TV series)">Blindspotting</a></i> on the STARZ network.
</p><p>In July 2022, Lionsgate's film <i>American Carnage</i> was released, featuring music and production by Paris. In August of that same year, Netflix's <i><a href="/wiki/Day_Shift_(film)" title="Day Shift (film)">Day Shift</a></i> was also released, starring <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Foxx" title="Jamie Foxx">Jaime Foxx</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> and featuring Paris' original works.
</p><p>Peacock's <i><a href="/wiki/Poker_Face_(TV_series)" title="Poker Face (TV series)">Poker Face</a></i> started off 2023 with featured music by Paris, starring <a href="/wiki/Natasha_Lyonne" title="Natasha Lyonne">Natasha Lyonne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Bratt" title="Benjamin Bratt">Benjamin Bratt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adrien_Brody" title="Adrien Brody">Adrien Brody</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Rel_Howery" title="Lil Rel Howery">Lil Rel Howery</a>, among others.
</p>
