<p><b>Shawn Antoine Ivy</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known as <b>Domino</b>, (born 1972 in <a href="/wiki/St._Louis" title="St. Louis">St. Louis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>), is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Being a <a href="/wiki/Crips" title="Crips">Crip</a> himself, he auditioned for the <a href="/wiki/Bloods_%26_Crips" title="Bloods &amp; Crips">Bloods &amp; Crips</a> project in the early 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  He is the first rapper, in order of appearance, in the title track <i><a href="/wiki/Bangin%27_on_Wax" title="Bangin&#39; on Wax">Bangin' on Wax</a></i> on the album of the same name.  His debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Domino_(Domino_album)" title="Domino (Domino album)">Domino</a></i>, spawned two major hits in the United States,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> including the Top 10 hit "Getto Jam", which reached No. 7 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_100" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot 100">Hot 100</a>. Several further albums were released, and Domino continued to score hits on the R&amp;B charts into the 2000s (decade).
</p><p>In 1996, Domino appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Organization" title="Red Hot Organization">Red Hot Organization</a>'s compilation CD, <i><a href="/wiki/America_is_Dying_Slowly" class="mw-redirect" title="America is Dying Slowly">America is Dying Slowly</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Biz_Markie" title="Biz Markie">Biz Markie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>, among many other prominent hip hop artists. The CD, meant to raise awareness of the AIDS epidemic among African American men, was heralded as "a masterpiece" by the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Source" title="The Source">Source</a></i> magazine. He also performed on many soundtracks, including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Show_(1995_film)" title="The Show (1995 film)">The Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mask_(1994_film)" title="The Mask (1994 film)">The Mask</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Clueless_(film)" class="mw-redirect" title="Clueless (film)">Clueless</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Blankman" title="Blankman">Blankman</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_From_the_Hood" class="mw-redirect" title="Tales From the Hood">Tales From the Hood</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Spooky_House" title="Spooky House">Spooky House</a></i>.
</p><p>While his self-titled debut album was critically and commercially well-received, many were quick to point out that his style was similar to that of fellow Long Beach resident and up-and-coming Dr. Dre protégé, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Doggy Dogg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Three years later, Domino released his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Physical_Funk" title="Physical Funk">Physical Funk</a></i>, which failed to become as successful as its predecessor.  The video for title track "Physical Funk" was a No. 1 video on MTV but the album was delayed<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (September 2011)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> before its release due to Def Jam (Russell Simmons record label) dropping Outburst Records (the record label Domino was released on) before the album was released.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
He is a born again Christian.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
