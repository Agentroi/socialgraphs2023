<p><b>Marqus Brown</b>, known as <b>DJ.Fresh</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland</a> and Los Angeles, California.<sup id="cite_ref-abouthome_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abouthome-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Acclaim for his work includes being named to <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)"><i>XXL</i> magazine</a>'s 30 Best Hip-Hop Producers of 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL2016_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL2016-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2006 DJ.Fresh began releasing his <i>The Tonite Show</i> series of albums, which have featured artists such as <a href="/wiki/Yukmouth" title="Yukmouth">Yukmouth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>. He has also released a number of mixtapes and has produced albums for other artists. His 2013 collaboration with <a href="/wiki/J._Stalin" title="J. Stalin">J. Stalin</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Miracle_%26_Nightmare_on_10th_Street" title="Miracle &amp; Nightmare on 10th Street">Miracle &amp; Nightmare on 10th Street</a></i>, peaked at #61 on the <a href="/wiki/R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> chart and at #28 on the <a href="/wiki/Heatseekers_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Heatseekers Albums">Heatseekers Albums</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusicawards_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicawards-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Fresh_(American_DJ)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marqus Brown was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="Baltimore, Maryland">Baltimore, Maryland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-abouthome_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abouthome-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> While an adolescent in Baltimore he became immersed in <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop culture">hip hop culture</a>, and he started mixing vinyl on <a href="/wiki/Turntables" class="mw-redirect" title="Turntables">turntables</a> at age nine.<sup id="cite_ref-qalou_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qalou-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Among his early musical influences were <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> artists popular in the 1970s and 1980s, including <a href="/wiki/Anita_Baker" title="Anita Baker">Anita Baker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Whitney_Houston" title="Whitney Houston">Whitney Houston</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Gap_Band" title="The Gap Band">the Gap Band</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-qalou_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qalou-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He moved with his mother and siblings to the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">San Francisco Bay Area</a> in California<sup id="cite_ref-shospital_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shospital-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> at nine years old.<sup id="cite_ref-abouthome_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abouthome-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The crew battled and performed around the Bay Area.<sup id="cite_ref-qalou_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qalou-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Fresh_(American_DJ)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Fresh_(American_DJ)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:The_World%27s_Freshest_(DJ_Fresh)_-_photo_2.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/The_World%27s_Freshest_%28DJ_Fresh%29_-_photo_2.jpg/250px-The_World%27s_Freshest_%28DJ_Fresh%29_-_photo_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/The_World%27s_Freshest_%28DJ_Fresh%29_-_photo_2.jpg/375px-The_World%27s_Freshest_%28DJ_Fresh%29_-_photo_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/The_World%27s_Freshest_%28DJ_Fresh%29_-_photo_2.jpg/500px-The_World%27s_Freshest_%28DJ_Fresh%29_-_photo_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="504" data-file-height="379" /></a><figcaption>DJ.Fresh mixing live</figcaption></figure>
<p>DJ.Fresh's first major break was DJing at a show in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> with <a href="/wiki/Common_(rapper)" title="Common (rapper)">Common</a> at the age of 18, and shortly afterwards he Deejayed on two tours for the rapper <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-qalou_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qalou-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He began producing his own music as well, creating beats with turntables and a <a href="/wiki/Digital_audio_workstation" title="Digital audio workstation">digital audio workstation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-abouthome_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abouthome-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 1999 he was named the 3rd best DJ in the US by ITF (International Turntablist Federation).<sup id="cite_ref-abouthome_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abouthome-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While he initially mixed with the moniker DJ Fresh, he adopted the name the Worlds Freshest (DJ.Fresh) to avoid confusion with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Fresh" title="DJ Fresh">DJ Fresh</a>, the English producer of the same name.<sup id="cite_ref-qalou_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qalou-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Tonite_Show_series"><i>The Tonite Show</i> series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Fresh_(American_DJ)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Tonite Show series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005 DJ.Fresh started "The Tonite Show" album series, where he produces albums with guest artists that have since included <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Gibbs" title="Freddie Gibbs">Freddie Gibbs</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Jacka" title="The Jacka">the Jacka</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Grouch_(rapper)" title="The Grouch (rapper)">the Grouch</a>, <a href="/wiki/J-Stalin" class="mw-redirect" title="J-Stalin">J-Stalin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yukmouth" title="Yukmouth">Yukmouth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mitchy_Slick" title="Mitchy Slick">Mitchy Slick</a>, <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-abouthome_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abouthome-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-qalou_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qalou-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His 2014 <i>Tonite Show</i> release with <a href="/wiki/Trae_Tha_Truth" class="mw-redirect" title="Trae Tha Truth">Trae Tha Truth</a> received a positive review from RapReviews.com, who stated the album "shows glimpses of a nice little partnership brewing".<sup id="cite_ref-rr_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rr-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_production">Recent production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Fresh_(American_DJ)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Recent production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006 DJ.Fresh began producing for a number of major artists, starting with the album <i>The Real World West Oakland</i> by <a href="/wiki/J._Stalin" title="J. Stalin">J. Stalin</a>. In the next two years he worked on albums by <a href="/wiki/Shady_Nate" title="Shady Nate">Shady Nate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bicasso" class="mw-redirect" title="Bicasso">Bicasso</a> as well, and that later extended to artists such as <a href="/wiki/Mitchy_Slick" title="Mitchy Slick">Mitchy Slick</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dubcnnetminan_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dubcnnetminan-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He won Best West Coast Producer in 2009&#8211;10.<sup id="cite_ref-abouthome_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abouthome-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> As of 2010 he ran the Whole Shabang production team, with producers such as Jamon Dru, Mr. Tower, Sneaky Mike, <a href="/wiki/Face_the_Music_(music_ensemble)" title="Face the Music (music ensemble)">Face the Music</a>, Mike Rimzo, Scandal Beats &amp; Young Gully.<sup id="cite_ref-shospital_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shospital-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:The_World%27s_Freshest_(DJ_Fresh)_-_photo_1.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/The_World%27s_Freshest_%28DJ_Fresh%29_-_photo_1.jpg/250px-The_World%27s_Freshest_%28DJ_Fresh%29_-_photo_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="169" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/The_World%27s_Freshest_%28DJ_Fresh%29_-_photo_1.jpg/375px-The_World%27s_Freshest_%28DJ_Fresh%29_-_photo_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/8f/The_World%27s_Freshest_%28DJ_Fresh%29_-_photo_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="437" data-file-height="295" /></a><figcaption>DJ.Fresh</figcaption></figure>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Miracle_%26_Nightmare_On_10th_Street" class="mw-redirect" title="Miracle &amp; Nightmare On 10th Street">Miracle &amp; Nightmare On 10th Street</a></i> is a double disc collaboration album between <a href="/wiki/J._Stalin" title="J. Stalin">J. Stalin</a> and DJ.Fresh, released on July 16, 2013. It peaked at #61 on the <a href="/wiki/R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> chart and at #28 on the Heatseekers Albums chart.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusicawards_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicawards-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He also released the album <i>Feet Match the Paint</i> with <a href="/wiki/Mitchy_Slick" title="Mitchy Slick">Mitchy Slick</a> in 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-siccnessinterview_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-siccnessinterview-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and in 2014 he released his <i>Boomin' System</i> Mixtape, which has guest artists such as <a href="/wiki/Scarface_(rapper)" title="Scarface (rapper)">Scarface</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>. The mixtape was received positively, and a review by <i>Complex</i> praised the variety and unpredictability of the production styles.<sup id="cite_ref-complexreview_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complexreview-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Style.2C_equipment"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Style,_equipment">Style, equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Fresh_(American_DJ)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Style, equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>DJ.Fresh incorporates a variety of styles in his production, including <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">EDM</a>, <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>, though he self-identifies as more of an R&amp;B than a hip hop producer.<sup id="cite_ref-qalou_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qalou-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He uses analog equipment including <a href="/wiki/Turntables" class="mw-redirect" title="Turntables">turntables</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gramophone_record" class="mw-redirect" title="Gramophone record">vinyl</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/MIDI_controller" title="MIDI controller">MIDI controller</a> <a href="/wiki/Keyboard_instrument" title="Keyboard instrument">keyboard</a>. He also uses <a href="/wiki/Ableton_Live" title="Ableton Live">Ableton Live</a> and occasionally brings in live instrumentation.<sup id="cite_ref-qalou_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qalou-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
