<p><b>Damian Alexander Hess</b> (born December 3, 1973), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>MC Frontalot</b>, is an American rapper and web designer.
Hess began releasing music as MC Frontalot in 1999. His first successes came through <a href="/wiki/Song_Fight!" title="Song Fight!">Song Fight!</a>, an online songwriting and recording competition, where he became known for consistently beating opponents. Throughout his history at Song Fight!, he has never lost a competition as MC Frontalot, although he has entered only seven entries in that name.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In one such song fight, entitled "Romantic Cheapskate", he likens Song Fight! to a neglected lover who favors him regardless of how he treats her.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The song went on to garner a total of 614 votes, while the next closest song ended the round with 28.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Frontalot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_days">Early days</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Frontalot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early days">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2000, Frontalot released the song "Nerdcore Hiphop". The song became an immediate hit in the <a href="/wiki/Geek" title="Geek">geek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nerd" title="Nerd">nerd</a> communities. The <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">rap</a> subgenre of <a href="/wiki/Nerdcore_hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Nerdcore hip hop">nerdcore</a>, which had already been in development by various performers, embraced the title and has since been expanding rapidly.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (April 2018)">by whom?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Many consider Hess the founder of nerdcore. However, he has pointed out on his information webpage that many artists came before whom he considers his peers.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hess released his first <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio album">studio album</a> on August 27, 2005. Entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Nerdcore_Rising" title="Nerdcore Rising">Nerdcore Rising</a></i>, the album contained six new songs, along with 10 remixed versions of past tracks. Some of the new tracks are produced by popular online musicians from Song Fight!, including <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> and hip hop artist Doctor Popular.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spotlight">Spotlight</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Frontalot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Spotlight">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:MCFrontalot_PAX04.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/MCFrontalot_PAX04.jpg/220px-MCFrontalot_PAX04.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="278" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/MCFrontalot_PAX04.jpg/330px-MCFrontalot_PAX04.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/MCFrontalot_PAX04.jpg/440px-MCFrontalot_PAX04.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1228" data-file-height="1551" /></a><figcaption>MC Frontalot performing live at <a href="/wiki/Penny_Arcade_Expo" class="mw-redirect" title="Penny Arcade Expo">PAX</a> in 2004</figcaption></figure>
<p>On March 18, 2002, popular <a href="/wiki/Webcomic" title="Webcomic">webcomic</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Penny_Arcade_(webcomic)" class="mw-redirect" title="Penny Arcade (webcomic)">Penny Arcade</a></i> (whose creators were long-time fans of Hess) declared Frontalot their rapper laureate, directing fans to his website.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He has acknowledged that single act made his popularity skyrocket.
</p><p>He appeared at every <a href="/wiki/Penny_Arcade_Expo" class="mw-redirect" title="Penny Arcade Expo">Penny Arcade Expo</a> from 2004 to 2013. "Penny Arcade Theme" and "Which MC Was That?" both appear in the dance video game <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Groove_(game)" class="mw-redirect" title="In the Groove (game)">In the Groove</a></i>. He  is featured on the Baddd Spellah track "Rhyme of the Nibelung", which won <a href="/wiki/CBC_Radio_2" class="mw-redirect" title="CBC Radio 2">CBC Radio 2</a>'s Remix the Ring contest.<sup id="cite_ref-CBCRemixTheRingWinner_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBCRemixTheRingWinner-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A new track 'Final Boss' featured over the end credits in the 2008 game <a href="/wiki/Penny_Arcade_Adventures:_On_the_Rain-Slick_Precipice_of_Darkness" title="Penny Arcade Adventures: On the Rain-Slick Precipice of Darkness">Penny Arcade Adventures: On the Rain-Slick Precipice of Darkness</a>- Episode 1.
</p><p>MC Frontalot appears occasionally in the <a href="/wiki/Webcomic" title="Webcomic">webcomic</a> Overcompensating by <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Rowland" title="Jeffrey Rowland">Jeffrey Rowland</a>.
</p><p>MC Frontalot has also been seen on commercials for G4 TV, as well as being on the show Freestyle 101 where he sang parts of his songs with freestyle lines connecting them.
</p><p>Additionally, comic book illustrator Tony Moore became a fan of both Frontalot and fellow nerdcore artist <a href="/wiki/MC_Hawking" title="MC Hawking">MC Hawking</a>, drawing both of them as zombies in an early issue of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(comics)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Walking Dead (comics)">The Walking Dead</a></i> series. Hess appears in a white shirt, tie and glasses, with the word "Front" written on his pocket protector; he is seen eating a deer.
</p><p>A song written and performed by Frontalot based on the webcomic <a href="/wiki/Achewood" title="Achewood">Achewood</a> titled "Living At the Corner of Dude and Catastrophe" has been that website's <a href="/wiki/Theme_music" title="Theme music">theme song</a> since 2006.  On September 2, 2008, as part of a <a href="/wiki/Penny_Arcade_(webcomic)" class="mw-redirect" title="Penny Arcade (webcomic)">Penny Arcade</a> download pack, "Living At the Corner of Dude and Catastrophe" was chosen for inclusion as a <a href="/wiki/Downloadable_content" title="Downloadable content">downloadable track</a> for the video game <a href="/wiki/Rock_Band" title="Rock Band">Rock Band</a>. The week of September 14, 2009 saw the release of "Origin of Species" (a satire of <a href="/wiki/Creation_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Creation Science">Creation Science</a>) which was included in the <a href="/wiki/Penny_Arcade_Expo" class="mw-redirect" title="Penny Arcade Expo">Penny Arcade Expo</a> track pack.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Frontalot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Nerdapalooza_2008_-_MC_Frontalot_(2655412276).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Nerdapalooza_2008_-_MC_Frontalot_%282655412276%29.jpg/220px-Nerdapalooza_2008_-_MC_Frontalot_%282655412276%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Nerdapalooza_2008_-_MC_Frontalot_%282655412276%29.jpg/330px-Nerdapalooza_2008_-_MC_Frontalot_%282655412276%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Nerdapalooza_2008_-_MC_Frontalot_%282655412276%29.jpg/440px-Nerdapalooza_2008_-_MC_Frontalot_%282655412276%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2304" /></a><figcaption>MC Frontalot performing at Nerdapalooza in July 2008</figcaption></figure>
<p>Although most of his fanbase is online, Hess gave a handful of live performances while living in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, and several more after moving to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. His first official tour started on May 12, 2006, with shows mostly in the Southeast United States.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> When he performs, he plays with a full ensemble, including keyboardist and frequent collaborator Gminor7, bassist <a href="/wiki/Brandon_Patton" title="Brandon Patton">Blak Lotus</a>, and drummer The SturGENiUS. Other occasional band members include G.LATINusKY00B, The Categorical Imperative, Vic 20, and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Lombardo" title="Mike Lombardo">56K</a>. He finished a tour with Schäffer The Darklord as of 25 November 2007 and started another tour on 1 November 2008 with MC Lars and YTCracker. As of June 2010, he's been touring with alternative rock musicians, <a href="/wiki/Wheatus" title="Wheatus">Wheatus</a>, on their <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a> leg of their 10th anniversary tour; on occasion providing guest vocals on some of their live jams, as well as Wheatus offering musical accompaniment on some of his tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television">Film and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Frontalot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Film and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hess starred as "TP Factory Rapper" in the <a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a> direct-to-video movie <i>Elmo's Potty Time</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Nerdcore_Rising_(film)" title="Nerdcore Rising (film)">Nerdcore Rising</a></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary</a>/<a href="/wiki/Concert_film" title="Concert film">concert film</a> starring Hess and various other nerdcore artists such as <a href="/wiki/MC_Chris" title="MC Chris">MC Chris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Optimus_Rhyme" title="Optimus Rhyme">Optimus Rhyme</a> and <a href="/wiki/MC_Lars" title="MC Lars">MC Lars</a> with contributors such as <a href="/wiki/%22Weird_Al%22_Yankovic" title="&quot;Weird Al&quot; Yankovic">"Weird Al" Yankovic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Huston" class="mw-redirect" title="Paul Huston">Prince Paul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Posehn" title="Brian Posehn">Brian Posehn</a>.  The film, directed and produced by <a href="/wiki/Negin_Farsad" title="Negin Farsad">Negin Farsad</a>, premiered at the 2008 <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> festival in Austin, Texas. It combines interviews about nerdcore and its origins, with footage of Frontalot's 2006 Nerdcore Rising national tour.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hess was interviewed in Alexandre O. Philippe's documentary, <i><a href="/wiki/The_People_vs._George_Lucas" title="The People vs. George Lucas">The People vs. George Lucas</a></i>, which premiered at the 2010 South by Southwest Film Festival. Hess attended the festival as a musician and panelist.
</p><p>Hess made an appearance as a judge on the sixth episode of the first season of TBS's <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Nerds" title="King of the Nerds">King of the Nerds</a>, originally aired February 21, 2013.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_influences">Musical influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Frontalot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Musical influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Much of his earlier music features samples from other artists' works, many times using music acquired from famous artists such as <a href="/wiki/Paul_Simon" title="Paul Simon">Paul Simon</a>, <a href="/wiki/They_Might_Be_Giants" title="They Might Be Giants">They Might Be Giants</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Brown_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="James Brown (musician)">James Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiona_Apple" title="Fiona Apple">Fiona Apple</a>, and many others.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He addresses his borrowing of various drum beats in the song "Good Old Clyde", a song commenting on and using the popular "<a href="/wiki/Funky_Drummer" title="Funky Drummer">Funky Drummer</a>" <a href="/wiki/Break_(music)" title="Break (music)">drum break</a> by <a href="/wiki/Clyde_Stubblefield" title="Clyde Stubblefield">Clyde Stubblefield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since he began selling his albums commercially, Hess has collaborated on almost all his tracks with Baddd Spellah, an electronic musician and hip-hop beatsmith, and Gaby 'Gminor7' Alter, a composer and keyboardist whose playing was the basis for many of Frontalot's earlier songs. Hess has also collaborated with fellow rappers such as <a href="/wiki/MC_Hawking" title="MC Hawking">MC Hawking</a> and Canadian rapper <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Dangerously" title="Jesse Dangerously">Jesse Dangerously</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
