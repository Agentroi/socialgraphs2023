<p><b>Demerick Ferm</b>, better known by his stage name <b>Demrick</b>, formerly known as <b>Young De</b>, is a rapper from <a href="/wiki/Spokane,_Washington" title="Spokane, Washington">Spokane, Washington</a>, now living in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. He has collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Too_$hort" class="mw-redirect" title="Too $hort">Too $hort</a>, <a href="/wiki/B-Real" title="B-Real">B-Real</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a>. He is a protege of B-Real of Cypress Hill and Xzibit with whom he formed <a href="/wiki/Serial_Killers_(musical_group)" title="Serial Killers (musical group)">Serial Killers</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2005-2010:_Audio_Hustlaz.2C_Vol._1.2C_and_Smoke_N_Mirrors"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2005-2010:_Audio_Hustlaz,_Vol._1,_and_Smoke_N_Mirrors">2005-2010: <i>Audio Hustlaz, Vol. 1</i>, and <i>Smoke N Mirrors</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2005-2010: Audio Hustlaz, Vol. 1, and Smoke N Mirrors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His career started in 2005 when he met Kurupt in Philadelphia, who urged him and his group Tangled Thoughts to move to California. They did, and released an album with Kurupt, <i>Philly 2 Cali</i>, in 2007.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2008, Demrick released his solo debut mixtape, <i>Audio Hustlaz, Vol. 1</i>, which was presented by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Skee" title="DJ Skee">DJ Skee</a>. B-Real contributed to the mixtape as a producer and featured on several tracks. Later that year, Demrick released the <i>Homeland Security</i> mixtape, a collaboration with Shady/Interscope's artist <a href="/wiki/Cashis" title="Cashis">Cashis</a>. <i>Homeland Security</i> was hosted by DJ Whoo Kid and featured tracks with <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>, Crooked I and Freeway, among others.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Demrick was featured on B-Real's first solo debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Smoke_N_Mirrors" class="mw-redirect" title="Smoke N Mirrors">Smoke N Mirrors</a></i> (2009), appearing on 5 of the 15 songs, including the single "Don't Ya Dare Laugh", and toured overseas with B-Real. In the middle of 2009, Demrick appeared on DJ Muggs' <i><a href="/wiki/Soul_Assassins:_Intermission" title="Soul Assassins: Intermission">Soul Assassins: Intermission</a></i> album, and the success of that song led him to spend two years touring with Xzibit.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2010 Cypress Hill released <i><a href="/wiki/Rise_Up_(Cypress_Hill_album)" title="Rise Up (Cypress Hill album)">Rise Up</a></i>, their eighth studio album. Demrick was featured on the single "It Ain't Nothin" and went on tour with Cypress, performing at festivals like Rock Am Ring and Rockin Rio. At the end of the year Demrick teamed up with DJ Fingaz for another mixtape, <i>De Is for Demrick</i>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2011-present:_Urban_Ammo.2C_.23HeadsUP.2C_All_The_Wrong_Things_.26_Serial_Killers"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011-present:_Urban_Ammo,_#HeadsUP,_All_The_Wrong_Things_&amp;_Serial_Killers">2011-present: <i>Urban Ammo</i>, <i>#HeadsUP</i>, <i>All The Wrong Things</i> &amp; <i>Serial Killers</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011-present: Urban Ammo, #HeadsUP, All The Wrong Things &amp; Serial Killers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, Xzibit and Demrick released two digital singles, "Man on the Moon" and "What It Is". Videos were shot by director Matt Alonzo and led to rotation on MTV.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In mid-2011, Demrick released an EP, <i>Neva Look Back</i>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Scoop_DeVille" title="Scoop DeVille">Scoop DeVille</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Demrick released his fourth mixtape, <i>#Heads Up</i>, in 2011. It included the singles "Money &amp; Weed" and "Burn Out" (produced by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jonsin" title="Jim Jonsin">Jim Jonsin</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2013 Demrick released an album with the hip hop group <a href="/wiki/Serial_Killers_(musical_group)" title="Serial Killers (musical group)">Serial Killers</a>, alongside Xzibit and B-Real.
</p>
