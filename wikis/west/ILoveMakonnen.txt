<p><b>Makonnen Kamali Sheran</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born April 12, 1989), better known by his stage name <b>iLoveMakonnen</b> (often stylized as <b>ILOVEMAKONNEN</b>), is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Makonnen is best known for his 2014 hit "<a href="/wiki/Tuesday_(iLoveMakonnen_song)" title="Tuesday (iLoveMakonnen song)">Tuesday</a>" which featured Canadian rapper <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ILoveMakonnen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Makonnen Kamali Sheran was born on April 12, 1989,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/South_Los_Angeles" title="South Los Angeles">South Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-xxl1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and was named after Makonnen, father of Ras Teferi who was later crowned as Ethiopian Emperor <a href="/wiki/Haile_Selassie" title="Haile Selassie">Haile Selassie I</a>. His father is a first generation <a href="/wiki/Immigration_to_the_United_States" title="Immigration to the United States">immigrant</a> from <a href="/wiki/Belizean-American" class="mw-redirect" title="Belizean-American">Belize</a> who worked as an electrician and his mother was a nail instructor who worked in the beauty business for over 30 years.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In an interview, he described himself as <a href="/wiki/Mixed_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Mixed race">mixed</a> with African American, Indian, Irish, Belgian, German and Chinese heritage.<sup id="cite_ref-vice2_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vice2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Growing up in Los Angeles,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> he moved to the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> area when he was 13 years old, due to his parents' divorce. There, he witnessed the death of a close friend in his senior year in high school.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ILoveMakonnen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.932013:_Career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–2013:_Career_beginnings">2008–2013: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ILoveMakonnen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008–2013: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1062260506">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:.5em 1.4em .8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:.5em 0 .8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{overflow:hidden;position:relative;margin:.5em auto .8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft span,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright span{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox>blockquote{margin:0;padding:0;border-left:0;font-family:inherit;font-size:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:110%;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote>:first-child{margin-top:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote:last-child>:last-child{margin-bottom:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .quote-title,.mw-parser-output .quotebox .quotebox-quote{display:block}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:640px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{width:100%!important;margin:0 0 .8em!important;float:none!important}}</style><div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:30%; ; padding:12px">

<p>"I was hanging out with drug dealers and thieves and the pretty girls that were popular. The kids thought I was kind of bad. Not reckless, but a badass. A lot of people really look up to me. I kind of represent them. It's like, they'll stand behind me, but they won't stand beside me."

<p>Makonnen started making songs on his computer and uploading them to <a href="/wiki/MySpace" class="mw-redirect" title="MySpace">MySpace</a>. On this website, he met rising musicians, such as <a href="/wiki/Adele" title="Adele">Adele</a>. Makonnen also created a blog, where he would post interviews with musicians such as <a href="/wiki/Lil_B" title="Lil B">Lil B</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Boy" title="Soulja Boy">Soulja Boy Tell'em</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bf1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After a period under house arrest following the accidental fatal shooting of a friend,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> he enrolled in a <a href="/wiki/Cosmetology" title="Cosmetology">cosmetology</a> school and became a part of a group called Phantom Posse.<sup id="cite_ref-bf1_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In January 2012, Makonnen met Atlanta producer <a href="/wiki/Mike_Will_Made_It" title="Mike Will Made It">Mike Will Made It</a> and formed a brief professional relationship with him. However, because of Mike Will's busy schedule, the two were not able to meet often, and soon were separated from each other.<sup id="cite_ref-vice2_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vice2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014:_Rise_to_prominence">2014: Rise to prominence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ILoveMakonnen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2014: Rise to prominence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2014, <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a> Mike Will Made It would reply to Makonnen and brought him to the DTP studios, where he played some of his songs towards a fellow producer <a href="/wiki/Metro_Boomin" title="Metro Boomin">Metro Boomin</a>. Metro instantly liked Makonnen and stayed in touch with him. Later, after recording six songs together, Metro brought these fellow Atlanta producers <a href="/wiki/Sonny_Digital" title="Sonny Digital">Sonny Digital</a> and <a href="/wiki/808_Mafia" title="808 Mafia">808 Mafia</a>, who were all unusually impressed with him. Two days later, Makonnen recorded the song, titled "Don't Sell Molly No More" with Sonny Digital, which would later launch his music career. Sonny Digital introduced him to the other Atlanta producers such as DJ Spinz, <a href="/wiki/Dun_Deal" title="Dun Deal">Dun Deal</a> and St. Louis producer Cammy Recklezz. He started gaining local fame within the community with songs such as "Living on the Southside", "Sneaky Lady" and "My New Friend".<sup id="cite_ref-xxl1-2_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl1-2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Makonnen was then featured on Mike Will's mixtape, titled Ransom.
</p><p>In July 2014, the singer released his self-titled EP <i><a href="/wiki/ILoveMakonnen_(EP)" title="ILoveMakonnen (EP)">iLoveMakonnen</a></i>, which included the singles "Tuesday" and "I Don't Sell Molly No More".<sup id="cite_ref-vice1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vice1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The latter reached pop singer <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>, who shared it on her Instagram. The post got over 210,000 likes and counting, that brought a lot of new fans to Makonnen.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl1-2_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl1-2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-in1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-in1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On August 15, 2014, <i><a href="/wiki/Vice_(magazine)" title="Vice (magazine)">Vice</a></i> included his song "Whip It" in their Staff Picks for the Week list, commenting: "Makonnen's voice is incredible! ... How could you not have a good time listening to this song? If there is anyone I trust to teach me how to whip it, it is definitely my friend Makonnen."<sup id="cite_ref-vice3_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vice3-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2014, after hearing "Tuesday", <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a> asked one of the song's producers, Sonny Digital, if he could remix the song. Makonnen agreed to send it to Drake, but didn't think much of it. However, two days later Drake released a remix.<sup id="cite_ref-mtv1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtv1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Makonnen later commented: "Yeah, I surprised. Shocking. Drake listens to Makonnen. It was great. I was excited. Surprised, shocked. Just overwhelmed."<sup id="cite_ref-xxl1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The remix, simply titled "Tuesday" went viral and led to increased media interest in iLoveMakonnen.<sup id="cite_ref-vice1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vice1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pf1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pf1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2014, Makonnen was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="XXL Magazine">XXL</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s The Come Up section, which focuses on new artists.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl1_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He later stated he was not looking for a major label deal and instead planned to go touring and record music independently: "I'm not too thirsty to sign a deal. Cause a label will really just take all your free music down and you have to be somebody's bitch." On September 1, 2014, it was announced that Makonnen had signed to Drake's label <a href="/wiki/OVO_Sound" title="OVO Sound">OVO Sound</a>, which is distributed by <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard1_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.932016:_OVO_Sound_and_I_LOVE_MAKONNEN_2"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–2016:_OVO_Sound_and_I_LOVE_MAKONNEN_2">2015–2016: OVO Sound and <i>I LOVE MAKONNEN 2</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ILoveMakonnen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015–2016: OVO Sound and I LOVE MAKONNEN 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fader" title="The Fader">The Fader</a></i> in December 2015, more than a year after signing to <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s <a href="/wiki/OVO_Sound" title="OVO Sound">OVO Sound</a>, Makonnen said the label delayed tracks from <i>I LOVE MAKONNEN 2</i> from coming out. The song "Second Chance," for example, was finished in January 2015 and intended for a summer release,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> but ended up being self-released by Makonnen in November 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Makonnen said, "Second Chance was supposed to be that summer banger this summer. That was supposed to be out, touching people and hitting them this summer. By the time next year they'll be ready for something else. It's all late. People are doubting me, doubting what the fuck I said when I made the fucking song. You wait a year later and it's whatever. Hopefully, it's worked right. The song is structured properly. Everything is all there for proper radio play to just spin it over and over to people. Who knows? I don't know what the fuck they want."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Makonnen also claimed "extra bullshit in the industry" stopped "Teach Me How To Whip It" and "No M'aam" from getting radio play. When asked if he still talked to Drake, Makonnen responded, "Here and there. By the time this comes out, I'll probably see him six more times or no more times. I don't know. I just wish everybody well in all that they do and I just go on and live my life and try to uplift motherfuckers. At the end of the day I'm tired of uplifting people. I just gotta uplift myself now because everything else is a waste of time."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Makonnen also described the music scene: "There's not even any money here anymore. It's all lies. The money's gone and everybody is now keeping up this front as if there's money there."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 8, 2016, it was announced that alongside <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Travis Scott (musician)">Travis Scott</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vic_Mensa" title="Vic Mensa">Vic Mensa</a>, Makonnen would be part of the "WANGSQUAD", an <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wang_(designer)" title="Alexander Wang (designer)">Alexander Wang</a> campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.93present:_Departure_from_OVO_Sound"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–present:_Departure_from_OVO_Sound">2016–present: Departure from OVO Sound</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ILoveMakonnen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2016–present: Departure from OVO Sound">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 18, 2016, Makonnen parted ways with Drake's label <a href="/wiki/OVO_Sound" title="OVO Sound">OVO Sound</a>, saying the "choice to be solely on <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a> was the right thing for me and for my best interest."<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On May 7, 2016, Makonnen announced he would be retiring from music to focus on acting but released a new song shortly after, with confirmation of a new project.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the late summer of 2017, Makonnen collaborated on an album with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Peep" title="Lil Peep">Lil Peep</a> in London. The album was scrapped after Makonnen leaked it online in May 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On August 17, 2018, Makonnen announced a single he co-wrote titled "<a href="/wiki/Falling_Down_(Lil_Peep_and_XXXTentacion_song)" title="Falling Down (Lil Peep and XXXTentacion song)">Falling Down</a>", a reworking of "<a href="/wiki/Falling_Down_(Lil_Peep_and_XXXTentacion_song)#&quot;Sunlight_on_Your_Skin&quot;" title="Falling Down (Lil Peep and XXXTentacion song)">Sunlight On Your Skin</a>" that he recorded with Peep during the sessions in London. The new version features since deceased rapper <a href="/wiki/XXXTentacion" title="XXXTentacion">XXXTentacion</a>, who recorded his verses following Peep's death.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The single was officially released on September 19, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-fallingdown_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fallingdown-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The original "Sunlight On Your Skin" version was released on September 27, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ILoveMakonnen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 20, 2017 via Twitter, Makonnen <a href="/wiki/Came_out" class="mw-redirect" title="Came out">came out</a> as gay.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GSN1_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GSN1-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
