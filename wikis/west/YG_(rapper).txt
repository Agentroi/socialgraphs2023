<p><b>Keenon Dequan Ray Jackson</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born March 9, 1990),<sup id="cite_ref-allmusicbio_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicbio-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>YG</b> (short for <b>Young Gangsta</b>), is an American rapper and actor from <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton, California</a>. In 2010, he released his debut single, "<a href="/wiki/Toot_It_and_Boot_It" title="Toot It and Boot It">Toot It and Boot It</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>), which peaked at number 67 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The single's success resulted in his signing to <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the following years, YG released mixtapes such as <i>The Real 4Fingaz, Just Re'd Up,</i> <i>Just Re'd Up 2</i>, <i>4 Hunnid Degreez,</i> among others.
</p><p>In June 2013, YG signed an additional deal with <a href="/wiki/Jeezy" title="Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a>'s imprint <a href="/wiki/CTE_World" title="CTE World">CTE World</a>. His 2013 single, "<a href="/wiki/My_Nigga" title="My Nigga">My Nigga</a>" (featuring Jeezy and <a href="/wiki/Rich_Homie_Quan" title="Rich Homie Quan">Rich Homie Quan</a>), peaked at number 19 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. He then released the singles "<a href="/wiki/Left,_Right" title="Left, Right">Left, Right</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Mustard_(record_producer)" title="Mustard (record producer)">DJ Mustard</a>) and "<a href="/wiki/Who_Do_You_Love%3F_(YG_song)" title="Who Do You Love? (YG song)">Who Do You Love?</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>), leading up to the release of his debut studio album in 2014. The album, <i><a href="/wiki/My_Krazy_Life" title="My Krazy Life">My Krazy Life</a>,</i> was released in March of that year by Pu$haz Ink, CTE World and Def Jam, and receiving commercial success and critical acclaim. Later in 2014, he was featured on his highest-charting single to date, "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Tell_%27Em" title="Don&#39;t Tell &#39;Em">Don't Tell 'Em</a>", with American singer <a href="/wiki/Jeremih" title="Jeremih">Jeremih</a>, peaking at number 6 on the Hot 100. In 2016, he released his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Still_Brazy" title="Still Brazy">Still Brazy</a>,</i> to further acclaim.
</p><p>In 2018, he released his third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Stay_Dangerous" title="Stay Dangerous">Stay Dangerous</a></i>, to generally positive reviews. It included the single, "<a href="/wiki/Big_Bank" title="Big Bank">Big Bank</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>)—his highest charting solo single peaking at number 16 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. His fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/4Real_4Real" title="4Real 4Real">4Real 4Real</a></i>, was released in 2019 in memory of deceased rapper and close colleague <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a>. His fifth and sixth studio albums, <i><a href="/wiki/My_Life_4Hunnid" title="My Life 4Hunnid">My Life 4Hunnid</a></i> (2020) and <i><a href="/wiki/I_Got_Issues" title="I Got Issues">I Got Issues</a></i> (2022) followed thereafter.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YG_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Keenon Dequan Ray Jackson was born on March 9, 1990, in <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusicbio_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicbio-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Roberts_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roberts-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He grew up on the 400 block of West Spruce Street in Compton, CA, which is the origin of his "4hunnid" tag (which along with being called out in his album titles and song lyrics is also the name of his record label and streetwear brands).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> His stage name "YG" stands for "Young Gangsta."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> YG joined the <a href="/wiki/Bloods" title="Bloods">Bloods</a> gang in 2006 at age 16.<sup id="cite_ref-Vibe_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> His father served time in jail for tax fraud.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YG_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.932012:_Beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–2012:_Beginnings">2008–2012: Beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YG_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008–2012: Beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>YG created the "Pu$haz Ink" record label and group with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Mustard" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Mustard">DJ Mustard</a> in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> After releasing several songs that garnered him a large following on the internet, such as "She A Model" and "Aim Me" YG signed to Def Jam in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> According to Max Gousse, the senior VP of A&amp;R at Island/Def Jam who inked the rapper, the signing stemmed from a newfound emphasis on West Coast artists by label president/CEO L.A. Reid, as well as, Y.G.’s skills and stage presence.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> However, just as he was getting his buzz up in mid-2009, he was arrested on a parole violation, stemming from a previous charge of residential burglary.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag1_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Following that, he worked on mixtapes, touring and running his 4Hunnid brand clothing line.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Toot_It_and_Boot_It" title="Toot It and Boot It">Toot It and Boot It</a>" featuring LA-based singer <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a> was released in 2010, and became both of their breakout hit.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag1_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He was also featured in <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL's 2011 Freshmen Class</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Beginning with YG's mixtape <i>The Real 4 Fingaz</i>, DJ Mustard begun significantly producing on YG's projects. Their work together resulted in songs such as "I'm Good", "Bitches Ain't Shit" featuring Tyga and Nipsey Hussle and "You Broke" again featuring Nipsey Hussle. The singles were met with moderate success, but not near as much as his debut single.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932014:_Signing_with_CTE_World_and_My_Krazy_Life"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2014:_Signing_with_CTE_World_and_My_Krazy_Life">2012–2014: Signing with CTE World and <i>My Krazy Life</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YG_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012–2014: Signing with CTE World and My Krazy Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/My_Krazy_Life" title="My Krazy Life">My Krazy Life</a></div>
<p>In 2012, he announced his debut album, then titled “I'm 4rm Bompton”. Later in June 2013, he revealed that Jeezy's record label <a href="/wiki/CTE_World" title="CTE World">CTE World</a> would release the album.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He was then featured on <a href="/wiki/Yo_Gotti" title="Yo Gotti">Yo Gotti</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Act_Right_(Yo_Gotti_song)" title="Act Right (Yo Gotti song)">Act Right</a>" also featuring Jeezy. It would peak at number 100 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He was then prominently featured on the CTE World mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Boss_Yo_Life_Up_Gang" title="Boss Yo Life Up Gang">Boss Yo Life Up Gang</a></i> in August 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 4, 2013, YG revealed that his debut album would be released on November 19, 2013, via <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> and that he has changed the album title to <i><a href="/wiki/My_Krazy_Life" title="My Krazy Life">My Krazy Life</a></i>. He also revealed that <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a> would be featured on a song titled "Who Do You Love?", produced by DJ Mustard. Shortly thereafter he released the album's <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> "<a href="/wiki/My_Nigga" title="My Nigga">My Nigga</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Rich_Homie_Quan" title="Rich Homie Quan">Rich Homie Quan</a> and Jeezy, also produced by DJ Mustard.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The song has since peaked at number 19 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On December 10, 2013, YG released the DJ Mustard-produced "<a href="/wiki/Left,_Right" title="Left, Right">Left, Right</a>" as the album's second single.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The following day, Def Jam announced that <i>My Krazy Life</i> would be released on March 18, 2014<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and that "<a href="/wiki/Who_Do_You_Love%3F_(YG_song)" title="Who Do You Love? (YG song)">Who Do You Love?</a>" featuring Drake would be the album's next single.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On February 18, 2014, YG revealed the cover artwork for his debut album <i>My Krazy Life</i>. The artwork features YG posing for a mugshot, with his name and album title detailed in the placard around his neck.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> It was released on March 18, 2014.
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.932019:_Still_Brazy.2C_Stay_Dangerous_and_4Real_4Real"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–2019:_Still_Brazy,_Stay_Dangerous_and_4Real_4Real">2015–2019: <i>Still Brazy</i>, <i>Stay Dangerous</i> and <i>4Real 4Real</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YG_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015–2019: Still Brazy, Stay Dangerous and 4Real 4Real">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Rapper_YG_2015.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Rapper_YG_2015.jpg/220px-Rapper_YG_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="300" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Rapper_YG_2015.jpg/330px-Rapper_YG_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Rapper_YG_2015.jpg/440px-Rapper_YG_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="472" data-file-height="643" /></a><figcaption>YG performing in 2015</figcaption></figure>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Still_Brazy" title="Still Brazy">Still Brazy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stay_Dangerous" title="Stay Dangerous">Stay Dangerous</a>, and <a href="/wiki/4Real_4Real" title="4Real 4Real">4Real 4Real</a></div>
<p>On June 24, 2015, in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i>, YG revealed that his second studio album would be called <i>Still Krazy</i> and it would be released in 2015<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On July 15, 2015, YG teased the first single for the album, "Twist My Fingaz" on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The same day, YG released the single, "Cash Money" featuring <a href="/wiki/Krayzie_Bone" title="Krayzie Bone">Krayzie Bone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The full version of "Twist My Fingaz" was released July 17, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On December 12, 2015, he released the second single, titled "I Want a Benz" featuring rappers <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a> and <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Still_Brazy" title="Still Brazy">Still Brazy</a></i> was released on June 17, 2016. The third single "<a href="/wiki/Why_You_Always_Hatin%3F" title="Why You Always Hatin?">Why You Always Hatin?</a>" premiered on OVO Sound Radio on May 21, 2016, featuring rappers <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kamaiyah" title="Kamaiyah">Kamaiyah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On November 25, 2016, he digitally released the <a href="/wiki/Black_Friday_(shopping)" title="Black Friday (shopping)">Black Friday</a> inspired <i>Red Friday</i>, containing 8 new tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>YG announced through social media <i>Just Re'd Up 3: Know Your Worth</i> on December 17, 2016, and again on March 16, 2017. The project was to be executively produced DJ Mustard. It has yet to release, with no comment from YG or DJ Mustard.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 3, 2017, the song "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_(Mariah_Carey_song)" title="I Don&#39;t (Mariah Carey song)">I Don't</a>", by American singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> was released, featuring YG.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Carey and YG performed "I Don't" live on <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i> on February 15, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On March 24, 2017, a remix featuring <a href="/wiki/Remy_Ma" title="Remy Ma">Remy Ma</a> and YG was released.<sup id="cite_ref-Remix_US_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Remix_US-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Remix_UK_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Remix_UK-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 19, 2018, YG announced his third studio album would be <i><a href="/wiki/Stay_Dangerous" title="Stay Dangerous">Stay Dangerous</a></i> on his Instagram page and would be released this summer. It was released in 2018.
</p><p>On April 3, 2019, YG announced a "surprise album" titled <i><a href="/wiki/4Real_4Real" title="4Real 4Real">4Real 4Real</a></i> via Twitter originally due for release on April 12, being delayed to May 24 due to the death of his close friend <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a>. It was released to positive reviews. The album includes the single "<a href="/wiki/Go_Loko" title="Go Loko">Go Loko</a>" and the diss track aimed at <a href="/wiki/6ix9ine" title="6ix9ine">6ix9ine</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Stop_Snitchin_(song)" title="Stop Snitchin (song)">Stop Snitchin</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2020.E2.80.93present:_My_Life_4Hunnid_and_Kommunity_Service"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020–present:_My_Life_4Hunnid_and_Kommunity_Service">2020–present: <i>My Life 4Hunnid</i> and <i>Kommunity Service</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YG_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2020–present: My Life 4Hunnid and Kommunity Service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/My_Life_4Hunnid" title="My Life 4Hunnid">My Life 4Hunnid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kommunity_Service" title="Kommunity Service">Kommunity Service</a></div>
<p>As of 2020, YG has released a number of songs, including the protest track, "FTP (Fuck the Police)", with the video shot at a <a href="/wiki/Black_Lives_Matter" title="Black Lives Matter">Black Lives Matter</a> protest in Hollywood, following the <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_George_Floyd" title="Murder of George Floyd">murder of George Floyd</a>. He also joined <a href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Enemy (band)">Public Enemy</a>, alongside&#160;<a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>,&#160;<a href="/wiki/Questlove" title="Questlove">Questlove</a>, and&#160;<a href="/wiki/Rapsody" title="Rapsody">Rapsody</a>, among others, at the&#160;<a href="/wiki/2020_BET_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 BET Awards">2020 BET Awards</a> for a performance of Public Enemy's classic track "<a href="/wiki/Fight_the_Power_(Public_Enemy_song)" title="Fight the Power (Public Enemy song)">Fight the Power</a>". On July 10, YG released the single "War", with a video where he is dressed as footballer <a href="/wiki/Colin_Kaepernick" title="Colin Kaepernick">Colin Kaepernick</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 15, 2020, YG took to his social media to announce his new album <i><a href="/wiki/My_Life_4Hunnid" title="My Life 4Hunnid">My Life 4Hunnid</a></i>, which was released on October 2, 2020. The album was entirely recorded during the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19" title="COVID-19">COVID-19</a> pandemic and was "highly influenced" by 2Pac. The same week, he released the single "Money Mouf", with <a href="/wiki/Saweetie" title="Saweetie">Saweetie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a>. On September 23, 2020, YG released the single, "Out on Bail", inspired by <a href="/wiki/2Pac" class="mw-redirect" title="2Pac">2Pac</a>'s song of the same name.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 3, 2021, YG announced his first collaborative album, with <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/Mozzy" title="Mozzy">Mozzy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Blistein_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blistein-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> It was announced under the working title <i>Perfect Timing</i>, but was later changed to <i>Kommunity Service</i> and released on May 21, 2021. The cover art serves as a tribute to late rapper <a href="/wiki/DMX" title="DMX">DMX</a> and the film <i><a href="/wiki/Belly_(film)" title="Belly (film)">Belly</a></i>, in which he starred in.<sup id="cite_ref-Complex_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The album was supported by two singles: "Bompton to Oak Park" and "Perfect Timing" featuring <a href="/wiki/Blxst" title="Blxst">Blxst</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Blistein_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blistein-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>When YG originally came up with the idea for a label (originally titled Pushaz Ink, stylized as Pu$haz Ink), he later attempted to co-found it with DJ Mustard and Ty Dolla $ign.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx1_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx1-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Originally the "label" was used as a promotional tool and a brand for YG and DJ Mustard's group of rap collaborators they had grown up with. But as they moved forward in laying the groundwork for the label and its roster, plans for the label were scrapped when their meeting with Capitol went south and the three artists decided to go their separate ways.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard.com_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were rumors throughout 2016 that YG would be launching a new label under the name <b>4Hunnid</b>. They originally begun when YG released a fashion line prominently featuring the 4Hunnid logo. This was confirmed to be true on August 17, 2016, when Billboard magazine reported that YG signed a distribution deal for the label under the 4Hunnid name with <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Empire_Distribution" title="Empire Distribution">Empire Distribution</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard.com_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> The name is a reference to the 400 block of West Spruce Street in Compton, CA, where he grew up.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YG_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>YG has two daughters from a previous relationship. YG is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton</a>-based Westside Tree Top Piru gang.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-billboard3_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard3-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> On January 25, 2012, shots were fired during the filming of YG's music video "I'm a Thug". The police shut down and closed the set.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard3_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard3-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 12, 2015, YG was shot in the hip at a recording studio in <a href="/wiki/Studio_City,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio City, California">Studio City, California</a>, resulting in three separate wounds in his hip.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Authorities said that YG was "very uncooperative" when asked about the incident. His manager later revealed that his injuries were not life-threatening and said he was "fine" and recovering.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>  He returned to the studio the next day.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>YG followed a <a href="/wiki/Veganism" title="Veganism">vegan</a> diet for about three months in 2016, citing health reasons.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 6, 2019, it was confirmed that he and singer <a href="/wiki/Kehlani" title="Kehlani">Kehlani</a> were dating,<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> but the relationship ended after he was caught <a href="/wiki/Infidelity" title="Infidelity">cheating</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>YG is a notable fan of the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Rams" title="Los Angeles Rams">Los Angeles Rams</a> NFL team,<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> and was known to have befriended then-running back <a href="/wiki/Todd_Gurley" title="Todd Gurley">Todd Gurley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> He was frequently spotted wearing a Rams home jersey in several video shoots and at several Rams’ home games, including one instance during the 2017 season against the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Texans" title="Houston Texans">Houston Texans</a> in which Rams’ receiver <a href="/wiki/Robert_Woods_(wide_receiver,_born_1992)" title="Robert Woods (wide receiver, born 1992)">Robert Woods</a> caught a touchdown and jumped onto the table in the back of the endzone to celebrate alongside YG.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=YG_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>YG was the subject of media attention and protests in October 2016 when it was reported that he had advocated for the targeted robbery of <a href="/wiki/Asian_Americans" title="Asian Americans">Asian Americans</a> in his music.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> YG's song "Meet the Flockers", from his 2014 debut album "My Krazy Life", was widely accused of racism against the Asian American community, especially Chinese Americans. In the song, YG raps "First, you find a house and scope it out / Find a Chinese neighborhood, cause they don't believe in bank accounts." Receiving little controversy when first released, the song drew renewed scrutiny in 2016 after an uptick in burglaries targeting Asian-Americans. YG has described "Meet the Flockers" as being representative of the culture he grew up in.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>YG faced controversy on October 28, 2019, after kicking out 17-year-old fan, Austin Joyner, at his concert after the fan refused to curse out "Fuck <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>" on stage. Joyner was pushed by the rapper and called a <a href="/wiki/Racism" title="Racism">racist</a> before being escorted off stage.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Turning_Point_USA" title="Turning Point USA">Turning Point USA</a> interviewed the 17-year-old on February 23, 2020, revealing that he was celebrating his birthday with friends and that he was <a href="/wiki/Apoliticism" title="Apoliticism">apolitical</a>. After being kicked off stage he was advised by staff to leave due to "YG putting a target on [his] back". Afterwards, Joyner received attention online from <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> and news publications soon leading to hate comments.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>YG was arrested on January 24, 2020  at his <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a> home on <a href="/wiki/Robbery" title="Robbery">robbery</a> charges, and was held on $250,000 <a href="/wiki/Bail_in_the_United_States" title="Bail in the United States">bail</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Men%27s_Central_Jail" title="Men&#39;s Central Jail">Men's Central Jail</a> for <a href="/wiki/Arraignment" title="Arraignment">arraignment</a> on January 28, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> YG had been scheduled to perform on January 26, 2020, at the <a href="/wiki/62nd_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="62nd Annual Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
