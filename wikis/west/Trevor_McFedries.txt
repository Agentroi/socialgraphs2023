<p><b>Trevor McFedries</b><sup id="cite_ref-Food_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Food-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born November 27, 1985),  (formerly known as <b>Yung Skeeter</b>) is a musician who worked as a <a href="/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a>, and director for acts including <a href="/wiki/Ke$ha" class="mw-redirect" title="Ke$ha">Ke$ha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azealia_Banks" title="Azealia Banks">Azealia Banks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Aoki" title="Steve Aoki">Steve Aoki</a>, and others.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He toured alongside Katy Perry on her 2011 <a href="/wiki/California_Dreams_Tour" title="California Dreams Tour">California Dreams Tour</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-listenlookandlive.blogspot.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-listenlookandlive.blogspot.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> McFedries has also performed at music festivals <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella</a>, served as an entrepreneur-in-residence at <a href="/wiki/Bad_Robot" class="mw-redirect" title="Bad Robot">Bad Robot</a>, and was an early employee at <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-listenlookandlive.blogspot.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-listenlookandlive.blogspot.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He is the co-founder and CEO of technology startup Brud and the creator of virtual pop star <a href="/wiki/Lil_Miquela" class="mw-redirect" title="Lil Miquela">Lil Miquela</a>.
</p><p>He currently sits on the boards of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_California_Institute_of_Architecture" title="Southern California Institute of Architecture">Southern California Institute of Architecture</a> “SCI-Arc” and Rhizome, the Digital Artwork arm of <a href="/wiki/The_New_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="The New Museum">The New Museum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trevor_McFedries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McFedries grew up in <a href="/wiki/Davenport,_Iowa" title="Davenport, Iowa">Davenport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LLL_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LLL-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MySpace_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MySpace-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/High_school" class="mw-redirect" title="High school">high school</a>, he was a "football star"<sup id="cite_ref-Worst_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Worst-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and enjoyed playing basketball.<sup id="cite_ref-R29_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R29-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He moved with his family to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> when he was 16.<sup id="cite_ref-Nooz_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nooz-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He earned a college scholarship to play football at <a href="/wiki/San_Jose_State_Spartans_Football" class="mw-redirect" title="San Jose State Spartans Football">San Jose State</a> but dropped out of college and moved back to LA.<sup id="cite_ref-Beatport_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beatport-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Los Angeles has been his "home base" since that time. He started DJing at the age of 19.<sup id="cite_ref-Nooz_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nooz-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trevor_McFedries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McFedries took the stage name DJ Skeet Skeet, and in 2007 he was introduced to <a href="/wiki/Shwayze" title="Shwayze">Shwayze</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cisco_Adler" title="Cisco Adler">Cisco Adler</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Joon_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Joon-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The three created a partnership that would culminate in the 2008 release of <a href="/wiki/Shwayze_(album)" title="Shwayze (album)">a self-titled album</a>. The album made it to the top 10 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> charts with two songs ("<a href="/wiki/Buzzin%27_(Shwayze_song)" title="Buzzin&#39; (Shwayze song)">Buzzin'</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Corona_and_Lime_(song)" title="Corona and Lime (song)">Corona and Lime</a>") that charted on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a> list.<sup id="cite_ref-Bill_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bill-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The popularity of the trio led to the creation of a short-lived <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">reality television</a> series on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> called <i><a href="/wiki/Buzzin%27_(TV_series)" title="Buzzin&#39; (TV series)">Buzzin'</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Paper02_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paper02-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Also in 2008, DJ Skeet Skeet was given the "People's Choice Award for Best DJ" by <i>Paper Magazine</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Paper01_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paper01-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He also became the first DJ to perform at the entire slate of shows on the <a href="/wiki/Warped_Tour" title="Warped Tour">Warped Tour</a> in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-Worst_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Worst-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By 2011, he had worked with acts like Chris Brown, <a href="/wiki/Ke$ha" class="mw-redirect" title="Ke$ha">Ke$ha</a>, <a href="/wiki/3OH!3" title="3OH!3">3OH!3</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bonde_do_Rol%C3%AA" title="Bonde do Rolê">Bonde do Rolê</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sky_Ferreira" title="Sky Ferreira">Sky Ferreira</a>, creating remixes of their tracks and producing various songs.<sup id="cite_ref-Nooz_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nooz-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, he also opened for Katy Perry on her California Dreams arena tour,<sup id="cite_ref-LLL_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LLL-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> sharing a stage with both Perry and <a href="/wiki/Robyn" title="Robyn">Robyn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TV_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TV-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> McFedries and Perry had been friends long before the tour.<sup id="cite_ref-Nooz_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nooz-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> During this time, he worked and as a <a href="/wiki/A%26R" class="mw-redirect" title="A&amp;R">A&amp;R</a> at <a href="/wiki/Photo_Finish_Records" title="Photo Finish Records">Photo Finish Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Alt_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alt-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Vegas_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vegas-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, he decided to change his stage name to "Yung Skeeter," He produced and provided beats for various songs for other artists. McFedries made his directorial debut in 2012, directing the music videos for Steve Aoki's "Control Freak" and <a href="/wiki/NERVO_(duo)" class="mw-redirect" title="NERVO (duo)">NERVO's</a> "<a href="/wiki/We%27re_All_No_One" title="We&#39;re All No One">We're All No One</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-BangTidy_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BangTidy-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Starting in 2012, McFedries has been an Artist Advocate at Spotify alongside <a href="/wiki/D.A._Wallach" class="mw-redirect" title="D.A. Wallach">D.A. Wallach</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chester_French" title="Chester French">Chester French</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LLL_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LLL-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> His relationship with Steve Aoki (who runs Dim Mak Records) vaulted Skeeter into a position hosting a radio show on <a href="/wiki/IHeartRadio" title="IHeartRadio">iHeartRadio</a> for Dim Mak Records. In 2013, he became a spokesman for VitaminWater alongside acts like <a href="/wiki/B.o.B." class="mw-redirect" title="B.o.B.">B.o.B.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matt_and_Kim" title="Matt and Kim">Matt and Kim</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Santigold" title="Santigold">Santigold</a>. He performed with these three acts in <a href="/wiki/Boring,_Oregon" title="Boring, Oregon">Boring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a> as part of VitaminWater's "Make Boring Brilliant" campaign. He has also appeared in ads for the company.<sup id="cite_ref-Fuse_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fuse-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>BANKS' debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Goddess_(Banks_album)" title="Goddess (Banks album)">Goddess</a></i>, was executive produced by Yung Skeeter.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian02_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian02-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He also produced "Wallace" on Azealia Banks' 2014 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Broke_with_Expensive_Taste" title="Broke with Expensive Taste">Broke with Expensive Taste</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Muse_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Muse-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Brud">Brud</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trevor_McFedries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Brud">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to his musical career, McFedries worked for Bad Robot, the production company of J.J. Abrams.<sup id="cite_ref-Lil_Miquela&#39;s_Body_Con_Job_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lil_Miquela&#39;s_Body_Con_Job-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, McFedries co-founded the tech company Brud<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> which is as a “transmedia company” that produces digital personas utilized to create digital story-driven worlds. McFedries is the company’s CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-labusinessjournal.com_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-labusinessjournal.com-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Of their creations has been Miquela, a CGI character and model that uses social media in order to detail her life, which includes a mixtures of fictional and non-fictional environments.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> They initially created Miquela anonymously,<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> only later revealing themselves as her creator. They have also created other characters to interact with Miquela as well as other parts of social media,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>  including Bermuda—whom McFedries created under an in-story faux rival company before revealing Brud had also created her.
</p><p>In 2018 McFedries and Brud received $6 million in funding from Sequoia Capital,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> and that year their creation became one of Time magazine’s “Most Influential People on the Internet”.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In 2019, investments raised by his company had totaled $25.5 million, with a valuation of $144.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-labusinessjournal.com_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-labusinessjournal.com-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Through his fundraising, McFedries and his cofounders have stated that they would only accept funding from investment firms where either a woman or a person of color was in a position of authority.<sup id="cite_ref-Lil_Miquela&#39;s_Body_Con_Job_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lil_Miquela&#39;s_Body_Con_Job-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 4, 2021 “Brud Inc”  was acquired by Dapper Labs for an undisclosed amount.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The acquisition sees Trevor become the CEO of “Dapper Collectives” a new org that will be “Mainstreaming DAOs.” The first DAO that Dapper Collectives will bring to life is “Brud DAO” the decentralization of Brud Inc. that will allow for the community of fans and creators to own the project and guide it alongside the Brud team.  In the acquisition McFedries stated “Really, what we're going to be doing is building tools for DAOs, storytellers, and creators to come and build out the future in web3 with us on the Flow blockchain.” <sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Friends_with_Benefits">Friends with Benefits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trevor_McFedries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Friends with Benefits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Friends With Benefits is an <a href="/wiki/Ethereum" title="Ethereum">Ethereum</a> based DAO created by McFedries in September 2020 and is considered to be the first Social DAO.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Art_.26_A.I."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Art_&amp;_A.I.">Art &amp; A.I.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trevor_McFedries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Art &amp; A.I.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McFedries was featured in Rhizome’s 7x7<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> program as a leader at the intersection of art and technology, as well as NYC Media Lab<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> where he discussed the future of storytelling, art, and A.I. with artist Aria Dean. McFedries spoke with Berkeley Art + Design<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> about how narrative and attitude make an avatar relatable and authentic.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trevor_McFedries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McFedries was a campaign surrogate for Bernie Sanders 2020 presidential campaign and fundraised for George Gascón<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> in his run to become District Attorney of Los Angeles County.
</p>
