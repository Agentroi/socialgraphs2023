					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American rapper</div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; font-size: 125%;"><div class="">Braille</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Bryan Winchester</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Also known as</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Braille Brizzy, Reflection</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, Oregon</a>, U.S.</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">1994–present</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data">Syntax, HipHop IS Music, Talking Textures, Humble Beast</td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Bryan "Braille" Winchester</b> (born 1981) is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> recording artist. He has been writing and recording hip-hop music since he was 13 years old. For a short time, Bryan and his family relocated to the <a href="/wiki/Marlton,_New_Jersey" title="Marlton, New Jersey">Marlton</a> section of <a href="/wiki/Evesham_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Evesham Township, New Jersey">Evesham Township, New Jersey</a> where he attended <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Regional_High_School" title="Eastern Regional High School">Eastern Regional High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Voorhees_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Voorhees Township, New Jersey">Voorhees Township</a>. Fully focused on music, Bryan Winchester adopted the stage name Braille Brizzy and headed back to experience the Portland music scene. The MC originally named himself "Reflection" and released one very narrowly distributed demo under the name, but later changed it to Braille.
</p><p>The concept behind the name Braille relates to "Helping People Understand the things they can't see."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He has worked with a host of premier hip hop recording acts including his group Lightheaded, with rapper-producer <a href="/wiki/Ohmega_Watts" title="Ohmega Watts">Ohmega Watts</a> and rapper Othello. Braille was also a part of a group called Acts 29 consisting of himself, Ohmega Watts, and Soul Plasma. Acts 29 has only released one album and is not planning on releasing any more material.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Braille was signed to Syntax Records in 1999 and continues to record for the label.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The majority of his recordings are credited to his own label imprint "Hip-hop Is Music."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Influenced by the positive vibe of the group <a href="/wiki/A_Tribe_Called_Quest" title="A Tribe Called Quest">A Tribe Called Quest</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Braille started recording Christian hip hop music. His first album called <i>Lifefirst: Half the Battle</i> was released when he was 17 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Braille shut down his HipHop is Music label and started Humble Beast with his friend and fellow musician Thomas Terry (aka Odd Thomas), which is dedicated to giving all its music away for free. Artists currently signed to Humble Beast include <a href="/wiki/Propaganda_(musician)" title="Propaganda (musician)">Propaganda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beautiful_Eulogy" title="Beautiful Eulogy">Beautiful Eulogy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Hill-Perry" class="mw-redirect" title="Jackie Hill-Perry">Jackie Hill-Perry</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hip-hop_honors">Hip-hop honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Braille_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Hip-hop honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004, Braille collaborated with his group Lightheaded (consisting of himself, <a href="/wiki/Ohmega_Watts" title="Ohmega Watts">Ohmega Watts</a>, and Othello), <a href="/wiki/Manchild_(rapper)" title="Manchild (rapper)">Manchild</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mars_Ill" title="Mars Ill">Mars Ill</a>, Sharlock Poems of <a href="/wiki/LA_Symphony" title="LA Symphony">LA Symphony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Stone_(music_producer)" title="Tony Stone (music producer)">Tony Stone</a>, Celph Titled, Kno of <a href="/wiki/Cunninlynguists" class="mw-redirect" title="Cunninlynguists">Cunninlynguists</a>, the Masterminds, <a href="/wiki/9th_Wonder" title="9th Wonder">9th Wonder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rob_Swift" title="Rob Swift">Rob Swift</a> to produce the album and accompanying video for "Shades of Grey".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> That year the national publication <a href="/wiki/URB_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="URB Magazine">URB Magazine</a> recognized him as an up-and-coming "Artist to Watch". He was also named "Lyricist of the Year" with <i>Shades of Grey</i> and awarded "Album of the Year" honors by Sphereofhiphop. The following year Braille was selected to open for the Godfather of Soul <a href="/wiki/James_Brown" title="James Brown">James Brown</a>'s World Tour in 2005 -2006.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The publication R&amp;B Showcase Magazine recognized Braille as "Hip-Hop's New Force" and honored him with a feature and cover story in the fall of 2006. He joined hip hop group <a href="/wiki/De_La_Soul" title="De La Soul">De La Soul</a> touring throughout the summer of 2007. The same year Braille added speaking engagements to his tour dates and spoke at a South Jersey benefit for Hold on to Education Foundation Inc. A youth motivational speaker, Braille was chosen by the organization as an honorary board member.
</p><p>The spiritual collection called <i>Box of Rhymes</i> was Braille's third full-length album, released exclusively in Japan on the Universal/Handcuts record label.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_projects">New projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Braille_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: New projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Beautiful_Eulogy" title="Beautiful Eulogy">Beautiful Eulogy</a></div>
<p>The appropriately titled fourth album <i>The IV Edition</i> was released in 2008 and featured a special guest appearance from Grammy Award-winning recording artist <a href="/wiki/Speech_(rapper)" title="Speech (rapper)">Speech</a> from <a href="/wiki/Arrested_Development_(group)" title="Arrested Development (group)">Arrested Development</a>. The titled track is included on the video game <i><a href="/wiki/NBA_Live_09" title="NBA Live 09">NBA Live 09</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In addition to his U.S. performances, Braille continues to tour internationally including in Europe and Japan. His most recent projects include <i>Cloud Nineteen</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> an album release and interactive DVD. He is also planning a special concert tour and youth presentation aimed at students of the arts, emphasizing the importance of education to all children.
</p><p>In 2012 Braille collaborated with Odd Thomas and Courtland Urbano to form the group <a href="/wiki/Beautiful_Eulogy" title="Beautiful Eulogy">Beautiful Eulogy</a>. David Kincannon of Rapzilla stated, "This musical triumvirate is responsible for some of the most creative hip hop of the last few years, and <i><a href="/wiki/Satellite_Kite" title="Satellite Kite">Satellite Kite</a></i> does nothing but add to that resume. From the concepts to the beats to the lyrics to the delivery, everything about this album is on point. There is a working relationship between these three men that is obvious by listening to this album. There is also a shared creative spirit that is obvious, and it's that creativity that makes <i>Satellite Kite</i> great."<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
