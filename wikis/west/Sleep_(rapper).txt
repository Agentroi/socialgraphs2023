<p><b>Christopher Tafoya</b> (born June 2, 1975), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Sleep</b> or <b>Sleep of Oldominion</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> artist from <a href="/wiki/Farmington,_New_Mexico" title="Farmington, New Mexico">Farmington, New Mexico</a>, who is currently based in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, Oregon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He is a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Northwest hip hop">Pacific Northwest hip hop</a> collective <a href="/wiki/Oldominion" title="Oldominion">Oldominion</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and hip hop duo <a href="/wiki/The_Chicharones" title="The Chicharones">The Chicharones</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Josh_Martinez" title="Josh Martinez">Josh Martinez</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He is signed to <a href="/wiki/Strange_Famous_Records" title="Strange Famous Records">Strange Famous Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sleep_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tafoya grew up in the small town community of <a href="/wiki/Farmington,_New_Mexico" title="Farmington, New Mexico">Farmington, New Mexico</a>. He is of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexican</a> descent.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> At the age of five, Sleep was already rapping and <a href="/wiki/B-boying" class="mw-redirect" title="B-boying">b-boying</a> with his friends and relatives in the small town, with himself naming <a href="/wiki/Run%E2%80%93D.M.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Run–D.M.C.">Run DMC</a> as an important influence when he first began.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to the hip hop he heard early on, his father was a former bass player for blues musician <a href="/wiki/Clarence_%22Gatemouth%22_Brown" title="Clarence &quot;Gatemouth&quot; Brown">Gatemouth Brown</a>, and would play a wide array of music, from Mariachi, to blues, to rock. Sleep also grew up with seven other brothers who all played musical instruments and had a grandfather who was also a multi instrumentalist.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Entering the world of rap, he felt that his talents as both an MC and a writer were overly slept on, hence the creation of his name Sleep.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Growing up in New Mexico, Sleep went to the same school as <a href="/wiki/Onry_Ozzborn" title="Onry Ozzborn">Onry Ozzborn</a>, which led them to meet Pale Soul.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Together, they formed the group Oraclez Creed, which would later become <a href="/wiki/Oldominion" title="Oldominion">Oldominion</a> after combining with Frontline (<a href="/wiki/Boom_Bap_Project" title="Boom Bap Project">Destro</a>, Nyqwil, and Snafu).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> After the join, the massive <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music_in_the_Pacific_Northwest" title="Hip hop music in the Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest hip hop</a> collective grew to over twenty members.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sleep_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2001-2008:_Riot_by_Candelight.2C_Christopher_and_the_Chicharones"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2001-2008:_Riot_by_Candelight,_Christopher_and_the_Chicharones">2001-2008: <i>Riot by Candelight</i>, <i>Christopher</i> and the Chicharones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sleep_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2001-2008: Riot by Candelight, Christopher and the Chicharones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sleep released his debut album, <i>Riot by Candlelight</i>, in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Following the release, he was heavily involved in the multiple albums by Oldominion.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In 2001, Sleep met Canadian rapper <a href="/wiki/Josh_Martinez" title="Josh Martinez">Josh Martinez</a> by chance encounter at the <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> festival in <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Together, the two would create the hip hop duo <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chicharones" title="The Chicharones">the Chicharones</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Sleep's second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Christopher_(Sleep_album)" title="Christopher (Sleep album)">Christopher</a></i>, was released in 2005 (and then re-released with bonus tracks in 2009).<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009-2013:_Strange_Famous_Records_and_Hesitation_Wounds">2009-2013: Strange Famous Records and <i>Hesitation Wounds</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sleep_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009-2013: Strange Famous Records and Hesitation Wounds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Leading up to 2009, Sleep had contacted <a href="/wiki/Sage_Francis" title="Sage Francis">Sage Francis</a> in an attempt to receive a feature for his upcoming album, which eventually led to his signing to Sage's independent label <a href="/wiki/Strange_Famous_Records" title="Strange Famous Records">Strange Famous Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, Sleep released his third solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Hesitation_Wounds_(album)" title="Hesitation Wounds (album)">Hesitation Wounds</a>.</i> It features verses from <a href="/wiki/Grayskul" title="Grayskul">Grayskul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Del_the_Funky_Homosapien" title="Del the Funky Homosapien">Del the Funky Homosapien</a> as well as production from likes of <a href="/wiki/Zavala_(producer)" title="Zavala (producer)">Zavala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reanimator_(producer)" title="Reanimator (producer)">Reanimator</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014-present:_Oregon_Failure">2014-present: <i>Oregon Failure</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sleep_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2014-present: Oregon Failure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sleep's fourth solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Oregon_Failure" title="Oregon Failure">Oregon Failure</a></i>, was released on April 15, 2014 on Strange Famous Records as well.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The entire album is produced by Maulskull and has features from <a href="/wiki/Onry_Ozzborn" title="Onry Ozzborn">Onry Ozzborn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ceschi" title="Ceschi">Ceschi</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-oregonfailure_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oregonfailure-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style">Style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sleep_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sleep's style has been described as "having intricate rhyme schemes and rapid-fire delivery," with his lyrics being both witty and personal.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
