<p><b>James  McCall</b> (born June 3, 1983), better known by his stage names <b>Nocando</b> and <b>All City Jimmy</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He is the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Scribble_Jam" title="Scribble Jam">Scribble Jam</a> champion<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and founder of <a href="/wiki/Hellfyre_Club" title="Hellfyre Club">Hellfyre Club</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He is a member of hip hop groups such as Customer Service,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bomb_Zombies" title="Bomb Zombies">Bomb Zombies</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Flash_Bang_Grenada" title="Flash Bang Grenada">Flash Bang Grenada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nocando&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nocando began gaining notoriety in Leimert Park's <a href="/wiki/Open-mic" class="mw-redirect" title="Open-mic">open-mic</a> workshops in <a href="/wiki/South_Los_Angeles" title="South Los Angeles">South Los Angeles</a>. In the battle circuit, it is  believed that he has won a hundred wins over many emcees.<sup id="cite_ref-law_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He has since focused on making and releasing music, only taking time out occasionally to battle.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Nocando released his official debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_the_Lock" title="Jimmy the Lock">Jimmy the Lock</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Pup_Records" title="Alpha Pup Records">Alpha Pup Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Thavius_Beck" title="Thavius Beck">Thavius Beck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nosaj_Thing" title="Nosaj Thing">Nosaj Thing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Free_the_Robots" title="Free the Robots">Free the Robots</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daedelus_(musician)" title="Daedelus (musician)">Daedelus</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Thorburn" title="Nicholas Thorburn">Nicholas Thorburn</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In that year, he also released <i><a href="/wiki/Sincerely_Yours_(Bomb_Zombies_EP)" title="Sincerely Yours (Bomb Zombies EP)">Sincerely Yours</a></i>, a collaborative EP with <a href="/wiki/Nobody_(producer)" title="Nobody (producer)">Nobody</a> as <a href="/wiki/Bomb_Zombies" title="Bomb Zombies">Bomb Zombies</a>, on <a href="/wiki/Hellfyre_Club" title="Hellfyre Club">Hellfyre Club</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, he released <i><a href="/wiki/10_Haters" title="10 Haters">10 Haters</a></i>, a collaborative album with <a href="/wiki/Busdriver" title="Busdriver">Busdriver</a> as <a href="/wiki/Flash_Bang_Grenada" title="Flash Bang Grenada">Flash Bang Grenada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <i>Alarm</i> included it on the "50 Unheralded Albums from 2011" list.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Nocando released a free EP, <i>Zero Hour</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> as well as the singles "Where's the Money?"<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and "Up in the Air".<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, he released a single, "Little Green Monsters".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> His second solo album, <i>Jimmy the Burnout</i>, was released on Hellfyre Club in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Nocando released <i>Severed</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a></i> called it "one of Nocando's most powerful albums to date."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Uproxx" title="Uproxx">Uproxx</a></i> named it the 24th best rap album of 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-uproxx2017_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uproxx2017-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In that year, he also released <i>King Snake</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-uproxx2017_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uproxx2017-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Nocando changed his stage name to All City Jimmy,<sup id="cite_ref-theskinny2019_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theskinny2019-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and later removed all Nocando titles from digital retailers.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
