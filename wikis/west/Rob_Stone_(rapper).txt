<p><b>Jaylen Anthony Robinson</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born January 25, 1995), known professionally as <b>Rob Stone</b> (stylized as <b>Rob $tone</b>), is an American rapper  from <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Stone_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jaylen Anthony Robinson was born on January 25, 1995, in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. From a young age, he was influenced by his father’s vintage music collection, and expanded his listening to <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He attended college in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, where he first started teaching himself how to rap.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Stone_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stone first released his first song "<a href="/wiki/Chill_Bill" title="Chill Bill">Chill Bill</a>" on June 10, 2014, on his <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He wrote the song in the back of a police car.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Stone then went on to release his debut mixtape <i>Straight Bummin<span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span></i> on February 8, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-Straight_Bummin_2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Straight_Bummin_2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The music video for "Chill Bill" was released on June 25, 2015, on his friend's <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel called "Twelve O'Seven".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Stone then released the song as his debut single on June 17, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  The song debuted at number 99 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, and peaked at number 29 on the chart.<sup id="cite_ref-hot100_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hot100-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The official remix of the song features American rappers <a href="/wiki/Shelley_FKA_DRAM" class="mw-redirect" title="Shelley FKA DRAM">DRAM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denzel_Curry" title="Denzel Curry">Denzel Curry</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cousin_Stizz" title="Cousin Stizz">Cousin Stizz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In September 2016, Stone released his second mixtape <i>I'm Almost Ready</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-I&#39;m_Almost_Ready_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-I&#39;m_Almost_Ready-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
