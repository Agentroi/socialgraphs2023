<p><b>Eden Daniel Pearlstein</b> (born February 19, 1980), better known by his stage name <b>Eprhyme</b> (pronounced "E-Prime"), is an American Jewish <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">producer</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. While attending <a href="/wiki/The_Evergreen_State_College" class="mw-redirect" title="The Evergreen State College">The Evergreen State College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Olympia,_Washington" title="Olympia, Washington">Olympia, Washington</a>, he became involved with the <a href="/wiki/Olympia_music_scene" class="mw-redirect" title="Olympia music scene">Olympia music scene</a> as half of the hip hop duo Saints of Everyday Failures, with which he released two albums. According to Nic Leonard of the <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Volcano" title="Weekly Volcano">Weekly Volcano</a></i>, Eprhyme "played a major roll [sic] in the creation of the Olympia hip-hop scene."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was noticed by local independent label <a href="/wiki/K_Records" title="K Records">K Records</a>, who released his first two singles, "<a href="/wiki/Punklezmerap" title="Punklezmerap">Punklezmerap</a>" and "Shomer Salaam". He then released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Waywordwonderwill" title="Waywordwonderwill">Waywordwonderwill</a></i> (2009), through <a href="/wiki/Shemspeed" class="mw-redirect" title="Shemspeed">Shemspeed Records</a>, before returning to <a href="/wiki/K_Records" title="K Records">K Records</a> for his follow-up, <i><a href="/wiki/Dopestylevsky" title="Dopestylevsky">Dopestylevsky</a></i> (2011). He is currently part of the <a href="/wiki/Alternative_hip_hop" title="Alternative hip hop">alternative hip hop</a> groups <a href="/wiki/Darshan_(band)" title="Darshan (band)">Darshan</a>, with vocalist <a href="/wiki/Basya_Schechter" title="Basya Schechter">Basya Schechter</a>, and Ruthless Cosmopolitans, with <a href="/wiki/Jon_Madof" title="Jon Madof">Jon Madof</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eprhyme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eden Pearlstein was born February 19, 1980, in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>. He grew up in a <a href="/wiki/Reform_Judaism" title="Reform Judaism">Reform Jewish</a> household, later describing it as "a standard non-<a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Judaism" title="Orthodox Judaism">Orthodox</a> West Coast Jewish upbringing—<a href="/wiki/Hebrew_school" title="Hebrew school">Hebrew school</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bar_mitzvah" class="mw-redirect" title="Bar mitzvah">bar mitzvah</a>, and summer camp. It was not very intriguing to me and I just walked away from it after my bar mitzvah."<sup id="cite_ref-jc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Pearlstein listened to hip hop as a child and began rapping when he was 14.<sup id="cite_ref-jc_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> During his middle school years, he became rebellious and dabbled in drugs and petty crime until, at age 17, he attended a youth arts program and became interested in poetry. In his early twenties, he visited <a href="/wiki/North_Dakota" title="North Dakota">North Dakota</a> to participate in a Native American <a href="/wiki/Sun_Dance" title="Sun Dance">Sun Dance</a>, during which he had a spiritual epiphany that inspired him to re-examine his Jewish roots.<sup id="cite_ref-haaretz_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-haaretz-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eprhyme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_music_in_Olympia">Early music in Olympia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eprhyme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early music in Olympia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pearlstein attended <a href="/wiki/Evergreen_State_College" title="Evergreen State College">The Evergreen State College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Olympia,_Washington" title="Olympia, Washington">Olympia, Washington</a>, where he studied <a href="/wiki/Comparative_theology" title="Comparative theology">comparative theology</a> and led classes in Alternative Jewish studies on the side.<sup id="cite_ref-haaretz_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-haaretz-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During this time, he became involved in the <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Olympia,_Washington" title="Music of Olympia, Washington">Olympia music scene</a>, working with producer Matt "Smoke" Smokovich of <a href="/wiki/Oldominion" title="Oldominion">Oldominion</a>.
</p><p>Pearlstein co-founded the hip hop group <b>Saints of Everyday Failures</b> alongside D-Scribe, Tha Goonie, MC Contradiction, and My Left Foot. They performed at the 2006 <a href="/wiki/Lakefair" title="Lakefair">Lakefair</a> festival alongside <a href="/wiki/One_Block_Radius" title="One Block Radius">One Block Radius</a> and released two albums together, <i>True Meaning of Survival</i> (2005) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_State_of_the_Art_is_Failure" class="mw-redirect" title="The State of the Art is Failure">The State of the Art is Failure</a></i> (2006), the latter of which featured contributions by <a href="/wiki/Awol_One" title="Awol One">Awol One</a>, <a href="/wiki/2Mex" title="2Mex">2Mex</a>, <a href="/wiki/Onry_Ozzborn" title="Onry Ozzborn">Onry Ozzborn</a>, and members of <a href="/wiki/Typical_Cats" title="Typical Cats">Typical Cats</a>. The album's sound was described by <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Volcano" title="Weekly Volcano">Weekly Volcano</a></i> as "a séance with <a href="/wiki/John_Coltrane" title="John Coltrane">John Coltrane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hermann_Hesse" title="Hermann Hesse">Hermann Hesse</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Enemy (band)">Public Enemy</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eprhyme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pearlstein gained the attention of <a href="/wiki/K_Records" title="K Records">K Records</a>, which released his first two solo singles, "<a href="/wiki/Punklezmerap" title="Punklezmerap">Punklezmerap</a>", which featured <a href="/wiki/Nomy_Lamm" title="Nomy Lamm">Nomy Lamm</a> on vocals, and "Shomer Salaam". This made him the label's first hip hop artist in a decade, as they were primarily associated with <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> artists.<sup id="cite_ref-jewcy_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jewcy-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Pearlstein relocated to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, where he became a regular at <a href="/wiki/SOB%27s" title="SOB&#39;s">SOB's</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jc_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> That same year, he released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Waywordwonderwill" title="Waywordwonderwill">Waywordwonderwill</a></i>, through <a href="/wiki/Shemspeed" class="mw-redirect" title="Shemspeed">Shemspeed Records</a>. In March 2010, Pearlstein performed at the <a href="/wiki/Sandys_Row_Synagogue" title="Sandys Row Synagogue">Sandys Row Synagogue</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> as part of a <a href="/wiki/Moishe_House" title="Moishe House">Moishe House</a> event.<sup id="cite_ref-jc_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pearlstein returned to K Records in 2011 for his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Dopestylevsky" title="Dopestylevsky">Dopestylevsky</a></i>. The album featured more collaborations, including <a href="/wiki/Labtekwon" title="Labtekwon">Labtekwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shneur_Hasofer" title="Shneur Hasofer">DeScribe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Y-Love" title="Y-Love">Y-Love</a>, and AKA.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, Pearlstein and Smoke M2D6, producing under the name Thee Xntrx, released <i><a href="/wiki/All_Your_Friend%27s_Friends" title="All Your Friend&#39;s Friends">All Your Friend's Friends</a></i>, a compilation of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music_in_the_Pacific_Northwest" title="Hip hop music in the Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a> rappers sampling older K Records artists. Those on the compilation included <a href="/wiki/The_Chicharones" title="The Chicharones">The Chicharones</a>, MG! The Visionary, <a href="/wiki/Oldominion" title="Oldominion">Oldominion</a>, and Pearlstein himself.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Darshan.2C_Chant_Records.2C_and_Ruthless_Cosmopolitans"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Darshan,_Chant_Records,_and_Ruthless_Cosmopolitans">Darshan, Chant Records, and Ruthless Cosmopolitans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eprhyme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Darshan, Chant Records, and Ruthless Cosmopolitans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Darshan_(band)" title="Darshan (band)">Darshan (band)</a></div>
<p>While attending a retreat at <a href="/wiki/Isabella_Freedman_Jewish_Retreat_Center" title="Isabella Freedman Jewish Retreat Center">Isabella Freedman Jewish Retreat Center</a> in 2008, Pearlstein met rabbi and singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Shir_Yaakov" title="Shir Yaakov">Shir Yaakov</a>. The two subsequently formed the <a href="/wiki/Alternative_hip_hop" title="Alternative hip hop">alternative hip hop</a> collective <a href="/wiki/Darshan_(band)" title="Darshan (band)">Darshan</a>, and in 2009 they released a debut EP, <i>Lishmah</i>, through <a href="/wiki/Erez_Safar" title="Erez Safar">Erez Safar</a>'s Shemspeed label. A debut full-length, <i>Deeper and Higher</i>, was released independently in 2015, with <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Saft" title="Jamie Saft">Jamie Saft</a> producing and contributions from drummer <a href="/wiki/Ben_Perowsky" title="Ben Perowsky">Ben Perowsky</a> and vocalists <a href="/wiki/Basya_Schechter" title="Basya Schechter">Basya Schechter</a> and Elana Brody, the former of whom became a full-time member of the group. In 2017 they signed to the independent label Chant Records (founded by guitarist/producer <a href="/wiki/Jon_Madof" title="Jon Madof">Jon Madof</a> and bassist/oudist <a href="/wiki/Shanir_Ezra_Blumenkranz" title="Shanir Ezra Blumenkranz">Shanir Ezra Blumenkranz</a>) and released a second album, <i>Raza</i>, with Pearlstein and Schecter, producer Frederik Rubens, and backing musicians including Blumenkranz, <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Lurie" title="Jessica Lurie">Jessica Lurie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Lindner" title="Jason Lindner">Jason Lindner</a>, and Schechter's <a href="/wiki/Pharaoh%27s_Daughter" title="Pharaoh&#39;s Daughter">Pharaoh's Daughter</a> bandmates Mathias Kunzli, Noah Hoffeld, and Daniel Freedman.
</p><p>In late 2020 Pearlstein formed another group, Ruthless Cosmopolitans, with Madof, guitarist <a href="/wiki/Yoshie_Fruchter" title="Yoshie Fruchter">Yoshie Fruchter</a>, and drummer Emmanuel "Manny" Laine (<a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>). The idea for the group originated in August 2017 when Pearlstein wrote the song "Make America Hate Again" in the wake of the infamous <a href="/wiki/Unite_the_Right_rally" title="Unite the Right rally">Unite the Right rally</a> and brought it to Madof.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> After releasing two music videos, "The Screen Age" on September 21 and "Make America Hate Again" on October 12, the group released a self-titled EP on October 23, 2020. Produced by Madof, the EP featured contributions from vocalists Elana Brody and Talia Madof and <a href="/wiki/Zion80" title="Zion80">Zion80</a> members Greg Wall, <a href="/wiki/Frank_London" title="Frank London">Frank London</a>, Brian Marsella, and Marlon Sobol.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eprhyme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to hip hop, Pearlstein's music contains elements of <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Klezmer" title="Klezmer">klezmer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Middle_Eastern_music" title="Middle Eastern music">Middle Eastern music</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jc_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He has been compared to artists like <a href="/wiki/A_Tribe_Called_Quest" title="A Tribe Called Quest">A Tribe Called Quest</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">The Roots</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tablet_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tablet-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> His lyrics deal with a number of religious and philosophical topics.<sup id="cite_ref-forward1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forward1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-forward2_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forward2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
