<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932015:_Career_beginnings_and_Cozz_.26_Effect"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2015:_Career_beginnings_and_Cozz_&amp;_Effect">2013–2015: Career beginnings and <i>Cozz &amp; Effect</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cozz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2013–2015: Career beginnings and Cozz &amp; Effect">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, Cozz began to take rap seriously, and recorded a demo tape. A few months later, he released the music video for "Dreams". Shortly after, he started to get notice, and having meetings with many different labels. Cozz's manager brought the music video to <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a> and was later signed to <a href="/wiki/Dreamville_Records" title="Dreamville Records">Dreamville Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 3, 2014, Cozz released his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Cozz_%26_Effect" title="Cozz &amp; Effect">Cozz &amp; Effect</a></i> The project was initially intended as a free mixtape upon recording. But according to Cozz, when it was finished, it sounded better than they had hoped and they therefore agreed it can be sold. <i><a href="/wiki/Dreamville_Records" title="Dreamville Records">Dreamville Records</a></i> released it on digital format firstly, followed by physical CD a short while afterwards. In 2015, Cozz was a part of <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/2014_Forest_Hills_Drive" title="2014 Forest Hills Drive">2014 Forest Hills Drive</a> Tour" along with other acts <a href="/wiki/Bas_(rapper)" title="Bas (rapper)">Bas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Omen_(musician)" title="Omen (musician)">Omen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jhene_Aiko" class="mw-redirect" title="Jhene Aiko">Jhene Aiko</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremih" title="Jeremih">Jeremih</a>, <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932020:_Nothin.27_Personal_and_Effected"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2020:_Nothin'_Personal_and_Effected">2016–2020: <i>Nothin' Personal</i> and <i>Effected</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cozz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2016–2020: Nothin&#039; Personal and Effected">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 4, 2016, Cozz released his second project, and first mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Nothin%27_Personal_(Cozz_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Nothin&#39; Personal (Cozz album)">Nothin' Personal</a></i>. The mixtape was entirely produced by Meez. Features include fellow west coast rapper <a href="/wiki/Boogie_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Boogie (rapper)">Boogie</a>, Bas, and Free Ackrite.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape was supported with four singles: "Tabs," "Growth," "My Side," and "Who Said."
</p><p>On February 13, 2018, he released his second album <i><a href="/wiki/Effected_(album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Effected (album)">Effected</a></i> which includes features from J. Cole and <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Singles and music videos for the album includes "Questions", "Demons N Distractions", and "Bout It".<sup id="cite_ref-India_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-India-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphop.com_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphop.com-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On April 4, 2018, Cozz announced The Effected Tour in North American in 16 cities, to further promote the album.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He was also featured on Los Angeles rapper JAG's album with <a href="/wiki/Reason_(American_rapper)" title="Reason (American rapper)">Reason</a> on the track "Black Boy Rise". On November 7, Cozz worked with DJ Megan Ryte on a 5-track mixtape called <i>Aftermath Of My Dreams</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2021.E2.80.93present:_Fortunate_EP"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2021–present:_Fortunate_EP">2021–present: <i>Fortunate</i> EP</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cozz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2021–present: Fortunate EP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 22, 2021, Cozz released the first single from the EP of the same name titled "Fortunate", produced by <a href="/wiki/T-Minus_(record_producer)" title="T-Minus (record producer)">T-Minus</a>, J. Cole, and Ced Breeze.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On November 17, he released the second single "Addicted", along with a music video.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On December 2, Cozz released the EP <i>Fortunate</i>, which features a guest appearance from <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
