<p><b>Solomon Samuel Simone</b> (born January 15, 1990), known by his stage name <b>Raz Simone</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;r&#39; in &#39;rye&#39;">r</span><span title="/æ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;bad&#39;">æ</span><span title="&#39;z&#39; in &#39;zoom&#39;">z</span></span><span class="wrap"> </span><span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="/ɪ/: &#39;i&#39; in &#39;kit&#39;">ɪ</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;m&#39; in &#39;my&#39;">m</span><span title="/oʊ/: &#39;o&#39; in &#39;code&#39;">oʊ</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span></span>/</a></span></span>), is an <a href="/wiki/African-American" class="mw-redirect" title="African-American">African-American</a> recording artist and songwriter from <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle, Washington</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> artist who started under the name <b>Razpy</b>, he built his brand by releasing EPs, touring, and filming music videos through his company Black Umbrella.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Simone gained recognition after releasing his debut solo EP, <i>Solomon Samuel Simone.</i><sup id="cite_ref-MTV_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  He became the subject of media coverage for his association with the <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Hill_Autonomous_Zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Capitol Hill Autonomous Zone">Capitol Hill Autonomous Zone</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/George_Floyd_protests_in_Seattle" title="George Floyd protests in Seattle">George Floyd protests in Seattle</a>, in which he distributed a gun to a protester amid rumors the <a href="/wiki/Proud_Boys" title="Proud Boys">Proud Boys</a> were gathering nearby.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raz_Simone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raz_Simone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Simone attended a private elementary school. In 2007, he worked for the <a href="/wiki/Boys_%26_Girls_Clubs_of_America" title="Boys &amp; Girls Clubs of America">Boys &amp; Girls Club</a> and was in the <a href="/wiki/Running_Start" title="Running Start">Running Start</a> dual enrollment program.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raz_Simone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Upon release of his debut solo EP, <i>Solomon Samuel Simone,</i> Simone garnered the attention of 300 Entertainment executives <a href="/wiki/Lyor_Cohen" title="Lyor Cohen">Lyor Cohen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Todd_Moscowitz" title="Todd Moscowitz">Todd Moscowitz</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Liles" title="Kevin Liles">Kevin Liles</a> which resulted in a partnership between the newly established <a href="/wiki/300_Entertainment" title="300 Entertainment">300 Entertainment</a> and his Black Umbrella Imprint.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the debut EP, Simone toured and released his first full-length album <i>Cognitive Dissonance</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2016 Simone released <i>Trap Spirituals</i>. He was nominated for <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a>'s Freshman List. In June 2016 Simone played shows in seven cities, opening for <a href="/wiki/Macklemore" title="Macklemore">Macklemore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Lewis" title="Ryan Lewis">Ryan Lewis</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In May 2020, at a parking lot at <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Center" title="Seattle Center">Seattle Center</a>, Simone held a "pop-up, drive-in concert" (a type of concert developed in response to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, in order to allow for <a href="/wiki/Social_distancing" title="Social distancing">social distancing</a>). <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raz_Simone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2020, during the <a href="/wiki/George_Floyd_protests_in_Seattle" title="George Floyd protests in Seattle">George Floyd protests in Seattle</a>, Simone emerged as an active member of the self-declared <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Hill_Autonomous_Zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Capitol Hill Autonomous Zone">Capitol Hill Autonomous Zone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a> described him as the "<a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a> leader of the autonomous zone."<sup id="cite_ref-Leader_says_many_leaving_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Leader_says_many_leaving-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>News outlets and publications including <i><a href="/wiki/Fox_News" title="Fox News">Fox News</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Townhall" title="Townhall">Townhall</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-Townhall_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Townhall-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/City_Journal" title="City Journal">City Journal</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-City_Journal_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-City_Journal-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> featured Simone prominently in their coverage of the zone, characterizing him as a "<a href="/wiki/Warlord" title="Warlord">warlord</a>" policing the area with an <a href="/wiki/AK-47" title="AK-47">AK-47</a> and highlighting an interaction where he allegedly assaulted a <a href="/wiki/Tagging_(graffiti)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tagging (graffiti)">tagger</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Townhall_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Townhall-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> A Facebook video shows Simone distributing a firearm to a protester.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/Snopes" title="Snopes">Snopes</a>, on June 15 <a href="/wiki/Andy_Ngo" title="Andy Ngo">Andy Ngo</a> presented a video clip from Simones’s June 8 Facebook feed where Simone <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sexual_abuse_and_assault_allegations">Sexual abuse and assault allegations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raz_Simone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sexual abuse and assault allegations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2021, two women stepped forward to publicly accuse Simone of <a href="/wiki/Coercion" title="Coercion">coercion</a> and repeated physical abuse.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2022, Simone was sued by five women alleging sexual abuse and assault. Four of them say that he <a href="/wiki/Sex_trafficking" title="Sex trafficking">sex trafficked</a> and abused them.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> One of the alleged victims, claims Simone sex trafficked her in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a> for over a year, until she finally broke free in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> She stated that Simone held her captive for three days within a confined space and that more than once he forcibly had sex with her and strangled her.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Court documents reportedly state Simone targets "young, vulnerable women" who are involved in <a href="/wiki/Sex_work" title="Sex work">sex work</a> or susceptible to it.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Simone has denied these allegations.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> However, Simone has acknowledged being convicted of third degree child endangerment in Georgia and being a pimp in Seattle.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
