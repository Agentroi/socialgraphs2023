<p><b>Kyle Thomas Harvey</b> (born May 18, 1993<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>), known <a href="/wiki/Mononym" title="Mononym">mononymously</a> as <b>Kyle</b> and <b>SuperDuperKyle</b> (formerly known as <b>K.i.D</b>, an acronym for <b>Kyle is Determined</b>), is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and actor from <a href="/wiki/Ventura,_California" title="Ventura, California">Ventura, California</a>. He is best known for his 2016 breakout single "<a href="/wiki/ISpy_(Kyle_song)" title="ISpy (Kyle song)">iSpy</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Lil_Yachty" title="Lil Yachty">Lil Yachty</a>), which peaked at number 4 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. Signed to Indie-Pop and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Atlantic_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlantic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Kyle is also known for his association with the SuperDuperCrew which consists of Brick, Jesus, and Maxx.
</p><p>Kyle has collaborated with artists such as Waywo, <a href="/wiki/Kehlani" title="Kehlani">Kehlani</a>, Lil Yachty, <a href="/wiki/G-Eazy" title="G-Eazy">G-Eazy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chance_the_Rapper" title="Chance the Rapper">Chance the Rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miguel_(singer)" title="Miguel (singer)">Miguel</a>, <a href="/wiki/MadeinTYO" title="MadeinTYO">MadeinTYO</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yuna_(singer)" title="Yuna (singer)">Yuna</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>, among others. Some of his songs in addition to "iSpy" include "Keep It Real", "Doubt It", "King Wavy" (featuring G-Eazy), and his 2018 hit single "<a href="/wiki/Playinwitme" title="Playinwitme">Playinwitme</a>" (featuring Kehlani).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> named him as "one of the ten artists you need to know".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His commercial mixtapes <i>Beautiful Loser</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Smyle_(Kyle_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Smyle (Kyle album)">Smyle</a></i> were released in 2013 and 2015, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Kyle's debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Light_of_Mine" title="Light of Mine">Light of Mine</a></i> was released in 2018 to positive reviews and reached within the top-30 of the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.
</p><p>Kyle made his acting debut in the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> original film <i><a href="/wiki/The_After_Party_(film)" title="The After Party (film)">The After Party</a></i>. He also released a single from the film "Moment" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> His second album <i><a href="/wiki/See_You_When_I_Am_Famous" title="See You When I Am Famous">See You When I Am Famous</a></i> was released on July 17, 2020.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyle_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harvey was born in the <a href="/wiki/Reseda,_Los_Angeles" title="Reseda, Los Angeles">Reseda</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, to a single mother, in a home of eight family members and two siblings.<sup id="cite_ref-RS_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He grew up one hour away in <a href="/wiki/Ventura,_California" title="Ventura, California">Ventura</a>. He stated that he has a <a href="/wiki/Speech_disorder" title="Speech disorder">speech impediment</a><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and that during his youth he was bullied because of it.<sup id="cite_ref-RS_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Harvey attended <a href="/wiki/Ventura_High_School" title="Ventura High School">Ventura High School</a><sup id="cite_ref-Vice_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vice-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and regularly took part in drama classes which helped his confidence largely.<sup id="cite_ref-RS_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He started singing at the age of 6 and was writing and performing his own songs by elementary school. Harvey started rapping when he was about 13 years old<sup id="cite_ref-Vice_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vice-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and began recording music after hearing <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Man_on_the_Moon:_The_End_of_Day" title="Man on the Moon: The End of Day">Man on the Moon: The End of Day</a></i>. Harvey's first songs were recorded on <a href="/wiki/PreSonus" title="PreSonus">PreSonus</a>' <a href="/wiki/Studio_One_(software)" title="Studio One (software)">Studio One</a> recording software using his aunt's computer while in high school.<sup id="cite_ref-RS_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyle_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932012:_Early_mixtapes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2012:_Early_mixtapes">2009–2012: Early mixtapes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyle_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009–2012: Early mixtapes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kyle started releasing <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a> such as <i>Senior Year</i>, <i>Second Semester</i>, <i>FxL</i>, <i>Super Duper</i>, and <i>K.i.D</i> under the monikers KiddKash and K.i.D starting in 2009 to 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932015:_Beautiful_Loser_and_Smyle"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2015:_Beautiful_Loser_and_Smyle">2013–2015: <i>Beautiful Loser</i> and <i>Smyle</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyle_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013–2015: Beautiful Loser and Smyle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, he released his commercial debut <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> <i>Beautiful Loser</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2014, he was featured in <a href="/wiki/G-Eazy" title="G-Eazy">G-Eazy</a>'s music video for "<a href="/wiki/I_Mean_It" title="I Mean It">I Mean It</a>" as a technical director on the fictional news set.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2015, he released his follow up commercial mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Smyle" title="Smyle">Smyle</a></i>. The album peaked on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at number 76. The album featured a guest spot by <a href="/wiki/Chance_the_Rapper" title="Chance the Rapper">Chance the Rapper</a> on the track "Remember Me?" and has garnered over 2 million views on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.
</p><p>Kyle's first music video to reach one million views was "Keep It Real", which was accomplished later that year.
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.93present:_Breakthrough.2C_Light_of_Mine_and_See_You_When_I_Am_Famous"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–present:_Breakthrough,_Light_of_Mine_and_See_You_When_I_Am_Famous">2016–present: Breakthrough, <i>Light of Mine</i> and <i>See You When I Am Famous</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyle_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2016–present: Breakthrough, Light of Mine and See You When I Am Famous">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2016, he released the single "<a href="/wiki/ISpy_(Kyle_song)" title="ISpy (Kyle song)">iSpy</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/Lil_Yachty" title="Lil Yachty">Lil Yachty</a>. The song gained widespread recognition for Kyle. The song peaked at number 4 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and number 14 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2017, Kyle signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Atlantic_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlantic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2017, Kyle was named as one of the ten 2017 <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> Freshmen along with <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a>, <a href="/wiki/PnB_Rock" title="PnB Rock">PnB Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Playboi_Carti" title="Playboi Carti">Playboi Carti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ugly_God" title="Ugly God">Ugly God</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amin%C3%A9" class="mw-redirect" title="Aminé">Aminé</a>, <a href="/wiki/MadeinTYO" title="MadeinTYO">MadeinTYO</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kamaiyah" title="Kamaiyah">Kamaiyah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kap_G" title="Kap G">Kap G</a>, and <a href="/wiki/XXXTentacion" title="XXXTentacion">XXXTentacion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2018, alongside the release of his single "<a href="/wiki/Playinwitme" title="Playinwitme">Playinwitme</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Kehlani" title="Kehlani">Kehlani</a>, Kyle announced that <a href="/wiki/Khalid_(singer)" title="Khalid (singer)">Khalid</a> would be featured on a track titled "iMissMe". Kyle's debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Light_of_Mine" title="Light of Mine">Light of Mine</a></i> was released on May 18, 2018. The album debuted at number 29 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He appeared as the main character in the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> original film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_After_Party_(film)" title="The After Party (film)">The After Party</a></i>, which debuted on August 24, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His second album&#160;<i><a href="/wiki/See_You_When_I_Am_Famous" title="See You When I Am Famous">See You When I Am Famous</a></i>&#160;was released on July 17, 2020, and was supported by the singles "Yes!", "What It Is" and "Bouncin".
</p><p>On January 28, 2022, Kyle released the album <i>It's Not So Bad</i>, his fifth project and first as a newly independent artist.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
