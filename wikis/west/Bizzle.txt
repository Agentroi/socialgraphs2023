<p><b>Mark Julian Felder</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born July 21, 1983), more commonly known as <b>Bizzle</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Christian_hip_hop" title="Christian hip hop">Christian hip hop</a> recording artist and entrepreneur.
</p><p>In January 2010, Bizzle released "You Got Some Explaining to Do". The song criticizes rapper <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> for negative references towards Jesus and Christianity in his music. Bizzle released his first mixtape, <i>The Messenger</i>, in March 2010. Shortly after the release, he founded God Over Money Records in Houston, Texas. Bizzle released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Tough_Love_%26_Parables" title="Tough Love &amp; Parables">Tough Love &amp; Parables</a></i>, on June 21, 2011. It debuted at No. 15 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a> Gospel Albums chart. <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Fight_(Bizzle_album)" title="The Good Fight (Bizzle album)">The Good Fight</a></i> was released on May 7, 2013 and debuted at No.&#160;2 on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a>'</i>s Gospel Albums chart, No.&#160;7 on <a href="/wiki/Christian_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian Albums">Christian Albums</a>, and No.&#160;11 on <a href="/wiki/Top_Rap_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Rap Albums">Top Rap Albums</a>. In 2013, Bizzle won Kingdom Choice Awards for Rap Single of The Year, Hip Hop Album of The Year, and Music Video of The Year.
</p><p>Bizzle received media attention in 2014 when he released "Same Love (A Response)" in reaction to the pro-gay rights single "<a href="/wiki/Same_Love" title="Same Love">Same Love</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Macklemore" title="Macklemore">Macklemore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Lewis" title="Ryan Lewis">Ryan Lewis</a>. The song was controversial because it expressed opposition to homosexuality.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mark Felder was raised mostly by his mother in Los Angeles, California. His father stayed in Compton, California. His family was poor and lived in <a href="/wiki/Section_8_(housing)" title="Section 8 (housing)">Section 8</a> housing.<sup id="cite_ref-mystep_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mystep-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Felder was approximately 13 years of age the first time he stepped in a music studio. Listening to <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac</a>, he began to establish a talent for writing rhymes.<sup id="cite_ref-mystep_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mystep-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Felder put out five mixtapes and began circulating them in malls, clubs, radio stations and anywhere else he could. He opened for <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Juelz_Santana" title="Juelz Santana">Juelz Santana</a> during their <i>I Can't Feel My Face</i> tour.<sup id="cite_ref-mystep_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mystep-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cant_feel_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cant_feel-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He also opened for <a href="/wiki/Lil_Boosie" class="mw-redirect" title="Lil Boosie">Lil Boosie</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Biography_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (July 2023)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Felder has stated that his "financial situation got so bad he was sleeping on a friend's floor and his car was a closet for his clothes".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Felder began selling drugs and became a <a href="/wiki/Pimp" class="mw-redirect" title="Pimp">pimp</a> to fund his music career.<sup id="cite_ref-Bizzle,_Christian_Rapper:_God_Provided_After_I_Quit_Chasing_Money._7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bizzle,_Christian_Rapper:_God_Provided_After_I_Quit_Chasing_Money.-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (July 2023)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2004.E2.80.9308_L.A.V._Mixtapes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2004–08_L.A.V._Mixtapes">2004–08 L.A.V. Mixtapes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2004–08 L.A.V. Mixtapes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Between 2004 and 2008, Bizzle released <i>L.A.V. Mixtapes</i> Volumes 1–5 in <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a>. He then released <i>Dirty West Mixtapes</i> 1 and 2 with J. Sin and 360 Records in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, Texas, and returned to California to release <i>Certified Mixtape</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Biography_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (July 2023)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2005, he was offered a deal by 360 Records out of Houston and declined.<sup id="cite_ref-Biography_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Two years later, Bizzle had been offered a deal by <a href="/wiki/Barry_Hankerson" title="Barry Hankerson">Barry Hankerson</a> to sign with <a href="/wiki/Blackground" class="mw-redirect" title="Blackground">Blackground</a>/<a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal</a>, but he also declined that offer.<sup id="cite_ref-Biography_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (July 2023)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Bizzle spent three years preparing his debut album <i>Grind Pays</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Biography_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (July 2023)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.9311_The_Jay-Z_diss.2C_The_Messenger_trilogy_.26_Tough_Love_.26_Parables"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–11_The_Jay-Z_diss,_The_Messenger_trilogy_&amp;_Tough_Love_&amp;_Parables">2008–11 <i>The Jay-Z diss, The Messenger trilogy &amp; Tough Love &amp; Parables</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2008–11 The Jay-Z diss, The Messenger trilogy &amp; Tough Love &amp; Parables">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The influence of Christianity prompted Bizzle's questioning of the imagery promoted through the music of rappers like Kanye West, Rick Ross, and Jay-Z.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On January 17, 2010 he released the track "You Got Some Explaining to Do". The song was directed towards <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, criticizing him for negative references towards Jesus in his lyrics.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The result of the <a href="/wiki/Diss_song" class="mw-redirect" title="Diss song">diss song</a> led Bizzle to being called the "Christian Rapper that dissed Jay Z".<sup id="cite_ref-Biography_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (July 2023)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Overwhelmed by the positive response and media attention he received, Bizzle decided to make music that proclaimed a Christian message and exposed influences within the music industry that he believed to be negative.<sup id="cite_ref-Biography_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (July 2023)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Bizzle released his first Christian <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, <i>The Messenger</i>, on March 30, 2010 on DatPiff.<sup id="cite_ref-TheMessenger_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheMessenger-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <i>The Messenger 2: Delivered</i> &amp; <i>The Messenger 3: Truth Music</i> followed in 2010 &amp; 2011 respectively.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Shortly after the release of "The Messenger", Bizzle founded God Over Money Records in Houston, Texas. Since its creation, the label has released eight mixtapes,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> a collaborative album with artist Willie 'P-Dub' Moore Jr.,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and three full-length studio albums;<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> it has also signed two artists.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bizzle released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Tough_Love_%26_Parables" title="Tough Love &amp; Parables">Tough Love &amp; Parables</a></i>, on June 21, 2011. It debuted at No. 15 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a> Gospel Albums chart.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> It appeared on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a> swoosh banner.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.9314_BoBW.2C_The_Good_Fight_and_working_with_Boi-1da"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–14_BoBW,_The_Good_Fight_and_working_with_Boi-1da">2012–14 <i>BoBW, The Good Fight and working with Boi-1da</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–14 BoBW, The Good Fight and working with Boi-1da">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 7, 2012, Felder and Willie Moore, Jr. released <i>Best of Both Worlds: The Album</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The album reached No. 5 on the iTunes Hip Hop/Rap Chart.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 7, 2013, Bizzle released his second studio album, <i>The Good Fight</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The album features production-works from Grammy-winning producer <a href="/wiki/Boi-1da" title="Boi-1da">Boi-1da</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Its first single, "Soldier" was released January 26, 2013<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and features <a href="/wiki/No_Malice" title="No Malice">No Malice</a> and included a music video. Later singles were "Dear Hip-Hop"<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and "My Confession" (featuring Sevin).<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <i>The Good Fight</i> debuted at No.&#160;2 on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a>'</i>s Gospel Albums chart, No.&#160;7 on <a href="/wiki/Christian_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian Albums">Christian Albums</a>, No.&#160;11 on <a href="/wiki/Top_Rap_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Rap Albums">Top Rap Albums</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and sold a total of 3,962 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> His previous works with Boi-1da include "Forgive Me" released on March 16, 2011<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> which features rapper <a href="/wiki/MC_Jin" title="MC Jin">MC Jin</a> and "Lost and Found" on his collaborative mixtape <i>Martyrs in the Making</i> with Bumps INF.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Bizzle stated that Boi-1da worked free of charge on the project.<sup id="cite_ref-Bizzle,_Christian_Rapper:_God_Provided_After_I_Quit_Chasing_Money._7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bizzle,_Christian_Rapper:_God_Provided_After_I_Quit_Chasing_Money.-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The collaborative work between the two was highlighted in the 2012 September edition of <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bizzle received nominations for <i>Rap/Hip Hop Gospel CD of The Year</i> at both the 2012<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> and 2014 <a href="/wiki/Stellar_Awards" title="Stellar Awards">Stellar Awards</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> sharing the category with artists <a href="/wiki/Derek_Minor" title="Derek Minor">Derek Minor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lecrae" title="Lecrae">Lecrae</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Thi%27sl" title="Thi&#39;sl">Thi'sl</a>. He also received three nominations at the 2012<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> and 2013 <i>Kingdom Choice Awards</i>. Although he did not win any Kingdom Choice Awards in 2012, he won in 2013 for Rap Single of The Year, Hip Hop Album of The Year, and Music Video of The Year.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id=".22Same_Love.22_controversy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="&quot;Same_Love&quot;_controversy">"Same Love" controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: &quot;Same Love&quot; controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 28, 2014, Bizzle released a single entitled "Same Love (A Response)"<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> in reaction to the pro-gay rights single "<a href="/wiki/Same_Love" title="Same Love">Same Love</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Macklemore" title="Macklemore">Macklemore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Lewis" title="Ryan Lewis">Ryan Lewis</a>. It opens with the rapper reciting a quotation from the <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Timothy" class="mw-redirect" title="Book of Timothy">Book of Timothy</a>, then leads into the same instrumental track as Macklemore's original.
<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The song is critical of the media in its promotion of homosexuality, stating that it is hypocritical to call for tolerance for same-sex relationships while at the same time branding opponents of homosexual behavior as "hateful".<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The single takes issue with the analogy of gay rights with the African-American civil rights movement.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> It also encourages Christians who struggle with homosexual attractions to "fight the good fight".<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Three days after its release, the song garnered over 30,000 views on YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>  <i><a href="/wiki/The_Huffington_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="The Huffington Post">The Huffington Post</a></i> called the track "very disturbing".<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Advocate_(LGBT_magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Advocate (LGBT magazine)">The Advocate</a></i>, a newspaper that represents the LGBTQ+ community, said Bizzle's single "uses the instrumental hook from Macklemore's 'Same Love' to say that LGBT people are sinful, violent, and just like pedophiles."<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Bizzle has reported that he has received death threats because of the song. He also created a website that chronicles the backlash against him<sup id="cite_ref-Galvan_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Galvan-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> and maintained that he was not homophobic but simply stating an opposing argument to that in popular media.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bizzle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bizzle became a Christian in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After 18 years of recording in Los Angeles,<sup id="cite_ref-Biography_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Bizzle relocated to <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, Texas. As of 2014, he resided with his wife and two children in Houston.<sup id="cite_ref-Galvan_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Galvan-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
