<p><b>Rod Sepand</b> (born June 30, 1968), better known professionally as <b>King Tech</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/American_people" class="mw-redirect" title="American people">American</a> <a href="/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJ</a>, <a href="/wiki/B-boy" class="mw-redirect" title="B-boy">B-boy</a>, VJ and audio engineer.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is one half of the duo <a href="/wiki/Sway_%26_King_Tech" title="Sway &amp; King Tech">Sway &amp; King Tech</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=King_Tech&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>DJ King Tech started off in the Hip Hop game as a B-Boy in the bay area. He initially joined the musical crews “Master City Breakers” and “Flynamic Force” and performed with them. By the time he met <a href="/wiki/Sway_Calloway" title="Sway Calloway">Sway Calloway</a>, b-boy crews were fading fast in the face of Hip Hop culture.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> With King Tech on the 1’s and 2’s and Sway on the mic the duo represented Flynamic Force on their debut single, “We Wanna Rock You.” It sold 20000 singles in the bay area. The second single “Follow For Now” sold over 40,000 singles.
</p><p>The duo were well known on the local scene<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and rather than joining any record, they started their own business venture, All City Records. This allowed Tech and Sway to independently release their music albums. The success of their indie 12" “Follow 4 Now/Time 4 Peace” resulted in a major label deal with <a href="/wiki/Giant_Records_(independent)" title="Giant Records (independent)">Giant Records</a> and their subsequent LP "Concrete Jungle".
</p><p>Upon entering and winning a local competition, the duo were given a one-shot deal for a 40-minute mix on local radio station <a href="/wiki/KMEL" title="KMEL">106.1 KMEL</a>. Radio execs realized that the show can be a success with DJ King Tech on the mix and Sway as the front man. The show was named The Wake Up Show, featured music and interviews with well-known hip hop artists as well as up-and-coming ones.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The show became very popular and later became NYC’s premiere Hip Hop outlet which aided <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> to get his start at a very early stage.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wakeup_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wakeup-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> King Tech is also noted for helping out <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jay_Z" class="mw-redirect" title="Jay Z">Jay Z</a>’s career in their early stages.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The duo from The Bay Area were changing the face of not just urban radio but popular music in general and the way that it was played and appreciated on regular FM dials across the planet. King Tech was named one of the 50 Most Influential DJ’s of all time by BET.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The popularity of the show helped duo in getting another major label record deal with  Interscope Records. The album, This or That, reached #30 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a>'s R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums chart and #1 on the Top Heatseekers chart in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, King Tech is one of the four main judges on the reality show <i>One Shot</i>. The show will be aired on <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a> in August 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In 2017 Tech, <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Smith_(record_producer)" title="Mike Smith (record producer)">Mike Smith</a> relaunched Razor Sharp Records.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
