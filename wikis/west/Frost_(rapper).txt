<p><b>Arturo R. Molina Jr.</b> (born May 31, 1964), better known as <b>Frost</b> (originally <b>Kid Frost</b>), is an American rapper, songwriter and record producer from Los Angeles. He charted in the 1990s with his first four albums: <i><a href="/wiki/Hispanic_Causing_Panic" title="Hispanic Causing Panic">Hispanic Causing Panic</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/East_Side_Story_(Kid_Frost_album)" title="East Side Story (Kid Frost album)">East Side Story</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Smile_Now,_Die_Later" title="Smile Now, Die Later">Smile Now, Die Later</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/When_Hell.A._Freezes_Over" title="When Hell.A. Freezes Over">When Hell.A. Freezes Over</a></i>. His most successful single is "<a href="/wiki/La_Raza_(song)" title="La Raza (song)">La Raza</a>" which hit number 6 on the rap songs chart in August 1990. Frost was an integral part of the Latin Alliance, releasing one album, 1991's <i><a href="/wiki/Latin_Alliance_(album)" title="Latin Alliance (album)">Latin Alliance</a></i>. 
</p><p>Frost's music entered the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> again in 2002 with the album <i><a href="/wiki/Still_Up_in_This_Shit!" title="Still Up in This Shit!">Still Up in This Shit!</a></i>. Frost is the father of record producer <a href="/wiki/Scoop_DeVille" title="Scoop DeVille">Scoop DeVille</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frost_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Molina was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>, and occasionally lived with his family in military bases in <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is of <a href="/wiki/Mexicans" title="Mexicans">Mexican</a> descent. He began his music career in 1982 as Kid Frost as a tribute to his rival <a href="/wiki/Ice-T" title="Ice-T">Ice-T</a>, whom he often battled in the music industry. In an interview Frost stated that his first actual DJ was in fact <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Yella" title="DJ Yella">DJ Yella</a>. He soon became a <a href="/wiki/Breakdancing" title="Breakdancing">breakdancer</a> for <a href="/wiki/Uncle_Jamms_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Uncle Jamms Army">Uncle Jamms Army</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frost_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the mid-1980s, Frost released several pre-gangsta 12" singles on Los Angeles-based labels Electrobeat and Baja.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the late 1980s, Kid Frost moved to <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Records" title="Virgin Records">Virgin Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Virgin released his biggest hit, "<a href="/wiki/La_Raza_(song)" title="La Raza (song)">La Raza</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Hispanic_Causing_Panic" title="Hispanic Causing Panic">Hispanic Causing Panic</a></i> was released in 1990. He also established a Latin rap <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> called Latin Alliance,<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> which released their only album, <i><a href="/wiki/Latin_Alliance_(album)" title="Latin Alliance (album)">Latin Alliance</a></i>, in 1991. His second album, <i><a href="/wiki/East_Side_Story_(Kid_Frost_album)" title="East Side Story (Kid Frost album)">East Side Story</a></i> was released in 1992,<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> which featured appearances from <a href="/wiki/MC_Eiht" title="MC Eiht">MC Eiht</a>, <a href="/wiki/A.L.T." title="A.L.T.">A.L.T.</a> and Ganxta Ridd from the <a href="/wiki/Boo-Yaa_T.R.I.B.E." title="Boo-Yaa T.R.I.B.E.">Boo-Yaa T.R.I.B.E.</a>
</p><p>In 1995, Frost dropped the "Kid" from his nickname and signed with <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records" title="Ruthless Records">Ruthless Records</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a>'s label (distributed by <a href="/wiki/Relativity_Records" title="Relativity Records">Relativity</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Smile_Now,_Die_Later" title="Smile Now, Die Later">Smile Now, Die Later</a></i> was released that year.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Above_the_Law_(group)" title="Above the Law (group)">Above The Law</a> were featured as guest rappers, alongside <a href="/wiki/A.L.T." title="A.L.T.">A.L.T.</a>, O.G.Enius and <a href="/wiki/Kokane" title="Kokane">Kokane</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rick_James" title="Rick James">Rick James</a> also appeared on Frost's version of "Mary Jane". His second album for Ruthless, <i><a href="/wiki/When_Hell.A._Freezes_Over" title="When Hell.A. Freezes Over">When Hell.A.Freezes Over</a></i>, was released in 1997. <a href="/wiki/Ice-T" title="Ice-T">Ice-T</a>, Scoop, O.G.Enius and <a href="/wiki/Domino_(rapper)" title="Domino (rapper)">Domino</a> also appeared as guest rappers. In 1998, Frost collaborated with <a href="/wiki/South_Park_Mexican" title="South Park Mexican">South Park Mexican</a> in "<i>El Jugador"</i> music video along with Low-G released by <a href="/wiki/Dope_House_Records" title="Dope House Records">Dope House Records</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Power_Moves:_The_Table" title="Power Moves: The Table">Power Moves: The Table</a> album. Frost was also featured in the songs: Cali-Tex Connect, and West Coast, Gulf Coast, East Coast also from the <a href="/wiki/Power_Moves:_The_Table" title="Power Moves: The Table">Power Moves: The Table</a> album <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1999, Frost moved to a small <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent label</a> called Celeb Entertainment Inc. His first album for Celeb Entertainment titled <i>This Was Then This Is Now Vol. I</i> was released in 1999. <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a>, <a href="/wiki/King_T" title="King T">King T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baby_Bash" title="Baby Bash">Baby Bash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Tee" title="Jay Tee">Jay Tee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jayo_Felony" title="Jayo Felony">Jayo Felony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a>, <a href="/wiki/B-Legit" title="B-Legit">B-Legit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cameo_(band)" title="Cameo (band)">Cameo</a> were featured on the CD. <i>That Was Then This Is Now Vol. II</i> was released in 2000. Frank V., Clika One, Jay Tee, Baby Bash and other guest rappers were also featured on the CD.
</p><p>2002's <i><a href="/wiki/Still_Up_in_This_Shit!" title="Still Up in This Shit!">Still Up In This Shit!</a></i>, released by Hit-A-Lick and <a href="/wiki/Koch_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Koch Records">Koch Records</a>, featured more Latin rap style and <a href="/wiki/G-funk" title="G-funk">g-funk</a> tracks as well as a hidden bonus <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> track titled "Cannabis". <a href="/wiki/Mellow_Man_Ace" title="Mellow Man Ace">Mellow Man Ace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daz_Dillinger" title="Daz Dillinger">Daz Dillinger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baby_Bash" title="Baby Bash">Baby Bash</a>, <a href="/wiki/A.L.T." title="A.L.T.">A.L.T.</a>, Nino Brown, <a href="/wiki/Don_Cisco" title="Don Cisco">Don Cisco</a> and other guest rappers appeared, and one track featured the group <a href="/wiki/Tierra_(band)" title="Tierra (band)">Tierra</a>.
</p><p>In 2004 <i>Welcome to Frost Angeles</i> was released on <a href="/wiki/Thump_Records" title="Thump Records">Thump Records</a>, which was produced almost entirely by Frost and his son Scoop DeVille. Only the Intro is produced by Binky Womack, and Philly Blunt co-produced one track. Guest rappers included Cameosis, Genovese and Jay Tee. Frost again signed to Low Profile Records and released his album <i>Till The Wheels Fall Off</i> in 2006. It had various guest appearances which included Baby Bash, Scoop DeVille and Mr. Sancho.
</p><p>Frost also performed music for films including "Bite the Bullet (Theme from Gunmen)" in the 1993 film <i>Gunmen</i> and "Tears Of A Mother" in the film <i>No Mothers Crying, No Babies Dying</i>, which featured Ice-T.
</p><p>Frost is also an accomplished actor appearing in several films, as well as doing voice roles for fictional characters such as <a href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Grand_Theft_Auto:_San_Andreas#T-Bone_Mendez" class="mw-redirect" title="List of characters in Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas">T-Bone Mendez</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto:_San_Andreas" title="Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas">Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas</a></i> and contributing his song "<a href="/wiki/La_Raza_(song)" title="La Raza (song)">La Raza</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was named vice president of the Music Division of Goldmark Industries on August 30, 2006. Frost also appeared in a cameo role in <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Vato_(song)" title="Vato (song)">Vato</a>" music video, as well as <a href="/wiki/B-Real" title="B-Real">B-Real</a> that same year.
</p><p>In 2016, Frost announced he has been diagnosed with cancer.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
