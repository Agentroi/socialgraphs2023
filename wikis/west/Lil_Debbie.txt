<p><b>Jordan Mary Capozzi</b> (born February 2, 1990), better known by her stage name <b>Lil Debbie</b>, is an American rapper, model and fashion designer.<sup id="cite_ref-iHeartComix_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iHeartComix-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Debbie started her career in 2011 as part of the now-defunct group <a href="/wiki/White_Girl_Mob" title="White Girl Mob">White Girl Mob</a>, with rappers <a href="/wiki/Kreayshawn" title="Kreayshawn">Kreayshawn</a> and <a href="/wiki/V-Nasty" title="V-Nasty">V-Nasty</a>. In 2012, Debbie started a solo career, releasing a series of singles with rappers <a href="/wiki/Riff_Raff_(rapper)" title="Riff Raff (rapper)">Riff Raff</a> and former White Girl Mob group member V-Nasty, some of which ended up on her first solo project, a mixtape titled <i>Keep It Lit</i>. In October 2013, Debbie released her first EP, titled <i>Queen D</i>. In March 2014, Debbie released her second EP, <i>California Sweetheart</i>, followed by <i>California Sweetheart Pt. 2</i> later in the same year. In November 2014, Debbie released her second mixtape, <i>Young B!tch</i>.  Debbie also DJs under the moniker 'CAPOZZI'.<sup id="cite_ref-Twitter_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Twitter-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Debbie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lil Debbie was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Albany,_California" title="Albany, California">Albany, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-karmaloop_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-karmaloop-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  She is of <a href="/wiki/Italian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Italian people">Italian</a> descent and has dual Italian and American citizenship.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After graduating high school, she went on to study fashion design at <a href="/wiki/FIDM" class="mw-redirect" title="FIDM">FIDM</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dffd_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dffd-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Later, she dropped out due to "having too much homework". When she was 15 years old, Debbie met Kreayshawn and V-Nasty, she was an only child and spent most of her time with the two, and considered them as her 'sisters'.<sup id="cite_ref-karmaloop_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-karmaloop-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The three later went on to create the group <a href="/wiki/White_Girl_Mob" title="White Girl Mob">White Girl Mob</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Debbie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, Lil Debbie appeared in the music video for <a href="/wiki/Kreayshawn" title="Kreayshawn">Kreayshawn's</a> debut single "<a href="/wiki/Gucci_Gucci" title="Gucci Gucci">Gucci Gucci</a>". In September 2011, it was reported that Debbie had been kicked out of the White Girl Mob, due to being disloyal to the other members of the group.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Debbie stated poor communication and misunderstandings in the group were the main reasons for her departure. Debbie later stated she didn't have much input in the group and she was just following the others' decisions.<sup id="cite_ref-karmaloop_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-karmaloop-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Debbie has also stated that she has no relationship with the members of the group as of 2014.
</p><p>In 2012, Debbie collaborated with Houston rapper <a href="/wiki/Riff_Raff_(rapper)" title="Riff Raff (rapper)">RiFF RaFF</a> on a number of songs together, including "Squirt", "Brain Freeze", "Michelle Obama" and "Suckas Askin' Questions". Debbie's videos for "Squirt", "2 Cups", and "Gotta Ball" became popular, each gaining over 2,000,000 views on YouTube, and the success of the songs helped to catapult her career.<sup id="cite_ref-iHeartComix_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iHeartComix-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dffd_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dffd-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Some of the singles released, including  "Squirt", "Brain Freeze", and "2 Cups", made it on to Debbie's first mixtape, <i>Keep It Lit</i>, released in July 2012. In February 2013, the single, "I Do It" featuring K00LJOHN was released.
</p><p>On October 22, 2013, Debbie released her first EP, titled <i>Queen D</i>, which includes three original songs and two remixes.<sup id="cite_ref-dffd_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dffd-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-queend_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-queend-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> A second EP, titled <i>California Sweetheart</i>, was set for release in the summer of 2013, but was delayed and eventually released on March 25, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-bill_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bill-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On August 5 of the same year, Debbie released her third EP, a follow-up to <i>California Sweetheart</i>, titled <i>California Sweetheart Pt. 2</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-apple.com_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apple.com-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> This was followed by the rapper's second mixtape <i>Young B!tch</i>, released on November 24, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-apple.com1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apple.com1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 10, 2015, Debbie's single "Lofty" was released on iTunes.<sup id="cite_ref-apple.com2_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apple.com2-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, Debbie debuted her line of baked goods titled "Cakes" at the Blazer's Cup in 2015.
</p><p>On March 3, 2017, Debbie released her fourth EP, titled <i>XXIII</i>, including five originals. <a href="/wiki/Cesqeaux" title="Cesqeaux">Cesqeaux</a>, Moksi, The Galaxy, Yung Felix, <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Claw_(DJs)" title="Yellow Claw (DJs)">Yellow Claw</a>, and FS Green are all featured on the EP.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
