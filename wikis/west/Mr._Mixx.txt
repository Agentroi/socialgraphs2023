					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American DJ, producer, and co-founder of rap group 2 Live Crew</div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; font-size: 125%;">Mr. Mixx</th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">David P. Hobbs</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday">1963-09-23</span>) </span>September 23, 1963<span class="noprint ForceAgeToShow"> (age&#160;59)</span></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Origin</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Santa_Ana,_California" title="Santa Ana, California">Santa Ana, California</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Miami_bass" title="Miami bass">Miami bass</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role"><a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Music_producer" class="mw-redirect" title="Music producer">music producer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vocalist" class="mw-redirect" title="Vocalist">vocalist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turntablism" title="Turntablism">turntablist</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Instrument(s)</th><td class="infobox-data note"><a href="/wiki/Turntables" class="mw-redirect" title="Turntables">Turntables</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keyboard_instrument" title="Keyboard instrument">keyboards</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">1983–present</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Luke_Skyywalker_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Luke Skyywalker Records">Luke Skyywalker Records</a><br />Lil’ Joe Records<br />MIXX INTL</td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>David P. Hobbs</b> (born September 23, 1963), also known by his stage name  <b>Mr. Mixx</b>, is a co-founder of the controversial <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">rap</a> group <a href="/wiki/2_Live_Crew" title="2 Live Crew">2 Live Crew</a>, along with being a <a href="/wiki/Turntablism" title="Turntablism">scratch DJ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">music producer</a> of the group. In 1986, the group released the single "Throw The 'D'"; it is now considered the blueprint of <a href="/wiki/Miami_bass" title="Miami bass">Miami bass</a>.
</p><p>Later in 1986, 2 Live Crew released their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_2_Live_Crew_Is_What_We_Are" title="The 2 Live Crew Is What We Are">The 2 Live Crew Is What We Are</a>.</i> The album established the group's signature style of comical sexually explicit lyrics. After a slew of successful releases, the group met with considerable controversy as a <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_Southern_District_of_Florida" title="United States District Court for the Southern District of Florida">U.S. district court</a> ruled the album legally obscene. They were prosecuted but acquitted.
</p><p>Hobbs produced popular 2 Live Crew singles such as "Throw the D", "<a href="/wiki/Me_So_Horny" title="Me So Horny">Me So Horny</a>", "Hoochie Mama", and other titles in the group's Miami bass musical catalog. Currently, Hobbs continues to DJ and produce other artists
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Mixx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hobbs was born on September 23, 1963, and raised in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Ana,_California" title="Santa Ana, California">Santa Ana, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1981, Hobbs graduated from <a href="/wiki/Corona_High_School" title="Corona High School">Corona High School</a> in nearby <a href="/wiki/Corona,_California" title="Corona, California">Corona, California</a>, and enlisted in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> shortly afterward.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Mixx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1984:_Military_service_and_founding_of_2_Live_Crew">1984:  Military service and founding of 2 Live Crew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Mixx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1984: Military service and founding of 2 Live Crew">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1984, while stationed at <a href="/wiki/March_Air_Force_Base" class="mw-redirect" title="March Air Force Base">March Air Force Base</a> near <a href="/wiki/Riverside,_California" title="Riverside, California">Riverside, California</a>, Hobbs met fellow musicians and <a href="/wiki/Airman#United_States_Air_Force" title="Airman">United States Air Force Airmen</a> Amazing Vee (Yuri Vielot) and <a href="/wiki/Fresh_Kid_Ice" title="Fresh Kid Ice">Fresh Kid Ice</a> (Christopher Wong Won).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The trio, with Hobbs as <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a> and Wong Won and Vielot as rappers, went on to form <a href="/wiki/2_Live_Crew" title="2 Live Crew">2 Live Crew</a> and would perform in small venues near the base, eventually releasing the group's first singles "The Revelation" and "2 Live Beat Box", on their own label <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent label</a> Fresh Beat Records, that same year.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"The Revelation" and "2 Live Beat Box" became popular in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, so much so that, following their discharge from the United States Air Force, Hobbs and Wong Won eventually relocated to <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a> at the behest of local <a href="/wiki/Tour_promoter" title="Tour promoter">concert promoter</a> <a href="/wiki/Luther_Campbell" class="mw-redirect" title="Luther Campbell">Luther Campbell</a>, who helped cultivate the momentum the group was establishing in the area. Hobbs DJing skills and prowess on the <a href="/wiki/Roland_TR-808" title="Roland TR-808">Roland 808 drum machine</a> were instrumental in the group's overall growth.
</p>
<h3><span id="1985.E2.80.931986:_Miami_Bass_DJ_and_2_Live_Crew_success"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1985–1986:_Miami_Bass_DJ_and_2_Live_Crew_success">1985–1986:  Miami Bass DJ and 2 Live Crew success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Mixx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1985–1986: Miami Bass DJ and 2 Live Crew success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1985, <a href="/wiki/2_Live_Crew" title="2 Live Crew">2 Live Crew</a> released their follow up single, "What I Like" on Fresh Beat Records.  That same year, the group entered into a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Luther_Campbell" class="mw-redirect" title="Luther Campbell">Campbell</a> and formed <a href="/wiki/Luke_Records" title="Luke Records">Luke Skyywalker Records</a>. Shortly after forming the record label, Campbell joined 2 Live Crew as a <a href="/wiki/Hype_man" title="Hype man">hype man</a>.  In April, 1986, rapper <a href="/wiki/Brother_Marquis" title="Brother Marquis">Brother Marquis</a> (Mark D. Ross) joined 2 Live Crew, replacing Vielot, who left the group shortly after its relocation to Miami .
</p><p>In January 1987, 2 Live Crew released the <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> "Throw the D" with "Ghetto Bass" on the B-side, with Hobbs as <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a> on both tracks."Throw the D" became an influential  blueprint as to how future <a href="/wiki/Miami_bass" title="Miami bass">Miami bass</a> songs were written and produced.<sup id="cite_ref-Bein2014_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bein2014-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Hobbs' performances on these releases made him the first <a href="/wiki/Miami_Bass" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami Bass">Miami Bass</a> scratch DJ.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The reconfigured group, featuring their most well known lineup (Hobbs, <a href="/wiki/Luther_Campbell" class="mw-redirect" title="Luther Campbell">Campbell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fresh_Kid_Ice" title="Fresh Kid Ice">Wong Won</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brother_Marquis" title="Brother Marquis">Ross</a>) became popular locally and nationally with the release of their <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_certification" title="Music recording certification">Gold-certified</a> debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_2_Live_Crew_Is_What_We_Are" title="The 2 Live Crew Is What We Are">The 2 Live Crew Is What We Are</a></i> on January 25, 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  Notorious for sexually explicit lyrics, the album made Hobbs and his bandmates rap superstars.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="1988.E2.80.931991:_Continued_2_Live_Crew_success_and_attendant_controversy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1988–1991:_Continued_2_Live_Crew_success_and_attendant_controversy">1988–1991:  Continued 2 Live Crew success and attendant controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Mixx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1988–1991: Continued 2 Live Crew success and attendant controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2_Live_Crew" title="2 Live Crew">2 Live Crew</a></div>
<p>In 1988, 2 Live Crew released their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Move_Somethin%27_(album)" title="Move Somethin&#39; (album)">Move Somethin'</a></i> It was also certified Gold and featured the singles "Move Somethin'" and "Do Wah Diddy Diddy".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The album improved on the charts from the previous album, making in to #68 on the <i>Billboard 200</i> and #20 on the <i>Top R&amp;B/Hip Hop Albums</i> charts.  A separate ”clean” version of the album, suitable for radio play, was released in addition to the explicit version.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/2_Live_Crew" title="2 Live Crew">2 Live Crew</a>’s third album <i><a href="/wiki/As_Nasty_As_They_Wanna_Be" class="mw-redirect" title="As Nasty As They Wanna Be">As Nasty As They Wanna Be</a></i> (1989), became the group’s most successful commercial studio album and was certified <a href="/wiki/Double_Platinum" class="mw-redirect" title="Double Platinum">double Platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a>. The album’s lead single "<a href="/wiki/Me_So_Horny" title="Me So Horny">Me So Horny</a>" peaked at 26 of the <i>Billboard Top 100</i> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  A clean version of the album, <i><a href="/wiki/As_Clean_As_They_Wanna_Be" class="mw-redirect" title="As Clean As They Wanna Be">As Clean As They Wanna Be</a></i> was released concurrently with the explicit version.
</p><p>In 1990, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_Southern_District_of_Florida" title="United States District Court for the Southern District of Florida">United States District Court for the Southern District of Florida</a> ruled  <i>As Nasty As They Wanna Be</i> to be legally <a href="/wiki/Obscene" class="mw-redirect" title="Obscene">obscene</a>, becoming the first album in history to be so declared by a <a href="/wiki/Federal_judiciary_of_the_United_States" title="Federal judiciary of the United States">federal court</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> this ruling was later overturned by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Court_of_Appeals_for_the_Eleventh_Circuit" title="United States Court of Appeals for the Eleventh Circuit">U.S. Court of Appeals for the Eleventh Circuit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> An obscenity trial followed, where all of the defendants, including Hobbs, were eventually acquitted of all charges.
</p><p>In late 1990, following the verdict in the obscenity trial, 2 Live Crew released their fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Banned_in_the_U.S.A." title="Banned in the U.S.A.">Banned in the U.S.A.</a></i>. The album included the hits "<a href="/wiki/Do_the_Bart" title="Do the Bart">Do the Bart</a>" and the title track which peaked at 20 on the <i>Billboard Top 100</i> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The eponymous title single referred to the earlier federal court obscenity ruling regarding the group's previous album <i><a href="/wiki/As_Nasty_As_They_Wanna_Be" class="mw-redirect" title="As Nasty As They Wanna Be">As Nasty As They Wanna Be</a>.</i>  <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a> granted the group permission to <a href="/wiki/Interpolation_(popular_music)" title="Interpolation (popular music)">interpolate</a> his song "<a href="/wiki/Born_in_the_U.S.A._(song)" title="Born in the U.S.A. (song)">Born in the U.S.A.</a>" for the single. <i>Banned in the U.S.A.</i> was also the first album to bear the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>-standard <a href="/wiki/Parental_Advisory" title="Parental Advisory">Parental Advisory</a> warning sticker.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Live_in_Concert_(2_Live_Crew_album)" title="Live in Concert (2 Live Crew album)">Live in Concert</a>,</i> 2 Live Crew's fifth album and only <a href="/wiki/Album#Live" title="Album">live album</a>, was also released in 1990. The album peaked at number 46 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a></i> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Sports_Weekend:_As_Nasty_as_They_Wanna_Be,_Pt._2" class="mw-redirect" title="Sports Weekend: As Nasty as They Wanna Be, Pt. 2">Sports Weekend: As Nasty as They Wanna Be, Pt. 2</a>,</i> featuring the single "<a href="/wiki/Pop_That_Coochie" title="Pop That Coochie">Pop That Coochie</a>," which reached 58 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_100" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot 100">Hot 100</a></i> chart, was released by 2 Live Crew in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The group's sixth album was a sequel of sorts to <i><a href="/wiki/As_Nasty_As_They_Wanna_Be" class="mw-redirect" title="As Nasty As They Wanna Be">As Nasty As They Wanna Be</a>,</i> and was also accompanied by a clean version, <i>Sports Weekend: As Clean As They Wanna Be, Pt. 2.</i>
</p><p><i>Sports Weekend: As Nasty as They Wanna Be, Pt. 2</i> would be the last studio album to include all of the most well known members of the group, Hobbs, <a href="/wiki/Fresh_Kid_Ice" title="Fresh Kid Ice">Wong Won</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brother_Marquis" title="Brother Marquis">Ross</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Luther_Campbell" class="mw-redirect" title="Luther Campbell">Campbell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="1993.E2.80.931999:_Final_albums_with_the_most_well_known_2_Live_crew_line_up.2C_solo_projects_and_2_Live_Crew_reunion"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1993–1999:_Final_albums_with_the_most_well_known_2_Live_crew_line_up,_solo_projects_and_2_Live_Crew_reunion">1993–1999:  Final albums with the most well known 2 Live crew line up, solo projects and 2 Live Crew reunion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Mixx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1993–1999: Final albums with the most well known 2 Live crew line up, solo projects and 2 Live Crew reunion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1994, Hobbs formed a group, Mr. Mixx and Da Roughneck Posse, which released the studio album <i>One Monkey Don't Stop No Show</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> That same year, with a group called Bass Poets, Hobbs released the album <i>Bass-Boom-Booty</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1995, Hobbs, <a href="/wiki/Fresh_Kid_Ice" title="Fresh Kid Ice">Wong Won</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brother_Marquis" title="Brother Marquis">Ross</a> reunited to release the single "Hoochie Mama" for the soundtrack of the popular 1995 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_(1995_film)" title="Friday (1995 film)">Friday</a></i>. The soundtrack reached No. 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, where it held its position for two weeks, and remained on the <i><a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a></i> chart for six weeks.
</p><p>Also in 1995, Hobbs teamed up with producer Billy Vidal to release several <a href="/wiki/Miami_Bass" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami Bass">Miami Bass</a> compilations from 1995 through 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1996, Hobbs again reunited with Wong Won and Ross as the 2 Live Crew, to perform, produce and release the group's seventh studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Shake_a_Lil%27_Somethin%27" title="Shake a Lil&#39; Somethin&#39;">Shake a Lil' Somethin'</a></i> whose hit song of the same name, peaked at #72 on the <i>Top 100</i> chart and #11 on the <i>Hot Rap Songs|Hot Rap Singles</i> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Two of the album's singles charted:  "Do the Damn Thing", which made it to #24 on the <i>Hot Rap Singles</i> chart, and "Be My Private Dancer", which peaked at #34.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> <i>Shake A Lil' Somethin'</i> would be the last 2 Live Crew album to feature Hobbs.
</p><p>In 1998, Hobbs released his first <a href="/wiki/Solo_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Solo album">solo album</a> <i>The Sex Files</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000-present:_Solo_albums_and_current_projects">2000-present: Solo albums and current projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Mixx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2000-present: Solo albums and current projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2002, Hobbs released his second solo <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio album">studio album</a> <i>Nasty Controversial &amp; Unauthorized Part 1</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, Hobbs released his third solo album <i>Vgnl Minded</i> on his own <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent label</a>, Mr. Mixx Recordings.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 2008 to 2011, Hobbs created and ran his own <a href="/wiki/Website" title="Website">website</a>, <i>Collegepeepshowtv.com</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Hobbs released his fourth solo album <i>The Money And The Booty </i>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Hobbs briefly reunited with former bandmates <a href="/wiki/Fresh_Kid_Ice" title="Fresh Kid Ice">Wong Won</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brother_Marquis" title="Brother Marquis">Ross</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Luther_Campbell" class="mw-redirect" title="Luther Campbell">Campbell</a> as 2 Live Crew, and were honored at the 2010 <i><a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_Honors#7th_VH1_Hip_Hop_Honors_(2010)" title="Hip Hop Honors">VH1 Hip-Hop Honors: The Dirty South Edition</a></i> awards show where they also performed.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, Hobbs reunited with Ross to reform 2 Live Crew shortly before <a href="/wiki/Fresh_Kid_Ice#Death" title="Fresh Kid Ice">Wong Won's death in 2017</a>.  The duo subsequently released two singles <i>How Bout Dem Cowboys</i> (2016) and <i>One Horse Sleigh</i> (2016), and continue to tour nationally as 2 Live Crew.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hobbs also currently tours nationally with <a href="/wiki/Afroman" title="Afroman">Afroman</a> as his tour DJ, and continues to work with and produce other hip hop artists.
</p>
