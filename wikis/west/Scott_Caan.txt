<p><b>Scott Andrew Caan</b> (born August 23, 1976)<sup id="cite_ref-UPI_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UPI-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is an American actor, director, photographer, writer, and former rapper. He received his breakthrough role in <i><a href="/wiki/Ocean%E2%80%99s_Eleven" class="mw-redirect" title="Ocean’s Eleven">Ocean’s Eleven</a></i> as Turk Malloy who he played in the <i><a href="/wiki/Ocean%27s_(film_series)" title="Ocean&#39;s (film series)">Ocean's trilogy</a></i> and starred as Detective <a href="/wiki/Danny_%22Danno%22_Williams" title="Danny &quot;Danno&quot; Williams">Danny "Danno" Williams</a> in the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Five-0_(2010_TV_series)" title="Hawaii Five-0 (2010 TV series)">Hawaii Five-0</a></i> (2010–2020), for which he was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a>. Caan had a recurring role as manager Scott Lavin in the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Entourage_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Entourage (U.S. TV series)">Entourage</a></i> (2009–2011). In the 1990s, he was a rapper and was a part of hip hop group <a href="/wiki/The_Whooliganz" title="The Whooliganz">The Whooliganz</a> with <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>, under the pseudonym <b>Mad Skillz</b>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Caan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Caan was born on August 23, 1976, in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, the son of actor <a href="/wiki/James_Caan" title="James Caan">James Caan</a> and Sheila Marie Ryan, an actress and former model.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His paternal grandparents were <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jewish</a> immigrants from Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-Caan_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Caan-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He has an older half-sister and three younger half-brothers.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Caan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Caan was a <a href="/wiki/Roadie" class="mw-redirect" title="Roadie">roadie</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> groups <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a> and <a href="/wiki/House_of_Pain" title="House of Pain">House of Pain</a>. Caan was also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Hip-hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip-hop">hip-hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/The_Whooliganz" title="The Whooliganz">The Whooliganz</a> as Mad Skillz (with <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a> and fellow <a href="/wiki/MC" class="mw-redirect" title="MC">MC</a> <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a> as Mudfoot).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The Alchemist and he, when performing as the rap duo the Whooliganz in the early 1990s, signed a record deal with Tommy Boy/Warner Bros. records. They recorded the album <i>Make Way for the W</i>, but after their first single "Put Your Handz Up",<sup id="cite_ref-VanityFair_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VanityFair-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> the album was shelved and the duo was dropped by Tommy Boy. In 1995, the Whooliganz' song "Whooliganz" was released as a single in the UK, but Caan and the Alchemist had already parted ways. Caan reunited with his former partner The Alchemist in 2014 on the Step Brothers project <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_Steppington" title="Lord Steppington">Lord Steppington</a></i>, performing on the song "Byron G" with musician <a href="/wiki/Evidence_(musician)" title="Evidence (musician)">Evidence</a>.
</p><p>After enrolling at the Playhouse West acting school in Los Angeles, Caan began acting in the late 1990s, appearing in a number of <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent films</a> and low-budget films. His first role in a major motion picture was that of Charlie Tweeder, a reckless <a href="/wiki/Philandering" class="mw-redirect" title="Philandering">philandering</a> <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> <a href="/wiki/High_school_football" title="High school football">high school football</a> <a href="/wiki/Wide_receiver" title="Wide receiver">wide receiver</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Teen_movie" class="mw-redirect" title="Teen movie">teen movie</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Varsity_Blues_(film)" title="Varsity Blues (film)">Varsity Blues</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/1999_in_film" title="1999 in film">1999</a>), alongside <a href="/wiki/James_Van_Der_Beek" title="James Van Der Beek">James Van Der Beek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Walker" title="Paul Walker">Paul Walker</a>.  In the same year, he played the role of Drew in the film <i>Saturn</i> (also known as <i>Speed of Life</i>). Caan subsequently appeared in several studio films, including <i><a href="/wiki/Ready_to_Rumble" title="Ready to Rumble">Ready to Rumble</a></i> (2000) co-starring <a href="/wiki/David_Arquette" title="David Arquette">David Arquette</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Boiler_Room_(film)" title="Boiler Room (film)">Boiler Room</a></i> (2000) co-starring <a href="/wiki/Vin_Diesel" title="Vin Diesel">Vin Diesel</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gone_in_60_Seconds_(2000_film)" title="Gone in 60 Seconds (2000 film)">Gone in 60 Seconds</a></i> (2000) as Tumbler, and <i><a href="/wiki/American_Outlaws_(2001_film)" title="American Outlaws (2001 film)">American Outlaws</a></i> (2001) co-starring <a href="/wiki/Colin_Farrell" title="Colin Farrell">Colin Farrell</a>, in which Caan played 19th-century outlaw <a href="/wiki/Cole_Younger" title="Cole Younger">Cole Younger</a>. In 2003, Caan made his directorial debut with the film <i><a href="/wiki/Dallas_362" title="Dallas 362">Dallas 362</a></i>, which won a prize at the 2003 Las Vegas Film Festival.
</p><p>Caan appeared in the feature film trilogy <i><a href="/wiki/Ocean%27s_Eleven" title="Ocean&#39;s Eleven">Ocean's Eleven</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ocean%27s_Twelve" title="Ocean&#39;s Twelve">Ocean's Twelve</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Ocean%27s_Thirteen" title="Ocean&#39;s Thirteen">Ocean's Thirteen</a></i>. In 2005, he co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Paul_Walker" title="Paul Walker">Paul Walker</a> (with whom he had appeared in <i>Varsity Blues</i>) in the action film <i><a href="/wiki/Into_the_Blue_(2005_film)" title="Into the Blue (2005 film)">Into the Blue</a></i>. Caan wrote and directed the 2006 comedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dog_Problem" title="The Dog Problem">The Dog Problem</a>,</i> and appeared as a supporting character in the film as well. He appeared on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Entourage_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Entourage (U.S. TV series)">Entourage</a></i> in a recurring role as talent manager Scott Lavin from seasons six to eight.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Caan played Detective <a href="/wiki/Danny_%22Danno%22_Williams" title="Danny &quot;Danno&quot; Williams">Danny "Danno" Williams</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Five-0_(2010_TV_series)" title="Hawaii Five-0 (2010 TV series)">Hawaii Five-0</a></i> (a re-imagining of the 1968 <i><a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Five-O_(1968_TV_series)" title="Hawaii Five-O (1968 TV series)">Hawaii Five-O</a></i> television series). The new series premiered on September 20, 2010, and in 2011 he was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor_%E2%80%93_Series,_Miniseries_or_Television_Film" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor – Series, Miniseries or Television Film">Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor – Series, Miniseries or Television Film</a> for his performance.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/BuddyTV" title="BuddyTV">BuddyTV</a> ranked him 95th on its list of "TV's Sexiest Men of 2011".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to acting, Caan has also pursued a career in photography.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Inspired and trained by cinematographer <a href="/wiki/Phil_Parmet" title="Phil Parmet">Phil Parmet</a> while working together on the 2003 film <i>Dallas 362</i>, Caan has been shooting ever since. "In preparing for the film," Caan said, "Phil inspired me to learn about lenses, lights, frames, and the operation of a 250 millimeter camera.  By the time the film was over, I wanted to shoot the next one."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, he published his first collection of photographs in a 256-page book titled <i>Scott Caan Photographs, Vol. 1</i>. The book was edited and designed by Howard Nourmand, and includes an introduction by Steve Olson.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Caan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Caan has a black belt in Brazilian jiu-jitsu.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In July 2014, Caan's girlfriend Kacy Byxbee gave birth to their daughter.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2012, Caan was an active volunteer with several organizations that introduce children with autism to surfing.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
