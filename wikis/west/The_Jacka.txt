<p><b>Shaheed Akbar</b> (August 12, 1977 – February 2, 2015; former name <b>Dominick Newton</b>), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>The Jacka</b>, was an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> from <a href="/wiki/Pittsburg,_California" title="Pittsburg, California">Pittsburg, California</a>.
The Jacka began his career as part of the rap group <a href="/wiki/Mob_Figaz" title="Mob Figaz">Mob Figaz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was killed in a shooting in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a>, on February 2, 2015. The murder is currently unsolved.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Jacka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Newton was born to 14-year-old parents and became the family breadwinner as a teenager, a role that landed him in prison for a year at age 18.
</p><p>Newton converted to Islam at a young age and changed his name to <i>Shaheed Akbar</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Lefebvre_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lefebvre-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Jacka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His career began with the Bay Area rap group <a href="/wiki/Mob_Figaz" title="Mob Figaz">Mob Figaz</a> after the teenaged Newton and several friends were approached by established rap artist C-Bo in a record store and went to a recording studio that day. The guest rappers appeared on "Ride Til We Die," the opening track on C-Bo's 1998 album <i>Til My Casket Drops.</i><sup id="cite_ref-Lefebvre_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lefebvre-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/C-Bo%27s_Mob_Figaz" title="C-Bo&#39;s Mob Figaz">C-Bo's Mob Figaz</a></i>, released in 1999 was a minor hit on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> Hip Hop chart entering in at #63, doing fairly well selling 160,000 units.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His first solo release <i>The Jacka of the Mob Figaz</i> in 2001 sold over 30,000 units. Jacka sold these units himself, in the Bay Area and shipping to one-stops across the country. Though great support came from the Bay Area, sixty percent of Jacka's sales came from outside of California.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2005, The Jacka released his second solo album <i>The Jack Artist</i> but failed to chart. In 2009, The Jacka came out with his third solo album <i>Tear Gas</i> making it #93 on the <i>Billboard</i> chart. The Jacka's #1 song in his 2009 album <i>Glamorous Lifestyle</i> was an instant classic in his hometown of Oakland and the Bay Area.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Jacka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On February 2, 2015, he was fatally shot by an unidentified gunman in Oakland on 94th Avenue and MacArthur Boulevard.<sup id="cite_ref-kpix022015_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kpix022015-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Prior to his death, he owned his own record label named The Artist Records.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
