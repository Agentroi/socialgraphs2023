<p><b>Terrance Scott</b> (born May 11, 1972),<sup id="cite_ref-Hip_Hop_in_America_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hip_Hop_in_America-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Cool Nutz</b>, is an American rapper, radio personality, entrepreneur, tour manager and record label owner.
He was born and raised in the <a href="/wiki/Woodlawn,_Portland,_Oregon" title="Woodlawn, Portland, Oregon">Woodlawn, Portland, Oregon</a> neighborhood. Growing up in Northeast Portland, he gained knowledge by the gangs and violence around him.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cool_Nutz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cool Nutz is most known for his involvement and promotion of the Portland, OR and <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">San Francisco Bay Area</a> hip-hop scene.<sup id="cite_ref-Spinner_Interview_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spinner_Interview-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He co-founded Jus Family Records in 1992 with Bosko Kante. His musical style originally used primarily <a href="/wiki/G-funk" title="G-funk">G-funk</a> synthesizer elements but has evolved to the addition of vocal and instrumental samples in his tracks.
</p><p>In 1995, Scott co-founded the Portland Oregon Hip-Hop (POH-Hop) Festival.<sup id="cite_ref-This_is_Portland_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-This_is_Portland-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He has worked very hard to create and maintain the hip-hop scene in Portland and has worked with many rappers from the bay area, most notably "<a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Yukmouth" title="Yukmouth">Yukmouth</a>." In 1997, he signed the group <a href="/w/index.php?title=G-Ism&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="G-Ism (page does not exist)">G-Ism</a>, made up of fellow Portland rappers Ray Ray and Young Randall to his label, Jus Family Records. They released their debut album, "<a href="/wiki/On_a_Mission_(G-Ism_featuring_Cool_Nutz_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="On a Mission (G-Ism featuring Cool Nutz album)">On a Mission</a>" in 1998, under Jus Family Records. <a href="/w/index.php?title=G-Ism&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="G-Ism (page does not exist)">G-Ism</a> disbanded in 2001 following the tragic and untimely passing of Young Randall.
</p><p>Cool Nutz is the radio host of the Breakout Show on <a href="/wiki/KXJM" title="KXJM">KXJM</a> JAM'N 107.5.<sup id="cite_ref-JAM&#39;N_107.5_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JAM&#39;N_107.5-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The show airs Sunday nights from 9 p.m. to midnight.
</p><p>Along with fellow Jus Family Records affiliate, Maniac Lok, Cool Nutz is featured on Track 11, "Pussy Loud," from the 2012 <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a> album <a href="/wiki/The_Block_Brochure:_Welcome_to_the_Soil_3" title="The Block Brochure: Welcome to the Soil 3">The Block Brochure: Welcome to the Soil 3</a>.
</p><p>Scott managed the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Group_Hug_Tour" class="mw-redirect" title="Group Hug Tour">Group Hug Tour</a> for <a href="/wiki/Kreayshawn" title="Kreayshawn">Kreayshawn</a> to promote her debut album, <a href="/wiki/Somethin%27_%27Bout_Kreay" title="Somethin&#39; &#39;Bout Kreay">Somethin' 'Bout Kreay</a>, released on <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Oregon_Live_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oregon_Live-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
