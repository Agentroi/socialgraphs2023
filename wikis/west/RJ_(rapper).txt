<p><b>Rodney Jerome Brown, Jr.</b> (born September 24, 1984), known professionally as <b>RJMrLA</b> (stylized as <b>RJmrLA</b>; or simply <b>RJ</b>) is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is known for his frequent collaborations with fellow <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast</a> artists <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mustard_(record_producer)" title="Mustard (record producer)">Mustard</a>, and was signed to their labels: 4Hunnid and 10 Summers Records respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His most celebrated releases to date are his <i>O.M.M.I.O.</i> mixtape trilogy.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RJ_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.93present:_O.M.M.I.O._series_and_collaborations"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–present:_O.M.M.I.O._series_and_collaborations">2013–present: <i>O.M.M.I.O.</i> series and collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RJ_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2013–present: O.M.M.I.O. series and collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, RJ released his debut mixtape <i>O.M.M.I.O</i> (which stands for <i>On My Mama I'm On</i>). The mixtape was hosted and largely produced by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Mustard" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Mustard">DJ Mustard</a>. The first song from this mixtape, "Ride Wit Me" was included and released, as the single. RJ then appeared on the songs; "Thank God (Interlude)" and "When I Was Gone" from <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>'s debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/My_Krazy_Life" title="My Krazy Life">My Krazy Life</a></i> (2014). In July 2014, RJ released his second mixtape <i>Takin Niggas Beats</i>, featuring local rappers; Shoota300, JaywhiteT, and <a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples" title="Vince Staples">Vince Staples</a> (on a contrary to his first mixtape). Unfortunately disagreements between them erupted, which led to RJ dropping their names from the project entirely. The beats on this mixtape were solely from made-up beats from the other rappers that were remixed by RJ. On the same year, DJ Mustard released his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/10_Summers" title="10 Summers">10 Summers</a></i>, where RJ appeared on the tracks; including "Low Low", "Throw Your Hood Up" and "No Reason".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>At the beginning of 2015, RJ collaborated with Choice to release a critically acclaimed mixtape, <i>Rich Off Mackin</i>. Additionally, RJ appeared on the DJ Mustard's mixtape, titled <i>Ketchup</i>, as well as one of YG's mixtapes <i>Just Re'd Up 2</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On May 26, 2015, RJ released a mixtape, <i>O.M.M.I.O. 2</i>, as the sequel to his debut mixtape <i>O.M.M.I.O</i>. The song "Get Rich" (with Choice featuring <a href="/wiki/Iamsu!" title="Iamsu!">Iamsu!</a>) was included and released as the lead single. Both of these singles; his 2013's single, "Ride Wit Me" and his 2015's single, "Get Rich", did not chart, both songs received radio airplay throughout the West Coast. He also contributed to the soundtrack for the <i><a href="/wiki/NBA_2K16" title="NBA 2K16">NBA 2K16</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;s</span> video game by being featured in the original song "You Don't Want It" with frequent collaborator and 10 Summers Records founder <a href="/wiki/DJ_Mustard" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Mustard">DJ Mustard</a>.
On May 12, 2016, he released <i>O.M.M.I.O 3</i> it has 19 tracks including a bonus track "Feda" that's only on SoundCloud. RJ also released TNB 2 (Takin Niggas Beats) on October 11, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On December 15, 2017, RJ &amp; DJ Mustard released a collaborative album called <i>The Ghetto</i>. 
</p><p>After a couple years with no projects released, RJ dropped what he said was his first real album called <i>On God</i> in 2019. The album had some notable features such as <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Thug" title="Young Thug">Young Thug</a>, <a href="/wiki/G-Eazy" title="G-Eazy">G-Eazy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>. RJ and the Seattle rapper Choice collaborated for the release of <i>Rich of Mackin 2</i> in April of 2020. In July that same year, RJ dropped another project titled <i>Let Me Talk My Shit</i>. In October 2022, RJ dropped another album called <i>Rodney Brown Jr.</i>, which featured guest appearances from prominent West Coast artists <a href="/wiki/Roddy_Ricch" title="Roddy Ricch">Roddy Ricch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>.
</p>
