<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glasses_Malone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Charles Penniman grew up in the <a href="/wiki/Watts,_Los_Angeles" title="Watts, Los Angeles">Watts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton</a> neighborhoods in Los Angeles County.<sup id="cite_ref-Empire_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Empire-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was a <a href="/wiki/Crip_gang" class="mw-redirect" title="Crip gang">Crip gang</a> member as a teenager, and got into street racing after breaking his gang membership and getting sober.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glasses_Malone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Traditionally associated with <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Wall_Street_Records" title="The Black Wall Street Records">The Black Wall Street Records</a> despite the fact that most of their artists were affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Bloods" title="Bloods">Bloods</a>, Malone signed with the record label, but later signed with <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money Records</a> in 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RapTalk_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RapTalk-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> turning down offers from many labels like <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam Records">Def Jam Records</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-RapTalk_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RapTalk-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>, <a href="/wiki/J_Records" title="J Records">J Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Glasses Malone was found by the same record executive who found <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>. Along with his signing, he was also given his own imprint, Blu Division.<sup id="cite_ref-RapTalk_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RapTalk-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Malone debuted with mixtapes <i>The Crack Mixtape</i> (2003) and <i>White Lightnin... Sticks</i> (2005).<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MTV_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i>White Lightnin... Sticks</i> sold 50,000 copies and included the popular street single "Two Hunned".<sup id="cite_ref-MTV_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Glasses Malone appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for <a href="/wiki/Tha_Dogg_Pound" title="Tha Dogg Pound">Tha Dogg Pound</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Cali_Iz_Active" title="Cali Iz Active">Cali Iz Active</a>" and also made a cameo appearance in the <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a> video for "Like Yeah". He also appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Madden_NFL_07#Soundtrack" title="Madden NFL 07">soundtrack to <i>Madden 2007</i></a> with a song called "Right Now" which was produced by <a href="/wiki/Scott_Storch" title="Scott Storch">Scott Storch</a>. On August 4, 2006 Glasses Malone appeared on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <i>You Hear It First</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Malone's first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Beach_Cruiser" title="Beach Cruiser">Beach Cruiser</a></i>, was originally scheduled to come out on February 20, 2007. However, Malone changed record labels, signing a new deal with <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hoo-Bangin%27_Records" title="Hoo-Bangin&#39; Records">Hoo-Bangin' Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The album's release was tentatively scheduled for April 2008. Two singles from the album debuted in 2008: "Certified" featuring Akon, and "Haterz" featuring Birdman and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>. His album will now be released on August 29, 2011, although it was leaked on the internet on August 24. In his album, Malone dedicated a song to American rapper <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, "Call Me T.I."
</p><p>In late 2009, Malone accompanied <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a> and hip-hop supergroup <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse_(group)" title="Slaughterhouse (group)">Slaughterhouse</a> on the K.O.D. Tour.
Malone was featured on former Cash Money R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/TQ_(singer)" title="TQ (singer)">TQ</a>'s 7th studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Kind_of_Blue_(TQ_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kind of Blue (TQ album)">Kind of Blue</a></i> in 2010. Glasses Malone is also found on a specific version of <a href="/wiki/Twiztid" title="Twiztid">Twiztid</a>'s 2012 album <i><a href="/wiki/Abominationz" title="Abominationz">Abominationz</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2014 Glasses Malone launched his own label, Division Movie Company. Glasses has also signed on with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Skee" title="DJ Skee">DJ Skee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dash_Radio" title="Dash Radio">Dash Radio</a> to develop radio content for his own station.
</p><p>On January 1, 2017 Glasses Malone was invited to participate in the <i>Juggalo March On Washington</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He accepted on January 2, 2017.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glasses_Malone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Glasses Malone is producing and starring in a film titled <i>The Division</i>. The film was written by <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_Mays" title="Malcolm Mays">Malcolm Mays</a> and is set to be directed by Max Albert in January 2011, according to The Boombox.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
Glasses Malone is also featured in season 1 Episode 2 of Netflix's series called The Fastest Car.
</p>
