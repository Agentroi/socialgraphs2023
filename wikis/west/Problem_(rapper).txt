<p><b>Jason L. Martin</b> (born May 8, 1987), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Problem</b>, is an American rapper, songwriter and record producer. He has released nineteen mixtapes in his career, the most recent being <i>S2</i>, released 2018. His debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Understand_Me" title="Understand Me">Understand Me</a></i>, was released on December 10, 2013. He is best known for his single "<a href="/wiki/Like_Whaaat" title="Like Whaaat">Like Whaaat</a>" and for featuring on <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Function_(song)" title="Function (song)">Function</a>".
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Problem_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Problem was born in <a href="/wiki/W%C3%BCrzburg" title="Würzburg">Würzburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a> and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He entered the industry by writing a verse for producer <a href="/wiki/Terrace_Martin" title="Terrace Martin">Terrace Martin</a>, for a song called "Be Thankful", featured on <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>'s compilation album <i>The Big Squeeze</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The track garnered praise from notable artist such as <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a> amongst others.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Problem began recording records with various <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast</a> artists such as <a href="/wiki/Dat_Nigga_Daz" class="mw-redirect" title="Dat Nigga Daz">Daz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a>, Snoop Dogg, and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a>. Problem's first big break was when Snoop Dogg heard a record which was also produced by Terrace Martin called "Neva Hafta To Wurry About That".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He went on to write three records on Snoop Dogg's <i><a href="/wiki/Ego_Trippin%27" title="Ego Trippin&#39;">Ego Trippin'</a></i> album.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Problem_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to his career as a solo artist, Martin served as a <a href="/wiki/Ghostwriter" title="Ghostwriter">ghostwriter</a> in 2008, the first of which being the single "Head of My Class" by rapper Scooter Smiff featuring <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>. He also made a guest appearance on fellow California artist <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>'s album, <i><a href="/wiki/Malice_N_Wonderland" class="mw-redirect" title="Malice N Wonderland">Malice N Wonderland</a></i>.
</p><p>Later that year, Problem launched the independent label, Diamond Lane Music Group.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Then in 2009, he signed an unsuccessful two-single major label deal with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However his breakout moment would be when he was featured on <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Function_(song)" title="Function (song)">Function</a>" along with <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG (rapper)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iamsu!" title="Iamsu!">Iamsu!</a>. Since that time, Problem has worked with the various artists such as <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Foxx" title="Jamie Foxx">Jamie Foxx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Game (rapper)">Game</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_V" title="Bobby V">Bobby V</a>, <a href="/wiki/9th_Wonder" title="9th Wonder">9th Wonder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown_(American_singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chris Brown (American singer)">Chris Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warren_G" title="Warren G">Warren G</a>, <a href="/wiki/Childish_Gambino" class="mw-redirect" title="Childish Gambino">Childish Gambino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travis_Barker" title="Travis Barker">Travis Barker</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_Legend" title="John Legend">John Legend</a> among many others.<sup id="cite_ref-thefader1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Over his career Problem has released seven mixtapes.<sup id="cite_ref-thefader1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On February 13, 2013, Problem released a collaborative mixtape with Iamsu! titled <i>Million Dollar Afro</i> with guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Juvenile_(rapper)" title="Juvenile (rapper)">Juvenile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too Short</a> and <a href="/wiki/Omarion" title="Omarion">Omarion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 13, 2013, he released a mixtape titled <i>The Separation</i> with guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>, <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, Snoop Dogg, <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">Game</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown_(American_singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chris Brown (American singer)">Chris Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tank_(American_singer)" title="Tank (American singer)">Tank</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a> and Wiz Khalifa among others.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> His 2013 song "<a href="/wiki/Like_Whaaat" title="Like Whaaat">Like Whaaat</a>," featuring Bad Lucc, peaked at #20 on the Bubbling Under <a href="/wiki/Hot_100_Singles" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot 100 Singles">Hot 100 singles</a> chart, becoming his most successful song. On July 23, 2013, he released "Say That Then," featuring <a href="/wiki/Glasses_Malone" title="Glasses Malone">Glasses Malone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> “Say That Then” and “Do it Big” featuring Bad Lucc, <a href="/wiki/Sage_the_Gemini" title="Sage the Gemini">Sage The Gemini</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Iamsu!" title="Iamsu!">Iamsu</a>! were featured on the 2013 hit game <a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a> by <a href="/wiki/Rockstar_Games" title="Rockstar Games">Rockstar Games</a> on the fictional radio station “<a href="/wiki/Radio_Los_Santos" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio Los Santos">Radio Los Santos</a>.”
</p><p>In November 2013, Problem announced that he would release his debut album independently through Diamond Lane Music Group. He revealed that he would be primarily producing the album along with League of Starz.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On December 10, 2013, Problem released a retail <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> called <i><a href="/wiki/Understand_Me" title="Understand Me">Understand Me</a></i>, featuring songs such as "Like Whaaat", "Say That Then" and "Understand Me".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Problem appeared in a branded music video alongside <a href="/wiki/Neil_Patrick_Harris" title="Neil Patrick Harris">Neil Patrick Harris</a> for the drink product Neuro in February 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Problem made a splash for his hometown city when he partnered with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Rams" title="Los Angeles Rams">Los Angeles Rams</a> to create the team's theme song, "My Squad," for the 2016-17 NFL season.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The release included an official music video that was shot to look like <a href="/wiki/Snapchat" title="Snapchat">Snapchat</a>.
</p><p>After gaining much success on the mixtape circuit, Problem finally released his debut album, <i>Selfish</i>, in November 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The project showcases some of his most personal work yet. As well as he released his second album <i>S2</i> in November 30, 2018.
</p><p>On April 4, 2020, Problem released a <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">short film</a> titled "A Compton Story". The short film is narrated by comedian <a href="/wiki/Mike_Epps" title="Mike Epps">Mike Epps</a> and has appearances from <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slink_Johnson" title="Slink Johnson">Slink Johnson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Long" title="Jackie Long">Jackie Long</a>. <a href="/wiki/Terrence_%22Punch%22_Henderson" class="mw-redirect" title="Terrence &quot;Punch&quot; Henderson">Terrence "Punch" Henderson</a> is credited as the <a href="/wiki/Executive_producer_(television)" class="mw-redirect" title="Executive producer (television)">executive producer</a> on the project.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
