<p><b>Zachary Lewis Beck</b> (born June 2, 1991), known professionally as <b>Futuristic</b>, is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter.<sup id="cite_ref-LAHWF_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAHWF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was born in <a href="/wiki/Bloomington,_Illinois" title="Bloomington, Illinois">Bloomington, Illinois</a> and currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Tempe,_Arizona" title="Tempe, Arizona">Tempe, Arizona</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Futuristic gained recognition after the release of his album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rise_(Futuristic_album)" title="The Rise (Futuristic album)">The Rise</a></i>, along with his single "The Greatest" in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-Rise_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rise-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Futuristic_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Zachary Lewis Beck was born in <a href="/wiki/Bloomington,_Illinois" title="Bloomington, Illinois">Bloomington, Illinois</a> on June 2, 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-LAHWF_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAHWF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Being one of eight kids, Beck started rapping when he was in the first grade with his brothers in a talent show. When he was in fifth grade, his parents separated and he moved with his mom to <a href="/wiki/Danville,_Illinois" title="Danville, Illinois">Danville, Illinois</a>. Later, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Tempe,_Arizona" title="Tempe, Arizona">Tempe, Arizona</a> and went to <a href="/wiki/McClintock_High_School" title="McClintock High School">McClintock High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Beck struggled through depression when his grandmother died, and he used music in order to develop himself.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Futuristic_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932014:_Career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2014:_Career_beginnings">2010–2014: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Futuristic_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010–2014: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2010, Futuristic released his first music video which was a remix to <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole's</a> song, "<a href="/wiki/Who_Dat_(J._Cole_song)" title="Who Dat (J. Cole song)">Who Dat</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In February 2012, Futuristic released his debut studio album, <i>Dream Big</i>, which was followed up in February 2013, with his second studio album, <i>Chasing Down a Dream</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-DB_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DB-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CDAD_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CDAD-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The album was supported by the single: "Don't Mind If I Do" featuring vocals from <a href="/wiki/Dizzy_Wright" title="Dizzy Wright">Dizzy Wright</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarren_Benton" title="Jarren Benton">Jarren Benton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He then followed that up with his debut extended play, <i>T.G.I.F</i>, in September 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-TGIF_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TGIF-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Futuristic later went on and released his third studio album, <i>Traveling Local</i> on June 9, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-TL_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TL-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The album was supported by four singles: "I Guess I'll Smoke" featuring vocals from Dizzy Wright and <a href="/wiki/Layzie_Bone" title="Layzie Bone">Layzie Bone</a>, "Dirty Chucks", "Plan A" featuring Jarren Benton and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Webby" title="Chris Webby">Chris Webby</a> and "In the Zone" featuring Collins, J Rob the Chief and Devvon Terrell.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The single, "No Way", was released on September 20, 2014, and was released as the lead single to his fourth studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.932016:_The_Rise.2C_Coast_2_Coast.2C_and_As_Seen_on_the_Internet"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–2016:_The_Rise,_Coast_2_Coast,_and_As_Seen_on_the_Internet">2015–2016: <i>The Rise</i>, <i>Coast 2 Coast</i>, and <i>As Seen on the Internet</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Futuristic_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2015–2016: The Rise, Coast 2 Coast, and As Seen on the Internet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 1, 2015, Futuristic announced his fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rise_(Futuristic_album)" title="The Rise (Futuristic album)">The Rise</a></i>, on his website with the released date of May 12.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Futuristic released his second single, "The Greatest", on March 14.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The music video was released four days later and shows Futuristic dressed as a <a href="/wiki/Nerd" title="Nerd">nerd</a> rapping for people in his old school, McClintock High School.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The song and music video gained recognition after it was <a href="/wiki/Cross-promotion" title="Cross-promotion">cross-promoted</a> by another video uploaded by BigDawsTv on the same day tilted "Nerd Raps Fast in <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton!</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The album was released on May 12, 2015, and debuted at number 139 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-BB200_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB200-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The album also debuted on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rap_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap Albums">Rap Albums</a> charts at number 13 and 10 respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;BRap_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;BRap-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RapAlbums_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RapAlbums-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Futuristic would later contribute vocals to the song, "<a href="/wiki/Hold_Each_Other" title="Hold Each Other">Hold Each Other</a>", with <a href="/wiki/A_Great_Big_World" title="A Great Big World">A Great Big World</a> in July 2015 with the music video being released in September.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The song would later chart on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> at number 99, becoming his first Hot 100 entry.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The song would also chart on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100"><i>Canadian</i> Hot 100</a> at number 69.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 13, 2015, Futuristic released a remix to the song, "<a href="/wiki/Blas%C3%A9_(song)" title="Blasé (song)">Blasé</a>", with New York singer and rapper <a href="/wiki/Devvon_Terrell" title="Devvon Terrell">Devvon Terrell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> They later announced that they would be releasing a collaborative album titled, "Coast 2 Coast", and leading up to the release of the album they would release various remixes to popular songs and dubbed them as "12 Days of Christmas" including "Bodied", a remix of Omaha rapper Cameron Brown's "Give Me the Crown" .<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The album was released on December 25, 2015, and debuted at number 82 on the US <i>Billboard</i> 200 chart.<sup id="cite_ref-BB200_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB200-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 26, 2016, Futuristic released his fifth studio album titled, <i><a href="/wiki/As_Seen_on_the_Internet" title="As Seen on the Internet">As Seen on the Internet</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-ASOTI_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASOTI-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The album features <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from <a href="/wiki/Hopsin" title="Hopsin">Hopsin</a>, Devvon Terrell, Karmin and Goody Grace and went on and charted at number 116 on the US <i>Billboard</i> 200 chart.<sup id="cite_ref-BB200_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB200-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> After the release of the album, Futuristic released eleven music videos all directed by Jakob Owens to promote the album.<sup id="cite_ref-Sway_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sway-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2017.E2.80.932019:_Blessings.2C_I_Am....2C_and_Zachary_Lewis"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–2019:_Blessings,_I_Am...,_and_Zachary_Lewis">2017–2019: <i>Blessings</i>, <i>I Am...</i>, and <i>Zachary Lewis</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Futuristic_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2017–2019: Blessings, I Am..., and Zachary Lewis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:FuturisticNov2018.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/FuturisticNov2018.jpg/170px-FuturisticNov2018.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/FuturisticNov2018.jpg/255px-FuturisticNov2018.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/FuturisticNov2018.jpg/340px-FuturisticNov2018.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3024" data-file-height="4032" /></a><figcaption>Futuristic performing at the Independent Grind Tour in November 2018</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2017, Futuristic later went on and released various singles from February through November of that year.<sup id="cite_ref-Wave_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wave-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FTIO_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FTIO-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> On November 24, 2017, Futuristic released his second extended play titled, <i>What More Could You Ask for?</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-WMCYAF_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WMCYAF-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> On December 25, 2017, Futuristic released his sixth studio album titled, <i><a href="/wiki/Blessings_(Futuristic_album)" title="Blessings (Futuristic album)">Blessings</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Blessings_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blessings-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The album was supported by three singles: "Epiphany" featuring vocals from <a href="/wiki/NF_(rapper)" title="NF (rapper)">NF</a>, "Get It Together" and "Talk" featuring vocals from <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a> and Devvon Terrell.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 14, 2018, Futuristic released his third extended play titled, <i>Songs About Girls</i>, which included the singles "Wave" and "Forget That's It Over".<sup id="cite_ref-SAG_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SAG-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Wave_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wave-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FTIO_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FTIO-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He later went on to release various singles throughout the year including: "Nobody Else", "My Own Zone" with Tech N9ne and Dizzy Wright and many more.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 12, 2019, Futuristic released his seventh studio album titled, <i>I Am</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The album was supported by four singles: "That's That", "Top 10" featuring gianni &amp; kyle, "48 Hours" and "Up".<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Futuristic later released his eighth studio album, <i>Zachary Lewis</i>, on September 1, which included the single "Main Homie".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Following the release of <i>Zachary Lewis</i>, He later went on and release music videos for every song on the album.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2020.E2.80.93present:_Still_on_the_Rise_and_Featuristic"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020–present:_Still_on_the_Rise_and_Featuristic">2020–present: <i>Still on the Rise</i> and <i>Featuristic</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Futuristic_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2020–present: Still on the Rise and Featuristic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 1, 2020, Futuristic announced on his social media accounts that the single, "Sorry for Y'all", would be released on January 24.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Futuristic's ninth studio album titled, <i>Still on the Rise</i>, was released on May 12, 2020, which is a follow-up to his 2015 album <i>The Rise</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> The album was supported by seven singles: "Sorry for Y'all", "Wishawoods", "Motion", "Champion", "Pull Up" featuring Kap G, "Like This" with <a href="/wiki/Rittz" title="Rittz">Rittz</a> and "OG Now" with <a href="/wiki/Chris_Webby" title="Chris Webby">Chris Webby</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 24, 2020, Futuristic released his tenth studio album titled, <i>Featuristic</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> The album was supported by six singles: "Cool AF", with Jzac, "I.O.F.W.R.", with <a href="/wiki/Justina_Valentine" title="Justina Valentine">Justina Valentine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Battles" title="Mark Battles">Mark Battles</a>, "Skid Row", with Ekoh and Jarren Benton, "Now or Never", with Crypt, "Michael Jordan", with <a href="/w/index.php?title=GAWNE&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="GAWNE (page does not exist)">GAWNE</a> and "Come Home", with <a href="/wiki/24hrs_(rapper)" title="24hrs (rapper)">24hrs</a> and <a href="/wiki/YBN_Almighty_Jay" class="mw-redirect" title="YBN Almighty Jay">YBN Almighty Jay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Futuristic_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 9, 2020, Futuristic announced his engagement to Paris Shantè Rodriguez by proposing to her on a cruise.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> The pair married on October 13, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
