<p><b>Daniel Anthony Farris</b> (born October 17, 1985), known professionally as <b>D Smoke</b>, is an American rapper and songwriter from <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He gained attention after winning the first season of the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> music competition show <i><a href="/wiki/Rhythm_%2B_Flow" title="Rhythm + Flow">Rhythm + Flow</a></i> in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The following year, he independently released his debut album <i>Black Habits</i>, which earned him nominations for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Best Rap Album</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_New_Artist" title="Grammy Award for Best New Artist">Best New Artist</a> at the <a href="/wiki/63rd_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="63rd Annual Grammy Awards">63rd Annual Grammy Awards</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D_Smoke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Farris grew up in a musical family with his mother, brothers, and cousin being <a href="/wiki/Gospel_music" title="Gospel music">gospel</a> singers in <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood, California</a>. His mother was backup singer for Tina Turner. His brother <a href="/wiki/Sir_(singer)" title="Sir (singer)">SiR</a> is signed to <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a>. In 2006, he started a songwriting group called WoodWorks with his brothers and his cousin <a href="/wiki/Tiffany_Gouch%C3%A9" title="Tiffany Gouché">Tiffany Gouché</a>, who wrote songs for <a href="/wiki/Ginuwine" title="Ginuwine">Ginuwine</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Pussycat_Dolls" title="The Pussycat Dolls">The Pussycat Dolls</a>, and is credited for co-writing "<a href="/wiki/Never_(Jaheim_song)" title="Never (Jaheim song)">Never</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Jaheim" title="Jaheim">Jaheim</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Vibe_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was also in a musical trio with his brother called N3D.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On May 9, 2006, he released his first album called <i>Producer of the Year</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, he appeared on SiR's independent album <i>Seven Sundays</i> on the song "You Ain't Ready".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Farris was a contestant on the Netflix competition show <i><a href="/wiki/Rhythm_%2B_Flow" title="Rhythm + Flow">Rhythm + Flow</a></i>, and was named the inaugural winner of the three-week series.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On October 24, he released his debut EP <i>Inglewood High</i>, a 7-track project including a feature from Gouché. <i><a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a></i> gave the EP a positive review, saying he could "utilize multiple flows, deliver introspective storytelling, could rap in Spanish incredibly well and had an ear for quality."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He performed at the <a href="/wiki/2019_Soul_Train_Music_Awards" title="2019 Soul Train Music Awards">2019 Soul Train Music Awards</a> with SiR.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He also appeared on <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Born_2_Rap" title="Born 2 Rap">Born 2 Rap</a></i> on the song "Cross on Jesus Back".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>D Smoke later appeared as himself in the <a href="/wiki/Peacock_(streaming_service)" title="Peacock (streaming service)">Peacock</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Bel-Air_(TV_series)" title="Bel-Air (TV series)">Bel-Air</a></i>, in the episode "PA to LA", based on  <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fresh_Prince_of_Bel-Air" title="The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air">The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air</a></i>.
</p>
