<p><b>Thebe Neruda Kgositsile</b> (born February 24, 1994), also known by his stage name <b>Earl Sweatshirt</b>, is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer. Kgositsile was originally known by the moniker <b>Sly Tendencies</b> when he began rapping in 2008, but soon changed his name when <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a> invited him to join his <a href="/wiki/Music_collective" class="mw-redirect" title="Music collective">alternative hip hop collective</a> <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a> in late 2009.
</p><p>He gained recognition and critical praise for his debut <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Earl_(mixtape)" title="Earl (mixtape)">Earl</a></i>, which he released in March 2010, at the age of 16. Shortly after its release, his mother sent him to a boarding school in <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a> for at-risk teens for a year and a half. He was unable to record music during his stay there, but returned to Los Angeles in February 2012, just before his eighteenth birthday. Kgositsile rejoined Odd Future and started producing new music, releasing his debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Doris_(album)" title="Doris (album)">Doris</a></i>, in August 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His second album, <i><a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Like_Shit,_I_Don%27t_Go_Outside" title="I Don&#39;t Like Shit, I Don&#39;t Go Outside">I Don't Like Shit, I Don't Go Outside</a></i>, followed in March 2015, and his third, <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Rap_Songs" title="Some Rap Songs">Some Rap Songs</a></i>, released in November 2018. His projects have all received widespread critical praise. He is currently signed to his independent label Tan Cressida, which was formerly distributed by <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>. On November 1, 2019, Kgositsile released an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/Feet_of_Clay_(EP)" title="Feet of Clay (EP)">Feet of Clay</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Earl released the deluxe version of <i>Feet of Clay</i> a few months after and then took a year off from music to start working on his next album. In late 2021, Earl released the single "2010" and then released two more singles before releasing his fourth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Sick!" title="Sick!">Sick!</a></i>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_Sweatshirt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thebe Neruda Kgositsile was born on February 24, 1994, in <a href="/wiki/Chicago,_Illinois" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicago, Illinois">Chicago, Illinois</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Cheryl_Harris" title="Cheryl Harris">Cheryl Harris</a>, a law professor and <a href="/wiki/Critical_race_theory" title="Critical race theory">critical race theorist</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Los_Angeles" title="University of California, Los Angeles">University of California, Los Angeles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Keorapetse_Kgositsile" title="Keorapetse Kgositsile">Keorapetse Kgositsile</a>, a South African poet and political activist. Harris and Kgositsile separated when Thebe was six years old.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> His father is often mentioned in Kgositsile's music and was called a "complicated figure" by <a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork</a>. He was presented as a person that was often absent in Kgositsile's life,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> due to him living in South Africa while Kgositsile lived in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>. Kgositsile stated that "Me and my dad had a relationship that's not uncommon for people to have with their fathers, which is a non-perfect one, talking to him is symbolic and non-symbolic, but it's literally closure for my childhood. Not getting to have that moment left me to figure out a lot with my damn self."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Kgositsile attended the <a href="/wiki/UCLA_Lab_School" title="UCLA Lab School">UCLA Lab School</a> in Los Angeles and New Roads Middle School &amp; High School in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica,_California" title="Santa Monica, California">Santa Monica, California</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_Sweatshirt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.932009:_Career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–2009:_Career_beginnings">2008–2009: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_Sweatshirt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008–2009: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kgositsile first started rapping in the seventh grade. In 2007, under the name Sly Tendencies,<sup id="cite_ref-Allmusicbio_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusicbio-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> he posted tracks from his mixtape, <i>Kitchen Cutlery</i>, via <a href="/wiki/MySpace" class="mw-redirect" title="MySpace">MySpace</a>. He and two of his friends, Loofy and JW Mijo, formed a rap trio called The Backpackerz. They intended to release a mixtape titled <i>World Playground</i>, but disbanded sometime in 2009.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932011:_Earl_and_hiatus_in_Samoa"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2011:_Earl_and_hiatus_in_Samoa">2009–2011: <i>Earl</i> and hiatus in Samoa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_Sweatshirt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009–2011: Earl and hiatus in Samoa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Earl_(mixtape)" title="Earl (mixtape)">Earl (mixtape)</a></div>
<p>In 2009, fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a> discovered Kgositsile via his <a href="/wiki/MySpace" class="mw-redirect" title="MySpace">MySpace</a> account after he reached out to Tyler to tell him he was a fan of his work. Kgositsile later changed his pseudonym to Earl Sweatshirt and eventually joined Tyler's rap group <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a>.
</p><p>His debut mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Earl_(album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Earl (album)">Earl</a></i>, was self-released on March 31, 2010, for free digital download on the Odd Future website.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Most of the mixtape was produced by Tyler, the Creator. <i>Earl</i> was named the 24th-best album of 2010 by <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite positive reactions from both critics and fans, various sources indicated that Kgositsile had stopped making music with Odd Future.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Posts from Tyler, the Creator's Twitter and <a href="/wiki/Formspring" class="mw-redirect" title="Formspring">Formspring</a> accounts seemed to indicate that Kgositsile's mother would not grant permission to release any of her son's music. Kgositsile later expressed in an interview that his mother sent him to <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a> due to getting into trouble with friends. Kgositsile attended Coral Reef Academy, a therapeutic retreat school for at-risk boys, located outside of the Samoan capital of <a href="/wiki/Apia" title="Apia">Apia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-backfromwilderness_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-backfromwilderness-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> At Coral Reef Academy, Kgositsile worked to earn back privileges and the opportunity to return home. At the beginning of his enrollment, he was unable to use the bathroom unsupervised.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> While there, he read <a href="/wiki/Manning_Marable" title="Manning Marable">Manning Marable</a>'s biography on <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_X" title="Malcolm X">Malcolm X</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Fari%C3%B1a" title="Richard Fariña">Richard Fariña</a>'s counterculture fiction. He wrote rhymes, including most of his verse on "Oldie", his only contribution to Odd Future's studio album <i><a href="/wiki/The_OF_Tape_Vol._2" title="The OF Tape Vol. 2">The OF Tape Vol. 2</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Kgositsile was brought back from Samoa by <a href="/wiki/Leila_Steinberg" title="Leila Steinberg">Leila Steinberg</a>, the first manager of <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>, who still manages Earl's career today.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kgositsile's hiatus from making music led to a movement known as "Free Earl", which stemmed from a post on Odd Future's <a href="/wiki/Tumblr" title="Tumblr">Tumblr</a>. Chants of the phrase would ensue at the group's shows, and would appear throughout several songs by both the collective and its individual members – leading to media coverage of the movement itself.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932013:_Return_from_Samoa_and_Doris"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2013:_Return_from_Samoa_and_Doris">2012–2013: Return from Samoa and <i>Doris</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_Sweatshirt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–2013: Return from Samoa and Doris">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Doris_(album)" title="Doris (album)">Doris (album)</a></div>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Earl_Sweatshirt_%26_Taco.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Earl_Sweatshirt_%26_Taco.jpg/220px-Earl_Sweatshirt_%26_Taco.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Earl_Sweatshirt_%26_Taco.jpg/330px-Earl_Sweatshirt_%26_Taco.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/ce/Earl_Sweatshirt_%26_Taco.jpg 2x" data-file-width="424" data-file-height="283" /></a><figcaption>Sweatshirt performing with Taco in September 2012</figcaption></figure>
<p>On February 8, 2012, rumors spread around the internet that Kgositsile had returned to the U.S. when a video of him surfaced on YouTube with a preview of a new song saying if viewers wanted "the full thing" they would have to give him 50,000 followers on Twitter.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He also later confirmed on his new Twitter account that he had returned to his home in Los Angeles.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kgositsile appeared on the song "Oldie" from Odd Future's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/The_OF_Tape_Vol._2" title="The OF Tape Vol. 2">The OF Tape Vol.&#160;2</a></i>. This was Earl's return to official Odd Future releases and his first appearance on an Odd Future Records release. On March 20, a video released on the official Odd Future YouTube page featured Kgositsile in a cipher-style music video rapping along to his verse from "Oldie" with the other members of the crew. That same day, Kgositsile performed with the group at the <a href="/wiki/Hammerstein_Ballroom" title="Hammerstein Ballroom">Hammerstein Ballroom</a> in New York City on March 20, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On April 9, 2012, rapper <a href="/wiki/Casey_Veggies" title="Casey Veggies">Casey Veggies</a> released a mixtape titled <i>Customized Greatly 3</i> that included a song featuring Earl Sweatshirt, Tyler the Creator, Domo Genesis, and Hodgy Beats titled "PNCINTLOFWGKTA." During that month Kgositsile signed on to create his own record label imprint, Tan Cressida, to be distributed through <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>. He turned down several other larger offers due to his priority of remaining close to Odd Future.<sup id="cite_ref-backfromwilderness_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-backfromwilderness-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Earl Sweatshirt was featured on the track "<a href="/wiki/Super_Rich_Kids" title="Super Rich Kids">Super Rich Kids</a>" from Frank Ocean's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Channel_Orange" title="Channel Orange">channel ORANGE</a></i> released digitally July 10, 2012. On July 16, <a href="/wiki/Domo_Genesis" title="Domo Genesis">Domo Genesis</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a> released the first official single, "Elimination Chamber", from their collaboration album <i>No Idols</i>, which featured Earl, <a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples" title="Vince Staples">Vince Staples</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson" title="Action Bronson">Action Bronson</a>. He was featured on the album again on the tracks "Daily News" (also featuring <a href="/wiki/SpaceGhostPurrp" title="SpaceGhostPurrp">SpaceGhostPurrp</a> &amp; Action Bronson) and "Gamebreaker". On July 23, 2012, <a href="/wiki/Flying_Lotus" title="Flying Lotus">Flying Lotus</a> released a song titled "Between Friends" on the <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">Adult Swim</a> Single series which featured Earl Sweatshirt and <a href="/wiki/Flying_Lotus" title="Flying Lotus">Captain Murphy</a>. Earl Sweatshirt was also featured on <a href="/wiki/MellowHype" title="MellowHype">MellowHype</a>'s second and final studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Numbers_(Mellowhype_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Numbers (Mellowhype album)">Numbers</a></i> on the track "P2".
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Earl_Sweatshirt_March_15,_2013_-_02.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Earl_Sweatshirt_March_15%2C_2013_-_02.jpg/220px-Earl_Sweatshirt_March_15%2C_2013_-_02.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Earl_Sweatshirt_March_15%2C_2013_-_02.jpg/330px-Earl_Sweatshirt_March_15%2C_2013_-_02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Earl_Sweatshirt_March_15%2C_2013_-_02.jpg/440px-Earl_Sweatshirt_March_15%2C_2013_-_02.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="1728" /></a><figcaption>Sweatshirt performing in 2013</figcaption></figure>
<p>On November 2, Kgositsile released his first solo single since his return from Samoa, titled "Chum".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On December 4, Kgositsile announced that his debut studio album would be called <i><a href="/wiki/Doris_(album)" title="Doris (album)">Doris</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> That same day the music video for "Chum" was posted on YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> <i>Doris</i> was reported early on to feature vocals and/or production from <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a>, Frank Ocean, Ommas Keith, Thundercat, Domo Genesis, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">the Neptunes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian_Rich" title="Christian Rich">Christian Rich</a>, Vince Staples, <a href="/wiki/BadBadNotGood" title="BadBadNotGood">BadBadNotGood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samiyam" title="Samiyam">Samiyam</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>, Casey Veggies, <a href="/wiki/The_Internet_(band)" title="The Internet (band)">The Internet</a> and <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On March 6, 2013, while performing with <a href="/wiki/Flying_Lotus" title="Flying Lotus">Flying Lotus</a> and Mac Miller, Earl premiered three new songs off <i>Doris</i>: "Burgundy" produced by <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Hive_(song)" title="Hive (song)">Hive</a>" featuring Vince Staples, and "Guild" featuring Mac Miller. Kgositsile also confirmed the next single to be titled "Whoa" featuring Tyler, the Creator. The song was released to iTunes on March 12, 2013, along with the music video, which was directed by Tyler, the Creator.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Tyler,_the_Creator_and_Earl_Sweatshirt..jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Tyler%2C_the_Creator_and_Earl_Sweatshirt..jpg/220px-Tyler%2C_the_Creator_and_Earl_Sweatshirt..jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="158" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Tyler%2C_the_Creator_and_Earl_Sweatshirt..jpg/330px-Tyler%2C_the_Creator_and_Earl_Sweatshirt..jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Tyler%2C_the_Creator_and_Earl_Sweatshirt..jpg/440px-Tyler%2C_the_Creator_and_Earl_Sweatshirt..jpg 2x" data-file-width="4383" data-file-height="3154" /></a><figcaption>Earl Sweatshirt performing alongside <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a> in 2013</figcaption></figure>
<p><i>Doris</i> was released on August 20, 2013, under Tan Cressida and Columbia Records. <i>Doris</i> featured <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from Odd Future members Domo Genesis, Frank Ocean, Tyler, the Creator, along with Vince Staples, RZA, Casey Veggies and Mac Miller. <a href="/wiki/Record_production" class="mw-redirect" title="Record production">Production</a> was primarily handled by Kgositsile under the pseudonym randomblackdude and production duo <a href="/wiki/Christian_Rich" title="Christian Rich">Christian Rich</a>. Additional production was provided by <a href="/wiki/Matt_Martians" title="Matt Martians">Matt Martians</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">the Neptunes</a>, RZA, <a href="/wiki/Samiyam" title="Samiyam">Samiyam</a>, <a href="/wiki/BadBadNotGood" title="BadBadNotGood">BadBadNotGood</a>, Frank Ocean, and Tyler, the Creator. In September 2013, <i>Complex</i> named Kgositsile the tenth best producer in hip hop.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Upon its release, <i>Doris</i> was met with universal critical acclaim from music critics, including perfect scores by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i>, which praised Kgositsile's rhyme schemes and lyrics along with the gritty underground production.<sup id="cite_ref-Metacritic_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metacritic-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The album also fared well commercially, debuting at number five on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and number one on US <a href="/wiki/Top_Rap_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Rap Albums">Top Rap Albums</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.932015:_I_Don.27t_Like_Shit.2C_I_Don.27t_Go_Outside_and_Solace"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2015:_I_Don't_Like_Shit,_I_Don't_Go_Outside_and_Solace">2014–2015: <i>I Don't Like Shit, I Don't Go Outside</i> and <i>Solace</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_Sweatshirt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014–2015: I Don&#039;t Like Shit, I Don&#039;t Go Outside and Solace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Like_Shit,_I_Don%27t_Go_Outside" title="I Don&#39;t Like Shit, I Don&#39;t Go Outside">I Don't Like Shit, I Don't Go Outside</a> and <a href="/wiki/Solace_(Earl_Sweatshirt_EP)" class="mw-redirect" title="Solace (Earl Sweatshirt EP)">Solace (Earl Sweatshirt EP)</a></div>
<p>On November 12, 2012, Kgositsile announced that he had begun working on his second studio project.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The forthcoming album was to be named <i>Gnossos,</i> <sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> but Kgositsile later decided against the title.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He cited inspiration from <a href="/wiki/Richard_Fari%C3%B1a" title="Richard Fariña">Richard Fariña</a>'s 1966 cult-classic novel <i><a href="/wiki/Been_Down_So_Long_It_Looks_Like_Up_to_Me" title="Been Down So Long It Looks Like Up to Me">Been Down So Long It Looks Like Up To Me</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Kgositsile ultimately decided to take the album in another direction under the name <i>I Don't Like Shit, I Don't Go Outside</i>. On October 10, 2014, Kgositsile confirmed that he had completed the follow-up album to <i><a href="/wiki/Doris_(album)" title="Doris (album)">Doris</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> On November 5, 2014, he released a new song entitled "45" produced by <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>.
On February 14, 2015, Earl debuted a new song titled "Quest/Power" via <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Kgositsile continued to perform unreleased tracks since early 2015, such as "Swamp Vermin", "Vultures", "I Be Outside", "Hell", and "Flowers on the Grave".
</p><p>On January 5, 2015, Kgositsile released a song titled <i>silenceDArapgame</i> with professional skateboarder Nakel Smith under the moniker Hog Slaughta Boyz.<sup id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_Slaughter_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork_Slaughter-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 16, 2015, The pre-order for Kgositsile's second studio album, titled: <i><a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Like_Shit,_I_Don%27t_Go_Outside" title="I Don&#39;t Like Shit, I Don&#39;t Go Outside">I Don't Like Shit, I Don't Go Outside</a></i> appeared on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a>, without prior announcement.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On March 17, 2015, Kgositsile released a music video for the song "Grief".<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The digital version of the full album was released on March 22, 2015; the physical version was released later on April 14, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> A later music video for the song "Off Top" was released on August 7, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Kgositsile stated in an interview with NPR that his record label gave him no notice they would release the album.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> He says he considers this his first album because he feels he can "back up everything, the good and the bad".
</p><p>On April 28, 2015, a ten-minute track named "Solace" was released via YouTube and has gained much attention; gaining more than 100,000 views on YouTube in 24 hours. He stated to NPR that he was making an album called "Solace" inspired by his mother; however, many believe it is instead an extended play. The project has not been addressed fully.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> It was widely debated if Kgositsile left Odd Future or not. He seemingly confirmed he had left through his Twitter on May 28, 2015, by tweeting "No sympathy for male virgins who're in their feelings about Tyler pointing out and solidifying the obvious" after Tyler, the Creator tweeted what everyone thought was the disbandment of Odd Future, although Tyler denies an Odd Future disbandment. Earl did not appear at Camp Flog Gnaw 2015 on November 14, 2015. Many speculated that Tyler, the Creator did not invite Earl to perform at the Carnival because of a feud or conflict between the two. However, Tyler tweeted the day after the carnival "Thebe and I are fine by the way".
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932019:_Some_Rap_Songs.2C_Feet_of_Clay_and_departure_from_Columbia_Records"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2019:_Some_Rap_Songs,_Feet_of_Clay_and_departure_from_Columbia_Records">2016–2019: <i>Some Rap Songs, Feet of Clay</i> and departure from Columbia Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_Sweatshirt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2016–2019: Some Rap Songs, Feet of Clay and departure from Columbia Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Some_Rap_Songs" title="Some Rap Songs">Some Rap Songs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Feet_of_Clay_(EP)" title="Feet of Clay (EP)">Feet of Clay (EP)</a></div>
<p>On January 25, 2016, Kgositsile released three new tracks on SoundCloud, "Wind in My Sails", produced by The Alchemist, and "Bary" and "Skrt Skrt", produced under his alias 'randomblackdude'. "Wind in My Sails" contains samples from Captain Murphy's song "Children of the Atom" and vocal samples from <a href="/wiki/Gene_McDaniels" title="Gene McDaniels">Gene McDaniels</a>'s song "The Parasite (For Buffy)". "Bary" contains vocal samples from <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s song "Barry Bonds". "Skrt Skrt" contains vocal samples from <a href="/wiki/21_Savage" title="21 Savage">21 Savage</a>'s song "Skrrt Skrrt".<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> On March 4, 2016, Kgositsile was featured on <a href="/wiki/Samiyam" title="Samiyam">Samiyam</a>'s 4th album, <i>Animals Have Feelings</i>. The song, "Mirror", was originally meant for <i>I Don't Like Shit, I Don't Go Outside</i>. According to Kgositsile, the song wasn't included since "the tracklist got fucked up".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The music video for "Mirror" was released on June 21, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> On August 1, 2016, an instrumental track called "Pelicula" was posted on <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music" title="Apple Music">Apple Music</a>. On August 17, 2016, Kgositsile was featured on <i>Adult Swim Singles Program 2016</i> on the track "Balance",<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> produced by <a href="/wiki/Knxwledge" title="Knxwledge">Knxwledge</a>. On September 4, a new Kgositsile track called "Death Whistles" was released on Earl's and Knxwledge's livestream show on <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Music_Academy" title="Red Bull Music Academy">Red Bull Music Academy</a>, produced by <a href="/wiki/King_Krule" title="King Krule">King Krule</a>.
</p><p>On September 21, 2018, frequent collaborator The Alchemist released the track listing to his EP <i>Bread</i>, which contains the track "E. Coli" featuring Earl Sweatshirt.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> On November 2, 2018, frequent collaborator <a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples" title="Vince Staples">Vince Staples</a> released his album <i><a href="/wiki/FM!" title="FM!">FM!</a></i> featuring the track "New earlsweatshirt – Interlude" containing a 20-second verse from the rapper. Vince later said "Earl is back" and that "his album coming soon" on his <a href="/wiki/Beats_1" class="mw-redirect" title="Beats 1">Beats 1</a> show 'Ramona Radio'.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 7, 2018, Kgositsile teased the release of new music on social media in a video clip captioned, "NOWHERE2GO TOMORROW MORNING TAP IN." The single "<a href="/wiki/Nowhere2go" title="Nowhere2go">Nowhere2go</a>" was released on November 8, 2018, and received positive reception.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> On November 20, 2018, Kgositsile released the single "The Mint" and announced his third studio album, <i>Some Rap Songs</i> which he slated for a November 30, 2018, release.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> According to Kgositsile, <i>Some Rap Songs</i> was intended to be themed around his father's death.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 30, 2018, Kgositsile released <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Rap_Songs" title="Some Rap Songs">Some Rap Songs</a></i> to widespread critical acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> In January 2019, Kgositsile said that <i>Some Rap Songs</i> would be his last album with Columbia Records and that he was "excited to be free because then [he] can do riskier shit".<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2019, Kgositsile appeared in a song with <a href="/wiki/Zelooperz" title="Zelooperz">Zelooperz</a> titled "Easter Sunday".<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> On November 1, 2019, Kgositsile released his EP <i><a href="/wiki/Feet_of_Clay_(EP)" title="Feet of Clay (EP)">Feet of Clay</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> A deluxe version of <i>Feet of Clay</i>, which included two bonus tracks, was released the following June.
</p>
<h3><span id="2021.E2.80.93present:_Sick.21_and_Voir_Dire"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2021–present:_Sick!_and_Voir_Dire">2021–present: <i>Sick!</i> and <i>Voir Dire</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_Sweatshirt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2021–present: Sick! and Voir Dire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Sick!" title="Sick!">Sick!</a> and <a href="/wiki/Voir_Dire_(Earl_Sweatshirt_%26_The_Alchemist_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Voir Dire (Earl Sweatshirt &amp; The Alchemist album)">Voir Dire (Earl Sweatshirt &amp; The Alchemist album)</a></div>
<p>On November 18, 2021, Kgositsile returned with a music video for the single "2010", the first commercial release since his 2019 EP <i><a href="/wiki/Feet_of_Clay_(EP)" title="Feet of Clay (EP)">Feet of Clay</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> On December 9, 2021, Kgositsile released a music video for another single titled "Tabula Rasa", featuring rap group <a href="/wiki/Armand_Hammer_(music_group)" title="Armand Hammer (music group)">Armand Hammer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> Alongside "Tabula Rasa", he announced his fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Sick!" title="Sick!">Sick!</a></i>, which was released on January 14, 2022. Kgositsile released the third and final single, the Black Noi$e-produced "Titanic" on January 7, 2022. The album features <a href="/wiki/ZelooperZ" class="mw-redirect" title="ZelooperZ">ZelooperZ</a>, and rap group <a href="/wiki/Armand_Hammer_(music_group)" title="Armand Hammer (music group)">Armand Hammer</a> with a range of production from Kgositsile himself, <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>, Black Noi$e, <a href="/wiki/Samiyam" title="Samiyam">Samiyam</a> and more.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2023, a hyperlink titled "hahaha" was added to Kgositsile's website that led to the Gala Music site that would update with new puzzles everyday, teasing a new project. Initially, the puzzles seemed to point to a tweet published by the Alchemist in 2021 in which he says him and Kgositsile had a full album together and had published it under a fake name on YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> On August 22, both the website and The Alchemist's Instagram page was updated with a track list and album name, <i>Voir Dire</i>. The next day, another post was shared announcing a listening party to take place in London on  August 25.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> The website updated once again on August 24 with a music video for the track "Sentry" featuring New York City-based rapper <a href="/wiki/Mike_(musician)" title="Mike (musician)">MIKE</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> The album was released for free on August 25, 2023, via Gala Music.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_Sweatshirt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kgositsile has been called a "hip-hop prodigy" and in 2011 was branded as "the most exciting rapper to emerge in years, a virtuoso who was just starting to figure out what he could do with words."<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> He is characterized primarily by his voice, which has been classified as a "deep baritone".<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kgositsile has taken influence from <a href="/wiki/MF_Doom" title="MF Doom">MF Doom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Z" class="mw-redirect" title="Jay Z">Jay Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/J_Dilla" title="J Dilla">J Dilla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madlib" title="Madlib">Madlib</a>, <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lupe_Fiasco" title="Lupe Fiasco">Lupe Fiasco</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Clipse" title="Clipse">Clipse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_Sweatshirt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kgositsile currently lives in the neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Mid-City,_Los_Angeles" class="mw-redirect" title="Mid-City, Los Angeles">Mid-City</a> in Los Angeles, California.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kgositsile struggled with drug abuse, which was one of the reasons why his mother sent him to live in <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a> for over a year. The time in Samoa led to Kgositsile getting sober, though, eventually, he <a href="/wiki/Relapse" title="Relapse">relapsed</a> upon returning to the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup> Kgositsile attributes his drug abuse to time with his collective <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a> and leaving a long-term relationship, saying he was often smoking <a href="/wiki/Marijuana" class="mw-redirect" title="Marijuana">marijuana</a> and drinking <a href="/wiki/Lean_(recreational_drug)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lean (recreational drug)">lean</a> before quitting as he reached adulthood.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kgositsile was raised as a <a href="/wiki/Nichiren_Buddhist" class="mw-redirect" title="Nichiren Buddhist">Nichiren Buddhist</a> and, after a period away from the belief system, stated in 2016 that he had returned to the religion.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2021, Kgositsile revealed on Twitter that he has a son.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
