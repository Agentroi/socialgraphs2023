<p><b>Anthony Terrell Smith</b> (born March 3, 1966), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Tone Lōc</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/oʊ/: &#39;o&#39; in &#39;code&#39;">oʊ</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span></span><span class="wrap"> </span><span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="/oʊ/: &#39;o&#39; in &#39;code&#39;">oʊ</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span></span>/</a></span></span>), is an American rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He is known for his raspy voice, his hit songs "<a href="/wiki/Wild_Thing_(Tone_L%C5%8Dc_song)" title="Wild Thing (Tone Lōc song)">Wild Thing</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Funky_Cold_Medina" title="Funky Cold Medina">Funky Cold Medina</a>", for which he was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Award</a>, and for being featured in "<a href="/wiki/We%27re_All_in_the_Same_Gang" title="We&#39;re All in the Same Gang">We're All in the Same Gang</a>", a collaborative single by the West Coast Rap All-Stars.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tone_Loc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Anthony Terrell Smith was born March 3, 1966, in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, the son of Margaret, who managed a retirement home, and James Smith.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His father died in 1972, and Smith and his three older brothers were raised by his mother.<sup id="cite_ref-CM_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was educated at the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Professional_School" title="Hollywood Professional School">Hollywood Professional School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CM_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As a teenager, he performed with the rap group Triple A.<sup id="cite_ref-CM_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tone_Loc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1989.E2.80.931991:_mainstream_success"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1989–1991:_mainstream_success">1989–1991: mainstream success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tone_Loc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1989–1991: mainstream success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tone Loc's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/L%C5%8Dc-ed_After_Dark" title="Lōc-ed After Dark">Lōc-ed After Dark</a></i>, was released in January 1989. The video for the first single, "<a href="/wiki/Wild_Thing_(Tone_L%C5%8Dc_song)" title="Wild Thing (Tone Lōc song)">Wild Thing</a>," became a staple on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> in the US. The song rose to No. 2 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, and the top twenty in Australia. The second single, "<a href="/wiki/Funky_Cold_Medina" title="Funky Cold Medina">Funky Cold Medina</a>," also became a hit. It reached the top 5 in the US, peaking at No. 3; the top ten in Australia; and the top twenty in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Lōc-ed After Dark</i> reached No. 1 in the US. Since its release, it has been certified 2× platinum there.
</p><p>Tone Loc's second album <i><a href="/wiki/Cool_Hand_L%C5%8Dc" title="Cool Hand Lōc">Cool Hand Lōc</a></i> was released in November 1991. The album's first single was "<a href="/wiki/All_Through_the_Night_(Tone_L%C5%8Dc_song)" title="All Through the Night (Tone Lōc song)">All Through the Night</a>", which found moderate success, reaching No. 80 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 and #16 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Songs" title="Hot Rap Songs">Hot Rap Songs</a> chart. It received frequent airplay on MTV and <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a>. He also contributed "If I'm Gonna Eat Somebody" for <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Studios" title="20th Century Studios">20th Century Fox</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/FernGully:_The_Last_Rainforest" title="FernGully: The Last Rainforest">FernGully</a></i>.
</p>
<h3><span id="1994.E2.80.932004:_Ace_Ventura:_Pet_Detective_franchise.2C_and_other_endeavors"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1994–2004:_Ace_Ventura:_Pet_Detective_franchise,_and_other_endeavors">1994–2004: <i>Ace Ventura: Pet Detective</i> franchise, and other endeavors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tone_Loc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1994–2004: Ace Ventura: Pet Detective franchise, and other endeavors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tone Loc's song "Ace Is in the House", which samples the <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a> song "<a href="/wiki/No_Sleep_till_Brooklyn" title="No Sleep till Brooklyn">No Sleep till Brooklyn</a>", is featured in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Ace_Ventura:_Pet_Detective" title="Ace Ventura: Pet Detective">Ace Ventura: Pet Detective</a></i> (1994) and <i><a href="/wiki/Ace_Ventura_Jr.:_Pet_Detective" title="Ace Ventura Jr.: Pet Detective">Ace Ventura Jr.: Pet Detective</a></i> (2009).
</p><p>He provided vocals for <a href="/wiki/Fefe_Dobson" title="Fefe Dobson">Fefe Dobson</a> for the track "Rock It Till You Drop It" on her <a href="/wiki/Fefe_Dobson_(album)" title="Fefe Dobson (album)">self-titled debut album</a>.
</p><p>Tone Loc also voiced Fūd Wrapper, the host of the <a href="/wiki/Animatronics" title="Animatronics">animatronic</a> show <a href="/wiki/Food_Rocks" title="Food Rocks">Food Rocks</a>. This was played at <a href="/wiki/Epcot" title="Epcot">Epcot</a> from 1994 to 2004. In this latter role, he sang the song "Always Read the Wrapper", a parody of his second hit single "<a href="/wiki/Funky_Cold_Medina" title="Funky Cold Medina">Funky Cold Medina</a>".
</p><p>He appeared as Albert in episode 8 of season 2 of <i><a href="/wiki/Touched_by_an_Angel" title="Touched by an Angel">Touched by an Angel</a></i>, which aired November 11, 1995.
</p>
<h3><span id="1992.E2.80.932016:_television.2C_film_and_voice_acting"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1992–2016:_television,_film_and_voice_acting">1992–2016: television, film and voice acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tone_Loc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1992–2016: television, film and voice acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tone Loc has performed in several feature films, including <i><a href="/wiki/Poetic_Justice_(film)" title="Poetic Justice (film)">Poetic Justice</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Blank_Check_(film)" title="Blank Check (film)">Blank Check</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Posse_(1993_film)" title="Posse (1993 film)">Posse</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-GKaufman-not-guilty_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GKaufman-not-guilty-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting" title="Voice acting">voice actor</a>, Tone Loc has voiced characters in several animated television series such as <i><a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Hill" title="King of the Hill">King of the Hill</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/C_Bear_and_Jamal" title="C Bear and Jamal">C Bear and Jamal</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-GKaufman-not-guilty_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GKaufman-not-guilty-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and was featured in the animated film <i><a href="/wiki/B%C3%A9b%C3%A9%27s_Kids" class="mw-redirect" title="Bébé&#39;s Kids">Bébé's Kids</a></i>, playing the wise-cracking baby Pee Wee.<sup id="cite_ref-IMDb_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMDb-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He voiced the character Lou the Goanna in the 1992 film <i><a href="/wiki/FernGully:_The_Last_Rainforest" title="FernGully: The Last Rainforest">FernGully: The Last Rainforest</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He has also provided his signature voice for episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Chowder_(TV_series)" title="Chowder (TV series)"><i>Chowder</i></a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Uncle_Grandpa" title="Uncle Grandpa">Uncle Grandpa</a></i>, both airing on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>. In 2022, Tone Loc was featured as the singing voice of Penny Proud in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Proud_Family:_Louder_and_Prouder" title="The Proud Family: Louder and Prouder">The Proud Family: Louder and Prouder</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Disney%2B" title="Disney+">Disney+</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.93present:_I_Love_the_90s_Tour"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–present:_I_Love_the_90s_Tour">2016–present: I Love the 90s Tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tone_Loc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2016–present: I Love the 90s Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Currently, he is among the list of performers on the I Love the 90s Tour, which has performed worldwide since April 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-love_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-love-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> as well as having featured appearances on the <a href="/wiki/I_Love_the_90s:_The_Party_Continues_Tour" title="I Love the 90s: The Party Continues Tour">I Love the 90s: The Party Continues Tour</a> from July to September 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tone_Loc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tone_Loc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2010, Tone Loc was arrested for an alleged <a href="/wiki/Driving_under_the_influence" title="Driving under the influence">DUI</a>. He was released on bail claiming a medical condition had caused a <a href="/wiki/Seizure" title="Seizure">seizure</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GKaufman-not-guilty_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GKaufman-not-guilty-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 18, 2011, Tone Loc was arrested for felony domestic violence and felony possession of a <a href="/wiki/Colt_AR-15" title="Colt AR-15">Colt AR-15</a> Sporter rifle,<sup id="cite_ref-no-contest-2011_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-no-contest-2011-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> heavily restricted by California law,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> but not involved in the domestic incident<sup id="cite_ref-no-contest-2011_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-no-contest-2011-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> after an altercation with the mother of one of his children.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He was released less than three hours later after posting $50,000 bail.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On October 3, 2011, he entered a plea of no contest to both charges, and was sentenced to one day in county jail, three years of probation, 52 weeks of anger management counseling and 30 days of community service.<sup id="cite_ref-no-contest-2011_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-no-contest-2011-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 23, 2019, Tone Loc was detained by the Midland Police Department in <a href="/wiki/Midland,_Texas" title="Midland, Texas">Midland, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It was later revealed that the reason for the detention was a heated argument caused when Loc confronted someone who was wearing a <a href="/wiki/Confederate_battle_flag" class="mw-redirect" title="Confederate battle flag">Confederate battle flag</a> baseball cap. After the two arguing parties agreed to separate, Loc was released and no charges were filed.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_issues">Health issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tone_Loc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Health issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tone Lōc has collapsed onstage multiple times since 1995; some if not all of these collapses have been due to <a href="/wiki/Seizure" title="Seizure">seizures</a>, according to at least one report.<sup id="cite_ref-watch_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-watch-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On May 29, 2009, he was rushed to a hospital<sup id="cite_ref-sohh_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sohh-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> after collapsing during a concert in <a href="/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">Pensacola, Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-watch_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-watch-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The rapper cut his elbow when he fell and was released the same day.<sup id="cite_ref-sohh_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sohh-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On October 15, 2011, he was hospitalized for exhaustion after collapsing onstage during a concert in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>. He was taken to a local hospital where he was intravenously rehydrated.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Another similar incident occurred in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-bridge_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On March 16, 2013, he collapsed onstage, at a performance at the Bridge Bash in <a href="/wiki/Des_Moines,_Iowa" title="Des Moines, Iowa">Des Moines, Iowa</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-bridge_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> but he refused hospital care.<sup id="cite_ref-watch_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-watch-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He collapsed onstage on December 6, 2013, during a performance in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>, and collapsed again on November 26, 2016, during a performance in <a href="/wiki/Sioux_Falls,_South_Dakota" title="Sioux Falls, South Dakota">Sioux Falls, South Dakota</a>, but he later returned to the stage.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He wears his signature sunglasses at every performance to help reduce the likelihood of a seizure.
</p>
