<p><b>Houston Edward Summers IV</b> (born October 26, 1983), known mononymously as <b>Houston</b>, is an American former <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> singer,<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic.com_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> best known for his hit single "<a href="/wiki/I_Like_That_(Houston_song)" title="I Like That (Houston song)">I Like That</a>".
</p><p>Houston made headlines in 2005 when he attempted suicide in a London hotel room, and later gouged his eye out with a fork on the 13th floor of his hotel building. He was subsequently dropped from his contract with <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Houston_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Summers attended the Academy of Music at <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Hamilton_High_School_(Los_Angeles)" title="Alexander Hamilton High School (Los Angeles)">Hamilton High School</a> in Los Angeles.<sup id="cite_ref-AMG_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He would regularly be invited to participate in activities taking place in the school's music academy. His management team recorded a videotape of Summers's performances, which they hoped would help the singer land a recording contract. He was signed to <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AMG_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Houston_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Houston's debut single, "<a href="/wiki/I_Like_That_(Houston_song)" title="I Like That (Houston song)">I Like That</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Chingy" title="Chingy">Chingy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a> was released on March 13, 2004, and was a hit therefore peaking at number three on the US Billboard <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> chart and number 11 on both <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a> and the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>. His debut and only album, <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_Already_Written" title="It&#39;s Already Written">It's Already Written</a></i> was released on August 10, 2004, being certified <a href="/wiki/Gold_certification" class="mw-redirect" title="Gold certification">gold</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2005_suicide_attempt">2005 suicide attempt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Houston_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2005 suicide attempt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 27, 2005, while on tour in London, England, Summers suffered an emotional breakdown and reportedly tried to commit suicide by jumping from a hotel window while under the influence of <a href="/wiki/Phencyclidine" title="Phencyclidine">PCP</a>. When people in his entourage stopped him, he was restrained and locked in a first floor room. While in that room, Summers gouged his left eye out with a plastic fork.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After the incident, Summers was arrested by London police and was sent to a rehablitation facility. After a two-week stint in rehab, Houston went home to Los Angeles and apologized for the incident. Houston attributed the incident to stress caused by the music industry, saying <i>"Everything was clobbering down on me. Everything was going a little bit too hard on me"</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
