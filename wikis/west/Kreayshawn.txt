<p><b>Natassia Gail Zolot</b> (born September 24, 1989),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> better known by her stage name <b>Kreayshawn</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singing" title="Singing">singer</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Music_video_director" title="Music video director">music video director</a> from <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a>. In 2011, while serving as a member of a <a href="/wiki/Rap_group" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap group">rap group</a> with her friends, she released the music video to her debut single, "<a href="/wiki/Gucci_Gucci" title="Gucci Gucci">Gucci Gucci</a>", to <a href="/wiki/Viral_video" title="Viral video">viral success</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kreayshawn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Natassia Zolot was born on September 24, 1989, in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>. She has Russian<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (May 2022)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> ancestry on her mother's side.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (May 2022)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Her mother, Elka Zolot, was a member of the San Francisco garage <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk</a> band <a href="/wiki/The_Trashwomen" title="The Trashwomen">The Trashwomen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-village_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-village-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Kreayshawn moved to <a href="/wiki/East_Oakland,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="East Oakland, California">East Oakland</a><sup id="cite_ref-complex_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and acquired her first video camera at age ten, whereupon she started documenting her raps and films about herself and everyday life.<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the age of 13, Natassia enrolled at <a href="/wiki/MetWest_High_School" title="MetWest High School">MetWest High School</a>, which she described as a "new alternative type of high school".<sup id="cite_ref-khs_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-khs-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Instead of having the usual classes, such as English and mathematics, she had to take an internship at a local radio station three days a week. She was dissatisfied with the lack of work and wanted to go to a normal high school, so she switched to <a href="/wiki/Oakland_High_School_(Oakland,_California)" title="Oakland High School (Oakland, California)">Oakland High School</a>. However, according to Kreayshawn, she "went to none of [her] classes, ever" at her new school, which led to her finishing the term with a 0.0 grade point average and getting expelled for <a href="/wiki/Truancy" title="Truancy">truancy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-khs_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-khs-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> After taking a short break, she went to <a href="/wiki/Alameda_High_School" title="Alameda High School">Alameda High School</a> but after a few months she was expelled.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> After the incident, she then enrolled in the <a href="/wiki/Continuation_high_school" title="Continuation high school">continuation high school</a>, <a href="/wiki/Island_High_School" title="Island High School">Island High</a>, where she then completed her second year. While attending, she again began skipping school for weeks on end. After two weeks, she returned to find out the school moved to a different location. Since she had no address or any contact with the school she was unable to find the new location and never went back.<sup id="cite_ref-khs_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-khs-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the age of 16, she moved to live with her aunt in <a href="/wiki/Berkeley,_California" title="Berkeley, California">Berkeley</a> and was taking care of her little cousin. She later moved again to live with her friend <a href="/wiki/V-Nasty" title="V-Nasty">V-Nasty</a> and enrolled in a work program, where she passed her <a href="/wiki/General_Educational_Development" title="General Educational Development">GED</a> test. After graduating, she received financial support from her work program and used the money to get her own apartment.<sup id="cite_ref-khs_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-khs-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> When she was 17, she got a laptop and began recording songs and exploring her interest in cinematography, by shooting music videos for local artists such as <a href="/wiki/Lil_B" title="Lil B">Lil B</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Her videos caught the attention of <a href="/wiki/Dean_(education)" title="Dean (education)">Dean</a> Patrick Kriwanek, and she attended <a href="/wiki/Berkeley_Digital_Film_Institute" title="Berkeley Digital Film Institute">Berkeley Digital Film Institute</a> with a full scholarship for two semesters.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She then moved to Los Angeles to pursue her music video career.<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Her manager Chioke McCoy, whom she met while directing a video for DB tha General, encouraged her take on a music career of her own.<sup id="cite_ref-vulture_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vulture-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kreayshawn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kreayshawn performed in the hip hop group the <a href="/wiki/White_Girl_Mob" title="White Girl Mob">White Girl Mob</a> (WGM). The group included her friends <a href="/wiki/V-Nasty" title="V-Nasty">V-Nasty</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Debbie" title="Lil Debbie">Lil Debbie</a>. The White Girl Mob, after many disputes, disbanded in 2012. However, the entire White Girl Mob crew appears in Kreayshawn's video "Go Hard (La.La.La)".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Kreayshawn released her first <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, <i>Kittys x Choppas</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> as well as her first music video for the song "Bumpin Bumpin." On May 16, 2011, she released a music video for her single "<a href="/wiki/Gucci_Gucci" title="Gucci Gucci">Gucci Gucci</a>", which generated nearly three million views on YouTube in the first three weeks. "Gucci Gucci," according to Kreayshawn, is a song coaxing people to wear their own styles and not succumb to being "<a href="/wiki/Basic_bitch" class="mw-redirect" title="Basic bitch">basic bitches</a>,"<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The popularity of her "Gucci Gucci" video caught the attention of several record labels, and Kreayshawn quickly signed a contract with <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Kreayshawn's management was contacted by the label representing the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a> about directing a music video<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> for the band's single, "<a href="/wiki/The_Adventures_of_Rain_Dance_Maggie" title="The Adventures of Rain Dance Maggie">The Adventures of Rain Dance Maggie</a>" (from the album, <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_with_You_(album)" title="I&#39;m with You (album)">I'm with You</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In August 2011, <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> ranked Kreayshawn number 34 on their <a href="/wiki/Social_50" class="mw-redirect" title="Social 50">Social 50</a> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">chart</a> for her presence on social media.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kreayshawn sparked controversy during a <a href="/wiki/Tinychat" title="Tinychat">Tinychat</a> <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a>, wherein she insulted rapper <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a> by calling him "fake." She later retracted her statements and described it as a misunderstanding;<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> however, a few days following her statement, Kreayshawn continued making negative remarks about Ross's body weight, once again calling him "fake" and stating that "he can't rap."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> These remarks eventually led to Rick Ross verbally threatening Kreayshawn and her manager Chioke "Stretch" McCoy<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/entourage" class="extiw" title="wikt:entourage">entourage</a> during the 2011 MTV Video Music Awards. She was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award_for_Best_New_Artist" title="MTV Video Music Award for Best New Artist">MTV Video Music Award for Best New Artist</a>, but lost the category to <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_The_Creator" class="mw-redirect" title="Tyler, The Creator">Tyler, The Creator</a>. She also hosted the <a href="/wiki/Red_Carpet" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Carpet">Red Carpet</a> event.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The same night, nude pictures of herself were leaked via her Twitter being hacked. She claimed the photos were taken when she was <a href="/wiki/Underage" class="mw-redirect" title="Underage">underage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2011, Kreayshawn's first physical release, <i>Nattymari presents Kreayshawn and Sortahuman Murder in Memphis Vol 1</i>, came out on cassette tape via Clan Destine Records.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Produced by Nattymari with features by V-Nasty and Yung Hawaii Slim<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> among others, the release was limited to 100 copies and celebrated Memphis' culture of murky, underground rap distributed via tape. Side A of the tape is available for free online via clothing company Mishka NYC, while the instrumental side B is tape exclusive.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, Kreayshawn was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/O_Music_Awards" title="O Music Awards">O Music Awards</a>.
</p><p>Kreayshawn's debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Somethin%27_%27Bout_Kreay" title="Somethin&#39; &#39;Bout Kreay">Somethin' 'Bout Kreay</a></i>, was released for digital download on September 14, 2012, by <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-Amazon_mp3_release_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Amazon_mp3_release-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> It was released on CD elsewhere on September 25, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Amazon_CD_release_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Amazon_CD_release-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The singles, "<a href="/wiki/Breakfast_(Syrup)" title="Breakfast (Syrup)">Breakfast</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>, and "Go Hard (La.La.La)", preceded the album. A music video was released for "Blasé Blasé" exclusively through HungerTV.
</p><p>In 2013 "Left Ey3" was included on the soundtrack of <i>The Heat,</i> starring Melissa McCarthy and Sandra Bullock, directed by Paul Feig.
</p><p>On one of her YouTube videos, Kreayshawn stated that she is working on an EP. On February 15, 2014, Kreayshawn vlogged herself at the shoot for her new line of jewelry, along with appearing in a new music video with Bukkweatbill for their song "Pipe Down".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Kreayshawn announced in a vlog released on February 22, 2014, that she has recorded several songs that might be on her upcoming EP. She also announced that she had an interest in getting a production crew together and to start directing again along with making a reel.<sup id="cite_ref-vlogfebruary22_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vlogfebruary22-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In the same vlog, she also said that she had been unsigned from her label after she had had her first child.
</p><p>On July 24, 2014, Kreayshawn released a collection of chokers called "Hella Cute". Kreayshawn has been the creative director of OK 1984 since releasing her first collection.<sup id="cite_ref-HellaCute1_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HellaCute1-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She also released a new song, "Pizza Song".<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> When responding to a fan on Twitter, Kreayshawn stated that she was working on music and hoped to have something out by the end of 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Kreayshawnnewmusictweet_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kreayshawnnewmusictweet-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Primary,_secondary_and_tertiary_sources" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable secondary sources. (February 2020)">non-primary source needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On October 22, 2014, Kreayshawn announced on her Facebook page that she had created a podcast, <i>Lost in Thot</i>. It features Kreayshawn and Chippy Nonstop.<sup id="cite_ref-LostinThotAnnouncment_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LostinThotAnnouncment-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Primary,_secondary_and_tertiary_sources" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable secondary sources. (February 2020)">non-primary source needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> <i>Lost in Thot</i> was released on SoundCloud and Stitcher until Chippy was deported to Canada.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kreayshawn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kreayshawn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kreayshawn has stated that the biggest influences on her rapping style are the <a href="/wiki/Spice_Girls" title="Spice Girls">Spice Girls</a> and <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Kreayshawn states that Missy Elliot is her strongest influence because "she's always been stylish, and she's always kept her own thing going." In an interview for M.I.S.S., Kreayshawn stated that there were several musicians she was a major fan of, some include <a href="/wiki/Waka_Flocka_Flame" title="Waka Flocka Flame">Waka Flocka Flame</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_Keith" title="Kool Keith">Kool Keith</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Aaliyah" title="Aaliyah">Aaliyah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-M.I.S.S.interview1_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-M.I.S.S.interview1-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-M.I.S.S.interview2_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-M.I.S.S.interview2-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kreayshawn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Business_Insider" title="Business Insider">Business Insider</a></i>  listed Kreayshawn as one of the 19 Most Important White Rappers in 2011, a list which also included <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kreayshawn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On May 5, 2013, Kreayshawn announced via her Twitter that she was expecting her first child.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> She gave birth to her son on September 18, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 21, 2015, Kreayshawn's bank account was seized by the <a href="/wiki/Internal_Revenue_Service" title="Internal Revenue Service">Internal Revenue Service</a> over unpaid taxes, which she claimed was caused by her accountant robbing her.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sometime in 2021, Kreayshawn got a divorce from DJ Two Stacks.
</p>
