<p><b>Santiago Leyva</b> (born October 19, 1988), known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Fashawn</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> recording artist from <a href="/wiki/Fresno,_California" title="Fresno, California">Fresno, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He first gained major attention after releasing his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Boy_Meets_World_(album)" title="Boy Meets World (album)">Boy Meets World</a></i> in 2009, which was produced entirely by <a href="/wiki/Exile_(producer)" title="Exile (producer)">Exile</a>. The album received critical acclaim and landed him on the cover of <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a> Magazine's Top 10 Freshmen issue in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-freshmen_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-freshmen-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Since then, Fashawn has collaborated with other artists and producers such as <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evidence_(musician)" title="Evidence (musician)">Evidence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aloe_Blacc" title="Aloe Blacc">Aloe Blacc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blu_(rapper)" title="Blu (rapper)">Blu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Mike_(rapper)" title="Big Mike (rapper)">Big Mike</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brother_Ali" title="Brother Ali">Brother Ali</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Alchemist (producer)">The Alchemist</a>, and <a href="/wiki/9th_Wonder" title="9th Wonder">9th Wonder</a>. In 2014, Fashawn performed the entrance theme to <a href="/wiki/WBO" class="mw-redirect" title="WBO">WBO</a> welterweight champion <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Bradley" title="Timothy Bradley">Timothy Bradley</a>'s rematch against <a href="/wiki/Manny_Pacquiao" title="Manny Pacquiao">Manny Pacquiao</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bradley_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bradley-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 9, 2014, Fashawn signed with <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>' new label <a href="/wiki/Mass_Appeal_Records" title="Mass Appeal Records">Mass Appeal Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-massappeal_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-massappeal-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  After years of anticipation, Fashawn released his second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ecology" title="The Ecology">The Ecology</a></i> on February 24, 2015. It ranked among the top 10 most downloaded albums on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> Hip-Hop Chart during its first week of release. Since then, songs from <i>The Ecology</i> have been featured in various forms of popular media.<sup id="cite_ref-madden_nfl_16_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-madden_nfl_16-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nba_live_15_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nba_live_15-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wwe_2k16_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwe_2k16-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashawn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1988.E2.80.932009:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1988–2009:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings">1988–2009: Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashawn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1988–2009: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Santiago Leyva was born on October 19, 1988, in <a href="/wiki/Fresno,_California" title="Fresno, California">Fresno, California</a> to a Black mother and a Mexican father, where he had a rough childhood due to his father being incarcerated for a long period of time, while his single mother also had to deal with drug addiction.<sup id="cite_ref-documentary_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-documentary-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> When he was 8 years old, Fashawn and his sister were both placed in a group home, before their Uncle Roy took them into his custody as a father figure. Fashawn recalls writing his first rhyme at 8 years old and became fully engrossed in writing by 2000. In 2006, Fashawn released his first mixtape, <i>Grizzly City,</i> catching the attention of fellow Fresno MC <a href="/wiki/Planet_Asia" title="Planet Asia">Planet Asia</a>, who invited him to tour with him. Knowing the opportunity that presented him, Fashawn dropped out of school and pursued his dream. From 2007 to 2008, he established his presence throughout the <a href="/wiki/Central_California" title="Central California">Central California</a> hip-hop scene, working with manager Aren Hekimian and releasing multiple mixtapes, including: <i>The Phenom,</i> <i>Grizzly City 2,</i> <i>The Phenom 2,</i> <i>Higher Learning,</i> and <i>One Shot One Kill,</i> which was released by Orisue Clothing. <i>One Shot One Kill</i> was well received by both fans and hip-hop websites, including OnSMASH and DatPiff, gaining him more attention and momentum as an upcoming artist.
</p>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932012:_Boy_Meets_World.2C_XXL_cover.2C_and_his_collaboration_album_with_Murs"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2012:_Boy_Meets_World,_XXL_cover,_and_his_collaboration_album_with_Murs">2009–2012: <i>Boy Meets World</i>, <i>XXL</i> cover, and his collaboration album with Murs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashawn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009–2012: Boy Meets World, XXL cover, and his collaboration album with Murs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Fashawn performed throughout the United States with various hip-hop acts, including separate tours with <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Grouch_(rapper)" title="The Grouch (rapper)">The Grouch</a>. On September 24, 2009, he released a project produced entirely by <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Alchemist (producer)">The Alchemist</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Antidote_(Fashawn_Album)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Antidote (Fashawn Album)">The Antidote</a></i> to get fans hyped up for his debut solo album. On October 22, 2009, Fashawn released his first full-length album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Boy_Meets_World_(album)" title="Boy Meets World (album)">Boy Meets World</a></i>, which was produced entirely by <a href="/wiki/Exile_(producer)" title="Exile (producer)">Exile</a>. The album received critical acclaim, and Fashawn was praised for his ability to articulate the inner struggles and dreams of a young kid growing up in a tough environment. <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a> Magazine gave the album an XL rating, concluding <i>Boy Meets World</i> is a strong debut from such a young MC, and it resonates a lot more than the work of some rappers decades his senior."<sup id="cite_ref-boymeetsworld_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boymeetsworld-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In March 2010, Fashawn appeared on the cover of XXL's Top 10 Freshmen issue, standing alongside <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Rock" title="Jay Rock">Jay Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Gibbs" title="Freddie Gibbs">Freddie Gibbs</a>, and among others. This represented a prestigious benchmark for upcoming rappers who demonstrated great potential and success.
</p><p>Between 2010 and 2011, Fashawn continued to embark on various tours with Wiz Khalifa, <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brother_Ali" title="Brother Ali">Brother Ali</a>. He also performed at the <a href="/wiki/Paid_Dues" title="Paid Dues">Paid Dues</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rock_The_Bells" class="mw-redirect" title="Rock The Bells">Rock The Bells</a> festivals alongside <a href="/wiki/Blu_(rapper)" title="Blu (rapper)">Blu</a> and Exile. On February 16, 2011, he released a mixtape called <i><a href="/wiki/Higher_Learning_Vol._2" title="Higher Learning Vol. 2">Higher Learning Vol. 2</a></i>, which spawned two videos, "Down That Road" and "Big Dreams" produced by <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>. On September 25, 2012, Fashawn and underground hip-hop legend <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a> released a collaborative album called <i><a href="/wiki/This_Generation_(Murs_and_Fashawn_album)" title="This Generation (Murs and Fashawn album)">This Generation</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-murs_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-murs-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Fashawn and Murs performed many tracks together at various shows to help promote the album, including Rock The Bells 2012. On November 17, 2012, Fashawn organized a hip-hop festival in his hometown of Fresno, California called Grizzly Fest 2012, which featured performances by himself, Murs, <a href="/wiki/Husalah" title="Husalah">Husalah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Strong_Arm_Steady" title="Strong Arm Steady">Strong Arm Steady</a> and many local talent and skateboarders.<sup id="cite_ref-grizzlyfest_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grizzlyfest-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On November 20, 2012, he released the mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Champagne_%26_Styrofoam_Cups" title="Champagne &amp; Styrofoam Cups">Champagne &amp; Styrofoam Cups</a></i> which included the song "Medicine Man" featuring Wiz Khalifa.<sup id="cite_ref-wiz_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiz-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932014:_His_performance_at_the_Pacquiao_vs._Bradley_II_and_signing_to_Nas.27_record_label"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2014:_His_performance_at_the_Pacquiao_vs._Bradley_II_and_signing_to_Nas'_record_label">2013–2014: His performance at the Pacquiao vs. Bradley II and signing to Nas' record label</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashawn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013–2014: His performance at the Pacquiao vs. Bradley II and signing to Nas&#039; record label">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Fashawn-mgm.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Fashawn-mgm.jpg/250px-Fashawn-mgm.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="250" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Fashawn-mgm.jpg/375px-Fashawn-mgm.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Fashawn-mgm.jpg/500px-Fashawn-mgm.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1214" data-file-height="1214" /></a><figcaption>Fashawn speaking about his song "Champion" during the final press conference for the <a href="/wiki/Manny_Pacquiao_vs._Timothy_Bradley_II" title="Manny Pacquiao vs. Timothy Bradley II">Manny Pacquiao vs. Timothy Bradley II</a> championship rematch in Las Vegas.</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2013, Fashawn continued to have a notable presence in various musical and mainstream outlets.  He performed at various major hip-hop festivals such as Paid Dues, Rock the Bells, and <a href="/wiki/Czech_hip_hop#Hip_Hop_Kemp" title="Czech hip hop">Hip Hop Kemp</a> where he joined <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> on stage for a duo freestyle session.<sup id="cite_ref-kendrick_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kendrick-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On November 6, 2013, Fashawn released a video for a new song called "The Beginning", produced by Evidence and directed by both Evidence and Punit Dhesi.  On December 9, 2013, Fashawn's artistry was the focus feature in a web episode of OTW Perspectives by <a href="/wiki/Vans" title="Vans">Vans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-vans_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vans-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On February 14, 2014, he released a new song "Ladies" for Valentine's Day, dedicating it to the women who struggle to stay strong and raise the next generation of kids.
</p><p>On March 24, 2014, a rap icon <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> brought out Fashawn during the Mass Appeal showcase at <a href="/wiki/SXSW" class="mw-redirect" title="SXSW">SXSW</a> 2014 in Austin, Texas, where Fashawn performed a new song "F.T.W." in front of the sold-out crowd.<sup id="cite_ref-sxsw_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sxsw-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, during a <a href="/wiki/Reddit" title="Reddit">Reddit</a> "Ask Me Anything" session, Nas mentioned Fashawn as one of two contemporary artists who recently caught his ear (the other artist being <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Nehru" title="Bishop Nehru">Bishop Nehru</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-reddit_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reddit-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On April 12, 2014, during the infamous <a href="/wiki/WBO" class="mw-redirect" title="WBO">WBO</a> welterweight title rematch <a href="/wiki/Manny_Pacquiao_vs._Timothy_Bradley_II" title="Manny Pacquiao vs. Timothy Bradley II">Manny Pacquiao vs. Timothy Bradley II</a> at the <a href="/wiki/MGM_Grand_Garden_Arena" title="MGM Grand Garden Arena">MGM Grand Garden Arena</a>, the champion <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Bradley" title="Timothy Bradley">Timothy Bradley</a> appointed Fashawn to lead him into the ring while performing "Champion" produced by Exile.<sup id="cite_ref-bradley_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bradley-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> "What I like about this is that it is real hip-hop," Bradley said in regards to Fashawn's big performance. "It is about heart and character, not about partying it up in a club or all that kind of stuff that so many records are about."
</p><p>On June 9, 2014, a variety of media outlets reported that Fashawn had officially signed to <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>' <a href="/wiki/Mass_Appeal_Records" title="Mass Appeal Records">Mass Appeal Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bet_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bet-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> "Fashawn is one of the most underrated in the game," Nas said in a statement. "The world is gonna know soon. We're excited to have him as a part of the family."
</p><p>On December 18, 2014, as a prelude to his upcoming sophomore album, Fashawn released an EP with <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/FASH-ionably_Late" title="FASH-ionably Late">FASH-ionably Late</a></i>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.93present:_The_Ecology"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–present:_The_Ecology">2015–present: <i>The Ecology</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fashawn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015–present: The Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Fashawn-nas-aloe-blacc.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Fashawn-nas-aloe-blacc.jpg/250px-Fashawn-nas-aloe-blacc.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="167" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Fashawn-nas-aloe-blacc.jpg/375px-Fashawn-nas-aloe-blacc.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Fashawn-nas-aloe-blacc.jpg/500px-Fashawn-nas-aloe-blacc.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2284" data-file-height="1522" /></a><figcaption>Fashawn is joined by <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aloe_Blacc" title="Aloe Blacc">Aloe Blacc</a> during <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ecology" title="The Ecology">The Ecology</a></i> album release concert in Los Angeles.</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2014, after years of anticipation, it was announced Fashawn's second full-length solo album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ecology" title="The Ecology">The Ecology</a></i> was in the works. In an interview, Fashawn stated, "<i>The Ecology</i> has been a concept that started at least five years ago for me. I took the time to really work on this project because I knew that it would be important not just for my career, but for hip-hop. There could be no better time for all these blessings to be coming my way, and I'm just so happy to say that <i>The Ecology</i> will be coming out."<sup id="cite_ref-dubcnn_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dubcnn-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2015, Fashawn announced <i>The Ecology</i> would be officially released on February 24, 2015, under <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>' <a href="/wiki/Mass_Appeal_Records" title="Mass Appeal Records">Mass Appeal Records</a>, and he would also embark on a nationwide tour, soon after its release.<sup id="cite_ref-release_date_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-release_date-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The official track list soon followed, which confirmed guest features by <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> (who also served as executive producer), <a href="/wiki/Aloe_Blacc" title="Aloe Blacc">Aloe Blacc</a>, <a href="/wiki/BJ_the_Chicago_Kid" title="BJ the Chicago Kid">BJ the Chicago Kid</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dom_Kennedy" title="Dom Kennedy">Dom Kennedy</a>. The production would be handled by <a href="/wiki/Exile_(producer)" title="Exile (producer)">Exile</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khalil" title="DJ Khalil">DJ Khalil</a>, among others. On February 20, 2015, Fashawn surprised eager fans by making <i>The Ecology</i> available for purchase on iTunes ahead of schedule. Physical CD and vinyl copies of <i>The Ecology</i> maintained the original release date of February 24, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-early_release_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-early_release-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> During its first week of release, <i>The Ecology</i> ranked among the Top 10 Hip-Hop Albums on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>. Since then, songs from the album, including "<a href="/wiki/Something_to_Believe_In_(Fashawn_song)" title="Something to Believe In (Fashawn song)">Something to Believe In</a>", have been featured in various forms of popular media, including the <a href="/wiki/Madden_NFL" title="Madden NFL">Madden NFL</a>, <a href="/wiki/NBA_Live" title="NBA Live">NBA Live</a>, and <a href="/wiki/WWE_2K" title="WWE 2K">WWE 2K</a> video game series.<sup id="cite_ref-madden_nfl_16_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-madden_nfl_16-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nba_live_15_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nba_live_15-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wwe_2k16_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwe_2k16-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2016, Fashawn joined forces with <a href="/wiki/Black_Thought" title="Black Thought">Black Thought</a>, <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Del_the_Funky_Homosapien" title="Del the Funky Homosapien">Del the Funky Homosapien</a> to record a new track called "Rise Up" for the <a href="/wiki/Capcom" title="Capcom">Capcom</a> video game <a href="/wiki/Street_Fighter_V" title="Street Fighter V">Street Fighter V</a>. A music video for the song was released prior to the game's launch date, and includes appearances by Fashawn and his fellow collaborators.<sup id="cite_ref-streetfighter_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-streetfighter-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On April 28, 2017, Fashawn released a single called "Rant".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
