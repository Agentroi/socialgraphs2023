<p><b>Teddy Park</b> (born <b>Park Hong-jun</b> on September 14, 1978)<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Korean_Americans" title="Korean Americans">Korean–American</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>. Born in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, Park moved to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> with his family as a young child. When he was 17 years old, Park and his friend <a href="/wiki/Taebin" title="Taebin">Danny</a> flew to South Korea during summer vacation to audition for <a href="/wiki/YG_Entertainment" title="YG Entertainment">YG Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Both were signed immediately, and after finishing high school in the States, moved to Korea to pursue music.
</p><p>In 1998, Park debuted in the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/1TYM" title="1TYM">1TYM</a> with his friend Danny, Jinhwan, and Baekyoung.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Teddy_Park_Interview_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Teddy_Park_Interview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Together, the group recorded five studio albums with Park as their primary songwriter and producer. After his performing career with 1TYM ended in January 2006, Park became an in-house producer for <a href="/wiki/YG_Entertainment" title="YG Entertainment">YG Entertainment</a> and co-founded <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Label" title="The Black Label">The Black Label</a> in 2016. He has co-produced and co-written songs for YG artists such as <a href="/wiki/Jinusean" title="Jinusean">Jinusean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seven_(Korean_singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Seven (Korean singer)">Seven</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Bang_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Bang (band)">Big Bang</a>, <a href="/wiki/2NE1" title="2NE1">2NE1</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hi" title="Lee Hi">Lee Hi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackpink" title="Blackpink">Blackpink</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jeon_Somi" title="Jeon Somi">Jeon Somi</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Uhm_Jung-hwa" title="Uhm Jung-hwa">Uhm Jung-hwa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sunmi" title="Sunmi">Sunmi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-major_works_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-major_works-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2NE1_works_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2NE1_works-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teddy_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teddy_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Park was born in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, but moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> as a young child.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He learned the piano as a child, but as he grew up, he was more interested in playing the guitar and beatboxing.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> After his father was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Bar" class="mw-redirect" title="Diamond Bar">Diamond Bar</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, Park attended <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Bar_High_School" title="Diamond Bar High School">Diamond Bar High School</a> and became friends with future bandmate <a href="/wiki/Taebin" title="Taebin">Danny Im</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Growing up, the two sang together at karaoke bars and tinkered with recording equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> They were discovered when they were teenagers by a producer who worked with <a href="/wiki/Yang_Hyun-suk" title="Yang Hyun-suk">Yang Hyun-suk</a>, founder of YG Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After auditioning for Yang and sending in demo tapes, the two were signed to his newly established label, and moved to <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Although Park enrolled in a university when he returned to South Korea, he dropped out to focus on his career.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="1998.E2.80.932006:_Work_with_1TYM"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1998–2006:_Work_with_1TYM">1998–2006: Work with 1TYM</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teddy_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1998–2006: Work with 1TYM">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1TYM" title="1TYM">1TYM</a></div>
<p>Park and Im were joined by rappers Jinhwan and Baekyoung and debuted as 1TYM in 1998 with the album, <i>One Time for Your Mind</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It became one of the best-selling albums of the year and won several major awards.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> After the first album, Park began to have more input into the songs, and had a more prominent role in producing. In 2000, 1TYM released their second album <i>2nd Round</i>. The songs 'One Love'<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and '쾌지나 칭칭' (Let's Sing and Dance Together),<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> which he wrote and produced, won first place on TV music programs. <i>This was followed by Third Time Fo' Yo' Mind</i> in 2001. In 2003, 1TYM released their fourth album <i>Once N 4 All,</i> which yielded two singles: "Hot 뜨거",<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> an up-beat hip-hop track with reggae rhythms, and "Without You", an emotional R&amp;B ballad.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The group went on a two-year hiatus in which member Im embarked on a brief solo career under the name of Taebin. The group reunited for the album <i>One Way</i> in 2005, their fifth and last studio album, before going on hiatus due to Jinhwan's <a href="/wiki/Conscription_in_South_Korea" title="Conscription in South Korea">mandatory military service</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Their albums have sold a combined total of 780,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Though they were predominately hip hop artists, 1TYM was influenced by <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%26B" class="mw-redirect" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>. 1TYM along with the duo <a href="/wiki/Jinusean" title="Jinusean">Jinusean</a> were credited for bringing hip hop music and YG Entertainment to the mainstream.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At 22 years old, Park also began producing for other artists on the label, most of whom were much older and more experienced than him.<sup id="cite_ref-1TYM_discography_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1TYM_discography-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.93present:_Producer_for_YG_Entertainment"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–present:_Producer_for_YG_Entertainment">2006–present: Producer for YG Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teddy_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006–present: Producer for YG Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With 1TYM on hiatus, Park transitioned into the role of producer for other YG artists, his first major contribution being "La La La" for Seven's album <i>Sevolution</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Productions_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Productions-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Big_Bang_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Bang (band)">Big Bang</a> for several of their songs,<sup id="cite_ref-Teddy_Park_Interview_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Teddy_Park_Interview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> most notably "<a href="/wiki/Sunset_Glow" title="Sunset Glow">Sunset Glow</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-major_works_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-major_works-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> When BigBang member <a href="/wiki/Taeyang" title="Taeyang">Taeyang</a> embarked on a solo career in 2008, Park produced his <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_(EP)" title="Hot (EP)">Hot</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-Teddy_Park_Interview_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Teddy_Park_Interview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The following year, Park produced "<a href="/wiki/Lollipop_(Big_Bang_and_2NE1_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lollipop (Big Bang and 2NE1 song)">Lollipop</a>" for a collaboration between BigBang and <a href="/wiki/2NE1" title="2NE1">2NE1</a>. The song went on to top the <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Chart" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaon Chart">Gaon Chart</a> during April.<sup id="cite_ref-2NE1_Gaon_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2NE1_Gaon-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Park would go on to have a hand in producing a majority of 2NE1's work, including their debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/2NE1_(2009_EP)" title="2NE1 (2009 EP)">2NE1</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-2NE1_works_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2NE1_works-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> (2009) and studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/To_Anyone" title="To Anyone">To Anyone</a></i> (2010). Two of the three main singles, "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Nobody_(2NE1_song)" title="Can&#39;t Nobody (2NE1 song)">Can't Nobody</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Go_Away_(2NE1_song)" title="Go Away (2NE1 song)">Go Away</a>," from the latter were also composed by Park.<sup id="cite_ref-Productions_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Productions-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> To continue producing for 2NE1, Park rejected an offer to work with <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-major_works_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-major_works-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He collaborated with Seven again to produce the <a href="/wiki/Promotional_single" class="mw-redirect" title="Promotional single">promotional single</a> "Better Together," in addition to other songs for the EP <i><a href="/wiki/Digital_Bounce" title="Digital Bounce">Digital Bounce</a></i> (2010)<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and was featured in <a href="/wiki/G-Dragon" title="G-Dragon">G-Dragon</a>'s song "The Leaders."<sup id="cite_ref-The_Leaders_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Leaders-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He also had a role in Taeyang's solo album <a href="/wiki/Solar_(Taeyang_album)" title="Solar (Taeyang album)"><i>Solar</i></a>, including the singles "Where U At" and "Wedding Dress."<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the year, Park also participated in the production of <a href="/wiki/GD_%26_TOP" title="GD &amp; TOP">GD &amp; TOP</a>'s <a href="/wiki/GD_%26_TOP_(album)" title="GD &amp; TOP (album)">album</a>, and co-composed both "High High" and "Oh Yeah".<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, he wrote and produced 2NE1's second EP <i><a href="/wiki/2NE1_(2011_EP)" title="2NE1 (2011 EP)"><i>2NE1</i></a></i>, scoring three number-one hits: "<a href="/wiki/Lonely_(2NE1_song)" title="Lonely (2NE1 song)">Lonely</a>", "<a href="/wiki/I_Am_the_Best" title="I Am the Best">I Am the Best</a>", and "Ugly".
</p><p>In the first half of 2013, Park produced <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hi" title="Lee Hi">Lee Hi</a> hit single "Rose," 2NE1's leader CL's solo debut single "<a href="/wiki/The_Baddest_Female" title="The Baddest Female">The Baddest Female</a>," and <a href="/wiki/Kang_Seung-yoon" title="Kang Seung-yoon">Kang Seung-yoon</a>'s YG debut "Wild &amp; Young." The following year, he wrote and produced 2ne1's second and final studio album, <a href="/wiki/Crush_(2NE1_album)" title="Crush (2NE1 album)">Crush</a>.
</p><p>In 2015, he wrote and produced <a href="/wiki/Big_Bang_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Bang (band)">Big Bang</a>'s special project single albums <i><a href="/wiki/M_(Big_Bang_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="M (Big Bang album)">M</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/A_(Big_Bang_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="A (Big Bang album)">A</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/D_(Big_Bang_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="D (Big Bang album)">D</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/E_(Big_Bang_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="E (Big Bang album)">E</a></i> which became major hits.
</p><p>In 2016, Park began working with girl group <a href="/wiki/Blackpink" title="Blackpink">Blackpink</a> and produced their first two single albums <i><a href="/wiki/Square_One_(single_album)" title="Square One (single album)">Square One</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Square_Two" title="Square Two">Square Two</a></i>, consisting of number-one hit "<a href="/wiki/Whistle_(Blackpink_song)" title="Whistle (Blackpink song)">Whistle</a>" and top-ten hits "<a href="/wiki/Boombayah" title="Boombayah">Boombayah</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Playing_with_Fire_(Blackpink_song)" title="Playing with Fire (Blackpink song)">Playing with Fire</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Stay_(Blackpink_song)" title="Stay (Blackpink song)">Stay</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> That same year, he founded <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Label" title="The Black Label">The Black Label</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> of <a href="/wiki/YG_Entertainment" title="YG Entertainment">YG Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The label currently houses <a href="/wiki/Zion.T" title="Zion.T">Zion.T</a>, Korea's second bestselling artist of 2015 after BigBang, and Korean-Canadian singer and former <a href="/wiki/I.O.I" title="I.O.I">I.O.I</a> member <a href="/wiki/Jeon_Somi" title="Jeon Somi">Jeon Somi</a>.
</p><p>In 2017, Park wrote and produced the hit single "<a href="/wiki/As_If_It%27s_Your_Last" title="As If It&#39;s Your Last">As If It's Your Last</a>" for Blackpink. In 2018, he wrote and produced their debut EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Square_Up_(EP)" class="mw-redirect" title="Square Up (EP)">Square Up</a></i>, including the number-one single "<a href="/wiki/Ddu-Du_Ddu-Du" title="Ddu-Du Ddu-Du">Ddu-Du Ddu-Du</a>" as well as "<a href="/wiki/Forever_Young_(Blackpink_song)" title="Forever Young (Blackpink song)">Forever Young</a>". Park also wrote and produced Blackpink member <a href="/wiki/Jennie_(singer)" title="Jennie (singer)">Jennie</a>'s number-one hit song "<a href="/wiki/Solo_(Jennie_song)" title="Solo (Jennie song)">Solo</a>". Park produced for the first time for a non-YG Entertainment artist with "Just Dance," the theme song for the <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">reality television</a> competition <i><a href="/wiki/Mix_Nine" title="Mix Nine">Mix Nine</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Participants singing and dancing to the song was released<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and three versions of the song were released overall: a male version led by Hyojin of <a href="/wiki/ONF_(band)" title="ONF (band)">ONF</a>, a female version led by Lee Sujin from Fave Entertainment, and a co-ed version performed by both teams.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He produced two songs for singer <a href="/wiki/Sunmi" title="Sunmi">Sunmi</a>: "<a href="/wiki/Gashina" title="Gashina">Gashina</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Heroine_(Sunmi_song)" title="Heroine (Sunmi song)">Heroine</a>".
</p><p>In 2019, Park wrote and produced Blackpink's second EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Kill_This_Love" title="Kill This Love">Kill This Love</a></i>. He also wrote and produced singer <a href="/wiki/Jeon_Somi" title="Jeon Somi">Jeon Somi</a>'s debut single "<a href="/wiki/Birthday_(Jeon_Somi_song)" title="Birthday (Jeon Somi song)">Birthday</a>".
</p><p>In 2020, Park played a major role in writing and producing Blackpink's debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Album_(Blackpink_album)" title="The Album (Blackpink album)">The Album</a></i>, which consisted of the number-one song "<a href="/wiki/How_You_Like_That" title="How You Like That">How You Like That</a>" as well as hit singles "<a href="/wiki/Ice_Cream_(Blackpink_and_Selena_Gomez_song)" title="Ice Cream (Blackpink and Selena Gomez song)">Ice Cream</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Lovesick_Girls" title="Lovesick Girls">Lovesick Girls</a>".
</p><p>In 2021, Park wrote and produced Blackpink members <a href="/wiki/Ros%C3%A9_(singer)" title="Rosé (singer)">Rosé</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lisa_(rapper)" title="Lisa (rapper)">Lisa</a>'s respective debut solo singles "<a href="/wiki/On_the_Ground" title="On the Ground">On the Ground</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Lalisa_(song)" title="Lalisa (song)">Lalisa</a>". He also wrote and produced many of the songs on Jeon Somi's debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/XOXO_(Jeon_Somi_album)" title="XOXO (Jeon Somi album)">XOXO</a></i>, which included top-ten single "<a href="/wiki/Dumb_Dumb_(Jeon_Somi_song)" title="Dumb Dumb (Jeon Somi song)">Dumb Dumb</a>".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_influences">Musical style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teddy_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Musical style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Park's production style often incorporates <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">contemporary R&amp;B</a>, especially when working with Taeyang and Seven.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He also features <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> in his songs,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> acknowledging the genre's influence on 2NE1's <a href="/wiki/2NE1_(2009_EP)" title="2NE1 (2009 EP)">debut EP</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2NE1_EP_style_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2NE1_EP_style-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> His work with 2NE1's first <a href="/wiki/To_Anyone" title="To Anyone">full-length album</a> and <a href="/wiki/2NE1_(2011_EP)" title="2NE1 (2011 EP)">2011 EP</a> contained songs that are <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a>, in addition to featuring <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house</a> elements.<sup id="cite_ref-To_Anyone_style_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-To_Anyone_style-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The song "<a href="/wiki/Lonely_(2NE1_song)" title="Lonely (2NE1 song)">Lonely</a>" by 2NE1 was praised for its <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_Song" class="mw-redirect" title="Acoustic Song">acoustic</a> sound.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Park also experimented with <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic music</a> when working with BigBang.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end of 2009, 10Asia named Park as one of the top 10 people of 2009 for his participation in some of the year's biggest <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">k-pop</a> hits.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, <i>Seoul Sports</i> published a list of "Most Influential Power People of K-pop," which was ranked by music industry executives. Park placed fifth in the category of best producer.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> That same year, he and <a href="/wiki/G-Dragon" title="G-Dragon">G-Dragon</a> tied for the most earned royalties for lyrics writing and song composition within the field of popular music from the Korean Music Copyright Association.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Park gets paid about US$850,000 a year from the Korean Music Copyright Association.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teddy_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2018, there were concerns that <a href="/wiki/Sunmi" title="Sunmi">Sunmi</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Heroine_(Sunmi_song)" title="Heroine (Sunmi song)">Heroine</a>" has plagiarized <a href="/wiki/Cheryl_Cole" class="mw-redirect" title="Cheryl Cole">Cheryl</a>'s debut solo single "<a href="/wiki/Fight_for_This_Love" title="Fight for This Love">Fight for This Love</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
Sunmi's agency said that they were investigating the issue.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> It later concluded with stating, "We unequivocally reveal that 'Heroine' is 100 percent an original creative work with absolutely no reference to the song that has been named in the controversy."<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teddy_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After becoming an in-house producer for YG Entertainment, Park has refused to appear on the air. In 2014, he purchased a building in <a href="/wiki/Hongdae,_Seoul" class="mw-redirect" title="Hongdae, Seoul">Hongdae, Seoul</a> worth US$9 million and opened his own cafe called Twosome Studio in that building.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, he purchased a US$6 million house in <a href="/wiki/Hannam-dong" title="Hannam-dong">Hannam-dong, Seoul</a> from <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Group" title="Hyundai Group">Hyundai Group</a> founder's nephew.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
