<p><b>Johnny Reed McKinzie Jr.</b> (born March 31, 1985), better known by his stage name <b>Jay Rock</b>, is an American rapper and songwriter. Born and raised in Los Angeles, California, he signed to indie label <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a> (TDE), in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Under TDE, Rock secured a record deal in 2007 with <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a> and later <a href="/wiki/Asylum_Records" title="Asylum Records">Asylum Records</a>, but mergers fell through and Rock departed from the label shortly after. He then signed with fellow American rapper <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Strange_Music" title="Strange Music">Strange Music</a>, in a <a href="/wiki/Joint-venture" class="mw-redirect" title="Joint-venture">joint-venture</a> deal with TDE.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2006, Rock has amassed several TDE-released <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a> and first gained major recognition following his 2008 commercial debut single, "<a href="/wiki/All_My_Life_(In_the_Ghetto)" title="All My Life (In the Ghetto)">All My Life (In the Ghetto)</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a>). In 2011, after signing his deal with Strange Music, Rock's often-delayed debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Follow_Me_Home_(album)" title="Follow Me Home (album)">Follow Me Home</a></i>, was released in July of that year. The album was supported by the aforementioned single, as well as "<a href="/wiki/Hood_Gone_Love_It" title="Hood Gone Love It">Hood Gone Love It</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>). His second album <i><a href="/wiki/90059" title="90059">90059</a></i> was released in September 2015 and was supported by the singles "<a href="/wiki/Money_Trees_Deuce" title="Money Trees Deuce">Money Trees Deuce</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Lance_Skiiiwalker" title="Lance Skiiiwalker">Lance Skiiiwalker</a>), "Gumbo" and the title-track. In June 2018, Rock released his third album <i><a href="/wiki/Redemption_(Jay_Rock_album)" title="Redemption (Jay Rock album)">Redemption</a></i>. 
</p><p>Apart from his solo career, Rock is also known for being a member of the hip hop supergroup <a href="/wiki/Black_Hippy" title="Black Hippy">Black Hippy</a>, alongside fellow <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast rappers</a> and TDE label-mates <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul" title="Ab-Soul">Ab-Soul</a>. In 2019, Jay Rock received his first <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance">Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance</a> for "<a href="/wiki/King%27s_Dead" title="King&#39;s Dead">King's Dead</a>".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jay_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1985.E2.80.932007:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1985–2007:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings">1985–2007: Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jay_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1985–2007: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jay Rock was born Johnny Reed McKinzie Jr. on March 31, 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-TDE_Artist_Page_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TDE_Artist_Page-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Rock was raised in <a href="/wiki/Watts,_Los_Angeles" title="Watts, Los Angeles">Watts</a>, a neighborhood in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-TDE_Artist_Page_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TDE_Artist_Page-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/Nickerson_Gardens" title="Nickerson Gardens">Nickerson Gardens</a> <a href="/wiki/Public_housing" title="Public housing">projects</a>. As a youth, he became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Bounty_Hunter_Bloods" class="mw-redirect" title="Bounty Hunter Bloods">Bounty Hunter Bloods</a> street gang and attended <a href="/wiki/Locke_High_School" title="Locke High School">Locke High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> During this time, he was jailed on two occasions, due to the area's anti-gang injunction.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic.com_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2005, Anthony "Top Dawg" Tiffith, <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">chief executive officer</a> (CEO) of independent record label <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a> (TDE), discovered Rock and signed him to his label, after hearing a <a href="/wiki/Verse_(popular_music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Verse (popular music)">verse</a> of his. After signing to TDE, Rock released several <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a> throughout his neighborhood and via the internet. With the help of TDE, Rock was able to secure a recording contract with <a href="/wiki/Asylum_Records" title="Asylum Records">Asylum Records</a> and later a joint venture deal with <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>, in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic.com_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-streetgangs_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-streetgangs-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> At TDE, Jay Rock met fellow labelmate <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, and the two would go on multiple tours together, honing their craft. The two often feature on each other's records, and along with <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul" title="Ab-Soul">Ab-Soul</a> and <a href="/wiki/ScHoolboy_Q" class="mw-redirect" title="ScHoolboy Q">ScHoolboy Q</a> would form the supergroup Black Hippy.
</p>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.932013:_Follow_Me_Home"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–2013:_Follow_Me_Home">2008–2013: <i>Follow Me Home</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jay_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008–2013: Follow Me Home">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Follow_Me_Home_(Jay_Rock_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Follow Me Home (Jay Rock album)">Follow Me Home (Jay Rock album)</a></div>
<p>Rock released his commercial debut single, "<a href="/wiki/All_My_Life_(In_the_Ghetto)" title="All My Life (In the Ghetto)">All My Life (In the Ghetto)</a>" in late 2008, featuring fellow American recording artists <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a>. The single was promoted as one of "<a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes</a>'s Free Downloads" in early 2009. In 2010, Rock was featured on the cover of <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)"><i>XXL</i></a>'s annual Top 10 Freshmen issue.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He was also named by <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> one of 2010's breakthrough MCs.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Jay Rock was featured on <a href="/wiki/Omarion" title="Omarion">Omarion</a>'s song "Hoodie" and was supposed to be featured on <a href="/wiki/Flo_Rida" title="Flo Rida">Flo Rida</a>'s third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Only_One_Flo" class="mw-redirect" title="Only One Flo">Only One Flo</a></i> but was left off the final track listing.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rock's mixtape <i>Tales From the Hood 2</i> went to iTunes on March 28, 2010, and his mixtape <i>From the Hood to the Cover of XXL</i> was released to iTunes on July 24, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He also toured with <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, and other rappers on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Invitation_Tour" title="The Invitation Tour">The Invitation Tour</a></i>. Around the same time he left Warner Bros. due to them delaying his album.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Jay Rock then signed with indie record label <a href="/wiki/Strange_Music" title="Strange Music">Strange Music</a> in the Fall of 2010, on a multi-album deal after leaving from Warner Bros.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag.com_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag.com-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Jay Rock then went on tour with <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>, <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glasses_Malone" title="Glasses Malone">Glasses Malone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kutt_Calhoun" title="Kutt Calhoun">Kutt Calhoun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> on the "Independent Grind National Tour".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> His mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Friday_(Jay_Rock_mixtape)" title="Black Friday (Jay Rock mixtape)">Black Friday</a></i>, was released December 7, 2010, to iTunes.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 21, 2011, Rock released his second single, "<a href="/wiki/Hood_Gone_Love_It" title="Hood Gone Love It">Hood Gone Love It</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> His debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Follow_Me_Home_(Jay_Rock_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Follow Me Home (Jay Rock album)">Follow Me Home</a></i> was released on July 26, 2011, under independent record labels Strange Music and Top Dawg.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag.com_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag.com-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The album debuted at #83 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, selling 5,300 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Hood_Gone_Love_It" title="Hood Gone Love It">Hood Gone Love It</a>" was featured in the character trailer for Franklin, a main protagonist in the video-game <a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a>. The song is also featured in the in-game radio station <i>Radio Los Santos</i>.
</p><p>During 2012, Rock toured with the rest of Black Hippy and MMG artist <a href="/wiki/Stalley" title="Stalley">Stalley</a>, on <a href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Entertainment Television">BET</a>'s Music Matters Tour.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In October 2012, Rock's appearance on Kendrick Lamar's major-label debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Good_kid,_m.A.A.d_city" class="mw-redirect" title="Good kid, m.A.A.d city">good kid, m.A.A.d city</a></i>, was highly significant as he was the only other rapper from Black Hippy, to be featured on the standard edition of the album. Rock is featured on the song "<a href="/wiki/Money_Trees" title="Money Trees">Money Trees</a>", for which his <a href="/wiki/Verse_(popular_music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Verse (popular music)">verse</a> was highly acclaimed.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He would later perform "Money Trees", with Lamar, at the <a href="/wiki/2013_BET_Hip_Hop_Awards" title="2013 BET Hip Hop Awards">2013 BET Hip Hop Awards</a>, along with appearing in the TDE Cypher. <i>XXL</i> named his verse the fourth best of the night.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932017:_90059"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2017:_90059">2013–2017: <i>90059</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jay_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013–2017: 90059">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/90059" title="90059">90059</a></div>
<p>In November 2013, Rock told <a href="/wiki/Power_105" class="mw-redirect" title="Power 105">Power 105</a> that his second studio album would be released in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> With the release of the <a href="/wiki/56th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="56th Annual Grammy Awards">56th Annual Grammy Awards</a> nominations, it was revealed Rock was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Album_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Album of the Year">Album of the Year</a> as a featured artist for participating on Kendrick Lamar's <i>good kid, m.A.A.d city</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> After the release of label-mate <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul" title="Ab-Soul">Ab-Soul</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/These_Days..._(Ab-Soul_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="These Days... (Ab-Soul album)">These Days...</a></i> in June 2014, TDE's CEO confirmed that Jay Rock would be the next artist from the label to release an album.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2014, <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a> revealed that Rock had left his <a href="/wiki/Strange_Music" title="Strange Music">Strange Music</a> imprint and is now only signed to Top Dawg Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On October 29, 2014, Jay Rock released a song titled "<a href="/wiki/Pay_for_It_(Jay_Rock_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pay for It (Jay Rock song)">Pay for It</a>", featuring Kendrick Lamar and <a href="/wiki/Chantal_Kreviazuk" title="Chantal Kreviazuk">Chantal Kreviazuk</a>, as a promotional single.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On November 15, 2014, Rock appeared on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> late-night live television sketch comedy and variety show <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>, alongside the musical guest Lamar, where they performed "Pay For It".<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 15, 2016, Jay Rock was involved in a harsh motorcycle accident according to <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a>'s CEO Anthony Tiffith.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> According to Complex, he recovered very well, however he had a lot of broken bones.<sup id="cite_ref-Jay_Rock_survives_accident_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jay_Rock_survives_accident-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He was in surgery and successfully came out of it.<sup id="cite_ref-Jay_Rock_survives_accident_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jay_Rock_survives_accident-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2018.E2.80.93present:_Redemption"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–present:_Redemption">2018–present: <i>Redemption</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jay_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2018–present: Redemption">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Redemption_(Jay_Rock_album)" title="Redemption (Jay Rock album)">Redemption (Jay Rock album)</a></div>
<p>On January 12, 2018, Jay Rock released the single "<a href="/wiki/King%27s_Dead" title="King&#39;s Dead">King's Dead</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Blake_(musician)" title="James Blake (musician)">James Blake</a>. The song was produced by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Will_Made_It" title="Mike Will Made It">Mike Will Made It</a> and <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Walton" title="Teddy Walton">Teddy Walton</a> serving as the lead single to his third studio album and as a single from the soundtrack <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Panther_(film)" title="Black Panther (film)">Black Panther: The Album</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> "King's Dead" became Jay Rock's first song to chart on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">US <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> On May 16, 2018, he released the single "<a href="/wiki/Win_(song)" title="Win (song)">Win</a>", which served as the second single from his third studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After teasing a 2018 release in January, the album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Redemption_(Jay_Rock_album)" title="Redemption (Jay Rock album)">Redemption</a></i>, was announced on May 21, 2018, alongside the cover art and its release date, which was released on June 15, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/61st_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="61st Annual Grammy Awards">2019 Grammy Awards</a>, both "King's Dead" and "Win" received nominations in the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance">Best Rap Performance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Song" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Song">Best Rap Song</a> categories, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> In February 2019, Rock won his first Grammy award for "King's Dead".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
