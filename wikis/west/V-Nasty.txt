					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American rapper</div>
<p class="mw-empty-elt">

</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; font-size: 125%;"><div class="">V-Nasty</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Vanessa Renee Reece</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday">1990-11-11</span>) </span>November 11, 1990<span class="noprint ForceAgeToShow"> (age&#160;32)</span><br /><a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a>, U.S.</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip Hop music">Hip hop</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role"><a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">Rapper</a>, singer</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">2009–present</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data">Pinnacle, jungle fever</td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Vanessa Renee Reece</b> (born November 11, 1990),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known by her stage name <b>V-Nasty</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a> from <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a>. She is best known for being part of the group <a href="/wiki/White_Girl_Mob" title="White Girl Mob">White Girl Mob</a> with <a href="/wiki/Kreayshawn" title="Kreayshawn">Kreayshawn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Debbie" title="Lil Debbie">Lil Debbie</a>, and for collaborating with <a href="/wiki/Atlanta,_GA" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta, GA">Atlanta</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_bio_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex_bio-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Reece released her debut studio album with Gucci Mane titled <i><a href="/wiki/BAYTL" title="BAYTL">BAYTL</a></i> in 2011 through <a href="/wiki/1017_Records" title="1017 Records">1017 Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, she signed a record deal with independent label Pinnacle Records and started working on her second album, as well as a reality TV show.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly3_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
In 2014, V-Nasty released a <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">Mixtape</a> titled "11Lem"<sup id="cite_ref-m.datpiff.com_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-m.datpiff.com-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> along with a new single titled "Tweekin".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span></h2>
<p>Reece grew up on the 35th block of <a href="/wiki/East_Oakland,_Oakland,_California" title="East Oakland, Oakland, California">East Oakland</a> in a neighborhood known for dealing drugs and a high crime rate.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> She was born to a <a href="/wiki/White_people" title="White people">white</a> mother and a <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_people" title="Vietnamese people">Vietnamese</a> father.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_bio_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex_bio-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-xxl1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Reece had a rough childhood – she was skipping classes, stealing alcohol, and was in and out of jail. She attended <a href="/wiki/Skyline_High_School_(Oakland,_California)" title="Skyline High School (Oakland, California)">Skyline High School</a> before dropping out of ninth grade. While in sixth grade, both of her parents were arrested. During her teen years, Reece has had various jobs, including working in a chocolate factory called Grand Avenue Chocolates.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly2_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> At 15, she gave birth to the first of her two children.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Reece was inspired by her cousin and her child's father to start rapping. She started <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyling</a> around 2009, and later, after gaining some popularity, realized she could pursue a career in music.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_bio_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex_bio-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2010, Reece was caught committing a robbery at a <a href="/wiki/Pick-n-Pull" class="mw-redirect" title="Pick-n-Pull">Pick-n-Pull</a> store. During a chase with the police, her car's transmission broke and would only go in reverse. She was arrested on robbery charges. She was released after a short prison sentence but was arrested again after committing another robbery in July.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly2_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She was then sentenced to serve six months in <a href="/wiki/Alameda_County,_California" title="Alameda County, California">Alameda County</a> <a href="/wiki/Santa_Rita_Jail" title="Santa Rita Jail">Santa Rita Jail</a>. Reece was released from jail in December 2010, while remaining on probation.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Reece met rapper <a href="/wiki/Kreayshawn" title="Kreayshawn">Kreayshawn</a>, after confronting her following rumors that she had made Reece's cousin start taking drugs. After the rumors turned out to be false, the two became friends and started performing together. They created the group <a href="/wiki/White_Girl_Mob" title="White Girl Mob">White Girl Mob</a>, together with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Debbie" title="Lil Debbie">Lil Debbie</a>. In April 2011, Reece released her debut mixtape, <i>Don't Bite, Just Taste,</i> recorded in one day and consisting entirely of freestyles.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_bio_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex_bio-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Reece was arrested again later in 2011. Shortly after, Kreayshawn started a campaign "Free V-Nasty" in support of her fellow rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_bio_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex_bio-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> When Kreayshawn's video for "<a href="/wiki/Gucci_Gucci" title="Gucci Gucci">Gucci Gucci</a>" became popular, Reece's alias V-Nasty gained fame, because of the lines: "I'm yelling 'Free V-Nasty' 'til my throat is raspy."<sup id="cite_ref-xxl1_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, Reece was contacted by Atlanta rapper <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a> who offered her to collaborate on an album. Before the release of the project, she was approached by several record labels, who offered to sign her, but she declined, feeling not ready for a major deal at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl1_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Her debut studio album with Gucci Mane, titled <i><a href="/wiki/BAYTL" title="BAYTL">BAYTL</a></i>, was released on December 13, 2011, through <a href="/wiki/Vice_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Vice Records">Vice Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>. <i>BAYTL</i> was produced almost entirely by Gucci Mane's frequent collaborator <a href="/wiki/Zaytoven" title="Zaytoven">Zaytoven</a> and features guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Mistah_F.A.B." title="Mistah F.A.B.">Mistah F.A.B.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Gang_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Taylor Gang Records">Berner</a> and Slim Dunkin among others.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_bio_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex_bio-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Gucci Mane called it "by far ... my most controversial mixtape to date" and commented that many people would be surprised by it.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The album was preceded by the single "Whip Appeal", which premiered on November 2, 2011, and was released officially on November 18.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On December 16, Reece was in Atlanta, shooting a video for the song "Push Ups" with Gucci Mane and Slim Dunkin. The latter got in an altercation with <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> Young Vito and a fight broke out, resulting in Dunkin being shot dead. The video shoot was cancelled, and Vito was arrested on murder charges. He was later acquitted of murder but convicted of aggravated assault and gun possession charges and sentenced to 25 years in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 2, 2012, Reece released her second mixtape <i>Doin' Number$</i>, featuring guest appearances from Mistah F.A.B., Roach Gigz and Erk Tha Jerk.<sup id="cite_ref-complex2_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex2-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In March 2013, Reece performed at the <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> (SXSW) Festival. In April, after parting ways with Kreayshawn, she signed to <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a>-based independent label Pinnacle Records in a highly publicized $750,000 deal. She also started working with <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> host <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Seacrest" title="Ryan Seacrest">Ryan Seacrest</a> on her reality TV show.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly3_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On her 24th birthday, she released a new <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">Mixtape</a> titled "11Lem", along with a new single titled "Tweekin". On February 16, 2015, she posted a short clip of the music video which has yet to be released.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_influence">Personal life and influence</span></h2>
<p>Reece has stated she is influenced by <a href="/wiki/Too_$hort" class="mw-redirect" title="Too $hort">Too $hort</a>, <a href="/wiki/3X_Krazy" title="3X Krazy">3X Krazy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Webbie" title="Webbie">Webbie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Boosie" class="mw-redirect" title="Lil Boosie">Lil Boosie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mac_Dre" title="Mac Dre">Mac Dre</a>, and listens to a lot of <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">Bay Area</a> music.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_bio_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex_bio-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Producer <a href="/wiki/Zaytoven" title="Zaytoven">Zaytoven</a> compared her style to that of <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>, saying "she'll get in trouble, she don't care about what she say, she just bein' free."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span></h2>
<p>Reece has been criticized for the use of the word "<a href="/wiki/Nigga" title="Nigga">nigga</a>" in her songs, although she defended it as being used in a friendly way and part of her city's culture.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl2_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl2-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Rapper <a href="/wiki/David_Banner" title="David Banner">David Banner</a> released a song condemning her actions,<sup id="cite_ref-xxl1_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/Mistah_F.A.B." title="Mistah F.A.B.">Mistah F.A.B.</a> came to her defense, stating that Oakland has a culture of its own and claimed Reece and her group White Girl Mob are a product of that environment.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl3_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl3-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Rapper <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a> also defended her, stating that "if rappers use the word, and rap music is for everyone, then it's misguided to lash out members of other races who pick up on it" and claiming that the word is not viewed as racist nowadays.<sup id="cite_ref-bet1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bet1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2012, Reece accused <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Boy" title="Soulja Boy">Soulja Boy</a> of being a "fake ass industry rapper" and compared him unfavorably to his girlfriend <a href="/wiki/Diamond_(rapper)" title="Diamond (rapper)">Diamond</a> of <a href="/wiki/Crime_Mob" title="Crime Mob">Crime Mob</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Soulja Boy refused to engage in a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_feud" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop feud">beef</a> commenting: "What would I look like fighting with a girl?"<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Her ongoing feud with her alleged ex-boyfriend Magneto Dayo seemed to have been over when they collaborated on a song entitled "Hate u"<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> (the breakup) but the fire was once again lit when she threatened him in an interview saying, "I'll kick his ass" and denying ever lying about the paternity of her child. Magneto Dayo retaliated with a song called "OJ Simpson" in which he claimed she turned her back on him and others that were there for her before her fame.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
