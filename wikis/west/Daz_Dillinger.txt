<p><b>Delmar Drew Arnaud</b> (born May 25, 1973), known professionally as <b>Daz Dillinger</b> or simply <b>Daz</b> (formerly <b>Dat Nigga Daz</b>), is an American rapper and music producer. In the 1990s at <a href="/wiki/Death_Row_Records" title="Death Row Records">Death Row Records</a>, he is credited with aiding the catapult of <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_rap" class="mw-redirect" title="West Coast rap">West Coast rap</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_rap" title="Gangsta rap">gangsta rap</a> into the mainstream. He is one half of the rap duo <a href="/wiki/Tha_Dogg_Pound" title="Tha Dogg Pound">tha Dogg Pound</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a>.
</p><p>Daz learned production from <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a> in working on Dre's debut solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chronic" title="The Chronic">The Chronic</a></i>, in 1992. Daz did more on Snoop Dogg's debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Doggystyle" title="Doggystyle">Doggystyle</a></i> in 1993, and secured his production standing on 2Pac's <i><a href="/wiki/All_Eyez_on_Me" title="All Eyez on Me">All Eyez on Me</a></i> in 1996. Since leaving Death Row in late 1998, Daz has focused on his own releases through his <a href="/wiki/D.P.G._Recordz" title="D.P.G. Recordz">D.P.G. Recordz</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daz_Dillinger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Start_at_Death_Row_.281992.E2.80.931995.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Start_at_Death_Row_(1992–1995)">Start at Death Row (1992–1995)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daz_Dillinger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Start at Death Row (1992–1995)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Daz Dillinger was raised in <a href="/wiki/Long_Beach,_California" title="Long Beach, California">Long Beach, California</a>, as was his older cousin <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>. Daz began his music career at the age of 19, when he signed to <a href="/wiki/Death_Row_Records" title="Death Row Records">Death Row Records</a> and learned music production from <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> After joining the Death Row label, Daz was featured on Dr. Dre's debut solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chronic" title="The Chronic">The Chronic</a></i>, in 1992; he contributed both rapping and production to the album.<sup id="cite_ref-rapnews.net_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rapnews.net-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During this period, Daz also befriended the rapper <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a>, and the two formed a duo called <a href="/wiki/Tha_Dogg_Pound" title="Tha Dogg Pound">Tha Dogg Pound</a>.
</p><p>The Dogg Pound appeared on Snoop Dogg's debut solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Doggystyle_(album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Doggystyle (album)">Doggystyle</a></i>, which was released as Death Row's second album on November 23, 1993. Formally, Daz received co-production credits on two of <i>Doggystyle</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s tracks, "Serial Killa" and "For All My Niggaz &amp; Bitches"; however, Daz maintains that he provided uncredited production for large portions of the album, and Death Row CEO <a href="/wiki/Suge_Knight" title="Suge Knight">Suge Knight</a> has remarked that "Daz did pretty much the whole album [<i>Doggystyle</i>]".<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Daz also produced tracks for the soundtracks to <i><a href="/wiki/Above_the_Rim_(soundtrack)" title="Above the Rim (soundtrack)">Above the Rim</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Murder_Was_the_Case" title="Murder Was the Case">Murder Was the Case</a></i>.
</p><p>In their single "<a href="/wiki/What_Would_You_Do_(Tha_Dogg_Pound_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="What Would You Do (Tha Dogg Pound song)">What Would You Do?</a>", Tha Dogg Pound sided with Dr. Dre against his former <a href="/wiki/N.W.A" title="N.W.A">N.W.A</a> groupmate <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a> and his label <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records" title="Ruthless Records">Ruthless Records</a>. Later, as the ongoing <a href="/wiki/East_Coast%E2%80%93West_Coast_hip_hop_rivalry" title="East Coast–West Coast hip hop rivalry">East Coast–West Coast hip hop rivalry</a> began to escalate, Tha Dogg Pound recorded the single "<a href="/wiki/New_York,_New_York_(Tha_Dogg_Pound_song)" title="New York, New York (Tha Dogg Pound song)">New York, New York</a>", which slighted the city. Tha Dogg Pound followed these tracks with their debut album, 1995's <i><a href="/wiki/Dogg_Food" title="Dogg Food">Dogg Food</a></i>, which was certified platinum by the RIAA.
</p>
<h3><span id="Growth_at_Death_Row_Records_.281996.E2.80.931998.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Growth_at_Death_Row_Records_(1996–1998)">Growth at Death Row Records (1996–1998)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daz_Dillinger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Growth at Death Row Records (1996–1998)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the East–West rap rivalry intensified, Death Row's lead producer Dr. Dre increasingly distanced himself from the studio, disliking the studio's atmosphere and Suge Knight's leadership.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In 1996, <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a> released his first Death Row album, <i><a href="/wiki/All_Eyez_on_Me" title="All Eyez on Me">All Eyez on Me</a></i>; this album featured only three tracks produced by Dr. Dre, while Daz produced five tracks for the album, including the singles "<a href="/wiki/2_of_Amerikaz_Most_Wanted" title="2 of Amerikaz Most Wanted">2 of Amerikaz Most Wanted</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/I_Ain%27t_Mad_at_Cha" title="I Ain&#39;t Mad at Cha">I Ain't Mad at Cha</a>". The commercial success of <i>All Eyez on Me</i> raised Daz's stature as a producer, and he provided production to numerous Death Row albums – including Snoop Dogg's <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Doggfather" title="Tha Doggfather">Tha Doggfather</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/G-Funk_Classics,_Vol._1_%26_2" title="G-Funk Classics, Vol. 1 &amp; 2">G-Funk Classics, Vol. 1 &amp; 2</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Lady_of_Rage" title="The Lady of Rage">the Lady of Rage</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Necessary_Roughness_(album)" title="Necessary Roughness (album)">Necessary Roughness</a></i> – in 1996 and 1997. Daz also contributed to the soundtrack of the 1997 film <i><a href="/wiki/Gridlock%27d" title="Gridlock&#39;d">Gridlock'd</a></i>.
</p><p>Dr. Dre left Death Row in March 1996 to found the label <a href="/wiki/Aftermath_Entertainment" title="Aftermath Entertainment">Aftermath Entertainment</a>. After Tupac was murdered in 1996, followed by Suge Knight being sentenced to prison for parole violations in 1997, numerous other artists (including Snoop Dogg, Kurupt, and <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a>) departed from Death Row as well; this left Daz as the only platinum-selling artist remaining on the Death Row roster.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Daz released his debut solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Retaliation,_Revenge_and_Get_Back" title="Retaliation, Revenge and Get Back">Retaliation, Revenge and Get Back</a></i>, through Death Row Records in March 1998; however, he too left the label later that year.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="D.P.G._Recordz_and_indie_releases_.28post-1999.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="D.P.G._Recordz_and_indie_releases_(post-1999)">D.P.G. Recordz and indie releases (post-1999)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daz_Dillinger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: D.P.G. Recordz and indie releases (post-1999)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After leaving Death Row Records, Daz produced for artists like Kurupt, <a href="/wiki/Soopafly" title="Soopafly">Soopafly</a>, and <a href="/wiki/B-Legit" title="B-Legit">B-Legit</a>. He also established his own label, <a href="/wiki/D.P.G._Recordz" title="D.P.G. Recordz">D.P.G. Recordz</a>, on which he released his second solo album, 2000's <i><a href="/wiki/R.A.W._(album)" title="R.A.W. (album)">R.A.W</a></i>. In the following years, Daz has continued to focus on his own indie releases and sales;<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> as of 2023, he has released a total of eighteen solo albums.
</p><p>Daz and Kurupt reunited to release a second collaborative album, <i><a href="/wiki/Dillinger_%26_Young_Gotti" title="Dillinger &amp; Young Gotti">Dillinger &amp; Young Gotti</a></i>, in 2001; because Death Row still owned the name "Tha Dogg Pound", they released <i>Dillinger &amp; Young Gotti</i> under the name DPG. Despite this collaboration, relations deteriorated between the two rappers after Kurupt signed to Death Row Records once more, and Daz repeatedly insulted Kurupt in songs and interviews.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Daz and Kurupt ultimately reconciled at a West Coast unity event that Snoop Dogg hosted in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> After gaining rights to the moniker "Tha Dogg Pound", Kurupt left Death Row again, and Daz closed his brief time at <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a>'s <a href="/wiki/So_So_Def_Recordings" title="So So Def Recordings">So So Def Recordings</a>. Since then, Tha Dogg Pound has released several more albums, including a <a href="/wiki/Tha_Dogg_Pound_discography" title="Tha Dogg Pound discography">prolific streak of five albums</a> between 2005 and 2010.
</p><p>In 2020 Daz united with Queens MC <a href="/wiki/Capone_(rapper)" title="Capone (rapper)">Capone</a> (one half of <a href="/wiki/Capone-N-Noreaga" title="Capone-N-Noreaga">Capone-N-Noreaga</a>) for a collaborative album titled <i>Guidelines</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
