<p><b>Nyle Kalin Parrish</b> (born June 2, 1981), better known by the stage name <b>Clyde Carson</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland</a>, California. He was originally known as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/The_Team_(group)" title="The Team (group)">The Team</a>. After the group went on hiatus, he was the one member of the group to break out as a solo artist. He was signed to rapper <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Wall_Street_Records" title="The Black Wall Street Records">The Black Wall Street Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a> in 2006. While signed there he released the <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> <i>Doin' That</i>. However, he wasn't signed to them for long, before deciding to go back to releasing music independently via Moe Doe Entertainment. After returning to just that label, he has released two EPs: <i>Bass Rock</i> and <i>Playboy</i>. He also released the song "Slow Down", which was featured on the 2013 video game <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a></i>.
</p><p>He blends <a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">hyphy</a> with street-styled rap.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clyde_Carson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Carson started his professional rapping career by selling his debut mixtape <i>The Story Vol. 1</i> out of the trunk of his car in 2001. After sneaking backstage at a <a href="/wiki/Total_Request_Live" title="Total Request Live">TRL</a> concert he met producer <a href="/wiki/Ty_Fyffe" title="Ty Fyffe">Ty Fyffe</a>, who he forged a close friendship with. Shortly after he moved to New York City with Ty Fyffe, staying there for almost a year. There he joined Fyffe to studio sessions with rappers such as <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This increased his confidence. On meeting, Fyffe he stated<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> "I wanted to get up out of Oakland. This was before they were playing local music on the radio, other than E-40."
</p><p>Then in 2002, Carson moved back to California and formed the hip hop group <a href="/wiki/The_Team_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Team (band)">The Team</a>, with local <a href="/wiki/Bay_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Bay Area">Bay Area</a> rappers Mayne Mannish and Kaz Kyzah. They released a project on local rapper <a href="/wiki/Keak_da_Sneak" title="Keak da Sneak">Keak da Sneak</a>'s record label<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and got put into local radio rotation. They then gained more popularity in late 2005 after releasing the singles "Just Go" and "Bottles Up" to promote their third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/World_Premiere_(The_Team_album)" title="World Premiere (The Team album)">World Premiere</a></i>, which peaked at number 95 on the US <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums"><i>Billboard</i> Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> chart, and number 50 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Independent_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Independent Albums"><i>Billboard</i> Top Independent Albums</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-AMG_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However the album was interminably delayed, blunting its impact.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With the help of rapper <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Black_Wall_Street_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Wall Street Records">Black Wall Street Records</a>, Carson secured a major record label deal with Moedoe and <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a> in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On his signing, he asserted<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> "As a rapper, there's no ceiling in L.A. [like there is in the Bay]." He followed that by touring with The Game.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Carson released his debut retail EP <i>Doin' That</i> on September 4, 2007.,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> which was supported by the single "Doin' It" featuring <a href="/wiki/Sean_Kingston" title="Sean Kingston">Sean Kingston</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Plans were then revealed for a debut album to be released by Black Wall Street and Capitol titled <i>Theater Music</i>, however it would be pushed back indefinitely.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He explained the deal falling through by saying, "timing wasn't right", the person who signed him left the label and the studio head died.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, Carson would not be officially released from Capitol until 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> To celebrate his independence, Carson released his second EP <i>Bass Rock</i> on July 21, 2009, by Moe Doe Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> It was released for digital download and made available in select record stores.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2011, Carson reunited with The Team to record the song "Slow Down".<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Then on June 26, 2012, "Slow Down" was released as a single by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Following its release, a DJ named J-12 invented a dance specifically for the song, inspiring dozens of kids to upload their own versions to YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It peaked at number 21 on the US <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Rap_Airplay" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap Airplay">Rap Airplay</a> chart<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and at number 28 on the <a href="/wiki/Rhythmic_(chart)" title="Rhythmic (chart)">Rhythmic</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The remix would feature <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>, <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, The Game, and <a href="/wiki/Dom_Kennedy" title="Dom Kennedy">Dom Kennedy</a> in place of his The Team comrades.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Off the strength of the song, The Team performed the song at <a href="/wiki/WQHT" title="WQHT">Hot 97</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Summer_Jam_(festival)" title="Summer Jam (festival)">Summer Jam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> "Slow Down" would then be included on The Team's reunion EP, <i>Hell of a Night</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> As it would turn out, the song would also be the first single from his second official mixtape <i>S.T.S.A. (Something To Speak About)</i>. <i>S.T.S.A.</i> featured guest appearances by E-40, <a href="/wiki/Iamsu!" title="Iamsu!">Iamsu!</a>, <a href="/wiki/Problem_(rapper)" title="Problem (rapper)">Problem</a>, and <a href="/wiki/B-Legit" title="B-Legit">B-Legit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> It was completely produced by The Team collaborator Sho Nuff.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Following its release he began working on his next project.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag1_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He released the song "Pour Up" as the first single for his next EP on June 4, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> "Pour Up" would later be remixed with additional verses by The Game and <a href="/wiki/Young_Jeezy" class="mw-redirect" title="Young Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-contactmusic1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contactmusic1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Then for the rest of 2013 he would be featured on albums from other rappers, including The Game's <i><a href="/wiki/OKE:_Operation_Kill_Everything" title="OKE: Operation Kill Everything">OKE: Operation Kill Everything</a></i>, E-40's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Block_Brochure:_Welcome_to_the_Soil_6" title="The Block Brochure: Welcome to the Soil 6">The Block Brochure: Welcome to the Soil 6</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Ya_Boy" title="Ya Boy">Ya Boy</a>'s <i>Rich Rocka</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Then on February 14, 2014, Carson released the music video for the EP song "Bring Em Out". Around the same time he placed his third solo EP <i>Playboy</i> available for pre-order on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On March 4, 2014, Carson released <i>Playboy</i> via Moe Doe Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> It takes its name from the rapper <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too Short</a>'s nickname Playboy Short.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag1_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The EP contained production from <a href="/wiki/DJ_Mustard" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Mustard">DJ Mustard</a>, League Of Starz, Dez Dynamic and Sho Nuff, with guest appearances including D-Lo, <a href="/wiki/August_Alsina" title="August Alsina">August Alsina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> It was met with generally positive reviews. Jameel Raeburn of <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> said, "A lack of variety may be the main detractor for new listeners on <i>Playboy</i> EP, but overall the project is filled to the brim with tracks that slap. Clyde has carved himself a solid niche with his consistent party anthems, and <i>Playboy</i> is a strong soundtrack to his own player antics."<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <i>Playboy</i> peaked at number 23 on the US <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers">Heatseekers Albums</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Carson was then featured on Master P's song "What the Business Is", which was released on April 4, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Carson plans to release <i>S.T.S.A. Part II</i> and then go on tour in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-contactmusic1_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contactmusic1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
