<p><b>Mark Anthony Spears</b> (born February 28, 1986), known professionally as <b>Sounwave</b>, is an American record producer and songwriter. He is a frequent collaborator of rapper <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, having worked on all of his projects since his <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar_(EP)" title="Kendrick Lamar (EP)">2009 self-titled extended play</a>. Sounwave has also worked with artists such as <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chloe_x_Halle" title="Chloe x Halle">Chloe x Halle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baby_Keem" title="Baby Keem">Baby Keem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bleachers_(band)" title="Bleachers (band)">Bleachers</a>. He has won three <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a>, including two for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Best Rap Album</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sounwave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sounwave was born Mark Anthony Spears on February 28, 1986, in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His parents are <a href="/wiki/Childhood_sweetheart" title="Childhood sweetheart">elementary school sweethearts</a> from <a href="/wiki/Selma,_Alabama" title="Selma, Alabama">Selma, Alabama</a>, and relocated to Los Angeles in the 1970s so his father could pursue a dancing career for <i><a href="/wiki/Soul_Train" title="Soul Train">Soul Train</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Spears has two older brothers and was raised in <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was first inspired to pursue music after hearing the instrumentals on "<a href="/wiki/Up_Jumps_da_Boogie" title="Up Jumps da Boogie">Up Jumps da Boogie</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a>. As a child, Spears used a <a href="/wiki/Korg" title="Korg">Korg</a> drum machine and a karaoke machine to create music.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> From there, he purchased a <a href="/wiki/Multitrack_recording" title="Multitrack recording">4-track</a> machine and began using the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation" title="PlayStation">PlayStation</a> video game <i><a href="/wiki/Music_2000" title="Music 2000">MTV Music Generator</a></i> (1999) to produce songs.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sounwave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1999.E2.80.932009:_Career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1999–2009:_Career_beginnings">1999–2009: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sounwave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1999–2009: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Spears was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Punch_(rapper)" title="Punch (rapper)">Terrance "Punch" Henderson</a>, co-president of <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a> (TDE), when he was 13 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After meeting with rapper <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Lamont" title="Bishop Lamont">Bishop Lamont</a>, he produced a song for him using <i>MTV Music Generator</i>, which later received radio placement.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Compton_High_School" title="Compton High School">Compton High School</a> in 2005 and was gifted an <a href="/wiki/Akai_MPC" title="Akai MPC">Akai MPC</a> by his cousin; it soon became one of his primary production tools.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Spears first met rapper <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> in a recording studio in <a href="/wiki/Gardena,_California" title="Gardena, California">Gardena, California</a>. Lamar was silent until Spears played a spin of <a href="/wiki/Aalon" title="Aalon">Aalon</a>'s 1977 song "Rock and Roll Gangster", which made him rush to the recording booth and rap for two minutes straight.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>One year later, Spears met TDE's founder and chief executive officer Anthony "Top Dawg" Tiffith, who was originally unimpressed with him. He persisted and honed his craft, which later blew him away.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Spears later reconnected with Lamar and became a member of TDE's in-house production team <a href="/wiki/Digi%2BPhonics" title="Digi+Phonics">Digi+Phonics</a>, alongside Tae Beast, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Free" title="Dave Free">Dave Free</a> and <a href="/wiki/Willie_B_(producer)" title="Willie B (producer)">Willie B</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His earliest work for the label includes production credits on Lamar's <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar_(EP)" title="Kendrick Lamar (EP)">2009 self titled extended play</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flo_Rida" title="Flo Rida">Flo Rida</a>'s second album <i><a href="/wiki/R.O.O.T.S." title="R.O.O.T.S.">R.O.O.T.S.</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932014:_Recognition"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2014:_Recognition">2010–2014: Recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sounwave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010–2014: Recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Spears was one of the primary producers of Lamar's final mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Overly_Dedicated" title="Overly Dedicated">Overly Dedicated</a></i> (2010) and his debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Section.80" title="Section.80">Section.80</a></i> (2011). For his work on the latter album, he was highlighted by <a href="/wiki/Complex_Networks" title="Complex Networks"><i>Complex</i></a> on their 15 New Producers to Watch list.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He produced three songs on Lamar's second album <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Kid,_M.A.A.D_City" title="Good Kid, M.A.A.D City">Good Kid, M.A.A.D City</a></i> (2012): "<a href="/wiki/Bitch_Don%27t_Kill_My_Vibe" class="mw-redirect" title="Bitch Don&#39;t Kill My Vibe">Bitch Don't Kill My Vibe</a>", "<a href="/wiki/M.A.A.D._City" class="mw-redirect" title="M.A.A.D. City">M.A.A.D. City</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Sing_About_Me,_I%27m_Dying_of_Thirst" title="Sing About Me, I&#39;m Dying of Thirst">Sing About Me, I'm Dying of Thirst</a>". As a member of Digi+Phonics, he was highlighted by <i>Complex</i> for a second time and earned his first <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Award</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Album_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Album of the Year">Album of the Year</a> for his work on <i>Good Kid, M.A.A.D City</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Spears also made contributions to <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a>'s second album <i><a href="/wiki/Habits_%26_Contradictions" title="Habits &amp; Contradictions">Habits &amp; Contradictions</a></i> (2012),<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> his third album <i><a href="/wiki/Oxymoron_(Schoolboy_Q_album)" title="Oxymoron (Schoolboy Q album)">Oxymoron</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Isaiah_Rashad" title="Isaiah Rashad">Isaiah Rashad</a>'s debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Cilvia_Demo" title="Cilvia Demo">Cilvia Demo</a></i> (both 2014).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.93present:_Breakthrough_and_other_projects"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–present:_Breakthrough_and_other_projects">2015–present: Breakthrough and other projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sounwave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015–present: Breakthrough and other projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Spears produced a majority of Lamar's third album <i><a href="/wiki/To_Pimp_a_Butterfly" title="To Pimp a Butterfly">To Pimp a Butterfly</a></i> (2015), which several publications have named as one of the best albums of the <a href="/wiki/2010s_in_music" title="2010s in music">2010s decade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/58th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="58th Annual Grammy Awards">58th Annual Grammy Awards</a>, he won <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Song" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Song">Best Rap Song</a> for co-writing the album's fourth single "<a href="/wiki/Alright_(Kendrick_Lamar_song)" title="Alright (Kendrick Lamar song)">Alright</a>" and received a second nomination for Album of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> For Lamar's fourth album <i><a href="/wiki/Damn_(Kendrick_Lamar_album)" title="Damn (Kendrick Lamar album)">Damn</a></i> (2017), Spears won <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Best Rap Album</a> and picked up a third Album of the Year nomination at the <a href="/wiki/60th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="60th Annual Grammy Awards">60th Annual Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, he co-wrote, produced, and handled <a href="/wiki/Artists_and_repertoire" title="Artists and repertoire">A&amp;R</a> for the film soundtrack <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Panther_(soundtrack)" title="Black Panther (soundtrack)">Black Panther: The Album</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and contributed to singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Kali_Uchis" title="Kali Uchis">Kali Uchis</a>' debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Isolation_(Kali_Uchis_album)" title="Isolation (Kali Uchis album)">Isolation</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <i>Black Panther: The Album</i> received five nominations at the <a href="/wiki/61st_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="61st Annual Grammy Awards">61st Annual Grammy Awards</a>, including for Album of the Year, marking the first time a soundtrack album was nominated for the honor since <i><a href="/wiki/O_Brother,_Where_Art_Thou%3F_(soundtrack)" title="O Brother, Where Art Thou? (soundtrack)">O Brother, Where Art Thou?</a></i> (2000).<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Its lead single, "<a href="/wiki/All_the_Stars" title="All the Stars">All the Stars</a>", earned nominations for Best Original Song at the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Awards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Awards" title="Golden Globe Awards">Golden Globe Awards</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Movie_Awards" title="Critics&#39; Choice Movie Awards">Critics' Choice Movie Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2019, Spears contributed to singer-songwriters <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>'s soundtrack album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King:_The_Gift" title="The Lion King: The Gift">The Lion King: The Gift</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a>'s seventh album <i><a href="/wiki/Lover_(album)" title="Lover (album)">Lover</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Know_(song)" title="Know (song)">Know</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The same year, he formed the <a href="/wiki/Synth-pop" title="Synth-pop">synth-pop</a> <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> <a href="/wiki/Jack_Antonoff#Red_Hearse" title="Jack Antonoff">Red Hearse</a> with musician and producer <a href="/wiki/Jack_Antonoff" title="Jack Antonoff">Jack Antonoff</a> and singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Sam_Dew" title="Sam Dew">Sam Dew</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Their self-titled debut album was released through <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> For his work as the primary producer of Lamar's fifth album <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Morale_%26_the_Big_Steppers" title="Mr. Morale &amp; the Big Steppers">Mr. Morale &amp; the Big Steppers</a></i> (2022), Spears won Best Rap Album for the second time at the <a href="/wiki/65th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="65th Annual Grammy Awards">65th Annual Grammy Awards</a>, and earned a fourth nomination for Album of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sounwave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
