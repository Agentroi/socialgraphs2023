<p><b>Robert Louis Carr III</b> (born May 3, 1985), better known by his stage name <b>Judge Da Boss</b>, is an American rapper born in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>. On July 23, 2014, it was announced that Judge signed to Louder Than Life/<a href="/wiki/Sony_Records" title="Sony Records">Sony Records</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Judge_Da_Boss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Judge attended several elementary schools growing up in the South side of Phoenix. It was in the seventh grade that his passion for music began to grow after being exposed to poetry, hip-hop and a plethora of other genres.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This inspired Judge to start rapping at the age of 12. After honing his rap skills for several years, Judge stepped into the recording studio for the first time during his freshman year of high school.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Judge_Da_Boss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Judge won “The Chosen One” rap competition, beating out over 100 other local artists. This caught the attention of NBA superstar, <a href="/wiki/Amar%27e_Stoudemire" title="Amar&#39;e Stoudemire">Amar'e Stoudemire</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> who ultimately signed him to his label, Hypocalypto Records.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Judge followed up by releasing his first mix tape under the label called “The Chosen One.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>" <a href="/wiki/Amar%27e_Stoudemire" title="Amar&#39;e Stoudemire">Amar'e Stoudemire</a> and DJ Quote hosted the project. While promoting the mix tape, Judge performed with a number of mainstream artists including <a href="/wiki/Young_Jeezy" class="mw-redirect" title="Young Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a>, <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>, <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plies_(rapper)" title="Plies (rapper)">Plies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He would go on to release a collaborative project with Phoenix artist, Vik Junior and a follow up solo record. His track “Stat and Melo”,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> a <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a> anthem, is still played in <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a> to this day.
After completing his deal with Hypocalypto Records, Judge began working with Rampage from the legendary Flip mode Squad. In 2013, his standout track “Retard Money” was used in <a href="/wiki/Movie_43" title="Movie 43">Movie 43</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On July 23, 2014, it was announced that Judge signed to Louder Than Life/<a href="/wiki/Sony_Records" title="Sony Records">Sony Records</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
