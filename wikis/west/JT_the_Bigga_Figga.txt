<p><b>Joseph Tom</b> (born November 8, 1973), better known by his stage name <b>JT the Bigga Figga</b> (also known as <b>Figg Panamera</b>), is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Music_executive" title="Music executive">record executive</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Fillmore_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Fillmore District">Fillmore</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, California.<sup id="cite_ref-sfweekly1995_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfweekly1995-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He has produced music on over one hundred albums, for artists including <a href="/wiki/Daz_Dillinger" title="Daz Dillinger">Daz Dillinger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Quinn" title="San Quinn">San Quinn</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Messy_Marv" title="Messy Marv">Messy Marv</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JT_the_Bigga_Figga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1991, JT founded the independent record label <a href="/wiki/Get_Low_Recordz" title="Get Low Recordz">Get Low Recordz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sfweekly1995_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfweekly1995-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> What started as a basement enterprise and a one-man operation, would later become one of the most successful independent labels in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-sfweekly2004_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfweekly2004-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>JT's rap debut came in 1992 with the self-released album <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Stop_til_We_Major" title="Don&#39;t Stop til We Major">Don't Stop til We Major</a></i> at the age of 18. The next year he followed it up with <i><a href="/wiki/Playaz_N_the_Game" title="Playaz N the Game">Playaz N the Game</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-sfweekly2004_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfweekly2004-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The album's first single, "Game Recognize Game" received significant airplay, thrusting JT into the spotlight. The success of his album led to a bidding war among record labels, and he eventually signed with <a href="/wiki/Priority_Records" title="Priority Records">Priority Records</a> in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-sfweekly1995_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfweekly1995-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sfweekly2004_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfweekly2004-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Subsequent albums released through Priority Records were unsuccessful, however, and in 1996 the label elected to release JT from his contract. JT The Bigga Figga appeared on <a href="/wiki/No_Limit_Records" title="No Limit Records">No Limit Records</a>'s May 1997 release, <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_Bout_It_(soundtrack)" title="I&#39;m Bout It (soundtrack)">I'm Bout It</a></i>, appearing on the song "Game Tight" with The Fast One. Both JT The Bigga Figga &amp; The Fast One produced the song.
</p><p>After the dissolution of his contract with Priority Records, JT went back to self-distributing his albums through Get Low Recordz. With the money from his deal with Priority, JT invested in a new state of the art <a href="/wiki/Recording_studio" title="Recording studio">recording studio</a> and began rapidly producing and releasing numerous albums. In 2000, after signing a modest distribution deal with Bayside Records, Get Low began expanding, releasing nearly 20 albums a year.<sup id="cite_ref-sfweekly2004_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfweekly2004-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2002, JT published an E-Book called <i>The CEO Manual.</i><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>JT is credited with discovering rapper <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>, and releasing his first two albums <i>Untold Story</i> and <i>QB 2 Compton</i> before The Game signed with <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a> and debuted as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Aftermath_Entertainment" title="Aftermath Entertainment">Aftermath Entertainment</a> camp.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In fall of 2005, JT was named <a href="/wiki/A%26R" class="mw-redirect" title="A&amp;R">A&amp;R</a> for the West Coast operations of the <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, Texas-based <a href="/wiki/Rap-A-Lot_Records" title="Rap-A-Lot Records">Rap-A-Lot Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>JT the Bigga Figga's film credits include the <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a> movie <i><a href="/wiki/Sucker_Free_City" title="Sucker Free City">Sucker Free City</a></i> (2004),<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and <i>SKUZZ TV</i> (2007),<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> a reality based documentary DVD released by 3MZ productions.
</p><p>In 2006, JT worked with <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> on a documentary DVD entitled <i>Mandatory Business</i>, which featured such names as <a href="/wiki/Russell_Simmons" title="Russell Simmons">Russell Simmons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a> and <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2015, JT founded the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a>-like on-demand service Trapflix, which showcases urban films, documentaries, concert footage, and music. JT has heavily been involved in filming and directing, releasing over 40 films and documentaries since 
2000. JT is now spreading his independent knowledge and the concept of <a href="/wiki/Pan-Africanism" title="Pan-Africanism">Pan-Africanism</a> in <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a>, and posts daily on his YouTube channel, Trapflix TV. He founded the label "Fillmoelanta". He is also one of the most recognized marketing/promotion talents in hip-hop and has served many high-profile clients such as <a href="/wiki/Rich_Gang" class="mw-redirect" title="Rich Gang">Rich Gang</a>, <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>, Snoop Dogg, and many more. He has also released a series of collaboration tapes with <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>.
</p>
