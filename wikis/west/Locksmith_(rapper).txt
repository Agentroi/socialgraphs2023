<p><b>Davood Ali Asgari</b>, better known by his stage name <b>Locksmith</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_California" title="Richmond, California">Richmond, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is half of the former underground duo <a href="/wiki/The_Frontline" title="The Frontline">The Frontline</a>, which pioneered the "New Bay" resurgence movement in the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">Bay Area</a> in the 2000s.<sup id="cite_ref-SFG_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EastBay2005_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EastBay2005-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Locksmith_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Locksmith was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_California" title="Richmond, California">Richmond, California</a> in the neighborhood of Crescent Park.<sup id="cite_ref-SFG_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His father is <a href="/wiki/Persians" title="Persians">Persian</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> who had immigrated from <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, and his mother was <a href="/wiki/African_American" class="mw-redirect" title="African American">African American</a> from <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Kickmag_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kickmag-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> originally from <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tcus2017_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcus2017-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> For the greater part of his childhood, he was raised predominantly by his mother's side of his family as his father was the only one from his Iranian side, until he had gotten older when other paternal family-members also arrived.<sup id="cite_ref-Kickmag_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kickmag-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He has four older <a href="/wiki/Half-sibling" class="mw-redirect" title="Half-sibling">half-siblings</a> from his mother's previous marriage.<sup id="cite_ref-EastBay2005_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EastBay2005-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Kickmag_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kickmag-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Between the 80s and 90s, his sister, who is a decade his senior,<sup id="cite_ref-Kickmag_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kickmag-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> rapped as MC Spice and had opened up for the likes of <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackalicious" title="Blackalicious">Blackalicious</a> and <a href="/wiki/Digital_Underground" title="Digital Underground">Digital Underground</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-EastBay2017_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EastBay2017-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, his mother died.<sup id="cite_ref-tcus2017_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcus2017-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Locksmith went to <a href="/wiki/De_Anza_High_School" title="De Anza High School">De Anza High School</a> in Richmond,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and attended <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">University of California, Berkeley</a>, where he graduated in <a href="/wiki/African_American_Studies" class="mw-redirect" title="African American Studies">African American Studies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SFW_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFW-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He played basketball during his youth, through which he met his childhood friend Left.<sup id="cite_ref-SFG_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After graduating from school, the pair worked as mentors at the <a href="/wiki/Contra_Costa_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Contra Costa County">West Contra Costa</a> Youth Service Bureau where they mentored teenagers and adolescents.<sup id="cite_ref-SFG_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He worked there, part-time,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> for a decade before moving on to concentrate on his solo music career.<sup id="cite_ref-SFW_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFW-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Locksmith_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_rap">Battle rap</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Locksmith_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Battle rap">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Locksmith first gained notoriety outside of the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">Bay Area</a> as a battle rapper. In 2003, after beating the likes of <a href="/wiki/Mistah_FAB" class="mw-redirect" title="Mistah FAB">Mistah FAB</a> and A-Wolf on radio station <a href="/wiki/KMEL" title="KMEL">106.1 KMEL</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MTV2003_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV2003-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> he qualified for and became a contender on <a href="/wiki/List_of_programs_broadcast_by_MTV" title="List of programs broadcast by MTV">MTV's <i>MC Battle</i></a> joining a pool of 32 contenders, judged by <a href="/wiki/Just_Blaze" title="Just Blaze">Just Blaze</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>. Locksmith was the only contender from the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-EastBay2005_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EastBay2005-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The event was the first-ever televised battle rap tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Locksmith then made it to the "elite eight" <a href="/wiki/Live_television" title="Live television">live telecast</a> on May 17, judged by <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Liles" title="Kevin Liles">Kevin Liles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV2003_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV2003-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  Despite his strong performance, he finished as the <a href="/wiki/Runner-up" title="Runner-up">runner-up</a> in the championship final decided by votes by viewers at home. The polling numbers and percentages were never shown. Speaking back on the upset, he states "I was a little surprised, but actually I expected it. Everybody kinda felt like there was a bias, which there was."<sup id="cite_ref-EastBay2005_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EastBay2005-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Between late 2008 and 2009, he did a series of battles in Grind Time battling the likes of Dizaster, Jonny Storm, Passwurdz and Daylyt.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Frontline">The Frontline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Locksmith_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Frontline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Locksmith met his childhood friend Left on the basketball court of their junior high school.<sup id="cite_ref-SFG_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 1996, the two formed <a href="/wiki/The_Frontline" title="The Frontline">The Frontline</a>. In 2002, the duo appeared on the <i>Watch Out Now</i> mixtape, along with several other Bay Area rap prospects, one of which was <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland</a> rapper Balance.<sup id="cite_ref-EastBay2005_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EastBay2005-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In December 2002, Locksmith and Balance appeared for the entire duration of a Saturday night broadcast of <i>The Wake Up Show</i> on KMEL, hosted by <a href="/wiki/Sway_Calloway" title="Sway Calloway">Sway Calloway</a>, <a href="/wiki/King_Tech" title="King Tech">King Tech</a> and DJ Revolution. Astonished by their performances, Sway commented, "These cats don't sound like regular Bay Area dudes." To which, Balance responded, "It's a New Bay."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Led by The Frontline and Balance at the forefront, the New Bay movement included <a href="/wiki/The_Team_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Team (band)">The Team</a>, Mistah FAB, <a href="/wiki/The_Federation_(group)" title="The Federation (group)">The Federation</a>, Certified Ryders, <a href="/wiki/San_Quinn" title="San Quinn">San Quinn</a>, F.E., and Esinchill.<sup id="cite_ref-Dub2004_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dub2004-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> It gained further momentum in 2003 following Locksmith's appearance on MTV's <i>MC Battle</i> in which he shouted out the "New Bay" in front of a nationwide audience.<sup id="cite_ref-EastBay2005_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EastBay2005-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Dub2004_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dub2004-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2004, The Frontline signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/E-A-Ski" title="E-A-Ski">E-A-Ski</a>'s label IMG. Between 2002 and 2004, the duo had released three mixtapes. They released their debut <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio album">studio album</a>, <i>Who R You</i>, on October 12, 2004, which peaked at #48 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Independent_Albums" title="Independent Albums">Independent Albums</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On May 10, 2005, The Frontline released their second studio album, <i>Now You Know</i>, which peaked at #46 on the Independent Albums chart.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, they released their third and final album, titled <i>Left &amp; Lock</i>, on September 25.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Locksmith_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2009, it was announced that Locksmith was working on a solo album titled <i>Frank the Rabbit</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> It was slated to be entirely produced by E-A-Ski.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It wasn't until he began his solo career in 2010, he started to cultivate a loyal fan base. Locksmith has toured with <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a>, <a href="/wiki/R.A._the_Rugged_Man" title="R.A. the Rugged Man">R.A. the Rugged Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vinnie_Paz" title="Vinnie Paz">Vinnie Paz</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jedi_Mind_Tricks" title="Jedi Mind Tricks">Jedi Mind Tricks</a> and collaborated with producers <a href="/wiki/9th_Wonder" title="9th Wonder">9th Wonder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ski_(record_producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ski (record producer)">Ski Beatz</a>. Locksmith has been featured on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>, <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sirius_XM" title="Sirius XM">Sirius XM</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Shade_45" title="Shade 45">Shade 45</a>, <a href="/wiki/WQHT" title="WQHT">Hot 97</a>'s "Who's Next?" concert series and all major hip hop blogs.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Locksmith released a series of mixtapes from 2010 to 2013.  His first official album <i>A Thousand Cuts</i> was released April 15, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Previously, Locksmith has collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Funk_Volume" title="Funk Volume">Funk Volume</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Hopsin" title="Hopsin">Hopsin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarren_Benton" title="Jarren Benton">Jarren Benton</a>, as well as, <a href="/wiki/Futuristic_(rapper)" title="Futuristic (rapper)">Futuristic</a> and Apathy. His second album, <i>Lofty Goals</i>, was  released on June 30, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The album features Jarren Benton, Futuristic, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Webby" title="Chris Webby">Chris Webby</a> and others.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2015, Locksmith performed at the <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> music festival in <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Locksmith released his 3rd album "Olive Branch" on March 31, 2017.
</p>
