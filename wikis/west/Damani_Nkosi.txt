<p><b>Damani Nkosi</b> is an American rapper. Nkosi has worked with well-known artists including <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-rs_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a> and <a href="/wiki/No_Malice" title="No Malice">Malice</a> of <a href="/wiki/Clipse" title="Clipse">Clipse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cent_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damani_Nkosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nkosi was brought up in <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rs_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His father chose an African name for him. "Damani" means "be famous" and "Nkosi" means "Chief, ruler or king".<sup id="cite_ref-hype_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hype-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He became part of Los Angeles' hip-hop underground.<sup id="cite_ref-rs_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Damani_Nkosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nkosi recorded his first track in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-cake_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cake-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2001, Nkosi was featured on Kurupt's album <i>Space Boogie: Smoke Odyssey</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-hip_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hip-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-all_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-all-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-la_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-la-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He released "Move", a track featuring <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a> and <a href="/wiki/No_Malice" title="No Malice">Malice</a> in 2002. The song was featured on hip-hop radio mixshows in Los Angeles including <a href="/wiki/KRRL" title="KRRL">92.3 FM, The Beat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-this_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-this-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2002, he released the mixtape <i>The Street Album</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-rs_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-this_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-this-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nkosi released <i>Congratulations Player</i>, a mixtape featuring the song "Gotta Stay Paid" in 2006. The song was featured on The Beat as well as <a href="/wiki/KPWR" title="KPWR">Power 106</a>. Later that year, he signed with <a href="/wiki/Sony_Urban_Music" title="Sony Urban Music">Sony Urban Music</a> under My Block Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-this_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-this-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-la_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-la-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, Nkosi appeared on the television series <a href="/wiki/Monk_(TV_series)" title="Monk (TV series)"><i>Monk</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-imdb_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Nkosi formed the group <a href="/wiki/Dubb_Union" title="Dubb Union">Dubb Union</a> (originally called Westurn Union) with Bad Lucc and producer Soopafly. <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> signed the group under Doggystyle Records. The group toured with Snoop Dogg and released <i>Snoop Dogg Presents: Dubb Union</i> in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-this_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-this-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-la_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-la-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Nkosi partnered with <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a> on its off-the-court EQT Campaign, which surrounded the relaunch of the EQT B-Ball Low. The limited edition shoes created included part of Nkosi's logo and the words "Congratulations Player" on the heel. He recorded "My Adidas" as part of the campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-ball_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ball-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-slam_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-slam-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sneak_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sneak-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, Nkosi released a single and video, "Here Comes Damani" featuring Snoop Dogg.<sup id="cite_ref-this_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-this-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-la_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-la-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nkosi released the album <i>On Vacation From Vacation</i> in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-hip_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hip-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The album featured Snoop Dogg, <a href="/wiki/Crooked_I" title="Crooked I">Crooked I</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mitchy_Slick" title="Mitchy Slick">Mitchy Slick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mr._Short_Khop" title="Mr. Short Khop">Short Khop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daz_Dillinger" title="Daz Dillinger">Daz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dub_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dub-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, Nkosi left his previous record labels, took a hiatus to travel internationally.<sup id="cite_ref-cent_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-source_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-source-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-red_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-red-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He went to China as part of a shoe design deal and was influenced by the economic gap between the rich and the poor.<sup id="cite_ref-red_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-red-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nkosi produced a single, "Now That's Love" featuring <a href="/wiki/Robert_Glasper" title="Robert Glasper">Robert Glasper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Musiq_Soulchild" title="Musiq Soulchild">Musiq Soulchild</a> in 2014 produced by <a href="/wiki/Warryn_Campbell" title="Warryn Campbell">Warryn Campbell</a>. "Now That's Love" was the first single from his album <i>Thoughtful King</i>. The album included other featured artists such as <a href="/wiki/PJ_Morton" title="PJ Morton">PJ Morton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/BJ_the_Chicago_Kid" title="BJ the Chicago Kid">BJ the Chicago Kid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rs_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cent_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cake_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cake-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, he produced the single "Stay Black" featuring Preston Harris, Ill Camille and Aneesa Strings.<sup id="cite_ref-up_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-up-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Nkosi appeared in the documentary "Manchild: The Schea Cotton Story" in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-man_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-man-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
