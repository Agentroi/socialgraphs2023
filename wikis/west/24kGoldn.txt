<p><b>Golden Landis Von Jones</b><sup id="cite_ref-Trojan_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trojan-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born November 13, 2000), known professionally as <b>24kGoldn</b>, is an American singer, rapper, and songwriter. In 2019, he rose to fame with his single, "<a href="/wiki/Valentino_(24kGoldn_song)" title="Valentino (24kGoldn song)">Valentino</a>", peaking at number 92 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. It was certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> in April 2020. In July 2020, 24kGoldn's single "<a href="/wiki/Mood_(song)" title="Mood (song)">Mood</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Iann_Dior" title="Iann Dior">Iann Dior</a>), topped the <i>Billboard</i> Hot  100, becoming the highest-charting song of his career. His debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/El_Dorado_(24kGoldn_album)" title="El Dorado (24kGoldn album)">El Dorado</a></i>, was released in March 2021.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=24kGoldn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Golden Landis Von Jones was born in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>, to an <a href="/wiki/Ashkenazi_Jewish" class="mw-redirect" title="Ashkenazi Jewish">Ashkenazi Jewish</a> mother Kimberly Landis and to an African-American <a href="/wiki/Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic">Catholic</a> father Torino Von Jones.<sup id="cite_ref-lat1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lat1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He has a younger sister named Sage Landis Von Jones. Both of his parents worked as fashion models before he was born.<sup id="cite_ref-lat1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lat1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was raised celebrating both <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hanukkah" title="Hanukkah">Hanukkah</a>, and attended <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_school" title="Hebrew school">Hebrew school</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> As a child, 24kGoldn acted in commercials. He sang in the choir in middle school and high school, and, with that, the importance of music in his life grew with his "own growth". Before he realized he could do music professionally, he planned on becoming a <a href="/wiki/Hedge_fund" title="Hedge fund">hedge fund</a> manager.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Break_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Break-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> 24kGoldn attended <a href="/wiki/Lowell_High_School_(San_Francisco)" title="Lowell High School (San Francisco)">Lowell High School</a>, a selective public high school, where he graduated in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>24kGoldn attended the&#160;<a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a>'s <a href="/wiki/USC_Marshall_School_of_Business" title="USC Marshall School of Business">Marshall School of Business</a>, joking that they accepted him because of a double entendre in "Valentino",<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  where he joined the&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Tau_Kappa_Epsilon_chapters_and_colonies" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Tau Kappa Epsilon chapters and colonies">Beta-Sigma Chapter of Tau Kappa Epsilon</a> <a href="/wiki/Fraternity" title="Fraternity">fraternity</a>, as a Spring 2019 initiate. He initially took a leave of absence from school to pursue his rap career.<sup id="cite_ref-usc_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usc-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>&#160;24kGoldn stated that he was not anticipating "that things were going to happen so fast when I came to USC", and did not think he would finish University, but he wanted to round off the school year to further develop the relationships he made, stating his love for being on campus, and that being a student kept him "grounded".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=24kGoldn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2017, 24kGoldn released his first music video for his first song, titled "Trappers Anthem".<sup id="cite_ref-TA_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TA-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In January 2019, he released the single, titled "<a href="/wiki/Valentino_(24kGoldn_song)" title="Valentino (24kGoldn song)">Valentino</a>", which received over 350 million plays on <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He received his first record deal through producer&#160;<a href="/wiki/D.a._doman" class="mw-redirect" title="D.a. doman">D.A. Doman</a>. 24kGoldn got his big break through his "high-energy" single "Valentino". However, in 2020, the artist said that "those ['Valentino'] were recorded over a year ago and don't exactly reflect the person I have become since then".<sup id="cite_ref-Trojan_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trojan-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In November 2019, 24kGoldn released his debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP), titled <i>Dropped Outta College</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-DOC_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DOC-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and he signed a record deal with Records, LLC and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Trojan_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trojan-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2020, 24kGoldn gained further attention with single, titled "<a href="/wiki/City_of_Angels_(24kGoldn_song)" title="City of Angels (24kGoldn song)">City of Angels</a>", which was officially released to US alternative radio on March 31;<sup id="cite_ref-AllAccess_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllAccess-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> as well as he follow with an official remix of the song by English singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Yungblud" title="Yungblud">Yungblud</a>, released in May 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On May 20, 24kGoldn was featured on the remix of <a href="/wiki/Olivia_O%27Brien" title="Olivia O&#39;Brien">Olivia O'Brien</a>'s single "Josslyn".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 11, 2020, he was included on <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s 2020 <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)#Annual_Freshman_Class_List" title="XXL (magazine)">Freshman Class</a>, placing in the tenth position after receiving the most fan votes.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Later that same month, 24kGoldn's July 2020 single, titled "<a href="/wiki/Mood_(song)" title="Mood (song)">Mood</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Iann_Dior" title="Iann Dior">Iann Dior</a>), hit the charts. It went on to peak at number one on the US <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. In October 2020, these four episodes of <i>Halloween in Hell</i>, a fictionalised musical horror podcast that Jones starred in, were released. The series also starred Iann Dior, <a href="/wiki/Machine_Gun_Kelly_(musician)" title="Machine Gun Kelly (musician)">Machine Gun Kelly</a>, Dana Dentata, Phem and <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Lee" title="Tommy Lee">Tommy Lee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 26, 2021, he released his first studio album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/El_Dorado_(24kGoldn_album)" title="El Dorado (24kGoldn album)">El Dorado</a></i>. The album features thirteen songs, which features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iann_Dior" title="Iann Dior">Iann Dior</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swae_Lee" title="Swae Lee">Swae Lee</a> and <a href="/wiki/DaBaby" title="DaBaby">DaBaby</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=24kGoldn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>24kGoldn's music spans a variety of genres, motivated by his want "never ... to be constricted by one genre". Regarding his style, he stated: "depending on what song you listen to, my music can be described different ways".<sup id="cite_ref-Trojan_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trojan-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He attributes his "maturity and development" to rapper Paypa Boy &amp; 24Kay, who initially got him into recording music.<sup id="cite_ref-Trojan_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trojan-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
