<p><b>Jeremy Hawkins</b> (born August 8, 1991) known professionally as <b>JHawk</b> or <b>JHawk Productions</b> , is an American record producer, songwriter, and actor from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. He is known for pioneering the <a href="/wiki/Jerkin%27" title="Jerkin&#39;">Jerkin'</a> Movement, accumulating tens of millions of views and streams, contributing heavily to the success of a number of Los Angeles-based artists.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Hawkins is perhaps best known for producing <a href="/wiki/The_Rej3ctz" title="The Rej3ctz">The Rej3ctz</a>'s breakout hit "<a href="/wiki/Cat_Daddy" title="Cat Daddy">Cat Daddy</a>" in 2011 which reached Number 77 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> and achieved Platinum certification independently.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JHawk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>JHawk grew up in a cultured park of Los Angeles, <a href="/wiki/Leimert_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Leimert Park">Leimert Park</a>. Both mother and father being musicians, mother being a local vocalist and father being a multi-instrumentalist. He is also the great nephew to <a href="/wiki/The_Chambers_Brothers" title="The Chambers Brothers">The Chambers Brothers</a>, (Time Has Come Today),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> often observing the band rehearse and play live shows for their great-grandmother. Shortly after receiving his first <a href="/wiki/Music_sequencer" title="Music sequencer">music sequencer</a> software Hip Hop <a href="/wiki/EJay" title="EJay">EJay</a> from his father, he started making beats at the age of 11 and desired to become a <a href="/wiki/Music_producer" class="mw-redirect" title="Music producer">music producer</a>. Stating to Killerhiphophop's Quez, "I put out my 1st song on the internet when I was about 15-16, and I've just been going hard ever since".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JHawk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.93present"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–present">2008–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JHawk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After 3 years and a junior at <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Murphy_High_School" title="Daniel Murphy High School">Daniel Murphy High School</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Archdiocese" class="mw-redirect" title="Archdiocese">Archdiocese</a> decided to shut the school's doors. Jeremy, who'd been building a name for himself on campus by recording students in his bedroom studio at his grandmother's was forced to relocate senior year. Stating to KHH, " I went to a private school called Daniel Murphy. It got shut down actually. I had to complete my last year of High School at a new High School".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After moving out of his grandmother's house, he moved in with his father. Gutting the garage and making a studio space in the spring of 2008. Often recording his cousin <a href="/wiki/Left_Brain" class="mw-redirect" title="Left Brain">Left Brain</a>, who brought <a href="/wiki/Tyler_The_Creator" class="mw-redirect" title="Tyler The Creator">Tyler The Creator</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hodgy_Beats" class="mw-redirect" title="Hodgy Beats">Hodgy Beats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Casey_Veggies" title="Casey Veggies">Casey Veggies</a>, of the year old collective <a href="/wiki/Odd_future" class="mw-redirect" title="Odd future">Odd future</a>. Recording and producing for the collective, <a href="/wiki/The_Odd_Future_Tape" class="mw-redirect" title="The Odd Future Tape">The Odd Future Tape</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Dena_Tape" title="The Dena Tape">The Dena Tape</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>JHawk attended The Academy of Music and Performing Arts <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Hamilton_High_School_(Los_Angeles)" title="Alexander Hamilton High School (Los Angeles)">Alexander Hamilton High School (Los Angeles)</a> in the summer of 2008. Due to a credit issue, he was required to attend a piano lesson class for admission and freshman PE class his senior year. Subsequently, adding an <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronic Music">Electronic Music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Music_History" class="mw-redirect" title="Music History">Music History</a> course, soon meeting friend <a href="/wiki/Syd_Tha_Kyd" class="mw-redirect" title="Syd Tha Kyd">Syd Tha Kyd</a> in Hamilton's Mr. Bruning <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic music</a> class. After being at <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Hamilton_High_School_(Los_Angeles)" title="Alexander Hamilton High School (Los Angeles)">Hamilton</a> for months and remaining unnoticed he aggressively fought to gain attention. Garnering his <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> page he began to send out beats, connecting with <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG (rapper)</a> on MySpace, the two collaborated on songs that would combine for over 3 Million plays.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Jerkin.27"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Jerkin'">Jerkin'</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JHawk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Jerkin&#039;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With a heavy influence from then <a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">Hyphy</a> movement, (<a href="/wiki/Keak_da_Sneak" title="Keak da Sneak">Keak da Sneak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turf_Talk" title="Turf Talk">Turf Talk</a>, <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>) and newfound fame from working with YG &amp; <i>The Pu$haz</i> Jeremy started to collaborate with anyone, often his fellow students on campus. Slowly building recognition, kids on campus started to dance to the music, in all styles, but most notably a new style called <a href="/wiki/Jerkin%27" title="Jerkin&#39;">Jerkin'</a>. Taking notice saying, "I never sought out to make a jerk beat. I always just did me. I did JHawk. People danced to my music and I kind of developed a formula to what they were grabbing off of my beats."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Eventually dance crews such as the Rangers formed on campus and videos were made with the music to match amassing tens of millions of hits on <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> and the newly created <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. At the age of 17 and having worked with almost all of La's male youth, he longed to form a group. On the bleachers in the PE class he was succumbed to is when he met 6 female freshman who all rapped. The same day they quickly formed the name of the group <i>Pink Dollaz</i>. After 2 months the music was streamed over 5 million times across <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> and <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> with radio spins from <i>Dj Carisma</i>'s <a href="/wiki/KPWR" title="KPWR">Power 106</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>   A slew of relationships such as these formed by spring 2009. Another song titled 'Tippin' today amasses 14 million plays on one <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> video alone.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/LA_Weekly" title="LA Weekly">LA Weekly</a>'s Jeff Weiss stating, "The wiry J-Hawk is the nexus that binds all of these groups together. After all, he's produced their hits, and to think it might not have happened if Daniel Murphy High hadn't shuttered last year, causing the 17-year-old Hawkins to transfer to Hamilton".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The genre, sound and culture exploded mainstream. Accidentally, JHawk was born. After amassing press mentions from <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/LA_Times" class="mw-redirect" title="LA Times">LA Times</a><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/LA_Weekly" title="LA Weekly">LA Weekly</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and a host of acts signed to major label deals.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/New_Boyz" title="New Boyz">New Boyz</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Rej3ctz" title="The Rej3ctz">The Rej3ctz</a>, <i>Cold Flamez</i>, <a href="/wiki/Audio_Push" title="Audio Push">Audio Push</a>, <i>The Bangz</i>, and <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG (rapper)</a>. JHawk stated, "I'm definitely fortunate for the movement and being one of the starters of it back in 2008".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cat_Daddy">Cat Daddy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JHawk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cat Daddy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a year and growing frustrated he re-connected with <a href="/wiki/The_Rej3ctz" title="The Rej3ctz">The Rej3ctz</a> sharing similar emotions, "We were major contributors, but we felt we weren't getting our shine. So we wanted to start a new movement."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In the spring of 2010 at JHawk's <a href="/wiki/Home_recording" title="Home recording">Home recording</a> studio crafted, <a href="/wiki/Cat_Daddy" title="Cat Daddy">Cat Daddy</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flight_In_Progress">Flight In Progress</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JHawk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Flight In Progress">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Is the Music Business Venture from Music Producer JHawk, which currently helps Independent Artist and Music Producers navigate Music Distribution, Music Streaming &amp; more in the ever-changing Music Industry. Currently the content is delivered via <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="22andlife">22andlife</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JHawk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 22andlife">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The production company founded by <b>Jeremy JHawk Hawkins</b>, currently it boats no artist and solely exists as a creative outlet for music projects, videos, street-wear and events.
</p>

<p>After creating a buzz in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, (2009) 17 year old JHawk was vigorously sought after for a <a href="/wiki/Music_publisher_(popular_music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Music publisher (popular music)">music publishing</a> deal.
