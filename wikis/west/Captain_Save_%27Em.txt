<p><i><b>Ant Banks Presents T.W.D.Y.: Derty Werk</b></i> is the debut studio album by American hip hop <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> T.W.D.Y. (The Whole Damn Yay). It was released on April 20, 1999 via <a href="/wiki/Thump_Records" title="Thump Records">Thump Street Records</a>. <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">Production</a> was handled entirely by member <a href="/wiki/Ant_Banks" title="Ant Banks">Ant Banks</a>, who also served as executive producer. It features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Otis_%26_Shug" title="Otis &amp; Shug">Otis &amp; Shug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gripsta" title="Gripsta">Gripsta</a>, Playa Metro, <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too $hort</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agerman" class="mw-redirect" title="Agerman">Agerman</a>, <a href="/wiki/B-Legit" title="B-Legit">B-Legit</a>, <a href="/wiki/CJ_Mac" title="CJ Mac">CJ Mac</a>, Dolla Will, J-Dubb, <a href="/wiki/Keak_da_Sneak" title="Keak da Sneak">Keak da Sneak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Mall" title="Mac Mall">Mac Mall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Shawn" class="mw-redirect" title="Mac Shawn">Mac Shawn</a>, MC Ant, <a href="/wiki/Pooh-Man" title="Pooh-Man">Pooh-Man</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spice_1" title="Spice 1">Spice 1</a>.
</p><p>The album debuted at number 135 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and at number 41 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> in the United States. Its lead single, "Players Holiday", peaked at number 90 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and become the group's most successful single, with music video starring all the three T.W.D.Y. members Ant Banks, <a href="/wiki/Rappin%27_4-Tay" title="Rappin&#39; 4-Tay">Rappin' 4-Tay</a> and Captain Save 'Em, as well as Mac Mall, Too $hort, Otis &amp; Shug, E-40 and <a href="/wiki/Ray_Luv" title="Ray Luv">Ray Luv</a>. The album's second single, "Drinks on Me", made it to number 18 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Songs" title="Hot Rap Songs">Hot Rap Songs</a> chart.
</p><p>Banks settled his rivalry with MC Pooh-Man when they appeared together on the song "Ride Wit Me". The album is dedicated to Anthony "M.C. Ant" Thomas, who was killed in early 1999 and made his posthumous appearance on the song "Shook Niggas".
</p>
