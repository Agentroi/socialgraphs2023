font-size: 125%;"><div class="fn">Messy Marv</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Marvin Watson Jr.</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Also known as</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Cashlord Mess, The Boy Boy, Lil Paper'd Up Mess</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday">1976-03-19</span>) </span>March 19, 1976<span class="noprint ForceAgeToShow"> (age&#160;47)</span><sup id="cite_ref-cbi_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbi-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast hip hop</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role"><a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">Rapper</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">1995–present</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data">Scalen LLC / Probiotics LLC / Click Clack Records/ Cashout Records / Mozzy Records(Current)</td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Marvin Watson Jr.</b> (born March 19, 1976),<sup id="cite_ref-cbi_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbi-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Messy Marv</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> who began his career in the <a href="/wiki/Fillmore_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Fillmore District">Fillmore District</a> of <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Messy was also known as a collaborator in the rap group Kaalman's Krew.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Messy_Marv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Messy Marv released his first rap album, <i><a href="/wiki/Messy_Situationz" title="Messy Situationz">Messy Situationz</a></i>, in 1996, when he was in 10th grade. <i><a href="/wiki/Explosive_Mode" title="Explosive Mode">Explosive Mode</a></i>, his 1998 collaboration album with <a href="/wiki/San_Quinn" title="San Quinn">San Quinn</a>, sold more than 50,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-mmm_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mmm-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, Messy Marv started his own record label, Scalen LLC,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and released a barrage of albums in 2004-2006. During the <a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">Hyphy movement</a>, his songs "Get on My Hype" and "So Hood" received frequent radio airplay.<sup id="cite_ref-mmm_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mmm-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 2, 2007, Messy Marv was released from jail after serving twelve months on weapons charges.<sup id="cite_ref-dream_deferred_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dream_deferred-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Before, during and following his jail sentence, he released several albums which made the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">charts</a>: <i><a href="/wiki/Cake_%26_Ice_Cream_2" title="Cake &amp; Ice Cream 2">Cake &amp; Ice Cream 2</a></i>, released in 2009 and peaked on the <a href="/wiki/R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> chart at #39 and on the <a href="/wiki/Rap_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap Albums">Rap Albums</a> chart at #12, and <i><a href="/wiki/Blow:_Blocks_and_Boat_Docks" title="Blow: Blocks and Boat Docks">Blow: Blocks and Boat Docks</a></i>, a collaboration album with <a href="/wiki/Berner_(rapper)" title="Berner (rapper)">Berner</a> released in 2010 which peaked at #48 on the R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums chart.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In the spring of 2012, Messy Marv released the mixtape, <i>Da Frank Lucas Dat Neva Wore Da Mink Coat</i> and the single, "You Gotta Pay Me". In 2013, he announced the release of his newest LP "Playboy Gangsta" under his new moniker "Lil Paper'd Up Mess", and released 3 singles as promotionals for the album: "We Killas", "Beautiful Kalifornia", &amp; "A Girlfriend Ain't What I Need". Marv confirmed via Twitter a release date of July 23, 2013 for the LP.  Early in 2014  with a  guest feature on single "World Wide Mob" alongside  Montana Montana Montana, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Blow" class="mw-redirect" title="Joe Blow">Joe Blow</a>, Ap.9, and Fed X<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 3, 2018, Messy Marv and San Quinn released a 12 song compilation titled <i>Joc Nation (Well Connected)</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 3, 2018, Messy Marv aka Cashlord Mess signed to Mozzy Records.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Messy_Marv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a teenager, Messy Marv <a href="/wiki/Dropping_out" title="Dropping out">dropped out</a> of high school because of family problems. Rapper <a href="/wiki/San_Quinn" title="San Quinn">San Quinn</a> and his mother took Messy Marv in when his living situation was unstable. As his music became more popular, Messy Marv struggled with <a href="/wiki/Substance_abuse" title="Substance abuse">substance abuse</a> and addiction.<sup id="cite_ref-mmm_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mmm-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In recent years, Messy Marv has been in and out of prison.<sup id="cite_ref-at_large_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-at_large-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dream_deferred_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dream_deferred-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He was arrested again in 2019 for robbery, among other charges.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Feuds">Feuds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Messy_Marv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Feuds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Messy Marv has feuded with a number of other rappers over the years.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Obie_Trice">Obie Trice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Messy_Marv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Obie Trice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, Messy Marv was involved in a feud with <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>, which escalated when <a href="/wiki/Obie_Trice" title="Obie Trice">Obie Trice</a>, a member of <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a>, said in a radio interview on <a href="/wiki/KMEL" title="KMEL">KMEL</a>: "in San Francisco I have to cover my ass."
</p><p>Following this, Guce and Messy Marv released a diss track, "50 Explanations", on the album <i>Pill Music: The Rico Act, Vol. 1</i>. <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sha_Money_XL" title="Sha Money XL">Sha Money XL</a> then exchanged words over the phone with Guce's camp. 
Guce stated afterwards, "it's over with, and it never kicked off".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="San_Quinn">San Quinn</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Messy_Marv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: San Quinn">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 22, 2008, Messy Marv initiated a feud with his cousin San Quinn, by calling him a <a href="/wiki/Informant" title="Informant">snitch</a>. On December 11, Quinn told HipHopDX that he "loves" Messy Marv despite the <a href="/wiki/Diss_tracks" class="mw-redirect" title="Diss tracks">diss tracks</a> that they have both exchanged.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In a 2010 interview with WordofSouth, he stated the reason for the beef was because he was "talking too much", but that they are attempting to squash the feud.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spider_Loc">Spider Loc</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Messy_Marv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Spider Loc">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 19, 2007, rapper <a href="/wiki/Spider_Loc" title="Spider Loc">Spider Loc</a> released a diss track towards Marv, and <a href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Game (rapper)">Game</a> called "Ova Killa" featuring Papa Smurf.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mistah_F.A.B.">Mistah F.A.B.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Messy_Marv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mistah F.A.B.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Messy Marv mocked rapper <a href="/wiki/Mistah_F.A.B." title="Mistah F.A.B.">Mistah F.A.B.</a>'s chain getting stolen repeatedly.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> This led to diss tracks being exchanged between the rappers, including F.A.B. sending one entitled "Okay" where he impersonates Watson Jr.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Too_.24hort"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Too_$hort">Too $hort</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Messy_Marv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Too $hort">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, Messy Marv began speaking negatively about <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too $hort</a>. Messy Marv released a <a href="/wiki/Diss_(music)" title="Diss (music)">diss track</a> called "Class of 84 (Fuck Too $hort)", and further criticized $hort during an interview with <a href="/wiki/AllHipHop" title="AllHipHop">AllHipHop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-beyond_beef_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-beyond_beef-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> $hort made a diss track called "Where You At" in response.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Philthy_Rich">Philthy Rich</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Messy_Marv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Philthy Rich">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 25, 2013, <a href="/wiki/Philthy_Rich" title="Philthy Rich">Philthy Rich</a> released a track entitled "Swear to God" featuring Kurt Diggler, where he dissed Messy Marv and other Bay Area rappers <a href="/wiki/Kafani" title="Kafani">Kafani</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/DB_Tha_General" class="mw-redirect" title="DB Tha General">DB Tha General</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On October 15 of the same year, Philthy Rich released a freestyle diss track about Marv over the beat of <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Pound_Cake_/_Paris_Morton_Music_2" title="Pound Cake / Paris Morton Music 2">Pound Cake / Paris Morton Music 2</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The next day, under his <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">alias</a> "Lil Paper'd Up Mess", Marv responded with the diss track "I'm Right Here",<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> originally with plans to follow up with a full album entitled <i>Philthy Rich Is A Bitch</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The same day, October 16, Philthy released a diss record entitled <i>Messy Marv Is A Fake Blood</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Years later, Philthy Rich discussed the feud in a 2022 interview.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
