<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Mr Capone E was born in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> to an ethnic <a href="/wiki/Hazaras" title="Hazaras">Hazara</a> family.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He and his family moved to <a href="/wiki/West_Covina,_California" title="West Covina, California">West Covina, California</a> at an early age.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He grew up around gangs and became a <a href="/wiki/Sure%C3%B1os" title="Sure√±os">Sure√±o</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> He was incarcerated at 18, and while serving time he decided he wanted to pursue a career in Hip Hop.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span></h2>
<h3><span id="2000-2005.2C_career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000-2005,_career_beginnings">2000-2005, career beginnings</span></h3>
<p>Mr. Capone-E released his first solo album in 2000 titled <i>Mr. Capone E &amp; The Southsiders</i> independently through his label Hi Power. This album received a lot of buzz and caught the attention of <a href="/wiki/Thump_Records" title="Thump Records">Thump Records</a>. He signed a three-year distribution deal and released his next album <i>Last Man Standing</i> in 2001.  
In 2003 Mr Capone E released his third album <i>Dedicated To The Oldies</i> which he claims sold over one million records through swap meets and discotecas.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He went on to release <i>Always and Forever</i> in 2003, which featured the single "I Like It" with <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a>. The album also featured guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Kokane" title="Kokane">Kokane</a>, Mr. Criminal and other artists from his label Hi Power.
</p><p>In 2005 he released the albums <i>A Soldier's Story</i> and <i>Ol Skool Music Vol.1 and Vol. 2</i> with <a href="/wiki/Zapp_(band)" title="Zapp (band)">Zapp</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2006-2013.2C_leave_from_Thump_Records.2C_independent_releases"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006-2013,_leave_from_Thump_Records,_independent_releases">2006-2013, leave from Thump Records, independent releases</span></h3>
<p>Money issues began to surface between Mr. Capone-E and Thump, which ultimately led to unpaid royalties to Mr. Capone-E.  He was encouraged to leave Thump based on the number of albums he was selling per release. He left and received  all the rights to his music released under Thump.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>That year Mr. Capone-E released his seventh album, distributed under <a href="/wiki/SMC_Recordings" title="SMC Recordings">SMC</a>, <i>Don't get it Twisted</i>, including the hit single "Don't Get it Twisted" ft. <a href="/wiki/Twista" title="Twista">Twista</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_singles_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_singles-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> which peaked at #18 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers">Top Heatseekers</a> Album Chart, #25 on the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Albums" title="Independent Albums">Independent Albums</a> Chart and #68 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> R&amp;B/Rap Albums.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_albums_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_albums-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, Mr. Capone-E signed an independent direct distribution deal with Koch, and released his eighth album, <i>Mr. Capone-E: Dedicated to the Oldies Part II</i>. It peaked at #17 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers">Top Heatseekers</a> Albums Chart<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_albums_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_albums-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  and #66 on the <i>Billboard</i> R&amp;B/ Hip Hop Album.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_albums_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_albums-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Capone-E released his ninth album, <i>Diary of a G</i>, including the single "<a href="/wiki/Light_my_Fire" class="mw-redirect" title="Light my Fire">Light my Fire</a>" ft. <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> and collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Game (rapper)">The Game</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glasses_Malone" title="Glasses Malone">Glasses Malone</a>, which peaked at #18 on the <i>Billboard</i> Rap Album Charts and #24 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers">Top Heatseekers</a> Album Charts.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_albums_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_albums-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> That year he also did a ring entrance song for the WBA Light Welterweight boxing champion at the time, <a href="/wiki/Amir_Khan_(boxer)" title="Amir Khan (boxer)">Amir Khan</a>.
</p><p>He continued to release albums independently over the years, releasing the albums <i>The Blue Album</i>, <i>Tears of a Soldier</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014-2016.2C_attempts_to_crack_the_mainstream.2C_No_Regrets_and_For_Respect"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014-2016,_attempts_to_crack_the_mainstream,_No_Regrets_and_For_Respect">2014-2016, attempts to crack the mainstream, <i>No Regrets</i> and <i>For Respect</i></span></h3>
<p>In 2014 Mr. Capone E started working on two albums, <i>No Regrets</i>, and <i>For Respect</i>. These albums have more of a mainstream sound to his previous albums, collaborating with artists such as <a href="/wiki/Migos" title="Migos">Migos</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ray_J" title="Ray J">Ray J</a>, and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Mustard" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Mustard">DJ Mustard</a>. To support the albums he released the mixtape <i>Los Angeles Times</i>. The albums were released in 2016.
</p>
<h3><span id="2017.2C_12_albums_in_12_months"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017,_12_albums_in_12_months">2017, 12 albums in 12 months</span></h3>
<p>In 2017 he released an album every month. He did this because he felt that he was not active musically over the past few years and needed to release a lot of music to become more relevant.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2018-present.2C_Narco_Valley.2C_autobiography.2C_and_other_ventures"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018-present,_Narco_Valley,_autobiography,_and_other_ventures">2018-present, <i>Narco Valley</i>, autobiography, and other ventures</span></h3>
<p>After releasing those 12 albums, he chose to focus more on building his label Hi Power and sign more up and coming talent.
</p><p>On March 13, 2018, he made his film debut in <i>Narco Valley</i>.
</p><p>He is also currently working on releasing his autobiography <i>Who Am I</i> detailing his childhood, his family and ethnicity, and his life and career.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hi_Power_Entertainment">Hi Power Entertainment</span></h3>
<p>Mr. Capone-E founded Hi Power records in 2000 with the release of his debut album. The independent label launched the career of fellow Chicano rap super star Mr. Criminal.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The label originally was only made up of Chicano rappers in his area, but as the label gained more popularity he signed artists like <a href="/wiki/Bizzy_Bone" title="Bizzy Bone">Bizzy Bone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Layzie_Bone" title="Layzie Bone">Layzie Bone</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs_N_Harmony" class="mw-redirect" title="Bone Thugs N Harmony">Bone Thugs N Harmony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Flip" class="mw-redirect" title="Lil Flip">Lil Flip</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Eight" class="mw-redirect" title="MC Eight">MC Eight</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bad_Azz_(rapper)" title="Bad Azz (rapper)">Bad Azz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suga_Free" title="Suga Free">Suga Free</a> among others.
</p>
