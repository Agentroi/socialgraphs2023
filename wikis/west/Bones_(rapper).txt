<p><b>Elmo Kennedy O'Connor</b> (born January 11, 1994), known professionally as <b>Bones</b> (stylized sometimes as <b>BONES</b>), is an American rapper, singer and songwriter from <a href="/wiki/Howell,_Michigan" title="Howell, Michigan">Howell, Michigan</a>. He is also the founder of the music collective TeamSESH.
</p><p>O'Connor is known for his pioneering work in sub-genres of hip-hop referred to as <a href="/wiki/Emo_rap" title="Emo rap">emo rap</a><sup id="cite_ref-The_FADER_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_FADER-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Trap_metal" class="mw-redirect" title="Trap metal">trap metal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kerrang_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kerrang-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Since 2010, O'Connor has released an extensive discography and developed a large fan base. As of 2019, he has released over 80 albums, mixtapes and <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EPs</a> across several aliases.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bones_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Elmo Kennedy O'Connor was born in <a href="/wiki/Marin_County,_California" title="Marin County, California">Marin County, California</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> to a mother who designed clothing and a father who was a web designer. His maternal grandfather was actor <a href="/wiki/Robert_Culp" title="Robert Culp">Robert Culp</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His family lived in <a href="/wiki/Muir_Beach,_California" title="Muir Beach, California">Muir Beach, California</a>, before moving to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. When O'Connor was seven years old, his family moved to <a href="/wiki/Howell,_Michigan" title="Howell, Michigan">Howell, Michigan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> O'Connor also lived in Minnesota for two years when he was thirteen. Around this time he met fellow artists <a href="/wiki/Xavier_Wulf" title="Xavier Wulf">Xavier Wulf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Travis" title="Chris Travis">Chris Travis</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eddy_Baker" title="Eddy Baker">Eddy Baker</a> online. The four artists would later come together to form the collective Seshollowaterboyz.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When he was 16, he dropped out of <a href="/wiki/Howell_High_School_(Howell,_Michigan)" title="Howell High School (Howell, Michigan)">Howell High School</a> and moved to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, where his brother and current manager, Elliott, had already been living.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Despite moving away from his parents at a young age, O'Connor speaks positively of them, stating, "If I tried to write a book about, 'oh, what would be dream parents?', I couldn't even make anything better than them. All they do is shower me in love. Unconditional love, forever."<sup id="cite_ref-dazed2017_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dazed2017-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> O'Connor describes Howell as a "mundane" place where its residents are "born and they die there",<sup id="cite_ref-dazed2017_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dazed2017-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> also calling it "one of the most racist towns in Michigan".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bones_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>O'Connor first started making music at the age of nine after his father purchased an <a href="/wiki/IMac_G3" title="IMac G3">iMac G3</a>. He would download <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> instrumentals from <a href="/wiki/SoundClick" title="SoundClick">SoundClick</a> and use the computer's built-in microphone to record himself rapping, though it wasn't until 2010 at the age of 16 that he would start releasing music online under the alias "Th@ Kid". After moving to Los Angeles, O'Connor connected with other artists he met online including <a href="/wiki/Xavier_Wulf" title="Xavier Wulf">Xavier Wulf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Travis" title="Chris Travis">Chris Travis</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eddy_Baker" title="Eddy Baker">Eddy Baker</a>, who at the time were members of the now semi-disbanded <a href="/wiki/Raider_Klan" title="Raider Klan">Raider Klan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He later officially changed his stage name to Bones in 2012.
</p><p>On July 4, 2012, O'Connor released his eponymous debut album under the alias Bones. From 2012 to 2014 he released 12 albums<sup id="cite_ref-:5_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> before gaining journalistic attention for his 2014 album <i>TeenWitch</i>, which gained controversy for its themes based around the <a href="/wiki/Columbine_High_School_massacre" title="Columbine High School massacre">Columbine High School Massacre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The album following <i>TeenWitch</i>, titled <i>Garbage</i>, was released on June 9, 2014, and was the first of O'Connor's albums to gain the attention of major music publications such as <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex Magazine">Complex Magazine</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/The_Fader" title="The Fader">The Fader</a><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> who labelled the project as "massive" and "boundary-pushing."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In April 2014, O'Connor, along with TeamSESH producer Greaf, formed "surrenderdorothy", a <a href="/wiki/Side_project" title="Side project">side project</a> that is notably less focused on rap, with a stronger focus on acoustic guitar and singing, with stylistic similarities to <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emo_rap" title="Emo rap">emo rap</a>. Although O'Connor does experiment with this style in his solo work, surrenderdorothy features it much more predominantly. Since 2014, the duo have released six <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EPs</a> as surrenderdorothy.
</p><p>O'Connor uploaded several albums in 2015<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and headlined his first sold-out show at <a href="/wiki/House_of_Blues" title="House of Blues">House of Blues</a> on March 4, 2015<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> before opening for electronic artist <a href="/wiki/Shlohmo" title="Shlohmo">Shlohmo</a> at <a href="/wiki/The_Fonda_Theatre" title="The Fonda Theatre">The Fonda Theatre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> O'Connor was later featured on <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a>'s "Canal St.", which was a remix of O'Connor's song "Dirt". The song later became O'Connor's first feature on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Billboard charts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Bones performed "Canal St." with ASAP Rocky on the <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i> show, but was cut from the TV broadcast due to his refusal to censor some of the song's lyrics while performing. Videos of the performance were made available online.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> In May 2015, O'Connor and Greaf started releasing songs under a second <a href="/wiki/Side_project" title="Side project">side project</a> called Oregontrail. The project is stylistically similar to Surrenderdorothy, but has been noted as having a "darker" and "rougher" tone. It also follows a loose narrative related to the <a href="/wiki/The_Oregon_Trail_(1985_video_game)" title="The Oregon Trail (1985 video game)">game</a> of the same name which is also reflected in the artwork of the singles. Since 2015, the duo have released seven singles as Oregontrail.
</p><p>O'Connor released 10 albums between 2015 and 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, he began releasing music under a new persona called Ricky A Go Go. He uploaded five tracks in total to a SoundCloud account for this persona with the fifth one being uploaded in 2017. There's a running joke in the description for each track as each one is the single for an album of a different name. None of these albums have released. These tracks have a <a href="/wiki/Disco" title="Disco">disco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Synthwave" title="Synthwave">synthwave</a> production style with sung vocals. There is a sixth track titled "Countdown" that is not on this account but has been uploaded by several other users. This track comes from the track "SeshRadio: Volume3" which is the final track on his <i>PaidProgramming2</i> project. This was the official debut of the persona. The Ricky A Go Go persona has made appearances on multiple other tracks in the Bones discography on various projects, most prominently the collaboration project <i>DamagedGoods</i> with Drew The Architect. However, a majority of these tracks still credit Bones as the artist.
</p><p>On January 19, 2018, O'Connor performed in <a href="/wiki/KOKO_(music_venue)" title="KOKO (music venue)">KOKO</a>, London, thus starting his first ever European tour, called the Deadboy Tour.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> It took him to countries such as Germany, Ukraine, Russia and Italy.
</p><p>O'Connor is a founding member of the collective Seshollowaterboyz, along with Xavier Wulf and Eddy Baker, with whom he often performs and collaborates.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto2_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The name of the collective is a mash-up of their self-releasing <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">labels</a> TeamSESH, Hollow Squad. Waterboyz and Healthy Boyz. They have performed at notable venues such as The Observatory in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Ana,_California" title="Santa Ana, California">Santa Ana, California</a> on November 13, 2015 <sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/The_Novo_by_Microsoft" title="The Novo by Microsoft">The Novo by Microsoft</a> in Los Angeles on January 30, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chris_Travis" title="Chris Travis">Chris Travis</a> was a founding member of the group but left in 2019.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_influences">Musical style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bones_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Musical style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although O'Connor is often considered to be one of the pioneers of the <a href="/wiki/Emo_rap" title="Emo rap">emo rap</a> sub-genre, O'Connor has said that he has no specific genre, though has been characterized as cloud rap, experimental hip-hop and "shadow rap".<sup id="cite_ref-:2_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto2_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> O'Connor's earlier music is a lot more futuristic (and is often compared to <a href="/wiki/SpaceGhostPurrp" title="SpaceGhostPurrp">SpaceGhostPurrp</a>'s<sup id="cite_ref-:1_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>) than his current music, which showcases production and vocal styles ranging from rapping, singing, and <a href="/wiki/Screaming_(music)" title="Screaming (music)">screaming</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-The_FADER_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_FADER-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> O'Connor's singing has been compared to <a href="/wiki/Grunge" title="Grunge">grunge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emo" title="Emo">emo</a>, while his rapping has been compared sub-genres such as <a href="/wiki/Horrorcore" title="Horrorcore">horrorcore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emo_rap" title="Emo rap">emo rap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> He is also considered an early pioneer of <a href="/wiki/Trap_metal" class="mw-redirect" title="Trap metal">trap metal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kerrang_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kerrang-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>O'Connor rarely talks about his musical influences, although during interviews he has mentioned  <a href="/wiki/Marvin_Gaye" title="Marvin Gaye">Marvin Gaye</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earth_Wind_and_Fire" class="mw-redirect" title="Earth Wind and Fire">Earth Wind and Fire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bootsy_Collins" title="Bootsy Collins">Bootsy Collins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Nicks" title="Stevie Nicks">Stevie Nicks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joni_Mitchell" title="Joni Mitchell">Joni Mitchell</a> as influences.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Over the years of his music career, O'Connor helped underground hip hop develop into new styles and has been noted as "one of the most influential underground artists of the internet age".<sup id="cite_ref-dazed2017_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dazed2017-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
