<p><b>Brandon Christopher McCartney</b> (born August 17, 1989),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> professionally known as <b>Lil B</b> and as his alter ego <b>the BasedGod</b>, is an American rapper. Lil B has recorded both solo and with <a href="/wiki/Bay_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Bay Area">Bay Area</a> group <a href="/wiki/The_Pack_(group)" title="The Pack (group)">the Pack</a>. His solo work spans several genres, including <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Age_music" class="mw-redirect" title="New Age music">new age</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Choral_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Choral music">choral music</a>. He describes his work as "based", a term which denotes a lifestyle of positivity and tolerance; and is noted for his extensive use of <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> to build an online <a href="/wiki/Cult_following" title="Cult following">cult following</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTJul2010_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTJul2010-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sfbg_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfbg-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>McCartney grew up in <a href="/wiki/Berkeley,_California" title="Berkeley, California">Berkeley</a>, California,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and attended high school at <a href="/wiki/Albany_High_School_(Albany,_California)" class="mw-redirect" title="Albany High School (Albany, California)">Albany High</a> in <a href="/wiki/Albany,_California" title="Albany, California">Albany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He adopted the name Lil B, and began rapping at age 15 with <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">San Francisco Bay Area</a> based hip hop group <a href="/wiki/The_Pack_(group)" title="The Pack (group)">The Pack</a>. After two locally successful mixtapes, at the peak of the Bay Area's <a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">hyphy movement</a>, the group's song "<a href="/wiki/Vans_(song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Vans (song)">Vans</a>" became a surprise hit. The song was ranked as the fifth best of 2006 by <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The strength of "Vans" led the group to release the <i><a href="/wiki/Skateboards_2_Scrapers" title="Skateboards 2 Scrapers">Skateboards 2 Scrapers</a></i> EP, featuring a "Vans" remix with Bay Area rappers <a href="/wiki/Too_$hort" class="mw-redirect" title="Too $hort">Too $hort</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mistah_F.A.B." title="Mistah F.A.B.">Mistah F.A.B.</a> In 2007, Lil B and The Pack released their first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Based_Boys" title="Based Boys">Based Boys</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span></h2>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.9310:_Solo_success_and_collaborations"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–10:_Solo_success_and_collaborations">2009–10: Solo success and collaborations</span></h3>
<p>On September 24, 2009, Lil B released his first <a href="/wiki/Digital_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital album">digital album</a>, <i>I'm Thraxx</i>, via independent label Permanent Marks.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On December 22, 2009, Lil B released his second digital album, <i>6 Kiss</i>, to critical reception.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On March 25, 2010, Lil B released his debut mixtape <i>Dior Paint</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On April 3, 2010, Lil B officially signed to fellow artist <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Boy" title="Soulja Boy">Soulja Boy</a>'s label <a href="/wiki/Stacks_on_Deck_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Stacks on Deck Entertainment">SODMG Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On May 7, 2010, Lil B released a mixtape entitled <i>Base World Pt. 1</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On July 5, 2010, Lil B released a collaboration mixtape with <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Boy" title="Soulja Boy">Soulja Boy</a> entitled <i>Pretty Boy Millionaires</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Lil B had recorded over 1,500 tracks as of July 2010, including hits "Like A Martian", "Wonton Soup", "Pretty Bitch", "I'm God", all of which were released for free.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On September 21, 2010, Lil B released his debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Rain_in_England" title="Rain in England">Rain in England</a></i>, through Weird Forest Records; it was described by <i> <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> as "a beatless, Beat poetry-style set where Lil B, voice a-quiver with earnestness, ponders love, beauty and all the bad things in the world over naïf new-age synth washes".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.93present:_Mixtapes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–present:_Mixtapes">2010–present: Mixtapes</span></h3>

<p>On December 29, 2010, it was announced and confirmed that Lil B apparently signed an album deal with <a href="/wiki/Amalgam_Digital" title="Amalgam Digital">Amalgam Digital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On July 10, 2011, Lil B released the EP <i>Paint</i>, through his label BasedWorld Records.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 18, 2011, Lil B released his fourth digital album entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Angels_Exodus" title="Angels Exodus">Angels Exodus</a></i>, through Amalgam Digital. On April 14, 2011, Lil B announced that his next album would be entitled <i>I'm Gay</i>, which caused a degree of controversy.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On June 29, 2011, Lil B released his fifth digital album, <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_Gay_(I%27m_Happy)" title="I&#39;m Gay (I&#39;m Happy)">I'm Gay (I'm Happy)</a></i>, through Amalgam Digital; the album entered the  <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a> <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> chart at number 56 and the <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers">Heatseekers Albums</a> chart at number 20 for the week of July 16, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 17, 2012, Lil B released his first instrumental album, <i><a href="/wiki/Choices_and_Flowers" title="Choices and Flowers">Choices and Flowers</a></i>, under the alias "The Basedgod".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On September 16, 2012, Lil B released a <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> single entitled "<a href="/wiki/California_Boy" title="California Boy">California Boy</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On December 30, 2012, Lil B released his second instrumental album entitled <i>Tears 4 God</i>, also under the alias "The Basedgod".<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 24, 2013, Lil B released the mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/05_Fuck_Em" title="05 Fuck Em">05 Fuck Em</a></i>, which contained 101 songs.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On June 1, 2014, Lil B released a mixtape entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Hoop_Life" title="Hoop Life">Hoop Life</a></i>, which would be known for containing a track entitled "F*ck KD" that called out <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">NBA</a> player <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Durant" title="Kevin Durant">Kevin Durant</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On October 14, 2014, Lil B released the <i>Ultimate Bitch</i> mixtape, featuring the song "<a href="/wiki/No_Black_Person_Is_Ugly" title="No Black Person Is Ugly">No Black Person Is Ugly</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On July 19, 2015, Lil B and <a href="/wiki/Chance_the_Rapper" title="Chance the Rapper">Chance the Rapper</a> announced that they recorded a new collaborative mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lil B was featured in <a href="/wiki/Terror_Jr" title="Terror Jr">Terror Jr</a>'s remix of their song "Come First" released, in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 17, 2017, Lil B released <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Ken" title="Black Ken">Black Ken</a></i>, describing it as his "first official mixtape."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape reached number 24 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers">Top Heatseekers</a> chart and number 44 on the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Albums" title="Independent Albums">Independent Albums</a> chart for the week of September 2, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Lil_B_at_Coachella_2011.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Lil_B_at_Coachella_2011.png/220px-Lil_B_at_Coachella_2011.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="157" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Lil_B_at_Coachella_2011.png/330px-Lil_B_at_Coachella_2011.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Lil_B_at_Coachella_2011.png/440px-Lil_B_at_Coachella_2011.png 2x" data-file-width="848" data-file-height="606" /></a><figcaption>Lil B at <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella</a> music festival in 2011</figcaption></figure>
<p>Lil B and music critics refer to his rapping style as "based", a word that Lil B also uses to describe a positive, tolerant lifestyle. "Based" is a <a href="/wiki/Reclaimed_word" class="mw-redirect" title="Reclaimed word">reclaimed word</a>, as described by Lil B in <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i>:<sup id="cite_ref-BASED-KQED_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BASED-KQED-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BASED-Complex_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BASED-Complex-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Based means being yourself. Not being scared of what people think about you. Not being afraid to do what you wanna do. Being positive. When I was younger, based was a negative term that meant like dopehead, or basehead. People used to make fun of me. They was like, "You're based." They'd use it as a negative. And what I did was turn that negative into a positive. I started embracing it like, "Yeah, I'm based." I made it mine. I embedded it in my head. Based is positive.</p></blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rapping_technique">Rapping technique</span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Slate_(magazine)" title="Slate (magazine)"><i>Slate</i></a> columnist Jonah Weiner labeled him as one of a "growing number of weird-o emcees", calling him a "brilliantly warped, post-<a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> <a href="/wiki/Deconstructionism" class="mw-redirect" title="Deconstructionism">deconstructionist</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Bay_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Bay Area">Bay Area</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Musical critic Willy Staley described Lil B's work as "variegated", because it ranges from critical parodies of the hip-hop genre to "half new age, half spoken word". He further notes that Lil B draws from a large variety of genres, especially those not commonly used by other rappers. In an interview with Staley, Lil B agrees with this analysis, saying, "I can do 'Swag OD' but then my favorite musical artist right now could be <a href="/wiki/Antony_and_the_Johnsons" class="mw-redirect" title="Antony and the Johnsons">Antony and the Johnsons</a>. That's the difference between me and these other rappers, and other musical artists in general."<sup id="cite_ref-sfbg_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfbg-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_ventures">Other ventures</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Author">Author</span></h3>
<p><i>Takin' Over by Imposing the Positive!</i> is a book written by McCartney and published through Kele Publishing in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>  The book is a collection of and written in the form of e-mails and text messages, and is written in such a way that the author is e-mailing the reader. Subjects include positivity, optimism, and living what he calls a "Based Lifestyle".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>  The book was passed out in an unscripted NYU lecture in March 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-NewYorker_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NewYorker-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On March 30, 2013, McCartney announced that he was in the process of writing his second book.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Motivational_speaker">Motivational speaker</span></h3>
<p>Lil B has given motivational lectures at several colleges, including <a href="/wiki/MIT" class="mw-redirect" title="MIT">MIT</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University" title="Carnegie Mellon University">Carnegie Mellon University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> They are generally focused around his personal experience in life and current events.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On May 28, 2015, the rapper gave a lecture at <a href="/wiki/UCLA" class="mw-redirect" title="UCLA">UCLA</a>, where he touched on subjects like money, the media, technology, space, awareness, and love.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Basedmoji_and_vegEMOJI_apps">Basedmoji and vegEMOJI apps</span></h3>
<p>Lil B launched the "Basedmoji" app on January 16, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> On January 17, 2015, Lil B released "vegEMOJI", in cooperation with vegan company "Follow Your Heart",<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BASED-DDot_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BASED-DDot-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> despite the fact that Lil B is not yet a <a href="/wiki/Vegan" class="mw-redirect" title="Vegan">vegan</a>, he has stated that he is cutting down on his consumption of processed foods, and that he is "ashamed of eating meat".<sup id="cite_ref-BASED-DDot_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BASED-DDot-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>On January 16, 2015, Lil B's apartment building in <a href="/wiki/Contra_Costa_County,_California" title="Contra Costa County, California">Contra Costa County, California</a>, caught on fire early in the morning on Thursday after an electrical fire spread through the building. Lil B and six other people were saved by 15-year-old Mateo Ysmael, who ran through the building to wake everyone up.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For the <a href="/wiki/2016_U.S._presidential_election" class="mw-redirect" title="2016 U.S. presidential election">2016 U.S. presidential election</a>, he endorsed <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a> Senator <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Sanders_presidential_campaign,_2016" class="mw-redirect" title="Bernie Sanders presidential campaign, 2016">Bernie Sanders</a>, citing his civil rights record.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies_and_feuds">Controversies and feuds</span></h2>
<h3><span id="I.27m_Gay"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="I'm_Gay"><i>I'm Gay</i></span></h3>
<p>When Lil B released his fifth album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_Gay_(I%27m_Happy)" title="I&#39;m Gay (I&#39;m Happy)">I'm Gay</a></i>, he received several death threats. Although he is <a href="/wiki/Heterosexual" class="mw-redirect" title="Heterosexual">heterosexual</a>, he says the title is a message of support to the <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a> community. Referring to the original definition of gay, he says he is <a href="/wiki/Gay" title="Gay">gay</a> because he is happy, and subsequently changed the title to <i>I'm Gay (I'm Happy)</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Joe_Budden">Joe Budden</span></h3>
<p>In 2010, a number of exchanges between Lil B and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a> were had over <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>. Budden had been seeming to speak mockingly about Lil B's "Based" movement and his tweets, to which Lil B responded, initially friendly but then with insults.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Lil B went on to release a diss track called "T Shirts &amp; Buddens",<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> which was then featured on his "Everything Based" mixtape. Lil B later apologized for his insults and noted his respect for Budden, calling him a "legend".<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Game">The Game</span></h3>
<p>In 2011, after hearing a verse from Lil B on the Lil Wayne mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Sorry_4_the_Wait" title="Sorry 4 the Wait">Sorry 4 the Wait</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Game (rapper)">Game</a> referred to Lil B as the "wackest rapper of all time." Lil B responded by calling Game "irrelevant," to which Game then threatened to knock out Lil B.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Game targeted Lil B in his verse in his track "Martians vs Goblins" featuring <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a>, with the line "Tie Lil B up to a tank full of propane, swag, now watch him cook". Lil B addressed this on his track "Tank of Propaine" on his "White Flame" mixtape. Several weeks later, the two settled their differences through Twitter after which Lil B urged fans to purchase Game's <i><a href="/wiki/The_R.E.D._Album" title="The R.E.D. Album">The R.E.D. Album</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Joey_Bada.24.24"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Joey_Bada$$">Joey Bada$$</span></h3>
<p>Lil B took offense to the lyrics in the song "Survival Tactics" by late rapper <a href="/wiki/Capital_STEEZ" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital STEEZ">Capital STEEZ</a>, a founding member of the group <a href="/wiki/Pro_Era" title="Pro Era">Pro Era</a>. In this, he raps, "They say hard work pays off / Well, tell the BasedGod don't quit his day job." Lil B responded with a song titled "I'm The Bada$$". <a href="/wiki/Joey_Bada$$" class="mw-redirect" title="Joey Bada$$">Joey Bada$$</a> then responded with a song titled "Don't Quit Your Day Job!"<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> When the feud became public on Twitter, Joey became a target of a lot of attacks from Lil B's fans, which ended up with Joey deleting his Twitter account,<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> though restoring it later. In an interview with <a href="/wiki/WWPR-FM" title="WWPR-FM">WWPR-FM</a>, Joey Badass denied that he deleted his Twitter account because of Lil B's fans.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Later, in an interview with VladTV, Joey admitted the feud was created for publicity, and admitted he's a fan of Lil B's more serious work.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kevin_Durant">Kevin Durant</span></h3>
<p>In 2011, <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">NBA</a> superstar <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Durant" title="Kevin Durant">Kevin Durant</a> tweeted his befuddlement with Lil B's popularity, and Lil B responded by "cursing" Durant that he would never win the NBA championship.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> The curse had been rescinded in 2012 but then reinstated in 2014. The feud between the two has simmered since then, resulting in Lil B releasing the diss track "<a href="/wiki/Hoop_Life" title="Hoop Life">Fuck KD</a>" in 2014 and a commercial on <a href="/wiki/NBA_TV" title="NBA TV">NBA TV</a>, where Lil B calls out Kevin Durant.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SportsNation_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SportsNation-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> Lil B has claimed the "curse of the Based God" to be responsible for Durant and his <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City_Thunder" title="Oklahoma City Thunder">Oklahoma City Thunder</a> team's loss to the Golden State Warriors in the <a href="/wiki/NBA_Conference_Finals#Conference" title="NBA Conference Finals">Western Conference Finals</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2016_NBA_Playoffs" class="mw-redirect" title="2016 NBA Playoffs">2016 NBA Playoffs</a>. The Thunder had been up 3 games to 1 in a best-of-7 series, but then went on to lose the series in stunning fashion after losing the next 3 games in a row.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> On July 4, 2016, following the announcement of Durant leaving the Thunder for the <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a>, Lil B rescinded the curse again.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> Coincidentally, Durant proceeded to win an NBA Championship the following two years in 2017 and 2018.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="James_Harden">James Harden</span></h3>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/NBA_Conference_Finals#Conference" title="NBA Conference Finals">Western Conference Finals</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2015_NBA_Playoffs" class="mw-redirect" title="2015 NBA Playoffs">2015 NBA Playoffs</a>, Lil B began questioning NBA superstar <a href="/wiki/James_Harden" title="James Harden">James Harden</a>'s "cooking dance", a dance allegedly coined by Lil B which he had been doing all season long, and tweeted that if he doesn't receive an answer from Harden regarding that dance then Harden will receive the "Based God curse" similar to Kevin Durant.<sup id="cite_ref-nbcsports_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nbcsports-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> Lil B attributed the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Rockets" title="Houston Rockets">Houston Rockets</a> loss to the <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a> with the score of 99–98 in Game 2, and again in Game 3 with the score of 115–80, to the curse.<sup id="cite_ref-nbcsports_67-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nbcsports-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> On May 24, 2015, Lil B announced on <i><a href="/wiki/TMZ" title="TMZ">TMZ Sports</a></i> that he has placed Harden under the "Based God curse" for the remainder of the playoffs and until further notice.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> On May 27, 2015, Lil B was present at <a href="/wiki/Oracle_Arena" class="mw-redirect" title="Oracle Arena">Oracle Arena</a> for Game 5 where the Warriors ultimately defeated the Rockets with the score of 104–90, becoming the Western Conference champions.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, during this same game Harden set an NBA Playoff record with 13 <a href="/wiki/Turnover_(basketball)" title="Turnover (basketball)">turnovers</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup> prompting Lil B to publicly consider lifting the curse.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> On June 4, 2017, Lil B announced on a live taping of <i><a href="/wiki/First_Take_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="First Take (TV series)">First Take</a></i> that he has lifted the curse from Harden.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie_and_PnB_Rock">A Boogie wit da Hoodie and PnB Rock</span></h3>
<p>At the 2017 Rolling Loud Bay Area festival, Lil B was forced to cancel his set due to an alleged altercation with <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a> backstage.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_74-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup> Upon taking the stage to announce the cancellation of his set, he told the crowd he was attacked by "A Boogie and his whole crew" and that his equipment was also stolen, attributing the event to his criticism of New York hip hop in a recent Tweet.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_75-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> Footage of the altercation subsequently surfaced, and Lil B fans immediately expressed outrage on social media.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_76-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup> Witnesses backstage also accused <a href="/wiki/PnB_Rock" title="PnB Rock">PnB Rock</a> of being involved in the attack. Despite the incident, Lil B maintained a positive stance and even urged his supporters to forgive A Boogie later that day on Twitter.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_74-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:6_76-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:7_77-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The incident led to an immediate wave of support of Lil B from fans and other figures in the music industry. <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a> and <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>, fellow performers at the festival, expressed their support for the rapper upon taking the stage for their respective sets.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> Other artists including <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skepta" title="Skepta">Skepta</a>, <a href="/wiki/G-Eazy" title="G-Eazy">G-Eazy</a>, <a href="/wiki/6lack" title="6lack">6lack</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kreayshawn" title="Kreayshawn">Kreayshawn</a>, <a href="/wiki/A-Trak" title="A-Trak">A-Trak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alison_Wonderland" title="Alison Wonderland">Alison Wonderland</a>, <a href="/wiki/SpaceGhostPurrp" title="SpaceGhostPurrp">SpaceGhostPurrp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lupe_Fiasco" title="Lupe Fiasco">Lupe Fiasco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kaytranada" title="Kaytranada">Kaytranada</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Dean_(record_producer)" title="Mike Dean (record producer)">Mike Dean</a> also expressed their support of the rapper on social media.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_75-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:7_77-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:8_78-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> Amidst the fallout of the incident, PnB Rock was pulled from the festival's lineup and replaced by Kreayshawn.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_77-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup> Lil B and A Boogie officially ended the feud two days later, through a phone call initiated by Kilo Curt of the late <a href="/wiki/Mac_Dre" title="Mac Dre">Mac Dre's</a> <a href="/wiki/Thizz_Entertainment" title="Thizz Entertainment">Thizz Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup> Both artists took to Twitter to announce the end of the feud.
</p>
