<p><b>Nicholas Diego Leanos</b><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (born September 6, 1996), better known as <b>Lil Xan</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;z&#39; in &#39;zoom&#39;">z</span><span title="/æ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;bad&#39;">æ</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">ZANN</span></i></a>) or simply <b>Diego</b>, is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter from <a href="/wiki/Redlands,_California" title="Redlands, California">Redlands, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He is best known for his song "<a href="/wiki/Betrayed_(Lil_Xan_song)" title="Betrayed (Lil Xan song)">Betrayed</a>", which was certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and peaked at number 64 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. On April 6, 2018, Leanos released his debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Xanarchy" title="Total Xanarchy">Total Xanarchy</a></i>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span></h2>
<p>Growing up, Leanos was <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poor</a> and his family lived in motels during most of his childhood.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Redlands_East_Valley_High_School" title="Redlands East Valley High School">Redlands East Valley High School</a>, but dropped out of high school in his freshman year, and spent several years at home unemployed.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nojumper_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nojumper-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Leanos took a job as a street cleaner and sold drugs<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> before he began rapping.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Leanos later pursued a photography career in support of several friends who were rappers. He eventually had his camera stolen, and chose to begin rapping in lieu of investing in a new camera.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL2_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Lil_Xan.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Lil_Xan.png/220px-Lil_Xan.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="313" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/76/Lil_Xan.png 1.5x" data-file-width="289" data-file-height="411" /></a><figcaption>Lil Xan performing in 2018</figcaption></figure>
<p>His stage name is derived from Xanax,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> the trade name of the prescription drug <a href="/wiki/Alprazolam" title="Alprazolam">alprazolam</a>. Leanos began to gain recognition through platforms such as <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> and <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. His popularity grew after the release of the music video for his song "Betrayed" in August 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The song peaked at number 64 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hot_1002_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hot_1002-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i>, Leanos announced his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Xanarchy" title="Total Xanarchy">Total Xanarchy</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL2_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The album includes collaborations with artists such as <a href="/wiki/Diplo" title="Diplo">Diplo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swae_Lee" title="Swae Lee">Swae Lee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In December 2017, Leanos announced his <i>Total Xanarchy</i> tour,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> which sold out in five hours according to <i>Billboard</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, Leanos considered changing his stage name to Diego in order to support his anti-drug message;<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> however, he has since expressed uncertainty toward officially going through with the pseudonym change.<sup id="cite_ref-nojumper_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nojumper-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Leanos released his debut studio album, <i>Total Xanarchy</i> on April 6, 2018. The album received mostly negative reviews from critics but was a moderate commercial success, debuting at number ten on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 and selling 28,000 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The following month in May 2018, Leanos announced the mixtape <i>Heartbreak Soldiers</i><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> which was released on July 8, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In September 2018, Leanos announced he had been working on an album titled <i>Be Safe</i> as a tribute to <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>, as these were the last words he said to Leanos before his death.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Leanos has given a number of contradictory statements regarding his yet-unreleased second album. Initially, he planned on releasing his <i>Be Safe</i> project in 2019, but the album entered production limbo and was eventually scrapped. In November 2019, he announced his next album would be titled <i>When September Ends</i> and released an accompanying album cover, but this also never came to fruition.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In January 2020, Leanos announced that his album was finished and that it would be called <i>Sorry I Didn't Quit</i>, revealing a new album cover that took inspiration from <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Sorry_4_the_Wait" title="Sorry 4 the Wait">Sorry 4 the Wait</a></i>, but this project appears to have been scrapped as well.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 24, 2022, Leanos released the EP <i>have a nice day</i> – his first project in over three years – as a collaboration with fellow emo rapper <a href="/wiki/Chris_Miles_(musician)" title="Chris Miles (musician)">Chris Miles</a>. The EP includes a feature from <a href="/wiki/Lil_Tracy" title="Lil Tracy">Lil Tracy</a> and was supported by the single "Miss Me".<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span></h2>
<p>Leanos has listed <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/N.E.R.D." title="N.E.R.D.">N.E.R.D.</a> as early influences in <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, with other influences including <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Monkeys" title="Arctic Monkeys">Arctic Monkeys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cage_the_Elephant" title="Cage the Elephant">Cage the Elephant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Queens_of_the_Stone_Age" title="Queens of the Stone Age">Queens of the Stone Age</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pigeons&amp;Planes2_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pigeons&amp;Planes2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Leanos has also described <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a> as musical inspirations.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard2_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to <i>Pigeons &amp; Planes</i>, Leanos' music started off as "typical trap" and later shifted towards "a murkier, dream-like sound".<sup id="cite_ref-Pigeons&amp;Planes2_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pigeons&amp;Planes2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Yorker" title="The New Yorker">The New Yorker</a></i> has described Leanos as part of a "sad rap" movement.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Leanos started dating singer and actress <a href="/wiki/Noah_Cyrus" title="Noah Cyrus">Noah Cyrus</a> on June 30, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The couple released the collaboration "Live or Die" in August 2018. They broke up in September 2018, with both parties accusing the other of being unfaithful; Leanos also claimed that the relationship was forced by the couple's record label, Columbia,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> though he later took responsibility for the breakup and said he has "nothing but love for the Cyrus family."<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Leanos has been open about his former addiction to <a href="/wiki/Benzodiazepine" title="Benzodiazepine">benzodiazepines</a> (particularly <a href="/wiki/Alprazolam" title="Alprazolam">Xanax</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Opiate" title="Opiate">opiates</a>. He overcame his Xanax addiction after two years.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Leanos is outspoken against Xanax abuse and urges people to stop using the drug altogether.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2019, Leanos announced that he and his then-fiancée Annie Smith were expecting a child.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Smith, however, claimed to have had a <a href="/wiki/Miscarriage" title="Miscarriage">miscarriage</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-auto_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> sharing the information about the incident via a video and photographs on Instagram.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> In a <i><a href="/wiki/Adam22" title="Adam22">No Jumper</a></i> interview weeks later, Leanos said he had suspicions that Smith had been faking her pregnancy after a number of the rapper's fans on social media pointed out that Smith's supposed <a href="/wiki/Ultrasound" title="Ultrasound">ultrasound</a> photos looked identical to ones that could be found using a <a href="/wiki/Google_Images" title="Google Images">Google Images</a> search. The couple would eventually split.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
