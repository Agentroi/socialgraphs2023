<p><b>Andre Manuel</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-Payback_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Payback-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> a disc jockey and record producer called <b>The Unknown DJ</b> or <b>DJ Unknown</b>, was a pioneer in the Los Angeles area's 1980s music scene, moving from electro <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a> and electro rap to <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_rap" title="Gangsta rap">gangsta rap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Regional_Guide_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Regional_Guide-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Now a legend of <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_Rap" class="mw-redirect" title="West Coast Rap">West Coast rap</a>, he worked with rapper <a href="/wiki/Ice-T" title="Ice-T">Ice-T</a> in the early 1980s, then with <a href="/wiki/Alonzo_Williams" title="Alonzo Williams">Alonzo "Grandmaster Lonzo" Williams</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Regional_Guide_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Regional_Guide-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and with <a href="/wiki/MC_Eiht" title="MC Eiht">MC Eiht</a>'s group <a href="/wiki/Compton%27s_Most_Wanted" title="Compton&#39;s Most Wanted">Compton's Most Wanted</a> in the early 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-WoodstraBush2008_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WoodstraBush2008-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Unknown_DJ&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Manuel started in the music industry through Alonzo Williams, a DJ and party promoter in the Los Angeles area.<sup id="cite_ref-Payback_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Payback-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Since his start in 1974, Williams, working under his brand name Disco Construction, earned the title "Grandmaster Lonzo."<sup id="cite_ref-Regional_Guide_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Regional_Guide-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 1979, Lonzo took over The Eve After Dark nightclub, about a quarter mile outside of Compton.<sup id="cite_ref-Regional_Guide_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Regional_Guide-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lonzo mentored Manuel, and they began working together.<sup id="cite_ref-Payback_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Payback-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 1984, Lonzo formed the <a href="/wiki/World_Class_Wreckin%27_Cru" title="World Class Wreckin&#39; Cru">Wreckin' Cru</a>, a social group of his nightclub's resident DJs.<sup id="cite_ref-Regional_Guide_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Regional_Guide-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> While working with the Cru, Manuel took on a DJ name, The Unknown DJ.<sup id="cite_ref-Payback_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Payback-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Wreckin' Cru, which included <a href="/wiki/DJ_Yella" title="DJ Yella">DJ Yella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, soon gained a rapper, CLI-nTEL, and began recording the electro rap.<sup id="cite_ref-Regional_Guide_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Regional_Guide-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After taking classes at <a href="/wiki/Long_Beach_City_College" title="Long Beach City College">Long Beach City College</a> to further his abilities in electronic music and recording, Unknown launched Techno Hop Records.<sup id="cite_ref-Regional_Guide_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Regional_Guide-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Since 1984, he produced or released such "techno hop" songs—fusions of electro <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a> and hip hop<sup id="cite_ref-Payback_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Payback-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>—as "Basstronic," "808 Beats," and "Let's Jam." He had also produced early tracks of <a href="/wiki/Ice-T" title="Ice-T">Ice-T</a>, and then <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_rap" title="Gangsta rap">gangsta rap</a>'s inaugural anthem, Ice-T's 1986 <a href="/wiki/A-side_and_B-side" title="A-side and B-side">B-side</a> "<a href="/wiki/6_in_the_Mornin%27" title="6 in the Mornin&#39;">6 in the Mornin'</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-Regional_Guide_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Regional_Guide-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Later, Unknown DJ produced for <a href="/wiki/MC_Eiht" title="MC Eiht">MC Eiht's</a> crew <a href="/wiki/Compton%27s_Most_Wanted" title="Compton&#39;s Most Wanted">Compton's Most Wanted</a>. Other artists include <a href="/wiki/A.L.T." title="A.L.T.">A.L.T.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brownside" title="Brownside">Brownside</a>, <a href="/wiki/N.W.A" title="N.W.A">N.W.A</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Jimmy" class="mw-redirect" title="Bobby Jimmy">Bobby Jimmy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/King_Tee" class="mw-redirect" title="King Tee">King Tee</a>.
</p><p>The Unknown DJ also figures, if tangentially, into the story of <a href="/wiki/Death_Row_Records" title="Death Row Records">Death Row Records</a>, whose cofounder Dr. Dre is a Wreckin' Cru alumnus. Reportedly, Dre's colleague <a href="/wiki/The_D.O.C." title="The D.O.C.">The D.O.C.</a> had suggested naming the newly forming label Def Row.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> But, already owning the rights to that name, DJ Unknown would later explain that he had made the name for a potential record label to open under <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Creek_Entertainment_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Morgan Creek Entertainment Group">Morgan Creek Entertainment Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  In July 1991, Unknown sold the rights to Dre's team, including <a href="/wiki/Suge_Knight" title="Suge Knight">Suge Knight</a> at the forming label's helm. However, the team chose, by 1992, to name the label more bluntly.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
