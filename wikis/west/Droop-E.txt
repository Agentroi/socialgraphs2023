<p><b>Earl Stevens Jr.</b> (born February 18, 1988), better known by his stage name <b>Droop-E</b>, is an American rapper and record producer. He is the son of the Bay Area rapper <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Droop-E&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Droop-E was born Earl Stevens Jr. on February 18, 1988, in <a href="/wiki/Vallejo,_California" title="Vallejo, California">Vallejo, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-first_family_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-first_family-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-born_to_rap_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-born_to_rap-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His family had rappers including father <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, his cousins <a href="/wiki/B-Legit" title="B-Legit">B-Legit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turf_Talk" title="Turf Talk">Turf Talk</a>, and with his uncles <a href="/wiki/D-Shot" title="D-Shot">D-Shot</a>, Mugzi and aunt <a href="/wiki/Suga-T" title="Suga-T">Suga-T</a>. His first record appearance came at age 5 when he was featured in "Questions" on E-40's <i><a href="/wiki/Federal_(album)" title="Federal (album)">Federal</a></i> album.<sup id="cite_ref-born_to_rap_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-born_to_rap-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He also rapped on the track "It's All Bad" from E-40's 1995 album <i><a href="/wiki/In_a_Major_Way" title="In a Major Way">In a Major Way</a></i> (credited as Lil E), mimicking his father's style. His production debut was at the age of 15 for the Turf Talk song "In The Heart Of The Ghetto" on <a href="/wiki/Mack_10" title="Mack 10">Mack 10</a>'s 2003 compilation, <i><a href="/wiki/Ghetto,_Gutter_%26_Gangster" class="mw-redirect" title="Ghetto, Gutter &amp; Gangster">Ghetto, Gutter &amp; Gangster</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
