<p><b>Alan Daniel Maman</b> (born October 25, 1977), professionally known as <b>the Alchemist</b>, is an American record producer, DJ, rapper and songwriter.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He began his music career in 1991 in the hip hop duo <a href="/wiki/The_Whooliganz" title="The Whooliganz">the Whooliganz</a> under the moniker Mudfoot, along with now-actor <a href="/wiki/Scott_Caan" title="Scott Caan">Scott Caan</a>. He has produced music since the 1990s.
</p><p>Together with collaborators <a href="/wiki/Oh_No_(musician)" title="Oh No (musician)">Oh No</a> (with whom he forms the group <a href="/wiki/Gangrene_(group)" title="Gangrene (group)">Gangrene</a>), <a href="/wiki/Woody_Jackson" title="Woody Jackson">Woody Jackson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tangerine_Dream" title="Tangerine Dream">Tangerine Dream</a> he composed the official <a href="/wiki/Sheet_music" title="Sheet music">score</a> to the 2013 video game <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a></i> and hosts the radio station "The Lab" in it.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Alchemist_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1991.E2.80.932003:_Early_career.2C_the_Whooliganz_and_Soul_Assassins"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1991–2003:_Early_career,_the_Whooliganz_and_Soul_Assassins">1991–2003: Early career, the Whooliganz and Soul Assassins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Alchemist_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1991–2003: Early career, the Whooliganz and Soul Assassins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Alchemist hails from Beverly Hills, California. As a teenager, he began to identify strongly with the rebellious lyrics and urban sounds of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, and he began writing his own lyrics. He joined up with other like-minded artists who wrote lyrics and rebelled against their suburban surroundings. These included <a href="/wiki/Evidence_(musician)" title="Evidence (musician)">Evidence</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scott_Caan" title="Scott Caan">Scott Caan</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/James_Caan_(actor)" class="mw-redirect" title="James Caan (actor)">James Caan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1991, a 14-year-old Maman and Scott Caan decided to become a duo named <a href="/wiki/The_Whooliganz" title="The Whooliganz">the Whooliganz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> While rhyming at a party in Los Angeles, they attracted the attention of <a href="/wiki/B-Real" title="B-Real">B-Real</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a>. He invited the teens to join his crew, <a href="/wiki/Soul_Assassins" title="Soul Assassins">Soul Assassins</a>, which also included the groups <a href="/wiki/House_of_Pain" title="House of Pain">House of Pain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Funkdoobiest" title="Funkdoobiest">Funkdoobiest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1993, the Whooliganz released their first single, "Put Your Handz Up". The song received little radio airplay and their record label, <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Boy_Records" title="Tommy Boy Records">Tommy Boy Records</a>, decided to shelve their album.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Rejected, Scott focused his creative energy on acting while Al became interested in process of making hip hop beats.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/DJ_Muggs" title="DJ Muggs">DJ Muggs</a> took Al under his wing and taught him how to use a <a href="/wiki/Sampler_(musical_instrument)" title="Sampler (musical instrument)">sampler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mixing_board" class="mw-redirect" title="Mixing board">mixing board</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After helping to produce a few tracks for Cypress Hill, Al became the main producer for his childhood friend Evidence and his group <a href="/wiki/Dilated_Peoples" title="Dilated Peoples">Dilated Peoples</a>.
</p><p>When Dilated Peoples debuted in 1998, it helped promote Maman as a key part of its sound. The Alchemist also produced several tracks on Defari's debut album <i>Focused Daily</i>, another Dilated Peoples and <a href="/wiki/Tha_Alkaholiks" title="Tha Alkaholiks">tha Alkaholiks</a> affiliate. In 1999, Muggs introduced Al to his good friends the popular hip hop group <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>. Al produced two songs for its <i><a href="/wiki/Murda_Muzik" title="Murda Muzik">Murda Muzik</a></i> album.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Impressed by his production skills and <a href="/wiki/Know-how" class="mw-redirect" title="Know-how">street smarts</a>, Mobb Deep continued using his beats on all of the group's subsequent albums. As the Alchemist's profile increased, he went on to produce for many of hip hop's most successful and prominent artists, such as <a href="/wiki/Nas_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Nas (rapper)">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He has also <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remixed</a> songs for bands and artists in other genres such as <a href="/wiki/Linkin_Park" title="Linkin Park">Linkin Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morcheeba" title="Morcheeba">Morcheeba</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1999, the Alchemist was set to produce three tracks for American rapper <a href="/wiki/Ras_Kass" title="Ras Kass">Ras Kass</a>' then-upcoming album <i>Van Gogh</i>, which was scheduled for a late 2000 release at the time. One of the said tracks was "Home Sweet Home", which was to be the lead single for Ras Kass' album. After the Alchemist received the first half of the payment for the beat, he said Ras Kass' record label <a href="/wiki/Priority_Records" title="Priority Records">Priority</a> was ignoring him. They later met up in August, at the <a href="/wiki/Source_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Source Awards">Source Awards</a> in Pasadena, California, where the Alchemist informed Kass that he'd been showcasing the beat to other artists. <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a> would then go on to record "<a href="/wiki/We_Gonna_Make_It" title="We Gonna Make It">We Gonna Make It</a>" over the same beat, on his 2001 release, <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_tha_Game_Goodbye" title="Kiss tha Game Goodbye">Kiss tha Game Goodbye</a></i>. When Ras Kass took notice, he attempted to stop the release by releasing his version of the song on mixtapes, to no avail.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This prompted Ras to diss the Alchemist in freestyles and on a track from <i>Van Gogh</i>. Eventually, the beef ended so he could concentrate on his legal problems but still cited a loss of respect for the producer. In an interview with www.hiphopgame.com, Ras admitted that he was being stubborn and refused to listen to what the Alchemist was trying to tell him. The two have since squashed the beef and the Alchemist produced the song "Past, Future and Present" and did the intro for the song that appeared on the 2006 mixtape <i>Revenge of the Spit</i>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2004.E2.80.932008:_1st_Infantry_and_Shady_Records"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2004–2008:_1st_Infantry_and_Shady_Records">2004–2008: <i>1st Infantry</i> and Shady Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Alchemist_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2004–2008: 1st Infantry and Shady Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, six years after his rapping career folded, the Alchemist put out his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/1st_Infantry_(album)" title="1st Infantry (album)">1st Infantry</a>.</i> The album yielded the single "<a href="/wiki/Hold_You_Down_(The_Alchemist_song)" title="Hold You Down (The Alchemist song)">Hold You Down</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a>, Illa Ghee and <a href="/wiki/Nina_Sky" title="Nina Sky">Nina Sky</a>, which hit number ninety-five on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> in 2005.
</p><p>Since <a href="/wiki/DJ_Green_Lantern" title="DJ Green Lantern">DJ Green Lantern</a> departed from <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a> in 2005 because of a dispute related to the feud of <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> and Jadakiss, the Alchemist is <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s official DJ.
</p><p>On July 13, 2005, the tour bus carrying Eminem's entourage, including rapper <a href="/wiki/Stat_Quo" title="Stat Quo">Stat Quo</a> as well as the Alchemist, swerved off the road and turned over. The Alchemist was treated for broken <a href="/wiki/Ribs" class="mw-redirect" title="Ribs">ribs</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Pneumothorax" title="Pneumothorax">collapsed lung</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, Eminem released a collaboration album with Shady Records called <i><a href="/wiki/Eminem_Presents:_The_Re-Up" title="Eminem Presents: The Re-Up">Eminem Presents: The Re-Up</a></i>. The Alchemist produced some of the tracks on the mixtape, as well as compiling the album in a mixtape fashion.
</p><p>In 2007, the Alchemist and Prodigy came together for a studio album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Return_of_the_Mac" title="Return of the Mac">Return of the Mac</a></i>, that was released on March 27, 2007. It debuted at number thirty-two on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, selling 27,000 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> As of December 2007, the album has sold 130,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932011:_Chemical_Warfare_and_Gangrene"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2011:_Chemical_Warfare_and_Gangrene">2009–2011: <i>Chemical Warfare</i> and Gangrene</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Alchemist_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009–2011: Chemical Warfare and Gangrene">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:GangreneEchoplexMarch2014.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/GangreneEchoplexMarch2014.jpg/290px-GangreneEchoplexMarch2014.jpg" decoding="async" width="290" height="189" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/GangreneEchoplexMarch2014.jpg/435px-GangreneEchoplexMarch2014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/GangreneEchoplexMarch2014.jpg/580px-GangreneEchoplexMarch2014.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5142" data-file-height="3358" /></a><figcaption>Gangrene performing in March 2014</figcaption></figure>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Gangrene_(group)" title="Gangrene (group)">Gangrene (group)</a></div>
<p>The Alchemist is one of the music producers behind the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto:_Chinatown_Wars" title="Grand Theft Auto: Chinatown Wars">Grand Theft Auto: Chinatown Wars</a></i> released by <a href="/wiki/Rockstar_Games" title="Rockstar Games">Rockstar Games</a> in 2009.
</p><p>On July 7, 2009, the Alchemist released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Chemical_Warfare_(The_Alchemist_album)" title="Chemical Warfare (The Alchemist album)">Chemical Warfare</a></i>. It contains two tracks by <a href="/wiki/Gangrene_(group)" title="Gangrene (group)">Gangrene</a>, the group consisting of the Alchemist himself and <a href="/wiki/Oh_No_(musician)" title="Oh No (musician)">Oh No</a>. Since then, they released three studio albums and, along with <a href="/wiki/Tangerine_Dream" title="Tangerine Dream">Tangerine Dream</a> and Woody Jackson, contributed music for the 2013 video game <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, the Alchemist produced an entire mixtape with rapper <a href="/wiki/Fashawn" title="Fashawn">Fashawn</a>, called <i><a href="/wiki/The_Antidote_(Fashawn_album)" title="The Antidote (Fashawn album)">The Antidote</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932014:_Russian_Roulette_and_Step_Brothers"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2014:_Russian_Roulette_and_Step_Brothers">2012–2014: <i>Russian Roulette</i> and Step Brothers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Alchemist_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–2014: Russian Roulette and Step Brothers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 17, 2012, the Alchemist released his third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Russian_Roulette_(The_Alchemist_album)" title="Russian Roulette (The Alchemist album)">Russian Roulette</a></i>.
</p><p>In August 2012, he produced <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a> member <a href="/wiki/Domo_Genesis" title="Domo Genesis">Domo Genesis</a> mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/No_Idols" title="No Idols">No Idols</a></i>. It was later released on a limited edition vinyl.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Rare_Chandeliers" title="Rare Chandeliers">Rare Chandeliers</a></i>, a collaboration mixtape with <a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson" title="Action Bronson">Action Bronson</a>, was released on November 15, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prodigy's second album with the Alchemist, <i><a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein_(album)" title="Albert Einstein (album)">Albert Einstein</a></i>, was released on June 11, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It debuted at number 175 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, with first-week sales of 3,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Boldy_James" title="Boldy James">Boldy James</a> signed a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Decon" title="Decon">Decon</a> for the Alchemist produced album;<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/My_1st_Chemistry_Set" title="My 1st Chemistry Set">My 1st Chemistry Set</a></i> was released on October 15, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 18, 2013, the Alchemist was named the runner-up for Producer of the Year by <i><a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2008, the Alchemist and fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Evidence_(musician)" title="Evidence (musician)">Evidence</a> have performed as the duo <a href="/wiki/Step_Brothers_(duo)" title="Step Brothers (duo)">Step Brothers</a>. Their debut album was announced to be titled <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_Steppington" title="Lord Steppington">Lord Steppington</a></i> and released on the <a href="/wiki/Rhymesayers_Entertainment" title="Rhymesayers Entertainment">Rhymesayers Entertainment</a> label.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Talking about the album in 2010, the Alchemist said, "I wanna create a sound for it. I don't want Step Brothers songs to sound like it could've been a Dilated Peoples song, or an Evidence song, that I'd rap on."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> A release date for the album was scheduled for November 19, 2013, but later the release date was pushed to January 20, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In an interview for Dead End Hip-Hop, the Alchemist stated that he had been working on a project related to the gospel. Later, on March 2, 2014, he posted a teaser photo to <i>The Good Book</i>, which will be a collaboration between the Alchemist and producer Budgie, and also retweeted a tweet from Frank the Butcher, the founder of BAU, which the project is going to be released on, announcing the project and saying that more information will be released two days after.
</p><p>Later, on March 5, they announced that the project is named <i> The Good Book</i>, and released the first single "In Heaven's Home", featuring <a href="/wiki/Roc_Marciano" title="Roc Marciano">Roc Marciano</a> and Prodigy. It comes in a well-designed cover in a shape of a bible book, covered in leather, and will be limited to one thousand. It released on March 11. The project includes mostly instrumentals and also some raps featuring Prodigy, Roc Marciano, Action Bronson, Domo Genesis and <a href="/wiki/Blu_(rapper)" title="Blu (rapper)">Blu</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.93present:_Craft_Singles_and_collaborative_albums"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–present:_Craft_Singles_and_collaborative_albums">2015–present: <i>Craft Singles</i> and collaborative albums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Alchemist_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2015–present: Craft Singles and collaborative albums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 6, 2015, a while after Rockstar Games announced the release of the PC version of <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a></i>, it was announced that the Alchemist and Oh No would have a new radio station called The Lab in the game. In addition to that, a new song "Play It Cool", featuring <a href="/wiki/Earl_Sweatshirt" title="Earl Sweatshirt">Earl Sweatshirt</a> and Samuel Herring, was released. The song is apart from a new album of tracks inspired by <i>GTA V</i> named <i><a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_Los_Santos" class="mw-redirect" title="Welcome to Los Santos">Welcome to Los Santos</a></i> that was released on April 21, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The album is a compilation album of new songs from a wide range of artists. The Alchemist and Oh No were involved in the production of the songs, and the album was released for the release of the PC version of the game.
</p><p>On November 18, the Alchemist released a single on his SoundCloud, featuring <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a> and <a href="/wiki/Migos" title="Migos">Migos</a>, titled "Jabroni". Later he said on Twitter that it would be released on limited 45 vinyl single with an instrumental version, as part of a new single series, called <i>Craft Singles</i>. On February 11, the Alchemist announced the dates of release of every one of the four <i>Craft Singles</i> vinyls. The tracks were: "Hoover Street (Original Version)" by <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a> (March 11, 2016), "Any Means" and "Supply" by MC Eiht and Spice One (April 8), "Cobb" and "Palisades" by Blu (May 6) and "Jabroni" (June 3). Some of those were already released online and the Alchemist released the "Hoover Street (Original Version)" online on the same day of the announcements. On August 11, another song was released: "All for It" by Roc Marciano.
</p><p>On January 13, 2016, the Alchemist and <a href="/wiki/Currensy" title="Currensy">Curren$y</a> announced their second collaboration, titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Carrollton_Heist" title="The Carrollton Heist">The Carrollton Heist</a></i>. The mixtape was all recorded in one day, January 4, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Later, Curren$y announced a release date and cover art on his Instagram.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The release date was set to February 14, 2016. This second collaboration of the Alchemist and Curren$y was very long-awaited since rumors of a <i><a href="/wiki/Covert_Coup" title="Covert Coup">Covert Coup</a></i> sequel were floating online back in 2012 until the Alchemist dismissed them in an interview. The mixtape was, indeed released on February 14, on DatPiff. Later, the Alchemist noticed that the version there was not the final mastered one, so he uploaded the full mastered version to his SoundCloud page.
</p><p>On March 2, 2016, <a href="/wiki/Havoc_(musician)" title="Havoc (musician)">Havoc</a> posted a photo on his Instagram stating that his next album is produced by the Alchemist, and is coming soon.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The title was revealed to be <i>The Silent Partner</i> and the first single, "Maintain", was released later. The album was released on May 20.
</p><p>In December 2016, mixer Eddie Sancho revealed that he was mixing a joint project by the Alchemist and rapper Jay Worthy. Much later, in April 2017, the Alchemist revealed the name and June 2 release date of the project, titled <i>Fantasy Island EP</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Alchemist teased the sequel to <i>The Good Book</i> project with producer Budgie on his Instagram Story a couple of times. Then, on May 7, 2017, he surprisingly tweeted a link to pre-order the project and a first single, "Brother Jedediah", featuring Action Bronson and Big Body Bess. The project was released on July 21. Like the previous <i>Good Book</i>, it contains two parts, one by the Alchemist and one by Budgie. The Alchemist's part features lyrics from Mobb Deep, <a href="/wiki/Westside_Gunn" title="Westside Gunn">Westside Gunn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conway_the_Machine" title="Conway the Machine">Conway the Machine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>, Action Bronson and more, as well as beats and some audio collages.
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Fetti" title="Fetti">Fetti</a></i>, a collaboration EP with Curren$y and <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Gibbs" title="Freddie Gibbs">Freddie Gibbs</a>, was first announced on January 4, 2017, through an Instagram post by Curren$y.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 21, 2018, nearly two years later, Curren$y announced on Instagram that he had completed his half of the project. In the post, Curren$y is recorded saying, "This message is to one Fredrick Gibbs. My half of <i>Fetti</i> is done. All produced by Alchemist."<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Gibbs would publicly respond to the post shortly after, commenting "N***a. Send It. I'm by the booth."<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On October 25, the tracklist and release date were posted by Gibbs on Instagram.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with the Grinds TV, Gibbs stated that his half of the EP took two days to complete.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2019, the Alchemist and Action Bronson announced that a new special edition vinyl of <i>Rare Chandeliers</i> is coming soon, along with a brand new collaboration EP, <i>Lamb Over Rice</i>. The EP was released on November 22 and it contains seven tracks. Five of them are brand new, but it also contains two previously released singles: "Descendant to the Stars", that was released on 2016 for Action Bronson's <i>Antient Aliens</i> show, and "Just the Way It Is" that was a part of the Alchemist's <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> mix "Diagnosis" from 2013, sampling a Coca-Cola commercial.
</p><p><i>Lulu</i>, a collaboration EP with Conway the Machine, was released on March 30, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 27, 2020, Freddie Gibbs and the Alchemist announced the release of new music via social media, before confirming the collaborative project named <i><a href="/wiki/Alfredo_(album)" title="Alfredo (album)">Alfredo</a></i> shortly after.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Stereogum_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stereogum-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> The announcement came alongside the option for pre-ordering the album in digital and physical format, including a comic book stylized based on the album.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 3, 2020, the Alchemist released <i>A Doctor, Painter &amp; An Alchemist Walk Into a Bar</i>, a collaborative project with DRx Romanelli and Spencer Lewis.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 25, 2023, <i><a href="/wiki/Voir_Dire_(Earl_Sweatshirt_and_the_Alchemist_album)" title="Voir Dire (Earl Sweatshirt and the Alchemist album)">Voir Dire</a></i>, a collaborative project with Earl Sweatshirt, was released for free on the Gala Music website.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Alchemist_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Alchemist is <a href="/wiki/Jewish" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish">Jewish</a>, and was named as one of the 10 most influential Jews in rap over the last 50 years.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
