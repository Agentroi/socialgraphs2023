<p><b>Matthew Dean Burdette</b> (born September 17, 1992),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Blxst</b> (pronounced "blast"), is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-the_Ringer_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-the_Ringer-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His record label Evgle partnered with <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Records" title="Red Bull Records">Red Bull Records</a> for the release of his debut EP <i>No Love Lost</i> on September 4, 2020, spawning the hit single "<a href="/wiki/Chosen_(Blxst_song)" title="Chosen (Blxst song)">Chosen</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-the_Ringer_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-the_Ringer-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He released his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Before_You_Go_(Blxst_album)" title="Before You Go (Blxst album)">Before You Go</a></i> on April 22, 2022.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blxst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Burdette grew up in <a href="/wiki/South_Los_Angeles" title="South Los Angeles">South Central Los Angeles</a>. He began rapping and producing shortly after he finished high school.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blxst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.932021:_No_Love_Lost_and_Sixtape_series"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2021:_No_Love_Lost_and_Sixtape_series">2014–2021: <i>No Love Lost</i> and <i>Sixtape</i> series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blxst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2014–2021: No Love Lost and Sixtape series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Blxst launched his music career with a hip hop collective called TIU Muzic, where he gained experience in the music industry and gathered a local following. In 2014, Blxst produced "Do Yo Gudda (Remix)" by <a href="/wiki/Hitta_J3" title="Hitta J3">Hitta J3</a> (featuring <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Problem_(rapper)" title="Problem (rapper)">Problem</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, he founded his label Evgle, and became a creative director for his graphic design and music videos.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Blxst released his first song "Who Would've Thought" in 2016, with a string of independent singles in 2018 including a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Eric_Bellinger" title="Eric Bellinger">Eric Bellinger</a>. In 2019, he released his first solo hit single called "Hurt", with millions of streams.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On August 28, 2019, he released a collaboration EP with Bino Rideaux titled <i>Sixtape</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, Evgle partnered with <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Records" title="Red Bull Records">Red Bull Records</a> for the release of his debut EP <i>No Love Lost</i> on September 4, with the singles "Overrated", "No Love Lost", and "Be Alone".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The EP peaked at number 3 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers"><i>Billboard</i> Heatseekers</a> chart. The deluxe edition was released on December 4, with additional features from <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dom_Kennedy" title="Dom Kennedy">Dom Kennedy</a>, and Bino Rideaux.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On March 12, 2021, he released a two-track EP titled <i>Just for Clarity</i>, including "Don't Forget" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Drakeo_the_Ruler" title="Drakeo the Ruler">Drakeo the Ruler</a>) and "Fck Boys" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Russ_(rapper)" title="Russ (rapper)">Russ</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> That same year, he was listed on <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i>'s <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)#Annual_Freshman_Class_list" title="XXL (magazine)">Annual Freshman Class</a>. On July 16, Blxst released <i>Sixtape 2</i> with Bino Rideaux.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2021.E2.80.93present:_Before_You_Go"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2021–present:_Before_You_Go">2021–present: <i>Before You Go</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blxst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2021–present: Before You Go">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 3, 2021, Blxst released the single "About You", with the music video being released on January 26, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He released another single on March 3 titled "Sometimes" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Zacari" title="Zacari">Zacari</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On April 22, 2022, Blxst released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Before_You_Go_(Blxst_album)" title="Before You Go (Blxst album)">Before You Go</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On May 13, Blxst appeared on <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Morale_%26_the_Big_Steppers" title="Mr. Morale &amp; the Big Steppers">Mr. Morale &amp; the Big Steppers</a></i>, on the song "Die Hard" with <a href="/wiki/Cover_Drive" title="Cover Drive">Amanda Reifer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On June 16 2023, Blxst was featured on EXIT 9, the 10th track from Killer Mike’s album MICHAEL.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blxst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blxst grew up listening to <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast hip hop</a> with influences from <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Leslie" title="Ryan Leslie">Ryan Leslie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>. His musical style has been compared to West Coast rapping and singing hybrids such as <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
