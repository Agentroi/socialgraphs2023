<p><b>Teren Delvon Jones</b> (born August 12, 1972),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Del the Funky Homosapien</b> (sometimes stylized as <b>Del tha Funkee Homosapien</b>) or <b>Sir DZL</b>, is an American rapper.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Del_the_Funky_Homosapien&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1988.E2.80.931997:_Early_life_and_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1988–1997:_Early_life_and_beginnings">1988–1997: Early life and beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Del_the_Funky_Homosapien&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1988–1997: Early life and beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a>, he is the cousin of seminal West Coast rapper <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>, and began his career writing lyrics for Ice Cube's group <a href="/wiki/Da_Lench_Mob" title="Da Lench Mob">Da Lench Mob</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hiero_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hiero-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 1991, with the help of Ice Cube, Del released his first solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/I_Wish_My_Brother_George_Was_Here" title="I Wish My Brother George Was Here">I Wish My Brother George Was Here</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-Hiero_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hiero-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> at the age of 19. The album was a commercial success largely due to the popularity of the hit single "<a href="/wiki/Mistadobalina" title="Mistadobalina">Mistadobalina</a>". Ultimately Del, who was not pleased with the limited musical range of the album, severed his production-artist relationship with Ice Cube for his next album, <i><a href="/wiki/No_Need_for_Alarm" title="No Need for Alarm">No Need for Alarm</a></i>.
</p><p><i>No Need for Alarm</i> saw the introduction of the Oakland clique <a href="/wiki/Hieroglyphics_(group)" title="Hieroglyphics (group)">Hieroglyphics</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Hiero_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hiero-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> whose original members included <a href="/wiki/Souls_of_Mischief" title="Souls of Mischief">Souls of Mischief</a> (Opio, A-Plus, Phesto and Tajai), Casual, Pep Love, Del, and producer Domino. <i>No Need for Alarm</i> helped to expose both the regional Oakland sound of hip hop, as well as the freestyle based, "golden era 90s" style of hip hop being expanded at the time.
</p>
<h3><span id="1998.E2.80.932006:_Middle_era"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1998–2006:_Middle_era">1998–2006: Middle era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Del_the_Funky_Homosapien&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1998–2006: Middle era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Del would not produce another album for five years. About a month before the release of his third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Future_Development" title="Future Development">Future Development</a></i>, Del received a letter from his label, <a href="/wiki/Elektra_Records" title="Elektra Records">Elektra</a>, stating that his contract had been terminated.<sup id="cite_ref-Hiero_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hiero-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Together with his crew, Del established his own independent record label, <a href="/wiki/Hieroglyphics_Imperium_Recordings" title="Hieroglyphics Imperium Recordings">Hieroglyphics Imperium Recordings</a>, which primarily consists of an expanded Hieroglyphics roster and a few other artists with whom the group collaborates on a regular basis.  <i>Future Development</i> became available in 1998 and was only available on the Hieroglyphics website in tape form<sup id="cite_ref-Larkin90_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin90-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> but was re-released in 2002 on the Hieroglyphics Imperium label. Del also released another collaborative work with the Hieroglyphics crew in 1998, which was also the Hieroglyphics crew's first album, <i><a href="/wiki/3rd_Eye_Vision" title="3rd Eye Vision">3rd Eye Vision</a></i>.
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Del_the_Funky_Homosapien.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Del_the_Funky_Homosapien.jpg/170px-Del_the_Funky_Homosapien.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Del_the_Funky_Homosapien.jpg/255px-Del_the_Funky_Homosapien.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f3/Del_the_Funky_Homosapien.jpg 2x" data-file-width="300" data-file-height="400" /></a><figcaption>Del performing in <a href="/wiki/Red_Deer,_Alberta" title="Red Deer, Alberta">Red Deer, Alberta</a> in 2006</figcaption></figure>
<p>Two years later, Del released his fourth solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Both_Sides_of_the_Brain" title="Both Sides of the Brain">Both Sides of the Brain</a></i>, as well as <i><a href="/wiki/Deltron_3030_(album)" title="Deltron 3030 (album)">Deltron 3030</a></i> which was a collaborative work with artists <a href="/wiki/Dan_the_Automator" title="Dan the Automator">Dan the Automator</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kid_Koala" title="Kid Koala">Kid Koala</a>. With Nakamura and Koala, Del was also a guest performer for <a href="/wiki/Gorillaz" title="Gorillaz">Gorillaz</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Gorillaz_(album)" title="Gorillaz (album)">eponymous 2001 album</a>. He appeared on the singles "<a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood_(song)" title="Clint Eastwood (song)">Clint Eastwood</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Rock_the_House_(Gorillaz_song)" title="Rock the House (Gorillaz song)">Rock the House</a>". 2003 saw the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Full_Circle_(Hieroglyphics_album)" title="Full Circle (Hieroglyphics album)">Full Circle</a></i>, the second full-length album from the Hieroglyphics crew.
</p><p>In 2004, Elektra released <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_of_Del_tha_Funkee_Homosapien:_The_Elektra_Years" title="The Best of Del tha Funkee Homosapien: The Elektra Years">The Best of Del tha Funkee Homosapien: The Elektra Years</a></i> without Del's approval. The CD only includes songs from his first two albums, along with a handful of B-sides from that era. Del was not pleased and advised people to not buy the CD, saying it was just Elektra trying to make money off of him due to his newfound fame.
</p>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.93present:_Recent_years"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–present:_Recent_years">2006–present: Recent years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Del_the_Funky_Homosapien&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006–present: Recent years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 11, 2008, Del released <i><a href="/wiki/Eleventh_Hour_(Del_the_Funky_Homosapien_album)" title="Eleventh Hour (Del the Funky Homosapien album)">Eleventh Hour</a></i> through <a href="/wiki/Definitive_Jux" title="Definitive Jux">Definitive Jux</a>. On March 31, 2009, Del's next album <i><a href="/wiki/Funk_Man_(The_Stimulus_Package)" title="Funk Man (The Stimulus Package)">Funk Man (The Stimulus Package)</a></i> was made available for free download on the internet. The album is available at his page on <a href="/wiki/Bandcamp" title="Bandcamp">Bandcamp</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  though he gave away some hard copies on his supporting Funk Man tour. In September of the same year, he released a new album, <i><a href="/wiki/Automatik_Statik" title="Automatik Statik">Automatik Statik</a></i>, for an unfixed rate with a minimum of $3.
</p><p>Del released his next album <i><a href="/wiki/It_Ain%27t_Illegal_Yet" title="It Ain&#39;t Illegal Yet">It Ain't Illegal Yet</a></i> on August 6, 2010. There is no fixed price for the album, allowing listeners to pay whatever they wish for the album. Paying certain prices for the album will give certain incentives, including opportunities for personal collaborations with Del. Del released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Era_(Del_the_Funky_Homosapien_album)" title="Golden Era (Del the Funky Homosapien album)">Golden Era</a></i> on April 19, 2011, in a three-disc set including <i><a href="/wiki/Funk_Man_(The_Stimulus_Package)" title="Funk Man (The Stimulus Package)">Funk Man (The Stimulus Package)</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Automatik_Statik" title="Automatik Statik">Automatik Statik</a></i>.
</p><p>Del shared a free LP entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Iller_Than_Most" title="Iller Than Most">Iller Than Most</a></i> on January 2, 2014. He uploaded the record to <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> under the username Zartan Drednaught COBRA. He described the project as "lyrically ill but fun to listen to, nothing super heavy." Del handled the production on the project as well.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_media">In other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Del_the_Funky_Homosapien&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Del's songs have seen frequent use in various forms of media including video games, film soundtracks, as well as skateboarding, rollerblading and snowboarding videos. In 2000 the song "Catch All This" from <i><a href="/wiki/Both_Sides_of_the_Brain" title="Both Sides of the Brain">Both Sides of the Brain</a></i> was featured in the game <i>Street Sk8er 2</i>, "Jaw Gymnastics", featuring Casual, was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Knockout_Kings" title="Knockout Kings">Knockout Kings</a></i>, and "Positive Contact" from <i>Deltron 3030</i> was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Mat_Hoffman%27s_Pro_BMX" title="Mat Hoffman&#39;s Pro BMX">Mat Hoffman's Pro BMX</a></i>. In 2001, "<a href="/wiki/If_You_Must_(Del_the_Funky_Homosapien_song)" title="If You Must (Del the Funky Homosapien song)">If You Must</a>" was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Tony_Hawk%27s_Pro_Skater_3" title="Tony Hawk&#39;s Pro Skater 3">Tony Hawk's Pro Skater 3</a></i>. In 2003, "Positive Contact" from <i>Deltron 3030</i> was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Tony_Hawk%27s_Underground" title="Tony Hawk&#39;s Underground">Tony Hawk's Underground</a></i> and "The Izm" was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_(video_game)" title="Rolling (video game)">Rolling</a></i>. In 2005, the Hieroglyphics song "Soweto" is featured on <i><a href="/wiki/ESPN_NBA_2K5" title="ESPN NBA 2K5">ESPN NBA 2K5</a></i> (the Hieroglyphics crew is unlockable as a bonus team) and Del's song "Burnt" was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Tony_Hawk%27s_American_Wasteland" title="Tony Hawk&#39;s American Wasteland">Tony Hawk's American Wasteland</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2006, "Catch a Bad One" was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Marc_Ecko%27s_Getting_Up:_Contents_Under_Pressure" title="Marc Ecko&#39;s Getting Up: Contents Under Pressure">Marc Ecko's Getting Up: Contents Under Pressure</a></i> and "At the Helm" featuring Hieroglyphics was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Tony_Hawk%27s_Project_8" title="Tony Hawk&#39;s Project 8">Tony Hawk's Project 8</a></i>. Also in 2006, his song "Dr. Bombay" was used in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Beerfest" title="Beerfest">Beerfest</a></i>. The Hieroglyphics song "Don't Hate the Player" was also featured in <i><a href="/wiki/NBA_2K7" title="NBA 2K7">NBA 2K7</a></i>, and "Clint Eastwood" was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/NBA_2K14" title="NBA 2K14">NBA 2K14</a></i>. "Teamwork" is featured on PlayStation Home. Del has had many songs featured in snowboarding movies. "Press Rewind" was the song for Eero Ettala's part in the film <i>White Balance</i> and "If You Must" was featured in <a href="/wiki/DC_Mountain_Lab#The_Video:_MTN._Lab" title="DC Mountain Lab"><i>DC Mountain Lab</i></a>.
</p><p>Del collaborated with John King of the <a href="/wiki/Dust_Brothers" title="Dust Brothers">Dust Brothers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Mothersbaugh" title="Mark Mothersbaugh">Mark Mothersbaugh</a> of <a href="/wiki/Devo" title="Devo">Devo</a> to produce the original score for  <i><a href="/wiki/Skate_3" title="Skate 3">Skate 3</a></i>, released in May 2010. In February 2016, Del, <a href="/wiki/Black_Thought" title="Black Thought">Black Thought</a>, <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fashawn" title="Fashawn">Fashawn</a> recorded a new track called "Rise Up" for the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Fighter_V" title="Street Fighter V">Street Fighter V</a></i>. A music video for the song was released by <a href="/wiki/Capcom" title="Capcom">Capcom</a>, and includes appearances by Del and his fellow collaborators.<sup id="cite_ref-early_release_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-early_release-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2018, Deltron 3030 collaborated with the <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Craig_of_the_Creek" title="Craig of the Creek">Craig of the Creek</a></i>, with Del making an appearance in the episode "The Kid from 3030" as Deltron. The episode featured original songs by Deltron 3030 made just for the episode of the show.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Deltron briefly returns in the Season 3 finale singing "Positive Contact" from Deltron 3030's debut album of the same name.
</p><p>He has also made cameo appearances in films, such as <a href="/wiki/Ted_Demme" title="Ted Demme">Ted Demme</a>'s directorial debut <i><a href="/wiki/Who%27s_the_Man%3F" title="Who&#39;s the Man?">Who's the Man?</a></i>, as a teen with two members of <a href="/wiki/Da_Youngsta%27s" title="Da Youngsta&#39;s">Da Youngsta's</a> and an uncredited <a href="/wiki/Ashanti_(singer)" title="Ashanti (singer)">Ashanti</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jonah_Hill" title="Jonah Hill">Jonah Hill</a>'s directorial debut <i><a href="/wiki/Mid90s" title="Mid90s">Mid90s</a></i>, as a homeless bum alongside <a href="/wiki/Chad_Muska" title="Chad Muska">Chad Muska</a>.
</p><p>He made an guest appearance as a rapper named "Old Skool" (paying homage to Del's prominence in the 1990s) on the <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Middlemost_Post" title="Middlemost Post">Middlemost Post</a></i> on the episode "The Same Ol' Same".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaborations">Collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Del_the_Funky_Homosapien&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1993, Del collaborated with the band <a href="/wiki/Dinosaur_Jr." title="Dinosaur Jr.">Dinosaur Jr.</a> to create the song "Missing Link", featured on the <a href="/wiki/Judgment_Night_(soundtrack)" title="Judgment Night (soundtrack)"><i>Judgment Night</i> soundtrack</a>.
In 1999–2000, Del collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Kid_Koala" title="Kid Koala">Kid Koala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dan_the_Automator" title="Dan the Automator">Dan the Automator</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> <a href="/wiki/Deltron_3030" title="Deltron 3030">Deltron 3030</a> whose <a href="/wiki/Deltron_3030_(album)" title="Deltron 3030 (album)">debut self-titled album</a> was released in May 2000. Exposure from the 3030 project helped to expand Del's fan base—the 3030 project worked well as a marketing move because the CD capitalized on the growing interest of computer technology, incorporating motifs of science fiction, telling stories about life and hip hop based in the year 3030, and infusing much of the popular internet terminology and culture in circulation at the time.
A follow-up album, "Event II", was released in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  In 2005 Del worked with the <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a> and their affiliates on the collaboration album <i><a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Meets_the_Indie_Culture" title="Wu-Tang Meets the Indie Culture">Wu-Tang Meets the Indie Culture</a></i>.
</p><p>Del collaborated with the virtual band <a href="/wiki/Gorillaz" title="Gorillaz">Gorillaz</a> on two songs on their debut album, "<a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood_(song)" title="Clint Eastwood (song)">Clint Eastwood</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Rock_the_House_(Gorillaz_song)" title="Rock the House (Gorillaz song)">Rock the House</a>", both of which became singles and videos and achieved chart success. Del was not, however, originally selected to collaborate on these songs. By the time Del came onto the project, the album was already finished, and British hip-hop group Phi Life Cypher had already recorded verses for "Clint Eastwood". But when Del finished making <i>Deltron 3030</i> with <a href="/wiki/Dan_the_Automator" title="Dan the Automator">Dan the Automator</a>, Dan asked if he could stay in the studio a little longer to record new verses for the Gorillaz songs.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> For the purposes of the music videos and the Gorillaz storyline canon, Del performed under the identity of "Del the Ghost Rapper", who was said to be a spirit that was hiding from death within the band's drummer, <a href="/wiki/Russel_Hobbs" class="mw-redirect" title="Russel Hobbs">Russel Hobbs</a>. Del later commented in an interview on the success of "Clint Eastwood" by saying that he actually wrote the song with the book <i>How to Write a Hit Song</i>, a book that he bought with a coupon his mother gave him. After the song went platinum he gave the plaque to his mother.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> As part of Russel Hobbs' back-story, the character of Del was one of Russel's friends that was gunned down in a drive-by shooting, whose ghost <a href="/wiki/Spirit_possession" title="Spirit possession">possessed</a> Russel.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Del is featured on the song "Lothar" by <a href="/wiki/Sleep_(rapper)" title="Sleep (rapper)">Sleep</a> off his album <i><a href="/wiki/Hesitation_Wounds_(album)" title="Hesitation Wounds (album)">Hesitation Wounds</a></i>, as well as on the song "Dreamin'" by <a href="/wiki/Gift_of_Gab_(rapper)" title="Gift of Gab (rapper)">Gift of Gab</a> off his album <i><a href="/wiki/Escape_2_Mars" title="Escape 2 Mars">Escape 2 Mars</a></i>. In March 2010, Del collaborated with independent pop/funk duo Modern Science on a track called "Do It Right Now" that is available on the band's Bandcamp website.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Del collaborated on the track "Smoke Rings" with <a href="/wiki/Dirty_Heads" title="Dirty Heads">Dirty Heads</a> on their 2012 album <i><a href="/wiki/Cabin_by_the_Sea" title="Cabin by the Sea">Cabin by the Sea</a></i>. In August 2012, Del appeared on the track "The Ride" from the EP <i>Thrift Store Halos</i> by <a href="/wiki/%C2%A1Mayday!" title="¡Mayday!">¡Mayday!</a>.
</p><p>In September 2013, Del appeared on two tracks from <a href="/wiki/Figure_(musician)" title="Figure (musician)">Figure</a>'s <i>Horns of the Apocalypse</i> EP: "Beast Mode" and "War Call". In January 2014, Del appeared on the track "Viberian Son" with <a href="/wiki/MF_DOOM" class="mw-redirect" title="MF DOOM">MF DOOM</a>. In March 2014, Del appeared on the track "Life and Time" with <a href="/wiki/Kool_A.D." title="Kool A.D.">Kool A.D.</a> In 2016, Del was featured on the track "World Renown" with Mr. Lif's album <i>Don't Look Down</i>. In April 2017, Del appeared (along with Carnage The Executor) on the track "Lone Wolves" from Cas One Vs Figure's "So Our Egos Don't Kill Us". In 2017, Del was a guest artist on the debut album of Halo Orbit (a band featuring <a href="/wiki/Juan_Alderete" title="Juan Alderete">Juan Alderete</a>, ex-<a href="/wiki/The_Mars_Volta" title="The Mars Volta">The Mars Volta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Guiliana" title="Mark Guiliana">Mark Guiliana</a>) on the track "One Of These Days". He also appeared on the track "Pizza Shop Extended" from the album <i>IWASVERYBAD</i> by <a href="/wiki/IDK_(rapper)" title="IDK (rapper)">Jay IDK</a>.
</p><p>Using his <a href="/wiki/Deltron_3030" title="Deltron 3030">Deltron</a> persona, Del guests on the track "3030 Meets the Doc, Pt. 1" from <a href="/wiki/Dr._Octagon" title="Dr. Octagon">Dr. Octagon</a>'s <i>Moosebumps: An Exploration Into Modern Day Horripilation</i>. Released on streaming services April 6, 2018, the album reunites <a href="/wiki/Kool_Keith" title="Kool Keith">Kool Keith</a> with <a href="/wiki/Dan_the_Automator" title="Dan the Automator">Dan the Automator</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Qbert" title="DJ Qbert">DJ Qbert</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MoosebumpsRelease2018_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MoosebumpsRelease2018-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 7, 2018, as he was appearing with Gorillaz during the final act of the <a href="/wiki/Roskilde_Festival#2018" title="Roskilde Festival">2018 Roskilde Festival</a>, Del fell off the stage during a performance of "Clint Eastwood". He was unable to get back up, and it was decided to cut the performance short. Although he was at first thought to have suffered only minor injuries, it later transpired that he had fractured seven ribs and punctured one of his lungs. He was still hospitalized in <a href="/wiki/Roskilde" title="Roskilde">Roskilde</a> four days after the accident.<sup id="cite_ref-RoskildeIncident_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RoskildeIncident-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> According to his Facebook page and official website, Del was back out touring in September 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Del once again collaborated with Gorillaz in 2023, appearing on the <i><a href="/wiki/Cracker_Island" title="Cracker Island">Cracker Island</a></i> deluxe edition bonus track "Captain Chicken".
</p>
