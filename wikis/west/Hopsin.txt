<p><b>Marcus Jamal Hopson</b> (born July 18, 1985), known professionally as <b>Hopsin</b>, is an American rapper, songwriter, and record producer from Los Angeles.
</p><p>In 2007, he was signed to <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records" title="Ruthless Records">Ruthless Records</a>, and had founded his own record label, <a href="/wiki/Funk_Volume" title="Funk Volume">Funk Volume</a>, in 2009. He would then go on to found another record label, <a href="/wiki/Undercover_Prodigy" title="Undercover Prodigy">Undercover Prodigy</a>, in 2016. Hopsin has released five studio albums, <i>Gazing at the Moonlight</i> through Ruthless Records, <i>Raw</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Knock_Madness" title="Knock Madness">Knock Madness</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Pound_Syndrome" title="Pound Syndrome">Pound Syndrome</a></i> along with the collaborative project <a href="/wiki/Haywire_(Hopsin_and_SwizZz_mixtape)" class="mw-redirect" title="Haywire (Hopsin and SwizZz mixtape)">Haywire</a> with <a href="/wiki/SwizZz" title="SwizZz">SwizZz</a> through Funk Volume and <i><a href="/wiki/No_Shame_(Hopsin_album)" title="No Shame (Hopsin album)">No Shame</a></i>. He is well known for his use of white colored <a href="/wiki/Contact_lens" title="Contact lens">eye contacts</a> in music videos, performances, and interviews.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Marcus Hopson was born on July 18, 1985, in Los Angeles, and was raised in the largely Hispanic <a href="/wiki/Panorama_City,_Los_Angeles" title="Panorama City, Los Angeles">Panorama City</a> neighborhood of Los Angeles. He attended <a href="/wiki/Monroe_High_School_(Los_Angeles)" class="mw-redirect" title="Monroe High School (Los Angeles)">James Monroe High School</a>, where he was placed into <a href="/wiki/Special_education" title="Special education">special education</a> classes.<sup id="cite_ref-hopsinbio_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hopsinbio-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Hopsin has been an avid <a href="/wiki/Skateboarder" class="mw-redirect" title="Skateboarder">skateboarder</a> since the age of twelve, and many of his music videos feature him skateboarding.<sup id="cite_ref-whoishopsin2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whoishopsin2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He started rapping at sixteen years old, and took his hobby more seriously by 2003 which is the year the majority of the tracks from Hopsin's self-produced album <i>Emurge</i> were recorded.<sup id="cite_ref-whoishopsin1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whoishopsin1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
In 2004, Hopsin and a group of friends were arrested for vandalizing James Monroe High School property. It was supposed to be a school prank but later they were all charged and held on $20,000 bail. Hopsin, who dropped out earlier that year, was sent to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_County_Jail" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles County Jail">Los Angeles County Jail</a> where he spent 8 days.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Upon his release, he began to pursue a career in music, purchased a microphone, and installed <a href="/wiki/FL_Studio" title="FL Studio">FL Studio</a> (known as FruityLoops at the time) on his computer.<sup id="cite_ref-hopsinbio_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hopsinbio-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He credits <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> as the rapper that initially got him interested in <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> music.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlnews_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlnews-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span></h2>
<h3><span id="2003.E2.80.932009:_Career_beginnings.2C_Ruthless_Records_and_Funk_Volume"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2003–2009:_Career_beginnings,_Ruthless_Records_and_Funk_Volume">2003–2009: Career beginnings, <i>Ruthless Records</i> and <i>Funk Volume</i></span></h3>
<p>Hopsin began recording his debut project <i>Emurge</i> in 2002 and was eventually released locally in 2003, copies of the project are very sparse and hard to find, a bootleg version appeared online in 2008 and had many extra songs on it, however to this day the album has never been released officially.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Hopsin had initially signed with <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records" title="Ruthless Records">Ruthless Records</a> in 2007 and even began recording his debut album as early as 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He was originally hailed as one of the driving forces behind attempting to bring Ruthless Records back to previous glory.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Hopsin's lead single from his debut album "Pans in the Kitchen" was released on May 27, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-iTunes_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iTunes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The album was set to be self-produced by Hopsin and feature no collaborations with other artists.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard1_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However his debut album, <i>Gazing at the Moonlight</i> was not released until October 27, 2009, with little to no promotion.<sup id="cite_ref-iTunes2_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iTunes2-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Shortly after the album's release, Hopsin sought his release from Ruthless Records due to lack of financial compensation, artist support, and promotion.<sup id="cite_ref-www.hiphopdx.com_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-www.hiphopdx.com-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Shortly before the departure from Ruthless Records, Hopsin founded his own <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent label</a>, <a href="/wiki/Funk_Volume" title="Funk Volume">Funk Volume</a>, with Damien Ritter. <a href="/wiki/SwizZz" title="SwizZz">SwizZz</a>, Damien Ritter's younger brother and a former classmate of Hopsin at Monroe High, was the first artist to be signed to Funk Volume. Shortly after launching Funk Volume, both Hopsin and SwizZz released a collaborative mixtape titled <i>Haywire</i> in June 2009 to promote the label.<sup id="cite_ref-bestofhardknock_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bestofhardknock-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Funk Volume originally wanted to sell it for retail sale, but were unable due to Hopsin still being contracted by Ruthless Records at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On mixtape website <a href="/wiki/DatPiff" title="DatPiff">DatPiff</a>, it has been certified Gold for being downloaded over 100,000 times and it later made available for purchase for <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital download</a> via <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932011:_Success_with_Funk_Volume_and_Raw"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2011:_Success_with_Funk_Volume_and_Raw">2010–2011: Success with Funk Volume and <i>Raw</i></span></h3>
<p>Hopsin released "Nocturnal Rainbows" as the first single off of his upcoming second album <i>Raw</i> on August 1, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On October 8, 2010, Hopsin released a music video for the song "Sag My Pants", the second single off <i>Raw</i> on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. The video became a YouTube success and currently has over 37 million views.<sup id="cite_ref-youtubesagmypants_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtubesagmypants-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In the song Hopsin pokes fun and disses other rappers such as <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drake_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (entertainer)">Drake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Boy" title="Soulja Boy">Soulja Boy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lupe_Fiasco" title="Lupe Fiasco">Lupe Fiasco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a> and Tomica Wright, the owner of Ruthless Records. Hopsin's second album, <i>Raw</i>, was released on November 19, 2010. In March 2011, Hopsin went on a two-month nationwide tour to promote <i>Raw</i> with the <i>I Am RAW</i> tour.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2011, Hopsin released the fourth installment of his "Ill Mind of Hopsin" video series which later received over 21 million views on YouTube. In it he disses <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a> of the Los Angeles hip hop collective, <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On October 31, 2011, Hopsin was featured in a mobile <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">battle rap</a> game, <a href="/wiki/Battle_Rap_Stars" title="Battle Rap Stars">Battle Rap Stars</a> by <a href="/wiki/Jump_Shot_Media" title="Jump Shot Media">Jump Shot Media</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932013:_Mainstream_breakthrough_and_Knock_Madness"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2013:_Mainstream_breakthrough_and_Knock_Madness">2012–2013: Mainstream breakthrough and <i>Knock Madness</i></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Knock_Madness" title="Knock Madness">Knock Madness</a></div>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Hopsin_2013.JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Hopsin_2013.JPG/220px-Hopsin_2013.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="312" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Hopsin_2013.JPG/330px-Hopsin_2013.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Hopsin_2013.JPG/440px-Hopsin_2013.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3437" data-file-height="4873" /></a><figcaption>Hopsin performing in Toronto on May 16, 2013</figcaption></figure>
<p>In January 2012, Hopsin landed a spot on <a href="/wiki/MTV2" title="MTV2">MTV2</a>'s "Sucker Free Sunday" by appearing in <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>'s music video for "<a href="/wiki/Am_I_a_Psycho%3F" title="Am I a Psycho?">Am I A Psycho?</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In February 2012 Hopsin appeared on the front cover of <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> as part of their annual "Top 10 Freshmen list" along with fellow rappers <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Machine_Gun_Kelly_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Machine Gun Kelly (rapper)">MGK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Brown_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Danny Brown (rapper)">Danny Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roscoe_Dash" title="Roscoe Dash">Roscoe Dash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iggy_Azalea" title="Iggy Azalea">Iggy Azalea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macklemore" title="Macklemore">Macklemore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Don_Trip" title="Don Trip">Don Trip</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kid_Ink" title="Kid Ink">Kid Ink</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In July 2012, Hopsin released the fifth installment of his "Ill Mind of Hopsin" video series which hit YouTube with huge success. It had received over 1 million views in less than 24 hours and currently has over 50 million views.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In "<a href="/wiki/Ill_Mind_of_Hopsin_5" title="Ill Mind of Hopsin 5">Ill Mind of Hopsin 5</a>" Hopsin expresses his frustration with jaded youth and disenchantment towards other rappers who are unrelatable. It had also charted at number 17 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a>'s Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Digital songs chart.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In October 2012, Hopsin made an appearance on a BET Cypher during the 2012 <a href="/wiki/BET_Hip_Hop_Awards" title="BET Hip Hop Awards">BET Hip Hop Awards</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mystikal" title="Mystikal">Mystikal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hopsin's album, <i><a href="/wiki/Knock_Madness" title="Knock Madness">Knock Madness</a></i> was released on November 26, 2013, to fairly good success in US Rap Charts.<sup id="cite_ref-Date_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Date-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Featured guests for the album include <a href="/wiki/Dizzy_Wright" title="Dizzy Wright">Dizzy Wright</a>, <a href="/wiki/SwizZz" title="SwizZz">SwizZz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jarren_Benton" title="Jarren Benton">Jarren Benton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>. He has said the album has more of a positive message and said it is "better than <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s <i>Detox</i>."<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Hopsin and the rest of the Funk Volume artists went on a three-month worldwide tour in the fall of 2012 which included 58 shows in 60 days in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2012, Hopsin had hinted on his Facebook and Twitter pages that he and Travis Barker are working on a project together, further details on the project were yet to be released. However, in late December, Travis Barker would say they are working on a collaboration <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> which would be released in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Then on February 5, 2012, Hopsin would say all the production had been finished for the EP.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> On January 24, 2013, Funk Volume released a music video featuring the entire roster; Hopsin, <a href="/wiki/Dizzy_Wright" title="Dizzy Wright">Dizzy Wright</a>, SwiZzZ, Jarren Benton and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Hoppa" title="DJ Hoppa">DJ Hoppa</a> for a song titled "Funk Volume 2013."<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On March 30, 2013, performed at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Paid_Dues" title="Paid Dues">Paid Dues</a> festival in San Bernardino, California.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 18, 2013, Hopsin released "Ill Mind Six: Old Friend" on his YouTube channel. At the end of the video, the release date for <i>Knock Madness</i> was confirmed as November 26, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-Date_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Date-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He later said that the song is not the sixth song in the "Ill Mind of Hopsin" series, and is instead a track on Knock Madness titled "Old Friend".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> <i>Knock Madness</i> was released on November 26, 2013, by Funk Volume and debuted at number 76 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> with first-week sales of 12,000 copies. The album contains guest appearances from SwizZz, Dizzy Wright, Jarren Benton and Tech N9ne along with being production primarily by Hopsin himself. It was also supported by the singles "<a href="/wiki/Hop_Is_Back" title="Hop Is Back">Hop Is Back</a>" and "Rip Your Heart Out". Following the Knock Madness tour beginning in December 2013, Hopsin planned to go on a hiatus also saying, "When I take a break, I am still going to be making music, I am [just] not going to be out publicly promoting shit. I am just going to be in my own house, doing whatever the fuck I want to do. Finding myself as a person."<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.932015:_Pound_Syndrome"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2015:_Pound_Syndrome">2014–2015: <i>Pound Syndrome</i></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Pound_Syndrome" title="Pound Syndrome">Pound Syndrome</a></div>
<p>On January 30, 2014, while on tour, Hopsin was scheduled to perform a show in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Collins,_Colorado" title="Fort Collins, Colorado">Fort Collins, Colorado</a>, but feeling deeply depressed and even suicidal he walked out the back door of the venue before the performance. He hid in a house under construction until he called a friend to pick him up.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> However, on July 11, 2015, to make amends, he performed a free show for fans in Fort Collins at the same venue where he was originally to perform and dedicated a song titled "Fort Collins" on his album <i><a href="/wiki/Pound_Syndrome" title="Pound Syndrome">Pound Syndrome</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 1, 2014, Hopsin posted a picture of his mugshot stating that he would be releasing "Ill Mind of Hopsin 7" on July 18, 2014. He then stated that it was for sure the realest song he has ever wrote in his career.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> The video for "Ill Mind of Hopsin 7" had gotten over 1 million YouTube views in a day. On "Ill Mind of Hopsin 7", Hopsin lyrically shares his religious beliefs, his views on other religious beliefs and the connections between religion, history and governance.
</p><p>Hopsin had revealed on his Instagram that he would be retiring from rap and moving to Australia. However, on December 25, 2014, Hopsin shared a video on his YouTube channel called "The REAL reason Hopsin left the music industry" with label mate Jarren Benton inspired by the film <i><a href="/wiki/Dumb_and_Dumber_To" title="Dumb and Dumber To">Dumb and Dumber To</a></i> which stated that he was not quitting rap and it was all a joke, also revealing that he will be releasing a new album called <i><a href="/wiki/Pound_Syndrome" title="Pound Syndrome">Pound Syndrome</a></i> in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 27, 2015, an interview was released on <a href="/wiki/Sway_Calloway" title="Sway Calloway">Sway Calloway</a>'s YouTube channel, touching on his appearance at Soundest Music Festival, and announcing that <i>Pound Syndrome</i> would be released on July 24. In the interview, he said that this is "definitely the best album that [he's] ever created, hands down."<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> In June 2015, both "Sag My Pants" and "Ill Mind of Hopsin 5" were certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>. On June 1, 2015, the first single off the album "Crown Me" was released. The second single "Fly" was released on July 8, 2015. Also in July 2015, it was announced that Hopsin had signed a distribution deal with <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>. <i>Pound Syndrome</i> was released on July 24, 2015. The album debuted at number 17 on the Billboard 200 with first-week sales of 17,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932018:_Undercover_Prodigy_and_No_Shame"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2018:_Undercover_Prodigy_and_No_Shame">2016–2018: <i>Undercover Prodigy</i> and <i>No Shame</i></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Undercover_Prodigy" title="Undercover Prodigy">Undercover Prodigy</a> and <a href="/wiki/No_Shame_(Hopsin_album)" title="No Shame (Hopsin album)">No Shame (Hopsin album)</a></div>
<p>In January 2016, Hopsin had announced on social media that his label Funk Volume, is "officially dead", due to ongoing business issues and financial disputes with his business partner and co-founder of the label, Damien Ritter.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> In March 2016, Hopsin released "<a href="/wiki/Ill_Mind_of_Hopsin_8" title="Ill Mind of Hopsin 8">Ill Mind of Hopsin 8</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> The song was a <a href="/wiki/Diss_track" class="mw-redirect" title="Diss track">diss track</a> directed towards Damien Ritter and Funk Volume.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Hopsin officially left Funk Volume after the song's release and went on to create a new label called <a href="/wiki/Undercover_Prodigy" title="Undercover Prodigy">Undercover Prodigy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Hopsin later went on to release more singles with his new label with, "Bout the Business" on April 20,<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> "Die This Way" on May 31,<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> "False Advertisement" on August 25,<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> and "Scrollin" with <a href="/wiki/Futuristic_(rapper)" title="Futuristic (rapper)">Futuristic</a> off his <i><a href="/wiki/As_Seen_on_the_Internet" title="As Seen on the Internet">As Seen on the Internet</a></i> album on August 26.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2017, Hopsin confirmed he was working on his next studio album and released the singles "All Your Fault", and "Bus That" on April 17.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> On September 22, 2017, Hopsin released the lead single to his fifth album titled, "The Purge".<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> The following single, "Happy Ending", was released as the second single on October 13.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> In the same month, Hopsin confirmed in an interview that he signed a distribution deal with <a href="/wiki/300_Entertainment" title="300 Entertainment">300 Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-300Ent_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-300Ent-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> On November 1, 2017, Hopsin released the third single, "Witch Doctor", and announced and revealed the album's title and cover of his fifth studio album titled, <i><a href="/wiki/No_Shame_(Hopsin_album)" title="No Shame (Hopsin album)">No Shame</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> The album was released on November 24, 2017, and debuted at number 42 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the release of <i>No Shame</i>, Hopsin later went on to release music videos from the album with, "Ill Mind of Hopsin 9", being released the same day as the album's fourth single.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> "Panorama City" and "Tell'em Who You Got It From" were released in January and February 2018, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> In July 2018, Hopsin appeared on a single and music video with Canadian rapper Dax titled, "YourWorthIt.org".<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> In December 2018, Hopsin released the singles "Low-Key" and "Hell's Carol".<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2019.E2.80.93present:_Hiatus_and_return_to_music"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–present:_Hiatus_and_return_to_music">2019–present: Hiatus and return to music</span></h3>
<p>On January 28, 2019, Hopsin released another collaboration with Dax titled, "You Should've Known".<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup> Hopsin later went on to release the singles "The Old Us" and "Picasso" on February 28 and May 8, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-oldus_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oldus-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup> On July 15, 2019, Hopsin released a new song titled, "I Don't Want It".<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup> In the song, he announces that he will be taking a break from music to recover from problems in his personal life.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 16, 2020, Hopsin uploaded a video to his YouTube channel announcing his return from his hiatus and announcing an upcoming album.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup> On April 21, Hopsin released a new single titled, "Covid Mansion".<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2020, Hopsin joined the advisory board of music social networking startup Artist Republik.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">&#91;85&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soulja_Boy">Soulja Boy</span></h3>
<p>Hopsin's feud with <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Boy" title="Soulja Boy">Soulja Boy</a> dates back to Hopsin's single "Sag My Pants", in which he disses Soulja. In late 2011, Soulja Boy called Hopsin "dope" but said that he wouldn't bother dissing him until Hopsin got more known.<sup id="cite_ref-defsounds.com_89-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-defsounds.com-89">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup> On August 25, 2012, Soulja Boy made controversial comments towards Hopsin on one of his webcam chats with his fans when Soulja stated "Fuck Hopsin. I'm about to go in the studio and record this Hopsin diss real quick". He continued, saying: "That nigga's a bitch; fuck that bitch ass nigga."<sup id="cite_ref-defsounds.com_89-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-defsounds.com-89">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup> Two days later on August 27, 2012, Hopsin and Soulja confronted each other on <a href="/wiki/Tinychat" title="Tinychat">Tinychat</a> where Hopsin goaded Soulja into dissing him.<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">&#91;90&#93;</a></sup> On September 3, 2012, Soulja released a Hopsin diss entitled "That Nigga Not Me", to which Hopsin has not responded. In an interview with <a href="/wiki/Tim_Westwood" title="Tim Westwood">Tim Westwood</a> following that diss, Hopsin put down the diss song as horrible and said the only rapper he has legitimate beef with is Soulja Boy. He also said he is contemplating "smashing the hell out of Soulja's career."<sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">&#91;91&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Orlando_arrest">Orlando arrest</span></h3>
<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Hopsin_mugshot_2012-8-26.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Hopsin_mugshot_2012-8-26.jpg/140px-Hopsin_mugshot_2012-8-26.jpg" decoding="async" width="140" height="175" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Hopsin_mugshot_2012-8-26.jpg/210px-Hopsin_mugshot_2012-8-26.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Hopsin_mugshot_2012-8-26.jpg/280px-Hopsin_mugshot_2012-8-26.jpg 2x" data-file-width="480" data-file-height="600" /></a><figcaption>Photo by the <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Police_Department" title="Orlando Police Department">Orlando Police Department</a>, August 26, 2012.</figcaption></figure>
<p>On August 25, 2012, Hopsin was scheduled to perform at Club 57 West, a popular night club located in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando, Florida</a>. At the last minute, the club promoters chose to pull the last opening act, causing a verbal altercation between the club promoters and the artist's entourage. Some witnesses claim Hopsin tried to settle the argument verbally, while Hopsin claims he tried to defuse a fight outside of the club on his way to <a href="/wiki/7-Eleven" title="7-Eleven">7-Eleven</a> (without indicating whether this was the same conflict, or something unrelated). At one point, Hopsin even offered 15 minutes of his own set to help find a solution to the problem.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">&#91;92&#93;</a></sup>  Hopsin was detained shortly thereafter once police responded to the initial call stemming from the altercation inside the club. According to court records, he was arrested for disorderly conduct. On September 21, the assistant state attorney decided not to officially file the information. Hopsin later commented on the case, referring to the police officer as a racist.<sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">&#91;93&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span></h2>
<p>Hopsin got his start in entertainment appearing as a background extra in movies and <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> TV shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/John_Tucker_Must_Die" title="John Tucker Must Die">John Tucker Must Die</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Even_Stevens" title="Even Stevens">Even Stevens</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Lizzie_McGuire" title="Lizzie McGuire">Lizzie McGuire</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cold_Case" title="Cold Case">Cold Case</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Malcolm_in_the_Middle" title="Malcolm in the Middle">Malcolm in the Middle</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Gilmore_Girls" title="Gilmore Girls">Gilmore Girls</a></i> among many others.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx.com_94-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx.com-94">&#91;94&#93;</a></sup> His most notable work was on <i><a href="/wiki/That%27s_So_Raven" title="That&#39;s So Raven">That's So Raven</a></i>, which he worked on for several years, starting when he was 15.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">&#91;95&#93;</a></sup> In the entire course of his appearances he only had a speaking part a single time.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx.com_94-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx.com-94">&#91;94&#93;</a></sup> He also made an appearance in the 2009 film <i><a href="/wiki/Fame_(2009_film)" title="Fame (2009 film)">Fame</a></i> as a rapper. In 2015, Hopsin co-starred in the second season of the TV drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Murder_in_the_First_(TV_series)" title="Murder in the First (TV series)">Murder in the First</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_image">Public image</span></h2>
<p>In 2004, Hopsin began wearing colored eye contacts in appearances in interviews, music videos, and performances. He stated that he used the contacts to give himself a memorable appearance and differentiate himself from other <a href="/wiki/African_American" class="mw-redirect" title="African American">African American</a> rappers.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlnews2_97-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlnews2-97">&#91;97&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hopsin is against drug and alcohol usage, and has criticized how mainstream entertainers promote usage of drugs and alcohol to youth. In some of his tracks, such as "Nocturnal Rainbows", he emphasizes his dislike for drugs (most notably <a href="/wiki/Methamphetamine" title="Methamphetamine">crystal meth</a>) and the irreversible damage they are capable of. Hopsin has stated that he tries to be a hip hop musician who can instill positive influences in people who listen to his music.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlnews2_97-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlnews2-97">&#91;97&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-killerhiphop_98-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-killerhiphop-98">&#91;98&#93;</a></sup> He follows a <a href="/wiki/Straight_edge" title="Straight edge">straight edge</a> lifestyle, and has claimed to have never drunk, taken recreational drugs, or smoked.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">&#91;99&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
