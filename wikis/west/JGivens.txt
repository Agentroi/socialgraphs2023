<p><b>Jeremiah Timothy Givens</b> (born May 24, 1987), also known by the stage name <b>JGivens</b>, is an American former <a href="/wiki/Christian_hip_hop" title="Christian hip hop">Christian hip hop</a> musician. He has released four studio albums: <i>Run</i> in 2011, <i>El v. Envy</i> in 2013, <i><a href="/wiki/Fly_Exam" title="Fly Exam">Fly Exam</a></i> in 2015, and <i>Domino</i> in 2019.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JGivens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jeremiah Timothy Givens<sup id="cite_ref-ASCAP_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASCAP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was born on May 24, 1987, in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Humble_Beast_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Humble_Beast-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CBI_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBI-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> to Brenda Banks.<sup id="cite_ref-CBI_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBI-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His mother moved to <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a> when Givens was five years old, to remove the family from the presence of gang life.<sup id="cite_ref-Humble_Beast_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Humble_Beast-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Givens obtained his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor&#39;s degree">bachelor's degree</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineering" title="Mechanical engineering">mechanical engineering</a> at the <a href="/wiki/USC_Viterbi_School_of_Engineering" title="USC Viterbi School of Engineering">Viterbi School of Engineering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Humble_Beast_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Humble_Beast-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JGivens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>JGivens' music career started in 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and his debut studio album, <i>Run</i>, was released on July 22, 2011, independently.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His subsequent studio album, <i>El v. Envy</i>, was released on August 6, 2013, independently.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> His third and most recent studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Fly_Exam" title="Fly Exam">Fly Exam</a></i>, was released on September 25, 2015, with Humble Beast Records.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, JGivens was featured on <a href="/wiki/Lecrae" title="Lecrae">Lecrae</a>'s mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Church_Clothes_3" title="Church Clothes 3">Church Clothes 3</a></i>, performing the song "Misconceptions 3" alongside Lecrae, <a href="/wiki/John_Givez" title="John Givez">John Givez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Hill_Perry" title="Jackie Hill Perry">Jackie Hill Perry</a>.
</p><p>In 2019, Givens transitioned away from Christian hip-hop and revealed he was addicted to drugs, openly gay, and <a href="/wiki/HIV" title="HIV">HIV</a>-positive.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
