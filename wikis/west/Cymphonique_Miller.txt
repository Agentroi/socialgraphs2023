<p><b>Cymphonique Miller</b> (born August 1, 1996),<sup id="cite_ref-bet_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bet-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> known professionally by her <a href="/wiki/Mononym" title="Mononym">mononym</a> <b>Cymphonique</b>, is an American singer and actress. She is the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Percy Robert Miller</a> (also known as Master P) and is the younger sister of <a href="/wiki/Romeo_Miller" title="Romeo Miller">Romeo Miller</a> (a.k.a. Romeo). She is known in the acting world for her leading role as Kacey Simon on the <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Rock" title="How to Rock">How to Rock</a></i>, as well as her voice role as Krystal on Nickelodeon's <i><a href="/wiki/Winx_Club" title="Winx Club">Winx Club</a></i>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cymphonique_Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career_and_guest_appearances">Early career and guest appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cymphonique_Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career and guest appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Miller has performed on several national tours and in theme parks across the United States. She has also toured with <a href="/wiki/Raven-Symon%C3%A9" title="Raven-Symoné">Raven-Symoné</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Tisdale" title="Ashley Tisdale">Ashley Tisdale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demi_Lovato" title="Demi Lovato">Demi Lovato</a>, <a href="/wiki/JoJo_(singer)" title="JoJo (singer)">JoJo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Girls" title="Wonder Girls">Wonder Girls</a>,  and <a href="/wiki/College_Boyys" class="mw-redirect" title="College Boyys">College Boyys</a>.
</p><p>She was a finalist in <a href="/wiki/Radio_Disney" title="Radio Disney">Radio Disney</a>'s <i>Next Big Thing</i> but came in second place to <a href="/wiki/Jasmine_Sagginario" class="mw-redirect" title="Jasmine Sagginario">Jasmine</a>. Her songs "Butterflies", "Lil Miss Swagger" and "Daddy I'm A Rockstar" received airplay on <a href="/wiki/Radio_Disney" title="Radio Disney">Radio Disney</a>. The music video for "Lil Miss Swagger," released when she was 12 years old, received more than 11 million YouTube views.  Miller has guest-starred on <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Time_Rush" title="Big Time Rush">Big Time Rush</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/True_Jackson,_VP" title="True Jackson, VP">True Jackson, VP</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Troop" title="The Troop">The Troop</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Just_Jordan" title="Just Jordan">Just Jordan</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-bet_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bet-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Music_and_acting_careers_.282011.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_and_acting_careers_(2011–present)">Music and acting careers (2011–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cymphonique_Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music and acting careers (2011–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:CYMPH_-_DOPE_Magazine_Cover_PRINCESS_-_Fall_2k12_AMBITION.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/CYMPH_-_DOPE_Magazine_Cover_PRINCESS_-_Fall_2k12_AMBITION.jpg/220px-CYMPH_-_DOPE_Magazine_Cover_PRINCESS_-_Fall_2k12_AMBITION.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="285" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/CYMPH_-_DOPE_Magazine_Cover_PRINCESS_-_Fall_2k12_AMBITION.jpg/330px-CYMPH_-_DOPE_Magazine_Cover_PRINCESS_-_Fall_2k12_AMBITION.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/CYMPH_-_DOPE_Magazine_Cover_PRINCESS_-_Fall_2k12_AMBITION.jpg/440px-CYMPH_-_DOPE_Magazine_Cover_PRINCESS_-_Fall_2k12_AMBITION.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2550" data-file-height="3300" /></a><figcaption>Miller on a cover of <a href="/wiki/Pure_DOPE" class="mw-redirect" title="Pure DOPE">Pure DOPE</a> magazine  in 2012</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2012, Miller starred the lead and contributed to the music for the <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Rock" title="How to Rock">How to Rock</a></i> along with <a href="/wiki/Lulu_Antariksa" title="Lulu Antariksa">Lulu Antariksa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bet_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bet-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-deadline_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deadline-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The show lasted one season. She sings the theme song of Nickelodeon's <i><a href="/wiki/Winx_Club" title="Winx Club">Winx Club</a></i> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Winx_Club_episodes#Revived_series_2" title="List of Winx Club episodes">revival series</a>, which premiered on Nickelodeon in June 2011. She was also the voice of Princess Krystal on the series.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She is featured on the <a href="/wiki/Big_Time_Rush_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Time Rush (band)">Big Time Rush</a> song, "I Know You Know".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2011, Miller was nominated for "<a href="/wiki/BET_Award_for_Best_Hip-Hop_Artists" class="mw-redirect" title="BET Award for Best Hip-Hop Artists">Best Female Hip-Hop Artist</a>" at the <a href="/wiki/BET_Awards" title="BET Awards">BET Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 7, 2012, Miller performed the halftime show at the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Sparks" title="Los Angeles Sparks">Los Angeles Sparks</a>' <a href="/wiki/Girl_Scouts_of_the_USA" title="Girl Scouts of the USA">Girl Scouts</a> night at <a href="/wiki/The_Staples_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="The Staples Center">The Staples Center</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Miller appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_TV" title="Mad TV">Mad TV</a></i>, in numerous national commercials, and in <a href="/wiki/Public_service_announcement" title="Public service announcement">public service announcements</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OfficalSite_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfficalSite-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, she released a mixtape entitled <i>Passion</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The same year, she appeared in the film <i>The Demsey Sisters</i> along with <a href="/wiki/Lynn_Whitfield" title="Lynn Whitfield">Lynn Whitfield</a> and Denyce Lawton.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
