<p><b>Diamonté Quiava Valentin Harper</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born July 2, 1993),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Saweetie</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="/ə/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;about&#39;">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;w&#39; in &#39;wind&#39;">w</span><span title="/iː/: &#39;ee&#39; in &#39;fleece&#39;">iː</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/i/: &#39;y&#39; in &#39;happy&#39;">i</span></span>/</a></span></span>; also pronounced as simply "sweetie"),<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> is an American rapper and singer. After the release of her debut single "<a href="/wiki/Icy_Grl" title="Icy Grl">Icy Grl</a>" in 2017, she was signed to her then-manager Max Gousse's record label Artistry Worldwide, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Warner_Records" title="Warner Records">Warner Records</a>.
</p><p>Saweetie released her debut extended play, <i><a href="/wiki/High_Maintenance_(Saweetie_EP)" title="High Maintenance (Saweetie EP)">High Maintenance</a></i>, in 2017. Her second EP, <i>Icy</i>, was released in March 2019 and spawned the <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">top 40</a> single "<a href="/wiki/My_Type_(Saweetie_song)" title="My Type (Saweetie song)">My Type</a>". Her upcoming debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Pretty_Bitch_Music" title="Pretty Bitch Music">Pretty Bitch Music</a></i> was preceded by the singles "<a href="/wiki/Tap_In" title="Tap In">Tap In</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Best_Friend_(Saweetie_song)" title="Best Friend (Saweetie song)">Best Friend</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Doja_Cat" title="Doja Cat">Doja Cat</a>), both of which reached the top 20 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. In 2021, she received two nominations for the <a href="/wiki/64th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="64th Annual Grammy Awards">64th Annual Grammy Awards</a> including one for <a href="/wiki/Best_New_Artist" class="mw-redirect" title="Best New Artist">Best New Artist</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saweetie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Diamonté Quiava Valentin Harper was born on July 2, 1993, in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clara,_California" title="Santa Clara, California">Santa Clara, California</a>, to Trinidad Valentin, a <a href="/wiki/Video_vixen" title="Video vixen">video vixen</a>, who is of mixed Filipino and Chinese heritage,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and Johnny Harper, who is African-American.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> She grew up in <a href="/wiki/Hayward,_California" title="Hayward, California">Hayward</a> and later in  <a href="/wiki/Sacramento" class="mw-redirect" title="Sacramento">Sacramento</a>, attending <a href="/wiki/Merrill_F._West_High_School" title="Merrill F. West High School">Merrill F. West High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tracy,_California" title="Tracy, California">Tracy</a> and graduating from <a href="/wiki/Monterey_Trail_High_School" title="Monterey Trail High School">Monterey Trail High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Elk_Grove,_California" title="Elk Grove, California">Elk Grove</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-VIBE-I_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VIBE-I-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HIPHOPDX_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HIPHOPDX-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-XXL_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> She began writing music at age 13. After high school, she attended <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_State_University" title="San Diego State University">San Diego State University</a>, where she studied communication and <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">business</a> before transferring to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a>, where she completed her degree and received a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> (BA) in <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">communication</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> After graduating, she began to focus on her rap career.<sup id="cite_ref-BILLBOARD_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BILLBOARD-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HUFFPOST_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HUFFPOST-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-XXL_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saweetie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932018:_High_Maintenance"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2018:_High_Maintenance">2016–2018: <i>High Maintenance</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saweetie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2016–2018: High Maintenance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Saweetie began posting clips of her freestyles to her <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> account in 2016. One video featured her rapping over the beat from fellow female rapper <a href="/wiki/Khia" title="Khia">Khia</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/My_Neck,_My_Back_(Lick_It)" title="My Neck, My Back (Lick It)">My Neck, My Back (Lick It)</a>", which eventually became "<a href="/wiki/Icy_Grl" title="Icy Grl">Icy Grl</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-VIBE-I_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VIBE-I-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-XXL_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The song was released on her <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> in the summer of 2017 and brought her to the attention of Max Gousse, a well-known producer and A&amp;R executive, who became her manager.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VIBE-II_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VIBE-II-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-XXL_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The video went viral online, accruing 100 million views on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> as of June 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-BILLBOARD_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BILLBOARD-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-XXL_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>
That same month, Saweetie released a freestyle rap, "High Maintenance", accompanied by a short clip of herself rapping a verse to the song, all while chilling in her kitchen. It too went viral, on Instagram and <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-VIBE-II_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VIBE-II-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-XXL_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In October 2017, she released a video for her song "Focus", which samples <a href="/wiki/DRAM_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DRAM (rapper)">DRAM</a>'s "Gilligan".<sup id="cite_ref-XXL-II_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-II-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></p><figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Saweetie_at_The_Great_Escape_2018_2.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Saweetie_at_The_Great_Escape_2018_2.png/220px-Saweetie_at_The_Great_Escape_2018_2.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Saweetie_at_The_Great_Escape_2018_2.png/330px-Saweetie_at_The_Great_Escape_2018_2.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Saweetie_at_The_Great_Escape_2018_2.png/440px-Saweetie_at_The_Great_Escape_2018_2.png 2x" data-file-width="1016" data-file-height="679" /></a><figcaption>Saweetie performing in 2018</figcaption></figure><p>In January 2018, Saweetie was named <a href="/wiki/Tidal_(service)" title="Tidal (service)">Tidal</a>'s Artist of the Week and one of <i><a href="/wiki/Pigeons_%26_Planes" class="mw-redirect" title="Pigeons &amp; Planes">Pigeons &amp; Planes</a></i>'s Best New Artists of the Month.<sup id="cite_ref-P&amp;P_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P&amp;P-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> During <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_LII" title="Super Bowl LII">Super Bowl LII</a> in February 2018, she was featured in an ad for <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>'s cosmetic company, Fenty Beauty.<sup id="cite_ref-HNHH_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HNHH-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> That month, she signed to Warner Bros. Records (since May 2019 <a href="/wiki/Warner_Records" title="Warner Records">Warner Records</a>) and Gousse's record label, Artistry Worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-BILLBOARD_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BILLBOARD-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LAT_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAT-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RU_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RU-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style></p><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I do a lot of covers but this is the first time I'm actually doing original content. I leave the studio and I come home wanting to listen to really myself.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8202;<cite>Saweetie for <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> in October 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></cite></div></blockquote>
<p>In February 2018, Saweetie launched her own record label, Icy Records.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Saweetie released her major label debut EP, <i><a href="/wiki/High_Maintenance_(Saweetie_EP)" title="High Maintenance (Saweetie EP)">High Maintenance</a></i>, on March 16, 2018. It has nine tracks and was produced by <a href="/wiki/CashMoneyAP" title="CashMoneyAP">CashMoneyAP</a>, Nyrell and Saweetie's cousin, <a href="/wiki/Zaytoven" title="Zaytoven">Zaytoven</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL-III_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-III-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VIBE-III_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VIBE-III-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The single "Icy Girl" was certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">Gold</a> in June 2018, for sales of 500,000 in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> In September 2019, the single was certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> multi-platinum and reached No. 1 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Billboard</a>'s rhythmic songs airplay chart.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2019.E2.80.932020:_Icy_and_other_ventures"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–2020:_Icy_and_other_ventures">2019–2020: <i>Icy</i> and other ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saweetie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2019–2020: Icy and other ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Saweetie released her second major label EP album, <i>Icy</i>, on March 29, 2019. Its first single, "<a href="/wiki/My_Type_(Saweetie_song)" title="My Type (Saweetie song)">My Type</a>", written by Saweetie and produced by <a href="/wiki/London_on_da_Track" title="London on da Track">London on da Track</a>, samples the 2004 song "<a href="/wiki/Freek-a-Leek" title="Freek-a-Leek">Freek-a-Leek</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Petey_Pablo" title="Petey Pablo">Petey Pablo</a>. Debuting on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 at number 81 on the charts, It was Saweetie's first song to enter the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> "My Type" peaked at number 21, becoming Saweetie's first top-40 hit on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> A remix of the song featuring <a href="/wiki/Jhen%C3%A9_Aiko" title="Jhené Aiko">Jhené Aiko</a> and <a href="/wiki/City_Girls" title="City Girls">City Girls</a> was released on August 23, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> "My Type" hit number 1 on the Rhythmic Radio charts in September 2019 and was later certified double platinum in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gousse_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gousse-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2019, Saweetie collaborated with <a href="/wiki/PrettyLittleThing" title="PrettyLittleThing">PrettyLittleThing</a> to launch a 59-piece clothing collection, PrettyLittleThing x Saweetie. She said the theme of the <a href="/wiki/Capsule_collection" class="mw-redirect" title="Capsule collection">capsule</a> was "a <a href="/wiki/Bourgeoisie" title="Bourgeoisie">boujie</a> rich girl enjoying the finer things in life. I want to show the boys and girls that if you hustle hard your work will pay off."<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> It debuted during <a href="/wiki/New_York_Fashion_Week" title="New York Fashion Week">New York Fashion Week</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Saweetie appeared on VH1's <i><a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon_Presents:_Wild_%27N_Out" class="mw-redirect" title="Nick Cannon Presents: Wild &#39;N Out">Nick Cannon Presents: Wild 'N Out</a></i> on January 28, 2020, as a guest performer, performing "My Type".
</p>
<h3><span id="2020.E2.80.93present:_Pretty_Bitch_Music"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020–present:_Pretty_Bitch_Music">2020–present: <i>Pretty Bitch Music</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saweetie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2020–present: Pretty Bitch Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Saweetie released the lead single, "<a href="/wiki/Tap_In" title="Tap In">Tap In</a>", from her upcoming debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Pretty_Bitch_Music" title="Pretty Bitch Music">Pretty Bitch Music</a></i>, on June 20, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The single peaked at number 20 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, becoming her first top-20 single and her first song to enter the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>, peaking at number 38. In September 2020, the single reached number one on <a href="/wiki/Mediabase" title="Mediabase">Mediabase</a>'s Urban Radio Chart as well as the Billboard x <a href="/wiki/Triller_(app)" title="Triller (app)">Triller</a> US and Global charts.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The track was Saweetie's second single to reach Billboard's Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> In August 2020, Saweetie released a <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remix</a> of the single, "Tap In Remix", that featured rappers <a href="/wiki/Post_Malone" title="Post Malone">Post Malone</a>, <a href="/wiki/DaBaby" title="DaBaby">DaBaby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Harlow" title="Jack Harlow">Jack Harlow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> This was followed by the promotional single "Pretty Bitch Freestyle" on July 2, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 31, 2020, Saweetie appeared alongside American rapper <a href="/wiki/Tay_Money" title="Tay Money">Tay Money</a> on the song "Bussin 2.0", with a music video premiering the same day.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On August 6, 2020, she was featured on <a href="/wiki/Ava_Max" title="Ava Max">Ava Max</a>'s song, "<a href="/wiki/Kings_%26_Queens,_Pt._2" class="mw-redirect" title="Kings &amp; Queens, Pt. 2">Kings &amp; Queens, Pt. 2</a>", alongside <a href="/wiki/Lauv" title="Lauv">Lauv</a>. On October 23, 2020, she released "<a href="/wiki/Back_to_the_Streets" title="Back to the Streets">Back to the Streets</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Jhen%C3%A9_Aiko" title="Jhené Aiko">Jhené Aiko</a>), which serves as the second single from <i>Pretty Bitch Music</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The album's third single, "<a href="/wiki/Best_Friend_(Saweetie_song)" title="Best Friend (Saweetie song)">Best Friend</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Doja_Cat" title="Doja Cat">Doja Cat</a>), was released on January 7, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, Saweetie was added to <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a>' <a href="/wiki/Forbes_30_Under_30" title="Forbes 30 Under 30">30 Under 30</a> Music list and <a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a>'s Hitmakers Impact list.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> In March 2020, she embarked on her first beauty collaboration by becoming the face of the KISS Colors edge fixer glue in the Edge Fixer Glued x Saweetie collection.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> In October 2020, she launched her own <a href="/wiki/Distance_education" title="Distance education">virtual educational</a> content series, Icy University.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of March 2021, Saweetie has also launched a <a href="/wiki/Jewellery" title="Jewellery">jewelry</a> line, a capsule clothing collection (partnering with retailer <a href="/wiki/PrettyLittleThing" title="PrettyLittleThing">PrettyLittleThing</a>), and a co-branded <a href="/wiki/Cosmetics" title="Cosmetics">makeup</a> collection with cosmetics retailer Morphe.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Her other brand partnerships include her February 2021 Essenchills collection with <a href="/wiki/Sinful_Colors" title="Sinful Colors">Sinful Colors</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> and the Saweetie Meal at <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald&#39;s">McDonald's</a> in August 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Saweetie also expanded her appearances by hosting Culture Con 2021 for creatives of color<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> and making her television acting debut as the character Indigo in 3 episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Grown-ish" title="Grown-ish">Grown-ish</a></i>.
</p><p>In April 2021, Saweetie released the extended play <i><a href="/wiki/Pretty_Summer_Playlist:_Season_1" title="Pretty Summer Playlist: Season 1">Pretty Summer Playlist: Season 1</a></i>. It was supported by the singles "Risky" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Drakeo_the_Ruler" title="Drakeo the Ruler">Drakeo the Ruler</a>) and "Talkin' Bout" by Loui, featuring Saweetie. On April 30, 2021, Saweetie collaborated with British girl group <a href="/wiki/Little_Mix" title="Little Mix">Little Mix</a> to be featured on the remix version of its song "<a href="/wiki/Confetti_(Little_Mix_song)" title="Confetti (Little Mix song)">Confetti</a>" with a video released the same day. The song peaked at number nine on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> . On May 7, 2021, Saweetie released "<a href="/wiki/Fast_(Motion)" title="Fast (Motion)">Fast (Motion)</a>" as the fourth single from <i>Pretty Bitch Music</i>.
</p><p>In November 2021, <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> released a sex-positive comedy special titled <i>Sex: Unzipped</i> in which Saweetie was the host.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> On November 19, she released the single "<a href="/wiki/Icy_Chain" title="Icy Chain">Icy Chain</a>" which she performed on <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i> the following day.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> During her SNL debut, she also performed "Tap In" and "Best Friend".<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> In January 2022, Saweetie appeared as the star of <a href="/wiki/MAC_Cosmetics" title="MAC Cosmetics">MAC Cosmetics</a>' "Challenge Accepted" campaign alongside <a href="/wiki/Cher" title="Cher">Cher</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 18, 2022, Saweetie released her fourth EP, <i>The Single Life</i>. The EP was preceded by the single "Don't Say Nothin'", which was released just hours ahead of the project's release.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2023, it was announced that Saweetie would co-headline a tour with YG and Tyga for the west coast and Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saweetie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saweetie is the first cousin once-removed of actress <a href="/wiki/Gabrielle_Union" title="Gabrielle Union">Gabrielle Union</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> Her grandfather is <a href="/wiki/Willie_Harper" title="Willie Harper">Willie Harper</a>, who played football for the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_49ers" title="San Francisco 49ers">San Francisco 49ers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Saweetie began dating American rapper <a href="/wiki/Quavo" title="Quavo">Quavo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Migos" title="Migos">Migos</a> in September 2018. They were first seen together during the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Fashion_Week" title="New York Fashion Week">New York Fashion Week</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> On March 19, 2021, Saweetie confirmed via social media that she and Quavo are no longer together. She also mentioned on social media that Quavo had been unfaithful, writing "Presents don't band aid scars and the love isn't real when the intimacy is given to other women."<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> In late March 2021, video footage surfaced showing the pair in a physical altercation that allegedly happened in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
