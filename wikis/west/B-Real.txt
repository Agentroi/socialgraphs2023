<p><b>Louis Mario Freese</b> (born June 2, 1970),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> known by his stage name <b>B-Real</b>, is an American rapper. Since 1991, he has been one of two lead rappers in the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Sen_Dog" title="Sen Dog">Sen Dog</a>. He has also been a part of the <a href="/wiki/Rap_metal" title="Rap metal">rap metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Kush_(band)" title="Kush (band)">Kush</a> (2000–2002), the hip hop supergroup <a href="/wiki/Serial_Killers_(musical_group)" title="Serial Killers (musical group)">Serial Killers</a> (2013–present) and the <a href="/wiki/Rap_rock" title="Rap rock">rap rock</a> supergroup <a href="/wiki/Prophets_of_Rage" title="Prophets of Rage">Prophets of Rage</a> (2016–2019). He has released a variety of solo <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a>, as well as two solo albums: <i><a href="/wiki/Smoke_n_Mirrors" title="Smoke n Mirrors">Smoke n Mirrors</a></i> (2009) and <i>Tell You Something</i> (2020).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B-Real&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in Los Angeles to a Mexican father and a Cuban mother, B-Real moved with his sister and mother out of his father's home to <a href="/wiki/South_Gate,_California" title="South Gate, California">South Gate, California</a> at the age of five. He later lived in <a href="/wiki/Bell,_California" title="Bell, California">Bell, California</a>. Before dropping out of <a href="/wiki/Bell_High_School_(Bell,_California)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bell High School (Bell, California)">Bell High School</a>, he befriended future <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a> members <a href="/wiki/Sen_Dog" title="Sen Dog">Sen Dog</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mellow_Man_Ace" title="Mellow Man Ace">Mellow Man Ace</a> (who later left the group to go solo) and became an active member of the Family Swan <a href="/wiki/Bloods" title="Bloods">Bloods</a>, known as the Neighborhood Family Bloods at that time. At the age of 17, Freese was shot in a gang-related shooting with a hollow-point .22 caliber bullet, which punctured his lung.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> B Real was married to his long time girlfriend in August of 2008 in Los Angeles, California. They have one daughter together (born in 2010). He also has an adult son, from a previous relationship.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B-Real&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After being introduced to <a href="/wiki/DJ_Muggs" title="DJ Muggs">DJ Muggs</a> by Julio G the <a href="/wiki/KDAY" title="KDAY">KDAY</a> Mixmaster, B-Real and Sen gained interest in Muggs's concept of an album based on experiences from Cypress Ave in South Gate. The group was signed with Ruffhouse/Columbia records in 1991 and released their influential debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill_(album)" title="Cypress Hill (album)">Cypress Hill</a></i>, that year. B-Real would use fictional stories telling of life-threatening experiences as material for the group's debut album, and subsequent releases.
</p><p>While working on their first album, B-Real developed his distinctive high-pitched, nasal rap style. Previously, he had rapped with something like his normal voice, but DJ Muggs told him that his rap delivery was boring and warned him that, unless he changed it, he would be overshadowed by Sen Dog. B-Real took inspiration from hip hop pioneer <a href="/wiki/Rammellzee" title="Rammellzee">Rammellzee</a>, who had occasionally rapped in a high-pitched voice. B-Real initially saw the high voice as a silly gimmick, but was surprised at the positive feedback he got for his new style and decided to keep going with it.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cypress Hill's self-titled debut album was a critical and commercial hit, selling 2 million copies, and their follow-up album, 1993's <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Sunday_(Cypress_Hill_album)" title="Black Sunday (Cypress Hill album)">Black Sunday</a></i>, was an even bigger hit, debuting at #1 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and eventually selling 3.4 million copies. They became the first Latin rap group to have platinum and multi-platinum albums, and remain the best-selling Latin rap group of all time.<sup id="cite_ref-SALES_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SALES-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In a 2010 interview, B-Real stated: "Fortunately I guess the fact we were able to achieve what we achieved, being Latino but without really exploiting that side of it, showed like a whole generation behind us how you could have that success without being labeled as just one thing. Because back in the day, when you were labeled 'a Latino rapper', the record companies would only try to market you to that Latin fan-base which didn't really exist yet!... So yeah, it feels good to see people recognizing us as some of the pioneers that opened the doors for a lot of these other Latino rappers to come through."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aside from Cypress Hill, B-Real has been involved in several other musical projects. In 1996, he contributed to the soundtrack for the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Space_Jam" title="Space Jam">Space Jam</a></i>. In 2002, he teamed up with Mellow Man Ace and <a href="/wiki/Son_Doobie" class="mw-redirect" title="Son Doobie">Son Doobie</a> for the short-lived Serial Rhyme Killas, which released one 12" single in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-DISCOG_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DISCOG-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The group recorded a full-length debut album, entitled <i>Deluxe Rapture</i>, but it was never released. B-Real formed a <a href="/wiki/Rap_metal" title="Rap metal">rap metal</a> group, <a href="/wiki/Kush_(band)" title="Kush (band)">Kush</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Deftones" title="Deftones">Deftones</a> guitarist <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Carpenter" title="Stephen Carpenter">Stephen Carpenter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fear_Factory" title="Fear Factory">Fear Factory</a> members <a href="/wiki/Christian_Olde_Wolbers" title="Christian Olde Wolbers">Christian Olde Wolbers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Herrera" title="Raymond Herrera">Raymond Herrera</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Downey_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Downey-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> According to B-Real, Kush is more aggressive than other bands in the genre.<sup id="cite_ref-Downey_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Downey-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He was also previously a member of the group <a href="/wiki/Psycho_Realm" title="Psycho Realm">The Psycho Realm</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-PSYCHO_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSYCHO-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and in 2007 announced that he would collaborate with Sick Jacken once again on a new album.<sup id="cite_ref-INTERVIEW_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-INTERVIEW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He revealed in an interview that he plans on recording a "vs." album with Cypress Hill member <a href="/wiki/DJ_Muggs" title="DJ Muggs">DJ Muggs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 2005 to 2007, B-Real released three <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a> as a solo artist: <i>The Gunslinger</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Gunslinger_Part_II:_Fist_Full_of_Dollars" title="The Gunslinger Part II: Fist Full of Dollars">The Gunslinger Part II: Fist Full of Dollars</a></i> and <i>The Gunslinger Part III: For a Few Dollars More</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-SOLO_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SOLO-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He released his first solo album, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Smoke_N_Mirrors" class="mw-redirect" title="Smoke N Mirrors">Smoke N Mirrors</a></i>, in 2009. He was a guest artist on the <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> single "<a href="/wiki/Vato_(song)" title="Vato (song)">Vato</a>", from Snoop Dogg's 2006 album <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Blue_Carpet_Treatment" title="Tha Blue Carpet Treatment">Tha Blue Carpet Treatment</a></i>. In 2017, he was featured on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Undead" title="Hollywood Undead">Hollywood Undead</a> song "Black Cadillac" from the band's fifth album <i><a href="/wiki/Five_(Hollywood_Undead_album)" title="Five (Hollywood Undead album)">Five</a>.</i>
</p><p>From 2016 to 2019, he was part of the group <a href="/wiki/Prophets_of_Rage" title="Prophets of Rage">Prophets of Rage</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Chuck_D" title="Chuck D">Chuck D</a> and former members of <a href="/wiki/Rage_Against_the_Machine" title="Rage Against the Machine">Rage Against the Machine</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_work">Production work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B-Real&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:B-Real_2006.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/B-Real_2006.jpg/180px-B-Real_2006.jpg" decoding="async" width="180" height="273" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b9/B-Real_2006.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="244" data-file-height="370" /></a><figcaption>B-Real in 2006</figcaption></figure>
<p>In addition to his career as an MC, B-Real has worked as a <a href="/wiki/Music_producer" class="mw-redirect" title="Music producer">music producer</a> for six years, with clients ranging from <a href="/wiki/Proof_(rapper)" title="Proof (rapper)">Proof</a> of <a href="/wiki/D12" title="D12">D12</a> to the <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-INTERVIEW_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-INTERVIEW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He intentionally kept his production for Cypress Hill down to a minimum, as not to interfere with DJ Muggs' involvement in the group, but has produced several tracks for his own solo projects. B-Real also manages a team of music producers known as the 'Audio Hustlaz'.<sup id="cite_ref-INTERVIEW_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-INTERVIEW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> B-Real produced three of the tracks on his solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Smoke_N_Mirrors" class="mw-redirect" title="Smoke N Mirrors">Smoke N Mirrors</a></i>: "Don't Ya Dare Laugh", "Fire" and "Dr. Hyphenstein". B-Real and his production worked alongside DJ Muggs and others on the 2010 Cypress Hill album <i><a href="/wiki/Rise_Up_(Cypress_Hill_album)" title="Rise Up (Cypress Hill album)">Rise Up</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-music_projects">Non-music projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B-Real&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Non-music projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1998, B-Real voiced the part of a newborn baby for the song "This World Is Something New to Me" in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rugrats_Movie" title="The Rugrats Movie">The Rugrats Movie</a></i>.
</p><p>In the early 2000s B-Real and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Muggs" title="DJ Muggs">DJ Muggs</a> co-founded and co-captained a professional <a href="/wiki/Paintball" title="Paintball">paintball</a> team, the Stoned Assassins. They competed regionally, nationally, and internationally, usually in the second-level divisions. B-Real and the Assassins also appeared in the console game <a href="/wiki/Greg_Hastings_Tournament_Paintball" class="mw-redirect" title="Greg Hastings Tournament Paintball">Greg Hastings Tournament Paintball</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SPIN_-_Feb_2005_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPIN_-_Feb_2005-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His live streaming site Breal.tv<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> features live interactive programming over the internet. He is the host of the video <a href="/wiki/Podcast" title="Podcast">podcasts</a> <i>The Dr. Greenthumb Podcast</i>, <i>The Smokebox</i> and <i>Meditation</i>.
</p><p>In 2018, B-Real partnered with marijuana subscription box company Daily High Club to craft a B-Real-themed smoking supply box for the month of March.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
