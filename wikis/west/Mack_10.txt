<p><b>Dedrick D'Mon Rolison</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born August 9, 1971), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Mack 10</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Larkin90_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin90-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He has sold nearly 11 million records combining his solo and group works. Mack 10 made his first appearance on Ice Cube's 1994 <i><a href="/wiki/Bootlegs_%26_B-Sides" title="Bootlegs &amp; B-Sides">Bootlegs &amp; B-Sides</a></i> compilation on the <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remixed</a> track "What Can I Do?" and was a member of hip hop <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> <a href="/wiki/Westside_Connection" title="Westside Connection">Westside Connection</a> along with <a href="/wiki/WC_(rapper)" title="WC (rapper)">WC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>. Mack 10 is also the creator of independent record label <a href="/wiki/Hoo-Bangin%27_Records" title="Hoo-Bangin&#39; Records">Hoo-Bangin' Records</a> and made his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Ingram_MAC-10" class="mw-redirect" title="Ingram MAC-10">Ingram MAC-10</a> <a href="/wiki/Submachine_gun" title="Submachine gun">submachine gun</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mack_10&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mack 10 was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood, California</a>. He is a member of the Queen Street Bloods gang in Inglewood.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mack_10&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mack 10 made his first appearance on Ice Cube's <i>Bootlegs &amp; B-Sides</i> compilation on the track "What Can I Do? (Remix)".
</p><p>His debut, gold selling album <i>Mack 10</i> was released in 1995 on the Priority label.<sup id="cite_ref-Larkin90_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin90-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He made his hip hop debut with the hit single, "Foe Life", off his self-titled album.<sup id="cite_ref-Larkin90_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin90-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His next two releases "Based on a True Story" (1997) and "The Recipe" (1998) also reached gold certification and peaked at No. 14 and No. 15 on the Billboard 200, respectively.
</p><p>Rolison is also the founder of independent record label Hoo-Bangin Records.<sup id="cite_ref-Larkin90_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin90-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He appeared with W.C. and Ice Cube in the 1996 all-star side project Westside Connection, and formed his own production company, Mack One-O,<sup id="cite_ref-Larkin90_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin90-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> which signed the acts Allfrumtha-I and the Comrades. Mack 10 also signed Glasses Malone to his Hoo-Bangin Records imprint through Cash Money Records.
</p><p>His most recent album, 2009's <i><a href="/wiki/Soft_White" title="Soft White">Soft White</a></i> was released on Hoo-Bangin' Records and Fontana Distribution. The first single was "Big Balla" featuring Birdman and Glasses Malone.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Feuds">Feuds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mack_10&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Feuds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mack 10 was involved in numerous feuds including with the rapper Common in the song "Westside Slaughterhouse" featured the rapper Ice Cube in his critically acclaimed self-titled album, the diss was a response to the song named "I Used to Love H.E.R." by Common. Earlier on, in 1996, when Mack 10 was a member in the rap supergroup Westside Connection, he was featured in the song "King of the Hill" by Ice Cube - a diss song directed to the rap group named <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a>. WC (a member of the rap supergroup Westside Connection) did not want to participate in the feud so as not to ruin his long-lasted relationship with Cypress Hill. The feud was later squashed by both parties.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mack_10&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rolison married <a href="/wiki/Tionne_Watkins" title="Tionne Watkins">Tionne "T-Boz" Watkins</a>, from <a href="/wiki/R%26B" class="mw-redirect" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> trio <a href="/wiki/TLC_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="TLC (band)">TLC</a> during August 2000. In October 2000, their daughter, Chase Anela Rolison, was born.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> They separated in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
