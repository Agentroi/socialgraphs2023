<p><b>Michael Lafayette Troy</b> (born January 15, 1969),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Myka 9</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a> from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is a member of <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_Fellowship" title="Freestyle Fellowship">Freestyle Fellowship</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haiku_d%27Etat" title="Haiku d&#39;Etat">Haiku d'Etat</a> and Magic Heart Genies.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Known for years as <b>Mikah Nine</b>, he changed the spelling to <b>Myka Nyne</b> with the release of <i>A Work in Progress</i> in 2003. Since Magic Heart Genies' album <i>Heartifact</i> in 2008, he has been credited as Myka 9.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myka_9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally known as Microphone Mike in the 1980s, Myka 9 was a member of the MC Aces with <a href="/wiki/Aceyalone" title="Aceyalone">Aceyalone</a> and Spoon Iodine. He ghost-wrote the Rappinstine song "Scream" on <a href="/wiki/N.W.A." class="mw-redirect" title="N.W.A.">N.W.A.</a>'s debut album <i><a href="/wiki/N.W.A._and_the_Posse" title="N.W.A. and the Posse">N.W.A. and the Posse</a></i> in 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_Fellowship" title="Freestyle Fellowship">Freestyle Fellowship</a>, he was instrumental in the scene at the <a href="/wiki/Good_Life_Cafe" title="Good Life Cafe">Good Life Cafe</a> in the early 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Released in 1993, Freestyle Fellowship's second album <i><a href="/wiki/Innercity_Griots" title="Innercity Griots">Innercity Griots</a></i> is one of the landmarks of Los Angeles hip hop. Their videos for "Inner City Boundaries" and "Hot Potato" brought them to the attention of a wider audience. Myka 9 was also featured on two tracks on <i>Project Blowed</i> compilation in 1994. In the late 1990s, Myka 9 came together with Aceyalone and <a href="/wiki/Abstract_Rude" title="Abstract Rude">Abstract Rude</a> to form <a href="/wiki/Haiku_D%27Etat" class="mw-redirect" title="Haiku D&#39;Etat">Haiku D'Etat</a>. In 2008, Myka 9 formed Magic Heart Genies with J the Sarge and DJ Drez.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Myka 9 has collaborated with <a href="/wiki/The_Wailers_Band" title="The Wailers Band">The Wailers Band</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prefuse_73" class="mw-redirect" title="Prefuse 73">Prefuse 73</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daddy_Kev" title="Daddy Kev">Daddy Kev</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busdriver" title="Busdriver">Busdriver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cut_Chemist" title="Cut Chemist">Cut Chemist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, and many others. He has worked extensively with producer and trumpeter Josef Leimberg, who produced the majority of <i>It's All Love: American Nightmare</i> and <i>A Work in Progress</i>. He has released three albums with Canadian producer <a href="/wiki/Factor_Chandelier" title="Factor Chandelier">Factor Chandelier</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2022 it was found that Myka 9 has the largest vocabulary in hip-hop history. Myka employs over 9,000 unique words throughout his catalog.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_and_legacy">Style and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myka_9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Style and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Myka 9 is known for rapid-fire, jazz-influenced, melodic rapping, often incorporating singing and occasionally scatting into his songs. Myka 9 has said of his style: "My rhymes take the direction of a jazz trumpet or sax solo, like <a href="/wiki/Miles_Davis" title="Miles Davis">Miles</a> or <a href="/wiki/John_Coltrane" title="John Coltrane">Trane</a>, if I was to rhyme in the same meter as those notes... that’s my concept."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Myka 9 has had a large influence on Los Angeles <a href="/wiki/Underground_hip_hop" title="Underground hip hop">underground hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle rap</a> in general. Discussing the influence of Myka 9's song "7th Seal" on Freestyle Fellowship's first album <i><a href="/wiki/To_Whom_It_May_Concern..." title="To Whom It May Concern...">To Whom It May Concern...</a></i> (1991), Ellay Khule said, "'7th Seal' blew everybody's mind for at least 2 years straight. People studied that shit backwards and forwards—even we don't know all those words. That made everybody say like 'I gotta get a tape out' or 'I can't rap like so-and-so no more. I can't be in 80s, now we movin' to the 90s.' That totally transferred our musical thought." Drummer and producer JMD said, "Mike was like the <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Parker" title="Charlie Parker">Charlie Parker</a> of all these motherfuckers."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Pigeon_John" title="Pigeon John">Pigeon John</a> said, "For me Mikah 9 was like <a href="/wiki/Miles_Davis" title="Miles Davis">Miles Davis</a>. He was a tall good looking dude, the girls always liked him and this dude just went on stage and there was no effort in him. He was a genius in what he did. He affected and influenced so many people in hip-hop including <a href="/wiki/Mos_Def" title="Mos Def">Mos Def</a>, <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, that whole scene. Years before <a href="/wiki/Leaders_of_the_New_School" title="Leaders of the New School">Leaders of the New School</a>, there are stories where Freestyle Fellowship, when they first came out and they went to New York. That whole shouting stuff, they had this ingenious way to do it and they had a backing jazz band before <a href="/wiki/Digable_Planets" title="Digable Planets">Digable Planets</a>. <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a> ran up to him (Myka 9) afterwards and said 'dude what kind of style is this,' and then lo and behold <a href="/wiki/Leaders_of_the_New_School" title="Leaders of the New School">Leaders of the New School</a> came out. So there's plenty of stories like that, but that's just like a taste of how potent that scene was at the Good Life."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myka_9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Myka 9 is featured in the documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Freestyle:_The_Art_of_Rhyme" title="Freestyle: The Art of Rhyme">Freestyle: The Art of Rhyme</a></i>, <i>Where We're From: The Elements Documentary</i>, and the <i>Hip Hop Evolution</i> Netflix series. He is also featured in the award-winning documentary <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_the_Life_(2008_film)" title="This Is the Life (2008 film)">This Is the Life</a></i>, chronicling the music movement that was birthed at The <a href="/wiki/Good_Life_Cafe" title="Good Life Cafe">Good Life Cafe</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Los_Angeles" title="South Los Angeles">South Central</a>, Los Angeles. The Good Life Cafe is the open-mic workshop where he first performed with Freestyle Fellowship in the early 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Myka_9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2022, Myka 9 released a book called <i>My Kaleidoscope,</i> which features song lyrics, a detailed oral history, and photography by <a href="/wiki/B%2B_(photographer)" title="B+ (photographer)">Brian "B+" Cross</a> and more.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>

</p>
