<p><b>Jonathan Edgar Park</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born February 18, 1986),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> known by his stage name <b>Dumbfoundead</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span><span title="/ʌ/: &#39;u&#39; in &#39;cut&#39;">ʌ</span><span title="&#39;m&#39; in &#39;my&#39;">m</span><span title="/ˌ/: secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="&#39;f&#39; in &#39;find&#39;">f</span><span title="/aʊ/: &#39;ou&#39; in &#39;mouth&#39;">aʊ</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span><span title="/ɪ/: &#39;i&#39; in &#39;kit&#39;">ɪ</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span></span>/</a></span></span><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>), is an Argentinian-born American rapper and actor.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He began his career in the 2000s as a <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">battle rapper</a> in Los Angeles and has since become one of the most prominent Asian American rappers in the United States, known for his witty and socially-conscious lyrics.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dumbfoundead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Park was born in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>, <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> to <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> immigrants. He has one younger sister. When he was three years old, Park's family immigrated to the United States by crossing the <a href="/wiki/Mexico%E2%80%93United_States_border" title="Mexico–United States border">Mexico–United States border</a> without <a href="/wiki/Green_card" title="Green card">green cards</a>. His family settled in the <a href="/wiki/Koreatown,_Los_Angeles" title="Koreatown, Los Angeles">Koreatown</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, California.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Park began rapping when he was 14 years old, inspired in part by the rappers he saw perform weekly at <a href="/wiki/Project_Blowed" title="Project Blowed">Project Blowed</a>, a local open mic workshop.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He dropped out of <a href="/wiki/John_Marshall_High_School_(Los_Angeles)" title="John Marshall High School (Los Angeles)">John Marshall High School</a> in his sophomore year and moved into a one-bedroom apartment with his sister and a roommate at the age of 16. Before becoming a full-time rapper, he worked as a bail bondsman, among other odd jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Park became a U.S. citizen when he was 19 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dumbfoundead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Park's first solo album, <i>DFD</i>, was released in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> His second album, <i>Take the Stares</i>, was released in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, Park released his third album, <i>Old Boy Jon</i>, which was produced entirely by Duke Westlake.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Park was a member of <a href="/wiki/Thirsty_Fish" title="Thirsty Fish">Thirsty Fish</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Open_Mike_Eagle" title="Open Mike Eagle">Open Mike Eagle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Psychosiz" title="Psychosiz">Psychosiz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He has also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Epik_High" title="Epik High">Epik High</a>, <a href="/wiki/Traphik" class="mw-redirect" title="Traphik">Traphik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wax_(rapper)" title="Wax (rapper)">Wax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Park" title="Jay Park">Jay Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kahi_(singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kahi (singer)">Kahi</a>, Jessi, GSoul, MC Jin, Year of the Ox, Rekstizzy, and <a href="/wiki/Anderson_.Paak" title="Anderson .Paak">Anderson .Paak</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-eph_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eph-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, he was featured on the remix of <a href="/wiki/Keith_Ape" title="Keith Ape">Keith Ape</a>'s "It G Ma", alongside <a href="/wiki/Waka_Flocka_Flame" title="Waka Flocka Flame">Waka Flocka Flame</a>, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a>, and Father.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Park began growing a web fanbase after video clips of his rap battles were posted to <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. In 2015, Park announced his return to battle rap, participating in <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a> and <a href="/wiki/OVO_Sound" title="OVO Sound">OVO</a>'s event <i><a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Dot" title="King of the Dot">King of the Dot</a> Blackout 5</i>, with Drake expressing his excitement at Park's return.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Park competed against <a href="/wiki/Wild_%27n_Out" title="Wild &#39;n Out">Wild 'n Out</a> cast member Conceited, and the battle was the most popular English rap battle of 2015<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Reliable citation needed for the most popular claim (May 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has been featured on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> for his viral video Jam Session 2.0, consisting of 8 different musicians from around the world sharing the spotlight individually via split screen but collaborating on one cohesive track.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He has also been featured by <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Call_with_Carson_Daly" title="Last Call with Carson Daly">Last Call with Carson Daly</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> MTV Hive,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Park played a supporting role in <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Kahn_(director)" title="Joseph Kahn (director)">Joseph Kahn</a>'s horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Detention_(2011_film)" title="Detention (2011 film)">Detention</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and would later appear in Kahn's 2017 film <i><a href="/wiki/Bodied" title="Bodied">Bodied</a></i> as battle-rapper Prospek.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, he released the music video "Safe," <sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> which gained widespread attention for superimposing Park's likeness onto the faces of white actors in famous movie scenes. The objective of this was to call attention to the fact that there were no Asian or Asian American actors at the Oscars, and that "the only yellow men were all statues."<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Furthermore, the music video was another call to "the obvious underrepresentation of people of color in Hollywood."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Park was also a starring member of the 2016 documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Bad_Rap_(documentary)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bad Rap (documentary)">Bad Rap</a></i>, which outlined the lives of four Asian-American artists trying to make it in the hip-hop scene. He also played Dylan Shin in the <a href="/wiki/Starz" title="Starz">Starz</a> drama <a href="/wiki/Power_(TV_series)" title="Power (TV series)"><i>Power</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, he co-wrote "Spirit Animal" and "Arrived" alongside <a href="/wiki/Jessi_(musician)" title="Jessi (musician)">Jessi</a> for her debut EP <i><a href="/wiki/Un2verse" title="Un2verse">Un2verse</a></i>.
</p><p>Since 2018, Park has hosted the <i>Fun With Dumb</i> podcast.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In April 2020, <a href="/wiki/Peacock_(streaming_service)" title="Peacock (streaming service)">Peacock</a> began development on a half hour comedy television series based on Park's life entitled <i>Big Dummie.</i><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Park's talk show with <a href="/wiki/Sasha_Grey" title="Sasha Grey">Sasha Grey</a>, <i>Grey Area,</i> debuted on the online <a href="/wiki/Television_network" class="mw-redirect" title="Television network">television network</a> <a href="/wiki/VENN" title="VENN">VENN</a> on August 5, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_the_United_States" title="COVID-19 pandemic in the United States">coronavirus pandemic</a>, Park promoted support for restaurant workers affected by <a href="/wiki/COVID-19" title="COVID-19">COVID-19</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LAT_2020-07-03_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAT_2020-07-03-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
