<p><b>Steady Mobb'n</b> was an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a>  duo composed of <b>Crooked Eye</b> and <b>Billy Bathgate</b>, from the <a href="/wiki/Ghost_Town,_Oakland,_California" title="Ghost Town, Oakland, California">Ghost Town</a> section of <a href="/wiki/West_Oakland" class="mw-redirect" title="West Oakland">West Oakland</a>. The duo were formally signed to <a href="/wiki/No_Limit_Records" title="No Limit Records">No Limit Records</a> &amp; Bomb Shelter Entertainment in the late 1990s.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steady_Mobb%27n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1995, Steady Mobb'n released a cassette EP titled <i>Blood Money</i>. Two years later the duo joined rapper/entrepreneur <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a>'s <a href="/wiki/No_Limit_Records" title="No Limit Records">No Limit Records</a>, and collaborated with Master P on the single "If I Could Change", which was produced by No Limit Records in-house producer DJ Daryl and served as the first single from the <a href="/wiki/I%27m_Bout_It_(soundtrack)" title="I&#39;m Bout It (soundtrack)"><i>I'm Bout It</i> soundtrack</a>. Steady Mobb'n's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Pre-Meditated_Drama" title="Pre-Meditated Drama">Pre-Meditated Drama</a></i>, was released in May 1997 and gave them their biggest chart success, peaking at #29 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_200" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard Hot 200">Billboard Hot 200</a> and #6 on <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a>. However, after the commercial disappointment of their 1998 follow-up, <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Mafia_(album)" title="Black Mafia (album)">Black Mafia</a></i> (#82 on the Billboard 200, #19 on Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums), Steady Mobb'n departed No Limit.
</p><p>In 2001, Steady Mobb'n reunited with No Limit producer DJ Daryl and signed with his label Bomb Shelter Music. Their third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Crime_Buddies" title="Crime Buddies">Crime Buddies</a></i>, was released that year but failed to chart. In 2003, they released their fourth and final record, the non-charting <i><a href="/wiki/Espionage_(album)" title="Espionage (album)">Espionage</a></i>, on the independent Big Body Entertainment. The duo split up thereafter.
</p>
