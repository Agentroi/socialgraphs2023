<p><b>Claudia Alexandra Madriz Meza</b> (born June 24, 1987)<sup id="cite_ref-birthname_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birthname-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><b>,</b> known professionally as <b>Snow Tha Product</b>, is a Mexican-American rapper, singer, actress, YouTuber, and podcaster. She was signed to <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a> from 2012 until November 2018. Since then, she has been an independent artist. Her most well-known projects are her 2013 mixtape <i>Good Nights &amp; Bad Mornings 2: The Hangover</i><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and the 2018 compilation album <i>VIBEHIGHER</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On April 28, 2021, Snow collaborated with Bizarrap on "<a href="/wiki/Snow_Tha_Product:_Bzrp_Music_Sessions,_Vol._39" title="Snow Tha Product: Bzrp Music Sessions, Vol. 39">BZRP Music Sessions #39</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Snow also appeared in several episodes of the USA network hit series, <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_of_the_South_(TV_series)" title="Queen of the South (TV series)">Queen of the South</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snow_Tha_Product&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Claudia Alexandra Madriz Meza was born on June 24, 1987, in <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose, California</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-AbqJArticle_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AbqJArticle-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> to Mexican parents. Her father was an undocumented immigrant from <a href="/wiki/Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Michoacán">Michoacán</a><sup id="cite_ref-Weekly_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weekly-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and her mother, who is from <a href="/wiki/Zacatecas" title="Zacatecas">Zacatecas</a>, immigrated legally to the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> She has a younger brother named Miguel Ángel, whom she endearingly calls "Ito".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During her younger years, Madriz was exposed to <a href="/wiki/Mariachi" title="Mariachi">mariachi</a> music on her father's side of the family as both her father and grandfather were mariachi singers.<sup id="cite_ref-Weekly_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weekly-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> At the age of 6, she appeared in school talent shows and began performing with her grandfather's mariachi band in <a href="/wiki/Redwood_City,_California" title="Redwood City, California">Redwood City</a>. She got her first exposure to <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> when she moved to San Diego and gradually became more involved with her own music. Claudia attended <a href="/wiki/Mira_Mesa_Senior_High_School" title="Mira Mesa Senior High School">Mira Mesa Senior High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> As a teenager, she started <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyling</a> with her friends.<sup id="cite_ref-Latina_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Latina-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snow_Tha_Product&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2007.E2.80.932010:_Beginnings_and_early_success"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007–2010:_Beginnings_and_early_success">2007–2010: Beginnings and early success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snow_Tha_Product&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007–2010: Beginnings and early success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After graduating high school, Madriz briefly attended <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Mesa_College" title="San Diego Mesa College">San Diego Mesa College</a> to begin studying to become a social worker. At the age of 19, she began taking music seriously and dropped out of her first semester of college to pursue a career as a rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-dfw_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dfw-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> She adopted her name from <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a>'s character <a href="/wiki/Snow_White_(Disney_character)" title="Snow White (Disney character)">Snow White</a>. Initially she used the name "Snow White", and then added "the Product". Then she changed it into her new moniker as "Snow White Tha Product",<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dfw_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dfw-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dope_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dope-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and ultimately "Snow Tha Product". After recording a number of songs in Spanish, she caught the attention of Mexican artist Jaime Kohen. They recorded a collaboration song, titled "Alguien", which became popular in Mexico and appeared on Kohen's album <i>Fotosíntesis</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-dfw_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dfw-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, Tha Product released her first compilation album <i>Verbal Assault Vol.1</i> with many local artists under her label Product Entertainment LLC. It was followed in 2008 with her first solo mixtape <i>Raising Tha Bar: Tha Mixtape</i>, which included many remixes of popular songs of the era. In 2009, <i>Verbal Assault Vol. 2</i> was released, with the single "Whatever It Takes". In 2010, Tha Product released <i>Wake Ya Game Up, Vol. 1</i>, along with the Wake Ya Game Up and Woke clothing brands and apparel.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010-2011:_Unorthodox_and_breakthrough">2010-2011: <i>Unorthodox</i> and breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snow_Tha_Product&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010-2011: Unorthodox and breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, Tha Product released the mixtape <i>Run Up or Shut Up</i>, which was hosted by DJ Ames from the UK and included the hit single "Drunk Love". On February 16, 2011, the official music video was featured in the popular Latino music television channel Mun2.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Tha Product then moved her and her family to <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and performed for two consecutive years at the <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> festival.<sup id="cite_ref-dfw_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dfw-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 26, 2011, Tha Product released her debut independent album on Street Science Ent. <i><a href="/wiki/Unorthodox_(Snow_Tha_Product_album)" title="Unorthodox (Snow Tha Product album)">Unorthodox</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-itunes_unorthodox_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itunes_unorthodox-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> "Drunk Love" was re-released as a single along with "Woke Wednesday".<sup id="cite_ref-drjays_bio_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-drjays_bio-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Her real breakthrough came with the song "Holy Shit". The 97-second-long song received acclaim and was featured on <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>'s site ThisIs50.com.<sup id="cite_ref-dfw_15-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dfw-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The success of her songs caught the attention of three major record labels, which got in contact with her – <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dfw_15-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dfw-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  Adam Bernard of RapReviews.com, impressed by her work, went on to write "[Snow] may very well be one of the leaders of ... a bold new era of female emcees."<sup id="cite_ref-rapreviews_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rapreviews-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932014:_Signing_with_Atlantic_Records_and_Good_Nights_.26_Bad_Mornings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2014:_Signing_with_Atlantic_Records_and_Good_Nights_&amp;_Bad_Mornings">2012–2014: Signing with Atlantic Records and <i>Good Nights &amp; Bad Mornings</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snow_Tha_Product&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–2014: Signing with Atlantic Records and Good Nights &amp; Bad Mornings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, Snow signed to <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a> following her independent success with Unorthodox. She appeared on songs by major artists, such as <a href="/wiki/DJ_Paul" title="DJ Paul">DJ Paul</a><sup id="cite_ref-2db_raw_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2db_raw-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Krizz_Kaliko" title="Krizz Kaliko">Krizz Kaliko</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2db_damage_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2db_damage-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In November, she performed at the <a href="/wiki/ABN_(rap_duo)" class="mw-redirect" title="ABN (rap duo)">ABN</a> Reunion concert in Houston, Texas, among rappers <a href="/wiki/Trae" title="Trae">Trae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Z-Ro" title="Z-Ro">Z-Ro</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-songkick_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-songkick-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On December 12, 2012, Snow released her anticipated mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Nights_%26_Bad_Mornings" class="mw-redirect" title="Good Nights &amp; Bad Mornings">Good Nights &amp; Bad Mornings</a></i>, which spawned 3 singles: "Cookie Cutter Bitches", "Damn It" and "Lord Be with You".<sup id="cite_ref-djbooth_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-djbooth-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The sequel to <i>Good Nights &amp; Bad Mornings</i> was released on October 14, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The tape, called <i>Good Nights &amp; Bad Mornings 2: The Hangover</i> has guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Cataracs" title="The Cataracs">The Cataracs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trae_tha_Truth" title="Trae tha Truth">Trae tha Truth</a>, <a href="/wiki/CyHi_the_Prynce" class="mw-redirect" title="CyHi the Prynce">CyHi the Prynce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dizzy_Wright" title="Dizzy Wright">Dizzy Wright</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hhdx2_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hhdx2-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On May 16, 2013, Snow released the first single "Cali Luv", produced by The Cataracs, which samples <a href="/wiki/2Pac" class="mw-redirect" title="2Pac">2Pac</a>'s hit <a href="/wiki/California_Love" title="California Love">California Love</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> She was featured on Dutch DJ <a href="/wiki/Chuckie_(DJ)" title="Chuckie (DJ)">Chuckie</a>'s single "Makin Papers" along <a href="/wiki/Lupe_Fiasco" title="Lupe Fiasco">Lupe Fiasco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too Short</a>. The video for the song premiered on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> on August 8, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-mp-mtv_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mp-mtv-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, Snow went on the Fight to Unite Tour with <a href="/wiki/Kottonmouth_Kings" title="Kottonmouth Kings">Kottonmouth Kings</a> and Dizzy Wright<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> and performed at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> (SXSW) and <a href="/wiki/Rock_the_Bells" title="Rock the Bells">Rock the Bells</a> festivals.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In February 2014, Snow went on a F#*K YOUR PLANS (COME KICK IT) tour. On March 19, 2014, Snow was featured on the song "Not for the Weak Minded" by <a href="/wiki/Crooked_I" title="Crooked I">Kxng Crooked</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.932017:_The_Rest_Comes_Later.2C_Half_Way_There..._Pt._1_and_winning_an_MTV_Video_Music_Award"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–2017:_The_Rest_Comes_Later,_Half_Way_There..._Pt._1_and_winning_an_MTV_Video_Music_Award">2015–2017: <i>The Rest Comes Later,</i> <i>Half Way There... Pt. 1</i> and winning an MTV Video Music Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snow_Tha_Product&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2015–2017: The Rest Comes Later, Half Way There... Pt. 1 and winning an MTV Video Music Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 30, 2015, Snow released the mixtape, <i>The Rest Comes Later</i>. Followed by a tour of the same name with <a href="/wiki/Audio_Push" title="Audio Push">Audio Push</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> On February 17, 2016, she was featured on the remix to <a href="/wiki/Joell_Ortiz" title="Joell Ortiz">Joell Ortiz</a>'s single "Kill at Will".<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> On May 2, 2016, she announced the Halfway There tour.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On June 17, 2016, she released her EP <i><a href="/wiki/Half_Way_There..._Pt._1" title="Half Way There... Pt. 1">Half Way There... Pt. 1</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2016, Snow contributed to <a href="/wiki/The_Hamilton_Mixtape" title="The Hamilton Mixtape">The Hamilton Mixtape</a>, joining rappers <a href="/wiki/K%27naan" title="K&#39;naan">K'naan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riz_MC" class="mw-redirect" title="Riz MC">Riz MC</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Residente" title="Residente">Residente</a> on the track "<a href="/wiki/Immigrants_(We_Get_the_Job_Done)" class="mw-redirect" title="Immigrants (We Get the Job Done)">Immigrants (We Get the Job Done)</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The music video for the song was released on June 28, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> In August 2017, the song has won Snow and her collaborators an <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="MTV Video Music Award">MTV Video Music Award</a> in the category of Best Fight Against the System.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2017 she released the single "Nuestra Cancion Pt. 2", which featured fellow American singer <a href="/wiki/Arc%C3%A1ngel_(singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Arcángel (singer)">Arcángel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2017.E2.80.932019:_VIBEHIGHER_and_going_independent"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–2019:_VIBEHIGHER_and_going_independent">2017–2019: <i>VIBEHIGHER</i> and going independent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snow_Tha_Product&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2017–2019: VIBEHIGHER and going independent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 4, 2017, Snow released the unfinished mixtape of <i>VIBEHIGHER</i> with artists Castro Escobar, Lex the Great, Jandro, and AJ Hernz on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. She followed this with a tour of the same name in 2017 and 2018.
</p><p>In 2017, Tha Product featured as Lil' Traviesa, also known as Lil' T, on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_of_the_South_(TV_series)" title="Queen of the South (TV series)">Queen of the South</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2018, Snow left Atlantic Records and went independent. She announced that she was releasing the full <i>VIBEHIGHER</i> mixtape on December 22, 2018, since it was finished and ready to be put out under her independent label Product Entertainment LLC.
</p><p>On May 1, 2019, Snow went on the 30-day Going Off tour.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On September 12, she went on tour again which she named Going Off: Part 2.
</p>
<h3><span id="2020.E2.80.93present:_First_nomination_for_Latin_GRAMMY_Awards.2C_To_Anywhere_and_Dale_Gas_tour"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020–present:_First_nomination_for_Latin_GRAMMY_Awards,_To_Anywhere_and_Dale_Gas_tour">2020–present: First nomination for Latin GRAMMY Awards, <i>To Anywhere</i> and Dale Gas tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snow_Tha_Product&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2020–present: First nomination for Latin GRAMMY Awards, To Anywhere and Dale Gas tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2021, Snow received her first nomination for <a href="/wiki/Latin_GRAMMY_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin GRAMMY Awards">Latin GRAMMY Awards</a> in the category of Best Rap/Hip-Hop Song for her collaboration song with Argentine producer Bizarrap "<a href="/wiki/Snow_Tha_Product:_Bzrp_Music_Sessions,_Vol._39" title="Snow Tha Product: Bzrp Music Sessions, Vol. 39">BZRP Music Sessions #39</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> She announced that her album VALEMADRE would be released in mid-2022.
</p><p>On February 11, 2022, Snow announced that she was going on a national tour throughout April and May 2022 which she named the Dale Gas tour.
</p><p>On September 9, 2022, Tha Product released "Piña" with Cuban-American singer <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Jauregui" title="Lauren Jauregui">Lauren Jauregui</a>, with the videoclip releasing on December 9. On October 21, 2022, Snow tha Product released the studio album <i>To Anywhere</i> featuring collaborations with vf7, AJ Hernz, Santa Fe Klan, <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a>, and Rotimi. <i>To Anywhere</i> is her first solo project in five years and her first studio album in a decade.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_influences">Musical style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snow_Tha_Product&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Musical style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Snow is known for her <a href="/wiki/Chopper_(rap)" title="Chopper (rap)">rapid fire style</a> of rapping.<sup id="cite_ref-dope_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dope-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> She stated she admires and supports artists who have their own original style. She is influenced by artists such as <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Da_Brat" title="Da Brat">Da Brat</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Latina_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Latina-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Big_Pun" title="Big Pun">Big Pun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aaliyah" title="Aaliyah">Aaliyah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amy_Winehouse" title="Amy Winehouse">Amy Winehouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_3000" title="André 3000">André 3000</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Cash" title="Johnny Cash">Johnny Cash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">2Pac</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-rapreviews_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rapreviews-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mac_Dre" title="Mac Dre">Mac Dre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brotha_Lynch_Hung" title="Brotha Lynch Hung">Brotha Lynch Hung</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> as well as Mexican actress <a href="/wiki/Mar%C3%ADa_F%C3%A9lix" title="María Félix">María Félix</a> and singers <a href="/wiki/Selena_Quintanilla" class="mw-redirect" title="Selena Quintanilla">Selena Quintanilla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Trevi" title="Gloria Trevi">Gloria Trevi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lupita_D%27Alessio" title="Lupita D&#39;Alessio">Lupita D'Alessio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hhdx2_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hhdx2-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Snow is bilingual, speaking fluent English and Spanish, and records in both languages.<sup id="cite_ref-drjays_bio_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-drjays_bio-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snow_Tha_Product&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a June 2018 interview, Snow revealed that she got married when she was 19 which lasted for a decade from 2006 until 2016. From her marriage, she has a child by the name of Andrew Feliciano Jr. (born April 2010) with her former husband, Andrew Feliciano.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> During an interview with Power 106 Los Angeles radio show in July 2018 she spoke about her experience raising her son and said her son was eight years old during the time of the interview.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_Tha_Product_On_Raising_An_8-Year-Old_&amp;_Representing_The_Mexican_Community_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow_Tha_Product_On_Raising_An_8-Year-Old_&amp;_Representing_The_Mexican_Community-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Snow started dating Julisa Aponte, also known as (Daddie) JuJu. On July 10, 2019, they announced their engagement when JuJu proposed to her.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> They have a <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel called "everydaydays" and a podcast called "everynightnights".<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> The YouTube channel was created on January 23, 2017, but was not officially used until November 28, 2018, for its first video.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Snow announced they ended their relationship in late March 2022, after a few difficult months together.
</p><p>In 2020, during the COVID-19 pandemic, Snow bought a ranch in California, wanting to embrace her Mexican roots of living on a ranch and centralizing all of her operations and businesses. She moved in September 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Snow has always been very open that she has <a href="/wiki/ADHD" class="mw-redirect" title="ADHD">ADHD</a>, and says that having ADHD is something that motivates her to put her energy in different projects.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
