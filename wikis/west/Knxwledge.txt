<p><b>Glen Earl Boothe</b><sup id="cite_ref-fullname1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fullname1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born March 8, 1988), professionally known as <b>Knxwledge</b> (pronounced "knowledge"), is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> record producer and songwriter based in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Since 2009, he has had over 100 releases via <a href="/wiki/Bandcamp" title="Bandcamp">Bandcamp</a>. His songs are often a mix of various genres, such as <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Old-school_hip_hop" title="Old-school hip hop">old-school hip hop</a>, and modern rap.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knxwledge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2010, Knxwledge released his debut album <i>Klouds</i> via All City. In 2013, he released a 53-track retrospective compilation titled <i>Anthology</i> on <a href="/wiki/Compact_Cassette" class="mw-redirect" title="Compact Cassette">cassette</a>, which was released by <a href="/wiki/Matthewdavid" title="Matthewdavid">Leaving Records</a>. 
</p><p>In 2015, Knxwledge released his debut album for <a href="/wiki/Stones_Throw_Records" title="Stones Throw Records">Stones Throw Records</a>, <i>Hud Dreems</i>, and produced the track "Momma" on <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>'s release <i><a href="/wiki/To_Pimp_a_Butterfly" title="To Pimp a Butterfly">To Pimp a Butterfly</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> which went on to win Best Rap Album at the <a href="/wiki/58th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="58th Annual Grammy Awards">58th annual Grammy Awards</a> in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> His work alongside <a href="/wiki/Anderson_.Paak" title="Anderson .Paak">Anderson .Paak</a> as the duo <a href="/wiki/NxWorries" title="NxWorries">NxWorries</a> has received critical acclaim. In March 2020 he released <i>1988</i>, his second album for Stones Throw.
</p><p>Knxwledge gained popularity by making his beats line up with freestyles from famous rappers like <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>. Meek Mill shortly thereafter threatened DMCA strikes against Knxwledge for using his personal content, causing Knxwledge to delete every Meek Mill mash-up.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
