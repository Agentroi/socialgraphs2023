<p><b>La'Reonte Wright</b> (born November 26, 1990), known professionally as <b>Dizzy Wright</b>, is an American rapper and record producer.
</p><p>In December 2011, Wright signed to rapper <a href="/wiki/Hopsin" title="Hopsin">Hopsin</a>'s independent record label, <a href="/wiki/Funk_Volume" title="Funk Volume">Funk Volume</a>. After signing, he released his debut studio album <i>SmokeOut Conversations</i> in April 2012. He followed that with <i>The First Agreement</i> in December of that year, and then a mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/The_Golden_Age_(Dizzy_Wright_album)" title="The Golden Age (Dizzy Wright album)">The Golden Age</a></i> in August 2013. Following touring with Hopsin in early 2014, Dizzy Wright released <i><a href="/wiki/State_of_Mind_(EP)" title="State of Mind (EP)">State of Mind</a></i>, an EP that would peak at number 54 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. On May 22, 2015, he released his second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Growing_Process_(Dizzy_Wright_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Growing Process (Dizzy Wright album)">The Growing Process</a></i> which peaked at number  47 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dizzy_Wright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wright looked up to <a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs-n-Harmony" title="Bone Thugs-n-Harmony">Bone Thugs-n-Harmony</a>, of which his uncles <a href="/wiki/Layzie_Bone" title="Layzie Bone">Layzie Bone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flesh-N-Bone" class="mw-redirect" title="Flesh-N-Bone">Flesh-N-Bone</a> are members.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At the age of four he moved with his mom and siblings to <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He began rapping at just 8 years old with the group DaFuture which included his brother and a very close friend. His mother wrote his raps at the time and was a concert promoter so he was exposed to the music industry early even doing youth reporting at major awards shows like the <a href="/wiki/BET_Awards" title="BET Awards">BET Awards</a> and interviewing <a href="/wiki/Tyrese_Gibson" title="Tyrese Gibson">Tyrese</a>, <a href="/wiki/St._Lunatics" title="St. Lunatics">St. Lunatics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boyz_II_Men" title="Boyz II Men">Boyz II Men</a>. He credits this for being the reason he wants to stay away from <a href="/wiki/Major_record_label" class="mw-redirect" title="Major record label">major record labels</a> and stick to independent labels.<sup id="cite_ref-HHDX_interview_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHDX_interview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In seventh grade Wright lived in a homeless shelter with his family for five months.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Throughout the rest of high school he lived in <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> and <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> before moving back to Las Vegas when he was in twelfth grade.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, Wright took part in and won the Sheikh Music Rip the Mic competition, which impressed record label <a href="/wiki/Funk_Volume" title="Funk Volume">Funk Volume</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FunkVolumeBio_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FunkVolumeBio-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, he also appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/106_%26_Park" title="106 &amp; Park">106 &amp; Park</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;s</span> Wild Out Wednesday, as Dizzy D Flashy.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dizzy_Wright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932012:_Signing_to_Funk_Volume_and_Smokeout_Conversations"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2012:_Signing_to_Funk_Volume_and_Smokeout_Conversations">2010–2012: Signing to Funk Volume and <i>Smokeout Conversations</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dizzy_Wright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010–2012: Signing to Funk Volume and Smokeout Conversations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, Dizzy Wright released his mixtape, titled <i>Soul Searchin' Next Level</i>, which would be a final release under Bluestar Records.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, Wright signed to rapper <a href="/wiki/Hopsin" title="Hopsin">Hopsin</a>'s independent record label <a href="/wiki/Funk_Volume" title="Funk Volume">Funk Volume</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Wright's debut studio album, <i>Smokeout Conversations</i>, was released on <a href="/wiki/420_(cannabis_culture)" title="420 (cannabis culture)">April 20</a>, 2012, in honor of the annual <a href="/wiki/Marijuana" class="mw-redirect" title="Marijuana">marijuana</a> type-of "holiday" under Funk Volume.<sup id="cite_ref-SOC_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SOC-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The album included the single "Can't Trust Em", and peaked at number 42 on the US <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> and number eight on the US <a href="/wiki/Heatseekers_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Heatseekers Albums">Heatseekers Albums</a> charts.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;BRap_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;BRap-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Heatseeker_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heatseeker-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Months later, Wright released a reloaded version of a mixtape, titled <i>Free SmokeOut Conversations</i>, which has been downloaded over 170,000 times.<sup id="cite_ref-FunkVolumeBio_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FunkVolumeBio-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This mixtape features guest appearances from a fellow rapper and his label-mate Hopsin, and <a href="/wiki/SwizZz" title="SwizZz">SwizZz</a>. It also contains his single "Independent Living". Not withstanding the original plan to release the mixtape prior to the album, but then instead Dizzy released the album.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The album's success allowed him to perform on a national 30-day headlining tour following its release.<sup id="cite_ref-FunkVolumeBio_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FunkVolumeBio-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He capped off the year performing on the Funk Volume Tour 2012 with Hopsin, SwizZz and <a href="/wiki/Jarren_Benton" title="Jarren Benton">Jarren Benton</a>. On December 3, 2012, Dizzy released his debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a>, titled <i>The First Agreement</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-FunkVolumeBio_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FunkVolumeBio-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The EP was named after <a href="/wiki/Don_Miguel_Ruiz" title="Don Miguel Ruiz">Don Miguel Ruiz</a>'s book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Four_Agreements" title="The Four Agreements">The Four Agreements</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-HHDX_interview_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHDX_interview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Its lead single, "Fly High", was released.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The EP debuted at number 41 on the Top R&amp;B/Hip Hop Albums chart and number 25 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Rap_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Rap Albums">Top Rap Albums</a> charts.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;BRap_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;BRap-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RapAlbums_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RapAlbums-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Wright had also hinted at the releases of more chapters in the series.<sup id="cite_ref-HHDX_interview_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHDX_interview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932014:_The_Golden_Age_and_State_of_Mind"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2014:_The_Golden_Age_and_State_of_Mind">2013–2014: <i>The Golden Age</i> and <i>State of Mind</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dizzy_Wright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013–2014: The Golden Age and State of Mind">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/The_Golden_Age_(Dizzy_Wright_album)" title="The Golden Age (Dizzy Wright album)">The Golden Age (Dizzy Wright album)</a> and <a href="/wiki/State_of_Mind_(EP)" title="State of Mind (EP)">State of Mind (EP)</a></div>
<p>On March 11, 2013, Wright released the first song from his upcoming mixtape, titled "Maintain" featuring fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Bada$$</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On March 26, it was announced that Wright would be included in <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL's</a></i> <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)#XXL_Annual_Freshman_List" title="XXL (magazine)">Freshman Class</a> of 2013 due to getting the "Peoples Choice" vote.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Three days later he would release the music video for a new song titled, "Still Movin'" which featured cameos from his Funk Volume label mates which was followed by "Killem Wit Kindness" a few days later.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> These songs were the second and third releases from his upcoming mixtape, which was shortly announced to be titled, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Golden_Age_(Dizzy_Wright_album)" title="The Golden Age (Dizzy Wright album)">The Golden Age</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Guest appearances on the mixtape included <a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a>, Hopsin, Joey Bada$$, Jarren Benton, SwizZz, <a href="/wiki/Logic_(rapper)" title="Logic (rapper)">Logic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kid_Ink" title="Kid Ink">Kid Ink</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honey_Cocaine" title="Honey Cocaine">Honey Cocaine</a> among others. Along with production from <a href="/wiki/DJ_Hoppa" title="DJ Hoppa">DJ Hoppa</a>, Rikio, Kato, <a href="/wiki/6ix_(record_producer)" title="6ix (record producer)">6ix</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cardo_(record_producer)" title="Cardo (record producer)">Cardo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape was released for free, and for purchase on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> on August 19, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Due to its retail release, the mixtape debuted on the Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums chart at number 39 becoming his highest charting project at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;BRap_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;BRap-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wright announced plans to drop <i>The Second Agreement EP</i>, and then his second studio album after the release of the mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On December 18, 2013, he was named a runner-up for "Rising Star of the Year" by <a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Starting 2014, Wright begun touring with Hopsin on the Knock Madness world tour.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> He was also featured on <i><a href="/wiki/Knock_Madness" title="Knock Madness">Knock Madness</a></i>, on the song "Who's There?" also featuring label mate Jarren Benton.<sup id="cite_ref-KM_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KM-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On February 16, 2014, Dizzy Wright announced he would be releasing <i><a href="/wiki/State_of_Mind_(EP)" title="State of Mind (EP)">State of Mind</a></i>, a new EP during March 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with <i>XXL</i> the following month, Dizzy Wright said <i>State of Mind</i> had no current release date and confirmed his second studio album would be released during 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> However, on April 8, Wright revealed the cover artwork and release date of April 15, 2014 for the EP.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> <i>State of Mind</i> debuted at number 54 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-BB200_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB200-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2014, Dizzy Wright revealed that his second studio album would be released in early 2015, with a guest appearance to come from <a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs-n-Harmony" title="Bone Thugs-n-Harmony">Bone Thugs-n-Harmony</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with Mr. Wavvy released on June 24, 2015, Wright confirmed that a project with <a href="/wiki/Logic_(rapper)" title="Logic (rapper)">Logic</a> is in the works. The rapper went on to confirm that the two have 4-5 songs already recorded for it.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.932017:_The_Growing_Process_and_multiple_projects"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–2017:_The_Growing_Process_and_multiple_projects">2015–2017: <i>The Growing Process</i> and multiple projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dizzy_Wright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015–2017: The Growing Process and multiple projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Growing_Process_(Dizzy_Wright_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Growing Process (Dizzy Wright album)">The Growing Process (Dizzy Wright album)</a></div>
<p>In February 2015, Wright released a collaborative mixtape with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Battles" title="Mark Battles">Mark Battles</a> titled, <i>Lost In Reality</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-LIR_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LIR-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On May 26, 2015, Wright released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Growing_Process_(Dizzy_Wright_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Growing Process (Dizzy Wright album)">The Growing Process</a></i>, which was released through Funk Volume and distributed by <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The album went on and peaked at number 47 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 chart.<sup id="cite_ref-BB200_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB200-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2016, Funk Volume disbanded.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> On February 5, 2016, Wright released his first project post-Funk Volume titled, <i>Wisdom and Good Vibes</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-WAGV_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WAGV-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On July 2, Wright released <i>The 702 EP</i> and was supported by two singles "East Side" and "What's In My Pot".<sup id="cite_ref-702ep_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-702ep-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> A few days after the release of the EP, Wright released a new single "They Know Why" commenting on controversial political issues.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> In November 2016, Wright released a collaborative mixtape with <a href="/wiki/Demrick" title="Demrick">Demrick</a> titled, <i>Blaze With Us</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-BWU_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWU-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 11, 2017, Wright released his third studio album <i>The Golden Age 2</i> named after his 2013 mixtape <i>The Golden Age</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-GA2_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GA2-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On November 24, Wright released his fourth studio album, <i>State of Mind 2</i>, named after his 2014 extended play, <i>State of Mind</i>, with features from ILL Camille, <a href="/wiki/Audio_Push" title="Audio Push">Audio Push</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demrick" title="Demrick">Demrick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Larry_June" title="Larry June">Larry June</a>, Reezy, <a href="/wiki/Jon_Connor" title="Jon Connor">Jon Connor</a>, A.D, and Chelle.<sup id="cite_ref-SOM2_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SOM2-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2018.E2.80.93present:_One_Week_Notice.2C_Don.27t_Tell_Me_It_Can.27t_Be_Done_and_Nobody_Cares.2C_Work_Harder_and_My_Hustle_Unmatched"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–present:_One_Week_Notice,_Don't_Tell_Me_It_Can't_Be_Done_and_Nobody_Cares,_Work_Harder_and_My_Hustle_Unmatched">2018–present: <i>One Week Notice</i>, <i>Don't Tell Me It Can't Be Done</i> and <i>Nobody Cares, Work Harder</i> and <i>My Hustle Unmatched</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dizzy_Wright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2018–present: One Week Notice, Don&#039;t Tell Me It Can&#039;t Be Done and Nobody Cares, Work Harder and My Hustle Unmatched">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 5, 2018, Still Movin record label owner Damian Ritter put together a collaboration project with Wright, Jarren Benton, <a href="/wiki/Audio_Push" title="Audio Push">Audio Push</a>, Demrick, <a href="/wiki/Emilio_Rojas" title="Emilio Rojas">Emilio Rojas</a>, Reezy, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Hoppa" title="DJ Hoppa">DJ Hoppa</a>, and Kato titled, <i>One Week Notice</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-OWN_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OWN-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> On June 1, Wright released the extended play, <i>Don't Tell Me It Can't Be Done</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-DTMICBD_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTMICBD-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> It included two singles: "Hit Em with the Pose" featuring Jazz Lazr, and "Vibe" featuring <a href="/wiki/Kid_Ink" title="Kid Ink">Kid Ink</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HEWTP_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HEWTP-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Vibe_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 15, 2019, Wright released his fifth studio album titled, <i>Nobody Cares, Work Harder</i>, which was distributed by <a href="/wiki/Empire_Distribution" title="Empire Distribution">Empire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NCWH_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NCWH-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> It included two singles: "Picture Perfect" featuring <a href="/wiki/Eric_Bellinger" title="Eric Bellinger">Eric Bellinger</a>, and "Champagne Service".<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Following the release of the album, Wright went on and released three singles, "Keep Up", "Heavy Handed" and "The Ride".<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 18, 2020, Wright released his sixth studio album, <i>My Hustle Unmatched</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
