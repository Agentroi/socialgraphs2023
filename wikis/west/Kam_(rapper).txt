<p><b>Craig A. Miller</b> (born October 24, 1969), better known by his stage name <b>Kam</b>, is an American rapper known primarily among <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> fans and music critics during the 1990s and early 2000s.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kam_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is known for his <a href="/wiki/Nation_of_Islam" title="Nation of Islam">Nation of Islam</a>–influenced lyrics and NOI membership, and affiliation with his cousin, fellow West Coast emcee <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>. He is also known for his powerfully commanding <a href="/wiki/Baritone" title="Baritone">baritone</a> voice and knack for using multisyllabic rhymes.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He has only released two albums since his well-received 1993 debut album <i>Neva Again</i>. That album featured inflammatory anti-government lyrics and hit single "Peace Treaty," a song about the <a href="/wiki/Watts,_Los_Angeles#History" title="Watts, Los Angeles">1992 Watts gang treaty</a>. The song reached #2 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Singles" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Rap Singles">Hot Rap Singles</a> chart in 1993.
</p><p>His first appearance on record was with the song "Every Single Weekend" from the 1991 <i><a href="/wiki/Boyz_n_the_Hood" title="Boyz n the Hood">Boyz n the Hood</a></i> soundtrack. Since then, he has collaborated with artists including <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coolio" title="Coolio">Coolio</a>, <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erick_Sermon" title="Erick Sermon">Erick Sermon</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Ren" title="MC Ren">MC Ren</a>, <a href="/wiki/C-Bo" title="C-Bo">C-Bo</a>, K9Compton, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Pooh" title="DJ Pooh">DJ Pooh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tha_Eastsidaz" title="Tha Eastsidaz">Tha Eastsidaz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>, <a href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Enemy (band)">Public Enemy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warren_G" title="Warren G">Warren G</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris_(rapper)" title="Paris (rapper)">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tha_Dogg_Pound" title="Tha Dogg Pound">Tha Dogg Pound</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyrese_Gibson" title="Tyrese Gibson">Tyrese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tank_(American_singer)" title="Tank (American singer)">Tank</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Clinton_(funk_musician)" title="George Clinton (funk musician)">George Clinton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dresta" title="Dresta">Dresta</a>, B.G. Knocc Out, <a href="/wiki/Yukmouth" title="Yukmouth">Yukmouth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afroman" title="Afroman">Afroman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Glasses_Malone" title="Glasses Malone">Glasses Malone</a> among many others.
He has released music on two record labels, <a href="/wiki/East_West_Records" title="East West Records">East West Records</a> and JCOR Entertainment. He is currently signed to his own record label, HEREAFTER RECORDS, and plans to release his fourth solo album.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is also currently a member of Snoop Dogg's West Coast supergroup <a href="/wiki/The_Warzone_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Warzone (group)">The Warzone</a> with <a href="/wiki/MC_Eiht" title="MC Eiht">MC Eiht</a> and <a href="/wiki/Goldie_Loc" class="mw-redirect" title="Goldie Loc">Goldie Loc</a>.
Kam and his biological younger brother, "Yung Bruh", are also a NOI-based rap group called, <i>Fruit Pruno</i>.
Their self-titled album was released during the NOI's Saviour's Day 2010 summit (specifically on February 28, 2010).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
