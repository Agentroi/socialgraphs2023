<p><b>Benjamin Hammond Haggerty</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born June 19, 1983), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Macklemore</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;m&#39; in &#39;my&#39;">m</span><span title="/æ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;bad&#39;">æ</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="/ə/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;about&#39;">ə</span><span title="/./: syllable break">.</span><span title="&#39;m&#39; in &#39;my&#39;">m</span><span title="/ɔːr/: &#39;ar&#39; in &#39;war&#39;">ɔːr</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">MAK</span>-lə-mor</i></a>;<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> formerly <b>Professor Macklemore</b>), is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. A native of <a href="/wiki/Seattle,_Washington" class="mw-redirect" title="Seattle, Washington">Seattle, Washington</a>, he started his career in 2000 as an independent artist and released three works: <i>Open Your Eyes</i> (2000), <i>The Language of My World</i> (2005) and <i>The Unplanned Mixtape</i> (2009). He rose to international success when he collaborated with producer <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Lewis" title="Ryan Lewis">Ryan Lewis</a> as the duo <a href="/wiki/Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis" title="Macklemore &amp; Ryan Lewis">Macklemore &amp; Ryan Lewis</a> (2009–2016).
</p><p>Macklemore's and Lewis's single "<a href="/wiki/Thrift_Shop" title="Thrift Shop">Thrift Shop</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Wanz" title="Wanz">Wanz</a>) reached number one on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The single was dubbed by <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> as the first song since 1994 to top the Hot 100 chart without the support of a major record label; however Macklemore, in a slightly unusual <a href="/wiki/Recording_contract" title="Recording contract">recording contract</a>, pays a nominal percentage of sales to use <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>'s radio promotion department to push his singles.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Their second single, "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Hold_Us" title="Can&#39;t Hold Us">Can't Hold Us</a>", also peaked at number one on the Hot 100 chart, making Macklemore and Lewis the first duo in the chart's history to have their first two singles both reach the peak position.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Their debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Heist_(album)" title="The Heist (album)">The Heist</a></i> was released on 9 October 2012, and charted at number 2 on the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Billboard_200" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Billboard 200">U.S. <i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. The duo won four <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a> at the <a href="/wiki/56th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="56th Annual Grammy Awards">2014 ceremony</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Best_New_Artist" class="mw-redirect" title="Best New Artist">Best New Artist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Best Rap Album</a> (<i>The Heist</i>), <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Song" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Song">Best Rap Song</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance">Best Rap Performance</a> ("Thrift Shop"). Macklemore's and Lewis's second album, <i><a href="/wiki/This_Unruly_Mess_I%27ve_Made" title="This Unruly Mess I&#39;ve Made">This Unruly Mess I've Made</a></i>, was released on February 26, 2016.
</p><p>In June 2017, Macklemore released "<a href="/wiki/Glorious_(Macklemore_song)" title="Glorious (Macklemore song)">Glorious</a>", a single featuring <a href="/wiki/Skylar_Grey" title="Skylar Grey">Skylar Grey</a>, which marked his return to the music industry as well as his first major single produced without Lewis since his mainstream breakthrough. Macklemore released a second solo studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Gemini_(Macklemore_album)" title="Gemini (Macklemore album)">Gemini</a></i>, in September that year. On February 24, 2023, he released a follow-up album entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Ben_(Macklemore_album)" title="Ben (Macklemore album)">Ben</a></i>. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_influences">Early life and influences</span></h2>
<p>Ben Haggerty was born in <a href="/wiki/Seattle,_Washington" class="mw-redirect" title="Seattle, Washington">Seattle, Washington</a>, on June 19, 1983, one of two sons born to Bill Haggerty and Julie Schott. He was raised with his brother Tim in Seattle's <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Hill_(Seattle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capitol Hill (Seattle)">Capitol Hill</a> neighborhood.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-buzzfeed-news-2017_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buzzfeed-news-2017-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He has <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> heritage and was raised <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Haggerty was six years old when <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> first came into his life by way of <a href="/wiki/Digital_Underground" title="Digital Underground">Digital Underground</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bet.com_1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bet.com_1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bet.com_2_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bet.com_2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> At the age of 15, he began to write lyrics.<sup id="cite_ref-bet.com_1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bet.com_1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bet.com_2_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bet.com_2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the time he started to sing, Haggerty listened to "a lot of <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast underground hip hop</a>", listing <a href="/wiki/Hieroglyphics_(group)" title="Hieroglyphics (group)">Hieroglyphics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_Fellowship" title="Freestyle Fellowship">Freestyle Fellowship</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aceyalone" title="Aceyalone">Aceyalone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Living_Legends_(group)" title="Living Legends (group)">Living Legends</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a> as his major influences.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He was interested in reaching a younger generation through his music and became part of a program focusing on education and cultural identity called "Gateways for Incarcerated Youth", where he facilitated music workshops.<sup id="cite_ref-matson1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-matson1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Haggerty attended <a href="/wiki/Garfield_High_School_(Seattle,_Washington)" class="mw-redirect" title="Garfield High School (Seattle, Washington)">Garfield High School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Hale_High_School_(Washington)" title="Nathan Hale High School (Washington)">Nathan Hale High School</a>. At Hale, he developed the stage name "Professor Macklemore" for an art project involving a made-up superhero,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and at Garfield, he started a hip-hop group named Elevated Elements with other students. The group released an album, <i>Progress</i>, in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Haggerty enrolled at the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Santa_Fe" class="mw-redirect" title="College of Santa Fe">College of Santa Fe</a> for a year, later saying in an interview that "[i]t was a very pivotal time in a lot of ways. <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe,_New_Mexico" title="Santa Fe, New Mexico">Santa Fe</a> was the place where I got good at rapping".<sup id="cite_ref-abq-journal-2017_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abq-journal-2017-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> After not getting into the music program, he moved back to Seattle.<sup id="cite_ref-buzzfeed-news-2017_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buzzfeed-news-2017-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Haggerty later enrolled at <a href="/wiki/The_Evergreen_State_College" class="mw-redirect" title="The Evergreen State College">The Evergreen State College</a> in Olympia and completed his bachelor's degree in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-matson1_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-matson1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-behind_the_awesome_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-behind_the_awesome-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<h3><span id="2000.E2.80.932008:_The_Language_of_My_World"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000–2008:_The_Language_of_My_World">2000–2008: <i>The Language of My World</i></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis_at_Sasquatch_2011.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis_at_Sasquatch_2011.jpg/170px-Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis_at_Sasquatch_2011.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="246" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis_at_Sasquatch_2011.jpg/255px-Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis_at_Sasquatch_2011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis_at_Sasquatch_2011.jpg/340px-Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis_at_Sasquatch_2011.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1300" data-file-height="1884" /></a><figcaption>Macklemore &amp; Ryan Lewis at <a href="/wiki/Sasquatch!_Music_Festival" title="Sasquatch! Music Festival">Sasquatch! Music Festival</a> (2011)</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2000, Haggerty recorded a mixtape titled <i>Open Your Eyes</i> under the name Professor Macklemore, which he distributed himself and released on October 21, 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Haggerty dropped "Professor" from his name and began working on his first official full-length album, called <i><a href="/wiki/The_Language_of_My_World" class="mw-redirect" title="The Language of My World">The Language of My World</a></i>. The album was released on January 1, 2005, with its lead single, "Love Song", being announced the same day. "Love Song" featured singer Evan Roman, and was produced by Budo, who would later go on to produce several more tracks for Macklemore.
</p><p>In 2006, Haggerty first met his future collaborator <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Lewis" title="Ryan Lewis">Ryan Lewis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Lewis, who would go on to release two albums with Haggerty as <a href="/wiki/Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis" title="Macklemore &amp; Ryan Lewis">Macklemore &amp; Ryan Lewis</a>, spent a few years working on Macklemore's promotion as a photographer. The two musicians would soon become good friends but would not formalize their collaborative efforts until 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In the meantime, Macklemore kept busy as a solo artist, appearing on <a href="/wiki/The_Physics_(music_group)" title="The Physics (music group)">The Physics</a>'s song "Good" in 2009,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> as well as performing at the Seattle major arts and music festival <a href="/wiki/Bumbershoot" title="Bumbershoot">Bumbershoot</a> in 2006, 2009, and 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Macklemore's second mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Unplanned_Mixtape" class="mw-redirect" title="The Unplanned Mixtape">The Unplanned Mixtape</a>,</i> was released on September 7, 2009. It would later reach No. 7 on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> Hip Hop chart. The <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> was accompanied by the singles "The Town" and "And We Danced", the latter of which featured singer Ziggy Stardust. "The Town" was later remixed by Sabzi of the <a href="/wiki/Blue_Scholars" title="Blue Scholars">Blue Scholars</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932017:_Career_with_Ryan_Lewis"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2017:_Career_with_Ryan_Lewis">2009–2017: Career with Ryan Lewis</span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Macklemore and <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Lewis" title="Ryan Lewis">Ryan Lewis</a> formalized the collaboration as a duo <a href="/wiki/Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis" title="Macklemore &amp; Ryan Lewis">Macklemore &amp; Ryan Lewis</a>. They released the EP <i>The VS. EP</i>.  They also released "Irish Celebration" in December 2009 in anticipation of the release of <i>The Vs. EP</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> In March 2010, the duo released "Stay At Home Dad", a track that didn't quite make <i>Vs.</i><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In October 2010, they created the <i>VS. Redux</i> EP. Macklemore used his experience with substance abuse to create the mixtape's song "Otherside", which samples the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a> song of the <a href="/wiki/Otherside" title="Otherside">same title</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On April 8, 2011, Macklemore and Ryan Lewis performed the song at the 2011 Mariners Opening Day in-front of a sold-out crowd of nearly 48,000 attendees.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Wings_(Macklemore_and_Ryan_Lewis_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Wings (Macklemore and Ryan Lewis song)">Wings</a>" was released on January 21, 2011, followed by "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Hold_Us" title="Can&#39;t Hold Us">Can't Hold Us</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ray_Dalton" title="Ray Dalton">Ray Dalton</a> on August 16, 2011. In February 2011, Macklemore and producer Ryan Lewis kicked off a multi-city tour in Seattle, which included three sold-out shows at the <a href="/wiki/Showbox_at_the_Market" class="mw-redirect" title="Showbox at the Market">Showbox at the Market</a> music venue.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> That same year, the rapper appeared at many U.S. music festivals, including <a href="/wiki/Bumbershoot" title="Bumbershoot">Bumbershoot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Outside_Lands_Music_and_Arts_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Outside Lands Music and Arts Festival">Outside Lands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_the_Bells" title="Rock the Bells">Rock the Bells</a>, SoundSet, <a href="/wiki/Sasquatch!_Music_Festival" title="Sasquatch! Music Festival">Sasquatch</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bonnaroo" class="mw-redirect" title="Bonnaroo">Bonnaroo</a>.
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Macklemore_The_Heist_Tour_2_cropped.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Macklemore_The_Heist_Tour_2_cropped.jpg/220px-Macklemore_The_Heist_Tour_2_cropped.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="161" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Macklemore_The_Heist_Tour_2_cropped.jpg/330px-Macklemore_The_Heist_Tour_2_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Macklemore_The_Heist_Tour_2_cropped.jpg/440px-Macklemore_The_Heist_Tour_2_cropped.jpg 2x" data-file-width="853" data-file-height="624" /></a><figcaption>Macklemore performing at The Heist Tour, in 2012</figcaption></figure>
<p>Their album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Heist_(album)" title="The Heist (album)">The Heist</a></i> was released in October 2012 and debuted on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at number 2 of the week dated October 27, 2012, selling over 78,000 copies. "<a href="/wiki/Same_Love" title="Same Love">Same Love</a>" was released on July 18, 2012 and after "<a href="/wiki/White_Walls" title="White Walls">White Walls</a>". In January 2013, <a href="/wiki/Music_Choice" title="Music Choice">Music Choice</a> featured Macklemore in the brand new series "Primed", which focuses on emerging artists. In May 2013, Haggerty was featured on <a href="/wiki/Clinton_Sparks" title="Clinton Sparks">Clinton Sparks</a>'s single "Gold Rush", along with <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a> and D.A.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The Heist World Tour began in August 2012 to promote <i>The Heist</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> In May 2014, Haggerty was accused of <a href="/wiki/Antisemitism" title="Antisemitism">antisemitism</a> after wearing a costume resembling a <a href="/wiki/Stereotypes_of_Jews" title="Stereotypes of Jews">common Jewish caricature</a> during a private concert in <a href="/wiki/Seattle,_Washington" class="mw-redirect" title="Seattle, Washington">Seattle, Washington</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2015, Macklemore announced via Twitter that his third studio album would be released sometime in the second half of that year.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Despite this, the album was not released until February 26, 2016. On August 5, 2015, Macklemore released a song for free download titled "Growing Up (Sloane's Song)", which features <a href="/wiki/Ed_Sheeran" title="Ed Sheeran">Ed Sheeran</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On 27 August 2015, he released a new song called "<a href="/wiki/Downtown_(Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis_song)" title="Downtown (Macklemore &amp; Ryan Lewis song)">Downtown</a>" which features <a href="/wiki/Foxy_Shazam" title="Foxy Shazam">Foxy Shazam</a> vocalist Eric Nally, <a href="/wiki/Kool_Moe_Dee" title="Kool Moe Dee">Kool Moe Dee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melle_Mel" title="Melle Mel">Melle Mel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_Caz" title="Grandmaster Caz">Grandmaster Caz</a>, which he performed at the <a href="/wiki/2015_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2015 MTV Video Music Awards">2015 MTV Video Music Awards</a> on August 30.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On his first tour in two years, An Evening with Macklemore and Ryan Lewis, Macklemore announced that his new album was finished and ready for release.
</p><p>On January 15, 2016, Macklemore released a teaser video on his <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel revealing the name of his third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/This_Unruly_Mess_I%27ve_Made" title="This Unruly Mess I&#39;ve Made">This Unruly Mess I've Made</a></i>, and announcing that it was scheduled to be released on February 26, 2016. On January 22, 2016, the duo released "<a href="/wiki/White_Privilege_II" title="White Privilege II">White Privilege II</a>", the second single on <i>This Unruly Mess I've Made</i>. On February 26, 2016, Macklemore and Ryan Lewis released the album <i>This Unruly Mess I've Made</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Also in 2016, Macklemore released two solo singles, "<a href="/wiki/Drug_Dealer_(song)" title="Drug Dealer (song)">Drug Dealer</a>" and "Wednesday Morning".<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> "Drug Dealer" features Macklemore rapping about his previous addictions and is featured in a documentary that includes clips of Macklemore discussing drug abuse with President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>. "Wednesday Morning" was released after the <a href="/wiki/2016_United_States_presidential_election" title="2016 United States presidential election">2016 US Election</a> and features Macklemore rapping about the political future of the country. The tracks were produced by <a href="/wiki/Budo_(musician)" title="Budo (musician)">Budo</a>. On June 15, 2017, Macklemore announced via his official <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> that the duo were on hiatus.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2017.E2.80.93present:_Gemini_.26_Ben"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–present:_Gemini_&amp;_Ben">2017–present: <i>Gemini</i> &amp; <i>Ben</i></span></h3>
<p>On June 15, 2017, Macklemore released "<a href="/wiki/Glorious_(Macklemore_song)" title="Glorious (Macklemore song)">Glorious</a>", featuring American singer <a href="/wiki/Skylar_Grey" title="Skylar Grey">Skylar Grey</a>, as the lead single from Macklemore's second solo studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Gemini_(Macklemore_album)" title="Gemini (Macklemore album)">Gemini</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> On July 26, 2017, Macklemore released "<a href="/wiki/Marmalade_(song)" title="Marmalade (song)">Marmalade</a>", featuring American rapper <a href="/wiki/Lil_Yachty" title="Lil Yachty">Lil Yachty</a>, as the second single from Macklemore's upcoming album. "<a href="/wiki/Good_Old_Days_(Macklemore_song)" title="Good Old Days (Macklemore song)">Good Old Days</a>", a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Kesha" title="Kesha">Kesha</a>, was released as a single on October 9, 2017. Macklemore released <i>Gemini</i> on September 22. The album was produced by longtime collaborator Budo.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> On October 1, Macklemore performed "Same Love" in a set at the opening of <a href="/wiki/2017_NRL_Grand_Final" title="2017 NRL Grand Final">the Grand Final</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Rugby_League" title="National Rugby League">National Rugby League</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, Australia — something that was considered by some to be controversial in the middle of a <a href="/wiki/Australian_Marriage_Law_Postal_Survey" title="Australian Marriage Law Postal Survey">national survey on same sex marriage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Macklemore stated that it was one of his best performances due to the circumstances and thanked the fans in Sydney for the reception that he received throughout. On December 11, 2017, Macklemore announced an upcoming co-headlining tour with Kesha titled <a href="/wiki/The_Adventures_of_Kesha_and_Macklemore" title="The Adventures of Kesha and Macklemore">The Adventures of Kesha and Macklemore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kesha_Tour_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kesha_Tour-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Macklemore_Tour_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Macklemore_Tour-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> This was Macklemore's fifth headlining tour, promoting his album <i>Gemini</i> and Kesha's sixth headlining tour, promoting her third solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_(Kesha_album)" title="Rainbow (Kesha album)">Rainbow</a></i> (2017).<sup id="cite_ref-Announcement_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Announcement-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> The tour began in Phoenix on June 6, 2018, and concluded in Tampa on August 5, 2018.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On October 29, 2021, Macklemore released a new single titled "Next Year", featuring American singer Windser. Macklemore also worked with Ryan Lewis on the single, marking their first collaboration in three years.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 22, 2022, Macklemore released a new single, "<a href="/wiki/Chant_(song)" title="Chant (song)">Chant</a>", with Australian musician <a href="/wiki/Tones_and_I" title="Tones and I">Tones and I</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> On August 19, 2022, Macklemore released the single, "Maniac", featuring American musician Windser.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> The third single, "Faithful", featuring <a href="/wiki/NLE_Choppa" title="NLE Choppa">NLE Choppa</a>, was released on October 28, 2022. The fourth single, "Heroes", featuring <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>, was released on January 20, 2023. Macklemore's third solo studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Ben_(Macklemore_album)" title="Ben (Macklemore album)">Ben</a></i>, was released on March 3, 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-Mackelmore_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mackelmore-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span></h3>
<p>Macklemore became engaged to his girlfriend of seven years, Tricia Davis, on January 21, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> On January 3, 2015, he announced on Twitter that he and his fiancée were expecting their first child that May.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> After the release of "Growing Up (Sloane's Song)", which featured English singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Ed_Sheeran" title="Ed Sheeran">Ed Sheeran</a>, the couple announced that their daughter, Sloane Ava Simone Haggerty,<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> had been born on May 29.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Following the birth of Sloane, they married on June 27, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> 
In September 2017, the couple announced that they were expecting their second child.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> On April 3, 2018, Macklemore announced during a performance at the <a href="/wiki/3Arena" title="3Arena">3Arena</a> that his wife had given birth to their second daughter. He did not tell the crowd her name. In 2021, Macklemore and his wife had their third child, Hugo.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span></h3>
<p>In August 2008, Macklemore admitted himself into <a href="/wiki/Drug_rehabilitation" title="Drug rehabilitation">drug rehabilitation</a> for <a href="/wiki/Drug_addiction" class="mw-redirect" title="Drug addiction">drug addiction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alcoholism" title="Alcoholism">alcoholism</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GQMay2013_65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GQMay2013-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> and celebrated three years of sobriety before a brief relapse in 2011, which he describes in his song "Starting Over".<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> He said in a 2012 documentary that he spent most of his twenties trying to combat his addictions and destructive way of life:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I want to be someone who is respected and not just in terms of my music. I want to be respected in terms of the way that I treat people... Music is my creative outlet in terms of expressing what is important to me; what has importance, what has a value. And I want to be respected for that.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote> 
<p>On May 14, 2016, Macklemore appeared in US President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>'s weekly address to talk about the dangers of <a href="/wiki/Opioid_use_disorder" title="Opioid use disorder">addiction to opioids</a> and prescription <a href="/wiki/Analgesic" title="Analgesic">painkillers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> Macklemore talked about his own experiences with abuse of painkillers, stating "When you're going through it, it's hard to imagine anything being worse than addiction. But the shame and stigma associated with the disease keeps too many people from seeking the help they actually need. Addiction isn't a personal choice or a personal failure."<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 29, 2018, Macklemore headlined Recovery Fest in <a href="/wiki/Pawtucket,_Rhode_Island" title="Pawtucket, Rhode Island">Pawtucket, Rhode Island</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> Recovery Fest was formed to have a drug- and alcohol-free concert and support charities that worked in combating opioid addiction.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the summer of 2020, during and in part due to the social isolation that took place during the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, Macklemore relapsed and suffered a near-fatal overdose. He was hospitalized and given treatment; he eventually recovered from the episode. This experience, as well as the ongoing challenge of overcoming addiction while also raising children, served as an inspiration for much of the lyrical content that makes up his third solo studio album, <i>Ben.</i><sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Activism">Activism</span></h3>
<p>Macklemore voiced his support of <a href="/wiki/LGBT_rights_by_country_or_territory" title="LGBT rights by country or territory">LGBT rights</a> and same-sex marriage in the song "<a href="/wiki/Same_Love" title="Same Love">Same Love</a>" released in 2012, which also condemns <a href="/wiki/Homophobia" title="Homophobia">homophobia</a> in mainstream hip-hop, society, and mass media.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 16, 2019, Macklemore received the Stevie Ray Vaughan Award from <a href="/wiki/MusiCares" title="MusiCares">MusiCares</a>, in recognition of his support of MusiCares and the addiction recovery process.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Interests">Interests</span></h3>
<p>Macklemore is a fan of the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Seahawks" title="Seattle Seahawks">Seattle Seahawks</a>. He created a <a href="/wiki/12th_man_(football)" title="12th man (football)">12th Man</a> promotional video and performed at the <a href="/wiki/2014_NFC_Championship_Game" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 NFC Championship Game">2014 NFC Championship Game</a>. He remains a fan of the now-<a href="/wiki/Seattle_SuperSonics_relocation_to_Oklahoma_City" title="Seattle SuperSonics relocation to Oklahoma City">relocated</a> <a href="/wiki/Seattle_SuperSonics" title="Seattle SuperSonics">Seattle SuperSonics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> In August 2019, Macklemore joined the ownership group of <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Sounders_FC" title="Seattle Sounders FC">Seattle Sounders FC</a>, the local <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a> club.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>He is a fan of the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Kraken" title="Seattle Kraken">Seattle Kraken</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a>; on April 18, 2022, the Kraken announced that Macklemore had joined its ownership group.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Macklemore has an eclectic taste in art, including outlandish garments spouting sequins, fringe and feathers, a <a href="/wiki/Kitsch" title="Kitsch">kitschy</a> velvet painting of a <a href="/wiki/Bald_eagle" title="Bald eagle">bald eagle</a>, an oil painting of <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a> dancing and a <a href="/wiki/Dan_Lacey" title="Dan Lacey">Dan Lacey</a> painting of a nude <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
