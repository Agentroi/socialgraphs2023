<p><b>William Thomas Trenell Brown</b> (born July 5, 1983), known professionally as <b>Willie B</b> or by the <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>The Ichiban Don</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> record producer and recording artist from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. He is an original member of <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast hip hop</a> production team <a href="/wiki/Digi%2BPhonics" title="Digi+Phonics">Digi+Phonics</a> of <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a> (TDE). He is also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_collective" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop collective">hip hop collective</a> A Room Full of Mirrors, alongside fellow rappers <a href="/wiki/Punch_(rapper)" title="Punch (rapper)">Punch</a> and Daylyt, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willie_B_(producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 6, 2011, Willie B released a mixtape titled <i>I'm Not a Producer</i> hosted by DJ Age and featuring <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from <a href="/wiki/Jay_Rock" title="Jay Rock">Jay Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Little_Brother_(group)" title="Little Brother (group)">Little Brother</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crooked_I" title="Crooked I">Crooked I</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Lamont" title="Bishop Lamont">Bishop Lamont</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glasses_Malone" title="Glasses Malone">Glasses Malone</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is best known for producing Kendrick Lamar's "Ignorance Is Bliss" and "Rigamortis" from Lamar's <i><a href="/wiki/Overly_Dedicated" title="Overly Dedicated">Overly Dedicated</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Section_80" class="mw-redirect" title="Section 80">Section 80</a></i>, respectively. He has also produced Ab-Soul's "Black Lip Bastard", and Schoolboy Q's "Gangsta in Designer (No Concept)", from his second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Habits_%26_Contradictions" title="Habits &amp; Contradictions">Habits &amp; Contradictions</a></i>, among other songs for TDE.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Outside of TDE he has produced for artists such as <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Gibbs" title="Freddie Gibbs">Freddie Gibbs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Childish_Gambino" class="mw-redirect" title="Childish Gambino">Childish Gambino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a> and Apollo the Great, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-googleusercontent1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-googleusercontent1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On July 27, 2013, he released the first ever <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>-only instrumental mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
