<p><b>Jaime Luis Gomez</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born July 14, 1975),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> better known by the stage names <b>Taboo</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> or <b>Taboo Nawasha</b> is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, actor, DJ, and comic book writer, best known as a member of the musical group <a href="/wiki/Black_Eyed_Peas" title="Black Eyed Peas">Black Eyed Peas</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taboo_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jaime Luis Gomez was born in Los Angeles, California.<sup id="cite_ref-romp_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-romp-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His parents were both from <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-romp_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-romp-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Rosemead_High_School" title="Rosemead High School">Rosemead High School</a> (class of 1993), and was educated at <a href="/wiki/Richard_Garvey_Intermediate_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Richard Garvey Intermediate School">Richard Garvey Intermediate School</a>. He also has <a href="/wiki/Shoshone" title="Shoshone">Shoshone</a> heritage from his grandmother.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taboo_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1995.E2.80.93present:_The_Black_Eyed_Peas"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1995–present:_The_Black_Eyed_Peas">1995–present: The Black Eyed Peas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taboo_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1995–present: The Black Eyed Peas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Black Eyed Peas date back to 1988, when eighth-graders William Adams (<a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a>) and Allan Pineda (<a href="/wiki/Apl.de.ap" title="Apl.de.ap">apl.de.ap</a>) met and began rapping and performing together around Los Angeles. The pair signed to <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records" title="Ruthless Records">Ruthless Records</a> (run by <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a>) in 1992, catching the attention of Eazy-E manager, <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Heller" title="Jerry Heller">Jerry Heller</a>'s nephew. Along with another friend of theirs, Dante Santiago, they called their band <a href="/wiki/Atban_Klann" title="Atban Klann">Atban Klann</a> (ATBAN: A Tribe Beyond a Nation).<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_page_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic_page-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Will 1X (aka will.i.am), apl.de.ap, Mookie Mook, DJ Motiv8 (aka Monroe Walker) and Dante Santiago formed Atban Klann. Their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Grass_Roots_(Atban_Klann_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Grass Roots (Atban Klann album)">Grass Roots</a></i>, was never released because Ruthless founder Eazy-E had died.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg/250px-Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg/375px-Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg/500px-Black_Eyed_Peas_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200" /></a><figcaption>The Black Eyed Peas signing autographs before a concert at <a href="/wiki/East_Stroudsburg_University_of_Pennsylvania" title="East Stroudsburg University of Pennsylvania">East Stroudsburg University of Pennsylvania</a> in 2004</figcaption></figure>
<p>After Eazy-E died in 1995, Atban Klann reformed and changed their name to Black Eyed Pods, and then Black Eyed Peas. Dante Santiago was replaced with Jaime Gomez (Taboo), and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Hill_(soul_musician)" title="Kim Hill (soul musician)">Kim Hill</a> became a steady background singer. Unlike many hip hop acts, they chose to perform with a live band and adopted a musical and clothing style that differed wildly from the <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_rap" title="Gangsta rap">gangsta rap</a> sounds of Los Angeles-based hip hop acts at the time. Taboo is known primarily for his guttural spoken reprises of the last few words of a sung lyric, as well as emphatic flourishes between vocal parts. After being signed to <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> and releasing their debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Behind_the_Front" title="Behind the Front">Behind the Front</a></i> (1998) the group (and their accompanying live band) earned critical acclaim. One of the singles from the album was "<a href="/wiki/Joints_%26_Jam" title="Joints &amp; Jam">Joints &amp; Jam</a>", and was featured on the <i><a href="/wiki/Bulworth" title="Bulworth">Bulworth</a></i> soundtrack. Their second album was <i><a href="/wiki/Bridging_the_Gap_(Black_Eyed_Peas_album)" title="Bridging the Gap (Black Eyed Peas album)">Bridging the Gap</a></i> (2000), which had the single "<a href="/wiki/Request_%2B_Line" title="Request + Line">Request + Line</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Macy_Gray" title="Macy Gray">Macy Gray</a>.
</p><p>In 2003, <a href="/wiki/Fergie_(singer)" title="Fergie (singer)">Fergie</a> became the fourth member of the group and the group gained international fame, having won six Grammy Awards and sold an estimated 75 million records worldwide. They released their third album <i><a href="/wiki/Elephunk" title="Elephunk">Elephunk</a></i>, with the hit singles "<a href="/wiki/Where_Is_the_Love%3F" title="Where Is the Love?">Where Is the Love?</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Shut_Up_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Shut Up (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Shut Up</a>". Their 2005 album <i><a href="/wiki/Monkey_Business_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Monkey Business (The Black Eyed Peas album)">Monkey Business</a></i> was a worldwide success, being certified 4× <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">Platinum</a> in the US, and spawning the singles, "<a href="/wiki/My_Humps" title="My Humps">My Humps</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Phunk_with_My_Heart" title="Don&#39;t Phunk with My Heart">Don't Phunk with My Heart</a>".
</p><p>In 2009, the group became one of only 12 artists to have simultaneously held the No. 1 and No. 2 spots on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, with their singles "<a href="/wiki/Boom_Boom_Pow" title="Boom Boom Pow">Boom Boom Pow</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/I_Gotta_Feeling" title="I Gotta Feeling">I Gotta Feeling</a>", with the next single "<a href="/wiki/Meet_Me_Halfway" title="Meet Me Halfway">Meet Me Halfway</a>" achieving similar success, from the album <i><a href="/wiki/The_E.N.D" class="mw-redirect" title="The E.N.D">The E.N.D</a></i>. These three singles topped the chart for an unprecedented 30 consecutive weeks in 2009. The album later produced a third Hot 100 number-one placement with "<a href="/wiki/Imma_Be" title="Imma Be">Imma Be</a>", making the group one of few to ever place three number one singles on the chart from the same album before being followed with "<a href="/wiki/Rock_That_Body" title="Rock That Body">Rock That Body</a>" which peaked in the Top 10 of Hot 100. "I Gotta Feeling" became the first single to sell more than 1 million downloads in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Black_Eyed_Peas_at_Walmart_meeting.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Black_Eyed_Peas_at_Walmart_meeting.jpg/280px-Black_Eyed_Peas_at_Walmart_meeting.jpg" decoding="async" width="280" height="187" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Black_Eyed_Peas_at_Walmart_meeting.jpg/420px-Black_Eyed_Peas_at_Walmart_meeting.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Black_Eyed_Peas_at_Walmart_meeting.jpg/560px-Black_Eyed_Peas_at_Walmart_meeting.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="683" /></a><figcaption>The Black Eyed Peas in 2011</figcaption></figure>
<p>The Black Eyed Peas were ranked 12th on the <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s Decade-End Chart Artist of the Decade, and 7th in the Hot 100 Artists of the Decade. In November 2010, they released the album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Beginning_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Beginning (The Black Eyed Peas album)">The Beginning</a></i>. The first single of the album was "<a href="/wiki/The_Time_(Dirty_Bit)" title="The Time (Dirty Bit)">The Time (Dirty Bit)</a>" and topped the charts in many countries. In February 2011, the group performed at the <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLV" title="Super Bowl XLV">Super Bowl XLV</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Super_Bowl_halftime_shows" title="List of Super Bowl halftime shows">halftime show</a>. The album's second single was "<a href="/wiki/Just_Can%27t_Get_Enough_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Just Can&#39;t Get Enough (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Just Can't Get Enough</a>" and was released in February 2011. The music video was filmed in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> one week before the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a>. The third single "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Stop_the_Party_(The_Black_Eyed_Peas_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Don&#39;t Stop the Party (The Black Eyed Peas song)">Don't Stop the Party</a>" was released in May 2011. In July 2011 the group announced they were taking a break.
</p>
<h3><span id="Solo_work_.282008.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_work_(2008–present)">Solo work (2008–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taboo_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Solo work (2008–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2008, Taboo was in the process of creating a solo album,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> but in a 2011 interview Taboo said it would be some time before he would begin a solo album.<sup id="cite_ref-JohnJohn_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JohnJohn-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sie7e" title="Sie7e">Sie7e</a> was also confirmed to have composed at least three of the Album's songs. "Revolution Ni Hao" can be heard on his official website.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Music he has co-written and performed has been featured on many movie soundtracks, including <i><a href="/wiki/Coach_Carter" title="Coach Carter">Coach Carter</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Legally_Blonde" title="Legally Blonde">Legally Blonde</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harold_%26_Kumar_Go_to_White_Castle" title="Harold &amp; Kumar Go to White Castle">Harold &amp; Kumar Go to White Castle</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Barbershop_2:_Back_in_Business" title="Barbershop 2: Back in Business">Barbershop 2: Back in Business</a></i>.
</p><p>In May 2014, Taboo released his solo single, "Zumbao", which is "all about having fun[...]being infected by positive energy."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The music video for the song features a handful of YouTube talent including <a href="/wiki/DeStorm_Power" title="DeStorm Power">DeStorm Power</a>, STRSkillSchool, <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_football" title="Freestyle football">freestyle footballer</a> <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9an_Garnier" title="Séan Garnier">Séan Garnier</a>, Mandy Jiroux, and Megan Batoon.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2016, Taboo released the single "The Fight" about his successful battle against testicular cancer. The track was made in partnership with the <a href="/wiki/American_Cancer_Society" title="American Cancer Society">American Cancer Society</a>, for which Taboo is a global ambassador. The ACS will receive all funds generated by the song. On the song, Taboo said "I'm so proud to release The Fight because it's truly an anthem of survivorship. I want to use this song and my story to inspire and motivate people all over the world to live their best, healthiest lives possible."<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_collaborations">Other collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taboo_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Taboo collaborated in the past with <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Key" title="Ryan Key">Ryan Key</a> of <a href="/wiki/Yellowcard" title="Yellowcard">Yellowcard</a> on the song "Gotta Get It Now". He also collaborated with race rapper David Rolas for the song "Morena", and rapper Fudge Dog on the song "Get It". In late 2007, Taboo also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Merengue_music" title="Merengue music">merengue</a> star <a href="/wiki/Juan_Luis_Guerra" title="Juan Luis Guerra">Juan Luis Guerra</a> on a remix of his hit song "<a href="/wiki/La_Llave_de_Mi_Coraz%C3%B3n_(song)" class="mw-redirect" title="La Llave de Mi Corazón (song)">La Llave de Mi Corazón</a>". In addition, he created a remix to the song "<a href="/wiki/La_Paga" title="La Paga">La Paga</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Juanes" title="Juanes">Juanes</a>. Taboo worked with <a href="/wiki/George_Pajon" title="George Pajon">George Pajon</a> Jr. and Andy Vargas to perform the Obama song "Yes We Can" (Si Se Puede).<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He performed together with <a href="/wiki/Juanes" title="Juanes">Juanes</a>, in the kick-off concert of South Africa Worldcup 2010, singing <a href="/wiki/La_Paga" title="La Paga">La Paga</a>. He is on Paulina Rubio's Brava album adding vocals for the song "Hoy Me Toca A Mi", which he also co-wrote.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taboo_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taboo_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Taboo's first acting role was in the film <i>Dirty</i> with <a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a>, playing the part of Ramírez. He then appeared in the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent film</a> <i>Cosmic Radio</i>, which also stars <a href="/wiki/Michael_Madsen" title="Michael Madsen">Michael Madsen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wes_Studi" title="Wes Studi">Wes Studi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irene_Bedard" title="Irene Bedard">Irene Bedard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daryl_Hannah" title="Daryl Hannah">Daryl Hannah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Chavira" class="mw-redirect" title="Ricardo Chavira">Ricardo Chavira</a>, and other well-known actors. He played the character <a href="/wiki/Vega_(Street_Fighter)" title="Vega (Street Fighter)">Vega</a> in the 2009 film <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Fighter:_The_Legend_of_Chun-Li" title="Street Fighter: The Legend of Chun-Li">Street Fighter: The Legend of Chun-Li</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Taboo appeared as a judge on the reality show <i><a href="/wiki/MTV%27s_Top_Pop_Group" title="MTV&#39;s Top Pop Group">MTV's Top Pop Group</a></i>. He also had a cameo in <i><a href="/wiki/Blue%27s_Big_City_Adventure" title="Blue&#39;s Big City Adventure">Blue's Big City Adventure</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing">Writing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taboo_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Writing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Taboo later collaborated with Benjamin Jackendoff and <a href="/wiki/Scot_Eaton" title="Scot Eaton">Scot Eaton</a> to write the third volume of the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Werewolf_by_Night" title="Werewolf by Night">Werewolf by Night</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2021, Taboo, who identifies as Native American and Mexican, authored a children's book called <i>A Kids Book About Identity</i>, stating that he "had a long journey of educating myself and learning about both of my cultures, both being rooted in an Indigenous ancestry of course."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2022, it was announced that Taboo and B.Earl would write a miniseries entitled <i>Deadly Neighborhood Spider-Man</i>, which sees <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">Spider-Man</a> going to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and facing off against the <a href="/wiki/Demon_Bear" class="mw-redirect" title="Demon Bear">Demon Bear</a>. The series debuted in October 2022 and was illustrated by Juan Ferreyra.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taboo_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taboo married Jaymie Dizon in <a href="/wiki/Pasadena,_California" title="Pasadena, California">Pasadena, California</a>, on July 12, 2008, at <a href="/wiki/St._Andrew%27s_Catholic_Church_(Pasadena,_California)" title="St. Andrew&#39;s Catholic Church (Pasadena, California)">St. Andrew's Catholic Church</a>, two days before his 33rd birthday. <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a> and <a href="/wiki/Apl.de.ap" title="Apl.de.ap">apl.de.ap</a> served as groomsmen.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The couple had their first child in 2009 and their second in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He has another son from a previous relationship, Joshua,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> who was born October 13, 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His autobiography, <i>Fallin' Up: My Story</i>, co-authored with Steve Dennis, was released in February 2011.
</p>
