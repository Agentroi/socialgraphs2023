<p><b>Vincent Jamal Staples</b> (born July 2, 1993) is an American rapper, singer, and actor. Staples was once a close associate of <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_G" class="mw-redirect" title="Mike G">Mike G</a> and <a href="/wiki/Earl_Sweatshirt" title="Earl Sweatshirt">Earl Sweatshirt</a> in particular. He is currently signed to <a href="/wiki/Motown" title="Motown">Motown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blacksmith_Records" title="Blacksmith Records">Blacksmith Records</a>.
</p><p>Staples rose to prominence with appearances on albums by <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a> members and his collaborative <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/Stolen_Youth_(mixtape)" title="Stolen Youth (mixtape)">Stolen Youth</a></i> with producer <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>. In October 2014, he released his debut EP <i><a href="/wiki/Hell_Can_Wait" title="Hell Can Wait">Hell Can Wait</a></i>, which included the singles "Hands Up" and "Blue Suede". His debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Summertime_%2706" title="Summertime &#39;06">Summertime '06</a></i>, was released in June 2015 to critical acclaim. He was also featured as a part of the <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="XXL Magazine">XXL</a></i> 2015 Freshman Class.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl2015_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl2015-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His second album <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Fish_Theory" title="Big Fish Theory">Big Fish Theory</a></i>, which contains the singles "BagBak", "<a href="/wiki/Big_Fish_(song)" title="Big Fish (song)">Big Fish</a>" and "Rain Come Down", incorporates <a href="/wiki/Avant-garde_music" title="Avant-garde music">avant-garde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic</a> influences. It was released in June 2017 to further acclaim from critics. In November 2018, Staples released his third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/FM!" title="FM!">FM!</a></i> In July 2021, he released his <a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples_(album)" title="Vince Staples (album)">self-titled fourth studio album</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Ramona_Park_Broke_My_Heart" title="Ramona Park Broke My Heart">Ramona Park Broke My Heart</a></i> in April 2022.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vince_Staples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Staples was born in <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton, California</a>, but grew up in <a href="/wiki/North_Long_Beach,_Long_Beach,_California" title="North Long Beach, Long Beach, California">North Long Beach</a>, after his mother decided she wanted to move away from Compton due to the <a href="/wiki/Crime_in_Los_Angeles" title="Crime in Los Angeles">high crime rates</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Staples is the youngest of five siblings, two brothers and three sisters.<sup id="cite_ref-Noisey-Date-2016_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Noisey-Date-2016-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Complex-ShyneColdchain2-2013_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-ShyneColdchain2-2013-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Staples grew up in poverty.<sup id="cite_ref-SPIN-Summertime-2015_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPIN-Summertime-2015-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Staples attended Optimal Christian Academy from 4th to 8th grade, which he said was an influential and positive experience.<sup id="cite_ref-Complex-ShyneColdchain2-2013_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-ShyneColdchain2-2013-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> During high school, Staples's mom sent him to Atlanta to stay with one of his sisters. He attended high school there for six months.<sup id="cite_ref-FADER-ESPN-HighlyQuestionable-2016_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FADER-ESPN-HighlyQuestionable-2016-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> After returning to Southern California, Staples attended other high schools: <a href="/wiki/Jordan_High_School_(Long_Beach,_California)" title="Jordan High School (Long Beach, California)">Jordan High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Long_Beach,_California" title="Long Beach, California">Long Beach</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Mayfair_High_School" title="Mayfair High School">Mayfair High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lakewood,_California" title="Lakewood, California">Lakewood</a>, Opportunity High School home schooling, <a href="/wiki/Esperanza_High_School" title="Esperanza High School">Esperanza High School</a> in Anaheim, and <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_High_School_(La_Palma,_California)" title="John F. Kennedy High School (La Palma, California)">Kennedy High School</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-Complex-ShyneColdchain2-2013_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-ShyneColdchain2-2013-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Staples has been upfront regarding his involvement with <a href="/wiki/Street_gang" class="mw-redirect" title="Street gang">street gangs</a> during his childhood,<sup id="cite_ref-FADER-ESPN-HighlyQuestionable-2016_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FADER-ESPN-HighlyQuestionable-2016-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NPR-Pre-BigFishTheory-2017_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPR-Pre-BigFishTheory-2017-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and is involved with speaking to the youth in his community about the dangers of the gang lifestyle.<sup id="cite_ref-Complex-ShyneColdchain2-2013_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-ShyneColdchain2-2013-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Growing up, Staples participated in sports when given the opportunity. Most notably, he played in <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>'s Snoop Youth Football League (SYFL). When interviewed by <a href="/wiki/Sean_Evans_(interviewer)" title="Sean Evans (interviewer)">Sean Evans</a> on the <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_Ones" title="Hot Ones">Hot Ones</a></i>, Staples recalled playing against the Carson Colts and Mission Viejo Cowboys who he claimed had "grown-ass men on the field." Staples then went on to state, "Snoop really did it big, we got our names on our jerseys, we had the best cleats, the best helmets, you know Snoop Dogg really loved football."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vince_Staples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is of <a href="/wiki/African_Americans" title="African Americans">African-American</a> and <a href="/wiki/Haitians" title="Haitians">Haitian</a> descent and lives in Southern California.<sup id="cite_ref-Noisey-Date-2016_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Noisey-Date-2016-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
Staples has stated that he follows a <a href="/wiki/Straight_edge" title="Straight edge">straight edge</a> lifestyle and he doesn't drink or do illicit drugs.<sup id="cite_ref-Vogue-FYF-2016_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vogue-FYF-2016-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Staples is a fan of the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Clippers" title="Los Angeles Clippers">Los Angeles Clippers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He isa fan of modern art and has referenced the feminist French-American sculptor <a href="/wiki/Louise_Bourgeois" title="Louise Bourgeois">Louise Bourgeois</a> in his song "Rain Come Down" and stated his appreciation for painter and photographer <a href="/wiki/Richard_Prince" title="Richard Prince">Richard Prince</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vince_Staples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932013:_Beginnings_and_Stolen_Youth"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2013:_Beginnings_and_Stolen_Youth">2009–2013: Beginnings and <i>Stolen Youth</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vince_Staples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009–2013: Beginnings and Stolen Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Staples was discovered by Dijon "LaVish" Samo and Chuck Wun, alongside his cousin Campbell Emerson. LaVish took Staples on a trip to Los Angeles, where he befriended the <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future collective</a>'s  members <a href="/wiki/Syd_tha_Kyd" class="mw-redirect" title="Syd tha Kyd">Syd tha Kyd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_G" class="mw-redirect" title="Mike G">Mike G</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Earl_Sweatshirt" title="Earl Sweatshirt">Earl Sweatshirt</a>. Although he had not intended to become a rapper, he made some <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> on their songs, most notably "epaR" from Earl Sweatshirt's March 2010 mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Earl_(mixtape)" title="Earl (mixtape)">Earl</a></i>. After featuring on some other tracks, he decided to pursue a career in rap. He released his official debut mixtape, called <i>Shyne Coldchain Vol. 1</i> on December 30, 2011, via applebird.com.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In October 2012, he released a collaborative mixtape with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Uzowuru" title="Michael Uzowuru">Michael Uzowuru</a>, titled <i>Winter in Prague</i>. It was produced entirely by Uzowuru.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Earl Sweatshirt returned from Samoa and reconnected with Vince. Earl then introduced him to fellow American rapper <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>. In June 2013, Miller (under the alias Larry Fisherman) and Staples released a mixtape titled <i><a href="/wiki/Stolen_Youth_(mixtape)" title="Stolen Youth (mixtape)">Stolen Youth</a></i>. The mixtape features guest appearances from Miller, <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul" title="Ab-Soul">Ab-Soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a>, Da$H, Hardo, and Staples's Cutthroat Boyz co-member, Joey Fatts.<sup id="cite_ref-thefader1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Following <i>Stolen Youth</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s release, he toured as a supporting act on Miller's <a href="/wiki/The_Space_Migration_Tour" class="mw-redirect" title="The Space Migration Tour">Space Migration Tour</a>. After making three appearances on Earl's debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Doris_(album)" title="Doris (album)">Doris</a></i>, including the single "<a href="/wiki/Hive_(song)" title="Hive (song)">Hive</a>", the <a href="/wiki/Liner_notes" title="Liner notes">liner notes</a> revealed Staples had recently signed to the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> record label <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.932015:_Shyne_Coldchain_Vol._2.2C_Hell_Can_Wait.2C_and_Summertime_.2706"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2015:_Shyne_Coldchain_Vol._2,_Hell_Can_Wait,_and_Summertime_'06">2014–2015: <i>Shyne Coldchain Vol. 2</i>, <i>Hell Can Wait</i>, and <i>Summertime '06</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vince_Staples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2014–2015: Shyne Coldchain Vol. 2, Hell Can Wait, and Summertime &#039;06">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Vince_Staples_2015_(cropped).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Vince_Staples_2015_%28cropped%29.jpg/180px-Vince_Staples_2015_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="180" height="270" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Vince_Staples_2015_%28cropped%29.jpg/270px-Vince_Staples_2015_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Vince_Staples_2015_%28cropped%29.jpg/360px-Vince_Staples_2015_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2328" data-file-height="3491" /></a><figcaption>Staples at NXNE in June 2015</figcaption></figure>
<p>On March 13, 2014, he released his fourth mixtape, called <i><a href="/wiki/Shyne_Coldchain_Vol._2" class="mw-redirect" title="Shyne Coldchain Vol. 2">Shyne Coldchain Vol. 2</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape features the production from Earl Sweatshirt, Michael Uzowuru, <a href="/wiki/Childish_Major" title="Childish Major">Childish Major</a>, <a href="/wiki/No_ID" class="mw-redirect" title="No ID">No ID</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evidence_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Evidence (rapper)">Evidence</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Complex-ShyneColdchain2-2013_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-ShyneColdchain2-2013-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/DJ_Babu" title="DJ Babu">DJ Babu</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Scoop_DeVille" title="Scoop DeVille">Scoop DeVille</a>; as well as guest appearances from singer-songwriters <a href="/wiki/Jhen%C3%A9_Aiko" title="Jhené Aiko">Jhené Aiko</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Fauntleroy" title="James Fauntleroy">James Fauntleroy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On March 2, 2014, Staples began touring in the United States with a fellow American rappers <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a> and <a href="/wiki/Isaiah_Rashad" title="Isaiah Rashad">Isaiah Rashad</a> on the <i>Oxymoron World Tour</i>, to support the release of Schoolboy Q's album <i><a href="/wiki/Oxymoron_(Schoolboy_Q_album)" title="Oxymoron (Schoolboy Q album)">Oxymoron</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 15, 2014, Staples released a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for "Blue Suede".<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The track was also made available on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On September 9, 2014, Vince released another new song titled "Hands Up" via iTunes. He released the EP <i><a href="/wiki/Hell_Can_Wait" title="Hell Can Wait">Hell Can Wait</a></i> on October 7, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Breihan_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Breihan-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Prior to the EP's release Staples revealed during an interview with <a href="/wiki/XXL_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="XXL Magazine">XXL Magazine</a> that it would include <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from <a href="/wiki/Aston_Matthews" title="Aston Matthews">A$ton Matthews</a> and <a href="/wiki/Teyana_Taylor" title="Teyana Taylor">Teyana Taylor</a>, along with productions from <a href="/wiki/No_I.D." title="No I.D.">No I.D.</a>, Infamous, and Hagler.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 4, 2015, Staples released the first single from his debut album, called "Señorita". He later announced it that his debut studio album would be titled <i><a href="/wiki/Summertime_%2706" title="Summertime &#39;06">Summertime '06</a></i>.,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In June 2015, Staples was named as one of the ten rappers of <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a>'</i>s "2015 Freshman Class", and was featured on the cover alongside fellow up-and-coming rappers <a href="/wiki/Dej_Loaf" title="Dej Loaf">Dej Loaf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fetty_Wap" title="Fetty Wap">Fetty Wap</a>, <a href="/wiki/GoldLink" title="GoldLink">GoldLink</a>, <a href="/wiki/K_Camp" title="K Camp">K Camp</a>, <a href="/wiki/OG_Maco" title="OG Maco">OG Maco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raury" title="Raury">Raury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shy_Glizzy" title="Shy Glizzy">Shy Glizzy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tink_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tink (rapper)">Tink</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kidd_Kidd" title="Kidd Kidd">Kidd Kidd</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl2015_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl2015-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On June 15, Staples released the second single from his debut album, "Get Paid" featuring Desi Mo. On June 22, he released the album's third and final single, "<a href="/wiki/Norf_Norf" title="Norf Norf">Norf Norf</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The track reached viral prominence after a video of a mom tearfully complaining about the song became popular on social media.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The album was released on June 30, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork.com_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork.com-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> It received widespread acclaim and debuted at number 39 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932017:_Prima_Donna_and_Big_Fish_Theory"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2017:_Prima_Donna_and_Big_Fish_Theory">2016–2017: <i>Prima Donna</i> and <i>Big Fish Theory</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vince_Staples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2016–2017: Prima Donna and Big Fish Theory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Vince_Staples_crop.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Vince_Staples_crop.jpg/180px-Vince_Staples_crop.jpg" decoding="async" width="180" height="367" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Vince_Staples_crop.jpg/270px-Vince_Staples_crop.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Vince_Staples_crop.jpg/360px-Vince_Staples_crop.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1888" data-file-height="3852" /></a><figcaption>Staples performing in 2017</figcaption></figure>
<p>On February 23, 2016, Staples was announced as part of the line up for the 2016 Osheaga Music Festival.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On August 25, 2016, Staples released his second EP, the seven-track <i><a href="/wiki/Prima_Donna_(EP)" title="Prima Donna (EP)">Prima Donna</a></i>, which was accompanied by a short film.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> On February 3, 2017, Staples released "BagBak", the first single from his next studio album. A remix of the song was later featured in the trailer for the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Studios" title="Marvel Studios">Marvel Studios</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Panther_(film)" title="Black Panther (film)">Black Panther</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> On March 23, 2017, he was featured on the <a href="/wiki/Gorillaz" title="Gorillaz">Gorillaz</a> track "<a href="/wiki/Ascension_(Gorillaz_song)" title="Ascension (Gorillaz song)">Ascension</a>" from their album <i><a href="/wiki/Humanz" title="Humanz">Humanz</a></i>. In an interview on <a href="/wiki/Zane_Lowe" title="Zane Lowe">Zane Lowe</a>'s show Beats 1, he announced his upcoming album would be called <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Fish_Theory" title="Big Fish Theory">Big Fish Theory</a></i> and released an accompanying single, "Big Fish",<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> which was followed by the album's third single, "Rain Come Down" on June 8, 2017, featuring <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>. The album was released on June 23 and received with widespread critical acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He and rapper <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a> announced on November 15 that they would be doing a tour around North America from January 26 to March 4, 2018. Staples then collaborated with film composer <a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a> on a remix of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League_Anthem" title="UEFA Champions League Anthem">UEFA Champions League Anthem</a> for the <i><a href="/wiki/FIFA_19" title="FIFA 19">FIFA 19</a></i> reveal trailer.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On December 15, Staples and celebrated singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Billie_Eilish" title="Billie Eilish">Billie Eilish</a> released the single "<a href="/wiki/%26Burn" title="&amp;Burn">&amp;Burn</a>", which would later appear on the reissue of Eilish's EP <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Smile_at_Me" title="Don&#39;t Smile at Me">Don't Smile at Me</a></i> later that month.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The song was <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_certification" title="Music recording certification">certified</a> gold in the United States by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) on April 2, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2018.E2.80.93present:_FM.21.2C_The_Vince_Staples_Show.2C_self-titled_album_and_Ramona_Park_Broke_My_Heart"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–present:_FM!,_The_Vince_Staples_Show,_self-titled_album_and_Ramona_Park_Broke_My_Heart">2018–present: <i>FM!</i>, <i>The Vince Staples Show</i>, self-titled album and <i>Ramona Park Broke My Heart</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vince_Staples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2018–present: FM!, The Vince Staples Show, self-titled album and Ramona Park Broke My Heart">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Vince_Staples_-_First_Avenue_-_Rap_-_Minnesota_-_Def_Jam_-_Big_Fish_Theory_(40607125092).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Vince_Staples_-_First_Avenue_-_Rap_-_Minnesota_-_Def_Jam_-_Big_Fish_Theory_%2840607125092%29.jpg/180px-Vince_Staples_-_First_Avenue_-_Rap_-_Minnesota_-_Def_Jam_-_Big_Fish_Theory_%2840607125092%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="180" height="124" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Vince_Staples_-_First_Avenue_-_Rap_-_Minnesota_-_Def_Jam_-_Big_Fish_Theory_%2840607125092%29.jpg/270px-Vince_Staples_-_First_Avenue_-_Rap_-_Minnesota_-_Def_Jam_-_Big_Fish_Theory_%2840607125092%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Vince_Staples_-_First_Avenue_-_Rap_-_Minnesota_-_Def_Jam_-_Big_Fish_Theory_%2840607125092%29.jpg/360px-Vince_Staples_-_First_Avenue_-_Rap_-_Minnesota_-_Def_Jam_-_Big_Fish_Theory_%2840607125092%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3419" data-file-height="2357" /></a><figcaption>Staples performing in March 2018</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2018, Staples set up a <a href="/wiki/GoFundMe" title="GoFundMe">GoFundMe</a> page titled "Get the Fuck Off My Dick" seeking $2 million as a response to people criticizing his work; they could pay to have him retire early. However, it was briefly taken down given the low response.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The money made from the page was subsequently donated to the Michelle Obama Neighborhood Library in Long Beach. On October 2, Staples released his third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/FM!" title="FM!">FM!</a></i>. Produced primarily by <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Beats" title="Kenny Beats">Kenny Beats</a>, the album is framed as a radio station takeover, featuring recurring skits hosted by Los Angeles radio host <a href="/wiki/Big_Boy_(radio_host)" title="Big Boy (radio host)">Big Boy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> In December 2018, Staples contributed the track "Home" to the <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man:_Into_the_Spider-Verse_(soundtracks)" class="mw-redirect" title="Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (soundtracks)">Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse soundtrack</a>, which was originally previewed in the movie's trailer in December 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, he released three singles: "So What?", "Sheet Music" and "Ad 01: Hell Bound", with the first two singles released alongside episodes from his YouTube Show <i>The Vince Staples Show</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2021, Staples said he was scheduled to release an album in mid-2021, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples_(album)" title="Vince Staples (album)">Vince Staples</a></i>. It was eventually released on July 9, to critical acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Staples was also developing a <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> show.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Staples released an album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Ramona_Park_Broke_My_Heart" title="Ramona Park Broke My Heart">Ramona Park Broke My Heart</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Motown_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Motown Records">Motown Records</a> on April 8, 2022 to further acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_sponsorship">Corporate sponsorship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vince_Staples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Corporate sponsorship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since 2015, Staples has appeared in advertising campaigns for <a href="/wiki/Sprite_(soft_drink)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sprite (soft drink)">Sprite</a> and promoted the brand through his Twitter feed.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vince_Staples&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 14, 2016, Staples announced his assistance in a <a href="/wiki/YMCA" title="YMCA">YMCA</a> program that will benefit young people in <a href="/wiki/North_Long_Beach" class="mw-redirect" title="North Long Beach">North Long Beach</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-YMCA_program_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YMCA_program-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> The Youth Institute would teach graphic design, 3D printing, product design, music production and film making to 20 eighth and ninth graders at Hamilton Middle School.<sup id="cite_ref-YMCA_program_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YMCA_program-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Staples donated an undisclosed amount to the program.<sup id="cite_ref-YMCA_program_52-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YMCA_program-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
