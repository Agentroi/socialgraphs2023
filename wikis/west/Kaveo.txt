<p><b>The Mossie</b> was an American rap group from <a href="/wiki/Vallejo,_California" title="Vallejo, California">Vallejo, California</a>, with members: Kaveo, Mugzi and Tap Dat Ass. They first appeared together on <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>'s 1993 EP, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mail_Man" title="The Mail Man">The Mail Man</a></i>. Before dropping their 1997 debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Have_Heart_Have_Money" title="Have Heart Have Money">Have Heart Have Money</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/Sick_Wid_It_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Sick Wid It Records">Sick Wid It</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>, they appeared together on several other Sick Wid It releases, including: <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hogg_in_Me" title="The Hogg in Me">The Hogg in Me</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hemp_Museum_(album)" title="The Hemp Museum (album)">The Hemp Museum</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Southwest_Riders" title="Southwest Riders">Southwest Riders</a></i>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mossie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Mossie's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Have_Heart_Have_Money" title="Have Heart Have Money">Have Heart Have Money</a></i>, was released in 1997 on <a href="/wiki/Sick_Wid_It_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Sick Wid It Records">Sick Wid It</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>. The album peaked at number 35 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers"><i>Billboard</i> Top Heatseekers</a> and at number 62 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums"><i>Billboard</i> Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AMG_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was <a href="/wiki/Executive_producer" title="Executive producer">executive produced</a> by <a href="/wiki/B-Legit" title="B-Legit">B-Legit</a> and <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a> and features guest performances by <a href="/wiki/Celly_Cel" title="Celly Cel">Celly Cel</a>, Levitti, <a href="/wiki/187_Fac" title="187 Fac">187 Fac</a>, Silk-E, <a href="/wiki/G-Note" class="mw-redirect" title="G-Note">G-Note</a>, B-Legit and E-40. Along with a single, a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> was released to promote the album, "Nobody Can Be You But You",<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> featuring E-40 and <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo appearances</a> by B-Legit, Celly Cel, <a href="/wiki/D-Shot" title="D-Shot">D-Shot</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suga-T" title="Suga-T">Suga-T</a>. The group then went on to appear on several <a href="/wiki/Bay_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Bay Area">Bay Area</a> artists' albums and compilations, together as a group and as solo artists.
</p><p>In 2001, the group resurfaced with their second studio album, <i>Point Seen, Money Gone</i>. It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Ant_Banks" title="Ant Banks">Ant Banks</a>, Bosko, Kevin Gardner, Redwine, <a href="/wiki/Sean_T_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sean T (rapper)">Sean T</a> and Tone Capone. Three years later Young Mugzi (a.k.a. Mugzilla) dropped a soundtrack on his own label, 30-30 Records titled, <i>Lifestyles of the Disobayish</i>. The album features guest performances by <a href="/wiki/Turf_Talk" title="Turf Talk">Turf Talk</a>, E-40, <a href="/wiki/San_Quinn" title="San Quinn">San Quinn</a>, Celly Cel, <a href="/wiki/Messy_Marv" title="Messy Marv">Messy Marv</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spice_1" title="Spice 1">Spice 1</a>, Laroo and The Mossie.
</p>
