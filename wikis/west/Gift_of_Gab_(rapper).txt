<p><b>Timothy Jerome Parker</b> (October 7, 1970 – June 18, 2021),<sup id="cite_ref-rolling-stone_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rolling-stone-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Gift of Gab</b>, was an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> best known for performing in the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">Bay Area</a> hip hop duo <a href="/wiki/Blackalicious" title="Blackalicious">Blackalicious</a> along with DJ Chief Xcel. He was also a member of <a href="/wiki/Quannum_Projects" title="Quannum Projects">Quannum Projects</a>, a Bay Area hip hop crew and record label, and performed and recorded as a solo artist.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gift_of_Gab_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Timothy Jerome Parker was born in 1970 and raised in the <a href="/wiki/Pacoima" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacoima">Pacoima</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-laist_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laist-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He had two brothers and a sister at time of death, he also had two sisters that passed before him. He attended high school in Sacramento with his future bandmate Xavier "Chief Xcel" Mosley.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaborations">Collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gift_of_Gab_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gift of Gab was featured on <a href="/wiki/Galactic" title="Galactic">Galactic</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/From_the_Corner_to_the_Block" title="From the Corner to the Block">From the Corner to the Block</a></i> (2007). He collaborated with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Z-Trip" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Z-Trip">DJ Z-Trip</a> on the track "Go Hard" for the <i><a href="/wiki/All-Pro_Football_2K8" title="All-Pro Football 2K8">All-Pro Football 2K8</a></i> Soundtrack. He also released <i>The Prelude</i> EP with <a href="/wiki/Lateef_the_Truthspeaker" title="Lateef the Truthspeaker">Lateef the Truthspeaker</a> and Headnodic as <a href="/wiki/The_Mighty_Underdogs" title="The Mighty Underdogs">The Mighty Underdogs</a>. He went on to do a song called "All In" with <a href="/wiki/The_Grouch_(rapper)" title="The Grouch (rapper)">The Grouch</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Eligh" title="Eligh">Eligh</a>. He was also featured on the track "The People Tree" on the 2009 N.A.S.A. release "The Spirit of Apollo".
</p><p>In 2002, Gift of Gab contributed to <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_%2B_Riot:_The_Music_and_Spirit_of_Fela_Kuti" title="Red Hot + Riot: The Music and Spirit of Fela Kuti">Red Hot + Riot: The Music and Spirit of Fela Kuti</a></i>, a compilation CD released by the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Organization" title="Red Hot Organization">Red Hot Organization</a> in tribute to the music and work of Nigerian musician, <a href="/wiki/Fela_Kuti" title="Fela Kuti">Fela Kuti</a>, that raised money for various charities devoted to raising AIDS awareness and fighting the disease. He collaborated with hip hop artist <a href="/wiki/Lateef_the_Truthspeaker" title="Lateef the Truthspeaker">Lateef the Truthspeaker</a> to remake Fela Kuti's song, "Kalakuta Show."
</p><p>In addition to this, Gift of Gab collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Del_the_Funky_Homosapien" title="Del the Funky Homosapien">Del the Funky Homosapien</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brother_Ali" title="Brother Ali">Brother Ali</a> on the track "Dreamin'" on his solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Escape_2_Mars" title="Escape 2 Mars">Escape 2 Mars</a></i>, as well as making tracks with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Vadim" title="DJ Vadim">DJ Vadim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cut_Chemist" title="Cut Chemist">Cut Chemist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Harper" title="Ben Harper">Ben Harper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chali_2na" title="Chali 2na">Chali 2na</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_5" title="Jurassic 5">Jurassic 5</a>.
</p><p>He also featured on fellow San Francisco rapper <a href="/wiki/Watsky" class="mw-redirect" title="Watsky">Watsky</a>'s song "Everything Turns Gold", and the track "They Warned Us" by Watsky's jazz-rap fusion band Invisible Inc.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life_and_death">Later life and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gift_of_Gab_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later life and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the years leading up to 2020, Parker had suffered from <a href="/wiki/Kidney_failure" title="Kidney failure">kidney failure</a> and was receiving <a href="/wiki/Kidney_dialysis" title="Kidney dialysis">dialysis</a> multiple times a week. In January 2020, Parker received a kidney transplant. Parker died on June 18, 2021, of natural causes.<sup id="cite_ref-laist_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laist-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
