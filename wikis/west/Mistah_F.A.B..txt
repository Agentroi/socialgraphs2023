<p><b>Stanley Petey Cox</b> (born January 23, 1982), better known by his stage name <b>Mistah F.A.B.</b> (backronym for <b>Money Is Something To Always Have–Forever After Bread</b>), is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, songwriter, entrepreneur, <a href="/wiki/Community_organizer" class="mw-redirect" title="Community organizer">community organizer</a> and activist.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>F.A.B.'s music career began in the late 1990s when he was discovered by Jazzy Jim and Gary Archer.  He was featured on a number of <a href="/wiki/Mac_Dre" title="Mac Dre">Mac Dre</a>'s Thizz Nation mixtape compilations and quickly gained prominence in the San Francisco Bay Area <a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">hyphy</a> movement.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> With burgeoning national interest in Bay Area hip-hop around 2005, his single in December "Yellow Bus" shortly followed by the album <i>Yellow Bus</i> in January 2006, F.A.B. became the subject of a major-label bidding war, signing with <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a> that September, under subsidiary, <a href="/wiki/Thizz_Entertainment" title="Thizz Entertainment">Thizz Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Howbeit F.A.B., who had deep independent ties with the music business in North Oakland, decided to forgo his major label deal and ventured out to launch his own imprint Faeva Afta Music in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>F.A.B. is known as one of the most prominent and colorful figures of the hyphy scene since the late 2000s, and is sometimes hailed as the scene's "Crown Prince".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He quickly become a central figure of the hyphy movement, a musical and cultural offshoot of hip-hop from the Bay Area that carries a bass-heavy beat, blaring synthesizers and an emphasis on having fun. When F.A.B. boasted of doing "the dummy retarded" in "Super Sic Wit It," he was describing an emerging aesthetic and philosophical ideal evolving within the Rap culture at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>F.A.B. garnered worldwide recognition on other fronts as well particularly as a battle rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> F.A.B. defeated many MCs in the niche genre, including <a href="/wiki/Clyde_Carson" title="Clyde Carson">Clyde Carson</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Team_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Team (band)">The Team</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jin_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jin (rapper)">Jin</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruff Ryders">Ruff Ryders</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royce_Da_5%279" class="mw-redirect" title="Royce Da 5&#39;9">Royce Da 5'9</a> of <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse_(hip_hop_group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Slaughterhouse (hip hop group)">Slaughterhouse</a>. In October 2011, Mistah F.A.B. was featured in a mobile battle rap game, <a href="/wiki/Battle_Rap_Stars" title="Battle Rap Stars">Battle Rap Stars</a> by <a href="/wiki/Jump_Shot_Media" title="Jump Shot Media">Jump Shot Media</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, F.A.B reemerged in the competitive battle rap circuit and went up against Arsonal Da Rebel in the Battle of the Bay 7.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>F.A.B. is an accredited multi-platinum songwriter and record producer, who has collaborated with major acts such as <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Too_$hort" class="mw-redirect" title="Too $hort">Too $hort</a>, <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Bellinger" title="Eric Bellinger">Eric Bellinger</a>. He co-wrote and published record charting platinum lead single Headband, off of <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a> third album <i><a href="/wiki/Underground_Luxury" title="Underground Luxury">Underground Luxury</a></i>, released in December 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> F.A.B. went on to co-write "Loyal" by Chris Brown, and the single was rewarded "Best Hip Hop Song of the Year" by the 2014 Soul Train Awards.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>F.A.B.'s community activism and philanthropic efforts have been highly visible throughout the years.  Most notably, he hosts and organizes annual Thanksgiving turkey giveaways, backpack and school supply drives, holiday toys event and various charitable events benefiting Cancer and Domestic Violence.  In August 2013, Mistah F.A.B. was named one of "10 Great Rappers With Great Charities" by Green Label a Mountain Dew Venture.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, Mistah F.A.B. was recognized by Oakland Mayor Jean Quan, who declared Saturday, February 8, 2014 "Stanley Cox aka Mistah F.A.B. Day" in recognition of the multi-talented hometown hero's efforts to give back to the community.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="1982.E2.80.932000:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1982–2000:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings">1982–2000: Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistah_F.A.B.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 1982–2000: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stanley P. Cox was born on January 23, 1982, in Oakland, California, the son of Stanley Cox Sr. and Desrie A. Jeffery. He was raised by his mother and grandparents.  F.A.B.'s father was incarcerated for much of his childhood. His father died of AIDS-related complications when F.A.B. was twelve years old.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He is the cousin of <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a> running back <a href="/wiki/Marshawn_Lynch" title="Marshawn Lynch">Marshawn Lynch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He attended <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Technical_High_School" title="Oakland Technical High School">Oakland Technical High School</a> and transferred to Emery High School where he later graduated. As a teenager, F.A.B. was an avid basketball player and point guard of his high school team. F.A.B. was forced to quit basketball after he sustained an injury on the court.
</p><p>Soon after, he began to enter local freestyle rap competitions and earn a reputation as a formidable rapper and freestyler.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistah_F.A.B.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mistah F.A.B., began his musical rise in Oakland, CA, as a young artist signed to Straight Hits Entertainment, where he dropped his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Nig-Latin" title="Nig-Latin">Nig-Latin</a></i> in 2003. Although this album garnered some success and recognition, F.A.B.'s recognition came from his skills as a battle rapper.  F.A.B. won battles against the likes of <a href="/wiki/Clyde_Carson" title="Clyde Carson">Clyde Carson</a> from <a href="/wiki/The_Team_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Team (band)">The Team</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royce_Da_5%279" class="mw-redirect" title="Royce Da 5&#39;9">Royce Da 5'9</a>. His popularity lead to F.A.B. getting signed to <a href="/wiki/Mac_Dre" title="Mac Dre">Mac Dre</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Thizz_Entertainment" title="Thizz Entertainment">Thizz Entertainment</a> in 2004. Under Thizz ENT, F.A.B. blossomed as a front runner in the <a href="/wiki/Bay_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Bay Area">Bay Area</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">hyphy</a> movement. With songs like "Super Sic Wit It," "N.E.W. Oakland," and "Kicked Out The Club," F.A.B.'s <i><a href="/wiki/Son_of_a_Pimp" title="Son of a Pimp">Son of a Pimp</a></i> album was an instant success around the Bay Area, with featured collaborations Dre, E-40, Turf Talk, and G-Stack of Oakland heroes the Delinquents—as well as Kanye West.
</p><p>However, a series of obstacles staggered F.A.B.'s career momentum. KMEL-FM, the Bay's top urban station, imposed an unofficial but crippling blacklist of F.A.B.'s songs and guest appearances, due to petty personal grievances as well as the perceived slight of his successful show on a rival station, thereby significantly undermining his hometown visibility. Later, his breakout single, "Ghost Ride It," was the subject of controversy on two fronts – a national clamor over the hyphy practice of "<a href="/wiki/Ghost_riding" title="Ghost riding">ghost riding</a>" described in the song (throwing a car in neutral, then jumping out and walking alongside or riding on the roof as it continues to move)<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and Columbia Pictures' threatened legal action over the use of the Ghostbusters logo in the video (the track samples the movie's theme song). This caused the video to be first heavily censored and then pulled from television altogether.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Da Yellow Bus Rydah, F.A.B.'s debut studio album for Atlantic, originally scheduled for spring of 2007, was delayed by over two years (and most recently slated for a 2009 release under the revised title The Bus Ride). However, thanks to an unusually liberal contract, he was able to keep busy with work on independent labels, instead releasing a Thizz full-length album, Da Baydestrian, in May 2007, and continuing his steady stream of mixtapes and album-length collaborations with G-Stack, Turf Talk, the Alchemist, and Glasses Malone through 2015 (including Hyphy Ain't Dead, a collaboration with Turf Talk).<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2011, Mistah F.A.B. was featured in a mobile battle rap game, <a href="/wiki/Battle_Rap_Stars" title="Battle Rap Stars">Battle Rap Stars</a> by <a href="/wiki/Jump_Shot_Media" title="Jump Shot Media">Jump Shot Media</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/MC_Hammer" title="MC Hammer">MC Hammer</a> released "Raider Nation (<a href="/wiki/Oakland_Raiders" title="Oakland Raiders">Oakland Raiders</a> Anthem)" along with a video in late 2013 and "<a href="/wiki/MC_Hammer#.22Raider_Nation.22_.26_.22All_In_My_Mind.22_.282013.2F2014.29" title="MC Hammer">All In My Mind</a>" (which samples "<a href="/wiki/Summer_Breeze_(song)#Isley_Brothers_version" title="Summer Breeze (song)">Summer Breeze</a>" by <a href="/wiki/The_Isley_Brothers" title="The Isley Brothers">The Isley Brothers</a>) in early 2014 with his newly formed group called Oakland Fight Club which features Mistah F.A.B.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, F.A.B co-wrote and published two record charting platinum lead singles Headband,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> off of <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a> third album Underground Luxury, and Loyal<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> by Chris Brown.
</p><p>In 2015, F.A.B reemerged in the competitive freestyle circuit in a lyrical exchange with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Arsonal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Arsonal (page does not exist)">Arsonal</a> at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_the_Bay_7&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Battle of the Bay 7 (page does not exist)">Battle of the Bay 7</a><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942"><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Personally, I am going to say how I feel and exhibit that in my music.  Im not afraid to lose.  I can take a chance and start again if I have to. I will give it my all because Im not afraid to go to zero.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
