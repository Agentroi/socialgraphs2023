<p><b>Dominique Marquis Cole</b> (born March 9, 1991), known professionally as <b>Domo Genesis</b>, is an American rapper and songwriter. He is best known for being a member of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> collective <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a>. He has since continued his solo career, releasing his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Genesis_(Domo_Genesis_album)" title="Genesis (Domo Genesis album)">Genesis</a></i> in 2016 and founding his own record label in 2018.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domo_Genesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932010:_Rolling_Papers"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2010:_Rolling_Papers">2009–2010: <i>Rolling Papers</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domo_Genesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2009–2010: Rolling Papers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Domo Genesis joined <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a> in mid-2009 because he was very good friends with <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a>. His debut mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Papers_(mixtape)" title="Rolling Papers (mixtape)">Rolling Papers</a></i>, was released on August 30, 2010. Production was done entirely by Tyler, the Creator, <a href="/wiki/Left_Brain" class="mw-redirect" title="Left Brain">Left Brain</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Syd_tha_Kyd" class="mw-redirect" title="Syd tha Kyd">Syd tha Kyd</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2011.E2.80.932012:_Under_the_Influence_and_No_Idols"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–2012:_Under_the_Influence_and_No_Idols">2011–2012: <i>Under the Influence</i> and <i>No Idols</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domo_Genesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011–2012: Under the Influence and No Idols">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His second mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Under_the_Influence_(mixtape)" title="Under the Influence (mixtape)">Under the Influence</a></i>, was released on September 20, 2011. Many saw this second release as a vast improvement over his first, citing "it's focused and rarely lacking in style — or yeah, SWAG.".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> On February 9, 2012, Domo released a track with <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, titled "Ground Up".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On February 22, 2012, it was announced that Domo Genesis would have a strain of <a href="/wiki/Marijuana" class="mw-redirect" title="Marijuana">medical marijuana</a> named after him, <i>Domo OG</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Domo Genesis made his TV debut in the tenth episode of the first season of Odd Future's show, <a href="/wiki/Loiter_Squad" title="Loiter Squad">Loiter Squad</a>, as Young Gunshot, his <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_rap" title="Gangsta rap">gangsta rap</a> alter ego. Domo Genesis and <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">Alchemist</a>'s collaboration mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/No_Idols" title="No Idols">No Idols</a></i>, was released for free download on July 31, 2012. The first official single, "<a href="/wiki/Elimination_Chamber_(song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Elimination Chamber (song)">Elimination Chamber</a>", which features <a href="/wiki/Earl_Sweatshirt" title="Earl Sweatshirt">Earl Sweatshirt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples" title="Vince Staples">Vince Staples</a> and <a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson" title="Action Bronson">Action Bronson</a>, was released on July 16, 2012.
</p>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932014:_MellowHigh_and_Under_the_Influence_2"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2014:_MellowHigh_and_Under_the_Influence_2">2013–2014: <i>MellowHigh</i> and <i>Under the Influence 2</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domo_Genesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013–2014: MellowHigh and Under the Influence 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 4, 2013, Domo, with fellow Odd Future members Tyler, The Creator and Earl Sweatshirt, performed the song "Rusty" off of Tyler, The Creator's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Wolf_(Tyler,_The_Creator_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Wolf (Tyler, The Creator album)">Wolf</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/The_Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" class="mw-redirect" title="The Late Show with David Letterman">The Late Show with David Letterman</a>. On October 31, 2013, Domo released <i><a href="/wiki/MellowHigh_(album)" title="MellowHigh (album)">MellowHigh</a></i> his collaboration album with <a href="/wiki/MellowHype" title="MellowHype">MellowHype</a> as <a href="/wiki/MellowHigh" title="MellowHigh">MellowHigh</a>. The album debuted at number 89 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 13, 2013, Domo Genesis revealed that he was working on a new mixtape <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_the_Influence_2&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Under the Influence 2 (page does not exist)">Under the Influence 2</a></i> and his debut studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-stupiddope.com_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stupiddope.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Then on April 3, 2014, Genesis said that he plans to release both projects before the end of 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He then hinted on Twitter that the release of <i>Under the Influence 2</i> would be on November 5, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.932017:_Genesis_and_Red_Corolla"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–2017:_Genesis_and_Red_Corolla">2015–2017: <i>Genesis</i> and <i>Red Corolla</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domo_Genesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015–2017: Genesis and Red Corolla">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 4, 2016, Domo released the song "KWYM" (Keep Working Young Man) on SoundCloud. This was then followed by the release of Domo's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Genesis_(Domo_Genesis_album)" title="Genesis (Domo Genesis album)">Genesis</a></i> in March 2016, with the <a href="/wiki/Anderson_.Paak" title="Anderson .Paak">Anderson .Paak</a>-assisted lead single, "Dapper" being released weeks prior. From May 29 to July 24, 2016, Domo embarked on the <i>2016 Chore Tour</i> in support of his album release.
</p><p>On June 16, 2017, Domo released the mixtape <i>Red Corolla</i>, which featured guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/King_Chip" title="King Chip">King Chip</a> and <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2018.E2.80.93present:_Aren.27t_U_Glad_You.27re_U.3F.2C_Facade_Records_and_Intros.2C_Outros_.26_Interludes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–present:_Aren't_U_Glad_You're_U?,_Facade_Records_and_Intros,_Outros_&amp;_Interludes">2018–present: <i>Aren't U Glad You're U?</i>, <i>Facade Records</i> and <i>Intros, Outros &amp; Interludes</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Domo_Genesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2018–present: Aren&#039;t U Glad You&#039;re U?, Facade Records and Intros, Outros &amp; Interludes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 19, 2018, Domo released the mixtape <i>Aren't U Glad You're U?</i> independently on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>. Executive produced by rapper/producer <a href="/wiki/Evidence_(musician)" title="Evidence (musician)">Evidence</a>, the mixtape featured a sole guest appearance from rapper <a href="/wiki/Phonte" title="Phonte">Phonte</a>. That same day, the official music video for the song "Me vs. Me" was released on the official <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a> YouTube channel. 
</p><p>Later in the year, Domo released the EP <i>Facade Records</i>, which was executive produced by production duo <a href="/wiki/Mike_%26_Keys" title="Mike &amp; Keys">Mike &amp; Keys</a>, with features from <a href="/wiki/Buddy_(rapper)" title="Buddy (rapper)">Buddy</a>, <a href="/wiki/IDK_(rapper)" title="IDK (rapper)">IDK</a>, <a href="/wiki/King_Chip" title="King Chip">King Chip</a> (under his previous name Chip tha Ripper) and <a href="/wiki/Cozz" title="Cozz">Cozz</a>. This project would expand into a supergroup between Domo and Mike &amp; Keys, releasing two EPs <i>Just in Case</i> and <i>Just in Case 2</i> in 2020.
</p><p>In 2021, Domo appeared on Tyler, the Creator's sixth album <i><a href="/wiki/Call_Me_If_You_Get_Lost" title="Call Me If You Get Lost">Call Me If You Get Lost</a></i>, on the song "MANIFESTO". 
</p><p>On July 29, 2022, Domo released another Evidence-produced project titled <i>Intros, Outros &amp; Interludes</i>. The mixtape featured guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Sage_Elsesser" title="Sage Elsesser">Navy Blue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Remy_Banks" title="Remy Banks">Remy Banks</a>, Evidence and <a href="/wiki/Boldy_James" title="Boldy James">Boldy James</a>.
</p><p>On April 20, 2023, Domo re-released <i><a href="/wiki/No_Idols" title="No Idols">No Idols</a></i>, his collaboration mixtape with <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>, to all streaming platforms.
</p><p>Similarly, on June 30, 2023, he re-released his mixtape <i>Red Corolla</i> to all streaming platforms.
</p>
