<p><b>Chauncey Alexander Hollis Jr.</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born May 21, 1987), better known by his stage name <b>Hit-Boy</b>, is an American record producer, rapper, singer, and songwriter. In May 2011, he signed a two-year deal with <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a> production branch. With this stimulus, he produced chart-topping singles such as the Diamond Certified "<a href="/wiki/Niggas_in_Paris" title="Niggas in Paris">Niggas in Paris</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Trophies_(song)" title="Trophies (song)">Trophies</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Clique_(song)" title="Clique (song)">Clique</a>" by <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/Racks_in_the_Middle" title="Racks in the Middle">Racks in the Middle</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/Roddy_Ricch" title="Roddy Ricch">Roddy Ricch</a>. "Racks in the Middle" won <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance">Best Rap Performance</a> at the <a href="/wiki/62nd_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="62nd Annual Grammy Awards">2020 Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2012, Hit-Boy signed a recording contract with <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> and subsequently founded his own record label imprint, Hits Since '87.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He has since expanded his production catalog with singles such as "<a href="/wiki/Drop_the_World" title="Drop the World">Drop the World</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Sorry_(Beyonc%C3%A9_song)" title="Sorry (Beyoncé song)">Sorry</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/Sicko_Mode" title="Sicko Mode">Sicko Mode</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>, all of which have been <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified platinum</a> or higher by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA).
</p>

<p>Hollis got his start on <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> when he received a message from <a href="/wiki/Polow_Da_Don" class="mw-redirect" title="Polow Da Don">Polow Da Don</a> stating simply "Let's get this paper, pimp", and reached out to say he wanted to meet Hollis and discuss signing him professionally.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He has produced for big-name artists such as <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown_(American_entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chris Brown (American entertainer)">Chris Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Selena_Gomez" title="Selena Gomez">Selena Gomez</a> and several others. The California native is one of the founding members of the collective brand Surf Club (along with fellow beat makers Chase N.Cashe, BCarr, and Chili Chill). Surf Club has become a team to host a number of artists as well as songwriters and other producers. Surf Club originated in southern California. They got their start in the industry in 2007, after linking with <a href="/wiki/Polow_Da_Don" class="mw-redirect" title="Polow Da Don">Polow Da Don</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Zone_4_(record_label)" title="Zone 4 (record label)">Zone 4</a>, an imprint of <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>. He began his work with Kanye West when he produced his GOOD Friday track, "Christmas in Harlem",<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Musiq_Soulchild" title="Musiq Soulchild">Musiq Soulchild</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cyhi_Da_Prynce" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyhi Da Prynce">Cyhi Da Prynce</a>. On May 2, 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> he signed to <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a> Label, produced the single "<a href="/wiki/Niggas_in_Paris" title="Niggas in Paris">Niggas in Paris</a>", on the collaborative "<a href="/wiki/Watch_the_Throne" title="Watch the Throne">Watch the Throne</a>" album created by <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, and produced the singles "<a href="/wiki/Goldie_(song)" title="Goldie (song)">Goldie</a>" and "1 Train" for <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a>.
</p><p>On June 7, 2012, Hollis released his first official song in which he raps, the promotional single "Jay-Z Interview", which was produced by <a href="/wiki/Bink!" class="mw-redirect" title="Bink!">Bink!</a>. Later in July 2012, Hollis was featured rapping on <a href="/wiki/CyHi_The_Prynce" class="mw-redirect" title="CyHi The Prynce">CyHi The Prynce</a>'s mixtape <i>Ivy League Club</i>, on the song "Entourage". Hollis then released the self-produced track "Old School Caddy", which features fellow GOOD Music member <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a>. These two tracks appeared on his first full-length project, a mixtape titled <i>HITstory</i>, which was released for free download on his official website. On December 23, 2012, Hollis secured a recording contract with <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>, under the record label Blueprint Group, along with the L.E.P. Bogus Boys. Soon after, he tweeted: "2012 has been great to me and my fam. Feeling more blessed than ever. Excited to see what God has planned for 2013. TU."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On January 23, 2013, it was announced that his record label Hits Since '87, is now an imprint of <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 29, 2013, he announced that he is no longer signed to GOOD Music, but still on good terms with everybody in the label.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, Hit-Boy and Local Los Angeles rapper <a href="/wiki/Dom_Kennedy" title="Dom Kennedy">Dom Kennedy</a> came together to work on a joint mixtape named <i>Half-A-Mil</i> EP, subsequently adopting the name (dropping the EP) as an alias for the duo. <i>Half-A-Mil</i> EP was released on December 11, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape consisted of five songs, with "100 Rounds" being the lead single off the mixtape. Following the release of their first mixtape, the pair decided to put out another mixtape a few months after their previous mixtape. <i>Half-A-Mil 2</i> EP released on February 24, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape contained six songs with one guest feature from B.Carr. The duo would take a break and would not release music under the <a href="/wiki/Half-A-Mil" class="mw-redirect" title="Half-A-Mil">Half-A-Mil</a> name. After some time, the frequent collaborators released <i>Half-A-Mil 3</i> EP, continuing the mixtape series and being the last one in the saga. <i>Half-A-Mil</i> 3 EP was August 8, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape only had two feature from <a href="/wiki/24hrs_(rapper)" title="24hrs (rapper)">24hrs</a> on "Don't run" and <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Miller" class="mw-redirect" title="Quentin Miller">Quentin Miller</a> on the tracks "Might as Well" and "In The Hills". Also, the duo released a music video for both songs on September 7, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> After releasing all three mixtapes, the pair finally released their first commercial album, <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_of_Half-a-Mil" class="mw-redirect" title="Courtesy of Half-a-Mil">Courtesy of Half-a-Mil</a>, on November 29, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The album contains 14 tracks and numerous features such as <a href="/wiki/Larry_June" title="Larry June">Larry June</a>, TYuS, B.Carr, Jay 305, and <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>. The Tandem would go on to take a hiatus and not release any new music. Nearly two and half years later, the group would release their second studio. "Also Known As" was released on July 31, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The album consists of 11 tracks and features from <a href="/wiki/03_Greedo" title="03 Greedo">03 Greedo</a>, <a href="/wiki/24hrs_(rapper)" title="24hrs (rapper)">24hrs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>.
</p><p>From 2020 to 2022, Hit-Boy teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> to executively produce the King's Disease series, <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Disease" title="King&#39;s Disease">King's Disease</a>, <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Disease_II" title="King&#39;s Disease II">King's Disease II</a>, and most recently <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Disease_III" title="King&#39;s Disease III">King's Disease III</a> to close out 2022. King's Disease 1 and 2 were consecutively nominated for Grammy Award for Best Rap Album of the Year, with <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Disease" title="King&#39;s Disease">King's Disease</a> winning and awarding Nas with the rapper's first Grammy award throughout his storied career. They also collaborated on Nas's fifteenth studio album, <a href="/wiki/Magic_(Nas_album)" title="Magic (Nas album)">Magic</a> in late 2022.
</p><p>In 2023, Hit-Boy has worked on and released a multitude of projects- including kicking off a series of his own albums titled <i>SURF OR DROWN</i> in March, featuring appearances from <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Currensy" title="Currensy">Curren$y</a> &amp; more. Not even three months later, Hit-Boy followed up the album with its sequel, <i>SURF OR DROWN Vol. 2</i>, on Father's Day in June alongside his father, Big Hit, who appears on nine of the ten songs. In May, Big Hit was released from prison after serving nine years and got right back to the studio to pick up where he left off. Before his incarceration in 2014, Big Hit had grown a fanbase following the release of the hit songs, <i>Grindin' My Whole Life</i> and <i>G'z Don't Cry</i>, both produced by Hit-Boy. <i>SURF OR DROWN Vol. 2</i> is a celebration of the reunification of the father son rap duo, and the continuation of Big Hit's career in the music industry.
</p><p>Hit-Boy executive produced a handful of albums and songs for different artists in 2023, including executive producing three Nas albums (<a href="/wiki/King%27s_Disease_III" title="King&#39;s Disease III">King's Disease III</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magic_2" title="Magic 2">Magic 2</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Magic_3" title="Magic 3">Magic 3</a>), <a href="/wiki/Musiq_Soulchild" title="Musiq Soulchild">Musiq Soulchild</a>'s <i>Victims &amp; Villains</i>, Madden 2024 Soundtrack (for the second year in a row), a single for Creed III Soundtrack with <a href="/wiki/Blxst" title="Blxst">Blxst</a>, collaborating again for <i>Bus Stop</i> and <i>Go Down</i>  with Don Toliver on his album <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Sick_(album)" title="Love Sick (album)">Love Sick</a></i>, "<a href="/wiki/Delresto_(Echoes)" title="Delresto (Echoes)">Delresto (Echoes)</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a> from Scott's <i><a href="/wiki/Utopia_(Travis_Scott_album)" title="Utopia (Travis Scott album)">Utopia</a></i> (producing on Travis' last three albums), confirmed to be executive producing alongside The Alchemist on <a href="/wiki/Benny_the_Butcher" title="Benny the Butcher">Benny the Butcher</a>'s new album <i>Everybody Can't Go</i>, and more.
</p>
