<p><b>Thizz Entertainment</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Sacramento" class="mw-redirect" title="Sacramento">Sacramento</a>-based originally, <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent record label</a>, started in 1999 by rapper and music producer Andre Hicks, who was professionally known as rapper <a href="/wiki/Mac_Dre" title="Mac Dre">Mac Dre</a>. Best known as a <a href="/wiki/Poster_child" title="Poster child">poster child</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hyphy_movement" class="mw-redirect" title="Hyphy movement">hyphy movement</a> that swept through the Bay Area in the 1990s and early 2000s.The label was relocated to the S.F. Bay Area shortly after his untimely death.
</p><p>In 2004, Hicks was murdered by an unknown assailant while traveling after a performance in <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City, Missouri</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Upon Hicks's death and at his written direction, his mother, Wanda Salvatto, (affectionally known as "Mac Wanda") became owner and CEO.
</p><p>There are no artists signed to the label and the company's primary focus is to manage Mac Dre's expansive music catalog of over 25 albums, preserve his legacy, and to hold artistic events to honor his memory.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thizz_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mac Dre was a rapper of many monikers, a natural comedian with an incredible work ethic, and an artist who forever changed the course of Bay Area hip-hop. From recording verses through a phone while serving time for a robbery charge to bringing phrases like "thizz," "go dumb," and "ghost ride the whip" into the lexicon, Dre left an indelible mark on his community during his short but prolific lifetime.  "It was always just great energy," friend and fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Mistah_F.A.B." title="Mistah F.A.B.">Mistah F.A.B</a>. says of his performances. "You felt like when you left the show, you left Mardi Gras. That's just how dope he was."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1999, Hicks had relocated to Sacramento and founded Thizz Entertainment.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thizz_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1999-2004">1999-2004</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thizz_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1999-2004">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From its creation, the label, has been fundamental in the growth and popularity of the <a href="/wiki/Bay_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Bay Area">Bay Area</a> <a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">Hyphy</a> movement. Mac Dre's most popular albums including, <i><a href="/wiki/Ronald_Dregan:_Dreganomics" class="mw-redirect" title="Ronald Dregan: Dreganomics">Ronald Dregan: Dreganomics</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Genie_of_the_Lamp" title="The Genie of the Lamp">The Genie of the Lamp</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Thizzelle_Washington" title="Thizzelle Washington">Thizzelle Washington</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mac_Dre%27s_the_Name" title="Mac Dre&#39;s the Name">Mac Dre's the Name</a></i>, were released under the label and distributed locally in partnership with City Hall Records in <a href="/wiki/San_Rafael,_California" title="San Rafael, California">San Rafael</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004_-_Present">2004 - Present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thizz_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2004 - Present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dedicated to protecting the works and legacy of Mac Dre, the label does not sign recording artists. Rappers and musicians that were previously associated with Mac Dre and his labels, and are still active in music, continue to support the label's efforts by promoting the brand within their own endeavors, paying homage by adding a "Thizz" prefix to their own label or production company. However, outside of this small and exclusive group, "Many in the rap game attach themselves to the term <i>“thizz”</i> to make a name for themselves and ride Mac Dre's coattails" said Black Dog Bone, editor of <a href="/wiki/Vallejo,_California" title="Vallejo, California">Vallejo</a>-based <i>Murder Dog</i> magazine, which chronicles the rap scene.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The label, unaware of how frequently rappers and others were self associating themselves the label was shocked to learn in April 2012, eight years after Hicks's death, Thizz Entertainment was named by <a href="/wiki/Drug_Enforcement_Administration" title="Drug Enforcement Administration">US Drug Enforcement agents</a> in connection with the mass arrest of 25 individuals in <a href="/wiki/Vallejo,_California" title="Vallejo, California">Vallejo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stockton,_California" title="Stockton, California">Stockton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fairfield,_California" title="Fairfield, California">Fairfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City" title="Oklahoma City">Oklahoma City</a>. Several that were arrested had falsely purported publicly to be signed and/or otherwise a part of the label.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2018, Former Thizz Entertainment artists continue to support and assist Salvatto in curtailing flagrant misuse or misrepresentation of affiliation to the current label or Mac Dre's brand by unauthorized third parties which is an on-going effort.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Annual_Events">Annual Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thizz_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Annual Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ten years after his death, his mother was still receiving an excess of requests, comments, letters from young people, with stories of how Mac Dre affected them or got them through tough times in their young lives. “I didn't realize how big his presence was in the Bay Area and actually across the United States ” stated Salvatto.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She wanted to give young artists a chance who may not otherwise have an opportunity to showcase their talent.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dre_Area:_The_Mac_Dre_Art_Show">Dre Area: The Mac Dre Art Show</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thizz_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Dre Area: The Mac Dre Art Show">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2015, the label began to host an annual <i>Dre Area:The Mac Dre Art Show</i> in Oakland. The event showcases art pieces by various artists nationwide that are inspired by Mac Dre legacy.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mac_Dre_Day">Mac Dre Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thizz_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mac Dre Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2018, the label held its 3rd annual Mac Dre Day in San Francisco at <a href="/wiki/Regency_Center" title="Regency Center">The Regency Ballroom</a> on July 5, what would have been Mac Dre's 48th birthday.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> “One of the reasons it's important” for the event to happen “is that he had an impact on young people in the Bay Area,” said Mac Wanda, going by the name because “that's what the kids call me.”<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The event was hosted by rapper Mally Mall and San Francisco radio station  <a href="/wiki/KMEL" title="KMEL">KMEL</a>'s DJ Amen and featured performances by <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nef_the_Pharaoh" title="Nef the Pharaoh">Nef The Pharaoh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mistah_F.A.B." title="Mistah F.A.B.">Mistah F.A.B</a>., <a href="/wiki/Philthy_Rich" title="Philthy Rich">Philthy Rich</a>, Coolio Da Unda Dogg, <a href="/wiki/Baby_Bash" title="Baby Bash">Baby Bash</a> more, including several known rappers from the bay area that Mac Dre would frequently collaborate on songs over the years.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  There has been no incidents of negative occurrence at the events with Salvatto stating that she thinks fans understand that the show's organizer is his mother.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_Media">Other Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thizz_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Other Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thizz Entertainment created the DVD series <i>Treal TV</i>
</p><p>In 2014, veteran actor, <a href="/wiki/Tray_Chaney" title="Tray Chaney">Tray Chaney</a>, best known on the popular HBO drama series, <a href="/wiki/The_Wire" title="The Wire">The Wire</a>, announced that he has been selected to produce as star in two upcoming Mac Dre films in partnership with Thizz Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Inspired by the rapper <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a> after he spoke to Salvatto on how much Mac Dre impacted his life and career, in 2015, Thizz Entertainment released the documentary, <i>Legend of the Bay,</i> narrated by rapper and journalist <a href="/wiki/Sway_Calloway" title="Sway Calloway">Sway Calloway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The documentary details the Bay Area legend's life and includes never-before-seen concert footage, home movies, exclusive interviews with Mac Dre's friends, family, and music artists such as <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Warren_G" title="Warren G">Warren G</a>.
</p>
