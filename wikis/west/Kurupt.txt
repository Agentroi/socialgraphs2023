<p><b>Ricardo Emmanuel Brown</b> (born November 23, 1972), better known by his stage name <b>Kurupt</b>, is an American rapper and record producer who aided <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_rap" title="Gangsta rap">gangsta rap</a>'s rise via 1990s verses helping set lasting trends.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He is one half of the rap duo <a href="/wiki/Tha_Dogg_Pound" title="Tha Dogg Pound">Tha Dogg Pound</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Daz_Dillinger" title="Daz Dillinger">Daz Dillinger</a>. His first solo album was released in 1998. After feuding, the duo reunited in 2005.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurupt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ricardo Emmanuel Brown was born in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-metallungies.com_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-metallungies.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He moved to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a> at age 16, first to <a href="/wiki/Hawthorne,_California" title="Hawthorne, California">Hawthorne</a>, then to <a href="/wiki/South_Los_Angeles" title="South Los Angeles">South Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crenshaw,_Los_Angeles" title="Crenshaw, Los Angeles">Crenshaw District</a> at age 18.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was engaged to rapper <a href="/wiki/Foxy_Brown_(rapper)" title="Foxy Brown (rapper)">Foxy Brown</a> from 1997 to 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In the early 2000s, he was engaged to <a href="/wiki/Natina_Reed" title="Natina Reed">Natina Reed</a>; the couple separated in 2002, following the birth of their son Tren Brown.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, Brown married Jovan Brown, who filed for divorce in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> That same year, he began dating Toni Calvert. The couple appeared on <a href="/wiki/Marriage_Boot_Camp" title="Marriage Boot Camp">Marriage Boot Camp</a>'s 17th season in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurupt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kurupt debuted as a recording artist via three songs on <a href="/wiki/The_S.O.S._Band" title="The S.O.S. Band">the S.O.S. Band</a>'s 1991 album <i><a href="/wiki/One_of_Many_Nights" title="One of Many Nights">One of Many Nights</a></i>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_Row_Records">Death Row Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurupt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death Row Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1992, at age 19, Kurupt signed to <a href="/wiki/Death_Row_Records" title="Death Row Records">Death Row Records</a>, newly formed by music manager <a href="/wiki/Suge_Knight" title="Suge Knight">Suge Knight</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, the rapper and music producer who had just left the rap group <a href="/wiki/N.W.A" title="N.W.A">N.W.A</a> and its <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records" title="Ruthless Records">Ruthless Records</a>. At Death Row, Kurupt joined a roster of artists—including <a href="/wiki/Daz_Dillinger" title="Daz Dillinger">Daz Dillinger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lady_of_Rage" class="mw-redirect" title="Lady of Rage">Lady of Rage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/RBX" title="RBX">RBX</a>—who entered the public eye by their features on Dr. Dre's debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chronic" title="The Chronic">The Chronic</a></i>. Kurupt and Daz soon teamed as a rap duo, <a href="/wiki/Tha_Dogg_Pound" title="Tha Dogg Pound">Tha Dogg Pound</a>, featured on Snoop Dogg's debut solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Doggystyle_(album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Doggystyle (album)">Doggystyle</a></i>, in the song "For All My Niggaz &amp; Bitchez".
</p><p>By 1996, the rap genre's East Coast–West Coast rivalry was escalating, spurred on by Death Row's CEO Suge and by rapper 2Pac, new to the label, who believed that a <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy Records</a> circle, in their hometown New York City, had fostered his November 1994 shooting there. Tha Dogg Pound released the single "<a href="/wiki/New_York,_New_York_(Tha_Dogg_Pound_song)" title="New York, New York (Tha Dogg Pound song)">New York, New York</a>," featuring Snoop Dogg, slighting the city. The duo's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Dogg_Food" title="Dogg Food">Dogg Food</a></i>, produced by Daz and mixed by Dre, drew favorable reviews and good sales.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_after_Death_Row">Career after Death Row</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurupt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Career after Death Row">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Tupac_Shakur" class="mw-redirect" title="Death of Tupac Shakur">1996 murder</a> triggered an exodus of artists from Death Row Records. Preceded only by Dr. Dre, Kurupt was second to leave. He then signed with <a href="/wiki/A%26M_Records" title="A&amp;M Records">A&amp;M Records</a>, where he founded the imprint <a href="/wiki/Antra_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Antra Records">Antra Records</a>, releasing his debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Kuruption!" title="Kuruption!">Kuruption!</a></i> in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Kurupt left the Antra label upon releasing his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Streetz_Iz_a_Mutha" title="Tha Streetz Iz a Mutha">Tha Streetz Iz a Mutha</a></i>, featuring the <a href="/wiki/Diss_track" class="mw-redirect" title="Diss track">diss track</a> "Calling Out Names", in which Kurupt insults New York rap figures <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irv_Gotti" title="Irv Gotti">Irv Gotti</a>, and <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>, accusing the latter of having an affair with Kurupt's then-fiancée <a href="/wiki/Foxy_Brown_(rapper)" title="Foxy Brown (rapper)">Foxy Brown</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During this time, he linked up with fellow Californian <a href="/wiki/Ras_Kass" title="Ras Kass">Ras Kass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>-affiliate <a href="/wiki/Killah_Priest" title="Killah Priest">Killah Priest</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Canibus" title="Canibus">Canibus</a> to form a new group, <a href="/wiki/The_HRSMN" class="mw-redirect" title="The HRSMN">The HRSMN</a>, although, despite promises of forthcoming material, has released only an unfinished white label.
</p><p>While Death Row owned rights to Tha Dogg Pound name, Kurupt and Daz operated as the DPG (Dogg Pound Gangstaz), and also with a larger group, including Nate Dogg, Snoop Dogg, <a href="/wiki/Soopafly" title="Soopafly">Soopafly</a>, and others intermittently, altogether the DPGC (Dogg Pound Gangsta Clique). Daz and Kurupt, as the DPG, released <i><a href="/wiki/Dillinger_%26_Young_Gotti" title="Dillinger &amp; Young Gotti">Dillinger &amp; Young Gotti</a></i>, which received lukewarm reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Kurupt's following solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Space_Boogie:_Smoke_Oddessey" title="Space Boogie: Smoke Oddessey">Space Boogie: Smoke Oddessey</a></i>, with production by Daz and by up-and-coming producer <a href="/wiki/Fredwreck" title="Fredwreck">Fredwreck</a>, and released by Artemis Records, fared better. Kurupt's then-fiancée, the late <a href="/wiki/Natina_Reed" title="Natina Reed">Natina Reed</a>, performed the hook of its single "It's Over".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Death_Row">Return to Death Row</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurupt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Return to Death Row">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In early 2002, an unofficial remix/compilation album titled <i><a href="/wiki/2002_(Tha_Dogg_Pound_album)" title="2002 (Tha Dogg Pound album)">2002</a></i>, by Tha Dogg Pound, provoked questions about the duo's relationship with Death Row.  Soon becoming its vice president, Kurupt signed again to Death Row.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Given Daz's especial enmity at Death Row's mogul Knight, Kurupt and Daz began feuding, repeatedly bashing each other on records and in interviews.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2003, Kurupt released <a href="/wiki/DJ_Tomekk" title="DJ Tomekk">DJ Tomekk</a> the single <a href="/wiki/Ganxtaville_Pt._III" title="Ganxtaville Pt. III">Ganxtaville Pt. III</a> and placed fifth on the German charts, as well as in the charts of Austria and Switzerland.
</p><p>While mentoring Death Row's new artists, including <a href="/wiki/Spider_Loc" title="Spider Loc">Spider Loc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crooked_I" title="Crooked I">Crooked I</a>, and Eastwood, Kurupt began his next album, <i><a href="/wiki/Against_tha_Grain" class="mw-redirect" title="Against tha Grain">Against tha Grain</a></i>. Further, he formed a new group, The Riflemen,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> consisting of Kurupt, <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jayo_Felony" title="Jayo Felony">Jayo Felony</a>, 40 Glocc, and <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Alchemist (producer)">The Alchemist</a>, although the likelihood of a record from this ensemble increasingly slimmed. In 2004, after repeated delays of Kurupt's forthcoming solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Originals_(Kurupt_album)" title="Originals (Kurupt album)">Originals</a></i>, a bootleg version was released, alike those of Crooked I and other Death Row artists.
</p><p>It was later revealed that Kurupt was more stung by his feud with Daz than he had acknowledged.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In April 2005, at a West Coast unity event hosted by Snoop Dogg, with Snoop as liaison, Kurupt and Daz reconciled and restored their rap duo under its original moniker, Tha Dogg Pound.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In August 2005, Kurupt's long delayed album <i>Against tha Grain</i> was released, once Kurupt had already left Death Row a second time; he would publicly denounce the disses that he had included on the album, but an EP called <i><a href="/wiki/Against_tha_Grain_E.P." title="Against tha Grain E.P.">Against tha Grain E.P.</a></i> surfaced in 2007, containing said disses.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dogg_Pound_reunited_and_HRSMN_returns">Dogg Pound reunited and HRSMN returns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurupt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Dogg Pound reunited and HRSMN returns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Tha_Dogg_Pound.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Tha_Dogg_Pound.jpg/220px-Tha_Dogg_Pound.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="181" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Tha_Dogg_Pound.jpg/330px-Tha_Dogg_Pound.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Tha_Dogg_Pound.jpg/440px-Tha_Dogg_Pound.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1182" data-file-height="972" /></a><figcaption>Kurupt with Daz Dillinger in 2015</figcaption></figure>
<p>Kurupt and Daz released <i>Dillinger &amp; Young Gotti II: The Saga Continuez</i> in 2005, <i>Cali iz Active</i> (as DPG with Snoop Dogg)<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> in 2006, and <i><a href="/wiki/Dogg_Chit" title="Dogg Chit">Dogg Chit</a></i> in 2007; while Kurupt originally touted <i>Dogg Chit</i> as the heir to <i>Dogg Food'</i>s legacy,<sup id="cite_ref-metallungies.com_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-metallungies.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> he later stated that none of the group's then intermittent releases were to be considered actual albums—more like <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a> – and that then upcoming <i><a href="/wiki/100_Wayz" title="100 Wayz">100 Wayz</a></i> (originally titled <i>Westcoast Aftershocc</i>) was to be considered the second actual Dogg Pound release.<sup id="cite_ref-dirty-dirty1_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dirty-dirty1-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In 2006, he also appeared on Snoop Dogg's album <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Blue_Carpet_Treatment" title="Tha Blue Carpet Treatment">Tha Blue Carpet Treatment</a></i>. After putting out <i><a href="/wiki/Digital_Smoke" title="Digital Smoke">Digital Smoke</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/J._Wells" title="J. Wells">J. Wells</a> in 2007, Kurupt released an album with his younger brother <a href="/wiki/Roscoe_(rapper)" title="Roscoe (rapper)">Roscoe</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Frank_and_Jess_Story" title="The Frank and Jess Story">The Frank and Jess Story</a></i> in 2008. He also appeared on Snoop Dogg's 2008 album <i><a href="/wiki/Ego_Trippin%27" title="Ego Trippin&#39;">Ego Trippin'</a></i>.
</p><p>Before <i>100 Wayz</i> was released in 2010, Tha Dogg Pound's <i><a href="/wiki/Gangsta_Grillz" class="mw-redirect" title="Gangsta Grillz">Gangsta Grillz</a></i> mixtape premiered, hosted by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> followed by an album called <i><a href="/wiki/BlaQKout" class="mw-redirect" title="BlaQKout">BlaQKout</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a> on April 28, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The <i>BlaQKout</i> single, "Fuck Y'all," is a diss to former Quik partners AMG, Hi-C and 2nd II None. During this time, Kurupt was featured on many tracks with other independent artists, and released Streetlights, his first solo album in four years, on April 20, 2010. In 2011, Kurupt started recording with <a href="/wiki/MC_Eiht" title="MC Eiht">MC Eiht</a>, <a href="/wiki/King_T" title="King T">King T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jayo_Felony" title="Jayo Felony">Jayo Felony</a>, <a href="/wiki/B.G._Knocc_Out" title="B.G. Knocc Out">B.G. Knocc Out</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tha_Chill" title="Tha Chill">Tha Chill</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sir_Jinx" title="Sir Jinx">Sir Jinx</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_group" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop group">hip hop group</a> project called "1st Generation".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Kurupt announced plans to release the long anticipated HRSMN project, <i>The Academy</i>, a compilation of collaborations between various artists, and guest featured alongside <a href="/wiki/Joell_Ortiz" title="Joell Ortiz">Slaughterhouse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Big_Dave_(rapper)" title="Big Dave (rapper)">Big Dave</a> on the single "Hey Girl" from <a href="/wiki/Rocko_(rapper)" title="Rocko (rapper)">Rocko</a>'s debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Self_Made_(album)" title="Self Made (album)">Self Made</a></i>. Following the death of his son Tren's mother (<a href="/wiki/Natina_Reed" title="Natina Reed">Natina Reed</a> of R&amp;B girl group <a href="/wiki/Blaque" title="Blaque">Blaque</a>) in a car accident in Atlanta,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> the planned release of the DJ Nik Bean-hosted mixtape, <i>Money, Bitches, Power</i> on November 7, 2012 was delayed.<sup id="cite_ref-www.hiphopdx.com_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-www.hiphopdx.com-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dubcnn.com_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dubcnn.com-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape, which features <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crooked_I" title="Crooked I">Crooked I</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masta_Killa" title="Masta Killa">Masta Killa</a>, <a href="/wiki/RBX" title="RBX">RBX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>, The Rejectz and more,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> was released on May 1, 2013. In early 2015, Kurupt released another hit single called "I.M.O." (In My Opinion) with hip hop rapper and producer KingThaRapper, signed under Famous Music Group. In May 2021, it was announced that the long delayed HRSMN project would finally be delivered to fans.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The album, now titled <i>The Last Ride</i>, was released on June 18, 2021. Kurupt was featured on the track "Curse of Canaan" from <a href="/wiki/Vinnie_Paz" title="Vinnie Paz">Vinnie Paz</a>'s album <i>Tortured in the Name of God's Unconditional Love</i> in 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influence_and_legacy">Influence and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurupt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Influence and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> cited Kurupt in a 2013 interview as one of his three most significant influences, alongside <a href="/wiki/2Pac" class="mw-redirect" title="2Pac">2Pac</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> stated in his song "<a href="/wiki/%27Till_I_Collapse" title="&#39;Till I Collapse">'Till I Collapse</a>" that he views Kurupt as one of the best rappers of all time, saying "I got a list—here's the order of my list that it's in, it goes <a href="/wiki/Redman_(rapper)" title="Redman (rapper)">Reggie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Z" class="mw-redirect" title="Jay Z">Jay Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">Biggie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_3000" title="André 3000">Andre from Outkast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jada</a>, Kurupt, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, and then me".<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kool_Moe_Dee" title="Kool Moe Dee">Kool Moe Dee</a> ranked Kurupt at 39 on his best MCs of all-time list in his book, <i><a href="/wiki/There%27s_a_God_on_the_Mic" title="There&#39;s a God on the Mic">There's a God on the Mic</a></i>.
</p>
