<p><b>Dominic Michael Merendino-Sarich</b> (born August 31, 1989), better known  by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Dom Sicily</b> (formerly <b>Def Dee</b>), is an American <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a> (DJ) from <a href="/wiki/Seattle,_Washington" class="mw-redirect" title="Seattle, Washington">Seattle, Washington</a>.  He is of <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicilian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatian</a> descent.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dom_Sicily&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dom Sicily started off originally as a DJ when he was in sixth grade before he became a producer/rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He went on a field trip to a music exhibit when he was in the sixth grade that specialized in <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a> history, after the trip he fell in love with the history of hip hop. He then became obsessed with his turntables and really believed that he was Q from <a href="/wiki/Juice_(1992_film)" title="Juice (1992 film)">Juice</a> in a past life. Sicily eventually wanted to create the music that he was hearing. He started off using <a href="/wiki/FL_Studio" title="FL Studio">Fruity Loops</a>, <a href="/wiki/GarageBand" title="GarageBand">GarageBand</a>, and then the <a href="/wiki/Music_Production_Controller" class="mw-redirect" title="Music Production Controller">MCP2000XL</a>.
</p><p>Dom Sicily's first album release was his collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a> rapper LA entitled "Gravity" that released in April 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> A year later, Dom Sicily released his first instrumental album "Cheap Heat" in 2011 that gained the attention of <a href="/wiki/Mello_Music_Group" title="Mello Music Group">Mello Music Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After the release of "Cheap Heat", he signed a developmental deal with Mello Music Group and re-released "Cheap Heat" under their banner in 2012. In 2013, he released his debut album "33 and a Third" which featured guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Black_Milk" title="Black Milk">Black Milk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oddisee" title="Oddisee">Oddisee</a>, <a href="/wiki/One_Be_Lo" title="One Be Lo">One Be Lo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenn_Starr_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kenn Starr (rapper)">Kenn Starr</a>, and more.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On September 9, 2014, Dom Sicily released his sophomore album entitled "Deja Vu" through Redefinition Records which featured <a href="/wiki/Roc_Marciano" title="Roc Marciano">Roc Marciano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blu_(rapper)" title="Blu (rapper)">Blu</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Da_Sensei" title="El Da Sensei">El Da Sensei</a> and others.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the summer of 2015, Dom Sicily released his second instrumental album "D-1000".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On April 29, 2016, Dom Sicily released a limited edition 7" vinyl for his EP "The Guide" through F5 Records.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In August, he changed his stage name from Def Dee to Dom Sicily.
</p>
