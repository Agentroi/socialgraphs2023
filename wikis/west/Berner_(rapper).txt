<p><b>Gilbert Anthony Milam Jr.</b> (born October 27, 1983), better known by his stage name <b>Berner</b>, is an American rapper and entrepreneur signed to <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Gang_Entertainment" title="Taylor Gang Entertainment">Taylor Gang Entertainment</a>. He has released over 40 albums, several of which have charted on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s "Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums" chart.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As an entrepreneur, he created the Cookies brand of <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">cannabis</a> products and clothing gear that formed out of San Francisco in the early 2010s.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The company has grown to include dozens of <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_dispensaries_in_the_United_States" title="Cannabis dispensaries in the United States">dispensaries</a> across the United States and abroad, along with multiple clothing stores.<sup id="cite_ref-ktvu_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ktvu-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berner_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Sunset_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunset District">Sunset District</a> native, Berner attended <a href="/wiki/Galileo_Academy_of_Science_and_Technology" title="Galileo Academy of Science and Technology">Galileo Academy of Science and Technology</a> before dropping out. His mother was an office worker and his father was a chef at a Mexican restaurant on <a href="/wiki/Fillmore_Street" title="Fillmore Street">Fillmore Street</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Huffington_Post_interview_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Huffington_Post_interview-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> When Berner was 13, the family moved to Arizona, where his father planned to open a restaurant. During high school, Berner began <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">battle rapping</a> and in 2006 he released his first mixtape, <i>Dirty Sneakers...Plenty Ways to Get It</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Huffington_Post_interview_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Huffington_Post_interview-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berner_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After forming his own label, Bern One Entertainment in 2007, Berner  released his debut album, <i>Track Money &amp; Pack Money</i>, with Equipto. His first solo album, <i>Weekend at Bernie's</i> arrived in 2009, along with four other albums.<sup id="cite_ref-Allmusic_rec_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic_rec-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2012, Berner signed to <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Gang_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Taylor Gang Records">Taylor Gang Records</a>. <i>Urban Farmer</i>, Berner's first collection to be released on Taylor Gang Records, was released on October 2, 2012. In 2013, Berner embarked performed on the "Under the Influence of Music Tour" alongside Khalifa, <a href="/wiki/A$AP_Rocky" class="mw-redirect" title="A$AP Rocky">A$AP Rocky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_Jame$" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinidad Jame$">Trinidad Jame$</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joey_Bada$$" class="mw-redirect" title="Joey Bada$$">Joey Bada$$</a>. In 2015, Berner released <i>Contraband</i>, a collaborative album with <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>, as well as <i>Drought Season 3</i> with deceased rapper <a href="/wiki/The_Jacka" title="The Jacka">The Jacka</a>. In 2016, Berner released the solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Hempire" title="Hempire">Hempire</a></i>, also producing a 4/20 show at the <a href="/wiki/Bill_Graham_Civic_Auditorium" title="Bill Graham Civic Auditorium">Bill Graham Civic Auditorium</a> with a lineup that included Cypress Hill, Juicy J, Dizzy Wright, J Boog, Kool John, and Berner.<sup id="cite_ref-Allmusic_rec_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic_rec-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LA_Music_Blog_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA_Music_Blog-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Urban Farmer</i>, another of Berner's albums, features <a href="/wiki/Iamsu!" title="Iamsu!">Iamsu!</a> from <a href="/wiki/The_HBK_Gang" title="The HBK Gang">The HBK Gang</a> on two tracks; and the Jacka, <a href="/wiki/Rydah_J._Klyde" title="Rydah J. Klyde">Rydah J. Klyde</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Quinn" title="San Quinn">San Quinn</a> on <i>21 Gunz</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-SFWeekly1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFWeekly1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Berner's single, <i>20 Lights</i> reached number 95 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart. It was released on January 6, 2015. "Best Thang Smokin" is the lead single in Berner's album <i>Hempire</i>, released April 1, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-Complex_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 4, 2019, he released the album <i>El Chivo</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cookies_cannabis_brand">Cookies cannabis brand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berner_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cookies cannabis brand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Berner began working in the <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_industry" title="Cannabis industry">cannabis industry</a> at age 18 at a San Francisco <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_dispensaries_in_the_United_States" title="Cannabis dispensaries in the United States">dispensary</a> named The Hemp Center.<sup id="cite_ref-sfw_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfw-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In the early 2000s he befriended a cultivator named Jai Chang (a.k.a. "Jigga"), who developed a <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_strain" title="Cannabis strain">strain of cannabis</a> that he named "Girl Scout Cookies".<sup id="cite_ref-forbes_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Berner and Chang began selling various strains that Chang developed to dispensaries, including Girl Scout Cookies.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> After the <a href="/wiki/Girl_Scouts_of_the_USA" title="Girl Scouts of the USA">Girl Scouts of the USA</a> sent a <a href="/wiki/Cease_and_desist" title="Cease and desist">cease and desist</a> letter, they named their brand simply "Cookies".<sup id="cite_ref-forbes_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Berner was also selling the strains to friends, including rapper <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> who he met in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-sfw_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfw-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-forbes_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Khalifa soon began mentioning Cookies in his rap songs, while Berner wore clothing bearing a Cookies logo in music videos that he appeared in.<sup id="cite_ref-sfw_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfw-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Berner went on to develop a line of Cookies clothing wear and opened his first Cookies clothing store in San Francisco in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The first Cookies cannabis dispensary followed in 2018,<sup id="cite_ref-bi_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bi-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> followed by the first on the East Coast opening in 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> By 2022 the number of dispensaries had grown to 49, to go along with clothing stores in San Francisco, Los Angeles, and New York.<sup id="cite_ref-bi_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bi-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2022, Berner was the first cannabis executive to be featured on the cover of <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes Magazine</a> since it launched in 1917.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Forbes conservatively estimated the Cookies brand to be worth $150 million.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berner_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Berner was diagnosed with stage 3 <a href="/wiki/Colorectal_cancer" title="Colorectal cancer">colon cancer</a> in 2021,<sup id="cite_ref-ktvu_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ktvu-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> but it went into remission after chemotherapy.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has a daughter.<sup id="cite_ref-ktvu_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ktvu-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
