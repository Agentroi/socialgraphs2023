<p><b>Seo Jung-kwon</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>&#58;&#160;<span title="Korean-language text"><span lang="ko-Hang">서정권</span></span>; born July 29, 1974), also known as <b>Tiger JK</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Korean_Americans" title="Korean Americans">Korean-American</a> rapper, record producer, and entrepreneur based in South Korea. He is best known as a founding member of <a href="/wiki/Korean_hip_hop" title="Korean hip hop">Korean hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/Drunken_Tiger" title="Drunken Tiger">Drunken Tiger</a>. He has also founded two record labels, <a href="/wiki/Jungle_Entertainment" title="Jungle Entertainment">Jungle Entertainment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Feel_Ghood_Music" title="Feel Ghood Music">Feel Ghood Music</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He is a member of the South Korean hip hop trio <a href="/wiki/MFBTY" title="MFBTY">MFBTY</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is considered a highly influential figure in the development of Korean hip-hop and is credited with helping bring the genre into the Korean mainstream.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <i>The Los Angeles Times</i> referred to him in 2011 as "perhaps the most popular Korean rapper in America, Asia and the world."<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiger_JK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tiger JK (Seo Jung-kwon) was born in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, South Korea on July 29, 1974. His father, Suh Byung Hoo (d. 2014), was a <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a> and one of the first Korean pop columnists, and the first <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a> correspondent in Korea in the 1970s.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Brett_Forrest_pp._154-160_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brett_Forrest_pp._154-160-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> His family moved to the United States when he was 12, and he was sent to briefly live with an uncle in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, where he practiced <a href="/wiki/Taekwondo" title="Taekwondo">Taekwondo</a>, becoming a 3rd "dan" black belt.<sup id="cite_ref-Brett_Forrest_pp._154-160_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brett_Forrest_pp._154-160-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He spent his teenage years in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, where he attended Glendale Hoover High School in Glendale, CA. As a teen in Los Angeles, Tiger JK witnessed the violence between Korean-Americans and African-Americans during the <a href="/wiki/1992_Los_Angeles_Riots" class="mw-redirect" title="1992 Los Angeles Riots">1992 Los Angeles Riots</a>. He later said he wanted to use hip hop to create a dialogue between the two communities.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tiger JK attended Santa Monica College and then transferred to <a href="/wiki/UCLA" class="mw-redirect" title="UCLA">UCLA</a><sup id="cite_ref-:2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and graduated with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">B.A.</a> degree in English from the Creative Writing program.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiger_JK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1995_.E2.80.93_2005:_Early_career_and_successes_with_Drunken_Tiger"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1995_–_2005:_Early_career_and_successes_with_Drunken_Tiger">1995 – 2005: Early career and successes with Drunken Tiger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiger_JK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1995 – 2005: Early career and successes with Drunken Tiger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1995, Tiger JK's first solo album, <i>Enter The Tiger,</i> was released in Korea by Oasis Records, a connection Tiger JK had made because his father was friends with the company's CEO. The album got little airplay due to its perceived explicit content.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tiger JK returned to the United States and teamed up with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Shine" title="DJ Shine">DJ Shine</a> to form the group <a href="/wiki/Drunken_Tiger" title="Drunken Tiger">Drunken Tiger</a> in 1998. The group debuted in Korea and, despite the explicit content of their lyrics and the group's rejection of mainstream music trends, they achieved popularity with their first album, <i>Year of the Tiger</i>, which included the hit songs, "I Want You" and "Do You Know Hip-hop."<sup id="cite_ref-:3_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Over the next several years, Drunken Tiger brought on new members and released multiple albums, winning numerous awards.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Founding member DJ Shine left the group in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006-2013:_Creation_of_Jungle_Entertainment_and_collaborations">2006-2013: Creation of Jungle Entertainment and collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiger_JK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006-2013: Creation of Jungle Entertainment and collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Tiger_JK.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Tiger_JK.jpg/220px-Tiger_JK.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="277" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Tiger_JK.jpg/330px-Tiger_JK.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Tiger_JK.jpg/440px-Tiger_JK.jpg 2x" data-file-width="501" data-file-height="631" /></a><figcaption>Tiger JK performing at the Lolipop Concert in May 2010</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2006, Tiger JK left Oasis Records and established his own hip-hop label, <a href="/wiki/Jungle_Entertainment" title="Jungle Entertainment">Jungle Entertainment</a>. The label represented Drunken Tiger, and prominent Korean rappers <a href="/wiki/Yoon_Mi-rae" title="Yoon Mi-rae">Yoon Mi-rae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leessang" title="Leessang">Leessang</a> were among the artists to join the label over the next few years.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tiger JK continued to release music with Drunken Tiger over the next few years, while also collaborating with other artists. In 2012, he featured on "Let This Die," a single by <a href="/wiki/Brian_Joo" title="Brian Joo">Brian Joo</a> of the R&amp;B duo <a href="/wiki/Fly_to_the_Sky" title="Fly to the Sky">Fly to the Sky</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> That same year, he featured on "Wicked," a song by new <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">k-pop</a> girl group <a href="/wiki/Fiestar" title="Fiestar">Fiestar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013-present:_Founding_MFBTY_and_Feel_Ghood_Music">2013-present: Founding MFBTY and Feel Ghood Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiger_JK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2013-present: Founding MFBTY and Feel Ghood Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, Tiger JK teamed up with Yoon Mi-rae and <a href="/wiki/Bizzy" title="Bizzy">Bizzy</a> to form the group <a href="/wiki/MFBTY" title="MFBTY">MFBTY</a>, an acronym for "My Fans are Better Than Yours." Soon after, he left Jungle Entertainment and founded a new label, <a href="/wiki/Feel_Ghood_Music" title="Feel Ghood Music">Feel Ghood Music</a>, which now releases MFBTY's music.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He has had multiple collaborations with popular boy group leader <a href="/wiki/RM_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="RM (rapper)">RM</a> of <a href="/wiki/BTS_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="BTS (band)">BTS</a> with the latter featuring on his final album Drunken Tiger X: Rebirth of Tiger JK<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> on the song Timeless.
He also featured in Seventeen's Hoshi's Tiger released in 2022.
In 2023, he featured on "TOPLINE", one of the single by K-pop group <a href="/wiki/Stray_Kids" title="Stray Kids">Stray Kids</a>, he also appeared in the official M/V.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiger_JK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Drug_conviction">Drug conviction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiger_JK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Drug conviction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tiger JK was arrested for using <a href="/wiki/Methamphetamine" title="Methamphetamine">methamphetamine</a> in Korea in 2000, at the same time that members of the Korean hip hop group <a href="/wiki/Uptown_(band)" title="Uptown (band)">Uptown</a> were also arrested on drug charges. Tiger JK said that, though he had used drugs in the past, he had not used them in Korea, and he said that he was ultimately found guilty due to false testimony from the members of Uptown. He spent a month and a half in jail and was given two years probation.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Illness">Illness</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiger_JK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Illness">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tiger JK was diagnosed with acute <a href="/wiki/Transverse_myelitis" title="Transverse myelitis">transverse myelitis</a>, a spinal cord condition that affects motor functions, in 2006. He was hospitalized and took a two-year hiatus from performing. As of 2014, he continued to take medication to treat the condition.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_family">Marriage and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiger_JK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Marriage and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2007, Tiger JK married rapper and singer <a href="/wiki/Yoon_Mi-rae" title="Yoon Mi-rae">Yoon Mi-rae</a> in a private ceremony in a Buddhist Temple. The wedding occurred a month before the death of his grandmother who had wanted to see them wed before her passing. Yoon gave birth to their son, Jordan, in March 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_projects">Other projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiger_JK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Other projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2006, Tiger JK launched a clothing line called <i>Lungta</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, under American rapper <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s "Beats By Dr. Dre" headphones line, Tiger JK released his own limited edition headphones. They retailed for 690,000 <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_won" title="South Korean won">won</a>, and all profits went to a child safety organization.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
