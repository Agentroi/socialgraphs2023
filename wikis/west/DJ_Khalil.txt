<p><b>Khalil Abdul-Rahman Hazzard</b> (born October 16, 1973), professionally known as <b>DJ Khalil</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Music_producer" class="mw-redirect" title="Music producer">music producer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a> from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. He is the instrumental half of the hip hop duo Self Scientific (along with rapper Chace Infinite) and a member of the group <a href="/wiki/The_New_Royales" title="The New Royales">The New Royales</a>, which also includes Liz Rodrigues, <a href="/wiki/Erik_Alcock" title="Erik Alcock">Erik Alcock</a> and Pranam <a href="/wiki/Chin_Injeti" title="Chin Injeti">Chin Injeti</a>. He produced the albums <i><a href="/wiki/Recovery_(Eminem_album)" title="Recovery (Eminem album)">Recovery</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Marshall_Mathers_LP_2" title="The Marshall Mathers LP 2">The Marshall Mathers LP 2</a></i> by the rapper <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, as well as the multi-platinum single "<a href="/wiki/The_Man_(Aloe_Blacc_song)" title="The Man (Aloe Blacc song)">The Man</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Aloe_Blacc" title="Aloe Blacc">Aloe Blacc</a>. He is the third born son of former UCLA player and coach and NBA player <a href="/wiki/Walt_Hazzard" title="Walt Hazzard">Walt Hazzard</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Khalil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>DJ Khalil was born Khalil Abdul-Rahman in <a href="/wiki/Seattle,_Washington" class="mw-redirect" title="Seattle, Washington">Seattle, Washington</a>, and raised in Los Angeles, California. His father, <a href="/wiki/Walt_Hazzard" title="Walt Hazzard">Walt Hazzard</a>, who later changed his name to Mahdi Abdul-Rahman, was a professional basketball player in the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a>. At age 13, at a party thrown by his parents, he met <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, for whom he would later become a staff producer.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Khalil played basketball at the high school and collegiate level, as a point guard at <a href="/wiki/North_Hollywood_High_School" title="North Hollywood High School">North Hollywood High School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morehouse_College" title="Morehouse College">Morehouse College</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Khalil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Khalil began his career as a <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a> (DJ), and graduated to producing music. He began creating tracks on the <a href="/wiki/Ensoniq_ASR-10" title="Ensoniq ASR-10">Ensoniq ASR-10</a> sampler workstation, later working in Propellerhead <a href="/wiki/Reason_(software)" title="Reason (software)">Reason</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He has produced for a large number of major artists in the hip-hop, R&amp;B and pop genres, including <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pink_(singer)" title="Pink (singer)">Pink</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Usher_(singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Usher (singer)">Usher</a> and Guess Who.
</p>
