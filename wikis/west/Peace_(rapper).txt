<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; <p><b>Mtulazaji Davis</b>, better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Peace</b> (usually styled <b>P.E.A.C.E.</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a> from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>. He is a member of <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_Fellowship" title="Freestyle Fellowship">Freestyle Fellowship</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Aceyalone" title="Aceyalone">Aceyalone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Myka_9" title="Myka 9">Myka 9</a> and <a href="/wiki/Self_Jupiter" title="Self Jupiter">Self Jupiter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He has released two solo albums.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peace_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Peace grew up mostly in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>. However, he stated that he lived in <a href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Dallas, Texas">Dallas, Texas</a> for about ten years. In elementary school, he played the <a href="/wiki/Accordion" title="Accordion">accordion</a>. He picked up the <a href="/wiki/Double_bass" title="Double bass">double bass</a> when he had joined his school's marching band. When he returned to Los Angeles, Peace started playing the <a href="/wiki/Saxophone" title="Saxophone">saxophone</a>.
</p><p>In an interview, Peace stated he started rapping in his high school years in the mid to late 1980s. One of his friends had told him that there was a guy who rapped just like him at <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Good_Life_Cafe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="The Good Life Cafe (page does not exist)">The Good Life Cafe</a>, a local open-mic venue, named <a href="/wiki/Myka_9" title="Myka 9">Myka 9</a>. Not believing his friend, he went to go check out Myka 9 at The Good Life Cafe. Peace waited around for a while, but Myka 9 didn't show up. So then he got up on stage and performed for a bit and blew a few whigs back. With no luck, Peace had to wait until the next Thursday night. When he returned, Myka 9 was there. Peace listened to him perform and somewhat agreed with his friend. They both had the fast rapping down, but Myka 9 had his own style. <a href="/wiki/Aceyalone" title="Aceyalone">Aceyalone</a> was there that night and had asked Peace what his name was. Peace said "Mtulazaji". Aceyalone then asked what his rap name was and he replied with "Peace".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Released independently and distributed by tape collectors, <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_Fellowship" title="Freestyle Fellowship">Freestyle Fellowship</a>'s first album <i><a href="/wiki/To_Whom_It_May_Concern..." title="To Whom It May Concern...">To Whom It May Concern...</a></i> was a landmark. Although it only contains a few songs where they all rap together, each individual rapper's songs truly showcased each of their styles. Peace had two songs, "Physical Form" and "For No Reason", on the album. Their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Innercity_Griots" title="Innercity Griots">Innercity Griots</a></i>, has been described as "an acknowledged underground masterpiece".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Peace released his first solo album, <i>Southern Fry'd Chicken</i>, in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His second solo album, <i>Megabite</i>, was released in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style">Style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peace_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is also known for his <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle rap</a> having gone to battle with <a href="/wiki/Eyedea" title="Eyedea">Eyedea</a> but lost to him at <a href="/wiki/Scribble_Jam" title="Scribble Jam">Scribble Jam</a> in 1999.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peace_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Peace is featured in the award-winning documentary <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_the_Life_(2008_film)" title="This Is the Life (2008 film)">This Is the Life</a></i>, chronicling the music movement that was birthed at The Good Life Cafe in <a href="/wiki/South_Central,_Los_Angeles" class="mw-redirect" title="South Central, Los Angeles">South Central, Los Angeles</a>. The Good Life Cafe is the open-mic workshop where he first performed with Freestyle Fellowship in the early 1990s.
</p>
