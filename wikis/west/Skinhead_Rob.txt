<p><b>Robert Aston</b> (born October 29, 1978), better known by his stage name <b>Skinhead Rob</b>, is an American rapper best known as a founding member of the rap rock group the <a href="/wiki/Transplants_(band)" title="Transplants (band)">Transplants</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-urlThe_Accidental_Career_Of_The_Transplants_Rob_Aston_–_Music,_Celebrity,_Artist_News_&#124;_MTV_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urlThe_Accidental_Career_Of_The_Transplants_Rob_Aston_–_Music,_Celebrity,_Artist_News_|_MTV-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He is also the lead vocalist in a <a href="/wiki/D-beat" title="D-beat">D-beat</a> punk band named Death March and a former member of hip-hop group <a href="/wiki/Expensive_Taste" title="Expensive Taste">Expensive Taste</a>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skinhead_Rob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aston's career began as a roadie for bands Boot Party, <a href="/wiki/AFI_(band)" title="AFI (band)">AFI</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rancid_(band)" title="Rancid (band)">Rancid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gwhatchet_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gwhatchet-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He eventually started a solo career, releasing the song "Show Me". He became involved in the <a href="/wiki/Transplants_(band)" title="Transplants (band)">Transplants</a> when <a href="/wiki/Tim_Armstrong" title="Tim Armstrong">Tim Armstrong</a> asked for lyrics to the songs Aston had been writing. The two started jamming in Armstrong's basement. They eventually invited <a href="/wiki/Travis_Barker" title="Travis Barker">Travis Barker</a> of <a href="/wiki/Blink-182" title="Blink-182">Blink-182</a> to provide drum tracks, instead of using drum-loops as originally planned.
</p><p>At the end of the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Vans_Warped_Tour" class="mw-redirect" title="Vans Warped Tour">Vans Warped Tour</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transplants_(band)" title="Transplants (band)">Transplants</a> took a hiatus and Rob and Travis joined <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/Paul_Wall" title="Paul Wall">Paul Wall</a>, who <a href="/wiki/Chopped_and_screwed" title="Chopped and screwed">chopped and screwed</a> the Transplants' <i><a href="/wiki/Haunted_Cities" title="Haunted Cities">Haunted Cities</a></i> album, to form a new group called <a href="/wiki/Expensive_Taste" title="Expensive Taste">Expensive Taste</a>. He has been interviewed by <a href="/wiki/Nardwuar_the_Human_Serviette" class="mw-redirect" title="Nardwuar the Human Serviette">Nardwuar the Human Serviette</a>, along with Travis. As of January 2010, according to drummer <a href="/wiki/Travis_Barker" title="Travis Barker">Travis Barker</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Transplants_(band)" title="Transplants (band)">Transplants</a> are active again and currently planning a third album.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As of  2021, it has indeed been released.
</p><p>Aston sang on the track "Red Hot Moon" about his sister, from <a href="/wiki/Rancid_(band)" title="Rancid (band)">Rancid</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Indestructible_(Rancid_album)" title="Indestructible (Rancid album)">Indestructible</a></i> album and participated in the music video. Aston also credited as additional background vocalist for <a href="/wiki/Death_by_Stereo" title="Death by Stereo">Death by Stereo</a>'s third album <i><a href="/wiki/Into_the_Valley_of_Death" title="Into the Valley of Death">Into the Valley of Death</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Travis_Barker" title="Travis Barker">Travis Barker</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Yelawolf" title="Yelawolf">Yelawolf</a>'s collaborative <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Psycho_White" title="Psycho White">Psycho White</a></i>. In 2006 Aston has made extra performance in stop motion-animated, musical independent film directed by <a href="/wiki/John_Roecker" title="John Roecker">John Roecker</a> "<i><a href="/wiki/Live_Freaky!_Die_Freaky!" title="Live Freaky! Die Freaky!">Live Freaky! Die Freaky!</a></i>".
</p>
