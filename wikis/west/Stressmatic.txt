<p><b>The Federation</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> group from <a href="/wiki/Fairfield,_California" title="Fairfield, California">Fairfield, California</a>. The group was formed in 2002, composed of producer <a href="/wiki/Rick_Rock" title="Rick Rock">Ricardo "Rick Rock" Thomas</a> and three rappers: Anthony "Goldie Gold" Caldwell, Marvin "Doonie Baby/Doon Koon" Selmon and <a href="/wiki/Stressmatic" class="mw-redirect" title="Stressmatic">Thomas "Stressmatic" Jackson</a>. The group released their <a href="/wiki/Federation:_The_Album" title="Federation: The Album">debut studio album</a> in 2004 on <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Records" title="Virgin Records">Virgin Records</a> and their <a href="/wiki/It%27s_Whateva" title="It&#39;s Whateva">sophomore album</a> through <a href="/wiki/Reprise_Records" title="Reprise Records">Reprise Records</a> in 2007, which is, to date, their recent effort.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Federation_(group)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rick_Rock" title="Rick Rock">Rick Rock</a> and Doonie Baby’s friendship and collaborative work dates back to the early ‘90s in <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>, where they recorded as two-thirds of the group <a href="/wiki/Cosmic_Slop_Shop" title="Cosmic Slop Shop">Cosmic Slop Shop</a> along with Big Lurch. At age 16 and using a wheelchair after being shot, the <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>-born, <a href="/wiki/Fairfield,_California" title="Fairfield, California">Fairfield</a>/<a href="/wiki/Vallejo,_California" title="Vallejo, California">Vallejo</a>-raised Federation member Goldie Gold met Rick Rock at a local mall, impressing Rock with his rapping skills.<sup id="cite_ref-emi_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-emi-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2002, Rick Rock recruited Doonie Baby, Goldie Gold, and Mr. Stress to form the group.<sup id="cite_ref-amg_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-amg-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The group debuted on Vallejo producer Mike Mosely's 2002 release <i>Major Work: The Soundtrack</i> with the Rick Rock-produced track "The Sickness", which sampled <a href="/wiki/Art_of_Noise" title="Art of Noise">Art of Noise</a>'s 1983 song "Moments of Love". Their first single "Hyphy", featuring <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, debuted in 2003, the title was based on a slang term established by Keak the Sneak in the 90s <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">Bay Area</a> hip hop music. Hyphy became an instant hit in the Bay Area. The song even induced a riot when The Federation performed "Hyphy" during halftime of the <a href="/wiki/AND1_Live_Tour" title="AND1 Live Tour">AND1 Live Tour</a> at <a href="/wiki/Oracle_Arena" class="mw-redirect" title="Oracle Arena">Oracle Arena</a> in June 2004. On the strength of "Hyphy" and their second single "Donkey", the group's self-titled debut album was released under <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Records" title="Virgin Records">Virgin Records</a> to critical reception. In 2005, Rick Rock signed the group under <a href="/wiki/Reprise_Records" title="Reprise Records">Reprise Records</a>, releasing the group's follow-up album <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_Whateva" title="It&#39;s Whateva">It's Whateva</a></i> in the summer of 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-amg_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-amg-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Stressmatic was featured in an interview in the book <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Rap" title="How to Rap">How to Rap</a></i>, along with several other rappers, including E-40, <a href="/wiki/Nelly" title="Nelly">Nelly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Twista" title="Twista">Twista</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>, <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/N.O.R.E." title="N.O.R.E.">N.O.R.E.</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Chuck_D" title="Chuck D">Chuck D</a>.
</p>
