<p><b>William Joseph Crawford</b> (born January 28, 1984), known by his stage name <b>Ya Boy</b> (also known as <b>Rich Rocka</b>), is an American rapper from the <a href="/wiki/Fillmore_District,_San_Francisco" title="Fillmore District, San Francisco">Fillmore District</a> of <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>. He is best known for his <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">feature</a> on <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a>'s 2011 song "<a href="/wiki/Third_Power_(album)" title="Third Power (album)">Lock Down</a>" alongside singer <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Throughout his career, he's been signed to <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Wall_Street_Records" title="The Black Wall Street Records">Black Wall Street Records</a>, Akon's labels <a href="/wiki/Konvict_Muzik" title="Konvict Muzik">Konvict Muzik</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kon_Live_Distribution" class="mw-redirect" title="Kon Live Distribution">Kon Live Distribution</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mack_10" title="Mack 10">Mack 10</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Hoo-Bangin%27_Records" title="Hoo-Bangin&#39; Records">Hoo-Bangin' Records</a>, and <a href="/wiki/San_Quinn" title="San Quinn">San Quinn</a>'s Done Deal Entertainment.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ya_Boy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Crawford grew up in San Francisco's <a href="/wiki/Fillmore_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Fillmore District">Fillmore District</a>, and graduated from <a href="/wiki/El_Camino_High_School_(South_San_Francisco)" class="mw-redirect" title="El Camino High School (South San Francisco)">El Camino High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_San_Francisco" class="mw-redirect" title="South San Francisco">South San Francisco</a>.
</p><p>His cousin is football player <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Johnson" title="Stevie Johnson">Stevie Johnson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ya_Boy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In late 2006, Ya Boy signed to <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">Game</a>’s Warner Brothers distributed label <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Wall_Street_Records" title="The Black Wall Street Records">The Black Wall Street Records</a>. After making an appearance on the label’s first release – a mixtape, <i>The Black Wall Street Journal, Vol. 1</i> – Ya Boy begun to work with a number of big-name producers including <a href="/wiki/Scott_Storch" title="Scott Storch">Scott Storch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Araab_Muzik" class="mw-redirect" title="Araab Muzik">Araab Muzik</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cool_%26_Dre" title="Cool &amp; Dre">Cool &amp; Dre</a> on his second full-length album <i>Holla at Ya Boy</i>. The album's lead single, "Holla at Ya Boy," was released in early 2008.
</p><p>In 2009 Ya Boy, with Nitevision, started his own record label, "Black Card Music" and signed to <a href="/wiki/Mack_10" title="Mack 10">Mack 10</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Hoo-Bangin%27_Records" title="Hoo-Bangin&#39; Records">Hoo-Bangin' Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However he would leave that label and in 2010 signed to <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a>'s label <a href="/wiki/Konvict_Muzik" title="Konvict Muzik">Konvict Muzik</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kon_Live_Distribution" class="mw-redirect" title="Kon Live Distribution">Kon Live Distribution</a>. In August 2010 he released his first single, "Party Girls," featuring <a href="/wiki/Rico_Love" title="Rico Love">Rico Love</a> and a second single "Lockdown" featuring his boss Akon on October 11, 2011. Ya Boy announced in early 2013 that he changed his name to <b>Rich Rocka</b>. He then released a mixtape "Road 2 Rocka" on July 4, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
