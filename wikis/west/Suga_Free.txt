<p><b>Dejuan Walker</b> (born January 17, 1970), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Suga Free</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suga_Free&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Suga Free was born in <a href="/wiki/Gardena,_California" title="Gardena, California">Gardena</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and raised in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Whitburn_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Whitburn-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> later becoming based in Pomona, California from the age of 11.
</p><p>He began his professional rapping career working with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a>, with Quik serving as the producer on his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Gospel" title="Street Gospel">Street Gospel</a></i>, released in 1997, which reached number 37 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Birchmeier_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Birchmeier-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Charts_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Charts-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He made <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> on <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Restless_(Xzibit_album)" title="Restless (Xzibit album)">Restless</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Last_Meal" title="Tha Last Meal">Tha Last Meal</a></i> in 2000, and released a second album in 2004, <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Testament_(The_Truth)" title="The New Testament (The Truth)">The New Testament</a></i>, which peaked at number 72 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Birchmeier_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Birchmeier-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Charts_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Charts-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Just_Add_Water_(Suga_Free_album)" title="Just Add Water (Suga Free album)">Just Add Water</a></i> followed in 2006, described by <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a>: "<i>Just Add Water</i> may not be the complete handbook to being a <a href="/wiki/Procuring_(prostitution)" title="Procuring (prostitution)">pimp</a>, but it certainly is a kind of missive on the life of Suga Free".<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The album saw Suga described as "charismatic and funny, with a rapid- fire-yet-conversational style" by the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Tribune" title="Chicago Tribune">Chicago Tribune</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Drake_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Drake-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After a ten-year break, Suga Free released the album <i>The Resurrection</i> on May 5, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-US_iTunes_The_Resurrection_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-US_iTunes_The_Resurrection-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
