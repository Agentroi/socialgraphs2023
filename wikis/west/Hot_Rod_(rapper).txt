<p><b>Rodney Toole</b> (born June 25, 1981) better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Hot Rod</b> (formerly <b>Young Hot Rod</b>),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> from <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento, California</a>, United States. In 2006, Hot Rod secured a recording contract with <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>-based rapper <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>'s <a href="/wiki/G-Unit_Records" title="G-Unit Records">G-Unit Records</a>. In 2010 he was moved to <a href="/wiki/G-Note_Records" title="G-Note Records">G-Note Records</a>, a subsidiary label of G-Unit Records, which focuses on <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> music.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hot_Rod_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Raised in <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento, California</a>, until the age of 24, Hot Rod worked as mortgage broker until spotted by the <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a> boss <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, who signed him to his label in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Hot Rod would send his homemade demo CD to 50 Cent's G-Unit office's in hope the record label would sign him. After 50 Cent heard the CD he thought that Hot Rod's easy flow reminded him of his own. It was December 2005 when the 24-year-old rapper received a call at his day job as a mortgage broker, and it wasn't from any random G-Unit associate but 50 Cent himself.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><br />
Hot Rod started in hip-hop as a teenager in the mid ‘90s, making beats. “When my pops made me his assistant music engineer at the church. That's when I realized my passion for music,” he reflects, “I've always had it in my blood so there was no stopping me from that point on”.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hot_Rod_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hot_Rod_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hot Rod released his first single in August 2006, called "Be Easy" which featured R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a> under the labels <a href="/wiki/G-Unit_Records" title="G-Unit Records">G-Unit</a> / <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The single failed to gain any major chart success and therefore he was dropped from the label <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope</a> in late 2006. Hot Rod was the first of various rappers to host the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Digital_Dynasty&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Digital Dynasty (page does not exist)">Digital Dynasty</a> series.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.93present:_My_Life"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–present:_My_Life">2010–present: <i>My Life</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hot_Rod_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010–present: My Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 16, 2010, Hot Rod released his second single in four years, "<a href="/wiki/Dance_with_Me_(Hot_Rod_song)" title="Dance with Me (Hot Rod song)">Dance with Me</a>", also the first single from his planned debut album <i>My Life</i>. Produced by Fuego and co-written by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tommy_Beringer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tommy Beringer (page does not exist)">Tommy Beringer</a> and Milana Leybovich of Nuthin Under a Million.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> This was the first single to be released under the newly-formed <a href="/wiki/G-Note_Records" title="G-Note Records">G-Note Records</a> / <a href="/wiki/G-Unit_Records" title="G-Unit Records">G-Unit Records</a> label.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with <i>Vibe</i> magazine Hot Rod revealed that his album was slated for a summer 2011 release date. He also stated that he had been working with producers such as <a href="/wiki/David_Guetta" title="David Guetta">David Guetta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Hot Rod had been recording music for his debut album, which was previously called <i>Fast Lane</i>, since he signed with G-Unit in 2006. Hot Rod tweeted that he was working with Snoop Dogg on the album.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In early 2011, a preview of Hot Rod's second single from the album, the title track "<a href="/wiki/My_Life_(Hot_Rod_song)" title="My Life (Hot Rod song)">My Life</a>", leaked onto the internet.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
