<p><b>Amisho Baraka Lewis</b> (born January 10, 1979), better known by his stage name <b>Sho Baraka</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Christian_hip-hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian hip-hop">Christian hip-hop</a> artist and writer who has recorded both independently and as a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/116_Clique" class="mw-redirect" title="116 Clique">116 Clique</a>. He was originally signed to <a href="/wiki/Reach_Records" title="Reach Records">Reach Records</a> until leaving in March 2011. He then co-founded a record label and group known as "High Society" with fellow Christian rappers <a href="/wiki/J.R._(musician)" title="J.R. (musician)">JR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swoope_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Swoope (rapper)">Swoope</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Suzy_Rock" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzy Rock">Suzy Rock</a>. His debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Turn_My_Life_Up" title="Turn My Life Up">Turn My Life Up</a></i> was released in November 2007. His second album <i>Lions and Liars</i>, was released March 2010. His third album <i><a href="/wiki/Talented_Xth" class="mw-redirect" title="Talented Xth">Talented Xth</a></i> was released in January 2013. His fourth album, <i>The Narrative,</i> was released in October 2016.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sho_Baraka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sho Baraka was born Amisho Baraka Lewis in <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, Canada<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> on January 10,<sup id="cite_ref-Day_One_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Day_One-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Day_Two_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Day_Two-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 1979.<sup id="cite_ref-Public_Records_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Public_Records-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Sho was introduced to hip hop at a young age. He was raised in Southern California during the height of <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_rap" title="Gangsta rap">gangsta rap</a> which inspired  Sho and some friends from his neighborhood in high school, formed a rap group that began touring with major recording artists. The rap group soon ended and many of Sho's close friends were killed or incarcerated. He decided to follow his father's advice and attend college, getting accepted into <a href="/wiki/Tuskegee_University" title="Tuskegee University">Tuskegee University</a>. There he came into contact with <a href="/wiki/Lecrae" title="Lecrae">Lecrae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tedashii" title="Tedashii">Tedashii</a> and eventually the <a href="/wiki/116_Clique" class="mw-redirect" title="116 Clique">116 Clique</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reach_Records" title="Reach Records">Reach Records</a> were formed.
</p><p>While attending Tuskegee, Sho converted to Christianity.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leaving_Reach_Records_and_new_website">Leaving Reach Records and new website</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sho_Baraka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Leaving Reach Records and new website">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On April 13, 2011, <a href="/wiki/Rapzilla" title="Rapzilla">Rapzilla</a> posted a blog from Sho stating that he planned to leave Reach Records after four years and two albums with the label. Although he is leaving the Reach label, Sho said he would continue to be an artist with Reach booking and continue to do concert dates and events with the label through 2011. He would also be featured on Reach projects released through the year.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Sho is now a part of the music &amp; art group High Society with <a href="/wiki/Swoope" title="Swoope">Swoope</a>, J.R., &amp; Suzy Rock. Lewis announced that he would release a new album, <i>Talented Xth</i>, on January 1, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 14, 2016, he signed with Christian hip-hop label, Humble Beast.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cameos">Cameos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sho_Baraka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cameos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sho can be spotted as <a href="/wiki/Trip_Lee" title="Trip Lee">Trip Lee</a>'s fictional boss in the video for Lee's song <i>The Invasion (hero)</i> off the album <i>Between Two Worlds</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Sho was in the music video for <a href="/wiki/Lecrae" title="Lecrae">Lecrae</a>'s lead single, "Don't Waste Your Life" from his 2008 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Rebel_(Lecrae_album)" title="Rebel (Lecrae album)">Rebel</a></i>, as a former gang member of Lecrae's who escaped arrest years before. Lecrae portrayed a converted Christian, writing to Sho's character about how God had changed his life.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
