<p><b>Dacoury Dahi Natche</b> (born March 10, 1983), professionally known as <b>Dahi</b>, is an American record producer, songwriter, <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a> and singer from <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood, California</a>. Dahi is best known for producing "<a href="/wiki/Money_Trees" title="Money Trees">Money Trees</a>" by American rapper <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, “<a href="/wiki/Worst_Behavior" title="Worst Behavior">Worst Behavior</a>" by Canadian rapper <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Fuck_with_You" title="I Don&#39;t Fuck with You">I Don't Fuck with You</a>" by American rapper <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>, which reached the <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">top 20</a> of the US Hot 100 chart.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Throughout his career Dahi has <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">produced</a> for a wide range of artists including <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>, <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>, <a href="/wiki/21_Savage" title="21 Savage">21 Savage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Don_Toliver" title="Don Toliver">Don Toliver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madonna" title="Madonna">Madonna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Childish_Gambino" class="mw-redirect" title="Childish Gambino">Childish Gambino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kali_Uchis" title="Kali Uchis">Kali Uchis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Lacy_(singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Steve Lacy (singer)">Steve Lacy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baby_Keem" title="Baby Keem">Baby Keem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vampire_Weekend" title="Vampire Weekend">Vampire Weekend</a>, <a href="/wiki/SZA" title="SZA">SZA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brent_Faiyaz" title="Brent Faiyaz">Brent Faiyaz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Post_Malone" title="Post Malone">Post Malone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kehlani" title="Kehlani">Kehlani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lupe_Fiasco" title="Lupe Fiasco">Lupe Fiasco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples" title="Vince Staples">Vince Staples</a>, <a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Producer_of_the_Year,_Non-Classical" title="Grammy Award for Producer of the Year, Non-Classical">Producer of the Year, Non-Classical</a>, Dahi won the <a <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Dahi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dahi learned disc jockeying while he was attending the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Santa_Cruz" title="University of California, Santa Cruz">University of California, Santa Cruz</a>, where he majored in Film and American studies.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> After returning to Los Angeles in 2007, he started collaborating with local rappers such as <a href="/wiki/Pac_Div" title="Pac Div">Pac Div</a>, TiRon, and <a href="/wiki/Fashawn" title="Fashawn">Fashawn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Dahi also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Dom_Kennedy" title="Dom Kennedy">Dom Kennedy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">ScHoolboy Q</a> before he achieved his first commercial success in 2012 with "<a href="/wiki/Money_Trees" title="Money Trees">Money Trees</a>" from <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>'s platinum album <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Kid,_M.A.A.D_City" title="Good Kid, M.A.A.D City">Good Kid, M.A.A.D City</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The album received several nominations including one for Grammy Album of the Year and Rap Album of the Year in <a href="/wiki/56th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="56th Annual Grammy Awards">2014</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-:3_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> while "Money Trees" was named by <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a> as</i> the ninth best instrumental of the previous five years.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-complex1_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Dahi produced "<a href="/wiki/Worst_Behavior" title="Worst Behavior">Worst Behavior</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a> that charted at number 89 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> after its release in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The following year, he co-produced the <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a> single <a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Fuck_with_You" title="I Don&#39;t Fuck with You">I Don't Fuck with You</a>, which reached the top of the US <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Billboard Hot R&amp;B/Hiphop songs</a> chart<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and received 8x Platinum certification from <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gold_&amp;_Platinum_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gold_&amp;_Platinum-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Dahi was also credited as a songwriter and producer for five tracks in Madonna's 2015 album <i><a href="/wiki/Rebel_Heart" title="Rebel Heart">Rebel Heart</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The same year Dahi co-produced three tracks on the album <i><a href="/wiki/Compton:_A_Soundtrack" class="mw-redirect" title="Compton: A Soundtrack">Compton: A Soundtrack</a></i> album by <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, Dahi co-produced "U with Me?" for the album <i>Views</i> by Drake, which earned him his second Grammy nomination for Album of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He also put forward a tentative release date for his own solo album titled <i>The Good Seed</i>, but the album did not materialize in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dahi collaborated a second time with Kendrick Lamar in 2017 and produced five tracks in the album <i>DAMN</i> that included "<a href="/wiki/Loyalty_(Kendrick_Lamar_song)" title="Loyalty (Kendrick Lamar song)">Loyalty</a>" featuring Rihanna and "<a href="/wiki/XXX_(Kendrick_Lamar_song)" title="XXX (Kendrick Lamar song)">XXX</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/U2" title="U2">U2</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The album received several critical accolades including a <a href="/wiki/Pulitzer_Prize_for_Music" title="Pulitzer Prize for Music">Pulitzer Prize for Music</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Best Rap Album</a> at the <a href="/wiki/60th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="60th Annual Grammy Awards">2018 Grammy Awards</a>. In 2020, the album was ranked 175th on <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s updated list of the <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone%27s_500_Greatest_Albums_of_All_Time" title="Rolling Stone&#39;s 500 Greatest Albums of All Time">500 Greatest Albums of All Time</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, Dahi produced two songs in 21 Savage's studio album <i><a href="/wiki/I_Am_Greater_than_I_Was" title="I Am Greater than I Was">I Am Greater than I Was</a>.</i> One of the songs, "a lot", earned him the 2020 Grammy Award for Rap Song of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Dahi collaborated with indie pop band <a href="/wiki/Vampire_Weekend" title="Vampire Weekend">Vampire Weekend</a> for their album <i><a href="/wiki/Father_of_the_Bride_(album)" title="Father of the Bride (album)">Father of the Bride</a></i> and co-produced the single "Big Blue", which charted number 33 in US <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rock_%26_Alternative_Songs" title="Hot Rock &amp; Alternative Songs">Hot Rock &amp; Alternative Songs</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He also co-produced the 2020 Childish Gambino album <i><a href="/wiki/3.15.20" title="3.15.20">3.15.20</a></i> that included <a href="/wiki/Time_(Childish_Gambino_song)" title="Time (Childish Gambino song)">"Time</a>" featuring Ariana Grande and "<a href="/wiki/12.38" title="12.38">12.38</a>" featuring 21 Savage, Ink and <a href="/wiki/Kadhja_Bonet" title="Kadhja Bonet">Kadhja Bonet</a> among seven other tracks co-produced by him<i>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In 2021, he produced "<a href="/wiki/Gravity_(Brent_Faiyaz_and_DJ_Dahi_song)" title="Gravity (Brent Faiyaz and DJ Dahi song)">Gravity</a>", a single by Brent Faiyaz and himself featuring <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a>, which reached a peak position on 71 in the US Billboard Hot 100 chart and was certified Platinum by RIAA.<sup id="cite_ref-Gold_&amp;_Platinum_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gold_&amp;_Platinum-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dahi teamed up with Kendrick Lamar again in 2022 and co-produced five tracks for <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Morale_and_the_Big_Steppers" class="mw-redirect" title="Mr. Morale and the Big Steppers">Mr. Morale and the Big Steppers</a>.</i> The album received nomination for Album of the Year and won in the Best Rap Album category in the <a href="/wiki/65th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="65th Annual Grammy Awards">2023 Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Dahi also received a Producer of the Year nomination for his work in <i>Mr. Morale &amp; the Big Steppers, <a href="/wiki/Gemini_Rights" title="Gemini Rights">Gemini Rights</a></i> (Steve Lacy album) and <i><a href="/wiki/Ramona_Park_Broke_My_Heart" title="Ramona Park Broke My Heart">RAMONA PARK BROKE MY HEART</a></i> (Vince Staples album).<i><sup id="cite_ref-:4_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></i>
</p><p>In 2023, Dahi appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary film</a> <i>Anthem</i> directed by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Nicks" title="Peter Nicks">Peter Nicks</a>, which followed Dahi and composer <a href="/wiki/Kris_Bowers" title="Kris Bowers">Kris Bowers</a> on a musical <a href="/wiki/Road_trip" title="Road trip">road trip</a> across the United States in a bid to reimagine the <a href="/wiki/The_Star-Spangled_Banner" title="The Star-Spangled Banner">national anthem</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The film premiered at <a href="/wiki/Tribeca_Festival" title="Tribeca Festival">Tribeca Festival</a> in June 2023 and was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Collective" title="Onyx Collective">Onyx Collective</a> for <a href="/wiki/Hulu" title="Hulu">Hulu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dahi has a publishing contract with <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Publishing" title="Sony Music Publishing">Sony Music Publishing</a><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and is a <a href="/wiki/SESAC" title="SESAC">SESAC</a> member.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
