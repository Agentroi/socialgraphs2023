<p><b>Andrew Robert Nielsen</b> (born October 6, 1982), known professionally as <b>MC Lars</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>, rapper, cartoonist, podcaster and educator. Lars is one of the self-proclaimed originators of "<a href="/wiki/Lit_Hop" title="Lit Hop">lit-hop</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and is the founder and CEO of the <a href="/wiki/Independent_music" title="Independent music">independent</a> <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a> <a href="#Horris_Records">Horris Records</a>.  Lars has been cited as the creator of the term "<a href="/wiki/IGeneration" class="mw-redirect" title="IGeneration">iGeneration</a>", which he was credited with doing in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Lars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lars attended <a href="/wiki/Stevenson_School" title="Stevenson School">Stevenson School</a>, a high school in <a href="/wiki/Pebble_Beach" class="mw-redirect" title="Pebble Beach">Pebble Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. For college and graduate school, he attended <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Oxford University">Oxford University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Lars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He has recorded for <a href="/wiki/Truck_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Truck Records">Truck Records</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sidecho_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Sidecho Records">Sidecho Records</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, Big Mouth Records in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Shock_Records" title="Shock Records">Shock Records</a> in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. He was formerly known as <b>MC Lars Horris</b> but changed his pseudonym to MC Lars in 2004 for purposes of simplicity.
</p><p>As a touring act, Nielsen has opened for <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/I_Fight_Dragons" title="I Fight Dragons">I Fight Dragons</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Matches" title="The Matches">the Matches</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lupe_Fiasco" title="Lupe Fiasco">Lupe Fiasco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simple_Plan" title="Simple Plan">Simple Plan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bowling_for_Soup" title="Bowling for Soup">Bowling for Soup</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gym_Class_Heroes" title="Gym Class Heroes">Gym Class Heroes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Say_Anything_(band)" title="Say Anything (band)">Say Anything</a>, <a href="/wiki/Streetlight_Manifesto" title="Streetlight Manifesto">Streetlight Manifesto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suburban_Legends" title="Suburban Legends">Suburban Legends</a>, <a href="/wiki/Test_Icicles" title="Test Icicles">Test Icicles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jack%27s_Mannequin" title="Jack&#39;s Mannequin">Jack's Mannequin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bayside_(band)" title="Bayside (band)">Bayside</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fightstar" title="Fightstar">Fightstar</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Frontalot" title="MC Frontalot">MC Frontalot</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Chris" title="MC Chris">MC Chris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wheatus" title="Wheatus">Wheatus</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Aquabats" title="The Aquabats">The Aquabats</a>, <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yung_Joc" title="Yung Joc">Yung Joc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cartel_(band)" title="Cartel (band)">Cartel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zebrahead" title="Zebrahead">Zebrahead</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Insane_Clown_Posse" title="Insane Clown Posse">Insane Clown Posse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_culture">Musical style and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Lars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Musical style and culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:MC_LARS.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/MC_LARS.jpg/250px-MC_LARS.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="183" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/MC_LARS.jpg/375px-MC_LARS.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/MC_LARS.jpg/500px-MC_LARS.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4381" data-file-height="3206" /></a><figcaption>MC Lars at the <a href="/wiki/Vans_Warped_Tour" class="mw-redirect" title="Vans Warped Tour">Vans Warped Tour</a> 2013.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Originally releasing tracks under the name Lars Horris, and then MC Lars Horris, Nielsen eventually dropped Horris (which later became the name of his record label), becoming MC Lars.  Lars plays with a laptop and occasionally a punk rock band to back him up, which he refers to as "post-punk laptop rap". <a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">Samples</a> from bands such as <a href="/wiki/Supergrass" title="Supergrass">Supergrass</a>, Piebald, <a href="/wiki/Brand_New_(band)" title="Brand New (band)">Brand New</a>, Fugazi, and Iggy Pop play a key role in MC Lars's music.  Hearts That Hate, whose song "Cry Tonight" is sampled in Lars' "Signing Emo", is a fictional group created by the rapper. The Grammy-nominated Texan band Bowling for Soup performed as Hearts That Hate when MC Lars has supported them on tour. A full version of "Cry Tonight" is available as a <a href="/wiki/B-side" class="mw-redirect" title="B-side">B-side</a> to the UK "Signing Emo" single.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>MC Lars has also shown an interest in using lyrics and song titles based on <a href="/wiki/English_literature" title="English literature">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/American_literature" title="American literature">American literature</a>. "Rapbeth" references <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s play <i><a href="/wiki/Macbeth" title="Macbeth">Macbeth</a></i>, while "Mr. Raven" is inspired by <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Allan_Poe" title="Edgar Allan Poe">Edgar Allan Poe</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/The_Raven" title="The Raven">The Raven</a>".  "Ahab" is about the novel <i><a href="/wiki/Moby_Dick" class="mw-redirect" title="Moby Dick">Moby Dick</a></i> and "Hey There Ophelia" on <i>This Gigantic Robot Kills</i> retells the story of Shakespeare's <i><a href="/wiki/Hamlet" title="Hamlet">Hamlet</a></i>.
</p><p>The term "<a href="/wiki/Generation_Y" class="mw-redirect" title="Generation Y">iGeneration</a>", coined by Lars in 2003, was used to describe the generation born primarily in the mid-to-late 1980s, was used in his song of the same name and given out for free to <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> users in August 2006 in conjunction with iTunes.  In early 2006, his song "Download This Song" was featured on the pop-culture <a href="/wiki/CBC_Radio" title="CBC Radio">CBC Radio</a> show <i>Definitely Not The Opera</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Intellectual_property_activism">Intellectual property activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Lars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Intellectual property activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2006, he released the single "<a href="/wiki/Download_This_Song" title="Download This Song">Download This Song</a>", which entered the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Singles_Chart" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian Singles Chart">Australian Singles Chart</a> at number 29, while working with the Canadian independent record label <a href="/wiki/Nettwerk" title="Nettwerk">Nettwerk Records</a> to release <i><a href="/wiki/The_Graduate_(MC_Lars_album)" title="The Graduate (MC Lars album)">The Graduate</a></i>. Following this release, Lars toured extensively throughout the US, then he toured UK and did shows in Japan,<sup id="cite_ref-Full_Effect_Magazine_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Full_Effect_Magazine-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and Australia. Not long after the album was released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>, Lars received an <a href="/wiki/E-mail" class="mw-redirect" title="E-mail">e-mail</a> from a 15-year-old fan Elisa Greubel on his web forum saying she identified with "Download This Song" since her family was one of many being sued by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a>. This led to Nettwerk executive <a href="/wiki/Terry_McBride_(CEO)" title="Terry McBride (CEO)">Terry McBride</a> and a team of major-label artists managed by Nettwerk to support the Greubels with their case.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> MC Lars returned to the UK in October 2006.  
</p><p>In April 2007, MC Lars and Nettwerk made the component tracks for his single "<a href="/wiki/White_Kids_Aren%27t_Hyphy" class="mw-redirect" title="White Kids Aren&#39;t Hyphy">White Kids Aren't Hyphy</a>" available for remix under a <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons_license" title="Creative Commons license">by-nc-sa</a> <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons" title="Creative Commons">Creative Commons</a> license on the Jamglue online mixing site as a contest.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Carnegie_Hall_performance_and_TEDx_Talks">Carnegie Hall performance and TEDx Talks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Lars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Carnegie Hall performance and TEDx Talks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After releasing the "Edgar Allan Poe EP", MC Lars performed at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Hall" title="Carnegie Hall">Carnegie Hall</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Scholastic_Art_%26_Writing_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Scholastic Art &amp; Writing Awards">Scholastic Art &amp; Writing Awards</a> National Ceremony.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Subsequently, he delivered a <a href="/wiki/TEDx_talk" class="mw-redirect" title="TEDx talk">TEDx talk</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a> on the ties between literature and rap, in collaboration with the <a href="/wiki/Annenberg_School_for_Communication_and_Journalism" class="mw-redirect" title="Annenberg School for Communication and Journalism">Annenberg School for Communication and Journalism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>  He later gave another TEDx talk in Homer, Alaska.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Horris_Records">Horris Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Lars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Horris Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following 2004's <a href="/wiki/The_Laptop_EP" class="mw-redirect" title="The Laptop EP">the Laptop EP</a>, Lars continued to grow his <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent record label</a> Horris Records.  Horris served as an imprint of <a href="/wiki/Nettwerk" title="Nettwerk">Nettwerk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oglio_Records" title="Oglio Records">Oglio</a> before becoming self-distributed.<sup id="cite_ref-hatchettv_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hatchettv-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In 2008, Horris signed a two-record distribution deal with Crappy Records, founded by <a href="/wiki/Jaret_Reddick" title="Jaret Reddick">Jaret Reddick</a>, from the American rock band <a href="/wiki/Bowling_For_Soup" class="mw-redirect" title="Bowling For Soup">Bowling For Soup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>   
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Mc_larsBang.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Mc_larsBang.jpg/220px-Mc_larsBang.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="98" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Mc_larsBang.jpg/330px-Mc_larsBang.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Mc_larsBang.jpg/440px-Mc_larsBang.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1206" data-file-height="537" /></a><figcaption>MC Lars performing with Australian band Wherewolves at Bang! <p>In 2008 and 2009, Lars worked with <a href="/wiki/%22Weird_Al%22_Yankovic" title="&quot;Weird Al&quot; Yankovic">"Weird Al" Yankovic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wheatus" title="Wheatus">Wheatus</a>, the <a href="/wiki/The_MC_Bat_Commander" class="mw-redirect" title="The MC Bat Commander">the MC Bat Commander</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Aquabats" title="The Aquabats">the Aquabats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suburban_Legends" title="Suburban Legends">Suburban Legends</a>, <a href="/wiki/Worm_Quartet" title="Worm Quartet">Worm Quartet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gabe_Saporta" title="Gabe Saporta">Gabe Saporta</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cobra_Starship" title="Cobra Starship">Cobra Starship</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brett_Anderson_(American_musician)" title="Brett Anderson (American musician)">Brett Anderson</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Donnas" title="The Donnas">the Donnas</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Frontalot" title="MC Frontalot">MC Frontalot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Dangerously" title="Jesse </p><p>In 2011, Horris signed <a href="/wiki/Josh_Eppard" title="Josh Eppard">Weerd Science</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hatchettv_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hatchettv-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Having his own label allowed Lars to experiment creatively.<sup id="cite_ref-MC_Lars_Kickstarter_Site_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MC_Lars_Kickstarter_Site-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>  <i><a href="/wiki/Lars_Attacks!" class="mw-redirect" title="Lars Attacks!">Lars Attacks!</a></i> was a return to basics, with less punk elements and more standard hip-hop stylings, funded by Kickstarter, featuring cameos from <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sage_Francis" title="Sage Francis">Sage Francis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mac_Lethal" title="Mac Lethal">Mac Lethal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MC_Lars_Kickstarter_Site_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MC_Lars_Kickstarter_Site-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>  MC Lars used Kickstarter to fund a "Greatest Hits" album on vinyl, which also included his "Edgar Allan Poe EP".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On November 6, 2015, Lars released <a href="/wiki/The_Zombie_Dinosaur_LP" title="The Zombie Dinosaur LP">the Zombie Dinosaur LP</a>, his fourth official album.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> After releasing various musical projects via <a href="/wiki/Bandcamp" title="Bandcamp">Bandcamp</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patreon" title="Patreon">Patreon</a> in the years that followed, Lars used <a href="/wiki/Kickstarter" title="Kickstarter">Kickstarter</a> to fund his fifth album “Blockchain Planet” in 2021. The name Horris Records is derived from a cartoon character Lars created in middle school which also served as the basis for Lars' original stage name, MC Lars Horris, named after a character from <a href="/wiki/Dr._Quinn_Medicine_Woman" class="mw-redirect" title="Dr. Quinn Medicine Woman">Dr. Quinn Medicine Woman</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hatchettv_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hatchettv-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_accompaniment">Live accompaniment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Lars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Live accompaniment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May, MC Lars toured the UK on the third installment of the Good To Go Tour, making friends with <a href="/wiki/Wheatus" title="Wheatus">Wheatus</a> front man Brendan B. Brown.  In July Lars and Brown recorded new songs, two of which would later appear on <a href="/wiki/This_Gigantic_Robot_Kills" title="This Gigantic Robot Kills">This Gigantic Robot Kills</a>. In November, Lars returned to the UK on tour with pop-punk band Last Letter Read who performed their own set and then on stage with Lars, debuting Lars' new song "Hey There Ophelia".
</p><p>On stage, Lars has been joined by <a href="/wiki/Wheatus" title="Wheatus">Wheatus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bowling_for_Soup" title="Bowling for Soup">Bowling for Soup</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simple_Plan" title="Simple Plan">Simple Plan</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Matches" title="The Matches">the Matches</a> and <a href="/wiki/Failsafe_(UK_band)" title="Failsafe (UK band)">Failsafe</a>.  While supporting <a href="/wiki/Zebrahead" title="Zebrahead">Zebrahead</a> in the UK and Europe in 2010, Ed Udhus, Greg Bergdorf and tour manager Bobby Conner joined Lars on stage. In 2019 MC Lars took to the stage with Newcastle-based unsigned alt rock outfit Ruled By Raptors as his backing band in the UK as part of his 10-year anniversary celebrations for This Gigantic Robot Kills. On the tour, MC Lars mentioned this was the first time that he had performed with a band without any form of backing track, instead relying on the band to recreate everything instrumentally or with samples and triggers.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
