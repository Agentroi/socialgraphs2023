<p><b>Sway Calloway</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radio_personality" title="Radio personality">radio personality</a>, <a href="/wiki/Executive_producer" title="Executive producer">executive producer</a> and former <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>. Known as <b>Sway</b>, he is known for hosting music, news, and culture programming. He was an on-air reporter and host for <a href="/wiki/MTV_News" title="MTV News">MTV News</a> and occasional non-news hosting, including numerous red carpet MTV award pre-shows, and reports on major events, concluding his tenture with the network as host of the short-lived <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Request_Live" title="Total Request Live">TRLAM</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He is co-hosting the nationally <a href="/wiki/Radio_syndication" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio syndication">syndicated</a> <a href="/wiki/Radio_show" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio show">radio show</a> <i>The Wake Up Show as</i> as one half of the duo <a href="/wiki/Sway_%26_King_Tech" title="Sway &amp; King Tech">Sway &amp; King Tech</a>; and hosting <i>Sway in the Morning</i> on <a href="/wiki/Sirius_XM_Satellite_Radio" class="mw-redirect" title="Sirius XM Satellite Radio">SiriusXM</a> <a href="/wiki/Shade_45" title="Shade 45">Shade45</a>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sway_Calloway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a <a href="/wiki/Teenager" class="mw-redirect" title="Teenager">teenager</a> growing up in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a>, Sway became a locally known <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> and <a href="/wiki/B-boy" class="mw-redirect" title="B-boy">b-boy</a> <a href="/wiki/Busking" class="mw-redirect" title="Busking">performing</a> on <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Pier_39" title="Pier 39">Pier 39</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He teamed up with <a href="/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJ</a> <a href="/wiki/King_Tech" title="King Tech">King Tech</a> after <a href="/wiki/High_school" class="mw-redirect" title="High school">high school</a> and the duo performed at various <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">San Francisco Bay Area</a> clubs. They also released <a href="/wiki/Indie_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Indie (music)">independent</a> albums. A <a href="/wiki/Major_label" class="mw-redirect" title="Major label">major label</a> <a href="/wiki/Record_deal" class="mw-redirect" title="Record deal">record deal</a> with <a href="/wiki/Giant_Records_(Warner)" title="Giant Records (Warner)">Giant Records</a> followed. The resulting <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a>, <i>Concrete Jungle</i> in 1990, got them the job of co-hosting their own show on <a href="/wiki/Radio_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio station">radio station</a> <a href="/wiki/KMEL" title="KMEL">KMEL</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Barack_Obama_and_Sway_Calloway.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Barack_Obama_and_Sway_Calloway.jpg/255px-Barack_Obama_and_Sway_Calloway.jpg" decoding="async" width="255" height="170" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Barack_Obama_and_Sway_Calloway.jpg/383px-Barack_Obama_and_Sway_Calloway.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Barack_Obama_and_Sway_Calloway.jpg/510px-Barack_Obama_and_Sway_Calloway.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4096" data-file-height="2731" /></a><figcaption>President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> adjusts Calloway's hat after a 2012 interview for a Live MTV special at the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a>.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Another album, <i>Back 2 Basics</i> was released in 2005 on Sway and Tech's own <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>, Bolo Entertainment, which is distributed by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Music">Universal Music</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio">Radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sway_Calloway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Wake Up Show</i> featured music and interviews with well-known <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> artists as well as up-and-coming ones. The show became very popular and began <a href="/wiki/Simulcasting" class="mw-redirect" title="Simulcasting">simulcasting</a> to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> on <a href="/wiki/KKLQ_(FM)#KKBT" title="KKLQ (FM)">KKBT</a> in 1993 and to <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> on <a href="/wiki/WNTD" title="WNTD">WEJM</a> by 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-wakeup_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wakeup-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ras_Kass" title="Ras Kass">Ras Kass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chino_XL" title="Chino XL">Chino XL</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> are among the rappers who made their <a href="/wiki/Broadcasting" title="Broadcasting">broadcasting</a> debuts on the show. Soon, Sway was also hosting his own morning <a href="/wiki/Drive_time" title="Drive time">drive time</a> show on the station.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The popularity of the show helped Sway and Tech get another record deal, this time with <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>. Their album, <i>This or That</i>, reached #30 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard magazine">Billboard</a>'s R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums chart and #1 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers">Top Heatseekers</a> chart in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The album featured contributions from hip hop artists such as <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharoahe_Monch" title="Pharoahe Monch">Pharoahe Monch</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">The Roots</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wakeup_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wakeup-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mtv_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Sway also made an appearance on <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>'s DVD, <a href="/wiki/The_Reel_Me" title="The Reel Me">The Reel Me</a> in late 2003.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Sway hosts a weekday morning show on Eminem’s <a href="/wiki/Shade_45" title="Shade 45">Shade 45</a> channel on <a href="/wiki/SiriusXM" class="mw-redirect" title="SiriusXM">SiriusXM</a>! “Sway in the Morning” launched on Monday, July 18, 2011 on Shade 45 (SiriusXM channel 45) and airs Monday-Friday from 8am-12 noon ET.  The second week he was there <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.fileswap.com/dl/NIdcVBaZ/sway_-_ludacris_-_bk1.mp3.html">phoned in on the show</a> and they talked about his music and movie career. In an interview with The Source in 2012, Sway talked about his experiences in both satellite and terrestrial radio.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2013, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> appeared as a guest on his show and had an infamous meltdown on air, uttering memorable lines like "You ain't got the answers Sway, You ain't been doing the education".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sway_Calloway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2000, Sway was approached by MTV to join the <a href="/wiki/Television_network" class="mw-redirect" title="Television network">network</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Correspondent" title="Correspondent">correspondent</a>, becoming a regular reporter for its <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> shows and <a href="/wiki/News" title="News">news</a> specials, including <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Request_Live" title="Total Request Live">Total Request Live</a></i> and the hip-hop music video show <i><a href="/wiki/Sucker_Free" title="Sucker Free">Direct Effect</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-mtv_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Because MTV's studios are based in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, King Tech and <i>The Wake Up Show</i> went along with Sway in his move. Sway's prominence on MTV made the radio show even more popular than it was before. This inevitably led to a TV version of the show on MTV running briefly in January 2004. Sway and Tech also have a popular series of <a href="/wiki/Mixtapes" class="mw-redirect" title="Mixtapes">mixtapes</a> that feature <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rapping" class="mw-redirect" title="Freestyle rapping">freestyles</a> performed on the show. Sway recorded the voice introduction for the <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XXXVIII_halftime_show" title="Super Bowl XXXVIII halftime show">Super Bowl XXXVIII halftime show</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2005, Sway decided not to renew his contract with MTV.  However, in 2006, Sway signed a new contract that included an ability to bring new projects to MTV, making him the first <a href="/wiki/TV_personality" class="mw-redirect" title="TV personality">TV personality</a> since <a href="/wiki/Carson_Daly" title="Carson Daly">Carson Daly</a> to negotiate such a deal (Daly turned down a similar contract). He established the opportunity to produce <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_programming" title="Broadcast programming">programming</a> with King Tech on MTV and other <a href="/wiki/Viacom_(2005%E2%80%93present)" class="mw-redirect" title="Viacom (2005–present)">Viacom</a> channels as well as continue to host programs.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He hosted coverage of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>'s death for the network.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sway appeared in cameo role voicing "Sway" in "The Story Of Gangstalicious", the December 11, 2005 episode of the animated series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boondocks" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="The Boondocks">The Boondocks</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He appeared as a guest star on the <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> sketch comedy show <i><a href="/wiki/Short_Circuitz" class="mw-redirect" title="Short Circuitz">Short Circuitz</a></i> in 2007.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
