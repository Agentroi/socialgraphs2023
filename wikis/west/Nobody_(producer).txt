<p><b>Elvin Estela</b> (born March 14, 1977<sup id="cite_ref-birthday_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birthday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>), better known by his stage name <b>Nobody</b>, is an American hip hop producer based in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is a resident DJ at <a href="/wiki/Low_End_Theory" title="Low End Theory">Low End Theory</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He has collaborated with <a href="/wiki/2Mex" title="2Mex">2Mex</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abstract_Rude" title="Abstract Rude">Abstract Rude</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_Fellowship" title="Freestyle Fellowship">Freestyle Fellowship</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Busdriver" title="Busdriver">Busdriver</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mystic_Chords_of_Memory" title="Mystic Chords of Memory">Mystic Chords of Memory</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He has also been a member of Blank Blue<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Bomb_Zombies" title="Bomb Zombies">Bomb Zombies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i>Better Propaganda</i> placed him at number 73 on the "Top 100 Artists of the Decade" list.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nobody_(producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nobody's solo debut album, <i>Soulmates</i>, was released on <a href="/wiki/Ubiquity_Records" title="Ubiquity Records">Ubiquity Records</a> in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The second solo album, <i>Pacific Drift: Western Water Music Vol. 1</i>, was released in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He released the third solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/And_Everything_Else..." title="And Everything Else...">And Everything Else...</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/Plug_Research" title="Plug Research">Plug Research</a> in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Mia_Doi_Todd" title="Mia Doi Todd">Mia Doi Todd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prefuse_73" class="mw-redirect" title="Prefuse 73">Prefuse 73</a> and Xololanxinxo, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nobody wrote the article about 1960s psychedelic rock for <i><a href="/wiki/XLR8R" title="XLR8R">XLR8R</a></i> in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008, Estela released the album, <i>Western Water Music Vol. II</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> on Ubiquity Records under the Blank Blue moniker, a collaboration with Niki Randa.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It is the official follow-up to his 2003 album, <i>Pacific Draft: Western Water Music Vol. 1</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The duo's sound has been compared to <a href="/wiki/The_Monkees" title="The Monkees">The Monkees</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Shadow" title="DJ Shadow">DJ Shadow</a>, and <a href="/wiki/J_Dilla" title="J Dilla">J Dilla</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Live the pair have been assisted by drummer Andres Renteria and bassist Brian Akio Martinez.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The fourth solo album, <i>One for All Without Hesitation</i>, was released on <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Pup_Records" title="Alpha Pup Records">Alpha Pup Records</a> in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He returned with <i>Vivid Green</i>, an album featuring <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Bixler-Zavala" title="Cedric Bixler-Zavala">Cedric Bixler-Zavala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nocando" title="Nocando">Nocando</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baths_(musician)" title="Baths (musician)">Baths</a>, in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, he released a studio album, <i>All Too Familiar</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
