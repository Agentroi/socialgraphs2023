<p><b>Gerard Damien Long</b> (born November 9, 1990), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Hodgy</b> (formerly <b>Hodgy Beats</b>), is an American rapper, record producer and music video director. He is best known for being a founding member of the hip hop collective <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hodgy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hodgy was an original member of Odd Future along with <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a>, <a href="/wiki/Left_Brain" class="mw-redirect" title="Left Brain">Left Brain</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Super_3" class="mw-redirect" title="The Super 3">The Super 3</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Casey_Veggies" title="Casey Veggies">Casey Veggies</a>. Hodgy was the first member of Odd Future to release a solo record, with <i>The Dena Tape</i> in 2009. As a member of the duo <a href="/wiki/MellowHype" title="MellowHype">MellowHype</a> with <a href="/wiki/Left_Brain" class="mw-redirect" title="Left Brain">Left Brain</a>, he has released four albums; <i><a href="/wiki/YelloWhite" title="YelloWhite">YelloWhite</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/BlackenedWhite" title="BlackenedWhite">BlackenedWhite</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Numbers_(Mellowhype_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Numbers (Mellowhype album)">Numbers</a></i>, and <i>INSA</i>.
</p><p>Hodgy has been featured on various albums including <i><a href="/wiki/Bastard_(Tyler,_The_Creator_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bastard (Tyler, The Creator album)">Bastard</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Goblin_(album)" title="Goblin (album)">Goblin</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Wolf_(Tyler,_The_Creator_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Wolf (Tyler, The Creator album)">Wolf</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_The_Creator" class="mw-redirect" title="Tyler, The Creator">Tyler, The Creator</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Earl_(album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Earl (album)">Earl</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Earl_Sweatshirt" title="Earl Sweatshirt">Earl Sweatshirt</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Papers_(Domo_Genesis_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rolling Papers (Domo Genesis album)">Rolling Papers</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Domo_Genesis" title="Domo Genesis">Domo Genesis</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/119_(album)" title="119 (album)">119</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Trash_Talk_(band)" title="Trash Talk (band)">Trash Talk</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Odd_Future_Tape" class="mw-redirect" title="The Odd Future Tape">The Odd Future Tape</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Radical_(mixtape)" class="mw-redirect" title="Radical (mixtape)">Radical</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_OF_Tape_Vol._2" title="The OF Tape Vol. 2">The OF Tape Vol. 2</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a>. He appeared on "Outta Control", a song from <a href="/wiki/Stones_Throw_Records" title="Stones Throw Records">Stones Throw</a> rapper M.E.D.'s album <i>Classic</i>, produced by fellow Los Angeles native <a href="/wiki/Madlib" title="Madlib">Madlib</a>. Hodgy also appeared in the music video for that song which was released after the song was released.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hodgy has released five solo mixtapes: <i>The Dena Tape</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Untitled_Hodgy_Beats_EP" class="mw-redirect" title="Untitled Hodgy Beats EP">Untitled EP</a></i>, <i>Untitled 2 EP</i>, <i>Dena Tape 2</i>, and <i>They Watchin' LoFi Series 1</i>. On October 31, 2013, <a href="/wiki/MellowHigh" title="MellowHigh">MellowHigh</a> (Left Brain, Hodgy Beats, Domo Genesis) released their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/MellowHigh_(album)" title="MellowHigh (album)">MellowHigh</a></i> on Odd Future Records. The album debuted at number 89 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, Hodgy revealed that he was almost done with his solo debut album. He also starred as a feature artist on Alison Wonderland's remix of The Buzz, by Hermitude.
</p><p>His debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Fireplace:_TheNotTheOtherSide" title="Fireplace: TheNotTheOtherSide">Fireplace: TheNotTheOtherSide</a></i>, was released in December 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 24th, 2022 after six years of inactivity, Hodgy released a single titled "Into Someone" and announced an upcoming project, <i>Entitled</i>, which was released on May 20, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hodgy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gerard Long was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Trenton,_NJ" class="mw-redirect" title="Trenton, NJ">Trenton</a>, New Jersey. He is of <a href="/wiki/Jamaicans" title="Jamaicans">Jamaican</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fijian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Filipino</a> descent<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. At the age of nine, he moved to Southern California with his sister after his mother got remarried.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Pasadena_High_School_(California)" title="Pasadena High School (California)">Pasadena High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pasadena,_California" title="Pasadena, California">Pasadena, California</a>.
</p><p>On August 1, 2011, his long-time ex-girlfriend Cortney Brown, gave birth to their son, named Trenton Gerald Long. Vyron Turner (Left Brain) is Trenton's godfather.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the release of <i>Entitled</i>, Long revealed in an interview that he had committed to a <a href="/wiki/Drug_rehabilitation" title="Drug rehabilitation">rehab</a> program in early 2020, before moving to <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> after his release. He currently lives there with his daughter and two sons.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
