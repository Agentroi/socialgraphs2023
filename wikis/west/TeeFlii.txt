<p><b>Christian Joel Jones</b> (born July 17, 1987), better known by his stage name <b>TeeFlii</b> (stylized as <b>TeeFLii</b>), is an American singer, songwriter, rapper, record producer, and dancer from <a href="/wiki/South_Los_Angeles" title="South Los Angeles">South Los Angeles</a>, California. He is best known for his debut commercial single "<a href="/wiki/24_Hours_(TeeFlii_song)" title="24 Hours (TeeFlii song)">24 Hours</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>), which was released in March 2014. The song was imcluded on his debut studio album, <i>Starr</i> which was released in 2015 by <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TeeFlii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>TeeFlii developed an interest in music as a child attending church, drawn to keyboards after seeing how much his grandfather, who was a pastor, paid his <a href="/wiki/Organ_(instrument)" class="mw-redirect" title="Organ (instrument)">organ</a> players.<sup id="cite_ref-thefader1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Frequenting his grandfather's church, he learned to play multiple instruments, such as the organ, piano, guitar and drums.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He played drums for <a href="/wiki/Barbra_Streisand" title="Barbra Streisand">Barbra Streisand</a> at a <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic Party</a> event. Music helped provide stability in his life, as his mother battled drug addiction throughout his childhood, and he did not know his biological father until his teens. TeeFlii began smoking marijuana at age eight and moved out of his parents' place for good at age thirteen.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He described his childhood self by saying, "I was bad—fighting, stealing. I'd go take your bike, bring it to my backyard, spray-paint it, try to ride it around."<sup id="cite_ref-thefader1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a teenager, TeeFlii began dancing and <a href="/wiki/Krumping" title="Krumping">krumping</a>, performing under the name Baby Tight Eyez, and earned placement in a few early <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a> music videos as well as appearing in a major role in <i><a href="/wiki/Rize_(film)" title="Rize (film)">Rize</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/David_LaChapelle" title="David LaChapelle">David LaChapelle</a>'s 2005 documentary about Los Angeles' Krump scene. After the film was released, by the time he was 19, he had been living at various friends' houses, and after having his first daughter he went through an issue with the mother, where he lost visitation rights. He then started writing songs for other people to "get the vent off" and started seeing a better chance at stability in his life, so he pushed himself as an artist.<sup id="cite_ref-thefader1_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He created his stage name partially from his experience in the movie "Rize." He says in an interview, "TeeFLii came from dancing. I was in the Rize movie, and my name in the movie was Baby Tight Eyez. I was under somebody in dancing named Tight Eyez, then I wanted to branch off and do my own thing when I started getting older. I wanted to have my own name and something I could say that I did for myself. I grew up under another dude named Flii Stylz who choreographed for Chris Brown, Usher, Omarion, everybody in the game. I took a liking to him and next thing I knew, I just took the name TeeFLii."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TeeFlii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:NipseyHussle-and-TeeFLii.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/NipseyHussle-and-TeeFLii.jpg/200px-NipseyHussle-and-TeeFLii.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="288" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/NipseyHussle-and-TeeFLii.jpg/300px-NipseyHussle-and-TeeFLii.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/NipseyHussle-and-TeeFLii.jpg/400px-NipseyHussle-and-TeeFLii.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1601" data-file-height="2304" /></a><figcaption>TeeFlii <i>(left)</i> performing with frequent collaborator <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a> in December 2013.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Early on his career prior to releasing his first project, TeeFlii collaborated with West Coast-based rappers such as <a href="/wiki/Problem_(rapper)" title="Problem (rapper)">Problem</a>, <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skeme" title="Skeme">Skeme</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His debut mixtape <i>AnNieRuO'TAY 1</i> was released on November 21, 2012. It featured collaborations with Skeme, Problem, <a href="/wiki/Bad_Lucc" class="mw-redirect" title="Bad Lucc">Bad Lucc</a>, and Nipsey Hussle among others.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <i>AnNieRuO'TAY 1</i> was met with generally positive reviews from music critics.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His second official mixtape <i>AnNieRuO'TAY 2: The TakeOver</i> was released on March 5, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-thefader2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It featured production by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Mustard" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Mustard">DJ Mustard</a>, <a href="/wiki/1500_or_Nothin%27" title="1500 or Nothin&#39;">1500 or Nothin'</a> and TeeFlii himself. At the same time he was recording his next mixtape, a project entirely produced by DJ Mustard to be released soon.<sup id="cite_ref-thefader2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He also indicated that he was working on a project with Nipsey Hussle.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i>AnNieRuO'TAY 2</i> was met with mainstream coverage from publications such as <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Reader" title="Chicago Reader">Chicago Reader</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Fact_(UK_magazine)" title="Fact (UK magazine)">Fact</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> among others.
</p><p>On July 4, 2013, TeeFlii released his third mixtape in eight months, the DJ Mustard-produced <i>Fireworks</i>. It featured collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Dom_Kennedy" title="Dom Kennedy">Dom Kennedy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Casey_Veggies" title="Casey Veggies">Casey Veggies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>, <a href="/wiki/Omarion" title="Omarion">Omarion</a> and <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape would receive positive reviews upon release, <a href="/wiki/Stereogum" title="Stereogum">Stereogum</a> would end up naming it the "Mixtape of the Week".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> also would name it the fourth best mixtape of 2013. They said, "Where Mustard's beats normally evoke a strip club's booty-whomp, here he pushes himself, brilliantly, towards R&amp;B smoothness."<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On July 16, 2013, the music video for "<a href="/wiki/This_D" title="This D">This D</a>" was premiered by <i><a href="/wiki/Vice_(magazine)" title="Vice (magazine)">Vice</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In August 2013, TeeFlii signed to <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a> after being actively persuaded by label executive <a href="/wiki/L.A._Reid" title="L.A. Reid">L.A. Reid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-thefader1_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Then on October 2, 2013, "This D" was released as his debut single.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The following day, he released the remix to "This D", featuring <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> After "This D" received major radio rotation in Los Angeles, he got a major co-sign from rapper <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The song would peak at number 35 on the US <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Rhythmic_(chart)" title="Rhythmic (chart)">Rhythmic</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2013, <i><a href="/wiki/LA_Weekly" title="LA Weekly">LA Weekly</a></i> named TeeFlii the best R&amp;B singer in Los Angeles.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On November 25, 2013, he released his fourth mixtape <i>AnnieRUO'TAY 3: Who The Fuck Is Annie?</i>. The seventeen track mixtape featured a collaboration with Dom Kennedy and production by <a href="/wiki/The_Futuristiks" class="mw-redirect" title="The Futuristiks">The Futuristiks</a> and himself, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On December 9, 2013, "That's How We Livin'" a collaboration with Snoop Dogg and <a href="/wiki/Warren_G" title="Warren G">Warren G</a> was released. It was then included on Snoop's mixtape <i>That's My Work Vol. 2</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 26, 2014, TeeFlii premiered his second official single "<a href="/wiki/24_Hours_(TeeFlii_song)" title="24 Hours (TeeFlii song)">24 Hours</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>, with production by DJ Mustard. Along with its release, it was announced that TeeFlii's debut studio album would be titled <i>Starr</i> and released during mid-2014.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> "24 Hours" was serviced to <a href="/wiki/Urban_contemporary" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban contemporary">urban contemporary</a> radio in the United States on April 7, 2014,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and then was serviced to <a href="/wiki/Rhythmic_contemporary" title="Rhythmic contemporary">rhythmic contemporary</a> radio on May 6, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The track peaked at number 85 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a> and served as a bonus track on <i>Starr</i>.
</p><p>On August 27, 2021, TeeFlii released his EP, "Today", which included his new single "Automatic" produced by Grammy Award Winning Producer <a href="/wiki/Los_Da_Mystro" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Da Mystro">Los Da Mystro</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=TeeFlii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>TeeFlii's invocations of his faith are frequent as he believes in Christianity.  His grandmother was a gospel singer and his aunt harmonized for <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a>'s backup group, <a href="/wiki/The_Sweet_Inspirations" title="The Sweet Inspirations">The Sweet Inspirations</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His grandmother is the one that taught him the most about singing.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He has five children; four daughters, and one son.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly1_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
