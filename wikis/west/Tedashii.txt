<p><b>Tedashii Lavoy Anderson</b> (born March 8, 1977), known simply as <b>Tedashii</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Christian_hip_hop" title="Christian hip hop">Christian hip hop</a> artist and member of the hip-hop troupe, <a href="/wiki/116_Clique" class="mw-redirect" title="116 Clique">116 Clique</a>. He also hosted the <a href="/wiki/NGEN_Radio" title="NGEN Radio">NGEN Radio</a> show "Serium". Tedashii has released five solo albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_People" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingdom People">Kingdom People</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Identity_Crisis_(Tedashii_album)" title="Identity Crisis (Tedashii album)">Identity Crisis</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Blacklight_(Tedashii_album)" title="Blacklight (Tedashii album)">Blacklight</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Below_Paradise" title="Below Paradise">Below Paradise</a></i>, and <i>Never Fold</i> on <a href="/wiki/Reach_Records" title="Reach Records">Reach Records</a>. Tedashii's delivery style is characterized by a deep voice that he can manipulate for rapping a wide range of flow schemes and patterns.
</p><p>In 2008, Tedashii toured with the 116 Clique during the "Unashamed Tour".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The "Don't Waste Your Life Tour" followed in 2009, after which he took a brief break.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In late 2010 he returned for the "Unashamed: The Movement Tour".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As soon as his album <i>Blacklight</i> was released in 2011 he went on a brief tour to promote it.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> He joined the "Man Up Tour" in 2011, taking a brief break in early 2012.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In late 2012 116 Clique toured 30 cities with the "Unashamed Tour: Come Alive".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Then in December, <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Sims" title="Natalie Sims">Suzy Rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/KB_(rapper)" title="KB (rapper)">KB</a> joined him for the "No Boundaries Concert Series".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Tedashii appeared in <a href="/wiki/Family_Force_5" title="Family Force 5">Family Force 5</a>'s song "Chainsaw" on the remix album <i><a href="/wiki/Reanimated_(Family_Force_5_album)" title="Reanimated (Family Force 5 album)">Reanimated</a></i> released in 2013. In 2021, Tedashii appeared on the song "Donde están (Watcho 6)" for producer <a href="/wiki/Cardec_Drums" title="Cardec Drums">Cardec Drums</a>' album <a href="/wiki/Sin_Verg%C3%BCenza_(116_album)" title="Sin Vergüenza (116 album)">Sin Vergüenza</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tedashii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tedashii Lavoy Anderson was born in <a href="/wiki/Lufkin,_Texas" title="Lufkin, Texas">Lufkin, Texas</a>, on March 8, 1977, to mother Vera Louise Anderson.<sup id="cite_ref-Public_Records_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Public_Records-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DRC_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRC-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tedashii currently lives with his wife in <a href="/wiki/Denton,_Texas" title="Denton, Texas">Denton, Texas</a>, serving at The Village Church.<sup id="cite_ref-DRC_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRC-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In March 2013 Tedashii's one-year-old son died. He recorded much of his spiritual struggle during that time and based his fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Below_Paradise" title="Below Paradise">Below Paradise</a></i>, off of his experiences.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ivm_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ivm-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
