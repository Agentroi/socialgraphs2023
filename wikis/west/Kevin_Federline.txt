<p><b>Kevin Earl Federline</b> (born March 21, 1978),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> often referred to, and <a href="/wiki/Also_known_as" class="mw-redirect" title="Also known as">also known as</a> <b>K-Fed</b>, is an American dancer, rapper, actor, model, professional wrestler and DJ.<sup id="cite_ref-model_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-model-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is known for his two-year marriage to American singer <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>, for whom he was previously a <a href="/wiki/Backup_dancer" title="Backup dancer">backup dancer</a>, and the child custody battle that followed,<sup id="cite_ref-amg_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-amg-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> which earned significant media coverage. His popularity led to him releasing his critically panned debut album <a href="/wiki/Playing_with_Fire_(Kevin_Federline_album)" title="Playing with Fire (Kevin Federline album)"><i>Playing with Fire</i></a> in 2006, which is commonly considered to be <a href="/wiki/List_of_music_considered_the_worst" title="List of music considered the worst">one of the worst albums ever released</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He has since made a number of television appearances, and began a career as a DJ.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_Federline&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kevin Earl Federline was born on March 21, 1978, in <a href="/wiki/Fresno,_California" title="Fresno, California">Fresno, California</a>, to parents Mike and Julie (née Story), a car mechanic and former bank teller from <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>, respectively. Federline's parents divorced when he was eight years old; then he moved to <a href="/wiki/Carson_City,_Nevada" title="Carson City, Nevada">Carson City, Nevada</a>, with his mother, but returned to Fresno when he was 11, when he and his brother Chris moved to his father's house. In the ninth grade, Federline dropped out of high school (later earning a certificate of <a href="/wiki/General_Educational_Development" title="General Educational Development">General Educational Development</a>) and began dancing with a nonprofit organization called Dance Empowerment.<sup id="cite_ref-people_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-people-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_Federline&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2000.E2.80.932005:_Early_career_as_a_dancer"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000–2005:_Early_career_as_a_dancer">2000–2005: Early career as a dancer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_Federline&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000–2005: Early career as a dancer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For a number of years, he worked as a <a href="/wiki/Backup_dancer" title="Backup dancer">backup dancer</a> for <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Destiny%27s_Child" title="Destiny&#39;s Child">Destiny's Child</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pink_(singer)" title="Pink (singer)">Pink</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/LFO_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="LFO (group)">LFO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-amg_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-amg-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-people_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-people-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fire_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fire-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He appeared as a Dirt Track Dancer in two episodes of the television <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> <a href="/wiki/Nikki_(TV_series)" title="Nikki (TV series)"><i>Nikki</i></a> (2001–2002) and as a dancer on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Drew_Carey_Show" title="The Drew Carey Show">The Drew Carey Show</a></i> (2001) and an uncredited role as an Angel Dancer for Cher on the sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Will_%26_Grace" title="Will &amp; Grace">Will &amp; Grace</a></i> (2002).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He appeared in several <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music videos</a> as a dancer, including <a href="/wiki/Christina_Milian" title="Christina Milian">Christina Milian</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/AM_to_PM" title="AM to PM">AM to PM</a>" (2001), <a href="/wiki/Pink_(singer)" title="Pink (singer)">Pink</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Get_the_Party_Started" title="Get the Party Started">Get the Party Started</a>" (2001) and Justin Timberlake's "<a href="/wiki/Like_I_Love_You" title="Like I Love You">Like I Love You</a>" (2002). He also appeared as a dancer in the dance drama film <i><a href="/wiki/You_Got_Served" title="You Got Served">You Got Served</a></i> (2004).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.932009:_Wrestling_and_musical_debut"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–2009:_Wrestling_and_musical_debut">2006–2009: Wrestling and musical debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_Federline&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006–2009: Wrestling and musical debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Federline and his then-wife <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a> appeared in <a href="/wiki/UPN" title="UPN">UPN</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">reality television</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Britney_%26_Kevin:_Chaotic" class="mw-redirect" title="Britney &amp; Kevin: Chaotic">Britney &amp; Kevin: Chaotic</a></i> (2005), which consisted of their home videos. The series chronicles the couple's relationship from their courtship, engagement and wedding.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He was nominated for a Choice TV: Male Reality/Variety Star award for his role on <i>Britney &amp; Kevin: Chaotic</i> at the <a href="/wiki/2005_Teen_Choice_Awards" title="2005 Teen Choice Awards">2005 Teen Choice Awards</a>. He portrayed a hunter in a commercial for Britney Spears' fragrance "<a href="/wiki/Fantasy_(Fragrance)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fantasy (Fragrance)">Fantasy</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Federline appeared on the cover and a 12-page shoot for <i>L'uomo Vogue</i>, an Italian version of <i><a href="/wiki/Men%27s_Vogue" title="Men&#39;s Vogue">Men's Vogue</a></i>, shot by <a href="/wiki/Steven_Meisel" title="Steven Meisel">Steven Meisel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He subsequently guest-starred as Cole "Pig" Tritt, an arrogant teenager who harasses investigators on a job, in <a href="/wiki/Fannysmackin%27_(CSI_episode)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fannysmackin&#39; (CSI episode)">an episode</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i> (2006).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He also appeared on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Game_show" title="Game show">game show</a> <i><a href="/wiki/1_vs._100_(American_game_show)" title="1 vs. 100 (American game show)">1 vs. 100</a></i> (2006), along with the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_(TV_series)" title="Las Vegas (TV series)">Las Vegas</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In June 2006, he teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Richard_Branson" title="Richard Branson">Richard Branson</a> to launch a "Pennies for a Purpose" campaign, which aimed to save the U.S. penny from annihilation.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Federline initially released a download-only song, "Y'all Ain't Ready", and a physical single, "<a href="/wiki/PopoZ%C3%A3o" class="mw-redirect" title="PopoZão">PopoZão</a>" in 2006, shortly after his marriage to Spears. Following poor critical reaction, neither song was included on his debut album.<sup id="cite_ref-amg_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-amg-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Instead, the first official single was "Lose Control", which he premiered on the <a href="/wiki/Teen_Choice_Awards" title="Teen Choice Awards">Teen Choice Awards</a> show on the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> network in late summer 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-amg_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-amg-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Playing_with_Fire_(Kevin_Federline_album)" title="Playing with Fire (Kevin Federline album)">Playing with Fire</a></i>, on October 31, 2006, with the album being panned by critics. Federline has been signed to model for the Five Star Vintage line by the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>-based Blue Marlin clothing company. The initial series of ads ran in August 2006 and was subsequently extended for Christmas 2006. The line he modeled for the fall 2006 was the top-selling line in <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy&#39;s">Macy's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kitson_(store)" title="Kitson (store)">Kitson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lord_%26_Taylor" title="Lord &amp; Taylor">Lord &amp; Taylor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Federline appeared on <a href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="World Wrestling Entertainment">WWE</a> programming to promote his album, <i>Playing with Fire</i>, and participate in an <a href="/wiki/Angle_(professional_wrestling)" class="mw-redirect" title="Angle (professional wrestling)">angle</a>. He first appeared on the October 16, 2006, and October 23, 2006, editions of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">WWE Raw</a></i>, in which he got into physical altercations with <a href="/wiki/WWE_Championship" title="WWE Championship">WWE Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Cena" title="John Cena">John Cena</a>. The following week, he came to the ring to aid <a href="/wiki/John_Morrison_(wrestler)" title="John Morrison (wrestler)">Johnny Nitro</a> in his match against John Cena. After Cena got in Federline's face, Federline slapped him hard. He appeared again during the November 5, 2006, <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> event <i><a href="/wiki/Cyber_Sunday_(2006)" title="Cyber Sunday (2006)">Cyber Sunday</a></i>, hitting Cena with King Booker's <a href="/wiki/World_Heavyweight_Championship_(WWE,_2002%E2%80%932013)" title="World Heavyweight Championship (WWE, 2002–2013)">World Heavyweight Championship</a> belt and helping Booker win the Champion of Champions match that was organized between Cena, Booker, and Big Show. The next night, November 6, 2006, he challenged Cena to a match at the New Year's Day edition of <i>Raw</i>, which Cena accepted. He went on to win this no-disqualification match thanks to interference from Johnny Nitro and <a href="/wiki/Umaga_(wrestler)" title="Umaga (wrestler)">Umaga</a>. Later in the night, after John Cena's main event match, Cena went after Federline and brought him into the ring and delivered his <a href="/wiki/Fireman%27s_carry_takeover" class="mw-redirect" title="Fireman&#39;s carry takeover">FU</a> finishing move twice before cameras stopped rolling. According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Wrestling_Observer_Newsletter" title="Wrestling Observer Newsletter">Wrestling Observer Newsletter</a></i>, Federline was well-received backstage. Sources say he worked so well with the company and personnel that the WWE may give him a regular role on the company's show <i>Raw</i> in the near future.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Other sources and wrestlers have spoken about Federline's generally good attitude backstage as well.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Federline was featured in a <a href="/wiki/Nationwide_Mutual_Insurance_Company" title="Nationwide Mutual Insurance Company">Nationwide Mutual Insurance Company</a> commercial during <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLI" title="Super Bowl XLI">Super Bowl XLI</a> on February 4, 2007, as part of its "Life Comes at You Fast" ad series. In the commercial, Federline initially appeared in a music video, rapping about living a high-flying lifestyle. Then reality sets in as Federline is shown now working in a fast-food restaurant with his manager yelling at him, the punchline of the ad being "Life comes at you fast".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Federline appeared on the December 2007 cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Details_(magazine)" title="Details (magazine)">Details</a></i> magazine, and he was named 7th most-influential man under 45, a spot he shared with <a href="/wiki/Dannielynn_Birkhead_paternity_case" title="Dannielynn Birkhead paternity case">Larry Birkhead</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/HuffPost" title="HuffPost">HuffPost</a></i> reported three months later, that the December 2007 issue was on track to sell more than 75,000 copies, nearly 15,000 copies more than Federline's March 2005 cover issue.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Federline guest-starred as Jason "J-Fed" Federline in three episodes of <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a>'s television series <i><a href="/wiki/One_Tree_Hill_(TV_series)" title="One Tree Hill (TV series)">One Tree Hill</a></i> (2008).<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He played Lollipop Love Dancer in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Onion_Movie" title="The Onion Movie">The Onion Movie</a></i> (2008), which was filmed in 2003, and a <a href="/wiki/Border_guard" title="Border guard">border guard</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/American_Pie_Presents:_The_Book_of_Love" title="American Pie Presents: The Book of Love">American Pie Presents: The Book of Love</a> (2009).</i><sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In February 2009, he announced plans to start a children's clothing line.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.93present:_Focus_on_DJing"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–present:_Focus_on_DJing">2010–present: Focus on DJing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_Federline&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010–present: Focus on DJing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Federline appeared on the seventh season of <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s weight-loss reality television show <i><a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Fit_Club_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Celebrity Fit Club (U.S. TV series)">Celebrity Fit Club</a></i> (2010).<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He told <i><a href="/wiki/Access_Hollywood" title="Access Hollywood">Access Hollywood</a>,</i> that the reason for his weight gain was feeling depressed about his divorce from Spears and not feeling happy with himself.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> While appearing on the show, Federline lost 30 pounds.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In November 2011, on a weight-loss show in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> called <i><a href="/wiki/Excess_Baggage_(TV_series)" title="Excess Baggage (TV series)">Excess Baggage</a></i> (2012), Federline was hospitalized for what was called "heat stress". Filming was taking place in November which is the hottest time of the year in the <a href="/wiki/Kimberley_(Western_Australia)" title="Kimberley (Western Australia)">Kimberley</a> region of northern Western Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On January 23, 2012, he suffered chest pains while filming the show.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 7, 2015, he worked as a DJ at Heat nightclub in <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_California" title="Orange County, California">Orange Country</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/DJ_The_Prophet" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ The Prophet">DJ Prophet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He also hosts club events regularly with his wife Victoria Prince such as a fantasy football event in 2015, Federline saying: "We take [fantasy football] so seriously that there are months of planning that go into the strategy of what your going to do with the other people. This is our 6th season. It’s been same people from the beginning. We have a waiting list to get into our league."<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He was featured as an <a href="/wiki/Ice_sculpture" title="Ice sculpture">ice sculpture</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fast-food_restaurant" title="Fast-food restaurant">fast-food restaurant</a> chain <a href="/wiki/Jack_in_the_Box" title="Jack in the Box">Jack in the Box</a>'s television ommercial in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> In April 2016, Federline released a music video for his new song, "Hollywood" with rapper Crichy Crich.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Produced by Styles &amp; Complete, the song was inspired by "the ridiculousness of Hollywood."<sup id="cite_ref-:2_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> In a 2016 interview with <i>Vegas Seven</i>, Federline said that he has been producing music for other artists.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, he was a DJ at Three Wisemen club in <a href="/wiki/Scottsdale,_Arizona" title="Scottsdale, Arizona">Scottsdale, Arizona</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He has also played DJ sets in Washington,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Texas<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> and California.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> In June 2019, Federline performed a DJ set at Harrah’s Hotel and Casino in <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_City,_New_Jersey" title="Atlantic City, New Jersey">Atlantic City, New Jersey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_Federline&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_Shar_Jackson">Relationship with Shar Jackson</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_Federline&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Relationship with Shar Jackson">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Federline was engaged to <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actress</a> <a href="/wiki/Shar_Jackson" title="Shar Jackson">Shar Jackson</a>, with whom he has a daughter, Kori Madison Federline (born July 31, 2002), and a son, Kaleb Michael Jackson Federline (born July 20, 2004).<sup id="cite_ref-people_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-people-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-details_interview_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-details_interview-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> The couple separated shortly before their son's birth. Federline ended things with Jackson for a relationship with pop singer <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>. Jackson later commented that Federline's relationship with Spears "wasn't like just breaking up a relationship. It was like breaking up a family",<sup id="cite_ref-people_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-people-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> but sustained an amicable relationship with Federline, complimenting his parenting.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_to_Britney_Spears">Marriage to Britney Spears</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_Federline&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Marriage to Britney Spears">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After three months of dating, Federline and Spears announced their engagement in July 2004; they were married on September 18 in a <a href="/wiki/Nondenominational" class="mw-redirect" title="Nondenominational">nondenominational</a> ceremony at a residence in <a href="/wiki/Studio_City,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio City, California">Studio City, California</a>, but became legally valid on October 6.<sup id="cite_ref-married_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-married-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Due to the fact that Federline got into a relationship with Spears when his ex-girlfriend Shar Jackson was pregnant with Federline's second child,<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> the romance between Federline and Spears received intense attention from the media, with accusations of Federline leaving Jackson when she was pregnant to get with Spears<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AllMusicBiography_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusicBiography-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> and Federline being seen as a "<a href="/wiki/Gold_digger" title="Gold digger">gold digger</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The controversy of his breakup with Jackson, the controversy of his relationship with Spears, the accusations of him being a "gold digger", and the disparity in fame between him and Spears all made Federline both a frequent target of ridicule and an extremely disliked celebrity.<sup id="cite_ref-AllMusicBiography_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusicBiography-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Federline and Spears have two sons together, born in September 2005<sup id="cite_ref-sean_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sean-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> and September 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-jayden_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jayden-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Spears filed for divorce from Federline on November 7, 2006, citing <a href="/wiki/Irreconcilable_differences" title="Irreconcilable differences">irreconcilable differences</a> and asking for both physical and legal custody of their two sons, with visitation rights for Federline.<sup id="cite_ref-divorce_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-divorce-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> The following day, Federline filed a response to Spears' divorce petition, seeking physical and legal custody of their children.<sup id="cite_ref-custody_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-custody-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> According to a representative for Federline's lawyer, the divorce filing "caught Kevin totally by surprise". On December 4, 2006, <a href="/wiki/MSNBC" title="MSNBC">MSNBC</a> reported that Federline set up a home for himself and his family in the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Hills" title="Hollywood Hills">Hollywood Hills</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> The couple reached a global settlement agreement in March 2007 and their divorce was finalized on July 30, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-agreement_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-agreement-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Federline maintains a relationship with Spears' parents for the benefit of their children. Members of Spears' family have been photographed in and out of his home and babysitting the boys while Kevin was out of town.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 1, 2007, a court ruling granted Federline sole physical custody of his children, with legal custody to be decided. On the late evening of January 3, 2008, police were called to Spears' home after the singer reportedly refused to relinquish custody of her children to Federline and locked herself in a room with her younger son, Jayden.<sup id="cite_ref-hospital_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hospital-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Spears was taken from her home on a stretcher and hospitalized at <a href="/wiki/Cedars-Sinai_Medical_Center" title="Cedars-Sinai Medical Center">Cedars-Sinai Medical Center</a> for appearing to be under the influence of an unknown substance.<sup id="cite_ref-hospital_57-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hospital-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> As a result of this, Federline was "awarded sole legal custody and sole physical custody of the minor children".<sup id="cite_ref-custody_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-custody-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 26, 2008, court papers were filed stating Federline would retain sole legal and physical custody of the children while Spears would get visitation rights that would increase over time. In addition to this, Federline will receive $20,000 per month from Spears in <a href="/wiki/Child_support" title="Child support">child support</a> as well as additional funds to cover any custody-related legal expenses.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_to_Victoria_Prince">Marriage to Victoria Prince</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_Federline&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Marriage to Victoria Prince">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Starting in 2008, Federline was linked to former volleyball player Victoria Prince. The two were both on the same recreational bowling team, the Party Animals.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Their daughter Jordan Kay was born in August 2011; she is Federline's fifth child and Prince's first.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> They were married on August 10, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> In April 2014 they had their second child together, another daughter.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> In the summer 2023, the family moved to <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>, because Prince was offered a job at a Hawaii university.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> Prince played volleyball and graduated from <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hawai%CA%BBi_at_M%C4%81noa" title="University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa">University of Hawaii at Manoa</a> in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
