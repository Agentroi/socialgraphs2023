<p><b>Dwan Howard</b>, better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Curtiss King</b><sup id="cite_ref-Nobody_Cares_Show_S3_#_9_Curtiss_King,_Qwestions,_Scot_Double_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nobody_Cares_Show_S3_#_9_Curtiss_King,_Qwestions,_Scot_Double-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> recording artist and record producer based out of the <a href="/wiki/Inland_Empire_(California)" class="mw-redirect" title="Inland Empire (California)">Inland Empire</a>. Signed to independent label, <a href="/wiki/Black_Cloud_Music" title="Black Cloud Music">Black Cloud Music</a>, King is also known for his productions credits with artists such as <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Noa_James" title="Noa James">Noa James</a>, <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a> recording artist <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ab-Soul (rapper)">Ab-Soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glasses_Malone" title="Glasses Malone">Glasses Malone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mack_10" title="Mack 10">Mack 10</a> and more.<sup id="cite_ref-Your_Favorite_Rapper_is_Poor:_Interview_With_Indie_Producer_Curtiss_King_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Your_Favorite_Rapper_is_Poor:_Interview_With_Indie_Producer_Curtiss_King-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Curtiss_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Before_2003:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings">Before 2003: Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Curtiss_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Before 2003: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Curtiss King grew up as a shy child, particularly while attending Curtiss Middle school, he found ways to combat his insecurities by listening to music and getting lost in 90s sitcoms; giving him his stage name Curtiss King.
From the beginning, with the help of a high school friend, <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, and a library card, Curtiss King set out to teach himself how to produce, engineer and write his own records, edit his own videos, design his own album covers, script his own websites, and build his brand from the ground up.<sup id="cite_ref-Featured_Musician:_Curtiss_King_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Featured_Musician:_Curtiss_King-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2003.E2.80.932008:_Notorious_Scholars_and_The_Storm_On_Mars"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2003–2008:_Notorious_Scholars_and_The_Storm_On_Mars">2003–2008: <i>Notorious Scholars</i> and <i>The Storm On Mars</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Curtiss_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2003–2008: Notorious Scholars and The Storm On Mars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shortly after graduating High School in 2003, Curtiss began creating and circulating numerous mixtapes to his Cerritos college classmates. After joining Myspace in 2005, he began to gain local popularity for his self pressed projects. In 2007 his production demand got a huge boost when he got a placement on the <a href="/wiki/Vans" title="Vans">Vans</a> Downtown Showdown Skate DVD as well as TDE artist <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ab-Soul (rapper)">Ab-Soul</a>’s first project <i>Longterm</i> with the song “Watch Yo Lady" ft. K-Dot (<a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>). Later that same year Curtiss King signed to Rocstarr Entertainment, an up-and-coming independent label run by producer <a href="/wiki/Tae_Beast" class="mw-redirect" title="Tae Beast">Tae Beast</a> and producer/engineer/songwriter Starr Lab based out of <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood</a>. Under the imprint he released two well received street albums <i>Notorious Scholars</i> and <i>The Storm On Mars</i> which boasted features from Ab-Soul and Young Rook as well as production from Tae Beast. After a few years Curtiss and Rocstarr Entertainment parted ways, and once again he found himself having to grind it out alone.<sup id="cite_ref-Unsigned_Hype:_Curtiss_King_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Unsigned_Hype:_Curtiss_King-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While working a part-time job, King soon began to attend <a href="/wiki/Orange_Coast_College" title="Orange Coast College">Orange Coast College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Costa_Mesa,_California" title="Costa Mesa, California">Costa Mesa</a> where he eventually earned his Entry Level Marketing Certificate. Shortly after his collegiate accomplishments, he lost both of his grandparents to Cancer. Seeking a brand new start, Curtiss eventually moved to the <a href="/wiki/Inland_Empire_(California)" class="mw-redirect" title="Inland Empire (California)">Inland Empire</a> with his mother. From spitting acapellas at local poetry venues to entering beat contests, he made it a point to engage himself in this vibrant music scene.
</p>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.93_2010:_Black_Cloud_Music.2C_Snick_.40_Nite_and_Jet_Pack_On_E"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–_2010:_Black_Cloud_Music,_Snick_@_Nite_and_Jet_Pack_On_E">2009– 2010: Black Cloud Music, <i>Snick @ Nite</i> and <i>Jet Pack On E</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Curtiss_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009– 2010: Black Cloud Music, Snick @ Nite and Jet Pack On E">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, he saw his fanbase and production demand skyrocketed after collaborating with and producing for highly acclaimed artist <a href="/wiki/Noa_James" title="Noa James">Noa James</a> on his <i>Sounds of A Monster</i> and Ab-Soul’s <i>Longterm 2</i>. Shortly after these collaborations, King’s work eventually caught the attention of engineer/producer/rapper Jynxx, who approached King about signing to his independent label, <a href="/wiki/Black_Cloud_Music" title="Black Cloud Music">Black Cloud Music</a>. Under the <a href="/wiki/Black_Cloud_Music" title="Black Cloud Music">Black Cloud Music</a> imprint Curtiss began to gain nationwide exposure as an artist with the release of projects such as <i>Snick @ Nite</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-GUEST_STAR:_I_DIDN&#39;T_WANT_TO_MAKE_IT_A_CORNY_PROJECT_WHERE_I&#39;M_THE_BLACK_DOUGs_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GUEST_STAR:_I_DIDN&#39;T_WANT_TO_MAKE_IT_A_CORNY_PROJECT_WHERE_I&#39;M_THE_BLACK_DOUGs-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and <i>Jet Pack On E</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ab-Soul_-_Longterm_2:_Lifestyles_Of_The_Broke_&amp;_Almost_Famous_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ab-Soul_-_Longterm_2:_Lifestyles_Of_The_Broke_&amp;_Almost_Famous-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, King’s production began to take on a world wide exposure when he was invited to compete in the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull" title="Red Bull">Red Bull</a> Big Tune Beat Battle. Curtiss would also see additional exposure when he scored his first major label placement on Glasses Malone and Mack 10’s album <i>Money Music</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Glasses_Malone&#39;s_Top_Five_Cash_Money_Albums_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Glasses_Malone&#39;s_Top_Five_Cash_Money_Albums-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2011.E2.80.93present:_Paid_Dues_Festival_2012.2C_Atychiphobia"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–present:_Paid_Dues_Festival_2012,_Atychiphobia">2011–present: Paid Dues Festival 2012, <i>Atychiphobia</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Curtiss_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011–present: Paid Dues Festival 2012, Atychiphobia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2011, King released his highly anticipated EP <i>DIY (Do It Yourself)</i>, hosted by Ashley Outrageous.<sup id="cite_ref-Curtiss_King:_D.I.Y._EP_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Curtiss_King:_D.I.Y._EP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Boasting indie favorites such as "Sinbad," "21Sev7n," and "PCC" with features from Dirty Birdy, <a href="/wiki/Noa_James" title="Noa James">Noa James</a>, Faimkills, and Sean Faylon, <i>DIY</i> was the tipping point for Curtiss King's career.<sup id="cite_ref-Don’t_Let_This_Curtiss_King_“D.I.Y.”_Mixtape_Slip_Through_the_Cracks_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Don’t_Let_This_Curtiss_King_“D.I.Y.”_Mixtape_Slip_Through_the_Cracks-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late 2011, King was given the rare opportunity to produce for a live action video game trailer for the popular computer game <a href="/wiki/APB:_All_Points_Bulletin" title="APB: All Points Bulletin">"APB: Reloaded"</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-APB_Reloaded_Trailer_Exclusive:_Music_by_Chaundon_&amp;_Curtiss_King_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-APB_Reloaded_Trailer_Exclusive:_Music_by_Chaundon_&amp;_Curtiss_King-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After putting together a 97-day campaign to get on the lineup for the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Paid_Dues" title="Paid Dues">Paid Dues Festival</a>, King was officially invited by hip hop artist <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a> to perform on the Dues Paid Stage at the festival, ending a successful campaign. Shortly after performing at <a href="/wiki/Paid_Dues" title="Paid Dues">Paid Dues</a> in April 2012, King made more noise a month later after producing Ab-Soul's single, "A Rebellion", featuring Alori Joh, from his highly anticipated album <i><a href="/wiki/Control_System" title="Control System">Control System</a></i>, which went on to be number one on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> Hip Hop Charts.<sup id="cite_ref-Producer-Emcee_Curtiss_King_Takes_A_Minute_To_Chat_About_Music_And_Marijuana_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Producer-Emcee_Curtiss_King_Takes_A_Minute_To_Chat_About_Music_And_Marijuana-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SAN_BERNARDINO:_Paid_Dues_festival_set_for_Saturday_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SAN_BERNARDINO:_Paid_Dues_festival_set_for_Saturday-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ab-Soul_Control_System_Album_Review_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ab-Soul_Control_System_Album_Review-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 3, 2019, King released "Ratchets Still Jockin" as his first official single from  his debut album <i>AtyChiphobia – The Fear Of Failure</i>, released during the summer of 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-Curtiss_King_“Atychiphobia“_Commercial_#1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Curtiss_King_“Atychiphobia“_Commercial_#1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, King produced the entirety of <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dee-1" title="Dee-1">Dee-1</a>'s 11-track album, <i>He's the Christian, I'm the Rapper</i>, released on September 18, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Curtiss_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rapping since the age of 17 and producing since 18, King names artists such as <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sade_(band)" title="Sade (band)">Sade</a>, <a href="/wiki/2pac" class="mw-redirect" title="2pac">2pac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cameo_(band)" title="Cameo (band)">Cameo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zapp_and_Roger" class="mw-redirect" title="Zapp and Roger">Zapp and Roger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earth_Wind_and_Fire" class="mw-redirect" title="Earth Wind and Fire">Earth Wind and Fire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jill_Scott_(singer)" title="Jill Scott (singer)">Jill Scott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>, and 80s synths as some of his inspirations.<sup id="cite_ref-Rightzones_Radio_interview_w/_Noa_James_&amp;_Curtiss_King_about_Paid_Dues_in_the_Inland_Empire_music_scene_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rightzones_Radio_interview_w/_Noa_James_&amp;_Curtiss_King_about_Paid_Dues_in_the_Inland_Empire_music_scene-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
