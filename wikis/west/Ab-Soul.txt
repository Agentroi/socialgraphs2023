<p><b>Herbert Anthony Stevens IV</b> (born February 23, 1987),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Ab-Soul</b>, is an American rapper, singer and songwriter. Raised in <a href="/wiki/Carson,_California" title="Carson, California">Carson, California</a>, he signed to indie record label <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a> (TDE) in 2007, where he eventually formed <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/Black_Hippy" title="Black Hippy">Black Hippy</a>, alongside fellow California-based rappers <a href="/wiki/Jay_Rock" title="Jay Rock">Jay Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a>. He is perhaps most known for his introspective lyrics and his five <a href="/wiki/Independent_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent album">independent albums</a> under TDE, <i><a href="/wiki/Longterm_Mentality" title="Longterm Mentality">Longterm Mentality</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Control_System" title="Control System">Control System</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/These_Days..._(Ab-Soul_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="These Days... (Ab-Soul album)">These Days...</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Do_What_Thou_Wilt." title="Do What Thou Wilt.">Do What Thou Wilt.</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Herbert_(album)" title="Herbert (album)">Herbert</a></i>, which were all released to positive reviews and commercial success.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ab-Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Herbert Anthony Stevens IV was born on February 23, 1987, in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. He spent the first four years of his life in Korea while his father was in the military, right up until his parents split and he and his mother subsequently moved back to the United States, to live in his grandmother's house in <a href="/wiki/Carson,_California" title="Carson, California">Carson, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-germany_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-germany-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He once recalled being five years old: "I was serious into <a href="/wiki/Video_games" class="mw-redirect" title="Video games">video games</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>, at five. But video games for sure. Five… What was that, <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a>? <a href="/wiki/Sega" title="Sega">Sega</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (series)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a>. <a href="/wiki/Teenage_Mutant_Ninja_Turtles" title="Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles">Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles</a> on TV. But I know early on, I really, really liked <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>, like we all did. Newborn babies loved Michael Jackson."<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At the age of 10, Stevens contracted <a href="/wiki/Stevens%E2%80%93Johnson_syndrome" title="Stevens–Johnson syndrome">Stevens–Johnson syndrome</a>, which caused him to be hospitalized and is the origin of his dark lips and <a href="/wiki/Light_sensitivity" class="mw-redirect" title="Light sensitivity">light-sensitive</a> eyes.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In his adolescent years, Stevens was severely <a href="/wiki/Teasing" title="Teasing">teased</a> about his condition.<sup id="cite_ref-complex4_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex4-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Stevens encountered music at a very young age, due to his family owning a <a href="/wiki/Record_store" class="mw-redirect" title="Record store">record store</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Stevens also began crafting his <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapping</a> skills at an early age: "I was on <a href="/wiki/BlackPlanet" title="BlackPlanet">BlackPlanet</a> <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a> <a href="/wiki/Online_chat" title="Online chat">chat</a>, rapping my ass off. Text <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">battle</a>. It is a very interesting world. I think that it still exists online, where you <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a> but you type it. They call it keystyle. I think that is where I developed my rhyming skills. I had been rapping a little earlier, maybe around 12 [years old], but when I hopped online and into that culture, that textcee culture. It really got me going as far as being a rapper."<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Stevens claims to have written his first <a href="/wiki/Verse_(popular_music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Verse (popular music)">verse</a> when he was 12 years old, to the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">beat</a> of <a href="/wiki/Twista" title="Twista">Twista</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Emotions_(Twista_song)" title="Emotions (Twista song)">Emotions</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-complex2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Rapping continued on as a <a href="/wiki/Hobby" title="Hobby">hobby</a> for Stevens, whose parents "saw a bright, college-type future" for him and enrolled him in advanced classes.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was not until Stevens graduated from high school that he began to take a career in music seriously.<sup id="cite_ref-complex2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ab-Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2002.E2.80.9310:_Career_beginnings_and_signing_to_TDE"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2002–10:_Career_beginnings_and_signing_to_TDE">2002–10: Career beginnings and signing to TDE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ab-Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2002–10: Career beginnings and signing to TDE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ab-Soul.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Ab-Soul.jpg/220px-Ab-Soul.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="317" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Ab-Soul.jpg/330px-Ab-Soul.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Ab-Soul.jpg/440px-Ab-Soul.jpg 2x" data-file-width="858" data-file-height="1237" /></a><figcaption>Ab-Soul in 2011.</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2002, Stevens recorded his first song. In 2005, he signed a <a href="/wiki/Recording_contract" title="Recording contract">recording contract</a> with StreetBeat Entertainment, but just a year later, in 2006, he met <a href="/wiki/Punch_(rapper)" title="Punch (rapper)">Punch</a>, <a href="/wiki/President_(corporate_title)" title="President (corporate title)">President</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chairman" class="mw-redirect" title="Chairman">Chairman</a> of Carson-based <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">indie record label</a>, <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a> (TDE), who as Stevens says, "saw more in his music than <a href="/wiki/Metaphor" title="Metaphor">metaphors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Punch_line" title="Punch line">punchlines</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-complex3_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex3-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Before signing to TDE, Stevens was a part of a <a href="/wiki/Rap_group" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap group">rap group</a> called Area 51, alongside <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>-native and fellow American rapper Snake Hollywood.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, Stevens officially became part of the TDE roster. In 2008, Stevens made a brief <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo appearance</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for his TDE-mate Jay Rock's commercial debut single, "<a href="/wiki/All_My_Life_(In_the_Ghetto)" title="All My Life (In the Ghetto)">All My Life (In the Ghetto)</a>". Also in 2008, he began recording music for his debut <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, at the TDE Recording Studio: House of Pain. In December 2008, Stevens released his first <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a>, for a song from the mixtape, titled "A Day in the Life", through <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape, entitled <i>Longterm</i>, was released in January 2009. It was the first in a series, that Stevens claimed would have four installments: "When I did the first <i>Longterm</i> I knew that there would be four of them. When I did the first one. So there will be four of them: <i>Longterm 1, 2, 3, and 4</i>. So right now we're at two. You'll have to wait for the next one. That's for the next Ab-Soul interview.".<sup id="cite_ref-complex5_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex5-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, he formed supergroup <a href="/wiki/Black_Hippy" title="Black Hippy">Black Hippy</a>, with his frequent collaborators and TDE label-mates Schoolboy Q, Jay Rock and K-Dot. Stevens released his second mixtape, and the <a href="/wiki/Sequel" title="Sequel">sequel</a> to his first, on June 28, 2010. The tape, titled <i>Longterm 2: Lifestyles of the Broke and Almost Famous</i> was highly acclaimed and featured Stevens <a href="/wiki/Singing" title="Singing">singing</a> on several songs.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2011.E2.80.9312:_Longterm_Mentality_and_Control_System"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–12:_Longterm_Mentality_and_Control_System">2011–12: <i>Longterm Mentality</i> and <i>Control System</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ab-Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2011–12: Longterm Mentality and Control System">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Longterm_Mentality" title="Longterm Mentality">Longterm Mentality</a> and <a href="/wiki/Control_System" title="Control System">Control System</a></div>
<p>In February 2011, Ab-Soul revealed he was working on an album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Longterm_Mentality" title="Longterm Mentality">Longterm Mentality</a></i>, and subsequently released "Hell Yeah", a track featuring Schoolboy Q.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On February 22, 2011, Stevens released another promotional recording taken from the album, "Moscato", a collaboration with another fellow Black Hippy, Kendrick Lamar.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In February 2011, Stevens also embarked on the "Road to <a href="/wiki/Paid_Dues" title="Paid Dues">Paid Dues</a>" <a href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">concert tour</a>, after being enlisted by fellow American rapper <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 21, 2011, Stevens released the music video for a song titled "Nothin' New", in promotion for the album.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On March 25, he released "Gone Insane", the fourth track liberated that was taken from the album.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On March 30, a <a href="/wiki/Trailer_(promotion)" title="Trailer (promotion)">trailer</a> for <i>Longterm Mentality</i>, was released via Top Dawg Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On April 1, 2011, he released the music video for "Gone Insane", directed by Top Dawg in-house directors Fredo Tovar and Scott Fleishman.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Stevens has stated the respective <a href="/wiki/Independent_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent album">independent albums</a> of his label-mates partly inspired material on <i>Longterm Mentality</i>: "I compare [the process behind] <i>Longterm Mentality</i> to the process with all of my joints. Kendrick's <i><a href="/wiki/Overly_Dedicated" title="Overly Dedicated">OD</a></i> had come out and Q's <i><a href="/wiki/Setbacks_(album)" title="Setbacks (album)">Setbacks</a></i> had come out. I had these bodies of work to go off of as references, as inspiration. I hear what Kendrick is talking about, I hear what Q is talking about, I heard what Jay Rock was talking about. So how am I going to piece this all together and add my own two cents, too? How do I continue the sound but have my individuality as an artist? It's the same challenge every single time."<sup id="cite_ref-complex6_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex6-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Stevens released his first <a href="/wiki/Independent_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent album">independent album</a>, under Top Dawg Entertainment, exclusively through <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> on April 5, 2011. The album subsequently peaked at #73 on the US <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The album, although its title is <i>Longterm Mentality</i>, is not the third part of his <i>Longterm</i> series.<sup id="cite_ref-complex5_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex5-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:SchoolboyQxAbSoul.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/SchoolboyQxAbSoul.jpg/220px-SchoolboyQxAbSoul.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/SchoolboyQxAbSoul.jpg/330px-SchoolboyQxAbSoul.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/SchoolboyQxAbSoul.jpg/440px-SchoolboyQxAbSoul.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3456" data-file-height="2304" /></a><figcaption>Black Hippy members Ab-Soul (left) and <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a> in April 2012.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Soon after the release of Schoolboy Q's second independent album, <i><a href="/wiki/Habits_%26_Contradictions" title="Habits &amp; Contradictions">Habits &amp; Contradictions</a></i> (2012), Stevens began promoting his second independent album, releasing a song titled "Black Lip Bastard", on January 17, 2012. The song was produced by TDE in-house producer <a href="/wiki/Willie_B_(producer)" title="Willie B (producer)">Willie B</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On February 28, 2012, Ab-Soul, alongside Schoolboy Q, appeared on <a href="/wiki/Sway_Calloway" title="Sway Calloway">Sway Calloway</a>'s #SwayInTheMorning <a href="/wiki/Radio_show" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio show">radio show</a>, where Ab-Soul called "Black Lip Bastard": "pretty much one of the <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/title_track" class="extiw" title="wikt:title track">title tracks</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The first song that appears on the album, to be released was "Showin' Love", also produced by Willie B.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 24, Schoolboy Q announced #TheGroovyTour; a month-long tour with Ab-Soul accompanying him the entire way. The tour began on April 20, at the <a href="/wiki/Bill_Graham_Civic_Auditorium" title="Bill Graham Civic Auditorium">Bill Graham Civic Auditorium</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, California.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On April 6, 2012, Ab-Soul liberated a song titled "Terrorist Threats". The song features frequent collaborator <a href="/wiki/Jhen%C3%A9_Aiko" title="Jhené Aiko">Jhené Aiko</a> and fellow American rapper <a href="/wiki/Danny_Brown_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Danny Brown (rapper)">Danny Brown</a>, who proclaimed on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> that "Black Hippy the new <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">Beatles</a> and I'm <a href="/wiki/Harry_Nilsson" title="Harry Nilsson">Harry Nilsson</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On April 17, 2012, Ab-Soul revealed the album's title to be <i><a href="/wiki/Control_System" title="Control System">Control System</a></i> and unveiled the release date to be May 11, 2012. That same day, he also released a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for "Pineal Gland", a song inspired by the <a href="/wiki/Psychedelic_drug" title="Psychedelic drug">psychedelic drug</a> <a href="/wiki/Dimethyltryptamine" class="mw-redirect" title="Dimethyltryptamine">DMT</a> and of course the <a href="/wiki/Pineal_gland" title="Pineal gland">pineal gland</a>, a small <a href="/wiki/Endocrine_system" title="Endocrine system">endocrine</a> gland in the <a href="/wiki/Vertebrate" title="Vertebrate">vertebrate</a> <a href="/wiki/Brain" title="Brain">brain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On April 24, 2012, Stevens released another song, "SOPA". The song, produced by Nez &amp; Rio, references the <a href="/wiki/Stop_Online_Piracy_Act" title="Stop Online Piracy Act">Stop Online Piracy Act</a> (SOPA), a United States <a href="/wiki/Bill_(proposed_law)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bill (proposed law)">bill</a> introduced by <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">U.S. Representative</a> <a href="/wiki/Lamar_S._Smith" class="mw-redirect" title="Lamar S. Smith">Lamar S. Smith</a>, that would have allowed the <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">U.S. government</a> to control the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 1, 2012, Ab-Soul released another music video, for a new song titled "Empathy", the song features vocals from Stevens' late longtime <a href="/wiki/Girlfriend" title="Girlfriend">girlfriend</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alori_Joh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alori Joh (page does not exist)">Alori Joh</a> (December 16, 1986 - February 6, 2012), who had committed <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a> earlier in February.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Joh's death had a huge impact on Stevens and the recording process of <i>Control System</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-alorijoh_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alorijoh-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On May 4, 2012, it was revealed "Black Lip Bastard" was <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remixed</a> and would feature his Black Hippy cohorts, Rock, Lamar and Schoolboy Q.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On May 8, 2012, his collaboration with Lamar, titled "ILLuminate", was released. The day before the album's release Ab-Soul revealed the track listing and released <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/snippet" class="extiw" title="wikt:snippet">snippets</a> from the album. The album, as written on the back cover, is "Dedicated to the beautiful soul of Loriana Angel Johnson aka Alori Joh".<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Control System</i> was released exclusively through iTunes on May 11, 2012, under Top Dawg Entertainment. The album sold approximately 5,300 units in an abbreviated week, debuting at number 83 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and appearing on several other <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts"><i>Billboard</i> charts</a> as well.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The album sold an estimated 3,700 the next week.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> During 2012, Stevens toured with the rest of Black Hippy and fellow American rapper <a href="/wiki/Stalley" title="Stalley">Stalley</a>, on <a href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Entertainment Television">BET</a>'s Music Matters Tour.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late 2012, Stevens was featured on <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a>'s mixtape <i>A Loose Quarter</i> on the track "Cut From a Different Cloth". The song received praise as one of the best songs on the project. Budden has since announced that he and Ab-Soul have recorded many songs together which will be seeing the light of day in the near future. Budden went on to praise Ab-Soul as "one of the best things going in hip hop right now".<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.9318:_These_Days.E2.80.A6_and_Do_What_Thou_Wilt."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–18:_These_Days…_and_Do_What_Thou_Wilt.">2012–18: <i>These Days…</i> and <i>Do What Thou Wilt.</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ab-Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–18: These Days… and Do What Thou Wilt.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/These_Days..._(Ab-Soul_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="These Days... (Ab-Soul album)">These Days... (Ab-Soul album)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Do_What_Thou_Wilt." title="Do What Thou Wilt.">Do What Thou Wilt.</a></div>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ab-Soul_-_The_Smokers_Club_Tour_2013_(cropped).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Ab-Soul_-_The_Smokers_Club_Tour_2013_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Ab-Soul_-_The_Smokers_Club_Tour_2013_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="221" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Ab-Soul_-_The_Smokers_Club_Tour_2013_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Ab-Soul_-_The_Smokers_Club_Tour_2013_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Ab-Soul_-_The_Smokers_Club_Tour_2013_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-Ab-Soul_-_The_Smokers_Club_Tour_2013_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3435" data-file-height="3456" /></a><figcaption>Ab-Soul performing in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> in 2013.</figcaption></figure>
<p>In October 2012, American singer-songwriter and record producer <a href="/wiki/JMSN" title="JMSN">JMSN</a>, who previously produced "Nibiru" for Stevens earlier in August, for what his label called "TDE Fan Appreciation Week", announced he and Stevens would be releasing a collaborative project together.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> for their collaborative album, which was set to be titled <i>Unit 6</i>, was officially released via iTunes on January 22, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> The single, titled "You're Gone", features vocals from both artists, as well as production from JMSN.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> On March 26, 2013, it was announced that Ab-Soul would be featured on <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s 2013 Freshman Class issue, alongside Schoolboy Q and other up-and-coming rappers.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 6, 2013, after a near four-month hiatus, Stevens released a new song produced by Willie B, titled "Christopher DRONEr", alluding to former <a href="/wiki/LAPD" class="mw-redirect" title="LAPD">LAPD</a> police officer <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Dorner" class="mw-redirect" title="Christopher Dorner">Christopher Dorner</a>. Along with the song, he announced he was working on a new solo project.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The following day he would speak to <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> and say that he and JMSN had finished the <i>Unit 6</i> album, however their management could not "see eye to eye", so the project was ultimately shelved for a possible later release, with Stevens saying: "As of right now it's sounding like prequel to my album." He went on to confirm that JMSN and Jhene Aiko, will appear on the upcoming solo project.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On October 17, 2013, Stevens stated that he was close to finalizing the project.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Then later that week, he confirmed that the project had been turned in for <a href="/wiki/Mixing_(mathematics)" title="Mixing (mathematics)">mixing</a> and cleared up rumors that it was titled <i>Black Lip Pastor</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> With the release of the <a href="/wiki/56th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="56th Annual Grammy Awards">56th Annual Grammy Awards</a> nominations, it was revealed Stevens was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Album_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Album of the Year">Album of the Year</a> for his participation on <a href="/wiki/Macklemore" title="Macklemore">Macklemore</a> <a href="/wiki/Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis" title="Macklemore &amp; Ryan Lewis">&amp;</a> <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Lewis" title="Ryan Lewis">Ryan Lewis</a>' debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Heist_(album)" title="The Heist (album)">The Heist</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 2, 2014, Stevens revealed he had turned in two projects to his label Top Dawg, before the year 2013, was over: "I turned in 2 projects last year, yea I know what your thinking, let's see where this year takes me…" he <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">tweeted</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> On January 6, 2014, in an interview with Bootleg Kev, when speaking on the album Stevens stated: "Ali is <a href="/wiki/Audio_mixing_(recorded_music)" title="Audio mixing (recorded music)">mixing</a> it right now," and that they were waiting on the perfect way to present the project, as a result of creative releases such as <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Crenshaw_(mixtape)" title="Crenshaw (mixtape)">Crenshaw</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-bootlegkev_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bootlegkev-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> He also revealed the album is about progression and confirmed there would be appearances from his TDE label-mates, as well as production from hip hop producer <a href="/wiki/Statik_Selektah" title="Statik Selektah">Statik Selektah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bootlegkev_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bootlegkev-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> On April 11, 2014, Stevens announced he was also working on the third installment in the <i>Longterm</i> series.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ab-Soul_-_AfroPunk_Festival_2015.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Ab-Soul_-_AfroPunk_Festival_2015.jpg/180px-Ab-Soul_-_AfroPunk_Festival_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="180" height="231" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Ab-Soul_-_AfroPunk_Festival_2015.jpg/270px-Ab-Soul_-_AfroPunk_Festival_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Ab-Soul_-_AfroPunk_Festival_2015.jpg/360px-Ab-Soul_-_AfroPunk_Festival_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2176" data-file-height="2788" /></a><figcaption>Ab-Soul performing in Brooklyn in 2015.</figcaption></figure>
<p>On May 5, 2014, Stevens took to <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> to announce his third album would be titled <i><a href="/wiki/These_Days..._(Ab-Soul_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="These Days... (Ab-Soul album)">These Days…</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> On May 30, 2014, Top Dawg management took to Twitter to reveal <i>These Days...</i>, is scheduled for a June 24 release date. That same day, TDE management also unveiled the cover art for the album and released a snippet of a music video for Stevens' song "Stigmata".<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> On June 21, 2014, Stevens released a music video for a song titled "Closure", taken from <i>These Days...</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> The album debuted at number 11 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, with first-week sales of 21,812 copies in the United States and has received generally positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_critics" class="mw-redirect" title="Music critics">music critics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2014, in an interview with Montreality, Stevens revealed the title for his first in-store retail project would be <i>Longterm 3</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> In February 2015, Ab-Soul premiered the song "47 Bars" online, produced by <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> In July 2015, Stevens tweeted that his next album was "almost done."<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> In July 2015, Stevens gave an update on the album: "My album's almost done..." he wrote on Twitter, adding, "It's a love story". He completed the post with the <a href="/wiki/Hashtag" title="Hashtag">hashtag</a> "DWTW", presumably the album title acronym to which he kept alluding.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 8, 2016, via Twitter, Stevens revealed he had completed his upcoming album and turned it in for <a href="/wiki/Audio_mixing_(recorded_music)" title="Audio mixing (recorded music)">mixing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> The album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Do_What_Thou_Wilt." title="Do What Thou Wilt.">Do What Thou Wilt.</a></i>, was released December 9, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, Stevens appeared on the song <i>Bloody Waters</i> with <a href="/wiki/Anderson_.Paak" title="Anderson .Paak">Anderson .Paak</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/James_Blake_(musician)" title="James Blake (musician)">James Blake</a> from <a href="/wiki/Black_Panther:_The_Album" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Panther: The Album">Black Panther: The Album</a>
</p>
<h3><span id="2019.E2.80.93present:_Herbert"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–present:_Herbert">2019–present: <i>Herbert</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ab-Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2019–present: Herbert">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Herbert_(album)" title="Herbert (album)">Herbert (album)</a></div>
<p>On November 3, 2019, Stevens performed his set at the Day N Vegas <a href="/wiki/Music_festival" title="Music festival">music festival</a> and revealed that he "been cookin' the bird up slow", referring to his album. He then ended his set by <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyling</a> and promising a new album in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 20, 2020, to celebrate <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a> founder and CEO Anthony Tiffith's birthday, as well as start off TDE's Fan Appreciation Week, Stevens released the single "Dangerookipawaa Freestyle".<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 22, 2022, Stevens released the single "Hollandaise", which was produced by TDE in-house producer Kal Banx.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 18, 2022, alongside the release of the single "Gang'Nem", Stevens announced his fifth studio album <i>Herbert</i>, would be released on December 16, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Herbert</i> was released on December 16, 2022, featuring guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Bada$$</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jhen%C3%A9_Aiko" title="Jhené Aiko">Jhené Aiko</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russ_(rapper)" title="Russ (rapper)">Russ</a>, Fre$h, <a href="/wiki/Ambr%C3%A9" title="Ambré">Ambré</a>, Alemeda, <a href="/wiki/Sir_(singer)" title="Sir (singer)">SiR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punch_(rapper)" title="Punch (rapper)">Punch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zacari" title="Zacari">Zacari</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Skiiiwalker" title="Lance Skiiiwalker">Lance Skiiiwalker</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> The album also included additional vocals or "hidden features" from the other Black Hippy members, Jay Rock, ScHoolboy Q, and Kendrick Lamar. 
</p><p>In February 2023, Stevens performed an <a href="/wiki/List_of_Tiny_Desk_Concerts" title="List of Tiny Desk Concerts">NPR Tiny Desk Concert</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2023, <i>These Days</i> was removed from <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music" title="Apple Music">Apple Music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> for currently unknown reasons. However, the album returned to most streaming platforms in July 2023.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ab-Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ab-Soul cites fellow American rappers <a href="/wiki/Twista" title="Twista">Twista</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canibus" title="Canibus">Canibus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lupe_Fiasco" title="Lupe Fiasco">Lupe Fiasco</a> as major influences, with <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> being his biggest influence.<sup id="cite_ref-influences_72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-influences-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> He stated: "There are so many others, but those are the ones that came to my mind at this time. Those artists are basically the template to my format as an <a href="/wiki/MC" class="mw-redirect" title="MC">MC</a>. They all capture me with their music, whether it be the vibe, delivery, <a href="/wiki/Cadence_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cadence (music)">cadence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Storytelling" title="Storytelling">story</a>, or just the <a href="/wiki/Lyrics" title="Lyrics">lyrics</a> in general."<sup id="cite_ref-influences_72-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-influences-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ab-Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stevens has cited the book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Autobiography_of_Malcolm_X" title="The Autobiography of Malcolm X">The Autobiography of Malcolm X</a></i> (1964) as being highly influential to him.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Stevens dated fellow label mate Alori Joh (born Loriana Johnson) from high school until her untimely and tragic death, where she took her own life by jumping off of a radio tower in Compton, CA. Stevens references the relationship and the fatal loss on his song "The Book of Soul" from his 2012 album, <a href="/wiki/Control_System" title="Control System">Control System</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup> Since then, Stevens has reportedly dated model Yaris Sanchez. In 2022, he attempted to take his own life by jumping from a highway overpass. A car broke his fall and saved his life. He revealed that his suicidal thoughts were drug-induced and that he would normally not have such feelings.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
