<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1066479718"></td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Sudan Ameer Williams</b> (born November 16, 1989), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Iamsu!</b> (stylized as <b>IamSu!</b> or <b>IAMSU!</b>), is an American rapper and record producer. He is also a part of the production team The Invasion and lead member of the hip hop group <a href="/wiki/The_HBK_Gang" title="The HBK Gang">the HBK Gang</a>. Iamsu! has released numerous mixtapes in his career including <i>Suzy 6 Speed</i>, <i>Million Dollar Afro</i> (with <a href="/wiki/Problem_(rapper)" title="Problem (rapper)">Problem</a>), and the <i>Kilt</i> series. He is also known for featuring on <a href="/wiki/LoveRance" title="LoveRance">LoveRance</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Up!_(LoveRance_song)" title="Up! (LoveRance song)">Up!</a>", <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Function_(song)" title="Function (song)">Function</a>", and <a href="/wiki/Sage_the_Gemini" title="Sage the Gemini">Sage the Gemini</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Gas_Pedal" title="Gas Pedal">Gas Pedal</a>". His debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Sincerely_Yours_(Iamsu!_album)" title="Sincerely Yours (Iamsu! album)">Sincerely Yours</a></i> was released in 2014.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iamsu!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sudan Ameer Williams was born on November 16, 1989,<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated7_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated7-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_California" title="Richmond, California">Richmond, California</a>. At a young age, he would be listening to artists such as <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/R._Kelly" title="R. Kelly">R. Kelly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aaliyah" title="Aaliyah">Aaliyah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marching_Band_(band)" title="Marching Band (band)">Marching Band</a>, The Grammar Band, and <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> music. At the age of 14, he began making music. Around that time, he was "always playing with instruments and messing around with music." Once he attended high school, he started learning how to make beats at a local Bay Area Youth Program "Youth Radio".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iamsu!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932012:_Career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2012:_Career_beginnings">2009–2012: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iamsu!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009–2012: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Iamsu! started rapping under his stage name "Su", but then he decided to change it to Iamsu! due to search engine optimization.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated5_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated5-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On July 4, 2010, he released his first mixtape, titled <i>Su! The Right Thing</i>. In 2011, Iamsu! produced, co-wrote and featured on <a href="/wiki/LoveRance" title="LoveRance">LoveRance</a>'s hit single "<a href="/wiki/Up!_(LoveRance_song)" title="Up! (LoveRance song)">Up!</a>", which also included fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> on a remix. The song peaked at number&#160;46 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and reached at number 2 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated7_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated7-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated6_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated6-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He followed with his appearance on <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>'s hit song "<a href="/wiki/Function_(song)" title="Function (song)">Function</a>", along with rappers <a href="/wiki/Problem_(rapper)" title="Problem (rapper)">Problem</a> and <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>. The song peaked at number&#160;62 on the US <i>Billboard</i> Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, he also featured on <a href="/wiki/Jonn_Hart" title="Jonn Hart">Jonn Hart</a>'s track "<a href="/wiki/Who_Booty" title="Who Booty">Who Booty</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>'s track "Bout Me".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated6_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated6-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932013:_Suzy_6_Speed.2C_Million_Dollar_Afro.2C_and_Kilt_II"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2013:_Suzy_6_Speed,_Million_Dollar_Afro,_and_Kilt_II">2012–2013: <i>Suzy 6 Speed</i>, <i>Million Dollar Afro</i>, and <i>Kilt II</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iamsu!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012–2013: Suzy 6 Speed, Million Dollar Afro, and Kilt II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 2, 2012, Iamsu! released his previous mixtapes: <i>Kilt</i>, <i>Young California</i>, and <i>Su! The Right Thing</i>, on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> for sale. On November 12, 2012, Iamsu! released another mixtape, titled <i>Suzy 6 Speed</i>. This mixtape features <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from <a href="/wiki/Juvenile_(rapper)" title="Juvenile (rapper)">Juvenile</a>, Problem, and Wiz Khalifa. It contained the single "Mobbin'" and received critical acclaim upon the release.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On February 13, 2013, both Iamsu! and Problem, released a collaboration mixtape, titled <i>Million Dollar Afro</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape would be praised by Tom Breihan of <a href="/wiki/Stereogum" title="Stereogum">Stereogum</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i>, and was named mixtape one of the best rap releases of 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> This mixtape features guest appearances from Juvenile, <a href="/wiki/King_Louie_(rapper)" title="King Louie (rapper)">King L</a>, Bad Lucc, <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too Short</a>, <a href="/wiki/Omarion" title="Omarion">Omarion</a>, Short Dawg, and Wiz Khalifa, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> To promote the mixtape, he been touring with Problem on the 10-stop "Million Dollar Afro Tour".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On March 5, 2013, <i>Spin</i> named Iamsu! as the tenth "Hottest MC in the Game".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2013, Iamsu! began his first headlining tour, in promotion of his then upcoming mixtape, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Kilt_II" title="Kilt II">Kilt II</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On June 6, 2013, Iamsu! released <i>Kilt II</i> as a retail mixtape, for free and to iTunes on July 9, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">production</a> was primarily handled by Iamsu!, along with other members of The Invasion.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork Media</a></i> gave the mixtape a 7.5 out of 10 rating saying, the mixtape "doubles down on all of his strongest and most likeable qualities: Largely self-produced, high-energy, and creative, it shows Iamsu! continuing to transform from a promising, agreeable Bay Area rap figure into a burgeoning star."<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated5_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated5-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <i>Spin</i> would also praise the mixtape saying, "As much a showcase of Iamsu!'s production prowess as it is evidence of his unique lyrical skills, the new tape is an enjoyably bumpy ride through treble-y bleeps, bassy bloops, tinny drums, and hard-hustling vocals."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Following its release, he told <i><a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a></i> that he was working with Wiz Khalifa and <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>. He would eventually appear on "Living" from 2 Chainz's album <i><a href="/wiki/B.O.A.T.S._II:_Me_Time" title="B.O.A.T.S. II: Me Time">B.O.A.T.S. II: Me Time</a></i> (2013).<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932016:_Sincerely_Yours_and_Kilt_3"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2016:_Sincerely_Yours_and_Kilt_3">2013–2016: <i>Sincerely Yours</i> and <i>Kilt 3</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iamsu!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2013–2016: Sincerely Yours and Kilt 3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Iamsu!.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Iamsu%21.jpg/190px-Iamsu%21.jpg" decoding="async" width="190" height="285" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Iamsu%21.jpg/285px-Iamsu%21.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Iamsu%21.jpg/380px-Iamsu%21.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3456" data-file-height="5184" /></a><figcaption>Iamsu! performing in 2014</figcaption></figure>
<p>Iamsu! was also featured on <a href="/wiki/Sage_the_Gemini" title="Sage the Gemini">Sage the Gemini</a>'s 2013's hit single "<a href="/wiki/Gas_Pedal" title="Gas Pedal">Gas Pedal</a>", which peaked at number 29 on the US <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, making it both artist's first top 40 entries and their most successful single to date. On August 12, 2013, Iamsu! and his group <a href="/wiki/The_HBK_Gang" title="The HBK Gang">the HBK Gang</a>, released a compilation mixtape, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Gang_Forever" title="Gang Forever">Gang Forever</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On December 3, 2013, Iamsu! released "Hipster Girls" as the second single from <i>Kilt II</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-itunes.apple.com_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itunes.apple.com-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On January 3, 2014, Iamsu! released a remix to <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Who_Do_You_Love%3F_(YG_song)" title="Who Do You Love? (YG song)">Who Do You Love?</a>", where he was hinted at being signed and planning to release his debut album. On January 7, 2014, he announced that his debut album would be titled <i><a href="/wiki/Sincerely_Yours_(Iamsu!_album)" title="Sincerely Yours (Iamsu! album)">Sincerely Yours</a></i> and be released in April 2014. He also confirmed that the first single would feature Sage the Gemini.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On January 28, 2014, he released the album's first single, titled "<a href="/wiki/Only_That_Real" title="Only That Real">Only That Real</a>" (featuring 2 Chainz and Sage the Gemini).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The album was then set for a release date of May 13, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, he began touring worldwide to promote his second studio album, <i>Kilt 3</i>. The first single "Up All Night" followed on February 14, 2016. The album was released on March 24, 2016.
</p>
