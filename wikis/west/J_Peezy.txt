<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; font-size: 125%;"><div class="">J Peezy</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Fresno,_California" title="Fresno, California">Fresno</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Origin</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose, California</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip Hop music">Hip Hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_Rap" class="mw-redirect" title="Pop Rap">Pop Rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">EDM</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role"><a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">Rapper</a>, song writer, <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneur" class="mw-redirect" title="Entrepreneur">entrepreneur</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">1998–present</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data">APB Entertainment</td></tr><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-header" style="background-color: #b0c4de"></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Website</th><td class="infobox-data"><span class="url"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://iamjpeezy.com">iamjpeezy<wbr />.com</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>J Peezy</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Song_writer" class="mw-redirect" title="Song writer">song writer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneur" class="mw-redirect" title="Entrepreneur">entrepreneur</a>.  J Peezy started in the rap group called <b>The Odyssey</b>, alongside American actor <a href="/wiki/Mahershalalhashbaz_Ali" class="mw-redirect" title="Mahershalalhashbaz Ali">Mahershalalhashbaz Ali</a>.   As an independent solo artist, J Peezy has worked with <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Jam_and_Terry_Lewis" title="Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis">Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis</a>, and performed or recorded with <a href="/wiki/Ne-Yo" title="Ne-Yo">Ne-Yo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>, <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too Short</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Baby_Bash" title="Baby Bash">Baby Bash</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> On June 10, 2011, J Peezy's single "It's My Time" was the "Most Added Urban Track" along with <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>'s single "Man Down" according to the DJ Times National Club Charts.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J_Peezy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>J Peezy grew up in <a href="/wiki/Fresno,_California" title="Fresno, California">Fresno, California</a>. While many of his childhood friends became involved in gangs and criminal activities, J Peezy focused his energy and time in football and freestyle rapping.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As a highly talented Football Player in high school J Peezy landed a full ride football scholarship at <a href="/wiki/San_Jose_State_University" title="San Jose State University">San Jose State University</a>.
</p><p>At <a href="/wiki/San_Jose_State_University" title="San Jose State University">San Jose State University</a> J Peezy started to build a reputation off the football field for his unique uncanny poetic freestyle rapping style. After a career ending football injury, J Peezy decided to pursue his music career full-time.
<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J_Peezy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:J_Peezy_on_stage.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/J_Peezy_on_stage.jpg/175px-J_Peezy_on_stage.jpg" decoding="async" width="175" height="117" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/J_Peezy_on_stage.jpg/263px-J_Peezy_on_stage.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/J_Peezy_on_stage.jpg/350px-J_Peezy_on_stage.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="400" /></a><figcaption>J Peezy Performing at Hot 104.7 Summer Fest 2009</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2002, J Peezy started working with <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Jam_and_Terry_Lewis" title="Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis">Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis</a> at <a href="/wiki/Flyte_Tyme_Records" title="Flyte Tyme Records">Flyte Tyme Records</a> with members of their production team. During J Peezy's tenure under Flyte Tyme's production team he collaborated with many well known artists from various genre's and backgrounds including Kenny Ski of <a href="/wiki/Rocafella_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Rocafella Records">Rocafella Records</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Christion" class="mw-redirect" title="Christion">Christion</a>, R&amp;B Great <a href="/wiki/Ne-Yo" title="Ne-Yo">Ne-Yo</a>, and the former lead singer from <a href="/wiki/Santana_(band)" title="Santana (band)">Santana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Lindsay" title="Tony Lindsay">Tony Lindsay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late 2006, J Peezy was introduced to radio programming icon &amp; Radio promoter John Christian. John Christians radio promotion company Pic Hitz began to gather radio spins for the lead single off the "Love Me" album called "This Old School".  This Old School featured then girl group DTL which young R&amp;B POP sensation Niah is currently a member of.  Niah is also featured on J Peezy's hit single "It's My Time" from his forthcoming album "It's My Time" set to be released late 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2011, J Peezy was featured in a mobile battle rap game that he helped create, <a href="/wiki/Battle_Rap_Stars" title="Battle Rap Stars">Battle Rap Stars</a> by <a href="/wiki/Jump_Shot_Media" title="Jump Shot Media">Jump Shot Media</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_ventures">Other ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J_Peezy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="eFuct_Web_Portal">eFuct Web Portal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J_Peezy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: eFuct Web Portal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1999, J Peezy joined forces with several Silicon Valley entrepreneurs to form a hip-hop related web portal company called eFuct. Initially J Peezy was brought on to help market the online portal by leveraging his growing fan base (through acting as a <a href="/wiki/Hype_man" title="Hype man">hype man</a>). However, as traffic to the portal began to grow, J Peezy started to take on a network administration role and became responsible for building and maintaining the company's datacenter. In late 2000, eFuct was forced to close its doors due to lack of funding, like many companies during the <a href="/wiki/Dot-com_bubble" title="Dot-com bubble">Dot-com bubble</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jump_Shot_Media">Jump Shot Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J_Peezy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Jump Shot Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, J Peezy co-founded the social and mobile game company <a href="/wiki/Jump_Shot_Media" title="Jump Shot Media">Jump Shot Media</a> where he currently serves as the company's Chief Operating Officer.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  In October 2011, <a href="/wiki/Jump_Shot_Media" title="Jump Shot Media">Jump Shot Media</a> Released the world's first <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">battle rap</a> game capable of scoring a gamers rap performance.  J Peezy was featured in this game along with rappers <a href="/wiki/Paul_Wall" title="Paul Wall">Paul Wall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mistah_F.A.B." title="Mistah F.A.B.">Mistah F.A.B.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hopsin" title="Hopsin">Hopsin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fresh_Caesar" title="Fresh Caesar">Fresh Caesar</a>.
</p>
