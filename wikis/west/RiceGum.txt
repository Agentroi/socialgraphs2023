<p><b>Bryan Quang Le</b><sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> (born November 19, 1996), better known as <b>RiceGum</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/YouTuber" title="YouTuber">YouTuber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Online_streamer" title="Online streamer">online streamer</a>. He is best known for his <a href="/wiki/Diss_tracks" class="mw-redirect" title="Diss tracks">diss tracks</a> and online feuds with other YouTube personalities.<sup id="cite_ref-BillboardJPvsRiceGum_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillboardJPvsRiceGum-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Since creating his YouTube channel, his channel has received over ten million subscribers and over two billion video views.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2017, Le was ranked at number 25 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> Emerging Artists chart.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> His song "<a href="/wiki/It%27s_Every_Night_Sis" title="It&#39;s Every Night Sis">It's Every Night Sis</a>" was <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America_certification#Singles" class="mw-redirect" title="Recording Industry Association of America certification">certified platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) in March 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Le was born to <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_people" title="Vietnamese people">Vietnamese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a> parents.<sup id="cite_ref-latimes_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Vista_High_School_(Nevada)" title="Sierra Vista High School (Nevada)">Sierra Vista High School</a>, where he was on the basketball team.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Le attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Nevada,_Las_Vegas" title="University of Nevada, Las Vegas">University of Nevada, Las Vegas</a> (UNLV) before dropping out during his first year to pursue his online career full-time.<sup id="cite_ref-latimes_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span></h3>
<p><span class="anchor" id="It&#39;s_Every_Night_Sis"></span>
In 2017, Le released a single titled "<a href="/wiki/It%27s_Every_Night_Sis" title="It&#39;s Every Night Sis">It's Every Night Sis</a>" featuring fellow YouTube personality Alissa Violet, which was a <a href="/wiki/Answer_song" title="Answer song">response track</a> to YouTuber <a href="/wiki/Jake_Paul" title="Jake Paul">Jake Paul</a>'s viral song "<a href="/wiki/It%27s_Everyday_Bro" title="It&#39;s Everyday Bro">It's Everyday Bro</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-BillboardJPvsRiceGum_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillboardJPvsRiceGum-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> It became Le's first charting single, having debuted at number 80 and number 55 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> charts respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-BB100_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB100-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CAN-100_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CAN-100-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The song later became certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) in March 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The single was followed by "God Church" in July 2017, its title was another reference to "It's Everyday Bro", which reached number one on the Comedy Digital Track Sales chart.<sup id="cite_ref-GC_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GC-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>RiceGum was featured in the video for "Earthquake" by YouTube personality and rapper <a href="/wiki/KSI" title="KSI">KSI</a>, released on August 12, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-D-EP_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-D-EP-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2017, RiceGum released "Frick da Police", which was a diss track towards YouTube personality <a href="/wiki/IDubbbz" title="IDubbbz">IDubbbz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FDP_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FDP-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The song charted at number 45 and number 67 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> charts respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-CAN-100_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CAN-100-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;B/HH_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;B/HH-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span></h3>
<p>RiceGum was featured in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Super_Bowl_commercials#2018_(LII)" title="List of Super Bowl commercials">Super Bowl LII commercial</a> for <a href="/wiki/Monster_Cable" title="Monster Cable">Monster</a> headphones. In the ad, he plays a man on a subway who is inspired by <a href="/wiki/Iggy_Azalea" title="Iggy Azalea">Iggy Azalea</a> to fashion a headset from scratch, which is accepted by an executive.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feuds_with_other_YouTube_personalities">Feuds with other YouTube personalities</span></h3>
<p>Ian W., a YouTuber by the channel name of <a href="/wiki/IDubbbz" title="IDubbbz">iDubbbz</a>TV, made an episode of his popular "Content Cop" series on RiceGum (a series where he critiques other YouTubers' content), which received over 50 million views and over 2 million likes. The end of the video featured the diss track "<a href="/wiki/Asian_Jake_Paul" title="Asian Jake Paul">Asian Jake Paul</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-TDD_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TDD-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> In response to the video, RiceGum made the diss track "Frick da Police". The diss track was met with a negative reception, currently holding 1.3 million dislikes since its release.<sup id="cite_ref-TDD_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TDD-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> RiceGum has also been involved in controversies with other popular YouTubers and musicians such as <a href="/wiki/James_Rallison" class="mw-redirect" title="James Rallison">James Rallison</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gabbie_Hanna" title="Gabbie Hanna">Gabbie Hanna</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Bhad_Bhabie" title="Bhad Bhabie">Bhad Bhabie</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hong_Kong_vlog_controversy">Hong Kong vlog controversy</span></h3>
<p>On June 12, 2018, RiceGum uploaded a video of himself in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> through his main YouTube channel. In the video, he asks strangers and a staff member in a local <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald&#39;s">McDonald's</a> if they have dog on the menu as he says "Asians eat cats and dogs in China." He also jokes about wanting to have dog and cat meat to eat as he is "always open to try new things".<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> He comments on <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_street_food" title="Hong Kong street food">Hong Kong street food</a> <a href="/wiki/Beef_entrails" class="mw-redirect" title="Beef entrails">beef entrails</a>, questioning if it is dog meat and saying "shit looks disgusting".<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> He filmed comedian M2THAK walking up to a man in the Hong Kong International Airport, yelling "Do you understand the words that are coming out of my mouth?", allegedly imitating a scene from the Hollywood movie <a href="/wiki/Rush_Hour_(1998_film)" title="Rush Hour (1998 film)"><i>Rush Hour</i></a> starring Jackie Chan and Chris Tucker to imply that Asian communities do not understand English.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> M2THAK was also filmed repositioning mannequins in a store window into sexually suggestive poses.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The video included a scene where they gave a half-eaten ice cream to a local Hong Kong man.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Online commentary has compared the video to <a href="/wiki/Logan_Paul" title="Logan Paul">Logan Paul</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Logan_Paul#Suicide_video_controversy" title="Logan Paul">Japan vlog controversy</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> saying that he is being disrespectful and culturally insensitive in a foreign country.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Wong" title="Jimmy Wong">Jimmy Wong</a> tweeted saying that he is disappointed as RiceGum's videos are now "disrespectful, ignorant, borderline racist, &amp; shameful to all creators, especially Asians" and asked him to "please grow up &amp; stop".<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two weeks later, on June 26, when the video received different criticism in China and other Asian countries, RiceGum released a video, saying that he was just "joking around".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> He defended himself saying that he was just trying to use Asian stereotypes to point to American comedy culture and that people are being too sensitive. He further explained that he believed it is acceptable as he is Asian.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> He also said that he wanted to return to Hong Kong, but says that he is "kind of scared now because the people may hit me and beat me up". However, the video was considered insincere; media site <i><a href="/wiki/Polygon_(website)" title="Polygon (website)">Polygon</a></i> described that his apology was done "in a somewhat glib manner" and <i><a href="/wiki/What%27s_Trending" title="What&#39;s Trending">What's Trending</a></i> said the "apology" "sounded [incredibly] forced".<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The video of his Hong Kong tour is currently removed from YouTube due to the violation of YouTube's Terms of Service.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mystery_box_website_promotion">Mystery box website promotion</span></h3>
<p>In January 2019, RiceGum, along with Jake Paul, came under fire for promoting <i>MysteryBrand</i>, a website which offers the chance to open a digital "mystery box" of pre-selected items with a promise to win one in real life at random. Many users have said they have not received prizes they won through the site, and concluded the site is a scam.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> In response, RiceGum made a video in which he pointed out that other YouTubers made their own videos promoting <i>MysteryBrand</i> months prior, saying "No-one said anything, it wasn't a problem back then. Why did no-one bring it up, or even talk about these guys? This mystery box thing has been on the internet for 3 or 4 months even from other creators, but as soon as I do it, it's a problem?" At the end of the video, he decided to give away <a href="/wiki/Amazon_(company)" title="Amazon (company)">Amazon</a> gift card codes, saying "There's nothing I can really do but say sorry and give you these Amazon gift cards". However, many people called him out for the Amazon codes already being expired.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Save_the_Kids_Token">Save the Kids Token</span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Save_the_Kids_Token" class="mw-redirect" title="Save the Kids Token">Save the Kids Token</a></div>
<p>In June 2021, RiceGum along with members of <a href="/wiki/FaZe_Clan" title="FaZe Clan">FaZe Clan</a> were influencer ambassadors for a <a href="/wiki/Cryptocurrency" title="Cryptocurrency">crypto</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-fungible_token" title="Non-fungible token">non-fungible token</a> called Save the Kids (KIDS) which was marketed as a charity token meant to give a percentage of the transaction fee to the <a href="/wiki/Binance" title="Binance">Binance</a> Charity Wallet.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (July 2023)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The token was then <a href="/wiki/Pump_and_dump" title="Pump and dump">pumped and dumped</a> by many of the ambassadors and the token's value quickly crashed shortly after when it was released.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
