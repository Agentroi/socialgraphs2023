<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antwon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Key_West,_Florida" class="mw-redirect" title="Key West, Florida">Key West, Florida</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> to an <a href="/wiki/African-Americans" class="mw-redirect" title="African-Americans">African-American</a> father and a <a href="/wiki/Filipino_Americans" title="Filipino Americans">Filipino</a> mother,<sup id="cite_ref-sfweekly-zoo_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfweekly-zoo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Antwon grew up in <a href="/wiki/Sunnyvale,_California" title="Sunnyvale, California">Sunnyvale, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He was a member of the Philadelphia-based hardcore band Leather,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> as well as the pop punk bands Sour Patch and How to: Summer.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Antwon's first mixtape, <i>Fantasy Beds</i>, was released in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In that year, he also released <i>My Westside Horizon</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Antwon released a mixtape, <i>End of Earth</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/SF_Weekly" title="SF Weekly">SF Weekly</a></i> included it on the "10 Best Bay Area Hip-Hop Records of 2012" list.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, he released a mixtape, <i>In Dark Denim</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It was listed as <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s "Rap Release of the Week".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His first official studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Heavy_Hearted_in_Doldrums" title="Heavy Hearted in Doldrums">Heavy Hearted in Doldrums</a></i>, was released on UNIF and Nature World in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, he released an EP, <i>Double Ecstasy</i>, on <a href="/wiki/Anticon" title="Anticon">Anticon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, he released a mixtape, <i>Sunnyvale Gardens</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull" title="Red Bull">Red Bull</a> placed it at number 20 on the "20 Best Hip-Hop Mixtapes of 2017" list.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_and_influences">Style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antwon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i> described Antwon as "an affable guy who might have one of rap's biggest, best-sounding voices, which he usually focuses on goofy jokes and surprisingly sweet bedroom raps."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He has often been compared to <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He noted <a href="/wiki/Kid_Rock" title="Kid Rock">Kid Rock</a> as his greatest influence.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antwon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2016, Antwon formed Secret Circle along with <a href="/wiki/Wiki_(rapper)" title="Wiki (rapper)">Wiki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Ugly_Mane" title="Lil Ugly Mane">Lil Ugly Mane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Since then, the group released several tracks and played live performances across North America.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, Lil Ugly Mane announced the breakup of the group, not initially indicating a specific reason for the breakup.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Antwon posted a statement on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>, alluding to unnamed allegations.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork2018_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork2018-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Wiki and Lil Ugly Mane subsequently indicated that the allegations mentioned in Antwon's statement, including abusive treatment of women, were the impetus for the breakup.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork2018_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork2018-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
