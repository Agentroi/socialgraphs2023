<p><b>Chelsea Chanel Dudley</b><sup id="cite_ref-OfficalBiography_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfficalBiography-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (born September 1, 1988),<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> better known by her stage name <b>Chanel West Coast</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-OfficalBiography_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfficalBiography-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> is an American television personality, rapper, and singer.
</p><p>She came to prominence for her roles in <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Rob_Dyrdek%27s_Fantasy_Factory" title="Rob Dyrdek&#39;s Fantasy Factory">Rob Dyrdek's Fantasy Factory</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Ridiculousness_(TV_series)" title="Ridiculousness (TV series)">Ridiculousness</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chanel_West_Coast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chanel was born in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. While growing up, she lived part of the year in <a href="/wiki/North_Hollywood,_Los_Angeles" title="North Hollywood, Los Angeles">North Hollywood</a> with her mother and in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> with her father, <sup id="cite_ref-hollywoodmask-boyfriend-2018-gender_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hollywoodmask-boyfriend-2018-gender-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-intouchweekly-dj-dad_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intouchweekly-dj-dad-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> a DJ in New York City. She began listening to rap music at eleven years old and began rapping at 14;<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> she says "<a href="/wiki/How_Do_U_Want_It" title="How Do U Want It">How Do U Want It</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a> is the song which inspired her to start rapping.<sup id="cite_ref-Soundtracked_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Soundtracked-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Chanel attended <a href="/wiki/William_Howard_Taft_Charter_High_School" title="William Howard Taft Charter High School">Taft High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Woodland_Hills,_Los_Angeles" title="Woodland Hills, Los Angeles">Woodland Hills</a>, Los Angeles for two years before homeschooling until graduation.<sup id="cite_ref-laweekly_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laweekly-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_career">Television career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chanel_West_Coast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Television career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chanel was introduced to television personality <a href="/wiki/Rob_Dyrdek" title="Rob Dyrdek">Rob Dyrdek</a> through mutual friends in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Shortly afterwards, Dyrdek offered Chanel a position as his receptionist while he was appearing on the <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> reality series <i><a href="/wiki/Rob_Dyrdek%27s_Fantasy_Factory" title="Rob Dyrdek&#39;s Fantasy Factory">Rob Dyrdek's Fantasy Factory</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The series was renewed for a second season by MTV.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The third season of the series premiered in 2011<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and the fourth season began airing later that year.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In 2011 Chanel appeared as Sheila in an episode of the MTV series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hard_Times_of_RJ_Berger" title="The Hard Times of RJ Berger">The Hard Times of RJ Berger</a></i>, making her acting debut. 
</p><p>In 2011, she joined the series <i><a href="/wiki/Ridiculousness_(TV_series)" title="Ridiculousness (TV series)">Ridiculousness</a></i>, in which she, along with Dyrdek and <a href="/wiki/Steelo_Brim" title="Steelo Brim">Sterling "Steelo" Brim</a>, review online internet videos.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, Chanel appeared in both the fifth season of <i>Fantasy Factory</i> and the second season of <i>Ridiculousness</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> That same year she began to voice the character of Flipz in the animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_Grinders" title="Wild Grinders">Wild Grinders</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> West Coast continued to voice the character into 2013, when she also appeared on the third season of <i>Ridiculousness</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The sixth season of <i>Fantasy Factory</i> was announced in 2013, and the fourth season of <i>Ridiculousness</i> premiered on January 2, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, Chanel joined the supporting cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_Hollywood_(season_4)" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: Hollywood (season 4)">Love &amp; Hip Hop: Hollywood</a></i> for season four; she had ten appearances. However she did not return for season four's reunion special.   In March 2023, she announced that she has left <i>Ridiculousness</i> and is working on some new productions with Paramount.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chanel_West_Coast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chanel began recording music in 2009, and began collaborating with numerous artists. That year, she recorded the song "Melting Like Ice Cream" featuring <a href="/wiki/Girlicious" title="Girlicious">Tiffanie Anderson</a>. She later appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Planet_Hollywood" title="Planet Hollywood">Planet Hollywood</a> song "PHAMOUS", collaborating with <a href="/wiki/Midi_Mafia" title="Midi Mafia">Midi Mafia</a> on the project. In 2011, Chanel confirmed she had signed with <a href="/wiki/Polow_Da_Don" class="mw-redirect" title="Polow Da Don">Polow Da Don</a> on his record label <a href="/wiki/Zone_4_(record_label)" title="Zone 4 (record label)">Zone 4</a>, though she later left the label without releasing any material. During this time, Chanel began releasing her own material for free download online, later recording a music video for the song "I Love Money".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> signed Chanel to his label <a href="/wiki/Young_Money_Entertainment" title="Young Money Entertainment">Young Money Entertainment</a> in 2012. She went on to release her mixtape <i>Now You Know</i> in 2013 featuring <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_$ign" class="mw-redirect" title="Ty Dolla $ign">Ty Dolla $ign</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robin_Thicke" title="Robin Thicke">Robin Thicke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honey_Cocaine" title="Honey Cocaine">Honey Cocaine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Riley Sky of <i>Dropkick Divas Media</i> gave the mixtape a grade of B−, saying "Now You Know accomplishes [the] mission" of "[selling] Chanel as an independent woman, its hip-hop-inspired vibe makes "Now You Know" fiercely individual".<sup id="cite_ref-Dropkick_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dropkick-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Chanel promoted her mixtape by going on two headlining tours; the Punch Drunk Love Tour and the Now You Know Tour. She also released a music video for "Been On", featuring French Montana.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> She released her second mixtape <i>WAVES</i>, featuring <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a> and <a href="/wiki/B-Real" title="B-Real">B-Real</a>, in August 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-waves_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-waves-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.93present:_Debut_studio_album"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–present:_Debut_studio_album">2014–present: Debut studio album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chanel_West_Coast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2014–present: Debut studio album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2014, West Coast confirmed that she had begun recording her debut studio album set to be released in 2016, which was originally projected to drop in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Bankruptcy_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bankruptcy-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The first single, called "Blueberry Chills" featuring Honey Cocaine was released on January 15, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The second single off the album, "New Feeling" was released on January 29.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The third single off the album, "Miles and Miles" was released on October 29, which features West Coast providing vocals for the first time. In 2015, West Coast released a new song that was featured on Rob Dyrdek's Fantasy Factory called "Bass In The Trunk".<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, West Coast released a song titled "Nobody."<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 23, 2019, Chanel released her Single "I Be Like" Featuring Dax, after performing to the public 2 years before the releasing date.
Then The Song Is remixed by the Armenian Music Producer "Harout Zadikian" on November 23, 2020, in Bass House Genre.
</p><p>On October 23, 2020, Chanel West Coast released her debut studio album <i>America's Sweetheart</i> after delaying it for 4 years as a "<a href="/wiki/Surprise_album" title="Surprise album">surprise album</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_ventures">Other ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chanel_West_Coast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2009, Chanel helped create a clothing line called Valleywood.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> As of 2019, she also released another clothing line named LOL CARTEL.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> In 2022, Chanel launched a line of eco-friendly body positivity swimwear called Coasty Swim.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, Chanel curated the Chanel West Coast collection of smoking supplies for the cannabis subscription box company Daily High Club.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chanel_West_Coast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p>
