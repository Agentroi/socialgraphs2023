<p><b>Edward Lee Knight</b> (November 2, 1971 &#8211; July 8, 2001), best known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Big Ed the Assassin</b>, often shortened to <b>Big Ed</b>, was an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>.
</p><p>Knight was signed to <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a>'s <a href="/wiki/No_Limit_Records" title="No Limit Records">No Limit Records</a> in 1992, and appeared on numerous No Limit releases during the 1990s, contributing to releases by <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a>, <a href="/wiki/C-Murder" title="C-Murder">C-Murder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_(rapper)" title="Mac (rapper)">Mac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Slim" title="Soulja Slim">Soulja Slim</a> and more. Knight later released his debut album, <a href="/wiki/The_Assassin_(album)" title="The Assassin (album)">The Assassin</a> through No Limit Records in 1998.
</p><p>Knight would go on to leave No Limit in 2000, starting his own label, Special Forces Records. Knight released his second album, <a href="/wiki/Special_Forces_(Big_Ed_album)" title="Special Forces (Big Ed album)">Special Forces</a>, through the label on May 9, 2000. Knights third and final album, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Lee_Knight_1971-2001" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward Lee Knight 1971-2001">Edward Lee Knight 1971-2001</a>, was released posthumously in August 2002.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Ed_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Knight was born in <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_California" title="Richmond, California">Richmond, California</a>, and attended <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Washington_University" title="Eastern Washington University">Eastern Washington University</a> from 1992 to 1993 on a basketball scholarship, but dropped out to pursue a career in rap music in the Bay Area with <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a>'s West Coast Bad Boy Clique.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Ed_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Big Ed was an original member of the rap group <a href="/wiki/TRU_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="TRU (group)">TRU</a> (The Real Untouchables), appearing on the group's first three albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Who%27s_Da_Killer%3F" class="mw-redirect" title="Who&#39;s Da Killer?">Who's Da Killer?</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Understanding_the_Criminal_Mind" title="Understanding the Criminal Mind">Understanding the Criminal Mind</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/True_(TRU_album)" title="True (TRU album)">True</a></i>. Throughout the 90s, Ed had guest spots on many of No Limit's other releases such as Master P's <i><a href="/wiki/99_Ways_to_Die_(album)" title="99 Ways to Die (album)">99 Ways to Die</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Silkk_the_Shocker" title="Silkk the Shocker">Silkk the Shocker</a>'s debut <i><a href="/wiki/The_Shocker_(album)" title="The Shocker (album)">The Shocker</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Mia_X" title="Mia X">Mia X</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Unlady_Like" title="Unlady Like">Unlady Like</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Ed_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Assassin_and_Special_Forces_Records">The Assassin and Special Forces Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Ed_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Assassin and Special Forces Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Assassin_(album)" title="The Assassin (album)">The Assassin</a></i>, was released on September 1, 1998, and peaked at #15 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and #3 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a>.
</p><p>In 2000, Big Ed left No Limit to start his own record label, Special Forces Records. He released his second album through this label, 2000's <i><a href="/wiki/Special_Forces_(Big_Ed_album)" title="Special Forces (Big Ed album)">Special Forces</a></i>, which featured contributions from his former No Limit labelmates.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Ed_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Big Ed the Assassin died from <a href="/wiki/Lymphoma_cancer" class="mw-redirect" title="Lymphoma cancer">lymphoma cancer</a> on July 8, 2001. His third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Edward_Lee_Knight_1971-2001" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward Lee Knight 1971-2001">Edward Lee Knight 1971-2001</a></i>, was released <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Special:Search/posthumous" class="extiw" title="wikt:Special:Search/posthumous">posthumously</a> the next month.
</p>
