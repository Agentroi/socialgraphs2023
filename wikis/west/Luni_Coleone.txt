<p><b>Monterrio Williams</b> (born August 10, 1978), better known by his stage names <b>Luni Coleone</b> or <b>Lunasicc</b>, is an American Garden Blocc Crip <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a> who began his musical career on the streets of <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento, California</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luni_Coleone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He began his career under the alias of Lunasicc in 1995, appearing on his cousin <a href="/wiki/X-Raided" title="X-Raided">X-Raided</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Xorcist_(album)" title="Xorcist (album)">Xorcist</a></i>. He released his solo debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Lunasicc" title="Mr. Lunasicc">Mr. Lunasicc</a></i>, in 1997 on AWOL Records, to strong reviews. <i><a href="/wiki/A_Million_Words,_a_Million_Dollars" title="A Million Words, a Million Dollars">A Million Words, a Million Dollars</a></i> followed in 1998, with stronger reviews. Later that year Lunasicc joined forces with Killa Tay and KJ to form the <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a>, Dosia. They went on to produce one album, <i>Waiting to Inhale</i>, released on November 3, 1998, on AWOL Records.
</p><p>In 2000, he changed his name to Luni Coleone.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The first album released under the new name is <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Recall_(Luni_Coleone_album)" title="Total Recall (Luni Coleone album)">Total Recall</a></i>, which was released on March 14, 2000. Some of Coleone's most famous ballads include "All I Wanna Do", "All I Ever Wanted To Do", and "Thug Sh*t". His lyrics describe the everyday life of American inner-city youth.
</p>

<p>Luni Coleone often collaborates with fellow West Coast artists such as <a href="/wiki/X-Raided" title="X-Raided">X-Raided</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Dre" title="Mac Dre">Mac Dre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brotha_Lynch_Hung" title="Brotha Lynch Hung">Brotha Lynch Hung</a>, Skandal, Skee 64 Oz., Hollow Tip, <a href="/wiki/C-Bo" title="C-Bo">C-Bo</a>, Killa Tay, and Marvaless,  and was produced by super producer Hugh Heff and Big Hollis. In 2006, he was featured on <a href="/wiki/Playalitical" title="Playalitical">Playalitical</a>'s album <i>Code Green</i> on the song "The New West" alongside the legendary <a href="/wiki/Spice_1" title="Spice 1">Spice 1</a>, Duce Stabs, Young Droop  Spoke-In-Wordz, who were also featured on MC Raw Thuggish.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
