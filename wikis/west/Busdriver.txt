<p><b>Regan Farquhar</b> (born February 12, 1978), better known by his stage name <b>Busdriver</b> (sometimes stylized in <b><a href="/wiki/All_caps" title="All caps">All caps</a></b>) is an American rapper and producer from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. He has collaborated with rappers such as <a href="/wiki/Myka_9" title="Myka 9">Myka 9</a>, <a href="/wiki/R.A.P._Ferreira" title="R.A.P. Ferreira">R.A.P. Ferreira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nocando" title="Nocando">Nocando</a>, <a href="/wiki/Open_Mike_Eagle" title="Open Mike Eagle">Open Mike Eagle</a>, <a href="/wiki/2Mex" title="2Mex">2Mex</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aesop_Rock" title="Aesop Rock">Aesop Rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Radioinactive" title="Radioinactive">Radioinactive</a>. His primary producers have been <a href="/wiki/Daedelus_(musician)" title="Daedelus (musician)">Daedelus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boom_Bip" title="Boom Bip">Boom Bip</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daddy_Kev" title="Daddy Kev">Daddy Kev</a>, <a href="/wiki/Loden_(musician)" title="Loden (musician)">Loden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris_Zax" title="Paris Zax">Paris Zax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Omid_Walizadeh" title="Omid Walizadeh">Omid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Segal" title="Kenny Segal">Kenny Segal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nobody_(producer)" title="Nobody (producer)">Nobody</a>. He has also worked with <a href="/wiki/D-Styles" title="D-Styles">D-Styles</a> on two albums.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Busdriver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Regan Farquhar was born on February 12, 1978, in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was introduced to <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> at an early age; his father, <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Farquhar" title="Ralph Farquhar">Ralph Farquhar</a>, wrote the 1985 film <i><a href="/wiki/Krush_Groove" title="Krush Groove">Krush Groove</a></i>. Farquhar began rapping at age nine. By age 13, he was part of the group 4/29, which was inspired by the <a href="/wiki/1992_Los_Angeles_riots" title="1992 Los Angeles riots">1992 Los Angeles riots</a>. At 16, he joined the <a href="/wiki/Project_Blowed" title="Project Blowed">Project Blowed</a> scene.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2002, Busdriver released his solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Temporary_Forever" title="Temporary Forever">Temporary Forever</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2004, he released <i><a href="/wiki/Cosmic_Cleavage" title="Cosmic Cleavage">Cosmic Cleavage</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Big_Dada" title="Big Dada">Big Dada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, Busdriver released <i><a href="/wiki/RoadKillOvercoat" title="RoadKillOvercoat">RoadKillOvercoat</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Epitaph_Records" title="Epitaph Records">Epitaph Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Another solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Jhelli_Beam" title="Jhelli Beam">Jhelli Beam</a></i>, was released on <a href="/wiki/Anti-_(record_label)" title="Anti- (record label)">Anti-</a> in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He is the executive producer of <a href="/wiki/Thirsty_Fish" title="Thirsty Fish">Thirsty Fish</a>'s 2009 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Watergate_(album)" title="Watergate (album)">Watergate</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Busdriver released a free mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Computer_Cooties" title="Computer Cooties">Computer Cooties</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-AGM_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AGM-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In that year, he also started a band Physical Forms with Jeff Byron, who is a former member of <a href="/wiki/The_Mae_Shi" title="The Mae Shi">The Mae Shi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Carnage_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carnage-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Physical Forms released a split 7-inch single, <i>Hoofdriver</i>, with <a href="/wiki/Deerhoof" title="Deerhoof">Deerhoof</a> on <a href="/wiki/Polyvinyl_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Polyvinyl Records">Polyvinyl Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Another of Busdriver's projects is <a href="/wiki/Flash_Bang_Grenada" title="Flash Bang Grenada">Flash Bang Grenada</a>, a collaboration with the rapper <a href="/wiki/Nocando" title="Nocando">Nocando</a>. The duo released the debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/10_Haters" title="10 Haters">10 Haters</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/Hellfyre_Club" title="Hellfyre Club">Hellfyre Club</a> in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-LA_Weekly_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA_Weekly-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Busdriver released his solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Beaus$Eros" title="Beaus$Eros">Beaus$Eros</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/Fake_Four_Inc." title="Fake Four Inc.">Fake Four Inc.</a> in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Later that year, he released a free EP, <i>Arguments with Dreams</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, he released <i><a href="/wiki/Perfect_Hair_(album)" title="Perfect Hair (album)">Perfect Hair</a></i> on Big Dada.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Busdriver appeared on <a href="/wiki/Flying_Lotus" title="Flying Lotus">Flying Lotus</a>' directorial debut feature film, <i><a href="/wiki/Kuso_(film)" title="Kuso (film)">Kuso</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, he released a studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Electricity_Is_on_Our_Side" title="Electricity Is on Our Side">Electricity Is on Our Side</a></i>, on Temporary Whatever.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
