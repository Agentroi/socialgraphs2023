<p><b>Lucas MacFadden</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born October 4, 1972),<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> better known as <b>Cut Chemist</b>, is an American DJ and record producer.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He is a former member of <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_5" title="Jurassic 5">Jurassic 5</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ozomatli" title="Ozomatli">Ozomatli</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He has collaborated with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Shadow" title="DJ Shadow">DJ Shadow</a> on a number of projects.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cut_Chemist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cut Chemist graduated from <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Los_Angeles" title="University of California, Los Angeles">University of California, Los Angeles</a> in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Cut Chemist also attended Glendale Community College and took several drawing and life drawing classes in the early 1990's.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cut_Chemist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cut Chemist became known through the Los Angeles-based rap group Unity Committee.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The group merged with another group, Rebels of Rhythm, to form <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_5" title="Jurassic 5">Jurassic 5</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2004, Cut Chemist released <i>The Litmus Test</i>, which included his solo tracks, remixes, and Jurassic 5 tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He left Jurassic 5 in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In a 2006 interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i>, he stated that he had left the group in order to get his solo album finished.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard-leftgroup_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-leftgroup-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His first solo studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Audience%27s_Listening" title="The Audience&#39;s Listening">The Audience's Listening</a></i>, was released on July 11, 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, it was listed by <i><a href="/wiki/Vice_(magazine)" title="Vice (magazine)">Vice</a></i> as the 49th greatest dance album of all time.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> "The Audience Is Listening Theme Song," the song from the album, has been featured in an <a href="/wiki/IPod_Nano" title="IPod Nano">iPod Nano</a> advertisement.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, he released a DJ mix, <i>Sound of the Police</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It was recorded using a turntable, a mixer, and a loop pedal.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His second solo studio album, <i>Die Cut</i>, was released on March 2, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It featured guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Chali_2na" title="Chali 2na">Chali 2na</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biz_Markie" title="Biz Markie">Biz Markie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mr._Lif" title="Mr. Lif">Mr. Lif</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Myka_9" title="Myka 9">Myka 9</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
