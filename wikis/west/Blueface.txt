<p><b>Johnathan Jamall Porter</b> (born January 20, 1997), known professionally as <b>Blueface</b>, is an American rapper. In October 2018, after releasing the music video for his song "<a href="/wiki/Respect_My_Cryppin%27" title="Respect My Cryppin&#39;">Respect My Cryppin'</a>", he became a viral meme due to his <a href="/wiki/Off-beat_(music)#On-beat_and_off-beat" class="mw-redirect" title="Off-beat (music)">offbeat</a> style of rapping.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The following month, he was signed to Cash Money West, the <a href="/wiki/West_coast_hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="West coast hip hop">West Coast</a> branch of <a href="/wiki/Birdman_(rapper)" title="Birdman (rapper)">Birdman</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money Records</a> label.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2019, a remix of his song "<a href="/wiki/Thotiana" title="Thotiana">Thotiana</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Cardi_B" title="Cardi B">Cardi B</a> and <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>), became his most successful single to date, peaking at number 8 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. In 2020, he released his debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Find_the_Beat" title="Find the Beat">Find the Beat</a></i>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Blueface was born on January 20, 1997,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> in Los Angeles, California. He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Mid-City,_Los_Angeles,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Mid-City, Los Angeles, California">Mid-City</a> in central Los Angeles and attended multiple elementary schools before moving in with his mother in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clarita_Valley" title="Santa Clarita Valley">Santa Clarita Valley</a>, and later settled in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland</a> with his father.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After relocating to the <a href="/wiki/San_Fernando_Valley" title="San Fernando Valley">San Fernando Valley</a>, Blueface attended <a href="/wiki/Arleta_High_School" title="Arleta High School">Arleta High School</a> and joined the marching band playing alto saxophone and football team, becoming the starting quarterback in 2014, standing at 6 ft 3 in (191 cm) and weighing 162 lbs (73.5 kg).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>As starting quarterback, Blueface led the team to an East Valley League championship in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Throwing for 1,234 yards and 17 touchdowns in 2013 and 1,724 yards and 21 touchdowns in 2014, Blueface committed to play for <a href="/wiki/Fayetteville_State_University" title="Fayetteville State University">Fayetteville State University</a> and briefly played college football before leaving in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Blueface references his past experiences as a football player in the video for the remix to "Thotiana", where the rapper portrays a top prospect high school quarterback disrespecting his coach due to his desire to pursue women and fame.<sup id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Blueface began to take an interest in <a href="/wiki/Rap_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap music">rap music</a> at a young age, and mainly listened to <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Prior to entering the music industry, he had spent most of his adult life unemployed, occasionally holding a few temporary jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<h3><span id="2017.E2.80.932018:_Viral_success.2C_Famous_Cryp.2C_and_.22Bleed_It.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–2018:_Viral_success,_Famous_Cryp,_and_&quot;Bleed_It&quot;">2017–2018: Viral success, <i>Famous Cryp</i>, and "Bleed It"</span></h3>
<p>Blueface began rapping in January 2017 under the name <b>Blueface Bleedem</b>, a reference to his ties to the School Yard <a href="/wiki/Crips" title="Crips">Crips</a> street gang. Originally moving back to Los Angeles after leaving <a href="/wiki/Fayetteville_State_University" title="Fayetteville State University">Fayetteville State University</a>, he was invited to his friend Laudiano's music studio to retrieve a phone charger, and after being challenged to rap over a beat, Blueface began to work to release his first song, "Deadlocs", produced by Laudiano, onto <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-laudiano_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laudiano-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In June 2018, he released his first full-length project, <i><a href="/wiki/Famous_Cryp" title="Famous Cryp">Famous Cryp</a></i>. In September 2018, after the song and EP helped him build a local following in California, he released his second EP, <i>Two Coccy</i>, onto SoundCloud and <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In October 2018, Blueface released a music video for his song, "<a href="/wiki/Respect_My_Cryppin%27" title="Respect My Cryppin&#39;">Respect My Cryppin'</a>", on <a href="/wiki/WorldstarHipHop" class="mw-redirect" title="WorldstarHipHop">WorldstarHipHop</a>'s <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel. Shortly after the song was posted to <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>, where it became a viral <a href="/wiki/Internet_meme" title="Internet meme">meme</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-laudiano_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laudiano-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The video's popularity led to more of Blueface's music receiving attention, with his songs "<a href="/wiki/Thotiana" title="Thotiana">Thotiana</a>" and "Next Big Thing" earning newfound popularity. His popularity increased even further after holding a contest on his <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> stories and posts using polls to determine which high school he should visit, with <a href="/wiki/Pasadena_High_School_(California)" title="Pasadena High School (California)">Pasadena High School</a> edging out a victory over <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica_High_School" title="Santa Monica High School">Santa Monica High School</a>. The social media challenge brought in new followers, increasing his already growing popularity. In November 2018, Blueface was signed to <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money Records</a>' <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip-hop" class="mw-redirect" title="West Coast hip-hop">West Coast</a> affiliate <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money West</a>, and posted clips of himself in the studio with Canadian rapper <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quavo" title="Quavo">Quavo</a> on his Instagram page.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-uproxx.com_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uproxx.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2018, Blueface again went viral thanks to an acoustic video with <a href="/wiki/Einer_Bankz" title="Einer Bankz">Einer Bankz</a> previewing his new song, "<a href="/wiki/Bleed_It" title="Bleed It">Bleed It</a>" which released officially in January 2019. He released the song two days later, including a video on the <a href="/wiki/Lyrical_Lemonade" class="mw-redirect" title="Lyrical Lemonade">Lyrical Lemonade</a> YouTube channel, directed by <a href="/wiki/Cole_Bennett" title="Cole Bennett">Cole Bennett</a>, that amassed over 2 million views in the first 24 hours of its release.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2019.E2.80.93present:_.22Thotiana.22.2C_Dirt_Bag.2C_and_Find_the_Beat"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–present:_&quot;Thotiana&quot;,_Dirt_Bag,_and_Find_the_Beat">2019–present: "Thotiana", <i>Dirt Bag</i>, and <i>Find the Beat</i></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Blueface_in_Feb_2020.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Blueface_in_Feb_2020.png/220px-Blueface_in_Feb_2020.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="290" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Blueface_in_Feb_2020.png/330px-Blueface_in_Feb_2020.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Blueface_in_Feb_2020.png/440px-Blueface_in_Feb_2020.png 2x" data-file-width="653" data-file-height="860" /></a><figcaption>Blueface performing in 2020</figcaption></figure>
<p>On January 26, 2019, Blueface's track, "<a href="/wiki/Thotiana" title="Thotiana">Thotiana</a>", debuted on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 at number 75, making it his first track to chart on a <i>Billboard</i> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The song was released as a remix with <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a> with a music video. The following week, he released a remix with <a href="/wiki/Cardi_B" title="Cardi B">Cardi B</a>, coinciding with another video from Cole Bennett. On streaming services, the two remixes are separate, but there is also a third where all three versions are put together.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On May 1, 2019, Blueface released the music video for his single, "Stop Cappin", which has amassed over 5 million views over the first four days on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On June 20, Blueface was announced to be part of <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s 2019 Freshman Class as the fan-voted eleventh entrant.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2019, Blueface released an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> titled <i>Dirt Bag</i>; the album was preceded by the singles "Stop Cappin'", "<a href="/wiki/Daddy_(Blueface_song)" title="Daddy (Blueface song)">Daddy</a>" and "Bussdown". At eight tracks, the EP featured guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rich_the_Kid" title="Rich the Kid">Rich the Kid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Offset_(rapper)" title="Offset (rapper)">Offset</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Pump" title="Lil Pump">Lil Pump</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mozzy" title="Mozzy">Mozzy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> His song "Daddy" featuring Rich the Kid on the EP peaked at No. 78 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, and "Stop Cappin'" with The Game reached No. 15 on the Bubbling Under Hot 100. The EP peaked at No. 48 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Despite commercial success, the EP received overwhelmingly negative critical reviews. Riley Wallace at Hip-Hop DX rated the project at 3/5, while listeners rated it at 2.33/5.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Album of the Year appraised a total critic score of 60/100, while listeners reviewed it at 51/100.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Fred Thomas of <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> followed a trend of middle-ranged reviews, giving the project a 3/5.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2019, Blueface announced his debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Find_the_Beat" title="Find the Beat">Find the Beat</a></i>, and its tracklist.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He released four singles from the album, "Close Up" featuring <a href="/wiki/Jeremih" title="Jeremih">Jeremih</a>, "First Class" featuring <a href="/wiki/Gunna_(rapper)" title="Gunna (rapper)">Gunna</a>, "Obama" featuring <a href="/wiki/DaBaby" title="DaBaby">DaBaby</a>, and "Holy Moly" featuring <a href="/wiki/NLE_Choppa" title="NLE Choppa">NLE Choppa</a>. On December 6, 2019, the album was set to release but was delayed due to trademark issues. On January 17, 2020, the second release date was confirmed, but it was delayed again. On March 13, 2020, the third release date, the album released, with features from <a href="/wiki/Polo_G" title="Polo G">Polo G</a>, Ambjaay, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Baby" title="Lil Baby">Lil Baby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stunna_4_Vegas" title="Stunna 4 Vegas">Stunna 4 Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/YBN_Nahmir" title="YBN Nahmir">YBN Nahmir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gunna_(rapper)" title="Gunna (rapper)">Gunna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremih" title="Jeremih">Jeremih</a>, <a href="/wiki/NLE_Choppa" title="NLE Choppa">NLE Choppa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/DaBaby" title="DaBaby">DaBaby</a>, totaling 16 songs.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert" title="Lil Uzi Vert">Lil Uzi Vert</a>, who released an album titled, <i><a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert_vs._the_World_2" title="Lil Uzi Vert vs. the World 2">Lil Uzi Vert vs. the World 2</a></i>, on the same day <i>Find the Beat</i> was released, praised the album. In a private exchange with Blueface, Lil Uzi Vert said "album fye" [<i><a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">sic</a></i>] and offered to collaborate with Blueface.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2021, Blueface opened a soul food restaurant in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clara,_California" title="Santa Clara, California">Santa Clara, California</a>, named Blue's Fish and Soul.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span></h2>
<p>Blueface's off-beat style of rapping and unique voice has been compared to fellow <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip-hop" class="mw-redirect" title="West Coast hip-hop">California rappers</a> <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suga_Free" title="Suga Free">Suga Free</a>, as well as rapper Silkk the Shocker.  Blueface has said that he writes "to the beat", and uses the instrumental as a base for all of his songs.<sup id="cite_ref-uproxx.com_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uproxx.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Boxing">Boxing</span></h2>
<p>In June 2021, Blueface announced he had signed to fight for <a href="/wiki/Bare_Knuckle_Fighting_Championship" title="Bare Knuckle Fighting Championship">Bare Knuckle Fighting Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut in a special gloved match against Tiktoker Kane Trujillo on July 23, 2021, at <a href="/wiki/2021_in_Bare_Knuckle_Fighting_Championship#Bare_Knuckle_Fighting_Championship_19" title="2021 in Bare Knuckle Fighting Championship">BKFC 19</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Blueface defeated Trujillo via <a href="/wiki/Unanimous_decision" title="Unanimous decision">unanimous decision</a> after three rounds.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Blueface was originally set to have two <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_fight" title="Exhibition fight">exhibition</a> matches in 2022, however, both organizers removed Blueface from their card. Blueface was scheduled to face <a href="/wiki/FaZe_Clan" title="FaZe Clan">FaZe</a> Temperrr as the co-feature on <a href="/wiki/KSI_vs_Swarmz_%26_Luis_Alcaraz_Pineda" class="mw-redirect" title="KSI vs Swarmz &amp; Luis Alcaraz Pineda">August 27</a>, however, due to an altercation he had with his girlfriend prior to the bout, he was removed.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Blueface is scheduled to face British TikTok star Ed Mathews on the preliminary card for the High Stakes Tournament on April 22, 2023 at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Arena" title="Wembley Arena">Wembley Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, England.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span></h2>
<p>In 2018, after a man tried to rob him at a gas station in <a href="/wiki/Newhall,_Santa_Clarita,_California" title="Newhall, Santa Clarita, California">Newhall, California</a>, Blueface shot at the suspect's car, and was later arrested and charged with shooting at an occupied vehicle, a felony in the state of California. Blueface was released on $69,000 bail on November 18.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Blueface was arrested in February 2019, for felony gun possession after law enforcement recovered a loaded, unregistered handgun in his possession.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 15, 2022, Porter was arrested on charges of attempted murder and shooting in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>, on October 8 of that year.  He was imprisoned in the <a href="/wiki/Clark_County_Detention_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="Clark County Detention Center">Clark County Detention Center</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Blueface has three children.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> In January 2023, <a href="/wiki/Chrisean_Rock" title="Chrisean Rock">Chrisean Rock</a> announced that she was pregnant with her first child by Blueface.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2019, Blueface garnered backlash after he posted a video of himself standing on top of a black SUV and throwing cash into a crowd of people in <a href="/wiki/Skid_Row,_Los_Angeles" title="Skid Row, Los Angeles">Skid Row</a>, a notoriously poverty-stricken area of Los Angeles. Although some saw it as a good gesture, it was criticized as "dehumanizing".<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
