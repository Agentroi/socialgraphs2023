<p><i><b>All Frames of the Game</b></i> is the debut album by American rap group Playaz Tryna Strive, released November 12, 1996, on <a href="/wiki/Sick_Wid_It_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Sick Wid It Records">Sick Wid It</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>. Playaz Tryna Strive, also known as P.T.S., is composed of Filthy Rich and T–Pup.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The album was produced by Filthy Rich, K-Lou, Mike D, Studio Ton and Wayniac, and <a href="/wiki/Executive_producer" title="Executive producer">executive produced</a> by <a href="/wiki/B-Legit" title="B-Legit">B-Legit</a> and <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>. The album peaked at number 100 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums"><i>Billboard</i> Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AMG_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It features guest performances by E-40, B-Legit, Levitti, Silk–E, <a href="/wiki/Suga-T" title="Suga-T">Suga-T</a> and E-Clipze.
</p><p>Along with a single, a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> was released for the song, "They Say Y.D.L.L." (Youngsta's Don't Live Long).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song, "Feel Ness Real", was originally released on the 1995 Sick Wid It Records compilation, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hogg_in_Me" title="The Hogg in Me">The Hogg in Me</a></i>.
</p>
