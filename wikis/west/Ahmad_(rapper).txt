<p><b>Ahmad Ali Lewis</b> (born October 12, 1975), <a href="/wiki/Mononymous_person" class="mw-redirect" title="Mononymous person">simply known as</a> <b>Ahmad</b> (often stylized <b>AHMAD</b>), is an American rapper, songwriter, <a href="/wiki/Motivational_speaker" title="Motivational speaker">motivational speaker</a> and author from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. He is perhaps best known for the 1994 single "<a href="/wiki/Back_in_the_Day_(Ahmad_song)" title="Back in the Day (Ahmad song)">Back in the Day</a>". Ahmad is also a former member and founder of the hip-hop fusion band <a href="/wiki/4th_Avenue_Jones" title="4th Avenue Jones">4th Avenue Jones</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ahmad_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ahmad made his recording debut in 1993 on the soundtrack of <a href="/wiki/The_Meteor_Man_(film)" title="The Meteor Man (film)">The Meteor Man</a> with the song "Who Can." Released when Ahmad was 18, "Back in the Day" the remix version (produced by Maurice Thompson of Barr 9 Productions) hit No. 26 on the US pop charts and No. 19 on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/R%26B" class="mw-redirect" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> charts on the strength of its <a href="/wiki/Hook_(music)" title="Hook (music)">hook</a>, sung over a sample of the <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Pendergrass" title="Teddy Pendergrass">Teddy Pendergrass</a> song "Love TKO." It also reached No. 64 in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was the first single off his first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Ahmad_(album)" title="Ahmad (album)">Ahmad</a></i>. The remix of "Back in the Day" is also featured on the soundtrack of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wood" title="The Wood">The Wood</a></i>, a 1999 motion picture. Ahmad secured his first recording contract as a senior in high school and earned a gold record for his chart-topping hit.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He later formed the hip-hop fusion band <a href="/wiki/4th_Avenue_Jones" title="4th Avenue Jones">4th Avenue Jones</a>.
</p><p>He went back to school and enrolled in <a href="/wiki/Long_Beach_City_College" title="Long Beach City College">Long Beach City College</a> in 2006; after graduating he was accepted by <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>, to acquire bachelor's degrees in sociology and African American studies.<sup id="cite_ref-Gordon_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gordon-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lewis was married to his former 4th Avenue Jones bandmate, Tena Jones, from 1998-2007. Their son, Yeshuwa Ali, was born in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-Gordon_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gordon-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lewis is a practicing Christian.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
