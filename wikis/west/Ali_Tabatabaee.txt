<p><b>Ali Tabatabaee</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span lang="fa" dir="rtl">علی طباطبایی‌پور</span>; born February 27, 1973)<sup id="cite_ref-Bio_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an Iranian-American musician who is one of two main vocalists in the rock band <a href="/wiki/Zebrahead" title="Zebrahead">Zebrahead</a>. Alongside bandmate <a href="/wiki/Matty_Lewis" title="Matty Lewis">Matty Lewis</a>, he performed the titular character's <a href="/wiki/His_World" class="mw-redirect" title="His World">theme song</a> in the 2006 video game <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(2006_video_game)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (2006 video game)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ali_Tabatabaee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tabatabaee was born in <a href="/wiki/Tehran" title="Tehran">Tehran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a><sup id="cite_ref-Bio_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> on February 27, 1973. Before joining <a href="/wiki/Zebrahead" title="Zebrahead">Zebrahead</a>, Tabatabaee was a pre-med student at <a href="/wiki/UC_Irvine" class="mw-redirect" title="UC Irvine">UC Irvine</a> majoring in Biology and minoring in Theater. After graduating, he decided to take a year off from school to take the <a href="/wiki/MCAT" class="mw-redirect" title="MCAT">MCAT</a> and to apply to medical school. It was during this time that he started singing with the other members of Zebrahead. After a year away from school he was accepted into the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago_Medical_School" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Chicago Medical School">University of Chicago Medical School</a>. However, due to Zebrahead's early success, he deferred acceptance for one year. "I didn't expect music to go anywhere. I thought I'd do it for a year and then I'd leave, and in a week's period, I got an acceptance to med school and we were supposed to do a showcase and the label was going to fly us to New York and I had to tell the school; they wanted to know. So I deferred for a year and then decided that I didn't want to go back after a year.... I think [music is] the ultimate way to express culture. To do that for a living? Everybody should aspire to do that."<sup id="cite_ref-ocweekly2000_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ocweekly2000-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Tabatabaee cites some of his favorite bands as <a href="/wiki/A_Tribe_Called_Quest" title="A Tribe Called Quest">A Tribe Called Quest</a>, <a href="/wiki/System_of_a_Down" title="System of a Down">System of a Down</a>, and <a href="/wiki/N.W.A." class="mw-redirect" title="N.W.A.">N.W.A.</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ali_Tabatabaee_-_Zebrahead.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Ali_Tabatabaee_-_Zebrahead.jpg/170px-Ali_Tabatabaee_-_Zebrahead.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="244" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Ali_Tabatabaee_-_Zebrahead.jpg/255px-Ali_Tabatabaee_-_Zebrahead.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Ali_Tabatabaee_-_Zebrahead.jpg/340px-Ali_Tabatabaee_-_Zebrahead.jpg 2x" data-file-width="482" data-file-height="692" /></a><figcaption>Tabatabaee performing in 2011</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 1999, while on the Warped Tour, Tabatabaee was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Hodgkin%27s_lymphoma" class="mw-redirect" title="Hodgkin&#39;s lymphoma">Hodgkin's Disease</a>, a type of cancer, which he underwent chemo therapy and radiation therapy to cure. While being treated, he continued to work with his bandmates in writing songs for their album, <i><a href="/wiki/Playmate_of_the_Year_(album)" title="Playmate of the Year (album)">Playmate of the Year</a></i>, and a few months before the album's release, he completed his therapy. He claims that because of what he was going through, some of the songs from that album are very special to him.<sup id="cite_ref-ocweekly2000_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ocweekly2000-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tabatabaee is featured on the <a href="/wiki/Reel_Big_Fish" title="Reel Big Fish">Reel Big Fish</a> DVD <i>You're All in This Together</i> on the song "Unity" (originally by <a href="/wiki/Operation_Ivy_(band)" title="Operation Ivy (band)">Operation Ivy</a>).
</p><p>Due to Zebrahead's growing popularity in Japan, Tabatabaee and Zebrahead bandmate <a href="/wiki/Matty_Lewis" title="Matty Lewis">Matty Lewis</a> were asked by <a href="/wiki/Sega" title="Sega">Sega</a> to record vocals for a new theme song, titled "<a href="/wiki/His_World" class="mw-redirect" title="His World">His World</a>", which appears in the 2006 game <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(2006_video_game)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (2006 video game)">Sonic The Hedgehog</a></i>. Tabatabaee also composes most of the songs that Zebrahead performs.
</p>
