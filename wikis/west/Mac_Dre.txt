class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Andre Louis Hicks</b> (July 5, 1970 – November 1, 2004), known by his stage name <b>Mac Dre</b>, was an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Vallejo,_California" title="Vallejo, California">Vallejo, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was an instrumental figure in the emergence of <a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">hyphy</a>, a cultural movement in the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">Bay Area</a> <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> scene that emerged in the early 2000s.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Hicks is considered one of the movement's key pioneers that fueled its popularity into mainstream, releasing songs with fast-paced rhymes and basslines that inspired a new style of dance.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> As the founder of the independent record label <a href="/wiki/Thizz_Entertainment" title="Thizz Entertainment">Thizz Entertainment</a>, Hicks recorded dozens of albums and gave aspiring rappers an outlet to release albums locally.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 1, 2004, Hicks was killed by an unknown assailant after a performance in <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City, Missouri</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> a case that remains unsolved.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mac_Dre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andre Louis Hicks was born in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a> on July 5, 1970, to Allen Hicks and Wanda Salvatto. They then moved to the <a href="/wiki/Vallejo,_California" title="Vallejo, California">Vallejo</a> area. He would often frequent the Country Club Crest neighborhood, known locally as The Crest. In 1989, the outgoing Hicks made waves with a cassette tape featuring the single, <i>"Too Hard for the F—in' Radio"</i> while still a student at Vallejo's Hogan High School. In 2013 <a href="/wiki/National_Public_Radio_(NPR)" class="mw-redirect" title="National Public Radio (NPR)">National Public Radio (NPR)</a> noted his sound as being <i>"fast and confident"</i> further writing that <i>"he built upon the bouncy bass that had its roots in the funk era."</i><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> When asked about his childhood, Hicks stated that "Situations came out for the better most of them, I went through the little trials and the shit that I went through."<sup id="cite_ref-530NorCal_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-530NorCal-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (May 2018)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Hicks first adopted the stage name MC Dre in 1984, but altered it to Mac Dre the following year because he considered that the name sounded "too <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_rap" class="mw-redirect" title="East Coast rap">East Coast</a>-ish".<sup id="cite_ref-530NorCal2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-530NorCal2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (May 2018)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Hicks recorded his first three <a href="/wiki/Extended_plays" class="mw-redirect" title="Extended plays">extended play (EP) recordings</a> as Mac Dre between 1988 and 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-Mac_Dre_-_Biography_(BIO)_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mac_Dre_-_Biography_(BIO)-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conviction">Conviction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mac_Dre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Conviction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city of Vallejo began experiencing a surge in bank robberies in the early 90s.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Vallejo police began focusing on the Crest neighborhood as a source of the crime. Hicks was vocal about the actions he saw being taken by the police and incorporated their aggressive surveillance of residents into his music. Hicks claimed he was rapping about attempts to "wake up the neighbors."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> As <a href="/wiki/Gangster_rap" class="mw-redirect" title="Gangster rap">gangster rap music</a> consistently grew in popularity, law enforcement officials began examining the lyrics of local rappers to utilize as evidence in criminal matters.<sup id="cite_ref-NPR_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPR-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 26, 1992, at age 21, Hicks was invited by friends on a road trip to <a href="/wiki/Fresno" class="mw-redirect" title="Fresno">Fresno</a>. Hicks had performed in that city two weeks prior and decided to go on the trip so that he could re-visit a woman he knew there. While driving back to Vallejo, the car was surrounded by <a href="/wiki/FBI" class="mw-redirect" title="FBI">FBI</a> agents and Fresno and Vallejo police officers. The police said that while Hicks was at a motel, his friends were allegedly casing a bank but had changed their mind when they saw a local Fresno TV News van in the bank's parking lot.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> When questioned by the police, Hicks said that he didn't leave the hotel, therefore did not know anything. The police subsequently charged him with conspiracy to commit <a href="/wiki/Robbery" title="Robbery">robbery</a>, although Hicks was not with his friends at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After he refused a plea deal for the conspiracy charge, he was tried, convicted, and sentenced to five years in federal prison.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The conviction hinged on a gun linked to the bank robberies found in his apartment months before his arrest, and a recording where he was heard to say "Shoot out the surveillance cameras".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The trial was listed among <a href="/wiki/Complex_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex Magazine">Complex Magazine</a>'s 30 Biggest Criminal Trials in Rap History.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> At the time of his conviction, Hicks owned the <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a> Romp Productions.<sup id="cite_ref-Mac_Dre_-_Biography_(BIO)_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mac_Dre_-_Biography_(BIO)-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Hicks was released a year early from prison for good behavior on August 2, 1996, after serving four years.<sup id="cite_ref-Mac_Dre_-_Biography_(BIO)_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mac_Dre_-_Biography_(BIO)-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It was during his time in prison that Hicks developed a "better appreciation for freedom, life, fun."<sup id="cite_ref-An_Oral_History_of_Hyphy_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-An_Oral_History_of_Hyphy-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-prison_career">Post-prison career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mac_Dre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post-prison career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After his release from <a href="/wiki/United_States_Penitentiary,_Lompoc" title="United States Penitentiary, Lompoc">Lompoc Prison</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Mac Dre and longtime collaborator Coolio Da' Unda Dogg (Troy Reddick) recorded tracks to pitch to major record labels. One song was sent to representatives of fellow Bay Area rapper <a href="/wiki/Too_Short" title="Too Short">Too Short</a> for an upcoming compilation, <i><a href="/wiki/Nationwide:_Independence_Day" title="Nationwide: Independence Day">Nationwide: Independence Day</a></i>, but was not selected.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Mac Dre moved to the <a href="/wiki/Arden-Arcade" class="mw-redirect" title="Arden-Arcade">Arden-Arcade</a> area of <a href="/wiki/Sacramento" class="mw-redirect" title="Sacramento">Sacramento</a> in 1998 in attempt to distance himself from Vallejo law enforcement. There, he founded his independent label <a href="/wiki/Thizz_Entertainment" title="Thizz Entertainment">Thizz Entertainment</a>, which is currently managed by his mother Wanda Salvatto.<sup id="cite_ref-An_Oral_History_of_Hyphy_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-An_Oral_History_of_Hyphy-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In the early 2000s, Dre's change in sound became influential in the <a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">hyphy movement</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-An_Oral_History_of_Hyphy_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-An_Oral_History_of_Hyphy-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> rapper and producer <a href="/wiki/Lil_Jon" title="Lil Jon">Lil Jon</a>, with Salvatto's blessing, incorporated Dre's vocals into his 2019 single "Ain't No Tellin".<sup id="cite_ref-Complex_Magazine_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex_Magazine-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mac_Dre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Hicks and other Thizz Entertainment members had performed a show in <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City, Missouri</a> on October 31, 2004, an unidentified gunman shot at the group's van as it traveled on <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_71" title="U.S. Route 71">U.S. Route 71</a> in the early morning hours of November 1. The van's driver crashed and called <a href="/wiki/9-1-1" title="9-1-1">911</a>, but Hicks was pronounced dead at the scene from a bullet wound to the neck.<sup id="cite_ref-SFGate_Death_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SFGate_Death-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Local rapper Anthony "Fat Tone" Watkins was alleged to have been responsible for the murder, but no evidence ever surfaced, and Watkins himself was shot dead the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hicks' funeral took place on November 9, 2004, at the Mt. Calvary Baptist Church.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He was given a public open-casket viewing, and then buried at the <a href="/wiki/Mountain_View_Cemetery_(Oakland,_California)" title="Mountain View Cemetery (Oakland, California)">Mountain View Cemetery</a> in Oakland.
</p>
