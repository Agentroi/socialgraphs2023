<p><b>Frank Lopes Jr.</b> (born December 22, 1994), known professionally as <b>Hobo Johnson</b>, is an American vocalist and frontman of Hobo Johnson &amp; the LoveMakers.<sup id="cite_ref-insightmusic-063017c_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-insightmusic-063017c-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hobo_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lopes is a Mexican/<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Americans" title="Portuguese Americans">Portuguese-American</a> from <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Vulture_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vulture-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> When he was 19, he was kicked out of his house by his father and subsequently lived in his car.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> For a stretch of time, Lopes worked at a pizza restaurant in <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento</a>, but he soon quit to pursue his passion for music.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He is a fan of the <a href="/wiki/Sacramento_Kings" title="Sacramento Kings">Sacramento Kings</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and has referenced them in the lyrics to his song "Sacramento Kings Anthem (We're Not That Bad)", written about the struggles the Kings have in the NBA.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hobo_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Early_work_and_The_Rise_of_Hobo_Johnson_.282015.E2.80.932017.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_work_and_The_Rise_of_Hobo_Johnson_(2015–2017)">Early work and <i>The Rise of Hobo Johnson</i> (2015–2017)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hobo_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early work and The Rise of Hobo Johnson (2015–2017)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At age 15, Lopes began performing music and hip-hop.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-insightmusic-063017c_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-insightmusic-063017c-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He came up with his stage name "Homeless Johnson" but soon made it "Hobo Johnson" while living in his car, a 1994 <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Corolla" title="Toyota Corolla">Toyota Corolla</a>, and named his 2015 debut album in honor of his car.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Starting in December 2016, he released a series of live recordings on YouTube subtitled "Live from <a href="/wiki/Oak_Park,_Sacramento,_California" title="Oak Park, Sacramento, California">Oak Park</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-sacbee_article212117874_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sacbee_article212117874-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In 2016, he released the album <i>The Rise of Hobo Johnson</i> independently, but later created a new version of the album in 2017 which had changes to the production and a different tracklist. After the release of the second version of "The Rise Of Hobo Johnson", Lopes signed to Reprise Records. That year, he won four Sacramento Area Music Awards: Artist of the Year, Best Hip-Hop/Rap, Best Emcee and Best New Artist.<sup id="cite_ref-insightmusic-063017c_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-insightmusic-063017c-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Tiny_Desk_Concert.2C_.22Peach_Scone.22.2C_and_The_Fall_of_Hobo_Johnson_.282018.E2.80.932019.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Tiny_Desk_Concert,_&quot;Peach_Scone&quot;,_and_The_Fall_of_Hobo_Johnson_(2018–2019)">Tiny Desk Concert, "Peach Scone", and <i>The Fall of Hobo Johnson</i> (2018–2019)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hobo_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tiny Desk Concert, &quot;Peach Scone&quot;, and The Fall of Hobo Johnson (2018–2019)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/The_Fall_of_Hobo_Johnson" title="The Fall of Hobo Johnson">The Fall of Hobo Johnson</a></div>
<p>Hobo Johnson and the LoveMakers released a music video on Facebook as part of <a href="/wiki/NPR" title="NPR">NPR</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tiny_Desk_Concert" class="mw-redirect" title="Tiny Desk Concert">Tiny Desk Contest</a> on March 7, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The song "Peach Scone" collected millions of views in just a few weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Following the attention of the "Peach Scone" video, their album <i>The Rise of Hobo Johnson</i> charted at #11 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers"><i>Billboard</i> Heatseekers Chart</a> for the week ending March 31, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-Heat_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heat-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> At this time, Lopes also drew criticism from the Sacramento chapter of <a href="/wiki/Black_Lives_Matter" title="Black Lives Matter">Black Lives Matter</a>, who accused him of using Oak Park's name without consent and of cultural appropriation.<sup id="cite_ref-sacbee_article212117874_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sacbee_article212117874-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On May 12, they staged a small protest that delayed the beginning of his sold-out show at Sacramento's Ace of Spades nightclub.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While not winning the Tiny Desk Contest, Hobo Johnson &amp; the LoveMakers appeared on Tiny Desk on an episode released on September 12, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, the band toured North America and Europe and appeared at Australia's <a href="/wiki/Falls_Festival" title="Falls Festival">Falls Music &amp; Arts Festival</a>. In 2019, the band appeared at <a href="/wiki/Bonnaroo_Music_Festival" title="Bonnaroo Music Festival">Bonnaroo Music and Arts Festival</a> in Manchester, Tennessee.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019 he released the singles "Typical Story" and "UglyKid" from the album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fall_of_Hobo_Johnson" title="The Fall of Hobo Johnson">The Fall of Hobo Johnson</a></i>, which was released by Warner Records on September 13, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> That same day, EA Sports released the video game <i><a href="/wiki/NHL_20" title="NHL 20">NHL 20</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> which featured the single "Typical Story" on its soundtrack.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="The_Revenge_of_Hobo_Johnson.2C_Hobo_Johnson_Alienates_His_Fanbase.2C_and_hiatus_.282021.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Revenge_of_Hobo_Johnson,_Hobo_Johnson_Alienates_His_Fanbase,_and_hiatus_(2021–present)"><i>The Revenge of Hobo Johnson</i>, <i>Hobo Johnson Alienates His Fanbase</i>, and hiatus (2021–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hobo_Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Revenge of Hobo Johnson, Hobo Johnson Alienates His Fanbase, and hiatus (2021–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lopes released the single "I Want to See the World" in June, 2021. He announced that the album <i>The Revenge of Hobo Johnson</i> would follow later that month.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
<i>The Revenge of Hobo Johnson</i> was subsequently released on June 23, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On August 1, 2021 he released the album <i>Hobo Johnson Alienates His Fanbase</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Iowa_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iowa-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2022, Lopes canceled several upcoming tours and broke up the LoveMakers band, citing a focus on school over music.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source. (March 2023)">unreliable source?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2023, Lopes began performing shows again and released a new song on Instagram and YouTube, titled <i>Sacramento 12 Step.</i><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
