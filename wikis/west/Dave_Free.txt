<p><b>David Isaac Friley</b><sup id="cite_ref-Birth_name_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Birth_name-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born November 13, 1986), known professionally as <b>Dave Free</b>, is an American filmmaker and record executive.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood, California</a>, he is best known for his creative partnership with rapper <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>. Together, they have directed several music videos and films under the collective name the Little Homies, and founded the entertainment company <a href="/wiki/PGLang" title="PGLang">PGLang</a> in 2020. Through their partnership, Free has won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Award</a> and three <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="MTV Video Music Awards">MTV Video Music Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_Free&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>David Isaac Friley was born on November 13, 1986, in <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood, California</a>; the son of a couple from the <a href="/wiki/Community_areas_in_Chicago" title="Community areas in Chicago">North</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Side,_Chicago" title="South Side, Chicago">South Sides</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He is the youngest of three children.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Friley is a childhood friend of rapper <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, having met while they were both in high school.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> After graduating, he worked as a computer technician while helping Lamar begin his rap career.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He used his job to showcase Lamar's music to Anthony "Top Dawg" Tiffith, founder and CEO of independent record label <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a> (TDE), which led to him signing a <a href="/wiki/Recording_contract" title="Recording contract">recording contract</a> with the label in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_Free&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shortly after Lamar signed with TDE, Free joined the label as an in-house producer. He became a member of the hip hop production team <a href="/wiki/Digi%2BPhonics" title="Digi+Phonics">Digi+Phonics</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Sounwave" title="Sounwave">Sounwave</a>, <a href="/wiki/Willie_B_(producer)" title="Willie B (producer)">Willie B</a> and Tae Beast and worked significantly on projects for the hip hop supergroup <a href="/wiki/Black_Hippy" title="Black Hippy">Black Hippy</a>, composed of Lamar, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Rock" title="Jay Rock">Jay Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul" title="Ab-Soul">Ab-Soul</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Their production work received critical praise; <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_Networks" title="Complex Networks">Complex</a></i> named Digi+Phonics as one of the top 25 new producers to watch out for in November 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a> named them as one of the top ten young producers on the rise in July 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Free's business career began as the social media director for TDE.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, he was named the label's co-president alongside <a href="/wiki/Punch_(rapper)" title="Punch (rapper)">Terrence "Punch" Henderson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Free subsequently became Lamar's manager during his tenure and co-directed several of his studio albums and accompanying music videos under the collective name The Little Homies.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Their filmmaking work earned praise and led to the duo winning the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Music_Video" title="Grammy Award for Best Music Video">Grammy Award for Best Music Video</a> and the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="MTV Video Music Awards">MTV Video Music Awards</a> for <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award_for_Best_Hip_Hop_Video" title="MTV Video Music Award for Best Hip Hop Video">Best Hip Hop Video</a> and <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award_for_Video_of_the_Year" title="MTV Video Music Award for Video of the Year">Video of the Year</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 4, 2019, <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> reported that Free had left TDE and was working independently. He was also helping rapper <a href="/wiki/Baby_Keem" title="Baby Keem">Baby Keem</a> as he started gaining recognition and expressed his intention of "getting heavy into the film game."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On March 5, 2020, Free and Lamar launched the "multi-lingual" entertainment company <a href="/wiki/PGLang" title="PGLang">PGLang</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Through the company, Free has written and directed several music videos and advertising campaigns for <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Klein_(fashion_house)" title="Calvin Klein (fashion house)">Calvin Klein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Converse_(shoe_company)" class="mw-redirect" title="Converse (shoe company)">Converse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cash_App" title="Cash App">Cash App</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On January 13, 2022, Free and Lamar announced they were producing an untitled comedy film with <i><a href="/wiki/South_Park" title="South Park">South Park</a></i> creators <a href="/wiki/Trey_Parker" title="Trey Parker">Trey Parker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matt_Stone" title="Matt Stone">Matt Stone</a> for <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
