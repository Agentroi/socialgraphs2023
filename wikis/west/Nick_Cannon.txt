<p><b>Nicholas Scott Cannon</b> (born October 8, 1980)<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is an American television host, actor, rapper, and comedian. In television, Cannon began as a teenager on <i><a href="/wiki/All_That" title="All That">All That</a></i> before going on to host <i><a href="/wiki/The_Nick_Cannon_Show" class="mw-redirect" title="The Nick Cannon Show">The Nick Cannon Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_%27n_Out" title="Wild &#39;n Out">Wild 'n Out</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent" title="America&#39;s Got Talent">America's Got Talent</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Lip_Sync_Battle_Shorties" title="Lip Sync Battle Shorties">Lip Sync Battle Shorties</a>,</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Masked_Singer_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Masked Singer (U.S. TV series)">The Masked Singer</a></i>. He acted in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Drumline_(film)" title="Drumline (film)">Drumline</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Don%27t_Cost_a_Thing_(film)" title="Love Don&#39;t Cost a Thing (film)">Love Don't Cost a Thing</a>,</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Roll_Bounce" title="Roll Bounce">Roll Bounce</a></i>.
</p><p>As a rapper he released his debut <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon_(album)" title="Nick Cannon (album)">self-titled album</a> in 2003 with the single "<a href="/wiki/Gigolo_(Nick_Cannon_song)" title="Gigolo (Nick Cannon song)">Gigolo</a>", a collaboration with singer <a href="/wiki/R._Kelly" title="R. Kelly">R. Kelly</a>. In 2007 he played the role of the fictional footballer TJ Harper in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Goal_II:_Living_the_Dream" title="Goal II: Living the Dream">Goal II: Living the Dream</a></i>. In 2006, Cannon recorded the singles "Dime Piece" and "My Wife" for the planned album <i>Stages</i>, which was never released.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Cannon was born on October 8, 1980, in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, California. Cannon was largely raised by his paternal grandfather, whom both he and his biological father called "dad". As a child, Cannon grew up in <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Park,_San_Diego" title="Lincoln Park, San Diego">Lincoln Park</a>'s Bay Vista Housing Projects, a gang-ridden section of <a href="/wiki/Southeast_San_Diego" title="Southeast San Diego">Southeast San Diego</a>. While in his teenage years, he was affiliated with the Lincoln Park Bloods street gang, but stated that he left behind his affiliations after losing a close friend.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1998, he graduated from <a href="/wiki/Monte_Vista_High_School_(Spring_Valley,_California)" title="Monte Vista High School (Spring Valley, California)">Monte Vista High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spring_Valley,_San_Diego_County,_California" title="Spring Valley, San Diego County, California">Spring Valley</a>, where he was president of the African Student Coalition and participated in <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Cannon received his first break doing <a href="/wiki/Stand-up_comedy" title="Stand-up comedy">stand-up comedy</a> on his father's local cable <a href="/wiki/Public_access_TV" class="mw-redirect" title="Public access TV">public access TV</a> program.<sup id="cite_ref-Peoples_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Peoples-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span></h2>
<h3><span id="1999.E2.80.932006:_Music_beginnings.2C_Nick_Cannon_and_Stages"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1999–2006:_Music_beginnings,_Nick_Cannon_and_Stages">1999–2006: Music beginnings, <i>Nick Cannon</i> and <i>Stages</i></span></h3>
<p>As a teenager, Cannon was one-third of the rap group "Da G4 Dope Bomb Squad", they opened for the likes of <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lyte_Funky_Ones" class="mw-redirect" title="Lyte Funky Ones">LFO</a>, <a href="/wiki/98_Degrees" title="98 Degrees">98 Degrees</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Montell_Jordan" title="Montell Jordan">Montell Jordan</a>.
</p><p>After signing with <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> in 2001, he was featured alongside <a href="/wiki/Romeo_Miller" title="Romeo Miller">Romeo Miller</a> and <a href="/wiki/3LW" title="3LW">3LW</a> on the <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Neutron:_Boy_Genius_(film)#Soundtrack_track_listing" class="mw-redirect" title="Jimmy Neutron: Boy Genius (film)">Jimmy Neutron: Boy Genius</a></i> soundtrack with a <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover</a> of the 1988 hit song "<a href="/wiki/Parents_Just_Don%27t_Understand" title="Parents Just Don&#39;t Understand">Parents Just Don't Understand</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> His debut <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon_(album)" title="Nick Cannon (album)">self titled</a> album was released in 2003 and included the single "Your Pops Don't Like Me (I Really Don't Like This Dude)".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, Cannon formed his own record label, Can-I-Ball Records, with plans to release his second studio album, entitled <i>Stages</i>, later that year.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The album's first single, "Can I Live?", an <a href="/wiki/Anti-abortion" class="mw-redirect" title="Anti-abortion">anti-abortion</a> (pro-life) song, was released in July 2005<sup id="cite_ref-Nick_Cannon_Discography_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nick_Cannon_Discography-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> followed by the second single "Dime Piece" in March 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-Nick_Cannon_Discography_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nick_Cannon_Discography-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> It was never released because of Nick Cannon's acting career.
</p>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932012:_N.27Credible_Entertainment.2C_Slick_Nick_and_Child_of_the_Corn"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2012:_N'Credible_Entertainment,_Slick_Nick_and_Child_of_the_Corn">2009–2012: N'Credible Entertainment, Slick Nick and <i>Child of the Corn</i></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Nick Cannon formed a new label after cancelling his old label Can-I-Ball Records entitled N'Credible Entertainment. In 2010, Cannon created a parody of hip hop pioneer <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a> entitled "Slick Nick". He released two songs by the character such as "I'm a Slick Rick", a freestyle of <a href="/wiki/Cali_Swag_District" title="Cali Swag District">Cali Swag District</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Teach_Me_How_to_Dougie" title="Teach Me How to Dougie">Teach Me How to Dougie</a>" in which he attempted to diss hip hop mega-star <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, and "Nick's Story", a freestyle rap version of <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Children%27s_Story" title="Children&#39;s Story">Children's Story</a>". On December 6, 2011, Nick Cannon released his debut mixtape entitled <i>Child of the Corn</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hosting">Hosting</span></h3>
<p>In 2005, Cannon created, produced, and hosted the <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> <a href="/wiki/Improvisation" title="Improvisation">improv</a> comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_%27N_Out" class="mw-redirect" title="Wild &#39;N Out">Wild 'N Out</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> That same year he hosted the so-called slime stunt on <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a>'s <a href="/wiki/2005_Kids%27_Choice_Awards" title="2005 Kids&#39; Choice Awards">2005 Kids' Choice Awards</a> in which a human cannonball was shot into slime. In addition, he was the DJ on <a href="/wiki/Ellen_DeGeneres" title="Ellen DeGeneres">Ellen DeGeneres</a>'s <i>Bigger, Longer, And Wider</i> show. On December 11, 2009, Cannon hosted the <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon_HALO_Awards" title="Nickelodeon HALO Awards">Nickelodeon HALO Awards</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hayden_Panettiere" title="Hayden Panettiere">Hayden Panettiere</a>, <a href="/wiki/LeBron_James" title="LeBron James">LeBron James</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Rowland" title="Kelly Rowland">Kelly Rowland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a>.
</p><p>In 2010, Cannon created and co-hosted the short-lived <a href="/wiki/TeenNick" title="TeenNick">TeenNick</a> original series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Nightlife" title="The Nightlife">The Nightlife</a>.</i>
</p><p>On July 4, 2011, Cannon hosted the <a href="/wiki/Nathan%27s_Hot_Dog_Eating_Contest" title="Nathan&#39;s Hot Dog Eating Contest">Nathan's Hot Dog Eating Contest</a>, an annual American competitive eating competition.
</p><p>On November 12, 2012, <a href="/wiki/MTV2" title="MTV2">MTV2</a> announced that the revival of <i>Wild 'N Out</i>. The revived show was produced by Cannon's Ncredible Entertainment, the series premiered in 2013 with a few returning faces joined by a roster of fresh talents. When asked about the show coming back, Cannon said, "With our show's original cast, everyone from <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Hart_(actor)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kevin Hart (actor)">Kevin Hart</a> to <a href="/wiki/Katt_Williams" title="Katt Williams">Katt Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Affion_Crockett" title="Affion Crockett">Affion Crockett</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taran_Killam" title="Taran Killam">Taran Killam</a> becoming household names, I am looking forward to working with MTV2 as we work to introduce the biggest comedic stars of tomorrow with the return of Wild 'N Out."<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Rapper <a href="/wiki/Big_Boi" title="Big Boi">Big Boi</a> of <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a> has confirmed in a tweet on Twitter he was shooting an episode on January 29, 2013, in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">NYC</a> and for people to join in the audience.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Season 5 of the show premiered on July 9, 2013, to the highest ratings in MTV2 history.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> It was renewed for season 6 and 7, which aired in two blocks.
</p><p>Meanwhile on December 1, 2012, Cannon would launch and host the <i>TeenNick Top 10</i> on TeenNick as part of his executive role with the network. The program counted down ten popular videos of the week, often with Cannon's input also a part of the week's playlist. The series would air until March 17, 2018, when TeenNick phased out all original programming.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span id="America.27s_Got_Talent_and_departure"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="America's_Got_Talent_and_departure"><i>America's Got Talent</i> and departure</span></h4>
<p>From 2009 to 2016, Cannon served as the host of <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent" title="America&#39;s Got Talent">America's Got Talent</a></i>. He announced he would not be back for 2017, citing creative differences between him and the executives of NBC. The resignation came in the wake of news that the network considered firing Cannon after he made disparaging remarks about NBC in his <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> comedy special, <i>Stand Up, Don't Shoot</i>. "I love art and entertainment too much to watch it be ruined by controlling corporations and big business," Cannon said in a statement, referring to the cable giant <a href="/wiki/Comcast" title="Comcast">Comcast</a>'s ownership of NBC.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Cannon, however, was technically under contract to host <i>America's Got Talent</i> and could have been potentially sued by NBC for breach of contract, though this was considered unlikely. NBC executives did not accept his resignation and had been stated to be keeping their fingers crossed that he would instead choose to return.<sup id="cite_ref-globalgrind_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globalgrind-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Cannon's resignation was ultimately accepted as final without further incident. He was replaced in his position as host of <i>America's Got Talent</i> by <a href="/wiki/Tyra_Banks" title="Tyra Banks">Tyra Banks</a>, known for also having hosted <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Next_Top_Model" title="America&#39;s Next Top Model">America's Next Top Model</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-tyrabanks_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tyrabanks-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Racism_controversy">Racism controversy</span></h3>
<p>On July 14, 2020, Nick Cannon was fired by <a href="/wiki/ViacomCBS" class="mw-redirect" title="ViacomCBS">ViacomCBS</a> after making racist and <a href="/wiki/Anti-Semitic" class="mw-redirect" title="Anti-Semitic">anti-Semitic</a> remarks during an episode of his podcast <i>Cannon's Class</i> with <a href="/wiki/Professor_Griff" title="Professor Griff">Professor Griff</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> Cannon endorsed conspiracy theories about <a href="/wiki/Antisemitic_canard#Controlling_the_world_financial_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Antisemitic canard">Jewish control of finance</a>, claimed that <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a> had stolen the identity of "<a href="/wiki/Black_Hebrew_Israelites" title="Black Hebrew Israelites">black people as the 'true Hebrews'</a><span style="padding-left:.15em;">"</span>, and cited <a href="/wiki/Louis_Farrakhan" title="Louis Farrakhan">Louis Farrakhan</a>, who is labeled as an anti-Semite by the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Poverty_Law_Center" title="Southern Poverty Law Center">Southern Poverty Law Center</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anti-Defamation_League" title="Anti-Defamation League">Anti-Defamation League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup> Cannon also made <a href="/wiki/Black_supremacy" title="Black supremacy">black supremacist</a> statements, calling white people "savages" who were "closer to animals", claiming the "only way that they can act is evil",<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup> citing the pseudoscientific <a href="/wiki/Melanin_theory" title="Melanin theory">melanin theory</a>. A statement from ViacomCBS noted that the company's relationship with Cannon was terminated due to his promotion of "hateful speech and... anti-Semitic conspiracy theories".<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two days later, Cannon released an apology only for his remarks regarding anti-Semitism, saying: "I want to assure my Jewish friends, new and old, that this is only the beginning of my education."<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> He also demanded complete ownership of <i>Wild 'n Out</i>, and an apology from ViacomCBS for his termination.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup> When <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> became aware of his podcast, the network immediately consulted him and accepted his public apology, allowing him to remain host of <i>The Masked Singer</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup> He donated his first paycheck to the <a href="/wiki/Simon_Wiesenthal_Center" title="Simon Wiesenthal Center">Simon Wiesenthal Center</a> in light of the incident.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup> On February 5, 2021, ViacomCBS announced that <i>Wild 'N Out</i> would resume production with Cannon as host, saying that Cannon has "taken responsibility for his comments" and "worked to educate himself" through conversations with Jewish leaders.<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday_78-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
