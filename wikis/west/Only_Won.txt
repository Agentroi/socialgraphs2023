<p><b>Baldwin Chiu</b> (born May 17, 1974), known professionally as <b>Only Won</b>, is an American rapper, actor, producer and stunt performer. As a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> artist, he writes/performs <a href="/wiki/Rap" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap">rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beatboxing" title="Beatboxing">beatboxing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Singing" title="Singing">singing</a>. He is also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild" title="Screen Actors Guild">Screen Actors Guild</a> involved in <a href="/wiki/Acting" title="Acting">acting</a> and choreographing <a href="/wiki/Stunt" title="Stunt">stunt</a> work. Only Won started rapping professionally in 1991 after being influenced by hip hop pioneers <a href="/wiki/Run_DMC" class="mw-redirect" title="Run DMC">Run DMC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a>, <a href="/wiki/DcTalk" class="mw-redirect" title="DcTalk">dcTalk</a>, and <a href="/wiki/T-Bone_(rapper)" title="T-Bone (rapper)">T-Bone</a>. Because he started rapping in both English and Cantonese, some consider him to be the first <a href="/wiki/Chinese_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinese American">Chinese American</a> bilingual rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-cummings1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cummings1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> "This Chinese American rapper has a refreshing and unique sound with a message born out of experience, having been in the game since '91."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At one point, he was labeled the "Christian" version of "<a href="/wiki/Jin_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jin (rapper)">Jin the MC</a>".
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Only_Won&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among these things, Only Won is also a professionally licensed <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineering" title="Mechanical engineering">mechanical engineer</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Professional_Engineer" class="mw-redirect" title="Professional Engineer">PE</a> License deeming him the "Lyrical Engineer".<sup id="cite_ref-cummings1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cummings1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The title track of his LP (The Lyrical Engineer), provides an <a href="/wiki/Exposition_(literary_technique)" class="mw-redirect" title="Exposition (literary technique)">exposition</a> of the life and livelihood of a professional engineer. It demonstrates Only Won's technological acumen as well as his lyrical wit and dexterity.
Only Won is also a favored guest speaker at conventions, schools, and youth events challenging and encouraging the next generation to pursue their dreams and passions while maintaining an education and focus on God.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Signed in 2009 under Los Angeles record label, LOG Records. Only Won has appeared as both a host and musical guest on <a href="/wiki/JCTV" class="mw-redirect" title="JCTV">JCTV</a> and has been featured on collaboration albums. Only Won's release, "The Lyrical Engineer" is produced by Grammy nominated group <a href="/wiki/Dynamic_Twins" title="Dynamic Twins">Dynamic Twins</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Billboard winner Maximillian and <a href="/wiki/Larissa_Lam" title="Larissa Lam">Larissa Lam</a>. The music video was directed and edited by Emmy Award-winning director, Marlon Jones. Only Won and Larissa Lam launched a viral music video title "Cantonese Boy"<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> which was a parody of the Grammy winning song <a href="/wiki/American_Boy_(Estelle_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="American Boy (Estelle song)">American Boy</a> by Kanye West and Estelle. After being seen performing "Cantonese Boy" in Chinatown, Only Won was asked to perform at the 2010 Green Globe Film Awards as they honored Asians in film and entertainment.
</p><p>As an actor and member of <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild" title="Screen Actors Guild">SAG</a>, he appears in many feature films such as <a href="/wiki/The_Matrix_Reloaded" title="The Matrix Reloaded">The Matrix Reloaded</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Matrix_Revolutions" title="The Matrix Revolutions">The Matrix Revolutions</a>, <a href="/wiki/XXx:_State_of_the_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="XXx: State of the Union">xXx2: State of the Union</a>, <a href="/wiki/Twisted_(2004_film)" title="Twisted (2004 film)">Twisted</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Pursuit_of_Happyness" title="The Pursuit of Happyness">Pursuit of Happyness</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He has also provided music for the soundtrack of <a href="/wiki/Dead_Man_Down" title="Dead Man Down">Dead Man Down</a> and the animated film, "The Monkey Prince".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is also an advanced level martial artists having trained under the lineage of <a href="/wiki/Kanbun_Uechi" title="Kanbun Uechi">Kanbun Uechi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wong_Doc-Fai" title="Wong Doc-Fai">Wong Doc-Fai</a>. He is a 3 time UC Berkeley Wushu Champion in tai chi, weapons, and push hands <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and teaches Tai Chi in Hollywood. He has used some of his skills in choreographing fight scenes in movies and doing stunt work for <a href="/wiki/Ang_Lee" title="Ang Lee">Ang Lee</a> in the movie <a href="/wiki/Hulk_(film)" title="Hulk (film)">Hulk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 29, 2010, <a href="/wiki/California_State_University,_Sacramento" title="California State University, Sacramento">California State University, Sacramento</a>, honored high-achieving alumni at an annual award ceremony called the "Distinguished Service Awards". This is the highest recognized award for CSU Sacramento. Baldwin was recognized for his contributions in engineering, his performances as Only Won: The Lyrical Engineer and other entertainment industry endeavors as well as his work with youth.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He received this award alongside KXTV ABC News anchor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cristina_Mendonsa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cristina Mendonsa (page does not exist)">Cristina Mendonsa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/Pi_day" class="mw-redirect" title="Pi day">Pi day</a> 2012, <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a> asked Only Won to be part of President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>'s national <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a> campaign titled, <a href="/wiki/Stay_With_It" class="mw-redirect" title="Stay With It">Stay With It</a>, designed to boost retention of students in undergraduate engineering programs.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Only_Won&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tai Chiu Music was set up in 2008. After moving its location from Sacramento, California, TCM joined forces with LOG Records and producer <a href="/wiki/Larissa_Lam" title="Larissa Lam">Larissa Lam</a> in Los Angeles.
</p><p>In 2011, Only Won released a "Geeked out" remix video of the hit song "<a href="/wiki/Billionaire_(song)" title="Billionaire (song)">Billionaire</a>", titled "I Wanna Be An Engineer",<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> which was a parody that reached viral status<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and caught the attention of <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a>, <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Channel" title="Discovery Channel">Discovery Channel</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Pentagon" title="The Pentagon">The Pentagon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/FIRST_Robotics_Competition" title="FIRST Robotics Competition">FIRST Robotics Competition</a>. The song became the anthem to those in the engineering, technology, and science community.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> It also reached No. 2 on Reddit.com on the viral hits list, gaining over 400,000 views in less than two weeks.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The song "Crush 'em Like Godzilla" was inspired by <a href="/wiki/Ed_Wang" title="Ed Wang">Ed Wang</a>, the first NFL player of full Chinese descent.
</p><p>In summer 2012, Only Won released two songs on the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Sleeping_Dogs_(video_game)#Cast" title="Sleeping Dogs (video game)">Sleeping Dogs</a></i>.
</p><p>In Spring 2013, the song "JUN BAY" (aka: Get ready) was featured on the film <a href="/wiki/Dead_Man_Down" title="Dead Man Down">Dead Man Down</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_Maker">Film Maker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Only_Won&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Film Maker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2015, Only Won produced a Documentary short titled, <a href="/wiki/Finding_Cleveland" title="Finding Cleveland">Finding Cleveland</a> with <a href="/wiki/Larissa_Lam" title="Larissa Lam">Larissa Lam</a> as the director and music composer. It garnished multiple nominations for Best Documentary.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, Finding Cleveland won Best Documentary at the Oxford Film Festival.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The story started as a journey for him to search for his family roots, but instead of taking him to the far east, it took him into the deep south of Cleveland, MS. While the 12-minute documentary focuses on the family's discoveries about their roots, it also touches on a broader history, such as the treatment of Asian immigrants in the region and the role the Chinese Exclusion Act played in that.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 2021, his multi-ward winning film <a href="/wiki/Far_East_Deep_South" title="Far East Deep South">Far East Deep South</a> released on <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a> <a href="/wiki/America_ReFramed" title="America ReFramed">America_ReFramed</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WABE_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WABE-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In March 2008, he married American talk show host, Singer/songwriter, director and producer <a href="/wiki/Larissa_Lam" title="Larissa Lam">Larissa Lam</a>. The couple currently reside in Los Angeles, California.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
