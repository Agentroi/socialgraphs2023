<p><b>Charles Kente Williams</b> (<a href="/wiki/Birth_name" title="Birth name">né</a> <b>Bowens</b>; born October 21, 1977), better known by his stage name <b>Keak da Sneak</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. He is known for his gravelly voice,<sup id="cite_ref-Mercury2008_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mercury2008-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> for coining the term <i><a href="/wiki/Hyphy" title="Hyphy">hyphy</a></i> in 1994,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and for his contributions to the hyphy movement.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has collaborated with <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keak_da_Sneak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1097763485"><table class="box-Expand_section plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="[icon]" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" decoding="async" width="20" height="14" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="44" data-file-height="31" /></a></span></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Keak_da_Sneak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.  <span class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">April 2017</span>)</i></span></div></td></tr></tbody></table> 
<p>Keak da Sneak was born in <a href="/wiki/Brewton,_Alabama" title="Brewton, Alabama">Brewton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>. As a newborn he  moved to Oakland, CA. Keak found popularity while attending Allendale Elementary School, which he parlayed into later friendships and <a href="/wiki/Talent_show" title="Talent show">talent show</a> performances at Oakland's <a href="/wiki/Bret_Harte" title="Bret Harte">Bret Harte</a> Junior High.  Through theater Keak met his collaborator <a href="/wiki/Agerman" class="mw-redirect" title="Agerman">Agerman</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keak_da_Sneak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Keak and Agerman formed <a href="/wiki/Dual_Committee" title="Dual Committee">Dual Committee</a> when Keak was 15 years old. The duo's performances were first recorded on "Murder Man" and "Stompin in My Steel Toes" on <a href="/wiki/C-BO" class="mw-redirect" title="C-BO">C-BO</a>'s 1994 EP <i><a href="/wiki/The_Autopsy" title="The Autopsy">The Autopsy</a></i>. Citing the personal growth of all three artists, he later signed as a solo artist with <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento</a>-based Moe Doe Records.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Because of that, he began to receive more radio airplay, especially on <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> hip-hop station <a href="/wiki/KMEL" title="KMEL">KMEL</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="3X_Krazy">3X Krazy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keak_da_Sneak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 3X Krazy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/3X_Krazy" title="3X Krazy">3X Krazy</a></div>
<p>By the end of their junior year in high school, He and his collaborators had added rapper B.A. to form the group <a href="/wiki/3X_Krazy" title="3X Krazy">3X Krazy</a>. Their first EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Sick-O" title="Sick-O">Sick-O</a></i>, was released independently on August 5, 1995. In 1996 they signed to Virgin Records, releasing the album <i><a href="/wiki/Stackin%27_Chips" class="mw-redirect" title="Stackin&#39; Chips">Stackin' Chips</a></i> on March 8, 1997 (with help from the single Keep It on the Real; the album received national attention), the second album <i><a href="/wiki/Immortalized_(3X_Krazy_album)" title="Immortalized (3X Krazy album)">Immortalized</a></i>, and then the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Real_Talk_2000" title="Real Talk 2000">Real Talk 2000</a></i> on January 18, 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The last 3X Krazy album, a collection of previously unreleased material and remixed songs from <i><a href="/wiki/Sick-O" title="Sick-O">Sick-O</a></i>, was <i><a href="/wiki/Flowamatic-9" title="Flowamatic-9">flowamatic-9</a></i>, which was released in 2004. The group eventually disbanded.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keak_da_Sneak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After years hustling in the underground scene, Keak began to see mainstream success in 2004 with the song <i><a href="/wiki/That%27s_My_Word" title="That&#39;s My Word">Super Hyphy</a></i>. His 2006 collaboration with E-40, <i><a href="/wiki/Tell_Me_When_to_Go" title="Tell Me When to Go">Tell Me When to Go</a></i>, received national attention.<sup id="cite_ref-Mercury2008_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mercury2008-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In total, Keak da Sneak has released almost 20 albums and several mixtapes.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keak_da_Sneak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Keak was featured on MTV's <i><a href="/wiki/My_Super_Sweet_16" title="My Super Sweet 16">My Super Sweet 16</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keak_da_Sneak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Keak da Sneak has been married to his wife Dee Bowens, a former clothing designer, since February 14, 2004. He has four children.
</p><p>After surviving a <a href="/wiki/Gun_violence" title="Gun violence">shooting</a>, Keak began carrying a <a href="/wiki/Firearm" title="Firearm">firearm</a> for protection, which led to a 2017 <a href="/wiki/Criminal_charge" title="Criminal charge">charge</a> for firearm possession as a convicted felon. During the trial, Keak was targeted in another shooting; he was shot eight times, fell into a three-day coma, and was left paralyzed.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He now uses a wheelchair to get around.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After a two-year legal battle, Keak was sentenced to 16 months in <a href="/wiki/California_Department_of_Corrections_and_Rehabilitation" title="California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation">state custody</a>. He served 5 months of his sentence at <a href="/wiki/California_Health_Care_Facility" title="California Health Care Facility">California Health Care Facility</a> before being released early due to <a href="/wiki/2016_California_Proposition_57" title="2016 California Proposition 57">Proposition 57</a>. Upon release, he returned home to his wife and children.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
