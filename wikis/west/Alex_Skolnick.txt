<p><b>Alex Skolnick</b> (born September 29, 1968) is an American musician. He is best known as the lead guitarist and one of the songwriters of the <a href="/wiki/Thrash_metal" title="Thrash metal">thrash metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Testament_(band)" title="Testament (band)">Testament</a> and has played with several other bands, including The Alex Skolnick Trio, <a href="/wiki/Trans-Siberian_Orchestra" title="Trans-Siberian Orchestra">Trans-Siberian Orchestra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savatage" title="Savatage">Savatage</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> supergroup <a href="/wiki/Metal_Allegiance" title="Metal Allegiance">Metal Allegiance</a>.
</p><p>Skolnick began his musical career at the age of 15 when he joined the local <a href="/wiki/Bay_Area_thrash_metal" title="Bay Area thrash metal">Bay Area thrash metal</a> band Legacy, which eventually became Testament, and performed on five albums with them, between <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legacy_(album)" title="The Legacy (album)">The Legacy</a></i> (1987) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ritual_(Testament_album)" title="The Ritual (Testament album)">The Ritual</a></i> (1992). By the time he left Testament in 1992, Skolnick had found considerable fame among the thrash metal and guitar communities; <i><a href="/wiki/Guitar_World" title="Guitar World">Guitar World</a></i> magazine named him one of the greatest guitarists of all time,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and as one of the "fastest guitarists of all time".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After his first departure from Testament, Skolnick went on to pursue various musical careers, including briefly replacing <a href="/wiki/Criss_Oliva" title="Criss Oliva">Criss Oliva</a> in Savatage for their album <i><a href="/wiki/Handful_of_Rain" title="Handful of Rain">Handful of Rain</a></i> (1994), and starting his <a href="/wiki/Jazz_fusion" title="Jazz fusion">jazz</a> band The Alex Skolnick Trio in the early 2000s, which remains active as of 2021. He briefly reunited with Testament in 2001 for the re-recordings of their early songs on the album <i><a href="/wiki/First_Strike_Still_Deadly" title="First Strike Still Deadly">First Strike Still Deadly</a></i>, and then returned to the band on a permanent basis in 2005, contributing to four more albums.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Skolnick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Skolnick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Skolnick was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Berkeley,_California" title="Berkeley, California">Berkeley, California</a>, in a non-traditional Jewish family.<sup id="cite_ref-went_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-went-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His parents, <a href="/wiki/Jerome_Herbert_Skolnick" class="mw-redirect" title="Jerome Herbert Skolnick">Jerome Skolnick</a> and Arlene, both <a href="/wiki/PhD" class="mw-redirect" title="PhD">PhD</a> graduates from <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a>, taught sociology at <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">University of California, Berkeley</a>.
</p><p>Skolnick began playing guitar at age nine. He was influenced by <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Van_Halen" title="Eddie Van Halen">Eddie Van Halen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Beck" title="Jeff Beck">Jeff Beck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimi_Hendrix" title="Jimi Hendrix">Jimi Hendrix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Clapton" title="Eric Clapton">Eric Clapton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Winter" title="Johnny Winter">Johnny Winter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Randy_Rhoads" title="Randy Rhoads">Randy Rhoads</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schenker" title="Michael Schenker">Michael Schenker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yngwie_Malmsteen" title="Yngwie Malmsteen">Yngwie Malmsteen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)">Kiss</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Scorpions_(band)" title="Scorpions (band)">Scorpions</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Metal_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metal-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He was a student of guitarist <a href="/wiki/Joe_Satriani" title="Joe Satriani">Joe Satriani</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bosso_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bosso-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_in_music">Career in music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Skolnick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career in music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Testament">Testament</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Skolnick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Testament">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Skolnick joined the <a href="/wiki/Bay_Area_thrash_metal" title="Bay Area thrash metal">San Francisco Bay Area thrash metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Testament_(band)" title="Testament (band)">Testament</a>, then-called Legacy, in 1984 as the replacement of original guitarist Derrick Ramirez. Skolnick was just eighteen years old when he recorded his debut album with Testament, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legacy_(album)" title="The Legacy (album)">The Legacy</a></i> (1987), which received critical acclaim from both music critics and fans alike. The band's next four albums, <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Order_(album)" title="The New Order (album)">The New Order</a></i> (1988), <i><a href="/wiki/Practice_What_You_Preach" title="Practice What You Preach">Practice What You Preach</a></i> (1989), <i><a href="/wiki/Souls_of_Black" title="Souls of Black">Souls of Black</a></i> (1990) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ritual_(Testament_album)" title="The Ritual (Testament album)">The Ritual</a></i> (1992), cemented Testament's reputation as one of the premier bands of the thrash metal genre and nearly gave them the same level of popularity as the "Big Four" (<a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Megadeth" title="Megadeth">Megadeth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slayer" title="Slayer">Slayer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anthrax_(American_band)" title="Anthrax (American band)">Anthrax</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard.com_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In late 1992, several months after the release of <i>The Ritual</i>, Skolnick opted to leave the band, citing his weariness of playing thrash metal music and his desire to explore musically outside of Testament as the reasons behind his departure.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Skolnick returned briefly to Testament by re-recording old material for their ninth album <i><a href="/wiki/First_Strike_Still_Deadly" title="First Strike Still Deadly">First Strike Still Deadly</a></i>, as well as the Thrash of the Titans performance in 2001. He officially rejoined the band in 2005 for a short European tour with some American dates appended. A CD/DVD release was put together from <a href="/wiki/Live_in_London_(Testament_album)" title="Live in London (Testament album)">the London show</a> of May 8, 2005. On April 29, 2008, Testament released <i><a href="/wiki/The_Formation_of_Damnation" title="The Formation of Damnation">The Formation of Damnation</a></i>, its first studio album with original material in nine years and with Skolnick in sixteen years. Their next album, <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Roots_of_Earth" title="Dark Roots of Earth">Dark Roots of Earth</a></i> (2012), debuted at No. 12 on the <i>Billboard</i> Top 200 Albums chart, at No.1 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hard Rock and Rock albums chart, and No. 9 on the World Chart.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard.com_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Skolnick has since recorded two more albums with Testament, <i><a href="/wiki/Brotherhood_of_the_Snake" title="Brotherhood of the Snake">Brotherhood of the Snake</a></i> (2016) and <i><a href="/wiki/Titans_of_Creation" title="Titans of Creation">Titans of Creation</a></i> (2020).
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_outside_of_Testament">Work outside of Testament</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Skolnick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Work outside of Testament">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After his first departure from Testament, Skolnick joined the band <a href="/wiki/Savatage" title="Savatage">Savatage</a> in 1994 for the recording of <i><a href="/wiki/Handful_of_Rain" title="Handful of Rain">Handful of Rain</a></i>, as well as its follow-up live album and home video release <i><a href="/wiki/Japan_Live_%2794" title="Japan Live &#39;94">Japan Live '94</a></i>. In 1991 he recorded and toured with the <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Hamm" title="Stuart Hamm">Stuart Hamm</a> band. He also played guitar for <a href="/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne" title="Ozzy Osbourne">Ozzy Osbourne</a> in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-ozzy_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ozzy-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 2004, he made a guest appearance on <a href="/wiki/Lamb_of_God_(band)" title="Lamb of God (band)">Lamb of God</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Ashes_of_the_Wake" title="Ashes of the Wake">Ashes of the Wake</a></i> album, recording a solo for the album's instrumental title track.
</p><p>During the mid to late 1990s, Skolnick led several projects in the San Francisco Bay Area, including Skol-Patrol, a funk band featuring <a href="/wiki/Michael_Manring" title="Michael Manring">Michael Manring</a> that played themes from police shows. He recorded two albums with Attention Deficit, a trio with Manring and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Alexander" title="Tim Alexander">Tim Alexander</a> of <a href="/wiki/Primus_(band)" title="Primus (band)">Primus</a>. In the late 90s, he collaborated with electric violinist Joe Deninzon (who he met in New York City at the New School) in the band <a href="/wiki/Stratospheerius" title="Stratospheerius">Stratospheerius</a>, recording <i>The Adventures of Stratospheerius</i> album. He also appears on their <i>Live Wires</i> album.<sup id="cite_ref-Romero_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Romero-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1998, Skolnick moved to New York City and began devoting all of his energy to <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, enrolling in <a href="/wiki/The_New_School" title="The New School">The New School</a> for Jazz &amp; Contemporary Music and graduating with a degree in jazz performance. Since 2002, he has toured and recorded as the Alex Skolnick Trio, a jazz group known for rousing <a href="/wiki/Bebop" title="Bebop">bebop</a> renditions of <a href="/wiki/Classic_rock" title="Classic rock">classic rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> tunes. The trio has performed at the <a href="/wiki/Rochester_International_Jazz_Festival" title="Rochester International Jazz Festival">Rochester International Jazz Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/Musikmesse_Frankfurt" title="Musikmesse Frankfurt">Musikmesse Frankfurt</a>, Germany, the National Center for the Arts, Mexico City, and the <a href="/wiki/Iridium_Jazz_Club" title="Iridium Jazz Club">Iridium Jazz Club</a> in New York City. Skolnick's trio has been praised by <i>Down Beat</i>, <i>Jazziz</i>, and <i>The Village Voice</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Their album <i>Veritas</i> (<a href="/wiki/Palmetto_Records" title="Palmetto Records">Palmetto</a>) reached No. 7 on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> jazz chart.
</p><p>Skolnick released a DVD Jazz tuition set with Rock House Method in May 2010. He recorded the album <i>What's Next?</i> with pianist Randy Klein in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-second_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-second-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="World_music_coalition">World music coalition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Skolnick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: World music coalition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Alex_N_Skolnick.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Alex_N_Skolnick.jpg/170px-Alex_N_Skolnick.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="213" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Alex_N_Skolnick.jpg/255px-Alex_N_Skolnick.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Alex_N_Skolnick.jpg/340px-Alex_N_Skolnick.jpg 2x" data-file-width="420" data-file-height="527" /></a><figcaption>Skolnick in 2011</figcaption></figure>
<p>On June 21, 2012, Summer Solstice Day, Skolnick announced his <a href="/wiki/World_music" title="World music">world music</a> album <i>Planetary Coalition</i> at the annual Make Music New York festival sponsored by <i><a href="/wiki/Guitar_World" title="Guitar World">Guitar World</a></i> magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-Mass_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mass-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He said that he intended to have, "musicians from all over the world, driven by acoustic guitar and bringing together inspirational melodies, in-depth improvisation and the passion of the musical styles of Gypsy, Middle Eastern, Indian, Latin, East Asian, Mediterranean, Balkan/Eastern European, African and other indigenous lands – Planetary Coalition has been guided by a single hope: that by weaving the threads that connect musical expressions with regional identities, we can bridge the gap between diverse cultures and people, and increase awareness of the ecological and social issues facing the planet, our island in the sky."<sup id="cite_ref-About_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Planetary Coalition</i> was released by <a href="/wiki/ArtistShare" title="ArtistShare">ArtistShare</a> on November 11, 2014 to commemorate the centennial of the end of the First World War. The album gathered over twenty musicians from five continents, including Indo-Canadian <a href="/wiki/Ghazal" title="Ghazal">ghazal</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Kiran_Ahluwalia" title="Kiran Ahluwalia">Kiran Ahluwalia</a>, Argentine bassist Pablo Aslan, Chinese <a href="/wiki/Pipa" title="Pipa">pipa</a> player <a href="/wiki/Yihan_Chen" class="mw-redirect" title="Yihan Chen">Yihan Chen</a>, American violinist Rachel Golub, Cuban drummer <a href="/wiki/Horacio_Hern%C3%A1ndez" class="mw-redirect" title="Horacio Hernández">Horacio Hernández</a>, Albanian accordionist Raif Hyseni, Palestinian <a href="/wiki/Oud" title="Oud">oud</a> player <a href="/wiki/Le_Trio_Joubran" title="Le Trio Joubran">Adnan Joubran</a>, Turkish <a href="/wiki/Qanun_(instrument)" title="Qanun (instrument)">kanun</a> player Tamer Pinarbasi, Mexican guitar duo <a href="/wiki/Rodrigo_y_Gabriela" title="Rodrigo y Gabriela">Rodrigo y Gabriela</a>, Israeli percussionist Gadi Seri, and Yacouba Sissoko, a <a href="/wiki/Kora_(instrument)" title="Kora (instrument)">kora</a> player from Mali.
</p><p>The project endeavored to combine artistic, ecological, political, and social issues, as well as showcase the landscape and architecture of other countries. Maddy Samaddar Johnson, an architect and urban planner, was hired as a consultant.<sup id="cite_ref-Maddy_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maddy-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Profile_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Profile-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Skolnick used a variety of guitars, including steel string, nylon string, acoustic, and electric. All compositions were by Skolnick, except two which were composed by Skolnick-Joubran and Skolnick-Hernandez. The tune from Mali and from China were first-time guitar-kora and guitar-pipa duos of traditional songs from those regions.
</p><p>The album was praised by many publications, newspapers, and blogs.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Harris_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harris-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Talks_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Talks-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Global_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Global-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> <i>Guitar World</i> magazine chose <i>Planetary Coalition</i> as one among the top 50 albums of 2014<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> and among the top 10 acoustic guitar albums of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_in_art_and_literature">Career in art and literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Skolnick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Career in art and literature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Skolnick's memoir, <i>Geek to Guitar Hero</i>, was published in January 2013<sup id="cite_ref-Geek_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Geek-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and received positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Bozza" title="Anthony Bozza">Anthony Bozza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Morrow" title="Bradford Morrow">Bradford Morrow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ned_Vizzini" title="Ned Vizzini">Ned Vizzini</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brad_Tolinski" title="Brad Tolinski">Brad Tolinski</a>, editor in chief of <i>Guitar World</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-went_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-went-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has been active in blending music with works of art and literature. In 2007, he contributed a solo for Egyptian artist <a href="/wiki/Nader_Sadek" title="Nader Sadek">Nader Sadek</a>'s project, "Faceless". The song was only to be heard in an art gallery in New York. In 2012, he performed live at the Feature Inc Gallery in New York, for a musical interpretation project for the works of visual artist Douglas Melini.<sup id="cite_ref-Melini_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Melini-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equipment">Equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Skolnick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Skolnick plays electric <a href="/wiki/ESP_Guitars" title="ESP Guitars">ESP Guitars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Godin_(guitar_manufacturer)" title="Godin (guitar manufacturer)">Godin</a> hollow-bodied models with <a href="/wiki/Seymour_Duncan" title="Seymour Duncan">Seymour Duncan</a> pickups. He formerly played <a href="/wiki/Heritage_Guitars" title="Heritage Guitars">Heritage</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ibanez" title="Ibanez">Ibanez</a> guitars.
</p><p>In 2009 Heritage released Alex's first signature guitar. His signature is a solid body based upon the H 150/157 model. In his early years with Testament he endorsed Ibanez guitars and mainly played the short lived 540P second generation Power series models, and later the 540SLTD Saber model. Throughout his days with Savatage he used the RV470 Radius model and an LA Custom Shop prototype based on the Ghostrider series. Alex endorsed Charvel guitars and was featured in their ads around 1995. For amplification, he uses <a href="/wiki/Budda_Amplification" title="Budda Amplification">Budda amps</a> and several Marshall amps including the discontinued Marshall Mode Four hybrid amps.
</p><p>In 2013, Budda released a signature amp for Skolnick, called the Preceptor,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> and later in the same year it was announced that Skolnick was signed to ESP guitars, with an ESP and LTD model in the works for 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
