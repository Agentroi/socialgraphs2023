<p><b>Elijah Blue Molina</b> (born October 15, 1987), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Scoop DeVille</b>, is an American record producer, rapper and DJ. DeVille has produced records for rappers such as <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>. He produced Kendrick Lamar's "<a href="/wiki/Poetic_Justice_(song)" title="Poetic Justice (song)">Poetic Justice</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>, as well as Snoop Dogg's "<a href="/wiki/I_Wanna_Rock_(Snoop_Dogg_song)" title="I Wanna Rock (Snoop Dogg song)">I Wanna Rock</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, both of which charted in the top 50 of the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scoop_DeVille&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Molina was born in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, and is the son of <a href="/wiki/Latino_hip-hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino hip-hop">Latino hip-hop</a> pioneer <a href="/wiki/Frost_(rapper)" title="Frost (rapper)">Kid Frost</a>. At age three, he was seen being carried in his father's arms in the video for Kid Frost's 1990 single “<a href="/wiki/La_Raza_(song)" title="La Raza (song)">La Raza</a>”. Raised in a musical family, Molina started rapping at the age of two, writing music at the age of nine, and produced his first hit single, "Mamacita" by <a href="/wiki/Baby_Bash" title="Baby Bash">Baby Bash</a>, at fifteen.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scoop_DeVille&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>DeVille has produced songs such as "The Recipe" by <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> 'Poetic Justice' Ft. <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> 'I Wanna Rock' ft. <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, "Wait Until Tonight" by <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, "(Haha) Slow Down" by <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a> ft. <a href="/wiki/Young_Jeezy" class="mw-redirect" title="Young Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a>, "Calm Down" by <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a> ft. <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, "Nate" by <a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples" title="Vince Staples">Vince Staples</a> ft. <a href="/wiki/James_Fauntleroy" title="James Fauntleroy">James Fauntleroy</a>, and "Trouble On Central" by <a href="/wiki/Buddy_(rapper)" title="Buddy (rapper)">Buddy</a>.
</p><p>DeVille was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Grammy" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy">Grammy</a> in the categories of <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Best Rap Album</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Album_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Album of the Year">Album of the Year</a> for his <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">production</a> on Kendrick Lamar's major-label debut <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Kid,_M.A.A.D_City" title="Good Kid, M.A.A.D City">Good Kid, M.A.A.D City</a></i>.
</p>
