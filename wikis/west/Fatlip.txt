<p><b>Derrick Stewart</b>, known professionally as <b>Fatlip</b>, is a Los Angeles-born <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> musician. He started his career in the hip hop group <a href="/wiki/The_Pharcyde" title="The Pharcyde">The Pharcyde</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fatlip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Pharcyde">The Pharcyde</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fatlip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Pharcyde">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a member of The Pharcyde, Fatlip contributed to the critically acclaimed <i><a href="/wiki/Bizarre_Ride_II_the_Pharcyde" title="Bizarre Ride II the Pharcyde">Bizarre Ride II the Pharcyde</a></i> as well as the follow-up release <i><a href="/wiki/Labcabincalifornia" title="Labcabincalifornia">Labcabincalifornia</a></i>. After the release of the <i>Labcabincalifornia</i> album, Fatlip was kicked out of the group, in part for leaving the supporting tour for their second album in favor of working on his own solo efforts.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the group, Fatlip could be recognized for his conversational-style delivery of his verses and for having the deepest voice in the group.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fatlip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2005, Fatlip released his solo debut, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Loneliest_Punk" title="The Loneliest Punk">The Loneliest Punk</a></i>. It stood as his only solo album until the release of <i>Torpor</i> 17 years later. The album includes his single from 2000, "What's Up Fatlip?" (which was included in the soundtrack for <i><a href="/wiki/Tony_Hawk%27s_American_Wasteland" title="Tony Hawk&#39;s American Wasteland">Tony Hawk's American Wasteland</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Youth_in_Revolt_(film)" title="Youth in Revolt (film)">Youth In Revolt</a></i> soundtrack). The music video for "What's Up Fatlip?" was directed by <a href="/wiki/Spike_Jonze" title="Spike Jonze">Spike Jonze</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During the filming process, Jonze compiled interviews with Fatlip and put them together in a documentary also titled "What's Up, Fatlip?".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The album features guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Chali_2na" title="Chali 2na">Chali 2na</a> (on the song "Today's Your Day", which is included in the skateboarding film <i><a href="/wiki/Yeah_Right!" title="Yeah Right!">Yeah Right!</a>)</i>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Shock_G" title="Shock G">Shock G</a> (on the song "Freaky Pumps"). The album contains songs produced by <a href="/wiki/J-Swift" title="J-Swift">J-Swift</a>, who was responsible for the production of The Pharcyde's first album <i>Bizarre Ride II the Pharcyde</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Fatlip appeared on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Jackass_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jackass (TV series)">Jackass</a></i> sliding down an <a href="/wiki/Escalator" title="Escalator">escalator</a> rail, and this was edited into his video for "Worst Case Scenario".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaborations">Collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fatlip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In 2001, Fatlip was featured on <a href="/wiki/L.A._Symphony" class="mw-redirect" title="L.A. Symphony">L.A. Symphony</a>'s <i>Big Broke L.A.</i> EP on the track "What You Say".
</p><p>Fatlip had a <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo</a> in <a href="/wiki/Southern_rap" class="mw-redirect" title="Southern rap">Southern rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>'s "Get Back" video.
</p><p>In 2007, he collaborated with <a href="/wiki/The_Chemical_Brothers" title="The Chemical Brothers">The Chemical Brothers</a> on their song "<a href="/wiki/The_Salmon_Dance" title="The Salmon Dance">The Salmon Dance</a>", which is featured on their album <i><a href="/wiki/We_Are_the_Night_(album)" title="We Are the Night (album)">We Are the Night</a></i>.
</p><p>Fatlip is featured on the track "Dream and Imaginate" by The Wascals.
</p><p>Fatlip has collaborated with a crew from <a href="/wiki/French_hip_hop" title="French hip hop">France</a> known as the Jazz Liberatorz. In 2008, he appeared on their album <i>Clin D'Oeil</i>, on two tracks, "Ease My Mind" and "Genius At Work", the latter of which also featured <a href="/wiki/Tre_Hardson" class="mw-redirect" title="Tre Hardson">Tre Hardson</a>, Omni, and T-Love. He also appeared twice on the subsequent Jazz Liberatorz album, <i>Fruit of the Past</i>, on "My Style Is Fly" and "Back Packers".
</p><p>Fatlip is featured on the song "Luxury Pool" from the 2008 album <i><a href="/wiki/Stainless_Style" title="Stainless Style">Stainless Style</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Neon_Neon" title="Neon Neon">Neon Neon</a>.
</p><p>In early 2009, Fatlip also appeared on two tracks on <i>The Spirit of Apollo</i>, the debut album by N.A.S.A., which was a collaboration project between <a href="/wiki/Sam_Spiegel_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sam Spiegel (musician)">Sam Spiegel</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Zegon" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Zegon">DJ Zegon</a>. The two songs were "Hip Hop" (also featuring <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a> and Tre Hardson) and "Strange Enough" (also featuring <a href="/wiki/Karen_O" title="Karen O">Karen O</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ol%27_Dirty_Bastard" title="Ol&#39; Dirty Bastard">Ol' Dirty Bastard</a>.) Other artists who appeared on the album included <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, Chali 2na, and <a href="/wiki/David_Byrne" title="David Byrne">David Byrne</a>, among others.
</p><p>2016 saw Fatlip collaborate with <a href="/wiki/Edo_G" class="mw-redirect" title="Edo G">Edo G</a> on a song called "Playtimee Over", produced by Tone Spliff. Then, in 2017, Fatlip was featured on the track "Mr. Lonely" by Portland-based band <a href="/wiki/Portugal._The_Man" title="Portugal. The Man">Portugal. The Man</a>. The song was included on the band's album <i><a href="/wiki/Woodstock_(Portugal._The_Man_album)" title="Woodstock (Portugal. The Man album)">Woodstock</a></i>, and Fatlip received a writing credit for it.
</p><p>In 2019, Fatlip appeared on "Aw Hell You Motherfuckin' Right", a song from <i>A Joy Which Nothing Can Erase</i>, the third full-length album by <a href="/wiki/Canadian_hip_hop" title="Canadian hip hop">Toronto</a> rapper The Mighty Rhino. The song also featured Ultra Magnus, and was produced by Myer Clarity, with <a href="/wiki/Scratching" title="Scratching">scratches</a> by DJ Digital Junkie.
</p><p>In 2020, Fatlip released a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Blu_(rapper)" title="Blu (rapper)">Blu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samuel_T._Herring" title="Samuel T. Herring">Samuel T. Herring</a>, the latter credited as Hemlock Ernst. The song was called "Good For The Soul". That year also saw Fatlip collaborate with <a href="/wiki/Percee_P" title="Percee P">Percee P</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phil_Da_Agony" class="mw-redirect" title="Phil Da Agony">Phil Da Agony</a> on the song "For You".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reunion_with_Tre_Hardson">Reunion with Tre Hardson</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fatlip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reunion with Tre Hardson">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fatlip has reunited with former Pharcyde member Tre Hardson to perform on The Over 30, Dirty Old Men Tour together.<sup id="cite_ref-Fatlipandslimkid3_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fatlipandslimkid3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fatlip was featured in the song "All I Want for Christmas (Is Somebody Else)" with Tre Hardson featuring St. Imey (formerly of The Wascals) and produced by <a href="/wiki/J-Swift" title="J-Swift">J-Swift</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Torpor:_Pharcyde_reunion"><i>Torpor</i>: Pharcyde reunion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fatlip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Torpor: Pharcyde reunion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 18, 2022, Fatlip released the lead single, “Dust in the Wind”, off of his new solo album, “Torpor”, produced by Los Angeles-based production team Sccit &amp; Siavash The Grouch. The single, featuring <a href="/wiki/Krayzie_Bone" title="Krayzie Bone">Krayzie Bone</a>, marks the first time a member of The Pharcyde has collaborated with a member of <a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs-N-Harmony" class="mw-redirect" title="Bone Thugs-N-Harmony">Bone Thugs-N-Harmony</a>. The second single, released on April 20, 2022, is a collaborative effort with <a href="/wiki/RBX" title="RBX">RBX</a> of <a href="/wiki/Death_Row_Records" title="Death Row Records">Death Row Records</a> &amp; M.O titled “Wake Up” and includes a music video themed around the Los Angeles homeless epidemic. The album was officially released exclusively on CD on January 29, 2022. The next single, titled “My Bad”, was released on August 1, 2022, as the first complete Pharcyde song in over 25 years (including all 4 original members). Surrounded by controversy, the reunion song made big headlines when Rolling Stone wrote a piece detailing the on-going inner conflict within the group. The album was finally released digitally on August 23, 2022. The 4th Single, “Energy”, was released September 9, 2022, and features Sccit on the 2nd verse, as well as RBX narrating. An eclectic video for the song was released that same day. “Torpor” also includes <a href="/wiki/Butch_Cassidy_(singer)" title="Butch Cassidy (singer)">Butch Cassidy (singer)</a>, as well as production by Jellyroll, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Taylor_(music_producer)" title="Chris Taylor (music producer)">Chris Taylor (music producer)</a> AKA The Glove and more.<sup id="cite_ref-DUBCNN_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DUBCNN-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ILLUMINATI2G_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ILLUMINATI2G-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
