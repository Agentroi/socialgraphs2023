<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1066479718"></td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Brian Todd Collins</b> (born April 1, 1986),<sup id="cite_ref-auto_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Kid Ink</b>, is an American rapper, singer-songwriter and record producer.<sup id="cite_ref-mtvuk_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtvuk-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He released his debut independent album <i><a href="/wiki/Up_%26_Away_(Kid_Ink_album)" title="Up &amp; Away (Kid Ink album)">Up &amp; Away</a></i> in 2012, following an EP titled <i><a href="/wiki/Almost_Home_(Kid_Ink_EP)" title="Almost Home (Kid Ink EP)">Almost Home</a></i>, and his first major studio album <i><a href="/wiki/My_Own_Lane" title="My Own Lane">My Own Lane</a></i> in 2014. The latter spawned the singles "<a href="/wiki/Show_Me_(Kid_Ink_song)" title="Show Me (Kid Ink song)">Show Me</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>), "<a href="/wiki/Iz_U_Down" title="Iz U Down">Iz U Down</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a>), and "<a href="/wiki/Main_Chick" title="Main Chick">Main Chick</a>" (featuring Chris Brown). On February 3, 2015, he released his third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Full_Speed_(album)" title="Full Speed (album)">Full Speed</a></i>, which spawned the singles "<a href="/wiki/Body_Language_(Kid_Ink_song)" title="Body Language (Kid Ink song)">Body Language</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Usher_(musician)" title="Usher (musician)">Usher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tinashe" title="Tinashe">Tinashe</a>), "<a href="/wiki/Hotel_(Kid_Ink_song)" title="Hotel (Kid Ink song)">Hotel</a>" (featuring Chris Brown), and "<a href="/wiki/Be_Real_(Kid_Ink_song)" title="Be Real (Kid Ink song)">Be Real</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Dej_Loaf" title="Dej Loaf">Dej Loaf</a>).
</p><p>In December 2015, Collins released a surprise commercial mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Summer_in_the_Winter" title="Summer in the Winter">Summer in the Winter</a></i> with the supporting single "<a href="/wiki/Promise_(Kid_Ink_song)" title="Promise (Kid Ink song)">Promise</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Fetty_Wap" title="Fetty Wap">Fetty Wap</a>). In 2016, he released the mixtape <i>RSS2</i> and the EP <i><a href="/wiki/7_Series_(EP)" title="7 Series (EP)">7 Series</a></i> with the lead single "<a href="/wiki/F_with_U" title="F with U">F with U</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>) in 2017. In December 2018, he released the EP <i>Missed Calls</i> supported by the single "YUSO" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saweetie" title="Saweetie">Saweetie</a>). In 2021, he released his first independent album in nine years, <i>Alive</i>, on April 9.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kid_Ink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2005.E2.80.932012:_Career_beginnings.2C_Tha_Alumni_Music_Group.2C_and_Up_.26_Away"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2005–2012:_Career_beginnings,_Tha_Alumni_Music_Group,_and_Up_&amp;_Away">2005–2012: Career beginnings, Tha Alumni Music Group, and <i>Up &amp; Away</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kid_Ink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2005–2012: Career beginnings, Tha Alumni Music Group, and Up &amp; Away">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During his teenage years, Collins attended after-school music programs, where he produced hip-hop beats and tracks. He cites <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a> as part of his inspiration for its work ethic with the production and songwriting, when he said: "These were artists and producers who were writing for other people in all types of genres. It just gave me an open ear for all different types of music and that's how I approached it". He landed his first genuine production credit for a <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a> single in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> At 22, Collins began <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapping</a> full-time, soon after<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> adopting the temporary <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> Rockstar. On February 1, 2010, Collins released his first official mixtape, titled <i>World Tour</i>. The mixtape gained popularity that grabbed some of the attention towards a radio <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a>, DJ Ill Will, the founder of <i><a href="/wiki/HotNewHipHop" title="HotNewHipHop">HotNewHipHop</a></i>. He later signed to his record label, Tha Alumni Group, and its joint label, 88 Classic. Collins also changed his stage name to Kid Ink, in respect for a label-mate, DJ Rockstar.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, Collins released his second mixtape, called <i>Crash Landing</i>. The mixtape features <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from <a href="/wiki/Roscoe_Dash" title="Roscoe Dash">Roscoe Dash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>, among others. During the summer of 2011, Collins released his third mixtape, called <i>Daydreamer</i> that made available for digital download. The mixtape features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Cory_Gunz" title="Cory Gunz">Cory Gunz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maejor" title="Maejor">Maejor Ali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ray_J" title="Ray J">Ray J</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Kingston" title="Sean Kingston">Sean Kingston</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bow_Wow_(rapper)" title="Bow Wow (rapper)">Bow Wow</a>, among others. In September 2011, Collins released his fourth mixtape, called <i>Wheels Up</i>. The mixtape features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2012, Collins was revealed to be a part of the <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i>'s yearly Freshman class alongside fellow emerging artists at the time, such as <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iggy_Azalea" title="Iggy Azalea">Iggy Azalea</a>, and <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, among others. On June 12, 2012, Collins released his debut <a href="/wiki/Album#Studio" title="Album">studio album</a>, called <i><a href="/wiki/Up_%26_Away_(Kid_Ink_album)" title="Up &amp; Away (Kid Ink album)">Up &amp; Away</a></i>; including the <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">production</a> that was provided from <a href="/wiki/Ned_Cameron" title="Ned Cameron">Ned Cameron</a>, Capsvl, and <a href="/wiki/Jahlil_Beats" title="Jahlil Beats">Jahlil Beats</a>, among others. The album debuted at number 20 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. Supporting this album was the release of his single, called "Time of Your Life", followed by a release of his second single, called "Lost in the Sauce".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In November of that year, Collins released his fifth mixtape, <i>Rocketshipshawty</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932014:_Signing_with_RCA.2C_Almost_Home.2C_and_My_Own_Lane"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2014:_Signing_with_RCA,_Almost_Home,_and_My_Own_Lane">2013–2014: Signing with RCA, <i>Almost Home</i>, and <i>My Own Lane</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kid_Ink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2013–2014: Signing with RCA, Almost Home, and My Own Lane">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Almost_Home_(Kid_Ink_EP)" title="Almost Home (Kid Ink EP)">Almost Home (Kid Ink EP)</a> and <a href="/wiki/My_Own_Lane" title="My Own Lane">My Own Lane</a></div><p>
On January 4, 2013, he announced that he had signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> and premiered his first major label single, titled "<a href="/wiki/Bad_Ass_(song)" title="Bad Ass (song)">Bad Ass</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>), produced by Devin Cruise.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV_-_RCA_Deal_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV_-_RCA_Deal-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The song would be released for digital download on January 22, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The song was released to Rhythm Crossover radio in the United States on February 26, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It has since peaked at number 90 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and number 27 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> chart. He released an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/Almost_Home_(Kid_Ink_EP)" title="Almost Home (Kid Ink EP)">Almost Home</a></i> on May 28, 2013. It featured other artists including French Montana, Wale, Meek Mill, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rico_Love" title="Rico Love">Rico Love</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Upon its release, the EP debuted at number 27 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. It also produced the single "<a href="/wiki/Money_and_the_Power" title="Money and the Power">Money and the Power</a>" which was featured in <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Hard_Knocks_(2001_TV_series)" title="Hard Knocks (2001 TV series)">Hard Knocks</a></i> and in the sports video game <i><a href="/wiki/NBA_Live_14" title="NBA Live 14">NBA Live 14</a></i> as well as being one of the official theme songs of <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_31" title="WrestleMania 31">WrestleMania 31</a>. On September 17, 2013, he released the first single from his second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/My_Own_Lane" title="My Own Lane">My Own Lane</a></i>, entitled "<a href="/wiki/Show_Me_(Kid_Ink_song)" title="Show Me (Kid Ink song)">Show Me</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>), produced by <a href="/wiki/Mustard_(record_producer)" title="Mustard (record producer)">DJ Mustard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He released <i>My Own Lane</i> on January 7, 2014. On January 13, 2014, at Collins' album release party, he was awarded his first Gold plaque from the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) for his single "Show Me".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></p><figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:KidInkMarch2012.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/KidInkMarch2012.jpg/220px-KidInkMarch2012.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="142" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/KidInkMarch2012.jpg/330px-KidInkMarch2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/KidInkMarch2012.jpg/440px-KidInkMarch2012.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1266" data-file-height="815" /></a><figcaption>Collins performing in March 2012</figcaption></figure>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.932016:_Full_Speed_and_Summer_in_the_Winter"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–2016:_Full_Speed_and_Summer_in_the_Winter">2015–2016: <i>Full Speed</i> and <i>Summer in the Winter</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kid_Ink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2015–2016: Full Speed and Summer in the Winter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Full_Speed_(album)" title="Full Speed (album)">Full Speed (album)</a></div>
<p>On September 9, 2014, "<a href="/wiki/Body_Language_(Kid_Ink_song)" title="Body Language (Kid Ink song)">Body Language</a>", which features <a href="/wiki/Usher_(musician)" title="Usher (musician)">Usher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tinashe" title="Tinashe">Tinashe</a>, was released as the first single from his third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Full_Speed_(album)" title="Full Speed (album)">Full Speed</a></i>. The album was released on February 3, 2015, and has since peaked at number 14 on the US <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a></i> album chart.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On December 25, 2015, Collins released a commercial mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Summer_in_the_Winter" title="Summer in the Winter">Summer in the Winter</a></i>, with songs featuring <a href="/wiki/Fetty_Wap" title="Fetty Wap">Fetty Wap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Omarion" title="Omarion">Omarion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Starrah" title="Starrah">Starrah</a>, and <a href="/wiki/B%C3%AFa_Krieger" title="Bïa Krieger">Bïa Krieger</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.93present:_RSS2_and_7_Series"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–present:_RSS2_and_7_Series">2016–present: <i>RSS2</i> and <i>7 Series</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kid_Ink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2016–present: RSS2 and 7 Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 19, 2016, Collins released the mixtape <i>RSS2</i> on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>. Collins wanted to get back into his old mixtape sound. <i>RSS2</i> features underground artists including his younger brother, Juliann Alexander. <i>RSS2</i> spawned the "One Day" single from the project, with a music video. <i>RSS2</i> was released on Spotify and every other streaming service on March 30, 2017. On April 7, Collins released the single "<a href="/wiki/F_with_U" title="F with U">F with U</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>), with DJ Mustard producing. His second EP <i><a href="/wiki/7_Series_(EP)" title="7 Series (EP)">7 Series</a></i> was released May 5, 2017.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kid_Ink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Collins has over 100 tattoos on his face, back, arms, hands, and torso.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He got his first tattoo when he was 16 and has been "addicted" to getting tattoos since. His favorites include portraits of his mother and grandfather on his chest and a tiger on the front of his neck, which represents his Chinese birth sign. He showed off his tattoos for one of <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">PETA</a>'s "Ink Not Mink" anti-fur ads in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Collins has been in a relationship with model Asiah Azante for over a decade. The couple became engaged in June 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Azante gave birth to a girl in February 2016 and another in February 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
