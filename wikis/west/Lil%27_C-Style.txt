<p>The <b>LBC Crew</b> (<b>Long Beach City Crew</b>) were a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip-hop</a> trio from <a href="/wiki/Long_Beach,_California" title="Long Beach, California">Long Beach, California</a>. They were the first act to be executively-produced by <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>.
</p><p>During their time with Death Row, the LBC Crew recorded a previously unreleased album entitled <i>Haven't U Heard...</i> which remained unreleased until February 8, 2011 when it was released by WideAwake/Death Row. They had a hit single with the song "<a href="/wiki/Beware_of_My_Crew" title="Beware of My Crew">Beware of My Crew</a>", from the <a href="/wiki/A_Thin_Line_Between_Love_and_Hate_(soundtrack)" title="A Thin Line Between Love and Hate (soundtrack)"><i>A Thin Line Between Love and Hate</i> soundtrack album</a>, which was originally intended to be for their debut album. They played on Snoop Dogg's second album <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Doggfather" title="Tha Doggfather">Tha Doggfather</a></i>. The group disbanded following the collapse of Death Row. Since then, unreleased material from the group emerged on the underground.
</p><p>The LBC Crew remained visible figures in West-Coast hip-hop. Bad Azz released two solo albums under the Doggy Style imprint. Lil C-Style remained part of disorganized Death Row camp. And Techniec recently signed to <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Wall_Street_Records" title="The Black Wall Street Records">The Black Wall Street Records</a> then left to start his own label.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Break-up">Break-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LBC_Crew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Break-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Early on in the LBC Crew's lifespan, a small dispute broke out between Lil' C-Style and Snoop Dogg over C-Style's royalties (he claimed he wasn't being paid enough for them). The argument was never settled, resulting in C-Style damaging the original tape of LBC's debut album beyond repair, and telling Snoop he no longer wanted to be part of the group.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
