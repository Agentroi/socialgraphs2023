<p><b>Terrace Jamahl Martin</b> (born December 28, 1978) is an American musician, rapper, singer, and record producer. He is perhaps best known for producing records for several prominent artists in the music industry, including <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">the Game</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Wilson_(singer)" title="Charlie Wilson (singer)">Charlie Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raphael_Saadiq" title="Raphael Saadiq">Raphael Saadiq</a> and <a href="/wiki/YG_(rapper)" title="YG (rapper)">YG</a>, among others. Martin is a <a href="/wiki/Multi-instrumentalist" title="Multi-instrumentalist">multi-instrumentalist</a> whose music production embodies <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Classical_music" title="Classical music">classical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>. Martin released his sixth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Velvet_Portraits" title="Velvet Portraits">Velvet Portraits</a></i>, on his label, Sounds of Crenshaw Records, through <a href="/wiki/Ropeadope_Records" title="Ropeadope Records">Ropeadope Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Terrace_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Martin's father, Ernest "Curly" Martin, was a jazz drummer and <a href="/wiki/Omaha_Black_Music_Hall_of_Fame" title="Omaha Black Music Hall of Fame">Omaha Black Music Hall of Fame</a> member, and his mother is a singer. He grew up listening to a broad range of music, including <a href="/wiki/John_Coltrane" title="John Coltrane">John Coltrane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Parliament_(band)" title="Parliament (band)">Parliament</a> and began playing the piano at age six. At 13, producing his first tracks on his <a href="/wiki/Casio_CZ_synthesizers#CZ-101" title="Casio CZ synthesizers">Casio CZ-101</a> Keyboard and an <a href="/wiki/E-mu_SP-1200" title="E-mu SP-1200">E-mu SP-1200</a>, Martin was encouraged to take up the <a href="/wiki/Saxophone" title="Saxophone">saxophone</a> and learned to play it by himself before enrolling into Santa Monica High School, to sharpen his musical skills. He transferred to <a href="/wiki/Locke_High_School" title="Locke High School">Locke High School</a>, to study under Reggie Andrews, where he became first chair of the All-State Jazz Band. As a child prodigy, Martin gained the interest of talk show host <a href="/wiki/Jay_Leno" title="Jay Leno">Jay Leno</a>, who presented him with a scholarship and later purchased his first professional horn.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> After high school, Martin attended <a href="/wiki/CalArts" class="mw-redirect" title="CalArts">CalArts</a> but decided school wasn't for him, and began touring with <a href="/wiki/Puff_Daddy" class="mw-redirect" title="Puff Daddy">Puff Daddy</a> and the gospel choir <a href="/wiki/God%27s_Property" title="God&#39;s Property">God's Property</a>.
</p><p>Martin is cousins with <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Bruner,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Ronald Bruner, Jr.">Ronald Bruner, Jr.</a> and his brother <a href="/wiki/Thundercat_(musician)" title="Thundercat (musician)">Stephen "Thundercat" Bruner</a>, with whom he has collaborated since childhood.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Alongside the Bruner brothers, he was a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_Get_Down" title="West Coast Get Down">West Coast Get Down</a> jazz collective, who began playing together in high school.<sup id="cite_ref-dazed_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dazed-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Terrace_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Martin was recognized by Hollywood and music industry elites and found favor in the jazz world, where he played as a member of <a href="/wiki/Billy_Higgins" title="Billy Higgins">Billy Higgins</a>' World Stage All-Stars. Martin's big break came when he produced a Power 106 radio drop with Snoop Dogg. He scored a minor hit with <a href="/wiki/213_(group)" title="213 (group)">213</a>'s "Joysticc" and went on to become one of Snoop Dogg's sought after producers, placing credits on <i><a href="/wiki/R%26G_(Rhythm_%26_Gangsta):_The_Masterpiece" title="R&amp;G (Rhythm &amp; Gangsta): The Masterpiece">R&amp;G (Rhythm &amp; Gangsta): The Masterpiece</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Ego_Trippin" class="mw-redirect" title="Ego Trippin">Ego Trippin</a></i> albums. In 2007, he signed a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>The Demo</i>, Martin's 2010 debut album, features him as a rapper and producer. The album includes beats and guest <a href="/wiki/Verse_(popular_music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Verse (popular music)">verses</a> from artists including <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pete_Rock" title="Pete Rock">Pete Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On September 28, 2010, Terrace Martin and radio personality <a href="/w/index.php?title=Devi_Dev&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Devi Dev (page does not exist)">Devi Dev</a> released the <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP) <i>Here, My Dear</i>. The first single from the EP is "Roll Up Another One", featuring <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Overdoz" title="Overdoz">Overdoz</a>. The EP features <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Wilson_(singer)" title="Charlie Wilson (singer)">Charlie Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/U-n-i" class="mw-redirect" title="U-n-i">U-n-i</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Fauntleroy_II" class="mw-redirect" title="James Fauntleroy II">James Fauntleroy II</a> and pianist Kenneth Croutch.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The EP is inspired by <a href="/wiki/Marvin_Gaye" title="Marvin Gaye">Marvin Gaye</a>'s 1978 <a href="/wiki/Here,_My_Dear" title="Here, My Dear">album of the same name</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On December 21, 2010, Terrace Martin and Devi Dev launched their online music series #DeviTerraceTuesday. On February 22, 2011, they released their second project together, <i>The SEX EP</i>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="3ChordFold">3ChordFold</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Terrace_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 3ChordFold">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, Martin released <i>3ChordFold</i>, a fusion studio album project.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 3ChordFold was followed up with <i>3ChordFold: Remixed</i> in December 2013, and <i>3ChordFold: Pulse</i> in Spring 2014, which featured many live performances with guest appearances from Robert Glasper and Thundercat, among others. In 2015, Martin was heavily involved in the development of Kendrick Lamar's critically acclaimed album, <i><a href="/wiki/To_Pimp_a_Butterfly" title="To Pimp a Butterfly">To Pimp a Butterfly</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On April 1, 2016, Martin released his long-awaited, sixth studio album, Velvet Portraits on his Sound of Crenshaw label through Ropeadope.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> His latest release features appearances from his To Pimp A Butterfly cohorts, <a href="/wiki/Kamasi_Washington" title="Kamasi Washington">Kamasi Washington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lalah_Hathaway" title="Lalah Hathaway">Lalah Hathaway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Glasper" title="Robert Glasper">Robert Glasper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thundercat_(musician)" title="Thundercat (musician)">Thundercat</a>. Recorded in Los Angeles and Omaha, Nebraska, the highly anticipated Velvet Portraits also taps into his roots, featuring his father Curly Martin on drums and legendary soul group, <a href="/wiki/The_Emotions" title="The Emotions">The Emotions</a>. Velvet Portraits was nominated in the "Best R&amp;B Album" category of the 59th Grammy Awards.
</p><p>In addition to releasing personal projects, Martin is currently producing albums for <a href="/wiki/Herbie_Hancock" title="Herbie Hancock">Herbie Hancock</a>, YG, <a href="/wiki/SZA_(singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="SZA (singer)">SZA</a> and Fergie.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On June 25, 2020, Terrace Martin, <a href="/wiki/Kamasi_Washington" title="Kamasi Washington">Kamasi Washington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Glasper" title="Robert Glasper">Robert Glasper</a>, and <a href="/wiki/9th_Wonder" title="9th Wonder">9th Wonder</a> announced the formation of a supergroup, Dinner Party. They released a single, "Freeze Tag". Their debut album was released on July 10, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Terrace_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Martin's role models include <a href="/wiki/Miles_Davis" title="Miles Davis">Miles Davis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Parker" title="Charlie Parker">Charlie Parker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jackie_McLean" title="Jackie McLean">Jackie McLean</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Coltrane" title="John Coltrane">John Coltrane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herbie_Hancock" title="Herbie Hancock">Herbie Hancock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sonny_Stitt" title="Sonny Stitt">Sonny Stitt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grover_Washington,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Grover Washington, Jr.">Grover Washington, Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battlecat_(record_producer)" title="Battlecat (record producer)">Battlecat</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pete_Rock" title="Pete Rock">Pete Rock</a>, and 1580 K-Day. Martin has said, "I started producing hip-hop tracks because it was the music of my time, but I never lost my love for jazz."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
