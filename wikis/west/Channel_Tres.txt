<p><b>Sheldon Jerome Young</b> (born May 26, 1991), known professionally as <b>Channel Tres</b>, is an American rapper, singer and record producer from <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Tres is best known for his singles "Controller" and "Topdown".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life  and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Channel_Tres&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Young grew up in southern <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, between <a href="/wiki/Compton,_California" title="Compton, California">Compton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lynwood,_California" title="Lynwood, California">Lynwood</a>. He was raised by his great-grandparents. He spent most days at church, where he played drums for the <a href="/wiki/Choir" title="Choir">choir</a>. Channel left home to study music at <a href="/wiki/Oral_Roberts_University" title="Oral Roberts University">Oral Roberts University</a>, a private Christian university in <a href="/wiki/Tulsa,_Oklahoma" title="Tulsa, Oklahoma">Tulsa, Oklahoma</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Channel_Tres&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Upon his return to Los Angeles, a childhood friend (<a href="/wiki/88rising" title="88rising">88rising</a> artist <a href="/wiki/August_08_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="August 08 (rapper)">August 08</a>) brought him along to studio sessions, where he rose to making beats for <a href="/wiki/Duckwrth" title="Duckwrth">Duckwrth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kehlani" title="Kehlani">Kehlani</a> and others.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In  2018  "Controller" received  airplay on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a>, and an Essential New Tune call-out from Pete Tong. Australian radio station <a href="/wiki/Triple_J" title="Triple J">Triple J</a> added "Controller" to their A-list rotation and called it "one of 2018's biggest cult hits." Channel Tres continued to see success with the release of his self-titled <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> in 2018 and further singles, including his single "Black Moses", which featured <a href="/wiki/JPEGMAFIA" class="mw-redirect" title="JPEGMAFIA">JPEGMAFIA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He played his first headline shows in the US in 2019.
</p>
