<p><b>Avante Ramone Santana</b> (<a href="/wiki/Birth_name" title="Birth name">né</a> <b>Brown</b>; born March 24, 1998),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Tanna Leone</b>, is an American rapper. Born in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, he began to gain recognition in the <a href="/wiki/Underground_hip_hop" title="Underground hip hop">underground hip hop</a> scenes of <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage names</a> <b>Noviiimber</b> and <b>Baby Santana</b>. In March 2022, Leone signed a <a href="/wiki/Recording_contract" title="Recording contract">recording contract</a> with <a href="/wiki/PGLang" title="PGLang">PGLang</a>, under the aegis of <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His debut studio album, <i>Sleepy Soldier,</i> was released the following month.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Leone earned his first <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Award</a> nomination for his work on <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>'s fifth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Morale_%26_the_Big_Steppers" title="Mr. Morale &amp; the Big Steppers">Mr. Morale &amp; the Big Steppers</a></i> (2022).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tanna_Leone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Avante Ramone Brown was born on March 24, 1998, in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. His mother is a <a href="/wiki/Spoken_word" title="Spoken word">spoken word</a> artist, and he "might share some relatives" with rapper <a href="/wiki/Baby_Keem" title="Baby Keem">Baby Keem</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Brown moved around a lot growing up and experienced many different environments, which he believes has made him very adaptive and socially well-rounded.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following in his mother's footsteps, Brown made his first song at age 11. He would continue to write songs for fun, and often performed them for friends at school. He would later join a poetry club that held cyphers, where he was able to refine his lyrical skills. His first performance in front of a crowd surprisingly felt "extremely natural," which opened him up to the idea of pursuing a professional career as an artist.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tanna_Leone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After finishing school, Brown relocated to <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, where he became serious about pursuing a rap career. He adopted the <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> Noviiimber and began releasing music through 680079 Records, as a member of the hip hop <a href="/wiki/Musical_collective" title="Musical collective">collective</a> Safe House Music Group.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> His first project, an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> titled <i>Do or Die</i>, was released on March 29, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Later in the year, he changed his stage name to Baby Santana and joined the group Dog Eat World, alongside local rappers Nino Shyne and Lone London.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, Brown moved back to Los Angeles and changed his stage name a third time to Tanna Leone. He then contacted filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Dave_Free" title="Dave Free">Dave Free</a> to show him <i>Die or Die</i> and a video he directed.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It sparked a conversation between the two and secured Leone a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint</a> <a href="/wiki/Recording_contract" title="Recording contract">recording contract</a> with <a href="/wiki/PGLang" title="PGLang">PGLang</a>, which Free founded with rapper <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> on March 9, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Following the announcement, he joined Baby Keem's the Melodic Blue Tour as an <a href="/wiki/Opening_act" title="Opening act">opening act</a> and released two singles, "Lucky" and "With the Villains"; the latter song was accompanied by a music video.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He shared another single, "Picasso", on April 14, before releasing his debut studio album, <i>Sleepy Solider</i>, on April 27, to generally positive reviews from music critics.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 4, 2022, Leone starred in PGLang's <a href="/wiki/Advertising_campaign" title="Advertising campaign">advertising campaign</a> for lifestyle brand <a href="/wiki/Converse_(brand)" title="Converse (brand)">Converse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He was featured on Lamar's fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Morale_%26_the_Big_Steppers" title="Mr. Morale &amp; the Big Steppers">Mr. Morale &amp; the Big Steppers</a></i>, on the track "Mr. Morale", which earned him a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Award</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Album_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Album of the Year">Album of the Year</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> From July to December, Leone accompanied Lamar on his <a href="/wiki/The_Big_Steppers_Tour" title="The Big Steppers Tour">Big Steppers Tour</a>; marking his first experience on a worldwide tour.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He appeared in the tour's <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar_Live:_The_Big_Steppers_Tour" title="Kendrick Lamar Live: The Big Steppers Tour">accompanying concert film</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tanna_Leone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Leone has described his sound as having the strength of a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">rap</a> song, and the melodies of a <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> or <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> song. He explained that his music strays from "following any type of rule book or guideline. It's just something you could feel."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Leone lists <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>, <a href="/wiki/DMX" title="DMX">DMX</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Edition" title="New Edition">New Edition</a>, and Lamar as some of his main influences, while also naming <a href="/wiki/Frank_Ocean" title="Frank Ocean">Frank Ocean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_3000" title="André 3000">André 3000</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Billie_Eilish" title="Billie Eilish">Billie Eilish</a> as some of his favorite artists.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
