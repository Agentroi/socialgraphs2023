<p><b>Al Hasan Naqiyy</b> (born <b>Arlandis Tremel Hinton</b>; born January 23, 1975), known professionally as <b>B.G. Knocc Out</b> is an American West Coast rapper and songwriter. He is best known for his collaboration on <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a>'s 1993 single "<i><a href="/wiki/Real_Muthaphuckkin_G%27s" title="Real Muthaphuckkin G&#39;s">Real Muthaphuckkin G's</a></i>". He is also the younger half-brother of West Coast rapper, <a href="/wiki/Dresta" title="Dresta">Dresta</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HipHopDX_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HipHopDX-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B.G._Knocc_Out&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Al Hasan Naqiyy was born in Compton, California on January 23, 1975, as Arlandis Hinton.<sup id="cite_ref-BG_Knocc_Out_&amp;_Gangsta_Dresta_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BG_Knocc_Out_&amp;_Gangsta_Dresta-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was raised by a single mother, Christine Thomas, and older half brother <a href="/wiki/Dresta" title="Dresta">Andre Wicker</a>. Growing up in Compton and Watts, along with its strong influence of gangs, Hinton became involved with the Nutty Blocc Compton Crip set along with his brother.<sup id="cite_ref-BG_Knocc_Out_&amp;_Gangsta_Dresta_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BG_Knocc_Out_&amp;_Gangsta_Dresta-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B.G._Knocc_Out&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>B.G Knocc Out first began his musical career after meeting Eazy-E through his brother. In August 1995, along with Dresta their first studio album "<i><a href="/wiki/Real_Brothas" title="Real Brothas">Real Brothas</a></i>" was released through <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam</a> records and Outburst records. The album had peaked #128 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a>. He has gone on to release nine additional albums including <i>Prince of Compton</i><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (2009), <i>Eazy-E's Protege</i><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> (2011), and <i>Blocc Boyz</i><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> (2015). On August 10, 2019, he released a single titled "Nobody Move", which featured Ricc Rocc and Michael ACE.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B.G._Knocc_Out&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After his work on "Real Muthaphuckkin G's", B.G. Knocc Out was convicted of attempted murder<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="&quot;After his work on &quot;Real Muthaphuckkin G&#39;s&quot; is too nonspecific. (August 2023)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and served ten years in prison. After his release he released his first solo album, <i>Eazy-E's Protege</i>, in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-HipHopDX_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HipHopDX-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He is a Muslim.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
