<p><b>Theresa Beth</b> (born January 18, 1965),<sup id="cite_ref-Allmusic_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Tairrie B</b> (pronounced "Terry"), is an American rapper and singer. She first established herself as a rapper under the tutelage of <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a>. After releasing her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Power_of_a_Woman_(Tairrie_B_album)" title="Power of a Woman (Tairrie B album)">Power of a Woman</a></i> (1990) on <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records" title="Ruthless Records">Ruthless Records</a>, she formed the <a href="/wiki/Rap_metal" title="Rap metal">rap metal</a> band Manhole (later <a href="/wiki/Tura_Satana_(band)" title="Tura Satana (band)">Tura Satana</a>). She would further established herself in metal with the band <a href="/wiki/My_Ruin" title="My Ruin">My Ruin</a>. Since 2015, she has primarily released rap albums.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tairrie_B&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She started her music career as part of the female dance group <a href="/wiki/Bardeux" title="Bardeux">Bardeux</a>. After the release of their debut single, "Three-Time Lover", in 1987, she left the group. Under the tutelage of <a href="/wiki/Eazy-E" title="Eazy-E">Eazy-E</a>, she was later signed to his label <a href="/wiki/Ruthless_Records" title="Ruthless Records">Ruthless Records</a> (under a new imprint called Comptown Records) and released her first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Power_of_a_Woman_(Tairrie_B_album)" title="Power of a Woman (Tairrie B album)">Power of a Woman</a></i>, which, unlike usual Ruthless fare, was distributed by <a href="/wiki/MCA_Records" title="MCA Records">MCA Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Allmusic_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Tairrie B alleges she was assaulted by <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a> at the 1990 <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a> for not collaborating with him on her first album:
</p><p>"Everyone with NWA – like <a href="/wiki/Above_the_Law_(group)" title="Above the Law (group)">Above the Law</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_D.O.C." title="The D.O.C.">The D.O.C.</a> – whenever they do an album, all of the guys appear on the last track. So they were going to do that with me at one point and Ice Cube was gonna write lyrics to a track called 'I Ain't Your Bitch'."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> She refused, and rewrote the track as a diatribe against her labelmates. When Dr. Dre heard the track, he turned up at the awards ceremony party, where he punched Tairrie "twice – once in the mouth and once in the eye. He hit me like Tyson, but I took it – I don't know how."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It turned out to be the only Comptown Records album release. Tairrie B later began working on her second full-length album <i>Single White Female</i>, but before releasing it decided to change her musical direction away from rap. After being released from her contract by Eazy-E, just a few weeks before his 1995 death, she then formed Manhole (later renamed <a href="/wiki/Tura_Satana_(band)" title="Tura Satana (band)">Tura Satana</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> initially a <a href="/wiki/Rap_metal" title="Rap metal">rap metal</a> band inspired by <a href="/wiki/Ice-T" title="Ice-T">Ice-T</a>'s band <a href="/wiki/Body_Count_(band)" title="Body Count (band)">Body Count</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Everley_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Everley-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She later formed <a href="/wiki/My_Ruin" title="My Ruin">My Ruin</a>. In December 2008, Tairrie B married her long-time partner and guitarist of My Ruin, <a href="/wiki/Mick_Murphy_(guitarist)" title="Mick Murphy (guitarist)">Mick Murphy</a>. She stated in the band's <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> blog that she had changed her last name to Murphy, but would continue to use the name Tairrie B in music.
</p><p>In August 2015, Tairrie B returned to her rap roots with her third studio rap album <i><a href="/wiki/Vintage_Curses" title="Vintage Curses">Vintage Curses</a></i>. On October 30, 2015, cult director <a href="/wiki/John_Waters" title="John Waters">John Waters</a> commented on Tairrie B's rap career and legacy in his interview with <a href="/wiki/Iggy_Pop" title="Iggy Pop">Iggy Pop</a> on BBC Radio 6 just before playing "Ruthless Bitch" saying, "..she was very much a part of the story, she was the first white girl rapper I remember."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 20, 2020, Tairrie B released a new album, <i>Feminenergy</i>, with 12 songs that she described as "socially conscious, politically reflective, hardcore tracks of hip hop, rock &amp; resistance."<sup id="cite_ref-Feminenergy,_by_TAIRRIE_B_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Feminenergy,_by_TAIRRIE_B-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The album addresses racism, social injustice, sexism, and contains tributes to the passing of civil rights icon <a href="/wiki/John_Lewis" title="John Lewis">John Lewis</a> and United States Supreme Court Justice <a href="/wiki/Ruth_Bader_Ginsburg" title="Ruth Bader Ginsburg">Ruth Bader Ginsburg</a>. The album features <a href="/wiki/Mick_Murphy_(guitarist)" title="Mick Murphy (guitarist)">Mick Murphy</a> (My Ruin), Paul Catten (Dead Sheeran, Barrabus, Murder One, Lazarus Blackstar, Medulla Nocte), and Spokane DJ, GrandMixer GMS (KFOX Nightbeat &amp; Global Frequency).<sup id="cite_ref-Feminenergy,_by_TAIRRIE_B_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Feminenergy,_by_TAIRRIE_B-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
