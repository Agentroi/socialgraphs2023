<p><b>William Alfred</b>, better known as <b>Aone</b>, is a Nigerian-born American <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>, signed to <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rick Ross (rapper)">Rick Ross</a>' label <a href="/wiki/Maybach_Music_Group" title="Maybach Music Group">Maybach Music Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He became active in the music industry in 2008 and is known for his work with urban music artists such as<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birdman_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Birdman (entertainer)">Birdman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rick Ross (rapper)">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Jeezy" class="mw-redirect" title="Young Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Lox" title="The Lox">D Block</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/The_Outlawz" class="mw-redirect" title="The Outlawz">The Outlawz</a>, and others. His major influences in producing come from <a href="/wiki/Just_Blaze" title="Just Blaze">Just Blaze</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drumma_Boy" title="Drumma Boy">Drumma Boy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>. His work is marked with a "You are now rocking with Aonebeats" tag in most of his recent music.
</p><p>AOne has also done production for high-profile artists such as <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rick Ross (rapper)">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a>, Juicy J, <a href="/wiki/Birdman_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Birdman (entertainer)">Birdman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wizkid_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Wizkid (musician)">Wizkid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yo_Gotti" title="Yo Gotti">Yo Gotti</a>, among many others. In the summer of 2013, AOne produced the bass thumping record "Gallardo" for Maybach Music Group and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>'s "Self Made Vol.3" Compilation Album, which debuted at No. 1 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart with first-week sales of 50,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On March 23, 2012 AOne released his first solo full-length project, a <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> titled <i>Aone Perico</i>, which featured guest appearances from several rappers including <a href="/wiki/Uncle_Murda" title="Uncle Murda">Uncle Murda</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trae_tha_Truth" title="Trae tha Truth">Trae tha Truth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mistah_F.A.B." title="Mistah F.A.B.">Mistah F.A.B.</a> and more. in May 2015, AOne teamed up with Livemixtapes to release his second <a href="/wiki/Solo_project" class="mw-redirect" title="Solo project">solo project</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP) titled <i>Cocaine, Steak &amp; Wine</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aone_Beats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aone gained mainstream attention through his collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rick Ross (rapper)">Rick Ross</a>, on the song "10 Bricks", which featured <a href="/wiki/Birdman_(rapper)" title="Birdman (rapper)">Birdman</a>, off of Ross's mixtape <a href="/wiki/Ashes_to_Ashes_(mixtape)" title="Ashes to Ashes (mixtape)">Ashes To Ashes</a> released on December 24, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Aone has also worked extensively with American rapper <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a>, producing records like "Brand New," which featured <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> as well as records like "In The Air," "Home Run," and "Drug Related," from Young Buck's <a href="/wiki/Live_Loyal_Die_Rich" title="Live Loyal Die Rich">Live Loyal Die Rich</a> mixtape. In May 2010, Aone produced ‘Pay Off,’ for American hip-hop group <a href="/wiki/Outlawz" title="Outlawz">Outlawz</a>, the record featured Rapper <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a>, and was included on The <a href="/wiki/Outlawz" title="Outlawz">Outlawz</a>’s sixth and final studio album, “<a href="/wiki/Perfect_Timing_(Outlawz_album)" title="Perfect Timing (Outlawz album)">Perfect Timing</a>.”
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.93present:_Aone_Perico.2C_Cocaine.2C_Steak_.26_Wine"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–present:_Aone_Perico,_Cocaine,_Steak_&amp;_Wine">2012–present: Aone Perico, Cocaine, Steak &amp; Wine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aone_Beats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2012–present: Aone Perico, Cocaine, Steak &amp; Wine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On Mar 23, 2012 AOne released his debut mixtape titled  Aone Perico, The mixtape was released in conjunction with popular mixtape website <a href="/wiki/DatPiff" title="DatPiff">DatPiff</a>. The mixtape features guest appearances from  Juicy J, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rick Ross (rapper)">Rick Ross</a>, Uncle Murda, French Montana, <a href="/wiki/Trae_tha_Truth" title="Trae tha Truth">Trae tha Truth</a>, Mistah F.A.B. and more. The mixtape is entirely self-produced and does not include any vocals of his.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 26, 2015, Aone partnered with popular music site Livemixtapes to announce the instalment of his second mixtape, <a href="/wiki/Cocaine,_Steak_%26_Wine" class="mw-redirect" title="Cocaine, Steak &amp; Wine">Cocaine, Steak &amp; Wine</a>, and a release of the mixtape's first single, "Listen", which features Bay Area MC <a href="/wiki/Mistah_F.A.B." title="Mistah F.A.B.">Mistah F.A.B.</a> and UK singer Katie London.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The project Cocaine, Steak &amp; Wine features songs with Mistah Fab, <a href="/wiki/Warren_G" title="Warren G">Warren G</a>, Creative Gold, <a href="/wiki/AZ_(rapper)" title="AZ (rapper)">A.Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Breed" class="mw-redirect" title="Young Breed">Young Breed</a>, JaeO Daftpick, and others.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_and_equipment">Style and equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aone_Beats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Style and equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aone has called himself a fan of producers such as <a href="/wiki/Havoc_(musician)" title="Havoc (musician)">Havoc (musician)</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Toomp" title="DJ Toomp">DJ Toomp</a>, but writes and produces a variety of genres. About his style, he has stated "It's a mix of the South, and it's a mix of New York. It's a mix of everything. It's something different."<sup id="cite_ref-auto_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Aone uses <a href="/wiki/FL_Studio" title="FL Studio">FL Studio</a> and custom plug-ins to create his beats.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He is known for sampling jazz, funk, and soul artists,<sup id="cite_ref-auto_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Like many other producers Aone uses a musical signature tag on many of the songs he has produced. His main tag is a Female pitched-up voice saying "You are now rocking with Aonebeats"
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="AOne_Music_Group">AOne Music Group</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aone_Beats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: AOne Music Group">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In March 2013, AOne Beats started his own record label called AOne Music Group. The label's first artist signed up was Grammy Nominated Singer and Songwriter Torica.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The record label's first release was Torica's studio album titled A Beautiful Mess: and featured Miami Rapper <a href="/wiki/Young_Breed" class="mw-redirect" title="Young Breed">Young Breed</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
