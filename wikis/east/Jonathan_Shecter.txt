					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American magazine editor and music promoter</div>
<p>

<b>Jonathan Miles Shecter</b> (born August 4, 1968), an American magazine editor and music promoter,<sup id="cite_ref-promoterJShecterNYT2012_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-promoterJShecterNYT2012-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is also known as "<b>Shecky Green</b>" and "<b>J the Sultan.</b>" He is the co-founder<sup id="cite_ref-CoFounSource.TheGradi2021_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoFounSource.TheGradi2021-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> of the music and culture magazine <a href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Source (magazine)"><i>The Source</i></a>, former Director of Programming for the <a href="/wiki/Wynn_Las_Vegas" title="Wynn Las Vegas">Wynn Las Vegas</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-promoterJShecterNYT2012_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-promoterJShecterNYT2012-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and current Editor-In-Chief for Cuepoint at <a href="/wiki/Medium_(service)" class="mw-redirect" title="Medium (service)">Medium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2Pizzo_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2Pizzo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FormerWynn.Gradi2017_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FormerWynn.Gradi2017-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jonathan_Shecter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shecter was born in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> and grew up in a Jewish family.<sup id="cite_ref-7Twitter_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7Twitter-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12Harvard_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12Harvard-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He is a member of the Class of 1986 of <a href="/wiki/Friends_Select_School" title="Friends Select School">Friends Select School</a>, a college-preparatory, Quaker school for pre-kindergarten through 12th grade in Philadelphia. In 1986, he enrolled in <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a> as an English major and graduated in 1990. During his 1988 sophomore year, he began overseeing a rap music radio show called <i>Street Beat</i> on Harvard College’s radio station, <a href="/wiki/WHRB" title="WHRB">WHRB</a> 95.3 FM, with his then roommate <a href="/wiki/David_Mays" title="David Mays">David M. Mays</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-12Harvard_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12Harvard-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> To promote this radio show Shecter started <i>The Source</i> magazine as a newsletter, using an issue for his senior thesis, <i>The Decade of <a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip Hop">Hip Hop</a></i>.With high-school friend Kevin "Kevi-Kev" Krakower, he co-founded a <a href="/wiki/Rap" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap">rap</a> group named <i>Big Man on Campus.</i><sup id="cite_ref-12Harvard_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12Harvard-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and they and produced their first <i><a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a></i> that same year.<sup id="cite_ref-12Harvard_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12Harvard-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jonathan_Shecter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Source"><i>The Source</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jonathan_Shecter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Source">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shecter co-founded<sup id="cite_ref-CoFounSource.TheGradi2021_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoFounSource.TheGradi2021-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i>The Source</i> magazine in 1988 in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_MA" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambridge, MA">Cambridge, MA</a> with his roommate <a href="/wiki/David_Mays" title="David Mays">David Mays</a> initially as an effort to promote his rap music radio show on <a href="/wiki/WHRB" title="WHRB">WHRB</a> 95.3 FM.Often cited as “The Bible of Hip Hop,” the magazine devoted coverage to the rising rap and hip-hop music genres.Shecter served as editor-in-chief of <i>The Source</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-Bawtamaw93_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bawtamaw93-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> while Mays handled publishing duties.The magazine's officers relocated to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in 1990 with the intention to expand the magazine into a mainstream market publication;--&gt;Shecter brought on additional staff. Mays secured the magazine's first newsstand distribution agreement, and publication's distribution increased. Funding was via selling ad space, including to rap performer Christopher Wallace, also known as <a href="/wiki/Notorious_B.I.G." class="mw-redirect" title="Notorious B.I.G.">Notorious B.I.G.</a> and performer/producer <a href="/wiki/Sean_John_Combs" class="mw-redirect" title="Sean John Combs">Sean John Combs</a>.
</p><p>Shecter left <i>The Source</i> magazine in 1995, which by then was recognized by <i>Audit Bureau of Circulation</i>..
</p>
<h3><span id="Wynn_.28Las_Vegas.29_Hotel:_Entertainment_director"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Wynn_(Las_Vegas)_Hotel:_Entertainment_director">Wynn (Las Vegas) Hotel: Entertainment director</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jonathan_Shecter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wynn (Las Vegas) Hotel: Entertainment director">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2003 Shecter relocated from New York City to <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a>. where he was recognized as a dance music promoter and DJ representative.Shecter partnered with <a href="/wiki/Ultra_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="Ultra Music">Ultra Music</a> to create compilation albums for the Wynn's nightclubs Surrender, Encore Beach Club, and Andrea's Restaurant. These albums were sold through retail outlets inside the Wynn Las Vegas.<sup id="cite_ref-6DJStudio_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6DJStudio-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He was instrumental in the development of a recording studio for the nightclub's resident DJs in late 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</p>
