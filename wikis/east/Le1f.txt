<p><b>Khalif Libasse Diouf</b>, known by the stage name <b>Kalifa</b> (born April 6, 1989), formerly known by the stage name <b>Le1f</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="/iː/: &#39;ee&#39; in &#39;fleece&#39;">iː</span><span title="&#39;f&#39; in &#39;find&#39;">f</span></span>/</a></span></span>),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is an American rapper and producer. Diouf also founded the record label Camp &amp; Street, with Boody, <a href="/wiki/DonChristian" title="DonChristian">DonChristian</a>, and Chaz Requina. Diouf garnered attention for unique musical and performance styles, as well as his role as an openly <a href="/wiki/LGBT_representations_in_hip_hop_music" title="LGBT representations in hip hop music">gay rapper</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Nichols,_James_Michael_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nichols,_James_Michael-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Phillymag_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Phillymag-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gawker_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gawker-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Artinfo_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Artinfo-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Following a series of well-received mixtapes and EPs,  Diouf's debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Riot_Boi" title="Riot Boi">Riot Boi</a></i>, was released in November 2015.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Le1f&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, Diouf studied <a href="/wiki/Ballet" title="Ballet">ballet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Modern_dance" title="Modern dance">modern dance</a>, attended the <a href="/wiki/Concord_Academy" title="Concord Academy">Concord Academy</a> with the class of 2007 and earned a degree in dance from <a href="/wiki/Wesleyan_University" title="Wesleyan University">Wesleyan University</a> before returning to the city to become a rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Le1f&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Initially known for producing tracks for Brooklyn-based alternative hip-hop group <a href="/wiki/Das_Racist" title="Das Racist">Das Racist</a>, such as their popular debut single "Combination Pizza Hut and Taco Bell",<sup id="cite_ref-Spin_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spin-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Diouf became known as a solo artist with the release of debut <a href="/wiki/Mixtape#Mixtapes_in_hip_hop" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_York" title="Dark York">Dark York</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-P4k-DY_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P4k-DY-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> released in April 2012. Its second single, the <a href="/wiki/Matrixxman" title="Matrixxman">5kinAndBone5</a>-produced "Wut" garnered attention as the standout track from the project and manifested into a music video in June 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P4k-Wut_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P4k-Wut-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2012, Le1f was featured on the song "Fuckin the DJ" by <a href="/wiki/Mykki_Blanco" title="Mykki Blanco">Mykki Blanco</a> (which was co-produced with Diouf collaborator, Boody) from their <i><a href="/wiki/Cosmic_Angel:_The_Illuminati_Prince/ss" title="Cosmic Angel: The Illuminati Prince/ss">Cosmic Angel: The Illuminati Prince/ss</a></i> mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Later that month, Le1f released a joint <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> with Boody, titled <i>Liquid</i>, which was followed by an accompanying video for the promotional single, "Soda".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Diouf's second mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Fly_Zone" title="Fly Zone">Fly Zone</a></i> was released to generally favorable reviews in January 2013 and later spawned a video for the successful "Spa Day". The mixtape featured guest appearances from artists such as <a href="/wiki/Heems" title="Heems">Heems</a>, <a href="/wiki/DonChristian" title="DonChristian">DonChristian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kitty_(rapper)" title="Kitty (rapper)">Kitty</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2013, Le1f publicly criticised the rapper <a href="/wiki/Macklemore" title="Macklemore">Macklemore</a> for the latter's song "<a href="/wiki/Thrift_Shop" title="Thrift Shop">Thrift Shop</a>", claiming that it borrowed its beat extensively from Le1f's own earlier song, "Wut", and proceeded to criticise Macklemore for another of his songs, "<a href="/wiki/Same_Love" title="Same Love">Same Love</a>", claiming that it was not appropriate for a heterosexual to exploit an <a href="/wiki/LGBT_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="LGBT rights">LGBT rights</a> issue for financial gain.<sup id="cite_ref-Nichols,_James_Michael_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nichols,_James_Michael-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</div>
<p>In September 2013, Diouf released a third full-length mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Tree_House_(Le1f_mixtape)" title="Tree House (Le1f mixtape)">Tree House</a></i> and later accompanied it with a music video for the second single "Hush Bb" directed by conceptual artist <a href="/wiki/Alex_Da_Corte" title="Alex Da Corte">Alex Da Corte</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2013, <a href="/wiki/Terrible_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Terrible Records">Terrible Records</a> announced Diouf's record deal as well as an EP, a five-track project titled <i>Hey</i>, which was later released on March 11, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On March 13, 2014, Diouf made an appearance on national television with a performance on the <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">Late Show with David Letterman</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2015, Diouf released a full-length studio album on <a href="/wiki/XL_Recordings" title="XL Recordings">XL</a> and <a href="/wiki/Terrible_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Terrible Records">Terrible Records</a>. The album features contributions and production work from artists such as <a href="/wiki/Evian_Christ" title="Evian Christ">Evian Christ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Junglepussy" title="Junglepussy">Junglepussy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Balam_Acab" title="Balam Acab">Balam Acab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lunice" title="Lunice">Lunice</a>, Boody, <a href="/wiki/Sophie_(musician)" title="Sophie (musician)">Sophie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dev_Hynes" title="Dev Hynes">Dev Hynes</a>, and Dubbel Dutch, among others. In 2017, Le1f was part of the line up for the 17th edition of the <a href="/wiki/Sonic_Acts" title="Sonic Acts">Sonic Acts</a> Festival in Amsterdam, which explored The Noise of Being. Diouf has also performed several times at <a href="/wiki/Sonic_Acts" title="Sonic Acts">Sonic Acts</a> club night called Progress Bar.
</p>
