<p><b>Robert Torres</b>, also known as <b>Sabor Latino</b> (born June 21, 1972) is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> artist and author. He is from <a href="/wiki/Jamaica,_Queens,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Jamaica, Queens, New York">Jamaica, Queens, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Positive_hip-hop_from_“Sabor_Latino”_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Positive_hip-hop_from_“Sabor_Latino”-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sabor_Latino_Makes_Large_Impact_In_The_Communities_As_Well_In_The_World_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sabor_Latino_Makes_Large_Impact_In_The_Communities_As_Well_In_The_World-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-La_Voz_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-La_Voz-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Sabor Latino creates music to inspire individuals to become productive members of society.<sup id="cite_ref-La_Voz_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-La_Voz-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sabor_Latino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sabor Latino, also known as Robert Torres, was raised in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica,_Queens" title="Jamaica, Queens">Jamaica, Queens, New York</a> by his Puerto Rican father and Dominican mother.<sup id="cite_ref-Hip-hop_with_a_positive_message_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hip-hop_with_a_positive_message-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Biography_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sabor_Latino_quiere_llegar_a_los_jovénes_con_su_hip_hop_positivo_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sabor_Latino_quiere_llegar_a_los_jovénes_con_su_hip_hop_positivo-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> While in middle school, he became interested in music at twelve.<sup id="cite_ref-Biography_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sabor_Latino:_Latino_Hip_Hop_Artist_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sabor_Latino:_Latino_Hip_Hop_Artist-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sabor_Latino_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sabor_Latino-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> During this time, Sabor Latino began writing about the life struggles, education and emotions he was experiencing.<sup id="cite_ref-Biography_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sabor_Latino:_Latino_Hip_Hop_Artist_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sabor_Latino:_Latino_Hip_Hop_Artist-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Boricua_College" title="Boricua College">Boricua College</a> with a bachelor's degree in education and a minor in psychology.<sup id="cite_ref-Biography_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sabor_Latino:_Latino_Hip_Hop_Artist_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sabor_Latino:_Latino_Hip_Hop_Artist-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sabor_Latino_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sabor_Latino-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Graduate_Student_Makes_Socially_Conscious_Hip-Hop_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Graduate_Student_Makes_Socially_Conscious_Hip-Hop-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Lehman_College" title="Lehman College">Lehman College</a> in January 2017 with a <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">master's</a> in education with a minor in administration.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sabor_Latino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sabor Latino worked as a social worker for a foster care agency in <a href="/wiki/South_Jamaica,_Queens" title="South Jamaica, Queens">South Jamaica, Queens</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Positive_hip-hop_from_“Sabor_Latino”_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Positive_hip-hop_from_“Sabor_Latino”-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sabor_Latino_Makes_Large_Impact_In_The_Communities_As_Well_In_The_World_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sabor_Latino_Makes_Large_Impact_In_The_Communities_As_Well_In_The_World-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hip-hop_with_a_positive_message_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hip-hop_with_a_positive_message-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Biography_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sabor_Latino_quiere_llegar_a_los_jovénes_con_su_hip_hop_positivo_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sabor_Latino_quiere_llegar_a_los_jovénes_con_su_hip_hop_positivo-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He released his first album, <i>Observaciones de Mi Vida, Vol. 1</i> on February 28, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-Observaciones_de_Mi_Vida,_Vol._1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Observaciones_de_Mi_Vida,_Vol._1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The album was mixed by producer Lee Evans.<sup id="cite_ref-Positive_hip-hop_from_“Sabor_Latino”_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Positive_hip-hop_from_“Sabor_Latino”-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sabor_Latino_quiere_llegar_a_los_jovénes_con_su_hip_hop_positivo_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sabor_Latino_quiere_llegar_a_los_jovénes_con_su_hip_hop_positivo-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The experiences Sabor Latino witnessed while working for the foster care agency influenced his first album.<sup id="cite_ref-Biography_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biography-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The album consists of eight songs and two poems that are meant to inspire children, adolescents, parents and adults to become productive members of society.<sup id="cite_ref-Positive_hip-hop_from_“Sabor_Latino”_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Positive_hip-hop_from_“Sabor_Latino”-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Observaciones_de_Mi_Vida,_Vol._1_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Observaciones_de_Mi_Vida,_Vol._1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Sabor Latino chose to produce the album in Spanish in attempt to create a positive image in the Latin American community.<sup id="cite_ref-Sabor_Latino_quiere_llegar_a_los_jovénes_con_su_hip_hop_positivo_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sabor_Latino_quiere_llegar_a_los_jovénes_con_su_hip_hop_positivo-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, Sabor Latino was recognized by <a href="/wiki/WQHT" title="WQHT">Hot 97</a> on its "Who's Next" list.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, Sabor Latino announced the release of his autobiography titled <i>Sabor Latino: My Life</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-latinola_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-latinola-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2014, Sabor Latino released his biography, <i>Sabor Latino: My Life</i>, which combines his personal experiences with his social-work career to inspire youth.<sup id="cite_ref-voxxi_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-voxxi-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Commenting on his music career, Torres said "he still identifies as a social worker, just of a different kind than before."<sup id="cite_ref-brandz_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brandz-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He announced that his single, "Anything In Life Is Possible," and his second book, <i>Sabor Latino's Greatest Poems &amp; Songs</i>, would be released in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> His second book was eventually titled "Mis Grandes Canciones y Poemas" and was released in 2017; his third, titled "125 Quotes Gathered from my Life Observations" was released in 2018. His fourth book, "My alma mater Lehman College" is set for publication in February 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-books_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-books-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Sabor Latino released his second album “Observaciones de mi Vida Vol. 2,” in October 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-South_Jamaica_hip-hop_artist_releases_second_album_to_inspire_and_uplift_youth_in_the_community_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-South_Jamaica_hip-hop_artist_releases_second_album_to_inspire_and_uplift_youth_in_the_community-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
