<p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; font-size: 125%;"><div class="">Beedie</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Brian Benjamin Green</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday">1988-01-19</span>) </span>January 19, 1988<span class="noprint ForceAgeToShow"> (age&#160;35)</span><br /><a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, U.S.</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Origin</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, U.S.</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role"><a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">Rapper</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">2007 – present</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data">Independent<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></td></tr><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-header" style="background-color: #b0c4de"></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Website</th><td class="infobox-data"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.beedieland.com/">www.beedieland.com</a></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Brian Benjamin Green</b> (born January 19, 1988), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Beedie</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> based out of <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania</a>. Born in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> to a family of working entertainers, he began his music career in 2007 with fellow Pittsburgh emcee <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a> as the duo <a href="/wiki/The_Ill_Spoken" class="mw-redirect" title="The Ill Spoken">The Ill Spoken</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beedie_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beedie was born in New York City and lived in Washington, D.C. and Oklahoma, before moving to Pittsburgh in 2001 with his mother, Maria Posvar, and younger brother.<sup id="cite_ref-http&#58;//pittnews.com/newsstory/beedie-seeks-to-establish-rap-culture-in-steel-city/_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http://pittnews.com/newsstory/beedie-seeks-to-establish-rap-culture-in-steel-city/-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Posvar graduated from Pittsburgh's <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University" title="Carnegie Mellon University">Carnegie Mellon University</a> and had done some acting, including a role in <i><a href="/wiki/Days_of_Our_Lives" title="Days of Our Lives">Days of Our Lives</a></i>. His father was a sports announcer. His maternal grandparents are the late <a href="/wiki/Wesley_Posvar" title="Wesley Posvar">Wesley Posvar</a>, who was the <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_(education)" title="Chancellor (education)">Chancellor</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pittsburgh" title="University of Pittsburgh">University of Pittsburgh</a> from 1967 to 1991, and his wife <a href="/wiki/Mildred_Miller" title="Mildred Miller">Mildred Miller</a>, an opera singer who performed at the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Opera" title="Metropolitan Opera">Metropolitan Opera</a> from the 1950s to 1970s and then founded the <a href="/wiki/Opera_Theater_of_Pittsburgh" class="mw-redirect" title="Opera Theater of Pittsburgh">Opera Theater of Pittsburgh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Beedie started writing rhymes starting at age 12. He attended <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Allderdice_High_School" title="Taylor Allderdice High School">Taylor Allderdice High School</a>, but dropped out after his parents kicked him out of the house. He took classes at <a href="/wiki/Community_College_of_Allegheny_County" title="Community College of Allegheny County">Community College of Allegheny County</a> to earn his <a href="/wiki/General_Educational_Development" title="General Educational Development">GED</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beedie_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Ill_Spoken">The Ill Spoken</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beedie_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Ill Spoken">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, when Beedie was 19, he and 15-year-old <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a> (then known as EZ Mac), founded the rap duo The Ill Spoken.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After performing at shows around Pittsburgh and releasing <i>How High: The Mixtape</i> with Miller on October 27, 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> the two emcees began focusing on solo careers.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Beedie has cited <a href="/wiki/J._Dilla" class="mw-redirect" title="J. Dilla">J. Dilla</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> as musical influences, and describes his sound as "classic hip-hop with a new-school twist".<sup id="cite_ref-http&#58;//pittnews.com/newsstory/beedie-seeks-to-establish-rap-culture-in-steel-city/_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http://pittnews.com/newsstory/beedie-seeks-to-establish-rap-culture-in-steel-city/-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="The_Beat_Bully_LP_.26_Varsity_Squad"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Beat_Bully_LP_&amp;_Varsity_Squad"><i>The Beat Bully LP</i> &amp; Varsity Squad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beedie_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Beat Bully LP &amp; Varsity Squad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 30, 2011, Beedie released his first album unofficially, titled <i>The Beat Bully LP</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-http&#58;//www.brainofbmw.com/2011/06/beatbullylp/_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http://www.brainofbmw.com/2011/06/beatbullylp/-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The album was well received by music critics.<sup id="cite_ref-http&#58;//www.pittsburghcitypaper.ws/pittsburgh/local-mc-beedie-is-ready-for-the-boom-bap-big-time/Content?oid=1397701_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http://www.pittsburghcitypaper.ws/pittsburgh/local-mc-beedie-is-ready-for-the-boom-bap-big-time/Content?oid=1397701-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> According to Beedie, this album shows a lot of growth as an artist, and he considers it his official debut.<sup id="cite_ref-http&#58;//hot16sand1.com/?p=419_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http://hot16sand1.com/?p=419-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Varsity Squad is a group/crew consisting of emcees Beedie and Jon Quest, who announced the forming of the group while performing alongside <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Redman_(rapper)" title="Redman (rapper)">Redman</a> in Pittsburgh in August 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-http&#58;//www.reppittsburghhiphop.com/2010/08/19/varsity-squad-debuts-at-meth-red-show/_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http://www.reppittsburghhiphop.com/2010/08/19/varsity-squad-debuts-at-meth-red-show/-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The two emcees released a collaborative album titled <i>New School Boom Bap</i> on February 7, 2012. Their work garnered the attention of many influential underground acts such as <a href="/wiki/One_Be_Lo" title="One Be Lo">One Be Lo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Binary_Star_(hip_hop_group)" title="Binary Star (hip hop group)">Binary Star</a>, who tapped Varsity Squad for a feature on his 2011 <i>Laborhood Part 2</i> mixtape in the song "U Minor, We Major",<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> as well as producer <a href="/wiki/Buckwild_(music_producer)" title="Buckwild (music producer)">Buckwild</a> of <a href="/wiki/Diggin%27_in_the_Crates_Crew" class="mw-redirect" title="Diggin&#39; in the Crates Crew">DITC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Above_The_Weather"><i>Above The Weather</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beedie_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Above The Weather">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After releasing a stream of viral music videos on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Beedie released his next solo effort titled <i>Above The Weather</i> on August 13, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The project featured production from the likes of <a href="/wiki/Statik_Selektah" title="Statik Selektah">Statik Selektah</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Buckwild_(music_producer)" title="Buckwild (music producer)">Buckwild</a> of <a href="/wiki/Diggin%27_in_the_Crates_Crew" class="mw-redirect" title="Diggin&#39; in the Crates Crew">DITC</a><sup id="cite_ref-:2_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and more. The release garnered the attention of many publications including the <i><a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Post-Gazette" title="Pittsburgh Post-Gazette">Pittsburgh Post-Gazette</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_City_Paper" title="Pittsburgh City Paper">Pittsburgh City Paper</a></i>, who featured Beedie for a cover story on September 26, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Soon after, Beedie and Mac Miller reunited to release another track as The Ill Spoken on October 23, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On January 5, 2013, producer Git released a single titled "Magic Spaceship" featuring <a href="/wiki/Sean_Price" title="Sean Price">Sean Price</a>, Beedie and fellow Pittsburgh rapper B. White<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Counter_Culture_.26_work_with_Wiz_Khalifa"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Counter_Culture_&amp;_work_with_Wiz_Khalifa"><i>Counter Culture</i> &amp; work with Wiz Khalifa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beedie_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Counter Culture &amp; work with Wiz Khalifa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Beedie appeared on <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Satellite_Radio" title="Sirius Satellite Radio">Sirius Radio</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Shade_45" title="Shade 45">Shade 45</a> station with Statik Selektah on May 30, 2013, where he announced his next release would be titled <i>Counter Culture</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> After many delays, the album was finally released digitally on December 22, 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-:5_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> followed shortly after with a mixtape titled <i>B-$ide$ Vol. 1: Lo$t Dream$</i> released on Beedie's 28th birthday, January 19, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> As suggested by the title, the mixtape featured lost and scrapped songs during the long recording process between <i>Above The Weather</i> and <i>Counter Culture</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Around this time Beedie formed a friendship with fellow Pittsburgh artist <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> who brought out Beedie to perform on various tour dates<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and eventually featuring on the song "Make A Wish" on <i>Counter Culture</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
