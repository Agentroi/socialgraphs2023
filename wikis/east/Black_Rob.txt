<p><b>Robert Ross</b> (June 8, 1968<sup id="cite_ref-variety_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> – April 17, 2021), known professionally as <b>Black Rob</b>, was an American rapper who was signed to <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Sean "Puffy" Combs</a>' <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Bad Boy Entertainment">Bad Boy Entertainment</a>. He was best known for his 2000 single "<a href="/wiki/Whoa!_(Black_Rob_song)" title="Whoa! (Black Rob song)">Whoa!</a>", which peaked at number 43 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BillboardBlackRob_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillboardBlackRob-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Rob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ross grew up in <a href="/wiki/East_Harlem" title="East Harlem">East Harlem</a> and began rapping between the ages of 11 and 12.<sup id="cite_ref-unkut1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unkut1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was inspired by the artists he listened to regularly, including <a href="/wiki/Spoonie_Gee" title="Spoonie Gee">Spoonie Gee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doug_E._Fresh" title="Doug E. Fresh">Doug E. Fresh</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> When he was 22, he joined his first rap group, the Schizophrenics, though they did not release any albums. He initially rapped under the alias "Bacardi Rob".<sup id="cite_ref-unkut1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unkut1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He began associating with the <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy</a> label as early as 1996, appearing on the Bad Boy remix to <a href="/wiki/112_(band)" title="112 (band)">112</a>'s "Come See Me". He was then featured on several Bad Boy releases, including remixes to <a href="/wiki/Total_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Total (band)">Total</a>'s "What About Us" (1997) and <a href="/wiki/Faith_Evans" title="Faith Evans">Faith Evans</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Love_Like_This_(Faith_Evans_song)" title="Love Like This (Faith Evans song)">Love Like This</a>" (1998), the song "<a href="/wiki/24_Hrs._to_Live" title="24 Hrs. to Live">24 Hrs. to Live</a>" (1997) from <a href="/wiki/Mase" title="Mase">Mase</a>, and albums by <a href="/wiki/Puff_Daddy" class="mw-redirect" title="Puff Daddy">Puff Daddy</a> &amp; the Bad Boy Family (1997) and <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a> (1999). He also made two appearances on <a href="/wiki/Cru_(group)" title="Cru (group)">Cru</a>'s 1997 album <i><a href="/wiki/Da_Dirty_30" title="Da Dirty 30">Da Dirty 30</a></i>, as well as albums by <a href="/wiki/Channel_Live" title="Channel Live">Channel Live</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benzino" title="Benzino">Benzino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Touch" title="Tony Touch">Tony Touch</a>, and others. These guest appearances earned him media buzz.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his earlier years, Black Rob headed the street rap team which he named "Alumni". His debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Life_Story_(album)" title="Life Story (album)">Life Story</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> was released in 1999 and rose above <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" class="mw-redirect" title="Music recording sales certification">platinum</a> sales. In early 2000, he released the hit single "<a href="/wiki/Whoa!_(Black_Rob_song)" title="Whoa! (Black Rob song)">Whoa!</a>". Produced by <a href="/wiki/Diggin%27_in_the_Crates_Crew" class="mw-redirect" title="Diggin&#39; in the Crates Crew">Diggin' in the Crates Crew</a> member <a href="/wiki/Buckwild_(music_producer)" title="Buckwild (music producer)">Buckwild</a>, "Whoa!" became his biggest hit. The song peaked at number 43<sup id="cite_ref-BillboardBlackRob_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillboardBlackRob-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and reached the top 10 on both the R&amp;B/Hip-Hop<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and Rap<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> charts. It was Rob's only solo single to reach the Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Black Rob's verse in the song "By A Stranger", was featured in the in-game radio station "Game Radio" from <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_III" title="Grand Theft Auto III">Grand Theft Auto III</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite subsequently appearing on releases by <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">P. Diddy</a> and <a href="/wiki/G._Dep" title="G. Dep">G. Dep</a>, he did not make another hit single. His second album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Black_Rob_Report" title="The Black Rob Report">The Black Rob Report</a></i> (2005) failed to perform as strongly as his debut and quickly fell off the <i>Billboard</i> 100 charts. In 2005, Jemal Mosley from Off The Block Entertainment started managing Black Rob's career. In 2010, he parted from Bad Boy and signed to independent label Duck Down Records.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, he released <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Tested,_Streets_Approved" title="Game Tested, Streets Approved">Game Tested, Streets Approved</a></i>, which peaked at number 44 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a>. Black Rob also started his own independent label, Box in One, with Jemal Mosley.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, he joined the reality series <i>Come Back Kings</i> with <a href="/wiki/Ed_Lover" title="Ed Lover">Ed Lover</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Richardson" title="Calvin Richardson">Calvin Richardson</a>, David "Davinch" Chance (of <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Endz" title="Ruff Endz">Ruff Endz</a>), Jeff Sanders, Jameio, <a href="/wiki/Mr._Cheeks" title="Mr. Cheeks">Mr. Cheeks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horace_Brown_(musician)" title="Horace Brown (musician)">Horace Brown</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, he appeared on the song "Take 'Em Off Da Map" on <a href="/wiki/Diamond_D" title="Diamond D">Diamond D</a>'s album, <i>The Diam Piece</i>. In 2015, Black Rob released his fourth studio album, <i>Genuine Article</i><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> on Slimstyle Records, which he produced with Jemal Mosley. Guest emcees include <a href="/wiki/Sean_Price" title="Sean Price">Sean Price</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smif-N-Wessun" title="Smif-N-Wessun">Tek</a>, battle legend <a href="/wiki/Murda_Mook" title="Murda Mook">Murda Mook</a>, and Quas Amill. Ron Browz, former Bad Boy label-mate Q. Parker (<a href="/wiki/112_(band)" title="112 (band)">112</a>), Kali Ranks, and Quan all sing choruses on the album. Producers include <a href="/wiki/Easy_Mo_Bee" title="Easy Mo Bee">Easy Mo Bee</a>, Coptic, Big French, and others.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Rob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Black Rob was sentenced to over seven years in prison in 2006 after failing to appear in court for his sentencing (which was set to be two-to-six years initially) on a charge of <a href="/wiki/Grand_larceny" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand larceny">grand larceny</a> from 2004, in which he pleaded guilty to criminal possession of more than US$6,000 worth of jewelry stolen in a hotel.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He was released from prison in May 2010, and was interviewed by <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a> two hours later.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life_and_death">Later life and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Rob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later life and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Towards the end of his life, Black Rob struggled with homelessness and health issues. A <a href="/wiki/GoFundMe" title="GoFundMe">GoFundMe</a> page was created to help him "find a home" and "pay for medical help". Over his last five years of life, he suffered four strokes.<sup id="cite_ref-Billbobi_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billbobi-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On April 17, 2021, Black Rob died of <a href="/wiki/Cardiac_arrest" title="Cardiac arrest">cardiac arrest</a> at age 52.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
