<p><b>Destiny Nicole Frasqueri</b><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> (born June 14, 1992),<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Princess Nokia</b>, is an American rapper and songwriter. She<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;a&#93;</a></sup> released her debut studio album, <i>Metallic Butterfly</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> in 2014, followed by the 2015 mixtape  <i>Honeysuckle</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> As Princess Nokia, she rose to prominence for her 2017 studio album <i><a href="/wiki/1992_Deluxe" title="1992 Deluxe">1992 Deluxe</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> She released another mixtape, titled <i>A Girl Cried Red</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> in 2018, followed by the release of two studio albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Everything_Sucks_(Princess_Nokia_album)" title="Everything Sucks (Princess Nokia album)">Everything Sucks</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Everything_Is_Beautiful_(Princess_Nokia_album)" title="Everything Is Beautiful (Princess Nokia album)">Everything Is Beautiful</a></i>, in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Nokia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frasqueri identifies as <a href="/wiki/Black_Indians_in_the_United_States" title="Black Indians in the United States">Afro-Indigenous</a><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and is of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Ricans" title="Puerto Ricans">Puerto Rican</a> descent.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> When Frasqueri was ten, she lost her mother to <a href="/wiki/AIDS" class="mw-redirect" title="AIDS">AIDS</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-vogue_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vogue-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Between the ages of nine and sixteen, she was in foster care, during which her foster mother was physically abusive.<sup id="cite_ref-vogue_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vogue-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> After Frasqueri left foster care, she went to live with her grandmother.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vogue_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vogue-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> She subsequently started writing rhymes.<sup id="cite_ref-paper_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paper-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Frasqueri lived around <a href="/wiki/East_Harlem" title="East Harlem">East Harlem</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Lower_East_Side" title="Lower East Side">Lower East Side</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-paper_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paper-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Nokia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932018:_Early_career_and_career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2018:_Early_career_and_career_beginnings">2010–2018: Early career and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Nokia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010–2018: Early career and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Princess_Nokia.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Princess_Nokia.jpg/220px-Princess_Nokia.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="275" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Princess_Nokia.jpg/330px-Princess_Nokia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Princess_Nokia.jpg/440px-Princess_Nokia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1997" data-file-height="2496" /></a><figcaption>Princess Nokia performing in 2014</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2010, Frasqueri recorded and released her first song, titled "Destiny", under the stage name Wavy Spice on her <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> page, and later released the song on her YouTube channel in mid-2012. Subsequently, she released her second song, titled "Bitch I'm Posh".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-paper_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paper-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> She next released "YAYA", a song that chronicles her <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Taíno">Taíno</a> ancestry.<sup id="cite_ref-Remezcla_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Remezcla-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> She later released the singles "Vicki Gotti" and "Versace Hottie".<sup id="cite_ref-Remezcla_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Remezcla-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Following the singles, Frasqueri changed her stage name to Princess Nokia, which she claimed was an <a href="/wiki/Alter_ego" title="Alter ego">alter ego</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> and introduced the character with the song "Nokia".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On May 12, 2014, Frasqueri released her debut studio album, titled <i>Metallic Butterfly</i>, which debuted on <i><a href="/wiki/Vice_(magazine)" title="Vice (magazine)">Vice</a></i> and SoundCloud.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On September 8, 2017, she released her second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/1992_Deluxe" title="1992 Deluxe">1992 Deluxe</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> an expanded version of her 2016 mixtape, <i>1992</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> It peaked at number 25 on the US <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s <a href="/wiki/Heatseekers_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Heatseekers Albums">Heatseekers Albums</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a></i> listed it as the 32nd best album of 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nokia debuted a radio show on Apple's Beats 1 Radio on February 18, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Episodes aired every other Sunday and allowed listeners to get acquainted with the inner workings of Nokia's mind. The show has a total of six episodes labeled "The Voices in My Head with Princess Nokia."<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> In September 2018, she was chosen as one of the six ambassadors by <a href="/wiki/Maison_Margiela" title="Maison Margiela">Maison Margiela</a> to promote their new fragrance, Mutiny.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> In December 2018, Nokia released a "remastered and expanded version" of the album <i>Metallic Butterfly</i>, which includes three new bonus tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-re-release_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-re-release-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> That same year, she released a mixtape titled <i>A Girl Cried Red</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Stefanie Fernández of <a href="/wiki/NPR" title="NPR">NPR</a> deemed it an expression of Frasqueri's artistic and emotional versatility and praised how she "transcends them."<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2019.E2.80.93present:_Everything_Sucks_and_Everything_Is_Beautiful"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–present:_Everything_Sucks_and_Everything_Is_Beautiful">2019–present: <i>Everything Sucks</i> and <i>Everything Is Beautiful</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Nokia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2019–present: Everything Sucks and Everything Is Beautiful">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2019, Nokia had her debut performance as an actress in the independent film <i>Angelfish</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> In September 2019, she released a song titled "Sugar Honey Iced Tea (S.H.I.T.)".<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> It was described as "a bouncy, empowering bop built around dismissive lyrics about Nokia's enemies."<sup id="cite_ref-DIY_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DIY-45">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> In February 2020, Nokia released two albums: <i>Everything Sucks</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Everything_is_Beautiful_(Princess_Nokia_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Everything is Beautiful (Princess Nokia album)">Everything Is Beautiful</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork</a></i> described <i>Everything Sucks</i> as "locust swarm of angst, restless and frantic" and regarded <i>Everything Is Beautiful</i> as "warm and expansive" in comparison to the former.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> In March 2021, Nokia released a music video for the song, titled "It's Not My Fault". The single was released through <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a> and marked the first one Nokia released through a major label.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2022, she voiced LaBrea, LaCienega's cousin in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Proud_Family:_Louder_and_Prouder" title="The Proud Family: Louder and Prouder">The Proud Family: Louder and Prouder</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Nokia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nokia's musical style has been described as "experimental" and "eagerly floating between genres such as <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-DIY_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DIY-45">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> She lists rappers <a href="/wiki/MC_Lyte" title="MC Lyte">MC Lyte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Queen_Latifah" title="Queen Latifah">Queen Latifah</a>, girl group <a href="/wiki/TLC_(group)" title="TLC (group)">TLC</a>, singer <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Nu_metal" title="Nu metal">nu metal</a> bands <a href="/wiki/Korn" title="Korn">Korn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slipknot_(band)" title="Slipknot (band)">Slipknot</a> as musical influences.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Nokia also cites <a href="/wiki/New_York_hardcore" title="New York hardcore">hardcore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punk_subculture" title="Punk subculture">punk</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rave" title="Rave">rave</a> cultures as influences for her performances.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Nokia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frasqueri identifies as <a href="/wiki/Bisexuality" title="Bisexuality">bisexual</a> and has stated such in a past interview and has also talked about how growing up near the <a href="/wiki/Queer" title="Queer">queer</a> community of New York City was an important part of her life. The early stages of Princess Nokia's musical career began through performing at gay clubs, as she gained popularity among the gay nightlife scene.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> She also identifies as a <a href="/wiki/Gender_variance" title="Gender variance">gender non-conforming</a> person and uses both <a href="/wiki/Singular_they" title="Singular they">they/them</a> and <a href="/wiki/She_(pronoun)" title="She (pronoun)">she/her</a> pronouns.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Frasqueri is a strong supporter of intersectional <a href="/wiki/Feminism" title="Feminism">feminism</a>, founding the <i>Smart Girl Club</i> with Milah Libin, a podcast where she discusses healthy living and urban feminism.<sup id="cite_ref-feminist_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-feminist-58">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> She is a practitioner of <a href="/wiki/Santer%C3%ADa" title="Santería">Santería</a>, and has shared her own experience with clairvoyance and spirituality that she infuses her music with.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Frasqueri punched a male concertgoer at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a>, who she accused of mouthing "dirty obscenities" at her.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> She later told the crowd that "that's what you do when a white boy disrespects you".<sup id="cite_ref-feminist_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-feminist-58">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Later that year, a video of a woman throwing hot soup in a man's face who was calling "a group of teenage boys" a racial slur whilst on a subway journey to <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> surfaced.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-64">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> Frasqueri took responsibility for the incident, saying that "everybody on the train backed [her] up".<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2023, Frasqueri's former backup dancer and friend, Tommy Playboy, died after being hit by a train in New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
