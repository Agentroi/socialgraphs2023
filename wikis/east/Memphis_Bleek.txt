<p><b>Malik Deshawn Cox</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born June 23, 1978),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> known by his stage name <b>Memphis Bleek</b>, is an American rapper who was a protégé of fellow New York rapper <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Cox started his own labels: <a href="/wiki/Get_Low_Records" title="Get Low Records">Get Low Records</a> in 1998, and <a href="#Warehouse_Music_Group">Warehouse Music Group</a> in 2016. He has released four albums, the first two certified Gold.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memphis_Bleek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Memphis Bleek was one of the first artists signed to <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella" class="mw-redirect" title="Roc-A-Fella">Roc-A-Fella</a>, as a protégé of <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>. He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Marcy_Houses" title="Marcy Houses">Marcy Projects</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Bedford%E2%80%93Stuyvesant" class="mw-redirect" title="Bedford–Stuyvesant">Bedford–Stuyvesant</a>, the same neighborhood as Jay-Z. He appears on the Jay-Z albums: <i><a href="/wiki/Reasonable_Doubt_(album)" title="Reasonable Doubt (album)">Reasonable Doubt</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Vol._2..._Hard_Knock_Life" title="Vol. 2... Hard Knock Life">Vol. 2... Hard Knock Life</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Vol._3%E2%80%A6_Life_and_Times_of_S._Carter" class="mw-redirect" title="Vol. 3… Life and Times of S. Carter">Vol. 3… Life and Times of S. Carter</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dynasty:_Roc_La_Familia" title="The Dynasty: Roc La Familia">The Dynasty: Roc La Familia</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blueprint_2:_The_Gift_%26_The_Curse" title="The Blueprint 2: The Gift &amp; The Curse">The Blueprint 2: The Gift &amp; The Curse</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Bleek's first official appearance was "Coming of Age" from <i>Reasonable Doubt</i>. After the huge success of <i>Hard Knock Life</i> and the exposure of the Roc-A-Fella camp, (a Memphis Bleek track off the aforementioned Jay-Z album, "It's Alright", actually charted on Billboard), the label released Memphis Bleek's debut album, <a href="/wiki/Coming_of_Age_(Memphis_Bleek_album)" title="Coming of Age (Memphis Bleek album)">Coming of Age</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a> was the week's Hot Shot Debut on the Billboard 200, peaking at #7 with 118,000 units sold in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
The name "Memphis", which the rapper explained paid homage to pimps, was an acronym for "Making Easy Money, Pimping Hoes In Style" while "Bleek" had been a childhood nickname, given to him by his younger sister.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
He has released three more albums since his debut, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Understanding_(Memphis_Bleek_album)" title="The Understanding (Memphis Bleek album)">The Understanding</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/M.A.D.E." title="M.A.D.E.">M.A.D.E.</a></i>, and <i>534</i> (Out of the three, <a href="/wiki/The_Understanding_(Memphis_Bleek_album)" title="The Understanding (Memphis Bleek album)">The Understanding</a> was certified Gold by the RIAA). In between the release of <i>The Understanding</i> and <i>M.A.D.E.</i>, he took a three-year hiatus, in which time he took care of his older brother, who was seriously injured in a motorcycle accident.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> There are three significant notes to take away from the album <i>534</i>, which was named after the <a href="/wiki/House_numbering" title="House numbering">building number</a> of the housing project, where he and Jay-Z grew up. First, it was the debut release from the "new" Roc-A-Fella, referring to Jay-Z becoming President of <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam">Def Jam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It also contains the first official Def Jam recording by label mate turned international star, <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>, who featured on the song "The One". And lastly, this album marks the time in which Jay-Z released his first song since beginning his brief "retirement" in late 2003. The song was called "<a href="/wiki/Dear_Summer" title="Dear Summer">Dear Summer</a>", in reference to what Jay called the time of the year when he would drop a classic album or single every year for eight straight years, and how he had to metaphorically leave "her", Summer.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Memphis Bleek's highest charting single was "<a href="/wiki/Is_That_Yo_Chick" class="mw-redirect" title="Is That Yo Chick">Is That Yo Chick</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a> and Jay-Z, which peaked at #7 on the Hot Rap Singles <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> Chart, and only one of two songs by the artist to crack the Billboard Hot 100, peaking at #68, and the only track to appear on one of his solo albums. The other single being "It's Alright", appearing on the Hard Knock Life album by Jay-Z, peaking at #61 on the Billboard Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He has had countless collaborations with fellow hip-hop artists such as <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>, <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Trick_Daddy" title="Trick Daddy">Trick Daddy</a>, with many of the features coming with other Roc-A-Fella artists, including <a href="/wiki/Beanie_Sigel" title="Beanie Sigel">Beanie Sigel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freeway_(rapper)" title="Freeway (rapper)">Freeway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amil_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Amil (rapper)">Amil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Gunz" title="Young Gunz">Young Gunz</a>, and of course Jay-Z.
</p><p>In fall 2005, Memphis Bleek told MTV that he was recording an album called <i><a href="/wiki/The_Process_(Memphis_Bleek_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Process (Memphis Bleek album)">The Process</a></i>, that he would describe as a make or break album, saying "I want to do an album that's through the roof, I want to do a classic album. I feel that if this album I'm not recording is a classic, I'm not even gonna put it out. I have to do it bigger than anybody has ever done it. I have to make a good record this time, not just talking-junk records."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The rapper has worked on the album since 2005 and during that time frame he would release the songs "Hustla", "Get Ya Money Off" and "Let It Off". During this time, he would also leave Roc-A-Fella after Jay-Z told him he could run his own company and had the proper distribution deals to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He found a new home in 2009 when he signed to Mass Appeal Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the summer of 2010, he backed Jay-Z on stage, during a performance at the Swedish festival Peace&amp;Love.
</p><p>On April 10, 2012, Memphis Bleek, working with The Liberty Music Group, re-released "The Movement". The release went on to garnish over 125,000 Downloads.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 6, 2012, Memphis Bleek, working again with The Liberty Music Group, released "Kush Vol. 2". The release went on to garnish over 59,000 Downloads and get a 3/5 Review from XXL Writer Adam Fleischer.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 21, 2014, Memphis Bleek released "The Movement 2" as a mixtape for free download.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memphis_Bleek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On December 13, 2014, Cox married longtime girlfriend, Ashley Coombs, in an evening ceremony at The Merion in <a href="/wiki/Cinnaminson,_New_Jersey" class="mw-redirect" title="Cinnaminson, New Jersey">Cinnaminson, New Jersey</a>, in which <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> was in attendance.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In July 2018, Cox announced the birth of their first child, a daughter.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Cox also has a son, born in June 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Cox is the cousin of fellow Brooklyn rapper <a href="/wiki/Sean_Price" title="Sean Price">Sean Price</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_ventures">Other ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memphis_Bleek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Warehouse_Music_Group">Warehouse Music Group</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memphis_Bleek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Warehouse Music Group">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1066479718"><table class="infobox vcard"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above fn org" style="background-color: LightBlue">Warehouse Music Group</th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Founded</th><td class="infobox-data">2016</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Founder</th><td class="infobox-data agent">Malik Cox</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Distributor(s)</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genre</th><td class="infobox-data category"><a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Country of origin</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Location</th><td class="infobox-data label"><a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>Getting the approval of <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> in 2015, Memphis Bleek started his own label, as CEO and founder of Warehouse Music Group.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The debut release from the label was Memphis Bleek's single "So Different" featuring Manolo Rose, which created a big buzz for the label.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On July 28, 2016, Memphis Bleek made the announcement of signing Manolo Rose and <a href="/wiki/Casanova_(rapper)" title="Casanova (rapper)">Casanova</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, Manolo Rose released "Ball Drop"<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and "Pink Fur".<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> That same year, Casanova released three big singles: "Don't Run",<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> "Go Best Friend"<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> and "Left, Right" featuring <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Films">Films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memphis_Bleek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2002, Memphis Bleek starred in <i><a href="/wiki/State_Property_(film)" title="State Property (film)">State Property</a></i> as "Blizz". That same year, he was also in <i><a href="/wiki/Paper_Soldiers" title="Paper Soldiers">Paper Soldiers</a></i>. He's had cameos in <i><a href="/wiki/Backstage_(2000_film)" title="Backstage (2000 film)">Backstage</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Fade_to_Black_(2004_film)" title="Fade to Black (2004 film)">Fade to Black</a></i> as well.
</p>
