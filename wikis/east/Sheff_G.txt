<p><b>Michael Kyle Williams</b> (born September 23, 1998), known professionally as <b>Sheff G</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="Flatbush, Brooklyn">Flatbush</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He rose to fame with his single "No Suburban" in 2017 which was a response to the song "Suburban" by <a href="/wiki/22Gz" title="22Gz">22Gz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Williams is widely known as one of the vanguards of the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_drill" title="Brooklyn drill">Brooklyn drill</a> movement.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On May 16, 2023, Williams was indicted with 31 other purported gang members responsible for several shooting incidents and at least one murder over a two-year span.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheff_G&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Michael Williams was born on September 23, 1998 in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Trinidadians_and_Tobagonians" title="Trinidadians and Tobagonians">Trinidadian</a> mother and <a href="/wiki/Haitians" title="Haitians">Haitian</a> father.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was raised in the <a href="/wiki/Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="Flatbush, Brooklyn">Flatbush</a> section of Brooklyn. He was influenced and inspired to rap by listening to <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">B.I.G.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drill_music" title="Drill music">Chicago drill</a> rappers like <a href="/wiki/Lil_Bibby" title="Lil Bibby">Lil Bibby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chief_Keef" title="Chief Keef">Chief Keef</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before getting into music, Sheff G joined the 83 (Eight Trey) Gangsta Crips at age 12, and was imprisoned for 2 years for a felony <a href="/wiki/Gun_charge" class="mw-redirect" title="Gun charge">gun charge</a>. In 2015, he went back to prison for robbery for two years. Williams was released from prison in 2017. When asked about his childhood before rap, Williams said: "Growing up, all I was around was violence, in school, or hanging with my niggas, it was rough in the streets. I had to be protected out there, so I rolled with the 83s, we was in <a href="/wiki/Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="Flatbush, Brooklyn">Flatbush</a> especially on my block, I was a block baby you heard like niggas kno my body. I would never think rapping would be n my future. I remember in 2014, at 15, we was&#32;&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">sic</a></i>&#93; growing up watching <a href="/wiki/GS9" title="GS9">GS9</a> the G-Stone Crips get big, and they from the 90s on the East side, and I would be like 'damn, imagine I'm like that.' But it was a dream then."
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheff_G&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Williams was influenced by Chicago Drill rappers <a href="/wiki/Chief_Keef" title="Chief Keef">Chief Keef</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Bibby" title="Lil Bibby">Lil Bibby</a> and <a href="/wiki/G_Herbo" title="G Herbo">G Herbo</a>. In 2017, his single "No Suburban (produced by AXL Beats)" went viral and he was credited as one of the pioneers of the Brooklyn drill music movement.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He made a remix of "No Suburban" with <a href="/wiki/GS9" title="GS9">GS9</a> affiliate rapper <a href="/wiki/Corey_Finesse" class="mw-redirect" title="Corey Finesse">Corey Finesse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-respect_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-respect-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lemonwire_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lemonwire-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sheff G, <a href="/wiki/Sleepy_Hallow" title="Sleepy Hallow">Sleepy Hallow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corey_Finesse" class="mw-redirect" title="Corey Finesse">Corey Finesse</a> were previously managed by former <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a> football player, <a href="/wiki/Junior_Galette" title="Junior Galette">Junior Galette’s</a> NuLa Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-lemonwire_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lemonwire-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-respect_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-respect-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, he released his mixtape called <i>The Unluccy Luccy Kid</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> His label Winners Circle Entertainment was founded in the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2020, he released his debut studio album called <i>One and Only</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> His single "No Suburban, Pt. 2" went viral with over 21 million streams.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It was later certified Gold by <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> in 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2020, Winners Circle Entertainment began a partnership with <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-complex_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Sheff G and Sleepy Hallow released the single "Tip Toe" produced by Great John, using a sample of <a href="/wiki/Tiny_Tim_(musician)" title="Tiny Tim (musician)">Tiny Tim</a>'s version of "<a href="/wiki/Tiptoe_Through_the_Tulips" title="Tiptoe Through the Tulips">Tiptoe Through the Tulips</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-complex_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It was the second Sheff G single to be certified Gold by RIAA.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 16, 2020, he released the mixtape <i>Proud Of Me Now</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Throughout 2021, a multitude of singles would release including "On Go" featuring <a href="/wiki/Polo_G" title="Polo G">Polo G</a> in July<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and "Run It Up" featuring Sleepy Hallow and <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_Wit_da_Hoodie" class="mw-redirect" title="A Boogie Wit da Hoodie">A Boogie Wit da Hoodie</a> in August.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_controversy">Legal controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheff_G&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legal controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In high school, Williams was arrested and went on probation after firing gunshots at Brooklyn’s Kings Plaza.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
One week before he turned 16, in 2014, he was arrested for the first time and booked in for fighting.
</p><p>On November 19, 2015, Williams was incarcerated for one year and four months in jail for a felony gun charge and robbery. Williams also stated the last time he was incarcerated was in October 2017 to January 2018, a brief stint which he explains was the reason for his break on music.
</p><p>On July 15, 2021, Williams was arrested in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, New York for second-degree gun possession. His bail was remanded, meaning he sat behind bars until his court hearing on August 18, 2021. He pleaded guilty on October 20, 2021, and was sentenced to two years in prison. He is currently serving his sentence at Bare Hill Correctional Facility.
</p>
