<p><b>Kiam Akasi Holley</b> (born February 8, 1976), better known by his stage name <b>Capone</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a> known as one half of the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast hip hop</a> duo <a href="/wiki/Capone-N-Noreaga" title="Capone-N-Noreaga">Capone-N-Noreaga</a> (C-N-N), alongside friend and fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/N.O.R.E." title="N.O.R.E.">N.O.R.E.</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capone_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Holley spent some time in jail after the release of C-N-N's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/The_War_Report" title="The War Report">The War Report</a>.</i> When he was released, he and <a href="/wiki/N.O.R.E." title="N.O.R.E.">N.O.R.E.</a> recorded their second album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Reunion_(Capone-N-Noreaga_album)" title="The Reunion (Capone-N-Noreaga album)">The Reunion</a></i>, but shortly after releasing the album, Capone was sent back to jail.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2004, he recorded the single "I Need Speed", for exclusive use in the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Need_for_Speed:_Underground_2" title="Need for Speed: Underground 2">Need for Speed: Underground 2</a></i>, which was featured on the game's soundtrack. In early 2005, <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> released Capone from his contract while retaining Noreaga, leaving the group's status in disarray. Capone released his debut album titled <i>Pain, Time &amp; Glory</i> in 2005, but it received poor sales. He also appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Metta_World_Peace" class="mw-redirect" title="Metta World Peace">Ron Artest</a> album <i>My World</i>.
</p><p>In 2020 Capone released collaborative album entitled "Guidelines" with one half of the Dogg Pound <a href="/wiki/Daz_Dillinger" title="Daz Dillinger">Daz Dillinger</a> under <a href="/wiki/Empire_Distribution" title="Empire Distribution">Empire Distribution</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
