<p><b>Asher Paul Roth</b> (born August 11, 1985) is an American rapper. He is best known for his debut single "<a href="/wiki/I_Love_College" title="I Love College">I Love College</a>". Roth released his debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Asleep_in_the_Bread_Aisle" title="Asleep in the Bread Aisle">Asleep in the Bread Aisle</a></i>, on April 20, 2009, by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Motown" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Motown">Universal Motown</a>, <a href="/wiki/SRC_Records" title="SRC Records">SRC</a>, and <a href="/wiki/School_Boy_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="School Boy Records">School Boy Records</a>; the latter of which was launched by Roth's former manager <a href="/wiki/Scooter_Braun" title="Scooter Braun">Scooter Braun</a>. Roth later left Schoolboy Records in late 2012, due to creative differences with Braun. In 2013, Roth signed with <a href="/wiki/Indie_record_label" class="mw-redirect" title="Indie record label">indie record label</a> Federal Prism, on which he released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/RetroHash" title="RetroHash">RetroHash</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span></h2>
<h3><span id="1985.E2.80.932007:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1985–2007:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings">1985–2007: Early life and career beginnings</span></h3>
<p>Roth was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Morrisville,_Bucks_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Morrisville, Bucks County, Pennsylvania">Morrisville, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The Billboard article says Roth is native to Morristown, not Morrisville. (September 2021)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is of <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jewish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> ancestry.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Philadelphia_Weekly_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Philadelphia_Weekly-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His mother, Elizabeth (<i>née</i> McConnell), is a <a href="/wiki/Yoga" title="Yoga">yoga</a> instructor, and his father, David Roth, is the executive director of a design firm.<sup id="cite_ref-timeout_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeout-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ref0924_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ref0924-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nytimesref09_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimesref09-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hhlive_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hhlive-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Pennsbury_High_School" title="Pennsbury High School">Pennsbury High School</a>. After graduating from high school, he entered <a href="/wiki/West_Chester_University" title="West Chester University">West Chester University</a> and became an Elementary Education major, while continuing to record verses over the popular <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">production</a> of other rappers.
</p><p>During his sophomore year, he posted some of his verses on his <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> page and sent a friend request to <a href="/wiki/Scooter_Braun" title="Scooter Braun">Scooter Braun</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>-based promoter and former VP of Marketing for <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a>'s <a href="/wiki/So_So_Def_Recordings" title="So So Def Recordings">So So Def Recordings</a>. One week after speaking to Braun, Roth flew down to Atlanta and was immediately signed by Braun, who subsequently became his manager.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.932010:_Asleep_in_the_Bread_Aisle"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–2010:_Asleep_in_the_Bread_Aisle">2008–2010: <i>Asleep in the Bread Aisle</i></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Asleep_in_the_Bread_Aisle" title="Asleep in the Bread Aisle">Asleep in the Bread Aisle</a></div>
<p>After linking up with Braun, Roth moved to Atlanta to pursue a hip-hop career full-time. As industry buzz grew, Roth was courted by a number of labels, including <a href="/wiki/SRC_Records" title="SRC Records">SRC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam">Def Jam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AllHipHop_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllHipHop-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  Roth would eventually sign a joint venture between Braun's Schoolboy and <a href="/wiki/Steve_Rifkind" title="Steve Rifkind">Steve Rifkind</a>, chairman of <a href="/wiki/SRC_Records" title="SRC Records">SRC</a>/Universal Records. On June 13, 2008, via the internet, Roth released his first professional release, the <a href="/wiki/Don_Cannon" title="Don Cannon">Don Cannon</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a>-helmed <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, <i>The Greenhouse Effect Vol. 1</i>. This resulted in Roth being the second white rapper to be featured on Drama and Cannon's <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_Grillz" class="mw-redirect" title="Gangsta Grillz">Gangsta Grillz</a> series. Roth subsequently began recording his major-label debut album. In late 2008, it was revealed Roth was included in <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)"><i>XXL</i></a>'s 2009 annual <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)#Top_10_Freshmen" title="XXL (magazine)">Freshman Class</a>, and was featured on the cover alongside fellow up-and-coming rappers <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>, <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Hamilton_(rapper)" title="Charles Hamilton (rapper)">Charles Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cory_Gunz" title="Cory Gunz">Cory Gunz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blu_(rapper)" title="Blu (rapper)">Blu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Factz" title="Mickey Factz">Mickey Factz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hood" title="Ace Hood">Ace Hood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Curren$y" class="mw-redirect" title="Curren$y">Curren$y</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Roth's debut single "<a href="/wiki/I_Love_College" title="I Love College">I Love College</a>", was released in January 2009 and received many hits across North America. The song also served as his debut album <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a>. His second single "<a href="/wiki/Lark_on_my_Go-Kart" class="mw-redirect" title="Lark on my Go-Kart">Lark on my Go-Kart</a>", was released March 24, 2009. After the two singles were released, Roth's debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Asleep_in_the_Bread_Aisle" title="Asleep in the Bread Aisle">Asleep in the Bread Aisle</a></i> was released on April 20, 2009, in honor of <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_culture" title="Cannabis culture">cannabis subculture</a>'s <a href="/wiki/420_(cannabis_culture)" title="420 (cannabis culture)">420 holiday</a>. During the summer of 2009, Roth joined fellow <i>XXL</i> freshmen alumni <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a> and <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a>, for 'The Great Hangover' <a href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">concert tour</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Roth also joined <a href="/wiki/Blink-182" title="Blink-182">Blink-182</a> in the second half of their reunion tour in the fall of 2009.
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932012:_The_Rawth_EP_and_other_projects"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2012:_The_Rawth_EP_and_other_projects">2010–2012: <i>The Rawth EP</i> and other projects</span></h3>
<p>After touring in 2009 and releasing a second mixtape titled <i>Seared Foie Gras with Quince and Cranberry</i> in March 2010,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Roth began working on his second studio album, then-titled <i>The Spaghetti Tree</i>. From his friend Boyder's YouTube page, he was seen working with high-profile record producers <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nottz_Raw" class="mw-redirect" title="Nottz Raw">Nottz Raw</a>. While working with Nottz, the two of them decided to collaborate on a project together, due to having a number of records rejected by the label due to <a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">sampling</a> and copyright issues.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The result was an eight track <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP), solely produced by Nottz, entitled <i>The Rawth EP</i>. The EP, which features appearances from Colin Munroe, D.A. (of <a href="/wiki/Chester_French" title="Chester French">Chester French</a>), <a href="/wiki/Rhymefest" title="Rhymefest">Rhymefest</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kardinal_Offishall" title="Kardinal Offishall">Kardinal Offishall</a>, was released December 27, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 26, 2010, Roth released "<a href="/wiki/G.R.I.N.D_(Get_Ready_It%27s_a_New_Day)" title="G.R.I.N.D (Get Ready It&#39;s a New Day)">G.R.I.N.D (Get Ready It's a New Day)</a>", the initial first single from <i>The Spaghetti Tree</i>, and promoted the song by traveling to different radio stations across the country. In a July 2010 <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> interview, while speaking on <i>The Spaghetti Tree</i>, Roth revealed four tracks from the album. One was said to feature West Coast rapper <a href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Game (rapper)">Game</a>, one produced by <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a> and another was revealed to be titled "Run it Back".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On May 25, 2011, it was announced that Roth's second studio album would no longer be called <i>The Spaghetti Tree</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-NahRight_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NahRight-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On July 19, 2011, Roth released a song titled "Last Man Standing", as a single. The song, which features African-American singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a>, was featured on the soundtrack to the 2011 video game <i><a href="/wiki/Madden_NFL_12" title="Madden NFL 12">Madden NFL 12</a></i>.
</p><p>In an interview with Rikki Martinez of <i><a href="/wiki/Power_106" class="mw-redirect" title="Power 106">Power 106</a></i>, Roth announced he would be releasing a mixtape titled <i>Pabst &amp; Jazz</i> and revealed the new title of his upcoming second album to be <i>Is This Too Orange?</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2Dope_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2Dope-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On November 11, 2011, he released the first offering off <i>Pabst &amp; Jazz</i>, a song titled "Common Knowledge". In November 2011, Roth signed with <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> through the resurface of defunct label <a href="/wiki/Loud_Records" title="Loud Records">Loud Records</a>, which is now still a subsidiary label of <a href="/wiki/SRC_Records" title="SRC Records">SRC Records</a>. In April 2012, Roth revealed he was once again working with producer Nottz Raw, as well as <a href="/wiki/Blink_182" class="mw-redirect" title="Blink 182">Blink 182</a> drummer <a href="/wiki/Travis_Barker" title="Travis Barker">Travis Barker</a>, on an EP titled <i>Rawther</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>  In late 2012, Roth revealed he scrapped the <i>Is This Too Orange?</i> title for his second album, out of respect for Def Jam label mate <a href="/wiki/Frank_Ocean" title="Frank Ocean">Frank Ocean</a>'s debut <i><a href="/wiki/Channel_Orange" title="Channel Orange">Channel Orange</a></i> (2012).
</p>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932016:_RetroHash"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2016:_RetroHash">2013–2016: <i>RetroHash</i></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/RetroHash" title="RetroHash">RetroHash</a></div>
<p>On June 5, 2013, Roth announced he would be reuniting with DJ Drama and Don Cannon, to release the sequel to his critically acclaimed mixtape <i>The Greenhouse Effect</i><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <i>The Greenhouse Effect Vol. 2</i> was released on June 25, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In December 2013, Roth revealed his long-awaited and often-delayed second studio album is titled <i>RetroHash</i> which was released on April 22, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In a December interview, on a <i>Juan Epstein</i> podcast with <a href="/wiki/Peter_Rosenberg" title="Peter Rosenberg">Peter Rosenberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cipha_Sounds" title="Cipha Sounds">Cipha Sounds</a>, Roth announced he is still planning on releasing his collaborative EP <i>Rawther</i>, with Nottz Raw and Travis Barker; as well as an EP with renowned hip hop producer <a href="/wiki/Pete_Rock" title="Pete Rock">Pete Rock</a>, tentatively titled <i>Pete Roth</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Upon its release, <i>RetroHash</i> debuted at number 45 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, with first-week sales of 6,100 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In March 2016, Roth announced that he is preparing his third studio album <i>Red Hot Revival</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.93present:_Flowers_on_the_Weekend"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–present:_Flowers_on_the_Weekend">2016–present: <i>Flowers on the Weekend</i></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Flowers_on_the_Weekend" title="Flowers on the Weekend">Flowers on the Weekend</a></div>
<p>In 2016 Asher Roth finally moved back to his home state of Pennsylvania after many years living away in Atlanta, New York and Los Angeles. He reconnected with an old musical friend called Rob Deckhart, and they stated working on a number of songs. Finally, over the years, they came up with the album <i>Flowers On the Weekend</i> that was released in April 2020.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span></h3>
<p>Asher Roth cites <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> among his influences in hip hop, mostly by Jay-Z's "<a href="/wiki/Hard_Knock_Life_(Ghetto_Anthem)" title="Hard Knock Life (Ghetto Anthem)">Hard Knock Life</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Growing up, Roth was exposed to little <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> in his family, with his parents preferring "<a href="/wiki/The_Temptations" title="The Temptations">The Temptations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earth,_Wind_%26_Fire" title="Earth, Wind &amp; Fire">Earth, Wind &amp; Fire</a> ... <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dire_Straits" title="Dire Straits">Dire Straits</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-XXL_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> According to Roth:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>The first CD I ever bought was <a href="/wiki/Dave_Matthews_Band" title="Dave Matthews Band">Dave Matthews Band</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Crash_(Dave_Matthews_Band_album)" title="Crash (Dave Matthews Band album)">Crash</a></i>... that is how suburban I am... I finally got into hip hop in '98 when I heard the <a href="/wiki/Hard_Knock_Life_(Ghetto_Anthem)" title="Hard Knock Life (Ghetto Anthem)">Annie sample</a> with <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>.... when I wrote my "<a href="/wiki/A_Milli" title="A Milli">A Milli</a>" <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_music" title="Freestyle music">freestyle</a>, that was me listening to 10 years of hip hop and not relating to it at all. Like, damn, I don't sell <a href="/wiki/Cocaine" title="Cocaine">coke</a>. Damn, I don't have cars or 25-inch <a href="/wiki/Rim_(wheel)" title="Rim (wheel)">rims</a>. I don't have guns. I finally got to a point where I had the confidence to do this thing myself, and I was making music for me. And it turns out, a lot of people feel the same way I do.<sup id="cite_ref-Vibe_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>He also stated:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942"><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Hip-hop has always been very influential in the 'burbs, [but] it's just a matter of where we could relate to it. You find a lot of kids that are really confused. You look at them and they're dressed out of character. They don't look right. I figured out, I don't have to dress this way, but I can still love hip-hop.<sup id="cite_ref-Sixshot_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sixshot-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Roth does not consider himself <a href="/wiki/Jewish" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish">Jewish</a>, although his name sometimes leads people to believe that he is, and he has Jewish ancestry.<sup id="cite_ref-forward_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forward-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Roth is open about his use of <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">cannabis</a>. As stated in an interview conducted and filmed by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Vlad" title="DJ Vlad">DJ Vlad</a>, Roth explained his views about the legalization of cannabis: "Cigarettes don't do anything for you except kill you... I honestly don't believe [marijuana] is the gateway drug, because I use it, and I've never done anything else... I'm trying to be open about my pot smoking."<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
