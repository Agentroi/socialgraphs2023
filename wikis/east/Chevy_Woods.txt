<p><b>Kevin Lee</b> "<b>Chevy</b>" <b>Woods</b> (born May 13, 1981) is an American rapper and songwriter from <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania">Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania</a>. Throughout his career he has been affiliated with rapper <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> and his respective <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Gang_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Taylor Gang Records">Taylor Gang record label</a>, also serving as Khalifa's hypeman on tour. His first major appearance was on Wiz Khalifa's song "<a href="/wiki/Taylor_Gang_(song)" title="Taylor Gang (song)">Taylor Gang</a>". Woods has been on more than 20 tours and has released several mixtapes including <i>The Cookout</i> with Khalifa and the <i>Gangland</i> series.<sup id="cite_ref-paper_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chevy_Woods&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2003.E2.80.932010:_Early_career.2C_Taylor_Gang_formation_and_mixtapes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2003–2010:_Early_career,_Taylor_Gang_formation_and_mixtapes">2003–2010: Early career, Taylor Gang formation and mixtapes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chevy_Woods&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2003–2010: Early career, Taylor Gang formation and mixtapes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Woods started rapping as a child with friends using instrumentals for background music.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After graduating high school, he attended <a href="/wiki/Robert_Morris_University" title="Robert Morris University">Robert Morris University</a> as an athlete and grew his passion for rapping.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-post_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-post-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Woods became serious about rap when he met another musician who created and distributed his own mixtapes. He wanted to pursue music and began writing and rapping under the name Kev Tha Hustla.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2004, he met fellow <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania">Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, through Pennsylvania producers <a href="/wiki/I.D._Labs" class="mw-redirect" title="I.D. Labs">I.D. Labs</a>, who collaborated with Khalifa.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Woods and Khalifa formed a partnership, which led to the formation of the group <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Gang_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Taylor Gang Records">Taylor Gang</a> in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-post_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-post-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Woods released two mixtapes, <i>The Corner's Correspondent</i> in 2008 and <i>Animal</i> in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His first two mixtapes featured collaborations with Wiz Khalifa, <a href="/wiki/Rostrum_Records" title="Rostrum Records">Boaz</a> and members of Heavy Hustle Records. <a href="/wiki/Record_production" class="mw-redirect" title="Record production">Production</a> was primarily handled by I.D. Labs and <a href="/wiki/Sledgren" class="mw-redirect" title="Sledgren">Sledgren</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-datpiff1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datpiff1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On August 3, 2010, Woods was featured on the track “Paper Route” from Mac Miller's mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/K.I.D.S." title="K.I.D.S.">K.I.D.S.</a></i> In September 2010, Woods released his third official mixtape <i>Pilot Shit</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011:_Red_Cup_Music_and_The_Cookout">2011: <i>Red Cup Music</i> and <i>The Cookout</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chevy_Woods&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011: Red Cup Music and The Cookout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2011, Wiz Khalifa released his ninth mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Cabin_Fever_(mixtape)" title="Cabin Fever (mixtape)">Cabin Fever</a></i>, which featured Woods on three tracks, including "<a href="/wiki/Taylor_Gang_(song)" title="Taylor Gang (song)">Taylor Gang</a>", "Middle of You" and "Homicide".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  Woods released his fourth mixtape <i>Red Cup Music</i>, which featured guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, and production by ID Labs and <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a> in March 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Woods told <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>, the <i>Red Cup Music</i> was "really when I found a style for myself, some laid-back, cool music. Drink a little bit, smoke a little bit."<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Khalifa released the music video for the <a href="/wiki/Lex_Luger_(record_producer)" title="Lex Luger (record producer)">Lex Luger</a>-produced track "Taylor Gang" in April 2011, which became an <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> bonus track on Khalifa's debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Papers_(Wiz_Khalifa_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rolling Papers (Wiz Khalifa album)">Rolling Papers</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The song peaked at number 22 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Bubbling_Under_Hot_100_Singles" class="mw-redirect" title="Bubbling Under Hot 100 Singles">Bubbling Under Hot 100 Singles</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In April 2013, Khalifa went on tour in support of <i>Rolling Papers</i> with Woods and <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> This tour introduced Woods to Khalifa's mainstream audience.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In August 2011, Woods released a mixtape, titled <i>The Cookout</i>, which featured Wiz Khalifa.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx1_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Khalifa officially founded <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Gang_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Taylor Gang Records">Taylor Gang Records</a>, Woods was immediately added to the roster. <i>The Cookout</i> mixtape, produced by ID Labs, Sledgren, <a href="/wiki/Cardo_(record_producer)" title="Cardo (record producer)">Cardo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Key_Wane" title="Key Wane">Key Wane</a> and Big Jerm, was released in September 2011 and featured Wiz Khalifa on eight of the eleven tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape received positive reviews from <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i>, who praised the production, but mentioned Woods was overshadowed by Khalifa throughout the tape.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> However, <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> disagreed calling Woods the mixtape's star and praised his laid back flow.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012-2015:_Gangland_mixtape_series_and_The_48_Hunnid_Project">2012-2015: <i>Gangland</i> mixtape series and The 48 Hunnid Project</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chevy_Woods&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012-2015: Gangland mixtape series and The 48 Hunnid Project">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:ChevyWoodsDecember2013_2.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/ChevyWoodsDecember2013_2.jpg/150px-ChevyWoodsDecember2013_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="198" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/ChevyWoodsDecember2013_2.jpg/225px-ChevyWoodsDecember2013_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/ChevyWoodsDecember2013_2.jpg/300px-ChevyWoodsDecember2013_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2578" data-file-height="3401" /></a><figcaption>Chevy Woods performing on The Smoker's Club Tour in December 2013.</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2012, Woods released a new mixtape, titled <i>Gangland</i>, which consisted of dark material influenced by Woods early life on the streets, and the television show <i><a href="/wiki/Gangland_(TV_series)" title="Gangland (TV series)">Gangland</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Woods along with the rest of Taylor Gang Records, <a href="/wiki/Lola_Monroe" class="mw-redirect" title="Lola Monroe">Lola Monroe</a>, Juicy J, and Wiz Khalifa appeared on the May 2012 cover of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Source (magazine)">The Source</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> <i>Gangland</i>, produced by Lex Luger, I.D. Labs, Cardo, <a href="/wiki/Sonny_Digital" title="Sonny Digital">Sonny Digital</a>, <a href="/wiki/Key_Wane" title="Key Wane">Key Wane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sledgren" class="mw-redirect" title="Sledgren">Sledgren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Fraud" title="Harry Fraud">Harry Fraud</a>, was released in June 2012 and featured Taylor Gang's Wiz Khalifa, Lola Monroe, Juicy J, Tuki Carter, as well as artists <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Boy" title="Soulja Boy">Soulja Boy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trae_Tha_Truth" class="mw-redirect" title="Trae Tha Truth">Trae Tha Truth</a>, and Young Jerz.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In a <i>XXL</i> review of <i>Gangland</i>, the magazine stated, "Over the past two years, Taylor Gang's Chevy Woods has gradually built up his name with mixtapes like <i>Red Cup Music</i> and <i>The Cookout</i>. Progressing with each release, Wiz's budding sidekick has shown he can hang high without resting on the back of his co-pilot. On his latest offering, <i>Gang Land</i>, Chevy flies high and gives the world an in depth look into his life as a Taylor Gang aviator."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
Following the release of <i>Gangland</i>, Woods worked on his next mixtape and his debut studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphopii_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphopii-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Woods went on the first Under the Influence of Music <a href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">concert tour</a> with Khalifa, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> in support of <i>Gangland</i> in July and August 2012, .<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He toured with the rest of Taylor Gang Records on the 2050 Tour from October through December 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> During the tour, Woods announced that his next mixtape would be titled <i>Gangland 2</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, he toured on the second Under the Influence tour with Khalifa, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Mob" title="ASAP Mob">ASAP Mob</a>, <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Badass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>, Pro Era, Berner, and <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_James" title="Trinidad James">Trinidad James</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2013, Woods told <i>HotNewHipHop</i>, his debut album would contain production by Sledgren and C4, the track list for <i>Gangland 2</i> was finalized, and it would be hosted by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphopii_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphopii-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Woods released the cover artwork for <i>Gangland 2</i> in September 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He toured North America with Joey Badass, <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul" title="Ab-Soul">Ab-Soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Underachievers" title="The Underachievers">The Underachievers</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pro_Era" title="Pro Era">Pro Era</a> on the fourth annual The Smoker's Club tour in October and November 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Woods released <i>Gangland 2</i>, a mixtape featuring <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from Wiz Khalifa, Juicy J, Trinidad James, <a href="/wiki/Young_Scooter" title="Young Scooter">Young Scooter</a>, <a href="/wiki/King_Los" class="mw-redirect" title="King Los">King Los</a> and Compton Menace, via Taylor Gang Records in October 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Woods released his first retail single "30 Deep" via Taylor Gang in April 2014 and announced in September 2014 that he would release a retail EP.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> He made two appearances on Wiz Khalifa's third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Blacc_Hollywood" title="Blacc Hollywood">Blacc Hollywood</a></i>, on "Still Down" and spoke on the intro to the album.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Woods toured with rapper <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Gates" title="Kevin Gates">Kevin Gates</a> in July and August 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2015, Woods released the third installment of his <i>Gangland</i> series featuring production from Ricky P, Zaytoven, Sonny Digital, Ty Dolla Sign and more.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> In August 2015, Woods produced and released <i>The 48 Hunnid Project</i>, his first retail release.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The project named for the neighborhood where Woods was raised, took 12 years to create and encompassed his experiences from being a child through adulthood.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> He also toured with Wiz Khalifa on the Boys of Zummer Tour with <a href="/wiki/Fall_Out_Boy" title="Fall Out Boy">Fall Out Boy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016-2017:_TGOD_Volume_1.2C_Gang_Sh.2At_Only.2C_and_Thanks_For_Everything"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016-2017:_TGOD_Volume_1,_Gang_Sh*t_Only,_and_Thanks_For_Everything">2016-2017: TGOD Volume 1, Gang Sh*t Only, and Thanks For Everything</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chevy_Woods&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2016-2017: TGOD Volume 1, Gang Sh*t Only, and Thanks For Everything">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 26, 2016, Chevy woods released a 4 track extended play called And The Story Goes. On October 11, 2016 <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Gang_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Taylor Gang Records">Taylor Gang Records</a> released a compilation mixtape titled TGOD Volume 1 with all the members including <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berner_(rapper)" title="Berner (rapper)">Berner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_$ign" class="mw-redirect" title="Ty Dolla $ign">Ty Dolla $ign</a> among others. On November 11, 2016, Gang Sh*t Only was released with features from Wiz Khalifa, Hardo, Stevie B, FTR Drama and <a href="/wiki/Rich_The_Kid" class="mw-redirect" title="Rich The Kid">Rich The Kid</a> with production <a href="/wiki/Honorable_C.N.O.T.E." title="Honorable C.N.O.T.E.">Honorable C.N.O.T.E.</a>, <a href="/wiki/808_Mafia" title="808 Mafia">808 Mafia</a>, <a href="/wiki/K.E._on_the_Track" title="K.E. on the Track">K.E. on the Track</a> among others. On May 13, 2017, Chevy Woods released a 14 track mixtape titled Freemix 1.5 Reloaded. Exactly What They Want was released on September 28, 2017, with features from Wiz Khalifa, Bigga Rankin and <a href="/wiki/K_Camp" title="K Camp">K Camp</a> with production from K.E. on the Track, Yung Lan, <a href="/wiki/Stevie_B" title="Stevie B">Stevie B</a> among others. Thanks For Everything was released on December 23, 2017, with features from Wiz Khalifa on the tracks All Love and Joint &amp; A Lighter with production from K.E. on the Track, Yung Lan, <a href="/wiki/OG_Parker" title="OG Parker">OG Parker</a> among others.
</p>
<h3><span id="2018-present:_81.2C_Lewis_Park_Legend_and_New_90.27s"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018-present:_81,_Lewis_Park_Legend_and_New_90's">2018-present: <i>81</i>, <i>Lewis Park Legend</i> and <i>New 90's</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chevy_Woods&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2018-present: 81, Lewis Park Legend and New 90&#039;s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 15, 2018, Chevy Woods released the mixtape titled <i>81</i> with a feature from <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> on the track Bitch You Lyin and production from <a href="/wiki/K.E._on_the_Track" title="K.E. on the Track">K.E. on the Track</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tay_Keith" title="Tay Keith">Tay Keith</a>, Beat Mechanics among others. On December 19, 2018, Chevy Woods dropped a new mixtape titled <i>Lewis Park Legend</i>. On May 13, 2019, Chevy Woods released a new mixtape titled <i>New 90's</i>. On July 3, 2020, Chevy Woods dropped a debut album titled <i>Since Birth</i>.
</p>
