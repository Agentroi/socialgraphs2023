<p><b>Prakazrel Samuel Michel</b> (born October 19, 1972), known professionally as <b>Pras</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;p&#39; in &#39;pie&#39;">p</span><span title="&#39;r&#39; in &#39;rye&#39;">r</span><span title="/ɑː/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;father&#39;">ɑː</span><span title="&#39;z&#39; in &#39;zoom&#39;">z</span></span>/</a></span></span>), is an American rapper. He is best known as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/Fugees" title="Fugees">Fugees</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a>. After the Fugees, he earned two <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">Top 40</a> hits on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy</a>-nominated song "<a href="/wiki/Ghetto_Supastar_(That_Is_What_You_Are)" title="Ghetto Supastar (That Is What You Are)">Ghetto Supastar (That Is What You Are)</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ol%27_Dirty_Bastard" title="Ol&#39; Dirty Bastard">Ol' Dirty Bastard</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BDa" class="mw-redirect" title="Mýa">Mýa</a> from the film <i><a href="/wiki/Bulworth" title="Bulworth">Bulworth</a>,</i> and "<a href="/wiki/Avenues_(song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Avenues (song)">Avenues</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Refugee_Camp_All-Stars" title="Refugee Camp All-Stars">Refugee Camp All-Stars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ky-Mani_Marley" title="Ky-Mani Marley">Ky-Mani Marley</a>. He also collaborated with Jean and rock band <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a> on the 1998 remix of "<a href="/wiki/Another_One_Bites_the_Dust#Wyclef_Jean_remix" title="Another One Bites the Dust">Another One Bites the Dust</a>", which reached the top five on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>. In 2017, he won a <a href="/wiki/Daytime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Digital_Daytime_Drama_Series" title="Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Digital Daytime Drama Series">Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Digital Daytime Drama Series</a>, as a producer on the web series <a href="/wiki/The_Bay_(web_series)" title="The Bay (web series)"><i>The Bay</i></a>.
</p><p>In 2019, he began to face legal issues when he was charged with <a href="/wiki/Criminal_conspiracy" title="Criminal conspiracy">criminal conspiracy</a> for allegedly illegally donating to the <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama_2012_presidential_campaign" title="Barack Obama 2012 presidential campaign">Barack Obama 2012 presidential campaign</a><sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Stack-Charged_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stack-Charged-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and lobbying the <a href="/wiki/Trump_administration" class="mw-redirect" title="Trump administration">Trump administration</a> to deter investigations into the <a href="/wiki/1Malaysia_Development_Berhad_scandal" title="1Malaysia Development Berhad scandal">1MDB scandal</a> involving fugitive Malaysian  businessman <a href="/wiki/Jho_Low" title="Jho Low">Jho Low</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On April 26, 2023, Pras was found guilty of 10 criminal counts in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_District_of_Columbia" title="United States District Court for the District of Columbia">U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Justice_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Justice-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CNN_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AP_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pras&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Michel was born in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, and raised in <a href="/wiki/Irvington,_New_Jersey" title="Irvington, New Jersey">Irvington, New Jersey</a>. He is <a href="/wiki/Haitian_Americans" title="Haitian Americans">Haitian-American</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Pras cultivated an early interest in music. When he was 15, he met <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a> while both attended <a href="/wiki/Columbia_High_School_(New_Jersey)" title="Columbia High School (New Jersey)">Columbia High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Maplewood,_New_Jersey" title="Maplewood, New Jersey">Maplewood, New Jersey</a>. In 1988, Pras introduced Hill to <a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a>. Pras, Jean, and Hill began to rehearse under the guidance of <a href="/wiki/Kool_and_the_Gang" class="mw-redirect" title="Kool and the Gang">Kool and the Gang</a>'s producer, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Bell_(musician)" title="Ronald Bell (musician)">Ronald Khalis Bell</a>, and subsequently they formed a musical group called The Rap Translators in 1989 (also known as Tranzlator Crew).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pras&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pras&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1994, The Fugees recorded their first album <i><a href="/wiki/Blunted_on_Reality" title="Blunted on Reality">Blunted on Reality</a></i>, under the supervision of Ronald Khalis Bell. Before and during this, Pras attended <a href="/wiki/Rutgers_University" title="Rutgers University">Rutgers University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a>, pursuing a double major in Philosophy and Psychology.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 1996, The Fugees achieved historic crossover success with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Score_(Fugees_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Score (Fugees album)">The Score</a></i>, which went multi-platinum.
</p><p>Pras has also forged a successful solo career, beginning with an international hit single from his first full-length solo LP, "<a href="/wiki/Ghetto_Supastar_(That_Is_What_You_Are)" title="Ghetto Supastar (That Is What You Are)">Ghetto Supastar (That Is What You Are)</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BDa" class="mw-redirect" title="Mýa">Mýa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ol%27_Dirty_Bastard" title="Ol&#39; Dirty Bastard">Ol' Dirty Bastard</a>. "Ghetto Superstar" became a top ten single in 1999, and the eighth most played single. The song earned Pras a performance at the <a href="/wiki/World_Music_Awards" title="World Music Awards">World Music Awards</a>. The hit single was included in the soundtrack for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Bulworth" title="Bulworth">Bulworth</a></i>. "Ghetto Supastar" spent eight weeks in the UK Top 5, peaking at No. 2 in July 1998,<sup id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and reached US No. 15 a month later. "Blue Angels", from the same album, was a UK Top 10 hit, reaching No. 6 in November.<sup id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pras also featured on the track "Turn You On" by Swedish artist <a href="/wiki/Denise_Lopez_(Swedish_singer)" title="Denise Lopez (Swedish singer)">DeDe</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> which was released in 2007. "Turn You On" was also written and produced by Pras. After nine weeks on Sweden's single chart, it peaked at No. 2. He also featured "Pushin'" from the album <i><a href="/wiki/Equalize" title="Equalize">Equalize</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Swami_(band)" title="Swami (band)">Swami</a>, which was released in 2007.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pras&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After his <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo</a> debut in the 1999 feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Mystery_Men" title="Mystery Men">Mystery Men</a></i>, Pras gained an interest in Hollywood. In 2000, Pras starred in the <a href="/wiki/New_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="New Line">New Line</a> film, <i><a href="/wiki/Turn_It_Up_(film)" title="Turn It Up (film)">Turn It Up</a>.</i> In early 2002, he appeared in the Sony and Urban World release <i>Higher Ed,</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Go_for_Broke_(2002_film)" title="Go for Broke (2002 film)">Go for Broke</a></i> in which he starred and produced. He also co-starred in three <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" class="mw-redirect" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a> films in 2007. These included <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mutant_Chronicles" class="mw-redirect" title="The Mutant Chronicles">The Mutant Chronicles</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2006, Pras created <i>Skid Row, Los Angeles</i>, a documentary account of his nine-day experience posing as a homeless person living in <a href="/wiki/Skid_Row,_Los_Angeles,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Skid Row, Los Angeles, California">downtown Los Angeles</a>. Using a hidden camera, Pras captured the reality of homelessness. The film was produced by Teryn Fogel.<sup id="cite_ref-Skid_Row_IMDB_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Skid_Row_IMDB-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <i>Skid Row</i> was released on August 24, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-Skid_Row,_83%_Rotten_Tomatoes_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Skid_Row,_83%_Rotten_Tomatoes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Pras travelled to <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a> to film the documentary <i>Paper Dreams</i> which examines <a href="/wiki/Piracy_off_the_coast_of_Somalia" title="Piracy off the coast of Somalia">piracy off the African coast</a>. During filming, pirates invaded the ship he was on, the <i><a href="/wiki/MV_Maersk_Alabama" class="mw-redirect" title="MV Maersk Alabama">MV Maersk Alabama</a></i>, and took the captain of the ship hostage. Due to be released in 2014, the documentary remains unfinished due to a legal dispute.<sup id="cite_ref-TMZ_Fugees_Rapper_Pras_Michel_Sues_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TMZ_Fugees_Rapper_Pras_Michel_Sues-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, Pras completed a documentary, <i>Sweet Micky For President</i>, which chronicles the rise of Haitian musician <a href="/wiki/Michel_Martelly" title="Michel Martelly">Michel Martelly</a>, through his election to fight corruption as President of Haiti, an election campaign that was strongly endorsed by Pras. The film had its world premiere at the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Slamdance_Film_Festival" title="Slamdance Film Festival">Slamdance Film Festival</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2017, he won a <a href="/wiki/Daytime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Digital_Daytime_Drama_Series" title="Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Digital Daytime Drama Series">Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Digital Daytime Drama Series</a> as a producer on the web series <a href="/wiki/The_Bay_(web_series)" title="The Bay (web series)"><i>The Bay</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_activism">Political activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pras&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Political activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013, Michel became a "top initial donor"<sup id="cite_ref-Bloom_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloom-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> to <a href="/wiki/Organizing_for_Action" title="Organizing for Action">Organizing for Action</a>, a political advocacy group formed by Obama associates in January 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_problems">Legal problems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pras&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Legal problems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/1Malaysia_Development_Berhad_scandal" title="1Malaysia Development Berhad scandal">1Malaysia Development Berhad scandal</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">United States Department of Justice</a> (DOJ) indicted Pras on May 10, 2019, for his part in an alleged <a href="/wiki/Criminal_conspiracy" title="Criminal conspiracy">criminal conspiracy</a> with Malaysian financier <a href="/wiki/Jho_Low" title="Jho Low">Jho Low</a>. The DOJ alleged that between June 2012 and November 2012, Michel aided in the illegal transfer of approximately $865,000 from foreign entities into the <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama_2012_presidential_campaign" title="Barack Obama 2012 presidential campaign">Barack Obama 2012 presidential campaign</a>. The DOJ alleged that these funds were disguised as purportedly legitimate contributions, but were stolen from the Malaysian <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_wealth_fund" title="Sovereign wealth fund">sovereign wealth fund</a> <a href="/wiki/1Malaysia_Development_Berhad" title="1Malaysia Development Berhad">1Malaysia Development Berhad</a> (1MDB).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Stack-Charged_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stack-Charged-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Fugee_Fugitive_FBI_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fugee_Fugitive_FBI-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Ultimately, the DOJ alleged that $21&#160;million in foreign campaign contributions were funneled into the 2012 US presidential election to benefit "Candidate A".<sup id="cite_ref-Stack-Charged_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stack-Charged-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>  The <i>Washington Post</i> and <i>The New York Times</i> identified "Candidate A" as Barack Obama.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Stack-Charged_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stack-Charged-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2021, Pras was charged by a federal grand jury for running a back-channel campaign to get the <a href="/wiki/Presidency_of_Donald_Trump" title="Presidency of Donald Trump">Trump administration</a> to drop <a href="/wiki/1Malaysia_Development_Berhad_scandal" title="1Malaysia Development Berhad scandal">an investigation</a> of Jho Low and the 1MDB investment company.<sup id="cite_ref-Fugee_Fugitive_FBI_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fugee_Fugitive_FBI-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was also accused of advocating for the extradition of a Chinese dissident, <a href="/wiki/Guo_Wengui" title="Guo Wengui">Guo Wengui</a>, from the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Fugee_Fugitive_FBI_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fugee_Fugitive_FBI-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In the plea documents of former DOJ employee George Higginbotham, Pras was accused of paying Republican fundraiser <a href="/wiki/Elliott_Broidy" title="Elliott Broidy">Elliott Broidy</a> and others to have Guo extradited to China. Though unnamed in the filings, Pras is "easily identified" due to linked cases and confirmation from sources close to the case.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Pras "vehemently and unequivocally" denied accusations related to Higginbotham's case.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2021<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pras&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, due to the charges against him, Pras cannot travel internationally.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In January 2022, Fugees cancelled their planned international reunion tour, citing the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 26, 2023, Pras was found guilty of 10 criminal counts in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_District_of_Columbia" title="United States District Court for the District of Columbia">U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Justice_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Justice-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CNN_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AP_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
