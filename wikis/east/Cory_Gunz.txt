<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cory_Gunz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At age 14, Gunz was first signed by <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Mottola" title="Tommy Mottola">Tommy Mottola</a> to <a href="/wiki/Casablanca_Records" title="Casablanca Records">Casablanca Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Dx_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dx-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> shepherded him into a joint venture through <a href="/wiki/The_Island_Def_Jam_Music_Group" title="The Island Def Jam Music Group">The Island Def Jam Music Group</a>. He was featured on a <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remix</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/If_It%27s_Lovin%27_That_You_Want" class="mw-redirect" title="If It&#39;s Lovin&#39; That You Want">If It's Lovin' That You Want</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Gunz mixtape <i>The Apprentice 3 – Season Finale</i> has amassed over 85,000 listens on the <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> website <a href="/wiki/DatPiff" title="DatPiff">DatPiff</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He also contributed a verse to the original version of <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/A_Milli" title="A Milli">A Milli</a>" before it was replaced with new verses by Wayne for the album version.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Gunz signed a contract with After Platinum Records. In early 2010, Gunz signed with <a href="/wiki/Young_Money_Entertainment" title="Young Money Entertainment">Young Money Entertainment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Universal_Motown" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Motown">Universal Motown</a>. Gunz has worked with actor and musician <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon" title="Nick Cannon">Nick Cannon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Cannon_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cannon-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, he was featured on Lil Wayne's single "<a href="/wiki/6_Foot_7_Foot" title="6 Foot 7 Foot">6 Foot 7 Foot</a>" from <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Carter_IV" title="Tha Carter IV">Tha Carter IV</a></i>. Also in 2011, Gunz starred on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s "Son of A Gun" a reality show that follows Gunz after and before he got signed by Young Money. The show features appearances from close friend <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon" title="Nick Cannon">Nick Cannon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It was announced that <a href="/wiki/Universal_Republic_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Republic Records">Universal Republic Records</a> was going defunct, all of the artists on the roster moved from the label including (Cory Gunz) was being moved to <a href="/wiki/Republic_Records" title="Republic Records">Republic Records</a> making the label itself revived.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Gunz was a featured artist on Dirti Diana's 2013 mixtape <i>R.I.P. To The Competition</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On July 16, 2013, he released his first mixtape in two years with <i>Datz WTF I'm Talkin Bout</i>, it featured guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, Charlie Rock, <a href="/wiki/Mack_Maine" title="Mack Maine">Mack Maine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a> among others.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In March 2014, Gunz revealed he was working on an EP titled <i>Kriminal Mind</i> and following its release he would release his debut studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cory_Gunz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cory_Gunz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 28, 2012, Gunz was arrested for possession of a gun and faced three and a half years in prison, which was lowered to a <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1154941027">.mw-parser-output .frac{white-space:nowrap}.mw-parser-output .frac .num,.mw-parser-output .frac .den{font-size:80%;line-height:0;vertical-align:super}.mw-parser-output .frac .den{vertical-align:sub}.mw-parser-output .sr-only{border:0;clip:rect(0,0,0,0);clip-path:polygon(0px 0px,0px 0px,0px 0px);height:1px;margin:-1px;overflow:hidden;padding:0;position:absolute;width:1px}</style><span class="frac" role="math">2<span class="sr-only">+</span><span class="num">1</span>&#8260;<span class="den">2</span></span>-year sentence.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Cory Gunz' father,  <a href="/wiki/Lord_Tariq_and_Peter_Gunz" title="Lord Tariq and Peter Gunz">Peter Gunz</a> admitted that he had encouraged his son to keep a low profile and stop releasing music for a while in the aftermath of his arrest. In an interview from December 17, 2013, Peter Gunz stated that Cory Gunz was trying to work on new records in Miami while on probation.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
