<p><b>M. K. Asante</b> (born November 3, 1982) is an American author, filmmaker, recording artist, and professor. He is the author of the 2013 best-selling memoir <a href="/wiki/Buck:_A_Memoir" title="Buck: A Memoir"><i>Buck</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-r1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-r1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-r2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-r2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M._K._Asante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Harare" title="Harare">Harare</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> and raised in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</a>, Asante is the son of scholar <a href="/wiki/Molefi_Kete_Asante" title="Molefi Kete Asante">Molefi Kete Asante</a> and choreographer <a href="/wiki/Kariamu_Welsh" title="Kariamu Welsh">Kariamu Welsh</a>.
</p><p>Asante is a graduate of <a href="/wiki/The_Crefeld_School" title="The Crefeld School">The Crefeld School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He studied film and literature at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a>, <a href="/wiki/School_of_Oriental_and_African_Studies" class="mw-redirect" title="School of Oriental and African Studies">School of Oriental and African Studies</a>, earned a BA in Africana Studies and English from <a href="/wiki/Lafayette_College" title="Lafayette College">Lafayette College</a>, and an M.F.A. in Screenwriting from <a href="/wiki/UCLA_School_of_Theater_Film_and_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="UCLA School of Theater Film and Television">UCLA School of Theater Film and Television</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M._K._Asante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Books">Books</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M._K._Asante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Books">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:BUCK-BY-MK-ASANTE.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/BUCK-BY-MK-ASANTE.jpg/180px-BUCK-BY-MK-ASANTE.jpg" decoding="async" width="180" height="266" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/BUCK-BY-MK-ASANTE.jpg/270px-BUCK-BY-MK-ASANTE.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/BUCK-BY-MK-ASANTE.jpg/360px-BUCK-BY-MK-ASANTE.jpg 2x" data-file-width="485" data-file-height="718" /></a><figcaption>Cover of <i>Buck: A Memoir</i> (<a href="/wiki/Random_House" title="Random House">Random House</a>) by MK Asante</figcaption></figure>
<p>Asante is the author of four books, most notably <i><a href="/wiki/Buck_(book)" class="mw-redirect" title="Buck (book)">Buck</a></i> (2013), a memoir about his troubled youth in Philadelphia. <i>Buck</i> was selected as a <a href="/wiki/Barnes_%26_Noble" title="Barnes &amp; Noble">Barnes &amp; Noble</a> Discover Great New Writers pick and made the <i><a href="/wiki/Washington_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington Post">Washington Post</a></i> bestseller list in 2014 and 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It was also included on the <a href="/wiki/In_the_Margins_Award" title="In the Margins Award">In the Margins</a> Book List in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Poet <a href="/wiki/Maya_Angelou" title="Maya Angelou">Maya Angelou</a>, who mentored Asante, described <i>Buck</i> as "a story of surviving and thriving with passion, compassion, wit, and style."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/HarperCollins" title="HarperCollins">HarperCollins</a> publishing imprint Amistad Press has announced the publication of <i>Nephew: A Memoir</i> by Asante, to be released May 21, 2024.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Films">Films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M._K._Asante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Asante is a <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Institute" title="Sundance Institute">Sundance Institute</a> Feature Film Fellow for the movie adaptation of his memoir <i>Buck</i>. Asante wrote and produced the 2005 documentary <i><a href="/wiki/500_Years_Later" title="500 Years Later">500 Years Later</a></i>, a documentary about slavery which received the Breaking the Chains Award from <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>. Asante directed and produced <i><a href="/wiki/The_Black_Candle" title="The Black Candle">The Black Candle</a></i> (2012), a documentary about <a href="/wiki/Kwanzaa" title="Kwanzaa">Kwanzaa</a>, co-written and narrated by <a href="/wiki/Maya_Angelou" title="Maya Angelou">Maya Angelou</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lectures_and_essays">Lectures and essays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M._K._Asante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lectures and essays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Asante has delivered numerous lectures, including the <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> Master's Tea; <a href="/wiki/Vanderbilt_University" title="Vanderbilt University">Vanderbilt University</a> Walter R. Murray Jr. Lecture; <a href="/wiki/Southern_Methodist_University" title="Southern Methodist University">Southern Methodist University</a> Willis M. Tate Distinguished Lecture; and commencement addresses at <a href="/wiki/UCLA" class="mw-redirect" title="UCLA">UCLA</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wisconsin" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Wisconsin">University of Wisconsin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arizona_State_University" title="Arizona State University">Arizona State University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vassar_College" title="Vassar College">Vassar</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>.
</p><p>Asante is featured in <i>Changing America: 1968 and Beyond</i>, a permanent exhibit at the Smithsonian <a href="/wiki/National_Museum_of_African_American_History_and_Culture" title="National Museum of African American History and Culture">National Museum of African American History and Culture</a>.
</p><p>Asante has written essays on art, Hip Hop, technology, and culture for <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Huffington_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="Huffington Post">Huffington Post</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Chronicle" title="San Francisco Chronicle">San Francisco Chronicle</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M._K._Asante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Asante is featured on the song "Bangers", along with Halo, from the album <i>Indie 500</i> by <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a> and <a href="/wiki/9th_Wonder" title="9th Wonder">9th Wonder</a>.
</p><p>Asante is the founder of Wonderful Sound Studios.
</p><p>Asante wrote the lyrics for the 2023-2024 official <a href="/wiki/Monday_Night_Football" title="Monday Night Football">Monday Night Football</a> anthem, "In the Air Tonight", performed by <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Stapleton" title="Chris Stapleton">Chris Stapleton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cindy_Blackman_Santana" title="Cindy Blackman Santana">Cindy Blackman Santana</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Professor">Professor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M._K._Asante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Professor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At 23, he joined the faculty of <a href="/wiki/Morgan_State_University" title="Morgan State University">Morgan State University</a>. He received <a href="/wiki/Tenure" class="mw-redirect" title="Tenure">tenure</a> at 26<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and is currently associate professor of creative writing and film in the Department of English and Language Arts.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, he was appointed to Distinguished Professor-in-Residence at the <a href="/wiki/MICA_(Institute_of_Strategic_Marketing_and_Communication)" class="mw-redirect" title="MICA (Institute of Strategic Marketing and Communication)">MICA (Institute of Strategic Marketing and Communication)</a> in India.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is the recipient of the 2021 Morgan State University Distinguished Achievement Award.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_shows">TV shows</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M._K._Asante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: TV shows">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Asante is the host and co-executive producer of <i><a href="/wiki/While_Black_with_MK_Asante" title="While Black with MK Asante">While Black with MK Asante</a></i>, a docuseries produced by <a href="/wiki/Snapchat" title="Snapchat">Snapchat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <i>While Black with MK Asante</i> takes the stories of America's black youth and gives them a platform in the smartphones of millions of America's teens.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <i>While Black with MK Asante</i> has nearly 17 million viewers.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Asante co-wrote the broadcast opens for the 2021 <a href="/wiki/NBA_Finals" title="NBA Finals">NBA Finals</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_performances">TV performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M._K._Asante&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: TV performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 30, 2020, he performed and debuted "We the Eagles" on <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>'s <a href="/wiki/NFL" class="mw-redirect" title="NFL">NFL</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Monday_Night_Football" title="Monday Night Football">Monday Night Football</a></i> for a live audience of 11.4 million viewers.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 29, 2021, he performed and debuted "Skate or D.I.E." at the 2021 <a href="/wiki/Dew_Tour" title="Dew Tour">Dew Tour</a> Skateboarding Olympic qualifier on NBC.
</p><p>On October 11, 2021, he co-wrote, co-produced, and starred in the intro for ESPN's NFL <i>Monday Night Football</i> week 5 featuring the <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Colts" title="Indianapolis Colts">Indianapolis Colts</a> Vs. <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Ravens" title="Baltimore Ravens">Baltimore Ravens</a>. In the intro, Asante is backed by the Morgan State University Marching Band and garnered 11.4 million viewers.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
