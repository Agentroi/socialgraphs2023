<p><b>Isis Naija Gaston</b> (born January<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>1, 2000), known professionally as <b>Ice Spice</b>, is an American rapper. She grew up in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>, New York City, and began her career in 2021 after meeting record producer RiotUSA.
</p><p>Ice Spice rose to prominence in late 2022 with her song "<a href="/wiki/Munch_(Feelin%27_U)" title="Munch (Feelin&#39; U)">Munch (Feelin' U)</a>", which achieved viral popularity on <a href="/wiki/TikTok" title="TikTok">TikTok</a>. She followed it up with singles "<a href="/wiki/Bikini_Bottom_(song)" title="Bikini Bottom (song)">Bikini Bottom</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/In_Ha_Mood" title="In Ha Mood">In Ha Mood</a>", leading up to her debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Like..%3F" title="Like..?">Like..?</a></i> (2023). She achieved her first entry on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart with the <a href="/wiki/Lil_Tjay" title="Lil Tjay">Lil Tjay</a> collaboration "<a href="/wiki/Gangsta_Boo_(song)" title="Gangsta Boo (song)">Gangsta Boo</a>". Her collaborations "<a href="/wiki/Boy%27s_a_Liar#Remix" title="Boy&#39;s a Liar">Boy's a Liar Pt. 2</a>" (with <a href="/wiki/PinkPantheress" title="PinkPantheress">PinkPantheress</a>), "<a href="/wiki/Princess_Diana_(song)" title="Princess Diana (song)">Princess Diana</a>" (with <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>), "<a href="/wiki/Karma_(Taylor_Swift_song)" title="Karma (Taylor Swift song)">Karma</a>" (with <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a>), and "<a href="/wiki/Barbie_World" title="Barbie World">Barbie World</a>" (with Minaj and <a href="/wiki/Aqua_(band)" title="Aqua (band)">Aqua</a>) have all peaked within the top 10 of the Hot 100; as such, Ice Spice became the artist with the most Hot 100 top-five singles in 2023. 
</p><p>Music journalists have noted Ice Spice's relaxed rapping style. In 2023, <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i> described her as a "<a href="/wiki/Breakout_star" class="mw-redirect" title="Breakout star">breakout star</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Publications such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> have dubbed her "<a href="/wiki/Honorific_nicknames_in_popular_music" title="Honorific nicknames in popular music">rap's new princess</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-Mamo_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mamo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She has been honored with the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award_for_Best_New_Artist" title="MTV Video Music Award for Best New Artist">MTV Video Music Award for Best New Artist</a>, and the Impact Award from the <a href="/wiki/BMI_Film_%26_TV_Awards" title="BMI Film &amp; TV Awards">BMI R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span></h2>
<p>Isis Naija Gaston was born on January 1, 2000, in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>, New York City,<sup id="cite_ref-rollingstone_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rollingstone-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYT_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> where she was raised in the <a href="/wiki/Fordham_Road" title="Fordham Road">Fordham Road</a> neighborhood. She is the oldest of five siblings.<sup id="cite_ref-intery_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intery-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Her father, Joseph Gaston, who was a former <a href="/wiki/Underground_hip_hop" title="Underground hip hop">underground rapper</a>, is African-American, while her mother, Charina Almanzar, who  worked at a <a href="/wiki/Car_dealership" title="Car dealership">car dealership</a> and gave birth to Gaston at age 17, is <a href="/wiki/People_of_the_Dominican_Republic" title="People of the Dominican Republic">Dominican</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The two first met at a <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald&#39;s">McDonald's</a><sup id="cite_ref-audiomack_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-audiomack-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and divorced when Gaston was two years old.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbrookie_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbrookie-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nojump_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nojump-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-intery_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intery-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Because her parents were frequently busy working, she spent much of her childhood with her grandparents and cousins.<sup id="cite_ref-intery_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intery-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She went to school in the Bronx until she was sent to <a href="/wiki/Sacred_Heart_High_School_(Yonkers,_New_York)" title="Sacred Heart High School (Yonkers, New York)">Sacred Heart High School</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> a Catholic high school in <a href="/wiki/Yonkers,_New_York" title="Yonkers, New York">Yonkers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nojump_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nojump-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> At age seven, she took a liking to <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> after listening to rappers like <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>, and others and wrote <a href="/wiki/Poetry" title="Poetry">poetry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle raps</a> from elementary school to high school. In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i>, Ice explained that she grew up listening to the likes of <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a> because of her father's rap background.<sup id="cite_ref-Mamo_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mamo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She would type out lyrics in the <a href="/wiki/Notes_(Apple)" title="Notes (Apple)">Notes</a> app of her iPhone, listening to hip-hop instrumentals and rapping out loud to them.<sup id="cite_ref-High_2023_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-High_2023-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-girlsunited_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-girlsunited-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbrookie_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbrookie-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> "When I saw Nicki [Minaj], I was so mesmerized," Ice Spice explained. "She's the first female rapper that I seen. And ever since then, I was kinda set on what I wanted to be." She chose Ice Spice as her stage name while she was a freshman in high school.<sup id="cite_ref-High_2023_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-High_2023-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ice Spice graduated from Sacred Heart High in 2018 and attended <a href="/wiki/State_University_of_New_York_at_Purchase" title="State University of New York at Purchase">State University of New York at Purchase</a>, where she was a defensive specialist on the school's volleyball team and studied biology.<sup id="cite_ref-High_2023_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-High_2023-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Issawi_2023_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Issawi_2023-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Purchase_bio_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Purchase_bio-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In seven matches, she had two kills and nine digs in the 2018 season.<sup id="cite_ref-Purchase_bio_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Purchase_bio-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Around her sophomore year, Ice Spice dropped out of SUNY Purchase, explaining that she did not believe the school was the right fit for her and attributing her "strenuous commute" for leaving college.<sup id="cite_ref-High_2023_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-High_2023-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As the eldest sister of her four siblings, Ice Spice was their <i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i> protector.<sup id="cite_ref-Issawi_2023_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Issawi_2023-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> She supported herself as a cashier at <a href="/wiki/Wendy%27s" title="Wendy&#39;s">Wendy's</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gap_Inc." title="Gap Inc.">The Gap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-High_2023_16-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-High_2023-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<h3><span id="2021.E2.80.932022:_Career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2021–2022:_Career_beginnings">2021–2022: Career beginnings</span></h3>
<p>Ice Spice started rapping in 2021 after meeting with a record producer, RiotUSA, while she was attending State University of New York at Purchase.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He produced her debut song, "Bully Freestyle", released in March 2021 after a video of Ice Spice doing the "<a href="/wiki/Buss_It" title="Buss It">Buss It</a>" challenge went viral on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-audiomack_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-audiomack-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Her song "Name of Love" gained traction on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>, which led to her becoming popular on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2022.E2.80.93present:_Breakthrough_with_Like.E2.80.A6.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2022–present:_Breakthrough_with_Like…?">2022–present: Breakthrough with <i>Like…?</i></span></h3>
<p>On August 10, 2022, Ice Spice released her song "<a href="/wiki/Munch_(Feelin%27_U)" title="Munch (Feelin&#39; U)">Munch (Feelin' U)</a>", accompanied with a video distributed by <a href="/wiki/WorldStarHipHop" title="WorldStarHipHop">WorldStarHipHop</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> as the lead single from her then-untitled debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Like..%3F" title="Like..?">Like..?</a></i>. The song gained popularity after getting support from <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>, who played the song on his <a href="/wiki/Sirius_XM" title="Sirius XM">Sirius XM</a> radio station, Sound 42.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> It subsequently went viral on Twitter and <a href="/wiki/TikTok" title="TikTok">TikTok</a>, and charted on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bubbling_Under_Hot_100" title="Bubbling Under Hot 100">Bubbling Under Hot 100</a> charts.<sup id="cite_ref-rollingstone2_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rollingstone2-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbrookie_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbrookie-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In September 2022, Ice Spice appeared as a featured artist on the song "One Time" by <a href="/wiki/B-Lovee" title="B-Lovee">B-Lovee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Later that month, she signed a record deal with <a href="/wiki/10K_Projects" title="10K Projects">10K Projects</a> and <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On October 28, she released the single "<a href="/wiki/Bikini_Bottom_(song)" title="Bikini Bottom (song)">Bikini Bottom</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Ice Spice's debut EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Like..%3F" title="Like..?">Like..?</a></i>, was released on January 20, 2023, and included the singles "Munch (Feelin' U)", "Bikini Bottom", and "<a href="/wiki/In_Ha_Mood" title="In Ha Mood">In Ha Mood</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ice_Spice_Next_Wav_by_Keinoflo_uploaded_by_James_Tamim_V7_2022.png" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="Ice Spice performing" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Ice_Spice_Next_Wav_by_Keinoflo_uploaded_by_James_Tamim_V7_2022.png/230px-Ice_Spice_Next_Wav_by_Keinoflo_uploaded_by_James_Tamim_V7_2022.png" decoding="async" width="230" height="129" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Ice_Spice_Next_Wav_by_Keinoflo_uploaded_by_James_Tamim_V7_2022.png/345px-Ice_Spice_Next_Wav_by_Keinoflo_uploaded_by_James_Tamim_V7_2022.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Ice_Spice_Next_Wav_by_Keinoflo_uploaded_by_James_Tamim_V7_2022.png/460px-Ice_Spice_Next_Wav_by_Keinoflo_uploaded_by_James_Tamim_V7_2022.png 2x" data-file-width="3840" data-file-height="2160" /></a><figcaption>Ice Spice performing in 2022</figcaption></figure>
<p>In February 2023, Ice Spice collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Tjay" title="Lil Tjay">Lil Tjay</a> on the tribute single "<a href="/wiki/Gangsta_Boo_(song)" title="Gangsta Boo (song)">Gangsta Boo</a>" to the late <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_Boo" title="Gangsta Boo">rapper of the same name</a>, which became her first song on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> at No. 82.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On February 3, 2023, the remix of singer <a href="/wiki/PinkPantheress" title="PinkPantheress">PinkPantheress</a>'s song, "<a href="/wiki/Boy%27s_a_Liar#Boy&#39;s_a_Liar_Pt._2" title="Boy&#39;s a Liar">Boy's a Liar Pt. 2</a>" featuring Ice Spice and the corresponding music video were released. The song peaked at No. 3 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, the highest charting position for either artist at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> In April 2023, she released the remix of "<a href="/wiki/Princess_Diana_(song)" title="Princess Diana (song)">Princess Diana</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The song peaked at number four on the Hot 100, earning Ice Spice her second top-ten hit on the chart and the twenty second top-ten hit for Minaj.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> On May 24, 2023, a remix of <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Karma_(Taylor_Swift_song)" title="Karma (Taylor Swift song)">Karma</a>" featuring Ice Spice, was announced for release on May 26, 2023. The remix is a bonus track on the special editions of Swift's tenth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Midnights" title="Midnights">Midnights</a></i> (2022).<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> A music video for the remix, featuring Swift and Ice Spice, was released on May 27. <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork</a></i> described 2023 as the breakout year of Ice Spice.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> "Karma" peaked at number two on the Hot 100, making Ice Spice the artist with the most Hot 100 top-five singles in 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The deluxe version of <i>Like..?</i>, featuring five new tracks, was released on July 21, 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span></h2>
<p>Ice Spice's music is primarily <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_drill" title="Brooklyn drill">Bronx</a> <a href="/wiki/Drill_music" title="Drill music">drill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Her name came from a "finsta" (fake Instagram account) she made at age 14.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> She has said she writes all her own lyrics.<sup id="cite_ref-intery_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intery-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span></h3>
<p>She was first inspired to start rapping by <a href="/wiki/Sheff_G" title="Sheff G">Sheff G</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pop_Smoke" title="Pop Smoke">Pop Smoke</a>, and has listed <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cardi_B" title="Cardi B">Cardi B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foxy_Brown_(rapper)" title="Foxy Brown (rapper)">Foxy Brown</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Remy_Ma" title="Remy Ma">Remy Ma</a> as musical influences due to their New York roots.<sup id="cite_ref-nojump_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nojump-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-complex1_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-intery_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intery-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She has also called <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a> inspirations because of their "graceful angelic vibe of timeless beauty".<sup id="cite_ref-bbrookie_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbrookie-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Talking to <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> in 2022, Ice Spice recalled: “When I was 13 or 14, I was playing [<a href="/wiki/The_1975" title="The 1975">The 1975</a>] all the time. It was my shit, I think it’s because they’re from Europe and he [<a href="/wiki/Matty_Healy" title="Matty Healy">Matty Healy</a>] has that accent. It was a thing for me...”<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
