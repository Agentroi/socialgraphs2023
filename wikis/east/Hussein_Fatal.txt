<p><b>Bruce Edward Washington Jr.</b> (April 3, 1973&#160;&#8211;&#32;July 10, 2015),<sup id="cite_ref-AJC_Death_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AJC_Death-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Hussein Fatal</b> or sometimes as <b>Fatal Hussein</b>, was an American rapper, best known for his collaborative work with <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a> as a member of the rap group <a href="/wiki/Outlawz" title="Outlawz">Outlaw Immortalz</a> or just Outlawz.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hussein_Fatal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fatal was born Bruce Edward Washington Jr. in New Jersey on April 3, 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-AJC_Death_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AJC_Death-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hussein_Fatal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Washington first appeared as "Hussein Fatal" in February 1996 on the songs "<a href="/wiki/All_Bout_U_(2Pac_Song)" class="mw-redirect" title="All Bout U (2Pac Song)">All Bout U</a>" and "When We Ride" from Shakur's album <i><a href="/wiki/All_Eyez_on_Me" title="All Eyez on Me">All Eyez on Me</a></i>. As a member of the Outlaw Immortalz, his name was chosen to evoke a villain, Iraqi president <a href="/wiki/Saddam_Hussein" title="Saddam Hussein">Saddam Hussein</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In June 1996, he was featured on Shakur's song "<a href="/wiki/Hit_%27Em_Up" title="Hit &#39;Em Up">Hit 'Em Up</a>", insulting Shakur's rivals in the song's second verse.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Shakur was fatally shot in September 1996 and Outlawz member <a href="/wiki/Yaki_Kadafi" title="Yaki Kadafi">Yaki Kadafi</a> was murdered in November, Washington did not appear on another recording until 1998 when <a href="/wiki/Relativity_Records" title="Relativity Records">Relativity Records</a> released his first solo album <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Line_of_Fire_(Hussein_Fatal_album)" title="In the Line of Fire (Hussein Fatal album)">In the Line of Fire</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The label went bankrupt; the album wasn't promoted and sold poorly.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Washington then signed with <a href="/wiki/Rap-A-Lot_Records" title="Rap-A-Lot Records">Rap-A-Lot Records</a> and began work on a second solo studio album, <i>Death Before Dishonor</i>, recording over 40 songs before being arrested in December 1999 for an assault committed three years earlier. Rap-A-Lot Records released the album as <i>Fatal</i> after he was paroled in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2003, Washington performed on four songs of <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Blood_in_My_Eye" title="Blood in My Eye">Blood in My Eye</a></i>: "The Life", "It's Murda (Freestyle)", "The Wrap (Freestyle)" and the eponymous title track, "Blood in My Eye". He also appeared on the remix of <a href="/wiki/Ashanti_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ashanti (entertainer)">Ashanti</a>'s "Rain On Me", and in Ja Rule's "Clap Back" music video.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He performed again with <a href="/wiki/Outlawz" title="Outlawz">Outlawz</a> on their 2010 mixtape <i>Killuminati 2K10</i> and their 2011 mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Killuminati_2K11" title="Killuminati 2K11">Killuminati 2K11</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and on the 2011 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Perfect_Timing_(Outlawz_album)" title="Perfect Timing (Outlawz album)">Perfect Timing</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He also appeared on the tracks "Emancipation" and "Welcome To Real Life",<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> from fellow Outlawz member <a href="/wiki/Young_Noble" title="Young Noble">Young Noble</a>'s 2012 album <i><a href="/wiki/Son_of_God_(Young_Noble_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Son of God (Young Noble album)">Son of God</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He continued to release mixtapes on his own label, Thugtertainment, until his death.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hussein_Fatal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fatal died in a car accident in <a href="/wiki/Banks_County,_Georgia" title="Banks County, Georgia">Banks County, Georgia</a> on July 10, 2015, at the age of 42. His girlfriend Zanetta Yearby was driving and was charged with <a href="/wiki/DUI" class="mw-redirect" title="DUI">DUI</a>, first-degree <a href="/wiki/Vehicular_homicide" title="Vehicular homicide">vehicular homicide</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Reckless_driving" title="Reckless driving">reckless driving</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
