<p><b>Barrington DeVaughn Hendricks</b> (born October 22, 1989),<sup id="cite_ref-:2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>JPEGMafia</b> (stylized in <a href="/wiki/All_caps" title="All caps">all caps</a>), is an American rapper, singer, and record producer born in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and based in <a href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="Baltimore, Maryland">Baltimore, Maryland</a>. His 2018 album <i><a href="/wiki/Veteran_(JPEGMafia_album)" title="Veteran (JPEGMafia album)">Veteran</a></i>, released through <a href="/wiki/Deathbomb_Arc" title="Deathbomb Arc">Deathbomb Arc</a>, received widespread critical acclaim and was featured on many year-end lists. It was followed by 2019's <i><a href="/wiki/All_My_Heroes_Are_Cornballs" title="All My Heroes Are Cornballs">All My Heroes Are Cornballs</a></i> and 2021's <i><a href="/wiki/LP!" title="LP!">LP!</a></i>, released to further critical acclaim. His most recent release, a collaborative studio album with <a href="/wiki/Danny_Brown" title="Danny Brown">Danny Brown</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/Scaring_the_Hoes" title="Scaring the Hoes">Scaring the Hoes</a></i>, was released in 2023.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JPEGMafia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hendricks was born in <a href="/wiki/Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="Flatbush, Brooklyn">Flatbush, Brooklyn</a>, to Jamaican parents.<sup id="cite_ref-mass_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mass-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tl_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tl-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He spent the majority of his childhood in Flatbush,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> before moving to <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a> at age 13, where he experienced a significant amount of <a href="/wiki/Racism" title="Racism">racism</a> that later had an intense effect on his music.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Hendricks moved to <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> and enlisted in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> at age 18. He served a tour of duty in <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> and also spent time in Kuwait, Germany, Japan, and North Africa,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> before being <a href="/wiki/Military_discharge" title="Military discharge">honorably discharged</a> after speaking up against his superiors' reported abuse.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Breiham_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Breiham-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Though he previously claimed to have a <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">master's degree</a> in <a href="/wiki/Journalism" title="Journalism">journalism</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Breiham_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Breiham-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Hendricks has since clarified that, though having studied the subject while deployed, he was not officially awarded a degree.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In April 2021, Hendricks revealed through social media that he had "a sexually, verbally, and physically <a href="/wiki/Child_abuse" title="Child abuse">abusive childhood</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JPEGMafia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hendricks developed an interest in music production at the age of 15, and he began producing after he learned how to <a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">sample</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tg1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tg1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Describing his production, Hendricks says "When I was first making beats, no one liked the beats. To this day I give people beats and they're just confused." He says "I started rapping because no one liked my beats." Noting that he started producing before he started rapping, Hendricks said "I'm a producer first kind of, and then a rapper second but I take both of them seriously."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During his military stay in Japan, he was producing and writing music under the name Devon Hendryx.<sup id="cite_ref-tg1_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tg1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>, where he began to make music under his moniker JPEGMafia,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>  under which he released his <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> <i>Communist Slow Jams</i> in April 2015. Only a month later, he released his mixtape <i>Darkskin Manson</i> which was inspired by the <a href="/wiki/2015_Baltimore_protests" title="2015 Baltimore protests">Freddie Gray protests</a> in Baltimore that were happening right as he had moved there.<sup id="cite_ref-tl_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tl-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
Following a number of mixtapes, Hendricks released his debut <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio album">studio album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Ben_Carson" title="Black Ben Carson">Black Ben Carson</a></i>, in February 2016 via <a href="/wiki/Deathbomb_Arc" title="Deathbomb Arc">Deathbomb Arc</a> featuring a much harsher and more distorted sound than his other projects.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Four months later, he released a collaborative <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> with fellow Baltimore-based rapper Freaky titled <i>The 2nd Amendment</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
After less than 3 years living there, Hendricks moved from Baltimore to Los Angeles for his next studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-fader2018-a_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fader2018-a-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In January 2018, he released his second studio album <a href="/wiki/Veteran_(JPEGMafia_album)" title="Veteran (JPEGMafia album)"><i>Veteran</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In an article on Bandcamp,<sup id="cite_ref-bndarticle_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bndarticle-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> he said "I wanted to show I'm not just a one-trick pony. I always do weird shit. I usually just keep it to myself. This time, I just let the filter go." At the time of its release, <i>Veteran</i> was considered JPEGMAFIA's most experimental album to date, receiving widespread critical acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-Breiham_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Breiham-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the release of <i>Veteran</i>, Hendricks started working on his next album. He recorded 93 songs, and whittled it down to 18 tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-gn93_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gn93-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He mixed and mastered it at the end of <a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples" title="Vince Staples">Vince Staples</a>' tour,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vtour_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vtour-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> posting percentage updates frequently on his <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Prior to the release, he would label the project as a "disappointment" in interviews and his <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a>. The first single from the album, "Jesus Forgive Me, I Am a Thot", was released on August 13, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He promoted the album by uploading a series of listening sessions to his <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel where friends and artists such as <a href="/wiki/Denzel_Curry" title="Denzel Curry">Denzel Curry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Tweedy" title="Jeff Tweedy">Jeff Tweedy</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Wilco" title="Wilco">Wilco</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Hannibal_Buress" title="Hannibal Buress">Hannibal Buress</a> discussed and reacted to cuts off of the album. <i><a href="/wiki/All_My_Heroes_Are_Cornballs" title="All My Heroes Are Cornballs">All My Heroes Are Cornballs</a></i> was released on September 13, 2019, to further critical acclaim,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lobf_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lobf-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> and was his first album to chart. In October 2019, he embarked on the JPEGMafia Type Tour to support his new album.<sup id="cite_ref-typetourannouncement_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-typetourannouncement-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, Hendricks released several singles across the span of the year, and compiled them into an EP, aptly titled <i><a href="/wiki/EP!" class="mw-redirect" title="EP!">EP!</a></i> It was released on Hendricks' Bandcamp page on November 6,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> and on streaming services with the addition of one extra single on December 10.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On February 12, 2021, Hendricks released his second extended play, <i><a href="/wiki/EP2!" title="EP2!">EP2!</a></i> On October 2, Hendricks announced the album <i><a href="/wiki/LP!" title="LP!">LP!</a></i> , which was released on October 22, 2021, his birthday, to further acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Due to sample-related issues, the album was released as two separate versions: <i>LP!</i> released on streaming services, and <i>LP! (Offline)</i> released for free on <a href="/wiki/Bandcamp" title="Bandcamp">Bandcamp</a>, <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, and <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">Soundcloud</a>. On February 24, 2022, Hendricks announced via Twitter that the songs not on the "Online" version had been released to streaming services as the <i>OFFLINE!</i> EP. On May 23, 2023, Hendricks released the complete "offline" version of the album to streaming platforms.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On November, 9, 2022, Hendricks was dropping hints to dropping three albums for 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 15, 2023, Hendricks made a hint on his social media that he was working on a collaborative project with fellow rapper, Danny Brown.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> On January 20, 2023, Hendricks officially announced three albums will be dropping the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> On February 10, 2023, Hendricks announced on his Twitter that the collaborative project with Danny Brown was finished,<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> and is working on his second album for the year.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> On February 28, 2023, on The Danny Brown Show, both Hendricks and Brown announced that their collaborative project is named Scaring the Hoes Vol. 1, and played a sneak peek of the first single on the project. On March 13, 2023, the first single of the project called <a href="/wiki/Lean_Beef_Patty" title="Lean Beef Patty">Lean Beef Patty</a> was released to streaming services,<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> and Hendricks announced that the collaborative project is set to release on March 24, 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> The second single "SCARING THE HOES" <sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> was released to streaming services along with a music video.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Three days later, the album was released.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> On July 11, an extended version of the album, titled <i>Scaring the Hoes: DLC Pack</i>, which included four more tracks, was released on streaming services. <sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JPEGMafia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His music has mostly been described as <a href="/wiki/Alternative_hip_hop" title="Alternative hip hop">experimental hip-hop</a><sup id="cite_ref-:2_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> but has been said to incorporate a wide range of genres into his music such as <a href="/wiki/Trap_music" title="Trap music">trap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vaporwave" title="Vaporwave">vaporwave</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Noise_rap" class="mw-redirect" title="Noise rap">noise rap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In an interview with <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Union" title="Cambridge Union">Cambridge Union</a>, Hendricks noted that his biggest influence is <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> He states that one of his earliest influences was the rock group <a href="/wiki/Hanson_(band)" title="Hanson (band)">Hanson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_57-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> He has also cited <a href="/wiki/Ol%27_Dirty_Bastard" title="Ol&#39; Dirty Bastard">Ol' Dirty Bastard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Throbbing_Gristle" title="Throbbing Gristle">Throbbing Gristle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skinny_Puppy" title="Skinny Puppy">Skinny Puppy</a>, <a href="/wiki/MF_DOOM" class="mw-redirect" title="MF DOOM">MF DOOM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_B" title="Lil B">Lil B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chief_Keef" title="Chief Keef">Chief Keef</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Rubin" title="Rick Rubin">Rick Rubin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cam%27Ron" class="mw-redirect" title="Cam&#39;Ron">Cam'Ron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk" title="Björk">Björk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Janelle_Mon%C3%A1e" title="Janelle Monáe">Janelle Monáe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">Radiohead</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Backstreet_Boys" class="mw-redirect" title="The Backstreet Boys">The Backstreet Boys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dirty_Beaches" title="Dirty Beaches">Dirty Beaches</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arca_(musician)" title="Arca (musician)">Arca</a> as influences.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_media">In other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JPEGMafia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In March 2020, Hendricks started a <a href="/wiki/Vlog" title="Vlog">vlog</a> series on his <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel called HTBAR (<i>How To Build A Relationship</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> The series features JPEGMafia talking to other artists and friends about various topics, mostly about music, life and politics. Each episode's soundtrack consist of unreleased songs, demos and instrumentals produced by JPEGMafia.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> Artists that have featured on the vlog series include <a href="/wiki/Danny_Brown" title="Danny Brown">Danny Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Beats" title="Kenny Beats">Kenny Beats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lykke_Li" title="Lykke Li">Lykke Li</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orville_Peck" title="Orville Peck">Orville Peck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saba_(rapper)" title="Saba (rapper)">Saba</a> and more.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> Hendricks has also appeared on The Cave, a YouTube series created by hip hop producer Kenny Beats.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hendricks is a fan of <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a>, and has incorporated several references to it in his songs.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> He made an appearance for wrestling promotion <a href="/wiki/All_Elite_Wrestling" title="All Elite Wrestling">All Elite Wrestling</a> (AEW) on an episode of their television show <i><a href="/wiki/AEW_Dynamite" title="AEW Dynamite">AEW Dynamite</a></i> in September 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> In 2021, AEW star <a href="/wiki/Darby_Allin" title="Darby Allin">Darby Allin</a> was featured on Hendricks' thirteenth HTBAR episode.
</p><p>Hendricks appeared on the 7th episode of season 5 of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Eric_Andre_Show" title="The Eric Andre Show">The Eric Andre Show</a></i>, "Named After My Dad's Penis", and competed in the third <i>Rapper Warrior Ninja</i> sketch - a <a href="/wiki/Parody" title="Parody">parody</a> of the TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Sasuke_(TV_series)" title="Sasuke (TV series)">Sasuke</a></i> (which aired in the United States of America under the title <i>Ninja Warrior</i>) and its American spin-off <i><a href="/wiki/American_Ninja_Warrior" title="American Ninja Warrior">American Ninja Warrior</a></i>. Hendricks competed along with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Yachty" title="Lil Yachty">Lil Yachty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trippie_Redd" title="Trippie Redd">Trippie Redd</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Zack_Fox" title="Zack Fox">Zack Fox</a> in a challenge to cross a platform while freestyling and dodging various attacks. Hendricks fell off the platform while freestyling and failed.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
