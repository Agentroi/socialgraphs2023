<p><b>Aaron Ocosice Phillip</b> (born January 31, 1974), better known by his stage name <b>Afu-Ra</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Underground_hip_hop" title="Underground hip hop">underground rapper</a>. A member of the <a href="/wiki/Gang_Starr_Foundation" title="Gang Starr Foundation">Gang Starr Foundation</a>, he has worked closely with its members, including <a href="/wiki/Gang_Starr" title="Gang Starr">Gang Starr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeru_the_Damaja" title="Jeru the Damaja">Jeru the Damaja</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Shug" title="Big Shug">Big Shug</a> and <a href="/wiki/Group_Home" title="Group Home">Group Home</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Afu-Ra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Afu-Ra grew up in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, with frequent collaborator <a href="/wiki/Jeru_the_Damaja" title="Jeru the Damaja">Jeru the Damaja</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Jeru_interview_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jeru_interview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Afu-Ra's first studio appearance was on Jeru's 1994 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sun_Rises_in_the_East" title="The Sun Rises in the East">The Sun Rises in the East</a></i>, on the song "Mental Stamina". He made another appearance on Jeru's second album <i><a href="/wiki/Wrath_of_the_Math" title="Wrath of the Math">Wrath of the Math</a></i>, on the track "Physical Stamina", in 1996. Afu's debut single, "Whirlwind Thru Cities", was released in 1998, reaching the top 20 on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i>'s <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Tracks" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Rap Tracks">Hot Rap Singles</a> chart. In 1999, he released his second single, "Defeat" b/w "Mortal Kombat". His debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Body_of_the_Life_Force" title="Body of the Life Force">Body of the Life Force</a></i>, was released in October 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The album featured production from <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Muggs" title="DJ Muggs">DJ Muggs</a>, <a href="/wiki/True_Master" title="True Master">True Master</a> and <a href="/wiki/Da_Beatminerz" title="Da Beatminerz">Da Beatminerz</a>. Guests included <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/GZA" title="GZA">GZA</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masta_Killa" title="Masta Killa">Masta Killa</a>, <a href="/wiki/M.O.P." title="M.O.P.">M.O.P.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ky-Mani_Marley" title="Ky-Mani Marley">Ky-Mani Marley</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Cocoa_Brovaz" class="mw-redirect" title="Cocoa Brovaz">Cocoa Brovaz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The album peaked at 183 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-BB_200_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB_200-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 13 on the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Albums" title="Independent Albums">independent</a> chart,<sup id="cite_ref-BB_IND_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB_IND-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and 42 on <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">hip hop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BB_HH_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB_HH-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Afu released his second effort <i><a href="/wiki/Life_Force_Radio" title="Life Force Radio">Life Force Radio</a></i> in May 2002 on <a href="/wiki/Koch_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Koch Records">Koch Records</a>, featuring production from DJ Premier, <a href="/wiki/True_Master" title="True Master">True Master</a>, <a href="/wiki/Easy_Mo_Bee" title="Easy Mo Bee">Easy Mo Bee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Domingo_(producer)" title="Domingo (producer)">Domingo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ayatollah_(producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ayatollah (producer)">Ayatollah</a>. Guests included <a href="/wiki/Guru_(rapper)" title="Guru (rapper)">Guru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a>, <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Teena_Marie" title="Teena Marie">Teena Marie</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The album was slightly less successful, peaking at 184 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200,<sup id="cite_ref-BB_200_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB_200-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 17 on the independent album chart,<sup id="cite_ref-BB_IND_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB_IND-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> though 29 on hip hop.<sup id="cite_ref-BB_HH_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB_HH-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2004, he released a compilation album titled <i>Afu-Ra presents Perverted Monks</i>, followed by a studio album in 2005, titled <i><a href="/wiki/State_of_the_Arts" title="State of the Arts">State of the Arts</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Decon_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Decon Records">Decon Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Jeru_interview_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jeru_interview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The latter featured production by DJ Premier and <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Nazareth" title="Bronze Nazareth">Bronze Nazareth</a> and guest vocals from Masta Killa, <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gentleman_(musician)" title="Gentleman (musician)">Gentleman</a>. In 2012, he independently released <i>Body Of The Life Force 2</i>, which was poorly received.
</p>
