<p><b>Jasun Wardlaw</b><sup id="cite_ref-Allmusic_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (April 6, 1973 &#8211; October 22, 2003) better known by his stage name <b>Half a Mill</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>-based American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Half_a_Mill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Half a Mill was considered an underground rapper in the early 1990s. His mainstream breakthrough came in 1997 when he scored a guest spot on supergroup <a href="/wiki/The_Firm_(hip_hop_group)" title="The Firm (hip hop group)">the Firm</a>'s only release, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Album_(The_Firm_album)" title="The Album (The Firm album)">The Album</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_death">Personal life and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Half_a_Mill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wardlaw committed suicide by shooting himself in the head in Brooklyn's Albany Projects on October 22, 2003. His body was found by police inside his apartment.<sup id="cite_ref-NYPD_Daily_Blotter_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYPD_Daily_Blotter-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VannDigit_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VannDigit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He left behind a son, Jasun Jabbar Wardlaw Jr., who is a rapper and actor.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Half a Mill was the subject of the 2010 full-length documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Player_Hating:_A_Love_Story" title="Player Hating: A Love Story">Player Hating: A Love Story</a></i> by filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Maggie_Hadleigh-West" title="Maggie Hadleigh-West">Maggie Hadleigh-West</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Player_Hating_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Player_Hating-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
