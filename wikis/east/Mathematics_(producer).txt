<p><b>Ronald Maurice Bean</b>, better known professionally as <b>Mathematics</b> (also known as <b>Allah Mathematics</b>) (born October 21, 1971), is a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> producer and DJ for the <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a> and its solo and affiliate projects. He designed the Wu-Tang Clan logo.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mathematics_(producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica,_Queens" title="Jamaica, Queens">Jamaica, Queens</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, Mathematics was introduced to hip hop by his brother who used to bring home recordings of the genre's pioneers like <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_Flash_%26_The_Furious_Five" class="mw-redirect" title="Grandmaster Flash &amp; The Furious Five">Grandmaster Flash &amp; The Furious Five</a>, Treacherous Three and <a href="/wiki/Cold_Crush_Brothers" class="mw-redirect" title="Cold Crush Brothers">Cold Crush Brothers</a>. He began his career in 1987 DJing block parties and park jams in Baisley Projects, going by the name Supreme Cut Master. In 1988, he became the full-time DJ for experienced rapper Victor C, doing countless shows in clubs and colleges in New York City.
</p><p>In 1990, Mathematics linked up with <a href="/wiki/GZA" title="GZA">GZA</a>/Genius, who would soon become one of the Wu-Tang Clan's founding members, but at the time was struggling to build a career on the <a href="/wiki/Prism_Records" title="Prism Records">Cold Chillin'</a> label. This partnership earned Mathematics a spot on his first official tour, The Cold Chillin Blizzard Tour (with popular acts such as <a href="/wiki/Biz_Markie" title="Biz Markie">Biz Markie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_G._Rap" class="mw-redirect" title="Kool G. Rap">Kool G. Rap</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/DJ_Polo" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Polo">DJ Polo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marley_Marl" title="Marley Marl">Marley Marl</a>).
</p><p>GZA left Cold Chillin after his first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Words_from_the_Genius" title="Words from the Genius">Words from the Genius</a></i>, did not achieve the sales target that was anticipated. He and Mathematics took to the road again, but this time with the help of GZA's cousins, <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ol%27_Dirty_Bastard" title="Ol&#39; Dirty Bastard">Ol' Dirty Bastard</a>. These three soon became the founding members of Wu-Tang Clan, then known as <a href="/wiki/All_In_Together_Now_Crew" class="mw-redirect" title="All In Together Now Crew">All In Together Now</a>. The group soon dissolved, however, and the trio set their minds on creating the Wu group. During the group's inception, Mathematics used his experience as a <a href="/wiki/Graffiti" title="Graffiti">graffiti</a> artist to design a logo for the up-and-coming crew, as well as various other logos and designs the Wu-Tang's artists would use. In the years to come, he became a Wu-Element under the guidance of RZA.
</p><p>Mathematics' first real exposure to production came late one night when he attended a session where he assisted RZA, his mentor, in constructing a beat from nothing. The track eventually developed into "Ice Cream" on <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Only_Built_4_Cuban_Linx" class="mw-redirect" title="Only Built 4 Cuban Linx">Only Built 4 Cuban Linx</a></i> album. RZA inspired Mathematics to follow the Wu-Tang, giving him advice on the nuances of hip hop production over the coming years. In 1996, Mathematics began record producing, in Staten Island, New York (Shaolin) and at home, in the P-Funk City, Plainfield, New Jersey, between heavily scheduled tour dates and rigorous road travelling with his father's gospel group, The Soul Seekers.
</p><p>His first track, "Fast Life" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> star <a href="/wiki/Andre_Rison" title="Andre Rison">Andre Rison</a>, was included in the <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=NFL_Jams_(1996_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="NFL Jams (1996 album) (page does not exist)">NFL Jams</a></i> compilation album. Though this track faded into obscurity somewhat, it led to several more collaborations between Mathematics and Ghostface; Mathematics also began to produce for many other Wu-Tang members and affiliates, including several tracks on GZA's second album <i><a href="/wiki/Beneath_the_Surface_(GZA_album)" title="Beneath the Surface (GZA album)">Beneath The Surface</a></i> as well as <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Tical_2000:_Judgement_Day" title="Tical 2000: Judgement Day">Tical 2000: Judgement Day</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Inspectah_Deck" title="Inspectah Deck">Inspectah Deck</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Uncontrolled_Substance" title="Uncontrolled Substance">Uncontrolled Substance</a></i> and Method Man &amp; <a href="/wiki/Redman_(rapper)" title="Redman (rapper)">Redman</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Blackout!_(Method_Man_%26_Redman_album)" title="Blackout! (Method Man &amp; Redman album)">Blackout!</a></i>. Eventually, he produced for the Clan as a group, with "Do You Really (Thang, Thang)", "Careful (Click, Click)".
</p><p>In 2003, Mathematics moved into TV work, as he produced the main theme and all original music for the short lived show <i><a href="/wiki/Wanda_at_Large" title="Wanda at Large">Wanda At Large</a></i>, which starred <a href="/wiki/Wanda_Sykes" title="Wanda Sykes">Wanda Sykes</a> and was broadcast by the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Network" class="mw-redirect" title="Fox Network">Fox Network</a>. During this time and between continuous touring, Mathematics started work on his first solo full length project, <i><a href="/wiki/Love,_Hell_Or_Right" class="mw-redirect" title="Love, Hell Or Right">Love, Hell Or Right</a></i>. Completely mixed, arranged and produced by himself, <i>Love Hell or Right</i> was released fall 2003 on his own Quewisha Records label in conjunction with High Times Records, and it went on to sell 30,000 units.
</p><p>As well as using rappers from Mathematics' home borough of Queens, Eyes-Low and Buddah Bless, <i>Love Hell Or Right</i> had appearances from all the Wu-Tang Clan members except GZA and the then-imprisoned <a href="/wiki/Ol%27_Dirty_Bastard" title="Ol&#39; Dirty Bastard">Ol' Dirty Bastard</a>. Mathematics soon signed to the popular independent hip hop label <a href="/wiki/Nature_Sounds" title="Nature Sounds">Nature Sounds</a> (home to Wu-Tang colleague <a href="/wiki/Masta_Killa" title="Masta Killa">Masta Killa</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Dumile" class="mw-redirect" title="Daniel Dumile">MF DOOM</a>) and released his second album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Problem_(album)" title="The Problem (album)">The Problem</a></i> in 2005. On this album the entire Wu-Tang Clan appeared, including a posthumous appearance from Ol' Dirty Bastard. As well as working on his solo albums, Mathematics has continued to contribute beats to many Wu-Tang releases, including the first albums by Masta Killa and Streetlife. In January 2012 he was to release a sequel to <i>The Problem</i> entitled <i>The Answer</i>, entirely produced by him with Wu-Tang members such as Raekwon, GZA, Method Man, <a href="/wiki/Cappadonna" title="Cappadonna">Cappadonna</a>, Masta Killa, Ol' Dirty Bastard, <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a>. Other artists include Redman and artists he is developing such as Ali Vegas, Eyeslow and Bad Luck.<sup id="cite_ref-vodpod_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vodpod-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2017, it was confirmed that <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a> will release a new album, <i>Wu-Tang: The Saga Continues</i>, which was entirely produced by Mathematics and was released on October 13, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The first track off the album is titled "People Say" and features <a href="/wiki/Redman_(rapper)" title="Redman (rapper)">Redman</a>.
</p>
