<p><b>Malik Abdul-Basit Smart</b> (November 14, 1972 – July 29, 2020), better known by his stage name <b>Malik B.</b>, was an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> and singer who was a founding member of <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">the Roots</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Davis_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Davis-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Shapiro_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shapiro-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He appeared on the first four albums released by the Roots, before departing the group. He returned as a featured artist for some later albums. He released one EP, entitled <i>Psychological</i>, on <a href="/wiki/Master_of_ceremonies" title="Master of ceremonies">MC</a> Haak Blast's publishing company Future Blast Publishing. He also released two studio albums, <i>Street Assault</i> (2005) and <i><a href="/wiki/Unpredictable_(Malik_B._album)" title="Unpredictable (Malik B. album)">Unpredictable</a></i> (2015) with <a href="/wiki/Mr._Green_(record_producer)" title="Mr. Green (record producer)">Mr. Green</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malik_B.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On the Roots' seventh studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Theory_(album)" title="Game Theory (album)">Game Theory</a></i>, released in August 2006, Malik B returned to the group,<sup id="cite_ref-Vibe_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> appearing on three tracks, including the title "Game Theory" (track 3; listed as 116 on North American releases), "In the Music" (track 5; listed as 118 on North American releases), and "Here I Come" (track 8; listed as 121 on North American releases). The nature of his relationship with the group was indicated by the fact that his tracks were billed as "featuring Malik B". In the liner notes, the Roots thanked Malik B, adding the statement: "Welcome Home". Malik B was also featured on two tracks on the Roots' 2008 album <i><a href="/wiki/Rising_Down" title="Rising Down">Rising Down</a></i>, "I Can't Help It" and "Lost Desire".
</p><p>Malik B, along with the rest of the Roots, was featured on <a href="/wiki/MC_Solaar" title="MC Solaar">MC Solaar</a>'s 1994 album <i><a href="/wiki/Prose_Combat" title="Prose Combat">Prose Combat</a></i>.
</p><p>Malik B's last work before his death included a collaboration album with New York-based producer <a href="/wiki/Mr._Green_(record_producer)" title="Mr. Green (record producer)">Mr. Green</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and being a featured member of the Philadelphia rap collective Beard Gang which includes fellow Philadelphia artist <a href="/wiki/Freeway_(rapper)" title="Freeway (rapper)">Freeway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malik_B.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Malik B died on July 29, 2020, at the age of 47. The cause of death was not released.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/The_Roots_discography" title="The Roots discography">The Roots discography</a></div>
