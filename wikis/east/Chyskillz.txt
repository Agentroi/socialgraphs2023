<p><b>Chylow Parker</b> (July 19, 1969 – February 13, 2018),<sup id="cite_ref-Vibe_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known by the stage name <b>Chyskillz</b>, was an American <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beatmaker" class="mw-redirect" title="Beatmaker">beatmaker</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hardcore_hip_hop" title="Hardcore hip hop">hardcore rapper</a>, and a member of multi-platinum hardcore rap group <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(hip_hop_group)" title="Onyx (hip hop group)">Onyx</a>.
</p><p>Chyskillz was discovered by the late great hip hop legend <a href="/wiki/Jam_Master_Jay" title="Jam Master Jay">Jam Master Jay</a> of <a href="/wiki/Run-D.M.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Run-D.M.C.">Run-D.M.C.</a> who signed Onyx on his label <a href="/wiki/JMJ_Records" title="JMJ Records">JMJ Records</a>. Onyx's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Bacdafucup" title="Bacdafucup">Bacdafucup</a></i>, produced by Chyskillz, helped the group to become a nominee for "Favorite Rap/Hip-Hop New Artist" on <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards" title="American Music Awards">American Music Awards</a> and won "Best Rap Album" on <a href="/wiki/Soul_Train_Music_Awards" title="Soul Train Music Awards">Soul Train Music Awards</a>.
</p><p>During his career, Chylow Parker has worked with well-known artists such as <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(hip_hop_group)" title="Onyx (hip hop group)">Onyx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Run-D.M.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Run-D.M.C.">Run-D.M.C.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biohazard_(band)" title="Biohazard (band)">Biohazard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shaquille_O%27Neal" title="Shaquille O&#39;Neal">Shaquille O'Neal</a>, <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, <a href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Enemy (band)">Public Enemy</a> and many others. Many songs were re-mixed, re-mastered and re-released after 1997.
</p><p>Five albums produced by Chyskillz, were certified as gold by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>: Public Enemy <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Misses" title="Greatest Misses">Greatest Misses</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Onyx <i>Bacdafucup</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-автоссылка2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-автоссылка2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Run-D.M.C. <i><a href="/wiki/Down_with_the_King_(album)" title="Down with the King (album)">Down With The King</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Shaquille O'Neal <i><a href="/wiki/Shaq-Fu:_Da_Return" class="mw-redirect" title="Shaq-Fu: Da Return">Shaq-Fu: Da Return</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and LL Cool J <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Smith_(album)" title="Mr. Smith (album)">Mr. Smith</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Two of them were certified as platinum: Onyx <i>Bacdafucup</i> and LL Cool J <i>Mr. Smith</i>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chyskillz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chylow Parker was born in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, New York City, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. At the age of 4 he and his family moved to <a href="/wiki/Jamaica,_Queens" title="Jamaica, Queens">Jamaica, Queens</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. At the age of 10, Chyskillz and his family moved to <a href="/wiki/Flushing,_Queens" title="Flushing, Queens">Flushing, Queens</a>. That's where he met <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a>, Neek The Exotic and <a href="/wiki/Mic_Geronimo" title="Mic Geronimo">Mic Geronimo</a>. <a href="/wiki/Royal_Flush_(rapper)" title="Royal Flush (rapper)">Royal Flush</a> used to live on the other side of the building he grew up in. 10-years-old, Chyskillz touched a pair of Technics turntables for the first time. At that moment he knew what he wanted to do with his life.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 80's Chyskillz and Large Professor were a members of b-boy crew "Stay Fresh Crew". Chylow could do "<a href="/wiki/Flare_(acrobatic_move)" title="Flare (acrobatic move)">Flare (acrobatic move)</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Airflare" title="Airflare">Airflare</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Headspin" title="Headspin">Headspin</a>". Chylow was a good graffiti writer, bombing the "I.S. 237" (Rachel Carson Intermediate School 237) roofs, you could see his tags "CHY" on Main Street (Flushing, Queens). Chylow also was a good BMX biker, rocking "Miami hoppers" before anyone else on Colden Playground (Flushing, Queens).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chyskillz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jam_Master_Jay">Jam Master Jay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chyskillz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Jam Master Jay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Chyskillz started his music career deejaying for <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a>, the founding member of <a href="/wiki/Main_Source" title="Main Source">Main Source</a>. Chyskillz working on a recording studio as an engineer and producer on Neek The Exotic's project. One day he met Fredro Starr, who saw him deejaying. Fredro liked his beats, so he gave him his phone number. <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(hip_hop_group)" title="Onyx (hip hop group)">Onyx</a>'s manager, Jeff Harris, called him and invited him to <a href="/wiki/Jam_Master_Jay" title="Jam Master Jay">Jam Master Jay</a>'s studio. When Chyskillz got with <a href="/wiki/Jam_Master_Jay" title="Jam Master Jay">Jam Master Jay</a> his first professional credit was the remix he did for Fam-Lee – "Runs in the Fam-Lee".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Onyx">Onyx</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chyskillz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Onyx">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> M.C. Neek The Exotic introduced DJ Chyskillz to the group Onyx in 1991. In Brian Coleman's book <i><a href="/wiki/Check_the_Technique" title="Check the Technique">Check the Technique</a></i> Fredro described how Onyx met their new producer:<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></p><blockquote><p>"...We met Chyskillz on Jamaica Avenue one day. We was buying weed at the weed spot and Chy was chasing my truck down the street, yelling, 'I got beats!' His stuff back then was jazzy, on some Tribe Called Quest shit, but it was hot. I knew he could put beats together right away. We brought him into our zone and made him do some grimy shit."</p></blockquote>
<p>When Chyskillz met Fredro and Sticky they were already signed to Def Jam. The group was called <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(hip_hop_group)" title="Onyx (hip hop group)">Onyx</a> and consisted of <a href="/wiki/Fredro_Starr" title="Fredro Starr">Fredro Starr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sticky_Fingaz" title="Sticky Fingaz">Sticky Fingaz</a>, Suave (a.k.a. <a href="/wiki/Sonny_Seeza" title="Sonny Seeza">Sonny Seeza</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Big_DS" title="Big DS">Big DS</a>. When Chyskillz first got with Onyx, they already have another producer, but their stuff was a little bit more commercial and friendly. Chyskillz let Fredro hear some beats and it started from there. Chyskillz began making beats for Onyx and the collaboration just worked. The first song that Chyskillz ever did for Onyx was "Nigga Bridges". He did one song for them and <a href="/wiki/Russell_Simmons" title="Russell Simmons">Russell Simmons</a> heard it and give Onyx an EP deal for 6 songs. But they did 10 songs on a budget of 6 songs, so Russell Simmons give them an album deal.
</p><p>Chyskillz and Jam Master Jay co-produced the hip-hop group Onyx's <a href="/wiki/Multi-platinum" class="mw-redirect" title="Multi-platinum">multi-platinum</a> selling debut album called <i><a href="/wiki/Bacdafucup" title="Bacdafucup">Bacdafucup</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-source_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-source-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The album is described as having a "tense, wired edge that amplifies the vividness of the threatening lyrics. Sonically, it has a hardcore <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast</a>/New York City cast, full of throbbing bass and screeching siren-like effects".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Chyskillz drew notice for his work on hit songs like "<a href="/wiki/Throw_Ya_Gunz" title="Throw Ya Gunz">Throw Ya Gunz</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Slam_(Onyx_song)" title="Slam (Onyx song)">Slam</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Jam_Master_Jay" title="Jam Master Jay">Jam Master Jay</a> of <a href="/wiki/Run-DMC" title="Run-DMC">Run-DMC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-source_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-source-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Onyx's hit song <i>Slam</i>, which Chyskillz co-produced with Jam Master Jay, was included in music writer Bruce Pollock's <i>Rock Song Index: The 7500 Most Important Songs for the Rock and Roll Era</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It also made the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Chyskillz left Onyx in the summer of 1993 cause <i>they don't need him like a producer record</i>. So Chyskillz signed with Wasteland Records.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2003 Chyskillz founded his own label "719 Music Inc.".<sup id="cite_ref-:2_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chyskillz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In an interview with <a href="/wiki/NPR" title="NPR">NPR</a>, hip hop artist and record producer <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a> described working with Chyskillz on one of his first demos:<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<blockquote><p>"...that's the style of production that we were doing at that time, where he would cut up "Synthetic Substitution" breakbeat on one tape. And you had the two and three tape decks, and he would put the tape in there. And then he would play that tape and overdub some bass lines."</p></blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Last_Year">Last Year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chyskillz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Last Year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the last year of his life, Chyskillz actively traveled by plane, now and then visiting the cities of Dallas (Texas), Atlanta (Georgia), New York and Miramar (Florida). In all the cities he had friends and there he found new clients – rap artists, for whom he produced music. On the Facebook he uploaded videos he shot at the airport, marked "Where in the world is Chyskillz now" so that his friends would try to guess the city in which the producer is located, leaving comments under the post. And in the video he used to say: "It's about catch a flight, I'll might be in your city".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chyskillz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On July 5, 2017, Chyskillz was involved in a car accident and had surgery in October on his shoulder and was scheduled to possibly have back surgery soon. He was in a lot of pain and not working so much. He was in Queens, New York. On February 10, 2018 he was suffering a bad cold for two weeks and went to the hospital and they informed him that he had the flu, should never have gone to the studio and should have been resting. On February 12, Fredro and Sticky flew to New York. Fredro picked him up in Queens to go to the studio to do some remixing for the shows. Chyskillz was so pleased with this, he certainly agreed. After the session he came home late that evening around 2am. The next morning his family discovered him. Doctors say he suffered a heart attack. Next week he had to go to Miami, and then to Dallas for work. He just finished work on the project with De La Soul.<sup id="cite_ref-Vibe_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
