<p><b>Milton Delano Martin III</b>, better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Marty Baller</b>, is an American rapper, songwriter, dancer, actor, and model from the <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. Aside from his solo career, he was a member of the hip-hop group <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Mob" title="ASAP Mob">A$AP Mob</a> and the official tour <a href="/wiki/Hype_man" title="Hype man">hype man</a> to <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> recording artist <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Marty Baller is often referred to as "Ferg's protégé" and the "Face of <a href="/wiki/Traplord" title="Traplord">Traplord</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Marty Baller gained recognition after releasing his debut <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>, "Big Timers" featuring <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a>, and the track "I'm A Dog", in collaboration with ASAP Ferg and <a href="/wiki/Migos" title="Migos">Migos</a>, on his debut mixtape <i>Marty G Raw</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Shortly after launching his solo career, Marty Baller collaborated with a variety of rappers including <a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert" title="Lil Uzi Vert">Lil Uzi Vert</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Always_Strive_and_Prosper" title="Always Strive and Prosper">Always Strive and Prosper</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Rich_The_Kid" class="mw-redirect" title="Rich The Kid">Rich The Kid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smoke_DZA" title="Smoke DZA">Smoke DZA</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fatman_Scoop" title="Fatman Scoop">Fatman Scoop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marty_Baller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2007, Marty Baller joined the <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Mob" title="ASAP Mob">A$AP Mob</a> crew, a Harlem-based collective of rappers, producers, music video directors, fashion designers, and bikers who shared similar interests in music, fashion, style, and art.<sup id="cite_ref-hypetrak1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hypetrak1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It had been formed by A$AP Yams, A$AP Bari and A$AP Illz.<sup id="cite_ref-vvblogs_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vvblogs-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Marty Baller's rapping skills were noticed by <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a> while growing up in Harlem.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Marty also gained wider attention and notoriety after being mentioned on <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a>'s single <a href="/wiki/Shabba_(song)" title="Shabba (song)">"Shabba"</a> and "<a href="/wiki/New_Level" title="New Level">New Level</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He also gained greater notoriety through weekly installments of Wavy Wednesdays through <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fader" title="The Fader">The Fader</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, drawing up inspiration from the <a href="/wiki/Big_Tymers" title="Big Tymers">Big Tymers</a>, Marty released the track "Big Timers" featuring <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a> to critical acclaim and later followed up the single.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Marty then released his solo mixtape <i>Marty G Raw</i> on November 11, 2016 with guest features from <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Migos" title="Migos">Migos</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marty_Baller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i>, Marty Baller "is carving out his own path in hip-hop.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Vice_(magazine)" title="Vice (magazine)">Noisey</a></i> wrote "Marty Baller is breaking through. Previously known for a supporting role in A$AP Mob, he’s…putting out promising tracks and [is] ready".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marty_Baller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In an interview with <a href="/wiki/The_Hundreds" title="The Hundreds">The Hundreds</a>, Marty Baller stated his influences are <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a>, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andre_3000" class="mw-redirect" title="Andre 3000">Andre 3000</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
