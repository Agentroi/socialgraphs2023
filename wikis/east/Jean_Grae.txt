<p><b>Tsidi Ibrahim</b><sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Warren_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Warren-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (born November 26, 1976), known professionally as <b>Jean Grae</b> (formerly <b>What? What?</b>), is an American rapper. She rose in the <a href="/wiki/Underground_hip_hop" title="Underground hip hop">underground hip hop</a> scene in New York City and has built an international fanbase. Grae's unique music and lyrical style have earned the artist recognition as a favorite <a href="/wiki/Master_of_ceremonies" title="Master of ceremonies">emcee</a> by many rap artists such as <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Black_Thought" title="Black Thought">Black Thought</a> of the Roots.<sup id="cite_ref-Smalls_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smalls-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jean_Grae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jean Grae was born Tsidi Ibrahim, in <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a>, South Africa, on November 26, 1976. The child of South African <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> musicians <a href="/wiki/Sathima_Bea_Benjamin" title="Sathima Bea Benjamin">Sathima Bea Benjamin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abdullah_Ibrahim" title="Abdullah Ibrahim">Abdullah Ibrahim</a>, Jean was raised in New York City, where the family moved after Jean's birth. Grae studied Vocal Performance at <a href="/wiki/Fiorello_H._LaGuardia_High_School" title="Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School">Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School</a>, before studying a Major in Music Business at <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>; dropping out after three weeks of class.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AskMen_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AskMen-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jean_Grae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1996.E2.80.931998:_Career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1996–1998:_Career_beginnings">1996–1998: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jean_Grae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1996–1998: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1995, Jean was discovered by <a href="/wiki/George_Rithm_Martinez" class="mw-redirect" title="George Rithm Martinez">George Rithm Martinez</a> when he recruited the artist for a five-song demo in his group Ground Zero. The demo earned "Unsigned Hype" honors in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Source (magazine)">The Source</a></i> in March 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-Warren_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Warren-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Jean later joined a hip hop group called Natural Resource<sup id="cite_ref-Warren_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Warren-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> along with rapper Ocean and <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a> James "AGGIE" Barrett. In 1996, they released two <a href="/wiki/12-inch_single" class="mw-redirect" title="12-inch single">12-inch singles</a> on their label, Makin' Records.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> They appeared on singles by <a href="/wiki/Pumpkinhead_(rapper)" title="Pumpkinhead (rapper)">Pumpkinhead</a> and Bad Seed, as well as on the O.B.S. (Original Blunted Soldiers) double <a href="/wiki/12-inch_single" class="mw-redirect" title="12-inch single">12-inch single</a>. They produced much of the material released under pseudonym "Run Run Shaw".
</p>
<h3><span id="1998.E2.80.932004:_Solo_career"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1998–2004:_Solo_career">1998–2004: Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jean_Grae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1998–2004: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Natural Resource dissolved in 1998, after which Jean changed her <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> from What? What? to Jean Grae, a reference to the <a href="/wiki/X-Men" title="X-Men">X-Men</a> character <a href="/wiki/Jean_Grey" title="Jean Grey">Jean Grey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Crockett_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Crockett-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Jean released the <a href="/wiki/Vinyl_record" class="mw-redirect" title="Vinyl record">LP</a>, <i>Attack of the Attacking Things,</i> on August 6, 2002, and released <i><a href="/wiki/This_Week_(album)" title="This Week (album)">This Week</a></i> on September 21, 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-Crockett_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Crockett-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Jean has recorded with major hip-hop artists such as <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere_(music_group)" title="Atmosphere (music group)">Atmosphere</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">The Roots</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phonte" title="Phonte">Phonte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mr._Len" title="Mr. Len">Mr. Len</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharoahe_Monch" title="Pharoahe Monch">Pharoahe Monch</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Herbaliser" title="The Herbaliser">The Herbaliser</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masta_Ace" title="Masta Ace">Masta Ace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Immortal_Technique" title="Immortal Technique">Immortal Technique</a>.
</p><p>Grae recorded an album with <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> producer <a href="/wiki/9th_Wonder" title="9th Wonder">9th Wonder</a>, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Jeanius" title="Jeanius">Jeanius</a></i>; the unfinished recording was leaked online, and work stopped.<sup id="cite_ref-Johnson_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Johnson-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> However, at a release party for <i>9th Wonder's Dream Merchant Volume 2,</i> Jean stated that <i>Jeanius</i> was still going to be released. It was released on June 24, 2008 on Zune Live Marketplace, then on disc on July 8, 2008. Jean's rapping was described by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Christgau" title="Robert Christgau">Robert Christgau</a> as "remarkable for its rapidity, clarity and idiomatic cadence. The writing has a good-humored polysyllabic literacy."<sup id="cite_ref-Christgau_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Christgau-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span id="2005.E2.80.932008:_Blacksmith_Music"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2005–2008:_Blacksmith_Music">2005–2008: Blacksmith Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jean_Grae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2005–2008: Blacksmith Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Previously signed to <a href="/wiki/Babygrande_Records" title="Babygrande Records">Babygrande Records</a>, Jean signed a deal in 2005 with <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Blacksmith_Records" title="Blacksmith Records">Blacksmith Records</a>. On April 28, 2008, Grae posted a blog entry on <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">MySpace</a> saying goodbye to fans.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Jean was disenchanted with the music industry and desired to start a family, but said that work on new material was ongoing.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In July 2008, Talib Kweli posted a blog entry explaining Grae's album, mentioning that Grae was not retiring. He encouraged fans to purchase the album, referring to Grae as "one of the last true MCs left." Grae returned to doing live performances later that year.<sup id="cite_ref-Chinen_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chinen-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.93present:_Freelance"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–present:_Freelance">2008–present: Freelance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jean_Grae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2008–present: Freelance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 18, 2008, Grae posted a <a href="/wiki/Craigslist" title="Craigslist">Craigslist</a> ad offering creative services for $800/16 bars.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Michaels_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Michaels-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On a blog, Jean stated, "I don't wanna complain anymore, I just wanna change some things about the way artists are treated and the way you guys are allowed to be involved, since it IS the digital age."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Since then, Jean's music has been self-released through the artist's website and <a href="/wiki/Bandcamp" title="Bandcamp">Bandcamp</a>.
</p><p>On June 25, 2011, Grae released a free mixtape entitled <i>Cookies or Comas</i>, which features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>, Talib Kweli and Pharoahe Monch; it also includes "Assassins" from Monch's <i><a href="/wiki/W.A.R._(We_Are_Renegades)" title="W.A.R. (We Are Renegades)">W.A.R.</a></i> album and "Uh Oh" From Talib Kweli's <i><a href="/wiki/Gutter_Rainbows" title="Gutter Rainbows">Gutter Rainbows</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> This was followed by the 10-track <i>Dust Ruffle</i> on January 2, 2013, featuring unreleased songs from 2004 to 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Between October and November 2013, Grae released EPs titled <i>Gotham Down Cycle 1: Love in Infinity (Lo-Fi)</i>, <i>Gotham Down Cycle II: Leviathan</i>, <i>Gotham Down Cycle 3: The Artemis Epoch</i>. In December 2013, Jean combined them into <i>Gotham Down Deluxe</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:All_My_Teeth_%E2%80%94_Molly_Lewis_and_Jean_Grae_at_the_first_Red_Team_show_on_JoCo_Cruise_2019.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/All_My_Teeth_%E2%80%94_Molly_Lewis_and_Jean_Grae_at_the_first_Red_Team_show_on_JoCo_Cruise_2019.png/220px-All_My_Teeth_%E2%80%94_Molly_Lewis_and_Jean_Grae_at_the_first_Red_Team_show_on_JoCo_Cruise_2019.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/All_My_Teeth_%E2%80%94_Molly_Lewis_and_Jean_Grae_at_the_first_Red_Team_show_on_JoCo_Cruise_2019.png/330px-All_My_Teeth_%E2%80%94_Molly_Lewis_and_Jean_Grae_at_the_first_Red_Team_show_on_JoCo_Cruise_2019.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/All_My_Teeth_%E2%80%94_Molly_Lewis_and_Jean_Grae_at_the_first_Red_Team_show_on_JoCo_Cruise_2019.png/440px-All_My_Teeth_%E2%80%94_Molly_Lewis_and_Jean_Grae_at_the_first_Red_Team_show_on_JoCo_Cruise_2019.png 2x" data-file-width="1920" data-file-height="1080" /></a><figcaption>Grae performing with <a href="/wiki/Molly_Lewis" title="Molly Lewis">Molly Lewis</a> in 2019</figcaption></figure>
<p>Grae branched out from music, releasing audiobook <i>The State of Eh</i> in January 2014,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and writing, directing and starring in the online sitcom <i>Life with Jeanie</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, Jean had a supporting role in indie film <i>Big Words</i><sup id="cite_ref-auto_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and in 2015 appeared on the <i>And The Crime Ring</i><sup id="cite_ref-auto_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> episode of <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/2_Broke_Girls" title="2 Broke Girls">2 Broke Girls</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On October 2, 2016, Jean hosted the Golden Probes.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On September 9, 2018, Grae and Quelle Chris released their 15 track joint album <i><a href="/wiki/Everything%27s_Fine_(Jean_Grae_and_Quelle_Chris_album)" title="Everything&#39;s Fine (Jean Grae and Quelle Chris album)">Everything's Fine</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> rated by <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a> as the 22nd best Hip Hop Album of 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_musical_style">Personal life and musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jean_Grae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life and musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On December 3, 2017, Grae got engaged to fellow rapper and producer <a href="/wiki/Quelle_Chris" title="Quelle Chris">Quelle Chris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On August 5, 2018, they were married at the W Loft in Brooklyn.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He is known for more than 15 studio albums and collaborative albums with fellow rappers.
</p><p>Grae identifies as <a href="/wiki/Non-binary" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-binary">non-binary</a><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and uses she/her pronouns.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Grae's rapping style relies on a complex interplay of shifting rhythms and slanted rhymes.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> An analysis by Matt Daniels for <i>The Pudding</i> indicated that Jean uses a higher-than-average range of vocabulary in her lyrics.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
