<p><b>Emile Haynie</b> (born July 13, 1980), often credited simply as <b>Emile</b>, is an American record producer. Born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a>, his range of production includes <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indie_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Indie music">indie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop music</a>. Haynie has worked with several prominent artists in the music industry including <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lana_Del_Rey" title="Lana Del Rey">Lana Del Rey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Mars" title="Bruno Mars">Bruno Mars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linkin_Park" title="Linkin Park">Linkin Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/FKA_Twigs" title="FKA Twigs">FKA Twigs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florence_Welch" title="Florence Welch">Florence Welch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Camila_Cabello" title="Camila Cabello">Camila Cabello</a>, among others. Additionally, Haynie received two <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a> for his work with English singer <a href="/wiki/Adele" title="Adele">Adele</a>, namely <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Album_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Album of the Year">Album of the Year</a> for the <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">diamond certified</a> <i><a href="/wiki/25_(Adele_album)" title="25 (Adele album)">25</a></i> as well as <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Record_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Record of the Year">Record of the Year</a> for "<a href="/wiki/Hello_(Adele_song)" title="Hello (Adele song)">Hello</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-GrammysHistory_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrammysHistory-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emile_Haynie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2003.E2.80.932008:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2003–2008:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings">2003–2008: Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emile_Haynie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2003–2008: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Emile Haynie is a native of <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a>. He started primarily as a <a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">sample</a>-driven <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">hip-hop producer</a> and got his first big break after handing off a <a href="/wiki/Instrumental_hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Instrumental hip hop">beat</a> <a href="/wiki/CD" class="mw-redirect" title="CD">CD</a> to now-deceased Detroit <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/Proof_(rapper)" title="Proof (rapper)">Proof</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He then began producing for various members of <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>’s Detroit camp, as well as <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> rappers <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cormega" title="Cormega">Cormega</a>, and <a href="/wiki/C-Rayz_Walz" title="C-Rayz Walz">C-Rayz Walz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He relocated to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and got his start as a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> producer in the early 2000s, collaborating with rappers <a href="/wiki/Obie_Trice" title="Obie Trice">Obie Trice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">The Roots</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cormega" title="Cormega">Cormega</a>, <a href="/wiki/M.O.P." title="M.O.P.">M.O.P.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhymefest" title="Rhymefest">Rhymefest</a>, and <a href="/wiki/AZ_(rapper)" title="AZ (rapper)">AZ</a>. His career grew and, by the second half of 2000's first decade, he had worked with <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse_(group)" title="Slaughterhouse (group)">Slaughterhouse</a>, Eminem, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.932010:_Dream_On_era"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–2010:_Dream_On_era">2008–2010: Dream On era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emile_Haynie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008–2010: Dream On era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, Haynie, along with record producer <a href="/wiki/Plain_Pat" title="Plain Pat">Patrick “Plain Pat” Reynolds</a>, began co-managing the career of then-up-and-coming musician Kid Cudi. The two would go on to executive produce Cudi’s debut mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/A_Kid_Named_Cudi" title="A Kid Named Cudi">A Kid Named Cudi</a></i> (2008). In late 2008, Haynie, Plain Pat and Kid Cudi launched their <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dream_On_(record_label)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dream On (record label)">Dream On</a>, in partnership with Kanye West's <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Universal_Motown" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Motown">Universal Motown</a>. Haynie <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remixed</a> <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>'s 1972 song, "Maria (You Were the Only One)", for the 2009 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Remix_Suite" title="The Remix Suite">Michael Jackson: The Remix Suite</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-levine_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-levine-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In February 2011 Kid Cudi announced Dream On had been dissolved.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Cudi stated to <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> magazine that they were still on good terms: "I wanted to try something new, and I wanted to take control of things myself.[...] There’s no hard feelings."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The label released Kid Cudi's albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Man_on_the_Moon:_The_End_of_Day" title="Man on the Moon: The End of Day">Man on the Moon: The End of Day</a></i> (2009) and <i><a href="/wiki/Man_on_the_Moon_II:_The_Legend_of_Mr._Rager" title="Man on the Moon II: The Legend of Mr. Rager">Man on the Moon II: The Legend of Mr. Rager</a></i> (2010).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932014:_Mainstream_hits"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2014:_Mainstream_hits">2010–2014: Mainstream hits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emile_Haynie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010–2014: Mainstream hits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Haynie was nominated for the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Album_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Album of the Year">Grammy Award for Album of the Year</a> for <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Recovery_(Eminem_album)" title="Recovery (Eminem album)">Recovery</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He co-produced Kanye West's 2010 single, "<a href="/wiki/Runaway_(Kanye_West_song)" title="Runaway (Kanye West song)">Runaway</a>", and expanded into <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indie_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Indie music">indie music</a>, working with <a href="/wiki/Lana_Del_Rey" title="Lana Del Rey">Lana Del Rey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Mars" title="Bruno Mars">Bruno Mars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fun_(band)" title="Fun (band)">Fun</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Haynie produced Lana Del Rey's 2012 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Born_to_Die_(Lana_Del_Rey_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Born to Die (Lana Del Rey album)">Born to Die</a></i>, which debuted on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart at number two, and at number one in Britain, Germany, Ireland, Switzerland and Austria, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Alongside <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Bhasker" title="Jeff Bhasker">Jeff Bhasker</a>, Haynie also produced <a href="/wiki/Fun_(band)" title="Fun (band)">Fun</a>'s second album <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Nights_(album)" title="Some Nights (album)">Some Nights</a></i> (2012). Haynie also co-wrote <a href="/wiki/Lady_Antebellum" class="mw-redirect" title="Lady Antebellum">Lady Antebellum</a>'s 2013's single, "<a href="/wiki/Compass_(Lady_Antebellum_song)" title="Compass (Lady Antebellum song)">Compass</a>".
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.93present:_We_Fall"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–present:_We_Fall">2015–present: <i>We Fall</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emile_Haynie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015–present: We Fall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/We_Fall" title="We Fall">We Fall</a></div>
<p>On January 19, 2015, Haynie announced he would be releasing his debut <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio album">studio album</a>, titled <i><a href="/wiki/We_Fall" title="We Fall">We Fall</a></i>, featuring <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Wyatt" title="Andrew Wyatt">Andrew Wyatt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Wilson" title="Brian Wilson">Brian Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Wainwright" title="Rufus Wainwright">Rufus Wainwright</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lana_Del_Rey" title="Lana Del Rey">Lana Del Rey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Gainsbourg" title="Charlotte Gainsbourg">Charlotte Gainsbourg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sampha" title="Sampha">Sampha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Devonte_Hynes" class="mw-redirect" title="Devonte Hynes">Devonte Hynes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nate_Ruess" title="Nate Ruess">Nate Ruess</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colin_Blunstone" title="Colin Blunstone">Colin Blunstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lykke_Li" title="Lykke Li">Lykke Li</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romy_Madley_Croft" title="Romy Madley Croft">Romy Madley Croft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Randy_Newman" title="Randy Newman">Randy Newman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Father_John_Misty" title="Father John Misty">Father John Misty</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Holter" title="Julia Holter">Julia Holter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The album, which was recorded over the course of six months in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>' <a href="/wiki/Chateau_Marmont" title="Chateau Marmont">Chateau Marmont</a>, was made available for pre-order the following day and was revealed to be released February 23, 2015, under <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2020, <a href="/wiki/Hipgnosis_Songs_Fund" title="Hipgnosis Songs Fund">Hipgnosis Songs Fund</a> confirmed the acquisition of Haynie’s music catalog. Details of the acquisition were first revealed in the <a href="/wiki/Merck_Mercuriadis" title="Merck Mercuriadis">Merck Mercuriadis</a>-led, UK-based company’s interim report, published the month prior. Hipgnosis acquired 100% of Haynie’s worldwide copyrights, including publishing and writer share, as well as  producer royalty income streams, of his catalog comprising 122 songs.<sup id="cite_ref-mbww_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mbww-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
