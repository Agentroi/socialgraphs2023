<p><b>Jarvis Antonio Mills</b> (born September 11, 1983), better known by his stage name <b>Jae Millz</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Harlem,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Harlem, New York">Harlem, New York</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-lastfm_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lastfm-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> who was signed to <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a> <a href="/wiki/Grand_Hustle_Records" title="Grand Hustle Records">Grand Hustle Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dx-deal_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dx-deal-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His debut major label studio album, titled <i>Nothing Is Promised</i> was scheduled for a 2015 release.<sup id="cite_ref-hhmusicc_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hhmusicc-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Millz is noted for participating in <a href="/wiki/Rap_battle" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap battle">rap battles</a> with MCs <a href="/wiki/Murda_Mook" title="Murda Mook">Murda Mook</a>, E Ness, K-Shine, <a href="/wiki/40_Cal." title="40 Cal.">40 Cal.</a> and <a href="/wiki/JR_Writer" class="mw-redirect" title="JR Writer">JR Writer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lastfm_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lastfm-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-natct_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-natct-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jae_Millz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jae Millz was born in Harlem, New York. He started writing lyrics while at junior high school. He followed his love for art and went to the New York High School for Cartoon and Fashion Architecture.<sup id="cite_ref-lwhq-interview_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lwhq-interview-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Millz grew to local fame by winning hip hop battles and doing performances and releasing a number of <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a>. In 2003, he released his debut single "No, No, No", which received radio play and peaked at number 89 on the US <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard magazine">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After the success of the song, Millz was offered a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>. However, due to creative differences, he left the label, without having an album released. Soon after that he signed with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Records_(defunct_record_label)" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Records (defunct record label)">Universal Records</a>. Eventually, he left Universal, too, due to the lack of promotion, as the label was giving priority to other artists.<sup id="cite_ref-dx-money_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dx-money-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jae Millz' 2004 single "Streetz Melting" was featured on the <a href="/wiki/NBA_Live_06#Soundtrack" title="NBA Live 06">soundtrack</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Arts" title="Electronic Arts">EA</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> <i><a href="/wiki/NBA_Live_06" title="NBA Live 06">NBA Live 06</a></i>.
</p><p>Millz has released 20 mixtapes since 2004, including <i>Back 2 Tha Future</i>, <i>Zone Out Season</i> and <i>Dead Presidents</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-ymhq_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ymhq-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-datpiff_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datpiff-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Jae_Millz_performing_at_General_Motors_Place.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Jae_Millz_performing_at_General_Motors_Place.jpg/220px-Jae_Millz_performing_at_General_Motors_Place.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="326" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Jae_Millz_performing_at_General_Motors_Place.jpg/330px-Jae_Millz_performing_at_General_Motors_Place.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9f/Jae_Millz_performing_at_General_Motors_Place.jpg 2x" data-file-width="414" data-file-height="614" /></a><figcaption>Millz performing in 2009</figcaption></figure>
<p>In January 2008, Millz was signed to <a href="/wiki/Young_Money_Entertainment" title="Young Money Entertainment">Young Money Entertainment</a>. He made a cameo appearance in the video for Lil Wayne's single "<a href="/wiki/Lollipop_(Lil_Wayne_song)" title="Lollipop (Lil Wayne song)">Lollipop</a>" from <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Carter_III" title="Tha Carter III">Tha Carter III</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-dx-deal_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dx-deal-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 21, 2009, Millz' collaboration album with <a href="/wiki/Young_Money_Entertainment" title="Young Money Entertainment">Young Money</a> <i><a href="/wiki/We_Are_Young_Money" title="We Are Young Money">We Are Young Money</a></i> was released. Millz was featured on eight of the songs, including the singles "<a href="/wiki/Every_Girl_(Young_Money_song)" title="Every Girl (Young Money song)">Every Girl</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Bedrock_(Young_Money_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bedrock (Young Money song)">BedRock</a>". The album landed at number nine on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, selling 142,000 copies in its first week,<sup id="cite_ref-xxl-ym_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl-ym-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and was later certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A debut album, <i>Nothing Is Promised</i>, was scheduled for a 2013 release. In an interview with <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tim_Westwood" title="Tim Westwood">Tim Westwood</a>, Millz explained that the title refers to his health problems – "The title come from [me] having two seizures in the previous year. I had them in my sleep. It's like you wake up and you don't know what happened."<sup id="cite_ref-dx-name_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dx-name-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The first single from the album, called "Forever Winning", was released in July 2011 and featured fellow Young Money rapper <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ss-single_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ss-single-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> His mixtape <i>Dead Presidents 2</i> was released on February 4, 2013.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On April 19, 2013, Millz released his 20th mixtape <i>Property Of Potentness 2</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On November 20, 2020 Millz released the album <i>Wood Panel Walls</i>, which included the single "High Rollers" featuring Anthiny King.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On February 12, 2021 Millz released the album, "Much Love" with Anthiny King (distributed through Empire Records), as a continuation of their collaboration.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
