<p><b>Aston George Taylor Jr.</b><sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born August 5, 1968),<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> professionally known as <b>Funkmaster Flex</b>, is an American DJ, rapper, record producer, and host on New York City's <a href="/wiki/WQHT" title="WQHT">Hot 97</a> radio station. In 1992, he became host of the first hip hop <a href="/wiki/Radio_show" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio show">radio show</a> on Hot 97 in New York, which was a pop radio station at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-PayBack_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PayBack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Funkmaster_Flex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aston Taylor Jr. was born in the Bronx borough of New York City to <a href="/wiki/Jamaicans" title="Jamaicans">Jamaican</a> immigrant parents.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His father, DJ Aston George Taylor Sr., was a sound system professional.<sup id="cite_ref-Vibe_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was raised in a strict, religious household.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> At the age of 16, he began DJing at local nightclubs.<sup id="cite_ref-Vibe_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_career">Radio career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Funkmaster_Flex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Radio career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By the age of 19, he began working with fellow Bronx native <a href="/wiki/DJ_Chuck_Chillout" title="DJ Chuck Chillout">Chuck Chillout</a> for <a href="/wiki/WRKS" title="WRKS">WRKS</a> 98.7 Kiss-FM in New York. He later left KISS, and spent a brief period at 107.5 <a href="/wiki/WBLS" title="WBLS">WBLS</a>-FM.<sup id="cite_ref-Shapiro_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shapiro-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In the early 1990s, Flex made club appearances at many of Vito Bruno's operated nightclubs. Bruno later convinced Joel Salkowitz, a regional vice president of Hot 97, to begin airing live broadcasts from clubs where he was performing. When they realized the resurgence of Hip Hop was coming fast from its decline in the eighties, they increased the hours of this urban radio programming.<sup id="cite_ref-PayBack_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PayBack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Due to the success of that programming, in the spring of 1992 Funkmaster Flex began mixing and hosting his own show, a specialized rap program on Hot 97. With this, Hot 97 became the first pop station in New York to showcase rap. Flex has been with Hot 97 ever since, and currently airs nationally through syndication during weeknights and weekends.<sup id="cite_ref-PayBack_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PayBack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is well known for his signature "bomb drop" sound effect over records.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Website">Website</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Funkmaster_Flex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Website">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On August 3, 2010, Flex launched his news website InFlexWeTrust.com.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He makes the audio, video and mp3's of artist freestyles from his show "exclusively" available on the site. The site also posts entertainment news, music, cars, models, sports and technology. It joined the Complex media network in September 2012, and has been mentioned in songs, including <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>' "So NY."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Funkmaster_Flex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, Flex was arrested for allegedly pushing his wife Monica Joseph-Taylor and sending her threatening texts.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> As of 2014, Flex is battling his ex-girlfriend in court over child support for their son.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Flex had a feud with rival NYC hip-hop radio station <a href="/wiki/Power_105.1" class="mw-redirect" title="Power 105.1">Power 105.1</a> and their radio personalities, including <a href="/wiki/DJ_Envy" title="DJ Envy">DJ Envy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charlamagne_Tha_God" class="mw-redirect" title="Charlamagne Tha God">Charlamagne Tha God</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, a petition was started online to get Funkmaster Flex to step down from hosting his radio program on NYC's <a href="/wiki/WQHT" title="WQHT">Hot 97</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Flex promised through a series of social media posts that he would air an exclusive premiere of <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>'s answer <a href="/wiki/Diss_(music)" title="Diss (music)">diss</a> track to rapper <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>'s "Charged Up" in the midst of their feud.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> During the actual broadcast on Monday, July 27, Flex told listeners that he had reference tracks that would "expose" Drake's usage of a ghostwriter.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> However, after many spins of <a href="/wiki/Fetty_Wap" title="Fetty Wap">Fetty Wap</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Trap_Queen" title="Trap Queen">Trap Queen</a>", <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Bitch_Better_Have_My_Money" title="Bitch Better Have My Money">Bitch Better Have My Money</a>", and songs off of <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>'s <a href="/wiki/DS2_(album)" title="DS2 (album)">DS2</a>, listeners started to become enraged, citing that they felt duped.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_production">Music production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Funkmaster_Flex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By the mid-1990s, Flex was signed by a major record label, Loud Records, for a series of mixtapes entitled <i>60 Minutes of Funk</i>. All four were certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">RIAA</a> in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-Shapiro_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shapiro-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1995 he formed <a href="/wiki/The_Flip_Squad" title="The Flip Squad">The Flip Squad</a> along with seven of New York City's most respected disc jockeys, including <a href="/wiki/Biz_Markie" title="Biz Markie">Biz Markie</a>, "BounceMasta" Doo Wop, <a href="/wiki/Big_Kap" title="Big Kap">Big Kap</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Enuff" title="DJ Enuff">DJ Enuff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mister_Cee" title="Mister Cee">Mister Cee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frankie_Cutlass" title="Frankie Cutlass">Frankie Cutlass</a>, DJ Riz, <a href="/wiki/Cipha_Sounds" title="Cipha Sounds">Cipha Sounds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Ronson" title="Mark Ronson">Mark Ronson</a>. Their self-titled debut LP was released on <a href="/wiki/MCA_Records" title="MCA Records">MCA</a> in late 1998. In 1999, he released <a href="/wiki/The_Tunnel_(album)" title="The Tunnel (album)">The Tunnel</a> with Def Jam, which included songs by artists <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, DJ Myth, <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Shapiro_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shapiro-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Other acts he has worked with include <a href="/wiki/Yvette_Michele" title="Yvette Michele">Yvette Michele</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pras" title="Pras">Pras</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Kool" title="DJ Kool">DJ Kool</a> ,  Foxy Brown and <a href="/wiki/Armand_Van_Helden" class="mw-redirect" title="Armand Van Helden">Armand Van Helden</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Artistopia_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Artistopia-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2020, Flex announced plans of an upcoming album. In December, he released the album's lead single, "Lurkin", with <a href="/wiki/King_Von" title="King Von">King Von</a>, while confirming that the album would feature an array of artists, including <a href="/wiki/Juice_Wrld" title="Juice Wrld">Juice Wrld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kodak_Black" title="Kodak Black">Kodak Black</a>, <a href="/wiki/Post_Malone" title="Post Malone">Post Malone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Baby" title="Lil Baby">Lil Baby</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pop_Smoke" title="Pop Smoke">Pop Smoke</a>, among others. A release date is yet to be announced, however the album will be released through Flex's own label IFWT Films and Records.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Funkmaster_Flex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Funkmaster Flex first appeared on television in the early nineties on <i><a href="/wiki/Yo!_MTV_Raps" title="Yo! MTV Raps">Yo! MTV Raps</a></i> hosted by <a href="/wiki/Fab_5_Freddy" class="mw-redirect" title="Fab 5 Freddy">Fab 5 Freddy</a>, DJ Myth, <a href="/wiki/Ed_Lover" title="Ed Lover">Ed Lover</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Dr%C3%A9" title="Doctor Dré">Doctor Dré</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Real_TV_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Real_TV-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He later did various hip-hop and automobile spots for MTV.
</p><p>In 2003, Flex debuted his first television series, <a href="/wiki/Ride_with_Funkmaster_Flex" title="Ride with Funkmaster Flex">Ride with Funkmaster Flex</a> on the Spike cable network.<sup id="cite_ref-Spike_TV_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spike_TV-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The show documented the subculture of cars popular in hip hop culture. Noteworthy moments in the series included a look into <a href="/wiki/Puff_Daddy" class="mw-redirect" title="Puff Daddy">Diddy's</a> private jet and a visit to <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s studio.<sup id="cite_ref-Spike_TV_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spike_TV-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Flex later developed a one-off Spike TV show race event – The Funkmaster Flex Super Series Invitational.<sup id="cite_ref-Super_Series_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Super_Series-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The race featured 60 late-model stock car drivers at The <a href="/wiki/Waterford_Speedbowl" title="Waterford Speedbowl">Waterford Speedbowl</a> in Connecticut.<sup id="cite_ref-Super_Series_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Super_Series-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Celebrities like <a href="/wiki/Orange_County_Choppers" title="Orange County Choppers">Orange County Choppers</a>, <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a> were brought out to watch the event.<sup id="cite_ref-Super_Series_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Super_Series-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>ESPN noticed <i>Ride with Funkmaster Flex</i> and decided to create a show with Flex focusing on custom cars and interviewing athletes from the <a href="/wiki/NBA" class="mw-redirect" title="NBA">NBA</a>, <a href="/wiki/NFL" class="mw-redirect" title="NFL">NFL</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>. Guests included <a href="/wiki/Danica_Patrick" title="Danica Patrick">Danica Patrick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Terrell_Owens" title="Terrell Owens">Terrell Owens</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jason_Giambi" title="Jason Giambi">Jason Giambi</a>. The show was called <i>All Muscle with Funkmaster Flex</i> and began airing early 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Later in 2007, a second show called Car Wars with Funkmaster Flex premiered on <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Car Wars asked different car customizers to modify existing vehicles for cash prizes. If featured automotive designers such as <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Schiavone" title="Patrick Schiavone">Patrick Schiavone</a>, former Ford North American Truck &amp; SUV Design Director and currently Vice President of Design at <a href="/wiki/Whirlpool" title="Whirlpool">Whirlpool</a>, Ford Explorer Exterior Design Manager Melvin Betancourt and Louis D'erasmo of Valanca Auto Concepts.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For <i>Fast Machines with Funkmaster Flex</i>, Flex returned to Spike TV to focus on making muscle and modern car customization for celebrities. Examples included a 1955 <a href="/wiki/Chevy_Bel_Air" class="mw-redirect" title="Chevy Bel Air">Chevy Bel Air</a> for <a href="/wiki/Dale_Earnhardt_Jr." title="Dale Earnhardt Jr.">Dale Earnhardt Jr.</a>, a 1966 <a href="/wiki/Pontiac_GTO" title="Pontiac GTO">Pontiac GTO</a> for Pontiac Enthusiast Magazine and a 1970 <a href="/wiki/Chevy_Chevelle" class="mw-redirect" title="Chevy Chevelle">Chevy Chevelle</a> for the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Marines" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Marines">U.S. Marines</a>. It also featured an interview with Royal Purple race driver Kathryn Minter.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Flex returned to <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> with a new show – <i><a href="/wiki/Funk_Flex_Full_Throttle" title="Funk Flex Full Throttle">Funk Flex Full Throttle</a></i>. The show featured Flex interviewing hip-hop artists and customizing cars; either on location at <a href="/wiki/Spring_Break" class="mw-redirect" title="Spring Break">Spring Break</a>, the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Auto_Show" class="mw-redirect" title="New York Auto Show">New York Auto Show</a> or in his garage. <i>Full Throttle</i> also integrated reality elements as Flex directed his team of car customizers at various builds and allowed viewers inside the lifestyle of Hip-Hop stars.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Funkmaster Flex appeared on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> as part of the reality show <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_Hot_97" title="This Is Hot 97">This Is Hot 97</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Car_Show_Tour">Car Show Tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Funkmaster_Flex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Car Show Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taylor developed and promotes an annual eight-city Funkmaster Flex Custom Car &amp; Bike Show Tour. It has had featured artists such as <a href="/wiki/Drake_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (entertainer)">Drake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>. Each stop showcases customized cars in competition for cash prizes and model and recording talent search. In 2010, he added the Funkmaster Flex Lifestyle Expo which included sneaker and DJ battles, video game competitions, <a href="/wiki/Skateboard" title="Skateboard">skateboard</a> demos.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He also displays some of his personal collection of roughly 40 <a href="/wiki/Muscle_cars" class="mw-redirect" title="Muscle cars">muscle cars</a> at each stop of the tour.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Auto_design">Auto design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Funkmaster_Flex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Auto design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In 2005, Ford Motor Company opened its Dearborn design studio doors to Taylor to look at how to customize its product line-up and add exposure to the brand. He has since customized several models for Ford.<sup id="cite_ref-Ford_Media_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ford_Media-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Funkmaster_Flex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is a voice actor in <i><a href="/wiki/Quest_for_the_Code" title="Quest for the Code">Quest for the Code</a></i> in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>FunkMaster Flex's namesake was used on the American release for the PS2 video game <i>Music 3000</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Music_3000" title="Music 3000">FunkMaster Flex's Digital Hitz Factory</a></i> in 2004.
</p><p>Flex is a featured DJ on The Beat 102.7 in <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_IV" title="Grand Theft Auto IV">Grand Theft Auto IV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_IV:_The_Lost_and_Damned" title="Grand Theft Auto IV: The Lost and Damned">Grand Theft Auto IV: The Lost and Damned</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ballad_of_Gay_Tony" class="mw-redirect" title="The Ballad of Gay Tony">The Ballad of Gay Tony</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is a celebrity adversary and an unlockable free agent fullback in season mode of <i><a href="/wiki/ESPN_NFL_2K5" title="ESPN NFL 2K5">ESPN NFL 2K5</a></i>. He also has an unlockable team, the Baurtwell Funkmasters.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Flex was an unlockable character and in-game narrator in <i><a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Vendetta" title="Def Jam Vendetta">Def Jam Vendetta</a></i>.
</p><p>Flex is also mentioned in the prelude of <i><a href="/wiki/NBA_2K18" title="NBA 2K18">NBA 2K18</a></i> when talking to ATM (cpu character).
</p>
