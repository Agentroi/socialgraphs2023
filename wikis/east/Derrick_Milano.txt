<p><b>Derrick Carrington Gray</b> (born December 24, 1993), known professionally as <b>Derrick Milano</b>, is an American songwriter, rapper, and singer. He grew up in Philadelphia and in <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a> before moving to <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> for college.<sup id="cite_ref-del-online_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-del-online-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He began his musical career as a rapper in 2013 before shifting to songwriting in 2019. He has since written songs for artists including <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_Smoke" title="Pop Smoke">Pop Smoke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Megan_Thee_Stallion" title="Megan Thee Stallion">Megan Thee Stallion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-del-online_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-del-online-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2021, he won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Song" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Song">Grammy Award for Best Rap Song</a> at the <a href="/wiki/63rd_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="63rd Annual Grammy Awards">63rd Annual Grammy Awards</a> for co-writing the rap song "<a href="/wiki/Savage_(Beyonc%C3%A9_remix)" class="mw-redirect" title="Savage (Beyoncé remix)">Savage Remix</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Megan_Thee_Stallion" title="Megan Thee Stallion">Megan Thee Stallion</a> and featuring <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Derrick_Milano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Derrick Gray was born in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</a>, to Virginia and Jeff Gray.<sup id="cite_ref-del-online_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-del-online-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Gray first gained experience in music as a member of his church's choir.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His family moved to <a href="/wiki/Brandywine_Hundred,_Delaware" class="mw-redirect" title="Brandywine Hundred, Delaware">Brandywine Hundred, Delaware</a>, when he was in seventh grade, and he attended Hanby Middle School, <a href="/wiki/Brandywine_High_School" title="Brandywine High School">Brandywine High School</a>, and Red Lion Christian Academy, before graduating from <a href="/wiki/Concord_High_School_(Delaware)" title="Concord High School (Delaware)">Concord High School</a> in 2012. While in high school, Gray would battle rap with friends and was a member of the school choir.<sup id="cite_ref-del-online_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-del-online-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After graduating from high school, Gray attended college in Florida, including at <a href="/wiki/Valencia_College" title="Valencia College">Valencia College</a><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Full_Sail_University" title="Full Sail University">Full Sail University</a>, where he was involved in <a href="/wiki/Business_Professionals_of_America" title="Business Professionals of America">Business Professionals of America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Derrick_Milano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Early_career_.282013.E2.80.932018.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career_(2013–2018)">Early career (2013–2018)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Derrick_Milano&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career (2013–2018)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Milano began his music career while in college, where he distributed mixtapes for his music. His breakout success came with the release of the "trap-happy" single "#ThatAintYaBitch",<sup id="cite_ref-:1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> which he released on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> in May 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> As a result of the single's success, he was invited to open for artists playing shows at Orlando-area venues, including for rappers <a href="/wiki/Young_Thug" title="Young Thug">Young Thug</a> and <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Following this early success, a dispute with his management made releasing and promoting music challenging for Milano.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Milano's first writing experience came on <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Gates" title="Kevin Gates">Kevin Gates</a>' 2019 album <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_Him" title="I&#39;m Him">I'm Him</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In May 2019, <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a>, who Milano first connected with when he was in high school,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> invited Milano to help with <a href="/wiki/Megan_Thee_Stallion" title="Megan Thee Stallion">Megan Thee Stallion</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Simon_Says_(Megan_Thee_Stallion_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Simon Says (Megan Thee Stallion song)">Simon Says</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This led to an uncredited writing opportunity on "<a href="/wiki/Hot_Girl_Summer" title="Hot Girl Summer">Hot Girl Summer</a>", another song by Megan Thee Stallion, which was released in July 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Milano continued to work with Megan Thee Stallion, co-writing "<a href="/wiki/Savage_Remix" class="mw-redirect" title="Savage Remix">Savage Remix</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-Tidal_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tidal-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> which features <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a> and was released in April 2020. In March 2021, Milano won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Song" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Song">Grammy Award for Best Rap Song</a> at the <a href="/wiki/63rd_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="63rd Annual Grammy Awards">63rd Annual Grammy Awards</a> for his work as a co-writer on "Savage Remix".<sup id="cite_ref-:2_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of March 2021, Milano was working on his debut album, which will include tracks featuring Juicy J, <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> and is scheduled to be released later in 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-del-online_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-del-online-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2023, Milano was featured on a remix to <a href="/wiki/Ciara" title="Ciara">Ciara</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Da_Girls" title="Da Girls">Da Girls</a>". It was released on May 12, 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
