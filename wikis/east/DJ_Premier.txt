<p><b>Christopher Edward Martin</b> (born March 21, 1966),<sup id="cite_ref-ALLMUSIC_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ALLMUSIC-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>DJ Premier</b> (also known as <b>Preemo</b>), is an American <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJ</a>. He is considered one of the greatest hip hop producers of all time. He was half of the hip hop duo <a href="/wiki/Gang_Starr" title="Gang Starr">Gang Starr</a>—alongside the rapper <a href="/wiki/Guru_(rapper)" title="Guru (rapper)">Guru</a>—and presently forms half of the hip hop duo <a href="/wiki/PRhyme" title="PRhyme">PRhyme</a>, together with <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Premier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Christopher Edward Martin was born in the <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Ward,_Houston" title="Fifth Ward, Houston">Fifth Ward</a> of <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a> on March 21, 1966. He was then raised in <a href="/wiki/Prairie_View,_Texas" title="Prairie View, Texas">Prairie View, Texas</a>, before moving to <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a>, during his teenage years.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Prairie_View_A%26M_University" title="Prairie View A&amp;M University">Prairie View A&amp;M University</a>, where he honed his musical skills as the campus DJ, and he also occasionally performed with the Marching Storm band.<sup id="cite_ref-Morning_news_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morning_news-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Premier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p><p>Premier collaborated with MC <a href="/wiki/Jeru_the_Damaja" title="Jeru the Damaja">Jeru the Damaja</a> on the album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sun_Rises_in_the_East" title="The Sun Rises in the East">The Sun Rises in the East</a></i>, released in 1994, as well as the 1996 follow-up, <i><a href="/wiki/Wrath_of_the_Math" title="Wrath of the Math">Wrath of the Math</a></i>. Premier produced and supervised <i><a href="/wiki/Livin%27_Proof" title="Livin&#39; Proof">Livin' Proof</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Group_Home" title="Group Home">Group Home</a>, also from the Gang Starr Foundation; although overlooked at the time of its 1995 release, the album eventually received acclaim. He produced several tracks on fellow Gang Starr Foundation member <a href="/wiki/Afu-Ra" title="Afu-Ra">Afu-Ra</a>'s debut album <a href="/wiki/Body_of_the_Life_Force" title="Body of the Life Force">Body of the Life Force</a> and <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>'s debut solo album <a href="/wiki/Return_of_the_Boom_Bap" title="Return of the Boom Bap">Return of the Boom Bap</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-ALLMUSIC_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ALLMUSIC-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, pop/R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a> recruited Premier to produce her album <i><a href="/wiki/Back_to_Basics_(Christina_Aguilera_album)" title="Back to Basics (Christina Aguilera album)">Back to Basics</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Premier explained the reservations he heard from people regarding the collaboration:<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<blockquote>
<p>When Christina [Aguilera] had me work with her, people were like, "Oh no. Please don't." Then, when they heard "Ain't No Other Man," "Back In The Day," and all the other records were still constructed like Premier tracks they were like, "Yo, I really love this album. I was really impressed. I was worried, but you totally sound like what we love about you." Until you hear it, don't say a word. I never want to be just attached to hip hop. I want to be attached to music—country, rap, soul, jazz, blues, it doesn't matter, gospel music—I come from all of that.
</p>
</blockquote>

</p><p>Premier produced the majority of <a href="/wiki/Blaq_Poet" title="Blaq Poet">Blaq Poet</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Blaqprint" title="Tha Blaqprint">Tha Blaqprint</a></i>, which was released in mid-2009.<sup id="cite_ref-Morning_news_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morning_news-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Premier contributed a song on <a href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Game (rapper)">Game</a>'s 2011 album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_R.E.D._Album" title="The R.E.D. Album">The R.E.D. Album</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Also in 2011, Premier appeared on the song "Gangster", on <a href="/wiki/Bushido_(rapper)" title="Bushido (rapper)">Bushido</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Jenseits_von_Gut_und_B%C3%B6se_(album)" title="Jenseits von Gut und Böse (album)">Jenseits von Gut und Böse</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>

</p><p>DJ Premier hosted a weekly two-hour show, <i>Live From HeadQCourterz</i>, on <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Satellite_Radio" title="Sirius Satellite Radio">Sirius Satellite Radio</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Shade_45" title="Shade 45">Shade 45</a>. The show ended in 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>DJ Premier served as one of the three curators for the soundtrack to the video game <i><a href="/wiki/NBA_2K16" title="NBA 2K16">NBA 2K16</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Mustard" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Mustard">DJ Mustard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaborations">Collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Premier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The early line-up of the <a href="/wiki/Gang_Starr_Foundation" title="Gang Starr Foundation">Gang Starr Foundation</a> in the mid-1990s included <a href="/wiki/Jeru_the_Damaja" title="Jeru the Damaja">Jeru the Damaja</a>, <a href="/wiki/Group_Home" title="Group Home">Group Home</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Shug" title="Big Shug">Big Shug</a>, and Gang Starr. Premier was fully responsible for the production of Jeru the Damaja's first two albums, <i>The Sun Rises in the East</i> and <i>Wrath of the Math</i>. Jeru released three subsequent albums that did not include Premier's involvement.<sup id="cite_ref-ALLMUSIC_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ALLMUSIC-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In terms of his Group Home collaboration, Premier commented in a 2003 interview: "They [Group Home] don't respect what fed them." He further explained that he only agreed to produce the track "The Legacy" because of Guru's involvement.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Other artists closely tied to the Gang Starr Foundation who Premier collaborated with include: <a href="/wiki/Afu_Ra" class="mw-redirect" title="Afu Ra">Afu Ra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bahamadia" title="Bahamadia">Bahamadia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Krumbsnatcha,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Big Shug,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Smiley the Ghetto Child,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and NYGz.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Afu Ra explained Premier's influence in a September 2014 interview with the "Lyrically Fit" website:<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Premier definitely coached me when I was in the booth. "harder..stronger..more this..more that"—I was an untamed artist and rapping next to recording your vocals are different. He gave me this knowledge through the work we did.
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Besides the Gang Starr Foundation, Premier is closely affiliated with <a href="/wiki/M.O.P." title="M.O.P.">M.O.P.</a>, which he names as one of his all-time favorite groups. The relationship started with the remix of "Rugged, Neva Smoove" in 1994, a single from the group's first album, which also included the exclusive B-side "Downtown Swinga." From then, Premier produced about one-third of the songs on each subsequent album and overseeing and mixing the projects. On M.O.P.'s 2009 <i><a href="/wiki/Foundation_(M.O.P._album)" title="Foundation (M.O.P. album)">Foundation</a></i> album, Premier provided only one track, which was called "What I Wanna B."
</p><p>On May 16, 2019, DJ Premier released a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo (New York)</a> rappers <a href="/wiki/Conway_The_Machine" class="mw-redirect" title="Conway The Machine">Conway The Machine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Westside_Gunn" title="Westside Gunn">Westside Gunn</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Benny_the_Butcher" title="Benny the Butcher">Benny the Butcher</a>, called "Headlines."
</p><p>In June 2020, DJ Premier produced a track for <a href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Enemy (band)">Public Enemy</a> called "State of the Union (STFU)".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2023, DJ Premier's collaboration clothing collection with AMIRI was released, marked with "PREEMO" alongside the Amiri typography, with a record print.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_and_influences">Style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Premier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:DJ_Preemo_-_Gods_of_Rap_Tour_2019_-_Berlin_(5_von_6).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/DJ_Preemo_-_Gods_of_Rap_Tour_2019_-_Berlin_%285_von_6%29.jpg/220px-DJ_Preemo_-_Gods_of_Rap_Tour_2019_-_Berlin_%285_von_6%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/DJ_Preemo_-_Gods_of_Rap_Tour_2019_-_Berlin_%285_von_6%29.jpg/330px-DJ_Preemo_-_Gods_of_Rap_Tour_2019_-_Berlin_%285_von_6%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/DJ_Preemo_-_Gods_of_Rap_Tour_2019_-_Berlin_%285_von_6%29.jpg/440px-DJ_Preemo_-_Gods_of_Rap_Tour_2019_-_Berlin_%285_von_6%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3240" data-file-height="2160" /></a><figcaption>DJ Premier in 2019</figcaption></figure>
<p>DJ Premier's style of production epitomizes the New York sound of his earlier peers. He is known for <a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">sampling</a> <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a> artists, as well as sampling an artist's past work when creating a new track for that same artist. For example, on Jay-Z's "So Ghetto", from the 1999 album <i><a href="/wiki/Vol._3..._Life_and_Times_of_S._Carter" title="Vol. 3... Life and Times of S. Carter">Vol. 3... Life and Times of S. Carter</a></i>, Premier samples an older Jay-Z song, 1997's "Who You Wit II".<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In addition, his encyclopedic memory of hip-hop lyrics allows him to distinctively speak with his hands by scratching in lyrics from several different songs to construct new phrases.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Premier's beats are known for his oft-imitated combinations of short vocal samples, often from multiple artists, to create a chorus. Premier has also experimented with <a href="/wiki/Atonality" title="Atonality">atonal</a> samples that are not confined to soul, jazz, and funk. For example, he sampled and chopped up seminal <a href="/wiki/Electroacoustic_music" title="Electroacoustic music">electro-acoustic</a> music from the 1960s on the track "Physical Stamina" by Jeru the Damaja.
</p><p>In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="XXL Magazine">XXL Magazine</a></i>, DJ Premier was asked how his sound evolved, to which he replied, "<a href="/wiki/Marley_Marl" title="Marley Marl">Marley Marl</a> is my number-one inspiration. <a href="/wiki/Jam_Master_Jay" title="Jam Master Jay">Jam Master Jay</a>, Mix Master Ice, and <a href="/wiki/UTFO" title="UTFO">UTFO</a>. Grandmaster D, and <a href="/wiki/Whodini" title="Whodini">Whodini</a>. <a href="/wiki/DJ_Cheese" title="DJ Cheese">DJ Cheese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_Flash" title="Grandmaster Flash">Grandmaster Flash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_Herc" class="mw-redirect" title="Kool Herc">Kool Herc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afrika_Bambaataa" title="Afrika Bambaataa">Afrika Bambaataa</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jazzy_Jay" title="Jazzy Jay">Jazzy Jay</a>—even <a href="/wiki/Cut_Creator" class="mw-redirect" title="Cut Creator">Cut Creator</a>. Seeing them do what they do. It's black music, it's black culture, it comes from the ghetto. How can you not relate to ghetto people when that's the rawest form of blackness? Even though it's not a good place in regards to the economy and how bad people have it in the neighborhood, the realism's there, and that's what we were born out of. So I very much pay respect by doing the same type of music in return."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> DJ Premier attended Prairie View A&amp;M University and may have been influenced by the musical atmosphere at the university while serving as the campus DJ and occasional performer with the world-famous Marching Storm band.<sup id="cite_ref-Morning_news_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morning_news-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was a major progenitor of the <a href="/wiki/Boom_bap" title="Boom bap">boom-bap</a> style of hip-hop production.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_career">Business career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Premier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Business career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:D%2Bd-01.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/D%2Bd-01.jpg/220px-D%2Bd-01.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/D%2Bd-01.jpg/330px-D%2Bd-01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/D%2Bd-01.jpg/440px-D%2Bd-01.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1070" data-file-height="711" /></a><figcaption>DJ Premier's studio at <a href="/wiki/D%26D_Studios" title="D&amp;D Studios">D&amp;D</a> in 2000</figcaption></figure>
<p>As a businessman, DJ Premier has his own record company named Year Round Records. Among its artists are New York group NYGz, New Jersey rapper Nick Javas, and Houston rapper <a href="/w/index.php?title=Khalil_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Khalil (rapper) (page does not exist)">Khalil</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Blaqprint" title="Tha Blaqprint">Tha Blaqprint</a></i> by Blaq Poet, which features 13 Premier productions, was released by Year Round Records through Fat Beats Records in June 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-Morning_news_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morning_news-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Premier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For its 20th-anniversary issue in 2008, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Source (magazine)">The Source</a></i> selected DJ Premier for its list of the 20 greatest producers in the magazine's history.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> He also made the finals in <i><a href="/wiki/Vibe_(magazine)" title="Vibe (magazine)">Vibe</a></i> magazine's 2010 search for the greatest hip hop producer of all time—he finished second after Dr. Dre received 62 percent of the votes.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
