<p><b>Reminisce Kioni Mackie</b> (<span title="Name at birth"><a href="/wiki/Birth_name#Maiden_and_married_names" title="Birth name">née</a></span> <b>Smith</b>; born May 30, 1980), known professionally as <b>Remy Ma</b>, is an American rapper. Discovered by <a href="/wiki/Big_Pun" title="Big Pun">Big Pun</a>, she came to prominence for her work as a member of <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>'s group, <a href="/wiki/Terror_Squad_(group)" title="Terror Squad (group)">Terror Squad</a>. In 2006, she released her debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/There%27s_Something_About_Remy:_Based_on_a_True_Story" title="There&#39;s Something About Remy: Based on a True Story">There's Something About Remy: Based on a True Story</a></i>, which became a modest success, peaking at number 33 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart. Ma's most commercially successful songs include "<a href="/wiki/Whuteva" class="mw-redirect" title="Whuteva">Whuteva</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Ante_Up_(song)" title="Ante Up (song)">Ante Up (Remix)</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Lean_Back" title="Lean Back">Lean Back</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Conceited_(There%27s_Something_About_Remy)" title="Conceited (There&#39;s Something About Remy)">Conceited</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/All_the_Way_Up_(Fat_Joe_and_Remy_Ma_song)" title="All the Way Up (Fat Joe and Remy Ma song)">All the Way Up</a>".
</p><p>She is one of only four multiple winners of the <a href="/wiki/BET_Award_for_Best_Female_Hip-Hop_Artist" class="mw-redirect" title="BET Award for Best Female Hip-Hop Artist">BET Award for Best Female Hip-Hop Artist</a>, which she won in 2005 and 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Ma is the recipient of two <a href="/wiki/Vibe_(magazine)" title="Vibe (magazine)"><i>Vibe</i> Awards</a>, two <a href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Source (magazine)">Source Awards</a>, and has been nominated for four <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a>. From 2015 to 2020, she starred on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s reality series <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_New_York" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York">Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York</a></i>, alongside her husband <a href="/wiki/Papoose_(rapper)" title="Papoose (rapper)">Papoose</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remy_Ma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career_beginnings">Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remy_Ma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Smith was born in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">The Bronx</a>, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Castle_Hill,_Bronx" title="Castle Hill, Bronx">Castle Hill Projects</a>. She was exposed to her family's <a href="/wiki/Drug_abuse" class="mw-redirect" title="Drug abuse">drug abuse</a> at an early age.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She was forced to take care of her younger brothers and sisters at a young age and retreated from her home life issues by writing poetry. Her reputation spread around the Bronx, eventually getting the attention of <a href="/wiki/Big_Pun" title="Big Pun">Big Pun</a>. After a meeting and <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a> session, Pun took Ma under his wing and became her mentor.<sup id="cite_ref-sing1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sing1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Ma kickstarted her singing career under the name "Remy Martin" on Big Pun's album <i><a href="/wiki/Yeeeah_Baby" title="Yeeeah Baby">Yeeeah Baby</a></i>, where she was featured on the tracks "Ms. Martin" and "You Was Wrong".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="True_Story.2C_There.27s_Something_About_Remy:_Based_on_a_True_Story_.282004.E2.80.932006.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="True_Story,_There's_Something_About_Remy:_Based_on_a_True_Story_(2004–2006)"><i>True Story</i>, <i>There's Something About Remy: Based on a True Story</i> (2004–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remy_Ma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: True Story, There&#039;s Something About Remy: Based on a True Story (2004–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the death of <a href="/wiki/Big_Pun" title="Big Pun">Big Pun</a>, rapper <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a> signed Ma to his imprint label under <a href="/wiki/SRC_Records" title="SRC Records">SRC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Universal_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Records">Universal</a> where she became a member of his hip hop group <a href="/wiki/Terror_Squad_(group)" title="Terror Squad (group)">Terror Squad</a>. She appeared on their second and final album, <i><a href="/wiki/True_Story_(Terror_Squad_album)" title="True Story (Terror Squad album)">True Story</a></i>, released on July 27, 2004. Their single, "<a href="/wiki/Lean_Back" title="Lean Back">Lean Back</a>", topped the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> for three weeks from August 21, 2004, garnered Ma her first Grammy nomination.
</p><p>On February 7, 2006, Ma released her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/There%27s_Something_About_Remy:_Based_on_a_True_Story" title="There&#39;s Something About Remy: Based on a True Story">There's Something About Remy: Based on a True Story</a></i>, which was supported by the singles "<a href="/wiki/Whuteva" class="mw-redirect" title="Whuteva">Whuteva</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Conceited_(There%27s_Something_About_Remy)" title="Conceited (There&#39;s Something About Remy)">Conceited</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Feels_So_Good_(Remy_Ma_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Feels So Good (Remy Ma song)">Feels So Good</a>". The album was a critical success, however a disappointment financially, moving 37,000 units in its opening week and 158,000 units within the first year. Remy was frustrated at the way the album was being promoted by Universal and tensions between her and the label caused a rift between Ma and Fat Joe. Ma and Universal parted ways, and she severed her associations with Joe and the Terror Squad.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Incarceration.2C_mixtapes_.282007.E2.80.932014.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Incarceration,_mixtapes_(2007–2014)">Incarceration, mixtapes (2007–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remy_Ma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Incarceration, mixtapes (2007–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 13, 2007, Ma told <i>Billboard</i> that she was working on her second album, <i>PunisHer</i>, as well as a collaboration album as part of the all-female rap <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> 3Sum, alongside female rappers <a href="/wiki/Shawnna" title="Shawnna">Shawnna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacki-O" title="Jacki-O">Jacki-O</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2007, Remy was arrested for shooting Makeda Barnes-Joseph at a Manhattan nightspot. She was found guilty on assault, weapons, and attempted coercion charges; on May 13, 2008, she was sentenced to eight years in prison. She maintained that the shooting was an accident.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While incarcerated, she released the mixtapes <i>The BX Files</i>, <i>Shesus Khryst</i> and <i>Blasremy</i>. In 2011, Ma's music was featured and discussed in the documentary <i>Black Lifestyle in Japan</i>, where she was praised for her style and listed as one of the most frequently listened to hip-hop musicians in Japan, among younger women.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ma was released from prison in 2014 after serving six years in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On October 31, 2014, she released the mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_Around" title="I&#39;m Around">I'm Around</a></i>. On April 28, 2015, she released <i>Remy on the Rocks</i>, a compilation of various tracks from her mixtapes.
</p>
<h3><span id="Love_and_Hip_Hop_and_television_career.2C_Plata_O_Plomo_.282015.E2.80.932017.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Love_and_Hip_Hop_and_television_career,_Plata_O_Plomo_(2015–2017)"><i>Love and Hip Hop</i> and television career, <i>Plata O Plomo</i> (2015–2017)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remy_Ma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Love and Hip Hop and television career, Plata O Plomo (2015–2017)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 7, 2015, Ma announced that she was joining <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_New_York" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York">Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York</a></i> for its sixth season, along with her husband <a href="/wiki/Papoose_(rapper)" title="Papoose (rapper)">Papoose</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> She has continued to appear on the show as a series regular in seasons seven, eight, nine and ten, as well as starring in the spin-off show <i><a href="/wiki/Remy_%26_Papoose:_Meet_the_Mackies" title="Remy &amp; Papoose: Meet the Mackies">Remy &amp; Papoose: Meet the Mackies</a></i>, and the specials <i>Remy &amp; Papoose: A Merry Mackie Holiday</i>, <i>Dirty Little Secrets</i>, <i>The Love Edition</i>, <i>Love &amp; Hip Hop Awards: Most Certified</i> and <i>40 Greatest Love &amp; Hip Hop Moments: The Reboot</i>.
</p><p>In 2016, <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a> revealed that he and Ma would be releasing a joint album.<sup id="cite_ref-Fat_Joe_and_Remy_Ma_release_album_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fat_Joe_and_Remy_Ma_release_album-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On March 2, 2016, the joint album was officially announced as <i><a href="/wiki/Plata_O_Plomo_(Fat_Joe_and_Remy_Ma_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Plata O Plomo (Fat Joe and Remy Ma album)">Plata O Plomo</a></i>, which translates from <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> to "silver or lead". The phrase was used by Colombian drug lord <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Escobar" title="Pablo Escobar">Pablo Escobar</a>, who would offer government officials and law enforcement the choice of taking a bribe, or having a murder contract placed against them.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The album's first single, "<a href="/wiki/All_the_Way_Up_(Fat_Joe_and_Remy_Ma_song)" title="All the Way Up (Fat Joe and Remy Ma song)">All the Way Up</a>", was released the same day.<sup id="cite_ref-LoC_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LoC-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> "All the Way Up" was a critical and commercial success, receiving <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" class="mw-redirect" title="Music recording sales certification">platinum</a> certification from the RIAA, and two <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nominations.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <i>Plata O Plomo</i> debuted at number 44 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> with first-week sales of 11,158 <a href="/wiki/Album-equivalent_unit" title="Album-equivalent unit">album-equivalent units</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The album received generally favorable reviews from music critics,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> despite lukewarm commercial success.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)"><i>XXL</i></a> gave the album a 4 out of 5 stars, noting that the album is a "a triumphant return" for the duo.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 20, 2017, Ma opened a women's clothing store, <i>Conceited</i> in <a href="/wiki/Raleigh" class="mw-redirect" title="Raleigh">Raleigh</a>, North Carolina.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Reminisce_.282017.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Reminisce_(2017–present)"><i>Reminisce</i> (2017–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remy_Ma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reminisce (2017–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 16, 2017, Ma released "<a href="/wiki/Wake_Me_Up_(Remy_Ma_song)" title="Wake Me Up (Remy Ma song)">Wake Me Up</a>", the first single from her upcoming album, then titled <i>7 Winters, 6 Summers</i>. It featured fellow New York rapper <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a> and a sample from Kim's "Queen Bitch" from the 1996 album <i><a href="/wiki/Hard_Core_(Lil%27_Kim_album)" title="Hard Core (Lil&#39; Kim album)">Hard Core</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On January 19, 2018, Ma released the album's second single "<a href="/wiki/Melanin_Magic" title="Melanin Magic">Melanin Magic</a>", featuring vocals by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a> and using a sample from <a href="/wiki/Mint_Condition" title="Mint Condition">Mint Condition</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Breakin%27_My_Heart_(Pretty_Brown_Eyes)" title="Breakin&#39; My Heart (Pretty Brown Eyes)">Breakin' My Heart (Pretty Brown Eyes)</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On April 26, 2018, Ma released the song "Company" featuring fellow Bronx artist <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On June 12, 2018, Ma released  "New Thang", with rapper <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, from the soundtrack for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Uncle_Drew" title="Uncle Drew">Uncle Drew</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since September 10, 2018, Ma has appeared as a host on the talk show <i><a href="/wiki/State_of_the_Culture" title="State of the Culture">State of the Culture</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2020, Ma announced during an appearance on radio show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Breakfast_Club_(radio_show)" title="The Breakfast Club (radio show)">The Breakfast Club</a></i> that the title of the album had been changed to <i>Reminisce</i>, and that a release date was being decided.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> In 2021, Ma starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/6:45" title="6:45">6:45</a>,</i> as the character Cassi.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remy_Ma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smith married Shamele Mackie, better known as fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Papoose_(rapper)" title="Papoose (rapper)">Papoose</a>, in February 2016. The couple were initially scheduled to be wed while Smith was incarcerated in 2008, but the wedding was called off after police caught Mackie attempting to smuggle a <a href="/wiki/Skeleton_key" title="Skeleton key">skeleton key</a> into prison and banned him from visiting her for six months.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Remy Ma has one child from a previous relationship, as well as three stepchildren with Papoose.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> She gave birth to the couple's first child together, Reminisce Mackenzie, on December 14, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remy_Ma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On July 13, 2007, Smith turned herself in to New York City police in relation to a shooting early that morning outside a Manhattan <a href="/wiki/Delicatessen" title="Delicatessen">delicatessen</a>. Police say that while she was with a group of people outside the Pizza Bar, a Manhattan nightspot, a verbal confrontation broke out at 4 a.m. During the incident, a gunshot to the torso wounded Makeda Barnes-Joseph, who had been accused of stealing three thousand dollars from Smith.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Police sources indicate that Barnes-Joseph later identified Smith as the shooter.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Security tapes from inside the club showed no evidence of any altercations or arguments. Smith pleaded not guilty to charges of <a href="/wiki/Attempted_murder" title="Attempted murder">attempted murder</a>, assault, and criminal weapon possession.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Smith was later charged with <a href="/wiki/Witness_tampering" title="Witness tampering">witness tampering</a> and assault after an August 2007 incident in which she was accused of leading several male friends to attack a <a href="/wiki/Witness" title="Witness">witness</a>'s boyfriend.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 27, 2008, Smith was convicted of assault, illegal weapon possession and attempted coercion in connection with these charges. She was immediately taken into custody pending sentencing scheduled for May 13. She was acquitted of witness tampering and gang assault.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> At her sentencing, the judge noted her disregard for the victim following the shooting—commenting that the rapper rifled through the victim's purse looking for the alleged stolen money, as Barnes-Joseph sat seriously wounded in the car. Smith then exited the vehicle, jumped into a waiting car and drove off, leaving Barnes-Joseph bleeding in the front seat. No money was found in Barnes-Joseph's purse.
</p><p>The shooting caused Barnes-Joseph to undergo several surgeries. In 2007, Barnes-Joseph filed an $80 million civil lawsuit against Smith for damages, along with pain and suffering. According to her lawyers, Smith was serving an eight-year term at the <a href="/wiki/Bedford_Hills_Correctional_Facility_for_Women" title="Bedford Hills Correctional Facility for Women">Bedford Hills Correctional Facility for Women</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bedford_Hills,_New_York" title="Bedford Hills, New York">Bedford Hills, New York</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Westchester_County,_New_York" title="Westchester County, New York">Westchester County</a> and was expected to be released in 2015.
</p><p>Smith married her fiancé, rapper <a href="/wiki/Papoose_(rapper)" title="Papoose (rapper)">Papoose</a>, while incarcerated in May 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> During an interview with <a href="/wiki/Hot_97" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot 97">Hot 97</a> host <a href="/wiki/Funkmaster_Flex" title="Funkmaster Flex">Funkmaster Flex</a> in July 2012, Smith announced that in <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1154941027">.mw-parser-output .frac{white-space:nowrap}.mw-parser-output .frac .num,.mw-parser-output .frac .den{font-size:80%;line-height:0;vertical-align:super}.mw-parser-output .frac .den{vertical-align:sub}.mw-parser-output .sr-only{border:0;clip:rect(0,0,0,0);clip-path:polygon(0px 0px,0px 0px,0px 0px);height:1px;margin:-1px;overflow:hidden;padding:0;position:absolute;width:1px}</style><span class="frac" role="math">22<span class="sr-only">+</span><span class="num">1</span>&#8260;<span class="den">2</span></span> months she would be released.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2014, her husband Papoose confirmed that Smith should be released during July 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> On August 1, 2014, Smith was released from prison after serving six years.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> In October 2018, Smith launched a new clothing line, with proceeds going to the <i>Remy Ma Foundation</i> to help women and their families who have been negatively affected by incarceration.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After being investigated by the NYPD for allegedly assaulting Brittney Taylor, one of her <i>Love &amp; Hip Hop</i> co-stars, Smith turned herself in to the authorities on May 6, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> On December 2, 2019, all charges were dropped due to lack of evidence.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remy_Ma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foxy_Brown">Foxy Brown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remy_Ma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Foxy Brown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A beef between Remy Ma and <a href="/wiki/Foxy_Brown_(rapper)" title="Foxy Brown (rapper)">Foxy Brown</a> originated in August 2004, when an interviewer noted to Remy: "Right now, it's a lack of female rappers in the game. Foxy don't got anything going on," Remy Ma responded: "How do I feel about them? Stay wherever they at, don't drop a song, don't do nothing, let me be the only one. I'm having so much fun. I hope they never come out. Nah, I wish everyone the best of luck. Just don't drop when I drop. That's all I got to say. Matter of fact, just don't drop at all. It'll even be better."<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> In September 2004, Remy recalled that after a rap battle with <a href="/wiki/Lady_Luck_(rapper)" title="Lady Luck (rapper)">Lady Luck</a> at Fight Klub, her refusal to "show some type of seniority or respect" to Brown became one of the building blocks in the feud.<sup id="cite_ref-yt3_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt3-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-yt5_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt5-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Remy also recalled that during the incident the two were arguing over a future rap battle, where Brown declined and allegedly stated, "What? A battle? That's for beginners. We sell records in Brooklyn!"<sup id="cite_ref-yt3_55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt3-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-yt5_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt5-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-yt1_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt1-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rhh_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rhh-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Remy dissed the rapper, responding, "When the last time you sold a record? Get the fuck out of here. I'd rather be a beginner that's on fire than be a washed-up veteran. Are you fucking serious? Don't try to degrade what I do."<sup id="cite_ref-yt3_55-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt3-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-yt5_56-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt5-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Remy additionally revealed that a prize of over $200,000 was offered to whoever won the rap battle.<sup id="cite_ref-yt1_57-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt1-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rhh_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rhh-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Another building block to the feud was Remy's recollection of her and Brown coincidentally being interviewed at the same events either "before or after" each other, where she would catch wind of Brown "subliminally" dissing her by making statements like: "I've been doing this for years. These new chicks, they('re) not on (my level)."<sup id="cite_ref-yt3_55-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt3-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-yt5_56-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt5-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rhh_58-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rhh-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> In another event, specifically a <i><a href="/wiki/Baby_Phat" title="Baby Phat">Baby Phat</a></i> after-party, Foxy Brown intentionally tried to bump into Remy.<sup id="cite_ref-yt1_57-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt1-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rhh_58-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rhh-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> The occurrence resulted in strands of Brown's weave getting caught in Remy's bracelet, causing Remy to snatch her wrist back and yank out the strands.<sup id="cite_ref-yt1_57-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt1-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rhh_58-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rhh-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Remy recalled that Brown in response "kept it moving (despite a scuffle) she had with "regular" girls in a bathroom (over cutting in line)."<sup id="cite_ref-yt1_57-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt1-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rhh_58-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rhh-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late January 2005, Remy Ma reportedly punched Foxy Brown inside <a href="/wiki/Island_Def_Jam" class="mw-redirect" title="Island Def Jam">Island Def Jam</a>/<a href="/wiki/Universal_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Records">Universal</a> offices,<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> roughly a few days after Brown "subliminal" disses Remy during a venue at Jay-Z's <i>Best of Both Worlds Tour</i> and allegedly dissed her during a <a href="/wiki/WQHT" title="WQHT">Hot 97</a> interview segment with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Clue%3F" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Clue?">DJ Clue</a>, where Brown disses Remy on a mixtape track ("We Hustlaz") and allegedly ranted: "Who drives 645's? That's wack. We sell records [in New York]. We don't just get on the radio station and pop shit."<sup id="cite_ref-yt3_55-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt3-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-yt5_56-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt5-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> In February 2006, Remy Ma released a freestyle record ("Most Anticipated") that contained "slick comments" about Brown's hearing problem and stated that she didn't care if it was offensive because "it was just good punchlines".<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> In September 2006, Remy again joked about Brown's hearing, addressing to a radio show: "now Foxy suddenly got her hearing back, (I) wanted to be the first to inform her that she's wack and old."<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2007, during her interview with <i>Sub 0 Magazine</i>, Foxy Brown dissed Remy Ma, stating: "When you lookin' at Fox(y) and Kim and sayin', 'Damn y'all make me wanna rap.' Well shit you disappointing me 'cause you sound horrible. You look horrible, ya sound horrible. If it walk like somethin', talk like somethin', act like somethin', then it is somethin'."<sup id="cite_ref-yt2_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt2-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> Brown also dissed Remy's recollection of their disagreement over the rap battle, addressing: "Some of these female rappers have the wrong approach, they wanna be my friend. And instead of approaching me like, '"Hey, how you doing", another chick (Remy), 'Yo, what up with that battle?' And I'm lookin' at her like, 'You bum ass. Naw, I don't do that, I'm a business woman baby.'"<sup id="cite_ref-yt2_63-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt2-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> In December 2007, Remy spoke more candidly on her dispute with Brown.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx2_65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx2-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> She confirmed that although she disliked Brown as a person, she admitted she liked Brown's music and added that she was influenced by her and Lil' Kim.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx2_65-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx2-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> Remy later concluded her take on the feud by giving a shout-out to Brown's rival <a href="/wiki/Jacki-O" title="Jacki-O">Jacki-O</a> and stating, "If you look at every female that came out after (Foxy Brown) or when she came out with (Lil') Kim, to Eve, me, Jacki-O, it even got to Latifah, like how do you (get in a fight) with <a href="/wiki/Queen_Latifah" title="Queen Latifah">Queen Latifah</a>? Like, are you serious? This chick is a problem."<sup id="cite_ref-yt3_55-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt3-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-yt5_56-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt5-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
In 2017, Foxy released a new single called "Breaks Over", a diss track aimed at Remy.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nicki_Minaj">Nicki Minaj</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remy_Ma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Nicki Minaj">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Shether" title="Shether">Shether</a></div>
<p>Disagreements between <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a> and Ma originated in 2007, when Ma took note of Minaj's freestyle record, nicknamed "Dirty Money", from her mixtape <i>Playtime Is Over</i> (2007),<sup id="cite_ref-:02_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> which uses the beat to "Yeah Yeah Yeah" by Terror Squad, a song that Ma rapped on. In the record, Minaj recites: "Tell that bitch with the crown to <a href="/wiki/Run_It!" title="Run It!">run it</a> like <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>/she won three rounds, I'ma need a hundred thou/like '<a href="/wiki/Chinatown" title="Chinatown">Chinatown</a>' — bitches better bow down/oh you ain't know, betcha bitches know now/fuck I got a gun — let her know that I'm the one",<sup id="cite_ref-nydailynews22_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nydailynews22-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> which many believed to be a reference to the crown Ma wore in the music video for "Whuteva". Minaj reportedly never confirmed or denied that the song was about Ma,<sup id="cite_ref-:02_67-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> however, Ma remarked in a 2010 interview: "To this day I still feel like (the song) is a stab at me; I'm gonna (diss Minaj) back for that one".<sup id="cite_ref-yt12_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yt12-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> Although Ma and Minaj shared words of encouragement,<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> their relationship soon soured, and they began releasing verses and songs with lyrics aimed at each other, including Ma's "<a href="/wiki/Money_Showers" title="Money Showers">Money Showers</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a> and Minaj's "<a href="/wiki/Swalla" title="Swalla">Swalla</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Jason_Derulo" title="Jason Derulo">Jason Derulo</a> &amp; "<a href="/wiki/Make_Love_(Gucci_Mane_and_Nicki_Minaj_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Make Love (Gucci Mane and Nicki Minaj song)">Make Love</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 25, 2017, Ma released "<a href="/wiki/Shether" title="Shether">Shether</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> which contained a number of accusations and allegations regarding Minaj's personal and professional life.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> She followed this with "Another One".<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the March 3, 2017, episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wendy_Williams_Show" title="The Wendy Williams Show">The Wendy Williams Show</a></i>, Ma accused Minaj of "trying to keep (her) off of <a href="/wiki/Red_carpet" title="Red carpet">red carpets</a>" and "trying to make sure awards don't go to (her)".<sup id="cite_ref-bbremy_75-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbremy-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> A week later, in an interview hosted by <i><a href="/wiki/BuzzFeed" title="BuzzFeed">BuzzFeed</a></i>, Ma stated that she had second thoughts about releasing the diss track and commented on the difficulties of being a woman in the music industry.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup> Minaj subsequently responded with the song "<a href="/wiki/No_Frauds" title="No Frauds">No Frauds</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup> in which she accused Ma of spreading <a href="/wiki/Deception" title="Deception">falsehoods</a> and made fun of Ma's poor record sales with <i><a href="/wiki/Plata_O_Plomo" title="Plata O Plomo">Plata O Plomo</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 12, 2017, Ma took the stage at Hot 97's Summer Jam. During her performance, she brought out <a href="/wiki/Queen_Latifah" title="Queen Latifah">Queen Latifah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_M.A" title="Young M.A">Young M.A</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Lyte" title="MC Lyte">MC Lyte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lady_of_Rage" class="mw-redirect" title="Lady of Rage">Lady of Rage</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cardi_B" title="Cardi B">Cardi B</a> to perform "<a href="/wiki/U.N.I.T.Y." title="U.N.I.T.Y.">U.N.I.T.Y.</a>" After the group left the stage, Ma launched into a performance of "Shether", while photos of Minaj were displayed on the stage screen behind her. During the performance, she repeatedly directed <a href="/wiki/Fuck#F-bomb" title="Fuck">f-bombs</a> at Minaj.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
