<p><b>Michael Januario</b> (born January 13, 1983) better known by his stage name <b>M-Dot</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Italian_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Italian American">Italian-American</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a> and producer. Before becoming a rap musician, Januario played college basketball at <a href="/wiki/Bunker_Hill_Community_College" title="Bunker Hill Community College">BHCC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_College_of_Liberal_Arts" title="Massachusetts College of Liberal Arts">MCLA</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fitchburg_State" class="mw-redirect" title="Fitchburg State">Fitchburg State</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M-Dot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Revere,_Massachusetts" title="Revere, Massachusetts">Revere, Massachusetts</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> M-Dot initially gained expanded exposure when touring Switzerland as the opener <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> for Lawrence rapper Krumb Snatcha (<a href="/wiki/Gang_Starr_Foundation" title="Gang Starr Foundation">Gang Starr Foundation</a>) in the mid 2000s.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
Frequent collaborations with respected rap groups,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and consistently touring overseas, have built M-Dot's audience.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> M-Dot is known for a high level of productivity and his multi-syllable rhyme schemes, having attained a bachelor's degree in English.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  On December 19, 2014 he dropped the single "Shine" featuring Dominique Larue, Katy Gunn and Wu Tang MC <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> it later appeared on his highly touted debut album "egO anD The eneMy" (released on 1/27/17). On December 30, 2017 renowned Hip-Hop outlet UGHH announced ‘egO anD The eneMy’ as the top selling release for the entire calendar year (of 2017). The album which received vast critical acclaim, boasts a noteworthy production lineup with <a href="/wiki/Marley_Marl" title="Marley Marl">Marley Marl</a>, <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hi_Tek" class="mw-redirect" title="Hi Tek">Hi Tek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marco_Polo_(producer)" title="Marco Polo (producer)">Marco Polo (producer)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buckwild_(music_producer)" title="Buckwild (music producer)">Buckwild</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_7L" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ 7L">DJ 7L</a> among others. On December 22, 2015 M-Dot &amp; his visual team released a music video titled "123 Flow", the minute and a half long clip is one of the first fully interactive 360° music videos showcasing a 6-person replicant effect and upon release almost immediately went viral.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  On March 13, 2018 <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blacklist_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Blacklist (TV series)">The Blacklist</a></i> used M-Dot’s audio single “True Lies” during episode 16 of season 5 titled “Capricorn Killer”. In February 2020 M-Dot released “The Atonement” produced by the iconic <a href="/wiki/Pete_Rock" title="Pete Rock">Pete Rock</a>.  On January 28, 2022, M-Dot released the album “Dining in Dystopia” featuring <a href="/wiki/Elzhi" title="Elzhi">Elzhi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conway_The_Machine" class="mw-redirect" title="Conway The Machine">Conway The Machine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Esoteric_(rapper)" title="Esoteric (rapper)">Esoteric (rapper)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Shug" title="Big Shug">Big Shug</a> &amp; more. The album received high praise and glowing reviews from news outlets, blogs and radios including <a href="/wiki/Okay_player" class="mw-redirect" title="Okay player">okay player</a>, <a href="/wiki/DigBoston" title="DigBoston">DigBoston</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Shade_45" title="Shade 45">shade 45</a>. On June 17, 2022 <a href="/wiki/Logic_(rapper)" title="Logic (rapper)">logic (rapper)</a> released his album <a href="/wiki/Vinyl_Days" title="Vinyl Days">Vinyl Days</a> on <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam">Def Jam</a>, the lead single with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>, also titled Vinyl Days, featured vocal samples from M-Dot for the tracks chorus and outro. 
</p><p>In 2008 M-Dot was named to Stuff Magazines Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  Also in '08 M-Dot shared the honor of "2 Dope 2 Sleep On" with <a href="/wiki/Casey_Veggies" title="Casey Veggies">Casey Veggies</a> presented by 2dopeboyz.com.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He went on to receive positive reviews for his mixtapes Money Doesn't Own Thought (2009) from DigBoston.com <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and Making Doubters Over Think (2010) from the Boston Globe.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  Continuing his European exposure, M-Dot performed at the 2010 Winter XGames <sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Tignes,_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Tignes, France">Tignes, France</a>. For years he has solidified a fan base through local shows <sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and mixtapes in New England.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> 
M-Dot won both major Boston <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip-hop</a> music awards in 2010: at the 22nd annual <i><a href="/wiki/The_Phoenix_(newspaper)" title="The Phoenix (newspaper)">Boston Phoenix</a> awards</i>, Best Hip Hop Act,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and at the 23rd annual <a href="/wiki/Boston_Music_Awards" title="Boston Music Awards">Boston Music Awards</a>, Hip Hop Artist of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He then went on to win Hip Hop Dependency "Artist of the Year 2011" <sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>M-Dot garnered more accolades for his first EP, a collaborative effort with DJ Jean Maron, Run MPC.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
The Run MPC single "You Don't Know About It" Ft. <a href="/wiki/Masta_Ace" title="Masta Ace">Masta Ace</a> reached number 2 on RapAttackLives.com <sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> college radio charts and the 12 inch record finished fifth in vinyl sales in 2010 at undergroundhiphop.com.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His third installment in his acronym series, More Doubters Over Thinking, was also well received.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In May 2012 in support of the release "Layer Cake," <sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> M-Dot embarked <sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> on a 33-day European tour <sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> with the likes of <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse_(group)" title="Slaughterhouse (group)">Slaughterhouse (group)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saigon_(rapper)" title="Saigon (rapper)">Saigon (rapper)</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/El_Da_Sensei" title="El Da Sensei">El Da Sensei</a>. The Layer Cake cd was also considered another example of acute lyricism.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Layercake <sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> finds M-Dot weaving a go-for-the-gold battle-rap narrative over beats from young producers <sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 2007 Cinematic feature "Street Team Massacre <sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>" used songs M-Dot created for the score.
M-Dot can also be seen acting in the comical sitcom "What it is",<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> which stars and is also produced by Makzilla &amp; Krizz Kaliko.  On October 11, 2012, M-Dot was featured on ABC's (New England WCVB) primetime TV show "Chronicle".<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_Kemp" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip Hop Kemp">Hip Hop Kemp</a>, which is statistically one of the largest music festivals in Europe, named M-Dot, amongst others, to be a headlining performer at the events 18th edition (taking the stage August 24, 2018 in Hradec Králové, <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>).
</p><p>As of June 2022, M-DOT is touring Switzerland.
