<p><b>Mahmood Abdul-Ghafur</b>, also known as <b>Oryn the Rebel</b><sup id="cite_ref-The_Daily_Item_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Daily_Item-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> from <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> and a frequent collaborator with many internationally established and up-and-coming acts. Oryn the Rebel is also known as a founding member of multi-genre <a href="/wiki/Fusion_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fusion (music)">fusion</a> group Project Hybrid.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Noise:_Rock_Around_Boston_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Noise:_Rock_Around_Boston-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He has been notably featured on the "Underground Heat" <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a> hosted by <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a> <a href="/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJ</a> <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_Caz" title="Grandmaster Caz">Grandmaster Caz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HipHopPress.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HipHopPress.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Oryn the Rebel originally went by the name Oryn, or O-ry-n, until Leo the Godless, a Malden-Revere-based rapper gave him the title "the Rebel".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oryn_the_Rebel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Oryn the Rebel comes from a <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> upbringing and a family of activists and philanthropists. His sister <a href="/wiki/Saleemah_Abdul-Ghafur" title="Saleemah Abdul-Ghafur">Saleemah Abdul-Ghafur</a>, author of <i>Living Islam Out Loud: American Muslim Women Speak</i>, was selected to participate in the Muslim Leaders of Tomorrow retreat hosted by the <a href="/wiki/World_Economic_Forum" title="World Economic Forum">World Economic Forum</a> in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen, Denmark</a> in 2006. She remains an outspoken activist on the front to combat deaths caused by <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">malaria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTimes.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Front_Company">United Front Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oryn_the_Rebel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: United Front Company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The United Front Company is a collection of creatives, musical and non-musical alike, and independently owned companies coming together to form a larger platform - The Artist Jumpoff. Together members of the UFC help one another to promote and earn revenue through their art by pooling their talent, skills, fans and knowledge. The common philosophy at the UFC is no contracts as the focus remains on the artistic aspect rather than the business one.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Underground_Heat">Underground Heat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oryn_the_Rebel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Underground Heat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_Caz#Compilations" title="Grandmaster Caz">Underground Heat Vol 1</a></i>, a mixtape series bringing together underground rappers from across the country, included two of Oryn's songs alongside works by <a href="/wiki/Freeway_(rapper)" title="Freeway (rapper)">Freeway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shock_G" title="Shock G">Shock G</a>. This collaboration was mixed and hosted by Grandmaster Caz.<sup id="cite_ref-datpiff.com_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datpiff.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
