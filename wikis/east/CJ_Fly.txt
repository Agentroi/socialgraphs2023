<p><b>Chaine St. Aubin Downer Jr.</b> (born July 6, 1993), better known by his stage name <b>CJ Fly</b>, is an American rapper and producer from <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is a founding member and the oldest member of the hip-hop collective group <a href="/wiki/Pro_Era" title="Pro Era">Pro Era</a>. Throughout 2012 and 2013, he was featured on many of the collective's projects and towards the end of 2013, he released his critically acclaimed mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Thee_Way_Eye_See_It" title="Thee Way Eye See It">Thee Way Eye See It</a></i>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_Fly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Downer Jr. was born on July 6, 1993, in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. He is the only child of a <a href="/wiki/Barbadian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Barbadian people">Bajan</a> mother and <a href="/wiki/Jamaicans" title="Jamaicans">Jamaican</a> father. He began writing poetry by the fourth grade and by the seventh grade he began rapping. He attended Edward R. Murrow High School for communication arts where he met <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Bada$$</a>, Powers Pleasant and <a href="/wiki/Capital_Steez" title="Capital Steez">Capital STEEZ</a>. The four of them would spend their school days <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_battle" class="mw-redirect" title="Freestyle battle">cyphering</a> in the auditorium.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, the four of them formed <a href="/wiki/Pro_Era" title="Pro Era">Pro Era</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LA_Weekly_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA_Weekly-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Fader_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Fader-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_Fly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012, Pro Era released their debut mixtape <i>The Secc$ Tape</i>. CJ Fly later made a breakthrough feature on Joey Bada$$'s debut mixtape <a href="/wiki/1999_(Joey_Bada$$_mixtape)" class="mw-redirect" title="1999 (Joey Bada$$ mixtape)"><i>1999</i></a>, on tracks "Hardknock" and "Don't Front". On December 21, 2012, he and Pro Era released their second mixtape <i>PEEP: The APROcalypse</i>. With production from Pro Era members Chuck Strangers, Kirk Knight, <a href="/wiki/Statik_Selektah" title="Statik Selektah">Statik Selektah</a> and others from <a href="/wiki/Brandun_DeShay" title="Brandun DeShay">Brandun DeShay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thelonious_Martin" title="Thelonious Martin">Thelonious Martin</a> and more.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, CJ went on the Beast Coastal Tour with fellow Pro Era members, The <a href="/wiki/Flatbush_Zombies" title="Flatbush Zombies">Flatbush Zombies</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Underachievers" title="The Underachievers">The Underachievers</a>. He, along with the rest of Pro Era, joined <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>'s Under The Influence Tour in 2013 with <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">A$AP Rocky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_James" title="Trinidad James">Trinidad James</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chevy_Woods" title="Chevy Woods">Chevy Woods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smoke_DZA" title="Smoke DZA">Smoke DZA</a>, and more. He later made an appearance on Joey's second mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Summer_Knights" title="Summer Knights">Summer Knights</a></i> along with the rest of Pro Era on the song "Sorry Bonita" produced by <a href="/wiki/Oddisee" title="Oddisee">Oddisee</a>. On October 5, 2013, he released his debut mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Thee_Way_Eye_See_It" title="Thee Way Eye See It">Thee Way Eye See It</a></i>. With features from various Pro Era members, <a href="/wiki/Buckshot_(rapper)" title="Buckshot (rapper)">Buckshot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul" title="Ab-Soul">Ab-Soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phife_Dawg" title="Phife Dawg">Phife Dawg</a> and production from Chuck Strangers, <a href="/wiki/Statik_Selektah" title="Statik Selektah">Statik Selektah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brandun_DeShay" title="Brandun DeShay">Brandun DeShay</a>, and more, the mixtape has received critical acclaim and made him a candidate for XXL's 2014 and 2015 Freshman List.
<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 9, 2016, a date which happened to coincide with his mother's 49th birthday, CJ Fly released his debut studio album entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Flytrap_(CJ_Fly_album)" title="Flytrap (CJ Fly album)">Flytrap</a></i>. He released the album exclusively with <a href="/wiki/Tidal_(service)" title="Tidal (service)">TIDAL</a> on December 8, and made the album available on all streaming services on December 9.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since the release of <i>FLYTRAP</i>, CJ has released videos for single "Now You Know", a track that was premiered with <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> in September 2016. He's also released the visual for "DOPE" which premiered on <a href="/wiki/Pornhub" title="Pornhub">Pornhub</a> on 4/20.
His single, "Now You Know" was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop">Love &amp; Hip Hop</a></i> Season 6 - Episode 7 (2017) <sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 6, 2020, CJ Fly released his sophomore studio album entitled <i>RUDEBWOY</i>. Entirely produced by <a href="/wiki/Statik_Selektah" title="Statik Selektah">Statik Selektah</a>, the album featured guest appearances from majority of the <a href="/wiki/Pro_Era" title="Pro Era">Pro Era</a> collective, as well as Haile Supreme, Oshun, <a href="/wiki/Conway_the_Machine" title="Conway the Machine">Conway the Machine</a> and former Pro Era member T'nah.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 18, 2021, CJ Fly released <i>The Way I H(ear) It, Vol. 1</i>; the first volume of his instrumental album series, inspired by the time he spent learning how to produce his own beats during the start of the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_lockdown" class="mw-redirect" title="COVID-19 lockdown">COVID-19 lockdown</a> in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 24, 2021, a release date chosen in tribute of <a href="/wiki/Kobe_Bryant" title="Kobe Bryant">Kobe Bryant</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> CJ Fly released <i>NOT WHAT YOU'RE EXPECTING</i>. CJ's third studio album, the project featured no appearances from Pro Era, however brought in Devin Tracy, Amon Tyson, and <a href="/wiki/Marlon_Craft" title="Marlon Craft">Marlon Craft</a>. The album also featured production from several producers, including CJ himself.
</p>
