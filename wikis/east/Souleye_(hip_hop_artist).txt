<p><b>Mario John Treadway</b> (born May 3, 1980), better known by his stage name, <b>Souleye</b>, is an acclaimed American hip hop artist. He has released 12 albums, including his latest, <i>Disguised As Tomorrow</i>, which was released in March 2023.  Among others, he has performed and recorded with BLVD, and his wife, <a href="/wiki/Alanis_Morissette" title="Alanis Morissette">Alanis Morissette</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Us/Who_Is_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Us/Who_Is-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CBS_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has also collaborated in the studio with artists such as South African rock musician Esjay Jones, Lynx, Lila Rose, fellow rappers like Big Samir, Main Flow and Chachillie and more.
</p><p>Souleye is a strong advocate for using his music to raise consciousness by writing about themes such as self-discovery, mental health, inner child work, therapy and spirituality. He is also known as a master of lyrical wordplay, polyrhythmic rapping, beatboxing, and rapid-fire freestyling.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Souleye_(hip_hop_artist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Souleye was born in <a href="/wiki/Sturbridge,_Massachusetts" title="Sturbridge, Massachusetts">Sturbridge, Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Medicine_Man_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Medicine_Man-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He began rapping in high school, from which he  graduated in 1999. Souleye went on to attend <a href="/wiki/Western_New_England_University" title="Western New England University">Western New England College</a> on a basketball scholarship for a year before leaving school. He then traveled the country, living in West Palm Beach, Florida all the way to Mount Shasta, California, Colorado, Los Angeles, and then up to the Bay Area, following his calling.<sup id="cite_ref-Medicine_Man_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Medicine_Man-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He had an older brother who died by suicide.<sup id="cite_ref-Medicine_Man_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Medicine_Man-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Souleye_(hip_hop_artist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Souleye_(hip_hop_artist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001 he began writing, performing and traveling with a loosely knit collective of <a href="/wiki/Conscious_hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Conscious hip hop">conscious hip hop</a> artists called the Transcendental Alliance; in 2003, he released an album titled <i>Soul Sessions</i> with underground hip hop artist Campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-Medicine_Man_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Medicine_Man-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The same year, Souleye's best friend was diagnosed with cancer. After his death, Souleye retreated to Mount Greylock in The Berkshires of Massachusetts, where he wrote the album <i>Flexible Morality</i>. In 2004, his brother died by suicide, and while cleaning out his apartment Souleye discovered that his brother had affixed a sticker which read "Music Matters" on the ceiling where he hanged himself.  Given the same sticker by his brother years previously, Souleye embraced "Music Matters" as his mission statement and released <i>UniverSoul Alchemy</i> that same year.<sup id="cite_ref-Medicine_Man_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Medicine_Man-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, he teamed up with producer Samuel "Sleepyhead" Pohner in Asheville, North Carolina, and as Souleye &amp; Sleepyhead, they released <i>Intergalactic Vibes.</i><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  The album won a new music contest sponsored by <i><a href="/wiki/Relix" title="Relix">Relix</a></i>, and as the contest winners, Souleye and Sleepyhead performed on the weeklong Jamcruise 4.  While there, Souleye freestyled with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Franti" title="Michael Franti">Michael Franti</a>, and was invited to play with Bassnectar.  Following the Jamcruise, he performed extensively with Bassnectar.  Souleye wrote and recorded  the song "Amorphous  Form" on Bassnectar's album <i><a href="/wiki/Underground_Communication" class="mw-redirect" title="Underground Communication">Underground Communication</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  In 2007, Souleye joined the San Francisco-based electronic band BLVD, with whom he recorded the album <i>Music for People</i>.  He toured with the band until 2009, and subsequently began performing once again as Souleye.<sup id="cite_ref-SactoBee_BLVD_etc_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SactoBee_BLVD_etc-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He met his wife Alanis Morissette at a meditation gathering in a Los Angeles residence in 2009, and they were married in 2010. Souleye opened Morissette's 2012 "<a href="/wiki/Guardian_Angel_Tour" title="Guardian Angel Tour">Guardian Angel</a>" tour and has since collaborated with her frequently.  He released the album <i>Iron Horse Running</i> in 2013 and <i>Identified Time</i> in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Shapeshifting_and_WILDMAN_.282015.E2.80.932017.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Shapeshifting_and_WILDMAN_(2015–2017)"><i>Shapeshifting</i> and <i>WILDMAN</i> (2015–2017)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Souleye_(hip_hop_artist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Shapeshifting and WILDMAN (2015–2017)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2015, Souleye released his album <i>Shapeshifting</i>. The full-length album featured focus tracks, "The Victim" and  "Labeled". The music video for "The Victim" premiered on Yahoo Music and "Labeled" made it to the Top 20 Official European Independent Music Charts. The album featured artwork by acclaimed visual artist Rob Prior.
</p><p>On November 11, 2016, Souleye released the debut single, "Follow Your Heart", to his forthcoming album <i>WILDMAN</i>. Following up to his first single, Souleye released "Snow Angel" featuring Alanis Morissette in January 2017. The music video premiered on <a href="/wiki/Fuse_(TV_channel)" title="Fuse (TV channel)">Fuse TV</a> and has been reviewed and featured by <a href="/wiki/The_Huffington_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="The Huffington Post">The Huffington Post</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The album was produced by Crush Effect and was released in September 2017. It featured a number of other hit singles, including the title track featuring Lynx.
</p>
<h3><span id="Soul_School_.282019.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Soul_School_(2019)"><i>Soul School</i> (2019)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Souleye_(hip_hop_artist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Soul School (2019)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2019, Souleye released <i>Soul School</i>, featuring his first collaboration with South African vocalist Esjay Jones, as well as other collaborations with 2Mex, Sabi, Donny Arcade, and Chachillie, on tracks like "Sacred Design", "Felicity" and "Come Down".
</p>
<h3><span id="Hunting_Teardrops_and_Disguised_as_Tomorrow_.282021.E2.80.93Present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Hunting_Teardrops_and_Disguised_as_Tomorrow_(2021–Present)"><i>Hunting Teardrops</i> and <i>Disguised as Tomorrow</i> (2021–Present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Souleye_(hip_hop_artist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Hunting Teardrops and Disguised as Tomorrow (2021–Present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He followed up that album with 2021's <i>hunting teardrops</i>, once again working with painter Rob Prior on the cover. The album spun off a number of hit singles and music videos for songs like "Ghost Steps", "Incredible Hulk", "Fireside", "Doorway to the Future" and "Time's Game".  
</p><p>The following year, Souleye released the single 'Emergency", featuring Morissette on the song's chorus. Shortly after, he enlisted veteran music producer ill.GATES to make a remix of the song. It became his most streamed song to date.
</p><p>On March 3, 2023, Souleye released his 12th full-length studio album, <i>Disguised As Tomorrow</i>, which features some of his most dynamic and ambitious tracks yet, including collaborations with Esjay Jones, fellow rappers Big Samir and Main Flow, and singer/songwriter and activist Lila Rose. The album has spurred a half dozen singles, including "Human Spirit", "Letter To The Old Me", "The Unheard", "Protected", "Weight of Your Soul", and "Exit the Maze", as well as "Emergency".
</p><p><i>Disguised As Tomorrow</i> was inspired by the COVID-19 pandemic and explores the idea that the 'tomorrow' we may anticipate for ourselves may not always be the one that comes to pass. The sound is a departure from the lighter tone of hunting teardrops, with much darker and heavier sounds for a more sonically complex musical journey. It has received tremendous praise from critics, some calling it 'album of the year' and "a whole-hearted masterpiece".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Souleye_(hip_hop_artist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Souleye and singer-actress <a href="/wiki/Alanis_Morissette" title="Alanis Morissette">Alanis Morissette</a> married on May 22, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Their son Ever was born on December 25, 2010,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> their daughter Onyx on June 23, 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and their son Winter on August 8, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
