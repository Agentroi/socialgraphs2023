<p><b>Bigram John Zayas</b> (born July 11, 1978), professionally known as <b>DVLP</b> or <b>Develop</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>, songwriter and <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a> (DJ). Hailing from <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, DVLP initially embarked on his career in music   as one-half of the production team Doe Boys, alongside his cousin Matthew "Filthy" Delgiorno. Working primarily in the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a>, and Latin genres, he has produced over 25 songs for American rapper <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, including the songs "<a href="/wiki/Fireman_(Lil_Wayne_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fireman (Lil Wayne song)">Fireman</a>" (2005) and "<a href="/wiki/Blunt_Blowin" title="Blunt Blowin">Blunt Blowin</a>" (2011). In 2013, he gained major recognition when he produced the <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> single "<a href="/wiki/Rap_God" title="Rap God">Rap God</a>", which went on to reach number seven on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">’’Billboard’’ Hot 100</a> chart.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DVLP&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Zayas was born in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bdetrick_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bdetrick-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He never had any formal musical training, although his family is involved in music.<sup id="cite_ref-bhacker_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bhacker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He first started DJing at the age of 11, using house and freestyle records. He went on to make mixtapes and perform at high school parties.<sup id="cite_ref-bhacker_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bhacker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DVLP&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Turntablism">Turntablism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DVLP&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Turntablism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While traveling around the world as a founding member of the turntablist collective The Allies (which also included <a href="/wiki/DJ_Craze" title="DJ Craze">Craze</a>, J-Smoke, Spictakular, <a href="/wiki/Infamous_(producer)" title="Infamous (producer)">Infamous</a>, <a href="/wiki/A-Trak" title="A-Trak">A-Trak</a> and Klever), he went by the moniker Develop. His emphasis was on beat juggling.<sup id="cite_ref-bdetrick_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bdetrick-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bhacker_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bhacker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 1998, he won the <a href="/wiki/International_Turntablist_Federation" title="International Turntablist Federation">ITF</a> Beat Juggling Championship, defeating Total Eclipse of <a href="/wiki/The_X-Ecutioners" title="The X-Ecutioners">The X-Ecutioners</a> in the finals.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 1999, he won the ITF Team Championship, and he was runner-up at the New York <a href="/wiki/DMC_World_DJ_Championships" title="DMC World DJ Championships">DMC</a> Regional in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-bhacker_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bhacker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2000, The Allies released an EP, <i>D-Day</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-bhacker_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bhacker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginnings_in_Production">Beginnings in Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DVLP&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Beginnings in Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2000, DVLP decided to end his career as a competitive DJ, in order to focus on producing and songwriting.<sup id="cite_ref-parnold_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parnold-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Shortly afterwards, he began collaborating with Filthy (his cousin, Matthew Delgiorno). The duo were known as the Doe Boys.<sup id="cite_ref-parnold_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parnold-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After taking a few years off to hone his production skills,<sup id="cite_ref-bhacker_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bhacker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> his first major production work was on the 2004 <a href="/wiki/Grafh" title="Grafh">Grafh</a> song "Damage is Done." He went on to produce four tracks on <a href="/wiki/Juelz_Santana" title="Juelz Santana">Juelz Santana</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/What_the_Game%27s_Been_Missing!" title="What the Game&#39;s Been Missing!">What the Game's Been Missing!</a></i> (2005), also assisting with recording and engineering.<sup id="cite_ref-bdetrick_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bdetrick-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>DVLP has gone on to write and produce for Shells, <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">The Diplomats</a> (aka Dipset), <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Rowland" title="Kelly Rowland">Kelly Rowland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Clue%3F" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Clue?">DJ Clue?</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pitbull_(rapper)" title="Pitbull (rapper)">Pitbull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Legend" title="John Legend">John Legend</a> and others.<sup id="cite_ref-bdetrick_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bdetrick-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bhacker_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bhacker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-parnold_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parnold-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_with_Lil_Wayne">Work with Lil Wayne</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DVLP&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Work with Lil Wayne">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>DVLP started working with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> in 2005, after <a href="/wiki/Juelz_Santana" title="Juelz Santana">Juelz Santana</a> introduced them at his studio.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The Doe Boys produced three tracks on <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Carter_II" title="Tha Carter II">Tha Carter II</a></i>, including the album's lead single, "<a href="/wiki/Fireman_(Lil_Wayne_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fireman (Lil Wayne song)">Fireman</a>,"<sup id="cite_ref-parnold_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parnold-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> which reached #10 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Rap_chart" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard Rap chart">Billboard Rap chart</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and #32 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> "Fireman" was Lil Wayne's first single following his split from former producer <a href="/wiki/Mannie_Fresh" title="Mannie Fresh">Mannie Fresh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rmarkman_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rmarkman-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> After <i>Tha Carter II</i>, DVLP continued working with Lil Wayne;<sup id="cite_ref-rmarkman_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rmarkman-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> he has produced over 25 songs for him through 2013.
</p><p>In 2011, he dropped the vowels from his moniker and became DVLP.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> That year, for <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Carter_IV" title="Tha Carter IV">Tha Carter IV</a></i>, he produced "<a href="/wiki/Blunt_Blowin" title="Blunt Blowin">Blunt Blowin</a>," which reached #33 on the Billboard Hot 100 charts.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Lil Wayne recorded the track on his first day back in the studio after being released from prison.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Other notable tracks DVLP has produced for Lil Wayne include "What's Wrong With Them" featuring <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>, from 2010's <i><a href="/wiki/I_Am_Not_a_Human_Being" title="I Am Not a Human Being">I Am Not a Human Being</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and "Beat The Shit" featuring <a href="/wiki/Gunplay_(rapper)" title="Gunplay (rapper)">Gunplay</a>, from the 2013 album <i><a href="/wiki/I_Am_Not_a_Human_Being_II" title="I Am Not a Human Being II">I Am Not a Human Being II</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_with_Eminem">Work with Eminem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DVLP&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Work with Eminem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>DVLP produced <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Rap_God" title="Rap God">Rap God</a>", released October 15, 2013, as the third single off <i><a href="/wiki/The_Marshall_Mathers_LP_2" title="The Marshall Mathers LP 2">The Marshall Mathers LP 2</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> DVLP first created the track in 2011, without any particular artist in mind. His manager, Stephen Hacker of Hebrew Hustle, sent the track to Eminem in 2012 after Eminem's management team reached out to Hacker for tracks for his upcoming album. DVLP has since said that he can't imagine any other rapper on the track.<sup id="cite_ref-emmanuelcm_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-emmanuelcm-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The song, about Eminem's history and the rappers who have inspired him,<sup id="cite_ref-kgrow_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kgrow-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> was praised as being in line with the quality of Eminem's best offerings.<sup id="cite_ref-kgrow_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kgrow-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It reached #1 on the Billboard Rap chart<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> and #7 on the Hot 100 charts.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> At the inaugural <a href="/wiki/YouTube_Music_Awards" title="YouTube Music Awards">YouTube Music Awards</a> in 2013, Eminem shot a live video for "Rap God."<sup id="cite_ref-emmanuelcm_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-emmanuelcm-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> An official music video for the song, shot in Detroit, was released in November 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pop_Work">Pop Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DVLP&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Pop Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>DVLP has collaborated with producer <a href="/wiki/RedOne" title="RedOne">RedOne</a> on records by a variety of pop artists. They co-produced <a href="/wiki/Paulina_Rubio" title="Paulina Rubio">Paulina Rubio</a>'s 2012 single "<a href="/wiki/Me_Gustas_Tanto" title="Me Gustas Tanto">Me Gustas Tanto</a>,"<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> which reached #1 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Latin_Songs" title="Hot Latin Songs">Hot Latin Songs</a> chart<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and #2 on the Billboard Latin Pop Songs chart;<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Pitbull_(rapper)" title="Pitbull (rapper)">Pitbull</a>'s 2012 single "<a href="/wiki/Get_It_Started" title="Get It Started">Get It Started</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> which reached #28 on the Billboard Latin Pop Songs chart,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> #89 on the Billboard Hot 100,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> and was <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" class="mw-redirect" title="Music recording sales certification">certified gold</a> in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, as a member of the DJ duo The WAV.s (with <a href="/wiki/Bilal_Hajji" title="Bilal Hajji">Bilal the Chef</a>), DVLP worked on <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Iglesias" title="Enrique Iglesias">Enrique Iglesias</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/I_Like_How_It_Feels" title="I Like How It Feels">I Like How It Feels</a>" featuring Pitbull,<sup id="cite_ref-map_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-map-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> which reached #1 on the Billboard <a href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Songs" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Dance Club Songs">Dance/Club Play Songs</a> chart,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> #74 on the Billboard Hot 100,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> and was certified platinum in Canada<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> and gold in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> DVLP and Bilal the Chef were featured in the song's video, directed by Iglesias.<sup id="cite_ref-map_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-map-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, DVLP formed the band Mad Ave Boys, now known as Dameht, producing their debut EP <i>Electric Tape</i>. He also played bass and synth on the release.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reggaeton">Reggaeton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DVLP&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Reggaeton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Around 2015, DVLP began producing Latin music for artists including <a href="/wiki/Fuego_(singer)" title="Fuego (singer)">Fuego</a>, <a href="/wiki/J_Balvin" title="J Balvin">J Balvin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Quiles" title="Justin Quiles">Justin Quiles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Hunter_(singer)" title="Matt Hunter (singer)">Matt Hunter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jencarlos_Canela" title="Jencarlos Canela">Jencarlos Canela</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> He produced two songs on J Balvin's album <i><a href="/wiki/Energia_(album)" title="Energia (album)">Energia</a></i>, which reached number 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Top_Latin_Albums" title="Billboard Top Latin Albums"><i>Billboard</i> Top Latin Albums</a> chart<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> and won Best Urban Music Album at the <a href="/wiki/17th_Annual_Latin_Grammy_Awards" title="17th Annual Latin Grammy Awards">2016 Latin Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> DVLP also produced "Ay Mi Dios" by <a href="/wiki/IAmChino" title="IAmChino">IAmChino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pitbull_(rapper)" title="Pitbull (rapper)">Pitbull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yandel" title="Yandel">Yandel</a> and Chacal, which reached number 1 on the <i>Billboard</i> Latin Airplay and Tropical Airplay charts.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transcend.ent">Transcend.ent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DVLP&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Transcend.ent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2017, DVLP launched the record label and management company Transcend.ent, which is partnered with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group</a>. Their first signing was <a href="/wiki/Matt_Hunter_(singer)" title="Matt Hunter (singer)">Matt Hunter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Looplabs">Looplabs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DVLP&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Looplabs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>DVLP is a partner in Looplabs, a cloud-based music studio collaborative allowing musicians to create and share tracks and compositions. In 2016, Looplabs partnered with <a href="/wiki/Beatport" title="Beatport">Beatport</a> to provide loops, sounds and samples to Looplabs users with Beatport accounts, and to allow these users to publish tracks to Beatport's streaming platform.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
