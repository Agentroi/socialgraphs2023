<p><b>Kashaun Rameek Rutling</b> (born May 16, 1989), known professionally as <b>Phresher</b> (stylized as <b>PHresher</b>), is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York. Phresher first gained recognition in 2016 for his single "Wait a Minute" featuring <a href="/wiki/Remy_Ma" title="Remy Ma">Remy Ma</a>, and found continued success with his single "Right Now" featuring <a href="/wiki/Cardi_B" title="Cardi B">Cardi B</a> in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-all_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-all-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In late 2017, he was featured on <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Chloraseptic_(song)" title="Chloraseptic (song)">Chloraseptic</a>" from his ninth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Revival_(Eminem_album)" title="Revival (Eminem album)">Revival</a></i>, which debuted at number one on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. In 2018, he was featured on an official remix of "Chloraseptic" alongside Eminem and <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phresher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rutling was born in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York, and is of <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">Trinidadian</a> descent.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Growing up in the Bamaz Projects, a heavily crime-ridden area in <a href="/wiki/East_New_York,_Brooklyn" title="East New York, Brooklyn">East New York, Brooklyn</a>, he was an only child raised by both his parents.<sup id="cite_ref-aahh_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aahh-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was an athlete in high school as a teenager, and attended <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson_High_School_(Brooklyn)" title="Thomas Jefferson High School (Brooklyn)">Thomas Jefferson High School</a>, graduating in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-alum_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was a member of the football team<sup id="cite_ref-alum_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and played safety and wide receiver in <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_II" title="NCAA Division II">Division II</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hhmw_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hhmw-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His first son was born when he was sixteen years old, forcing him to quit sports to focus on raising his son.<sup id="cite_ref-monster_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monster-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It was not until his father sent him a hip hop beat that he began to explore his rapping skills. He decided to pursue a life in music when he was 21. At the beginning of his career, he wrote over 100 songs which were never released, but drastically helped him improve his writing and delivery.<sup id="cite_ref-all_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-all-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phresher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phresher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rutling was a <a href="/wiki/Paraprofessional_educator" title="Paraprofessional educator">paraprofessional</a> in New York City public schools for 10 years before he decided to pursue a musical career.<sup id="cite_ref-alum_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During his time as a paraeducator, Rutling worked with <a href="/wiki/Special_education" title="Special education">special education</a> students and often worked in <a href="/wiki/Youth_detention_center" title="Youth detention center">youth detention centers</a>. At the time, he made music on the side as a novice; however, it wasn't until he realized his potential talent that he decided to focus on music full-time.<sup id="cite_ref-alum_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In addition to making music, Rutling also owns a clothing line named "SO BarKlay Apparel."<sup id="cite_ref-aahh_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aahh-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phresher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Phresher released his debut song, "Polo Song", in 2010. Around 2012, Phresher started to release singles, EPs, and music videos onto the internet, which saw minor success. He released his debut mixtape, <i>Take It Personal</i>, in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, his single "Wait a Minute" was pitched to the YouTube global music team by The Young 'N Grateful Group, becoming an instant success. Following "Wait a Minute" receiving several million views, the video caught the attention of rapper <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, who later appeared on a remix of the song along with rapper <a href="/wiki/Remy_Ma" title="Remy Ma">Remy Ma</a>. The song appeared as a bonus cut on the <a href="/wiki/DJ_Whoo_Kid" title="DJ Whoo Kid">DJ Whoo Kid</a> mixtape <i>Lost Flash Drive</i> and released on G-Unit Records. Rapper <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a> also put forth a freestyle remix over "Wait a Minute." Following Royce promoting the remix via social media, Eminem made a notable mention of the freestyle. <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s heavy revere as to Royce's word play ultimately led the Detroit rapper to become interested in Phresher.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag.com_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Phresher's management duo Jay Andino and Michael Caseau had become sought after by Shady Records staff; initially, to aid in production for the upcoming <i><a href="/wiki/Revival_(Eminem_album)" title="Revival (Eminem album)">Revival</a></i> album. After several hundred instrumentals were sent by the Young 'N Grateful collective to no avail, Caseau suggested to Shady Records A&amp;R's, stating:
</p>
<dl><dd><i>"Phresher collaborating with Em' could give that same platform and vibe Royce gave on the remix. Instead of using a similar beat, we just need any beat with him on it to recreate the energy, not the sound design.”<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag.com_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></i></dd></dl>
<p>Thereafter, Caseau worked closely with <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a>' A&amp;Rs. In 2017, Eminem's long-time manager <a href="/wiki/Paul_Rosenberg_(music_manager)" title="Paul Rosenberg (music manager)">Paul Rosenberg</a> contacted Andino, stating Eminem had interest in collaborating with Phresher.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Eminem's mix engineer flew to New York to meet with Phresher to record "Chloraseptic," listed as track No. 3 on <i>Revival</i>. Phresher spoke about the collaboration, stating:
</p>
<dl><dd><i>"When I first heard that he wanted me on the project, I was astonished. I didn't believe anything until they actually flew in and recorded the record. That's when I was like, 'Oh! This is real.' It was about two months ago, three the most. That was just amazing, man. We met in the studio. That was just crazy."</i><sup id="cite_ref-complex_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>
<p>On April 5, 2018, Phresher released the song "Teamwork" as the lead single from his debut studio album, <i>PH</i> (2018).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The song's music video was released four days later on April 9.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The second single from the album, "You Do", was released on May 11, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The music video for "You Do" was released on May 23, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On June 3, 2018, Phresher was brought out to perform "<a href="/wiki/Chloraseptic_(song)" title="Chloraseptic (song)">Chloraseptic (Remix)</a>" alongside Eminem on the third day of the <a href="/wiki/Governors_Ball_Music_Festival" title="Governors Ball Music Festival">Governors Ball Music Festival</a> in <a href="/wiki/Randall%27s_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Randall&#39;s Island">Randall's Island</a>, New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After multiple delays, <i>PH</i> was released on June 15, 2018. The album contains sixteen tracks and features guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Badass</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cardi_B" title="Cardi B">Cardi B</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From December 2019 to March 2020, Phresher starred as a cast member on VH1's reality series <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_New_York" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York">Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 10, 2020, Phresher released his second extended play, <i>Brick by Brick</i>. It features guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Stunna_4_Vegas" title="Stunna 4 Vegas">Stunna 4 Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fivio_Foreign" title="Fivio Foreign">Fivio Foreign</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Casanova_(rapper)" title="Casanova (rapper)">Casanova</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phresher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In an interview, Phresher stated that his influences include <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a><sup id="cite_ref-xxl_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phresher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Phresher is known for his versatile sound; when asked by <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> about his music style, he noted that "it's all over the place. People say I sound like I'm from the South, some say I sound like I'm from California."<sup id="cite_ref-xxl_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phresher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Phresher has two children, a son and a daughter with Jennifer Coreano. In his spare time, he coaches basketball and football.<sup id="cite_ref-hhmw_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hhmw-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2020_assault_and_robbery">2020 assault and robbery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phresher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2020 assault and robbery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 20, 2020, Phresher was the target of a robbery and attempted kidnapping. According to a post he made on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>, he was robbed at gunpoint, <a href="/wiki/Pistol-whipping" title="Pistol-whipping">pistol-whipped</a>, and nearly kidnapped by a group of individuals.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Among the items stolen from him were his car, his cash, and his jewelry. On November 26, he shared a post on Instagram to express his gratitude after surviving the attack and confirmed that he was recovering well.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
