<p><b>Irving Domingo Lorenzo Jr.</b> (born June 26, 1970),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> professionally known as <b>Irv Gotti</b> (or <b>DJ Irv</b>), is an American <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a>, music producer, record executive, and the CEO and co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Murder_Inc._Records" title="Murder Inc. Records">Murder Inc. Records</a>. He is best known for producing multiple number-one records for <a href="/wiki/Ashanti_(singer)" title="Ashanti (singer)">Ashanti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>, as well as his collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>. Gotti is also the creator of the <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Tales_(TV_series)" title="Tales (TV series)">Tales</a></i>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irv_Gotti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Irv Gotti was born in the <a href="/wiki/Hollis,_Queens" title="Hollis, Queens">Hollis</a> neighborhood of the borough of <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a> in New York City. <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irv_Gotti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Irv Gotti, under the moniker <i>DJ Irv</i>, produced the song <i>Can I Live</i> on <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>'s June 1996 debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Reasonable_Doubt_(album)" title="Reasonable Doubt (album)">Reasonable Doubt</a></i>. 
</p><p>In 1997,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> through Irv Gotti, Def Jam signed <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruff Ryders">Ruff Ryders</a> artist <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>. DMX's first studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_Dark_and_Hell_is_Hot" class="mw-redirect" title="It&#39;s Dark and Hell is Hot">It's Dark and Hell is Hot</a></i> was released on May 12, 1998 and was co-executive produced by Irv Gotti. The album debuted and peaked at number 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Top_200" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard Top 200">Billboard Top 200</a> and sold over 250,000 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The album went on to sell four million copies in America, being certified quadruple platinum by the RIAA, and sold five million copies worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>After helping <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam</a> with the success of <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russell_Simmons" title="Russell Simmons">Russell Simmons</a> gave Irv Gotti his own label under Def Jam. While watching <i><a href="/wiki/Biography_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Biography (TV series)">Biography</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/A%26E_(TV_channel)" class="mw-redirect" title="A&amp;E (TV channel)">A&amp;E</a> during gangster week, a Murder, Inc. logo appeared on the screen and Irv Gotti decided to use the name for his label because <a href="/wiki/Murder,_Inc." title="Murder, Inc.">Murder, Inc.</a> put out hits for murder and Irv wanted to put out hit records.
</p><p>Irv Gotti co-produced all of Ja Rule's 1999 debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Venni_Vetti_Vecci" title="Venni Vetti Vecci">Venni Vetti Vecci</a></i>, as well as his 2000 second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Rule_3:36" title="Rule 3:36">Rule 3:36</a></i>. Irv co-produced Rule 3:36's singles <i><a href="/wiki/Put_It_On_Me_(Ja_Rule_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Put It On Me (Ja Rule song)">Put It On Me</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Between_Me_and_You" title="Between Me and You">Between Me And You</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2002, Irv Gotti released <i><a href="/wiki/Irv_Gotti_Presents_The_Inc" class="mw-redirect" title="Irv Gotti Presents The Inc">Irv Gotti Presents The Inc</a></i>, featuring much of Murder Inc.'s roster. Irv cop produced much of the album, including the album's hit single <i><a href="/wiki/Down_4_U" title="Down 4 U">Down 4 U</a></i>.
</p><p>Irv, Ja Rule and the label of Murder Inc. were all involved in a well-publicized feud with G-Unit Records, Aftermath Records and Shady Records over history between 50 Cent and Ja Rule. After this Murder Inc. would change their name to <i>The Inc.</i> 
</p><p>Under The Inc. banner, Irv would move the label to <a href="/wiki/Universal_Motown_Records" title="Universal Motown Records">Universal Motown</a> and sign <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Carlton" title="Vanessa Carlton">Vanessa Carlton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Gotti co-produced Vanessa Carlton's third album, <i>Heroes and Thieves</i>, which was released October 9, 2007, with Channel 7, Rick Rubin and Stephan Jenkins.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In an interview with <a href="/wiki/Angie_Martinez" title="Angie Martinez">Angie Martinez</a> in early May 2009, Gotti stated that his label, Murder Inc., was leaving Universal Records.
</p><p>In September 2013, Murder Inc. was relaunched as an umbrella label under Irv Gotti's new label, Visionary.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irv_Gotti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Money_Laundering_case">Money Laundering case</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irv_Gotti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Money Laundering case">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On Friday, January 3, 2003, federal agents and <a href="/wiki/New_York_Police_Department" class="mw-redirect" title="New York Police Department">New York Police Department</a> investigators raided the headquarters of Murder Inc. Records, located at <a href="/wiki/One_Worldwide_Plaza" title="One Worldwide Plaza">One Worldwide Plaza</a> in <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Manhattan" title="Midtown Manhattan">Midtown Manhattan</a>. The raid was a part of a yearlong investigation into the connection between The Lorenzo brothers and American drug lord <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_%E2%80%9CSupreme%E2%80%9D_McGriff" class="mw-redirect" title="Kenneth “Supreme” McGriff">Kenneth “Supreme” McGriff</a>. Investigators believed that the Lorenzos launched Murder Inc. Records using <a href="/wiki/Illicit_drug_trade" class="mw-redirect" title="Illicit drug trade">drug money</a> bankrolled by McGriff to launder McGriff’s drug money, as well as help McGriff launder drug money through the making of the 2003 film <i>Crime Partners</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Although computers and documents were seized, no charges were immediately filed, and no arrests were immediately made. The raid was dramatized in the music video for Ja Rule’s <i>Murder Reigns</i>.
</p><p>Chris and Irv Gotti’s history with McGriff dates back to 1994, when McGriff, newly paroled from prison, met Chris and Irv on the set of a music video in Queens, New York. McGriff wanted to go into cinematography and sought help from Chris and Irv Gotti to produce and direct a film based on <a href="/wiki/Donald_Goines" title="Donald Goines">Donald Goines</a>’ Kenyatta series’ novel Crime Partners. The Lorenzos since then maintained a friendship with McGriff, having him appear in the music video for Ja Rule’s 1999 single “Murda 4 Life” and helped finance McGriff’s dream project, <i>Crime Partners</i>, around 2000.
</p><p>On January 25, 2005, Chris and Irv Gotti surrendered to authorities in New York City and were officially charged with money laundering and conspiracy to launder money. During the trial, Irv Gotti was represented by <a href="/wiki/Gerald_B._Lefcourt" title="Gerald B. Lefcourt">Gerald B. Lefcourt</a>, and Chris Gotti was represented by <a href="/wiki/Gerald_Shargel" title="Gerald Shargel">Gerald Shargel</a>. In December 2005, Chris and Irv Gotti were found not guilty of the charges against them.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
