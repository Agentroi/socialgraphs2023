<p><b>Paul William</b> "<b>Sage</b>" <b>Francis</b> (born November 18, 1976) is an American independent <a href="/wiki/Underground_rap" class="mw-redirect" title="Underground rap">underground</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a> from <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence, Rhode Island</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Dusted-MakeshiftPatriot-2002_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dusted-MakeshiftPatriot-2002-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SLAP-HealthyDistrust-2005_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SLAP-HealthyDistrust-2005-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is the founder and CEO of <a href="/wiki/Strange_Famous_Records" title="Strange Famous Records">Strange Famous Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Exclaim-Life-2010_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Exclaim-Life-2010-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sage_Francis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Francis was born in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, but grew up in <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence, Rhode Island</a>. He is an only child and grew up for most of his childhood with his mother and stepfather after his parents divorced when he was young. He lived in different parts of <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a> including <a href="/wiki/North_Smithfield,_Rhode_Island" title="North Smithfield, Rhode Island">North Smithfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Woonsocket,_Rhode_Island" title="Woonsocket, Rhode Island">Woonsocket</a>. His mom worked as a bartender and his stepfather worked as a locksmith.<sup id="cite_ref-Dusted-MakeshiftPatriot-2002_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dusted-MakeshiftPatriot-2002-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Francis began writing and recording original lyrics at age 8, being inspired by acts such as <a href="/wiki/Run_DMC" class="mw-redirect" title="Run DMC">Run DMC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Public_Enemy" title="Public Enemy">Public Enemy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IllestLyrics-Life-2010_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IllestLyrics-Life-2010-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TheBeeShine-CopperGone-2014_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheBeeShine-CopperGone-2014-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/Artistdirect" title="Artistdirect">Artistdirect</a>, Francis began sneaking out of his parents' house at age 12 to participate in rap battles.<sup id="cite_ref-Artistdirect-Bio_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Artistdirect-Bio-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Francis received a degree in <a href="/wiki/Communications_studies" class="mw-redirect" title="Communications studies">communications</a> from <a href="/wiki/Dean_College" title="Dean College">Dean College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Franklin,_Massachusetts" title="Franklin, Massachusetts">Franklin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> and a degree in <a href="/wiki/Journalism" title="Journalism">journalism</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Rhode_Island" title="University of Rhode Island">University of Rhode Island</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DaBelly-HealthyDistrust-2005_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DaBelly-HealthyDistrust-2005-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sage_Francis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1996, he recorded his first official demo tape, and by 1998 he had a radio show on <a href="/wiki/WRIU" title="WRIU">WRIU</a> called <i>True School Session</i>.
</p><p>Francis won the Superbowl MC Battle in Boston in 1999, beating <a href="/wiki/Esoteric_(rapper)" title="Esoteric (rapper)">Esoteric</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Superbowl-MC-Battle-Akrobatik-1999_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Superbowl-MC-Battle-Akrobatik-1999-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Superbowl-MC-Battle-Esoteric-1999_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Superbowl-MC-Battle-Esoteric-1999-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Francis won the <a href="/wiki/Scribble_Jam" title="Scribble Jam">Scribble Jam</a> emcee battle in 2000, beating <a href="/wiki/Blueprint_(rapper)" title="Blueprint (rapper)">Blueprint</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IllestLyrics-Life-2010_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IllestLyrics-Life-2010-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DaBelly-HealthyDistrust-2005_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DaBelly-HealthyDistrust-2005-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ScribbleJam-BrotherAli-2000_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ScribbleJam-BrotherAli-2000-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ScribbleJam-Blueprint-2000_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ScribbleJam-Blueprint-2000-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2001, the song "<a href="/wiki/Makeshift_Patriot" title="Makeshift Patriot">Makeshift Patriot</a>," recorded on October 11, 2001, became an Internet hit for its critique of American media during and immediately following the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-RT-BreakingTheSet-VonnegutBusy-2014_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RT-BreakingTheSet-VonnegutBusy-2014-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Francis considers "Makeshift Patriot" to be the song that really made things come together for him. It was on the 2002 mix tape, <i>The Known Unsoldier – <a href="/wiki/Sick_of_Waging_War" title="Sick of Waging War">Sick Of Waging War...</a></i> and was widely circulated for free.<sup id="cite_ref-HHKMusic-CopperGone-2015_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHKMusic-CopperGone-2015-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He released the critically acclaimed first solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Personal_Journals" title="Personal Journals">Personal Journals</a></i> in 2002. The record <i><a href="/wiki/Hope_(Non-Prophets_album)" title="Hope (Non-Prophets album)">Hope</a>,</i> his only album with Joe Beats released under the name <a href="/wiki/Non-Prophets" title="Non-Prophets">Non-Prophets</a>, was named after the state of <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>'s motto of hope, and was meant to be very Rhode Island-centric.<sup id="cite_ref-HHKMusic-CopperGone-2015_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHKMusic-CopperGone-2015-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Francis broke music genre barriers by getting signed to <a href="/wiki/Epitaph_Records" title="Epitaph Records">Epitaph Records</a>, making himself the first hip hop artist to sign with the punk rock label. He released three albums with Epitaph: <i><a href="/wiki/A_Healthy_Distrust" title="A Healthy Distrust">A Healthy Distrust</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Human_the_Death_Dance" title="Human the Death Dance">Human the Death Dance</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Life_(Sage_Francis_album)" title="Life (Sage Francis album)">Life</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-PopMatters-HealthyDistrust-Review-2005_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PopMatters-HealthyDistrust-Review-2005-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PopMatters-HumanDeathDance-2007_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PopMatters-HumanDeathDance-2007-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Francis has described <i><a href="/wiki/A_Healthy_Distrust" title="A Healthy Distrust">A Healthy Distrust</a></i> as being a political record, which reflected his mistrust of government and big business with a focus on socio-political matters.<sup id="cite_ref-HHKMusic-CopperGone-2015_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHKMusic-CopperGone-2015-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2007, a music video of the song "Got Up This Morning" from <i><a href="/wiki/Human_the_Death_Dance" title="Human the Death Dance">Human the Death Dance</a></i> was released. The song featured vocals by <a href="/wiki/Jolie_Holland" title="Jolie Holland">Jolie Holland</a> and beats produced by <a href="/wiki/Buck_65" title="Buck 65">Buck 65</a>. The video featured cameos from <a href="/wiki/B._Dolan" title="B. Dolan">B. Dolan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brother_Ali" title="Brother Ali">Brother Ali</a>, Buck 65, Divinci from <a href="/wiki/Solillaquists_of_Sound" title="Solillaquists of Sound">Solillaquists of Sound</a>, Holland, <a href="/wiki/Slug_(rapper)" title="Slug (rapper)">Slug</a>, and Tom Inhaler from Francis' band and <a href="/wiki/Strange_Famous_Records" title="Strange Famous Records">Strange Famous Records</a><sup id="cite_ref-Epitaph-GotUpThisMorning-MakingOf-2007_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Epitaph-GotUpThisMorning-MakingOf-2007-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Life_(Sage_Francis_album)" title="Life (Sage Francis album)">Life</a></i> was a shift into a different style for Francis, focusing on spoken word and indie rock.<sup id="cite_ref-PopMatters-Life-Review-2010_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PopMatters-Life-Review-2010-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The record featured contributions and collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Califone" title="Califone">Califone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Walla" title="Chris Walla">Chris Walla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Lytle" title="Jason Lytle">Jason Lytle</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Linkous" title="Mark Linkous">Mark Linkous</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IllestLyrics-Life-2010_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IllestLyrics-Life-2010-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Francis made a guest appearance on <a href="/wiki/Bad_Religion" title="Bad Religion">Bad Religion</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Empire_Strikes_First" title="The Empire Strikes First">The Empire Strikes First</a></i>, on the track "Let Them Eat War".
</p><p>In 2014, Francis released <i><a href="/wiki/Copper_Gone" title="Copper Gone">Copper Gone</a></i> after a four-year break from touring.<sup id="cite_ref-PopMatters-CopperGone-Review-2014_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PopMatters-CopperGone-Review-2014-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ProvidenceJournal-CopperGone-2015_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ProvidenceJournal-CopperGone-2015-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The album was named after the phenomenon Francis saw in his neighborhood where abandoned buildings would have their copper pipes stripped to be sold, and then tagged as "Copper Gone" to let others know that there was no copper left to steal.<sup id="cite_ref-HHKMusic-CopperGone-2015_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHKMusic-CopperGone-2015-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Francis says that he has been releasing mix tapes consistently since 1999, and will continue to release the <i>Sick of...</i> mix tape series on an ongoing basis.<sup id="cite_ref-HHKMusic-CopperGone-2015_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHKMusic-CopperGone-2015-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Francis has toured extensively,<sup id="cite_ref-TheFilmGuys-GoingThroughHell-2016_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheFilmGuys-GoingThroughHell-2016-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and has performed shows with <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere_(music_group)" title="Atmosphere (music group)">Atmosphere</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brother_Ali" title="Brother Ali">Brother Ali</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eyedea_%26_Abilities" title="Eyedea &amp; Abilities">Eyedea &amp; Abilities</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Epic_Beard_Men">Epic Beard Men</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sage_Francis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Epic Beard Men">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Francis collaborated with <a href="/wiki/B._Dolan" title="B. Dolan">B. Dolan</a> under the name <a href="/wiki/Epic_Beard_Men" title="Epic Beard Men">Epic Beard Men</a>. The duo's official debut single, "War on Christmas", was released in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, the duo released the <i>Season 1</i> EP,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> as well as "Five Hearts", a single from the EP.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In 2019, the duo released the first studio album, <i>This Was Supposed to Be Fun</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> It includes "You Can't Tell Me Shit", which became <i><a href="/wiki/The_Stranger_(newspaper)" title="The Stranger (newspaper)">The Stranger</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s "Inbox Jukebox Track of the Day".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The album was placed at number 20 on <i><a href="/wiki/PopMatters" title="PopMatters">PopMatters</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span> "20 Best Hip-Hop Albums of 2019" list.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Strange_Famous_Records">Strange Famous Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sage_Francis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Strange Famous Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Strange_Famous_Records" title="Strange Famous Records">Strange Famous Records</a></div>
<p>Francis is the founder and CEO of the independent hip hop record label <a href="/wiki/Strange_Famous_Records" title="Strange Famous Records">Strange Famous Records</a>. The label first began in 1996 as a way for him to release his previously unreleased songs in the late 1990s. It has since evolved into an official enterprise with an expanding roster of like-minded artists. He released several <i>Sick Of</i> mixtapes available on his website.<sup id="cite_ref-RT-BreakingTheSet-VonnegutBusy-2014_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RT-BreakingTheSet-VonnegutBusy-2014-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_activism">Social activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sage_Francis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Social activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Francis worked with fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/B._Dolan" title="B. Dolan">B. Dolan</a> who had the idea to start an organization and website named KnowMore.org aka KnowMore: The People's Corporation Watch Project which focused on connecting consumers with social responsibility information about corporations and responsible consumerism.<sup id="cite_ref-IllestLyrics-Life-2010_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IllestLyrics-Life-2010-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Francis went to <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> to work on solutions to <a href="/wiki/AIDS_in_South_Africa" class="mw-redirect" title="AIDS in South Africa">AIDS in South Africa</a>, especially in regards to children. As part of that effort he was part of a documentary on HIV in South Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-aboveGround-SouthAfrica-Doc-2011_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aboveGround-SouthAfrica-Doc-2011-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sage_Francis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Francis is also part of the contemporary <a href="/wiki/Spoken_word" title="Spoken word">spoken word</a> movement. His relationship with the Providence <a href="/wiki/Poetry_slam" title="Poetry slam">poetry slam</a> community (he was on their 1998, 1999 and 2002 national poetry slam teams)<sup id="cite_ref-GotPoetry-PoetrySlams_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GotPoetry-PoetrySlams-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> led to it being called "The House that Sage Francis Built."<sup id="cite_ref-WordsInYourFace-2008_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WordsInYourFace-2008-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> From 2000 to 2002, he also DJ-ed for the NYC-Urbana poetry slam, a weekly slam series held at the legendary punk rock venue <a href="/wiki/CBGB" title="CBGB">CBGB</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WordsInYourFace-2008_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WordsInYourFace-2008-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
