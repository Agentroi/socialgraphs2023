<p><b>Michael P. Delaney</b>, better known by his stage name <b>Dub-L</b>, is an American record producer, recording artist and filmmaker.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dub-L&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Delaney was born and raised in the <a href="/wiki/East_Village,_Manhattan" title="East Village, Manhattan">East Village</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-urbansmarts_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urbansmarts-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-apatil_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apatil-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He began writing graffiti in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-apatil_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apatil-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The name Dub-L comes from his last name, Delaney, which he shortened to Dell for his tag name in high school when he was doing graffiti. People started calling him Double-L, which turned into Dub-L.<sup id="cite_ref-africas_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-africas-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dub-L&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dub-L&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dub-L produced <a href="/wiki/Aesop_Rock" title="Aesop Rock">Aesop Rock</a>'s first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Music_for_Earthworms" title="Music for Earthworms">Music for Earthworms</a></i>, in 1997. Dub-L has said that he is in possession of the masters but will not re-release it, because he likes the fact that it's "rare and legendary."<sup id="cite_ref-urbansmarts_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urbansmarts-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Also in 1997, Dub-L met fellow New York City native and DJ JS-1, who was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Rock_Steady_Crew" title="Rock Steady Crew">Rock Steady Crew</a>. They immediately began collaborating as Ground Original, releasing their debut album in 2002, also called <i>Ground Original</i>, which featured well-known MCs such as <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masta_Ace" title="Masta Ace">Masta Ace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a> and <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a>, as well as underground MCs such as <a href="/wiki/C-Rayz_Walz" title="C-Rayz Walz">C-Rayz Walz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Planet_Asia" title="Planet Asia">Planet Asia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mcusenza_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mcusenza-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> They released <i>Claimstake</i> the next year, which included appearances from <a href="/wiki/Common_(rapper)" title="Common (rapper)">Common</a>, <a href="/wiki/J-Live" title="J-Live">J-Live</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jeru_the_Damaja" title="Jeru the Damaja">Jeru the Damaja</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mcusenza_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mcusenza-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2003, Dub-L released his debut solo album, <i>Dub-L's In Town</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-africas_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-africas-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> following it up in 2004 with his second album, <i>Day of the Mega Beast!</i> It features guest appearances from artists including <a href="/wiki/Rahzel" title="Rahzel">Rahzel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Professor_X" title="Professor X">Professor X</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hangar_18_(band)" title="Hangar 18 (band)">Hangar 18</a>, DJ JS-1 and <a href="/wiki/MF_Grimm" title="MF Grimm">MF Grimm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ugrap_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ugrap-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Dub-L produced and rapped on his two solo albums.<sup id="cite_ref-ugrap_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ugrap-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dub-L's other work includes the trip hop group The Controls, which he formed with singer Ann Colville in 1998, putting out their debut album the following year. An instrumental version of the album was released in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2004, he produced two tracks from <a href="/wiki/MF_Doom" title="MF Doom">MF Doom</a>'s second Viktor Vaughn album, <i><a href="/wiki/Venomous_Villain" class="mw-redirect" title="Venomous Villain">Venomous Villain</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-ugrap_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ugrap-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He has remixed artists including <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Simpson" title="Jessica Simpson">Jessica Simpson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-apatil_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apatil-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Dub-L and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Cassidy" title="DJ Cassidy">DJ Cassidy</a> occasionally team up as Cass &amp; Dubs; in 2008, they produced all of O'Neal McKnight's singles, including "Check Your Coat", featuring <a href="/wiki/Greg_Nice" class="mw-redirect" title="Greg Nice">Greg Nice</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> After disbanding Cass &amp; Dubs in 2012, he and rapper Chase Phoenix formed the group Monday Morning High.<sup id="cite_ref-apatil_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apatil-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dub-L&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His short film <i>Baby Show</i>, based on his <a href="/wiki/Public-access_television" title="Public-access television">Public-access television</a> comedy sketches from the late-1990s, was an official selection at the 2016 International Filmmaker Festival of World Cinema Nice, where Delaney won Best Editing of a Short Film<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and was nominated for Best Comedy.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It was also selected for the NewFilmmakers New York Spring 2016 festival, winning the Audience Choice Award for Best Short Film.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
