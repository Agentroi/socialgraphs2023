<p><b>Durron Maurice Butler</b> (born April 30, 1967),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known as <b>Turbo B</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beatboxer" class="mw-redirect" title="Beatboxer">beatboxer</a>. He was once the frontman of the German electronic music group <a href="/wiki/Snap!" title="Snap!">Snap!</a>
</p><p>After completing a period of service with the United States Army in Germany, Butler went on tour with <a href="/wiki/The_Fat_Boys" title="The Fat Boys">the Fat Boys</a>. He was soon discovered by the managers of the Snap! and contributed to the albums <i><a href="/wiki/World_Power" title="World Power">World Power</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Madman%27s_Return" title="The Madman&#39;s Return">The Madman's Return</a></i> and their respective number-one hits, "<a href="/wiki/The_Power_(Snap!_song)" title="The Power (Snap! song)">The Power</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Rhythm_Is_a_Dancer" title="Rhythm Is a Dancer">Rhythm Is a Dancer</a>".
</p><p>After leaving Snap!, Butler pursued a solo career and also formed the group <a href="/wiki/Centory_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Centory (band)">Centory</a>, which had several minor hits. Butler is known to tour with other artists who were prominent in the 1990s. He has contributed to singles by <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Silvstedt" title="Victoria Silvstedt">Victoria Silvstedt</a>, <a href="/wiki/H-Blockx" title="H-Blockx">H-Blockx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Master_Blaster_(band)" title="Master Blaster (band)">Master Blaster</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Regi_Penxten" title="Regi Penxten">Regi Penxten</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Turbo_B&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/McKeesport" class="mw-redirect" title="McKeesport">McKeesport</a>, Pennsylvania, Butler started his music career as a drummer for a heavy metal band in his hometown. After joining the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> in July 1985, he completed basic training (C-4-3) at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Dix" title="Fort Dix">Fort Dix</a> and was transferred to the 8th SC, Redstone Arsenal in Alabama for advanced training, where he qualified to become an ammunition specialist. With his training complete, he was sent to <a href="/wiki/Friedberg,_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Friedberg, Germany">Friedberg, Germany</a> to join the 60th Ordnance Company in <a href="/wiki/Ray_Barracks,_Friedberg,_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Ray Barracks, Friedberg, Germany">Ray Barracks</a>. After completing his service in the army he returned to the US but went back to Germany shortly thereafter to tour with <a href="/wiki/The_Fat_Boys" title="The Fat Boys">the Fat Boys</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 1989, DJ Rico Sparx discovered Butler's rapping talent and introduced him to  Snap! producers Michael Münzing and Luca Anzilotti.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The duo picked him to replace <a href="/wiki/Chill_Rob_G" title="Chill Rob G">Chill Rob G</a>'s vocals in their song "<a href="/wiki/The_Power_(Snap!_song)" title="The Power (Snap! song)">The Power</a>". He chose the stage name Turbo B, which had been his nickname since childhood.
</p>
<h2><span id="Success_with_Snap.21"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Success_with_Snap!">Success with Snap!</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Turbo_B&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Success with Snap!">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"The Power" became a major international hit, and Butler became recognizable as the frontman of the group. To promote their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/World_Power" title="World Power">World Power</a></i>, they toured widely.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In the run-up to the 1992 release of Snap!'s second album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Madman%27s_Return" title="The Madman&#39;s Return">The Madman's Return</a></i>, "<a href="/wiki/Rhythm_Is_a_Dancer" title="Rhythm Is a Dancer">Rhythm Is a Dancer</a>" was planned to be the first single. Butler reportedly hated the song, especially the line "I'm serious as cancer when I say rhythm is a dancer",<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and wanted
"<a href="/wiki/Colour_of_Love_(Snap_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Colour of Love (Snap song)">Colour of Love</a>" to be issued instead.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In December 1991, he got his wish, but "Colour of Love" went fairly unnoticed in the UK and US charts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Münzing and Anzilotti then insisted on releasing "Rhythm Is a Dancer" as the second single, and it became the group's second major international hit. Butler left Snap! shortly after due to growing dissent with the group and its managers.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2000, Snap! and Turbo B rejoined for a comeback and released the single "Gimme a Thrill". The song failed to chart, and their planned album <i>One Day on Earth</i> was subsequently never released.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career_and_Centory">Solo career and Centory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Turbo_B&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo career and Centory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After leaving Snap!, Butler pursued a solo career, releasing the album <i>Make Way for the Maniac</i> in 1993 on <a href="/wiki/Polydor_Records" title="Polydor Records">Polydor Records</a>. It featured the singles "I'm Not Dead", "Get Wild", "What You See", and "Nice &amp; Smooth".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He went on to form the band <a href="/wiki/Centory_(pop_band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Centory (pop band)">Centory</a> in 1994, with Gary Carolla, Delgado (Kevin Estevez), and Alex Trime (Eddie Gibson). They released the album <i>Alpha Centory</i>, which spawned four minor hits in the European charts. Butler toured with Centory following their success. He did not feature in their last single, a cover of <a href="/wiki/Milli_Vanilli" title="Milli Vanilli">Milli Vanilli</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Girl_You_Know_It%27s_True_(song)" title="Girl You Know It&#39;s True (song)">Girl You Know It's True</a>", which instead featured singer Trey D. The song was Centory's least successful single, reaching No. 50 in the German Charts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Butler did not issue any more solo material until 2005, when his single "New Day" was released under Holy Chaos Recordings.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He has contributed to singles by <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Silvstedt" title="Victoria Silvstedt">Victoria Silvstedt</a>, <a href="/wiki/H-Blockx" title="H-Blockx">H-Blockx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Master_Blaster_(band)" title="Master Blaster (band)">Master Blaster</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Regi_Penxten" title="Regi Penxten">Regi Penxten</a>, among others.
</p>
