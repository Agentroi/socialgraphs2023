<p><b>Jeremie Damon Pennick</b> (born November 27, 1984), known professionally as <b>Benny the Butcher</b> or simply <b>Benny</b>, is an American rapper. He is part of the hip hop collective <a href="/wiki/Griselda_Records" title="Griselda Records">Griselda</a> with frequent collaborators and first-cousins <a href="/wiki/Westside_Gunn" title="Westside Gunn">Westside Gunn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conway_the_Machine" title="Conway the Machine">Conway the Machine</a>. He is also a part of the collective Black Soprano Family.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_the_Butcher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Benny the Butcher self-released his mixtapes in the late 2000s as&#160;B.E.N.N.Y., including <i>The Mayor's Back</i> and&#160;<i>Chains Bond</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-AllMusic_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, Westside Gunn founded Griselda Records, through which Benny, Conway and Westside Gunn would self-release their own projects. In 2016, Benny released his album titled <i>My First Brick</i> followed by the mixtape <i>Butcher on Steroids</i> with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Green_Lantern" title="DJ Green Lantern">DJ Green Lantern</a> in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 3, 2017, Griselda (as a collective), as well as Pennick's cousins Westside Gunn and Conway the Machine, signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a>, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope</a>. In April 2017, Benny the Butcher and his collective Black Soprano Family signed a label deal with <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_One_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="Entertainment One Music">Entertainment One Music</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 23, 2018, he released his debut studio album <i>Tana Talk 3</i>, produced by&#160;<a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>&#160;and Griselda producer&#160;Daringer, and includes features from <a href="/wiki/Westside_Gunn" title="Westside Gunn">Westside Gunn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conway_the_Machine" title="Conway the Machine">Conway the Machine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>. The album was critically acclaimed and brought widespread attention to the rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>June 21, 2019 marked the release of his fourth extended play, <i>The Plugs I Met</i>  which included guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Black_Thought" title="Black Thought">Black Thought</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, <a href="/wiki/38_Spesh" title="38 Spesh">38 Spesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Conway_the_Machine" title="Conway the Machine">Conway the Machine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On August 6, it was announced that Pennick signed a management deal with <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Writing about Benny the Butcher's 2019 project, Dan-O of Freemusicempire said "Benny The Butcher did an EP with the greatest guest list of the year. Right after the intro skit you hear him next to Black Thought. Next song Jadakiss, and closing out the project with Pusha T's second best guest appearance of 2019 on 18 Wheeler. No matter who was with him Benny blew like Sonny Rollins on the Sax, whether Conway or Eminem was coming on next...it didn't matter. The Butcher has a gift and no fear."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 27, 2020, Benny the Butcher announced in an interview with <a href="/wiki/Adam22" title="Adam22">Adam22</a> for No Jumper that he would release an album under eOne called <i><a href="/wiki/Burden_of_Proof_(Benny_the_Butcher_album)" title="Burden of Proof (Benny the Butcher album)">Burden of Proof</a></i> between late September and early October.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The album was eventually released on October 16 to rave reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 19, 2021, Benny the Butcher released the sequel to <i>The Plugs I Met</i> EP, with a collaboration EP with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Fraud" title="Harry Fraud">Harry Fraud</a>, named <i><a href="/wiki/The_Plugs_I_Met_2" title="The Plugs I Met 2">The Plugs I Met 2</a></i>. This EP received widespread critical acclaim from various music critics.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 12, 2021, he officially signed a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> for upcoming releases. The announcement was made by American rapper <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, a creative consultant for the label, on <a href="/wiki/Joe_Rogan" title="Joe Rogan">Joe Rogan</a>'s podcast <a href="/wiki/The_Joe_Rogan_Experience" title="The Joe Rogan Experience">The Joe Rogan Experience</a> a week after Benny the Butcher was featured on Snoop Dogg's single "Murder Music" alongside <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 28, 2022, Benny released the single, "<a href="/wiki/Johnny_P%27s_Caddy" title="Johnny P&#39;s Caddy">Johnny P's Caddy</a>", with rapper <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>. The song is the lead single from Benny's then-upcoming album, <i>Tana Talk 4</i>, which he has labeled "album of the year".<sup id="cite_ref-Caddy_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Caddy-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <i>Tana Talk 4</i> was eventually released on March 11 to generally favorable reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_the_Butcher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 2020, in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, Texas, Benny the Butcher was shot in the leg during an attempted robbery in a Walmart parking lot.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 27, 2021, Benny the Butcher cancelled a concert at <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Saint_Andrew%27s_Hall_(Detroit)" title="Saint Andrew&#39;s Hall (Detroit)">Saint Andrew's Hall</a> after he was hospitalized with <a href="/wiki/Asthma" title="Asthma">asthma</a>, a condition he has both rapped about and spoken about in interviews.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In a verified annotation on <a href="/wiki/Genius_(website)" class="mw-redirect" title="Genius (website)">Genius</a>'s lyric page for his song "Where Would I Go" off the 2020 album <i><a href="/wiki/Burden_of_Proof_(Benny_the_Butcher_album)" title="Burden of Proof (Benny the Butcher album)">Burden of Proof</a></i>, he wrote he has lived with asthma for 25 years and the condition forces him to go to the emergency room two or three times a year: "I got to be careful, I got to make sure I wear my mask, I got to make sure I'm taking my medicine. Running the streets and being in and out of prison, it's probably something I haven't done the best of. But being older, that's something that I take care of".<sup id="cite_ref-Genius_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Genius-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
