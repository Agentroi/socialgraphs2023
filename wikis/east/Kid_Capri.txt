<p><b>David Anthony Love Jr. </b>(born February 7, 1967) is an American <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a> and rapper better known by his stage name <b>Kid Capri</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-Larkin90_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin90-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kid_Capri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kid Capri's humble roots can be traced back to his parents' home in the Bronx, where ten year old David Anthony Love would scratch records on his father's old Zenith stereo system. As soon as he was old enough, he hit the pavement and caught a buzz hustling his DJ mixtapes on the streets and performing local gigs.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (August 2023)">original research?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kid_Capri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grammy award winning DJ and producer, Kid Capri, is globally known<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (August 2023)">by whom?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> as an originator, innovator, and pioneer of DJ culture<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. His grass roots hustler mentality became a template for success emulated by the DJs that followed in his footsteps. Dubbed The Guru of Mixtapes<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (August 2023)">by whom?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>, Kid Capri literally redefined the term "DJ"<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (August 2023)">according to whom?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> as he blazed a trail with his array of mixtapes and crowd hyping performances. 
</p><p>Kid Capri was the first DJ to perform on major networks like BET and VH1. He starred in the popular HBO series Russel Simmons Def Comedy Jam and recently<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (August 2023)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> returned for The Netflix Def Comedy Jam's 25th Anniversary Special. Kid has produced tracks for artists such as Snoop Dogg, Jay-Z, Madonna, Heavy D, and 50 Cent. He has years of touring experience with legends like Diddy, Jay Z, Aaliyah, Salt-N-Pepa, Timbaland, and most recently RBRM<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. Kid won a Grammy for producing a song on Jay-Z's album, Hard Knock Life. In 2017 Kid narrated Kendrick Lamar's DAMN album; the only hip hop album to ever win a Pulitzer Prize. Kid premiered his signature crowd hyping vocals at Jennifer Lopez's ALL I HAVE  Las Vegas Residency during the magical portion of her show dedicated to The Bronx.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (August 2023)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Kid Capri deejayed for seven seasons of <i><a href="/wiki/Def_Comedy_Jam" title="Def Comedy Jam">Def Comedy Jam</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Larkin90_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin90-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">produced</a> tracks for <a href="/wiki/Boogie_Down_Productions" title="Boogie Down Productions">Boogie Down Productions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heavy_D" title="Heavy D">Heavy D</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_L" title="Big L">Big L</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grand_Puba" title="Grand Puba">Grand Puba</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Larkin90_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin90-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He eventually released an LP on <a href="/wiki/Warner_Records" title="Warner Records">Warner Brothers Records</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tape_(album)" title="The Tape (album)">The Tape</a></i> in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-Larkin90_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin90-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He appeared in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Who%27s_the_Man%3F" title="Who&#39;s the Man?">Who's the Man?</a></i> in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-Larkin90_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin90-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 1997, Kid Capri signed with the <a href="/wiki/Trackmasters" title="Trackmasters">Track Masters</a>' <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>-distributed label after he appeared on the 1997 <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Puff Daddy</a> and the Family World Tour. He subsequently released <i><a href="/wiki/Soundtrack_to_the_Streets" title="Soundtrack to the Streets">Soundtrack to the Streets</a></i> in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-Larkin90_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin90-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kid Capri was involved in his own record label, No Kid'n Records. He was featured on the itshiphop.tv show <i>The Breakdown</i>, in which he talked about his current and future projects, his views on Japan's hip hop scene, and the origin of his stage name. On July 29, 2007, Kid Capri made an appearance during <a href="/wiki/Rock_the_Bells_2007" class="mw-redirect" title="Rock the Bells 2007">Rock the Bells 2007</a> as <a href="/wiki/Rakim" title="Rakim">Rakim</a>'s DJ. Kid Capri also made an appearance as Rakim's DJ during the Rock the Bells 2008 show on July 19, 2008, in <a href="/wiki/Tinley_Park,_Illinois" title="Tinley Park, Illinois">Tinley Park, Illinois</a>.
</p><p>Kid was featured as lead judge on <a href="/wiki/Smirnoff" title="Smirnoff">Smirnoff</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Master_of_the_Mix" title="Master of the Mix">Master of the Mix</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a> <a href="/wiki/Reality_TV" class="mw-redirect" title="Reality TV">reality TV</a> competition in search of the nation's best DJ. In March 2012, Kid was commissioned to remix "<a href="/wiki/Masterpiece_(Madonna_song)" title="Masterpiece (Madonna song)">Masterpiece</a>", an exclusive track featured on the special Smirnoff Nightlife Edition of <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>'s album, <i><a href="/wiki/MDNA_(album)" title="MDNA (album)">MDNA</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He joined her for the album release party in Miami as a part of <a href="/wiki/Winter_Music_Conference" title="Winter Music Conference">Winter Music Conference</a>, and the album was released on March 26, 2012. Kid was also featured on <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Element_(song)" title="Element (song)">ELEMENT</a>." in which he has vocal parts in the intro and closing.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 15, 2021,  Kid Capri was a <a href="/wiki/Bronx_Walk_of_Fame" title="Bronx Walk of Fame">Bronx Walk of Fame</a> inductee.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
