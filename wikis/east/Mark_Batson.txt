<p><b>Mark Batson</b> is an American producer, musician and songwriter. He has worked on albums by artists including <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Matthews_Band" title="Dave Matthews Band">Dave Matthews Band</a>, <a href="/wiki/LeAnn_Rimes" title="LeAnn Rimes">LeAnn Rimes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Hamilton_(musician)" title="Anthony Hamilton (musician)">Anthony Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/India_Arie" title="India Arie">India Arie</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maroon_5" title="Maroon 5">Maroon 5</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skylar_Grey" title="Skylar Grey">Skylar Grey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grace_Potter_and_the_Nocturnals" title="Grace Potter and the Nocturnals">Grace Potter and the Nocturnals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seal_(musician)" title="Seal (musician)">Seal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sting_(musician)" title="Sting (musician)">Sting</a>.  He has also composed music for films and television shows including <i><a href="/wiki/I,_Tonya" title="I, Tonya">I, Tonya</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/American_Hustle" title="American Hustle">American Hustle</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sharp_Objects_(miniseries)" title="Sharp Objects (miniseries)">Sharp Objects</a></i>,  <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_2" title="Spider-Man 2">Spider-Man 2</a></i> and  <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Book_IV:_Force" title="Power Book IV: Force">Power Book IV: Force</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Batson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Batson was formally trained in classical piano as a child growing up in the Bushwick Houses in Brooklyn, New York. He performed classical piano pieces at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Hall" title="Carnegie Hall">Carnegie Hall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Academy_of_Music" title="Brooklyn Academy of Music">Brooklyn Academy of Music</a> at the ages of 12. He later studied jazz piano at <a href="/wiki/Howard_University" title="Howard University">Howard University</a>. He was the pianist for the <a href="/wiki/Smithsonian_Institution" title="Smithsonian Institution">Smithsonian Institution</a>'s African American Culture department. He first mixed hip hop sampling and drum programming with live instrumentation as part of the group Get Set V.O.P. This blend is characteristic of much of his later production work.<sup id="cite_ref-Vibe_early_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe_early-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pareles_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pareles-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Batson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Batson has composed music for the feature films <i><a href="/wiki/Beauty_Shop" title="Beauty Shop">Beauty Shop</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Miami_Vice_(film)" title="Miami Vice (film)">Miami Vice</a>,</i> <i><a href="/wiki/American_Hustle" title="American Hustle">American Hustle</a>.</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Triple_9" title="Triple 9">Triple 9</a></i>. He is also the writer of the graphic novel series <i>Loaded</i>, and is developing the music for the series with <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Batson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Batson won <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Awards</a> in 2010 and 2011 for Best Rap Album For <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Relapse_(Eminem_album)" title="Relapse (Eminem album)">Relapse</a></i> (2010) and <i><a href="/wiki/Recovery_(Eminem_album)" title="Recovery (Eminem album)">Recovery</a></i> (2011).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He also won the Grammy for Best R&amp;B album in 2004 for <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Dangerously_in_Love" title="Dangerously in Love">Dangerously in Love</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  In 2016, he produced albums for <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skylar_Grey" title="Skylar Grey">Skylar Grey</a>, <a href="/wiki/LeAnn_Rimes" title="LeAnn Rimes">LeAnn Rimes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Hamilton_(musician)" title="Anthony Hamilton (musician)">Anthony Hamilton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-All_Music_credits_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All_Music_credits-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
