<p><b>Lee Jason Kilber</b>, known professionally as <b>DJ Logic</b> (born 1972), is an American <a href="/wiki/Turntablism" title="Turntablism">turntablist</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> active primarily in <a href="/wiki/Nu-jazz" class="mw-redirect" title="Nu-jazz">nu-jazz</a>/<a href="/wiki/Acid_jazz" title="Acid jazz">acid jazz</a> and with <a href="/wiki/Jam_band" title="Jam band">jam bands</a>.
</p><p>Kibler was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx,_NY" class="mw-redirect" title="The Bronx, NY">The Bronx, NY</a>. Because of an early interest in <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, Kibler started using the <a href="/wiki/Phonograph" title="Phonograph">turntables</a>, practicing often. Kibler was also interested in <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> music and began collaborating with various musicians. His own recordings are perhaps best described as contemporary <a href="/wiki/Soul_jazz" title="Soul jazz">soul jazz</a> with a strong <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> feel.
</p><p>Kibler often tours with his own group, Project Logic, and has recorded or performed with <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Reid" title="Vernon Reid">Vernon Reid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moe." class="mw-redirect" title="Moe.">moe.</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Mayer_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="John Mayer (musician)">John Mayer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Medeski_Martin_%26_Wood" title="Medeski Martin &amp; Wood">Medeski Martin &amp; Wood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bob_Belden" title="Bob Belden">Bob Belden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Johnson_(musician)" title="Jack Johnson (musician)">Jack Johnson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Whitley" title="Chris Whitley">Chris Whitley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uri_Caine" title="Uri Caine">Uri Caine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian_McBride" title="Christian McBride">Christian McBride</a> and others. On September 10 2001, Project Logic performed the final official show at <a href="/wiki/Wetlands_Preserve" title="Wetlands Preserve">Wetlands Preserve</a> before the closing of the venue (this performance featured special guests <a href="/wiki/Warren_Haynes" title="Warren Haynes">Warren Haynes</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Allman_Brothers_Band" title="The Allman Brothers Band">The Allman Brothers Band</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gov%27t_Mule" title="Gov&#39;t Mule">Gov't Mule</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Jordan" title="Stanley Jordan">Stanley Jordan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Gordon" title="Mike Gordon">Mike Gordon</a>).  On April 6 2006, Kibler sat in with <a href="/wiki/Bob_Weir" title="Bob Weir">Bob Weir</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ratdog" class="mw-redirect" title="Ratdog">Ratdog</a>, adding his style to the famous <a href="/wiki/Grateful_Dead" title="Grateful Dead">Grateful Dead</a> song combo "China Cat Sunflower/I Know You Rider". He is a founding member of <a href="/wiki/The_John_Popper_Project" title="The John Popper Project">The John Popper Project</a>. On August 15, 2009, Kibler sat in with <a href="/wiki/O.A.R._(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="O.A.R. (band)">O.A.R.</a> at <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a>, adding his style to their latest single "This Town".
</p><p>Jason Kiblers mentor had always been <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Reid" title="Vernon Reid">Vernon Reid</a>, and the twosome recorded and toured occasionally as the “Yohimbe Brothers” with various guests. Kibler has appeared on studio and live performances with Reid's solo projects; Reid also did a guest solo on one track from Kibler's original band, <a href="/wiki/Eye_%26_I" title="Eye &amp; I">Eye and I</a>, which also featured D.K. Dyson and <a href="/wiki/Melvin_Gibbs" title="Melvin Gibbs">Melvin Gibbs</a>, the latter of whom has played on all of the DJ Logic solo releases as well.
</p>
