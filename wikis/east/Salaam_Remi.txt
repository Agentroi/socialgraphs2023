<p><b>Salaam Remi Gibbs</b> (born May 14, 1972)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is an American record producer. He is most known for producing material for close associates <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amy_Winehouse" title="Amy Winehouse">Amy Winehouse</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jazmine_Sullivan" title="Jazmine Sullivan">Jazmine Sullivan</a>, but has also produced for other mainstream acts including <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fugees" title="Fugees">Fugees</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doja_Cat" title="Doja Cat">Doja Cat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miguel_(singer)" title="Miguel (singer)">Miguel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fergie_(singer)" title="Fergie (singer)">Fergie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alessia_Cara" title="Alessia Cara">Alessia Cara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nelly_Furtado" title="Nelly Furtado">Nelly Furtado</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Estelle_(musician)" title="Estelle (musician)">Estelle</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Gibbs is noted for his <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a>-tinged (often referred to as "broken-bottle") approach to production.
</p><p>With 10 <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nominations, Gibbs was awarded his first in 2022 for his production on <i><a href="/wiki/The_United_States_vs._Billie_Holiday_(soundtrack)" title="The United States vs. Billie Holiday (soundtrack)">The United States vs. Billie Holiday soundtrack</a></i> (2021).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salaam_Remi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career_beginnings">Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salaam_Remi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Salaam Remi Gibbs was born in Queens, New York to a Trinidadian and Barbadian father, studio musician Van Joseph Gibbs. Remi first appeared on record as the keyboard player on <a href="/wiki/Kurtis_Blow" title="Kurtis Blow">Kurtis Blow</a>'s 1986 release <i><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_Blow" title="Kingdom Blow">Kingdom Blow</a></i>. In the late 1980s, Remi began mixing records. His first full production was in 1992, for the hip-hop group Zhigge. He worked with the <a href="/wiki/Fugees" title="Fugees">Fugees</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Sheep_(group)" title="Black Sheep (group)">Black Sheep</a>.
</p><p>Salaam Remi has been associated with releases like <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Flip" title="Lil&#39; Flip">Lil' Flip</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/I_Need_Mine" title="I Need Mine">I Need Mine</a>", <a href="/wiki/Ini_Kamoze" title="Ini Kamoze">Ini Kamoze</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Here_Comes_the_Hotstepper" title="Here Comes the Hotstepper">Here Comes the Hotstepper</a>", <a href="/wiki/The_Fugees" class="mw-redirect" title="The Fugees">The Fugees</a>' <a href="/wiki/Multi-platinum" class="mw-redirect" title="Multi-platinum">multi-platinum</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Score_(Fugees_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Score (Fugees album)">The Score</a></i> LP,  and <a href="/wiki/Toni_Braxton" title="Toni Braxton">Toni Braxton</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/You%27re_Makin%27_Me_High" title="You&#39;re Makin&#39; Me High">You're Makin' Me High</a>". He produced 10 tracks on the 2002 UK sensation <a href="/wiki/Ms._Dynamite" title="Ms. Dynamite">Ms. Dynamite</a>'s album titled <i>A Little Deeper</i>, including the hugely successful lead single "Dy-Na-Mi-Tee", which had moderate crossover success in the U.S. market.
</p><p>Remi contributed tracks to <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_5" title="Jurassic 5">Jurassic 5</a>'s 2006 LP <i><a href="/wiki/Feedback_(Jurassic_5_album)" title="Feedback (Jurassic 5 album)">Feedback</a></i> and Nas's 2006 album <i><a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_Is_Dead" title="Hip Hop Is Dead">Hip Hop Is Dead</a></i>, and racked up production credits on <i><a href="/wiki/Frank_(Amy_Winehouse_album)" title="Frank (Amy Winehouse album)">Frank</a></i>, the platinum debut from North London soul-jazz singer <a href="/wiki/Amy_Winehouse" title="Amy Winehouse">Amy Winehouse</a>. He worked on Winehouse's followup, <i><a href="/wiki/Back_to_Black" title="Back to Black">Back to Black</a></i>, and was producing her third album that was to be released in 2011, up until her death. Remi worked with <a href="/wiki/Nelly_Furtado" title="Nelly Furtado">Nelly Furtado</a> on her first Spanish language album, <i><a href="/wiki/Mi_Plan" title="Mi Plan">Mi Plan</a></i>. His projects in late 2008 included working with <a href="/wiki/Jazmine_Sullivan" title="Jazmine Sullivan">Jazmine Sullivan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Nas (rapper)">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leona_Lewis" title="Leona Lewis">Leona Lewis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corinne_Bailey_Rae" title="Corinne Bailey Rae">Corinne Bailey Rae</a>.
</p><p>Remi has also done film work. He worked on the soundtracks for <i><a href="/wiki/Office_Space" title="Office Space">Office Space</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Zoolander" title="Zoolander">Zoolander</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Departed" title="The Departed">The Departed</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Blood_Diamond_(film)" class="mw-redirect" title="Blood Diamond (film)">Blood Diamond</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Sex_and_the_City_(film)" title="Sex and the City (film)">Sex and the City</a></i>.  He also scored the <a href="/wiki/Mike_Tyson" title="Mike Tyson">Mike Tyson</a> documentary, <i>TYSON</i>, worked as executive music producer on <i><a href="/wiki/After_the_Sunset" title="After the Sunset">After the Sunset</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Rush_Hour_3" title="Rush Hour 3">Rush Hour 3</a></i>, and was music supervisor and composer for a 2008 TV pilot called <i>Blue Blood</i>. Salaam Remi was nominated for Non-Classical Producer of the Year at the <a href="/wiki/55th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="55th Grammy Awards">55th Grammy Awards</a>, an award won by <a href="/wiki/Dan_Auerbach" title="Dan Auerbach">Dan Auerbach</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Louder_Than_Life">Louder Than Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salaam_Remi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Louder Than Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, Remi started his own label imprint, Louder Than Life, as a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music" title="Sony Music">Sony Music</a>, with two subimprints, Re Mi Fa Music and Flying Buddha Records. <i>New York: A Love Story</i> by <a href="/wiki/Mack_Wilds" class="mw-redirect" title="Mack Wilds">Mack Wilds</a> was the first release under Remi's label.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Since 2013, Remi has released multiple projects under Louder Than Life including BoxTalk with <a href="/wiki/Joell_Ortiz" title="Joell Ortiz">Joell Ortiz</a>, Northside of Linden, Westside of Slauson with <a href="/wiki/Terrace_Martin" title="Terrace Martin">Terrace Martin</a>, and Black On Purpose featuring performances by <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Hudson" title="Jennifer Hudson">Jennifer Hudson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_Thought" title="Black Thought">Black Thought</a>, <a href="/wiki/CeeLo_Green" title="CeeLo Green">CeeLo Green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mumu_Fresh" class="mw-redirect" title="Mumu Fresh">Mumu Fresh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doug_E._Fresh" title="Doug E. Fresh">Doug E. Fresh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bilal_(American_singer)" title="Bilal (American singer)">Bilal (American singer)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Teedra_Moses" title="Teedra Moses">Teedra Moses</a>, <a href="/wiki/D-Nice" title="D-Nice">D-Nice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mack_Wilds" class="mw-redirect" title="Mack Wilds">Mack Wilds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Common_(rapper)" title="Common (rapper)">Common (rapper)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Case_(singer)" title="Case (singer)">Case (singer)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Betty_Wright" title="Betty Wright">Betty Wright</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Poyser" title="James Poyser">James Poyser</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Marley_(musician)" title="Stephen Marley (musician)">Stephen Marley (musician)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Hamilton_(musician)" title="Anthony Hamilton (musician)">Anthony Hamilton (musician)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syleena_Johnson" title="Syleena Johnson">Syleena Johnson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Super_Cat" title="Super Cat">Super Cat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spragga_Benz" title="Spragga Benz">Spragga Benz</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chronixx" title="Chronixx">Chronixx</a>.
</p>
