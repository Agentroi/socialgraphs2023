<p><b>John David Jackson</b> (born November 18, 1977), better known by his stage name <b>Fabolous</b>, is an American rapper. Raised in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, he first gained recognition while still a senior in high school, when he performed live on American music executive <a href="/wiki/DJ_Clue" title="DJ Clue">DJ Clue</a>'s radio show, then on <a href="/wiki/WQHT" title="WQHT">Hot 97</a>. Jackson subsequently signed to DJ Clue's <a href="/wiki/Desert_Storm_Records" title="Desert Storm Records">Desert Storm</a> imprint, and later secured a distribution deal with major-label, <a href="/wiki/Elektra_Records" title="Elektra Records">Elektra</a>. Jackson gained prominence with his first release, <i><a href="/wiki/Ghetto_Fabolous" title="Ghetto Fabolous">Ghetto Fabolous</a></i> (2001), which spawned the <a href="/wiki/Hit_single" class="mw-redirect" title="Hit single">hit singles</a> "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Deny_It" title="Can&#39;t Deny It">Can't Deny It</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a>) and "<a href="/wiki/Young%27n_(Holla_Back)" title="Young&#39;n (Holla Back)">Young'n (Holla Back)</a>". His second release was 2003's <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Dreams_(Fabolous_album)" title="Street Dreams (Fabolous album)">Street Dreams</a></i>, which was supported by two US <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">top 10</a> singles, "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Let_You_Go" title="Can&#39;t Let You Go">Can't Let You Go</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Mo" title="Lil&#39; Mo">Lil' Mo</a>) and "<a href="/wiki/Into_You_(Fabolous_song)" title="Into You (Fabolous song)">Into You</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Tamia" title="Tamia">Tamia</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ashanti_(singer)" title="Ashanti (singer)">Ashanti</a>).
</p><p>Including the aforementioned songs, Jackson's series of hit singles extended throughout the 2000s, including "<a href="/wiki/Street_Dreams_(Fabolous_album)" title="Street Dreams (Fabolous album)">Trade It All, Pt. 2</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jagged_Edge_(American_group)" title="Jagged Edge (American group)">Jagged Edge</a>), "<a href="/wiki/Breathe_(Fabolous_song)" title="Breathe (Fabolous song)">Breathe</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Make_Me_Better" title="Make Me Better">Make Me Better</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Ne-Yo" title="Ne-Yo">Ne-Yo</a>), "<a href="/wiki/Baby_Don%27t_Go_(Fabolous_song)" title="Baby Don&#39;t Go (Fabolous song)">Baby Don't Go</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a>), "<a href="/wiki/Throw_It_in_the_Bag" title="Throw It in the Bag">Throw It in the Bag</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/The-Dream" title="The-Dream">The-Dream</a>) and "<a href="/wiki/You_Be_Killin%27_Em" class="mw-redirect" title="You Be Killin&#39; Em">You Be Killin' Em</a>". He is also known for his appearances on several <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> singles, including "<a href="/wiki/Superwoman_Pt._II" title="Superwoman Pt. II">Superwoman Pt. II</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Mo" title="Lil&#39; Mo">Lil' Mo</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Shawty_Is_a_10" title="Shawty Is a 10">Shawty Is a 10</a>" by The-Dream, "<a href="/wiki/Addiction_(Ryan_Leslie_song)" title="Addiction (Ryan Leslie song)">Addiction</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Leslie" title="Ryan Leslie">Ryan Leslie</a>, "<a href="/wiki/She_Got_Her_Own" class="mw-redirect" title="She Got Her Own">She Got Her Own</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Foxx" title="Jamie Foxx">Jamie Foxx</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/Say_Aah" title="Say Aah">Say Aah</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Trey_Songz" title="Trey Songz">Trey Songz</a>, among others.
</p><p>In 2004, Jackson signed to <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a> (another subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Warner_Music_Group" title="Warner Music Group">Warner Music Group</a>), after leaving Elektra, where he released <i><a href="/wiki/Real_Talk_(Fabolous_album)" title="Real Talk (Fabolous album)">Real Talk</a></i>, his first and only album under Atlantic. In 2006, Jackson was let out of his contract with Atlantic and officially signed with <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> (owned by Warner competitor <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group</a>). Also in 2006, Jackson founded his own record label, Street Family Records. In 2007, he released <i><a href="/wiki/From_Nothin%27_to_Somethin%27" title="From Nothin&#39; to Somethin&#39;">From Nothin' to Somethin'</a></i>, under Def Jam. In 2009, he released his fifth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Loso%27s_Way" title="Loso&#39;s Way">Loso's Way</a></i>. Throughout the years, Jackson has amassed a number of self-released <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a>, including several installments of his highly acclaimed <i>There Is No Competition</i> series and <i>The S.O.U.L. Tape</i> series. He released his sixth album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_OG_Project" title="The Young OG Project">The Young OG Project</a></i>, in 2014.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabolous&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1977.E2.80.932000:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1977–2000:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings">1977–2000: Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabolous&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1977–2000: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fabolous was born John David Jackson on November 18, 1977, and is of <a href="/wiki/Dominican_people_(Dominican_Republic)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dominican people (Dominican Republic)">Dominican</a> and African-American descent.<sup id="cite_ref-Allmusic_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-galaxy_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galaxy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Breevort_Houses" class="mw-redirect" title="Breevort Houses">Breevort Houses</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bedford-Stuyvesant,_Brooklyn" class="mw-redirect" title="Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn">Bedford-Stuyvesant</a> neighborhood of the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> borough of New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jackson attended <a href="/wiki/High_School_of_Art_%26_Design" class="mw-redirect" title="High School of Art &amp; Design">High School of Art &amp; Design</a> and <a href="/wiki/City-As-School_High_School" title="City-As-School High School">City-As-School High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, and eventually <a href="/wiki/Boys_and_Girls_High_School" title="Boys and Girls High School">Boys and Girls High School</a> in Bed-Stuy.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> While in his senior year of high school, Jackson began to pursue a career in <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop music</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fabolous_life_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fabolous_life-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In the early years of his career, he rapped under the name <b>Fabolous Sport</b>, in reference to <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Lauren" title="Ralph Lauren">Ralph Lauren</a>'s Polo Sport line,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> however this was later shortened to simply <b>Fabolous</b>. The misspelling was unintentional; Jackson originally intended to be simply called "Fabulous" but incorrectly spelled it during a freestyle and it stuck.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He was invited to <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rap</a> live on American record producer and music executive <a href="/wiki/DJ_Clue" title="DJ Clue">DJ Clue</a>'s radio show, then on New York City radio station <a href="/wiki/WQHT" title="WQHT">Hot 97</a>. Fabolous and <a href="/wiki/N.O.R.E." title="N.O.R.E.">N.O.R.E.</a> rapped over the instrumental to <a href="/wiki/The_Lox" title="The Lox">The Lox</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Money,_Power_%26_Respect_(song)" title="Money, Power &amp; Respect (song)">Money, Power &amp; Respect</a></i>, and DJ Clue subsequently signed Fabolous to his record label, <a href="/wiki/Desert_Storm_Records" title="Desert Storm Records">Desert Storm Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fabolous_life_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fabolous_life-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Fabolous was then featured on several <a href="/wiki/DJ_Clue" title="DJ Clue">DJ Clue</a> <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a>, as well as mixtapes with Roc-A-Fella artists.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> This earned Fabolous and Desert Storm a distribution deal with <a href="/wiki/Elektra_Records" title="Elektra Records">Elektra Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-galaxy_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galaxy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In a September 6, 2001, interview with <i>Hot104.com</i>, Fabolous said he never planned on becoming a rapper and told the website: "I was just trying to make some money, ya know? I got tired of being broke. This was something where I could make some money. It just happened for me."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2001.E2.80.932003:_Ghetto_Fabolous_and_Street_Dreams"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2001–2003:_Ghetto_Fabolous_and_Street_Dreams">2001–2003: <i>Ghetto Fabolous</i> and <i>Street Dreams</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabolous&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2001–2003: Ghetto Fabolous and Street Dreams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fabolous released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Ghetto_Fabolous" title="Ghetto Fabolous">Ghetto Fabolous</a></i>, on September 11, 2001.  It debuted at number four on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, selling over 140,000 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The album's first single, "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Deny_It" title="Can&#39;t Deny It">Can't Deny It</a>",  was produced by <a href="/wiki/Rick_Rock" title="Rick Rock">Rick Rock</a> and features a chorus by <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a> interpolating <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>'s song "Ambitionz Az a Ridah".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It charted on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart, along with two of the subsequent singles.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard_singles_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard_singles-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The other charting singles were "Young'n (Holla Back)", which was produced by <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">The Neptunes</a> and "Trade It All", which features vocals from <a href="/wiki/Jagged_Edge_(American_group)" title="Jagged Edge (American group)">Jagged Edge</a> and was produced by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Clue" title="DJ Clue">DJ Clue</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ken_Duro_Ifill" title="Ken Duro Ifill">Duro</a>.
</p><p>Fabolous released his second album <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Dreams_(Fabolous_album)" title="Street Dreams (Fabolous album)">Street Dreams</a></i> on March 4, 2003. Powered by a <a href="/wiki/Just_Blaze" title="Just Blaze">Just Blaze</a> beat and guest vocals from <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Mo" title="Lil&#39; Mo">Lil' Mo</a> and Mike Shorey, "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Let_You_Go" title="Can&#39;t Let You Go">Can't Let You Go</a>" reached number one on the Rhythmic Top 40 chart and number four on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 chart. "<a href="/wiki/Into_You_(Fabolous_song)" title="Into You (Fabolous song)">Into You</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Tamia" title="Tamia">Tamia</a> also reached number four on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. Also released on Street Dreams was the lead single club banger "This Is My Party" and "Trade It All Pt. 2" which featured <a href="/wiki/Jagged_Edge_(American_group)" title="Jagged Edge (American group)">Jagged Edge</a> as it did on the <a href="/wiki/Ghetto_Fabolous" title="Ghetto Fabolous">Ghetto Fabolous</a> version, as well as <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a>.
</p><p>Exactly seven months later, on November 4, 2003, Fabolous dropped his official mixtape, <a href="/wiki/More_Street_Dreams,_Pt._2:_The_Mixtape" title="More Street Dreams, Pt. 2: The Mixtape">More Street Dreams, Pt. 2: The Mixtape</a>. It was an official release by his record label, <a href="/wiki/Elektra_Records" title="Elektra Records">Elektra</a>. The album featured remixes and tracks not originally on <i>Street Dreams</i>. This album was also an outlet for his three-man crew, known as the Triangle Offense, consisting of himself, Paul Cain, and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a>. The album actually features a remix to song <i>Fire</i>, which was originally on Joe Budden's <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden_(album)" title="Joe Budden (album)">self-titled debut album</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2004.E2.80.932008:_Real_Talk_and_From_Nothin.27_to_Somethin.27"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2004–2008:_Real_Talk_and_From_Nothin'_to_Somethin'">2004–2008: <i>Real Talk</i> and <i>From Nothin' to Somethin'</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabolous&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2004–2008: Real Talk and From Nothin&#039; to Somethin&#039;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Fabolous_by_Frank_DeMaria.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Fabolous_by_Frank_DeMaria.jpg/240px-Fabolous_by_Frank_DeMaria.jpg" decoding="async" width="240" height="139" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Fabolous_by_Frank_DeMaria.jpg/360px-Fabolous_by_Frank_DeMaria.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Fabolous_by_Frank_DeMaria.jpg/480px-Fabolous_by_Frank_DeMaria.jpg 2x" data-file-width="748" data-file-height="433" /></a><figcaption>Fabolous at <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Satellite_Radio" title="Sirius Satellite Radio">Sirius Satellite Radio</a> in June 2007</figcaption></figure>
<p>Fabolous' third album <i><a href="/wiki/Real_Talk_(Fabolous_album)" title="Real Talk (Fabolous album)">Real Talk</a></i> was released on November 5, 2004. It debuted at number six on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 with 179,000 copies and had two charting singles, the lowest in his career. The two charting singles are his street anthem "<a href="/wiki/Breathe_(Fabolous_song)" title="Breathe (Fabolous song)">Breathe</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Baby_(Fabolous_song)" title="Baby (Fabolous song)">Baby</a>," which features Mike Shorey, and shows his more sensitive side that he has shown on many songs in the past. His second single was not promoted until weeks after the album's release. "Tit 4 Tat" was his third single. <a href="/wiki/Pharrell" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharrell">Pharrell</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">The Neptunes</a> produced it and did the hook. Fab feels that the single didn't hit it as big as it should have due to poor advertising. Making the music video for his fourth single, "Do the Damn Thing" cost Jackson $30,000. The song featured <a href="/wiki/Young_Jeezy" class="mw-redirect" title="Young Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a>, who became known to the public through the video.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The same year, Fabolous was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for his collaboration on the "<a href="/wiki/Dip_It_Low" title="Dip It Low">Dip It Low</a>" remix by <a href="/wiki/Christina_Milian" title="Christina Milian">Christina Milian</a>. Fabolous stated in 2004 that he wanted to release his own clothing line. The line called "Rich Yung Society" was launched in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-clothingline_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-clothingline-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2006, Fabolous was let out of his contract with Atlantic and officially signed a recording contract with <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>, after a <i>de facto</i> trade that sent Def Jam artist <a href="/wiki/Musiq" class="mw-redirect" title="Musiq">Musiq</a>, to Atlantic.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/From_Nothin%27_to_Somethin" class="mw-redirect" title="From Nothin&#39; to Somethin">From Nothin' to Somethin</a></i>, was released in June 2007. Fabolous took the number one spot on <i>Billboard'</i>s Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums and Top Rap Albums charts for the first time in his career and it debuted at number two on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, selling 159,000 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The album was certified Gold in July 2007. It is his first album on <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>. He was featured on the cover of video game <i><a href="/wiki/Def_Jam:_Icon" title="Def Jam: Icon">Def Jam: Icon</a></i>.
</p><p>The first single and video, "<a href="/wiki/Diamonds_(Fabolous_song)" title="Diamonds (Fabolous song)">Diamonds</a>", features <a href="/wiki/Young_Jeezy" class="mw-redirect" title="Young Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a> who also appeared on the <i>Real Talk</i> track "Do the Damn Thing". <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Remy_Ma" title="Remy Ma">Remy Ma</a> are featured on the remix. His second single was "Return of the Hustle" which featured <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>, also came out before the album release, to some acclaim, but little airplay. His third single though, "<a href="/wiki/Make_Me_Better" title="Make Me Better">Make Me Better</a>," which features fellow Def Jam artist <a href="/wiki/Ne-Yo" title="Ne-Yo">Ne-Yo</a>, and is produced by <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a>, was his biggest hit to date, spending 14 weeks at number one on the Hot Rap Track Billboard Chart. The fourth single was "<a href="/wiki/Baby_Don%27t_Go_(Fabolous_song)" title="Baby Don&#39;t Go (Fabolous song)">Baby Don't Go</a>." <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a> produced it and <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a> sings the hook. However, in music video version of the song, Jermaine Dupri sings the hook. This song also found success, reaching number four on the Hot Rap Track Chart.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932010:_Loso.27s_Way"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2010:_Loso's_Way">2009–2010: <i>Loso's Way</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabolous&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2009–2010: Loso&#039;s Way">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fabolous released his fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Loso%27s_Way" title="Loso&#39;s Way">Loso's Way</a></i>, in July 2009. The album is based on the 1993 film <i><a href="/wiki/Carlito%27s_Way" title="Carlito&#39;s Way">Carlito's Way</a></i>, similar to <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/American_Gangster_(album)" title="American Gangster (album)">American Gangster</a></i>. The first official single is "<a href="/wiki/Throw_It_in_the_Bag" title="Throw It in the Bag">Throw It in the Bag</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/The-Dream" title="The-Dream">The-Dream</a>, and is produced by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Stewart_(music_producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Christopher Stewart (music producer)">Christopher "Tricky" Stewart</a>. The second single is "<a href="/wiki/My_Time_(Fabolous_song)" title="My Time (Fabolous song)">My Time</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/Jeremih" title="Jeremih">Jeremih</a>, and is produced by <a href="/wiki/The_Runners_(production_duo)" title="The Runners (production duo)">The Runners</a> and Kevin "KC" Cossom. The third single is "Everything, Everyday, Everywhere", featuring <a href="/wiki/Keri_Hilson" title="Keri Hilson">Keri Hilson</a>, and is produced by <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Leslie" title="Ryan Leslie">Ryan Leslie</a>. The album debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, selling 99,000 copies in its first week. It became Fabolous' first album to top the chart.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In its second week of sales, the album dropped 8 spots from #1 to #9, selling 37,000 copies. A deluxe album was released on the same day as the standard release. The edition features a bonus DVD, ("Loso's Way" the movie). The movie features Fabolous (and three other of his friends). Fabolous is leaving a restaurant when he is shot. His three friends quickly rush Fabolous to the hospital, but are stopped by the police after running a red light. Fellow rapper Styles P has a role in the movie. DJ Clue, DJ Khaled, Jadakiss, Swizz Beatz, DJ Envy, and Ryan Leslie make cameo appearances. The movie "Loso's Way" runs for 33:03 minutes. In order to view "Loso's Way", the deluxe album "Loso's Way" must be purchased.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In September 2009, Fabolous was ranked number eight on MTV's <a href="/wiki/MTV%27s_Annual_Hottest_MC_List" class="mw-redirect" title="MTV&#39;s Annual Hottest MC List">Hottest MC in the Game</a> list.<sup id="cite_ref-MC_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MC-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In an interview on December 10, 2009, with MTV, he announced that he would be releasing part two to his mixtape series with DJ Drama entitled: "There is No Competition – Part 2: The Funeral Service." Fabolous announced that it would be released on Christmas Day as a gift to his fans. He then pushed back the release date multiple times via Twitter before he and DJ Drama finally released the mixtape online on March 4, 2010.
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932014:_There_Is_No_Competition_series_and_The_Soul_Tape_series"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2014:_There_Is_No_Competition_series_and_The_Soul_Tape_series">2010–2014: <i>There Is No Competition</i> series and <i>The Soul Tape</i> series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabolous&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010–2014: There Is No Competition series and The Soul Tape series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/There_Is_No_Competition_2:_The_Grieving_Music_EP" title="There Is No Competition 2: The Grieving Music EP">There Is No Competition 2: The Grieving Music EP</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_S.O.U.L._Tape" class="mw-redirect" title="The S.O.U.L. Tape">The S.O.U.L. Tape</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_S.O.U.L._Tape_2" title="The S.O.U.L. Tape 2">The S.O.U.L. Tape 2</a></div>
<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Fabolous_UAlbany_Parkfest_2012.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Fabolous_UAlbany_Parkfest_2012.jpg/220px-Fabolous_UAlbany_Parkfest_2012.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="220" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Fabolous_UAlbany_Parkfest_2012.jpg/330px-Fabolous_UAlbany_Parkfest_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Fabolous_UAlbany_Parkfest_2012.jpg/440px-Fabolous_UAlbany_Parkfest_2012.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2227" data-file-height="2227" /></a><figcaption>Fabolous performing in 2012</figcaption></figure>
<p>On March 5, 2010, Fabolous released <i>There Is No Competition 2: The Funeral Service</i>, his fourth solo <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> and the sequel to 2008's <i>There Is No Competition</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-nocompetition2_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nocompetition2-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Due to the popularity of the mixtape, Fabolous re-released it for retail in the form of an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP) titled <i><a href="/wiki/There_Is_No_Competition_2:_The_Grieving_Music_EP" title="There Is No Competition 2: The Grieving Music EP">There Is No Competition 2: The Grieving Music EP</a></i>. The EP, which was preceded by the <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Leslie" title="Ryan Leslie">Ryan Leslie</a>-produced single "<a href="/wiki/You_Be_Killin_Em" title="You Be Killin Em">You Be Killin Em</a>", was released on August 29, 2010. The single peaked at number 68 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.
</p><p>After it was announced that his sixth album would once again be delayed, Fabolous chose to release a new project titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_S.O.U.L._Tape" class="mw-redirect" title="The S.O.U.L. Tape">The S.O.U.L. Tape</a></i>. The mixtape was released on April 22, 2011, and features Fabolous rapping over <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>-<a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">sampling</a> <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">production</a>, inspired by a <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a> he had done over the instrumental to <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Devil_in_a_New_Dress" title="Devil in a New Dress">Devil in a New Dress</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-www.hiphopdx.com_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-www.hiphopdx.com-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On Christmas Day 2011, Fabolous released <i><a href="/wiki/There_Is_No_Competition_3:_Death_Comes_in_3%27s" title="There Is No Competition 3: Death Comes in 3&#39;s">There Is No Competition 3: Death Comes in 3's</a></i>, the third installment in his <i>There Is No Competition</i> mixtape series.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On November 22, 2012, Fabolous released his seventh mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/The_S.O.U.L._Tape_2" title="The S.O.U.L. Tape 2">The S.O.U.L. Tape 2</a></i>. <i>The S.O.U.L. Tape 2</i> featured guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Trey_Songz" title="Trey Songz">Trey Songz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cassie_(singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cassie (singer)">Cassie</a>, <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-www.vibe.com_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-www.vibe.com-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape's production was handled by Streetrunner, Treddy da Don, <a href="/wiki/AraabMuzik" title="AraabMuzik">AraabMuzik</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cardiak" title="Cardiak">Cardiak</a>, among others, and follows the theme of the first album by utilising heavy usage of soul samples throughout.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late 2012, Fabolous announced his sixth album, then-titled <i>Loso's Way 2: Rise to Power</i>, would be released in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>  The first official single from <i>Loso's Way 2</i>, "<a href="/wiki/Ready_(Fabolous_song)" title="Ready (Fabolous song)">Ready</a>" featuring singer <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown_(American_singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chris Brown (American singer)">Chris Brown</a> was released on January 17, 2013. The second single "<a href="/wiki/When_I_Feel_Like_It" title="When I Feel Like It">When I Feel Like It</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a> was released on July 9, 2013. In an interview Fabolous revealed the list of features included <a href="/wiki/Trey_Songz" title="Trey Songz">Trey Songz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ne-Yo" title="Ne-Yo">Ne-Yo</a>, <a href="/wiki/FYUTCH" class="mw-redirect" title="FYUTCH">FYUTCH</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Young_Jeezy" class="mw-redirect" title="Young Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a> and stated that the album was about him "evolving and growing as a person, as a man, as an artist, as a father, as a friend."<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> <i>Loso's Way 2</i> was scheduled to be released in 2013 by Desert Storm and Def Jam, however, the album was delayed all year long and subsequently rescheduled for release in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> On July 30, 2014, Fabolous announced that the lead single from the album would be released by "the end of summer" and would again feature <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>. He stated that "Ready" was just a "warmup record" for this song and that it would be very melodic.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.93present:_The_Young_OG_Project_series.2C_Summertime_Shootout_series.2C_and_collab_album_with_Jadakiss"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–present:_The_Young_OG_Project_series,_Summertime_Shootout_series,_and_collab_album_with_Jadakiss">2014–present: <i>The Young OG Project</i> series, <i>Summertime Shootout</i> series, and collab album with Jadakiss</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabolous&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2014–present: The Young OG Project series, Summertime Shootout series, and collab album with Jadakiss">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 22, 2014, Fabolous announced that his much delayed sixth studio album had been renamed to <i>Young OG</i>. This was later changed to <i>The Young OG Project</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On December 1, 2014, Fabolous announced the release date for <i>The Young OG Project</i> to be December 25, 2014, with the announcement posted on his new <a href="/wiki/Keek" title="Keek">Keek</a> page.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Speaking to MTV, Fabolous said the album would be  "very '90s-inspired and '90s themed.".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> On the same day of the album release announcement, <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a> announced Fabolous had signed a management deal.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_OG_Project" title="The Young OG Project">The Young OG Project</a></i> was released on Christmas Day 2014 and debuted at number 12 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, with first-week sales of 71,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2015, Fabolous confirmed that he would be releasing two new projects by the end of the year, announcing one of them would follow the trend set by <i><a href="/wiki/There_Is_No_Competition_2:_The_Grieving_Music_EP" title="There Is No Competition 2: The Grieving Music EP">There Is No Competition 2</a></i> and <i>The Young OG Project</i> by releasing on Christmas Day.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On November 26, Fabolous released the free mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Summertime_Shootout" class="mw-redirect" title="Summertime Shootout">Summertime Shootout</a></i>, and announced the second installment of <i>The Young OG Project</i> for December 25,<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> however, it has since been delayed indefinitely.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On February 29, 2016, Fabolous and <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a> announced that work had begun on their first collaborative project, a mixtape pre-emptively titled <i><a href="/wiki/Freddy_vs._Jason_(album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Freddy vs. Jason (album)">Freddy vs. Jason</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On April 2, 2016, they released a freestyle to <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Wicked_(Future_song)" title="Wicked (Future song)">Wicked</a>" that they confirmed would appear on the project, which they also announced would not be a mixtape, but a full album.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> On April 29, 2016, Fabolous worked with <a href="/wiki/K-Pop" class="mw-redirect" title="K-Pop">K-pop artist</a> <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Jung" title="Jessica Jung">Jessica Jung</a> as a featured artist and co-writer on a track titled "Fly", the lead single for her first mini album, <a href="/wiki/With_Love,_J" title="With Love, J">With Love, J</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> On September 3, 2016, Fabolous released the second installment of the Summertime Shootout mixtape series, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Summertime_Shootout_2:_The_Level_Up" class="mw-redirect" title="Summertime Shootout 2: The Level Up">Summertime Shootout 2: The Level Up</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> On October 31, 2017, the first single off the album "Stand Up" featuring <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> On November 21, 2017, it was announced that the album title was changed to <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_on_Elm_Street" title="Friday on Elm Street">Friday on Elm Street</a></i>. The album was released on November 24, 2017, and had features from <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>, and <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>.
</p><p>On July 8, 2018, Fabolous released a single called "Ooh Yeah" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 14, 2019, released a single called "Choosy" featuring <a href="/wiki/Jeremih" title="Jeremih">Jeremih</a> and <a href="/wiki/Davido" title="Davido">Davido</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 19, 2019, Fabolous released a 30-second trailer on his <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">instagram</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Summertime_Shootout_3:_Coldest_Summer_Ever" title="Summertime Shootout 3: Coldest Summer Ever">Summertime Shootout 3: Coldest Summer Ever</a></i> along with its release date, confirming that it would be released November 29, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> A week later, the tracklist and the cover art were released for the album and it was also confirmed to be the final installment of the Summertime Shootout series.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The album debuted at number 7 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> with first week sales of 44,000 album-equivalent units, making it Fabolous's seventh top-10 album.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabolous&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fabolous and his girlfriend Emily Bustamante have two sons &amp; one daughter, born in 2008, 2015 &amp; 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabolous&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January and March 2003, Fabolous was arrested for possessing an unlicensed gun in his car. His bodyguard later showed proof of ownership for the gun.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the morning of October 17, 2006, Fabolous was shot once near his leg after exiting Justin's, a restaurant owned by <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Sean "Diddy" Combs</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fabolous_life_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fabolous_life-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> After the shooting, he and his entourage were charged with criminal possession of a weapon and criminal possession of a defaced firearm after being pulled over for running a red light, in which police discovered two unlicensed guns.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Later on, Fabolous was treated at a local hospital and was released eight days later.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 29, 2018, Fabolous was arrested for <a href="/wiki/Domestic_violence" title="Domestic violence">domestic violence</a> after allegedly assaulting his girlfriend Emily Bustamante.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> On October 10, 2018, he was indicted by a grand jury in New Jersey on four felony charges of domestic assault.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> He reportedly accepted a <a href="/wiki/Plea_deal" class="mw-redirect" title="Plea deal">plea deal</a> in March 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
