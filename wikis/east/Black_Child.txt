<p><b>Ramel Leroy Gill</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born October 18, 1973), better known by his stage name <b>Black Child</b>, is an American rapper from New York City. He is best known for his time being signed to <a href="/wiki/Irv_Gotti" title="Irv Gotti">Irv Gotti</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Murder_Inc._Records" title="Murder Inc. Records">Murder Inc. Records</a> and being affiliated with Irv Gotti, <a href="/wiki/Ashanti_(singer)" title="Ashanti (singer)">Ashanti</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>. He appeared on hit singles with Ja Rule and did various songs with other members of Murder Inc.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Child&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gill was born in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York.<sup id="cite_ref-http&#58;//www.murderinc-online.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http://www.murderinc-online.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His family moved to <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a> when he was young.<sup id="cite_ref-http&#58;//www.murderinc-online.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http://www.murderinc-online.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> During his childhood years, one of family friends wrote verses for him at the age of 11.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> While Gill was spitting rhymes in his neighborhood, he began to write some of his own rhymes to use that motivation from his family and friends.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Learning his ways of life, he had a few run-ins with the law, doing time in jail for a <a href="/wiki/Criminal_possession_of_a_weapon" title="Criminal possession of a weapon">gun case</a> that lasted only 30 months, while after being signed by <a href="/wiki/Jam_Master_Jay_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Jam Master Jay Records">Jam Master Jay Records</a> (now defunct after Jay's death in 2002) in 1993, which was a label owned by the late <a href="/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJ</a> <a href="/wiki/Jam_Master_Jay" title="Jam Master Jay">Jam Master Jay</a>. Before that, he was already making songs with a few other aspiring upcoming artists who were trying to make a name for themselves, including <a href="/wiki/Irv_Gotti" title="Irv Gotti">Irv Gotti</a> who was under the moniker <b>DJ Irv</b> (whose Gill was around at that time) produced "Can I Live" for <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> off the <i><a href="/wiki/Reasonable_Doubt_(album)" title="Reasonable Doubt (album)">Reasonable Doubt</a></i> album and <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a> with "Cash Money Click" featuring Nemesis and Chris Black.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> While being incarcerated, he began to write rhymes, joining freestyle battles and showing his unique talent to infiltrate the rap world.<sup id="cite_ref-http&#58;//www.murderinc-online.com_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http://www.murderinc-online.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> After spending a few years in jail, Gill reached out to <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>, showing him his lyrical ability to prove himself as a rapper which <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a> liked and accepted him to sign with <a href="/wiki/Murder_Inc_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Murder Inc Records">Murder Inc</a> after being released from <a href="/wiki/Jam_Master_Jay_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Jam Master Jay Records">Jam Master Jay Records</a> due to a gun charge.<sup id="cite_ref-http&#58;//www.murderinc-online.com_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http://www.murderinc-online.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Feud_with_50_Cent">Feud with 50 Cent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Child&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Feud with 50 Cent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Black Child has also been involved in the Murder Inc. and G-Unit feud, where he, Ja Rule, and other Murder Inc. associates had an altercation with <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Yayo" title="Tony Yayo">Tony Yayo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a> associates at <a href="/wiki/The_Hit_Factory" title="The Hit Factory">The Hit Factory</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, March 2000. Black Child admitted that he stabbed <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, saying it was in self defense and 50 was the one reaching for a gun.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He released a <a href="/wiki/Diss_(music)" title="Diss (music)">diss</a> song called "The Real Wanksta" which attacked <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> labeling him as an <a href="/wiki/Informant" title="Informant">informant</a>, which Black Child claims that 50 Cent had filed an order of protection on Murder Inc.
</p>
