<p><b>Kenyatta Blake</b> (born November 19, 1974), known professionally as <b>Buckshot</b>, is an American rapper from New York, best known as a frontman of hip hop groups <a href="/wiki/Black_Moon_(group)" title="Black Moon (group)">Black Moon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boot_Camp_Clik" title="Boot Camp Clik">Boot Camp Clik</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fleury_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fleury-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HHG_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is a founder of <a href="/wiki/Duck_Down_Music" title="Duck Down Music">Duck Down Music</a> record label with <a href="/wiki/Drew_%22Dru-Ha%22_Friedman" title="Drew &quot;Dru-Ha&quot; Friedman">Drew "Dru-Ha" Friedman</a>. He has released one solo studio album – three with producer <a href="/wiki/9th_Wonder" title="9th Wonder">9th Wonder</a>, one with <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>, four albums with Black Moon and four albums with the Boot Camp Clik.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckshot_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York City, Buckshot Shorty began rapping in neighborhood rhyming ciphers. In high school, he befriended Karim "<a href="/wiki/5ft" class="mw-redirect" title="5ft">5ft</a>" Reid, Ewart "DJ Evil Dee" Dewgarde and Walter "Mr. Walt" Dewgarde, who would evolve into the super production team <a href="/wiki/Da_Beatminerz" title="Da Beatminerz">Da Beatminerz</a>.
</p><p>In 1992, Blake, Reid and Ewart Dewgarde formed <a href="/wiki/Black_Moon_(group)" title="Black Moon (group)">Black Moon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The trio released the single "Who Got Da Props?" in late 1992, which reached number 86 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart, landing them a recording deal with <a href="/wiki/Nervous_Records" title="Nervous Records">Nervous Records</a>. Later that year, Black Moon released their first full-length album, <i><a href="/wiki/Enta_da_Stage" title="Enta da Stage">Enta da Stage</a></i> to positive critical acclaim. The album featured appearances by <a href="/wiki/Havoc_(musician)" title="Havoc (musician)">Havoc</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>, as well as future Boot Camp Clik members <a href="/wiki/Tek_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tek (rapper)">Tek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steele_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Steele (rapper)">Steele</a> of <a href="/wiki/Smif-N-Wessun" title="Smif-N-Wessun">Smif N Wessun</a>.
</p><p>In 1994, with <a href="/wiki/Drew_%22Dru-Ha%22_Friedman" title="Drew &quot;Dru-Ha&quot; Friedman">Drew "Dru-Ha" Friedman</a>, the two left Nervous Records and formed <a href="/wiki/Duck_Down_Music" title="Duck Down Music">Duck Down Management</a>. That same year, Blake signed and oversaw the creation of Smif-N-Wessun's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Dah_Shinin%27" title="Dah Shinin&#39;">Dah Shinin'</a></i>, as well as adding <a href="/wiki/Heltah_Skeltah" title="Heltah Skeltah">Heltah Skeltah</a> (<a href="/wiki/Rock_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rock (rapper)">Rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sean_Price" title="Sean Price">Ruck</a>) and <a href="/wiki/O.G.C._(band)" title="O.G.C. (band)">Originoo Gunn Clappaz</a> (<a href="/wiki/Starang_Wondah" class="mw-redirect" title="Starang Wondah">Starang Wondah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Top_Dog_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Dog (rapper)">Top Dog</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louieville_Sluggah" class="mw-redirect" title="Louieville Sluggah">Louieville Sluggah</a>) to Duck Down's roster. During the summer, Blake took part in the collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Masta_Ace" title="Masta Ace">Masta Ace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Special_Ed_(rapper)" title="Special Ed (rapper)">Special Ed</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Crooklyn_Dodgers" title="Crooklyn Dodgers">Crooklyn Dodgers</a>, releasing a single by the same name, on the <a href="/wiki/Crooklyn_(soundtrack)" title="Crooklyn (soundtrack)">soundtrack</a> of <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a>'s 1994 film <i><a href="/wiki/Crooklyn" title="Crooklyn">Crooklyn</a></i>.
</p><p>Officially titled Duck Down Entaprizez in 1996, Blake, Friedman and Smif-N-Wessun began work with <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a> on the unreleased studio album <i>One Nation</i>. During this time Blake was dubbed as "The BDI Thug" by 2Pac.
</p><p>March 1997 marked a milestone in Duck Down history. The collective of:Buckshot, Smif-N-Wessun,, Heltah Skeltah and O.G.C. formed a <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> the <a href="/wiki/Boot_Camp_Clik" title="Boot Camp Clik">Boot Camp Clik</a>, who released their debut album titled <i><a href="/wiki/For_the_People_(Boot_Camp_Clik_album)" title="For the People (Boot Camp Clik album)">For the People</a></i>. A remix of "Night Riders" was recorded featuring <a href="/wiki/Aaliyah" title="Aaliyah">Aaliyah</a>, but the single was never released, due to conflicts between record labels.
</p><p>In 1999 Black Moon released their second album <i><a href="/wiki/War_Zone_(album)" title="War Zone (album)">War Zone</a></i>. It was their first full-length album in more than 5 years. Featuring guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Q-Tip_(musician)" title="Q-Tip (musician)">Q-Tip</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/M.O.P." title="M.O.P.">M.O.P.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heather_B._Gardner" title="Heather B. Gardner">Heather B.</a> and produced by Da Beatminerz, the album sold over 200,000 units and was met with strong reviews. Blake's solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_BDI_Thug" title="The BDI Thug">The BDI Thug</a></i>, was released later that year but to mediocre reviews.
</p><p>In July 2002, Duck Down ended its two-year search for a new distribution deal, inking a three-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_One" title="Entertainment One">Koch Distribution</a>. Boot Camp Clik released their second full-length album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chosen_Few_(Boot_Camp_Clik_album)" title="The Chosen Few (Boot Camp Clik album)">The Chosen Few</a></i>. The album sold over 60,000 copies, making it one of Duck Down's most successful releases. In 2004 Black Moon returned with <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Eclipse_(Black_Moon_album)" title="Total Eclipse (Black Moon album)">Total Eclipse</a></i>. The album peaked at #47 on the Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums and ranked #23 for Independent Albums charts.
</p><p>Blake and North Carolina-based producer <a href="/wiki/9th_Wonder" title="9th Wonder">9th Wonder</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Little_Brother_(group)" title="Little Brother (group)">Little Brother</a> and <a href="/wiki/Justus_League" title="Justus League">Justus League</a>) collaborated to release <i><a href="/wiki/Chemistry_(Buckshot_and_9th_Wonder_album)" title="Chemistry (Buckshot and 9th Wonder album)">Chemistry</a></i> in summer 2005 to positive reviews. This album was a part of Duck Down's 'Triple Threat Campaign', preceded by <a href="/wiki/Sean_Price" title="Sean Price">Sean Price</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Monkey_Barz" title="Monkey Barz">Monkey Barz</a></i> and followed by <a href="/wiki/Smif-N-Wessun" title="Smif-N-Wessun">Tek &amp; Steele</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Smif_%27n%27_Wessun:_Reloaded" title="Smif &#39;n&#39; Wessun: Reloaded">Smif 'n' Wessun: Reloaded</a></i>.
</p><p>Boot Camp Clik returned in summer 2006 with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Stand_(Boot_Camp_Clik_album)" title="The Last Stand (Boot Camp Clik album)">The Last Stand</a></i>. With an all-star cast of producers (<a href="/wiki/Pete_Rock" title="Pete Rock">Pete Rock</a>, Da Beatminerz, 9th Wonder, <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marco_Polo_(producer)" title="Marco Polo (producer)">Marco Polo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illmind" title="Illmind">Illmind</a>, Coptic, Sic Beats and Ken BB), it was a critical success and marked the return of the entire Boot Camp Clik for the first time in several years. In addition, Black Moon released, <i>Alter The Chemistry</i>, laced with 1970s influenced rhythms and samples it takes a new approach to <i>Chemistry</i>.
</p><p>In April 2008, Buckshot and 9th Wonder released their sophomore studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Formula_(album)" title="The Formula (album)">The Formula</a></i>. In September same year, Blake was hospitalized after suffering a series of seizures. His business partner, Friedman, said he had just returned from a three-week tour through <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> prior to the episodes. After a short hospitalization,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> he was released and returned to performing and recording.
</p><p>Buckshot and <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a> also released an album together called <i><a href="/wiki/Survival_Skills_(album)" title="Survival Skills (album)">Survival Skills</a></i> on September 15, 2009, via Duck Down Records.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Its first single "Robot", produced by Havoc, was released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> on May 5, 2009.
</p><p>During 2010s, Blake has released the third album with 9th Wonder, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Solution_(Buckshot_and_9th_Wonder_album)" title="The Solution (Buckshot and 9th Wonder album)">The Solution</a></i> (2012), <i>Backpack Travels</i> (2014) with DJ <a href="/wiki/P-Money" title="P-Money">P-Money</a> from New Zealand, and the fourth Black Moon album titled <i>Rise of da Moon</i> (2019), which is his recent project to-date.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckshot_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blake is a <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>, and his Muslim name is Ḥanīf aṣ-Ṣiddīq حَنيْف الـصِّدِّيْق.  He can also speak <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
