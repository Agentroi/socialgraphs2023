<p><b>Chron Smith</b> (born October 1, 1976), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Hell Razah</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>. He is best known as a member of <a href="/wiki/Sunz_of_Man" title="Sunz of Man">Sunz of Man</a>, an early <a href="/wiki/List_of_Wu-Tang_Clan_affiliates" title="List of Wu-Tang Clan affiliates">affiliate group</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>.
</p><p>Razah is rumored to have suffered a <a href="/wiki/Brain_aneurysm" class="mw-redirect" title="Brain aneurysm">brain aneurysm</a> in April 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was quick to recover and continue recording. He came to adopt the moniker <b>Heaven Razah</b>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recording_history">Recording history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hell_Razah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Recording history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Da_Last_Future_.26_Sunz_of_Man"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Da_Last_Future_&amp;_Sunz_of_Man">Da Last Future &amp; Sunz of Man</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hell_Razah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Da Last Future &amp; Sunz of Man">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Razah made his rap debut in the early 90s as a member of the group Da Last Future, an early incarnation of the Sunz of Man, which included <a href="/wiki/Killah_Priest" title="Killah Priest">Killah Priest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shabazz_the_Disciple" title="Shabazz the Disciple">Shabazz the Disciple</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prodigal_Sunn" title="Prodigal Sunn">Prodigal Sunn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Supreme_(producer)" title="Supreme (producer)">Supreme</a> and 7th Ambassador.<sup id="cite_ref-SoM_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SoM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Razah and 7th Ambassador self-released the single "Livin' in Hell" b/w "Mental Combat" independently before the group adopted the Sunz of Man moniker.<sup id="cite_ref-AB_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AB-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Razah recorded a number of tracks with the group before their induction into the Wu-Tang family, including "Deep in the Water", "Writing Rhymes With a Liquid Pen" (aka "Elements"), "Lulla Bye" and "Psychic Hotline".<sup id="cite_ref-WL_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WL-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1995, the group adopted the moniker Sunz of Man, and signed a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Priority_Records" title="Priority Records">Wu-Tang/Priority Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> SoM released a number of singles on Wu-Tang Records, including "Soldiers of Darkness", "No Love Without Hate" and "Bloody Choices",<sup id="cite_ref-NL_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NL-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> in anticipation of their 1996 debut album <i>Nothing New Under the Sun</i>. Due to internal conflicts and label complications, the album was shelved.<sup id="cite_ref-BS_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BS-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Sunz of Man later signed a record deal with Red Ant Entertainment for the release of their official 1998 debut <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Shall_Be_First_(Sunz_of_Man_album)" title="The Last Shall Be First (Sunz of Man album)">The Last Shall Be First</a></i>, with the group's roster shaved down to just Razah, Killah Priest, Prodigal Sunn and <a href="/wiki/60_Second_Assassin_(emcee)" class="mw-redirect" title="60 Second Assassin (emcee)">60 Second Assassin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-RY_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RY-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The album was one of the most successful Wu-Tang affiliate releases, debuting at No.&#160;20 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> album chart, and No.&#160;7 on the R&amp;B/Hip-Hop chart.<sup id="cite_ref-CP_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CP-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the departure of Killah Priest in 1998, the group took a hiatus. 1999 saw the release of <i><a href="/wiki/The_First_Testament" class="mw-redirect" title="The First Testament">The First Testament</a></i>, which featured previously recorded material from the group, including the early tracks from Da Last Future.<sup id="cite_ref-FT_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FT-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Razah, Prodigal Sunn and 60 Second Assassin returned in 2002 with the album <i><a href="/wiki/Saviorz_Day" title="Saviorz Day">Saviorz Day</a></i>, the second official Sunz of Man studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-SD_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SD-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The group has yet to release a third studio album, but it has been reported that the group has reconciled with Shabazz the Disciple, Killah Priest and Supreme.<sup id="cite_ref-SoM_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SoM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hell_Razah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Razah made his solo debut in 2001, though quietly, with the independent release <i>When All Hell Breaks Loose</i>. The album featured guest appearances from 7th Ambassador, Killah Priest, Prodigal Sunn and <a href="/wiki/Timbo_King" title="Timbo King">Timbo King</a> of <a href="/wiki/Royal_Fam" title="Royal Fam">Royal Fam</a>, and production from Supreme.<sup id="cite_ref-BL_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BL-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The album spawned the single "Champaigne Room" b/w "Ghetto Love".<sup id="cite_ref-CR_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CR-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The tracks "Must B tha Music", "Oh! Now You Bangin'", "Ghetto Government", "Champaigne Room", "Like It or Not", "What We Came to Do", "B.B.P. (Business Before Pleasure)" and "Rowdy, Rowdy" made up roughly half of the 2004 Sunz of Man compilation <i>Elements</i>, with the other half made up of tracks from <i>The First Testament</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-EM_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EM-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Razah's next solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Renaissance_Child" title="Renaissance Child">Renaissance Child</a></i>, was released in early 2007 on <a href="/wiki/Nature_Sounds" title="Nature Sounds">Nature Sounds Records</a>. The album featured appearances from <a href="/wiki/Timbo_King" title="Timbo King">Timbo King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tragedy_Khadafi" title="Tragedy Khadafi">Tragedy Khadafi</a>, <a href="/wiki/R.A._the_Rugged_Man" title="R.A. the Rugged Man">R.A. the Rugged Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Dumile" class="mw-redirect" title="Daniel Dumile">Viktor Vaughn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Nazareth" title="Bronze Nazareth">Bronze Nazareth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Killah_Priest" title="Killah Priest">Killah Priest</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ras_Kass" title="Ras Kass">Ras Kass</a>, and production from <a href="/wiki/List_of_Wu-Tang_Clan_affiliates#Godz_Wrath" title="List of Wu-Tang Clan affiliates">Godz Wrath</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Dumile" class="mw-redirect" title="Daniel Dumile">MF DOOM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Nazareth" title="Bronze Nazareth">Bronze Nazareth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Krohme" title="Krohme">Krohme</a> and <a href="/wiki/4th_Disciple" title="4th Disciple">4th Disciple</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-RC_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RC-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The album was a well-received, critical success and featured the singles "The Renaissance"<sup id="cite_ref-TR_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TR-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and "Buried Alive" b/w "Project Jazz".<sup id="cite_ref-BA_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BA-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
In October 2007, Razah released his next album, <i><a href="/wiki/Razah%27s_Ladder" title="Razah&#39;s Ladder">Razah's Ladder</a></i>, entirely produced by the production duo <a href="/wiki/Blue_Sky_Black_Death" title="Blue Sky Black Death">Blue Sky Black Death</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-RL_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RL-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The album also was acclaimed,<sup id="cite_ref-AC_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AC-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> due to BSBD's soulful production work, and also to Razah's new spiritual lyrical direction, inspired by the recent death of his father.<sup id="cite_ref-ITV_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITV-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The album featured guest appearances from Prodigal Sunn, Shabazz the Disciple, <a href="/wiki/Crooked_I" title="Crooked I">Crooked I</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ill_Bill" title="Ill Bill">Ill Bill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sabac_Red" title="Sabac Red">Sabac Red</a>, and featured no singles.<sup id="cite_ref-WIN_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WIN-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Self-funded videos were shot for the tracks "Razah's Ladder"<sup id="cite_ref-LD_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LD-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and "Audiobiography", the latter directed by Sunz of Man producer Supreme,<sup id="cite_ref-ADB_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADB-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> and "Razah's Ladder",<sup id="cite_ref-LD_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LD-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaborations">Collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hell_Razah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, Hell Razah and <a href="/wiki/4th_Disciple" title="4th Disciple">4th Disciple</a> released a full-length collaboration album, <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freedom_of_Speech_(Hell_Razah_%26_4th_Disciple_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Freedom of Speech (Hell Razah &amp; 4th Disciple album) (page does not exist)">Freedom of Speech</a></i>, under the title "Sunz of Man presents". The album featured fifteen newly recorded tracks with 4th Disciple, and five other previously released tracks from <i>When All Hell Breaks Loose</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-FS_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FS-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The album featured the single "Article One" b/w "Project Love".<sup id="cite_ref-AO_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AO-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, Razah, Killah Priest, Royal Fam's Timbo King, Tragedy Khadafi and William Cooper formed the underground <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> <a href="/wiki/Black_Market_Militia" title="Black Market Militia">Black Market Militia</a>. The group released their self-titled debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Market_Militia_(album)" title="Black Market Militia (album)">Black Market Militia</a></i> that same year, with production from Godz Wrath and Bronze Nazareth, and guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Dead_prez" class="mw-redirect" title="Dead prez">dead prez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abiodun_Oyewole" title="Abiodun Oyewole">Abiodun Oyewole</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Last_Poets" title="The Last Poets">The Last Poets</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BMM_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BMM-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The album spawned the singles "Audobon Ballroom" b/w "Thug Nation"/"Hood Lullabye" and "Gem Stars" b/w "Mayday!".<sup id="cite_ref-BM_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BM-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> By the end of 2005, Hell Razah had named Saul Abraham the latest addition to the line-up. Razah's next album collaboration album was with Shabazz the Disciple and producer <a href="/wiki/Ayatollah_(producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ayatollah (producer)">Ayatollah</a> as the group T.H.U.G. Angelz, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_Red_Hook_Houses" title="Welcome to Red Hook Houses">Welcome to Red Hook Houses</a></i>, to be released on July 8, 2008 on <a href="/wiki/Babygrande_Records" title="Babygrande Records">Babygrande Records</a>. Other releases came in 2008 such as solo album titled <i>Heaven Razah</i>, and <i>Article 2: Right to Bare Arms</i>, a second full-length collaboration with <a href="/wiki/4th_Disciple" title="4th Disciple">4th Disciple</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UC_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UC-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> In an October 2007 interview, Razah also announced plans for numerous other releases for the year. He announced that the Sunz of Man were reunited and planned on releasing an album sometime soon. He also stated that <a href="/wiki/The_Maccabeez" class="mw-redirect" title="The Maccabeez">The Maccabeez</a>, a group consisting of himself, Killah Priest and Timbo King, were nearly finished recording their debut album. Black Market Militia are also planning for another album. Another possible release is a collaboration between Razah, Shabazz and Blue Sky Black Death, and a project from G.G.O. (Ghetto Government Officialz), which consists of Razah, 7th Ambassador, Lazarus, Face God and other Red Hook locals.<sup id="cite_ref-IV_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IV-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hell Razah suffered a <a href="/wiki/Brain_Aneurysm" class="mw-redirect" title="Brain Aneurysm">Brain Aneurysm</a> in 2010. After surviving that near death experience, he began to go under the name <i>Heaven Razah</i>.
</p><p>In 2011, Hell Razah's health was improving and he said on Twitter several albums will come out in the future, including Heaven Razah 2,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> the second album with <a href="/wiki/Shabazz_the_Disciple" title="Shabazz the Disciple">Shabazz the Disciple</a> as <a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_Red_Hook_Houses" title="Welcome to Red Hook Houses">T.H.U.G. Angelz</a> (produced by <a href="/wiki/Ayatollah_(producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ayatollah (producer)">Ayatollah</a> and <a href="/wiki/4th_Disciple" title="4th Disciple">4th Disciple</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Razah's Ladder 2 produced by <a href="/wiki/Blue_Sky_Black_Death" title="Blue Sky Black Death">Blue Sky Black Death</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> a gospel/rock album produced by Krohme called Krohme Razahs,<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> an album with <a href="/wiki/Shyheim" title="Shyheim">Shyheim</a> called The Verrazano<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> and the sequel to <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freedom_of_Speech_(Hell_Razah_%26_4th_Disciple_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Freedom of Speech (Hell Razah &amp; 4th Disciple album) (page does not exist)">Freedom of Speech</a></i> called Write to Bare Arms produced by 4th Disciple.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> An album with <a href="/wiki/Killah_Priest" title="Killah Priest">Killah Priest</a> as Thug Vaticans called The Lamb’s Supper will come out in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Another collaboration album, with producer Tokyo Cigar under the name Heavenly Cartel, is planned for 2013 and is called Spiritual Kung Fu.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> He's also doing an album with producer <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Nazareth" title="Bronze Nazareth">Bronze Nazareth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> He also started a hiphop/soul group together with producer Shroom and singer Tarone Rachamim called <i>The Dutch</i> and they are planning to release their first album in the future, which is called Music from the Heart.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Heaven Razah Part 2</i> became <i>Living After Death</i> and was released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> on March 31, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-itunes.apple.com_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itunes.apple.com-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> In May, Hell Razah announced the formal roster of his new label, Hell Razah Music Inc. to include Razah's oldest son "Heaven Sun" aka Yung Razah, Queen the Prophet, Scripture, and a few more to formally represent Razah's long time "Ghetto Govt" global movement also known as "GGO" (Ghetto Govt Officialz). The Ghetto Govt Movement has global chapters that are dedicated to making a positive impact in their communities as well as in the underground hip-hop community.
</p><p><b>Risen: The Story of Chron "Hell Razah" Smith - Documentary</b>
</p><p>Risen: The Story of Chron “Hell Razah” Smith which is a documentary directed by Frank Meyer and stars Wu Tang’s RZA, Cappadonna and Popa Wu, Sunz of Man members Killah Priest, Prodigal Sunn, 60-Second Assassin and Shabazz the Disciple, producers A. Supreme Wilder and Tom "Shroom" Johnson, rappers RA The Rugged Man and Ras Kass, industry heavyweight Randy Philips (AEG, Global Ent.), Hell Razah’s family and friends, and many more. Risen is executive produced by Digital Reacharound CEO Robert Juster, produced by film maker Paul Harb (The Expendables, Rambo, Rocky Balboa), and Esquire Network’s Michael D’Alonzo, with Cinematography by Jason Valdez. Risen: The Story of Chron “Hell Razah” Smith is available on Amazon Prime Video, Tubi, and You Tube. <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://www.amazon.com/Risen-Story-Chron-Razah-Smith/dp/B085RC21RB">https://www.amazon.com/Risen-Story-Chron-Razah-Smith/dp/B085RC21RB</a>
</p><p>In 2019, Razah was featured in the song "The Grandmaster" on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cryptik_Soul&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cryptik Soul (page does not exist)">Cryptik Soul</a>'s album <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Killer%27s_Blood&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Killer&#39;s Blood (page does not exist)">Killer's Blood</a></i>.
</p>
