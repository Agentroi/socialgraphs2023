<p><b>Stelios Phili</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a Cypriot-American songwriter, record producer, and composer based in <a href="/wiki/New_York,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="New York, New York">New York, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He has produced tracks featuring artists such as <a href="/wiki/A$AP_Ferg" class="mw-redirect" title="A$AP Ferg">A$AP Ferg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Thug" title="Young Thug">Young Thug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lolo_Zouai" class="mw-redirect" title="Lolo Zouai">Lolo Zouai</a>, <a href="/wiki/SZA_(singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="SZA (singer)">SZA</a>, <a href="/wiki/M.I.A._(rapper)" title="M.I.A. (rapper)">MIA</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Songwriting_and_production">Songwriting and production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stelios_Phili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Songwriting and production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to an interview with MTV,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Stelios Phili met <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/A$AP_Ferg" class="mw-redirect" title="A$AP Ferg">A$AP Ferg</a> while working at <a href="/wiki/GQ" title="GQ">GQ</a> in 2013. The two began collaborating after fellow A$AP Mob member A$AP Nast passed along one of the producer's beats, which would later become the A$AP Ferg song "Ja-Rule" featuring <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>. In 2014, Phili produced the first single from A$AP Ferg's mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Ferg_Forever" title="Ferg Forever">Ferg Forever</a></i>. The song, "Doe-Active", was met with positive reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" class="mw-redirect" title="Pitchfork Media">Pitchfork Media</a> described the song as "a minimalist beat that could've slid right into <i>Yeezus</i>", <a href="/wiki/Stereogum" title="Stereogum">Stereogum</a> called it "a total nutso banger", and <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> named it a "certified banger, with club-destroying drums". Phili's production was featured on four additional tracks on <i>Ferg Forever</i>.
</p><p>In 2015, Phili produced DonMonique's debut EP, <i>Thirst Trap</i>. The <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Times" class="mw-redirect" title="New York Times">New York Times</a></i> described it as "full of transfixing slo-mo production by Stelios Phili somewhere between modern-day Internet-rap sparseness and the hard, murky slap of mid-1990s New York street rap."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Phili began collaborating with New York-based artist Lolo Zouaï. Their first collaboration, "High Highs to Low Lows", topped Spotify's Fresh Finds Best of 2017 list.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Since then, he has co-written and produced Zouaï's two albums on <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>, 2019's <i>High Highs to Low Lows</i> and 2022's <i>Playgirl</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, Phili produced the <a href="/wiki/Young_Thug" title="Young Thug">Young Thug</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a> collaboration "High". According to <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>, the song came together after Phili read an interview with Elton John, who expressed his admiration for Young Thug. After Phili built a new beat around an edited version of the "Rocket Man" a cappella, <a href="/wiki/300_Entertainment" title="300 Entertainment">300 Entertainment</a> A&amp;R Geoff Ogunlesi played the song to Young Thug. On September 24, 2018, "High" was released on his EP <i><a href="/wiki/On_the_Rvn" title="On the Rvn">On the Rvn</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The song was awarded Best New Music by <a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" class="mw-redirect" title="Pitchfork Media">Pitchfork Media</a>, with <a href="/wiki/The_Fader" title="The Fader">The Fader</a> describing the song's reception as "swift and rapturous" and "a return to iconoclastic form for Young Thug."<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtracks_and_scores">Soundtracks and scores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stelios_Phili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Soundtracks and scores">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fashion">Fashion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stelios_Phili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fashion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, Stelios Phili composed the runway music for En Noir's Spring/Summer 2014 show.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In early 2014, he composed the music for designer <a href="/wiki/Mark_McNairy" title="Mark McNairy">Mark McNairy</a>'s Fall/Winter 2014 show.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, Phili founded of the sound design studio SCÈNES. Through his studio, Phili has scored campaigns for <a href="/wiki/Yves_Saint_Laurent_(brand)" class="mw-redirect" title="Yves Saint Laurent (brand)">Yves Saint Laurent</a>, <a href="/wiki/NARS_Cosmetics" title="NARS Cosmetics">Nars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Klein" title="Calvin Klein">Calvin Klein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Vogue_(magazine)" title="Vogue (magazine)">Vogue</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Ford" title="Tom Ford">Tom Ford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_Companies" title="Estée Lauder Companies">Estée Lauder</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Vuitton" title="Louis Vuitton">Louis Vuitton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In 2020, he remixed <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a>’s “<a href="/wiki/Sine_from_Above" title="Sine from Above">Sine from Above</a>” for <a href="/wiki/Valentino_(fashion_designer)" title="Valentino (fashion designer)">Valentino</a>’s Voce Viva campaign, directed by <a href="/wiki/Harmony_Korine" title="Harmony Korine">Harmony Korine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In 2022, Phili produced a remix of <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>’s <a href="/wiki/A_Kind_of_Magic_(song)" title="A Kind of Magic (song)">“A Kind of Magic”</a> for a global <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> campaign that launched Coke Studio.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2022, Phili composed the music for designer Kim Shui's Spring/Sumer 2023 runway show.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_TV">Film and TV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stelios_Phili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Film and TV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Phili’s music has been featured in TV shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/Euphoria_(American_TV_series)" title="Euphoria (American TV series)">Euphoria</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Insecure_(TV_series)" title="Insecure (TV series)">Insecure</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Afterparty_(TV_series)" title="The Afterparty (TV series)">The Afterparty</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> For <a href="/wiki/Apple_TV" title="Apple TV">Apple TV</a>’s <i>The Afterparty</i> (2022), he composed an EP for the fictional rapper, Xavier, played by <a href="/wiki/Dave_Franco" title="Dave Franco">Dave Franco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>  He has also contributed music to the award-winning films <i>Lowland Kids</i> (2019) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fallout_(film)" title="The Fallout (film)">The Fallout</a></i> (2021).<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stelios_Phili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Phili's production style is described as fresh and understated. According to <i><a href="/wiki/Pigeons_%26_Planes" class="mw-redirect" title="Pigeons &amp; Planes">Pigeons &amp; Planes</a></i>, "Stelios Phili is one of those producers you can recognize from the jump—he utilizes a unique collection of sounds that is both muted and aggressive: murky synths and pillowy cymbal taps are cut by a fierce snare, or a saxophone seamlessly transforms into an electric guitar. It's all about subtlety with SP, and he's got it down to a science."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
