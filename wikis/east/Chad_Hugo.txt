<p><br />
<b>Charles Edward Hugo</b> (born February 24, 1974) is an American record producer and multi-instrumentalist. With close friend <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, he formed the production duo <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">the Neptunes</a> in the early 1990s, with whom he has produced songs for many recording artists. He is also a member of the band <a href="/wiki/N.E.R.D." title="N.E.R.D.">N.E.R.D.</a>, alongside Williams and <a href="/wiki/Shay_Haley" title="Shay Haley">Shay Haley</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_Life">Early Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chad_Hugo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Charles Edward Hugo was born February 24, 1979, in <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth,_Virginia" title="Portsmouth, Virginia">Portsmouth, Virginia</a> to a Filipino-American family. His mother was a lab technician and his father served in the United States Navy.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His early musical experiences included recording <a href="/wiki/Mixtapes" class="mw-redirect" title="Mixtapes">mixtapes</a> in his home using a boombox. He later joined his school band, playing the saxophone, through which he met <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, with whom he began producing music. They formed <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">The Neptunes</a>, and in 1992, and upon entering a local talent contest, they were discovered by Teddy Riley, whose studio was close to Williams' school.<sup id="cite_ref-TheAge_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheAge-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Williams also introduced Hugo to Hayley,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> with whom they formed the group <a href="/wiki/N.E.R.D" class="mw-redirect" title="N.E.R.D">N.E.R.D</a> in 1999.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chad_Hugo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">The Neptunes</a> and <a href="/wiki/N.E.R.D" class="mw-redirect" title="N.E.R.D">N.E.R.D</a></div>
<p>Through working with Riley, the Neptunes wrote <a href="/wiki/Wreckx-n-Effect" title="Wreckx-n-Effect">Wreckx-n-Effect</a>'s 1992 song "<a href="/wiki/Rump_Shaker_(song)" title="Rump Shaker (song)">Rump Shaker</a>" while still in school.<sup id="cite_ref-TheAge_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheAge-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> They also worked with Riley's group <a href="/wiki/Blackstreet" title="Blackstreet">Blackstreet</a>, co-writing the single "<a href="/wiki/Tonight%27s_the_Night_(Blackstreet_song)" title="Tonight&#39;s the Night (Blackstreet song)">Tonight's the Night</a>" from <a href="/wiki/Blackstreet_(album)" title="Blackstreet (album)">their self-titled debut album</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TheAge_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheAge-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Neptunes continued their production work, contributing to projects by <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>-based rapper <a href="/wiki/N.O.R.E." title="N.O.R.E.">Noreaga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ol%27_Dirty_Bastard" title="Ol&#39; Dirty Bastard">Ol' Dirty Bastard</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kelis" title="Kelis">Kelis</a><sup id="cite_ref-TheAge_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheAge-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> for whom they would entirely produce her debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Kaleidoscope_(Kelis_album)" title="Kaleidoscope (Kelis album)">Kaleidoscope</a></i> (1999), and her 2001 album <i><a href="/wiki/Wanderland" title="Wanderland">Wanderland</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kaleidoscope_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kaleidoscope-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The Neptunes also produced hip hop duo <a href="/wiki/Clipse" title="Clipse">Clipse</a>'s album, <i><a href="/wiki/Exclusive_Audio_Footage" title="Exclusive Audio Footage">Exclusive Audio Footage</a></i> (1999), which went unreleased until 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Hugo met <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> during a studio session for his second studio album, <a href="/wiki/In_My_Lifetime,_Vol._1" title="In My Lifetime, Vol. 1">In My Lifetime, Vol. 1</a>, contributing on saxophone to <a href="/wiki/The_City_Is_Mine" title="The City Is Mine">The City Is Mine</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> with the Neptunes going on to produce a number of tracks with Jay-Z.
</p><p>In 2001, <a href="/wiki/N.E.R.D." title="N.E.R.D.">N.E.R.D.</a> released their debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/In_Search_of..._(N.E.R.D._album)" title="In Search of... (N.E.R.D. album)">In Search of...</a></i>, featuring production from the Neptunes.<sup id="cite_ref-InSearchOfPitchfork_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-InSearchOfPitchfork-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In the same year, the Neptunes gained their first worldwide hit with <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>' single, "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Slave_4_U" title="I&#39;m a Slave 4 U">I'm a Slave 4 U</a>", for her eponymous third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Britney_(album)" title="Britney (album)">Britney</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The Neptunes wrote and produced <a href="/wiki/NSYNC" title="NSYNC">NSYNC</a>'s final single, "<a href="/wiki/Girlfriend_(NSYNC_song)" title="Girlfriend (NSYNC song)">Girlfriend</a>", then wrote and produced most of co-lead singer <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a>'s debut solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Justified_(album)" title="Justified (album)">Justified</a></i> (2002), including lead singles "<a href="/wiki/Like_I_Love_You" title="Like I Love You">Like I Love You</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Rock_Your_Body" title="Rock Your Body">Rock Your Body</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Se%C3%B1orita_(Justin_Timberlake_song)" title="Señorita (Justin Timberlake song)">Señorita</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2003, the Neptunes released a compilation album, <i><a href="/wiki/Clones_(album)" title="Clones (album)">Clones</a></i>, featuring songs and remixes from various <a href="/wiki/Star_Trak_Entertainment" title="Star Trak Entertainment">Star Trak</a> artists. The Neptunes won "Producer of the Year" at the <a href="/wiki/46th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="46th Annual Grammy Awards">2004 Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2004Grammys_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2004Grammys-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hugo and <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a> were announced to be inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Songwriters_Hall_of_Fame" title="Songwriters Hall of Fame">Songwriters Hall of Fame</a> as a part of the 2020 class.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
