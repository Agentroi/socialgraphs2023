<p><b>Kejuan Waliek Muchita</b> (born May 21, 1974), better known by his stage name <b>Havoc</b>, is an American rapper and record producer. He was one half of the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> duo <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a> with <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Havoc_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Havoc was born in Brooklyn and raised in the <a href="/wiki/Queensbridge_Houses" title="Queensbridge Houses">Queensbridge Houses</a>. At a later age, Havoc found out his paternal grandfather was jazz musician <a href="/wiki/Bill_Dixon" title="Bill Dixon">Bill Dixon</a>. In his teenage years, he shot a Def Jam employee in the belly by accident.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He graduated from the <a href="/wiki/High_School_of_Art_and_Design" title="High School of Art and Design">High School of Art and Design</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Manhattan, New York">Manhattan, New York</a> in 1992, where he met <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a>. He is one half of the iconic hip hop duo Mobb Deep, and in addition to producing nearly the entire Mobb Deep catalogue, has also produced songs for <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Ceremonies" class="mw-redirect" title="Master of Ceremonies">MCs</a> such as <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/112_(band)" title="112 (band)">112</a>, <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Puff Daddy</a>, <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rohff" title="Rohff">Rohff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Noyd" title="Big Noyd">Big Noyd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Kim" class="mw-redirect" title="Lil Kim">Lil Kim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Capone-N-Noreaga" title="Capone-N-Noreaga">Capone-N-Noreaga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foxy_Brown_(rapper)" title="Foxy Brown (rapper)">Foxy Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Onyx (band)">Onyx</a>, and his partner <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a> on his solo work. In 2005, Mobb Deep recorded for fellow Queens rapper <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>'s record label <a href="/wiki/G-Unit_Records" title="G-Unit Records">G-Unit Records</a> and left the label in 2009, in a mutually agreed-to release.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Havoc is renowned as one of the most iconic figures in New York hip hop. Popular music magazine <i>Complex</i> ranked Havoc among the top hip hop producers of 1995 after <i>The Infamous</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Beattips.com ranked him as the 24th most influential beatmaker of all time<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> citing his dual abilities of rapping and producing. Following <i><a href="/wiki/The_Infamous" title="The Infamous">The Infamous</a></i>, he tweaked his skills and transitioned to a more atmospheric production style that incorporated samples from classical music, most notably visible on the 1996 album <i><a href="/wiki/Hell_on_Earth_(Mobb_Deep_album)" title="Hell on Earth (Mobb Deep album)">Hell on Earth</a></i>. As a rapper, he is known for his hardcore lyricism and consistent flow. He also contributed background vocals and a verse on <a href="/wiki/Black_Moon_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Moon (band)">Black Moon</a>'s debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Enta_da_Stage" title="Enta da Stage">Enta da Stage</a></i> in 1993.
</p><p>In July 2009, Havoc was featured alongside  in <a href="/wiki/J_Dilla" title="J Dilla">J Dilla</a>'s music video for "24K Rap" off of the <i><a href="/wiki/Jay_Stay_Paid" title="Jay Stay Paid">Jay Stay Paid</a></i> album, the video was directed by <a href="/wiki/Derek_Pike" title="Derek Pike">Derek Pike</a>. In 2010, Havoc produced a beat for <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> that resulted in a song called "Untitled" which is a hidden track on Eminem's album <i><a href="/wiki/Recovery_(Eminem_album)" title="Recovery (Eminem album)">Recovery</a></i> and contributed to the <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> bonus track to Raekwon's album <a href="/wiki/Shaolin_vs._Wu-Tang" title="Shaolin vs. Wu-Tang"><i>Shaolin vs. Wu-Tang</i></a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Bad_Meets_Evil" title="Bad Meets Evil">Bad Meets Evil</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Hell:_The_Sequel" title="Hell: The Sequel">Hell: The Sequel</a> EP</i>. He revealed that he plans to record a new Mobb Deep album with <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/13_(Havoc_album)" title="13 (Havoc album)">13</a></i> was released on May 7, 2013.
</p><p>Havoc worked with <a href="/wiki/Kavinsky" title="Kavinsky">Kavinsky</a> on his 2013 album <a href="/wiki/OutRun_(album)" title="OutRun (album)">OutRun</a>. He wrote the lyrics and sang the vocals for "Suburbia", the sixth track on the album.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, Havoc helped produce "<a href="/wiki/Real_Friends_(Kanye_West_song)" title="Real Friends (Kanye West song)">Real Friends</a>" and "Famous", two tracks on <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_of_Pablo" title="The Life of Pablo">The Life of Pablo</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His original productions have been featured in numerous commercially successful films, well known television series and popular video games, and he is a character in <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam:_Fight_For_NY" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam: Fight For NY">Def Jam: Fight For NY</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
Havoc produced the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> official 2020 anthem, "Squad Up", that features verses from both Havoc and <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Feuds">Feuds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Havoc_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Feuds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prodigy">Prodigy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Havoc_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Prodigy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2012, Havoc wrote a series of derogatory comments about Prodigy on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>, after Prodigy denied engaging in homosexual relationships in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> At first, Havoc claimed that his Twitter account was hacked.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> However, he later confirmed that he wrote the tweets and expressed his frustrations with Prodigy in an interview with <a href="/wiki/AllHipHop" title="AllHipHop">AllHipHop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He stated that Mobb Deep was on an "indefinite hiatus" until the duo worked out their differences. Havoc later released a <a href="/wiki/Diss_track" class="mw-redirect" title="Diss track">diss track</a> aimed at Prodigy, which was titled "Separated (Real from the Fake)".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Prodigy did not respond to Havoc's song and even stated publicly that Mobb Deep would eventually reconcile.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In March 2013, the duo announced that they had reconciled and were going on tour.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
