<p><b>Nathaniel De La Rosa</b> (born March 17, 1985), known professionally as <b>Bodega Bamz</b>, is of Dominican/Puerto Rican descent rapper/actor from <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Harlem" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Harlem">Spanish Harlem</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-insanulahmed_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-insanulahmed-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> De La Rosa first attracted attention for his 2012 mixtape, <i>Strictly 4 My P.A.P.I.Z</i>, which featured <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joell_Ortiz" title="Joell Ortiz">Joell Ortiz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aston_Matthews" title="Aston Matthews">A$ton Matthews</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tego_Calderon" class="mw-redirect" title="Tego Calderon">Tego Calderon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flatbush_Zombies" title="Flatbush Zombies">Flatbush Zombies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-datpiff.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datpiff.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> De La Rosa later collaborated with <a href="/wiki/The_Martinez_Brothers" title="The Martinez Brothers">the Martinez Brothers</a> and released an EP, <i>Sunday Service</i> (2014).<sup id="cite_ref-patricklyons_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-patricklyons-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, he released his debut album, <i>Sidewalk Exec</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-itunes_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itunes-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, he was cast in the Showtime series <i><a href="/wiki/SMILF" title="SMILF">SMILF</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bodega_Bamz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2012 critically acclaimed mixtape release of Strictly 4 My P.A.P.I.Z,<sup id="cite_ref-datpiff.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datpiff.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> showcased Bodega Bamz artistry and solidified his place in the hip-hop community. Strictly 4 My P.A.P.I.Z. includes features from <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joell_Ortiz" title="Joell Ortiz">Joell Ortiz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aston_Matthews" title="Aston Matthews">A$ton Matthews</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Flatbush_Zombies" title="Flatbush Zombies">Flatbush Zombies</a>. Bodega Bamz teamed up with <a href="/wiki/The_Martinez_Brothers" title="The Martinez Brothers">The Martinez Brothers</a> and released an EP, Sunday Service (2014).<sup id="cite_ref-patricklyons_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-patricklyons-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, Bodega Bamz released his debut album Sidewalk Exec.<sup id="cite_ref-itunes_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itunes-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Bodega Bamz also showcased another one of his talents by making his acting debut in his own cult film The Streets Owe Me, Hell Train and most recently cast in the Showtime series, <i><a href="/wiki/SMILF" title="SMILF">SMILF</a></i>. As of March 2020, he had a guest-starring role on the second season <a href="/wiki/FBI_(TV_series)" title="FBI (TV series)"><i>FBI</i></a>.
</p>
