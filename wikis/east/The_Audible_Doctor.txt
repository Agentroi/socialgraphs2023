<p><b>Mark Vincent Woodford</b> (born May 24, 1984 in <a href="/wiki/Madison,_Wisconsin" title="Madison, Wisconsin">Madison, Wisconsin</a>), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>The Audible Doctor</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>-based <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a> and underground rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Audible Doctor has been credited as producer in critically acclaimed studio albums and EPs including <i>Made In The Streets</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/2057_(EP)" title="2057 (EP)">2057</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ports" title="The Ports">The Ports</a></i>, <i>Reporting Live</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Computer_Era" title="Computer Era">Computer Era</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Free_Agent_(album)" title="Free Agent (album)">Free Agent</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Thug_Matrix_3" title="Thug Matrix 3">Thug Matrix 3</a></i>. On 3 January 2014, he was listed in <i><a href="/wiki/AllHipHop" title="AllHipHop">AllHipHop</a></i> 's "Top 50 Underground/Indie/Emerging Artists Of 2013"<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> before he went on to make a <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearance</a> at the 2014 edition of the annual <a href="/wiki/The_Brooklyn_Hip-Hop_Festival" title="The Brooklyn Hip-Hop Festival">Brooklyn Hip-Hop Festival</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Brown_Bag_AllStars" title="Brown Bag AllStars">Brown Bag AllStars</a>, a group of emcees he joined while interning at Fat Beats in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Audible_Doctor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Madison,_Wisconsin" title="Madison, Wisconsin">Madison, Wisconsin</a>, The Audible Doctor started music having piano and guitar lessons while growing up until his days in high school when he started collecting records and DJing at friends' parties.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2002, The Audible Doctor moved to New York to attend an audio engineering and recording school before he started working at Fat Beats store after graduating from college. While interning at Fat Beats, he collaborated with funk group <a href="/wiki/Skull_Snaps" title="Skull Snaps">Skull Snaps</a> to release his first production project titled <i>Skull Snaps Meet The Audible Doctor</i> in 2005 and <i>It's A New Day Redux</i> in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, The Audible Doctor left Fat Beats and released his first solo EP titled <i>The Crackers</i> then <i>Brownies Deluxe</i> in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He had his first major break as a producer after he was credited in <a href="/wiki/Joell_Ortiz" title="Joell Ortiz">Joell Ortiz</a>'s second studio album entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Free_Agent_(album)" title="Free Agent (album)">Free Agent</a></i>, an album that debuted at #173 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a></i> with 4,000 copies sold in its first week released.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 25 September 2012, The Audible Doctor released his critically acclaimed EP titled <i>I Think That...</i><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> which further earned him more attention from music critics before he went on to release an album titled <i>Doctorin</i> on 30 October 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On 24 November 2014, he released an EP titled <i>Can't Keep The People Waiting</i>, the EP featured vocal appearances from acts like <a href="/wiki/Astro_(rapper)" title="Astro (rapper)">Astro</a>, Hassan Mackey of <a href="/wiki/Mello_Music_Group" title="Mello Music Group">Mello Music Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Consequence_(rapper)" title="Consequence (rapper)">Consequence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Foxxx" title="Freddie Foxxx">Bumpy Knuckles</a>, Guilty Simpson and John Robinson.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 20 June 2015, The Audible Doctor released an EP entitled <i>The Spring Tape</i> off his <i>Seasons</i> EP set which include <i>The Winter Tape</i> and <i>The Summer Tape</i>. His style of production has seen him work with notable acts like <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> for the freestyle titled "This Is Murder Not Music," <a href="/wiki/Astro_(rapper)" title="Astro (rapper)">Astro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Koncept" title="Koncept">Koncept</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fredro_Starr" title="Fredro Starr">Fredro Starr</a>, Joell Ortiz, and many more.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
