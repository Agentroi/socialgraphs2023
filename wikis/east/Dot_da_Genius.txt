<p><b>Oladipo Omishore</b>, known professionally as <b>Dot da Genius</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Audio_engineer" title="Audio engineer">audio engineer</a> from <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a>. He first gained recognition in 2008 for producing "<a href="/wiki/Day_%27n%27_Nite" title="Day &#39;n&#39; Nite">Day 'n' Nite</a>" by American musician <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a>, with whom he is a longtime friend and frequent collaborator; the two would later form a <a href="/wiki/Rock_band" class="mw-redirect" title="Rock band">rock band</a> in 2010, known as <a href="/wiki/WZRD_(band)" title="WZRD (band)">WZRD</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Engaged in various entrepreneurial pursuits, Omishore launched his own trans-media company HeadBanga Muzik Group which has collaborated with numerous brands and labels, such as: Universal Music Group, BMG, Nike, and Splice. His <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">production</a> has been featured on albums by several prominent artists in the music industry, including <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denzel_Curry" title="Denzel Curry">Denzel Curry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Nas_X" title="Lil Nas X">Lil Nas X</a>, <a href="/wiki/JID" title="JID">JID</a>, <a href="/wiki/6LACK" class="mw-redirect" title="6LACK">6LACK</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jhen%C3%A9_Aiko" title="Jhené Aiko">Jhené Aiko</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dot_da_Genius_production_discography" title="Dot da Genius production discography">among others</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sound_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sound-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dot_da_Genius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1986.E2.80.932005:_Early_life"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1986–2005:_Early_life">1986–2005: Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dot_da_Genius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1986–2005: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Oladipo Omishore is from the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> borough of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. He is of <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigerian</a> heritage, of the Yoruba tribe.<sup id="cite_ref-naij_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-naij-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-net_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-net-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His parents always encouraged his interest in music, and at the age of seven, his father sent him to the Brooklyn Music School for <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a> lessons, where he studied with Tom Coote. Omishore preferred playing the guitar, but learning how to play a <a href="/wiki/Keyboard_instrument" title="Keyboard instrument">keyboard instrument</a> was very beneficial later in life when he started producing. He attended <a href="/wiki/Polytechnic_Institute_of_New_York_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Polytechnic Institute of New York University">Polytechnic Institute of New York University</a> in New York, where he studied <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a> and graduated in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-sound_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sound-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.932009:_Career_beginnings_and_producing_for_Kid_Cudi"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–2009:_Career_beginnings_and_producing_for_Kid_Cudi">2006–2009: Career beginnings and producing for Kid Cudi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dot_da_Genius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006–2009: Career beginnings and producing for Kid Cudi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Omishore began his career in sound-mixing in 2003 at New York University by using <a href="/wiki/Image-Line" title="Image-Line">Image-Line</a>'s <a href="/wiki/FL_Studio" title="FL Studio">FL Studio</a> program. In 2004, Omishore created a studio in his basement with equipment bought by his parents and would later call it "Head Banga Muzik Studios."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After gaining attention for his production, he decided to change locations to the Brewery Recording Studio. In May 2009, Da Genius partnered up with Andrew Krivonos, owner of Brewery Recording Studio, and helped move the studio to North Brooklyn in a new facility.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Omishore and <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a> met in 2006, and they lived together for two years. Both artists found a genuine liking for one another, and chose to work with each other on several music productions. "Day N' Nite" turned out to be the biggest hit production that both of them worked on together, with Omishore providing the beats and Cudi rapping and singing over the music. He produced the song in Head Banga Muzik Studios, and used several different methods of mixing and synthesizing the beats on this track. The song took a total of two days to complete, from the <a href="/wiki/Instrumental" title="Instrumental">instrumental</a> being made to the track's final arrangements. No edits were made after that two-day process ended. It was finished in 2007 and became popular two years later, in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-sound_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sound-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
"Day N' Nite" reached the top three in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sound_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sound-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was released under the labels <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fool%27s_Gold_Records" title="Fool&#39;s Gold Records">Fool's Gold Records</a>, <a href="/wiki/Universal_Motown_Records" title="Universal Motown Records">Universal Motown Records</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Data_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Data Records">Data Records</a>. It is featured on the album <i><a href="/wiki/Man_on_the_Moon:_The_End_of_Day" title="Man on the Moon: The End of Day">Man on the Moon: The End of Day</a></i>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932018:_WZRD"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2018:_WZRD">2010–2018: WZRD</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dot_da_Genius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010–2018: WZRD">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/WZRD_(band)" title="WZRD (band)">WZRD (band)</a> and <a href="/wiki/WZRD_(album)" title="WZRD (album)">WZRD (album)</a></div>
<p>WZRD is Omishore and Kid Cudi's collaboration <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a> band. Kid Cudi focuses on the vocals, while Omishore is responsible for the production.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Together, they produced a self-made album titled WZRD. Much of their inspiration behind the album came from their love for Nirvana, The Pixies, and Pink Floyd.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> They recorded much of the album in either their tour bus while they were on the road, or Cudi's basement studio in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Hills" title="Hollywood Hills">Hollywood Hills</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> This album contained absolutely no profanity, including the "n word" that many rappers loosely use.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The album was first released on February 28, 2012. It reportedly sold 70,499 copies in its opening week.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Although Cudi finds the marketing and promotion of the album weak by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group</a>, it landed the number three spot on the album sales chart.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Universal Music Group also allegedly under-shipped copies of the album.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> While "Dose of Dopeness" was released prior to the release of WZRD, it was not featured on the album.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2018.E2.80.93present:_Solo_debut_album"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–present:_Solo_debut_album">2018–present: Solo debut album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dot_da_Genius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2018–present: Solo debut album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2018, Omishore and Woodro Skillson announced the signing of James Japan to their label, HeadBanga Muzik Group.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, Omishore scored his first number-one hit single on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, when "<a href="/wiki/The_Scotts" title="The Scotts">The Scotts</a>," a collaborative effort between Cudi and his protege <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>, debuted at the top spot in the US chart.
</p><p>On June 24, 2022, Omishore released "Talk About Me", the lead single from his upcoming solo debut album. The song features vocals from American rappers Kid Cudi, <a href="/wiki/Denzel_Curry" title="Denzel Curry">Denzel Curry</a>, and <a href="/wiki/JID" title="JID">JID</a>. In July 2022, <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> interviewed Omishore on his upcoming debut album.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dot_da_Genius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Among Omishore’s favorite albums of all time are <a href="/wiki/Pink_Floyd" title="Pink Floyd">Pink Floyd</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Side_of_the_Moon" title="The Dark Side of the Moon">The Dark Side of the Moon</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chronic" title="The Chronic">The Chronic</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/ATLiens" title="ATLiens">ATLiens</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Coldplay" title="Coldplay">Coldplay</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Viva_la_Vida_or_Death_and_All_His_Friends" title="Viva la Vida or Death and All His Friends">Viva la Vida or Death and All His Friends</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dot_da_Genius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Omishore has one daughter, from a previous relationship.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In September 2014, American singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Jhen%C3%A9_Aiko" title="Jhené Aiko">Jhené Aiko</a>, revealed she and Omishore were in a relationship. The two revealed their marriage in March 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-naij_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-naij-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On August 9, 2016, Jhené Aiko filed for divorce citing irreconcilable differences.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The divorce was finalized in October 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
