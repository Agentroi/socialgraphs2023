<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=22Gz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jeffrey Mark Alexander was born on November 29, 1997, the <a href="/wiki/Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="Flatbush, Brooklyn">Flatbush</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> His father died before he was born. He started his entertainment career on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Subway" title="New York City Subway">New York City Subway</a> performing <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(busking)" title="Showtime (busking)">showtime</a> dance routines.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He is of Guyanese descent.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=22Gz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After releasing the singles "Blixky" and "Suburban" in 2016, 22Gz gained a following from his <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> videos. "Suburban", produced by London-based <a href="/wiki/Drill_music" title="Drill music">drill</a> producer AXL Beats,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> was regarded as one of the first major Brooklyn drill songs to become popular.<sup id="cite_ref-Complex_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HNHH_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HNHH-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> 22Gz gained attention, leading him to signing with <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic</a> in 2018 and his label A&amp;R helping him getting noticed by Kodak Black <sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Complex_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> One of his first songs released on the label, "Spin the Block", was a collaboration track with <a href="/wiki/Kodak_Black" title="Kodak Black">Kodak Black</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-STB_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-STB-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>22Gz released his first mixtape with Atlantic, <i>The Blixky Tape</i>, in July 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> His follow-up mixtape <i>Growth &amp; Development</i> was released on April 10, 2020, and was co-produced by London-based drill producer <a href="/wiki/Ghosty_(producer)" title="Ghosty (producer)">Ghosty</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Torsten Ingvaldsen of <i>Hypebeast</i> praised the mixtape, stating, 22Gz has "riotous energy, bringing forth aggressive lyrics and militant deliveries that continue to sculpt out his fast-paced rise".<sup id="cite_ref-GD_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GD-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_controversies">Legal controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=22Gz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legal controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2014, he was charged with conspiracy to commit murder, however the charges were dropped.<sup id="cite_ref-Complex_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> 22Gz spent five months in jail in 2017 on second-degree murder charges related to a shooting in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, however these were later dropped.<sup id="cite_ref-Miami_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Miami-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> 22Gz recorded a <a href="/wiki/Facebook_Live" class="mw-redirect" title="Facebook Live">Facebook Live</a> video from an <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department" title="New York City Police Department">NYPD</a> holding cell in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Alexander, along with four other New York rappers including <a href="/wiki/Casanova_(rapper)" title="Casanova (rapper)">Casanova</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_Smoke" title="Pop Smoke">Pop Smoke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheff_G" title="Sheff G">Sheff G</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Don_Q_(rapper)" title="Don Q (rapper)">Don Q</a>, were removed from <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Loud" title="Rolling Loud">the Rolling Loud concert</a>. NYPD cited "a higher risk of violence" if the artists were to perform.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He has beefed with the "Woo's" including rappers like <a href="/wiki/Pop_Smoke" title="Pop Smoke">Pop Smoke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fivio_Foreign" title="Fivio Foreign">Fivio Foreign</a>.
</p><p>On June 12, 2022, 22Gz was arrested in New York City on attempted murder charges over a March shooting in which three people were injured.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He was released on $500,000 bail four days later.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
