<p><b>Tierra Helena Whack</b> (born August 11, 1995)<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> is an American rapper and singer. She originally performed as "Dizzle Dizz" as a teenager, but later on reverted to her birth name in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Coscarelli18_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coscarelli18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Her debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Whack_World" title="Whack World">Whack World</a></i>, was released in May 2018 and received widespread critical acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-ww_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ww-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Her song "Mumbo Jumbo" was nominated at the <a href="/wiki/61st_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="61st Annual Grammy Awards">61st Annual Grammy Awards</a> for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Music_Video" title="Grammy Award for Best Music Video">Best Music Video</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Whack has since collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tierra_Whack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Whack was born in 1995 and raised by her mother in <a href="/wiki/North_Philadelphia" title="North Philadelphia">North Philadelphia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She has two younger siblings. In a 2018 interview with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fader" title="The Fader">The Fader</a></i>, Whack mentioned that she and her father are estranged.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Whack was an introverted child who wrote to help herself deal with her insecurities.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> As a child, Whack loved the author <a href="/wiki/Dr._Seuss" title="Dr. Seuss">Dr. Seuss</a> so much that she would rhyme together each of her sentences for her homework assignments.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Whack pinpointed the exact moment when her love for rhyming began. She was to rhyme for a homework assignment for class, and after receiving positive feedback, she continued with it. She later asked her mother for composition books to fill with rhymes and lyrics before she eventually started recording herself.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Whack attended <a href="/wiki/The_Arts_Academy_at_Benjamin_Rush" title="The Arts Academy at Benjamin Rush">The Arts Academy at Benjamin Rush</a> for three years before finishing high school in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> At Benjamin Rush, she was a vocal major, and was one of the few black students in a predominately white graduating class.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> With some difficulty, she and her friends persuaded their principal to let them perform the finale number from <i><a href="/wiki/Sister_Act_2:_Back_in_the_Habit" title="Sister Act 2: Back in the Habit">Sister Act 2: Back in the Habit</a></i> for the school vocal showcase,<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and Whack performed a rap verse. Later in Whack's career, she toured with <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a>, who starred in the 1993 film.
</p><p>At around the age of fifteen, Whack who went by the moniker "Dizzle Dizz" appeared in a 2011 <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a> video produced by Philadelphia's underground music collective We Run the Streets.<sup id="cite_ref-Bailey18_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bailey18-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Her mother drove past the group of men freestyling and encouraged her to join.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> She released several tracks as Dizzle Dizz, including "Dizzy Rascvls", but struggled with <a href="/wiki/Depression_(mood)" title="Depression (mood)">depression</a> in the months following.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Despite still being a teenager, Whack gained notoriety in the streets of Philadelphia as "Dizzle Dizz."<sup id="cite_ref-:5_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Despite her growing fame, Whack grew bored. There was no way to make money in freestyling and no way to creatively grow. She claimed there was little experimentation and growth beyond <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">battle rapping</a> if she were to stay in Philadelphia.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Her mother elected to move them both out of Philadelphia so Whack could finish school.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After moving to Atlanta, Whack began working at a Mister Car Wash, which allowed her to save up for a Mac laptop and start recording her music. She was often the only woman working on the line at the car wash, and her former manager said she was a memorable person with an impressive drive, which Whack credits to her mother.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Atlanta, Whack kept a low profile, having felt the effects of fame back home. She wanted to keep to herself to have a chance to focus solely on her music.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> By the time she returned to Philadelphia, she had a laptop full of two years' worth of music, which she never ended up sharing with anyone.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Whack eventually returned to Philadelphia, leaving her family behind in Atlanta. It was during this time that she was homeless for three months. She hopped around friends' homes but refused to return to Atlanta. She believed staying in Philadelphia would be the best for her music career.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tierra_Whack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.932017:_Career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–2017:_Career_beginnings">2015–2017: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tierra_Whack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2015–2017: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By 2015, Whack had moved back to Philadelphia, and she reconnected with Kenete Simms, a sound engineer and music producer whom she knew from her teenage years.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Whack credits Simms as her collaborator.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It was during this time in which she reconnected with Simms that she learned how to properly mix her own music. Simms helped her learn how to use the equipment in order to help her make music more easily.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, she signed with <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Johnny Montina is her manager.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2017, Whack made her debut as Tierra Whack with 3 tracks, including "Toe Jam" and "Shit Happens".
</p><p>In October 2017, Whack released "Mumbo Jumbo", a hip-hop single and accompanying music video that featured the young rapper performing while wearing a <a href="/wiki/Mouth_prop" title="Mouth prop">mouth prop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Most of the lyrics to the track are purposefully unintelligible.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She also toured with <a href="/wiki/Flying_Lotus" title="Flying Lotus">Flying Lotus</a> in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2018.E2.80.93present:_Whack_World_and_weekly_singles"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–present:_Whack_World_and_weekly_singles">2018–present: <i>Whack World</i> and weekly singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tierra_Whack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2018–present: Whack World and weekly singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Whack's debut fifteen track album, <i><a href="/wiki/Whack_World" title="Whack World">Whack World</a></i>, was released on May 30, 2018, and received a "Best New Music" accolade from <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork</a></i>. Critics praised the unusual format of the album — each song lasting about 1 minute each. Whack released each short track on Instagram, each accompanied by a short film directed by Thibaut Duverneix and Mathieu Léger.<sup id="cite_ref-Coscarelli18_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coscarelli18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The multimedia project received widespread critical acclaim. <a href="/wiki/Robert_Christgau" title="Robert Christgau">Robert Christgau</a> gave the album an A-minus and reported in <i><a href="/wiki/Vice_(magazine)" title="Vice (magazine)">Vice</a></i> that his wife, fellow critic <a href="/wiki/Carola_Dibbell" title="Carola Dibbell">Carola Dibbell</a>, loved the video, saying "it gave me reason for living".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Often referred to as the '<a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>' of this generation, Whack has gained a great amount of applaud for the creativity and eccentricity of her music videos. The track "Mumbo Jumbo" received a nomination for Best Music Video for the <a href="/wiki/61st_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="61st Annual Grammy Awards">2019 Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In October 2018, Whack traveled to <a href="/wiki/Tokyo,_Japan" class="mw-redirect" title="Tokyo, Japan">Tokyo, Japan</a> for a long-term artistic sabbatical.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In an episode of <a href="/wiki/Genius_(website)" class="mw-redirect" title="Genius (website)">Genius</a>'s "The Cosign" series on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> in which current artists who have been in the industry have the opportunity to watch videos of newer artists, rapper <a href="/wiki/Remy_Ma" title="Remy Ma">Remy Ma</a> chose to cosign Whack. Despite the fact that she mentioned seeing potential in each of the artists, Remy said, "I pick her because I feel like she brought the entire package as far as the song, the delivery, the vocals, and the actual visual. I feel like she did something that was super out of the ordinary, and I enjoyed it. And I wanna watch it again." In the episode of The Cosign, Whack's music video for her single "Unemployed" was put in a group with music videos from other female MCs including <a href="/wiki/Kash_Doll" title="Kash Doll">Kash Doll</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cupcakke" title="Cupcakke">CupcakKe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mulatto_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mulatto (rapper)">Mulatto</a>, Tay Money, and That Girl Lay Lay. She has recorded as-yet-unreleased music with <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Donald_Glover" title="Donald Glover">Childish Gambino</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, she toured with <a href="/wiki/6lack" title="6lack">6lack</a> on his world tour, From East Atlanta with Love. Briana Younger, the writer for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fader" title="The Fader">The Fader</a></i>, wrote that "popular and mainstream rap hasn't championed a darker-skinned woman since Missy Elliot...and Whack seems poised to be the one." Whack acknowledged that women in the music industry often aren't afforded the privilege to make art for art's sake, but she refuses to let that inhibit her.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Starting February 19, 2019, Whack released one single per week for five weeks in a series she called "#whackhistorymonth."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> These singles included "Only Child", "Clones", "Gloria", "Wasteland", and "Unemployed".<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>  Whether these tracks will be released under a full-length album has not yet been revealed. Whack has stated that she is not thinking about a timeline for a new album, saying "I'm not gonna drive myself crazy. I'm having fun creating what I'm creating."<sup id="cite_ref-:2_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Whack also collaborated with Flying Lotus on the song "Yellow Belly" for his 2019 album <i><a href="/wiki/Flamagra" title="Flamagra">Flamagra</a></i> and she was later named as one of the members of <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL's</a></i> "2019 Freshman Class" on June 20, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> She appeared at many festivals in the 2019 festival season including <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Primavera_Sound" title="Primavera Sound">Primavera Sound</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Outside_Lands_Music_and_Arts_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Outside Lands Music and Arts Festival">Outside Lands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Camp_Flog_Gnaw_Carnival" title="Camp Flog Gnaw Carnival">Camp Flog Gnaw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osheaga_Festival" title="Osheaga Festival">Osheaga</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Austin_City_Limits_Music_Festival" title="Austin City Limits Music Festival">Austin City Limits</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Her song "Unemployed" is featured in the football game <a href="/wiki/FIFA_20" title="FIFA 20">FIFA 20</a>.
</p><p>Whack co-wrote and was featured in <a href="/wiki/Melanie_Martinez" title="Melanie Martinez">Melanie Martinez</a>'s single "Copy Cat", which was released on February 10, 2020. This marks the first time that Whack has worked in a professional capacity with <a href="/wiki/Melanie_Martinez_discography" title="Melanie Martinez discography">Melanie Martinez</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> In May 2020, Whack would appear alongside American rapper <a href="/wiki/Lil_Yachty" title="Lil Yachty">Lil Yachty</a> on the song "<a href="/wiki/T.D_(song)" title="T.D (song)">T.D</a>" which features rappers <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a>. The song would peak at #84 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a>, marking Whack's first entry on the chart. Whack would feature on the song "Me x 7" by <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a>, taken from her seventh studio album, <a href="/wiki/Alicia_(album)" title="Alicia (album)"><i>Alicia</i></a>, in September 2020.
</p><p>Her 2017 single, "Mumbo Jumbo", had an accompanying music video that garnered a Best Music Video nomination for the <a href="/wiki/61st_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="61st Annual Grammy Awards">2019 Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2020, Whack released her first solo single of the year titled "Dora".<sup id="cite_ref-:9_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In November 2020, Whack appeared in the 2020 Apple holiday commercial "The Magic of Mini" which featured the new singles, "Peppers and Onions" and "feel good" which were released coinciding the commercial.<sup id="cite_ref-SpawnKohane_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SpawnKohane-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2021, Whack released a single titled "Link". The music video, created in partnership with <a href="/wiki/The_Lego_Group" title="The Lego Group">the LEGO Group</a>, had Tierra Whack sit down with a group of schoolchildren as part of <a href="/wiki/Lego" title="Lego">Lego</a>'s "Rebuild the World" campaign, using their ideas as inspiration for the video.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2021, Whack released three EPs, <i>Rap?</i>, <i>Pop?</i> and <i>R&amp;B?</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tierra_Whack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Whack's beginnings in rap started out in battle rapping, and it is something she looks upon fondly. While she appreciates the foundation that it gave her for her music, she wanted to aim higher than just rap battles. Seeing artists such as <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_3000" title="André 3000">Andre 3000</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> had inspired her to become a true artist. In doing so, she would be seen as something more serious than just a battle rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Whack cites <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kelis" title="Kelis">Kelis</a> as some of her musical influences.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Whack claims there are many different things that inspire and motivate her and she wants to be able to portray those to her fans in her music.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> She even credits things such as <a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a> or <a href="/wiki/No,_David!" title="No, David!">No! David</a>  as some of her influences; these are clearly seen through her visuals from her album's music video.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Her inspirations come from everything around her; from her relationships to people to inanimate objects.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Her single, "Only Child", for example, was created after dating a man whose behavior she aligned with that of someone who grew up an only child.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Whack's mother is often credited as one of, if not the, biggest inspirations for her music. She recalls the days her mother would play gangster rap during car rides.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> For her single, "Unemployed", her mother had actually helped her create the hook for the song.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tierra_Whack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Whack has written poetry since she was a child.<sup id="cite_ref-Doreen18_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Doreen18-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Writing was a way for her to deal with her shyness and insecurities.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> She is also an accomplished <a href="/wiki/Spoken_word" title="Spoken word">spoken word</a> performer.<sup id="cite_ref-Bailey18_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bailey18-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She currently lives in Philadelphia with her mother and two siblings.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She is still "Philly-based," saying that Philadelphia is her home and where she feels grounded.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> She owns a cat, whom she named Starkey after a <a href="/wiki/Whole_Foods" class="mw-redirect" title="Whole Foods">Whole Foods</a> brand of sparkling water.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She does not drink alcohol or smoke cigarettes.<sup id="cite_ref-Kameir18_4-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kameir18-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She is severely allergic to insects, a condition she satirizes in the music video for her 2018 track, "Bugs Life".<sup id="cite_ref-Doreen18_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Doreen18-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
