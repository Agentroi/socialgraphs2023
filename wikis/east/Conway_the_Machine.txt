<p><b>Demond Price</b> (born February 16, 1982), known professionally as <b>Conway the Machine</b> (or simply <b>Conway</b>),<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is an American rapper. Alongside his paternal half-brother <a href="/wiki/Westside_Gunn" title="Westside Gunn">Westside Gunn</a> and cousin <a href="/wiki/Benny_the_Butcher" title="Benny the Butcher">Benny the Butcher</a>, Conway was a member of Griselda Records. On February 21, 2022, Conway announced his departure from Griselda Records.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conway_the_Machine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932019:_Early_mixtapes_and_G.O.A.T."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2019:_Early_mixtapes_and_G.O.A.T.">2012–2019: Early mixtapes and <i>G.O.A.T.</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conway_the_Machine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2012–2019: Early mixtapes and G.O.A.T.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At age 23, Conway spent some time in jail, which he has cited as a motivation for weaning from a gangster lifestyle to focus on his career in music.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Conway was shot in the head, neck and shoulder;<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> his face was partially paralyzed, giving him a signature slur.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Westside Gunn founded <a href="/wiki/Griselda_Records" title="Griselda Records">Griselda Records</a>, through which Conway and Westside Gunn would self-release their own projects and the works of artists such as Benny the Butcher and Mach-Hommy.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, Conway released his first two official mixtape projects through Griselda Records, <i>The Devil's Reject</i> and <i>Reject 2</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Conway and Westside Gunn also released two collaborative EPs through London-based record label Daupe!,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> the first of which was 2015's <i>Hall &amp; Nash</i>, followed by 2016's <i>Griselda Ghost</i>. In April 2016, during <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a> and <a href="/wiki/Smif-N-Wessun" title="Smif-N-Wessun">Smif-N-Wessun</a>'s "Hell On Earth" tour, Conway was introduced to <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a> of Mobb Deep, who would become one of his earliest notable collaborators and supporters.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 3, 2017, Griselda Records signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On October 10, 2017, Conway contributed an a cappella rap verse to the 2017 <a href="/wiki/BET_Hip_Hop_Awards" title="BET Hip Hop Awards">BET Hip Hop Awards</a> Shady cypher.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The same year, <a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a> included Westside Gunn and Conway in their list of "15 Best Unofficial Rap Duos in the Game",<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> as well as two of the "17 Artists to Watch in 2017".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following Griselda's deal with Shady Records, Conway's first official release was <i>G.O.A.T.</i> on December 21, 2017, which included production from Griselda's in-house producer Daringer and <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>, and featured <a href="/wiki/Royce_Da_5%279%22" class="mw-redirect" title="Royce Da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce Da 5'9"</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Earlier in 2017, he released a collaborative mixtape with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Green_Lantern" title="DJ Green Lantern">DJ Green Lantern</a> titled <i>More Steroids</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2019.E2.80.93present:_Studio_albums"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–present:_Studio_albums">2019–present: Studio albums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conway_the_Machine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2019–present: Studio albums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On May 16, 2019, Conway, Benny The Butcher and Westside Gunn released their first single with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a> titled "Headlines".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Conway released "<a href="/wiki/Bang_(Conway_The_Machine_single)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bang (Conway The Machine single)">Bang</a>", at the time expected to be the first single from his album <i>God Don't Make Mistakes</i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> on July 19, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <i>God Don't Make Mistakes</i> had been intended to be Conway's debut studio album, and Westside Gunn had stated it was "90 percent complete" in 2018;<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> however, Conway's first studio album would instead be the project <i><a href="/wiki/From_King_to_a_God" title="From King to a God">From King to a God</a></i>, which he released on September 11, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> During this period, Conway continued releasing mixtapes and collaborative albums as well.
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/God_Don%27t_Make_Mistakes" title="God Don&#39;t Make Mistakes">God Don't Make Mistakes</a></i> was ultimately released on February 25, 2022, as Conway's second studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
