<p><b>Greg Hogan</b> (born May 15, 1985), better known by his stage name <b>Frenchie</b>, is an American rapper. He was in <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>'s <a href="/wiki/1017_Records" title="1017 Records">1017 Brick Squad</a> and <a href="/wiki/1017_Records" title="1017 Records">So Icey Boyz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Waka_Flocka_Flame" title="Waka Flocka Flame">Waka Flocka Flame</a>'s <a href="/wiki/1017_Records" title="1017 Records">Brick Squad Monopoly</a>. Frenchie is credited for introducing <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> to Debra's Mizay Entertainment before their mainstream breakouts.<sup id="cite_ref-Power105_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Power105-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  He is currently serving a twelve year prison sentence after being convicted of burglary charges in <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_County,_New_York" title="Suffolk County, New York">Suffolk County</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-patch_article_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-patch_article-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenchie_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frenchie was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, New York.<sup id="cite_ref-AHH2014_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AHH2014-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He spent much of his life travelling back and forth between Queens and <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> His cousin is Southern rapper Waka Flocka Flame.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusicbio_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicbio-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His childhood friend is East Coast rapper <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and his neighbors included other members who founded <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a> namely <a href="/wiki/Tony_Yayo" title="Tony Yayo">Tony Yayo</a> and <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SouthsideStory_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SouthsideStory-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBA_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> According to interviews Frenchie's father Big Frenchie was a notable <a href="/wiki/Gangster" title="Gangster">gangster</a> and <a href="/wiki/Illegal_drug_trade" title="Illegal drug trade">drug dealer</a> in <a href="/wiki/Borough_(New_York_City)" class="mw-redirect" title="Borough (New York City)">borough</a> in the early 1990s who 50 Cent referenced and idolized in his earlier work most notably on 50 Bars and Ghetto Quran.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBA_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Frenchie wanted to become a rapper having been inspired after watching <a href="/wiki/Kris_Kross" title="Kris Kross">Kris Kross</a>' song "<a href="/wiki/Jump_(Kris_Kross_song)" title="Jump (Kris Kross song)">Jump</a>" on <a href="/wiki/Video_Music_Box" title="Video Music Box">Video Music Box</a> at an early age.<sup id="cite_ref-EME_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EME-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He is of <a href="/wiki/Guyanese_people" title="Guyanese people">Guyanese</a> ancestry.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenchie_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frenchie hails from a musical family background with several of his relatives in the industry. His cousin Bimmy was an <a href="/wiki/Artists_and_repertoire" title="Artists and repertoire">A&amp;R</a> for <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> while his uncle from his father's side <a href="/wiki/DJ_Hurricane" title="DJ Hurricane">DJ Hurricane</a> worked with <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Jus24x7_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jus24x7-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> His cousin/aunt<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (December 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Debra Antney was manager to Gucci Mane and <a href="/wiki/OJ_da_Juiceman" title="OJ da Juiceman">OJ Da Juiceman</a>. Frenchie was rapping when he was 19 and would go on to record his first professionally produced track Shirt Off in late 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-AHH2014_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AHH2014-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Prior to the record appeared in various street DVDs performing freestyles and cyphers.
</p>
<h2><span id="2008.E2.80.932011:_So_Icey_Entertainment.2C_So_Icey_Boyz_and_1017_Brick_Squad"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–2011:_So_Icey_Entertainment,_So_Icey_Boyz_and_1017_Brick_Squad">2008–2011: So Icey Entertainment, So Icey Boyz and 1017 Brick Squad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenchie_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008–2011: So Icey Entertainment, So Icey Boyz and 1017 Brick Squad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During a trip to Atlanta to attend a family reunion Frenchie's aunt Debra Antney had introduced him to Gucci Mane who signed him to his So Icey Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-DJBoogz_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJBoogz-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The song "Shirt Off" from Gucci Mane's The Movie mixtape released in September 2008 which featured Frenchie, Wooh Da Kid and OJ da Juiceman as <a href="/wiki/1017_Records" title="1017 Records">So Icey Boyz</a> – would be Frenchie's breakout track becoming a hit on Atlanta radio.<sup id="cite_ref-BBA_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Since then he had been managed by Debra Antney's Mizay Entertainment.
</p><p>Frenchie released his debut mixtape French Connection on February 22, 2009, hosted by DJ Big Mike and Trap-A-Holics.<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape01_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape01-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Later in the year his camp would go on to form 1017 Brick Squad. His second mixtape <i>Chicken Room</i>, released on September 15,<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape02_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape02-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> would be his first under the new imprint. Frenchie would be featured on "Candy Lady" from Gucci Mane's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Burrprint" title="The Burrprint">The Burrprint</a></i> released in October 2009 alongside OJ da Juiceman, Wooh Da Kid and Waka Flocka Flame which would be the second and last time 1017 Brick Squad collaborated on one song since Shirt Off the year prior.<sup id="cite_ref-DJBoogz_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJBoogz-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to interviews Frenchie has been the one to introduce Nicki Minaj to Debra Antney who then became her manager,prior to the latter's mainstream breakout.<sup id="cite_ref-Power105_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Power105-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with DJ Lazy K in December 2009 Frenchie announced plans for an upcoming collaborative tape with French Montana.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Although the tape never materialized, Montana would go on to collaborate with Frenchie and others from Brick Squad the following year. Frenchie had introduced Montana to Debra who would sign him later in the year.<sup id="cite_ref-Power105_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Power105-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EME_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EME-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In March 13, 2010, Frenchie's friend and frequent collaborator G-Baby was shot and killed in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica,_Queens" title="Jamaica, Queens">Jamaica, Queens</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The two had collaborated on several tracks off of Frenchie's fifth mixtape Trap To The Future released on March 29,<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape05_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape05-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and his sixth mixtape French Connection Part 2 released on July 18.<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape06_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape06-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="2011.E2.80.932014:_Brick_Squad_Monopoly"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–2014:_Brick_Squad_Monopoly">2011–2014: Brick Squad Monopoly</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenchie_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2011–2014: Brick Squad Monopoly">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Starting in 2011 Frenchie would spend most of his time in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> and touring with <a href="/wiki/1017_Records" title="1017 Records">1017 Brick Squad</a> and Mizay Entertainment. That year Waka Flocka Flame would go on to form <a href="/wiki/1017_Records" title="1017 Records">Brick Squad Monopoly</a> with Frenchie as one of the original members. He released his eighth mixtape Bringing Gangsta Back on June 25<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape08_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape08-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and his ninth mixtape <i>Concrete Jungle</i> on October 17.<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape09_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape09-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In December 2011, fellow BSM member Slim Dunkin was shot to death during an altercation.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In 2012 Frenchie released his tenth mixtape French-Elo Anthony on March 10,<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape11_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape11-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and his eleventh mixtape Concrete Jungle 2 on December 17.<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape10_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape10-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 21, 2013, he released his twelfth mixtape Long Over Due<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape13_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape13-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> aptly titled so having felt the need to showcase a solo effort with fewer guest features compared to his previous records.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Later that year, Gucci Mane and Waka Flocka Flame would become embroiled in a rather public falling-out, with Frenchie noticeably on the side of his cousin Waka Flocka.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> However, when asked about the incident a year later, Frenchie affirmed that the event was blown out of proportion by the media and they were all currently on good terms.<sup id="cite_ref-AHH2014_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AHH2014-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DJBoogz_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJBoogz-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2013 Frenchie released singles Power Move and Don't Know Em which peaked at <a href="/wiki/Mediabase" title="Mediabase">Mediabase</a> urban charts at #44 and #89, respectively. He appeared on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s RapFix Live on March 27,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and on the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> show <a href="/wiki/Black_Ink_Crew" title="Black Ink Crew">Black Ink Crew</a> which aired on October 7.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On December 29, his cousin and fellow BSM member KayO Redd committed suicide.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> KayO who is Debra's youngest son died from a self-inflicted gunshot.
</p><p>In 2014 Frenchie released a collaborative mixtape entitled Underrated with D-Dash on January 14,<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape14_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape14-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> and released his fourteenth mixtape <i>Long Over Due 2</i> on March 3.<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape15_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape15-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> It would be his last releases under Brick Squad Monopoly and manager Debra Antney with Frenchie venturing off under his own label later in the year. Having accompanied Waka Flocka Flame, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Aoki" title="Steve Aoki">Steve Aoki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Borgore" title="Borgore">Borgore</a> on tour the year before, he also showed a new-found interest in <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">EDM</a> which he expressed plans to pursue in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="2014.E2.80.93present:_Protekted_Records"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–present:_Protekted_Records">2014–present: Protekted Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenchie_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2014–present: Protekted Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 9, 2014, Frenchie announced he was joining Protekted Records an independent label he helped found with Karen Fraiberg and Jon Gornbein.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Under the new management, he would go on to release his fifteenth mixtape on September 16 entitled <i>Fukk Fame</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape16_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape16-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> That year, he released the single Ain't Goin Nowhere featuring <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chanel_West_Coast" title="Chanel West Coast">Chanel West Coast</a>, which peaked at #29 on <a href="/wiki/Mediabase" title="Mediabase">Mediabase</a> urban charts.<sup id="cite_ref-Jus24x7_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jus24x7-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, Frenchie released a collaborative mixtape entitled <i>Phil Jackson</i> with his Artist rapper/producer Jusjoose on November 8.<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape17_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape17-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> His seventeenth mixtape Chicken Room 2 came out in 2015.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criminal_conviction">Criminal conviction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenchie_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Criminal conviction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On June 22, 2022, Frenchie was convicted in <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_County,_New_York" title="Suffolk County, New York">Suffolk County</a> of burglary for an armed home invasion which occurred in 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Frenchie was determined to have been one of seven men, some armed with handguns, who entered the home of a sleeping family because they incorrectly believed there was $100,000 worth of marijuana inside.<sup id="cite_ref-fox5_article_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fox5_article-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On April 6, 2023, Frenchie was sentenced to twelve years in prison for this crime.<sup id="cite_ref-patch_article_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-patch_article-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
