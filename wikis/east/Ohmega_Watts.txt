					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American rapper</div>

font-size: 125%;"><div class="fn">Ohmega Watts</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Milton Campbell</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Origin</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">2004 to present</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Ubiquity_Records" title="Ubiquity Records">Ubiquity Records</a><br />Tres Records<br />Mellow Orange</td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Ohmega Watts</b>, real name <b>Milton Campbell</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> producer and vocalist from <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a>. He releases music under the name Ohmega Watts and as a part of the Lightheaded crew.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ohmega_Watts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Campbell was born and raised in Brooklyn, New York, by West Indian Parents from Jamaica. He lived in Brooklyn for eight years before moving to Laurelton, Queens, New York.
</p><p>He grew up listening to many types of music including <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calypso_music" title="Calypso music">calypso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soca_music" title="Soca music">soca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Death_metal" title="Death metal">death metal</a>, 80's pop and <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip-hop</a>, but only started to take a serious interest in music when he was around 11 years old. He gravitated towards hip-hop, listening to "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Age_Hip_Hop&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Golden Age Hip Hop (page does not exist)">Golden Age</a>" artists including <a href="/wiki/Run%E2%80%93D.M.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Run–D.M.C.">Run–D.M.C.</a>, <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dana_Dane" title="Dana Dane">Dana Dane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boogie_Down_Productions" title="Boogie Down Productions">BDP</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_B." title="Eric B.">Eric B.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rakim" title="Rakim">Rakim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pete_Rock" title="Pete Rock">Pete Rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/CL_Smooth" title="CL Smooth">CL Smooth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gang_Starr" title="Gang Starr">Gang Starr</a>, but the artists who influenced him the most sonically / production wise were artists in the <a href="/wiki/Native_Tongues_Posse" class="mw-redirect" title="Native Tongues Posse">Native Tongues Posse</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/A_Tribe_Called_Quest" title="A Tribe Called Quest">A Tribe Called Quest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jungle_Brothers" title="Jungle Brothers">Jungle Brothers</a>, <a href="/wiki/De_La_Soul" title="De La Soul">De La Soul</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Black_Sheep_(group)" title="Black Sheep (group)">Black Sheep</a>.
</p><p>Ohmega Watts' first release was “A Request”, a soulful and DJ-friendly solo 12" featuring his group Lightheaded and, former hip hop trio, <a href="/wiki/The_Procussions" title="The Procussions">The Procussions</a>. “A Request” received praise from respected DJs, including  Mr. Scruff, Gilles Peterson, Greyboy, and the Los Angeles-based Heavyweight Record Pool. Ohmega Watts' sound was compared to playfulness of Digital Underground and big drums and chopped samples the likes of Pete Rock.
</p><p>A record deal with <a href="/wiki/Ubiquity_Records" title="Ubiquity Records">Ubiquity Records</a> was signed shortly after this first release. Ohmega Watts' first solo album, "The Find", was released on Ubiquity in 2005.
</p><p>Producing since 1997 and MCing since 1993 the Ohmega Watts resume includes tracks produced for Mars Ill on Ill Boogie, for Listener on Mush, plus he’s recorded as Lightheaded for Day By Day Ent, and as Return To Sender on the Piece of The Action compilation. Hailing from Flatbush, Brooklyn, brought up by Jamaican parents Campbell moved to Florida for college and then settled in Portland, Oregon. He is now part of a bustling Northwest independent scene that includes the Lifesavas, Boom Bap, Soul Plasma, DJ and MC, Libretto, Lightheaded and The Blacknotes.
</p><p>He has produced for Beautiful Eulogy and also produced the entire Half Dead EP by Cataphant.
</p>
