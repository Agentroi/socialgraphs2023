<p><b>Mach-Hommy</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haitian</a>-<a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> rapper and record producer. He is well-known for his reclusive nature, concealing his real name and face in all public appearances.<sup id="cite_ref-GQ_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GQ-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This behavior has led him to be described as "one of hip-hop's most elusive artists".<sup id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mach-Hommy has gained a <a href="/wiki/Cult_following" title="Cult following">cult following</a> as a result of his work ethic and elusiveness. His lyrics are often heavily related to the social and political history of his Haitian heritage. Though he predominantly raps in English, he also frequently incorporates <a href="/wiki/Haitian_Creole" title="Haitian Creole">Haitian Kreyòl</a> into his lyrics. He forbids lyric sites to post transcriptions of the words to his songs,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and he charges hundreds and even thousands of dollars for his releases, most of which are not available on <a href="/wiki/Streaming_services" class="mw-redirect" title="Streaming services">streaming services</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mach-Hommy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mach-Hommy was raised predominantly in <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a>, but also spent much of his childhood in <a href="/wiki/Port-au-Prince" title="Port-au-Prince">Port-au-Prince</a>. His father was a folk guitarist, and Mach credits his father's musical style as an influence on his own.<sup id="cite_ref-GQ_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GQ-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The first music Mach recorded was at the age of 13, when he performed a <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a> over the beat to <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>'s "Verbal Intercourse".<sup id="cite_ref-fader-part1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fader-part1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his early career, Mach found the music recording process to be an "unhealthy" environment and sought to take up filmmaking instead. However, at the encouragement of <a href="/wiki/Westside_Gunn" title="Westside Gunn">Westside Gunn</a>, he returned to rapping in the early 2010s.<sup id="cite_ref-fader-part2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fader-part2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mach-Hommy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mach-Hommy first received widespread attention after the release of his 2016 album, <i>H.B.O. (Haitian Body Odor)</i>, which he sold via <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GQ_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GQ-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Mach pressed only 187 copies of the <i>H.B.O.</i> CD and sold them for $300 apiece, before eventually uploading the album to <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-DJBooth_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJBooth-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He released numerous other albums in the following years, many of which were sold at high prices through services like <a href="/wiki/Bandcamp" title="Bandcamp">Bandcamp</a> and never released on streaming services.<sup id="cite_ref-GQ_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GQ-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2021, Mach-Hommy released his album <i><a href="/wiki/Pray_for_Haiti" title="Pray for Haiti">Pray for Haiti</a></i> on most streaming services. Mach stated that he would donate his share of profits from the album to the Pray for Haiti Trust Fund, a charity he established to fund educational services and institutions in Haiti.<sup id="cite_ref-GQ_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GQ-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i>Pray for Haiti</i> also brought Mach a greater level of attention from the music press, with <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork</a></i> honoring the album with a "Best New Music" award and <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> describing it as "a modern hip-hop classic".<sup id="cite_ref-PrayForHaiti-PF_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PrayForHaiti-PF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PrayForHaiti-RS_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PrayForHaiti-RS-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mach-Hommy has intermittently associated with the <a href="/wiki/Griselda_Records" title="Griselda Records">Griselda Records</a> label. Though Mach was once considered part of the Griselda collective, he separated from them after a falling-out with <a href="/wiki/Westside_Gunn" title="Westside Gunn">Westside Gunn</a>, and as of 2017 Mach-Hommy was unaffiliated with the label.<sup id="cite_ref-DJBooth_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJBooth-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PrayForHaiti-PF_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PrayForHaiti-PF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Mach and Gunn eventually reconciled in late 2020, as Westside Gunn served as an executive producer of <i>Pray for Haiti</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-PrayForHaiti-PF_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PrayForHaiti-PF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mach-Hommy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In an interview with <a href="/wiki/Billboard.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard.com">Billboard.com</a>, Mach stated that he was fluent in the majority of the <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">Romance languages</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mach-Hommy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mach-Hommy cites a wide range of writers as influential upon him, including <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinua_Achebe" title="Chinua Achebe">Chinua Achebe</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Orwell" title="George Orwell">George Orwell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lorraine_Hansberry" title="Lorraine Hansberry">Lorraine Hansberry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fyodor_Dostoevsky" title="Fyodor Dostoevsky">Fyodor Dostoevsky</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dante" class="mw-redirect" title="Dante">Dante</a>. He also cites political and philosophical thinkers such as <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_X" title="Malcolm X">Malcolm X</a>, <a href="/wiki/C._L._R._James" title="C. L. R. James">C. L. R. James</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Aquinas" title="Thomas Aquinas">Thomas Aquinas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Nietzsche" title="Friedrich Nietzsche">Friedrich Nietzsche</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fader-part1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fader-part1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
