<p><b>Mike Jaggerr</b> (born August 20, 1989) is a <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a> and former band member of the hip-hop group Bassline.  Mike was born in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>, raised in <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_Delaware" title="Wilmington, Delaware">Wilmington, Delaware</a> and currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_NY" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, NY">Brooklyn, NY</a>.  Mike is also a graduate from <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>’s <a href="/wiki/Full_Sail_University" title="Full Sail University">Full Sail University</a> and studied film.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Eleventh_Hour"><i>The Eleventh Hour</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Jaggerr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The Eleventh Hour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mike has released his debut <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> titled <i>The Eleventh Hour</i> (January 11, 2011) where he served as the exclusive producer, rapper and <a href="/wiki/Singing" title="Singing">singer</a>.  The mixtape release party was celebrated February 4, 2011 in Wilmington, Delaware and TheWakeUpShow/MTV’s Sway was host.  Mike also performed, February 8, 2011, at New York’s Art Center during the Faces in the Crowd showcase.
</p><p>Its lead single is "Away", which is an opening and introductory song about his family and troubled childhood.  Mike raps, "Promised my Pop I wouldn’t cry a tear / but sometimes I get so tired, Goodyear / name the last time I had a good year with no dysfunction / guess it’s my curse conjunction / to stay in this pain of world I know / got so much pain I got to let it show/but I can’t live like this I got to let it go".
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">video</a> has been shot by the productive team Creative Control for "Away", who have also produced for <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Currensy" title="Currensy">Curren$y</a>.  A fellow buzz track has been Mike's collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam</a>’s <a href="/wiki/Big_K.R.I.T." title="Big K.R.I.T.">Big K.R.I.T.</a> for "Cruise Control".  The tastemaker website DJBooth.net exclusively posted "Cruise Control" as well as XXLmag.com, VIBE.com, and <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a> <a href="/wiki/Mick_Boogie" class="mw-redirect" title="Mick Boogie">Mick Boogie</a> chose the song as his "jam of the week".
</p><p>The mixtape <i>The Eleventh Hour</i> has received Internet recognition and sponsorship from influential blogs and websites such as <a href="/wiki/AllHipHop" title="AllHipHop">AllHipHop</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> where they stated in reference to his story; "Often times it is through the story of others that we learn more about ourselves.  Regardless of the relevance, there are simply those aspects of  struggle and triumph that evoke emotion. When reading Mike Jaggerr’s 'story,'  I became excited." DJBooth.net<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> also featured Mike's effort as did Datpiff.com,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which awarded <i>The Eleventh Hour</i> with five stars.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Jaggerr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mike's been featured on NodFactor.com,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Atlanta's <i>AUC</i> magazine, on BET.com<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Tonight" title="Entertainment Tonight">Entertainment Tonight</a></i> host Kevin Frazier's HipHollywood.com.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  The project has earned a 98/100 rating on <a href="/wiki/ThisIsRealMusic.com" title="ThisIsRealMusic.com">ThisIsRealMusic.com</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  <i>TheSource</i><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> magazine also tapped the rapper for a spotlight in their legendary Unsigned Hype column.
</p><p><i>The Eleventh Hour</i> has also been posted on RapRadar.com,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  2DopeBoyz.com<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Okayplayer" title="Okayplayer">OkayPlayer.com</a> among others. Additionally, New York's Power 105's morning host Angela Yee tapped the set as her "Mix of the Week"<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/KIIS-FM" title="KIIS-FM">KIIS-FM</a>’s <a href="/wiki/DJ_Skee" title="DJ Skee">DJ Skee</a> selected Mike Jaggerr’s single "Away" (track 27) to be included on his exclusive <a href="/wiki/MetroPCS" class="mw-redirect" title="MetroPCS">MetroPCS</a> sponsored <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl" title="Super Bowl">Super Bowl</a>/<a href="/wiki/NBA_All-Star_Game" title="NBA All-Star Game">NBA All-Star</a> mixtape "Android for All",<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> released February 20, 2011.
