<p><b>Ackquille Jean Pollard</b> (born August 4, 1994),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Bobby Shmurda</b>, is an American rapper. Along with <a href="/wiki/Rowdy_Rebel" title="Rowdy Rebel">Rowdy Rebel</a>, Shmurda is considered a pioneer of <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_drill" title="Brooklyn drill">Brooklyn drill</a> music.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He rose to international fame in 2014 when his song "<a href="/wiki/Hot_Nigga" title="Hot Nigga">Hot Nigga</a>" peaked at number six on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-US-100_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-US-100-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  The success of the single led to Shmurda signing a deal with <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a>. His debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Shmurda_She_Wrote" title="Shmurda She Wrote">Shmurda She Wrote</a></i>, was released in November 2014.
</p><p>In December 2014, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Police" class="mw-redirect" title="New York City Police">New York City Police</a> arrested Shmurda and charged him, along with several other members of <a href="/wiki/GS9" title="GS9">GS9</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Conspiracy_to_murder" title="Conspiracy to murder">conspiracy to murder</a>, weapons possession, and reckless endangerment.<sup id="cite_ref-WaPo_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WaPo-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, he pleaded guilty and was sentenced to seven years in prison, which was reduced to five years after receiving credit for the two years he already served awaiting trial.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> After over six years in prison, Shmurda was released from <a href="/wiki/Clinton_Correctional_Facility" title="Clinton Correctional Facility">Clinton Correctional Facility</a> in February 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> His release was widely celebrated across the hip-hop community.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Ackquille Pollard was born in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dailybeast1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailybeast1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His mother is <a href="/wiki/African-American" class="mw-redirect" title="African-American">African-American</a> and his father is <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaican</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  His mother moved to <a href="/wiki/East_Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="East Flatbush, Brooklyn">East Flatbush</a> from Florida after his father's incarceration.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
Pollard had run-ins with the law while living in Brooklyn, including fifteen months spent in detention for probation violation and being arrested on <a href="/wiki/Gun_charges" class="mw-redirect" title="Gun charges">gun charges</a> that were later dropped. According to his 2014 indictment, Pollard was the ringleader of a criminal enterprise called "GS9" that regularly entered into disputes with criminal gangs, was responsible for murders and non-fatal shootings, and engaged in <a href="/wiki/Illegal_drug_trade" title="Illegal drug trade">drug trafficking</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> along <a href="/wiki/Kings_Highway_(Brooklyn)" title="Kings Highway (Brooklyn)">Kings Highway</a> to <a href="/wiki/East_Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="East Flatbush, Brooklyn">East Flatbush</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<p>The first song that he remixed was <a href="/wiki/Crime_Mob" title="Crime Mob">Crime Mob</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Knuck_If_You_Buck" title="Knuck If You Buck">Knuck If You Buck</a>";<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> however, he did not receive any  attention until 2014, with the release of his song "<a href="/wiki/Hot_Nigga" title="Hot Nigga">Hot Nigga</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-jamaica_observer1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jamaica_observer1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The song uses the instrumental from <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>' 2012 song "Jackpot".<sup id="cite_ref-fader1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fader1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The song and its accompanying <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> went viral shortly after being uploaded to YouTube in the spring of 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Shmurda's signature "Shmoney dance", which he performs in the video, soon became an <a href="/wiki/Internet_meme" title="Internet meme">internet meme</a> and was featured in numerous popular <a href="/wiki/Vine_(service)" title="Vine (service)">Vines</a> from the latter half of 2014;<sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphop1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphop1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> it was also replicated by <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> during their <a href="/wiki/On_the_Run_Tour_(Beyonc%C3%A9_and_Jay-Z)" title="On the Run Tour (Beyoncé and Jay-Z)">On the Run Tour</a><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and by Canadian rapper <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a> while hosting the 2014 <a href="/wiki/ESPY_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="ESPY Awards">ESPY Awards</a>, among other celebrities.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Freestyles over the instrumental were made by a number of rappers after its release, including <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gunplay_(rapper)" title="Gunplay (rapper)">Gunplay</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Following the song's viral success, Shmurda was signed to <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a>, and the song was officially released as his debut single under Epic.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The song topped the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_songs" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop songs</a> chart, and peaked at number 6 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a>, eventually being certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a>. The music video received over 649 million views on YouTube as of July 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-billpake_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billpake-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The song's official <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remix</a>—featuring <a href="/wiki/Guest_vocals" class="mw-redirect" title="Guest vocals">guest vocals</a> from <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rowdy_Rebel" title="Rowdy Rebel">Rowdy Rebel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yo_Gotti" title="Yo Gotti">Yo Gotti</a>—was released on September 4, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphop1_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphop1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> remix of the song was also released in August 2014 that featured <a href="/wiki/Junior_Reid" title="Junior Reid">Junior Reid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mavado_(singer)" title="Mavado (singer)">Mavado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Popcaan" title="Popcaan">Popcaan</a>, and Jah X.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Shmurda also became known for his song "<a href="/wiki/Bobby_Bitch" title="Bobby Bitch">Bobby Bitch</a>" which peaked at number 92 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. His debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Shmurda_She_Wrote" title="Shmurda She Wrote">Shmurda She Wrote</a></i>, was released on November 10, 2014.
</p><p>Shmurda's debut studio album with <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a> was scheduled for release in 2016 and was going to be produced by <a href="/wiki/Jahlil_Beats" title="Jahlil Beats">Jahlil Beats</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fader1_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fader1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It was postponed, however, due to his imprisonment. In February 2017, Shmurda freestyled for rapper <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a> and claimed that he was still writing music while in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The following year, Shmurda featured on fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/6ix9ine" title="6ix9ine">6ix9ine</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Stoopid_(6ix9ine_song)" title="Stoopid (6ix9ine song)">Stoopid</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6ix9ine_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6ix9ine-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> phoning in his verses over the prison telephone. The single peaked at number 25 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100.
</p><p>Shmurda made his first concert performance appearance since being released from prison at Rolling Loud Festival in Miami, Florida on Friday, July 23, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 3, 2021, Shmurda released his first single since his release from jail, "No Time for Sleep (Freestyle)".<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> On March 27, 2022, after years of conflict with Epic Records, Bobby Shmurda's request for release was approved and finalized.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criminal_conviction">Criminal conviction</span></h2>
<p>On June 3, 2014, Shmurda was arrested and charged with felony criminal possession of a weapon. Police say they saw him flashing the gun in an apartment, and when they went to investigate he tried to hide it in a couch. He was set free on <a href="/wiki/US$" class="mw-redirect" title="US$">US$</a>10,000 bail.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> On December 17, 2014, police arrested Shmurda and charged him with conspiracy, <a href="/wiki/Reckless_endangerment" class="mw-redirect" title="Reckless endangerment">reckless endangerment</a>, and drug and gun possession; charges against the others included murder, attempted murder, assault, and drug dealing.<sup id="cite_ref-WaPo_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WaPo-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  Shmurda pleaded not guilty and was held on $2 million <a href="/wiki/Bail" title="Bail">bail</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Police said they had been investigating the gang for murder and shooting indiscriminately at crowds in public places long before Shmurda rose to fame. Police said Shmurda was "the driving force" in a gang also known as GS9 (standing for "Grimey Shooters," "Gun Squad" or "G Stone Crips."),<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> the name of his label, as they dealt <a href="/wiki/Crack_cocaine" title="Crack cocaine">crack cocaine</a> and waged deadly battles with rival <a href="/wiki/Gangs_in_the_United_States" title="Gangs in the United States">gangs</a> for territory. He faced a maximum sentence of 8–25 years.<sup id="cite_ref-Yahoo_News_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo_News-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>James Essig, head of the <a href="/wiki/NYPD" class="mw-redirect" title="NYPD">NYPD</a> unit that made the arrests, said Shmurda's songs and videos were "almost like a real-life document of what they were doing on the street."<sup id="cite_ref-Yahoo_News_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo_News-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> In his song "<a href="/wiki/Hot_Nigga" title="Hot Nigga">Hot Nigga</a>", Shmurda rapped that "I been selling crack since like the fifth grade" because "Jaja taught me"; Shmurda also rapped about his crew's past and future murders.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Shmurda has asserted that the lyrics represent his real life in several interviews, though the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_New_Jersey" title="Supreme Court of New Jersey">Supreme Court of New Jersey</a> recently ruled that lyrics cannot be read at trial as evidence unless they have a "strong nexus" to a specific crime.<sup id="cite_ref-WaPo_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WaPo-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> During his jail time, Shmurda was involved in a fight suspected to be related to the <a href="/wiki/Bloods" title="Bloods">Bloods</a> vs. <a href="/wiki/Crips" title="Crips">Crips</a> rivalry.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Between late June and early July 2015, the rapper and his ex-girlfriend were caught by officials after smuggling a knife into Rikers Island jail.<sup id="cite_ref-Vice_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vice-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Both were charged with two counts of promoting contraband, in addition to one count of criminal possession of a weapon in the fourth degree, which could result in an additional seven-year sentence. Shmurda appeared in the Bronx Supreme Court to face his charges of prison contraband plus 25 years for his previous charge in late 2014. He and his ex-girlfriend pleaded not guilty to the contraband charge, but Shmurda was still facing a maximum sentence of 25 years for the drug and gun charges.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conviction">Conviction</span></h3>
<p>On September 9, 2016, as part of a <a href="/wiki/Plea_deal" class="mw-redirect" title="Plea deal">plea deal</a>, Shmurda pleaded guilty to one count of third-degree conspiracy and one count of weapons possession, and was sentenced to seven years in prison. Under the deal, Shmurda could not appeal the sentence, but was to be given credit for two years served, leaving five years left on his sentence, followed by five years of probation.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> His lawyer, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Spiro" title="Alex Spiro">Alex Spiro</a>, expected that with good behavior Shmurda would only serve approximately three and a half years.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> In early 2017, Shmurda was given a four-year sentence for sneaking a makeshift knife (<a href="/wiki/Shiv_(weapon)" title="Shiv (weapon)">shiv</a>) into his cell. The sentence was to run concurrently to his ongoing seven-year sentence, so that he would not face any additional prison time.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Incarceration">Incarceration</span></h3>
<p>Shmurda was first imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Rikers_Island" title="Rikers Island">Rikers Island</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> He was involved in numerous incidents while incarcerated. In May 2015, Shmurda took part in a brawl that included members from the Bloods and Crips street gangs.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> In November 2016, Shmurda was involved in a brawl between several inmates that led to him losing phone privileges for an unnamed period of time and being subject to <a href="/wiki/Solitary_confinement" title="Solitary confinement">solitary confinement</a> for a month.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, he was moved to <a href="/wiki/Clinton_Correctional_Facility" title="Clinton Correctional Facility">Clinton Correctional Facility</a> in upstate New York, to finish his sentence in <a href="/wiki/Protective_custody" title="Protective custody">protective custody</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Images of Shmurda while incarcerated surfaced online after three years served of the five-year sentence.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span></h3>
<p>A hearing in the week of August 18, 2020 denied Shmurda parole.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Shmurda's conditional release date was set to December 11, 2020, after serving his full sentence, by his parole board.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> However, after a review by the Department of Corrections, Shmurda's credit for good institutional behavior was restored, qualifying him for conditional release on February 23, 2021, with the rest of his sentence to be served on parole.<sup id="cite_ref-bobbyrelease1_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bobbyrelease1-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bobbyrelease2_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bobbyrelease2-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Shmurda was released on February 23, 2021 under supervision by fellow community members in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Kings County, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> He will be under parole until the end of his full sentence on February 23, 2026.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> Rapper <a href="/wiki/Quavo" title="Quavo">Quavo</a> picked up Shmurda from prison on February 23, 2021, after stating in an interview with <i>Billboard</i> that he was "...personally (gonna go) pick up Bobby Shmurda."<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
