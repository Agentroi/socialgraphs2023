<p><b>Paul Nathaniel Barman</b> (born October 30, 1974), better known by his stage name <b>MC Paul Barman</b>, is an American rapper and illustrator. Having emerged during the early 2000s, Barman became a definitive voice within the realm of <a href="/wiki/Alternative_hip_hop" title="Alternative hip hop">alternative hip hop</a>, noted for his intricate rhyme schemes and humorous narrative style, which he infuses with an array of literary devices. He is particularly recognized for his usage of <a href="/wiki/Category:Word_games" title="Category:Word games">word games</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Acrostic" title="Acrostic">acrostics</a><sup id="cite_ref-SeattleT09_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SeattleT09-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Palindrome" title="Palindrome">palindromes</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Brown03_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown03-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Crimson03_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Crimson03-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> in crafting his rap lyrics. The SymmyS Awards, a competition once organized by <a href="/wiki/The_Palindromist" title="The Palindromist">The Palindromist</a>, had Barman as a past panel judge.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Barman's complex and multilayered approach to songwriting has earned him both acclaim and attention, marking him as a distinctive and influential figure within the alternative hip hop scene.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Paul_Barman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barman is from <a href="/wiki/Ridgewood,_New_Jersey" title="Ridgewood, New Jersey">Ridgewood, New Jersey</a>. He is Jewish. He attended <a href="/wiki/Brown_University" title="Brown University">Brown University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Paul_Barman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MC Paul Barman released his debut EP, <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_Very_Stimulating" title="It&#39;s Very Stimulating">It's Very Stimulating</a></i>, on Wordsound in 2000; it was produced by <a href="/wiki/Prince_Paul_(producer)" title="Prince Paul (producer)">Prince Paul</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His first studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Paullelujah!" title="Paullelujah!">Paullelujah!</a></i>, was released on Coup d'État in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Thought_Balloon_Mushroom_Cloud" title="Thought Balloon Mushroom Cloud">Thought Balloon Mushroom Cloud</a></i>, on Househusband in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has toured with <a href="/wiki/Blackalicious" title="Blackalicious">Blackalicious</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He has taught a hip hop class to high school kids at the <a href="/wiki/Bank_Street_College_of_Education" title="Bank Street College of Education">Bank Street College of Education</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, he released <i>(((Echo Chamber)))</i>, his first studio album since 2009's <i>Thought Balloon Mushroom Cloud</i>, on <a href="/wiki/Mello_Music_Group" title="Mello Music Group">Mello Music Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It included productions from <a href="/wiki/MF_Doom" title="MF Doom">MF Doom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Questlove" title="Questlove">Questlove</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Ronson" title="Mark Ronson">Mark Ronson</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> as well as guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Open_Mike_Eagle" title="Open Mike Eagle">Open Mike Eagle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masta_Ace" title="Masta Ace">Masta Ace</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_influences">Musical style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Paul_Barman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Musical style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> called his music "a surreal departure from the rap norm".<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> His music has been influenced by <a href="/wiki/Boogie_Down_Productions" title="Boogie Down Productions">Boogie Down Productions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jungle_Brothers" title="Jungle Brothers">Jungle Brothers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Lyte" title="MC Lyte">MC Lyte</a>, and <a href="/wiki/De_La_Soul" title="De La Soul">De La Soul</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
