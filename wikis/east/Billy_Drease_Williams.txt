<p><b>Edreys Wajed</b>, also known as <b>Billy Drease Williams</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> artist, <a href="/wiki/Emcee" class="mw-redirect" title="Emcee">emcee</a>, singer and producer from <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a>; known for his uptempo production, clean lyrics, and motivational messages. Billy Drease Williams started as part of a short lived hip hop group called The Elements (formed with <a href="/wiki/Soulive" title="Soulive">Soulive</a> members Alan Evans and Neal Evans) and later was one half of a duo known as Raw Intel (with producer Trevor "TrevThorne" Drayton). Acclaimed Music critic Jeff Miers has described him as “the most promising, adventurous, nigh-on-visionary hip hop artist Buffalo has yet produced.”<sup id="cite_ref-buffalospree1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buffalospree1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Raw Intel found success early, placing five songs on the <a href="/wiki/ESPN_NFL_2K5" title="ESPN NFL 2K5">ESPN NFL 2K5</a> Video Game,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and producing a little-known but highly acclaimed self-titled album in 2002/2003. Billy Drease also collaborated with Soulive again for their <a href="/wiki/Turn_It_Out_Remixed" title="Turn It Out Remixed">Turn It Out Remixed</a> Album and released a project on Velour Records with drummer Alan Evans, entitled M.E.K.A. 54.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the Raw Intel album failed to provide Billy with a viable major label deal, he focused his efforts on production and visual art: placing several tracks on films and television, including <a href="/wiki/Lionsgate" title="Lionsgate">Lionsgate</a> Films' "The Farce of the Penguins" and <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Lifetime Television">Lifetime Television</a>'s "Officer Down"; as well as opening an independent art gallery in Buffalo called Gallery 51.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, he produced several tracks for <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stat_Quo" title="Stat Quo">Stat Quo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Soon thereafter, Billy Drease decided to embrace the industry's movement from mainstream to indie <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and signed a deal with Buffalo-based independent label <a href="/w/index.php?title=Deep_Thinka_Records&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Deep Thinka Records (page does not exist)">Deep Thinka Records</a>, which then proceeded to re-brand themselves as <a href="/w/index.php?title=DTR45&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="DTR45 (page does not exist)">DTR45</a>. Creatively invigorated by the deal, Billy's first single "I Like It" – a raw chopped up rendition of a Betty Wright tune –  secured features in <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard Magazine">Billboard Magazine</a><sup id="cite_ref-billboard1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and on <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a>; and the accompanying music video was featured by YouTube and <a href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Entertainment Television">BET</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Originally performing as solo artist under his birth name, Edreys decided to take on the moniker Billy Drease Williams right around the same time I Like It dropped. This change in stage name was both in homage to the actor <a href="/wiki/Billy_Dee_Williams" title="Billy Dee Williams">Billy Dee Williams</a> and as a way for the pronunciation of birth name to still be present within his stage name.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Billy Drease Williams has released three albums with Deep Thinka Records / DTR45, including Good Morning Amy – an inspirational hip hop album which garnered Grammy buzz<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  – and made several high profile features and appearances, including <a href="/wiki/Warped_Tour" title="Warped Tour">Warped Tour</a>, <a href="/wiki/NXNE" class="mw-redirect" title="NXNE">NXNE</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-r1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-r1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/CMJ_Music_Marathon" class="mw-redirect" title="CMJ Music Marathon">CMJ Music Marathon</a><sup id="cite_ref-d1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-d1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> performances. His hit single, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Just_Doin%27_It&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Just Doin&#39; It (page does not exist)">Just Doin' It</a> has proven to be his biggest commercial success to date, and the groundbreaking music video has received over half a million views to date.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Art_of_Hip-Hop">The Art of Hip-Hop</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Billy_Drease_Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The Art of Hip-Hop">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Billy Drease is the Executive Director of The Art of Hip Hop, a not for profit organization dedicated
to "Educating the Future and Celebrating a Culture", achieved through the following guidelines:
</p>
<ul><li>To educate the public on the importance of the preservation, appreciation and positive influences of the culture of Hip-Hop and all forms of music.</li>
<li>To produce programming designed to improve scholastic abilities in math, reading and the sciences through after school tutoring, mentor-ship and educational excursions to help fight juvenile delinquency.</li>
<li>To utilize the business behind the music industry to teach youth about entrepreneurship and business functions.</li>
<li>To utilize the visual arts in helping youth express emotion and life issues, and help eliminate prejudice, discrimination and violence.</li></ul>
<p>The Art of Hip Hop has been celebrated as an event/festival in Buffalo the past years, and is currently on its fifth installment.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_highlights">Career highlights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Billy_Drease_Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career highlights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>2019: Solo exhibition entitled PLAYLIST received recognition while on view at Flight Gallery Buffalo.</li>
<li>2017: Selected as one of four artists to create The Freedom Wall, an art installation located in Buffalo, New York.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2012: Cover Feature of Buffalo Spree Magazine, as a Buffalo Game Changer.<sup id="cite_ref-buffalospree1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buffalospree1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2010: Featured Performer at YouTube Musician's Wanted Showcase at CMJ New Music Marathon<sup id="cite_ref-d1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-d1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2010: Awarded 'Artvoice' Best in Buffalo Hip Hop Artist for 2010<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li>
