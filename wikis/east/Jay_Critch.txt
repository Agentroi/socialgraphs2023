<p><b>Jason Cole Critchlow</b> (born February 25, 1998), known professionally as <b>Jay Critch</b>, is an American rapper, singer and songwriter. He was formerly signed with <a href="/wiki/Rich_Forever_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="Rich Forever Music">Rich Forever Music</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jay_Critch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jason Cole Critchlow grew up in the <a href="/wiki/Clinton_Hill,_Brooklyn" title="Clinton Hill, Brooklyn">Clinton Hill</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>. He was born on February 25, 1998, to a <a href="/wiki/Trinidadian" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinidadian">Trinidadian</a> mother.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As a child, he was fascinated by listening to his older brother rap.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Critch grew up listening to <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, as well as many rappers from New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jay_Critch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jay Critch's first major collaboration was with <a href="/wiki/Rowdy_Rebel" title="Rowdy Rebel">Rowdy Rebel</a> on the song "Man Down" in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, he began uploading songs on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He became the subject of widespread attention in 2016, when his song "Did It Again" was remixed by <a href="/wiki/Rich_the_Kid" title="Rich the Kid">Rich the Kid</a>, who signed him to <a href="/wiki/Rich_Forever_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="Rich Forever Music">Rich Forever Music</a> in November.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Their collaboration went viral, with the music video gaining over a million views in the first week of its release.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2018, as a newly signed artist, Jay Critch released his debut solo mixtape, <i>Hood Favorite</i>. It features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Offset_(rapper)" title="Offset (rapper)">Offset</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He also was featured on <a href="/wiki/Lil_Tjay" title="Lil Tjay">Lil Tjay</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/True_2_Myself" title="True 2 Myself">Ruthless</a>", which was certified platinum.
</p><p>In December 2020, Jay Critch released his third mixtape, <i>Signed with Love</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-CritchTape_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CritchTape-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 5, 2021, Jay returned with his mixtape, <i>Critch Tape</i>. Announced just hours before its release, the project contains 23 songs, with features from <a href="/wiki/Fivio_Foreign" title="Fivio Foreign">Fivio Foreign</a>,&#160;<a href="/wiki/Lil_Tjay" title="Lil Tjay">Lil Tjay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Drakeo_the_Ruler" title="Drakeo the Ruler">Drakeo the Ruler</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-CritchTape_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CritchTape-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
