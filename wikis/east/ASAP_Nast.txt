<p><b>Tariq Amar Devega</b> (born July 26, 1990), known professionally as <b>ASAP Nast</b> (stylized <b>A$AP Nast</b>), is an American rapper, songwriter and model from <a href="/wiki/Harlem,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Harlem, New York">Harlem, New York</a>. Nast is best known as a member of the hip hop collective <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Mob" title="ASAP Mob">A$AP Mob</a>. As part of A$AP Mob, they released their first project as a group, a mixtape titled <i><a href="/wiki/Lords_Never_Worry" title="Lords Never Worry">Lord$ Never Worry</a></i> in 2012. His breakout song, "<a href="/wiki/Trillmatic" title="Trillmatic">Trillmatic</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a> was released on December 4, 2013.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASAP_Nast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ASAP Nast was raised in <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">West Harlem</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> on 116th and Morningside Avenue, and frequently spent time with his cousin <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a> when they were children.<sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphop1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphop1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-villagevoice2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villagevoice2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> When Rocky moved to <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">The Bronx</a> as a teen, the two lost contact, but were reunited by ASAP Bari, one of <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Mob" title="ASAP Mob">ASAP Mob</a>'s founding members.<sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphop1_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphop1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Nast went to <a href="/wiki/Martin_Luther_King,_Jr._Educational_Campus" class="mw-redirect" title="Martin Luther King, Jr. Educational Campus">Martin Luther King, Jr. High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> but dropped out before  graduating  to pursue a  career in music. After dropping out of high school, he worked at the <a href="/wiki/Sneakers" title="Sneakers">sneaker</a> store Atmos.<sup id="cite_ref-villagevoice2_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villagevoice2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASAP_Nast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2007.E2.80.9312:_Career_beginnings_with_ASAP_Mob"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007–12:_Career_beginnings_with_ASAP_Mob">2007–12: Career beginnings with ASAP Mob</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASAP_Nast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007–12: Career beginnings with ASAP Mob">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>ASAP Nast was one of the first members to join the hip hop collective <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Mob" title="ASAP Mob">ASAP Mob</a>, that was formed by ASAP Yams, ASAP Bari and ASAP Illz in Harlem during 2007. He was originally known by the name New York Nast, however he adjusted his stage name to include the ASAP prefix, as all the other Mob members have; aside from <a href="/wiki/Dash_(rapper)" title="Dash (rapper)">Dash</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Nast made his official debut on ASAP Rocky's early song "Uptown" and then he appeared on two <i><a href="/wiki/Live.Love.ASAP" class="mw-redirect" title="Live.Love.ASAP">Live.Love.ASAP</a></i> songs, "Purple Swag: Chapter 2" and "Trilla".<sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphop1_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphop1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 27, 2012, ASAP Mob released their debut project, a mixtape titled <i><a href="/wiki/Lords_Never_Worry" title="Lords Never Worry">Lords Never Worry</a></i>, as a free download.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Nast appeared on four of the mixtape's tracks, including the standout "Black Mane".<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Following the mixtape's release, Nast toured with the rest of ASAP Mob. Throughout that tour they performed 48 shows in 61 days, the majority of which were sold out.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.9314:_L.O.R.D."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–14:_L.O.R.D.">2013–14: <i>L.O.R.D.</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASAP_Nast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013–14: L.O.R.D.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Singer <a href="/wiki/Tinashe" title="Tinashe">Tinashe</a> released the remix to her song "Who Am I Working For?" on April 16, 2013, featuring Nast.<sup id="cite_ref-complex2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In July 2013, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a> announced that ASAP Mob would release their debut album after the release of his solo debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Trap_Lord" title="Trap Lord">Trap Lord</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> During October 2013, ASAP Nast along with ASAP Rocky, Ferg, Twelvy and Ant, participated in the ASAP Mob's cypher during the <a href="/wiki/2013_BET_Hip_Hop_Awards" title="2013 BET Hip Hop Awards">2013 BET Hip Hop Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On December 3, 2013, it was announced that the album would be released on March 4, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The following day, the album's first <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Trillmatic" title="Trillmatic">Trillmatic</a>" by ASAP Nast, was released along with an accompanying music video. The single, produced by ASAP Ty Beats, features a <a href="/wiki/Verse_(popular_music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Verse (popular music)">verse</a> from American rapper and New York City-native, <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mv_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mv-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It had previously been previewed at the end of the music video for ASAP Ant's song "The Way It Go".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The single was met with generally positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_critic" class="mw-redirect" title="Music critic">music critics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex2_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The album was then changed to be titled <i><a href="/wiki/L.O.R.D._(album)" class="mw-redirect" title="L.O.R.D. (album)">L.O.R.D.</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It has been predicted that <i>L.O.R.D.</i> could've resulted in Nast being the next breakout member of the group.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Originally, he was being considered for the 2014 <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> freshman class,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> but was not added. <i>L.O.R.D.</i> was expected for a 2014 release, however, ASAP Mob leader, the late ASAP Yams announced on his <a href="/wiki/Tumblr" title="Tumblr">Tumblr</a> account that the album was officially scrapped.<sup id="cite_ref-tumblr_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tumblr-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASAP_Nast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> said that ASAP Nast's style is deeply rooted in 1990's <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast hip hop</a>, omitting the <a href="/wiki/Trap_music_(hip_hop)" class="mw-redirect" title="Trap music (hip hop)">trap</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southern_hip_hop" title="Southern hip hop">southern hip hop</a> influence that some other ASAP Mob members have.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Vibe_(magazine)" title="Vibe (magazine)">Vibe</a></i> called him a brash, rapid-fire rhymer.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He has been credited as able to "adapt his flow to match beats of all styles and eras." His verses are usually boastful and full of multi-syllable rhyme schemes.<sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphop1_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphop1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaborations">Collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASAP_Nast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Various brands such as <a href="/wiki/Converse_(shoe_company)" class="mw-redirect" title="Converse (shoe company)">Converse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reebok" title="Reebok">Reebok</a> have acknowledged his influence and thus granted him the opportunity to design sneakers that merge heritage with his street-style sensibilities.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The rapper has released a number of collaborative pairs with Converse as they played with silhouettes from One Stars and Chuck 70s to the Jack Purcell Mid.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In Spring of 2021, ASAP Nast formed a relationship with Reebok as they dropped a new Classic Leather Legacy colorway followed by a Zig Kinetica II Edge.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
