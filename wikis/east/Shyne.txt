<p><b>Moses Michael Levi Barrow</b> (born <b>Jamal Michael Barrow</b>, best known by his stage name <b>Shyne</b>; November 8, 1978) is a <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belizean</a> rapper and politician.<sup id="cite_ref-orthodoxshyne_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-orthodoxshyne-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-name1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-name1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is the <a href="/wiki/Leader_of_the_Opposition_(Belize)" title="Leader of the Opposition (Belize)">Leader of the Opposition</a> in the&#160;Belize House of Representatives, and the leader of the <a href="/wiki/United_Democratic_Party_(Belize)" title="United Democratic Party (Belize)">Belize United Democratic Party</a>. Barrow was born in Belize, but moved to <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> in New York City as a child, and began to rap as a teenager. He is perhaps best known for his 2000 singles “<a href="/wiki/Bad_Boyz" title="Bad Boyz">Bad Boyz</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Bonnie_%26_Shyne" title="Bonnie &amp; Shyne">Bonnie &amp; Shyne</a>". He also wrote and performed on a number of <a href="/wiki/Multi-platinum" class="mw-redirect" title="Multi-platinum">multi-platinum albums</a>, such as Usher's <i><a href="/wiki/Confessions_(Usher_album)" title="Confessions (Usher album)">Confessions</a></i>, Lil Wayne's <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Carter_IV" title="Tha Carter IV">Carter IV</a></i>, Notorious B.I.G's <i><a href="/wiki/Born_Again_(The_Notorious_B.I.G._album)" title="Born Again (The Notorious B.I.G. album)">Born Again</a></i>, Mase's <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Up_(Mase_album)" title="Double Up (Mase album)">Double Up</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Puff_Daddy" class="mw-redirect" title="Puff Daddy">Puff Daddy</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Forever_(Puff_Daddy_album)" title="Forever (Puff Daddy album)">Forever</a></i> among other top-selling albums during his tenures with his former labels, <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>.
</p><p>Shyne and his mother lived in the Brooklyn neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/East_Flatbush" class="mw-redirect" title="East Flatbush">East Flatbush</a>, where after he was  discovered as he was singing in a barbershop, he became a musician and rapper. On the verge of releasing his debut album, on the evening of <span class="nowrap">December 27, 1999</span>, he went to a nightclub with his mentor/label boss <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Sean Combs</a>, and Combs' then-girlfriend singer/actress <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>. An argument broke out in the club between Combs and another man, guns were drawn, shots fired, and three bystanders were injured. In June&#160;2001 he was convicted of assault, and sentenced to 10&#160;years in prison. His 2000 debut album was nevertheless a success, and he continued to record music while incarcerated.
</p><p>While serving his prison sentence, Shyne became interested in <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Judaism</a>, became observant&#160;– practicing <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Judaism" title="Orthodox Judaism">Orthodox Judaism</a>, and  officially changed his name to <b>Moses Michael Levi Barrow</b> in 2006. After he was released from prison in 2009, he was deported to Belize. In 2010, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>, where he spent his time studying the <a href="/wiki/Torah" title="Torah">Torah</a> up to 12 hours a day. He returned to Belize in 2013, and was appointed the Belize Music and Goodwill Ambassador, charged with the sustainable development of the music industry in Belize, and donated hundreds of thousands of dollars to develop it.
</p><p>Shyne was elected to the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_(Belize)" title="House of Representatives (Belize)">Belizean House of Representatives</a> in the 2020 general election, as a member of the center-right <a href="/wiki/United_Democratic_Party_(Belize)" title="United Democratic Party (Belize)">Belize United Democratic Party</a>. He became the <a href="/wiki/Leader_of_the_Opposition_(Belize)" title="Leader of the Opposition (Belize)">Belize Leader of the Opposition</a> in the&#160;House of Representatives and the leader of the Belize United Democratic Party, in both June 2021 (until September 2021) and February 2022.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shyne was born Jamal Michael Barrow, in <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">Belize City</a> in <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>, to Frances Imeon Myvette and <a href="/wiki/Dean_Barrow" title="Dean Barrow">Dean Barrow</a>, who were not married.<sup id="cite_ref-auto12_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto12-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-name1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-name1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Shyne's mother is the sister of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Finnegan_(Belizean_politician)" title="Michael Finnegan (Belizean politician)">Michael Myvett, now going by the surname Finnegan</a>, one of Dean Barrow's long-time political colleagues in Belize. Barrow's middle name comes from his uncle.<sup id="cite_ref-dean2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dean2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Shyne's Jewish maternal grandmother had emigrated from <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> to Belize.<sup id="cite_ref-auto13_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto13-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a child, Barrow's time was divided between his mother in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York City, and his lawyer/politician father, who initially failed to acknowledge his son, and was years later elected in 2008 as the first black <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_Belize" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Prime Ministers of Belize">Prime Minister of Belize</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto13_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto13-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto10_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto10-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto9_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto9-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dean1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dean1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fights_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fights-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  Barrow's mother moved to the United States when he was three years old, leaving her son with his father who was busy with politics, and left Shyne between the care of his mother's brother Michael and father's sister Denise in Belize City. His father was in a relationship with another woman, and Barrow recalls: “The nigga said his two other kids were made out of love. It was devastating. That shit really fucked me up.”<sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When he was seven years old, Barrow moved to Brooklyn to live with his mother in <a href="/wiki/Crown_Heights,_Brooklyn" title="Crown Heights, Brooklyn">Crown Heights</a> and then <a href="/wiki/East_Flatbush" class="mw-redirect" title="East Flatbush">East Flatbush</a>, in near poverty, while spending summers in Belize with his father.<sup id="cite_ref-auto13_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto13-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> His mother supported them by cleaning homes and taking care of children, and he slept on the sofa in their small apartment on Church Avenue and East 18th Street.<sup id="cite_ref-auto12_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto12-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto13_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto13-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto9_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto9-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> After he moved to Brooklyn, he began to develop a strong interest in the <a href="/wiki/Hip-hop_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip-hop culture">hip-hop culture</a> of the 1980s and 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-mercy_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mercy-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> At age 15, while in a fight he was shot in his right shoulder by another kid in <a href="/wiki/Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="Flatbush, Brooklyn">Flatbush, Brooklyn</a>, leaving him with a six-inch scar.<sup id="cite_ref-auto9_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto9-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After graduating high school three years later, part of the time of which he spent attending high school in Belize at <a href="/wiki/Wesley_College_(Belize)" title="Wesley College (Belize)">Wesley College</a> in 1993, he enrolled in a <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_College_of_Technology" title="New York City College of Technology">New York City College of Technology</a> computer program.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto11_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto11-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto13_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto13-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He paid for his tuition by working as a bike messenger, buying an 18-speed bicycle and riding it from Brooklyn over the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Bridge" title="Brooklyn Bridge">Brooklyn Bridge</a> into Manhattan, where he made deliveries around the borough.<sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto13_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto13-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He left NYC College of Technology to pursue a career in music.<sup id="cite_ref-auto11_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto11-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_music_career">Early music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1997.E2.80.9399:_Career_beginnings.2C_and_signing_to_Bad_Boy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1997–99:_Career_beginnings,_and_signing_to_Bad_Boy">1997–99: Career beginnings, and signing to Bad Boy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1997–99: Career beginnings, and signing to Bad Boy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1998, while Barrow was <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyling</a> in a barbershop on Church Avenue in Brooklyn, he was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> producer <a href="/wiki/DJ_Clark_Kent" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Clark Kent">DJ Clark Kent</a>, who was working on <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">the Notorious B.I.G.</a>'s first posthumous album, <i><a href="/wiki/Born_Again_(The_Notorious_B.I.G._album)" title="Born Again (The Notorious B.I.G. album)">Born Again</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-BadBoyinfluence_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BadBoyinfluence-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He quickly took Barrow to <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Bad Boy Entertainment">Bad Boy Studios</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Sean_%22Puff_Daddy%22_Combs" class="mw-redirect" title="Sean &quot;Puff Daddy&quot; Combs">Sean "Puff Daddy" Combs</a> signed him on the spot to his label.<sup id="cite_ref-BadBoyinfluence_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BadBoyinfluence-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> It was reported that Shyne received millions of dollars, 3 cars of his choice and 2 homes just for signing.<sup id="cite_ref-BadBoyinfluence_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BadBoyinfluence-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The contract also included a 5 <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio album">studio album</a> deal.<sup id="cite_ref-BadBoyinfluence_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BadBoyinfluence-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> This caused a small media shockwave.<sup id="cite_ref-BadBoyinfluence_18-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BadBoyinfluence-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He abruptly had his life shift from sleeping on his mother's couch, to jetting to Beverly Hills first class to record with top rappers in hip-hop.<sup id="cite_ref-auto13_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto13-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Not long after, Shyne began making appearances on recordings made by his <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Bad Boy Entertainment">Bad Boy Entertainment</a> label-mates. He was notably featured on <a href="/wiki/Mase" title="Mase">Mase</a>'s second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Up_(Mase_album)" title="Double Up (Mase album)">Double Up</a></i> (1999), and on a <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remix</a> of <a href="/wiki/Total_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Total (group)">Total</a>'s "Sittin At Home" <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fromscratch_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fromscratch-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In the same year he featured on chief executive Combs' second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Forever_(Puff_Daddy_album)" title="Forever (Puff Daddy album)">Forever</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-foreverdiddy_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-foreverdiddy-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_New_York_shooting">Club New York shooting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Club New York shooting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Shooting_and_trial_.281999-2001.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Shooting_and_trial_(1999-2001)">Shooting and trial (1999-2001)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Shooting and trial (1999-2001)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the early hours of December 27, 1999, Shyne and his mentor/label boss, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Sean Combs</a>, and Combs' then-girlfriend singer/actress <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>, were involved in a shooting incident.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto4_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto4-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> 
The three of them were at a Manhattan nightclub named Club New York, on West 43rd Street in <a href="/wiki/Times_Square" title="Times Square">Times Square</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto2_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto6_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto6-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto4_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto4-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Witnesses said that a felon named Matthew "Scar" Allen started an argument with Combs.<sup id="cite_ref-auto2_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto6_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto6-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto4_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto4-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Witnesses later  testified that Allen also threatened to kill Shyne.<sup id="cite_ref-auto3_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto3-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prosecutors charged that Allen, Combs, and Shyne all drew guns, and that Shyne shot three times in the packed nightclub.<sup id="cite_ref-auto5_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto5-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto6_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto6-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The shooting resulted in three bystanders being injured.<sup id="cite_ref-auto6_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto6-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto4_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto4-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Shyne was accused by prosecutors as was Combs of drawing a gun in the confrontation; Shyne said he acted in self-defense.<sup id="cite_ref-auto4_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto4-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto14_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto14-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> One injured witness said she saw both Combs and Shyne shoot guns.<sup id="cite_ref-auto3_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto3-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Shyne was accused of firing three shots that wounded three people; he maintained that he had fired into the air, and did not believe that it was bullets from his gun that injured the bystanders.<sup id="cite_ref-auto12_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto12-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nytconv_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytconv-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto4_22-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto4-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> At his trial, an eyewitness and a ballistics expert testified that the three injuries may have been caused not by Shyne but by a second gunman, and the ballistics expert said that at least one injury may have been caused by a <a href="/wiki/40_caliber" class="mw-redirect" title="40 caliber">40 caliber</a> weapon while Shyne had a <a href="/wiki/9%C3%9719mm_Parabellum" title="9×19mm Parabellum">9 millimeter</a> gun; 40-caliber shells were found on the floor.<sup id="cite_ref-auto2_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Shyne was convicted at trial by a jury on two counts of <a href="/wiki/Assault" title="Assault">assault</a> and of <a href="/wiki/Reckless_endangerment" class="mw-redirect" title="Reckless endangerment">reckless endangerment</a>, and criminal possession of an illegal weapon, after a seven-week trial alongside Combs.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto3_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto3-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He was acquitted of charges of attempted murder, on a third count of intentional assault in the first degree of a third victim, and one other count.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto3_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto3-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>  He was sentenced on June 1, 2001, to ten years in prison, without eligibility for parole until 2009; he served almost nine years in New York prisons.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto7_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto7-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto9_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto9-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Conrad_Tillard" title="Conrad Tillard">Conrad Tillard</a>, then known as Hip-Hop Minister Conrad Muhammad, said: "Shyne’s mother and grandmother placed this young man in the care and custody of Sean “Puffy” Combs, who they believed was a responsible executive of a company. Puffy has the same responsibility as a teacher, as a coach. This boy, Shyne, was out with his idol on that fateful night. When I put my child in your hands, I don't expect him to end up dead or in jail."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Combs and his bodyguard, in contrast, were acquitted on all counts.  Combs had been arrested and charged with illegally having two 9 millimeter guns and with bribery, but was acquitted of the charges at the trial. Combs' bodyguard, who had been charged with illegal possession of a gun and bribery, was also acquitted at the trial.<sup id="cite_ref-auto3_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto3-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto1_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Shyne's debut album was released in 2000 and was successful.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Incarceration_.282001-09.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Incarceration_(2001-09)">Incarceration (2001-09)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Incarceration (2001-09)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shyne began serving his sentence at the <a href="/wiki/Maximum_security_prison" title="Maximum security prison">maximum security prison</a> <a href="/wiki/Clinton_Correctional_Facility" title="Clinton Correctional Facility">Clinton Correctional Facility</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dannemora_(village),_New_York" title="Dannemora (village), New York">Dannemora</a>, New York.<sup id="cite_ref-sentence1_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sentence1-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sentence2_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sentence2-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sentence3_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sentence3-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> This seemed to spell the end of his rap career; his legal team's appeal for a <a href="/wiki/Suspended_sentence" title="Suspended sentence">suspended sentence</a> ultimately failed. Shyne severed all ties with Sean Combs and Bad Boy Records.<sup id="cite_ref-ppl_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ppl-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-defjam_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-defjam-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2006 he changed his name legally, to Moses Michael Levi Barrow. He had already been studying <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Judaism</a> at the time of his arrest. In prison, with <a href="/wiki/Rabbi" title="Rabbi">rabbis</a> he became a practicing Jew, keeping <a href="/wiki/Kosher" class="mw-redirect" title="Kosher">kosher</a>, and celebrating the <a href="/wiki/Jewish_Sabbath" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish Sabbath">Jewish Sabbath</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jewish_holidays" title="Jewish holidays">Jewish holidays</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nytconv_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytconv-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barrow has said that from the age of 13, he began to identify as an Israelite, after learning that his great-grandmother was a descendant of the <a href="/wiki/Beta_Israel" title="Beta Israel">Beta Israel</a>, ancient <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Jews" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethiopian Jews">Ethiopian Jews</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nytconv_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytconv-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto7_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto7-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> As a teenager, he began to study Judaism and the <a href="/wiki/Old_Testament" title="Old Testament">Old Testament</a> on his own, and to pray daily.<sup id="cite_ref-nytconv_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytconv-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He formally converted to <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Judaism" title="Orthodox Judaism">Orthodox Judaism</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>, Israel, in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-name1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-name1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nytconv_29-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytconv-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006 Shyne went to court to challenge New York's application of the <a href="/wiki/Son_of_Sam_law" title="Son of Sam law">'Son of Sam' law</a>, which had resulted in his assets being frozen and which limited his ability to make record deals and generate income.<sup id="cite_ref-freeze_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-freeze-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> He and his lawyers argued that by the court allowing Shyne to make deals with record producers, it would enable him to pay a higher potential settlement to victims of the night club shooting, who were separately pursuing a civil suit against him.<sup id="cite_ref-name2_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-name2-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite his convictions, his incarceration drew many sympathizers, as well as the admiration of many in the hip hop community.<sup id="cite_ref-blessings_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blessings-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> His adherence to the <a href="/wiki/Code_of_silence" title="Code of silence">code of silence</a>, which he referred to several times on his eponymous debut album, earned him a hardcore reputation in the prison community and on the streets. Even while incarcerated, he was visited by representatives of record labels who wanted to make deals.<sup id="cite_ref-irv_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-irv-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wanted_man_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wanted_man-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> At the same time, Shyne was not without detractors. Fellow New York rapper <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> called him a "punk," and made light of his situation and his part in the "night club incident." Shyne retaliated on his 2004 release <i>Godfather Buried Alive</i>, with a track entitled "For the Record".<sup id="cite_ref-millions_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-millions-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Describing his nine years of incarceration, Shyne said: 
</p>
<blockquote>
<p>The entire process was devastating.... ten hours of incarceration is ten hours too much. So, for a human being to be animalized for ten years, there is no quick fix to that.... It's like being shot by an assailant, and you are running away for your life. You didn't even realize you got shot in your leg because you are running on adrenaline. It's not until you get to a place of safety that you realize you have a hole in your leg, and you collapse; you can't stand up, and that was that experience was for me. When I came out I didn't even realize how wounded and devastated I was because I numbed myself to the pain and destruction that I suffered.
</p><p>I remember my mother used to come and see me on the visit floor. My mother couldn't look at me; she would start to shake, and she would go off the floor and go to the bathroom. I couldn't process that because if I cried in front of her then that would make her life go to shambles. If I cried in front of the prison guard, they would think that I was weak. So I go back to the yard and lift some weights, smoke a cigar, and act like nothing happened.
</p><p>
When you come out from that, how do you recover ...? How do you put back your life together?<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<h3><span id="Release_and_deportation_.282009.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_deportation_(2009)">Release and deportation (2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Release and deportation (2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 26, 2009, Shyne was released and deported to his place of birth, Belize. Some of the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> community rallied to celebrate his release, and the events were followed by mainstream media.<sup id="cite_ref-google_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-google-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rally_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rally-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The rapper had been transferred to <a href="/wiki/Rikers_Island" title="Rikers Island">Rikers Island</a> where he was held for a <a href="/wiki/Parole" title="Parole">parole</a> hearing, and then to the <a href="/wiki/Woodbourne_Correctional_Facility" title="Woodbourne Correctional Facility">Woodbourne Correctional Facility</a>, where he spent the last months of his incarceration.<sup id="cite_ref-parole_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parole-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> A Manhattan judge signed an order that would schedule Shyne for release on October 6, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-release_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-release-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> He had served more than 9 years of a 10-year sentence, on which no terms for post-release supervision had originally been placed. At the request of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_Department_of_Correctional_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="New York State Department of Correctional Services">New York State Department of Correctional Services</a>, a mandatory <a href="/wiki/Probation" title="Probation">probation</a> period of at least two and half years was added to Shyne's sentence.<sup id="cite_ref-paroled_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paroled-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Shyne and his attorney, Oscar Michelen, had hoped to avoid probation.<sup id="cite_ref-mtv_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bet_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bet-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 6, 2009, Shyne was released from New York State custody; however, he was immediately taken into Federal custody.<sup id="cite_ref-released?_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-released?-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-released!_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-released!-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> He was detained by <a href="/wiki/U.S._Immigration_and_Customs_Enforcement" title="U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement">U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement</a> at a "detention facility in Western New York State". One source indicated that the facility in question was the Buffalo Federal Detention Facility in <a href="/wiki/Batavia_(city),_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Batavia (city), New York">Batavia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Buffalo?_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Buffalo?-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> According to his attorney, Federal officials were reviewing the rapper's immigration status and making a determination on whether or not he would be deported to his native Belize.<sup id="cite_ref-deportation?_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deportation?-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Shyne had a permanent resident <a href="/wiki/Permanent_residence_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Permanent residence (United States)">"green card"</a>, and his mother was a U.S. citizen, but he himself had never become a <a href="/wiki/Naturalization" title="Naturalization">naturalized</a> citizen.<sup id="cite_ref-fights_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fights-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto13_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto13-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> There was speculation that Shyne might be released on bail in the U.S. while his case was being resolved.<sup id="cite_ref-xilla_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xilla-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Shyne's uncle, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Finnegan_(Belizean_politician)" title="Michael Finnegan (Belizean politician)">Michael Finnegan</a>, said that the family had prepared for the rapper's potential return to Belize.<sup id="cite_ref-uncle_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uncle-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> He also revealed that Shyne and his representatives had been expecting to be intercepted by ICE officials upon his release, and had directed  members of his legal team to prepare the necessary documents in an effort to address the situation.<sup id="cite_ref-highrank_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-highrank-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dean_Barrow" title="Dean Barrow">Dean Barrow</a>, Belize's Prime Minister and Shyne's father, sent a petition to New York Governor <a href="/wiki/David_Paterson" title="David Paterson">David Paterson</a> asking Patterson to pardon his son.<sup id="cite_ref-pardonplea_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pardonplea-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> In 2008 Governor Paterson had pardoned rapper <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a> who, in a similar situation, also faced deportation.<sup id="cite_ref-pardonedrick_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pardonedrick-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-after_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-after-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> Barrow said he had been assured that the Governor received his letter and that his request was under consideration, but that he did not expect to have any influence in swaying the decision.<sup id="cite_ref-BPM_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BPM-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His family enlisted the assistance of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Ogletree" title="Charles Ogletree">Charles Ogletree</a>, an attorney and <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Law_School" title="Harvard Law School">Harvard Law School</a> professor, and part of President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>'s circle, in Shyne's attempt to forestall deportation and later to regain entry into the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-deport_65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deport-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-circle_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-circle-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> In October 2009, Finnegan said  that all legal matters regarding Shyne's case had been turned over to Ogletree.<sup id="cite_ref-BPM_64-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BPM-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ogletreetakesit_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ogletreetakesit-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> Nevertheless, Shyne was deported to Belize on October 28, 2009, though he continued to fight without success for residency in the United States.  Given that he was a convicted felon, the U.S. would not allow him back into the country.<sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-deportation">Post-deportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-deportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2009, upon his return to Belize, he reconnected with his estranged father.<sup id="cite_ref-auto14_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto14-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2010, the rapper was refused entry into the United Kingdom due to his conviction as a felon. He had begun his journey in <a href="/wiki/Cancun" class="mw-redirect" title="Cancun">Cancun</a>, Mexico, but was deported upon arrival in the UK after immigration officials refused to allow him to enter the country.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Jerusalem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010 Shyne moved to <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>.  In November 2010, he was living in Jerusalem, having become observant and changing his name officially to Moses Michael Levi.<sup id="cite_ref-nytconv_29-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytconv-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He spent his time learning the <a href="/wiki/Torah" title="Torah">Torah</a>, and spent up to 12 hours a day studying it.<sup id="cite_ref-nytconv_29-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytconv-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>  He said that there was nothing at odds between the hip-hop world and being a Torah-observant Jew, saying: "There’s nothing in the <i><a href="/wiki/Chumash_(Judaism)" title="Chumash (Judaism)">Chumash</a></i> that says I can’t drive a <a href="/wiki/Lamborghini" title="Lamborghini">Lamborghini</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-auto9_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto9-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Jerusalem, he underwent a formal conversion to <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Judaism" title="Orthodox Judaism">Orthodox Judaism</a>. He also prayed wearing Jewish ritual <i><a href="/wiki/Tefillin" title="Tefillin">tefillin</a></i> in the mornings, studied <a href="/wiki/Talmud" title="Talmud">Talmud</a> with rigorous strictly Orthodox rabbis at the <a href="/wiki/Ohr_Somayach,_Jerusalem" title="Ohr Somayach, Jerusalem">Or Sameach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belz_(Hasidic_dynasty)" title="Belz (Hasidic dynasty)">Belz</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mir_Yeshiva_(Jerusalem)" title="Mir Yeshiva (Jerusalem)">Mir</a> yeshivas, and grew <i><a href="/wiki/Payot" title="Payot">payot</a></i> (sidecurls; worn by some men in the Orthodox Jewish community based on their interpretation of the requirements of Jewish law).<sup id="cite_ref-auto9_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto9-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto13_8-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto13-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He had studied seriously with rabbis while in prison, and adopted the <a href="/wiki/Jewish_law" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish law">Jewish laws</a> to create boundaries and order in his life. He officially changed his name to <b>Moses Levi</b> to reflect his commitment to Judaism.<sup id="cite_ref-nytconv_29-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytconv-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He said: "My entire life screams that I have a Jewish <i><a href="/wiki/Soul#Judaism" title="Soul">neshama</a></i> ["soul", in Hebrew]."<sup id="cite_ref-auto9_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto9-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2011, almost 12 years after the initial shooting, back in New York City, Matthew "Scar" Allen was shot and killed outside of Brooklyn's Footprints nightclub.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2012, Shyne traveled to <a href="/wiki/Kyiv" title="Kyiv">Kyiv</a>, Ukraine, where he was a featured speaker at a conference on inter-religious understanding.  The conference – called <i>Global Winds of Change: Religions' Role in Today's World;The Challenges in Democracies and Secular Societies</i> – brought together members of the world's many faiths to discuss the role of religion in modern society. <a href="/wiki/Oleksandr_Feldman" title="Oleksandr Feldman">Oleksandr Feldman</a>, a member of the Ukrainian parliament and the President of the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Jewish_Committee" title="Ukrainian Jewish Committee">Ukrainian Jewish Committee</a>, hosted the three-day event.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Belize">Return to Belize</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Return to Belize">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, Shyne returned to Belize.<sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, he married a businesswoman named Catherine, and a year later they had a daughter, Naomi.<sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He returned to the U.S. for a visit in August 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-auto10_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto10-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In March 2022, Shyne received a <a href="/wiki/Doctorate_of_Humane_Letters" class="mw-redirect" title="Doctorate of Humane Letters">Doctorate of Humane Letters</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Honoris_causa" class="mw-redirect" title="Honoris causa">honoris causa</a></i>) from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Liberia" title="University of Liberia">University of Liberia</a> for his life's work "as a Humanitarian, Musician, and Legislator."<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_music_career">Later music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Later music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1999.E2.80.932008:_Shyne.2C_prison.2C_and_Godfather_Buried_Alive"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1999–2008:_Shyne,_prison,_and_Godfather_Buried_Alive">1999–2008: <i>Shyne</i>, prison, and <i>Godfather Buried Alive</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 1999–2008: Shyne, prison, and Godfather Buried Alive">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shyne released <i><a href="/wiki/Shyne_(album)" title="Shyne (album)">Shyne (album)</a></i> in September 2000. The album featured guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Barrington_Levy" title="Barrington Levy">Barrington Levy</a> and <a href="/wiki/112_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="112 (group)">112</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Slim_(singer)" title="Slim (singer)">Slim</a>, as well as production from Bad Boy's in-house producers <a href="/wiki/The_Hitmen_(production_team)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Hitmen (production team)">The Hitmen</a>. Shyne's self-titled debut album was recorded prior to the rapper's arrest. It made <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s number five spot on the charts, rose to the top of the Urban charts, and sold more than 900,000&#160;copies.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto13_8-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto13-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Many labels met with Shyne while he was incarcerated at the <a href="/wiki/Clinton_Correctional_Facility" title="Clinton Correctional Facility">Clinton Correctional Facility</a>. The rapper ultimately signed with <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam Records">Def Jam Records</a> for a $3 million contract.<sup id="cite_ref-abc_75-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abc-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2004, Shyne released his second album <i><a href="/wiki/Godfather_Buried_Alive" title="Godfather Buried Alive">Godfather Buried Alive</a>,</i> while imprisoned. The album, partially recorded prior to imprisonment and partially during his imprisonment over the phone in calls from prison, sold 434,000&#160;copies, and debuted very strongly at number three on Billboard's album chart, and hit No.&#160;1 on <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop chart.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-abc_75-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abc-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> Given that he was imprisoned, it did so without the benefit of the standard concert tour, and appearances on radio stations.<sup id="cite_ref-auto7_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto7-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Furthermore, as he had started to observe the <a href="/wiki/Jewish_Sabbath" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish Sabbath">Jewish Sabbath</a>, he was completely unavailable for any publicity contacts on Friday nights and Saturdays.<sup id="cite_ref-auto7_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto7-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.93present:_Release_from_prison_and_third_album"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–present:_Release_from_prison_and_third_album">2009–present: Release from prison and third album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2009–present: Release from prison and third album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Upon his early release in 2009 from his 10-year prison sentence, the Belize native was deported to his homeland as a non-citizen felon.<sup id="cite_ref-nytconv_29-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytconv-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 16, 2010, Shyne signed a seven-figure deal with <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam Records">Def Jam Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV_77-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Shyne_in_2010.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Shyne_in_2010.jpg/220px-Shyne_in_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Shyne_in_2010.jpg/330px-Shyne_in_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/07/Shyne_in_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="600" /></a><figcaption>Shyne in 2010</figcaption></figure>
<p>After a trip to <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>, Israel, in 2010, where he formally converted to <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Judaism" title="Orthodox Judaism">Orthodox Judaism</a> and underwent a symbolic circumcision,<sup id="cite_ref-nytconv_29-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytconv-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto14_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto14-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Shyne collaborated with Jewish-American <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> and rock musician <a href="/wiki/Matisyahu" title="Matisyahu">Matisyahu</a> on his single "Messiah", released in April 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup> He released "Roller Song" in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He later announced that he was recording two <a href="/wiki/LP_album" class="mw-redirect" title="LP album">LPs</a> for release in 2010, and <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam Records">Def Jam Records</a>, the largest hip-hop label, approached him to sign a distribution agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-auto13_8-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto13-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mg_81-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mg-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup> <i>Messiah</i> was set to be the first of his 2010 release schedule, while <i>Gangland</i>, was set to be his second.<sup id="cite_ref-mg_81-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mg-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2010, Shyne criticized Def Jam and announced hopes of signing to <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money Records</a>: "I'm definitely trying to get with Cash Money…. I'm not signed to Def Jam anyway, I just need to find another distributor. I might just have Cash Money do everything. Who knows? That's the beauty about being in the business for yourself. You can decide where you want to go and what you want to do."<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although he blamed Def Jam CEO <a href="/wiki/L.A._Reid" title="L.A. Reid">L.A. Reid</a> for his frustration, later that month Shyne issued an apology.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup> In November 2010, Birdman said that Shyne's deportation had stalled his Cash Money deal, as it would prevent him from appearing in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011 he performed in Jerusalem, forming a collaboration with Matisyahu.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">&#91;85&#93;</a></sup> Shyne made a surprise guest appearance in the Matisyahu and <a href="/wiki/HaDag_Nachash" class="mw-redirect" title="HaDag Nachash">HaDag Nachash</a> concert in Jerusalem's Kikar Safra on June 23, 2011. He rapped into Matisyahu's set for "King Without a Crown". He shouted "Free <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Pollard" title="Jonathan Pollard">Jonathan Pollard</a>, Free <a href="/wiki/Gilad_Shalit" title="Gilad Shalit">Gilad Shalit</a>" to thunderous applause before he exited the stage. Matisyahu and Shyne have shared Judaism and have become best friends. They have discussed collaborating more frequently in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">&#91;86&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">&#91;87&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">&#91;88&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2011, Shyne appeared on the track "Outro", from <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Multi-platinum" class="mw-redirect" title="Multi-platinum">platinum-selling</a> ninth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Carter_IV" title="Tha Carter IV">Tha Carter IV</a></i> (2011), along with <a href="/wiki/Bun_B" title="Bun B">Bun B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">&#91;90&#93;</a></sup> In December 2011, Shyne said signing to Cash Money Records "is still a possibility".<sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">&#91;91&#93;</a></sup> In March 2012, Shyne and <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a> finally reconciled. The two were photographed together at Fashion Week in Paris; Diddy tweeted, "Me and Shyne Po front row at Kenzo #ParisIsBurning RT to da world!!!!", and Shyne said: "It’s a new day. <i><a href="/wiki/Haim#L&#39;Chaim_toast" title="Haim">L’chaim</a></i>!"<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">&#91;92&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Shyne released a mixtape, <i>Gangland</i>. Shyne called out <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, due to his blasphemous <i>Black Bar Mitzvah</i> mixtape, as well as Jewish-Canadian rapper <a href="/wiki/Drake_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (entertainer)">Drake</a>. He has recorded <a href="/wiki/Diss_track" class="mw-redirect" title="Diss track">diss tracks</a> towards <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a>, Swag Blanket's DLSupreme and <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-worst_93-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-worst-93">&#91;93&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2012, Shyne became involved in a <a href="/wiki/Diss_track" class="mw-redirect" title="Diss track">feud</a> with West Coast rapper <a href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Game (rapper)">Game</a>, after Shyne described West Coast rapper <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>'s debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Good_kid,_m.A.A.d_city" class="mw-redirect" title="Good kid, m.A.A.d city">good kid, m.A.A.d city</a></i> as "trash". Shyne dissed Game on tracks such as "Bury Judas" and "Psalms 68 (Guns &amp; Moses)".<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">&#91;94&#93;</a></sup> These tracks reflected his study of Judaism.<sup id="cite_ref-nytconv_29-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytconv-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Complex_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex Magazine">Complex Magazine</a></i> in December 2012 noted Shyne on its list of rapper stars who had suffered the "30 worst fall-offs in rap history", ranking him as # 23. But Shyne's <i>Gangland</i> mixtape appeared to reflect a revival of his popularity; it was downloaded more than 100,000 times on DatPiff.com, earning him a "Gold" ranking.<sup id="cite_ref-worst_93-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-worst-93">&#91;93&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shyne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Shyne_Barrow_at_the_U.S._Embassy_in_Belize.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Shyne_Barrow_at_the_U.S._Embassy_in_Belize.jpg/220px-Shyne_Barrow_at_the_U.S._Embassy_in_Belize.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Shyne_Barrow_at_the_U.S._Embassy_in_Belize.jpg/330px-Shyne_Barrow_at_the_U.S._Embassy_in_Belize.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Shyne_Barrow_at_the_U.S._Embassy_in_Belize.jpg/440px-Shyne_Barrow_at_the_U.S._Embassy_in_Belize.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a><figcaption>Shyne (center) with his wife Catherine and American Chargé d'Affaires <a href="/wiki/Keith_R._Gilges" title="Keith R. Gilges">Keith R. Gilges</a> (2019)</figcaption></figure>
<p>In May 2010, Shyne was appointed the Belize Music and Goodwill Ambassador.<sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">&#91;95&#93;</a></sup> Over the years, he brought Combs, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, and <a href="/wiki/J._Prince" class="mw-redirect" title="J. Prince">J. Prince</a> to Belize, promoted the country, and worked with its youth.<sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto13_8-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto13-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2012, Shyne, critical of the Obama administration, endorsed <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> nominee <a href="/wiki/Mitt_Romney" title="Mitt Romney">Mitt Romney</a> for president of the United States, faulting incumbent <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">President Obama</a> for not doing anything to prevent the rapper's deportation to Belize.<sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">&#91;97&#93;</a></sup> Asked if black voters should vote based on race alone, Shyne said: "I don't believe in all that, if you black you get a pass."<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">&#91;98&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, Shyne was nominated by the center-right <a href="/wiki/United_Democratic_Party_(Belize)" title="United Democratic Party (Belize)">Belize United Democratic Party</a> to stand as a candidate for the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_(Belize)" title="House of Representatives (Belize)">Belize House of Representatives</a> in the Belize City-based <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia_(Belize_House_constituency)" title="Mesopotamia (Belize House constituency)">Mesopotamia</a> constituency in the <a href="/wiki/2020_Belizean_general_election" title="2020 Belizean general election">2020 Belizean general election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto10_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto10-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On November 11, 2020, Barrow won the House of Representatives seat for Mesopotamia.<sup id="cite_ref-udp_99-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-udp-99">&#91;99&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100">&#91;100&#93;</a></sup>  Part of his platform is to strengthen Belizean governance with increased penalties for those who commit crimes, to raise the salaries of its police, to strengthen the judicial system so as to address violent crime, and for the government to provide student loans at low <a href="/wiki/Interest_rates" class="mw-redirect" title="Interest rates">interest rates</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Shyne was subsequently also appointed the Opposition Leader in the&#160;House of Representatives and the leader of the Belize United Democratic Party, in both June 2021 (until September 2021) and February 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101">&#91;101&#93;</a></sup> He returned to the US for a state visit in August 2021, meeting with US politicians.<sup id="cite_ref-auto10_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto10-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_City_Council" title="Atlanta City Council">Atlanta City Council</a> dесlаrеd Аuguѕt 20, 2021, Ѕhуnе Ваrrоw Dау "fоr hіѕ dеdісаtіоn, соmmіtmеnt аnd ѕеrvісе tо the grеаt реорlе оf Веlіzе, Сеntrаl Аmеrіса."<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">&#91;102&#93;</a></sup> He hopes to be elected Belize's next prime minister in 2025.<sup id="cite_ref-auto8_13-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
