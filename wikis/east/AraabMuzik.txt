<p><b>Abraham Orellana</b> (born June 16, 1989), better known as his stage name <b>AraabMuzik</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/eɪ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;face&#39;">eɪ</span><span title="&#39;r&#39; in &#39;rye&#39;">r</span><span title="/æ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;bad&#39;">æ</span><span title="&#39;b&#39; in &#39;buy&#39;">b</span><span title="/ˌ/: secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="&#39;m&#39; in &#39;my&#39;">m</span><span title="/j/: &#39;y&#39; in &#39;yes&#39;">j</span><span title="/uː/: &#39;oo&#39; in &#39;goose&#39;">uː</span><span title="&#39;z&#39; in &#39;zoom&#39;">z</span><span title="/ɪ/: &#39;i&#39; in &#39;kit&#39;">ɪ</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">AY</span>-rab-mew-zik</i></a>;<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> stylized as <b>araabMUZIK</b>), is an American record producer and <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a>. He made a name for himself by performing beats and instrumentals live and in real time on a <a href="/wiki/Akai_Professional" class="mw-redirect" title="Akai Professional">AkaiPro</a> <a href="/wiki/Music_Production_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="Music Production Center">Music Production Center</a> (MPC) <a href="/wiki/Drum_machine" title="Drum machine">drum machine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He uses MPC to produce rapid, rhythmic drum patterns and creates melodies with <a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">samples</a> and other sounds.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Araabmuzik's musical style includes <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">electronic dance music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, with live elements making a significant contribution to his sound.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He has performed on the electronic dance music festival circuit and other live club performances.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Over the years, Araabmuzik has continued to build his fame through releasing solo instrumental projects, and producing for hip hop artists such as <a href="/wiki/Cardi_B" title="Cardi B">Cardi B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Electronica" title="Jay Electronica">Jay Electronica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_East" title="Dave East">Dave East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kollegah" title="Kollegah">Kollegah</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AraabMuzik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1989-2005:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings">1989-2005: Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AraabMuzik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1989-2005: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Araabmuzik was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence, Rhode Island</a>, into a musical household. His mother was a professional singer. He is the middle child of three and is of Dominican and Guatemalan ancestry. Abraham has said he learned to play the drums at age three and later began playing the keyboard.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite not being well known for it, Abraham found a lot of hip hop inspiration in his native home town of Providence. "There's definitely a lot of Hip-Hop here. You'll find a lot of local rap groups, solo artists out here that are trying make it, it's just the fact that we're not on the map like that just yet."<sup id="cite_ref-:5_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Having a musical background lead to Araabmuzik to begin producing hip hop, and had begun getting traction in the industry at a very early age. Having a natural inkling towards the MPC, he went on to buy his first unit in 2005. By 2006, at age 17, he had begun being noticed by <a href="/wiki/Diplomat_Records" title="Diplomat Records">Diplomat Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AraabMuzik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.932010:_Diplomats"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–2010:_Diplomats">2006–2010: Diplomats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AraabMuzik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006–2010: Diplomats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After meeting with record producer and <a href="/wiki/Artists_and_repertoire" title="Artists and repertoire">A&amp;R</a> of <a href="/wiki/Diplomat_Records" title="Diplomat Records">Diplomat Records</a>, <a href="/wiki/DukeDaGod" title="DukeDaGod">DukeDaGod</a> in 2006, he became affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">Dipset</a> label. He began making beats for Diplomat artists such as <a href="/wiki/Hell_Rell" title="Hell Rell">Hell Rell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, AraabMuzik was featured as a main producer on rapper Cam'ron's album <i><a href="/wiki/Crime_Pays_(Cam%27ron_album)" title="Crime Pays (Cam&#39;ron album)">Crime Pays</a></i>, producing the single "Get It in Ohio".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> From 2009 to 2010, he contributed many instrumentals to a series of mixtapes called <i>Boss of All Bosses</i> by Cam'ron and upcoming rapper <a href="/wiki/Vado_(rapper)" title="Vado (rapper)">Vado</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932015:_Trance_Party_.26_Electronic_Dream"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2015:_Trance_Party_&amp;_Electronic_Dream">2010–2015: <i>Trance Party</i> &amp; <i>Electronic Dream</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AraabMuzik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010–2015: Trance Party &amp; Electronic Dream">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, The Diplomats released Dipset Trance Party,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> an instrumental mixtape featuring artists (including AraabMuzik) employing a style that melded trance music with hip hop influences. This would prove to become a large influence on AraabMuzik's future sound. His track "South Beach" sampled <a href="/wiki/Alice_Deejay" title="Alice Deejay">Alice Deejay</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Better_Off_Alone_(Alice_Deejay_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Better Off Alone (Alice Deejay song)">Better Off Alone</a>", which AraabMuzik has said sparked his signature samples of electronic music.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first popularization of his new sound appeared in the comeback single "Salute" of rap group <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">The Diplomats</a>, also in 2010. In an interview with NPR he called "Salute" a different sound "that pretty much got me into the EDM".<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>AraabMuzik released his breakout independent project <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Dream" title="Electronic Dream">Electronic Dream</a> in 2011, which brought this signature sound of his production to the forefront. It was met with largely positive reviews<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and garnered him a large enough audience to begin touring internationally,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> getting booked for various clubs and shows to perform his music using the drum machine as an instrument.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Along with additional releases, Abraham also steadily gained more popularity from videos on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> of him using the MPC to make live beats and drum patterns. He was also featured on the <a href="/wiki/Akai" title="Akai">Akai Pro</a> website as an official Akai Pro artist, alongside other MPC users such as <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Alchemist (producer)">Alchemist</a> and DiViNCi.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016:_Dream_World">2016: <i>Dream World</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AraabMuzik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2016: Dream World">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>To complement <i>Electronic Dream's</i> sound and success, AraabMuzik began working on a full length official album <i>Dream World</i>, slated for a 2015 release. However, it ended up being delayed numerous times; he was quoted saying "I pushed it back so I could still really perfect it and work on it more, because I didn't really feel like I was satisfied with it."<sup id="cite_ref-:6_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Dream World</i> was released in July 2016, to generally favorable reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Although initially meant to sound like <i>Electronic Dream</i>, AraabMuzik decided to venture into a more eclectic album, catering to fans of different genres, both Electronic and Hip Hop, as well as experimental beats such as "War Cry".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AraabMuzik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Artist_name">Artist name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AraabMuzik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Artist name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Abraham's artist name was given to him by a friend when he began producing - initially Young Arab, "I pretty much just ran with it, added an extra 'a' to it, and then later down the line I added 'muzik."<sup id="cite_ref-:6_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Although the name has caused confusions about his origins, he became very famous very quickly, resulting in it being too late to change it. "It's something I'm stuck with, like you have your first phone number and you never changed it throughout the years, you just kept it."
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AraabMuzik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Abraham has cited <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a> as a major influence, and the two have said to have a brotherly relationship.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He's gone on to say, "He had his own distinguished sound. He was always using the keyboards and playing his own stuff. Nothing was sounding like anything he was doing."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Other influences include <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">Premier</a>, <a href="/wiki/J_Dilla" title="J Dilla">J Dilla</a>, <a href="/wiki/9th_Wonder" title="9th Wonder">9th Wonder</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">Alchemist</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_style">Production style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AraabMuzik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Production style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>AraabMuzik's style often employs samples arranged and triggered in real time on the MPC drum machine, with fast-paced drum patterns involving rapid <a href="/wiki/Hi-hat" title="Hi-hat">hi-hats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kickdrums" class="mw-redirect" title="Kickdrums">kick drums</a>. He has used <a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">samples</a> from a variety of music, including electro, <a href="/wiki/Dubstep" title="Dubstep">dubstep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trap_music_(EDM)" class="mw-redirect" title="Trap music (EDM)">trap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gabber" title="Gabber">gabber</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Trance_music" title="Trance music">trance music</a>. Although his main tool is still an <a href="/wiki/Akai" title="Akai">Akai</a> Music Production Center (MPC) drum machine, he has also used keyboard controllers and occasionally live drums.
</p><p>In 2020 during the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a> he decided to spend time sharpening his skills on the <a href="/wiki/Akai" title="Akai">Akai</a> Music Production Center (MPC) drum machine. 
</p><p>He has worked with several artists including <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a> and <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Mob" title="ASAP Mob">ASAP Mob</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azealia_Banks" title="Azealia Banks">Azealia Banks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vado_(rapper)" title="Vado (rapper)">Vado</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">The Diplomats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hell_Rell" title="Hell Rell">Hell Rell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, ArJaye the King, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse_(hip_hop_group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Slaughterhouse (hip hop group)">Slaughterhouse</a> among others.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Creative_process">Creative process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AraabMuzik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Creative process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In an interview Araabmuzik revealed that he made all of his music while sober.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Araabmuzik has sampled 187 songs from other artists, including <a href="/wiki/The_Who" title="The Who">The Who</a>'s "Won't Get Fooled Again" which he restyled as "Outer Limits."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AraabMuzik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 2012, trance producer Adam K accused Araabmuzik of plagiarism for the records that he had sampled on <i>Electronic Dream</i> without giving proper credit.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early May 2013, AraabMuzik was shot in an attempted <a href="/wiki/Robbery" title="Robbery">robbery</a>. He was walking around his neighborhood in the company of his friends when several men attempted to take his money. He resisted and was shot in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2015, a track was supposedly leaked from the unreleased <i>Dream World</i> album featuring R&amp;B artist <a href="/wiki/Kelela" title="Kelela">Kelela</a>, named "Final Hour", much to Kelela's disappointment and frustrations which she expressed on Twitter.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Araabmuzik's management released an official PR release claiming no responsibility for the leak.
</p><p>In February 2016, AraabMuzik was shot again as a victim of an attempted robbery in <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a>. He had gunshot wounds to his jaw and arm.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Abraham has spoken very little about these incidents having said "There's really not much to speak on [with] something like that" in an interview with Noisey.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
