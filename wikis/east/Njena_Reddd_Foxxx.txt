<p><b>Njena Reddd Foxxx</b> was the stage name of <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> artist Njena Surae Jarvis, active from 2012 to 2013.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Njena_Reddd_Foxxx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012:_Ima_Read">2012: <i>Ima Read</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Njena_Reddd_Foxxx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2012: Ima Read">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Originally from <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>, Jarvis moved to New York in 2000. She performed on <a href="/wiki/Zebra_Katz" title="Zebra Katz">Ojay Morgan</a>'s 2012 single "Ima Read".<sup id="cite_ref-Holmund_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Holmund-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The song was recorded in one night, directly into a computer microphone, and hasn't been re-recorded since.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template"><span style="white-space: nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Link_rot" title="Wikipedia:Link rot"><span title="&#160;Dead link tagged August 2023">dead link</span></a></i>&#93;</span></sup> On January 18, 2012, the song manifested<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. (August 2023)">vague</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> into a music video directed by Ruben XYZ and quickly became a viral underground sensation.<sup id="cite_ref-Ima_Read_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ima_Read-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (August 2023)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (August 2023)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1062260506">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:.5em 1.4em .8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:.5em 0 .8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{overflow:hidden;position:relative;margin:.5em auto .8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft span,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright span{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox>blockquote{margin:0;padding:0;border-left:0;font-family:inherit;font-size:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:110%;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote>:first-child{margin-top:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote:last-child>:last-child{margin-bottom:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .quote-title,.mw-parser-output .quotebox .quotebox-quote{display:block}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:640px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{width:100%!important;margin:0 0 .8em!important;float:none!important}}</style><div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:50%; ;">
<blockquote class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">
<p>"[Reddd Foxxx] was given to me by Ojay on the night we recorded "Ima Read" -- it kind of just stuck. I think I loved it so much because I really love the comedian <a href="/wiki/Redd_Foxx" title="Redd Foxx">Redd Foxx</a> a lot... There's a lot of what I love about his comedy in my work. <p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">—Jarvis, on the origin and spelling of her stage name<sup id="cite_ref-Holmund_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Holmund-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></cite>

<p>In May 2013, Jarvis and Katz opened for <a href="/wiki/Die_Antwoord" title="Die Antwoord">Die Antwoord</a> and toured European festivals through August.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The duo opened for the British leg of <a href="/wiki/Azealia_Banks" title="Azealia Banks">Azealia Banks</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Fantasea_(mixtape)" title="Fantasea (mixtape)">Fantasea</a></i> tour in late 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> "Ima read" also became the soundtrack for <a href="/wiki/Rick_Owens" title="Rick Owens">Rick Owens</a>' and Alex Mattsson's Fall-Winter 2012/2013 and Fall-Winter 2013 collections, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013:_Needful_Things">2013: <i>Needful Things</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Njena_Reddd_Foxxx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2013: Needful Things">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the unexpected success of "Ima Read", Jarvis began penning several songs under her Reddd Foxxx persona, releasing them periodically on her <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> account.
</p><p>In September 2013, Jarvis was featured in a short film, modeling for British designer Kitty Joseph in association with <a href="/wiki/Absolut_Vodka" title="Absolut Vodka">Absolut Vodka</a>. Jarvis' single "Watercolour" was used throughout the film.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template"><span style="white-space: nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Link_rot" title="Wikipedia:Link rot"><span title="&#160;Dead link tagged August 2023">dead link</span></a></i>&#93;</span></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Absolut_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Absolut-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On December 15, 2013, Jarvis released her debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> <i>Needful Things</i>, in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>-based producer Jepordise.
</p>
