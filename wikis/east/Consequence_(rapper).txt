<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Consequence_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Consequence debuted in 1996, appearing several times on <a href="/wiki/A_Tribe_Called_Quest" title="A Tribe Called Quest">A Tribe Called Quest</a>'s fourth album <i><a href="/wiki/Beats,_Rhymes,_and_Life" class="mw-redirect" title="Beats, Rhymes, and Life">Beats, Rhymes, and Life</a></i>, as he is the cousin of member <a href="/wiki/Q-Tip_(musician)" title="Q-Tip (musician)">Q-Tip</a>. In March 2007, he released his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Quit_Your_Day_Job!" title="Don&#39;t Quit Your Day Job!">Don't Quit Your Day Job!</a></i>, under <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a>. Consequence left GOOD Music in 2011, on bad terms, after being signed with the label since 2005. He and West later reconciled, despite Consequence speaking ill of West. Consequence consequently launched his own record label, Band Camp Records, following his departure from GOOD Music.
</p>
