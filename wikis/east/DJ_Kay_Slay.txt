<p><b>Keith Grayson</b> (August 14, 1966 – April 17, 2022), professionally known as <b>DJ Kay Slay</b>, was an American <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a> (DJ) from New York City. He is referred to by <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> as "Hip Hop's One-Man Ministry of Insults".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He released four successful studio albums, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Streetsweeper,_Vol._1" title="The Streetsweeper, Vol. 1">The Streetsweeper, Vol. 1</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Streetsweeper,_Vol._2" title="The Streetsweeper, Vol. 2">The Streetsweeper, Vol. 2</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Champions:_North_Meets_South" title="The Champions: North Meets South">The Champions: North Meets South</a></i> (with Greg Street), and <i><a href="/wiki/More_Than_Just_a_DJ" title="More Than Just a DJ">More Than Just a DJ</a></i>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Kay_Slay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grayson was born August 14, 1966, in New York City. He was originally a prominent <a href="/wiki/Graffiti" title="Graffiti">graffiti</a> artist, having been featured in the 1983 hip hop documentary, <i><a href="/wiki/Style_Wars" title="Style Wars">Style Wars</a></i>. One of Grayson's better known <a href="/wiki/Tag_(graffiti)" title="Tag (graffiti)">tags</a> was "Dez".<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> As a youth involved in New York's flourishing <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> scene, Keith witnessed firsthand the ascent of legendary disc jockeys such as <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_Flash" title="Grandmaster Flash">Grandmaster Flash</a>,&#160;<a href="/wiki/Grand_Wizzard_Theodore" title="Grand Wizzard Theodore">Grand Wizzard Theodore</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kool_DJ_Red_Alert" title="Kool DJ Red Alert">Kool DJ Red Alert</a>, in the late 1970s and&#160;into the 1980s. "I didn't so much set out to be a DJ," he said. "It was just something to do that was fun and that I enjoyed doing."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> With the decline of the graffiti movement in the late 1980s, Dez began dealing with <a href="/wiki/Narcotics" class="mw-redirect" title="Narcotics">narcotics</a> and consequently ended up in jail by the late 1980s. Grayson was released from jail in 1990, and claimed to have abstained from using drugs thereafter. He hailed from the East River Houses located in <a href="/wiki/East_Harlem" title="East Harlem">East Harlem</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>.In the early years of his life, he also met long-time friend <a href="/wiki/Sauce_Money" title="Sauce Money">Sauce Money</a>, whom he was friends with until his death.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Kay_Slay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2003.E2.80.932009:_Streetsweeper_series"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2003–2009:_Streetsweeper_series">2003–2009: <i>Streetsweeper</i> series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Kay_Slay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2003–2009: Streetsweeper series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>DJ Kay Slay released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Streetsweeper,_Vol._1" title="The Streetsweeper, Vol. 1">The Streetsweeper, Vol. 1</a></i>, on May 20, 2003. In the summer of 2003, Kay Slay released a <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>, accompanied by a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a>, for a song titled "Too Much For Me". The single, which features a <a href="/wiki/Refrain" title="Refrain">chorus</a> sung by then-up-and-coming singer <a href="/wiki/Amerie" title="Amerie">Amerie</a>, also features <a href="/wiki/Verse_(popular_music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Verse (popular music)">verses</a> from American rappers <a href="/wiki/Birdman_(rapper)" title="Birdman (rapper)">Birdman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Nas (rapper)">Nas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Foxy_Brown_(rapper)" title="Foxy Brown (rapper)">Foxy Brown</a>. The song peaked at number 53 on the US <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> chart, making it the DJ's highest-charting single to date. The single's music video includes <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo appearances</a> by <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>, <a href="/wiki/N.O.R.E." title="N.O.R.E.">N.O.R.E.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/WC_(rapper)" title="WC (rapper)">WC</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Although the song was not a major success, its music video was aired on <a href="/wiki/MTV_Jams" class="mw-redirect" title="MTV Jams">MTV Jams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Entertainment Television">BET</a>. The "Too Much For Me" video did not feature Nas (because of Nas' solo projects) or Baby; so <a href="/wiki/Loon_(rapper)" title="Loon (rapper)">Loon</a> was featured instead. This replacement started a feud between Nas and Kay Slay.
</p><p>On March 30, 2004, Kay Slay's second album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Streetsweeper,_Vol._2" title="The Streetsweeper, Vol. 2">The Streetsweeper, Vol. 2</a></i>, was released. Another single and video were released for "Who Gives A...Where You From" with <a href="/wiki/Three_6_Mafia" title="Three 6 Mafia">Three 6 Mafia</a>, which peaked at number 89 on the Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs. Kay Slay and the song were featured on the 2004 <a href="/wiki/NFL_Street" title="NFL Street">NFL Street</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932022:_More_Than_Just_a_DJ_and_Rhyme_or_Die"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2022:_More_Than_Just_a_DJ_and_Rhyme_or_Die">2010–2022: <i>More Than Just a DJ</i> and <i>Rhyme or Die</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Kay_Slay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010–2022: More Than Just a DJ and Rhyme or Die">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After releasing <i><a href="/wiki/More_Than_Just_a_DJ" title="More Than Just a DJ">More Than Just a DJ</a></i> in 2010, <i>Rhyme or Die</i> was released. The first album's initial two singles "60 Second Assassins" featuring <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Layzie_Bone" title="Layzie Bone">Layzie Bone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Twista" title="Twista">Twista</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jaz-O" title="Jaz-O">Jaz-O</a> and "The Kings of the Streets" featuring <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a>, DJ Doo Wop, and Fly Nate were released in 2011. In 2013, "About That Life" featuring <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nelly" title="Nelly">Nelly</a>, and <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> was released as a single from <i>Rhyme or Die</i>. It debuted and peaked at #54 on the Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs chart, making it one of Kay Slay's most successful singles to date. In the beginning of 2014, "Free Again" was released featuring <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a> and <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, which came as a surprise for some seeing as how they had "beef" before, but  later reconciled. In 2021, Dj Kay Slay released the track "Rolling 110 Deep" which featured 110 hip hop artists with contributing verses from <a href="/wiki/Ice-T" title="Ice-T">Ice-T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shaquille_O%27Neal" title="Shaquille O&#39;Neal">Shaq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coke_La_Rock" title="Coke La Rock">Coke La Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roy_Jones_Jr." title="Roy Jones Jr.">Roy Jones Jr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Omar_Epps" title="Omar Epps">Omar Epps</a>, and others.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Illness_and_death">Illness and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Kay_Slay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Illness and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January  2022, DJ Kay Slay's brother said he was in the hospital after contracting <a href="/wiki/COVID-19" title="COVID-19">COVID-19</a>, but was "in a recovery state".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He died from COVID-19 in New York City, on April 17, 2022, at the age of 55.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
