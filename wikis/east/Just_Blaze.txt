<p><b>Justin Gregory Smith</b> (born January 8, 1978), known professionally as <b>Just Blaze</b>, is an American hip hop producer and DJ. Born in <a href="/wiki/Paterson,_New_Jersey" title="Paterson, New Jersey">Paterson, New Jersey</a>, Blaze attended <a href="/wiki/Rutgers_University" title="Rutgers University">Rutgers University</a> for three years before dropping out to pursue his musical career.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His stage name was inspired by the character <a href="/wiki/Blaze_Fielding" class="mw-redirect" title="Blaze Fielding">Blaze Fielding</a> from the <i><a href="/wiki/Streets_of_Rage" title="Streets of Rage">Streets of Rage</a></i> video game series.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Blaze gained recognition as a producer for <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-A-Fella Records</a> in the early 2000s, utilizing the "<a href="/wiki/Chipmunk_soul" class="mw-redirect" title="Chipmunk soul">chipmunk soul</a>" sampling style popularized and used alongside <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, that was prominent in the early to mid-2000s hip-hop scene.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He appears in the video for the third single from <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem's</a> 2010 album <i><a href="/wiki/Recovery_(Eminem_album)" title="Recovery (Eminem album)">Recovery</a></i> entitled "<a href="/wiki/No_Love" title="No Love">No Love</a>", which he produced. Blaze is also the founder and CEO of <a href="/wiki/Fort_Knocks_Entertainment" title="Fort Knocks Entertainment">Fort Knocks Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He has also composed soundtracks for video games, and was a character in <i><a href="/wiki/NBA_Street_Vol._2" title="NBA Street Vol. 2">NBA Street Vol. 2</a></i> .<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His hometown of Paterson honored him in July 2018 with a key to the city in recognition of his "outstanding cultural contribution" to the city.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
