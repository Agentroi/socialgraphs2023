<p><b>Felicia Pearson</b> (born May 18, 1980) is an American television actress, rapper, published author and convicted murderer. She played <a href="/wiki/Snoop_(The_Wire)" title="Snoop (The Wire)">Felicia "Snoop" Pearson</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wire_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Wire (TV series)">The Wire</a></i> and wrote a 2007 memoir, <i><a href="/wiki/Grace_After_Midnight" title="Grace After Midnight">Grace After Midnight</a>,</i> detailing her troubled childhood and time in prison for <a href="/wiki/Second-degree_murder" class="mw-redirect" title="Second-degree murder">second-degree murder</a>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Felicia_Pearson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pearson was born in <a href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="Baltimore, Maryland">Baltimore, Maryland</a>, the daughter of two incarcerated drug addicts, and was raised in an East Baltimore <a href="/wiki/Foster_home" class="mw-redirect" title="Foster home">foster home</a>. Born premature and weighing three pounds, she was not expected to live.<sup id="cite_ref-bsunLegal311201_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bsunLegal311201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  She was so small that she was fed with an eyedropper until she could be fed normally.<sup id="cite_ref-bsunLegal311201_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bsunLegal311201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> According to her memoir, <i><a href="/wiki/Grace_After_Midnight" title="Grace After Midnight">Grace After Midnight</a></i>, she met her biological parents very few times, her mother was a <a href="/wiki/Crack_cocaine" title="Crack cocaine">crack</a> addict, and her father was an armed robber. As a result of this, she decided to go by her foster family's surname.
</p><p>Pearson was a <a href="/wiki/Tomboy" title="Tomboy">tomboy</a> from a young age and worked as a <a href="/wiki/Drug_dealer" class="mw-redirect" title="Drug dealer">drug dealer</a> as a teenager. At the age of fourteen, she was convicted of <a href="/wiki/Second_degree_murder" class="mw-redirect" title="Second degree murder">second degree murder</a> after the shooting of a girl named Okia Toomer. Pearson was sentenced to two eight-year terms, to be served consecutively, at the <a href="/wiki/Maryland_Correctional_Institution_for_Women" title="Maryland Correctional Institution for Women">Maryland Correctional Institution for Women</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jessup,_Maryland" title="Jessup, Maryland">Jessup, Maryland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She was released after six and a half years.
</p><p>Pearson said her life turned around at the age of eighteen, when Arnold Loney, a local drug dealer who looked out for her and sent her money in prison, was shot and killed.<sup id="cite_ref-bsunLegal311201_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bsunLegal311201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He had coined her nickname "Snoop," because she reminded him of <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Brown" title="Charlie Brown">Charlie Brown</a>'s beagle <a href="/wiki/Snoopy" title="Snoopy">Snoopy</a> in the comic strip <i><a href="/wiki/Peanuts" title="Peanuts">Peanuts</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-bsunLegal311201_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bsunLegal311201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> While in prison, Pearson earned her <a href="/wiki/GED" class="mw-redirect" title="GED">GED</a>. She was released in 2000,<sup id="cite_ref-bsunLegal311201_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bsunLegal311201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and landed a local job <a href="/wiki/Metal_fabrication" title="Metal fabrication">fabricating</a> car bumpers, but was fired after two weeks when her employer learned she had a prison record.<sup id="cite_ref-New_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Felicia_Pearson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Felicia_Pearson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pearson met <a href="/wiki/Michael_K._Williams" title="Michael K. Williams">Michael K. Williams</a>, who played <a href="/wiki/Omar_Little" title="Omar Little">Omar Little</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wire" title="The Wire">The Wire</a>,</i> in a Baltimore club. He invited her to come to the set one day, and introduced her to the writers and the producers. After subsequent auditions, she was offered a role in the series.<sup id="cite_ref-bsunLegal311201_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bsunLegal311201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> For her performance in <i>The Wire,</i> <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a> called her "perhaps the most terrifying female villain to ever appear in a television series."<sup id="cite_ref-New_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Her appearance on the show kickstarted her acting career, leading to appearances in music videos for R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Mo" title="Lil&#39; Mo">Lil' Mo</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Dem_Boyz_(Lil%27_Mo_song)" title="Dem Boyz (Lil&#39; Mo song)">Dem Boyz</a>"; rapper <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rick Ross (rapper)">Rick Ross</a>' "<a href="/wiki/The_Boss_(Rick_Ross_song)" title="The Boss (Rick Ross song)">The Boss</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Here_I_Am_(Rick_Ross_song)" title="Here I Am (Rick Ross song)">Here I Am</a>"; rapper <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hood" title="Ace Hood">Ace Hood</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Cash_Flow_(song)" title="Cash Flow (song)">Cash Flow</a>"; <a href="/wiki/A$AP_Ferg" class="mw-redirect" title="A$AP Ferg">A$AP Ferg</a>'s "Shabba (feat <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a>)" and <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/So_Many_Pros" title="So Many Pros">So Many Pros</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She has appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a> films <i><a href="/wiki/Da_Sweet_Blood_of_Jesus" title="Da Sweet Blood of Jesus">Da Sweet Blood of Jesus</a>,</i><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Chi-Raq" title="Chi-Raq">Chi-Raq</a>,</i><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and in the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> police procedural drama <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Bloods_(TV_series)" title="Blue Bloods (TV series)">Blue Bloods</a>,</i> titled "<a href="/wiki/Blue_Bloods_(season_7)#ep135" title="Blue Bloods (season 7)">Good Cop Bad Cop</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pearson joined the cast of the VH1 <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">reality television</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_New_York" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York">Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York</a></i> for its <a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_New_York_(season_7)" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York (season 7)">seventh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_New_York_(season_8)" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York (season 8)">eighth</a> seasons.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Felicia_Pearson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pearson is featured in the song "It's a Stick Up" with <a href="/wiki/Tony_Yayo" title="Tony Yayo">Tony Yayo</a> and Mazaradi Fox, with its music video featuring clips from <i>The Wire</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Volunteer_work">Volunteer work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Felicia_Pearson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Volunteer work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pearson has volunteered as a prison visitor, worked on anti-violence and literacy campaigns for youth, and supported <i>The Stay Strong Foundation</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-danaroc_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-danaroc-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cannick_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cannick-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Felicia_Pearson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On March 10, 2011, Pearson and sixty others were arrested and charged with drug offenses. The arrest was made during a predawn raid at her home in Baltimore, following a five-month <a href="/wiki/Drug_Enforcement_Administration" title="Drug Enforcement Administration">DEA</a> operation.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> At the first hearing after Pearson's arrest, Judge John Addison Howard denied her bail due to Pearson's acting ability, stating: "Well, you can change your appearance, I've seen the episodes of <i>The Wire</i> in which you appear. You look very different than you do here today, and I'm not talking about the jumpsuit, I'm talking about your general appearance."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  After a month in jail, Pearson was offered bail of $50,000 on April 8, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  In August 2011, she pleaded guilty to the charges the day before her trial was to begin.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt9AugPlea_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt9AugPlea-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> She was sentenced to a suspended seven-year prison term, with credit for time served, and given three years of supervised probation.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pearson is a <a href="/wiki/Lesbian" title="Lesbian">lesbian</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
