<p><b>Tione Jayden Merritt</b><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Pronunciation" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Pronunciation"><span title="English IPA transcription needed for this term. (August 2023)">needs  IPA</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> (born April 30, 2001), known professionally as <b>Lil Tjay</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/i/: &#39;y&#39; in &#39;happy&#39;">i</span><span title="/ʒ/: &#39;s&#39; in &#39;pleasure&#39;">ʒ</span><span title="/eɪ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;face&#39;">eɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">TEE</span>-jay</i></a>),<sup id="cite_ref-Tjay_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tjay-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an American rapper and singer. He rose to prominence in 2017 with his song "Resume", and then the release of his breakthrough song "Brothers" which led him to sign with <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> later during that year. Lil Tjay's debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/True_2_Myself" title="True 2 Myself">True 2 Myself</a></i>, was released in 2019 and debuted at number 5 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, marking the album his first charting project. The following year, he released the mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/State_of_Emergency_(Lil_Tjay_EP)" title="State of Emergency (Lil Tjay EP)">State of Emergency</a></i>, that featured only <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_drill" title="Brooklyn drill">drill</a> artists from <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. 
</p><p>His second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Destined_2_Win" title="Destined 2 Win">Destined 2 Win</a></i>, was released in 2021 and included the single "<a href="/wiki/Calling_My_Phone" title="Calling My Phone">Calling My Phone</a>" (with <a href="/wiki/6lack" title="6lack">6lack</a>); his highest-charting song to date. In 2023, released the song "<a href="/wiki/Gangsta_Boo_(song)" title="Gangsta Boo (song)">Gangsta Boo</a>" with fellow New York artist <a href="/wiki/Ice_Spice" title="Ice Spice">Ice Spice</a>, and released his third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/222_(album)" title="222 (album)">222</a></i>, in July of that year.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Tjay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Merritt was born and raised in the <a href="/wiki/Fordham,_Bronx" title="Fordham, Bronx">Fordham</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Tjay_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tjay-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was raised by a single mother and has two younger siblings in what was described as a "roomy" apartment. Merritt called himself the problematic child out of the three, as he would often get himself into petty robberies and school fights. At the age of 15, Merritt was arrested for one of his robberies and was sentenced for a year to a <a href="/wiki/Youth_detention_center" title="Youth detention center">youth detention center</a> where he would begin to write raps; they included one of his hit songs "Resume", which he released on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Merritt's childhood friend, Smelly, was fatally stabbed in August 2016. Merritt pays tribute to his childhood friend in a number of his songs referencing his name, "Smelly".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Tjay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932019:_Beginnings.2C_record_deal.2C_and_True_2_Myself"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2019:_Beginnings,_record_deal,_and_True_2_Myself">2016–2019: Beginnings, record deal, and <i>True 2 Myself</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Tjay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2016–2019: Beginnings, record deal, and True 2 Myself">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2016, Merritt began releasing music on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork.com_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> "Resume", one of his first songs, was released when Merritt was 16 years old, and quickly began spreading online.
</p><p>On March 10, 2018, Merritt competed and placed first at the NYC All Ages Edition of Coast 2 Coast LIVE, where his performance gained the attention of a record label <a href="/wiki/Artists_and_repertoire" title="Artists and repertoire">A&amp;R</a> who was in attendance to judge the showcase.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Soon after, Merritt released the single "<a href="/wiki/Brothers_(Lil_Tjay_song)" title="Brothers (Lil Tjay song)">Brothers</a>", which became his biggest song at the time and led to him signing with <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Released on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> in 2018, Merritt earned himself recognition through his sampling of <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Baby_(Justin_Bieber_song)" title="Baby (Justin Bieber song)">Baby</a>" on his single, "None of Your Love".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Within the first ten months of Merritt's music career, he had released five tracks that recorded over one million plays on SoundCloud.<sup id="cite_ref-Elevator_Exploding_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Elevator_Exploding-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AllMusic_Bio_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic_Bio-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> "Brothers", "Resume", "Goat", and "<a href="/wiki/Leaked_(song)" title="Leaked (song)">Leaked</a>" each accumulated tens of millions of streams.<sup id="cite_ref-SoundCloud_Lil_Tjay_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SoundCloud_Lil_Tjay-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In July 2018, Merritt worked with producer <a href="/wiki/CashMoneyAP" title="CashMoneyAP">CashMoneyAP</a> to release "None of Your Love", which also amassed tens of millions of plays.<sup id="cite_ref-Elevator_Exploding_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Elevator_Exploding-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Merritt released his debut EP <i>No Comparison</i> at the end of 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-AllMusic_Bio_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic_Bio-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2019, Merritt was featured on fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Polo_G" title="Polo G">Polo G</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Pop_Out" title="Pop Out">Pop Out</a>", which peaked at number 11 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Itunes_Lil_Tjay_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Itunes_Lil_Tjay-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In July, he was featured on boy band <a href="/wiki/PrettyMuch" title="PrettyMuch">PrettyMuch</a>'s "Lying".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Later that year, he released an EP titled <i>F.N</i>; the project's lead single, "<a href="/wiki/F.N_(song)" title="F.N (song)">F.N</a>", peaked at number 56 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 and became his first solo charting song. Merritt released his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/True_2_Myself" title="True 2 Myself">True 2 Myself</a></i> on October 11, 2019. It debuted and peaked at number 5 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. In December, he was featured on a remix of <a href="/wiki/24kGoldn" title="24kGoldn">24kGoldn</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Valentino_(24kGoldn_song)" title="Valentino (24kGoldn song)">Valentino</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2020.E2.80.932021:_State_of_Emergency_and_Destined_2_Win"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020–2021:_State_of_Emergency_and_Destined_2_Win">2020–2021: <i>State of Emergency</i> and <i>Destined 2 Win</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Tjay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2020–2021: State of Emergency and Destined 2 Win">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In early 2020, Merritt reached the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 again with his single "<a href="/wiki/20/20_(song)" title="20/20 (song)">20/20</a>", which peaked at number 94 on the chart. On May 8, 2020, Merritt released the EP <i><a href="/wiki/State_of_Emergency_(Lil_Tjay_EP)" title="State of Emergency (Lil Tjay EP)">State of Emergency</a></i>, which focused heavily on <a href="/wiki/Drill_music" title="Drill music">drill music</a>, a popular subgenre in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SOE_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SOE-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The seven-song EP included the track "Zoo York" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Fivio_Foreign" title="Fivio Foreign">Fivio Foreign</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pop_Smoke" title="Pop Smoke">Pop Smoke</a>), which peaked at number 65 on the Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In July, Merritt appeared on Pop Smoke's debut posthumous album, <i><a href="/wiki/Shoot_for_the_Stars,_Aim_for_the_Moon" title="Shoot for the Stars, Aim for the Moon">Shoot for the Stars, Aim for the Moon</a></i>, on the track "<a href="/wiki/Mood_Swings_(Pop_Smoke_song)" title="Mood Swings (Pop Smoke song)">Mood Swings</a>", which reached number 17 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On August 11, Merritt was included on <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i>'s 2020 <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)#Annual_Freshman_Class_list" title="XXL (magazine)">Freshman Class</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In October, Merritt hinted at a new project on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 12, 2021, Merritt released his single "<a href="/wiki/Calling_My_Phone" title="Calling My Phone">Calling My Phone</a>", a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> singer <a href="/wiki/6lack" title="6lack">6lack</a>, which was accompanied by a music video directed by Cam Busby.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The song debuted at number three on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, becoming the highest-charting single of Merritt's career.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On March 19, Merritt released "<a href="/wiki/Headshot_(song)" title="Headshot (song)">Headshot</a>", a collaboration with Polo G and Fivio Foreign as the second single from his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Destined_2_Win" title="Destined 2 Win">Destined 2 Win</a></i>, which was released on April 2, 2021. The album consists of twenty-one tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-Destined_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Destined-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On October 22, Merritt released the lead single to his upcoming third studio album "<a href="/wiki/Not_in_the_Mood" title="Not in the Mood">Not in the Mood</a>" (featuring Fivio Foreign and <a href="/wiki/Kay_Flock" title="Kay Flock">Kay Flock</a>). The single was teased in snippets using <a href="/wiki/TikTok" title="TikTok">TikTok</a> from September prior to its release.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2022.E2.80.93present:_222"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2022–present:_222">2022–present: <i>222</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Tjay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2022–present: 222">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In early March 2022, Merritt began teasing a new song titled "<a href="/wiki/In_My_Head_(Lil_Tjay_song)" title="In My Head (Lil Tjay song)">In My Head</a>" which uses a sample of <a href="/wiki/Iyaz" title="Iyaz">Iyaz</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Replay_(Iyaz_song)" title="Replay (Iyaz song)">Replay</a>". It was eventually released on April 1, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On April, Merritt released a freestyle "Lavish" on his YouTube channel. The following day, he announced his EP <i>Strictly 4 My Fans</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On August 26, Merritt released his single "Beat the Odds", which details his near-fatal shooting and an update on his recovery.<sup id="cite_ref-hypebeast.com_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hypebeast.com-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On November 17, Merritt teased a trailer for his single "Give You What You Want", which was released the same day at 4 p.m. on YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late June 2023, Merritt revealed on social media that a new album was on the way<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> and released its lead singles "June 22nd" on June 23,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> which that he revealed the album's title <i><a href="/wiki/222_(album)" title="222 (album)">222</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/Project_Walls" title="Project Walls">Project Walls</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/YoungBoy_Never_Broke_Again" title="YoungBoy Never Broke Again">YoungBoy Never Broke Again</a> on July 7,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> on which he also revealed the album's cover and tracklist with features from <a href="/wiki/Summer_Walker" title="Summer Walker">Summer Walker</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Kid_LAROI" class="mw-redirect" title="The Kid LAROI">The Kid LAROI</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fivio_Foreign" title="Fivio Foreign">Fivio Foreign</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polo_G" title="Polo G">Polo G</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coco_Jones" title="Coco Jones">Coco Jones</a>. Before the album's release, Merritt released short documentaries on his social media and performed "Bla Bla" with Fivio Foreign at <a href="/wiki/Wireless_Festival" title="Wireless Festival">Wireless Festival</a> 2023. <i>222</i> was released on July 14, 2023. <i>222</i> sold 22k in its first week.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2022_shooting">2022 shooting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Tjay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2022 shooting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On June 22, 2022, Merritt was shot seven times during an attempted robbery in <a href="/wiki/Edgewater,_New_Jersey" title="Edgewater, New Jersey">Edgewater, New Jersey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He was flown to <a href="/wiki/Hackensack_University_Medical_Center" title="Hackensack University Medical Center">Hackensack University Medical Center</a> and underwent emergency surgery, while a second victim—22-year-old Antoine Boyd—who is friends with Merritt, was also shot once and was in stable condition.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, the shooter was also hit by gunfire, and escaped with help from an unidentified co-conspirator.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The Bergen County Prosecutor's Office reported that the shooting did not appear random, and that one victim was upgraded from critical to stable condition, while the other was in good condition.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Later that day, police arrested 27-year-old Mohamed Konate, who was charged with three counts of first-degree <a href="/wiki/Attempted_murder" title="Attempted murder">attempted murder</a>, three counts of first-degree armed robbery, and multiple weapons offenses.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Jeffery Valdez, who was with Merritt but was not injured, and Antoine Boyd were both charged with second-degree unlawful possession of a weapon, but charges were later dropped. Merritt was not charged with any crime.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 23, 2022, it was reported that Merritt was still unconscious following the shooting.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Days following a lack of any credible updates on Merritt's condition, rumors began to surface claiming that he was paralyzed and possibly brain dead. However, multiple sources later disproved these rumors, as news came out on July 1, 2022, stating that Merritt was still hospitalized but "alert and talking."<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 24, 2022, Merrit uploaded an update video on his social media accounts. In the video, he states how he's doing better and is "going to come back stronger than ever" while wearing a neck brace.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 26, 2022, Merritt released his single "Beat the Odds", which details his near-fatal experience and recovery.<sup id="cite_ref-hypebeast.com_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hypebeast.com-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Tjay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 16, 2023, Merritt was arrested in the Bronx and was placed in police custody, following a traffic stop.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Merritt was on his way to film with fellow Bronx rapper <a href="/wiki/Ice_Spice" title="Ice Spice">Ice Spice</a> at the time of the arrest. The police found a handgun in the vehicle, before arresting him. He was charged with weapon possession and a court date was set for February 14.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx.com_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx.com-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown whether Merritt attended the trial, and what the results of the trial were.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Merritt's attorney, Dawn Florio, claims the gun found by police does not belong to Merritt.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> She also claims that the search conducted by officers was conducted illegally, as the car was allegedly parked legally on the street and Merritt was allegedly a passenger in the car.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx.com_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx.com-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lil Tjay and director Fakedell’s film crew were arrested in June of 2023 for a firearm used during shooting of a music video. The incident was caught on video by a crowd of kids first pleading with the cops to not arrest him, and that the gun was just a prop.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Tjay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Influenced by his upbringing in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>, Merritt is a melodic rapper who frequently incorporates <a href="/wiki/Auto-Tune" title="Auto-Tune">Auto-Tune</a> in his songs.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_Feel_Old_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone_Feel_Old-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> He has drawn comparisons to fellow <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>, he revealed that his biggest musical influences are <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Usher_(musician)" title="Usher (musician)">Usher</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_Feel_Old_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone_Feel_Old-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> In the same interview, Merritt described his music as having a melodic sound when singing or rapping about his past struggles. Merritt also credits his success to his year spent in prison, where he claims that his ability to write songs peaked.<sup id="cite_ref-AllMusic_Bio_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic_Bio-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)"><i>Pitchfork</i></a>'s featured article on Merritt, he is referred to as a "hip-hop heartthrob".<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork.com_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Later in the interview, Merritt explained that he is often called the "Bronx Justin Bieber" because of his sampling of <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Baby_(Justin_Bieber_song)" title="Baby (Justin Bieber song)">Baby</a>" on his song, "None of Your Love".<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork.com_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Tjay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Merritt accompanied <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/Lil_Mosey" title="Lil Mosey">Lil Mosey</a> on his nationwide tour throughout 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> He also has performed at the three-day <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Loud" title="Rolling Loud">Rolling Loud</a> music festival along with <a href="/wiki/Migos" title="Migos">Migos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kid_Cudi" title="Kid Cudi">Kid Cudi</a> in <a href="/wiki/Miami_Gardens,_Florida" title="Miami Gardens, Florida">Miami Gardens, Florida</a>, in May 2019<sup id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_Rolling_Loud_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork_Rolling_Loud-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> and at the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> festival in December. He frequently appears and performs on concerts with close friend Jay Gwuapo.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In September 2022, a few months after the shooting, Merritt performed his first concert since being shot. He returned to the stage for his 2022 Rolling Loud performance on September 23 in New York.
</p>
