<p><b>Anthony “Ace” Patterson</b> (August 15, 1989), also known by his stage name <b>Call Me Ace</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Bridgeport,_Connecticut" title="Bridgeport, Connecticut">Bridgeport, Connecticut</a>, and currently lives in the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">San Francisco Bay Area</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dailycal070519_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailycal070519-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In December 2016, Call Me Ace released his first EP, <i>Misinterpretations</i> while still a business consultant at <a href="/wiki/Deloitte_Consulting_LLP" class="mw-redirect" title="Deloitte Consulting LLP">Deloitte Consulting LLP</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nookandkranny112816_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nookandkranny112816-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-poetsandquants022018_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poetsandquants022018-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In March 2019 he released <i>Airplane Mode</i>, which debuted at #3 on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> Top 40 US Hip-Hop Album Chart and at #50 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> R&amp;B / Hip-Hop Album Sales Chart.<sup id="cite_ref-diymusician_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-diymusician-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, Call Me Ace released his first EP, <i>Misinterpretations</i>, solely produced by Bay Area legend producer, <a href="/wiki/Sean_T" title="Sean T">Sean T</a>, who has produced for <a href="/wiki/Mac_Dre" title="Mac Dre">Mac Dre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mistah_F.A.B." title="Mistah F.A.B.">Mistah F.A.B.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nookandkranny112816_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nookandkranny112816-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i>Misinterpretations</i> ended up being the #9 most-spun album on KUSF radio in February 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In August 2017, Call Me Ace released the <i>2interpretations</i> mixtape, which reached #19 on <i>Thizzler'</i>s Bay Area Music Chart that same month (rank of 50).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In March 2019 he released his fourth project and first independent studio album, <i>Airplane Mode</i>, which debuted at #50 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> R&amp;B / Hip-Hop Album Sales Chart.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-diymusician_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-diymusician-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In February 2020, Call Me Ace released his 2nd EP, <i>Working From Home</i>, which reached over 1 million streams on Spotify, despite having recently changed jobs from Facebook to YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In June 2020, he quickly followed up with the EP, <i>Working From Home: Extended</i> - inspired by the COVID-19 global pandemic and recent social unrest - and used his album campaign as a way to fundraise over $10,000 in effort to combat racial injustice towards black and brown communities in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2021, Call Me Ace released his second studio album, <i>Out Of Office</i>, which is his third project created and released during the pandemic.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In the same month, Call Me Ace was also recognized in Symphonic Distribution's "20 Black Artists Who Are On The Rise in 2021" list.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In April 2021, Call Me Ace reached the Top 10 of <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a> Music's national emerging talent search competition, BET AmpliFind.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="Early_life.2C_education.2C_and_career"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life,_education,_and_career">Early life, education, and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Call_Me_Ace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life, education, and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ace Patterson was born in <a href="/wiki/Bridgeport,_Connecticut" title="Bridgeport, Connecticut">Bridgeport, Connecticut</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dailycal070519_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailycal070519-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His parents emigrated from <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> before they had him.<sup id="cite_ref-dailycal070519_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailycal070519-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Growing up, Ace was interested in drawing, acting, poetry, and, eventually, rapping by the time he finished middle school. He went to Greens Farms Academy for high school and middle school<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, Ace enrolled at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-columbiaspectator051811_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-columbiaspectator051811-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, he co-founded the student group, <i>Columbia University Society of Hip-Hop</i> (CUSH).<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-poetsandquants022018_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poetsandquants022018-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Before graduating, he and his group CUSH opened up for <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> at the annual Bacchanal concert.<sup id="cite_ref-columbiaspectator051811_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-columbiaspectator051811-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, Ace received his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> in <a href="/wiki/Anthropology" title="Anthropology">Anthropology</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Ace was quoted and referenced in a chapter of the Oxford Handbook of Critical Improvisation titled, "Improvisation in Freestyle Rap," written by Ellie Hisama.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Ace left his role at <a href="/wiki/Success_Academy_Charter_Schools" title="Success Academy Charter Schools">Success Academy Charter Schools</a> to attend graduate school at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">University of California, Berkeley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haas_School_of_Business" title="Haas School of Business">Haas School of Business</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-poetsandquants022018_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poetsandquants022018-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During his last semester, he and a classmate made a parody of Drake's “<a href="/wiki/The_Motto_(Drake_song)" title="The Motto (Drake song)">The Motto</a>” in honor of the school and called it, “YOHO (You Only Haas Once)”. The music video was featured on <i>Poets &amp; Quants</i> the day of release and has over 10,000 views to date.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, Ace received his <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Business_Administration" title="Master of Business Administration">Master of Business Administration</a> and joined <a href="/wiki/Deloitte_Consulting" class="mw-redirect" title="Deloitte Consulting">Deloitte Consulting</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-poetsandquants022018_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poetsandquants022018-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ace worked in <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">Marketing</a> at <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> and is part of the 2019 class of ADCOLOR FUTURES, a nationally selective program that celebrates diverse influencers that excel in their careers and also give back to the community.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Currently, Ace works on the Creator &amp; Artist Development team at <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> as the Global Program Manager for Music Label Partnerships.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_influences">Musical influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Call_Me_Ace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Call Me Ace has noted <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> as rappers that inspired him when he was younger.<sup id="cite_ref-nookandkranny112816_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nookandkranny112816-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Ace has eclectic music tastes, having referenced genres like <a href="/wiki/Cuban_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Cuban music">Cuban music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">Blues</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">Jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dancehall" title="Dancehall">Dancehall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afrobeats" title="Afrobeats">Afrobeats</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethiopian music">Ethiopian music</a> as being additional sources of inspiration.<sup id="cite_ref-nookandkranny112816_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nookandkranny112816-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dailycal070519_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailycal070519-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His music style is influenced by his Jamaican roots, the East Coast, the <a href="/wiki/Bay_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Bay Area">Bay Area</a>, and his international travel experiences.<sup id="cite_ref-dailycal070519_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailycal070519-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Call Me Ace has stated that he and his wife have visited over 100 international cities across 50 countries, are both multilingual, and are both children of immigrants - all of which have given him a global, cross-cultural outlook on life and in his music.<sup id="cite_ref-diymusician_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-diymusician-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
