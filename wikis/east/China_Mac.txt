<p><b>Raymond Yu</b> (born February 5, 1982),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>China Mac</b>, is an American rapper, entertainer, activist, and former gang member.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China_Mac&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yu was born and raised in Brooklyn to Chinese immigrants, from <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He moved into a <a href="/wiki/Group_home" title="Group home">group home</a> at the age of 8.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Yu joined the <a href="/wiki/Ghost_Shadows" title="Ghost Shadows">Ghost Shadows</a> gang when he was 12.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In his teenage years, he would partake in <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle rap</a> battles with other kids at the <a href="/wiki/Juvenile_detention" class="mw-redirect" title="Juvenile detention">juvenile detention</a> center.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China_Mac&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2000.E2.80.932013:_Incarceration"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000–2013:_Incarceration">2000–2013: Incarceration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China_Mac&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000–2013: Incarceration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the age of 18, Mac was sentenced to three years in prison for gang related crimes in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 9, 2003, Mac was involved in an altercation with <a href="/wiki/MC_Jin" title="MC Jin">MC Jin</a> at a bar in <a href="/wiki/Chinatown,_Manhattan" title="Chinatown, Manhattan">Chinatown, Manhattan</a>, where he shot Jin's acquaintance, rapper Christopher "LS" Louie, in the back.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Mac later went <a href="/wiki/Fugitive" title="Fugitive">on the run</a> for over a year and was apprehended in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, Washington when he tried to leave the country with a fake passport.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In prison, he was nicknamed "China Mac" by the Mac Ballers gang. He was released on parole in November 2013 and founded the Red Money Records record label and pet store with the money he saved up while in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.93present:_Music_production_.26_activism"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–present:_Music_production_&amp;_activism">2014–present: Music production &amp; activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China_Mac&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2014–present: Music production &amp; activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mac returned to prison for an accused parole violation and was later released in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Since then, he has uploaded video content, including the food show <i>Mac Eats</i>, onto his YouTube channel, China Mac TV.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mac released his album <i>MITM</i> in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, Mac was a prominent critic of <a href="/wiki/Lil_Pump" title="Lil Pump">Lil Pump</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Butterfly_Doors" title="Butterfly Doors">Butterfly Doors</a>", which used the pejorative <a href="/wiki/Ching_chong" title="Ching chong">ching chong</a> slur.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, he released the dual EP, <i>Yin</i> and <i>Yang.</i><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> That same year, Mac released a Chinese/Spanish record with <a href="/wiki/Tali_Goya" title="Tali Goya">Tali Goya</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2020, amidst the <a href="/wiki/Xenophobia_and_racism_related_to_the_COVID-19_pandemic#United_States" title="Xenophobia and racism related to the COVID-19 pandemic">rise in anti-Asian hate crimes during the COVID-19 pandemic</a> in the United States, an 89 year old Chinese grandmother was assaulted and set on fire in <a href="/wiki/Bensonhurst,_Brooklyn" title="Bensonhurst, Brooklyn">Bensonhurst, Brooklyn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Mac and actor Will Lex Ham organized a march in that neighborhood on August 1, 2020 as a response to raise awareness about anti-Asian hate crimes.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The "They Can't Burn Us All" rallying cry has transformed into a national protest for "unity amongst all people against hate crimes and racism."<sup id="cite_ref-:5_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The duo later held rallies in both Los Angeles and San Francisco.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> These events have garnered hundreds to participate.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:5_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>China Mac's activism led him to release the single "They Can't Burn Us All" on October 30, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (November 2020)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
