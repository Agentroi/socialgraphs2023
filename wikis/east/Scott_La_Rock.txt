<p><b>Scott Monroe Sterling</b> (March 2, 1962 – August 27, 1987), known by the stage name <b>DJ Scott La Rock</b>, was an American hip-hop disc jockey and music producer from <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>, New York. He was a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/Boogie_Down_Productions" title="Boogie Down Productions">Boogie Down Productions</a>. He was also known for his association with the rapper KRS-One who was a member of Boogie Down Productions.
</p><p>Sterling's death in August 1987 is said to be the first murder of a major hip hop artist.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<p>Scott Sterling was born on March 2, 1962, in the Bronx, New York City. His parents separated when he was four years old, so he was raised by his mother, Carolyn Morant, a municipal employee.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> They moved from <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, New York City, to the Bronx: first <a href="/wiki/Morrisania,_Bronx" title="Morrisania, Bronx">Morrisania</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Morris_Heights,_Bronx" title="Morris Heights, Bronx">Morris Heights</a>.
</p><p>He excelled in both academics and sports at <a href="/wiki/Our_Saviour_Lutheran_High_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Our Saviour Lutheran High School">Our Saviour Lutheran High School</a>, graduating in 1980. He attended <a href="/wiki/Castleton_State_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Castleton State College">Castleton State College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a> and earned a varsity letter in basketball there.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_La_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>La Rock returned to New York City in hopes of finding work and making inroads to the music industry. Through a connection of his mother's, La Rock landed a job as a social worker at Franklin Armory Men's Shelter on 166th Street in the Bronx. At night, though, he spun records at the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> hot spot the Broadway Repertoire Theatre.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During his time as a social worker, La Rock met <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a> in 1986 at Franklin Men's Shelter where KRS resided. The pair formed <a href="/wiki/Boogie_Down_Productions" title="Boogie Down Productions">Boogie Down Productions</a> (BDP) with DJ <a href="/wiki/D-Nice" title="D-Nice">Derrick "D-Nice" Jones</a>, a cousin of the shelter's security guard, Floyd Payne.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The group's 1987 debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Criminal_Minded" title="Criminal Minded">Criminal Minded</a></i>, is considered a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> classic.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Shooting">Shooting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_La_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Shooting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On August 27, 1987, D-Nice was assaulted by men upset that he had been talking to one of their ex-girlfriends. D-Nice asked La Rock to help defuse the situation. Later that day, La Rock, Scotty "Manager Moe" Morris, DJ McBooo, D-Nice and BDP bodyguard Darrell rode a <a href="/wiki/Jeep_CJ-7" class="mw-redirect" title="Jeep CJ-7">Jeep CJ-7</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Highbridge,_Bronx" title="Highbridge, Bronx">Highbridge Homes Projects</a><sup id="cite_ref-xxl_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ambrosia_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ambrosia-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> building on University Avenue in the <a href="/wiki/South_Bronx" title="South Bronx">South Bronx</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As they were leaving, bullets were fired through the side and top of the Jeep. La Rock struck his head on the dashboard, not realizing at the time he had been struck by a bullet in the back of his head.<sup id="cite_ref-ambrosia_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ambrosia-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was driven to <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Hospital_(Bronx,_New_York)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lincoln Hospital (Bronx, New York)">Lincoln Hospital</a>. He was conscious as he was wheeled into the emergency department, telling doctors he was feeling cold and tired. He died in the operating room.
</p><p>Two men were arrested and charged with La Rock's murder, but they were acquitted at the trial.<sup id="cite_ref-xxl_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxl-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
