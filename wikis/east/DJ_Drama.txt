<p><b>Tyree Cinque Simmons</b> (born April 22, 1978),<sup id="cite_ref-XXLBday_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-All_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> professionally known as <b>DJ Drama</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a> (DJ), <a href="/wiki/Music_executive" title="Music executive">record executive</a> and <a href="/wiki/Promoter_(entertainment)" title="Promoter (entertainment)">music promoter</a>. He initially gained recognition as the official DJ for <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>-based rapper <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a> and would continue to gain prominence hosting <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a>, namely his trademark <i>Gangsta Grillz</i> series. Numerous high-profile artists have utilized Drama's <i>Gangsta Grillz</i> branding in projects he's compiled, including <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeezy" title="Jeezy">Jeezy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert" title="Lil Uzi Vert">Lil Uzi Vert</a>, <a href="/wiki/YoungBoy_Never_Broke_Again" title="YoungBoy Never Broke Again">YoungBoy Never Broke Again</a>, among others. 
</p><p>DJ Drama is co-founder of the Atlanta-based <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_collective" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop collective">hip hop collective</a> known as the Aphilliates. He is also co-founder (with <a href="/wiki/Don_Cannon" title="Don Cannon">Don Cannon</a>) of Generation Now, an <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a> imprint with artists including <a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert" title="Lil Uzi Vert">Lil Uzi Vert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Harlow" title="Jack Harlow">Jack Harlow</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Skeme" title="Skeme">Skeme</a> on its roster. Drama has released five studio albums, curating tracks with both <a href="/wiki/Underground_hip_hop" title="Underground hip hop">underground</a> and prominent artists, the latest of which being <i><a href="/wiki/Quality_Street_Music_2" title="Quality Street Music 2">Quality Street Music 2</a></i> (2016).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Drama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tyree Simmons was born in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</a> and raised largely in <a href="/wiki/Germantown,_Philadelphia" title="Germantown, Philadelphia">Germantown, Philadelphia</a>. At age 13, his sister took him to New York City where he purchased his first mixtape. He began making his own tapes soon after, including one titled <i>Illadelph</i> which featured artists like <a href="/wiki/Black_Thought" title="Black Thought">Black Thought</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dice_Raw" title="Dice Raw">Dice Raw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malik_B." title="Malik B.">Malik B.</a>, and others. He also started experimenting with <a href="/wiki/Turntablism" title="Turntablism">turntables</a>. Simmons attended <a href="/wiki/Central_High_School_(Philadelphia)" title="Central High School (Philadelphia)">Central High School</a> in Philadelphia and graduated in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-PW_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PW-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Boom_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boom-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After high school, he moved to Atlanta to attend <a href="/wiki/Clark_Atlanta_University" title="Clark Atlanta University">Clark Atlanta University</a> (CAU) where he studied <a href="/wiki/Mass_communication" title="Mass communication">mass communication</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PW_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PW-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYTOutlaw_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOutlaw-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> While there, he met two other Philadelphia-raised DJs: DJ Sense and Don Cannon. The three would eventually form the Aphilliates DJ and artist collective. During his studies, Simmons began making and releasing mixtapes under the moniker, DJ Drama.<sup id="cite_ref-PW_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PW-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> One of his first series (called <i>Electric Relaxation</i>) included a variety of <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-Boom_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boom-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1998, he released his first mixtape (<i><a href="/wiki/Jim_Crow_Laws" class="mw-redirect" title="Jim Crow Laws">Jim Crow Laws</a></i>) composed exclusively of <a href="/wiki/Southern_hip_hop" title="Southern hip hop">Southern hip hop</a> acts like <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Three_6_Mafia" title="Three 6 Mafia">Three 6 Mafia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mystikal" title="Mystikal">Mystikal</a>. The success of that tape led DJ Drama to start the mixtape series, <i>Gangsta Grillz</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-PW_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PW-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYTOutlaw_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOutlaw-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He would go on to graduate from CAU in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-LJS_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LJS-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Drama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2003.E2.80.932010:_Career_beginnings_and_Gangsta_Grillz:_The_Album_series"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2003–2010:_Career_beginnings_and_Gangsta_Grillz:_The_Album_series">2003–2010: Career beginnings and <i>Gangsta Grillz: The Album</i> series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Drama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2003–2010: Career beginnings and Gangsta Grillz: The Album series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:DJ_Drama_2.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/DJ_Drama_2.jpg/200px-DJ_Drama_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="283" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/DJ_Drama_2.jpg/300px-DJ_Drama_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/DJ_Drama_2.jpg/400px-DJ_Drama_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1430" data-file-height="2027" /></a><figcaption>DJ Drama in 2008</figcaption></figure>
<p>DJ Drama, alongside Don Cannon and DJ Sense, officially founded the Aphilliates Music Group (AMG) in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTOutlaw_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOutlaw-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DJB2006_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJB2006-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The group distributed DJ Drama's <i>Gangsta Grillz</i> mixtape series, which saw early successes with T.I.'s <i>Down With the King</i> (2004) and Jeezy's <i>Trap or Die</i> (2005).<sup id="cite_ref-Boom_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boom-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> By 2005, Drama and his AMG cohorts were hosting radio shows on Atlanta's <a href="/wiki/WHTA" title="WHTA">Hot 107.9</a> and on <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Shade_45" title="Shade 45">Shade 45</a> channel on <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Satellite_Radio" title="Sirius Satellite Radio">Sirius Satellite Radio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PW_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PW-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYTOutlaw_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOutlaw-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In late 2005, Drama produced and hosted Lil Wayne's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dedication" title="The Dedication">The Dedication</a></i> mixtape, which would spawn a long series of <i>Dedication</i> tapes. <i><a href="/wiki/Dedication_2" title="Dedication 2">Dedication 2</a></i>, released in May 2006, appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> chart and was labeled by <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> as "one of the 10 best recordings of 2006."<sup id="cite_ref-NYTOutlaw_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOutlaw-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HNHH30_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HNHH30-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>AMG signed a joint venture deal with <a href="/wiki/Asylum_Records" title="Asylum Records">Asylum Records</a> in September 2006. The first artist signed was American rapper <a href="/wiki/Willie_the_Kid_(rapper)" title="Willie the Kid (rapper)">Willie the Kid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTOutlaw_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOutlaw-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DJB2006_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJB2006-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In January 2007, AMG's Atlanta studios were raided by <a href="/wiki/Fulton_County,_Georgia" title="Fulton County, Georgia">Fulton County</a> police officers, who seized over 80,000 mixtapes, 4 vehicles, and some recording equipment. Both DJ Drama and Don Cannon were taken into custody on <a href="/wiki/Racketeer_Influenced_and_Corrupt_Organizations_Act" title="Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act">RICO</a> charges.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTOutlaw_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOutlaw-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BillRaid_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillRaid-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYTRaid_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTRaid-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Hard drives containing tracks from Drama's first "official" album, <i><a href="/wiki/Gangsta_Grillz:_The_Album" title="Gangsta Grillz: The Album">Gangsta Grillz: The Album</a></i>, were also seized despite the fact that the album was due to be released "above-board" by <a href="/wiki/Grand_Hustle_Records" title="Grand Hustle Records">Grand Hustle Records</a> via Atlantic Records. No further legal action was ever taken against Drama or Cannon in relation to the raid.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTOutlaw_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOutlaw-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-XXLRaid_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLRaid-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite the setback, DJ Drama's <i>Gangsta Grillz: The Album</i> was released in December 2007 on the Grand Hustle label.<sup id="cite_ref-Pitch_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pitch-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The album's first <a href="/wiki/Buzz_single" class="mw-redirect" title="Buzz single">buzz single</a>, "Takin' Pictures", was released in March 2007 and featured Jeezy, Willie the Kid, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rick Ross (rapper)">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a> and T.I.<sup id="cite_ref-DJBTakin_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJBTakin-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The album's <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a>—"<a href="/wiki/5000_Ones" title="5000 Ones">5000 Ones</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Nelly" title="Nelly">Nelly</a>, T.I., <a href="/wiki/Yung_Joc" title="Yung Joc">Yung Joc</a>, Willie the Kid, Jeezy, and <a href="/wiki/Twista" title="Twista">Twista</a>—debuted on <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DJB5000_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJB5000-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The second single, "<a href="/wiki/The_Art_of_Storytellin%27_Part_4" class="mw-redirect" title="The Art of Storytellin&#39; Part 4">The Art of Storytellin' Part 4</a>", was released in 2008 and featured <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marsha_Ambrosius" title="Marsha Ambrosius">Marsha Ambrosius</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DJBArt_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJBArt-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The album peaked at number 26 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bill200_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bill200-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2009, DJ Drama released his second album entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Gangsta_Grillz:_The_Album_(Vol._2)" title="Gangsta Grillz: The Album (Vol. 2)">Gangsta Grillz: The Album (Vol. 2)</a></i>, with the singles "<a href="/wiki/Day_Dreaming_(DJ_Drama_song)" title="Day Dreaming (DJ Drama song)">Day Dreaming</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> and T.I.)<sup id="cite_ref-MTVDay_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTVDay-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and "<a href="/wiki/Ridiculous_(DJ_Drama_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ridiculous (DJ Drama song)">Ridiculous</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yo_Gotti" title="Yo Gotti">Yo Gotti</a>, Lonnie Mac and <a href="/wiki/OJ_da_Juiceman" title="OJ da Juiceman">OJ da Juiceman</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-DJBVol2_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJBVol2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In addition to his own studio albums, DJ Drama also continued working on mixtapes, including those for Gucci Mane (<i>Mr. Zone 6</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-FaderGucci_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FaderGucci-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a> (<i>In My Zone 2</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-RUCB_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RUCB-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dead_Prez" title="Dead Prez">Dead Prez</a> (<i>Revolutionary but Gangsta Grillz</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-MTVDead_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTVDead-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/From_the_Hut,_to_the_Projects,_to_the_Mansion" title="From the Hut, to the Projects, to the Mansion">From the Hut, to the Projects, to the Mansion</a></i>), among others.<sup id="cite_ref-BillWyclef_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillWyclef-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In March 2010, Drama appeared on <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a>'s musical television special <i><a href="/wiki/Freaknik:_The_Musical" title="Freaknik: The Musical">Freaknik: The Musical</a></i>, in a <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting" title="Voice acting">voice role</a> as a radio announcer named Mr. Thanksgiving.<sup id="cite_ref-Freak_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Freak-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2011.E2.80.93present:_Third_Power_and_Quality_Street_Music_series.2C_Generation_Now_record_label"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–present:_Third_Power_and_Quality_Street_Music_series,_Generation_Now_record_label">2011–present: <i>Third Power</i> and <i>Quality Street Music</i> series, Generation Now record label</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Drama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2011–present: Third Power and Quality Street Music series, Generation Now record label">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Drama released his third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Third_Power_(album)" title="Third Power (album)">Third Power</a></i> in October 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-ComplexThird_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ComplexThird-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> It featured the single, "<a href="/wiki/Oh_My_(DJ_Drama_song)" title="Oh My (DJ Drama song)">Oh My</a>" (featuring Fabolous, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Roscoe_Dash" title="Roscoe Dash">Roscoe Dash</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-RUOM_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RUOM-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and reached number 95 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_100" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot 100">Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bill100_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bill100-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He followed that with the release of his fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Quality_Street_Music" title="Quality Street Music">Quality Street Music</a></i>, in late 2012. The album was preceded by the singles "<a href="/wiki/We_in_This_Bitch" title="We in This Bitch">We in This Bitch</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/My_Moment_(DJ_Drama_song)" title="My Moment (DJ Drama song)">My Moment</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-Boom_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boom-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MTVWITB_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTVWITB-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The album peaked at number 15 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, which is the highest a DJ Drama album has gotten on the chart to date.<sup id="cite_ref-Bill200_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bill200-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2013, Drama co-founded The Academy, a DJ incubator and art collective, with Don Cannon and DJ Sense.<sup id="cite_ref-AHHAug_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AHHAug-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The Academy announced its first roster of 23 DJs in September of that year.<sup id="cite_ref-AHHSep_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AHHSep-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In January 2014, it was announced that DJ Drama would join the <a href="/wiki/Artists_and_repertoire" title="Artists and repertoire">A&amp;R</a> at Atlantic Records.<sup id="cite_ref-BillAR_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillAR-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Within his first year, he had signed several artists to the label, including <a href="/wiki/Kap_G" title="Kap G">Kap G</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spenzo" title="Spenzo">Spenzo</a>, and Que.<sup id="cite_ref-ComplexAR_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ComplexAR-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Throughout this time, he continued hosting and producing mixtapes for artists like <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/The_Lost_Tape_(mixtape)" title="The Lost Tape (mixtape)">The Lost Tape</a></i>),<sup id="cite_ref-RU50_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RU50-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Crenshaw_(mixtape)" title="Crenshaw (mixtape)">Crenshaw</a></i>),<sup id="cite_ref-DXNipsey_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DXNipsey-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Snoop Dogg (<i><a href="/wiki/That%27s_My_Work_Volume_3" class="mw-redirect" title="That&#39;s My Work Volume 3">That's My Work Volume 3</a></i>),<sup id="cite_ref-VibeSnoop_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VibeSnoop-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Big_K.R.I.T." title="Big K.R.I.T.">Big K.R.I.T.</a> (<i>It's Better This Way</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-DXKRIT_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DXKRIT-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, DJ Drama and Don Cannon founded the Atlantic Records imprint, Generation Now. One of their first signees was <a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert" title="Lil Uzi Vert">Lil Uzi Vert</a>, and Drama served as an executive producer on Uzi's 2015 debut commercial mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Luv_Is_Rage" title="Luv Is Rage">Luv Is Rage</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLBday_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sway_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sway-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Generation Now also signed Skeme to the label that year.<sup id="cite_ref-GripSkeme_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GripSkeme-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> In July 2016, Drama released his fifth studio album, <i>Quality Street Music 2</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-DXQSM2_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DXQSM2-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> with the single "<a href="/wiki/Wishing_(DJ_Drama_song)" title="Wishing (DJ Drama song)">Wishing</a>" featuring Chris Brown, Skeme, and Lyquin.<sup id="cite_ref-ComplexWishing_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ComplexWishing-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Generation Now had its first number one record on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert" title="Lil Uzi Vert">Lil Uzi Vert</a>'s August 2017 debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Luv_Is_Rage_2" title="Luv Is Rage 2">Luv Is Rage 2</a></i>. DJ Drama served as executive producer on the album.<sup id="cite_ref-RO2017_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RO2017-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Other acts signed to Generation Now by Drama include <a href="/wiki/Skeme" title="Skeme">Skeme</a>, Lyquin,<sup id="cite_ref-ComplexLyquin_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ComplexLyquin-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jack_Harlow" title="Jack Harlow">Jack Harlow</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-BillHarlow_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillHarlow-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lil_James" title="Lil James">Lil James</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-HHNMJames_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHNMJames-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> and Killumantii.<sup id="cite_ref-HNHHKill_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HNHHKill-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2021, Drama narrated <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Call_Me_If_You_Get_Lost" title="Call Me If You Get Lost">Call Me If You Get Lost</a></i>, and was credited as a feature on the first track <a href="/wiki/Sir_Baudelaire" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Baudelaire">Sir Baudelaire</a>. The album's "intros" and sound was partly inspired by Drama's "Gangsta Grillz" series, and is often credited as a nod to that "mixtape" sound.
</p><p>A further instalment of the Gangsta Grillz series, <i>D-Day: A Gangsta Grillz Mixtape</i>, was released on March 31, 2022, in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Dreamville" class="mw-redirect" title="Dreamville">Dreamville</a> and <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>.
</p>

The name of the label derives from a 2004 mixtape Drama and Cannon curated.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
