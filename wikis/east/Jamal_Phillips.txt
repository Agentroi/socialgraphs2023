<p><b>Jamal Phillips</b> (born April 26, 1979), known professionally <a href="/wiki/Mononym" title="Mononym">mononymously</a> as <b>Jamal</b> and formerly as <b>Mally G</b>, is an American rapper and record producer. He started his career as one-half of rap duo <a href="/wiki/Illegal_(group)" title="Illegal (group)">Illegal</a>, the rap act formed by <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Austin" title="Dallas Austin">Dallas Austin</a> in the early 1990s.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamal_Phillips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Illegal_and_going_solo">Illegal and going solo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamal_Phillips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Illegal and going solo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Phillips' tenure with Illegal was short-lived; following the lukewarm response to 1993's <a href="/wiki/The_Untold_Truth" title="The Untold Truth">The Untold Truth</a> — the group's full-length debut — Phillips embarked on a solo career. He teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Erick_Sermon" title="Erick Sermon">Erick Sermon</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Def_Squad" title="Def Squad">Def Squad</a> to release 1995's <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Chance,_No_Breaks" title="Last Chance, No Breaks">Last Chance, No Breaks</a></i>, his debut release.
</p><p>The album (produced by <a href="/wiki/Easy_Mo_Bee" title="Easy Mo Bee">Easy Mo Bee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Redman_(rapper)" title="Redman (rapper)">Redman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rockwilder" title="Rockwilder">Rockwilder</a> and Sermon) peaked at #10 and #37 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard magazine">Billboard magazine</a>'s <i>Heatseekers</i> and <i>Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</i> charts respectively. The album also spawned a pair of hit singles — "Fades Em All", which sampled <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a>'s "Ready to Die", and "Keep It Real", which sampled <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a>'s "Ribbon in the Sky".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Smoke-a-Lot_Records">Smoke-a-Lot Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamal_Phillips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Smoke-a-Lot Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, Jamal signed a deal with rapper <a href="/wiki/Yukmouth" title="Yukmouth">Yukmouth</a> to be a part of his <a href="/wiki/Smoke-a-Lot_Records" title="Smoke-a-Lot Records">Smoke-a-Lot Records</a> label.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_collaborations">Other collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamal_Phillips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1995 he performed on <i><a href="/wiki/Conspiracy_(Junior_M.A.F.I.A._album)" title="Conspiracy (Junior M.A.F.I.A. album)">Conspiracy</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">LP</a> from <a href="/wiki/Junior_M.A.F.I.A." title="Junior M.A.F.I.A.">Junior M.A.F.I.A.</a> He has since gone on to collaborate with other renowned rappers; in 1996, he was featured on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Coming" title="The Coming">The Coming</a></i> the LP from <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Keith_Murray_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Keith Murray (rapper)">Keith Murray</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Enigma_(Keith_Murray_album)" title="Enigma (Keith Murray album)">Enigma</a></i>. The following year, he teamed up again with Busta Rhymes, performing on that rapper's 1997 LP, <i><a href="/wiki/When_Disaster_Strikes" class="mw-redirect" title="When Disaster Strikes">When Disaster Strikes</a></i>. In 1998, he collaborated with the Def Squad on <i><a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o_(Def_Squad_album)" title="El Niño (Def Squad album)">El Niño</a></i>, their debut release.
</p><p>He also had production duties on <i><a href="/wiki/B.Coming" class="mw-redirect" title="B.Coming">B.Coming</a></i>, the 2005 release from <a href="/wiki/Beanie_Sigel" title="Beanie Sigel">Beanie Sigel</a>.
</p><p>Jamal appears on Illegal bandmate Malik's recent (2005) CD "Malik M.D. aka Hershey Locc Hefner: The Game Needs Me".
</p><p>He has also appeared on songs with <a href="/wiki/Shyheim" title="Shyheim">Shyheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heltah_Skeltah" title="Heltah Skeltah">Heltah Skeltah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coolio" title="Coolio">Coolio</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Breed" title="MC Breed">MC Breed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Zee" title="Young Zee">Young Zee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs_N_Harmony" class="mw-redirect" title="Bone Thugs N Harmony">Bone Thugs N Harmony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Treach" title="Treach">Treach</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_D.O.C." title="The D.O.C.">The D.O.C.</a> and others.
</p><p>Jamal also appears on Skullz' 2018 single "Everybody Can Get It" feat. Juxx Diamondz.
</p>
