<p><b>Rory William Quigley</b> (born January 26, 1987), known professionally as <b>Harry Fraud</b>, is an American record producer.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Primarily a hip hop producer, Fraud began producing at an early age and began interning at a recording studio after graduating high school.<sup id="cite_ref-NahRight_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NahRight-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> First discovered by <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> during a recording session in <a href="/wiki/Chinatown,_Manhattan" title="Chinatown, Manhattan">Chinatown</a> in early 2009,<sup id="cite_ref-RollingStone_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RollingStone-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Fraud has gone on to become a fixture in the New York <a href="/wiki/Underground_hip_hop" title="Underground hip hop">underground</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> scene. He gained mainstream attention when his song with French Montana "<a href="/wiki/Shot_Caller_(French_Montana_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Shot Caller (French Montana song)">Shot Caller</a>" became one of the most added tracks on <a href="/wiki/Urban_contemporary" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban contemporary">urban contemporary</a> radio during 2011–2012.<sup id="cite_ref-SDinterview_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SDinterview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fraud has produced collaborative records with various rappers, such as Wiz Khalifa before projects such as <i>Flight 2011</i> (2011) with Chinx Drugz; <i>Rugby Thompson</i> (2012) with Smoke DZA; <i><a href="/wiki/Cigarette_Boats" title="Cigarette Boats">Cigarette Boats</a></i> (2012) with Currensy, which was awarded <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a>'s "2012 EP of the Year"; <i><a href="/wiki/Saaab_Stories" title="Saaab Stories">Saaab Stories</a></i> (2013) with Action Bronson, which served as the latter's major-label debut, and <i>The Appetizer</i> (2014) with French Montana. On March 8, 2013, he released his debut mixtape <i>Adrift</i>. That same year, Fraud teamed up with car-maker <a href="/wiki/Scion_A/V" class="mw-redirect" title="Scion A/V">Scion</a> to produce the compilation <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> <i>Scion A/V Presents: High Tide</i> released on May 7, and on May 10 released his <a href="/wiki/Instrumental" title="Instrumental">instrumental</a> EP <i>Royal Palm</i>. In April 2014, he released his instrumental EP <i>Blue/Green</i>. Fraud has done production for various high-profile artists such as <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Playboi_Carti" title="Playboi Carti">Playboi Carti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, among others. He has also worked on production for the <a href="/wiki/International_Academy_of_Television_Arts_and_Sciences" title="International Academy of Television Arts and Sciences">International Emmy Awards</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harry_Fraud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harry_Fraud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Harry Fraud comes from a musical family background, where his parents performed in a band together. His father is a guitar player, while his mother is a singer.<sup id="cite_ref-NahRight_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NahRight-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Life+Times_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Life+Times-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His father was in the concert business, which gave Fraud access to instruments and recording equipment at an early age.<sup id="cite_ref-Life+Times_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Life+Times-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He received his first turntables on Christmas, and the next year had gotten what he describes as a "piece of shit sampler". It's after acquiring his first <a href="/wiki/Music_Production_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="Music Production Center">MPC</a> that he began seriously working on beat production.<sup id="cite_ref-Life+Times_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Life+Times-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Upon graduating high school, Fraud began interning at a recording studio, which became his first exposure to <a href="/wiki/Pro_Tools" title="Pro Tools">Pro Tools</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NahRight_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NahRight-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  He was also influenced by Fafu of BlesteNation, who was a producer at the same building.<sup id="cite_ref-NahRight_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NahRight-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> After getting into college, he acquired Pro Tools for himself, quickly immersing himself in front of the computer and becoming his main distraction, "I didn't do any [school] work. I wouldn't even go to class. It was terrible," claimed Fraud in an interview with Nah Right.<sup id="cite_ref-NahRight_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NahRight-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> During his time in college, he met his friend Red Walrus – now the musical director of the band which Fraud works with – where the two bonded over digging through crates and buying <a href="/wiki/Vinyl_records" class="mw-redirect" title="Vinyl records">vinyl records</a> from places such as <a href="/wiki/Goodwill_Industries" title="Goodwill Industries">Goodwill</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Salvation_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Salvation Army">Salvation Army</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NahRight_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NahRight-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His musical composition has been used by the <a href="/wiki/International_Academy_of_Television_Arts_and_Sciences" title="International Academy of Television Arts and Sciences">International Emmy Awards</a>, which Fraud credits his father for getting through to its <a href="/wiki/Television_producer" title="Television producer">producers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HipHopDX_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HipHopDX-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Fraud's composition work would go on to be praised by <a href="/wiki/Simon_Cowell" title="Simon Cowell">Simon Cowell</a>, who, at the <a href="/wiki/38th_International_Emmy_Awards" title="38th International Emmy Awards">2010 Emmy Awards</a> ceremony took notice of Fraud's walk up music, even requesting it be replayed briefly during his speech.<sup id="cite_ref-HipHopDX_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HipHopDX-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="French_Montana_and_the_Coke_Boys">French Montana and the Coke Boys</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harry_Fraud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: French Montana and the Coke Boys">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Harry Fraud rose to prominence through his collaboration with French Montana on the song "New York Minute", featuring <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, off of French's fifth mixtape <i>Mac Wit Da Cheese</i> released on April 19, 2009. In a 2013 interview with <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a> magazine, Harry Fraud revealed first meeting close friend and artist French Montana during a recording session at a studio in <a href="/wiki/Chinatown,_Manhattan" title="Chinatown, Manhattan">Chinatown</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-RollingStone_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RollingStone-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Fraud recalls recording a song for someone which French happened to have a guest feature on. In an unusual turn of events, the building was raided by police after a tip-off that there were counterfeit Gucci and Louis Vuitton purses being manufactured at the same building.<sup id="cite_ref-RollingStone_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RollingStone-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Having lost his studio spot, Fraud went on to form his own studio in Brooklyn, where the two "met and formed a bond".<sup id="cite_ref-RollingStone_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RollingStone-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the release of "New York Minute", the two artists would go on to record "We Playin In The Wind" off of French Montana's sixth mixtape <i>The Laundry Man 2</i>, released on June 29, 2009. An extended version of the song would be included on French's seventh mixtape <i>Cocaine Konvicts</i> released on September 25. Since then, Harry Fraud's production has been a staple among French's music, becoming Fraud's gateway to working with other high-profile artists since then. With the success of "New York Minute", rapper <a href="/wiki/Ma$e" class="mw-redirect" title="Ma$e">Ma$e</a> had approached French Montana to hop on the remix,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> released on French's eighth mixtape <i>Coke Wave 2</i> in November that year. Fraud would go on to produce several tracks off of French's ninth mixtape <i>Mac &amp; Cheese 2</i> released on May 6, 2010, and French's tenth mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Coke_Boys_(2010)" class="mw-redirect" title="Coke Boys (2010)">Coke Boys</a></i> released on September 15, collaborating with Coke Boys rappers Chinx Drugz, Cheeze, Flip and Brock.
</p><p>He gained mainstream attention when his track with French Montana "<a href="/wiki/Shot_Caller_(French_Montana_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Shot Caller (French Montana song)">Shot Caller</a>" became one of the most added tracks on <a href="/wiki/Urban_contemporary" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban contemporary">urban contemporary</a> radio in 2011–2012.<sup id="cite_ref-SDinterview_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SDinterview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The song which was re-released as a single on January 10, 2012, was originally off of French's eleventh mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Mister_16:_Casino_Life" class="mw-redirect" title="Mister 16: Casino Life">Mister 16: Casino Life</a></i> released on February 15, 2011. Harry Fraud executive produced the overall mixtape, and his subsequent twelfth mixtape <i>Coke Boys 2</i> released on August 19 of that same year.<sup id="cite_ref-Life+Times_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Life+Times-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Through French, Fraud was introduced to his fellow Coke Boy rapper Chinx Drugz. The two would go on to work on their collaborative mixtape <i>Flight 2011</i> which was released on March 11, 2011, and several tracks off of his subsequent <i>Cocaine Riot</i> mixtape released on April 19 that year. Fraud's production would also be included in French's collaborative mixtape with <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a> and <a href="/wiki/Project_Pat" title="Project Pat">Project Pat</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Cocaine_Mafia" title="Cocaine Mafia">Cocaine Mafia</a></i> released on December 20, 2011. By 2012, with the popularity of "Shot Caller", a remix was released featuring rappers <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.93present:_Cigarette_Boats.2C_Adrift_and_Saaab_Stories"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–present:_Cigarette_Boats,_Adrift_and_Saaab_Stories">2012–present: <i>Cigarette Boats</i>, <i>Adrift</i> and <i>Saaab Stories</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harry_Fraud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012–present: Cigarette Boats, Adrift and Saaab Stories">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 10, 2012, Fraud and rapper <a href="/wiki/Currensy" title="Currensy">Currensy</a> released a collaborative <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> mixtape titled <i><a href="/wiki/Cigarette_Boats" title="Cigarette Boats">Cigarette Boats</a></i>, which <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a> would go on to award its "2012 EP of the Year."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On March 8, 2013, Fraud released his debut <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> titled <i>Adrift</i>, that has 115,000 downloads as of April 21, 2013, on <a href="/wiki/DatPiff" title="DatPiff">DatPiff</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape features guest appearances from Action Bronson, <a href="/wiki/Bun_B" title="Bun B">Bun B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slim_Thug" title="Slim Thug">Slim Thug</a>, Rick Ross, French Montana, <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a>, Wiz Khalifa, Juicy J, <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trae_tha_Truth" title="Trae tha Truth">Trae tha Truth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinx_Drugz" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinx Drugz">Chinx Drugz</a>, Currensy, <a href="/wiki/Chevy_Woods" title="Chevy Woods">Chevy Woods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Brown_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Danny Brown (rapper)">Danny Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a> among many others. The mixtape is entirely self-produced and does not include any vocals of his.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 8, 2013, Fraud and rapper Eddie B released a collaborative EP mixtape titled <i>Horsepower</i> through Man Bites Dog Records.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Fraud also released an EP through <a href="/wiki/Scion_AV" class="mw-redirect" title="Scion AV">Scion AV</a> on May 7, 2013, titled <i>Scion A/V Presents: High Tide</i>. The free EP features guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Earl_Sweatshirt" title="Earl Sweatshirt">Earl Sweatshirt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>, French Montana, Action Bronson, <a href="/wiki/Riff_Raff_(rapper)" title="Riff Raff (rapper)">Riff Raff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mistah_F.A.B." title="Mistah F.A.B.">Mistah F.A.B.</a>, Troy Ave, <a href="/wiki/Smoke_DZA" title="Smoke DZA">Smoke DZA</a> and Chinx Drugz.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On the same day he released an EP on iTunes of instrumentals all produced by himself titled <i>Royal Ralm</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Royal_Palm_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Royal_Palm-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On June 11, 2013, Fraud released his collaboration project with Action Bronson titled, <i><a href="/wiki/Saaab_Stories" title="Saaab Stories">Saaab Stories</a></i>. The project featured collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> and Prodigy. It debuted at #63 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a></i>.
</p><p>On July 22, 2013, it was announced that Fraud's Surf School <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a> signed to the new <a href="/wiki/Priority_Records" title="Priority Records">Priority Records</a> which operates under <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Music_Group" title="Capitol Music Group">Capitol Music Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He has promised a project with French Montana to be released after Montana's album <i><a href="/wiki/Excuse_My_French_(album)" title="Excuse My French (album)">Excuse My French</a></i> (2013).<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He also revealed plans to release a collaboration mixtape with Riff Raff and his debut album in late 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-HipHopDX_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HipHopDX-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Fraud and Eddie B also announced they would release a full-length album titled <i>Paper, Piff &amp; Polo</i> during 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On October 6, 2013, Smoke DZA announced he would release a collaboration EP with Currensy, entirely produced by Harry Fraud.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He has said he wants to work with New York rappers <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a> in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Fraud has called <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Alchemist (producer)">The Alchemist</a> as his favorite producer.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_style">Production style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harry_Fraud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Production style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harry Fraud's production has been described as "New York tinged, sample heavy, foggy music."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Fraud's production leans heavily on samples, and he's become known for turning obscure tracks into hard-knocking beats.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with HipHopDX Fraud described his style saying, "What's most important to me with production is that I'm just always trying to do something unique. If I approach a production—whether it be sample-based or starting with something that originates in my head—I always try to do it in a way that is not typical at all. So if it's something that I feel where I might be going down a path that sounds too much like what's going on now, I'll make sure to try and take a left turn and do something that's my own. I try not to have a style, so to speak."<sup id="cite_ref-HipHopDX_Article_with_Smoke_DZA_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HipHopDX_Article_with_Smoke_DZA-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He went on to describe the use of drums on his beats stating, "I'm more about trying to have a strong drum presence—whether the drum is a big, huge snare or a little, tiny rimshot—I wanna make sure that the presence of the drums is felt no matter what."<sup id="cite_ref-HipHopDX_Article_with_Smoke_DZA_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HipHopDX_Article_with_Smoke_DZA-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Fraud's signature tag at the beginning of his productions consists of singer Fanesha Fabre saying "La musica de Harry Fraud".<sup id="cite_ref-HipHopDX_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HipHopDX-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Surf_School_Recordings">Surf School Recordings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harry_Fraud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Surf School Recordings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012, Harry Fraud founded his own record label "Surf School Recordings", the name a tribute to <a href="/wiki/Max_B" title="Max B">Max B</a> and Fraud's own passion for <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">surfing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Life+Times_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Life+Times-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His father, who was a frequent traveler, was a surfer who would often take Fraud on trips with him where he discovered his passion for surfing.<sup id="cite_ref-HipHopDX_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HipHopDX-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, Fraud signed a deal making it an <a href="/wiki/Imprint_(trade_name)" title="Imprint (trade name)">imprint</a> under <a href="/wiki/Priority_Records" title="Priority Records">Priority Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In a 2014 interview with <a href="/wiki/Life%2BTimes" class="mw-redirect" title="Life+Times">Life+Times</a> he noted,
</p>
<blockquote><p>The SRFSCHL label comes from the concept of what <a href="/wiki/Max_B" title="Max B">Max B</a> brought to the light with "the wave" and his philosophy and what me and <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French</a> have been carrying on. It also kind of came out of me being into surfing. I feel like people get a lot of influence off of the stuff we do. We're teaching people how to ride the wave anyway, just off of them listening to our music. I'm a deeply philosophical person, so SRFSCHL has since taken on many different meanings.<sup id="cite_ref-Life+Times_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Life+Times-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
