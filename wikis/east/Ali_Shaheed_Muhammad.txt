<p><b>Ali Shaheed Muhammad</b> (born August 11, 1970) is an American hip hop DJ, record producer, and rapper, best known as a member of <a href="/wiki/A_Tribe_Called_Quest" title="A Tribe Called Quest">A Tribe Called Quest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-linearlabs_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-linearlabs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> With <a href="/wiki/Q-Tip_(musician)" title="Q-Tip (musician)">Q-Tip</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phife_Dawg" title="Phife Dawg">Phife Dawg</a> (and sometimes <a href="/wiki/Jarobi_White" title="Jarobi White">Jarobi White</a>), the group released five studio albums from 1990 to 1998 before disbanding; <a href="/wiki/We_Got_It_from_Here..._Thank_You_4_Your_Service" title="We Got It from Here... Thank You 4 Your Service">their final album</a> was released in 2016. A native of Brooklyn, New York, Muhammad currently lives in Los Angeles.<sup id="cite_ref-linearlabs_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-linearlabs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ali_Shaheed_Muhammad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ali_shaheed_muhammad.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Ali_shaheed_muhammad.jpg/220px-Ali_shaheed_muhammad.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Ali_shaheed_muhammad.jpg/330px-Ali_shaheed_muhammad.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Ali_shaheed_muhammad.jpg/440px-Ali_shaheed_muhammad.jpg 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="600" /></a><figcaption>Muhammad speaking at <a href="/wiki/Apple_Fifth_Avenue" title="Apple Fifth Avenue">Apple Fifth Avenue</a> in New York City in 2006</figcaption></figure>
<p>Muhammad is a <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Together with <a href="/wiki/J_Dilla" title="J Dilla">Jay Dee</a> and Q-Tip, he formed the music-production collective <a href="/wiki/The_Ummah" title="The Ummah">the Ummah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-linearlabs_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-linearlabs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After A Tribe Called Quest disbanded, Muhammad formed the <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> supergroup <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Pearl" title="Lucy Pearl">Lucy Pearl</a> with <a href="/wiki/Dawn_Robinson" title="Dawn Robinson">Dawn Robinson</a>, formerly of <a href="/wiki/En_Vogue" title="En Vogue">En Vogue</a> and <a href="/wiki/Raphael_Saadiq" title="Raphael Saadiq">Raphael Saadiq</a>, formerly of <a href="/wiki/Tony!_Toni!_Ton%C3%A9!" title="Tony! Toni! Toné!">Tony! Toni! Toné!</a>, releasing one album in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-linearlabs_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-linearlabs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> On October 12, 2004, he released his debut solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Shaheedullah_and_Stereotypes" title="Shaheedullah and Stereotypes">Shaheedullah and Stereotypes</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-linearlabs_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-linearlabs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Muhammad co-hosted the <i>Microphone Check</i> radio show, which ran on NPR until 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and on Spotify until 2019<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Muhammad worked with producer <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Younge" title="Adrian Younge">Adrian Younge</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Souls_of_Mischief" title="Souls of Mischief">Souls of Mischief</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/There_Is_Only_Now" title="There Is Only Now">There Is Only Now</a></i>, as the album's narrator.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Since then Muhammad and Younge have collaborated to co-produce <a href="/wiki/Luke_Cage_(soundtrack)" title="Luke Cage (soundtrack)">the soundtrack</a> for both seasons of the <i><a href="/wiki/Luke_Cage_(TV_series)" title="Luke Cage (TV series)">Luke Cage</a></i> TV series,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and they have toured together since 2018 as the Midnight Hour, releasing an album by the same name.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Muhammad contributed to Saadiq's fifth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Lee_(album)" title="Jimmy Lee (album)">Jimmy Lee</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, Muhammad and Younge launched the <a href="/wiki/Jazz_Is_Dead_(record_label)" title="Jazz Is Dead (record label)">Jazz Is Dead</a> album series, collaborating with jazz musicians <a href="/wiki/Roy_Ayers" title="Roy Ayers">Roy Ayers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marcos_Valle" title="Marcos Valle">Marcos Valle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doug_Carn" title="Doug Carn">Doug Carn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gary_Bartz" title="Gary Bartz">Gary Bartz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Donato" title="João Donato">João Donato</a>, and the trio <a href="/wiki/Azymuth" title="Azymuth">Azymuth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
