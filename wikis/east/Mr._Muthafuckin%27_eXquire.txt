<p><b>Hugh Anthony Allison</b> (born 1986), better known by his stage name <b>Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire</b> (often censored as <b>Mr. MFN eXquire</b> or simply <b>eXquire</b>), is an American rapper. Born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, he debuted with the release of his first <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, <i>The Big Fat Kill</i>, in 2008. Allison would follow-up with what is considered to be his breakout project, the mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_in_Translation_(mixtape)" title="Lost in Translation (mixtape)">Lost in Translation</a></i>, released by <a href="/wiki/Mishka_NYC" title="Mishka NYC">Mishka NYC</a> in 2011. He signed his first major-label record deal with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Republic_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Republic Records">Universal Republic Records</a>, in 2012, which he subsequently left. In 2017, with the release of Allison's fourth EP <i>Brainiac</i>, he launched Chocolate Rabbit, his own record label imprint. His <a href="/wiki/Mr._Muthafuckin%27_eXquire_(album)" title="Mr. Muthafuckin&#39; eXquire (album)">self-titled debut studio album</a> was independently released in 2019.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Muthafuckin%27_eXquire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hugh Anthony Allison<sup id="cite_ref-vice2013_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vice2013-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was born in the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> borough of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He grew up in Brooklyn's <a href="/wiki/Crown_Heights,_Brooklyn" title="Crown Heights, Brooklyn">Crown Heights</a> neighborhood.<sup id="cite_ref-vice2013_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vice2013-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Since his parents separated at an early age, he spent his weekdays with his mother and weekends with his father.<sup id="cite_ref-vice2013_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vice2013-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He dropped out of high school at the age of 15.<sup id="cite_ref-vice2013_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vice2013-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Muthafuckin%27_eXquire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008, eXquire released his first full-length project, a mixtape titled <i>The Big Fat Kill</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-thefader2011_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader2011-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He first began gaining major recognition in June 2011, when he released the music video for a song titled "Huzzah".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On September 11, 2011, eXquire released his second mixtape, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_in_Translation_(mixtape)" title="Lost in Translation (mixtape)">Lost in Translation</a></i>, which features "Huzzah".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape, which was released on <a href="/wiki/Mishka_NYC" title="Mishka NYC">Mishka NYC</a>, includes 18-tracks, one of them being the remix to "Huzzah", titled "The Last Huzzah". The remix, which features guest verses from fellow American rappers <a href="/wiki/Despot_(rapper)" title="Despot (rapper)">Despot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heems" title="Heems">Heems</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_A.D." title="Kool A.D.">Kool A.D.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Brown" title="Danny Brown">Danny Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/El-P" title="El-P">El-P</a>, was called one of the best songs of 2011 by <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> eXquire's third mixtape, titled <i>Merry eXmas &amp; Suck My Dick</i>, was released on December 25, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 15, 2012, it was announced eXquire had signed a recording contract with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Republic_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Republic Records">Universal Republic Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In November 2012, eXquire released his first major record label project, an EP titled <i><a href="/wiki/Power_%26_Passion" title="Power &amp; Passion">Power &amp; Passion</a></i>, on Universal Republic.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In May 2013, eXquire announced he would release a new project titled <i>Kismet</i> and premiered a song titled "Illest Niggaz Breathin'".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In June 2013, he released a 16-track mixtape titled <i>Kismet</i>, which was recorded mostly in <a href="/wiki/Woodstock,_New_York" title="Woodstock, New York">Woodstock, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In October 2013, eXquire re-released <i>Kismet</i>, re-titling it <i>Kismet: Blue Edition</i> and adding 16 tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On October 7, 2013, Universal Republic released eXquire from his contract.<sup id="cite_ref-RS_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On May 13, 2014, he released a song titled "I Ain't Even Fuck <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a> aka Mr. Lime-A-Rita Papi", in which he details his signing with a major record label and then getting dropped without accomplishing anything.<sup id="cite_ref-RS_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 7, 2015, eXquire released <i>Merry eXmas &amp; SMD 2</i>, the sequel to his 2011 Christmas mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-smd2_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smd2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On January 8, 2015, eXquire released another mixtape, this one titled <i>Live from the Danger Room</i>, which is compiled of unreleased tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-LDR_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDR-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On October 8, 2015, eXquire released his second EP, titled <i>Live Forever</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On July 12, 2019, eXquire released his <a href="/wiki/Mr._Muthafuckin%27_eXquire_(album)" title="Mr. Muthafuckin&#39; eXquire (album)">self-titled debut studio album</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
