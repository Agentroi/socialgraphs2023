<p><b>Le'Quincy Anderson</b> (born April 28, 1990), better known by his stage name <b>Don Q</b>, is an American rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is best known for his collaborations with labelmate <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Don_Q_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Le'Quincy Anderson was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a> in New York City. He began writing raps in the 5th grade but did not start taking rap seriously until high school, he first rapped in a booth at the age of 16. Don grew up listening to artists such as <a href="/wiki/Cassidy_(rapper)" title="Cassidy (rapper)">Cassidy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As he got older he began battle rapping in the streets, parks, and different blocks which helped him build confidence. Don began battling because he was inspired by the Smack DVD series. Don's first stage name was Q da Don, he would then change it to <a href="/wiki/Don_Q" title="Don Q">Don Q</a>, after the popular rum after seeing it on the shelves at local liquor stores. Don released his first mixtape at 17. The mixtape was in dedication to Andre "Pop" Davidson, a standout basketball player at <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_High_School_(New_York_City)" title="John F. Kennedy High School (New York City)">John F. Kennedy High School</a> who died. Pop was Don's best friend and the first person to co-sign Don's rapping talent.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape, however, did not receive much traction outside of Highbridge. Don almost quit rapping losing motivation as he did not have money to pay for studio sessions. Don was still worried about continuing his school studies and the streets but he came to the conclusion that rap was going to be his way to a better life. He made appearances in the local battle circuit where he rapped for several leagues such as Battle On The Beat, Blue Collar Television and Come Up Groundz.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Don_Q_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016:_Career_beginnings">2016: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Don_Q_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2016: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2016, Don signed to Highbridge the Label, a label ran by friends Quincy "QP" Acheampong and Sambou "Bubba" Camara. He and <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie</a> were the first artists signed to the label. He was heavily featured on the compilation, <i>Highbridge the Label: The Takeover Vol. 1</i>, which dropped on May 28, 2016. Don gained more notoriety after appearing on <a href="/wiki/Funkmaster_Flex" title="Funkmaster Flex">Funkmaster Flex</a>'s freestyle series on <a href="/wiki/Hot_97" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot 97">Hot 97</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a> who had become one of the most popular up and coming rappers in New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Don delivered an impressive freestyle over <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>' <a href="/wiki/Oochie_Wally" title="Oochie Wally">Oochie Wally</a>. Soon, the songs Bando and Bag On Me, featuring Don Q began getting radio play in the Tri-State area. In July 2016, Highbridge the Label partnered with <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Don dropped his first official mixtape in August 2016. The mixtape titled <i>Don Season</i> was hosted by <a href="/wiki/Don_Cannon" title="Don Cannon">DJ Don Cannon</a>. The mixtape has features from <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie</a>, <a href="/wiki/PnB_Rock" title="PnB Rock">PnB Rock</a> and Keir.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In September 2016, he was featured on the <a href="/wiki/BET_Hip_Hop_Awards" title="BET Hip Hop Awards">BET Hip Hop Awards</a> cypher alongside <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russ_(rapper)" title="Russ (rapper)">Russ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kent_Jones_(rapper)" title="Kent Jones (rapper)">Kent Jones</a> and Nick Grant.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> During the year, Don collaborated with artists like <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_East" title="Dave East">Dave East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Scooter" title="Young Scooter">Young Scooter</a> and <a href="/wiki/PnB_Rock" title="PnB Rock">PnB Rock</a> among others.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2017">2017</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Don_Q_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2017">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2017, Don dropped his second mixtape titled <i>Corner Stories</i> featuring <a href="/wiki/Dave_East" title="Dave East">Dave East</a>, <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie" class="mw-redirect" title="A Boogie">A Boogie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>, Zoey Dollaz, <a href="/wiki/PnB_Rock" title="PnB Rock">PnB Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a> and Loso Loaded,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> he would later follow up with a remastered version titled <i>Corner Stories Reloaded</i> with extra songs adding one featuring <a href="/wiki/Rowdy_Rebel" title="Rowdy Rebel">Rowdy Rebel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Don is featured on <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie" class="mw-redirect" title="A Boogie">A Boogie</a>'s debut album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bigger_Artist" title="The Bigger Artist">The Bigger Artist</a></i> on the tracks "Somebody" and "Money Sprung".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Don collaborated with Dominican recording artist Chimbala, on a track titled Matalo. Don Q released his mixtape <i>Don Talk</i> featuring <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tee_Grizzley" title="Tee Grizzley">Tee Grizzley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Desiigner" title="Desiigner">Desiigner</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2018">2018</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Don_Q_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2018">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Don Q released his follow-up mixtape to <i>Don Season</i> titled <i>Don Season 2</i> featuring <a href="/wiki/G_Herbo" title="G Herbo">G Herbo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_East" title="Dave East">Dave East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Durk" title="Lil Durk">Lil Durk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moneybagg_Yo" title="Moneybagg Yo">Moneybagg Yo</a> and more. The lead single for the project is titled "Yeah Yeah" and features <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a> and <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Murda_Beatz" title="Murda Beatz">Murda Beatz</a>. Don Q closed out the year dropping a 6 song <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> titled <i>Underrated</i>. He was also featured on A-Boogie's Platinum album, <i><a href="/wiki/Hoodie_SZN" title="Hoodie SZN">Hoodie SZN</a></i> on a collaboration titled "Bosses and Workers" with fellow labelmate Trap Manny.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2019">2019</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Don_Q_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2019">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>To start off 2019, Don responded to <a href="/wiki/Tory_Lanez" title="Tory Lanez">Tory Lanez</a>'s battle challenge. Lanez had engaged in a battle with <a href="/wiki/Joyner_Lucas" title="Joyner Lucas">Joyner Lucas</a> and had proclaimed himself the best rapper alive. Don proclaimed that Tory could not be the best rapper alive as he accused him of stealing lyrics from his Hot 97 freestyle. After a brief exchange of words via social media, Don released a diss track titled, "I'm Not Joyner." Lanez responded with his own diss entitled "Don Queen". The following day, Don Q retaliated with another diss track, called "This Is Ya King?"<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Don Q was one of 5 rappers banned from New York City's Rolling Loud festival due to safety concerns by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department" title="New York City Police Department">NYPD</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2020">2020</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Don_Q_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2020">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With a short hiatus from music, Don Q returned to music by releasing "Flood My Wrist" featuring <a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert" title="Lil Uzi Vert">Lil Uzi Vert</a> and <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a> on July 1, 2020. He went on to release "I'm the One" a week later, and "Blood Sweat &amp; Tears" on July 24. Another A Boogie-collaboration, "Vroom Vroom", was released on September 2, 2020.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2021">2021</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Don_Q_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2021">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After another long hiatus, Don Q made his departure from Atlantic Records and signed a new record deal with record label EMPIRE. Don Q a released mixtape called 'Double or Nothing' on October 22, 2021, featuring a song with American drill rapper <a href="/wiki/B-Lovee" title="B-Lovee">B-Lovee</a>.
</p>
