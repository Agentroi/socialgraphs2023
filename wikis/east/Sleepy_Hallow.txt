<p><b>Tegan Joshua Anthony Chambers</b> (born December 20, 1999), known professionally as <b>Sleepy Hallow</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is an American rapper making <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_drill" title="Brooklyn drill">Brooklyn drill</a> music.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Originally from Jamaica, Chambers grew up in the <a href="/wiki/Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="Flatbush, Brooklyn">Flatbush</a> section of Brooklyn in New York City alongside frequent collaborator <a href="/wiki/Sheff_G" title="Sheff G">Sheff G</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2018, he had his first breakthrough viral single with "Flows", which also featured Sheff G.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In August 2020, his single "<a href="/wiki/Deep_End_Freestyle" title="Deep End Freestyle">Deep End Freestyle</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Foushe%C3%A9" title="Fousheé">Fousheé</a> was certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-riaa.com_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riaa.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In August 2021, his single "<a href="/wiki/2055_(song)" title="2055 (song)">2055</a>" peaked at number 51 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, becoming his highest-charting song. He is currently signed to Winners Circle Entertainment and <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 16, 2023, Chambers was indicted along with 32 other alleged gang members including Sheff G for a multitude of gang conspiracy charges.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 30, 2023, Chambers announced on his Instagram story that he got bailed out, and is now waiting for the trial to start.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sleepy_Hallow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a child, Chambers immigrated from Jamaica to the <a href="/wiki/Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="Flatbush, Brooklyn">Flatbush</a> section of <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York City. Around the age of 12, he started to rap. He later picked the name "Sleepy Hallow" as his rap name as that was his name in the streets. Chambers dropped out of school in the ninth grade.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He first met friend and collaborator <a href="/wiki/Sheff_G" title="Sheff G">Sheff G</a> when the two were preparing to fight, but stopped when Sheff G was impressed with how Chambers carried himself.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sleepy_Hallow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He, <a href="/wiki/Sheff_G" title="Sheff G">Sheff G</a>, and DizzyGotBands were signed to former <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a> player, <a href="/wiki/Junior_Galette" title="Junior Galette">Junior Galette</a>'s music record label, Nula Entertainment from 2017 to 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Sleepy Hallow released his debut mixtape, <i>Don't Sleep</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In the same year, Winners Circle Entertainment was founded, with Sleepy Hallow as one of the first signees.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, he released his second mixtape, <i>Sleepy for President</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape featured the hit single "<a href="/wiki/Deep_End_Freestyle" title="Deep End Freestyle">Deep End Freestyle</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Foushe%C3%A9" title="Fousheé">Fousheé</a>, which peaked on the US <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 chart at number 80 on June 20, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The single has over 85 million streams and was <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_certification" title="Music recording certification">certified gold</a> by <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> in August 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-riaa.com_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riaa.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2020, Winners Circle Entertainment started a partnership with <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Sleepy Hallow and Sheff G released the single "Tip Toe", produced by Great John.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2021, he released his first studio album, <i>Still Sleep?</i><sup id="cite_ref-Revolt_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Revolt-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The album includes the single, "<a href="/wiki/2055_(song)" title="2055 (song)">2055</a>", which was released alongside a video that sees an animated Sleepy Hallow in an alternate, futuristic universe.<sup id="cite_ref-Revolt_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Revolt-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The single would be certified double platinum in March 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2022, he released his single "<a href="/wiki/Die_Young_(Sleepy_Hallow_song)" title="Die Young (Sleepy Hallow song)">Die Young</a>", which features Canadian rapper <a href="/wiki/347aidan" title="347aidan">347aidan</a>.
</p><p>In September 2022, he released his single "2 Mins of Pain".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2022, he released the single "Marie".<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2023, Sleepy Hallow was charged in a 140-count indictment, as the district attorney's office said the gang is linked to 12 shootings and a murder that was caught on camera.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 15, 2023, Sleepy Hallow released his first album since getting out of jail “Boy Meets World". It features songs such as "GBG" featuring <a href="/wiki/Marshmello" title="Marshmello">Marshmello</a> and "For You" featuring TaTa.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
