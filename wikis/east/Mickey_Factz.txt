<p><b>Mark Anthony Williams Jr.</b> (born July 13, 1982), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Mickey Factz</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> recording artist from <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a> borough of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mickey_Factz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mickey Factz attended Adlai E. Stevenson High School, which is known for being the high school that <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> pioneer <a href="/wiki/Afrika_Bambaataa" title="Afrika Bambaataa">Afrika Bambaataa</a> attended. The school is notable for delivering students of musical prowess.<sup id="cite_ref-spin_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spin-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-thefader_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-billboard_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vibe_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vibe-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Factz was exposed to music at an early age and his father would frequently rap to him. In return, he began reciting his very own rhymes, watching music videos, and studying the performances of his favorite music artists.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> After high school, Factz went to <a href="/wiki/New_York_University_School_of_Law" title="New York University School of Law">New York University School of Law</a> where he dropped out to pursue music <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mickey_Factz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="In_Search_of_N.2AE.2AR.2AD_.282006.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Search_of_N*E*R*D_(2006)">In Search of N*E*R*D (2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mickey_Factz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In Search of N*E*R*D (2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mickey Factz did many freestyles over <a href="/wiki/N.E.R.D" class="mw-redirect" title="N.E.R.D">N.E.R.D</a> instrumentals on <i>In Search of N*E*R*D</i> (2006) in which he specifically rapped over N*E*R*D instrumentals.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Here Mickey Factz collaborated with Steve-O who pushed him to really begin to listen to N*E*R*D and served as creative inspiration. Factz then worked to chop up the beats and make the project unique, gaining the attention of <a href="/wiki/Pharrell" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharrell">Pharrell</a> himself.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flashback_Volume:_1:_Back_to_the_Future">Flashback Volume: 1: Back to the Future</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mickey_Factz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Flashback Volume: 1: Back to the Future">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Flashback Vol.1: Back to the Future</i> (2007), which was created in order to pay homage to the type of music that Factz grew up with.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  Mickey recorded about 40 tracks in total for this album and even after recording, he entered into another process that included the remix of over half the songs and instrumentals.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="The_Leak.2C_Volume_1:_The_Understanding"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Leak,_Volume_1:_The_Understanding">The Leak, Volume 1: The Understanding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mickey_Factz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Leak, Volume 1: The Understanding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For 2008's <i>The Leak, Vol.1: The Understanding</i>, Factz leaked one song every week on <a href="/wiki/MySpace" class="mw-redirect" title="MySpace">MySpace</a> for 17 weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-vibe_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vibe-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> One of the songs, "I'm Sean (50 Shots More)", was written from the perspective of <a href="/wiki/Sean_Bell_shooting_incident" class="mw-redirect" title="Sean Bell shooting incident">Sean Bell</a>, who was killed by New York police in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-spin_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spin-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Mixtape_Success_.26_Freshman_XXL_Cover_.282008-2009.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Mixtape_Success_&amp;_Freshman_XXL_Cover_(2008-2009)">Mixtape Success &amp; Freshman XXL Cover (2008-2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mickey_Factz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mixtape Success &amp; Freshman XXL Cover (2008-2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In a 2008 interview, Factz recalled, "I had heard a few records from some New York artists, whose names I won’t mention, and I wasn’t very pleased with what they were doing. [...] I decided that I was going to get in the shoes of Sean Bell and talk about the night of him dying, him at his funeral, him seeing the press conferences and him just talking to the youth and letting them know that we have to come together and do better than what’s going on right now."<sup id="cite_ref-interview_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-interview-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> 
Factz's song-per-week release strategy was also used for <i>The Leak, Vol.2: The Inspiration</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-billboard_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> which contains guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Aubrey_Graham" class="mw-redirect" title="Aubrey Graham">Drake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travie_McCoy" title="Travie McCoy">Travie McCoy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tanya_Morgan" title="Tanya Morgan">Tanya Morgan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> 
The <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for "Rockin' N Rollin'", featuring <a href="/wiki/The_Cool_Kids" title="The Cool Kids">The Cool Kids</a> and produced by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Precize&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Precize (page does not exist)">Precize</a>, was added to rotation at <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Factz was named to <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> magazine's "Freshmen Class of '09" issue in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Battery_Records_.26_The_Achievement_.282010-present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Battery_Records_&amp;_The_Achievement_(2010-present)">Battery Records &amp; The Achievement (2010-present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mickey_Factz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Battery Records &amp; The Achievement (2010-present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Factz contributed a song, "Rocker", to the soundtrack of the <i><a href="/wiki/Fight_Night_Round_4" title="Fight Night Round 4">Fight Night Round 4</a></i> video game,<sup id="cite_ref-videogame_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-videogame-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> 
and he signed to <a href="/wiki/Battery_Records_(hip_hop)" title="Battery Records (hip hop)">Battery Records</a> in May 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-allhiphop_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allhiphop-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
Mickey Factz recently finished touring with <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a> and is working on his album <i>The Achievement</i>, which is set to be released in Winter 2013/2014<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
Mickey Factz became the new member of All City Chess Club when <a href="/wiki/Lupe_Fiasco" title="Lupe Fiasco">Lupe Fiasco</a> said at a radio station "Mickey Factz is part of All City Chess Club."
While in the studio recording for his debut album, Mickey tweeted that he and Lupe Fiasco are collaborating on a track. Also pictures of the two together have surfaced on the internet recently. "I’m Better Than You<i> (2010) was Mickey’s way of expressing his sense of confidence and recognition of the competitiveness of music.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> "Love.Lust.Lost" (2011) was an album based on one of Factz’ past relationship and was his attempt to get the girl he loved back. Although in the end, she slipped away, this album represents Mickey Factz’ growth not only as an artist but also as a person. Deeply personal and honest, this was enjoyed by a wide variety of people who could relate to his deep and heartfelt lyrics.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></i>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mickey_Mause">Mickey Mause</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mickey_Factz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Mickey Mause">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, Factz tried to release Mickey MauSe under his label, but they considered it to be too artistic and did not back it up. Factz went on to release this experimental project himself and in the album, Mickey Mause became his pseudo character, thrown into the 1980s as a graffiti writer who was later kicked out by his parents for being a troubled youth. This project represented what it truly meant to be an artist and much research was done in order to learn about Mickey Mouse and that era of time.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Lyrically, this mixtape is known for being one of Factz’ best and gained him the opportunity to become closer to Lupe Fiasco.
</p><p>In a 2015 interview with hip hop blog <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://themusicstash.com/">The Music Stash</a>, Factz said a sequel to Mickey Mause called Mau2e was scheduled for Christmas.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_ventures">Other ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mickey_Factz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Other ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mickey_Factz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Factz appeared in a <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a> commercial later that year as a spokesman for the <a href="/wiki/Honda_Accord" title="Honda Accord">Accord</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-advertisingage_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-advertisingage-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_Rap">Battle Rap</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mickey_Factz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Battle Rap">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Factz has a long history as a competitor in the sport of <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">battle rap</a> dating back to the original battle leagues, and hosting battles at Ultimate Rap League events.
</p><p>In 2017, Factz was involved in the battle rap league KOTD's Massacre event where he battled West Coast rapper Daylyt.
</p>
