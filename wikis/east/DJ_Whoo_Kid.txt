<p><b>Yves Mondesir</b> (born October 12, 1975) better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>DJ Whoo Kid</b>, is a Haitian-American <a href="/wiki/Hip-hop_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip-hop music">hip-hop</a> <a href="/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJ</a>. He is currently signed to <a href="/wiki/G-Unit_Records" title="G-Unit Records">G-Unit Records</a> and its subsidiary label, Shadyville Entertainment. He is the host of <i>The Whoolywood Shuffle</i> on <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Satellite_Radio" title="Sirius Satellite Radio">Sirius/XM Radio</a> Shade 45. His mixtapes often incorporate a soundbite of him shouting his own name. He's had two albums chart on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, <i>XXL Presents: Bad Season</i> with <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a> (#118 in 2011) and <i>The Whoodlum Ball</i> with Smith and Hay and Ranna Royce (#156 in 2018).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Whoo_Kid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>DJ Whoo Kid was born '<i>Yves Mondesir</i> on October 12, 1972, in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a>, and is of Haitian descent. He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Queens_Village,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Queens Village, New York">Queens Village, New York</a>, and has strong ties to <a href="/wiki/Cap-Ha%C3%AFtien,_Haiti" class="mw-redirect" title="Cap-Haïtien, Haiti">Cap-Haïtien, Haiti</a>.
</p>
