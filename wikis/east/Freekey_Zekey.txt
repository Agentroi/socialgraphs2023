font-size: 125%;"><div class="fn">Freekey Zekey</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Ezekiel Giles</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday">1975-10-13</span>) </span>October 13, 1975<span class="noprint ForceAgeToShow"> (age&#160;47)</span><br /><a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, U.S.</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role"><a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">Rapper</a>, record executive</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">1996–present</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Koch_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Koch Records">Koch</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Big_Cat_Records_(U.S._record_label)" title="Big Cat Records (U.S. record label)">Big Cat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diplomat_Records" title="Diplomat Records">Diplomat</a>, 730 Dips</td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Ezekiel Giles</b> (born October 13, 1975), better known by his stage name <b>Freekey Zekey</b>, is an American rapper and music executive best known as a founding member of <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">The Diplomats</a>, a group he helped form in <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a> in 1997 alongside his childhood friend <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a> and cousin <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>. Freekey also serves as the owner/CEO of 730 Dips Records.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aside from The Diplomats, he is also a member of fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a>' <a href="/wiki/ByrdGang" class="mw-redirect" title="ByrdGang">ByrdGang</a>, and has made a habit of yelling his name in songs; it is his signature on tracks, whether he is featured or the solo artist.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freekey_Zekey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Giles, along with his cousin <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a> and their friend <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a> formed the <a href="/wiki/Hip-hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip-hop">hip-hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">The Diplomats</a> in their hometown of <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> in 1997. Giles adopted the stage name <b>Freekey Zekey</b> and the group began writing and performing songs together. In the early years of the group, Zekey served mostly as a <a href="/wiki/Hype_man" title="Hype man">hype man</a>, yelling adlibs or his name during other members' songs and performances.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Zekey was responsible for writing and performing almost all of the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_skit" title="Hip hop skit">skits</a> on Diplomat mixtapes and Cam'ron's albums, and did this until he was sentenced to 35–42 months in prison in February 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> While in prison, Zekey began writing his own songs and honed his skills as a rapper. Just a few weeks after being released from prison in November 2006, Freekey signed a contract with <a href="/wiki/Asylum_Records" title="Asylum Records">Asylum Records</a> to release his debut album, <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Ezekiel_(album)" title="Book of Ezekiel (album)">Book of Ezekiel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic.com_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The album was released on July 24, 2007, and featured guest appearances from other members of The Diplomats, including Cam'ron, Jim Jones and <a href="/wiki/Juelz_Santana" title="Juelz Santana">Juelz Santana</a>. The album peaked at #23 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard</a> <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip_Hop_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip Hop Albums</a> chart. After The Diplomats effectively disbanded in 2007, Freekey went on to form his own label/group, the 730 Dips, and also joined Jim Jones' new group, <a href="/wiki/ByrdGang" class="mw-redirect" title="ByrdGang">ByrdGang</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-730dipsyoutube_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-730dipsyoutube-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-jjfzbg_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jjfzbg-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freekey_Zekey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Giles is the first cousin of <a href="/wiki/Cam%E2%80%99ron" class="mw-redirect" title="Cam’ron">Cam’ron</a>, and was a childhood friend of <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Damon_Dash" title="Damon Dash">Damon Dash</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2003_shooting">2003 shooting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freekey_Zekey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2003 shooting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 25, 2003, Giles and his crew were involved in a minor car collision outside of a <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> nightclub, after another car collided with the back of the vehicle Giles and his crew were inside. After stepping out of their vehicle, the men in the other car attempted to rob Giles and his friends. Giles was shot once in the abdomen and sustained minor injuries after the incident. In total, over 44 bullets were fired at Giles and his crew during the altercation, and one member of Giles’ entourage, his bodyguard Eric Mangrum, was killed. In December 2005, Chauncey Dillon was sentenced to 30 years in prison for both robbery and the murder of Mangrum.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freekey_Zekey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Giles was sentenced to 35–42 months in prison on February 5, 2004, for running an <a href="/wiki/Ecstasy_(drug)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecstasy (drug)">ecstasy</a> ring.
