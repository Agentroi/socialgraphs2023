<p><b>Steven Adam Markowitz</b><sup id="cite_ref-Penn_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Penn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born August 19, 1988), known professionally as <b>Hoodie Allen</b>, is an American rapper, singer and songwriter from <a href="/wiki/Plainview,_New_York" title="Plainview, New York">Plainview, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HoodieiTunes_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HoodieiTunes-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> After graduating from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania" title="University of Pennsylvania">University of Pennsylvania</a>, he began working at Google before ultimately quitting to pursue a music career full-time. In 2012, he released his first official EP titled <i><a href="/wiki/All_American_(Hoodie_Allen_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="All American (Hoodie Allen album)">All American</a></i> which debuted at No. 10 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200. In October 2014, Hoodie officially released his debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/People_Keep_Talking" title="People Keep Talking">People Keep Talking</a></i> which was successful with first week sales of over 30,000 along with a debut position of No. 8 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200. Hoodie followed up on his success in January 2016 and released his second studio album, <i>Happy Camper.</i>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoodie_Allen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Steven Markowitz was born on <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a> and raised in a <a href="/wiki/Jewish" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish">Jewish</a> household in <a href="/wiki/Plainview,_New_York" title="Plainview, New York">Plainview</a> along with his brother, Daniel.<sup id="cite_ref-hipsterjew_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hipsterjew-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He started writing lyrics as a child, and would perform raps for his friends at house parties. Markowitz first attended the <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_School_for_the_Gifted" title="Long Island School for the Gifted">Long Island School for the Gifted</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Huntington,_New_York" title="South Huntington, New York">South Huntington</a>, and later attended <a href="/wiki/Plainview_%E2%80%93_Old_Bethpage_John_F._Kennedy_High_School" title="Plainview – Old Bethpage John F. Kennedy High School">Plainview – Old Bethpage John F. Kennedy High School</a>.
</p><p>While attending the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania" title="University of Pennsylvania">University of Pennsylvania</a>, he pledged the <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Epsilon_Pi" title="Alpha Epsilon Pi">Alpha Epsilon Pi</a> fraternity. He also played as defensive back for Penn's <a href="/wiki/Sprint_football" title="Sprint football">sprint football</a> team. After graduating in 2010 with a degree in marketing and finance, he worked at <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> as an AdWords associate in their Standardized AdWords Reseller Training (START) program.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mashable_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mashable-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He would leave at 7:00&#160;am for a bus ride to the <a href="/wiki/Googleplex" title="Googleplex">Googleplex</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mountain_View,_California" title="Mountain View, California">Mountain View</a>, work a full day at Google, return home at 6:00&#160;pm, write songs, answer fan emails, and schedule concerts until 2:00 or 3:00 AM.<sup id="cite_ref-baycitizen_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-baycitizen-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Reflecting on this, Allen said, "I was moving so fast, and even while I was at Google there was so much going on that I felt like I was doing two full-time jobs."<sup id="cite_ref-mashable_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mashable-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His dream and passion was music, so when he got opportunities to do live shows, he decided to leave Google.<sup id="cite_ref-baycitizen_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-baycitizen-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-w2fy_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-w2fy-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoodie_Allen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.9312:_Beginnings_and_Leap_Year"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–12:_Beginnings_and_Leap_Year">2009–12: Beginnings and <i>Leap Year</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoodie_Allen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009–12: Beginnings and Leap Year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hoodie Allen originally was the duo of Steve Markowitz and Obey City (Samuel Obey, a childhood friend) on vocals and production, respectively. The name came about because Steven's nickname growing up was "Hoodie," and he wanted a name that "would stick in peoples' minds and be a little bit funny and representative of who I am" so a play off of famed filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Woody_Allen" title="Woody Allen">Woody Allen</a> was settled on.<sup id="cite_ref-thekollection_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thekollection-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Steven and Obey's first two releases were the <i>Bagels &amp; Beats EP</i> and <i>Making Waves</i> mixtape. These earned Hoodie a nomination for <a href="/wiki/MTVU" title="MTVU">MTVU</a>'s Best Music on Campus Award in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The single "UPENN Girls" also received notable attention. However, in 2010 Obey City ceased to produce in Hoodie for unknown reasons, and Steven continued making music with RJ Ferguson (aka RJF), giving himself the name Hoodie Allen.<sup id="cite_ref-tgl_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tgl-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In June 2010, he released "You Are Not a Robot," which sampled "I Am Not a Robot" by <a href="/wiki/Marina_and_the_Diamonds" class="mw-redirect" title="Marina and the Diamonds">Marina and the Diamonds</a> and which hit No. 1 on <a href="/wiki/Hype_Machine" title="Hype Machine">Hype Machine</a>, an aggregator that collects the most-blogged about music in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-baycitizen_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-baycitizen-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> After seeing the response, he spent the summer working, and finished his mixtape <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://hoodieallen.com/album/pep-rally/">Pep Rally</a></i> by September.<sup id="cite_ref-tgl_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tgl-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The album was largely produced by RJF, and sampled songs from <a href="/wiki/Death_Cab_for_Cutie" title="Death Cab for Cutie">Death Cab for Cutie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flight_Facilities" title="Flight Facilities">Flight Facilities</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marina_and_the_Diamonds" class="mw-redirect" title="Marina and the Diamonds">Marina and the Diamonds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ellie_Goulding" title="Ellie Goulding">Ellie Goulding</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Two_Door_Cinema_Club" title="Two Door Cinema Club">Two Door Cinema Club</a>. He picked the name "Pep Rally" because he said that it "captured the energy of the record" and something "new and exciting."<sup id="cite_ref-wgtc_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wgtc-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He self-financed a video for the lead single "You Are Not A Robot," which helped the mixtape get downloaded over 200,000 times.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2011, Hoodie released his third mixtape, <i>Leap Year</i>. It reached 250,000 <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> plays in its first week of release.<sup id="cite_ref-billboardaug5_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboardaug5-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In support of the album, Hoodie headlined a 15-city tour across North America, including stops in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, with supporting act Fortune Family opening on several venues.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Previously, he had toured with <a href="/wiki/The_Cataracs" title="The Cataracs">The Cataracs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Das_Racist" title="Das Racist">Das Racist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiddy_Bang" title="Chiddy Bang">Chiddy Bang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Posner" title="Mike Posner">Mike Posner</a>, and <a href="/wiki/RJD2" title="RJD2">RJD2</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012:_All_American">2012: <i>All American</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoodie_Allen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012: All American">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/All_American_(Hoodie_Allen_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="All American (Hoodie Allen album)">All American (Hoodie Allen album)</a></div>
<p>On March 4, 2012, Hoodie announced via Twitter that he would be releasing his first EP, titled <a href="/wiki/All_American_(Hoodie_Allen_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="All American (Hoodie Allen album)"><i>All American</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He decided on this title because he credited his rise to the <a href="/wiki/American_Dream" title="American Dream">American Dream</a>, because he felt the songs showcased his best music to date, and because of a hometown restaurant with the same title. Hoodie spent five months developing the album, building tracks from scratch with his producer, RJF, rather than using sampled beats.<sup id="cite_ref-billboardep_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboardep-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Regarding the writing process, Hoodie stated that, "I would describe it as liberating... It was like, 'Okay, I hear this idea in my head, I hear these original ideas, [and] I'm putting them and piecing them together.'"<sup id="cite_ref-billboardep_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboardep-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On March 29, 2012, Hoodie released the first single from <i>All American</i> entitled "No Interruption," as well as its music video.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The music video for his second single on <i>All American</i>, titled "No Faith In Brooklyn (feat. Jhameel)," was released on April 9.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <i>All American</i> was released on April 10, 2012, and debuted as the No. 1 album on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> and at No. 10 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Over the months of April and May, Hoodie made a 22-stop tour across the US in support of <i>All American</i>, featuring rapper <a href="/wiki/Wax_(rapper)" title="Wax (rapper)">Wax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jared_Evan" title="Jared Evan">Jared Evan</a>, and others varied from show to show. The I Work Better In The UK Tour was his first time performing overseas. The Excellent Adventure Tour, which featured <a href="/wiki/G-Eazy" title="G-Eazy">G-Eazy</a>, began on September 7, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013:_Crew_Cuts_and_Americoustic">2013: <i>Crew Cuts</i> and <i>Americoustic</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoodie_Allen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2013: Crew Cuts and Americoustic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 11, 2013, <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)"><i>XXL</i></a> premiered the music video for "Cake Boy," the first single off of Hoodie's upcoming mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The single was later released on iTunes for purchase.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> A week later, the music video for "Fame Is For Assholes" (abbreviated FIFA) premiered on YouTube which features rapper <a href="/wiki/Chiddy_Bang" title="Chiddy Bang">Chiddy Bang</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Hoodie commented, "I wanted to write a track that mixed a classic doo-wop vibe with the more upbeat hip-hop style that my fans have come to love."<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hoodie embarked on his Cruisin' USA Tour with Aer and <a href="/wiki/Jared_Evan" title="Jared Evan">Jared Evan</a> which began on March 5, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/G-Eazy" title="G-Eazy">G-Eazy</a>, instead of Aer, accompanied Hoodie on his Boston, Philly, and NYC shows. During the Cruisin' USA Tour, Hoodie directed the audience to chant that they wanted to see him on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Jimmy_Fallon" title="Late Night with Jimmy Fallon">Late Night with Jimmy Fallon</a></i>. Leading up to <a href="/wiki/Roseland_Ballroom" title="Roseland Ballroom">Roseland Ballroom</a>, Hoodie started the hashtag "#GetHoodieAllenOnFallon" on Twitter to rally his fans. After trending worldwide, Fallon asked to speak with Hoodie.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hoodie performed his new single "Make It Home" on <a href="/wiki/Fuse_TV" class="mw-redirect" title="Fuse TV">Fuse</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Kina_Grannis" title="Kina Grannis">Kina Grannis</a> which was released on April 30. The studio version of the single was released onto iTunes on May 15, 2013, where it reached the top ten of the iTunes Hip-Hop/Rap charts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On July 30, 2013, Hoodie released the music video for "No Interruption (Acoustic)". His acoustic EP <i>Americoustic</i> was released on August 13, 2013, and reached No. 1 on the iTunes Hip-Hop/Rap album chart and No. 4 on the iTunes overall albums chart.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The EP's guitar work was composed, recorded and produced by <a href="/wiki/Our_Last_Night" title="Our Last Night">Our Last Night</a> guitarist Matt Wentworth.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014:_People_Keep_Talking">2014: <i>People Keep Talking</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoodie_Allen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014: People Keep Talking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/People_Keep_Talking" title="People Keep Talking">People Keep Talking</a></div>
<p>The lead single off of Hoodie's debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/People_Keep_Talking" title="People Keep Talking">People Keep Talking</a></i>, was released on May 7, 2014, titled "Show Me What You're Made Of." It premiered along with its music video, which parodied the film <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_Gilmore" title="Happy Gilmore">Happy Gilmore</a></i>, and featured fellow rapper D-WHY and <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Lee" title="Tommy Lee">Tommy Lee</a> from <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe" title="Mötley Crüe">Mötley Crüe</a>. The single was made available for purchase on iTunes and debuted as the No. 2 song overall.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Hoodie then made his television debut on <i><a href="/wiki/WTXF-TV" title="WTXF-TV">Good Day Philadelphia</a></i> playing an acoustic version of "Show Me What You're Made Of" after a short interview on May 15.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>To promote the album, Hoodie went on a world tour titled <i>People Keeping Talking World Tour</i>, this included shows in the US, Europe, Canada, and Australia, featuring artists <a href="/wiki/Chiddy_Bang" title="Chiddy Bang">Chiddy Bang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Max_Schneider" title="Max Schneider">MAX</a>. The tour began on October 29, 2014, at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Oak_Music_Theater" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Oak Music Theater">Royal Oak Music Theater</a> in <a href="/wiki/Royal_Oak,_Michigan" title="Royal Oak, Michigan">Royal Oak, Michigan</a>, and ran into June 2015.
</p><p>After tweeting to bassist <a href="/wiki/Pete_Wentz" title="Pete Wentz">Pete Wentz</a> about touring together, it was announced that Hoodie would be a special guest on the <a href="/wiki/American_Beauty/American_Psycho_Tour#Tour_dates" title="American Beauty/American Psycho Tour">Boys of Zummer Tour</a> with co-headliners <a href="/wiki/Fall_Out_Boy" title="Fall Out Boy">Fall Out Boy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932017:_Happy_Camper_and_The_Hype"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2017:_Happy_Camper_and_The_Hype">2016–2017: <i>Happy Camper</i> and <i>The Hype</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoodie_Allen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2016–2017: Happy Camper and The Hype">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 22, 2016, Hoodie released his second studio album, <i>Happy Camper</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Leading up to its release, the eighth track, "Champagne and Pools" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Blackbear_(musician)" title="Blackbear (musician)">blackbear</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kyle_(musician)" title="Kyle (musician)">KYLE</a>), and the album's lead single, "Are U Having Any Fun?" (featuring Meghan Tonjes), were released.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The lead single was also accompanied by its music video which followed the style of a video game similar to <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a></i>. <i>Happy Camper</i> was also made available for download on iTunes peaking at No. 2 on the overall albums chart.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On September 29, 2017, he released his third studio album, <i>The Hype</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2019:_Whatever_USA">2019: <i>Whatever USA</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoodie_Allen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2019: Whatever USA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 16, 2019, Hoodie released his fourth studio album <i>Whatever USA</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Leading up to its release, the ninth track "Never Going Back" was released on March 1, 2019. He then released "Come Around“, the second single on the album, with <a href="/wiki/Christian_French_(singer)" title="Christian French (singer)">Christian French</a> on June 21, 2019. The third track released before the album released was "Hell of a Time" which was released on August 2, 2019.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2020:_Allegations_of_Sexual_Misconduct">2020: Allegations of Sexual Misconduct</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoodie_Allen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2020: Allegations of Sexual Misconduct">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2020, Sarah Flores accused touring drummer Jimmy Kadesch of sexual misconduct. Hoodie replied later on Twitter saying he had fired a band member accused of sexual misconduct <sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2023:_Return_to_music_and_bub">2023: Return to music and <i>bub</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoodie_Allen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2023: Return to music and bub">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a three year hiatus, Hoodie returned to music by releasing "Wouldn't That Be Nice"<i>,</i> on May 13, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> This would later be used as the lead single for his upcoming album named <i>bub.</i>
</p><p>On March 17, 2023, Hoodie released his fifth album named <i>bub.</i><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoodie_Allen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hoodie has appeared in four episodes of the <a href="/wiki/CollegeHumor" title="CollegeHumor">CollegeHumor</a> series <a href="/wiki/Jake_and_Amir_(web_series)" title="Jake and Amir (web series)"><i>Jake and Amir</i></a> since March 3, 2011. He made his CollegeHumor debut as Amir's rap teacher in "Jake and Amir / Rap Teacher (With Hoodie Allen)" and returned to that role three more times in videos titled "Jake and Amir / Rap Teacher 2 (with Hoodie Allen)", "Jake and Amir / Rap Teacher 3 (with Hoodie Allen)", and "Jake and Amir / Jake and Amir Finale Part 5: The Auditions".<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoodie_Allen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2011, Hoodie cracked the Top 10 of <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a>'</i>s Uncharted Territory.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> For the week of August 5, 2011, he was No. 2 on <i>Billboards</i> Uncharted Territory, with <i>Billboard</i> noting that his "growing popularity is undeniable."<sup id="cite_ref-billboardaug5_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboardaug5-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 10, 2012, Hoodie's <i>All American</i> EP went to No. 1 on the iTunes charts within hours of its release. <i>All American</i> also debuted at No. 10 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a>'s Top Albums and was featured on its hip-hop and R&amp;B column, The Juice.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
