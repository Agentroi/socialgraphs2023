<p><b>Marcel Theo Hall</b> (April 8, 1964 – July 16, 2021), known professionally as <b>Biz Markie</b>, was an American rapper and singer. Markie's 1989 single "<a href="/wiki/Just_a_Friend" title="Just a Friend">Just a Friend</a>", became a <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">Top 40</a> hit in several countries and was named No. 100 on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s list of the 100 greatest <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip-hop</a> songs of all time in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-vh1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vh1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Markie was sometimes referred to as the "<a href="/wiki/Honorific_nicknames_in_popular_music" title="Honorific nicknames in popular music">Clown Prince of Hip Hop</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DAVID_MA_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DAVID_MA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Markie was born in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> in the neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a>, New York City, on April 8, 1964.<sup id="cite_ref-AllMusic_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-variety.com_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety.com-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He was raised on <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a> in the hamlet of <a href="/wiki/Brentwood,_New_York" title="Brentwood, New York">Brentwood</a><sup id="cite_ref-DAVID_MA_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DAVID_MA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and the village of <a href="/wiki/Patchogue,_New_York" title="Patchogue, New York">Patchogue</a>, where he spent his teenage years and where, on September 25, 2021, the intersection of South Street and West Avenue, across the street from his then-home, was dedicated as Biz Markie Way.<sup id="cite_ref-BizMarkieWay_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BizMarkieWay-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Longwood_High_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Longwood High School">Longwood High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Middle_Island,_New_York" title="Middle Island, New York">Middle Island</a> in 1982.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Markie began his career in New York City nightclubs and later gained regional recognition by performing at colleges in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Manteuffel_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Manteuffel-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
Regarding the origin of his stage name, the rapper said:
</p>
<blockquote><p>
Biz comes from the first hip-hop tape I heard. It was '77, '78, from the L Brothers. Grand Wizard Theodore was the DJ, and the rappers was Kevvy Kev, Master Rob and Busy Bee Starski. I loved Busy Bee. Busy Bee just stuck with me. My name used to be Bizzy B Markie, and after a while I put the Biz with the Markie. My nickname in my neighborhood was Markie.<sup id="cite_ref-Manteuffel_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Manteuffel-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s">1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1980s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Biz Markie was interviewed in the 1986 cult documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Fun_in_the_Big_Town" title="Big Fun in the Big Town">Big Fun in the Big Town</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Markie released his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Goin%27_Off" title="Goin&#39; Off">Goin' Off</a></i> in 1988, which attracted a fair amount of attention, largely due to the lead single, "Make the Music with Your Mouth, Biz". The album also featured the underground hit singles "Nobody Beats The Biz", "<a href="/wiki/Vapors_(song)" title="Vapors (song)">Vapors</a>", and "Pickin' Boogers".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Biz also appeared briefly in the music video for <a href="/wiki/Rob_Base" class="mw-redirect" title="Rob Base">Rob Base</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/It_Takes_Two_(Rob_Base_%26_DJ_E-Z_Rock_song)" title="It Takes Two (Rob Base &amp; DJ E-Z Rock song)">It Takes Two</a>".
</p><p>On October 10, 1989, Markie's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Biz_Never_Sleeps" title="The Biz Never Sleeps">The Biz Never Sleeps</a></i> was released on Cold Chillin'/Warner Bros. Records, produced by Markie, his cousin Cool V and Paul C. The single "<a href="/wiki/Just_a_Friend" title="Just a Friend">Just a Friend</a>", in which he alternates between rap and caterwauling, became Markie's most successful single, reaching No. 9 on the <i>Billboard</i> charts.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The song interpolates the 1968 song "You Got What I Need" by singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Scott" title="Freddie Scott">Freddie Scott</a>, whose basic chord and melody provided the base for the song's chorus.<sup id="cite_ref-npr_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-npr-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> "Just a Friend" was ranked 81st on VH1's 100 Greatest One-Hit Wonders in 2000, and later as number 100 on VH1's 100 Greatest Songs of Hip Hop in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-vh1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vh1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The music video, directed by <a href="/wiki/Lionel_C._Martin" title="Lionel C. Martin">Lionel C. Martin</a>, chronicles the rapper's woman problems.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Markie's third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/I_Need_a_Haircut" title="I Need a Haircut">I Need a Haircut</a></i> was released on August 27, 1991, on Cold Chillin'/Warner Bros. Records; it was produced by Markie and his cousin Cool V. Sales of the album were already low when Markie was served a lawsuit by <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_O%27Sullivan" title="Gilbert O&#39;Sullivan">Gilbert O'Sullivan</a>, who claimed that the album's "Alone Again" featured an unauthorized sample from his hit "<a href="/wiki/Alone_Again_(Naturally)" title="Alone Again (Naturally)">Alone Again (Naturally)</a>". O'Sullivan's claim was upheld in a <a href="/wiki/Landmark_ruling" class="mw-redirect" title="Landmark ruling">landmark ruling</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Upright_Music,_Ltd._v._Warner_Bros._Records_Inc." title="Grand Upright Music, Ltd. v. Warner Bros. Records Inc.">Grand Upright Music, Ltd. v. Warner Bros. Records Inc.</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> that altered the landscape of hip-hop, finding that all samples must be cleared with the original artist before being used. In accordance with the ruling, Warner Bros., the parent company of Cold Chillin', had to pull <i>I Need a Haircut</i> from circulation, and all companies had to clear samples with the samples' creators before releasing the records. This development reflected the increasing popularity of hip-hop and the financial stakes over which were set. Markie responded in 1993 with the mischievously titled <i><a href="/wiki/All_Samples_Cleared!" title="All Samples Cleared!">All Samples Cleared!</a></i>, but his career had been hurt by the publicity emanating from the lawsuit, and the record suffered accordingly.<sup id="cite_ref-npr_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-npr-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For the remainder of the decade, Markie occasionally made television appearances, including guest appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/In_Living_Color" title="In Living Color">In Living Color</a>;</i> as contestant Damian "Foosball" Franklin in the recurring game show sketch "The Dirty Dozens"; as Marlon Cain in "Ed Bacon: Guidance Counselor";<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> in a 1996 <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle rap</a> commercial on <a href="/wiki/MTV2" title="MTV2">MTV2</a>; and in the 1993 superhero film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Meteor_Man_(film)" title="The Meteor Man (film)">Meteor Man</a></i>. He also made numerous guest appearances with the <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Check_Your_Head" title="Check Your Head">Check Your Head</a></i> (1992), <i><a href="/wiki/Ill_Communication" title="Ill Communication">Ill Communication</a></i> (1994), <i><a href="/wiki/Hello_Nasty" title="Hello Nasty">Hello Nasty</a></i> (1998), and their anthology <i><a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys_Anthology:_The_Sounds_of_Science" title="Beastie Boys Anthology: The Sounds of Science">The Sounds of Science</a></i> (1999).<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He also rapped on the song "Schizo Jam" on Don Byron's 1998 release, <i><a href="/wiki/Nu_Blaxploitation" title="Nu Blaxploitation">Nu Blaxploitation</a></i> (Blue Note/Capitol) and worked with <a href="/wiki/Canibus" title="Canibus">Canibus</a> on the first track on the <a href="/wiki/Office_Space_soundtrack" class="mw-redirect" title="Office Space soundtrack"><i>Office Space</i> soundtrack</a> (1999). He also rapped on the track "So Fresh" alongside <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a> on <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a>'s 1999 album <i><a href="/wiki/Willennium" title="Willennium">Willennium</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1996, Markie appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Organization" title="Red Hot Organization">Red Hot Organization</a>'s compilation CD, <i><a href="/wiki/America_Is_Dying_Slowly" title="America Is Dying Slowly">America Is Dying Slowly</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coolio" title="Coolio">Coolio</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>, among others. The CD was meant to raise awareness of the <a href="/wiki/AIDS" class="mw-redirect" title="AIDS">AIDS</a> epidemic among African-American men.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In 1997, a sample of a Markie recording appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stones" class="mw-redirect" title="Rolling Stones">Rolling Stones</a>' song "<a href="/wiki/Anybody_Seen_My_Baby%3F" title="Anybody Seen My Baby?">Anybody Seen My Baby?</a>" from their album <i><a href="/wiki/Bridges_to_Babylon" title="Bridges to Babylon">Bridges to Babylon</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Markie also teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Frankie_Cutlass" title="Frankie Cutlass">Frankie Cutlass</a> on his third single and music video titled "The Cypher Part 3" with some of <a href="/wiki/Marley_Marl" title="Marley Marl">Marley Marl</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Juice_Crew" title="Juice Crew">Juice Crew</a> veterans.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He also appeared of <a href="/wiki/F%C3%BCnf_Sterne_deluxe" title="Fünf Sterne deluxe">Fünf Sterne Delxue</a> Will Smith, Meer Gayne? from the album <i>Sillium</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1999, Markie appeared on <a href="/wiki/Len_(band)" title="Len (band)">Len's</a> song "Beautiful Day" on their album <i><a href="/wiki/You_Can%27t_Stop_the_Bum_Rush" title="You Can&#39;t Stop the Bum Rush">You Can't Stop the Bum Rush</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-New_York_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_York-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> as well as on <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Ethnik" title="Alliance Ethnik">Alliance Ethnik</a>'s album <i>Fat Comeback</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Biz_Markie_at_Amager_Bio_4.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Biz_Markie_at_Amager_Bio_4.jpg/220px-Biz_Markie_at_Amager_Bio_4.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="308" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Biz_Markie_at_Amager_Bio_4.jpg/330px-Biz_Markie_at_Amager_Bio_4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Biz_Markie_at_Amager_Bio_4.jpg/440px-Biz_Markie_at_Amager_Bio_4.jpg 2x" data-file-width="731" data-file-height="1024" /></a><figcaption>Markie performing in 2007</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2002, Markie appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Men_in_Black_II" title="Men in Black II">Men in Black II</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Lee_Jones" title="Tommy Lee Jones">Tommy Lee Jones</a>, playing an alien parody of himself, whose native language sounded exactly like <a href="/wiki/Beatboxing" title="Beatboxing">beatboxing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He also provided rap vocals and sang the hook for the remixed version of <a href="/wiki/Mario_(singer)" title="Mario (singer)">Mario</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Just_a_Friend_2002" title="Just a Friend 2002">Just a Friend 2002</a>", which sampled his own song from 13 years before. Between 2002 and 2003, he appeared in episode 5 of the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Fastlane_(TV_series)" title="Fastlane (TV series)">Fastlane</a></i> playing himself as a nightclub DJ. In 2003, he appeared in the international television series <i><a href="/wiki/Kung_Faux" title="Kung Faux">Kung Faux</a></i> performing a series of voice-over characters featured in a variety of episodes.  In 2005, Markie detoured from his recording duties to appear on the first season of the television show <i><a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Fit_Club_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Celebrity Fit Club (U.S. TV series)">Celebrity Fit Club</a></i>, which challenged celebrities to lose weight by a combination of diet and exercise. Markie lost more weight than anybody else in the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> That year, he was also in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Andy_Milonakis_Show" title="The Andy Milonakis Show">The Andy Milonakis Show</a></i>. In 2006, his song "Pickin' Boogers" appeared on the soundtrack of the <a href="/wiki/Volition_(company)" title="Volition (company)">Volition</a> video game <i><a href="/wiki/Saints_Row_(2006_video_game)" title="Saints Row (2006 video game)">Saints Row</a></i>. Markie was a special guest on <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon" title="Nick Cannon">Nick Cannon</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_%27n_Out" title="Wild &#39;n Out">Wild 'n Out</a></i> on season 1 and a cast member on season 4. Markie also had a beatboxing segment, "Biz's Beat of the Day", on the <a href="/wiki/Nick_Jr._Channel" title="Nick Jr. Channel">Nick Jr. Channel</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Yo_Gabba_Gabba!" title="Yo Gabba Gabba!">Yo Gabba Gabba!</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Markie began opening in 2008 for <a href="/wiki/Chris_Rock" title="Chris Rock">Chris Rock</a> on the comedian's "No Apologies" tour. Markie's act included spinning records ranging from <a href="/wiki/Old_school_hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Old school hip hop">old school hip hop</a> to <a href="/wiki/Lynyrd_Skynyrd" title="Lynyrd Skynyrd">Lynyrd Skynyrd</a> and then performing "Just a Friend".<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> In December 2009, Biz Markie appeared in a <a href="/wiki/RadioShack" title="RadioShack">RadioShack</a> commercial, repeating the line: "Oh Snap! Guess what I saw!" from his song "Just a Friend".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> That same year saw his debut with <a href="/wiki/Andy_Milonakis" title="Andy Milonakis">Andy Milonakis</a> in television commercials for the commercial Internet service Tune Up.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, Markie appeared on VH1's <i>100 Greatest Artists of All Time</i>, providing commentary throughout the series. Markie himself was not included on the list. On November 9, 2010, he appeared on <a href="/wiki/The_Aquabats!" class="mw-redirect" title="The Aquabats!">the Aquabats!</a> new EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Radio_Down!" title="Radio Down!">Radio Down!</a></i> on the title track. On November 11, 2010, Markie sat in with <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">the Roots</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Jimmy_Fallon" title="Late Night with Jimmy Fallon">Late Night with Jimmy Fallon</a></i>, and performed "Just a Friend" with actor <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Goldblum" title="Jeff Goldblum">Jeff Goldblum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Markie toured with the <i><a href="/wiki/Yo_Gabba_Gabba!" title="Yo Gabba Gabba!">Yo Gabba Gabba!</a></i> live show.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> That year, his song, "Just a Friend" was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Saints_Row_IV" title="Saints Row IV">Saints Row IV</a></i>, which included the Pop station, 107.7 The Mix FM. He appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_(TV_series)" title="Mad (TV series)">Mad</a></i>, as the <a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit:_An_Unexpected_Journey" title="The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey">Hip Hop Hobbit</a>. He voiced Rudd Rhymez's DJ Tiny Timmy Scratch It in the <i><a href="/wiki/Randy_Cunningham:_9th_Grade_Ninja" title="Randy Cunningham: 9th Grade Ninja">Randy Cunningham: 9th Grade Ninja</a></i> episode "Hip Hopocalypse Now".<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Markie guest starred in the <i><a href="/wiki/SpongeBob_SquarePants" title="SpongeBob SquarePants">SpongeBob SquarePants</a></i> episode "Kenny the Cat" in the episode's title role.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> His voice acting work also includes the voice of Snorlock the Beatboxing Slug in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time" title="Adventure Time">Adventure Time</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Markie appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Syfy</a> network film <i><a href="/wiki/Sharknado_2:_The_Second_One" title="Sharknado 2: The Second One">Sharknado 2: The Second One</a></i>. That same year, he threw a <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_first_pitch" title="Ceremonial first pitch">ceremonial first pitch</a> for an <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Athletics" title="Oakland Athletics">Oakland Athletics</a> <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> game.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, his song, "Just a Friend" was featured in the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> Series <i><a href="/wiki/Love_(TV_series)" title="Love (TV series)">Love</a></i> as an ending theme for episode 4. He also made an appearance in the song "The Noisy Eater" off the album <i>Wildflower</i> by <a href="/wiki/The_Avalanches" title="The Avalanches">the Avalanches</a>. Also in 2016, he appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Empire_(2015_TV_series)" title="Empire (2015 TV series)">Empire</a></i> as himself, where he performed "Just a Friend".<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Markie was the <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a> on the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> game show <i><a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_Squares" title="Hip Hop Squares">Hip Hop Squares</a></i> between 2017 and 2019, a spin-off of the popular game show <i><a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Squares" title="Hollywood Squares">Hollywood Squares</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> He also rapped alongside singer <a href="/wiki/Ke$ha" class="mw-redirect" title="Ke$ha">Ke$ha</a> on <a href="/wiki/The_Flaming_Lips" title="The Flaming Lips">the Flaming Lips</a> song "<a href="/wiki/2012_(You_Must_Be_Upgraded)" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 (You Must Be Upgraded)">2012 (You Must Be Upgraded)</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> He made an appearance in the season 3 finale of the <a href="/wiki/ABC_Studios" class="mw-redirect" title="ABC Studios">ABC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Black-ish" title="Black-ish">Black-ish</a></i> in 2017. He performed a personal version of the song "Just a Friend", in which he added the names of the characters.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2020s">2020s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2020s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2020, Markie started hosting a radio show on <a href="/wiki/SiriusXM" class="mw-redirect" title="SiriusXM">SiriusXM</a> on <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>'s Rock the Bells Radio (channel 43) every weekday from 1:00 to 4:00 EST.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1991, Biz Markie was sued for copyright infringement by Gilbert O'Sullivan. Judge Duffy ordered the rapper to pay $250,000 in damages.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2022, Markie's widow, Tara Hall, sued Jennifer Izumi, his former business manager for damages and control of Biz Markie Inc. over widespread alleged intellectual property violations, including claims the manager stole song royalties and sold unauthorized merchandise.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Illness_and_death">Illness and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Illness and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In April 2020, Markie was hospitalized due to severe complications from <a href="/wiki/Type_2_diabetes" title="Type 2 diabetes">type 2 diabetes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AJC_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AJC-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> In December 2020, it was reported that Markie was staying in a rehabilitation facility as a result of a <a href="/wiki/Stroke" title="Stroke">stroke</a> he had suffered after going into a <a href="/wiki/Diabetic_coma" title="Diabetic coma">diabetic coma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-missing_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-missing-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 1, 2021, rumors of his death circulated on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>. His representative told <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>, "The news of Biz Markie's death is not true, Biz is still under medical care, surrounded by professionals who are working hard to provide the best healthcare possible."<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Markie died at a Baltimore hospital 15 days later on July 16, at age 57.<sup id="cite_ref-variety.com_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety.com-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> His wife, Tara Hall, held his hand as he took his last breath.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> He was buried at Pinelawn Memorial Park and Arboreum in <a href="/wiki/Farmingdale,_New_York" title="Farmingdale, New York">Farmingdale, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biz_Markie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Upon news of his death, many celebrities and figures expressed their sympathies. His wife, Tara Hall, was sent condolences from <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">President Barack Obama</a> and his wife <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Obama" title="Michelle Obama">Michelle Obama</a>. His funeral in <a href="/wiki/Patchogue,_New_York" title="Patchogue, New York">Patchogue, NY</a> was attended by friends and family, including the likes of <a href="/wiki/Ice-T" title="Ice-T">Ice T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montell_Jordan" title="Montell Jordan">Montell Jordan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Al_B._Sure!" title="Al B. Sure!">Al B Sure</a>. His eulogy was read by <a href="/wiki/Al_Sharpton" title="Al Sharpton">Reverend Al Sharpton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2022, <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Global" title="Paramount Global">Paramount Global</a> announced that <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> in conjunction with <a href="/wiki/Mass_Appeal_(media)" title="Mass Appeal (media)">Mass Appeal</a> would be releasing a documentary on Biz Markie's life.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> The documentary, <a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Up_in_the_Biz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="All Up in the Biz (page does not exist)"><i>All Up in the Biz</i></a>, was directed by <a href="/wiki/Sacha_Jenkins" title="Sacha Jenkins">Sacha Jenkins</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> The movie features reenactments, interviews, and stock footage. Featured in the documentary are <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rakim" title="Rakim">Rakim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doug_E._Fresh" title="Doug E. Fresh">Doug E Fresh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon" title="Nick Cannon">Nick Cannon</a>,<a href="/wiki/Tracy_Morgan" title="Tracy Morgan">Tracy Morgan</a>, his wife <a href="/wiki/Tara_Hall_(Davis)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tara Hall (Davis)">Tara Hall</a> and more.<sup id="cite_ref-Ivey_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ivey-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> The score was produced by Markie's friend <a href="/wiki/Prince_Paul_(producer)" title="Prince Paul (producer)">Prince Paul</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ivey_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ivey-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>  In June 2023, Hall announced the Just A Friend Foundation and confirmed the documentary, <i>All Up In The Biz</i>, was premiering at the <a href="/wiki/Tribeca_Festival" title="Tribeca Festival">Tribeca Film Festival</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ivey_54-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ivey-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The original vinyl from Gilbert O'Sullivan's album 'Back to Front', on which Biz Markie sampled 'Alone Again (Naturally)', can still be found today at the <a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%A9diath%C3%A8que_musicale_de_Paris" class="extiw" title="fr:Médiathèque musicale de Paris">Médiathèque Musicale de Paris</a>, a specialized music library located in Paris. Biz Markie visited the library during a trip to Paris in 1991, in search of samples for his album. At the time, the establishment was renowned among American and French hip-hop artists for its extensive collection of vinyl records available for self-service access.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
