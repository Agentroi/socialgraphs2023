					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American rapper</div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; font-size: 125%;"><div class="">EX-PLICIT LINEZ</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Brandon Jermaine Yun</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Also known as</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">EXP</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data">30 January 1988<span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="dtstart bday">1988-01-30</span>)</span> (age&#160;<span class="currentage"></span>35)<br /><a href="/wiki/Bronx" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronx">Bronx</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast hip hop</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role"><a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">Rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radio_personality" title="Radio personality">Radio personality</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">1996–Present</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data">OTM Entertainment</td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Brandon Jermaine Yun</b>, better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>EXP</b> (pronounced "e-ex-pee"), formerly <b>EX-PLICIT LINEZ</b>, is an  American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> and former <a href="/wiki/Radio_personality" title="Radio personality">radio personality</a> for New York Korean radio <i>NYKR 1660 AM</i>, a mutual protégé of <a href="/wiki/Drunken_Tiger" title="Drunken Tiger">Drunken Tiger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hyun_Do" class="mw-redirect" title="Lee Hyun Do">Lee Hyun Do</a> a.k.a. D.O. EXP first appeared on the underground rap scene in The Bronx in the mid 1990s on a DJ Platinum's mixtape series, <i>Hate in Da Blood</i>. He is one of the first generation of Asian American rappers to be involved in the Hip-Hop scene along with Jamez, John "shogunna" Chee and <a href="/wiki/Mountain_Brothers" title="Mountain Brothers">Mountain Brothers</a>. He is also known as one of the first Korean rappers to mix Korean and English words to rhyme in the song. He is of Korean descent.
</p><p>Aside from releasing multiple mixtapes that gained heavy rotations in college radio stations and internet radios, EXP has gained notoriety for involvement in feuds with other Korean artists.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ex-plicit_linez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He became a member of <b>The Movement</b> in 2001. In 2007 he released his first United States radio single, <i>Like That</i>, which was produced by former member of (<a href="/wiki/Deux_(band)" title="Deux (band)">DEUX</a>), <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hyun_Do" class="mw-redirect" title="Lee Hyun Do">Lee Hyun Do</a> a.k.a. D.O. The single received spins throughout <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> radio stations. In addition to his solo works, EXP made numerous guest appearances internationally throughout Japan,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> China, France and <a href="/wiki/Philippine" class="mw-redirect" title="Philippine">Philippine</a>.
</p><p>EXP's Supreme Hustle set a record for most downloaded Hip Hop album in Chinese music sites in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, prior to releasing his 7th album, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebirth_of_YSJ&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rebirth of YSJ (page does not exist)">Rebirth of YSJ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yoo_Seung-jun" title="Yoo Seung-jun">Yoo Seung-jun</a> showed interest in working with EXP, whom expressed his interest in making music in China.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>EXP was briefly signed to <a href="/wiki/Tiger_JK" title="Tiger JK">Tiger JK</a>'s Jungle Entertainment in 2008 and Warner Music Korea under D.O from Deux. In 2010, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hyun_Do" class="mw-redirect" title="Lee Hyun Do">Lee Hyun Do</a> a.k.a. D.O. announced that he was producing for EXP.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He also worked with K-Pop producer Seung Wook Yang (Producer of <a href="/wiki/Click-B" title="Click-B">Click-B</a>'s Oh Jong Hyuk and ezllife)
</p><p>In December 2010, EXP became Urban A&amp;R at <a href="/wiki/S.M._Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="S.M. Entertainment">S.M. Entertainment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(Korea)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mnet (Korea)">Mnet (Korea)</a> affiliate, MarCan Entertainment. He left the company due to mutual Differences.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>EXP recorded an entrance song "Baki's Theme" for Korean MMA Fighter Won sik "Parky" Park <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> for his bout against Kosuke Umeda. Park lost the bout via Unanimous Decision.
</p><p>In 2014, EXP coached and prepared <a href="/wiki/Sang_A_Yim-Propp" class="mw-redirect" title="Sang A Yim-Propp">Sang A Yim-Propp</a> for her performance on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a> Channel's Thanksgiving special show "Yul chang Club Some-Sing".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Movement">The Movement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ex-plicit_linez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Movement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>EXP is part of <b>The Movement</b>, which is a hip hop collective formed by Korean American hip hop group Drunken Tiger and Dynamic Duo/CB Mass.

<p>In 2009, EXP supported and performed for Relay for Life (American Cancer Society) event in East Village, New York.
</p><p>EXP is a fan of <a href="/wiki/Mixed_Martial_Arts" class="mw-redirect" title="Mixed Martial Arts">Mixed Martial Arts</a>. He is a practitioner of Russian Martial Arts <a href="/wiki/Sambo_(martial_art)" title="Sambo (martial art)">Sambo (martial art)</a> and holds 5th Dan (Godan) Black belt in <a href="/wiki/Aikido" title="Aikido">Aikido</a>.
</p><p>In July 2014, EXP announced his involvement in upcoming horror movie <i>Vamp Bikers DOS</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-imdb.com_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imdb.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The movie also featured <a href="/wiki/Melle_Mel" title="Melle Mel">Melle Mel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_Caz" title="Grandmaster Caz">Grandmaster Caz</a>.
</p><p>In October 2018, EXP announced his acting appearance in <i>Amazon Original</i> produced <i>Japanese Borscht</i> <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Remake with distribution by <i>Sony</i>. He also appears on the original soundtrack for the film.
</p><p>In November 2020, EXP launched a clothing company, <i>Thick Bad Wolf</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Thickbadwolf.com_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thickbadwolf.com-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>EXP also has hinted a return to the music scene from long hiatus.
</p>
