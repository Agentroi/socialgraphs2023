<p><b>Duane Stacy Bridgeford</b>, better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>NOE</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> recording artist from <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>. He is best known for his affiliation with <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a> and his involvement in <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/ByrdGang" class="mw-redirect" title="ByrdGang">ByrdGang</a>. He was featured on most of the songs for <i><a href="/wiki/M.O.B.:_The_Album" title="M.O.B.: The Album">M.O.B.: The Album</a></i> (2008) and was a prominent writer and <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">featured artist</a> on Jim Jones' <i><a href="/wiki/Pray_IV_Reign" title="Pray IV Reign">Pray IV Reign</a></i> (2009). His song, “Done Did It,” is licensed to <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>’s number one crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Detroit_1-8-7" title="Detroit 1-8-7">Detroit 1-8-7</a></i> for the first episode, which was watched by 13 million viewers.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NOE_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings">2005: Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NOE_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2005: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bridgeford was born and raised in the inner city of <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>. NOE's early adult years were engulfed in the violence and dilapidated neighborhoods that continue to blanket Baltimore City. During a phone interview while at a New York recording studio, Bridgeford wasn't very candid about growing up in East Baltimore. He opts not to mention the name of his high school, and sums up his criminal history as "two arrests, one was for drugs, one was for a gun violation, all in all I did about three years." He does mention that he regrets not being closer to his family, and looks at that rift as the reason he decided to express himself through poetry. In 2002 Bridgeford relocated from Baltimore to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in search of a <a href="/wiki/Recording_contract" title="Recording contract">record deal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BaltimoreCityPaper_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BaltimoreCityPaper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After being rejected by <a href="/wiki/A%26R" class="mw-redirect" title="A&amp;R">A&amp;R</a> men throughout the business for sounding like <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, Bridgeford's business partner Nicole "Nicety" Chaplin, who worked with <a href="/wiki/Dipset" class="mw-redirect" title="Dipset">Dipset</a> President <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a> while he was still signed to Jay-Z's <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella" class="mw-redirect" title="Roc-A-Fella">Roc-A-Fella</a> label, introduced the two. Chaplin also introduced Bridgeford to Conrad Dimanche, senior vice president at <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy Records</a>, who was impressed with his work but not interested in signing a new act: "[Dimanche] just told me to start writing, and it just so happened that <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a> was working on the Danity Kane project," Bridgeford says. "Writing is where the money is at. There's less scrutiny. I don't have to worry about sounding like anybody or whatever." He went on to <a href="/wiki/Ghostwriter" title="Ghostwriter">write</a> Diddy's lyrics for <a href="/wiki/Danity_Kane" title="Danity Kane">Danity Kane</a>'s debut single "<a href="/wiki/Show_Stopper_(Danity_Kane_song)" title="Show Stopper (Danity Kane song)">Show Stopper</a>". Working with Diddy has also given him a chance to work side by side with Jay-Z. "It was a real professional situation," Bridgeford says. "He was doing the hook on a song that ended up not being on the Danity Kane album, and I had to come through to write the lyrics."<sup id="cite_ref-BaltimoreCityPaper_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BaltimoreCityPaper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006-2009:_ByrdGang">2006-2009: ByrdGang</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NOE_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006-2009: ByrdGang">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006 <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jones</a> launched <a href="/wiki/ByrdGang_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="ByrdGang Records">ByrdGang Records</a> and formed hip hop collective ByrdGang along with Mel Matrix, NOE, <a href="/wiki/Freekey_Zeekey" class="mw-redirect" title="Freekey Zeekey">Freekey Zeekey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chink_Santana" title="Chink Santana">Chink Santana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ByrdGang_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ByrdGang-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Jones' first featured Bridgeford on his 2008 <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Harlem%27s_American_Gangster" title="Harlem&#39;s American Gangster">Harlem's American Gangster</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Asylum_Records" title="Asylum Records">Asylum Records</a> began distributing ByrdGang Records in 2008 where the label released their first album under Asylum &amp; ByrdGang titled <i><a href="/wiki/M.O.B.:_The_Album" title="M.O.B.: The Album">M.O.B.: The Album</a></i>. NOE is featured on 13 tracks of the 18 tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-M.O.B:The_Album_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-M.O.B:The_Album-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  
NOE's lyrics and song writing abilities are also displayed on over 90 percent of the Byrdgang Album. The album debuts at # 5 on the Billboard Rap Charts. <a href="/wiki/M.O.B.:_The_Album" title="M.O.B.: The Album">M.O.B.: The Album</a> earns a rating of XL (equivalent to 4 out of 5 stars) based on the XXL Magazine rating system.
Pray_IV_Reign by Jim Jones', also features NOE. It was released March 24, 2009. The album peaked at #9 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart. On July 8, Jones released a promotional single entitled "<a href="/wiki/The_Good_Stuff" title="The Good Stuff">The Good Stuff</a>" featuring NOE.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Good_Stuff_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Good_Stuff-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>2008 featured Bridgeford perform a freestyle on BET's Rap City "Da Basement". <a href="/wiki/Vibe_magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Vibe magazine">VIBE Magazine</a> highlights the song by NOE called "Headlights". NOE is featured in the 11th Anniversary <a href="/wiki/Xxl_magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Xxl magazine">XXL Magazine</a> Issue representing Baltimore, MD in the Show &amp; Prove section. The <a href="/wiki/The_Fader" title="The Fader">FADER Magazine</a> music review featured the single "Headlights". NOE is featured in the 11th Anniversary <a href="/wiki/Xxl_magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Xxl magazine">XXL Magazine</a> Issue representing Baltimore, MD in the Show &amp; Prove section.
2008 The Byrdgang<sup id="cite_ref-prize_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prize-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>/ShadyVille DJ's "NOE MORE&#160;?'S" Mixtape, a full length mixtape featuring NOE, is released. It reportedly rakes in a whopping 40,000 listens in one day according to DatPiff.com within hours of its release.  "NOE MORE&#160;?'s" features songs such as "Every Night" produced by Afro Keys, "Lime Light" produced by Uncle Ruck and "Headlights" produced by Jay Funk.
</p><p>In 2009 Bridgeford was ultimately a writer and featured guest on <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Pray_IV_Reign" title="Pray IV Reign">Pray IV Reign</a></i>. He was also featured on the critically acclaimed <a href="/wiki/Dame_Dash" class="mw-redirect" title="Dame Dash">Dame Dash</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Keys" title="The Black Keys">the Black Keys</a> compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/Blakroc" title="Blakroc">Blakroc</a></i>. His composition "Done Did It" featuring <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Wray" title="Nicole Wray">Nicole Wray</a> is licensed to number one TV shows such as <a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">Ellen</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010-present:_NOE_Remorse">2010-present: <i>NOE Remorse</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NOE_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010-present: NOE Remorse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"Done Did It" is licensed to ABC's number one crime drama "Detroit 187" for the first episode. The first episode was seen in over 13 million homes. Switzerland recording artist Sempe, featured NOE in the song and video for his track entitled, “Angels” with Paula Bright on the chorus. NOE releases his first single called "We City Boyz" from the upcoming summer album release entitled NOE Remorse with his marketing campaign called  NOE the Brand via digital release.
</p>
