Reed</b> (born April 22, 1966),<sup id="cite_ref-auto_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Bahamadia</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip-hop</a> artist and singer. Bahamadia released her debut <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kollage_(album)" title="Kollage (album)">Kollage</a></i>, in 1996,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> followed by the independently released <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> <i><a href="/wiki/BB_Queen" title="BB Queen">BB Queen</a></i> in 2000. She then released a full-length album, <i>Good Rap Music</i>, in 2005. Bahamadia has also released the singles “Dialed Up Vol. 1” (in 2013), “Here” (in 2015), and “Dialed Up Vol. 2” (in 2018).
</p><p>Bahamadia has been a featured artist on tracks by musicians including <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">The Roots</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jedi_Mind_Tricks" title="Jedi Mind Tricks">Jedi Mind Tricks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morcheeba" title="Morcheeba">Morcheeba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guru_(rapper)" title="Guru (rapper)">Guru</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Towa_Tei" title="Towa Tei">Towa Tei</a>, among others.
</p><p>In November 2016, Bahamadia appeared as a guest client on season 15 of <i><a href="/wiki/Project_Runway" title="Project Runway">Project Runway</a></i>, in which her son, Mah-Jing Wong, was a contestant. His father is part Chinese, part African-American; he was named after his great-grandfather.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
