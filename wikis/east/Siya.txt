<p><b>Michele Andrea Sherman</b> (born March 23, 1987), better known by her stage name <b>Siya</b>, is an American rapper. In 2012, she signed with <a href="/wiki/Tank_(American_singer)" title="Tank (American singer)">Tank</a>'s R&amp;B Money label imprint.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She starred in the Oxygen reality TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Sisterhood_of_Hip_Hop" title="Sisterhood of Hip Hop">Sisterhood of Hip Hop</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Siya has collaborated with artists such as <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sage_the_Gemini" title="Sage the Gemini">Sage the Gemini</a> and Kirko Bangz. Siya has performed alongside artists such as <a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a> at the world famous B.B. Kings, as well as opened up for <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a> during a concert at Nassau Coliseum in Long Island, New York.  Currently starring in the BET Plus show Angel.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Siya was born in Barstow, California to an African American father and a Puerto Rican mother, and has three sisters.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Due to her mother's drug abuse and her incarcerated father, Siya was sent to live with her grandmother who raised her in the Eleanor Roosevelt <a href="/wiki/Housing_projects" class="mw-redirect" title="Housing projects">housing projects</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bedford%E2%80%93Stuyvesant,_Brooklyn" title="Bedford–Stuyvesant, Brooklyn">Bedford–Stuyvesant, Brooklyn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-VV_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VV-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Siya began rapping at the age of seven and entered into the music industry at the age of 12. Siya's childhood influence was the rap group called <a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs-n-Harmony" title="Bone Thugs-n-Harmony">Bone Thugs-n-Harmony</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> She states&#160;: "What I loved about them was how melodic their music was and that they tried different shit."<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Music became an outlet for Siya to comprehend and deal with the things she was exposed to and was feeling. After moving to Atlanta to pursue her music career in her mid 20s she built a rep and fan base for herself and caught the attention of well known artists and major labels. Siya faced a lot of obstacles throughout her early time in the industry due to her being one of the first openly gay female rappers. While some people in the industry were afraid of how an openly gay female rapper would fit into the genre, Siya remained determined and refused to compromise who she was and stayed true to her own voice and style.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>R&amp;B legend <a href="/wiki/Tank_(American_singer)" title="Tank (American singer)">Tank</a> soon found her and directly messaged Siya on Twitter and from there she was signed to <a href="/wiki/Tank_(American_singer)" title="Tank (American singer)">Tank</a>'s label R&amp;B Money LLC. Siya released her singles&#160;: "I'm Gone", "Smoke Drink", and <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> <i>Elevator Dreams</i> in 2011.
</p><p>Siya released her singles "One Hunnid" and "Real MVP" in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> She released a <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> titled <i>Better Late Than Never,</i> and <i>What Never Happened</i>  in 2015. She went on to release her debut studio album <i>SIYAvsSIYA,</i> on December 9, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The album included collaborations with artists such as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashley_Rose&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ashley Rose (page does not exist)">Ashley Rose</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jake%26Papa" class="mw-redirect" title="Jake&amp;Papa">Jake&amp;Papa</a>, Lyric Wright and <a href="/wiki/Kreesha_Turner" title="Kreesha Turner">Kreesha Turner</a> with the debut single being "New York."
</p><p>On February 6, 2017, Siya released a song titled "Front Door", in promotion for her six song <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP), <i>Commitment.</i><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The EP was released on February 10, 2017, under <a href="/wiki/Tank_(American_singer)" title="Tank (American singer)">Tank</a>'s label imprint, R&amp;B Money LLC.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The EP features the single "Don't You (Say Yes)" featuring Tank.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In 2017 she released "Houston Girls" ft <a href="/wiki/Kirko_Bangz" title="Kirko Bangz">Kirko Bangz</a> &amp; "Hot Girl" with her EP <i>383- For Roosevelt</i>.  In 2018 she released "No Race" and "Circle Watching" as a preview for her next EP <i>Mad Energy</i>  expected to drop fall 2018.
</p>
