<p><b>Kevin Keaton</b> (born August 26, 1963), professionally known by his stage name <b>Special K</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Old-school_hip_hop" title="Old-school hip hop">old-school hip hop</a> <a href="/wiki/Master_of_ceremonies" title="Master of ceremonies">emcee</a> from the <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">Bronx, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was prominent in the late 1970s, throughout the 1980s and early 1990s, and best known as member of the <a href="/wiki/Treacherous_Three" title="Treacherous Three">Treacherous Three</a>. He is the younger brother of <a href="/wiki/T_La_Rock" title="T La Rock">T La Rock</a> and the older brother of rapper Tony Tone (later known as T.O.N.E.-z) who was a member of the Hip Hop duo Style.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Special_K_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Special_K_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the late 1970s, he met <a href="/wiki/Kool_Moe_Dee" title="Kool Moe Dee">Kool Moe Dee</a> and DJ Easy Lee. Special K joined with other rappers Kool Moe Dee and L.A. Sunshine as well as DJ Easy Lee to form the influential old school hip hop group the <a href="/wiki/Treacherous_Three" title="Treacherous Three">Treacherous Three</a>. It is with the Treacherous Three that Special K performed his <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a>. In 1981, they moved to <a href="/wiki/Sugar_Hill_Records_(hip_hop_label)" title="Sugar Hill Records (hip hop label)">Sugar Hill Records</a> along with another Enjoy Records act <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_Flash_and_the_Furious_Five" title="Grandmaster Flash and the Furious Five">Grandmaster Flash and the Furious Five</a>. The Treacherous Three became well known for their singles "Feel the Heartbeat" and "Whip It". They released their debut full-length album and were featured in the 1984 film the <a href="/wiki/Breakdancing" title="Breakdancing">breakdance</a> cult-movie <i><a href="/wiki/Beat_Street" title="Beat Street">Beat Street</a></i> performing the song "Xmas Rap" with <a href="/wiki/Doug_E._Fresh" title="Doug E. Fresh">Doug E. Fresh</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> but disbanded shortly afterwards with each member pursuing solo careers. Later on during his life he met <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kornel_Misiek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kornel Misiek (page does not exist)">Kornel Misiek</a> who went on to fund his second album which sadly never was released to a larger audience.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Special_K_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Special K issued his debut solo <a href="/wiki/12-inch_single" class="mw-redirect" title="12-inch single">12-inch single</a> on Republic Records in 1987 which was titled "Special-K Is Good", and another one 12" in 1998 titled "Knockout" via PKO Records. In 1990 he made his guest appearance on <a href="/wiki/Boogie_Down_Productions" title="Boogie Down Productions">Boogie Down Productions</a>' track "Original Lyrics" from <i><a href="/wiki/Edutainment_(album)" title="Edutainment (album)">Edutainment</a></i>, and in 1991 he co-produced <a href="/wiki/Ed_O.G." title="Ed O.G.">Ed O.G.</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Life_of_a_Kid_in_the_Ghetto" title="Life of a Kid in the Ghetto">Life of a Kid in the Ghetto</a></i> LP. The Treacherous Three came back together with their reunion sophomore album <i><a href="/wiki/Old_School_Flava" title="Old School Flava">Old School Flava</a></i> in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
