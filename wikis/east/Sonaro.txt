<p><b>Christopher Darnell Cook</b> (born October 5, 1988), professionally known as <b>Sonaro</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Music_producer" class="mw-redirect" title="Music producer">music producer</a> from <a href="/wiki/Far_Rockaway,_Queens" title="Far Rockaway, Queens">Far Rockaway</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. He is perhaps best known for producing songs for Brooklyn-bred rapper <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>. He has also produced for several other rappers, such as <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rich_Homie_Quan" title="Rich Homie Quan">Jeezy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flipp_Dinero" title="Flipp Dinero">Flipp Dinero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Cain_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Paul Cain (musician) (page does not exist)">Paul Cain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones" title="Jim Jones">Jim Jones</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career_beginnings">Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sonaro was born in <a href="/wiki/Far_Rockaway,_Queens" title="Far Rockaway, Queens">Far Rockaway</a> and raised in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>. Interested in <a href="/wiki/Music_production" class="mw-redirect" title="Music production">music production</a> from an early age, Sonaro became an in-house producer for <a href="/wiki/Needlz" title="Needlz">Needlz</a> where he learned to craft his sound.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> During this time, Sonaro landed his first placement on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stack_Bundles&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stack Bundles (page does not exist)">Stack Bundles</a> <i>Biddin' War Vol 2.</i> hosted by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Clue" title="DJ Clue">DJ Clue</a>, producing the song "Rockin' it” featuring <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones" title="Jim Jones">Jim Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Max_B" title="Max B">Max B</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Sonaro soon followed this up with "Thang On Me" from <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a>’s <i><a href="/wiki/Ms._G.O.A.T." title="Ms. G.O.A.T.">Ms. G.O.A.T.</a></i> project, which featured <a href="/wiki/Maino_(rapper)" title="Maino (rapper)">Maino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mister_Cee" title="Mister Cee">Mister Cee</a>, and Sha Dash.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_Breakthrough">Commercial Breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial Breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 5, 2010, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a> released the <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> <i>There Is No Competition 2: The Funeral Service</i> which featured the Sonaro produced street single "Body Ya."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The song was quickly added to <a href="/wiki/WQHT" title="WQHT">Hot 97</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam">Def Jam</a> officially released the song on <i><a href="/wiki/There_Is_No_Competition_2:_The_Grieving_Music_EP" title="There Is No Competition 2: The Grieving Music EP">There Is No Competition 2: The Grieving Music EP</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> featured additionally production from Sonaro, and new contributions from <a href="/wiki/Lex_Luger" title="Lex Luger">Lex Luger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Leslie" title="Ryan Leslie">Ryan Leslie</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jahlil_Beats" title="Jahlil Beats">Jahlil Beats</a>.
</p><p>Later work with <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a> from this period includes "<a href="/wiki/The_S.O.U.L._Tape" class="mw-redirect" title="The S.O.U.L. Tape">Leaving You</a>," "<a href="/wiki/There_Is_No_Competition_3:_Death_Comes_in_3%27s" title="There Is No Competition 3: Death Comes in 3&#39;s">Swag Champ</a>,"<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/There_Is_No_Competition_3:_Death_Comes_in_3%27s" title="There Is No Competition 3: Death Comes in 3&#39;s">B.E.T.</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/The_S.O.U.L._Tape_2" title="The S.O.U.L. Tape 2">Want You Back</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Teyana_Taylor" title="Teyana Taylor">Teyana Taylor</a>. Sonaro also contributed production to projects by <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones" title="Jim Jones">Jim Jones</a>, Nya Lee, <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>, and <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, and licensed several instrumentals for the TV show <a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop">Love &amp; Hip Hop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_S.O.U.L._Tape_3"><i>The S.O.U.L. Tape 3</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The S.O.U.L. Tape 3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 25, 2013 <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a> released <i>The S.O.U.L. Tape 3</i> which featured Sonaro on three songs; "Playa," "Situationships" featuring <a href="/wiki/Mack_Wilds" class="mw-redirect" title="Mack Wilds">Mack Wilds</a>, and "Cuffin' Season."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> "Cuffin' Season" was instantly added to radio and received major press from <a href="/wiki/WQHT" title="WQHT">Hot 97</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Funkmaster_Flex" title="Funkmaster Flex">Funkmaster Flex</a> who called it "the hottest record in the Big Apple."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The official video for "Cuffin' Season"  premiered on <a href="/wiki/Revolt_(TV_network)" title="Revolt (TV network)">REVOLT</a> on April 25, 2014, and features Sonaro performing the chorus alongside Fabolous.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Summertime_Shootout"><i>Summertime Shootout</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sonaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Summertime Shootout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 26, 2015 <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a> released <i><a href="/wiki/Summertime_Shootout" class="mw-redirect" title="Summertime Shootout">Summertime Shootout</a>.</i> The project contains four songs produced by Sonaro; "For The Summer," "Vanilla" featuring <a href="/wiki/Rich_Homie_Quan" title="Rich Homie Quan">Rich Homie Quan</a>, "Motivation," and "The Plug."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 27, the <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for the Sonaro produced "The Plug" was released exclusively to <a href="/wiki/World_Star_Hip_Hop" class="mw-redirect" title="World Star Hip Hop">World Star Hip Hop</a> and gained over 1,800,000 views in less one day.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
