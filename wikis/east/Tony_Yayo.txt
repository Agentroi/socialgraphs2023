<p><b>Marvin Bernard</b> (born March 31, 1978),<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Tony Yayo</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>. He was best known as a member of <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> group he formed with his childhood friends, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>. Yayo released his debut, and to date, only studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Thoughts_of_a_Predicate_Felon" title="Thoughts of a Predicate Felon">Thoughts of a Predicate Felon</a></i>, on August 30, 2005, which debuted at number two on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.
</p><p>His stage name is derived from the 1983 film, <i><a href="/wiki/Scarface_(1983_film)" title="Scarface (1983 film)">Scarface</a></i>, referencing the main character <a href="/wiki/Tony_Montana" title="Tony Montana">Tony Montana</a>, and Yayo, the slang word for <a href="/wiki/Cocaine" title="Cocaine">cocaine</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Yayo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marvin Bernard was born on March 31, 1978, in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica,_Queens" title="Jamaica, Queens">Jamaica</a>, a neighborhood in <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, one of the five <a href="/wiki/Boroughs_of_New_York_City" title="Boroughs of New York City">boroughs</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. His parents were <a href="/wiki/Haitian_Americans" title="Haitian Americans">Haitian</a> immigrants; he was the youngest of three children.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At age fifteen, he dropped out of school in the tenth grade and dealt drugs to support his family. A year later, his parents divorced. As a child, he met his lifelong friends, <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a> and <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Yayo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="G-Unit">G-Unit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Yayo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: G-Unit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After his release from prison on drug charges,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> the trio formed the group, <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>, and began working on music together. The first underground mixtape featuring Bernard (who now went under the stage name Tony Yayo), was <i>134 All Starz</i>, released in late 2000, referencing his old street he grew up on. However, G-Unit went on a hiatus after 50 Cent was shot nine times and released from <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Two years later, G-Unit continued releasing a number of mixtapes in the early 2000s, including 2002's <i><a href="/wiki/50_Cent_is_the_Future" class="mw-redirect" title="50 Cent is the Future">50 Cent is the Future</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/No_Mercy,_No_Fear" title="No Mercy, No Fear">No Mercy, No Fear</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/God%27s_Plan_(album)" title="God&#39;s Plan (album)">God's Plan</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While Yayo was incarcerated in 2003, his comrade 50 Cent was granted his own record label, <a href="/wiki/G-Unit_Records" title="G-Unit Records">G-Unit Records</a> after the success of his debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Rich_or_Die_Tryin%27" title="Get Rich or Die Tryin&#39;">Get Rich or Die Tryin'</a></i>, which was released that February; Yayo appears on the album's track, "Like My Style". 50 immediately signed G-Unit members Yayo and Lloyd Banks to the label, and eventually <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a> was signed and added to the group as the official fourth member; Buck was later retraced as Yayo's temporary replacement due to his incarceration.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> From 2003 to 2010, most of G-Unit's artists (including Yayo) were distributed by <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>. G-Unit released their debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Beg_for_Mercy" title="Beg for Mercy">Beg for Mercy</a></i>, on November 14, 2003. It went on to sell over four million copies in the United States alone.<sup id="cite_ref-Beg_for_Mercy_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beg_for_Mercy-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The album was released during which time Yayo was still finishing a prison sentence on various charges, but he was still featured on the album and his face was also shown on the album cover. G-Unit's sophomore and final album, <i><a href="/wiki/T%C2%B7O%C2%B7S_(Terminate_on_Sight)" title="T·O·S (Terminate on Sight)">T.O.S: Terminate on Sight</a></i>, was released on July 31, 2008, and went on to sell 102,000 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to issues between Yayo, 50 and Young Buck, G-Unit went under in 2014, but later reunited as a quintet with all original four members and a newly added <a href="/wiki/Kidd_Kidd" title="Kidd Kidd">Kidd Kidd</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> They only released two EPs, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Beauty_of_Independence" title="The Beauty of Independence">The Beauty of Independence</a></i> (2014), and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Beast_Is_G_Unit" title="The Beast Is G Unit">The Beast Is G Unit</a></i> (2015), before 50 Cent publicly dissolved the group again in 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Yayo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:2005_1104hoopsmania0016.JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/2005_1104hoopsmania0016.JPG/220px-2005_1104hoopsmania0016.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/2005_1104hoopsmania0016.JPG/330px-2005_1104hoopsmania0016.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/2005_1104hoopsmania0016.JPG/440px-2005_1104hoopsmania0016.JPG 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a><figcaption>Tony Yayo performing at Villanova Hoops Mania in 2005</figcaption></figure>
<p>His career as a rapper was halted by his ongoing jail sentence at the time throughout early 2003 until mid-2004. With Yayo incarcerated, G-Unit and close friend <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> began a campaign called "Free Yayo". He said that he was unaware of the attention the campaign was getting until he saw Eminem wearing a "Free Yayo" shirt on stage at the <a href="/wiki/45th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="45th Annual Grammy Awards">2003 Grammy Awards</a>, where he performed his <a href="/wiki/8_Mile_(film)" title="8 Mile (film)"><i>8 Mile</i></a> theme song, "<a href="/wiki/Lose_Yourself" title="Lose Yourself">Lose Yourself</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Though his appearances on numerous mixtapes throughout his jail time announced his return on a street level, it was not until the release of <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>' "Ain't No Click" and its parent album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_for_More" title="The Hunger for More">The Hunger for More</a></i> (2004), which featured Yayo that the latter received widespread recognition. His solo career hype was followed up by his debut single, "<a href="/wiki/So_Seductive" title="So Seductive">So Seductive</a>" featuring 50 Cent,
On August 30, 2005, Tony Yayo released his debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Thoughts_of_a_Predicate_Felon" title="Thoughts of a Predicate Felon">Thoughts of a Predicate Felon</a></i>, debuting at number two on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> behind <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Registration" title="Late Registration">Late Registration</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He also appeared on the track, "Fake Love", which made its way to the <a href="/wiki/Get_Rich_or_Die_Tryin%27_(soundtrack)" title="Get Rich or Die Tryin&#39; (soundtrack)">soundtrack</a> for 50 Cent's acting debut, <a href="/wiki/Get_Rich_or_Die_Tryin%27_(film)" title="Get Rich or Die Tryin&#39; (film)"><i>Get Rich or Die Tryin'</i></a>, which released that November.
</p><p>Since 2007, Tony Yayo has confirmed that work was in progress for his second studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-AHH_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AHH-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> As of 2023, the planned second album has not been released. In 2009, Yayo and Lloyd Banks were released from Interscope due to disagreements between the two members, 50 Cent and their former label's co-founder <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Iovine" title="Jimmy Iovine">Jimmy Iovine</a>.
</p><p>On May 20, 2010, Yayo released the single, "<a href="/wiki/Pass_the_Patron" title="Pass the Patron">Pass the Patron</a>", once again featuring 50 Cent. The single's release was distributed by G-Unit Records and <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI Music Distribution</a> (which is now known today as <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Music_Label_%26_Artist_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="Virgin Music Label &amp; Artist Services">Virgin Music Label &amp; Artist Services</a>). who will both promote and distribute the album.
On March 2, 2011, Yayo released another single called "<a href="/wiki/Haters_(Tony_Yayo_song)" title="Haters (Tony Yayo song)">Haters</a>". The song features 50 Cent, <a href="/wiki/Shawty_Lo" title="Shawty Lo">Shawty Lo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roscoe_Dash" title="Roscoe Dash">Roscoe Dash</a>. The single was released via the This Is 50 website after its premiere on <a href="/wiki/WQHT" title="WQHT">Hot 97</a> by <a href="/wiki/Funkmaster_Flex" title="Funkmaster Flex">Funkmaster Flex</a>. The single rapidly started to gain success on the urban radio charts and soon debuted on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs"><i>Billboard</i> Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop song chart</a> at number one-hundred-twelve.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Yayo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Felony_charges_for_weapon_possession.2C_bail_jumping_and_forged_passport.3B_incarceration_.282002-04.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Felony_charges_for_weapon_possession,_bail_jumping_and_forged_passport;_incarceration_(2002-04)">Felony charges for weapon possession, bail jumping and forged passport; incarceration (2002-04)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Yayo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Felony charges for weapon possession, bail jumping and forged passport; incarceration (2002-04)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Along with 50 Cent and several other affiliates, Yayo was arrested on December 31, 2002, during a New Year's Eve party in <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Manhattan" title="Midtown Manhattan">Midtown Manhattan</a> for first-degree possession of a loaded firearm by a convicted felon. He had a <a href="/wiki/Glock" title="Glock">Glock 20</a> handgun and a <a href="/wiki/Colt_AR-15" title="Colt AR-15">Colt AR-15</a> in the trunk of his car. When police discovered that Yayo had a prior 1996 conviction for drug possession and an outstanding warrant for a previous gun charge, Yayo was sentenced for <a href="/wiki/Bail" title="Bail">bail jumping</a> and spent a year in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Yayo served time in the <a href="/wiki/Lakeview_Shock_Incarceration_Correctional_Facility" title="Lakeview Shock Incarceration Correctional Facility">Lakeview Shock Incarceration Correctional Facility</a> in the village of <a href="/wiki/Brocton,_New_York" title="Brocton, New York">Brocton</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chautauqua_County,_New_York" title="Chautauqua County, New York">Chautauqua County, New York</a>. He was paroled on January 8, 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-NYSDOCS_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYSDOCS-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The next day, he was arrested for possessing a <a href="/wiki/Identity_document_forgery" title="Identity document forgery">forged passport</a> and served time in federal prison until February 24, 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Assault_charge_and_dismissal_.282007-08.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Assault_charge_and_dismissal_(2007-08)">Assault charge and dismissal (2007-08)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Yayo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Assault charge and dismissal (2007-08)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 24, 2007, Tony Yayo and childhood friend, Lowell Fletcher (alias Lodi Mack), were both arrested for the alleged simple assault on fourteen-year-old James Rosemond, Jr. (alias Lil Henchman), who is confirmed to be the son of Czar Entertainment founder and drug trafficker <a href="/wiki/James_Rosemond" title="James Rosemond">James Rosemond</a> (alias Jimmy Henchman). The incident was perceived to have had a connection with Tony Yayo and G-Unit's ongoing dispute with former colleague and Henchman's client at the time, <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> 50 Cent was also accused of approaching the child, along with members of his entourage, and pushing him against a nearby wall. However, he had proven his innocence with video footage showing him at his former <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a> mansion at the time of the alleged incident.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Yayo reportedly slapped the boy several times and yelled at him "fuck Czar Entertainment, nigga!" His co-conspirator, Fletcher, also pistol whipped Rosemond, Jr., and apparently threatened him with the same gun, causing the adolescent child to run off in fear.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tony Yayo made his first court appearance on the morning of April 25, 2007, in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_Eastern_District_of_New_York" title="United States District Court for the Eastern District of New York">United States District Court for the Eastern District of New York</a>. The case was adjourned to July 24. Yayo plead not guilty to all charges. His lawyer has expressed his belief that Yayo is innocent and commented on the situation in an interview with MTV:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>After a thorough investigation, I steadfastly and adamantly state to you that my client [Tony Yayo] did not slap anyone. I am hopeful that when [authorities] complete their investigation, they will come to the same conclusion. Marvin Bernard, as a father, understands the allegations and would never do anything to harm a child.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>A week before his court appearance, on April 18, the house of Yayo's mother was struck with bullets. The attack is believed to be in retaliation for his assault and to be ordered by Jimmy Henchman as vengeance for assaulting his son. Yayo references the attack on the G-Unit song, "Straight Outta Southside" (2008).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 24, 2007, Tony Yayo attended a court hearing regarding his alleged assault. The prosecution offered him a nine-month prison sentence if he pleaded guilty, which he rejected.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Ultimately, prosecutors dropped all charges against him in February 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The mother of Rosemond's son sued him and 50 Cent two months later.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Eventually, his friend Lodi Mack took legal responsibility for the action and pleaded guilty to aggravated assault with a weapon and a gun violated in relation.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He, in November 2007, was sentenced to two years and nine months in prison.
</p>
<h3><span id="Murder_of_close_friend_and_homicide_attempts_by_Jimmy_Henchman_.282009-14.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Murder_of_close_friend_and_homicide_attempts_by_Jimmy_Henchman_(2009-14)">Murder of close friend and homicide attempts by Jimmy Henchman (2009-14)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Yayo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Murder of close friend and homicide attempts by Jimmy Henchman (2009-14)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After Yayo's friend, Lowell "Lodi Mack" Fletcher was paroled in late-September 2009, Fletcher was murdered outside of a grocery store in the New York City borough of <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>. Also, there were numerous murder attempts on the lives of Tony Yayo and 50 Cent ordered by Jimmy Henchman, including an attempt to explode a "bulletproof" limousine with the two inside of it.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> 50 responded by recruiting <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> gang members to place a dead rat on the doormat of Henchman's home in New York.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2014, after a hung jury in a federal trial which charged Rosemond for drug trafficking, murder for hire, conspiracy to commit <a href="/wiki/Homicide" title="Homicide">homicide</a> and other related federal charges in connection to the murder of Yayo's friend, Lodi Mack, and <a href="/wiki/Continuing_Criminal_Enterprise_Statute" title="Continuing Criminal Enterprise Statute">continuing to run his criminal enterprise</a>, he was found guilty of all charges and sentenced to life in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</p>
