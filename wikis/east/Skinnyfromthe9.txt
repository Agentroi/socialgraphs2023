<p><b>David Alex Villegas</b> (born December 19, 1995),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known by his stage name <b>Skinnyfromthe9</b> (formerly stylized <b>$kinnyFromThe9</b>), is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a> and singer from <a href="/wiki/Somerville,_New_Jersey" title="Somerville, New Jersey">Somerville</a>, New Jersey. He first gained widespread attention after the release of his song "Love Blast" in late 2017. He then signed to <a href="/wiki/L.A._Reid" title="L.A. Reid">L.A. Reid</a>'s record label, HITCO, in 2018. He released his debut mixtape, <i>It's An Evil World</i>, in November 2018.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skinnyfromthe9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>David Alex Villegas<sup id="cite_ref-Complex_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was born in <a href="/wiki/Hialeah,_Florida" title="Hialeah, Florida">Hialeah, Florida</a>. He is of mixed Puerto Rican, Colombian, and Dominican descent. He grew up largely in <a href="/wiki/Somerville,_New_Jersey" title="Somerville, New Jersey">Somerville, New Jersey</a>, although he and his family moved around frequently. As a child, his father was imprisoned, and his mother became involved with another man. During Villegas' adolescence, his mother and stepfather engaged in scams and identity fraud until his stepfather was eventually caught and spent time in prison. Villegas also spent time living in homeless shelters and hotels as a youth. He began focusing on <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> as a teenager after his father purchased a mic for him.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLBreak_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBreak-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Noisey_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Noisey-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He adopted the stage name, Skinnyfromthe9, because his nickname was "Skinny" and he was from <a href="/wiki/Area_code_908" title="Area code 908">area code 908</a> (colloquially, "the 9").<sup id="cite_ref-RespectInt_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RespectInt-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skinnyfromthe9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Skinnyfromthe9 began putting his music on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> in 2016. In 2017, he released the songs, "Family Rich" featuring <a href="/wiki/Jay_Critch" title="Jay Critch">Jay Critch</a><sup id="cite_ref-XXLBreak_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBreak-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and "Count It" featuring <a href="/wiki/Stitches_(rapper)" title="Stitches (rapper)">Stitches</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Woke_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Woke-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In late 2017, he released the single, "Love Blast,"<sup id="cite_ref-RespectLove_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RespectLove-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> which would go on to accumulate more than 3.5&#160;million streams on SoundCloud.<sup id="cite_ref-NJAug_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NJAug-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In January 2018, Skinny joined <a href="/wiki/Fetty_Wap" title="Fetty Wap">Fetty Wap</a> on his "FMF Tour."<sup id="cite_ref-XXLBreak_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBreak-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Later that year, it was announced that he had signed to L.A. Reid's new record label, HITCO.<sup id="cite_ref-Vlad_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vlad-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-XXLJump_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLJump-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2018, he released a music video for "Love Blast"<sup id="cite_ref-XXLApr_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLApr-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and also released the single, "Back When I Was Broke."<sup id="cite_ref-Hype_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hype-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The following month, he published music videos for "Back When I Was Broke"<sup id="cite_ref-Noisey_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Noisey-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and "Pink Choppas."<sup id="cite_ref-XXLPink_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLPink-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Rappers <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russ_(rapper)" title="Russ (rapper)">Russ</a> made cameos in the former video.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLBack_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBack-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Later that month, Skinny also released the songs, "Space" and "I Drip."<sup id="cite_ref-XXLSpace_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLSpace-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Beginning in June 2018, Skinny was supposed to join rapper, 24hrs, on his "3200 Lenox Rd." tour, but 24hrs was hospitalized in June, and the tour was canceled.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLSpace_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLSpace-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HNHH24_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HNHH24-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In July 2018, Skinny made a cameo appearance in the video for <a href="/wiki/PnB_Rock" title="PnB Rock">PnB Rock</a>'s "London."<sup id="cite_ref-XXLJul_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLJul-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The following month, he released the single, "Jump Out That," featuring PnB Rock.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLJump_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLJump-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Later in August, Skinny was arrested on suspicion of simple assault and theft.<sup id="cite_ref-MCJ01_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCJ01-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MCJ03_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCJ03-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2018, while serving time in jail, he released a freestyle rap along with two more songs, "Alone" and "Songs About You."<sup id="cite_ref-XXLOct_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLOct-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He was released from jail in November 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLRelease_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLRelease-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Soon after his release, he published the music video for his song, "Just Left Jail."<sup id="cite_ref-Wave_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wave-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> His debut mixtape, <i>It's An Evil World</i>, was released on November 30, 2018, through HITCO. It featured guest appearances from Fetty Wap, PnB Rock, and <a href="/wiki/Kap_G" title="Kap G">Kap G</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLEvil_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLEvil-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HNHHDec_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HNHHDec-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> going into 2020 skinnyfromthe9 did a song with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hitta_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hitta Castro (page does not exist)">Hitta Castro</a> that blow up called "In My Feelings2.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skinnyfromthe9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 2014, Skinnyfromthe9 has been charged with several minor offenses such as <a href="/wiki/Shoplifting" title="Shoplifting">shoplifting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reckless_driving" title="Reckless driving">reckless driving</a>. Many of those charges were later dropped.<sup id="cite_ref-NJJun_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NJJun-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2018, Skinny, his father, his brother, and a fourth defendant were arrested and charged with <a href="/wiki/Kidnapping" title="Kidnapping">kidnapping</a> and aggravated assault in relation to an incident in <a href="/wiki/Somerset_County,_New_Jersey" title="Somerset County, New Jersey">Somerset County, New Jersey</a> in which the four men were accused of luring another man into a vehicle and striking him.<sup id="cite_ref-MCJ01_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCJ01-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In September, the charges for Skinny were downgraded to luring an adult, simple assault, and theft after he took a <a href="/wiki/Plea_deal" class="mw-redirect" title="Plea deal">plea deal</a> that also allowed him to avoid prison time in exchange for a <a href="/wiki/Probation" title="Probation">probation</a> sentence. Skinny has maintained his innocence, claiming that the person who brought charges against him stole a chain worth $8,000.<sup id="cite_ref-MCJ03_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCJ03-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MCJ04_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCJ04-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
