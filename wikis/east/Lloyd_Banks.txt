<p><b>Christopher Charles Lloyd</b> (born April 30, 1982),<sup id="cite_ref-Birthday_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Birthday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Lloyd Banks</b>, is an American rapper. He began his career as a member of <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>, alongside childhood friends <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Yayo" title="Tony Yayo">Tony Yayo</a>. After the group's 2003 release of their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Beg_for_Mercy" title="Beg for Mercy">Beg for Mercy</a></i>, Banks released his first solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_for_More" title="The Hunger for More">The Hunger for More</a></i>, in 2004, featuring the top ten hit single "<a href="/wiki/On_Fire_(Lloyd_Banks_song)" title="On Fire (Lloyd Banks song)">On Fire</a>". His second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Rotten_Apple_(Lloyd_Banks_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rotten Apple (Lloyd Banks album)">Rotten Apple</a></i>, was released in 2006.
</p><p>Banks left <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> in 2009 and, along with the rest of G-Unit, signed with <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a> the following year<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> to distribute Banks third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_for_More_2" class="mw-redirect" title="The Hunger for More 2">The Hunger for More 2</a></i>, which was released in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Christopher Lloyd was born in <a href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="Baltimore, Maryland">Baltimore, Maryland</a>, and raised in the <a href="/wiki/South_Jamaica,_Queens" title="South Jamaica, Queens">South Jamaica</a> section of the <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a> borough in New York City;<sup id="cite_ref-Allhiphop.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allhiphop.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> he is of mixed <a href="/wiki/Stateside_Puerto_Ricans" title="Stateside Puerto Ricans">Puerto Rican</a> and <a href="/wiki/African_Americans" title="African Americans">African-American</a> descent.<sup id="cite_ref-Sixshot_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sixshot-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His father spent most of Lloyd's childhood in prison,<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> leaving his mother to raise him and his two siblings.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Lloyd attended <a href="/wiki/August_Martin_High_School" title="August Martin High School">August Martin High School</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> but dropped out at the age of 16.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> For his stage name, he took his great, great-grandfather's name Banks, which was passed on by his uncles who also shared it.<sup id="cite_ref-Sixshot_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sixshot-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<h3><span id="Beginnings_with_G-Unit_.281999.E2.80.932004.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginnings_with_G-Unit_(1999–2004)">Beginnings with G-Unit (1999–2004)</span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a> was founded when childhood friends Lloyd Banks, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Yayo" title="Tony Yayo">Tony Yayo</a> decided to form a group.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> They met <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a> when <a href="/wiki/UTP_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="UTP Records">UTP</a> group came to New York and  50 Cent heard Young Buck rapping. After 50 Cent signed his contract with Aftermath Entertainment they took Young Buck in the group and signed him.
Tony Yayo, being an older and more experienced rapper, joined 50 Cent on the Nas Promo Tour, the Cash Money Tour and the Ruff Ryders Tour. As Banks remained at home waiting for 50 and Yayo to return, he started rapping around the neighborhood to further increase his buzz on the streets. He then hooked up with neighborhood producers and recorded tracks for local mixtapes, becoming renowned on the mixtape scene. While Banks was recording Mixtapes,<sup id="cite_ref-AskMen_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AskMen-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 50 Cent was soon granted his own record label by <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a> and released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Rich_or_Die_Tryin%27_(album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Get Rich or Die Tryin&#39; (album)">Get Rich or Die Tryin'</a></i>; Lloyd Banks was featured on the song "Don't Push Me", and the remixed version of "<a href="/wiki/P.I.M.P" class="mw-redirect" title="P.I.M.P">P.I.M.P</a>". Soon after the group had established their own record label, <a href="/wiki/G-Unit_Records" title="G-Unit Records">G-Unit Records</a>, G-Unit released their first official group album <i><a href="/wiki/Beg_for_Mercy" title="Beg for Mercy">Beg for Mercy</a></i> in November 2003, which went on to be certified double platinum.<sup id="cite_ref-Beg_for_Mercy_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beg_for_Mercy-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Shooting">Shooting</span></h4>
<p>On September 10, 2001, Banks was shot twice while leaving a nightclub in Southside Jamaica, Queens.  He was hit in the back and stomach, and ran to the nearest hospital after suffering the injuries. He woke up the next morning to news of the 9/11 terrorist attacks and watched from his hospital bed as the Twin Towers fell to the ground.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_Issues">Legal Issues</span></h4>
<p>During August 2005, Lloyd Banks, Young Buck, and their entourage were traveling in a van, when the vehicle was pulled over after passing through a red light in midtown Manhattan.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Officers said they discovered a loaded handgun and another weapon in the van. Prosecutors asked a judge to dismiss the charges after an investigation determined that neither Lloyd Banks nor Young Buck were in possession of the weapons. Felony gun charges against Lloyd Banks and Young Buck were dropped on November 8, 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the early morning hours of January 9, 2010, in a Kitchener, Ontario hotel, an alleged altercation took place between Banks and a concert promoter over performance fees. Banks, and three of his associates, were later charged with forcible confinement, aggravated assault and robbery, and released on $50,000 bail.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Feud_with_The_Game">Feud with The Game</span></h4>
<p>The most well known feud involving Lloyd Banks is the feud between himself and <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>, which became public after The Game left G-Unit. Banks speculates that The Game was jealous about the attention Banks received after the success of his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_for_More" title="The Hunger for More">The Hunger for More</a></i> as well as Banks winning a lyricist title.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Solo_career_and_The_Hunger_for_More_.282004.E2.80.932005.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career_and_The_Hunger_for_More_(2004–2005)">Solo career and <i>The Hunger for More</i> (2004–2005)</span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_for_More" title="The Hunger for More">The Hunger for More</a></div>
<p>Lloyd Banks released his solo debut album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_for_More" title="The Hunger for More">The Hunger for More</a></i> in June 2004. The first single was the summer smash hit "<a href="/wiki/On_Fire_(Lloyd_Banks_song)" title="On Fire (Lloyd Banks song)">On Fire</a>" which featured 50 Cent, however he was uncredited.
Banks explained the reasoning behind the album's title:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>When I say The Hunger for More, it could be referring to more success. It could be more money. Or Respect. More power. More understanding. All those things lead up to that hunger for more, because my more isn't everybody else's more. I feel like I made it already, because I got already what everybody on the corners of the neighborhood I grew up in is striving to get.<sup id="cite_ref-BillBoard_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillBoard-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>The album was a commercial success, receiving positive reviews.  It debuted at number 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard magazine">Billboard</a> charts with over 433,000 copies sold in the first week.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The album has since sold over 1.5 million copies<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>  and has been certified platinum by the RIAA.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During an interview, Lloyd Banks commented on his first week sales:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942"><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>That's the kind of debut that veteran artists have," says Banks. "That showed me that following 50's moves and studying the way that he played the game had put me in an incredible position.</p></blockquote>
<p>Two other singles were also released from <i>The Hunger For More</i>, "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_So_Fly" title="I&#39;m So Fly">I'm So Fly</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Karma_(Lloyd_Banks_song)" title="Karma (Lloyd Banks song)">Karma</a>", the latter was a hit reaching number 17 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, number 9 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_songs" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop songs">R&amp;B</a> charts and number 6 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Tracks" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Rap Tracks">rap</a> charts. 
</p><p>At the time of the album's release, Lloyd Banks was managed by <a href="/wiki/Sha_Money_XL" title="Sha Money XL">Sha Money XL</a> and Teamwork Music.
</p>
<h3><span id="Rotten_Apple_.282006.E2.80.932008.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Rotten_Apple_(2006–2008)"><i>Rotten Apple</i> (2006–2008)</span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Apple_(Lloyd_Banks_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rotten Apple (Lloyd Banks album)">Rotten Apple (Lloyd Banks album)</a></div>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Lloyd_Banks_by_Mikey_Hennessy.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Lloyd_Banks_by_Mikey_Hennessy.jpg/220px-Lloyd_Banks_by_Mikey_Hennessy.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Lloyd_Banks_by_Mikey_Hennessy.jpg/330px-Lloyd_Banks_by_Mikey_Hennessy.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Lloyd_Banks_by_Mikey_Hennessy.jpg/440px-Lloyd_Banks_by_Mikey_Hennessy.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1365" /></a><figcaption>Banks performing in 2006</figcaption></figure>
<p><i>The Big Withdrawal</i> was originally intended to be Lloyd Banks' second album. The album was leaked to the internet after Banks had a <a href="/wiki/Threesome" title="Threesome">ménage à trois</a> encounter with two women and left the CD album behind.<sup id="cite_ref-SOHH_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SOHH-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The leaked version of the album contained 23 tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>
During an interview, Lloyd Banks commented on the issue, he said: <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942"></p><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>It's truth to that rumor. That comes from me doing too much. I was just lost in my ways, fucked two women at one time. It's the little things you don't pay attention to. I had the CD in an actual DJ Whoo Kid mixtape cover. It was just a blank CD with just tracks. I got over 70, 80 tracks, so you can't get them all on one CD. So I might have had 14 tracks of the last two weeks or something like that. Next thing I know, [I] can't find the CD. I'm assuming it had to be from one of those situations when you got too much going on around you. Maybe I'mma just stick to one girl from now on. That's the only explanation that makes sense. Sticky fingers.<sup id="cite_ref-SOHH_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SOHH-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Due to the leak, Lloyd Banks began work on <i><a href="/wiki/Rotten_Apple_(Lloyd_Banks_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rotten Apple (Lloyd Banks album)">Rotten Apple</a></i>. "Rotten Apple" is a play on New York City's nickname "The Big Apple". It was released on October 10, 2006. The album debuted at #3, selling 143,000 copies in its first week. <i>Rotten Apple</i> was not considered as a commercial success because his debut album sold a significantly higher number of copies in its first week. He has released three singles from <i>Rotten Apple</i>: "Hands Up", "The Cake", and "Help".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Departure_from_Interscope_.282009.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Departure_from_Interscope_(2009)">Departure from Interscope (2009)</span></h3>
<p>Banks was dropped from Interscope Records in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
<sup id="cite_ref-Hungry_Like_A_Wolf_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hungry_Like_A_Wolf-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He is still signed to G-Unit Records, which will release his new album.<sup id="cite_ref-Hungry_Like_A_Wolf_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hungry_Like_A_Wolf-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Banks released a mixtape on his 27th birthday, which fell on April 30, 2009; it included the track "<a href="/wiki/Officer_Down_(song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Officer Down (song)">Officer Down</a>" that dissed <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rick Ross (rapper)">Rick Ross</a>. Banks also released a mixtape in 2009 called V5.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, a year later, Interscope Records reached back out to try to re-sign Lloyd Banks. Banks did not go for it and put his third album out under G-Unit Records.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="The_Hunger_for_More_2_and_signing_with_EMI_.282010.E2.80.932013.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Hunger_for_More_2_and_signing_with_EMI_(2010–2013)"><i>The Hunger for More 2</i> and signing with EMI (2010–2013)</span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_for_More_2" class="mw-redirect" title="The Hunger for More 2">The Hunger for More 2</a></div>
<p>In late January 2010, it was announced that Banks would release a new song, "<a href="/wiki/Beamer,_Benz,_or_Bentley" title="Beamer, Benz, or Bentley">Beamer, Benz, or Bentley</a>" featuring Juelz Santana, from his upcoming third studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-bbb_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbb-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> "Beamer, Benz, or Bentley" charted at #49 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Beamer_Benz_or_Bentley_-_Billboard_Charts_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beamer_Benz_or_Bentley_-_Billboard_Charts-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> In early June, Banks released the second single off the album, "<a href="/wiki/Any_Girl" title="Any Girl">Any Girl</a>" featuring R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_(singer)" title="Lloyd (singer)">Lloyd</a>. Later on November 2, 2010, he released the third single <a href="/wiki/Start_It_Up_(song)" title="Start It Up (song)">Start It Up</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Leslie" title="Ryan Leslie">Ryan Leslie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>. Lloyd released the fourth single off the album, "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Deserve_You" title="I Don&#39;t Deserve You">I Don't Deserve You</a>", featuring R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/Jeremih" title="Jeremih">Jeremih</a>.
</p><p>Banks release info via his Twitter announcing that the title for his third album has tentatively been called 'The Hunger for More 2' and will attempt to take him back to his best work. However, the G-Unit label later stated that the title is not set in concrete and still may be altered. The title of the album was confirmed by <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_for_More_2" class="mw-redirect" title="The Hunger for More 2">The Hunger for More 2</a></i> in an interview with MTV News.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> According to Banks, Interscope is trying to get him back due to the success of the single "Beamer, Benz, or Bentley",<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> but failed when Lloyd Banks revealed that he had signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a>.
</p><p>After being dropped by  <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> in 2009, Lloyd Banks announced on Friday, August 13, 2010, on <a href="/wiki/MTV_News" title="MTV News">MTV News</a> that <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a> Label Services has signed a deal with 50 Cent's label, <a href="/wiki/G-Unit_Records" title="G-Unit Records">G-Unit Records</a>, in which EMI will distribute and promote releases on the G-Unit roster in North America including Lloyd Banks third album <i><a href="/wiki/H.F.M._2_(Hunger_for_More_2)" class="mw-redirect" title="H.F.M. 2 (Hunger for More 2)">H.F.M. 2 (Hunger for More 2)</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hiphop-n-more.com_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hiphop-n-more.com-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> On October 18, 2011, Banks was featured on the remix of Queen's Rapper <a href="/wiki/Trav_(musician)" title="Trav (musician)">Trav</a>'s single "Ride The Wave" alongside Juelz Santana.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>
Banks' had this to say about the deal,<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942"></p><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>It's refreshing to see the EMI staff excited about my project, they have the passion and energy I haven't seen in while.</p></blockquote>
<h3><span id="Resurgence_with_G-Unit_.282014.E2.80.932018.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Resurgence_with_G-Unit_(2014–2018)">Resurgence with G-Unit (2014–2018)</span></h3>
<p>During a performance at BB King's in New York City on August 2, 2015, Banks addressed his long-awaited <i>Cold Corner 3</i> mixtape and expressed disappointment at the current state of hip-hop. He explained that the project would be released commercially, and available for pre-order. However, if he's not satisfied with the pre-order numbers, it will not be released.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Departure_from_G-Unit_and_50_Cent_feud_.282018.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Departure_from_G-Unit_and_50_Cent_feud_(2018–present)">Departure from G-Unit and 50 Cent feud (2018–present)</span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Lloyd_Banks_performing_2019.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Lloyd_Banks_performing_2019.jpg/260px-Lloyd_Banks_performing_2019.jpg" decoding="async" width="260" height="195" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Lloyd_Banks_performing_2019.jpg/390px-Lloyd_Banks_performing_2019.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Lloyd_Banks_performing_2019.jpg/520px-Lloyd_Banks_performing_2019.jpg 2x" data-file-width="850" data-file-height="638" /></a><figcaption>Banks performing in Australia in 2019</figcaption></figure>
<p>Lloyd banks left G-Unit Records in June 2018 announced in an Instagram post by <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> at the time it seemed as if the two were still friends as 50 would tell fans to check out Banks's new mixtape<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> however it seems their relationship is no longer amicable. 50 Cent in an interview with <a href="/wiki/Big_Boy_(radio_host)" title="Big Boy (radio host)">Big Boy</a> spoke about the different members of G-Unit and said "Banks, he just... I don't even know he couldn't even tell you like at any point when you speak to him where the problem is. I put him like where I put Marquise like they just have something internal going on with them that gives them some sort of resentment towards me and I just don't even care about whats going on".<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> However Trav a former associate of G-Unit went on Instagram live and spoke on the matter "Banks father died bro and Banks was sad bro all Banks wanted to do was him to pull up like yo bro I'm sorry for your condolences because the ni**a 50 don't come from no family he tried to play like this fake role like man you ain't even know your father".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> 50 cent went on to call Lloyd banks Lazy in his book Hustle Harder, Hustle Smarter<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> which Lloyd banks would seemingly respond to in his song Stranger Things. Lloyd banks hasn't spoke on the matter publicly however he has sent words that seem to be aimed at 50 cent on numerous songs including cold summer freestyle and Stranger Things. In-between Lloyd banks departure from G-Unit and June 2021 Lloyd banks remained relatively quiet other than introducing a clothing line and touring places such as Australia, Lloyd banks wasn't doing interviews or releasing any new projects other than some freestyles in fact Lloyd banks hadn't released a new project since his mixtape Halloween Havoc 3 in 2016. He would also go on to twitter to tweet things such as "Let's be real..ain't nobody checking for banks anymore" leaving fans wondering if Lloyd banks was done with music. However fans would be surprised as in April 2021 he would release a  post on Instagram that teased possible new music which continued on to May 25 were a video was released with a brief spoken-word monologue from poet Rashan Brown and on May 25 a tracklist and cover art was revealed. The album was released on June 4, 2021, as his 4th album and first album in 11 years named <i>The Course of the Inevitable</i> which sold 12,000 copies in its first week. The album received very positive reviews and Lloyd banks seemed happy with the reception as he spoke about more upcoming music at a concert he held in Sony Hall.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span></h2>
<p>In 2004, Banks was awarded 2004's Mixtape artist of the Year at the Mixtape Awards. Lloyd Banks commented on this award in an interview with AOL Sessions:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942"><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>When I won the mixtape artist of the year 2004, after that I didn't want to get away from it because that right there is where I got my satisfaction, from the fans giving me that award. It wasn't like an award show when you got some big fat guys in suits on the podium that never even heard my album giving me a rating or judging what I deserve... that's actually my most proudest trophy right now.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>In 2005, "On Fire" was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Grammy" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy">Grammy</a> award for Best Rap Solo Performance.  In 2006, "Touch It [Remix]" was nominated for Hip-Hop Video of the Year, and won for Best Collaboration at the <a href="/wiki/BET_Awards" title="BET Awards">BET Awards</a>.  The award was shared by Banks and his collaborators, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rah_Digga" title="Rah Digga">Rah Digga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Papoose_(rapper)" title="Papoose (rapper)">Papoose</a>, and <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>. In 2010 with the excitement surrounding the release of <i>H.F.M. 2 (Hunger for More 2)</i> and his return to Hip-Hop's spotlight Hip-Hop news website HipHopDX wrote that Banks had the "Comeback of the Year".<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
