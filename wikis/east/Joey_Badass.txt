<p><b>Jo-Vaughn Virginie Scott</b> (born January 20, 1995),<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Joey Badass</b> (stylized as <b>Joey Bada$$</b>), is an American rapper. A native of <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York City, he is a founding member of the hip-hop collective <a href="/wiki/Pro_Era" title="Pro Era">Pro Era</a>, with whom he has released three mixtapes as well as his numerous solo projects.
</p><p>Joey Badass released his debut mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/1999_(mixtape)" title="1999 (mixtape)">1999</a></i>, in June 2012 to critical acclaim and recognition, followed by <i>Rejex</i> in September, and <i><a href="/wiki/Summer_Knights" title="Summer Knights">Summer Knights</a></i> in July 2013. His debut <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio album">studio album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/B4.Da.$$" title="B4.Da.$$">B4.Da.$$</a></i>, was released in January 2015. In 2016, he made his television debut on the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Robot" title="Mr. Robot">Mr. Robot</a></i>. His second and third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/All-Amerikkkan_Badass" title="All-Amerikkkan Badass">All-Amerikkkan Badass</a> and</i> <i><a href="/wiki/2000_(Joey_Badass_album)" title="2000 (Joey Badass album)">2000</a></i>, were released in April 2017 and July 2022, respectively.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joey_Badass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jo-Vaughn Virginie Scott was born on January 20, 1995, and was the first of his immediate <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> family to be born in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> His 
maternal family originated from the Caribbean island of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">St. Lucia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> while his paternal family originates from the island of Jamaica.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He was born in the <a href="/wiki/East_Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="East Flatbush, Brooklyn">East Flatbush</a> section of <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York City. He was raised in <a href="/wiki/Bedford%E2%80%93Stuyvesant" class="mw-redirect" title="Bedford–Stuyvesant">Bed-Stuy</a> and attended the <a href="/wiki/Edward_R._Murrow_High_School" title="Edward R. Murrow High School">Edward R. Murrow High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He originally enrolled at Edward R. Murrow to study <a href="/wiki/Acting" title="Acting">acting</a>, but transitioned towards music around the time of 9th grade, with a focus on <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapping</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He started rapping under the name "JayOhVee", but eventually changed it to Joey Badass. He says that the name change is due to the media's focus on rappers with more cynical names, saying it was "what sounded cool at the time, what fit [his] mood".<sup id="cite_ref-complex_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He began writing poetry and songs at the age of 11.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Joey_Badass_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Joey_Badass-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Along with <a href="/wiki/CJ_Fly" title="CJ Fly">CJ Fly</a>, he is a founding member of the collective known as Progressive Era, or <a href="/wiki/Pro_Era" title="Pro Era">Pro Era</a> for short,<sup id="cite_ref-complex_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Intervew_mag_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Intervew_mag-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> which was formed by his high school friends <a href="/wiki/Capital_Steez" title="Capital Steez">Capital Steez</a> and Powers Pleasant.<sup id="cite_ref-LA_Weekly_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA_Weekly-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Fader_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Fader-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joey_Badass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932014:_Beginnings_and_breakthrough"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2014:_Beginnings_and_breakthrough">2010&#8211;2014: Beginnings and breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joey_Badass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010–2014: Beginnings and breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Joey_Badass,_New_York%27s_SOB%27s,_June_2012.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Joey_Badass%2C_New_York%27s_SOB%27s%2C_June_2012.jpg/220px-Joey_Badass%2C_New_York%27s_SOB%27s%2C_June_2012.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Joey_Badass%2C_New_York%27s_SOB%27s%2C_June_2012.jpg/330px-Joey_Badass%2C_New_York%27s_SOB%27s%2C_June_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Joey_Badass%2C_New_York%27s_SOB%27s%2C_June_2012.jpg/440px-Joey_Badass%2C_New_York%27s_SOB%27s%2C_June_2012.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1100" data-file-height="733" /></a><figcaption>Joey Badass performing in 2012</figcaption></figure>
<p>In October 2010, Joey Badass uploaded a video of himself <a href="/wiki/Freestyling" class="mw-redirect" title="Freestyling">freestyling</a> to <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. The video was re-posted on to American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> site <a href="/wiki/World_Star_Hip_Hop" class="mw-redirect" title="World Star Hip Hop">World Star Hip Hop</a> and caught the attention of <a href="/wiki/Jonny_Shipes" title="Jonny Shipes">Jonny Shipes</a>, the president of <a href="/wiki/Cinematic_Music_Group" title="Cinematic Music Group">Cinematic Music Group</a>, who soon after became his manager.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Joey_Badass_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Joey_Badass-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2012, Joey Badass and <a href="/wiki/Capital_Steez" title="Capital Steez">Capital Steez</a> released a video for "Survival Tactics" via their YouTube channel "PROfckingERA", which samples the <a href="/wiki/2000_Fold" title="2000 Fold">2000 Fold</a> song of the same name by <a href="/wiki/Styles_of_Beyond" title="Styles of Beyond">Styles of Beyond</a>. In February 2012, Pro Era released their debut mixtape entitled <i>The Secc$ Tap.e</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On March 21, 2012, <a href="/wiki/MTV_News" title="MTV News">MTV News</a> featured Joey Badass' verse from the video during a RapFix Live segment and subsequently appeared as a featured guest on <i><a href="/wiki/Sucker_Free" title="Sucker Free">Sucker Free</a></i> the following month. The song was succeeded with the video premiere of "Hardknock" featuring CJ Fly on <a href="/wiki/MTV2" title="MTV2">MTV2</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 12, 2012, Joey Badass released his debut solo mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/1999_(mixtape)" title="1999 (mixtape)">1999</a></i>. This album was only released to music streaming platforms such as Spotify and Apple Music in 2018. A video for the song "Waves" was released in June 2012. The mixtape immediately increased his popularity in the underground scene.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape was named the 38th best album of 2012 by <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> magazine<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> and the best mixtape of 2012 by <a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On September 6 of the same year, he released another mixtape titled <i>Rejex</i>, which featured all the tracks that did not make it on the 1999 mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Joey_Badass_performing_at_Hovefestivalen_2013.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Joey_Badass_performing_at_Hovefestivalen_2013.jpg/220px-Joey_Badass_performing_at_Hovefestivalen_2013.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="123" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Joey_Badass_performing_at_Hovefestivalen_2013.jpg/330px-Joey_Badass_performing_at_Hovefestivalen_2013.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Joey_Badass_performing_at_Hovefestivalen_2013.jpg/440px-Joey_Badass_performing_at_Hovefestivalen_2013.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1599" data-file-height="893" /></a><figcaption>Joey Badass performing in 2013</figcaption></figure>
<p>Subsequently, American rapper <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a> reached out to Joey Badass through Twitter to collaborate. He appeared on the song "America" from the mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Macadelic" title="Macadelic">Macadelic</a></i> and opened for Miller at the <a href="/wiki/Roseland_Ballroom" title="Roseland Ballroom">Roseland Ballroom</a> in April 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Along with headliner <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a>, Joey Badass and Pro Era performed on The Smoker's Club's One Hazy Summer Tour, presented by <a href="/wiki/Eck%C5%8D_Unltd." class="mw-redirect" title="Eckō Unltd.">Eckō Unltd.</a> and livemixtapes.com.  The 30-date tour began in July 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He was featured on the <a href="/wiki/Posse_cut" title="Posse cut">posse cut</a> track "1 Train" off <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Long.Live.ASAP" class="mw-redirect" title="Long.Live.ASAP">Long.Live.ASAP</a></i> (2013) which also features <a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson" title="Action Bronson">Action Bronson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yelawolf" title="Yelawolf">Yelawolf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_K.R.I.T." title="Big K.R.I.T.">Big K.R.I.T.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Danny_Brown_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Danny Brown (rapper)">Danny Brown</a>.
</p><p>In December 2012 it was rumored that Joey Badass would be signing to <a href="/wiki/Jay_Z" class="mw-redirect" title="Jay Z">Jay Z's</a> <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a> record label,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> but in January 2013, he dispelled the rumors saying he would rather stay independent than sign to a major label for the time being.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On December 21, 2012, Pro Era released the <i><a href="/wiki/P.E.E.P:_The_aPROcalypse" class="mw-redirect" title="P.E.E.P: The aPROcalypse">P.E.E.P: The aPROcalypse</a></i> mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 9, 2012, Joey Badass released a track with <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul" title="Ab-Soul">Ab-Soul</a> produced by <a href="/wiki/Lee_Bannon" class="mw-redirect" title="Lee Bannon">Lee Bannon</a> called "Enter the Void" along with "Waves". These songs were his first official releases to retail. On January 14, 2013, he released the first single off his second mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Summer_Knights" title="Summer Knights">Summer Knights</a></i>, "<a href="/wiki/Unorthodox_(Joey_Badass_song)" title="Unorthodox (Joey Badass song)">Unorthodox</a>", which was produced by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On February 8, he released a music video featuring <a href="/wiki/Big_K.R.I.T." title="Big K.R.I.T.">Big K.R.I.T.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Smoke_DZA" title="Smoke DZA">Smoke DZA</a> titled "Underground Airplay". The song was featured on the <a href="/wiki/Eck%C5%8D_Unltd." class="mw-redirect" title="Eckō Unltd.">Eckō Unltd.</a> mixtape. On March 5, 2013, he released the music video for "Unorthodox".<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On March 21, 2013, he began the Beast Coast concert tour with Pro Era, <a href="/wiki/The_Underachievers" title="The Underachievers">The Underachievers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flatbush_Zombies" title="Flatbush Zombies">Flatbush Zombies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> On March 26, 2013, it was announced that Joey Badass would be a part of <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="XXL Magazine">XXL Magazine</a>'s</i> 2013 Freshman Class.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 29, 2013, <i>Summer Knights EP</i> was released. It featured four songs from the mixtape of the same name, and three new songs including a remix.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Upon release, the EP debuted at number 48 on the US <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.932015:_B4.DA..24.24"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2015:_B4.DA.$$">2014–2015: <i>B4.DA.$$</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joey_Badass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2014–2015: B4.DA.$$">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/B4.Da.$$" title="B4.Da.$$">B4.Da.$$</a> and <a href="/wiki/All-Amerikkkan_Badass" title="All-Amerikkkan Badass">All-Amerikkkan Badass</a></div>
<p>In August 2014, Joey Badass appeared in a quasi-autobiographical short film entitled <i>No Regrets</i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Rik_Cordero" title="Rik Cordero">Rik Cordero</a>, in which he portrays himself.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> In an interview on <a href="/wiki/Sirius_XM_Holdings" class="mw-redirect" title="Sirius XM Holdings">Shade 45 Radio</a> in March 2016, he explained that "it's been my dream to act since I was in like eighth grade. I always wanted to be an actor. And I feel like the music just made it a little bit easier for me to chase that dream."<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 4, 2014, he released the first single from his upcoming debut studio album titled "Big Dusty" with the music video coming out 8 days later.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On September 30, he premiered the second single from the album titled "<a href="/wiki/Christ_Conscious" title="Christ Conscious">Christ Conscious</a>" on <a href="/wiki/MTV_Jams" class="mw-redirect" title="MTV Jams">MTV Jams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The third single from the album entitled "No. 99" was released on December 9. On December 23, the fourth single, "Curry Chicken" was released.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On January 13 appearing on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Jimmy_Fallon" title="The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a></i>, he performed the song "Like Me" live with <a href="/wiki/BJ_the_Chicago_Kid" title="BJ the Chicago Kid">BJ the Chicago Kid</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">The Roots</a> and <a href="/wiki/Statik_Selektah" title="Statik Selektah">Statik Selektah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On January 20, 2015, his 20th birthday, the album entitled <i><a href="/wiki/B4.Da.$$" title="B4.Da.$$">B4.DA.$$</a></i> was released to critical acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 3, 2015, Joey Badass released a collaborative single with Korean hip-hop group <a href="/wiki/Epik_High" title="Epik High">Epik High</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tablo" title="Tablo">Tablo</a> and producer <a href="/wiki/Code_Kunst" title="Code Kunst">Code Kunst</a> called "Hood". On October 6, 2015, he collaborated with British <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Glass_Animals" title="Glass Animals">Glass Animals</a>, releasing a single titled "Lose Control".
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932019:_All-Amerikkkan_Bada.24.24"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2019:_All-Amerikkkan_Bada$$">2016–2019: <i>All-Amerikkkan Bada$$</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joey_Badass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2016–2019: All-Amerikkkan Bada$$">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/All-Amerikkkan_Badass" title="All-Amerikkkan Badass">All-Amerikkkan Badass</a></div>
<p>On March 4, 2016, Joey Badass made his television debut in the second season of the hit TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Robot" title="Mr. Robot">Mr. Robot</a></i>, where he was cast in the recurring role of Leon, who is an associate of Elliot (played by <a href="/wiki/Rami_Malek" title="Rami Malek">Rami Malek</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> On May 26, 2016, he released another single called "<a href="/wiki/Devastated_(Joey_Badass_song)" title="Devastated (Joey Badass song)">Devastated</a>". The song peaked at number 25 on the <a href="/wiki/Bubbling_Under_Hot_100_Singles" class="mw-redirect" title="Bubbling Under Hot 100 Singles">Bubbling Under Hot 100 Singles</a> chart, which became Joey's highest charting single to date (as a solo artist).<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> The single was certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> on January 6, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> On January 20, 2017, Joey Badass released "<a href="/wiki/Land_of_the_Free_(Joey_Bada$$_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Land of the Free (Joey Bada$$ song)">Land of the Free</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> On March 2, 2017, Joey Bada$$ revealed the title of his second studio album titled "<a href="/wiki/All-Amerikkkan_Badass" title="All-Amerikkkan Badass">All-Amerikkkan Bada$$</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-AmerikkkanBadass_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AmerikkkanBadass-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> On April 7, 2017, Joey Badass released the album.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> On May 12, 2017, it was announced that Joey Badass will go on <a href="/wiki/Logic_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Logic (musician)">Logic</a>'s Everybody's Tour with Big Lenbo, in support of Logic's third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Everybody_(Logic_album)" title="Everybody (Logic album)">Everybody</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 28, 2017, Joey Badass announced via Twitter that he was originally featured on <a href="/wiki/Post_Malone" title="Post Malone">Post Malone</a>'s track "<a href="/wiki/Rockstar_(Post_Malone_song)" title="Rockstar (Post Malone song)">Rockstar</a>" along with <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a>. Badass also admitted to ghostwriting the track.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Florida rapper <a href="/wiki/XXXTentacion" title="XXXTentacion">XXXTentacion</a> announced on Instagram that he had a collaboration project with Joey Badass, Badass later confirmed this on January 20, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> The two dropped a remix of the song "<a href="/wiki/King%27s_Dead" title="King&#39;s Dead">King's Dead</a>" on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> on March 9, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> The two later on dropped a song titled "Infinity (888)" on XXXTentacion's second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/%3F_(XXXTentacion_album)" title="? (XXXTentacion album)">?</a></i> one week later on March 16.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> With the <a href="/wiki/Killing_of_XXXTentacion" class="mw-redirect" title="Killing of XXXTentacion">killing of XXXTentacion</a>, though, their collaborative project is all but confirmed to be not happening. On June 12, 2018, Joey re-issued <i>1999</i> on all streaming services and vinyl for its 6th anniversary,<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> and in a following interview with <a href="/wiki/Peter_Rosenberg" title="Peter Rosenberg">Peter Rosenberg</a>, revealed his fatherhood, making public his first child, then 2-month-old Indigo Rain.<sup id="cite_ref-Saponara_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Saponara-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In December 2019, he was also featured in XXXTentacion's second posthumous and last album, <i><a href="/wiki/Bad_Vibes_Forever" title="Bad Vibes Forever">Bad Vibes Forever</a></i>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2020.E2.80.93present:_2000"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020–present:_2000">2020–present: <i>2000</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joey_Badass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2020–present: 2000">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2020, Joey Badass released a 3-track EP entitled <i>The Light Pack</i>. In November, he featured on <a href="/wiki/Phony_Ppl" title="Phony Ppl">Phony Ppl's</a> "On My Shit".<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 11, 2022, he announced his upcoming third studio album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/2000_(Joey_Badass_album)" title="2000 (Joey Badass album)">2000</a></i> The album was to be released on June 17, 2022, however due to sample clearance issues, he announced on Twitter that he would have to delay the project.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> The album was released on July 22, 2022, with guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Westside_Gunn" title="Westside Gunn">Westside Gunn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Larry_June" title="Larry June">Larry June</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Capella_Grey" title="Capella Grey">Capella Grey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/JID" title="JID">JID</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> He also went on a 20-date summer concert tour called the <i>1999-2000 Tour</i> in support, which began July 1, 2022 in Boston and concluded July 28, 2022 in Los Angeles.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joey_Badass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In an interview with <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a>, Joey Badass stated that he was influenced by <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">2Pac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_Thought" title="Black Thought">Black Thought</a>, <a href="/wiki/MF_DOOM" class="mw-redirect" title="MF DOOM">MF DOOM</a>, <a href="/wiki/J_Dilla" title="J Dilla">J Dilla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andre_3000" class="mw-redirect" title="Andre 3000">Andre 3000</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sabotagetimes.com_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sabotagetimes.com-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> He has been compared to rapper <a href="/wiki/Big_L" title="Big L">Big L</a> and Nas during his <i><a href="/wiki/Illmatic" title="Illmatic">Illmatic</a></i> era.<sup id="cite_ref-sabotagetimes.com_63-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sabotagetimes.com-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joey_Badass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issue">Legal issue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joey_Badass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Legal issue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2015, Joey Badass was arrested and charged with assault for allegedly breaking a security guard's nose<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> before performing at the <a href="/wiki/Falls_Festival" title="Falls Festival">Falls Festival</a> in <a href="/wiki/Byron_Bay" title="Byron Bay">Byron Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>, Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feud_with_Troy_Ave">Feud with Troy Ave</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joey_Badass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Feud with Troy Ave">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2015, tensions between Joey Badass and fellow Brooklyn native <a href="/wiki/Troy_Ave" title="Troy Ave">Troy Ave</a> began brewing after the former declared himself the "#1 <a href="/wiki/Independent_music" title="Independent music">independent</a> hip hop artist/brand in the world" in a series of <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">tweets</a>. Troy Ave responded in another tweet by pointing out that Joey Badass is signed to Cinematic Music Group, whose music is distributed by <a href="/wiki/RED_Distribution" class="mw-redirect" title="RED Distribution">RED Distribution</a>, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony Music Entertainment</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> but Joey Badass later denied reports of a feud between the two of them.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 7, 2015, in an interview with <a href="/wiki/Ebro_Darden" title="Ebro Darden">Ebro Darden</a> on <a href="/wiki/Hot_97" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot 97">Hot 97</a>, Joey Badass had words of praise for Troy Ave, stating: "At some point you gotta have some level of respect for Troy because, at the end of the day, we're both independent artists who are standing on major platforms next to a whole bunch of major artists and they know who we are."<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> However, in February 2016, he reignited the feud in a single entitled "Ready", where he rapped: "60k, first week for the badass / 200k to this day, I know you niggas mad / With that 80-20 split, my nigga do the math / my nigga Kirk just outsold Troy Ave" (in reference to <a href="/wiki/Kirk_Knight" title="Kirk Knight">Kirk Knight</a>'s debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Knight_Special" title="Late Knight Special">Late Knight Special</a></i> and Troy Ave's second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Major_Without_a_Deal" title="Major Without a Deal">Major Without a Deal</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>	
</p><p>In response to "Ready", Troy Ave released a <a href="/wiki/Diss_track" class="mw-redirect" title="Diss track">diss track</a> entitled "Badass" the following week, in which he not only insulted Joey Badass, but also targeted Capital Steez and his suicide.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup> Following the song's release, Troy Ave defended his actions on <a href="/wiki/Shade_45" title="Shade 45">Shade 45</a>'s <i>Sway in the Morning</i>, stating: "He didn't pass away; he killed himself. There's a difference. He took his own life. God gave you life, it ain't your right to take that. That's a fact. I got niggas in jail who got life sentences, they might as well be dead. They could've traded their life for his."<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> He then received heavy criticism from both fans and fellow artists, including <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Mob" title="ASAP Mob">A$AP Ant</a>, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Twelvyy" title="ASAP Twelvyy">A$AP Twelvyy</a><sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a> who wrote, amongst a series of other tweets: "If you never experienced a family [member] committing suicide you have no fukn [sic] idea about that pain. NONE." Troy Ave then replied to Styles P's comments and apologized to his fans on Twitter.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> In an interview on <a href="/wiki/DJ_Vlad" title="DJ Vlad">VladTV</a>, another Brooklyn rapper, <a href="/wiki/Maino_(rapper)" title="Maino (rapper)">Maino</a>, stated that mentioning Capital Steez's suicide in the song was "a bit much".<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>...He took a leap of faith and only brightened his light / You took a cheap shot at hate and only shortened your life / Difference between you and him is that he lives forever / You'll be the first to die that nobody ever remembers / 'cause the city never needed you ever / You committed career suicide and made New York better...</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8202;<cite>Joey Badass, on his "Five Fingers of Death" freestyle on <i>Sway in the Morning</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-FFOD-1_77-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FFOD-1-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FFOD-2_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FFOD-2-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup></cite></div></blockquote>
<p>In a <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a> on <i>Sway in the Morning</i> on March 29, 2016, Joey Badass responded to "Badass" in a series of lines aimed at Troy Ave, though never referring to him directly. Among other lines, he rapped: "I destroy average rappers, it's just practice […] Men lie, women lie, numbers sure don't / Got the whole <a href="/wiki/Beast_Coast" title="Beast Coast">Beast Coast</a> coming for your skull / The karma gon' catch up, your album sales won't".<sup id="cite_ref-FFOD-1_77-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FFOD-1-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FFOD-2_78-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FFOD-2-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a></i> the following day, <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a> stated that he personally reached out to both Joey Badass and Troy Ave to try and squash the feud, but failed.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joey_Badass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p>
