<p><b>Cary Guy</b> (born December 13, 1961), better known as <b>Big Shug</b>,  is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, a co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Gang_Starr" title="Gang Starr">Gang Starr</a><sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and a member of the <a href="/wiki/Gang_Starr_Foundation" title="Gang Starr Foundation">Gang Starr Foundation</a> collective.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Shug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in Boston, Cary spent much of his early life in Boston's <a href="/wiki/Mattapan,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Mattapan, Massachusetts">Mattapan</a> area (known locally as "Murdapan").<sup id="cite_ref-Cordor_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cordor-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Growing up, he forged a friendship with rapper <a href="/wiki/Guru_(rapper)" title="Guru (rapper)">Guru</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Cordor_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cordor-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> leading to them eventually founding the group <a href="/wiki/Gang_Starr" title="Gang Starr">Gang Starr</a> in <a href="/wiki/Morehouse_College" title="Morehouse College">Morehouse College</a> with Shug's younger brother DJ Suave D.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The group disbanded when Shug was imprisoned and eventually Guru moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>, where he added <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a> to Gang Starr.
</p><p>On his release, Shug again hooked up with Guru, joined the Gang Starr Foundation, and contributed to each of Gang Starr's albums, beginning with <i><a href="/wiki/Hard_to_Earn" title="Hard to Earn">Hard to Earn</a></i> in 1994 (on "F.A.L.A.").<sup id="cite_ref-Cordor_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cordor-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He also appeared on <a href="/wiki/DJ_Krush" title="DJ Krush">DJ Krush</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Meiso" title="Meiso">Meiso</a></i> in 1995 as well as "The Militia" from 1998's <i><a href="/wiki/Moment_of_Truth_(Gang_Starr_album)" title="Moment of Truth (Gang Starr album)">Moment of Truth</a></i>, and contributed to Guru's <a href="/wiki/Jazzmatazz" title="Jazzmatazz">Jazzmatazz</a> albums.<sup id="cite_ref-Frampton_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Frampton-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the 1990s, Shug recorded a few 12-inch singles produced by DJ Premier for <a href="/wiki/Payday_Records" title="Payday Records">Payday</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chrysalis_Records" title="Chrysalis Records">Chrysalis</a>, but his first proper solo album, <i>Who's Hard</i> was not released until 2005 and half of the songs were handled by DJ Premier.<sup id="cite_ref-Cordor_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cordor-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He signed to New York's <a href="/wiki/Babygrande_Records" title="Babygrande Records">Babygrande</a> in 2007, and released his second album, <i>Streetchamp</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Cordor_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cordor-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The album was produced by <a href="/wiki/MoSS" title="MoSS">MoSS</a> and DJ Premier and features guest appearances from DJ Premier and <a href="/wiki/Sean_Price" title="Sean Price">Sean Price</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hartlaub_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hartlaub-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A third solo album, <i>Otherside of the Game</i>, was released in November 2008, and features DJ Premier, <a href="/wiki/Bumpy_Knuckles" class="mw-redirect" title="Bumpy Knuckles">Bumpy Knuckles</a>, Billy Danze of <a href="/wiki/M.O.P." title="M.O.P.">M.O.P.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Termanology" title="Termanology">Termanology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blaq_Poet" title="Blaq Poet">Blaq Poet</a>, and Singapore Kane.  He performed at the 2009 Boston hemp fest.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is featured on the single "Tap Out", which appears on <a href="/wiki/M-Dot" title="M-Dot">M-Dot</a> &amp; DJ Jean Maron's 2010 album Run MPC. The video of "tap out" was directed by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Steven_TAPIA&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Steven TAPIA (page does not exist)">Steven TAPIA</a>. In 2010 he also collaborated with (artist) Aztech and (producer) Reel Drama on their album Hybrid Genetics.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2010 he collaborated with west coast hip hop artist Spinz <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> on a song called "Round Tha World" that was recorded while touring Europe. In 2012 he worked again with Dj Jean Maron for the release of the official remix of "Heavy" a posse cut with M-Dot, Benefit &amp; B.A.M (M.O.P/Lil Fame's cousin).
</p><p>In 2013 Guy appeared in two movies.  In the HBO movie "<a href="/wiki/Clear_History" title="Clear History">Clear History</a>," he was door security for the band <a href="/wiki/Chicago_(band)" title="Chicago (band)">Chicago</a>.  In "<a href="/wiki/The_Heat_(film)" title="The Heat (film)">The Heat</a>," starring Sandra Bullock and Melissa McCarthy, he was "Tough Guy #2."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He also appeared in  Boston-based films "The Town", and "Black Mass".
</p><p>Big Shug released his most recent studio album "Triple OGzus" in March 2015. Shug's upcoming project is entitled "The Living Room Project" according to his Instagram page. As of January 2019, there is no word on the release date of the album. In January 2019, Big Shug announced his new album will be entitled “The Diamond Report” while “The Living Room Project” has been rumored to have been shelved or pushed off but Shug has not confirmed this.
</p><p>A promotional single entitled "Still Big" for Shug's new album coming out later that month was released on October 7. On October 25, 2019, Big Shug released his seventh studio album "The Diamond Report". On the same day, Shug released the first single from the album "EMF" which was produced by long time friend of Guy, DJ Premier. The following week, Big Shug was featured on Gang Starr's newest album "<a href="/wiki/One_of_the_Best_Yet" title="One of the Best Yet">One of the Best Yet</a>" on two tracks, "One of the Best Yet (Big Shug Interlude)" and "Take Flight (Militia, Pt. 4)".
</p><p>Big Shug's music is known to frequently be featured on DJ Premier's radio show on Sirius XM.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Shug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012 and 2014, Big Shug led his Braintree Youth Basketball team to two championships. In 2013, he was defeated in the semi-final which led him to saying "Damn, these kids sign up to hoop, but they can't hoop. They damn bad." This caused an uproar from parents of players in the Braintree Youth Basketball organization. The BYB committee later handed him a two-game suspension which was appealed. After a local court session, the suspension was overturned due to Shug's right to the first amendment. This led to Shug being "pissed off". This made him go into the 2013-14 season on fire. He coached his team led by his son, Trumayne "The Big Dawg" Guy to a second championship in three years. Due to his tremendous season, Shug was awarded "BYB Coach of the Year". In his winning speech, he said "What I said last year was totally disrespectful to all players trying to get better at basketball, a game I love. I'm not apologizing fam' because it was my freedom of speech to say that but I'm happy that I was able to win this year and help make a group of good basketball players great."
</p>
