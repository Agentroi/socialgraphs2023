<p><b>Moshe Reuven Sheradsky</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span lang="he" dir="rtl">משה ראובן</span>), known professionally as <b>Moshe Reuven</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rabbi" title="Rabbi">rabbi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Billboard-charting</a> artist, singer, <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, songwriter, writer, <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes 30 Under 30</a> entrepreneur, <a href="/wiki/Music_executive" title="Music executive">executive</a>, and public speaker.
</p><p>Sheradsky first received recognition in music when his debut single went viral on several social media and streaming platforms.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Following the release of his debut, "You Are Not Alone", he signed to <a href="/wiki/Create_Music_Group" title="Create Music Group">Create Music Group</a>. The single managed to reach charts in five different countries, including becoming a <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Billboard</a> certified single in the US. 
</p><p>Sheradsky is a <a href="/wiki/Hasidic_Judaism" title="Hasidic Judaism">Hasidic Jew</a> and is one of four Orthodox Jews in history to have signed to a major international music label.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:7_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:8_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:9_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moshe_Reuven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sheradsky was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood,_Florida" title="Hollywood, Florida">Hollywood, Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> At a young age he was mainly known amongst family and friends for his abilities in basketball<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:5_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and math.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Sheradsky wrote his first song at the age of 8 as a form of self-expression.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In high school, he began making music with his friends and recorded his first songs with <a href="/wiki/Mr._Cheeks" title="Mr. Cheeks">Mr. Cheeks</a>’s former producer, Hayden.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It was during his later years in high school and the beginning of college, at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Central_Florida" title="University of Central Florida">The University of Central Florida</a>, that Sheradsky began to seek a more spiritual path, delving more deeply into <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Judaism" title="Orthodox Judaism">Orthodox Judaism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:9_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Throughout his late teens and college years, Sheradsky began devoting time daily to <a href="/wiki/Hitbonenus" class="mw-redirect" title="Hitbonenus">prayer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Torah_study" title="Torah study">Torah study</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:6_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He received mentorship from various rabbis. Eventually, Sheradsky received his <a href="/wiki/Semikhah" title="Semikhah">rabbinic ordination</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Rabbinical_College_of_America" title="Rabbinical College of America">Rabbinical College of America</a> in 2021, after spending multiple years studying in various <a href="/wiki/Yeshiva" title="Yeshiva">yeshivas</a> in America and Israel.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moshe_Reuven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_and_business">Writing and business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moshe_Reuven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Writing and business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sheradsky's first writings were published in <i><a href="/wiki/HuffPost" title="HuffPost">The Huffington Post</a></i> in 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-:3_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> and he made his first contribution to <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In 2018 he joined the Forbes Technology Council and Young Entrepreneurs Council, invite-only councils for founders and executives in companies worth $1M or more.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Sheradsky was primarily invited due to having been at the head of multiple <a href="/wiki/Startup_company" title="Startup company">technology startups</a>, including his own, <i>Wedu</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> that had reached $1M+ in revenue or capital raise, as either a founder, chief executive officer or CMO.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He's been a founding member of three startups that have reached the $1M+ range for either metrics.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> From 2020 to 2021, he contributed as a writer for <i><a href="/wiki/Entrepreneur_(magazine)" title="Entrepreneur (magazine)">Entrepreneur</a></i> magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, inspired by the teachings of the <a href="/wiki/Menachem_Mendel_Schneerson" title="Menachem Mendel Schneerson">Lubavitcher Rebbe</a>, Sheradsky began writing on topics of <a href="/wiki/Hasidic_philosophy" title="Hasidic philosophy">chassidus</a> for <a href="/wiki/Thrive_Global" title="Thrive Global">Thrive Global</a> and Live Kabbalah,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> as well on <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a>. He was appointed to this position after the founder of Live Kabbalah read his writings in <i>Huffington Post</i> and saw that he had a talent for blending Chassidic teachings with practical life.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He quickly amassed a following, primarily on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>, sharing from <a href="/wiki/Hasidic_philosophy" title="Hasidic philosophy">Chassidus</a> and regular day-to-day life via quotes and music. He became the only Hasidic Jew and rabbi on the platform to surpass 1 million followers.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moshe_Reuven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2020, Sheradsky released his debut single, "You Are Not Alone".<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The song was aimed at helping those dealing with issues during the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The song had a local and international impact,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> with signs being placed alongside roads as a reminder to drivers, stating "You Are Not Alone". Over a year after its release, the song became a <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a>-certified single as part of a compilation that reached No. 22 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Billboard charts</a> for digital album sales.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The success of "You Are Not Alone" caught the attention of a record executive at <a href="/wiki/Create_Music_Group" title="Create Music Group">Create Music Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He was then connected with their team's <a href="/wiki/A%26R" class="mw-redirect" title="A&amp;R">A&amp;R</a> reps, who concluded that they should sign him. Upon signing his first record deal, Sheradsky became the fourth Orthodox Jew to sign to a top record label, alongside <a href="/wiki/Matisyahu" title="Matisyahu">Matisyahu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Clare" title="Alex Clare">Alex Clare</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Shulem_Lemmer" title="Shulem Lemmer">Shulem Lemmer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> He's the only Orthodox Jew currently active on a top record label, with the others not actively releasing music with the labels they had signed to whether due to contracts running out or other reasons.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2022, Sheradsky released the song "Say" with <a href="/wiki/Julian_Marley" title="Julian Marley">Julian Marley</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> included Sheradsky in their Global Artist Spotlight, highlighting the song.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Throughout the latter half of 2022, Sheradsky performed shows with as many as 3,000 and 5,000 people attending,<sup id="cite_ref-:8_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> as well as giving private performances throughout the United States. Sheradsky and Marley performed together in Florida for the first time in mid-December.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> In the same year, Sheradsky was included in the <a href="/wiki/Forbes_30_Under_30" title="Forbes 30 Under 30"><i>Forbes</i> 30 Under 30</a> list.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> In August, <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> included Moshe in their Global Artists Spotlight List for 2022. In 2023, he additionally became a member of the Recording Academy.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
