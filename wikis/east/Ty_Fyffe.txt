<p><b>Tyrone Gregory Fyffe</b> (born June 4, 1971), also known as <b>Sugarless</b>, is an American record producer from <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, New York City. During his career he produced tracks for <a href="/wiki/MC_Lyte" title="MC Lyte">MC Lyte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foxy_Brown_(rapper)" title="Foxy Brown (rapper)">Foxy Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canibus" title="Canibus">Canibus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jayo_Felony" title="Jayo Felony">Jayo Felony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Pen" title="Queen Pen">Queen Pen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>, <a href="/wiki/54th_Platoon" title="54th Platoon">54th Platoon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>, remixed songs for <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Party_(band)" title="The Party (band)">The Party</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Flush_(rapper)" title="Royal Flush (rapper)">Royal Flush</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robin_Thicke" title="Robin Thicke">Robin Thicke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, and collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Riley" title="Teddy Riley">Teddy Riley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erick_Sermon" title="Erick Sermon">Erick Sermon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irv_Gotti" title="Irv Gotti">Irv Gotti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>.
</p><p>Fyffe met <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Riley" title="Teddy Riley">Teddy Riley</a> through fellow music engineer Franklin Grant.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 1992, Fyffe joined Riley, Grant, David Wynn and <a href="/wiki/Wreckx-n-Effect" title="Wreckx-n-Effect">Wreckx-n-Effect</a> on the production duties of <i><a href="/wiki/Hard_or_Smooth" title="Hard or Smooth">Hard or Smooth</a></i>, which spawned the <a href="/wiki/Hit_song" title="Hit song">hit song</a> "<a href="/wiki/Rump_Shaker_(song)" title="Rump Shaker (song)">Rump Shaker</a>". In January 1993, the single received 2× Platinum certification by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> and the album went Platinum. Fyffe continued to work in a team with Riley and Grant, and from 1993 to 1994 he contributed on <a href="/wiki/Father_MC" title="Father MC">Father MC</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Lyte" title="MC Lyte">MC Lyte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shinehead" title="Shinehead">Shinehead</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ed_Lover" title="Ed Lover">Ed Lover</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Dr%C3%A9" title="Doctor Dré">Doctor Dré</a> projects.
</p><p>In 1995, he eventually began credited as 'Sugarless' during his collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Erick_Sermon" title="Erick Sermon">Erick Sermon</a>. By the end of the 1990s, his portfolio includes works with Sermon, <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, <a href="/wiki/Redman_(rapper)" title="Redman (rapper)">Redman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Craig_Mack" title="Craig Mack">Craig Mack</a>, <a href="/wiki/McGruff_(rapper)" title="McGruff (rapper)">McGruff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foxy_Brown_(rapper)" title="Foxy Brown (rapper)">Foxy Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Pun" title="Big Pun">Big Pun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Murder_Inc._(rap_group)" title="Murder Inc. (rap group)">Murder Inc.</a> among others. He produced <a href="/wiki/A%2B_(rapper)" title="A+ (rapper)">A+</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Enjoy_Yourself_(A%2B_song)" title="Enjoy Yourself (A+ song)">Enjoy Yourself</a>" to international success, and provided additional production to <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Judgement_Day_(Method_Man_song)" title="Judgement Day (Method Man song)">Judgement Day</a>". After working on <a href="/wiki/Murder_Inc._(rap_group)" title="Murder Inc. (rap group)">Murder Inc.</a>'s "Murdergram" song from <i><a href="/wiki/Streets_Is_Watching_(soundtrack)" title="Streets Is Watching (soundtrack)">Streets Is Watching Soundtrack</a></i>, he began to collaborate with <a href="/wiki/Irv_Gotti" title="Irv Gotti">Irv Gotti</a> and became a recurring producer for <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a> albums and fellow <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-A-Fella</a> acts, such as <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amil" title="Amil">Amil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beanie_Sigel" title="Beanie Sigel">Beanie Sigel</a>.
</p><p>Since 2001, Fyffe has begun collaborating with hip hop artists across the United States, since up to 2000, his entire clientele consisted of <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast hip hop</a> musicians. Thus, he had a hand in the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_hip_hop" title="West Coast hip hop">West Coast hip hop</a> artists such as <a href="/wiki/Jayo_Felony" title="Jayo Felony">Jayo Felony</a>'s 2001 <i><a href="/wiki/Crip_Hop" title="Crip Hop">Crip Hop</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a>'s 2002 <i><a href="/wiki/Man_vs._Machine" title="Man vs. Machine">Man vs. Machine</a></i>, Midwest hip hop acts such as <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>'s 2002 <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_City_(Royce_da_5%279%22_album)" title="Rock City (Royce da 5&#39;9&quot; album)">Rock City</a></i> and 2004 <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Is_Certain" title="Death Is Certain">Death Is Certain</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs-n-Harmony" title="Bone Thugs-n-Harmony">Bone Thugs-n-Harmony</a>'s 2007 <i><a href="/wiki/Strength_%26_Loyalty" title="Strength &amp; Loyalty">Strength &amp; Loyalty</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Twista" title="Twista">Twista</a>'s 2017 <i>Crook County</i>, and <a href="/wiki/Southern_hip_hop" title="Southern hip hop">Southern hip hop</a> groups such as <a href="/wiki/UGK" title="UGK">UGK</a>'s 2002 <i><a href="/wiki/Side_Hustles" title="Side Hustles">Side Hustles</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/54th_Platoon" title="54th Platoon">54th Platoon</a>'s 2003 <i><a href="/wiki/All_or_Nothin%27_(54th_Platoon_album)" title="All or Nothin&#39; (54th Platoon album)">All or Nothin'</a></i>. After the success of "<a href="/wiki/Straight_to_the_Bank" title="Straight to the Bank">Straight to the Bank</a>", which was co-produced with <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, Fyffe became a recurring producer for several <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a> projects, such as 2008 <i><a href="/wiki/T%C2%B7O%C2%B7S_(Terminate_on_Sight)" title="T·O·S (Terminate on Sight)">T·O·S (Terminate on Sight)</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>'s 2009 <i><a href="/wiki/Before_I_Self_Destruct" title="Before I Self Destruct">Before I Self Destruct</a></i> and 2014 <i><a href="/wiki/Animal_Ambition" title="Animal Ambition">Animal Ambition</a></i> albums and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Yayo" title="Tony Yayo">Tony Yayo</a>'s 2011 <i>El Chapo</i> mixtape. In 2010, Fyffe released a mixtape, <i>The Pyrex</i>, hosted by DJ Superstar Jay.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 2010 to 2020, he produced tracks for <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a> ("Smooth Sailing", which appeared on Wu-Tang Killa Bees' <i>Pollen: The Swarm Part Three</i>, and its remix version was included in <i><a href="/wiki/Wu-Massacre" title="Wu-Massacre">Wu-Massacre</a></i>), Redman's 2010 <i><a href="/wiki/Reggie_(album)" title="Reggie (album)">Reggie</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>'s 2011 <i><a href="/wiki/Master_of_Ceremonies_(Styles_P_album)" title="Master of Ceremonies (Styles P album)">Master of Ceremonies</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Lazee" title="Lazee">Lazee</a>'s 2011 mixtape <i>One Way Ticket</i>, <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a>'s 2012 <i><a href="/wiki/H.N.I.C._3" title="H.N.I.C. 3">H.N.I.C. 3</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>'s 2015 <i><a href="/wiki/Top_5_Dead_or_Alive" title="Top 5 Dead or Alive">Top 5 Dead or Alive</a></i>, and Erick Sermon's 2019 <i><a href="/wiki/Vernia_(album)" title="Vernia (album)">Vernia</a></i>.
</p><p>Alongside fellow American producers Swede of <a href="/wiki/808_Mafia" title="808 Mafia">808 Mafia</a>, Mars of <a href="/wiki/1500_or_Nothin%27" title="1500 or Nothin&#39;">1500 or Nothin'</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rockwilder" title="Rockwilder">Rockwilder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scoop_DeVille" title="Scoop DeVille">Scoop DeVille</a> and Tony Dofat, and Parisian producers Nicko of Soulchildren, Oliver of Drum Dreamers, Cedric Pistol, Nick D. Green and Tarik Azzouz, and Amsterdamer producer Shroom, Fyffe was one of twelve juries of the 3rd annual Beat Battle Music Producer Challenge, which took place at Gnarly Music in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has his own studio, The Music Factory, in <a href="/wiki/West_Hempstead,_New_York" title="West Hempstead, New York">West Hempstead, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
