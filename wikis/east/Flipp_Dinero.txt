<p><b>Christopher Saint Victor</b> (born November 16, 1995), better known by his stage name <b>Flipp Dinero</b>, is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He is best known for his 2018 single "Leave Me Alone", which is certified 4× platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>, and has since received over 600 million plays on <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a>. Being from <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York, he was later able to connect with another Brooklyn, New York rapper, <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Badass</a> who later co-signed him along with the record labels <a href="/wiki/Cinematic_Music_Group" title="Cinematic Music Group">Cinematic Music Group</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a>, and <a href="/wiki/We_the_Best_Music_Group" title="We the Best Music Group">We the Best Music group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, Dinero gained recognition when football wide-receiver <a href="/wiki/Odell_Beckham_Jr." title="Odell Beckham Jr.">Odell Beckham Jr.</a> danced to "<a href="/wiki/Leave_Me_Alone_(Flipp_Dinero_song)" title="Leave Me Alone (Flipp Dinero song)">Leave Me Alone</a>" and when <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">NBA</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Bell" title="Jordan Bell">Jordan Bell</a> rapping to the song on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> Causing the song to go viral.<sup id="cite_ref-bb23_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bb23-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The song peaked at 20 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> in 2019<sup id="cite_ref-Hot_1003_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hot_1003-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and later went <a href="/wiki/Triple_platinum" class="mw-redirect" title="Triple platinum">triple platinum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flipp_Dinero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Flipp Dinero is from the <a href="/wiki/Canarsie,_Brooklyn" title="Canarsie, Brooklyn">Canarsie</a> neighborhood of Brooklyn and of <a href="/wiki/Haitians" title="Haitians">Haitian</a> descent. He was raised in a Christian home and grew up attending private school in New York.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flipp_Dinero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Flipp Dinero's music combines R&amp;B and gospel singing with rapping, hip-hop, and trap music. His music career began in 2016 with support of his brother, Lows, a sound engineer. Lows promoted Flipp's music and connected him with other artists.<sup id="cite_ref-:72_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:72-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> During this time, Flipp Dinero received early recognition from <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn-based</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Badass</a> who had heard his tracks, Smoke2This, I Do, and others.<sup id="cite_ref-:72_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:72-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Flipp Dinero signed with Cinematic Music Group that summer of 2016<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bb23_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bb23-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and then later signed with record labels, Epic Records and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled's</a> We the Best Music Group.
</p><p>In 2017, Flipp Dinero was featured five times on Spotify's popular hip-hop 'Most Necessary' playlist. Later in 2017, Flipp made his first performance on Complex, with his single "I do." This led to the release of his debut EP titled <i>The Guala Way</i> which was released in June 2017 and which reached over 2 million streams.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With the help of his brother, Flipp Dinero recorded and released his hit song, "<a href="/wiki/Leave_Me_Alone_(Flipp_Dinero_song)" title="Leave Me Alone (Flipp Dinero song)">Leave Me Alone</a>" in 2018. The song was written as an expression of his troubles with a woman that would not leave him alone.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In 2018 his song "Leave Me Alone" aired on multiple radio stations.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> "Leave Me Alone" was later recognized by the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Browns" title="Cleveland Browns">Cleveland Browns'</a> <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a> football player, <a href="/wiki/Odell_Beckham_Jr." title="Odell Beckham Jr.">Odell Beckham Jr.</a>, who posted a dancing video to this song on Instagram, which led the song to "<a href="/wiki/Go_viral" class="mw-redirect" title="Go viral">go viral</a>" and increase rapidly in popularity.<sup id="cite_ref-bb23_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bb23-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Months later, famous <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>, singer, <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>, featured "<a href="/wiki/Leave_Me_Alone_(Flipp_Dinero_song)" title="Leave Me Alone (Flipp Dinero song)">Leave Me Alone</a>" on his Instagram Story as a song which had inspired him while producing his new album <a href="/wiki/Scorpion_(Drake_album)" title="Scorpion (Drake album)">Scorpion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:6_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> "Leave Me Alone", became certified platinum by the RIAA on January 15, 2019<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and later went <a href="/wiki/Double-platinum" class="mw-redirect" title="Double-platinum">double-platinum</a> on May 23, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:8_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Flipp Dinero was featured on hip-hop media company <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a> 2019 Freshman class after performing his freestyle "What I do".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Later in 2019, Flipp released his album Love for Guala,<sup id="cite_ref-:12_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:12-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> which reached #132 on US Billboard 200.<sup id="cite_ref-:13_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Flipp Dinero's record, "How I Move", came to be because he wanted to share his current experiences in Canarsie as a rapper being from an area that where not many people have or are able to obtain big money in their pockets.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Flipp includes the word "Guala" into his album titles. The lifestyle term is a reference to Gods, Unique, Accolade, Life, Acquired and holds great meaning to him.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
