<p><b>Christopher Douglass Roney</b> (born December 15, 1969), known by the stage name <b>Cool C</b>, is an American former rapper active in the late 1980s. He is also known for his involvement in the murder of <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Police_Department" title="Philadelphia Police Department">Philadelphia Police</a> officer <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_Lauretha_Vaird" title="Murder of Lauretha Vaird">Lauretha Vaird</a> during a bank robbery in January 1996, for which he was sentenced to death. He is currently on death row.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cool_C&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cool_C&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the mid-1980s, Roney was an original member of the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>-based Hilltop Hustlers hip hop crew. His 1987 debut single, "Juice Crew Dis", which took aim at the New York-based hip hop crew run by influential rap producer <a href="/wiki/Marley_Marl" title="Marley Marl">Marley Marl</a> (a group that included <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a> and <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a>), gained Roney a good amount of attention.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A pair of 1988 singles for Hilltop and City Beat Records landed Roney a contract with <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>, where he released two full-length solo albums: his debut <i><a href="/wiki/I_Gotta_Habit" title="I Gotta Habit">I Gotta Habit</a></i> in 1989 and <i>Life in the Ghetto</i> in 1990. Both albums stayed on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> for numerous weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="C.E.B.">C.E.B.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cool_C&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: C.E.B.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1991, Roney put his solo career aside to join <a href="/wiki/Hardcore_hip_hop" title="Hardcore hip hop">hardcore hip hop</a> group C.E.B. (which stood for "Countin' Endless Bank") with fellow Philadelphia rappers Warren McGlone (<a href="/wiki/Steady_B" title="Steady B">Steady B</a>) and Ultimate Eaze. To disappointing sales and reviews, the trio released their only album, <i>Countin' Endless Bank</i>, on <a href="/wiki/Ruffhouse_Records" title="Ruffhouse Records">Ruffhouse Records</a> in 1993. The single "Get the Point" reached number 5 on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Songs" title="Hot Rap Songs">Hot Rap Singles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1992, an independent label, Rags to Riches Records, released the single "Get the Point." After extraordinary success with the single, Rags to Riches Records founders, Malik Abd-hadi and Bilal "bilally b" Salaam signed the trio Cool C, Steady B and Ultimate Eaze (C.E.B.) to Ruffhouse Records. At that point, Abd-hadi and Salaam became their managers. The project was short-lived mainly because Ultimate Eaze had legal troubles and never showed up for a promotional tour to promote the release of the first album on Ruffhouse. The group was dropped before Steady B, and Cool C got back off tour.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Murder_conviction">Murder conviction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cool_C&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Murder conviction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_Lauretha_Vaird" title="Murder of Lauretha Vaird">Murder of Lauretha Vaird</a></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1066479718"><table class="infobox biography vcard"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="font-size:125%;"><div class="fn" style="display:inline">Christopher Roney</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday">1969-12-15</span>) </span>December 15, 1969<span class="noprint ForceAgeToShow"> (age&#160;53)</span></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Other&#160;names</th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Cool C</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Criminal status</th><td class="infobox-data category">In custody on death row</td></tr><tr><td colspan="2" class="infobox-full-data"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1066479718"></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Conviction" title="Conviction">Conviction(s)</a></span></th><td class="infobox-data">Guilty on all counts, October 30, 1996; sentenced to death on December 13, 1996</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Criminal charge</th><td class="infobox-data">First-degree murder, bank robbery, grand larceny</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Penalty</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Capital_punishment" title="Capital punishment">Death</a><br /><small>(Granted stay of execution as of January 8, 2015)</small></td></tr><tr style="display:none"><td colspan="2">
</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>On January 2, 1996, during the same time period that he was recording a comeback <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Philly_MC_Cool_C_awaits_his_fate_on_death_row_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Philly_MC_Cool_C_awaits_his_fate_on_death_row-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Roney, along with C.E.B. bandmate McGlone (a.k.a. <a href="/wiki/Steady_B" title="Steady B">Steady B</a>), and another local Philadelphia rapper, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Canty" class="mw-redirect" title="Mark Canty">Mark Canty</a>, attempted a bank robbery at a <a href="/wiki/PNC_Financial_Services" title="PNC Financial Services">PNC bank</a> branch in <a href="/wiki/Feltonville,_Philadelphia" title="Feltonville, Philadelphia">Feltonville</a>. During the botched heist, Roney shot and killed <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Police_Department" title="Philadelphia Police Department">Philadelphia Police</a> officer <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_Lauretha_Vaird" title="Murder of Lauretha Vaird">Lauretha Vaird</a>, who responded to the bank's silent alarm.<sup id="cite_ref-Law_Enforcement_News&#58;_Around_the_Nation_–_Pennsylvania_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Law_Enforcement_News:_Around_the_Nation_–_Pennsylvania-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Shoot_the_Messenger?_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shoot_the_Messenger?-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> As he exited the bank, Roney exchanged fire with another police officer, before he and Canty dropped their weapons at the scene and fled in a stolen <a href="/wiki/Minivan" title="Minivan">minivan</a> driven by McGlone.<sup id="cite_ref-Pennsylvania_v._Roney_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pennsylvania_v._Roney-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Philadelphia_police_charge_rappers_with_female_officer&#39;s_murder;_suspect_still_at_large_–_alleged_accomplice_of_Warren_McGlone_and_Christopher_Roney_sought_in_shooting_of_city&#39;s_first_Black_woman_police_officer,_Lauretha_Vaird_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Philadelphia_police_charge_rappers_with_female_officer&#39;s_murder;_suspect_still_at_large_–_alleged_accomplice_of_Warren_McGlone_and_Christopher_Roney_sought_in_shooting_of_city&#39;s_first_Black_woman_police_officer,_Lauretha_Vaird-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Roney was arrested on October 30, 1996, and convicted of <a href="/wiki/First-degree_murder" class="mw-redirect" title="First-degree murder">first-degree murder</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Law_Enforcement_News&#58;_Around_the_Nation_–_Pennsylvania_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Law_Enforcement_News:_Around_the_Nation_–_Pennsylvania-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> At his subsequent sentencing hearing, Roney was sentenced to death by <a href="/wiki/Lethal_injection" title="Lethal injection">lethal injection</a>. On January 10, 2006, his death warrant was signed by <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> Governor <a href="/wiki/Ed_Rendell" title="Ed Rendell">Ed Rendell</a>, and his execution date was set for March 9, 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-Rapper_Cool_C&#39;s_Execution_Warrant_Signed_by_PA_Governor_Ed_Rendell_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rapper_Cool_C&#39;s_Execution_Warrant_Signed_by_PA_Governor_Ed_Rendell-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He was granted a <a href="/wiki/Stay_of_execution" title="Stay of execution">stay of execution</a> from Pennsylvania Judge Gary Glazer on February 1, 2006, until all post-conviction litigation is resolved.<sup id="cite_ref-Philadelphia_Rapper_Cool_C_Granted_Temporary_Stay_Of_Execution_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Philadelphia_Rapper_Cool_C_Granted_Temporary_Stay_Of_Execution-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> His execution was set for January 8, 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-Philadelphia_Rap_Icon_Cool_C_To_Be_Executed_In_January_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Philadelphia_Rap_Icon_Cool_C_To_Be_Executed_In_January-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> but Roney was once again granted a stay of execution from Pennsylvania Judge <a href="/wiki/L._Felipe_Restrepo" title="L. Felipe Restrepo">L. Felipe Restrepo</a> on December 5, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Philly_Rapper_Cool_C_Granted_Stay_of_Execution_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Philly_Rapper_Cool_C_Granted_Stay_of_Execution-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Roney has maintained his innocence throughout the <a href="/wiki/Trial" title="Trial">trial</a> and <a href="/wiki/Appeal" title="Appeal">appeals</a> process, despite the testimony of three eyewitnesses who placed him at the scene of the robbery, as well as <a href="/wiki/Ballistics" title="Ballistics">ballistic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Forensic_science" title="Forensic science">forensic</a> evidence and surveillance video that linked him to the murder.<sup id="cite_ref-Philly_MC_Cool_C_awaits_his_fate_on_death_row_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Philly_MC_Cool_C_awaits_his_fate_on_death_row-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pennsylvania_v._Roney_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pennsylvania_v._Roney-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He is currently an inmate at the <a href="/wiki/State_Correctional_Institution_%E2%80%93_Phoenix" title="State Correctional Institution – Phoenix">State Correctional Institution – Phoenix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Rapper_Cool_C&#39;s_Execution_Warrant_Signed_by_PA_Governor_Ed_Rendell_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rapper_Cool_C&#39;s_Execution_Warrant_Signed_by_PA_Governor_Ed_Rendell-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> His inmate ID number is DF1973.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_Inmate_Locator_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA_Inmate_Locator-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cool_C&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In prison, Cool C converted and accepted Islam as his religion. The exact period he converted is not clear.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
