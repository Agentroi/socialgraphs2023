<p><b>Patrick Owen Baril</b> (born January 23, 1982), professionally known as <b>Statik Selektah</b>, is an American record producer, disc jockey (DJ) and radio personality originally from the <a href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a> area. He is also the founder of Showoff Records. In 2010, he formed the hip hop group <a href="/wiki/1982_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="1982 (group)">1982</a>, alongside American rapper <a href="/wiki/Termanology" title="Termanology">Termanology</a>. He has resided in New York City since 2004.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Statik_Selektah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Statik Selektah was born on January 23, 1982, in <a href="/wiki/Lawrence,_Massachusetts" title="Lawrence, Massachusetts">Lawrence, Massachusetts</a>. He was introduced to music at a young age, experimenting with his parents' eight-track machine, cassette recorders, and turntable.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> By middle school, he was already DJing school functions, but he was inspired to be a professional DJ after hearing <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Funkmaster_Flex" title="Funkmaster Flex">Funkmaster Flex</a> scratch and mix records on Hot 97 in New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Statik began doing radio at Phillips Exeter Academy's WPEA, where he also occasionally DJ'd some of the Afro-Latino Society Parties.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopgalaxy1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopgalaxy1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Naming himself DJ Statik, (the "Selektah" came later after hearing a local reggae artist say it unconsciously<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopgalaxy1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopgalaxy1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>) he began to start to DJ at clubs and private parties up and down the New England Coast. In 2000, Selektah moved back to Massachusetts from New Hampshire to pursue an audio production degree at Boston's <a href="/wiki/New_England_Institute_of_Art" title="New England Institute of Art">New England Institute of Art</a>. Around this time, Statik started to put out his own <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> series titled "Spell My Name Right." In 2003, Statik created ShowOff Marketing, which has had clients such as <a href="/wiki/Reebok" title="Reebok">Reebok</a>, <a href="/wiki/G-Unit_Records" title="G-Unit Records">G-Unit Records</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Records" title="Virgin Records">Virgin Records</a>, Capital, <a href="/wiki/Universal_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Records">Universal Records</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puff_Daddy" class="mw-redirect" title="Puff Daddy">Puff Daddy</a>'s Vote or Die campaign and many others.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopgalaxy1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopgalaxy1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2006, Statik Selektah made Showoff into a record label and put out <a href="/wiki/Termanology" title="Termanology">Termanology</a>'s Out the Gate as the labels first release. In 2007, Statik released his first LP, titled <i>Spell My Name Right</i>, and came out with a second <i><a href="/wiki/Stick_2_the_Script" title="Stick 2 the Script">Stick 2 the Script</a></i> in 2009. In 2010, Statik Selektah dropped another album <i><a href="/wiki/100_Proof:_The_Hangover" title="100 Proof: The Hangover">100 Proof: The Hangover</a></i> which reached 37 on Billboard's Heatseeker Albums Chart.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Besides dropping his own albums, he also has produced on many other artist's projects. Statik currently has a show on Thursday nights on <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Shade_45" title="Shade 45">Shade 45</a> XM/Sirus Radio Station.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Statik Selektah appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto:_Episodes_from_Liberty_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand Theft Auto: Episodes from Liberty City">Grand Theft Auto: Episodes from Liberty City</a></i>, featured as a DJ on the hip hop radio station The Beat 102.7.
</p><p>While recording with rapper <a href="/wiki/Freeway_(rapper)" title="Freeway (rapper)">Freeway</a> on <a href="/wiki/Ustream" class="mw-redirect" title="Ustream">Ustream</a>, he announced a song off his next album, <i>Population Control</i>, called "Groupie Love" which features <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>.  This track was included on the final release of the album in 2011.
</p><p>Since 2015, the publishing interest of Statik Selektah's catalog has been represented by Reservoir Media Management.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2017, Statik announced with <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a> that he signed a management deal with <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a> plus announcing his eighth studio album titled <i><a href="/wiki/8_(Statik_Selektah_album)" title="8 (Statik Selektah album)">8</a></i>. It was later released on December 8, 2017.
</p><p>On November 27, 2020, Statik released his ninth studio album titled The Balancing Act featuring collaborations with Joey Badass, Black Thought and Bun B.
</p>
