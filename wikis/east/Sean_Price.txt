<p><b>Sean Duval Price</b> (March 17, 1972&#160;&#8211;&#32;August 8, 2015)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was an American rapper and member of the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> collective <a href="/wiki/Boot_Camp_Clik" title="Boot Camp Clik">Boot Camp Clik</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_bio_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic_bio-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was one half of the duo <a href="/wiki/Heltah_Skeltah" title="Heltah Skeltah">Heltah Skeltah</a>, performing under the name <b>Ruck</b>, along with partner Rock.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sean_Price&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Growing up in the borough of <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, Price was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Decepticons_(gang)" title="Decepticons (gang)">Decepticons</a> in high school.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sean_Price&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A member of the underground hip hop collective <a href="/wiki/Boot_Camp_Clik" title="Boot Camp Clik">Boot Camp Clik</a>, Price first appeared on <a href="/wiki/Smif-N-Wessun" title="Smif-N-Wessun">Smif-N-Wessun</a>'s 1995 album <i><a href="/wiki/Dah_Shinin%27" title="Dah Shinin&#39;">Dah Shinin'</a></i>. He would soon resurface in 1996 as one half of the critically acclaimed Brooklyn duo <a href="/wiki/Heltah_Skeltah" title="Heltah Skeltah">Heltah Skeltah</a>, who released their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Nocturnal_(Heltah_Skeltah_album)" title="Nocturnal (Heltah Skeltah album)">Nocturnal</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Duck_Down_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Duck Down Records">Duck Down Records</a> that year. Heltah Skeltah would go on to release two more albums: 1998's <i><a href="/wiki/Magnum_Force_(album)" title="Magnum Force (album)">Magnum Force</a></i> and 2008's <i><a href="/wiki/D.I.R.T._(Heltah_Skeltah_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="D.I.R.T. (Heltah Skeltah album)">D.I.R.T.</a></i>.
</p><p>In the 2000s, Price established himself as a strong force in the underground hip hop community. He was featured on Grand Theft Auto 3's GAME FM radio station in 2001. In 2005, Price released his first solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Monkey_Barz" title="Monkey Barz">Monkey Barz</a></i>, to critical acclaim. He followed this in 2007 with <i><a href="/wiki/Jesus_Price_Supastar" title="Jesus Price Supastar">Jesus Price Supastar</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Mic_Tyson" title="Mic Tyson">Mic Tyson</a></i> in 2012.
</p><p>Price was interviewed on the online series <i>Grand Angel TV</i> in 2008<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and 2010,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/Paid_Dues" title="Paid Dues">Paid Dues</a> performance footage alongside Boot Camp Clik and <a href="/wiki/Drew_%22Dru_Ha%22_Friedman" class="mw-redirect" title="Drew &quot;Dru Ha&quot; Friedman">Dru Ha</a>, respectively.
</p><p>In 2011, Price was a judge on the <i>Ultimate MC</i> TV show alongside <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>, <a href="/wiki/Organik_(musician)" title="Organik (musician)">Organik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Planet_Asia" title="Planet Asia">Planet Asia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pharoahe_Monch" title="Pharoahe Monch">Pharoahe Monch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Also in 2011, he formed the group <a href="/wiki/Random_Axe" title="Random Axe">Random Axe</a> with <a href="/wiki/Guilty_Simpson" title="Guilty Simpson">Guilty Simpson</a> and producer <a href="/wiki/Black_Milk" title="Black Milk">Black Milk</a>.
</p><p>He had a planned collaborative album with rapper <a href="/wiki/Ill_Bill" title="Ill Bill">Ill Bill</a> in the works, called <i>The Pill</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i>Songs in the Key of Price</i>, a mixtape recorded before Price's death, was released posthumously on August 21, 2015. The posthumous release of his fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Imperius_Rex" title="Imperius Rex">Imperius Rex</a></i>, followed in August 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sean_Price&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On August 8, 2015, Price died at the age of 43.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> A statement from his reps confirmed his death, while adding that he died in his sleep.
</p><p>"It is with beyond a heavy heart that Duck Down Music is sadly confirming that Sean Price passed away early this morning in his Brooklyn apartment, Saturday, August 8th, 2015," read the statement. "The cause of death is currently unknown, but it was reported that he died in his sleep. He's survived by his three children."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sean_Price&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Price was a cousin of <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-A-Fella Records</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Bleek" title="Memphis Bleek">Memphis Bleek</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and actor <a href="/wiki/Michael_K._Williams" title="Michael K. Williams">Michael K. Williams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He was also close friends with actor <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Phillippe" title="Ryan Phillippe">Ryan Phillippe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Price lived in <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Brooklyn" title="Brownsville, Brooklyn">Brownsville, Brooklyn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He was a <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>, having converted in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
