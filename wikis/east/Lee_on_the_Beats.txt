<p><b>Anthony Leedel Norris</b> (born February 11, 1990) is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>, professionally known as <b>Lee on the Beats</b>. Lee has produced for artists such as <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kid_Ink" title="Kid Ink">Kid Ink</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hood" title="Ace Hood">Ace Hood</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Freeway_(rapper)" title="Freeway (rapper)">Freeway</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, Lee was signed to <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>'s <a href="/wiki/We_the_Best_Music_Group" title="We the Best Music Group">We the Best Music Group</a>. He is best known for producing French Montana’s “<a href="/wiki/Pop_That" title="Pop That">Pop That</a>” and co-producing Khaled’s “<a href="/wiki/Do_You_Mind_(DJ_Khaled_song)" title="Do You Mind (DJ Khaled song)">Do You Mind</a>”.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_on_the_Beats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a teenager Lee on the Beats started <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapping</a> and begun <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">producing</a> for local acts in <a href="/wiki/Queens,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Queens, New York">Queens, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bestofbothoffices1_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bestofbothoffices1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His family was more involved in the business and executive side of things. For example, His uncle, Skane Dolla was <a href="/wiki/DJ_Clue" title="DJ Clue">DJ Clue</a>'s manager as well as one of the owners of <a href="/wiki/Desert_Storm_Records" title="Desert Storm Records">Desert Storm Records</a>. He helped A&amp;R projects for artists like <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a> for <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam</a>. Lee was around for all of it, so he learned a lot growing up. Lee started a team called, The Amazinz with his cousin Roc Da Producer. From there they started looking for producers they could build the team. The current members are Roc, Dane, Jabarrie, Murrille, Mae N. Maejor, Robbin, and LDB.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Lee kn the Beats early major production credits included <a href="/wiki/Kid_Ink" title="Kid Ink">Kid Ink</a>'s "Is It You" and co-production on <a href="/wiki/Freeway_(rapper)" title="Freeway (rapper)">Freeway</a>'s "Superstar" featuring <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, it only took Lee On the Beats three hours to create the beat for "<a href="/wiki/Pop_That" title="Pop That">Pop That</a>", which he then gave to New York rapper <a href="/wiki/Chinx_Drugz" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinx Drugz">Chinx Drugz</a>, who then passed it on to <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rapradar1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rapradar1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/2_Live_Crew" title="2 Live Crew">2 Live Crew</a> sampling song, that would feature rappers <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, became the <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> from Montana's debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Excuse_My_French_(album)" title="Excuse My French (album)">Excuse My French</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The song would end up peaking at number 36 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, and would be shortly certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">Platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2013, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a> announced that he had signed Lee On the Beats, to his <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a> <a href="/wiki/We_the_Best_Music_Group" title="We the Best Music Group">We the Best Music Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Following his signing, he produced two song's on <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hood" title="Ace Hood">Ace Hood</a>'s fourth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Trials_%26_Tribulations_(Ace_Hood_album)" title="Trials &amp; Tribulations (Ace Hood album)">Trials &amp; Tribulations</a></i> including the single "<a href="/wiki/We_Outchea" title="We Outchea">We Outchea</a>". In July 2013, Lee On the Beats was named one of the "top ten young producers on the rise", by <a href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Entertainment Television">BET</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On August 2, 2013, DJ Khaled released "<a href="/wiki/I_Wanna_Be_With_You_(DJ_Khaled_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="I Wanna Be With You (DJ Khaled song)">I Wanna Be With You</a>", the second single from his album <i><a href="/wiki/Suffering_from_Success" title="Suffering from Success">Suffering from Success</a></i>, which was produced by Lee On the Beats and featured Rick Ross, <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rapradar2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rapradar2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He also co-produced two more songs on the album, "You Don't Want These Problems" and "I'm Still".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In October 2013, Lee revealed that he would release an instrumental project in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-rapradar2_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rapradar2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The instrumental mixtape titled <i>Catch the Vibe</i> was released on February 11, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_style">Production style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_on_the_Beats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lee on the Beats has said that his production style is influenced by <a href="/w/index.php?title=IC3T3K_2K&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="IC3T3K 2K (page does not exist)">IC3T3K 2K</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Leslie" title="Ryan Leslie">Ryan Leslie</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Beatz" class="mw-redirect" title="Swiss Beatz">Swiss Beatz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rapradar1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rapradar1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
