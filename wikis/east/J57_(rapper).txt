<p><b>James Victor Heinz</b>, known professionally as <b>J57</b>, is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, record producer,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> record executive,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and music video director that infuses <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> with indie rock, pop, folk &amp; Americana music.  He is a member of the  <a href="/wiki/Brown_Bag_AllStars" title="Brown Bag AllStars">Brown Bag AllStars</a>, a group of emcees he helped create while working at <b>Fat Beats</b> in 2004, and Jamo Gang, a hip-hop group along with <a href="/wiki/Ras_Kass" title="Ras Kass">Ras Kass</a> and El Gant. J57 cites DJ Eclipse and his mentor  <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> as his musical influences.<sup id="cite_ref-above_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-above-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He owns a record label called <b>FiveSe7en Collective</b> which is the home of a lot of new, promising talent in different genres of music.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span id="2003.E2.80.932007:_Early_beginnings_and_Brown_Bag_AllStars"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2003–2007:_Early_beginnings_and_Brown_Bag_AllStars">2003–2007: Early beginnings and Brown Bag AllStars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J57_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 2003–2007: Early beginnings and Brown Bag AllStars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>, New York, J57 started his music career <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">battle rapping</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beatboxing" title="Beatboxing">beatboxing</a> in high school under the stage name <b>JLOG1C</b> with a group of friends.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2003, he started producing and went on to record his first recording with his crew. In 2004, JLOG1C started working at Fat Beats, a popular record store in New York where he met the Brown Bag AllStars and went on to record songs with the group at <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Shatkin" title="Jesse Shatkin">Jesse Shatkin</a>'s record studio.
</p>
<h2><span id="2008.E2.80.9313:_2057.2C_The_Ports_and_The_J57_Collection"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–13:_2057,_The_Ports_and_The_J57_Collection">2008–13: <i>2057</i>, <i>The Ports</i> and <i>The J57 Collection</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J57_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2008–13: 2057, The Ports and The J57 Collection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008, JLOG1C changed his name to J57 before Brown Bag AllStars released their first mixtape <i>The Brown Tape,</i> exclusively at Fat Beats.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On May 14, 2010 he released the <i>Digital Society</i> mixtape through Balanced Records, featuring vocals from <a href="/wiki/Homeboy_Sandman" title="Homeboy Sandman">Homeboy Sandman</a>, the Brown Bag AllStars, <a href="/wiki/Soul_Khan" title="Soul Khan">Soul Khan</a>, Andrew Reid, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Brace" title="DJ Brace">DJ Brace</a> and many notable acts.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2012 he released his <a href="/wiki/DJ_Concept" title="DJ Concept">DJ Concept</a>-promoted mixtape <i>The J57 Collection</i> and two solo <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EPs</a> <i><a href="/wiki/2057_(EP)" title="2057 (EP)">2057</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ports" title="The Ports">The Ports EP</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> all which did well to gain him grounds and further led him to being ranked No. 20 in <i><a href="/wiki/AllHipHop" title="AllHipHop">AllHipHop</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s list of "Top 50 in  Underground Hip-Hop".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> After overseeing projects for <a href="/wiki/Koncept" title="Koncept">Koncept</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soul_Khan" title="Soul Khan">Soul Khan</a>, Sene and other artistes, including Homeboy Sandman's fourth studio album titled <i><a href="/wiki/First_of_a_Living_Breed" title="First of a Living Breed">First of a Living Breed</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> he went on to release <i>Walk in the Sun</i>, a collaborative album he did with Blame One which was released in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="2014.E2.80.932017:_I.27m_the_J57"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2017:_I'm_the_J57">2014–2017: <i>I'm the J57</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J57_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2014–2017: I&#039;m the J57">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2014, J57 was featured in <a href="/wiki/DJ_Rhettmatic" title="DJ Rhettmatic">DJ Rhettmatic</a>'s single titled "Louis Vuitton Wallets" and made vocal appearance and record production credits in the 2014 release of the annual Brown Bag AllStars <i>A Year in Review</i> mixtapes.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In mid-2015, he produced "Purple Tape", one of the lead singles from <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>'s fifth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Meth_Lab" title="The Meth Lab">The Meth Lab</a></i> which went on to peak at No. 57 on the US <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a></i> and was received to positive reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On November 20, 2015, J57 teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Koncept" title="Koncept">Koncept</a> to release a joint EP titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fuel" title="The Fuel">The Fuel</a></i> which was positively received among music critics.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 19, 2016, J57 released his first solo album titled <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_the_J57" title="I&#39;m the J57">I'm the J57</a></i> to positive reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> before he went on to produce the entirety of  <i>Elixir</i>, a 7-track gritty hip-hop studio album by Aaron Rose which gained multiple media attention. In an interview with <a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a>, J57 revealed that 5 hip-hop albums including <i><a href="/wiki/Moment_of_Truth_(Gang_Starr_album)" title="Moment of Truth (Gang Starr album)">Moment of Truth</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Madvillainy" title="Madvillainy">Madvillainy</a></i>,  <i><a href="/wiki/Be_(Common_album)" title="Be (Common album)">Be</a></i>,  <i><a href="/wiki/Below_the_Heavens" title="Below the Heavens">Below the Heavens</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Ironman_(Ghostface_Killah_album)" title="Ironman (Ghostface Killah album)">Ironman</a></i> inspired him in creating the beats for the album.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The beginning of 2017 saw J57 become a member of hip-hop group Jamo Gang which DJ Premier formally announced in January.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In June, he released the music video for "Legacy" as a promotional tool to mark the release of the deluxe version of <i>I'm the J57</i> and further decided to donate all proceeds earned from the album to people who were affected by the <a href="/wiki/Flint_water_crisis" title="Flint water crisis">Flint water crisis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> As part of the group Jamo Gang, J57 produced the entirety of their debut project <i>Jamo Gang EP</i>, an 8-track hip-hop, <a href="/wiki/Boom_bap" title="Boom bap">boom bap</a> EP <i>HipHopDX</i> described as "intoxicating".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2018:_FiveSe7en_Collective">2018: FiveSe7en Collective</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J57_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2018: FiveSe7en Collective">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>June 2018, J57 launched his record label FiveSe7en Collective.&#160; Jamo Gang<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Ras_Kass" title="Ras Kass">Ras Kass</a>, J57, El Gant)'s debut self-titled EP was released, featuring DJ Premier, <a href="/wiki/Snak_the_Ripper" title="Snak the Ripper">Snak the Ripper</a> and more. J57 released 3 albums and 3 EP's; one of which titled "LP2"<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> an avant-garde release available exclusively for one week on J57's website.
</p>
<h2><span id="2019-2020:_DJ_Premier.2C_Atmosphere.2C_Jamo_Gang"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019-2020:_DJ_Premier,_Atmosphere,_Jamo_Gang">2019-2020: DJ Premier, Atmosphere, Jamo Gang</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J57_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2019-2020: DJ Premier, Atmosphere, Jamo Gang">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2019, J57 released self-produced “VOLTAIRE.” EP &#160;as well as the Heem Stogied &amp; J57 “False Guidance” LP, capping the year off with his 2nd studio album “We Can Be Kings”<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> which features Sid Wilson from Slipknot, Jay Jennings from Snark Puppy, Jamo Gang &amp; more. 100% of the proceeds from the VOLTAIRE. limited edition CD release went to the Flint Water Crisis.
</p><p>J57 released/was a part of over 400 songs released in 2020; over 20 albums, over 10 EP’s &amp; over 30 singles. &#160;The debut album from Jamo Gang “Walking with Lions” released in May 2020 via Fat Beats Records which is where J57 made his directorial debut for the single/music video “The 1st Time” featuring DJ Premier &amp; Slug from Atmosphere. &#160;As a joke he created an EP as Tiger King’s Joe Exotic in under an hour and released it. &#160;A couple of the songs went viral on TikTok, Instagram &amp; Twitter. &#160;He also created a new sub-genre; DRILL BAP<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> which is a cross between boom bap hiphop and Brooklyn Drill.  J57 donates 100% of the proceeds from a Bandcamp-only EP<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> to the NAACP LDF and 100% of the proceeds from one of his singles to 540W Main (an online hub for antiracist education &amp; events that promote justice for all).
</p>
<h2><span id="2021-2022:_Chavez_Sound.2C_solo_projects.2C_CHROMESTHESIA_EP"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2021-2022:_Chavez_Sound,_solo_projects,_CHROMESTHESIA_EP">2021-2022: Chavez Sound, solo projects,  <i>CHROMESTHESIA EP</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J57_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2021-2022: Chavez Sound, solo projects, CHROMESTHESIA EP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2021 J57 produced lead singles and key album tracks for <a href="/wiki/Skyzoo" title="Skyzoo">Skyzoo</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Blu_(rapper)" title="Blu (rapper)">Blu (rapper)</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> &amp; Mon Rovîa. He and Ric Chavez launched their new record label, Chavez Sound,<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> which is a subsidiary of FiveSe7en Collective and Soulspazm. Then in 2022, J57 released three self-produced vocal EP’s; "Death By Astral Projection"<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> (featuring <a href="/wiki/Lil_B" title="Lil B">Lil B</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> "Anti-Social Media 2 EP",<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> and "CHROMESTHESIA EP"<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> (featuring <a href="/wiki/Homeboy_Sandman" title="Homeboy Sandman">Homeboy Sandman</a>, Skyzoo, Blu &amp; more).
In early 2022, J57 released one beat tape per week for the entirety of January, February and March, totaling 13 beat tapes/instrumental albums; one per week for 3 months straight.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Upcoming_projects">Upcoming projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J57_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Upcoming projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
