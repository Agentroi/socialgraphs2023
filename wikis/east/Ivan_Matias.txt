<p><b>Ivan Matias</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Music_of_the_United_States" title="Music of the United States">American singer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Producer_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Producer (music)">producer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrangement" title="Arrangement">arranger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> <a href="/wiki/Ghostwriter" title="Ghostwriter">ghostwriter</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneurship" title="Entrepreneurship">entrepreneur</a>. He is primarily known for writing and producing hit songs for artists like <a href="/wiki/En_Vogue" title="En Vogue">En Vogue</a> ("Don't Let Go (Love)"),<sup id="cite_ref-dont_let_go_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dont_let_go-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Angie_Stone" title="Angie Stone">Angie Stone</a> ("Wish I Didn't Miss You"),<sup id="cite_ref-wish_I_didnt_miss_you_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wish_I_didnt_miss_you-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-discogonline_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discogonline-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/SWV" title="SWV">SWV</a> ("You're The One"),<sup id="cite_ref-youre_the_one_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youre_the_one-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Blu_Cantrell" title="Blu Cantrell">Blu Cantrell</a> ("Breathe")<sup id="cite_ref-breathe_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-breathe-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> among others which have sold over 44 million records worldwide and appear on over 100 greatest hits and compilation albums.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Matias has also had four major label recording deals with <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>, <a href="/wiki/London_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="London Records">London Records</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Elektra_Records" title="Elektra Records">Elektra Entertainment</a>. Only his Arista artist album was released and produced a #1 Dance Single "I've Had Enough".<sup id="cite_ref-ive_had_enough_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ive_had_enough-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
Matias has been featured on several soundtracks including <i><a href="/wiki/Dr._Dolittle_(1998_film)" title="Dr. Dolittle (1998 film)">Dr. Dolittle</a></i> ("Do Little Things"),<sup id="cite_ref-doolittle_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-doolittle-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Set_It_Off_(film)" title="Set It Off (film)">Set It Off</a></i> ("Set It Off"),<sup id="cite_ref-set_it_off_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-set_it_off-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mod_Squad_(film)" title="The Mod Squad (film)">The Mod Squad</a></i> ("Messin' Around")<sup id="cite_ref-messing_around_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-messing_around-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  and <i><a href="/wiki/Sprung_(film)" title="Sprung (film)">Sprung</a></i> ("Since You've Gone Away").<sup id="cite_ref-sprung_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sprung-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ivan_Matias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ivan began his career as a backing singer &amp; dancer for many artists including <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mtv_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He went on from these humble beginnings to write and produce songs recorded by many of the industry's leading acts including Lauryn Hill, Jay-Z, Pink, Dr. Dre, Curtis Mayfield, Janet Jackson, Nelly, Lil' Kim, Timbaland, Swizz Beatz, Outkast, Queen Latifah, Toni Braxton, En Vogue, SWV, Angie Stone, Blu Cantrell, Sean Paul, Silk, Az Yet, Traci Spencer, Fatboy Slim, Chico Debarge, Ray J, Changing Faces, Tomcraft, Xscape,The Braxtons, Immature, City High, Another Level, Jody Watley, Eric Sermon and Bette Midler.<sup id="cite_ref-mtv_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Many of his songs have been featured worldwide on MTV, BET, VH1, NBC, FOX, ABC, CBS, BBC, ITV &amp; performed on AMERICAN IDOL, THE X FACTOR (around the world), DANCING WITH THE STARS, THE VOICE, EMMY AWARDS, R&amp;B DIVAS &amp; SO YOU THINK YOU CAN DANCE and many other commercial placements.<sup id="cite_ref-All_Music_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All_Music-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-musicvf_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-musicvf-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
Matias was signed to Warner/Chappell Music at the age of 19 making him one of the youngest and longest-running active writers in Warner's history. Matias has also had one of his songs appear on a newly released album for 18 consecutive years since his writing career began in 1995.
His career has included collaboration with top producers and writers including Dr.Dre, Timbaland, Swizz Beatz, Arif Mardin, Diane Warren, Wyclef Jean, Rodney Jerkins, Narada Michael Walden, Mervyn Warren, Just Blaze, Full Force, Frankie Knuckles, Randy Jackson, Rhett Lawrence, CJ Mackintosh and others. Many of Matias' hits were written with longtime songwriting partner <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Martin_(musician)" title="Andrea Martin (musician)">Andrea Martin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-discogonline_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discogonline-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-disco_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-disco-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Caushun_The_Gay_Rapper">Caushun The Gay Rapper</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ivan_Matias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Caushun The Gay Rapper">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2000, Matias created a parody artist, <a href="/wiki/Caushun" title="Caushun">Caushun</a>, who received attention after he was announced as the first openly <a href="/wiki/Gay" title="Gay">gay</a> rapper to be signed to a major record label, including a <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Times" class="mw-redirect" title="New York Times">New York Times</a></i> cover &amp; features in several publications, documentaries &amp; television networks including Newsweek, Fox News, MTV, BET VH1 and recurring appearances on NY's HOT 97 and WBLS radio stations.<sup id="cite_ref-allhiphop_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allhiphop-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Matias negotiated with hip hop mogul <a href="/wiki/Russell_Simmons" title="Russell Simmons">Russell Simmons</a> to release an album of the parody rapper.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Matias' creation has been credited with building the bridge between the LGBT community and the hip hop community as well as cultivating and drawing attention to the underground <a href="/wiki/LGBT_hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="LGBT hip hop">LGBT hip hop</a> scene.<sup id="cite_ref-discogonline_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discogonline-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lgbtu_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lgbtu-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-metro_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-metro-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_News">Recent News</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ivan_Matias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Recent News">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In December 2013 Matias' song "Don't Let Go (Love)" soared back to #1 &amp; #3 simultaneously on the iTunes chart after Little Mix performed on The X Factor UK. Australian singer Greg Gould’s version of the track topped the charts globally in 2017 and the music video has had over 20 million views. Matias put Gould in touch with En Vogue’s Maxine Jones and the two re-recorded Gould’s arrangement of the song as a duet and then embarked on a tour together in late 2017. <sup id="cite_ref-grapejuice_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grapejuice-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
