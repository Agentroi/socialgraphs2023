<p><b>Alvin Lamar Worthy</b> (born July 27, 1982), known professionally as <b>Westside Gunn</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>. He is a co-founder of independent hip-hop label <a href="/wiki/Griselda_Records" title="Griselda Records">Griselda Records</a>. He is the brother of rapper <a href="/wiki/Conway_the_Machine" title="Conway the Machine">Conway the Machine</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and cousin of rapper <a href="/wiki/Benny_the_Butcher" title="Benny the Butcher">Benny the Butcher</a>, both of whom he frequently collaborates with. Westside Gunn has released four studio albums in addition to an extensive catalog of mixtapes. Gunn was affiliated with <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a> from 2017 to 2020, but has since returned to independent status.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Westside_Gunn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Westside Gunn released his first mixtape in 2005, however his career was derailed by run-ins with the law.  Gunn did not release any music in the years 2006-2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Pryor_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pryor-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Westside Gunn co-founded the Griselda Records label with his paternal half-brother <a href="/wiki/Conway_the_Machine" title="Conway the Machine">Conway the Machine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mach-Hommy" title="Mach-Hommy">Mach-Hommy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Shady-Griselda-deal_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shady-Griselda-deal-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Gunn was inspired to revitalize his career by the shooting of his brother Conway that same year, which left him with permanent disabilities, as well as by the 2006 murder of his cousin Machine Gun Black. Gunn's first project for Griselda was the mixtape <i>Hitler Wears Hermes.</i> While the name attracted controversy for its reference to <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>, Gunn explained that the name is a play on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Devil_Wears_Prada_(film)" title="The Devil Wears Prada (film)">The Devil Wears Prada</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pryor_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pryor-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Over the next few years, Gunn released a steady stream of mixtapes and EPs, including multiple sequels to <i>Hitler Wears Hermes,</i> on the Griselda label. Gunn's official studio album debut was <i>Flygod,</i> released on March 11, 2016.
</p><p>On March 3, 2017, Griselda Records signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a>, making Westside Gunn and Conway the first rappers from Buffalo to sign with a major label.<sup id="cite_ref-Shady-Griselda-deal_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shady-Griselda-deal-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After signing with Shady, Gunn played at <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Firefly_Music_Festival" title="Firefly Music Festival">Firefly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Governors_Ball_Music_Festival" title="Governors Ball Music Festival">Governors Ball</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On October 2, 2020, Westside Gunn released his <a href="/wiki/Aftermath_Entertainment" title="Aftermath Entertainment">Shady Records</a> debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Made_the_Sunshine" title="Who Made the Sunshine">Who Made the Sunshine</a></i>, his only solo project with <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a>.
</p><p>Shortly after signing with Shady Records, Westside released <i>Flygod Is Good... All the Time</i>, as a collaborative EP with producer <a href="/wiki/Mr._Green_(record_producer)" title="Mr. Green (record producer)">Mr. Green</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  Ambrosia For Heads named 2018's <i><a href="/wiki/Supreme_Blientele" title="Supreme Blientele">Supreme Blientele</a></i> one of the best albums of 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He also signed with <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a> alongside label mate Benny the Butcher on August 6, 2019, under a management deal.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 17, 2020, he released his third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Pray_for_Paris" title="Pray for Paris">Pray for Paris</a></i>. The album has features from artists such as <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator" title="Tyler, the Creator">Tyler, the Creator</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Badass</a> and <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Gibbs" title="Freddie Gibbs">Freddie Gibbs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On July 3, 2020, Westside Gunn released his tenth mixtape <i>Flygod Is an Awesome God II</i>, the sequel to his July 2019 mixtape&#160;<i>Flygod Is an Awesome God</i>. The album features guest appearances by frequent collaborators Benny The Butcher, Keisha Plum, <a href="/wiki/Boldy_James" title="Boldy James">Boldy James</a>, and Daringer.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 14, 2020, in an interview with <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a>, Gunn announced he had fulfilled his contractual obligations to Shady Records and had become an independent artist.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2021, he released two songs titled "TV Boy" and "Julia Lang". The track "Julia Lang" was titled after fashion entrepreneur <a href="/wiki/Julia_Lang_(fashion_entrepreneur)" class="mw-redirect" title="Julia Lang (fashion entrepreneur)">Julia Lang</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2023, Gunn appeared on American rapper <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>'s fourth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Utopia_(Travis_Scott_album)" title="Utopia (Travis Scott album)">Utopia</a></i> on the track "Lost Forever."
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Westside_Gunn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gunn has stated within various interviews that he's a "devout wrestling fan" and often references this within his song lyrics and song titles. He often can be seen in the front-row seats of the audience of <a href="/wiki/Impact_Wrestling" title="Impact Wrestling">Impact Wrestling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Wrestling" title="Major League Wrestling">Major League Wrestling</a>, <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> and <a href="/wiki/All_Elite_Wrestling" title="All Elite Wrestling">All Elite Wrestling</a> events. He maintains friendships with wrestlers <a href="/wiki/Parker_Boudreaux" title="Parker Boudreaux">Parker Boudreaux</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lio_Rush" title="Lio Rush">Lio Rush</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finn_B%C3%A1lor" title="Finn Bálor">Finn Bálor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bianca_Belair" title="Bianca Belair">Bianca Belair</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montez_Ford" class="mw-redirect" title="Montez Ford">Montez Ford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montel_Vontavious_Porter" title="Montel Vontavious Porter">Montel Vontavious Porter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sonjay_Dutt" title="Sonjay Dutt">Sonjay Dutt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Garcia_(wrestler)" title="Daniel Garcia (wrestler)">Daniel Garcia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Myron_Reed_(wrestler)" title="Myron Reed (wrestler)">Myron Reed</a>. When asked who were his top 5 wrestlers on fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Smoke_DZA" title="Smoke DZA">Smoke DZA</a>'s "Personal Podcast", he stated "It always changes but <a href="/wiki/Hulk_Hogan" title="Hulk Hogan">Hulk Hogan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dwayne_Johnson" title="Dwayne Johnson">The Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bret_Hart" title="Bret Hart">Bret Hart</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Undertaker" title="The Undertaker">The Undertaker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Benoit" title="Chris Benoit">Chris Benoit</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-SMOKE_DZA_PERSONAL_PODCAST_INTERVIEW_WITH_WESTSIDE_GUNN_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SMOKE_DZA_PERSONAL_PODCAST_INTERVIEW_WITH_WESTSIDE_GUNN-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On the September 7, 2022 edition of <i><a href="/wiki/AEW_Dynamite" title="AEW Dynamite">AEW Dynamite</a></i>, Gunn escorted wrestler, close friend and fellow Buffalo native Daniel Garcia to the ring to face <a href="/wiki/Wheeler_Yuta" title="Wheeler Yuta">Wheeler Yuta</a> in an <a href="/wiki/ROH_Pure_Championship" title="ROH Pure Championship">ROH Pure Championship</a> match, which Garcia won.<sup id="cite_ref-Westside_Gunn_Performs_On_AEW_Dynamite_Wrestling_Show_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Westside_Gunn_Performs_On_AEW_Dynamite_Wrestling_Show-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
