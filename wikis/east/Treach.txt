<p>With <a href="/wiki/Naughty_by_Nature" title="Naughty by Nature">Naughty by Nature</a>, Treach worked with fellow group members Vin Rock and DJ Kay Gee. Sometimes he shared verses on songs with Vin Rock, but often, especially on the group's earlier albums, Treach was the sole performer on the song. Examples of this are some of the group's most well-known singles from their self-titled second album, such as "<a href="/wiki/O.P.P._(song)" title="O.P.P. (song)">O.P.P.</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Everything%27s_Gonna_Be_Alright_(Naughty_by_Nature_song)" title="Everything&#39;s Gonna Be Alright (Naughty by Nature song)">Ghetto Bastard</a>".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Treach is the older brother of Diesel, who is also a rapper and who is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Rottin_Razkals" title="Rottin Razkals">Rottin Razkals</a>, a group closely affiliated with Naughty by Nature.
</p><p>Treach had a close friendship with rapper <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> They collaborated on Shakur's song "5 Deadly Venomz" and Treach appeared in his music video "<a href="/wiki/Temptations_(song)" title="Temptations (song)">Temptations</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriages">Marriages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marriages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Treach was married to <a href="/wiki/Sandra_Denton" class="mw-redirect" title="Sandra Denton">Sandra Denton</a>, better known as Pepa of the hip hop group <a href="/wiki/Salt-n-Pepa" class="mw-redirect" title="Salt-n-Pepa">Salt-n-Pepa</a>, from July 1999 until 2001. The couple had one daughter, Egypt Jahnari Criss (born September 2, 1998).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Denton documented the marriage in her 2008 memoir <i>Let's Talk About Pepa</i>, which included allegations of abuse. In April 2017, Treach responded on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>, saying they were all false.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>, Treach has been with his common-law wife Cicely Evans for the last ten years.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (March 2018)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> They have two children together. The couple starred on season 5 of <i><a href="/wiki/Couples_Therapy_(2012_TV_series)" title="Couples Therapy (2012 TV series)">Couples Therapy</a></i> on VH1 in 2014. Treach and Cicely Evans married on September 8, 2019, in New Jersey.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Treach was arrested in <a href="/wiki/Union_City,_New_Jersey" title="Union City, New Jersey">Union City, New Jersey</a>, on April 10, 2014. He allegedly passed traffic police in his <a href="/wiki/Cadillac_Escalade" title="Cadillac Escalade">Cadillac Escalade</a> at 55 miles per hour in a 25-mile per hour zone and attempted to elude law enforcement officers after being given a signal to stop, leading them on a high-speed chase for nine blocks before stopping. He then left his vehicle and ran towards the police car; the officers drew their weapons and ordered him to return to his vehicle. Treach later complied with the officers' orders and was cited for reckless driving, speeding and driving with a suspended license. Officers also found there was a warrant for his arrest out of <a href="/wiki/Essex_County,_New_Jersey" title="Essex County, New Jersey">Essex County, New Jersey</a>, for failing to appear at a hearing related to child support, and warrants out of Union City and <a href="/wiki/Secaucus,_New_Jersey" title="Secaucus, New Jersey">Secaucus, New Jersey</a>, for failing to appear at hearings related to motor vehicle violations.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He made his first court appearance on the charge in <a href="/wiki/Jersey_City,_New_Jersey" title="Jersey City, New Jersey">Jersey City, New Jersey</a>, on April 11 and spent the night in <a href="/wiki/Hudson_County,_New_Jersey" title="Hudson County, New Jersey">Hudson County</a> jail in <a href="/wiki/Kearny,_New_Jersey" title="Kearny, New Jersey">Kearny</a> before being released after posting $15,000 bail.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
