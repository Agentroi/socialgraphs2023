<p><b>Barry Adrian Reese</b> (born July 7, 1982), better known by his stage name <b>Cassidy</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>. He is perhaps best known for his singles "<a href="/wiki/Hotel_(Cassidy_song)" title="Hotel (Cassidy song)">Hotel</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/R._Kelly" title="R. Kelly">R. Kelly</a>), "<a href="/wiki/Get_No_Better" class="mw-redirect" title="Get No Better">Get No Better</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Mashonda" title="Mashonda">Mashonda</a>), "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Hustla_(song)" title="I&#39;m a Hustla (song)">I'm a Hustla</a>", "B-Boy Stance", and "<a href="/wiki/My_Drink_n_My_2_Step" title="My Drink n My 2 Step">My Drink n My 2 Step</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>).
</p><p>Cassidy first garnered attention for his <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyles</a> and competitions as a <a href="/wiki/Battle_rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle rapper">battle rapper</a> in the late 1990s. American record producer <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a> found Cassidy in 2002 and signed him to his <a href="/wiki/Full_Surface_Records" title="Full Surface Records">Full Surface Records</a> imprint under <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders_Entertainment" title="Ruff Ryders Entertainment">Ruff Ryders Entertainment</a> and <a href="/wiki/J_Records" title="J Records">J Records</a>, which helped him gain major recognition. He rose to prominence with his debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Split_Personality_(Cassidy_album)" title="Split Personality (Cassidy album)">Split Personality</a></i>, which was released in March 2004 and debuted at number two on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cassidy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2002.E2.80.9305:_Split_Personality_and_I.27m_a_Hustla"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2002–05:_Split_Personality_and_I'm_a_Hustla">2002–05: <i>Split Personality</i> and <i>I'm a Hustla</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cassidy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2002–05: Split Personality and I&#039;m a Hustla">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Split_Personality_(Cassidy_album)" title="Split Personality (Cassidy album)">Split Personality (Cassidy album)</a> and <a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Hustla" title="I&#39;m a Hustla">I'm a Hustla</a></div>
<p>In 2002, Cassidy signed a <a href="/wiki/Recording_contract" title="Recording contract">recording contract</a> with American music producer <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Full_Surface_Records" title="Full Surface Records">Full Surface</a> imprint, under the aegis of <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders_Entertainment" title="Ruff Ryders Entertainment">Ruff Ryders Entertainment</a> and <a href="/wiki/J_Records" title="J Records">J Records</a>. On November 29, 2003, he released his commercial debut single, "<a href="/wiki/Hotel_(Cassidy_song)" title="Hotel (Cassidy song)">Hotel</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/R._Kelly" title="R. Kelly">R. Kelly</a>. The song reached the <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">Top 10</a> of the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-Chart_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chart-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The second single, "Get No Better", features <a href="/wiki/Mashonda" title="Mashonda">Mashonda</a>. On March 16, 2004, Cassidy released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Split_Personality_(Cassidy_album)" title="Split Personality (Cassidy album)">Split Personality</a></i>. It was <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified gold</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA), the following month.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 15, 2005, Cassidy released the single "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Hustla_(song)" title="I&#39;m a Hustla (song)">I'm a Hustla</a>". The next single, "B-Boy Stance", features vocals and production from his mentor, <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>. On June 28, 2005, Cassidy released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Hustla" title="I&#39;m a Hustla">I'm a Hustla</a></i>. It debuted at number five on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, with 93,000 copies sold in the first week.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.9311:_B.A.R.S._and_C.A.S.H."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–11:_B.A.R.S._and_C.A.S.H.">2006–11: <i>B.A.R.S.</i> and <i>C.A.S.H.</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cassidy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006–11: B.A.R.S. and C.A.S.H.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/B.A.R.S._The_Barry_Adrian_Reese_Story" title="B.A.R.S. The Barry Adrian Reese Story">B.A.R.S. The Barry Adrian Reese Story</a> and <a href="/wiki/C.A.S.H._(album)" title="C.A.S.H. (album)">C.A.S.H. (album)</a></div>
<p>In 2006, after his release from prison and recovering from an auto wreck, Cassidy began work on his third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/B.A.R.S._The_Barry_Adrian_Reese_Story" title="B.A.R.S. The Barry Adrian Reese Story">B.A.R.S. The Barry Adrian Reese Story</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-discussion_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discussion-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The album was released on November 6, 2007, debuting at number 10 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, with 63,000 copies sold in the first week.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The album was led by the single "<a href="/wiki/My_Drink_n_My_2_Step" title="My Drink n My 2 Step">My Drink n My 2 Step</a>", featuring vocals and production from <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>.
</p><p>In late 2009, Cassidy signed with <a href="/wiki/Carmelo_Anthony" title="Carmelo Anthony">Carmelo Anthony</a>'s label <a href="/wiki/Kross_Over_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Kross Over Entertainment">Kross Over Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> While preparing his fourth studio album, he released a mixtape series entitled <i>Apply Pressure</i>. On August 24, 2010, Cassidy released the five-track EP <i><a href="/wiki/Face_2_Face_EP" class="mw-redirect" title="Face 2 Face EP">Face 2 Face</a></i>, with the promotional single "Face 2 Face".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On November 16, 2010, Cassidy's fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/C.A.S.H._(album)" title="C.A.S.H. (album)">C.A.S.H. (Cass a Straight Hustla)</a></i> was released. On its first week it had sold about 5,200 copies, much lower than his previous studio albums.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> "Drumma Bass" was the only official single from the album.
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.93present:_Mr._Hip-Hop_and_Da_Science"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–present:_Mr._Hip-Hop_and_Da_Science">2012–present: <i>Mr. Hip-Hop</i> and <i>Da Science</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cassidy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012–present: Mr. Hip-Hop and Da Science">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On May 21, 2012, Cassidy hosted a release party for his mixtape <i>Mayhem Music</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On July 8, 2012, he released the mixtape <i>Mayhem Music: AP3</i>. After fellow Philly rapper <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a> said he would <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">battle</a> Cassidy, which Cassidy later said he would accept if the money was right. They participated in a "twitter beef" after which Cassidy released "The Diary of a Hustla" which was originally thought to be a diss towards Meek. This was later refuted by Cassidy.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> They both were asking for $100,000 each for the battle to take place.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> However, after Meek Mill dissed Cassidy's song "Condom Style" (a remake of <a href="/wiki/Psy" title="Psy">Psy</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Gangnam_Style" title="Gangnam Style">Gangnam Style</a>") Cassidy released a song titled "Me, Myself &amp; iPhone" where he took shots at Meek Mill.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Afterwards, Meek Mill responded with the diss track "Repo" which Mill later said would be the final diss record he would release against Cassidy.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Cassidy later said the feud was not personal, saying it was all in the spirit of hip hop.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On January 6, 2013, Cassidy released a 10-minute-long diss response titled "Raid".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On October 6, Cassidy released his album Da Science.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_ventures">Other ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cassidy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2007, Cassidy signed a deal as the "new face" for Lot 29's fall clothing line.<sup id="cite_ref-discussion_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discussion-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, Cassidy appeared in the comedy film, <i><a href="/wiki/Next_Day_Air" title="Next Day Air">Next Day Air</a></i>.
</p><p>In late 2012, Cassidy remade South Korean singer <a href="/wiki/Psy" title="Psy">Psy</a>'s international hit single "<a href="/wiki/Gangnam_Style" title="Gangnam Style">Gangnam Style</a>, re-titling it "Condom Style". The remake reportedly earned Cassidy an endorsement deal with <a href="/wiki/Trojan_(brand)" title="Trojan (brand)">Trojan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Cassidy released a biography titled <i>Behind B.A.R.S: The Authorized Biography of Cassidy</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late 2014, Cassidy returned to <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">battle rap</a> by battling Los Angeles-based veteran Dizaster, on December 6, 2014, at Filmon.com's "Ether" event.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cassidy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Murder_case">Murder case</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cassidy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Murder case">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 15, 2005, three men, including <a href="/wiki/AR-Ab" title="AR-Ab">AR-Ab</a> and Cassidy, armed with pistols and an <a href="/wiki/AK-47" title="AK-47">AK-47</a> assault rifle, fired on three unarmed men during an argument that occurred in the <a href="/wiki/West_Oak_Lane,_Philadelphia" title="West Oak Lane, Philadelphia">West Oak Lane neighborhood</a> in Northwest Philadelphia. Desmond Hawkins was killed by a shot in the back. Hawkins' two other friends were treated at hospitals for gunshot wounds and released.
</p><p>On June 9, a warrant was issued for Cassidy's arrest on charges of murder, attempted murder, reckless endangerment, aggravated assault, conspiracy and weapons possession. Cassidy surrendered to Philadelphia police on the afternoon of June 17.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> At his arraignment on Saturday, June 18, he was denied bail, and sent to Philadelphia's Curran-Fromhold Correctional Facility, a high-security prison, where he would serve his time during the trial and after he was sentenced.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cassidy's murder case took a turn when the primary witness withdrew his confession. This resulted in the judge stating that Cassidy would stand trial for <a href="/wiki/Third-degree_murder" class="mw-redirect" title="Third-degree murder">third-degree murder</a>. On August 16, 2005, Municipal Judge Marsha Neifield ruled that prosecutors had sufficient evidence to charge Cassidy with third-degree murder, attempted murder and weapons offenses. This was later overturned to the original, first degree charge - negating the possibility of parole. On January 25, 2006, Cassidy was convicted of involuntary manslaughter, two counts of aggravated assault and possession of an instrument of crime for his involvement in the shooting. He was sentenced to 11 to 23 months in prison plus probation and was credited with the 7 months he had already served.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cassidy was released from Pennsylvania's Curran-Fromhold Correctional Facility on March 2, 2006, after serving eight months.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cassidy talks about his arrest and time in prison in the 2011 film <i>Rhyme and Punishment</i>, which documents various hip-hop artists who have done county jail or state/federal prison time.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car_wreck">Car wreck</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cassidy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Car wreck">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cassidy was seriously injured in a vehicle wreck on the night of October 5, 2006, when a commercial truck collided with his <a href="/wiki/Sport_utility_vehicle" class="mw-redirect" title="Sport utility vehicle">SUV</a>, in which he was a passenger. He was taken to the <a href="/wiki/Jersey_City_Medical_Center" title="Jersey City Medical Center">Jersey City Medical Center</a>, where he was diagnosed with a fractured skull and several broken bones on the left side of his face. He still has visible scars from the wreck.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

"And then they put me into a medicated coma, so that if I woke up I wouldn't be traumatized by what I went through. When I first woke up out of my coma, I had <a href="/wiki/Amnesia" title="Amnesia">amnesia</a>, so I couldn't really remember everything. I didn't have full amnesia; I could remember certain things. The only people I really knew were my mom, my son, people that's real close that you knew all your life. I knew their names but even certain experiences I couldn't remember at first. I couldn't remember none of my raps, even the songs I performed — 'Hotel,' 'I'm a Hustla,' none of that. The doctor told me that I suffered brain damage, but it was temporary, it wasn't permanent. So the same way it takes time for your body to heal, he said it's gonna take your brain time to heal."<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
