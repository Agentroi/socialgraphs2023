<p><b>Sean Divine Jacobs</b> (born November 9, 1974), better known as <b>Sheek Louch</b> (or simpy <b>Sheek</b>), is an American rapper best known as a member of <a href="/wiki/The_Lox" title="The Lox">The LOX</a> and founder of <a href="/wiki/D-Block_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="D-Block Records">D-Block Records</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_career">Personal life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheek_Louch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At around the age of 12, he was inspired to rhyme by his friend Jason Phillips (<a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>). He and Jason would later form The LOX with <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a> "discovered" the trio, and they would soon be signed to <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Sean Combs</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy Records</a>. Their first album, <a href="/wiki/Money,_Power_%26_Respect" title="Money, Power &amp; Respect">Money, Power &amp; Respect</a>, was released on January 13, 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sheek and his fellow LOX members later parted ways with Bad Boy Records, and signed a record deal with their management company, <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruff Ryders">Ruff Ryders</a>, after the company formed a record label in the late 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Ruff Ryders was also home to <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>. The group's second album, <i><a href="/wiki/We_Are_the_Streets" title="We Are the Streets">We Are the Streets</a></i>, was released on January 25, 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, Sheek, an aspiring entrepreneur, began working on their <a href="/wiki/The_Lox" title="The Lox">D-Block</a> brand, building a studio, and looking for new talent to join their roster. In October 2006 he opened a D-Block car wash and gas station in their hometown of <a href="/wiki/Yonkers,_New_York" title="Yonkers, New York">Yonkers, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sheek released his solo debut <i><a href="/wiki/Walk_Witt_Me" class="mw-redirect" title="Walk Witt Me">Walk Witt Me</a></i> on Universal Records through <a href="/wiki/D-Block_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="D-Block Records">D-Block Records</a>. Early promotional material, such as within the liner notes of Styles P's album <i><a href="/wiki/A_Gangster_and_a_Gentleman" title="A Gangster and a Gentleman">A Gangster and a Gentleman</a></i>, indicate that the album's original title was <i>Walk With Me</i>. His fifth album, <i>Silverback Gorilla</i>, released in 2008, featured his hit single, "Good Love", which sampled <a href="/wiki/Betty_Wright" title="Betty Wright">Betty Wright</a>'s "Tonight Is The Night" and "Pure Love" songs.
</p><p>He was also featured on an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/World%27s_Wildest_Police_Videos" title="World&#39;s Wildest Police Videos">World's Wildest Police Videos</a></i>, which demonstrated his notorious past.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>He has worked somewhat prolifically with <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a>. They collaborated on a joint album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Wu_Block" title="Wu Block">Wu Block</a></i> featuring members of <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a> and D-Block after drawing inspiration from one another while touring and recording. In January 2017 Ghostface and Sheek Louch announced on their respective social media accounts that a second collaborative album was in the works.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
