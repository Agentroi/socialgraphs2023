<p><b>Benjamin David Goldberg</b> (born September 24, 1998), known professionally as <b>Token</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Salem,_Massachusetts" title="Salem, Massachusetts">Salem, Massachusetts</a>.
</p><p>He has appeared on <a href="/wiki/Sway_Calloway" title="Sway Calloway">Sway</a>’s Friday's Fire Cipher and has a catalog of videos on YouTube. His most recent video, "Ain't It Funny", uses a sample of his son playing the recorder.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Token_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ben Goldberg was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Marblehead,_Massachusetts" title="Marblehead, Massachusetts">Marblehead, Massachusetts</a>. He was introduced to hip-hop music at age sixteen by Justin Bieber. <sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His mother, Leslie Goldberg, described him as having incidents of "explosive anger". In his younger years, Goldberg was often sent home from school by his teachers because of this. Additionally, he had difficulty in understanding expressive language. Goldberg was eventually diagnosed with autism. As a child, Goldberg suffered from obesity, weighing nearly 240 pounds in the fourth grade. He began running on the treadmill after school and lost 50 pounds halfway through fifth grade as a result.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At a young age, Goldberg had a dream of becoming a pop star; a psychiatrist noted that it seemed to be the only thing on his mind. An outlet for Goldberg was writing poetry in his diary, which eventually evolved into writing pop songs at the age of 17. According to Goldberg, the main artists he listened to were <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanilla_Ice" title="Vanilla Ice">Vanilla Ice</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Limp_Bizkit" title="Limp Bizkit">Limp Bizkit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Token_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932014:_Career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2014:_Career_beginnings">2012–2014: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Token_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2012–2014: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Goldberg first began rhyming under the name BDG (his initials). He began uploading his content on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> after a childhood friend discovered music that he had recorded and encouraged him to create a channel to gain a following. His first song was a freestyle to "<a href="/wiki/Party_in_the_USA" class="mw-redirect" title="Party in the USA">Party in the USA</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>. The students at his school reacted negatively towards his work, but he began building a fanbase online.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In the sixth grade, Goldberg started collaborating with his friend Colin Mitchell, a local emcee known as C-Mitch. Their first project was <i>Lethal Combination</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> While the reception from the public was generally negative as well, one of the duo's CDs ended up in the possession of producer Jon Glass, who saw their potential and became a mentor to them.
</p><p>Goldberg later adopted the stage name Token, which came out of feeling different from other emcees  as a white Jewish kid from Salem. He started uploading his music videos through YouTube when he was 14.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He released his debut mixtape, <i>The Mindstate</i>, in February 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.932016:_.E2.80.9CNo_Sucka_MCs.E2.80.9D_and_early_success"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–2016:_“No_Sucka_MCs”_and_early_success">2015–2016: “No Sucka MCs” and early success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Token_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2015–2016: “No Sucka MCs” and early success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2015, Token released a video of himself rhyming while walking through a Marblehead neighborhood for the "No Sucka MCs" hip hop contest. The video amassed over 500,000 views in just three days. It gained the attention of several celebrities, including <a href="/wiki/Fred_Durst" title="Fred Durst">Fred Durst</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Wahlberg" title="Mark Wahlberg">Mark Wahlberg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Soon, Token became friends with Wahlberg, who began inviting him to his house in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In April 2016, he appeared on the radio show <i><a href="/wiki/Sway_in_the_Morning" class="mw-redirect" title="Sway in the Morning">Sway in the Morning</a></i>, on which he performed a freestyle over the beat of Lil Wayne's "<a href="/wiki/Believe_Me_(Lil_Wayne_song)" title="Believe Me (Lil Wayne song)">Believe Me</a>". His performance brought co-host Tracy G to tears as a result. The video of his appearance went viral, which earned him further recognition.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Token performed a free hip hop show presented by Wahlberg in May 2016, which was filmed for Wahlberg's reality TV show <a href="/wiki/Wahlburgers_(TV_series)" title="Wahlburgers (TV series)"><i>Wahlburgers</i></a>. Wahlberg also helped Token earn a role in the film <a href="/wiki/Patriots_Day_(film)" title="Patriots Day (film)"><i>Patriots Day</i></a>, of <a href="/wiki/Dzhokhar_Tsarnaev" title="Dzhokhar Tsarnaev">Dzhokhar Tsarnaev</a>'s college roommate (Andrew Dwinells of <a href="/wiki/University_of_Massachusetts_Dartmouth" title="University of Massachusetts Dartmouth">UMASS Dartmouth</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On September 23, 2016, Token released his second mixtape <i>Eraser Shavings</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The song "Exception" from the mixtape has reached millions of streams.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Token also first started touring in 2016, beginning in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2017.E2.80.932019:_Singles_and_Between_Somewhere"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–2019:_Singles_and_Between_Somewhere">2017–2019: Singles and <i>Between Somewhere</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Token_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2017–2019: Singles and Between Somewhere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From January 2017 to mid-2018, Token consecutively released non-album singles and videos through YouTube, including "Doozy",<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> "Patty Cake"<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and "Code Red",<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> all three of which amassed millions of streams and views on YouTube. In August 2018, Token released his song "Flamingo", the lead single from his third mixtape <i>Between Somewhere</i>, which was released on December 7, 2018. <i>Between Somewhere</i> features guest vocals from <a href="/wiki/IDK_(rapper)" title="IDK (rapper)">IDK</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bas_(rapper)" title="Bas (rapper)">Bas</a> and was rated 3.3 out of 5 stars in a <i><a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a></i> review. Token also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a> in a bonus track from the mixtape, "YouTube Rapper".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2019, Token signed to <a href="/wiki/EOne_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="EOne Music">eOne Music</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2020.E2.80.93present:_Signing_with_Atlantic_Records_and_Pink_is_Better"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020–present:_Signing_with_Atlantic_Records_and_Pink_is_Better">2020–present: Signing with Atlantic Records and <i>Pink is Better</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Token_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2020–present: Signing with Atlantic Records and Pink is Better">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2020, Goldberg founded his own label, Never Too Different, and signed a distribution deal for his label with <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>. In August 2020, he released the single "30 People", his first song under the newly founded label.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> His debut studio album <i>Pink Is Better</i> was released on January 14, 2022. The release of his album was preceded by four singles, two solo tracks ("Chit Chat" and "Sip"), "High Heels", featuring <a href="/wiki/Rico_Nasty" title="Rico Nasty">Rico Nasty</a>, and "IOD", featuring <a href="/wiki/Lil_Skies" title="Lil Skies">Lil Skies</a>.
</p>
