<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil%27_Cease&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As part of Junior M.A.F.I.A., Cease's first album <i><a href="/wiki/Conspiracy_(Junior_M.A.F.I.A._album)" title="Conspiracy (Junior M.A.F.I.A. album)">Conspiracy</a></i>  was released in 1995. He gained mainstream attention with a memorable verse on "<a href="/wiki/Player%27s_Anthem" title="Player&#39;s Anthem">Player's Anthem</a>", the album's highest charting single. Frequent <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/name-drop#English" class="extiw" title="wikt:name-drop">name dropping</a> from mentor <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a> throughout Cease's career helped keep the young rapper in the spotlight.
</p><p>Cease featured on <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam</a>'s soundtrack for the 1997 comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Be_a_Player" class="mw-redirect" title="How to Be a Player">How to Be a Player</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/LeVert" title="LeVert">LeVert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mase" title="Mase">Mase</a>. He is also known for being a featured artist on <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a>'s record <a href="/wiki/Crush_on_You_(Lil%27_Kim_song)" title="Crush on You (Lil&#39; Kim song)">Crush on You</a> from her debut album <a href="/wiki/Hard_Core_(Lil%27_Kim_album)" title="Hard Core (Lil&#39; Kim album)">Hard Core</a>, which was a number 1 hit on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Songs" title="Hot Rap Songs">Hot Rap Songs</a> chart.  Rapper Cam'ron revealed that he wrote the original rendition of the song <i>Crush on You</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After making guest appearances on other artists' albums, Cease released his debut and only studio album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wonderful_World_of_Cease_A_Leo" title="The Wonderful World of Cease A Leo">The Wonderful World of Cease A Leo</a></i> in 1999. It peaked at #26 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and #3 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a>, and features <a href="/wiki/Jay_Z" class="mw-redirect" title="Jay Z">Jay Z</a>, Lil' Kim and Bristal. In 2005, Lil' Cease, Banger and MC Klepto reunited Junior M.A.F.I.A. and released the group's second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Riot_Musik" title="Riot Musik">Riot Musik</a></i>. In January 2009, Lil' Cease released "Letter to B.I.G.", which sampled "Letter to B.I.G." by <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Notorious_(soundtrack)" title="Notorious (soundtrack)">soundtrack</a> to the feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Notorious_(2009_film)" title="Notorious (2009 film)">Notorious</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
