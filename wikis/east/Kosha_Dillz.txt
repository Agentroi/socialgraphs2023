<p><b>Rami Matan Even-Esh</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span lang="he" dir="rtl">רמי מתן אבן-אש</span>; born August 26, 1981),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Kosha Dillz</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/Israeli_Americans" title="Israeli Americans">Israeli-American</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-MC_pals&#39;_flows_are_Kosha,_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MC_pals&#39;_flows_are_Kosha,-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Maxim_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maxim-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kosha_Dillz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rami Even-Esh was born in <a href="/wiki/Perth_Amboy,_New_Jersey" title="Perth Amboy, New Jersey">Perth Amboy, New Jersey</a> to <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a> immigrant parents and was raised in <a href="/wiki/Edison,_New_Jersey" title="Edison, New Jersey">Edison, New Jersey</a>, spending summers in <a href="/wiki/Kiryat_Tiv%27on" title="Kiryat Tiv&#39;on">Kiryat Tiv'on, Israel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MC_pals&#39;_flows_are_Kosha,_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MC_pals&#39;_flows_are_Kosha,-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He rhymes in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hebrew" class="mw-redirect" title="Hebrew">Hebrew</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yiddish" title="Yiddish">Yiddish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Caught_live_at_CMJ:_Kosha_Dillz,_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Caught_live_at_CMJ:_Kosha_Dillz,-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> A graduate of <a href="/wiki/Rutgers_University" title="Rutgers University">Rutgers University</a>, he was a member of the wrestling team<sup id="cite_ref-Weiss_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weiss-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and studied creative writing, which he has said helped to expand his writing skills.<sup id="cite_ref-mtvU_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtvU-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In his youth, he played the trumpet.<sup id="cite_ref-mtvU_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtvU-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was initially exposed to hip hop at <a href="/wiki/Bar_Mitzvah" class="mw-redirect" title="Bar Mitzvah">Bar Mitzvah</a> dances, and when he heard the “funky beats in the background” of “old school bike videos” he would watch with his brother.<sup id="cite_ref-ugs_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ugs-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kosha_Dillz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Even-Esh started rapping at the age of 17. For several years beginning in 1999, he was heavily involved with the <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a> <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">battle rap</a> scene at <a href="/wiki/Nuyorican_Poets_Caf%C3%A9" title="Nuyorican Poets Café">Nuyorican Poets Café</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. Other prominent participants were <a href="/wiki/Mos_Def" title="Mos Def">Mos Def</a>, <a href="/wiki/Immortal_Technique" title="Immortal Technique">Immortal Technique</a> and <a href="/wiki/C-Rayz_Walz" title="C-Rayz Walz">C-Rayz Walz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Owen_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Owen-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> That same year, he attended an <a href="/wiki/MF_Doom" title="MF Doom">MF Doom</a> concert at <a href="/wiki/The_Wetlands_Preserve" class="mw-redirect" title="The Wetlands Preserve">The Wetlands</a>, and recalls, "That’s when I knew I loved the underground hip hop scene. That show and Braggin' Rights at Nuyorican Poets Café changed my life for sure."<sup id="cite_ref-mtvU_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtvU-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He originally went by Kosher Dill, saying, "I was <a href="/wiki/Jewish" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish">Jewish</a> and I wanted something cool." He changed his name to KD Flow when he was in the battle scene because he was "ashamed" of his MC name's Jewish association. After some substance abuse issues and a stint in jail, he became Kosha Dillz, coinciding with a decision to return to his Jewish identity.<sup id="cite_ref-Owen_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Owen-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kosha Dillz put out his first 12-inch, "Chainsaw Music," in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-ugs_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ugs-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2006, he appeared alongside <a href="/wiki/Matisyahu" title="Matisyahu">Matisyahu</a> on the song "Childhood" off the <a href="/wiki/C-Rayz_Walz" title="C-Rayz Walz">C-Rayz Walz</a> album <i>The Dropping</i>. Two years later, C-Rayz contacted Kosha about collaborating. They released <i>Freestyle vs. Written</i>, an album on which Kosha rapped written verses, and Walz freestyled.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Okayplayer" title="Okayplayer">Okayplayer</a> stated that the album contains “witty, irreverent lyrics and candor.”<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>2009 saw the release of his debut solo LP, <i>Beverly Dillz</i>, recorded in <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills" class="mw-redirect" title="Beverly Hills">Beverly Hills</a> with local producer Belief.<sup id="cite_ref-Weiss_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weiss-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A lot of the LP was written while "in socks on a couch."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Okayplayer called the album “an interesting mix of danceable tracks and powerful rhyming” and "at times wildly original and entertaining."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2011, he has been at work on a documentary, <i>Kosha Dillz Is Everywhere</i>, about his life as an Israeli-American Jewish hip hop artist.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Along with <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drake_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (entertainer)">Drake</a>, Kosha has a playable character in the basketball video game <i><a href="/wiki/NBA_2K11" title="NBA 2K11">NBA 2K11</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> His song "Cellular Phone" was featured in a <a href="/wiki/Bud_Light" class="mw-redirect" title="Bud Light">Bud Light</a> commercial that debuted in 2012 during <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVI" title="Super Bowl XLVI">Super Bowl XLVI</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Kosha's second solo album, <i>Awkward In a Good Way</i>, was released on <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a>' label, Murs 316. Murs and producer <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Shatkin" title="Jesse Shatkin">Jesse Shatkin</a> under the alias <i>Belief</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-Djbooth_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Djbooth-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> assisted in shaping the feel of the album.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The album also featured <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_Boo" title="Gangsta Boo">Gangsta Boo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Three_6_Mafia" title="Three 6 Mafia">Three 6 Mafia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hotnewhiphop_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hotnewhiphop-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>What I Do All Day And Pickle</i> was released on July 15, 2016 on Oy Vey!, led by the single "Dodging Bullets" featuring reggae artist <a href="/wiki/Matisyahu" title="Matisyahu">Matisyahu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The video for "Dodging Bullets" premiered on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> on June 28, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <i>What I Do All Day And Pickle</i> peaked at number 15 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> <a href="/wiki/Heatseekers_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Heatseekers Albums">Heatseekers Albums</a> chart, number 42 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a>, and number 50 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> <a href="/wiki/Independent_Albums" title="Independent Albums">Independent Albums</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In march of 2020 Kosha collaborated and was featured on a song with <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">EDM</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Kaskade" title="Kaskade">Kaskade</a> called "Sexy".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On October 16, 2020 Dillz released his 14 track album "Nobody Cares Except you" that was crowdfunded on <a href="/wiki/Kickstarter" title="Kickstarter">Kickstarter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Knockturnal_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Knockturnal-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 26, 2021 Dillz was freestyle rapping outside <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a> Game during a <a href="/wiki/Nor%27easter" title="Nor&#39;easter">Nor'easter</a> in New York City and caught the attention of rapper <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a> who walked by. Fat Joe started rapping with Kosha and the performance was caught on video which caused the performance to go viral.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>October 30, 2021 Fat Joe was performing with DJ Nasty (Johnny David Mollings) of <a href="/wiki/Nasty_Beatmakers" title="Nasty Beatmakers">Nasty Beatmakers</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Maxim_(magazine)" title="Maxim (magazine)">Maxim</a> Masquerade party in Denver, Co and invited Kosha Dillz up on stage to perform<sup id="cite_ref-Maxim_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maxim-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> after seeing him in the crowd. <a href="/wiki/UNILAD" title="UNILAD">UNILAD</a> made a short documentary on Kosha after they saw the performance.<sup id="cite_ref-Hypebot_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hypebot-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2022, the <i><a href="/wiki/Jewish_Journal" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish Journal">Jewish Journal</a></i> named him one of "The Top 10 Jewish Reality TV Stars of All Time."<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Performances">Performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kosha_Dillz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kosha has toured and performed with a variety of artists, including <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matisyahu" title="Matisyahu">Matisyahu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cage_the_Elephant" title="Cage the Elephant">Cage the Elephant</a>, <a href="/wiki/C-Rayz_Walz" title="C-Rayz Walz">C-Rayz Walz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yak_Ballz" title="Yak Ballz">Yak Ballz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aesop_Rock" title="Aesop Rock">Aesop Rock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Weiss_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weiss-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Broadley_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Broadley-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> He won the 2009 <a href="/wiki/WQHT" title="WQHT">Hot 97</a> "Summer Jam" Rap Battle at <a href="/wiki/Giants_Stadium" title="Giants Stadium">Giants Stadium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He performed at <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance</a> in 2009<sup id="cite_ref-Broadley_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Broadley-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/South_By_Southwest" class="mw-redirect" title="South By Southwest">South By Southwest</a> in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-Owen_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Owen-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He performed in 2011 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Yo_Gabba_Gabba!" title="Yo Gabba Gabba!">Yo Gabba Gabba!</a></i> Live tour.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> He hosted official showcases at <a href="/wiki/South_By_Southwest" class="mw-redirect" title="South By Southwest">South By Southwest</a> in 2015, 2016 and 2017, called <i>Kosha Dillz Presents: Oy Vey</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honors">Honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kosha_Dillz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kosha was honored as one of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jewish_Week" title="The Jewish Week">The Jewish Week</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s 2013 36 Under 36, an annual list of "young visionaries reshaping and broadening the Jewish community."<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He was also mentioned as #14 on <a href="/wiki/BuzzFeed" title="BuzzFeed">BuzzFeed</a>'s 2016 List of Best Jewish Rappers: Passover Edition.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistic_style">Artistic style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kosha_Dillz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Artistic style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kosha has said the majority of opportunities he's received in his career stem from wowing crowds with his freestyle ability, which eventually earned him notice from <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Wu_Tang_Clan" class="mw-redirect" title="Wu Tang Clan">Wu Tang Clan</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Weiss_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weiss-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> with whom he has since recorded, on the 2010 track "Operator," also featuring <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Okay_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Okay-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> RZA calls Kosha "one of the rawest Jewish kids I know, proving again that hip-hop comes in all shapes, sizes, forms and races."<sup id="cite_ref-Okay_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Okay-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> observed, "Though he's a technically skilled rhymer, Dillz's greatest gift is his willingness to engage the crowd. He'll do goofball Russian dance kicks, crack jokes and single out individual audience members."<sup id="cite_ref-Weiss_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weiss-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kosha is influenced by <a href="/wiki/Wu_Tang_Clan" class="mw-redirect" title="Wu Tang Clan">Wu Tang Clan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a> and jazz artists such as <a href="/wiki/Herb_Alpert" title="Herb Alpert">Herb Alpert</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mtvU_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtvU-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Growing up, he listened to <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Notorious_B.I.G." class="mw-redirect" title="Notorious B.I.G.">Notorious B.I.G.</a>, Wu Tang Clan, <a href="/wiki/Rancid_(band)" title="Rancid (band)">Rancid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Green_Day" title="Green Day">Green Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pantera" title="Pantera">Pantera</a> and "music in Hebrew."<sup id="cite_ref-Owen_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Owen-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
