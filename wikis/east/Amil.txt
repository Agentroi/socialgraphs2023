<p><b>Amil Kahala Whitehead</b> (born September 19, 1973) is a former American rapper and singer.<sup id="cite_ref-books.google.com_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-books.google.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> She was prominent in the late 1990s as a <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> protégé, and recorded the single "<a href="/wiki/Can_I_Get_A..." title="Can I Get A...">Can I Get A...</a>" with him for the <i><a href="/wiki/Rush_Hour_(1998_film)" title="Rush Hour (1998 film)">Rush Hour</a></i> soundtrack.
</p><p>Amil's debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/All_Money_Is_Legal" title="All Money Is Legal">All Money Is Legal</a></i> (2000), peaked at number 45 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart and spawned two singles, "<a href="/wiki/I_Got_That" title="I Got That">I Got That</a>" (with vocals from <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>) and "<a href="/wiki/4_da_Fam" title="4 da Fam">4 da Fam</a>".
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amil was born on September 19, 1973 in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1997, Amil was a member of Major Coins, an all-female group. The group met <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, who was looking for a woman to provide vocals on his third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Vol._2..._Hard_Knock_Life" title="Vol. 2... Hard Knock Life">Vol. 2... Hard Knock Life</a></i>. Jay-Z asked Amil to <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a> and liked it, and selected her for the role.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Major Coins broke up, Amil decided to follow a solo career with Jay-Z's label, <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-A-Fella Records</a>, and joined the 1999 Hard Knock Life Tour. After the tour, she appeared on songs with <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a>, <a href="/wiki/AZ_(rapper)" title="AZ (rapper)">AZ</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Kay_Slay" title="DJ Kay Slay">DJ Kay Slay</a>, <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Funkmaster_Flex" title="Funkmaster Flex">Funkmaster Flex</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She has appeared on Jay-Z songs such as "<a href="/wiki/Nigga_What,_Nigga_Who" class="mw-redirect" title="Nigga What, Nigga Who">Nigga What, Nigga Who</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Can_I_Get_A..." title="Can I Get A...">Can I Get A...</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Do_It_Again_(Put_Ya_Hands_Up)" title="Do It Again (Put Ya Hands Up)">Do It Again (Put Ya Hands Up)</a>", "S Carter", "You, Me, Him and Her", and "Pop 4 Roc".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In August 2000, Amil released her debut solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/All_Money_Is_Legal" title="All Money Is Legal">All Money Is Legal</a></i>. The album featured the single "<a href="/wiki/I_Got_That" title="I Got That">I Got That</a>", a duet with Beyoncé,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and All-Star Roc-A-Fella single  "<a href="/wiki/4_Da_Fam" class="mw-redirect" title="4 Da Fam">4 Da Fam</a>". The album also featured Jay-Z, <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Bleek" title="Memphis Bleek">Memphis Bleek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carl_Thomas_(singer)" title="Carl Thomas (singer)">Carl Thomas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eve_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Eve (entertainer)">Eve</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Beanie_Sigel" title="Beanie Sigel">Beanie Sigel</a>.
</p><p>Album sales were disappointing, and the singles did not sell well either. Following that album, her last Roc-A-Fella/Def Jam single, "<a href="/wiki/Hey_Papi" class="mw-redirect" title="Hey Papi">Hey Papi</a>", a song from the soundtrack to the feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Nutty_Professor_II:_The_Klumps" title="Nutty Professor II: The Klumps">Nutty Professor II: The Klumps</a></i>, was released. In 2002, Amil had a small role in <i><a href="/wiki/State_Property_(film)" title="State Property (film)">State Property</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, Amil temporarily re-formed "Major Coinz" and released songs on the <a href="/wiki/Mixtape#Mixtapes_in_hip_hop" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> circuit including the single "Glamorous Life" which was featured on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s Mixtape Monday.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008, Amil released mixtapes titled Az Iz and Amil Returns: The Lost Classics Edition, delivering lyrically with songs such as the emotional "Tears of a Teenage Mother" and the Caribbean vibed "Don't Worry".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Amil released the song "Stop" in July 2012. The song was intended to promote the rapper's unreleased mixtape, A Time to Kill.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Amil released the song "Remember" in 2014. It was also intended to promote a mixtape titled <i>Another Moment in Life</i>, which remains unreleased.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
