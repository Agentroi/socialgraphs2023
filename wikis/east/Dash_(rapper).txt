<p><b>Darien Corey Dash, Jr.</b> (born July 21, 1992), known professionally as <b>Dash</b> (stylized as <b>Da$H</b>), is an American rapper. Apart from his solo career, Dash was a member of Heir Global, alongside former friend and collaborator, Retch. Dash is also an affiliate of <a href="/wiki/A$AP_Mob" class="mw-redirect" title="A$AP Mob">ASAP Mob</a>, making his first high-profile appearance on their debut mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Lords_Never_Worry" title="Lords Never Worry">Lords Never Worry</a></i>, in 2012. He frequently works with record producer Mordecai Beats, with whom he released the <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> <i>La Cienega</i> (2012). Da$h has also collaborated with several prominent rappers in the hip-hop industry, including <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul" title="Ab-Soul">Ab-Soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson" title="Action Bronson">Action Bronson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earl_Sweatshirt" title="Earl Sweatshirt">Earl Sweatshirt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples" title="Vince Staples">Vince Staples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suicideboys" title="Suicideboys">Suicideboys</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dash_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.9314:_Career_beginnings_and_V.I.C.E.S."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–14:_Career_beginnings_and_V.I.C.E.S.">2010–14: Career beginnings and <i>V.I.C.E.S.</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dash_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2010–14: Career beginnings and V.I.C.E.S.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dash began <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapping</a> around the age of 16 and subsequently uploaded music on <a href="/wiki/MySpace" class="mw-redirect" title="MySpace">MySpace</a>. Dash first gained major recognition in August 2012, when he appeared on three tracks from East Coast hip hop <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Mob" title="ASAP Mob">ASAP Mob</a>'s debut <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Lords_Never_Worry" title="Lords Never Worry">Lord$ Never Worry</a></i>. Also in 2012, Dash formed the hip hop duo The Heirs (or The H'z for short), which stands for <b>H</b>igh <b>E</b>veryday <b>I</b>gnoring <b>R</b>ules, with fellow New Jersey-based rapper RetcH.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In 2013, Dash released his sixth solo mixtape <i>V.I.C.E.S.</i> (which stands for <i>Very Ignorant Content Exposing Success</i>), to moderate success.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape features <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from <a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson" title="Action Bronson">Action Bronson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples" title="Vince Staples">Vince Staples</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Mob" title="ASAP Mob">ASAP Mob</a> members <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Nast" title="ASAP Nast">ASAP Nast</a> and <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Ferg" title="ASAP Ferg">ASAP Ferg</a>, among others. The production was handled by <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Larry Fisherman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earl_Sweatshirt" title="Earl Sweatshirt">Randomblackdude</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thelonious_Martin" title="Thelonious Martin">Thelonious Martin</a> and more. Dash followed up with the release of a two-track <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP) titled, <i>That Time We Smoked Dust</i> (2014), in collaboration with RetcH.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
In 2014, in an interview with New York music magazine <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fader" title="The Fader">The Fader</a></i>, former <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a> member <a href="/wiki/Earl_Sweatshirt" title="Earl Sweatshirt">Earl Sweatshirt</a> said that Dash was staying at his apartment in Los Angeles, and that they were working on music together.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Earl had previously produced the track "Whalé" from <i>V.I.C.E.S</i>, as well as appearing with him on <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>'s track, "New Faces".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.93present:_Skrewface_and_17_More_Minutes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–present:_Skrewface_and_17_More_Minutes">2015–present: <i>Skrewface</i> and <i>17 More Minutes</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dash_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2015–present: Skrewface and 17 More Minutes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2015, Da$h released an EP titled <i>Skrewface,</i> followed by a mixtape titled <i>17 More Minutes.</i> Music videos were released on Da$h's YouTube page for the tracks "RicHie K II," "Hello," and "Seymour," along with a short video series, "DEADMANWALKIN," a documentary-style installation following his experiences on the "Live Now x Die Later" <a href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">concert tour</a>. In 2017, Da$h released his debut album <i>Loose Skrew</i>, which was released under his independent label <i>H'z Global</i>. He would later go on to release two more albums under H'z Global; <i>5 Deadly Venoms</i> in 2019 with rapper V Don, and <i>Walk The Plank</i> in 2020.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
