<p><b>Sandra Jacqueline Denton</b> (born 9 November 1964<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> or 1969<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>), better known by her stage name <b>Pepa</b> or <b>Pep</b>, is a Jamaican-American rapper, best known for her work as a member of the female rap trio <a href="/wiki/Salt-N-Pepa" title="Salt-N-Pepa">Salt-N-Pepa</a>. Denton starred in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Salt-N-Pepa_Show" title="The Salt-N-Pepa Show">The Salt-N-Pepa Show</a></i>, a reality TV series focusing on reforming the group which aired on the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> network in 2008. Since January 2016, Denton has appeared as a supporting cast member on the music <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">reality television</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Growing_Up_Hip_Hop" title="Growing Up Hip Hop">Growing Up Hip Hop</a></i> which airs on <a href="/wiki/We_TV" title="We TV">We TV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pepa_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sandra Jacqueline Denton<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> was born in <a href="/wiki/Kingston,_Jamaica" title="Kingston, Jamaica">Kingston, Jamaica</a> to Charles and Enid Denton (née Hyacinth),<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> the youngest of eight children. Denton lived on a farm in Jamaica with her grandmother until she was six years old.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Denton's family moved to <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, New York, when she was three; and she later joined them. Denton has been a performer since childhood. Denton was molested as a child. Denton released a book about her life in 2008 entitled <i>Let's Talk About Pep</i> in which she talked about being molested, her many abusive relationships, and how Salt-N-Pepa formed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 1979, 15-year-old<sup id="cite_ref-VH1145_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VH1145-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Denton was sent to live with her elder sister Patsy in <a href="/wiki/Logan,_Utah" title="Logan, Utah">Logan, Utah</a>, when her family home in Queens caught fire and so she could have a better life.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VH1145_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VH1145-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> According to Denton, she was the only Black student attending <a href="/wiki/Logan_High_School_(Utah)" title="Logan High School (Utah)">Logan High School</a> during that time. Denton became inspired by <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock music</a>; citing <a href="/wiki/AC/DC" title="AC/DC">AC/DC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a> as her inspirations during her time in Utah. After a year in Utah, Denton returned to New York, later graduating from <a href="/wiki/Springfield_Gardens_High_School" title="Springfield Gardens High School">Springfield Gardens High School</a>. After high school, Denton enrolled at <a href="/wiki/Queensborough_Community_College" title="Queensborough Community College">Queensborough Community College</a> to study <a href="/wiki/Nursing" title="Nursing">nursing</a>. While attending college, Denton met fellow nursing student <a href="/wiki/Salt_(rapper)" title="Salt (rapper)">Cheryl James</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pepa_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With the production by <a href="/wiki/Hurby_Azor" title="Hurby Azor">Hurby "Luv Bug" Azor</a> whom she and James met while working as customer service representatives at <a href="/wiki/Sears" title="Sears">Sears</a>, James and Denton released a single called "The Showstopper" which became a moderate <a href="/wiki/R%26B" class="mw-redirect" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> hit in late 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> They were joined with <a href="/wiki/Latoya_Hanson" class="mw-redirect" title="Latoya Hanson">Latoya Hanson</a> who was the original DJ of the group. Shortly after in 1986, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Spinderella" title="DJ Spinderella">Deidra "Spinderella" Roper</a> joined as the group's DJ as a full-length debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Hot,_Cool_%26_Vicious" title="Hot, Cool &amp; Vicious">Hot, Cool &amp; Vicious</a></i>, was being released. The trio released a total of five studio albums: <i>Hot, Cool, and Vicious</i> (1986), <i><a href="/wiki/A_Salt_with_a_Deadly_Pepa" title="A Salt with a Deadly Pepa">A Salt with a Deadly Pepa</a></i> (1988), <i><a href="/wiki/Blacks%27_Magic" title="Blacks&#39; Magic">Blacks' Magic</a></i> (1990), <i><a href="/wiki/Very_Necessary" title="Very Necessary">Very Necessary</a></i> (1993), and <i><a href="/wiki/Brand_New_(Salt-n-Pepa_album)" title="Brand New (Salt-n-Pepa album)">Brand New</a></i> (1997), plus several greatest hits albums. Salt-N-Pepa disbanded in 2002, several months after their <i>Brand New</i> album was released on Red Ant Records. Denton's co-group member Cheryl James had stated she was ready to leave the music industry. The trio reunited for a performance on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_Honors" title="Hip Hop Honors">Hip Hop Honors</a></i> program on 22 September 2005. In 2005, Denton was a cast member of VH1's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Surreal_Life" title="The Surreal Life">The Surreal Life</a></i> (season 5).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Denton's acting credits also include the motion picture <i><a href="/wiki/Joe%27s_Apartment" title="Joe&#39;s Apartment">Joe's Apartment</a></i>, an appearance in the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> movie <i>First Time Felon</i>, and a stint as Officer <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Phelan" class="mw-redirect" title="Andrea Phelan">Andrea Phelan</a> on the HBO drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Oz_(TV_series)" title="Oz (TV series)">Oz</a></i>. She starred on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Surreal_Life:_Fame_Games" title="The Surreal Life: Fame Games">The Surreal Life: Fame Games</a></i>. Salt-N-Pepa reformed in 2008, and are still<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (February 2021)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> in the process of releasing an album since reforming as they work out past issues.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Denton teamed up with James for VH1's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Salt-N-Pepa_Show" title="The Salt-N-Pepa Show">The Salt-N-Pepa Show</a></i>. She starred in her own reality show on the network entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Talk_About_Pep" title="Let&#39;s Talk About Pep">Let's Talk About Pep</a></i>, word-play on the group's hit song "<a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Talk_About_Sex" title="Let&#39;s Talk About Sex">Let's Talk About Sex</a>". She can be heard speaking Jamaican Patois in the song "<a href="/wiki/Need_U_Bad" title="Need U Bad">Need U Bad</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Jazmine_Sullivan" title="Jazmine Sullivan">Jazmine Sullivan</a>. In August 2008, Denton released her autobiography, which was also entitled <i>Let's Talk About Pep</i>. It was co-written by <a href="/wiki/Karen_Hunter" title="Karen Hunter">Karen Hunter</a>, and it offered a look behind the fame, family, failures, and successes of her life in one of hip-hop's most successful groups. It features an introduction by <a href="/wiki/Queen_Latifah" title="Queen Latifah">Queen Latifah</a>, and an epilogue by <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>. To accommodate the book, Pepa launched her own social network for her fans. On 23 October 2008, Salt-N-Pepa performed at the BET Hip Hop Awards. In January 2011, Denton appeared in an episode of the <a href="/wiki/TBS_(U.S._TV_channel)" class="mw-redirect" title="TBS (U.S. TV channel)">TBS</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Are_We_There_Yet%3F_(TV_series)" title="Are We There Yet? (TV series)">Are We There Yet?</a></i> as Tammy, a woman who falls for the <a href="/wiki/Terry_Crews" title="Terry Crews">Terry Crews</a> character of Nick. Denton joined the reality television show <i><a href="/wiki/Growing_Up_Hip_Hop" title="Growing Up Hip Hop">Growing Up Hip Hop</a></i> as a supporting cast member in January 2016 along with her daughter Egypt (a main cast member) and niece Tahira Francis.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pepa_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Denton once dated and was engaged to rapper <a href="/wiki/Prince_Markie_Dee" title="Prince Markie Dee">Prince Markie Dee</a> sometime during the mid-1980s.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On 6 July 1990, Denton gave birth to her first child, a son she had with New York rapper Tyran "Tah-Tah" Moore<sup id="cite_ref-BN_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BN-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> whom she named Tyran Denton Jr. She made a guest appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/Ricki_Lake_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ricki Lake (TV series)">Ricki Lake</a></i> in 1994, teaching teenage girls the responsibilities of being a mother. Denton was the owner of HollyHood, a retail clothing store in the Buckhead section of <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, which opened in November 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On 2 April 1999, Denton married <a href="/wiki/Treach" title="Treach">Treach</a> (whose real name is Anthony Criss) of the rap group <a href="/wiki/Naughty_by_Nature" title="Naughty by Nature">Naughty by Nature</a> at a Kansas City tattoo parlor after dating on and off for seven years.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Their wedding ceremony was held at their home in <a href="/wiki/Morristown,_New_Jersey" title="Morristown, New Jersey">Morristown, New Jersey</a>, on 24 July 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Denton and Treach had one daughter, Egypt Jahnari Criss (born 2 September 1998). After allegations of physical abuse inflicted by Treach, they divorced in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
