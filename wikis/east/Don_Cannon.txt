<p><b>Donald Cannon</b> (born August 2, 1979) is an American <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a>, record producer, songwriter, and record executive. Cannon is a co-founder of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a> imprint, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">Generation Now</a>. He was also a member of the prominent <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> collective, Aphilliates Music Group, with his long-time collaborator and fellow Generation Now founder, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a>. Over the course of his career, Cannon has produced tracks and albums for artists like <a href="/wiki/Jeezy" title="Jeezy">Jeezy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Logic_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Logic (musician)">Logic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert" title="Lil Uzi Vert">Lil Uzi Vert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, and numerous others. He also served as the Vice President of <a href="/wiki/A%26R" class="mw-redirect" title="A&amp;R">A&amp;R</a> at <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Don_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cannon was born on August 2, 1979, in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLBday_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was raised largely in <a href="/wiki/West_Philadelphia" title="West Philadelphia">West Philadelphia</a>. At the age of 5, he received his first set of <a href="/wiki/Turntablism" title="Turntablism">turntables</a>, a <a href="/wiki/DJ_mixer" title="DJ mixer">mixer</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Karaoke_machine" class="mw-redirect" title="Karaoke machine">karaoke machine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Boom_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boom-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Zilla_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zilla-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> By age 11, he had DJed numerous events, including his aunt's wedding reception.<sup id="cite_ref-Boom_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boom-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TUD_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TUD-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After high school, Cannon moved to Atlanta, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> to attend <a href="/wiki/Clark_Atlanta_University" title="Clark Atlanta University">Clark Atlanta University</a>. While there, he met up with other Philadelphia natives, DJ Drama and DJ Sense. The three formed the Aphilliates while in college together as a loose collection of DJs. Cannon also began DJing parties and clubs in Atlanta's inner city.<sup id="cite_ref-Boom_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boom-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Zilla_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zilla-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HipHop_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HipHop-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Don_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cannon, along with DJ Drama and DJ Sense, officially co-founded the Aphilliates Music Group (AMG) in Atlanta in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-DJB2006_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJB2006-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Cannon often produced tracks on DJ Drama's prominent mixtape series, <i>Gangsta Grillz</i>, which featured artists like Jeezy, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>, Ludacris, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, and numerous others.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLBday_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Boom_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boom-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-XXLGangsta_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLGangsta-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some of Cannon's most notable early production credits with Aphilliates included Jeezy's "<a href="/wiki/Go_Crazy_(Young_Jeezy_song)" title="Go Crazy (Young Jeezy song)">Go Crazy</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>'s "Man Down," and Ludacris' "Everybody Hates Chris." Cannon also helped develop mixtape series like Lil Wayne's <i><a href="/wiki/Dedication_2" title="Dedication 2">Dedication</a></i>, T.I. and <a href="/wiki/P$C" class="mw-redirect" title="P$C">P$C</a>'s <i>In da Streets</i>, and Jeezy's <i>Trap or Die</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-DX2008_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DX2008-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DJB2016_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJB2016-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He would go on to co-host a show on Atlanta's <a href="/wiki/WHTA" title="WHTA">WHTA</a> and became a music director for the show <i>Streets Iz Watchin</i> on the <a href="/wiki/Sirius_XM_Radio" class="mw-redirect" title="Sirius XM Radio">Sirius XM Radio</a> station, <a href="/wiki/Shade_45" title="Shade 45">Shade 45</a>. <i>Good Day Atlanta</i>, the morning show on <a href="/wiki/WAGA-TV" title="WAGA-TV">Atlanta's Fox affiliate</a>, also named him "Atlanta's Best DJ."<sup id="cite_ref-Zilla_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zilla-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-THR2013_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-THR2013-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2006, the Aphilliates Music Group entered into a strategic partnership with <a href="/wiki/Asylum_Records" title="Asylum Records">Asylum Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DJB2006_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJB2006-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In January 2007, however, Aphilliates' Atlanta offices were raided by local police at the behest of the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>, and Cannon and DJ Drama were arrested on <a href="/wiki/Racketeer_Influenced_and_Corrupt_Organizations_Act" title="Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act">RICO</a> charges. Authorities seized 81,000 mixtapes and various pieces of recording equipment in the raid.<sup id="cite_ref-BillRaid_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillRaid-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYTRaid_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTRaid-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>That raid and others like it were subject to public outcry in the aftermath, with some music industry insiders describing the RIAA and its constituent members as "schizophrenic." Aphilliates had largely operated with the express permission of major record labels at the time, because AMG helped promote their artists.<sup id="cite_ref-Mashed_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mashed-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Neither Cannon nor DJ Drama were ever convicted in relation to the raid.<sup id="cite_ref-MTVRaid_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTVRaid-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-XXLRaid_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLRaid-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2008, Cannon announced his departure from the Aphilliates, opting to focus on his own production and promotion company, Cannon Music, LLC.<sup id="cite_ref-DX2008_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DX2008-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-THR2013_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-THR2013-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, he produced the theme song for <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Perry" title="Tyler Perry">Tyler Perry</a>'s <a href="/wiki/TBS_(U.S._TV_channel)" class="mw-redirect" title="TBS (U.S. TV channel)">TBS</a> sitcom, <i><a href="/wiki/Tyler_Perry%27s_House_of_Payne" title="Tyler Perry&#39;s House of Payne">House of Payne</a></i>. He also produced season themes for the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Thrashers" title="Atlanta Thrashers">Atlanta Thrashers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-THR2013_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-THR2013-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HNHH30_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HNHH30-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> From 2009 to 2012, Cannon produced, mixed, and/or hosted numerous mixtapes and tracks for artists like <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Finally_Famous_Vol._3:_Big" title="Finally Famous Vol. 3: Big">Finally Famous Vol. 3: Big</a></i>),<sup id="cite_ref-DJB2016_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJB2016-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Cool_Kids" title="The Cool Kids">The Cool Kids</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Gone_Fishing_(album)" title="Gone Fishing (album)">Gone Fishing</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/Curren$y" class="mw-redirect" title="Curren$y">Curren$y</a> (<i>Smokee Robinson</i>), Jeezy (<i>Trap or Die II</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-FaderTOD_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FaderTOD-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FaderTOD2_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FaderTOD2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Trouble_(rapper)" title="Trouble (rapper)">Trouble</a> (<i>Green Light</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-ComplexTrouble_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ComplexTrouble-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Reese" title="Lil Reese">Lil Reese</a> (<i>Don't Like</i>; hosted with DJ Drama).<sup id="cite_ref-FaderReese_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FaderReese-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2013, Cannon was named the Vice President of A&amp;R at Def Jam Recordings.<sup id="cite_ref-THR2013_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-THR2013-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He is credited with signing and fostering numerous artists while at the label.<sup id="cite_ref-DJB2016_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DJB2016-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ComplexAR_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ComplexAR-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In August 2013, Cannon reunited with DJ Drama and DJ Sense to co-found a new media collective called The Academy.<sup id="cite_ref-AHHAug_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AHHAug-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In September of that year, The Academy announced its initial roster of 23 DJs.<sup id="cite_ref-AHHSep_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AHHSep-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, Cannon and DJ Drama founded the record label, Generation Now, as an imprint of Atlantic Records. One of the first artists they signed that year was Lil Uzi Vert. Cannon served as executive producer on Uzi's 2015 debut commercial mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Luv_Is_Rage" title="Luv Is Rage">Luv Is Rage</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLBday_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sway_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sway-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He also served as executive producer on Uzi's 2017 studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Luv_Is_Rage_2" title="Luv Is Rage 2">Luv Is Rage 2</a></i>, which topped the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLLIR2_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLLIR2-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BillLIR2_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillLIR2-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Other artists that Cannon and Drama have signed to the label include, <a href="/wiki/Skeme" title="Skeme">Skeme</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GripSkeme_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GripSkeme-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jack_Harlow" title="Jack Harlow">Jack Harlow</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-BillHarlow_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillHarlow-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lil_James" title="Lil James">Lil James</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-HHNMJames_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHNMJames-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> and Killumantii.<sup id="cite_ref-HNHHKill_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HNHHKill-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
