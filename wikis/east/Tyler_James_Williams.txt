<p><b>Tyler James Williams</b> (born October 9, 1992) is an American actor. He began his career as a child actor, making several appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Bill" title="Little Bill">Little Bill</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Williams later rose to prominence for playing the role of <a href="/wiki/Chris_Rock" title="Chris Rock">Chris Rock</a> on the <a href="/wiki/UPN" title="UPN">UPN</a>/<a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">CW</a> <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Everybody_Hates_Chris" title="Everybody Hates Chris">Everybody Hates Chris</a></i> (2005–09). Following this, he starred as songwriter Cyrus DeBarge in the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Let_It_Shine_(film)" title="Let It Shine (film)">Let It Shine</a></i> (2012), and Noah on the <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a> horror television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(TV_series)" title="The Walking Dead (TV series)">The Walking Dead</a></i> (2014–15)<i>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He currently stars as Gregory Eddie on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Abbott_Elementary" title="Abbott Elementary">Abbott Elementary</a></i>, for which he received critical acclaim as well as a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Awards" title="Golden Globe Awards">Golden Globe Award</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Awards" title="Screen Actors Guild Awards">Screen Actors Guild Award</a> and two nominations for the <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actor_in_a_Comedy_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series</a>.
</p><p>Additionally, Williams has also appeared in films such as <a href="/wiki/Detroit_(film)" title="Detroit (film)"><i>Detroit</i></a> (2017) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_United_States_vs._Billie_Holiday" title="The United States vs. Billie Holiday">The United States vs. Billie Holiday</a></i> (2021).
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyler_James_Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Williams was born in <a href="/wiki/Westchester_County,_New_York" title="Westchester County, New York">Westchester County, New York</a>, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Yonkers,_New_York" title="Yonkers, New York">Yonkers, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-filmr_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-filmr-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His mother, Angela Williams, is a counselor, and his father, Le'Roy Williams, is a teacher and retired police sergeant.<sup id="cite_ref-filmr_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-filmr-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Williams has two younger siblings; <a href="/wiki/Tyrel_Jackson_Williams" title="Tyrel Jackson Williams">Tyrel Jackson Williams</a> (b. 1997) and <a href="/wiki/Tylen_Jacob_Williams" title="Tylen Jacob Williams">Tylen Jacob Williams</a> (b. 2001), both are actors.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyler_James_Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Williams began his acting career at age 4.<sup id="cite_ref-ebony_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ebony-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He later starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Bill" title="Little Bill">Little Bill</a></i> as the voice of Bobby, briefly replacing Devon Malik Beckford in 2000, and played himself (or an eponymous character, "Tyler") on the <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a></i> from 2000 to 2005. He rose to fame in September 2005 by playing the title character in the hit series <i><a href="/wiki/Everybody_Hates_Chris" title="Everybody Hates Chris">Everybody Hates Chris</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> which finished in May 2009. He won a <a href="/wiki/Young_Artist_Award" title="Young Artist Award">Young Artist Award</a> in 2007 for his work on the show. He was also a guest star in <i><a href="/wiki/Two_for_the_Money_(2005_film)" title="Two for the Money (2005 film)">Two for the Money</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>, and the show <i><a href="/wiki/Hi-Jinks" title="Hi-Jinks">Hi-Jinks</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In late 2009, Williams appeared in the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/True_Jackson,_VP" title="True Jackson, VP">True Jackson, VP</a></i> as True's love interest Justin "Lil' Shakespeare" Weber in the two-part episode "Flirting with Fame."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Williams got the role of Owen in the series <a href="/wiki/Go_On_(TV_series)" title="Go On (TV series)"><i>Go On</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He also played the lead role of Cyrus DeBarge in the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Let_It_Shine_(film)" title="Let It Shine (film)">Let It Shine</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> alongside <a href="/wiki/Coco_Jones" title="Coco Jones">Coco Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Jackson_(actor)" class="mw-redirect" title="Trevor Jackson (actor)">Trevor Jackson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brandon_Mychal_Smith" title="Brandon Mychal Smith">Brandon Mychal Smith</a>. Williams was also featured on nine songs on the film's soundtrack, showing off his rapping skills. The film premiered on June 15, 2012, and his songs "Don't Run Away," "Me and You," "Guardian Angel," "Let It Shine" and "Moment of Truth" all received airplay on <a href="/wiki/Radio_Disney" title="Radio Disney">Radio Disney</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The videos were also played frequently on the Disney Channel. Williams also guest starred in the <a href="/wiki/Disney_XD" title="Disney XD">Disney XD</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Lab_Rats_(American_TV_series)" title="Lab Rats (American TV series)">Lab Rats</a></i> as a future version of the character played by his younger brother <a href="/wiki/Tyrel_Jackson_Williams" title="Tyrel Jackson Williams">Tyrel Jackson Williams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Williams starred in <a href="/wiki/Justin_Simien" title="Justin Simien">Justin Simien</a>'s independent film <i><a href="/wiki/Dear_White_People" title="Dear White People">Dear White People</a></i> as Lionel Higgins,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and was cast as the recurring character Noah on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(TV_series)" title="The Walking Dead (TV series)">The Walking Dead</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, Williams played Russ "Monty" Montgomery in the <i><a href="/wiki/Criminal_Minds" title="Criminal Minds">Criminal Minds</a></i> spin-off <i><a href="/wiki/Criminal_Minds:_Beyond_Borders" title="Criminal Minds: Beyond Borders">Criminal Minds: Beyond Borders</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, Williams was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Crohn%27s_disease" title="Crohn&#39;s disease">Crohn's disease</a> after experiencing symptoms for three years. Since his diagnosis, he has undergone treatment, including multiple surgeries.<sup id="cite_ref-Crohns_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Crohns-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In 2019, Williams was cast as Jake in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wedding_Year" title="The Wedding Year">The Wedding Year</a></i>, a teen <a href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy" title="Romantic comedy">romantic comedy</a> film directed by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Luketic" title="Robert Luketic">Robert Luketic</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Hyland" title="Sarah Hyland">Sarah Hyland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenna_Dewan" title="Jenna Dewan">Jenna Dewan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Shively" title="Matt Shively">Matt Shively</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Anna_Camp" title="Anna Camp">Anna Camp</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, Williams played the role of Paul in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Argument_(film)" title="The Argument (film)">The Argument</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In 2021, he began playing the role of Gregory Eddie in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> <a href="/wiki/Mockumentary" title="Mockumentary">Mockumentary</a> sitcom Abbott Elementary.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/74th_Primetime_Emmy_Awards" title="74th Primetime Emmy Awards">74th Primetime Emmy Awards</a>, Williams earned a nomination for <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actor_in_a_Comedy_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series">Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series</a> for the role.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
