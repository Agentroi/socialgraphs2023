<p><b>LaRon Louis James</b> (born February 18, 1982),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Juelz Santana</b>, is an American rapper and member of <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">The Diplomats</a>, also known as Dipset. He is best known for his appearances on <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>'s 2002 singles, "<a href="/wiki/Oh_Boy_(Cam%27ron_song)" title="Oh Boy (Cam&#39;ron song)">Oh Boy</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Hey_Ma_(Cam%27ron_song)" title="Hey Ma (Cam&#39;ron song)">Hey Ma</a>", which peaked at numbers 4 and 3 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> respectively, as well as <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>'s 2005 triple-platinum single, "<a href="/wiki/Run_It!" title="Run It!">Run It!</a>", which topped the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 for five weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His albums, <i><a href="/wiki/From_Me_to_U" title="From Me to U">From Me to U</a></i> (2003) and <i><a href="/wiki/What_the_Game%27s_Been_Missing!" title="What the Game&#39;s Been Missing!">What the Game's Been Missing!</a></i> (2005) peaked at number 8 and number 9 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> respectively, the latter containing the platinum single, "<a href="/wiki/There_It_Go_(The_Whistle_Song)" title="There It Go (The Whistle Song)">There It Go (The Whistle Song)</a>".
</p><p>In December 2018, Santana was sentenced to 27 months in prison for carrying an unconcealed weapon in his bag at an airport. He was released early from prison on August 5, 2020, after serving 19 of his 27-month sentence.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juelz_Santana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>LaRon James was born in New York City to <a href="/wiki/African-American" class="mw-redirect" title="African-American">African-American</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Americans" title="Dominican Americans">Dominican</a> parents. He was raised in the <a href="/wiki/Sugar_Hill,_Harlem" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar Hill, Harlem">Sugar Hill, Harlem</a> section of Manhattan and began rapping at the age of 5. He cites a live-in <a href="/wiki/People_of_the_Dominican_Republic" title="People of the Dominican Republic">Dominican</a> godmother as a strong influence on his early upbringing.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juelz_Santana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Beginnings_.281994.E2.80.932001.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginnings_(1994–2001)">Beginnings (1994–2001)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juelz_Santana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Beginnings (1994–2001)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At 12, James formed the rap duo, Draftpick, with Malik, a neighbourhood friend. The group first gained recognition after several performances around New York City, including Amateur Night at the <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Theater" title="Apollo Theater">Apollo Theater</a>, where they won two weeks in a row. The two got signed to <a href="/wiki/Priority_Records" title="Priority Records">Priority Records</a> when James was 15 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-Def_Jam_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Def_Jam-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> They released one single on the label, "Play Ruff", with <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Bleek" title="Memphis Bleek">Memphis Bleek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tracey_Lee" title="Tracey Lee">Tracey Lee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1998, James was introduced to <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a> through his cousin, <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and shortly thereafter, took on the name Juelz Santana and became a member of his hip hop collective, <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">The Diplomats</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Freekey_Zekey" title="Freekey Zekey">Freekey Zekey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Santana's first collaboration with the group was the <a href="/wiki/Trackmasters" title="Trackmasters">Trackmasters</a> remix of "What Means the World to You", followed by the tracks "Double Up" and "All the Chickens", on Cam'ron's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/S.D.E._(album)" class="mw-redirect" title="S.D.E. (album)">S.D.E.</a></i>, released on September 19, 2000, through <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="From_Me_to_U_.282002.E2.80.932003.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_Me_to_U_(2002–2003)"><i>From Me to U</i> (2002–2003)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juelz_Santana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: From Me to U (2002–2003)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001, Cam'ron signed to <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-a-Fella</a> Records<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and released his third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Come_Home_with_Me" title="Come Home with Me">Come Home with Me</a></i>, on May 14, 2002, which featured further collaborations with Santana, including "<a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_New_York_City" title="Welcome to New York City">Welcome to New York City</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Hey_Ma_(Cam%27ron_song)" title="Hey Ma (Cam&#39;ron song)">Hey Ma</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Oh_Boy_(Cam%27ron_song)" title="Oh Boy (Cam&#39;ron song)">Oh Boy</a>", which earned Santana his first <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Best_Rap_Performance_by_a_Duo_or_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group">Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group</a> at the 2003 <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Awards</a>.
</p><p>Cam'ron's signing also led to Santana signing a deal with Roc-a-Fella, under the imprint <a href="/wiki/Diplomat_Records" title="Diplomat Records">Diplomat Records</a>. After four mixtapes, the group released their first album on <i><a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_Immunity" class="mw-redirect" title="Diplomatic Immunity">Diplomatic Immunity</a></i> on March 25, 2003, which debuted at number 8 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, selling 92,000 copies in its first week and eventually being certified Gold by the RIAA.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Santana caused a minor controversy before the album's release when he described himself as a "young <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_Atta" class="mw-redirect" title="Mohammed Atta">Mohammed Atta</a>" on a leaked version of the track "I Love You", just months after the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In a retrospective, <a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a> named the album one of the classic albums of the decade.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 19, 2003, Santana released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/From_Me_to_U" title="From Me to U">From Me to U</a></i>, which peaked at number 8 in the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. The album was preceded by the mixtape, <i>Final Destination</i>, released on June 17, 2003, and the single "<a href="/wiki/Dipset_(Santana%27s_Town)" title="Dipset (Santana&#39;s Town)">Dipset (Santana's Town)</a>", which peaked at number 70 on the <i>Billboard</i> Top 100, received a nomination for <a href="/wiki/Best_Rap_Performance_by_a_Duo_or_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group">Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group</a> at the <a href="/wiki/46th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="46th Annual Grammy Awards">46th</a> <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Awards</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="What_the_Game.27s_Been_Missing.21_.282004.E2.80.932006.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="What_the_Game's_Been_Missing!_(2004–2006)"><i>What the Game's Been Missing!</i> (2004–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juelz_Santana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: What the Game&#039;s Been Missing! (2004–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Spring_break_O6_4_(37).JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Spring_break_O6_4_%2837%29.JPG/220px-Spring_break_O6_4_%2837%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Spring_break_O6_4_%2837%29.JPG/330px-Spring_break_O6_4_%2837%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Spring_break_O6_4_%2837%29.JPG/440px-Spring_break_O6_4_%2837%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200" /></a><figcaption>James (yellow shirt) in 2006</figcaption></figure>
<p>Following disputes at the label between Cam'ron and Roc-A-Fella Records head <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> the Diplomats severed ties with Roc-A-Fella and <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam</a> and signed to independent label <a href="/wiki/MNRK_Music_Group" title="MNRK Music Group">Koch Records</a>. The group released their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_Immunity_2" title="Diplomatic Immunity 2">Diplomatic Immunity 2</a></i>, on November 23, 2004, with new members <a href="/wiki/Hell_Rell" title="Hell Rell">Hell Rell</a>, <a href="/wiki/40_Cal." title="40 Cal.">40 Cal.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/J._R._Writer" class="mw-redirect" title="J. R. Writer">J. R. Writer</a>.
</p><p>On December 23, 2004, Santana released "<a href="/wiki/Mic_Check_(Juelz_Santana_song)" title="Mic Check (Juelz Santana song)">Mic Check</a>", the lead single from his sophomore album. The single peaked at number 35 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2005, Santana announced that he had returned to <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam</a> and signed a new joint-venture deal with them.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On May 29, 2005, Santana released the album second single, "<a href="/wiki/There_It_Go_(The_Whistle_Song)" title="There It Go (The Whistle Song)">There It Go (The Whistle Song)</a>", which became his highest-charting single to date, peaking at number 6 on the Hot 100 and number 3 on the Hot Rap tracks charts. A third single, "<a href="/wiki/Oh_Yes" title="Oh Yes">Oh Yes</a>", reached number 56 on the Hot 100 and number 8 on the Rap charts, and a fourth, "<a href="/wiki/Clockwork_(Juelz_Santana_song)" title="Clockwork (Juelz Santana song)">Clockwork</a>" peaked at number 68. On June 30, 2005, Santana was featured on <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Run_It!" title="Run It!">Run It!</a>", which went to number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> for five weeks.
</p><p>On November 22, 2005, Santana released <i><a href="/wiki/What_the_Game%27s_Been_Missing!" title="What the Game&#39;s Been Missing!">What the Game's Been Missing!</a></i>, his second album. Both the album and the single "There It Go" were certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Unreleased_Born_to_Lose.2C_Built_to_Win_album.2C_Skull_Gang.2C_collaborations.2C_mixtapes_and_Love_.26_Hip_Hop:_New_York_appearances_.282007.E2.80.932016.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Unreleased_Born_to_Lose,_Built_to_Win_album,_Skull_Gang,_collaborations,_mixtapes_and_Love_&amp;_Hip_Hop:_New_York_appearances_(2007–2016)">Unreleased <i>Born to Lose, Built to Win</i> album, Skull Gang, collaborations, mixtapes and <i>Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York</i> appearances (2007–2016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juelz_Santana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Unreleased Born to Lose, Built to Win album, Skull Gang, collaborations, mixtapes and Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York appearances (2007–2016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, rifts and disagreements between the various Dipset members was beyond repair and the group disbanded.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In August 2008, it was reported that Cam'ron had sold his contract to Def Jam,<sup id="cite_ref-juelzsold_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-juelzsold-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> leaving Santana to form the hip hop collective <a href="/wiki/Skull_Gang" title="Skull Gang">Skull Gang</a> and label imprint <a href="/wiki/Skull_Gang_Entertainment" title="Skull Gang Entertainment">Skull Gang Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The group released the mixtapes <i>Takeover</i> on September 19, 2008, and the Christmas-themed <i><a href="/wiki/A_Tribute_to_Bad_Santa_Starring_Mike_Epps" title="A Tribute to Bad Santa Starring Mike Epps">A Tribute to Bad Santa Starring Mike Epps</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a> on November 25, 2008, before their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Skull_Gang_(album)" title="Skull Gang (album)">Skull Gang</a></i>, was released on May 5, 2009.
</p><p>In 2008, Santana began working on his third album, <i>Born to Lose, Built to Win</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Despite several single releases, including 2007's "The Second Coming" with <a href="/wiki/Just_Blaze" title="Just Blaze">Just Blaze</a>, 2009's "Mixin' Up the Medicine" with <a href="/wiki/Yelawolf" title="Yelawolf">Yelawolf</a> and "<a href="/wiki/Back_to_the_Crib" title="Back to the Crib">Back to the Crib</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown_(American_singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chris Brown (American singer)">Chris Brown</a>, the album was never released. During this time, Juelz' contributions to <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Tha_Carter_III" title="Tha Carter III">Tha Carter III</a></i> led to his third Grammy Award nomination, Album of the Year at the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Awards</a>.
</p><p>In April 2010, Cam'ron and Jim Jones officially announced the end of their feud,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> and the Diplomats reunited for several singles after signing with <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> In 2011 and 2012, Santana made brief appearances on the first two seasons of <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> reality series <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_New_York" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York">Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York</a></i>, alongside other Dipset group members and their significant others.
</p><p>On December 21, 2012, Santana announced the release of the mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/God_Will%27n" title="God Will&#39;n">God Will'n</a></i>, his first solo project in eight years.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> It was released on January 14, 2013, with <i>XXL</i> later naming it the 9th best mixtape of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> It was preceded by the singles "Bodies" featuring Lil Reese, and "Soft" featuring <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Santana also released music videos for "Nobody Knows" featuring <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>, "Awesome" featuring <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>, and "Everything Is Good" featuring <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, saying that he had shot music videos for every song on the tape.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> A month later, Santana announced his collaboration with Queens-based clothing brand collective, S.B.O.E (Slow Bucks Over Everything), with the release of the mixtape <i>All We Got Is Us</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2016, Santana joined the supporting cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_New_York" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York">Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York</a></i> in its seventh season, releasing the freestyle "Up In The Studio Gettin Blown".<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Dipset_reunion_and_Love_.26_Hip_Hop:_New_York_comeback_.282017.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Dipset_reunion_and_Love_&amp;_Hip_Hop:_New_York_comeback_(2017–present)">Dipset reunion and <i>Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York</i> comeback (2017–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juelz_Santana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Dipset reunion and Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York comeback (2017–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 21, 2017, Santana, Cam'ron, Jim Jones and Freekey Zekey reunited onstage for a performance at the Hammerstein Ballroom.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, the group went on a national tour, <i>Dipset Forever</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> and released their third album, <i>Diplomatic Ties</i> on November 22, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2018, Santana returned to <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_New_York" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York">Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York</a></i> as a main cast member for its ninth season, which chronicled his ongoing legal struggles.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juelz_Santana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On November 23, 2018, Santana proposed to urban model and <a href="/wiki/Video_vixen" title="Video vixen">video vixen</a> Kimbella Vandehee during the Diplomats show at the Apollo Theater.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> They married on January 10, 2019.
</p><p>Santana and Vanderhee were in an on-again, off-again relationship since 2009 and have three children together, Juelz James, Bella Monroe James, and Santana James. Santana also has a son from a previous relationship, LaRon Jr., born in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juelz_Santana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2011, Santana's recording studio in <a href="/wiki/Bergenfield,_New_Jersey" title="Bergenfield, New Jersey">Bergenfield, New Jersey</a>, was raided after a 10-month investigation by the Bergen County Prosecutor's Office and Gang Unit. They discovered two loaded 9&#160;mm handguns, boxes of <a href="/wiki/Ammunition" title="Ammunition">ammunition</a> and 84 grams of <a href="/wiki/Marijuana" class="mw-redirect" title="Marijuana">marijuana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx.com_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx.com-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 2, 2011, Santana was arrested and charged with possession of a firearm, possession of a handgun without a permit, possession of a controlled dangerous substance and possession with intent to distribute a controlled substance within 1,000 feet of a school.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> On March 22, 2011, Santana missed a performance at <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown_(American_entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chris Brown (American entertainer)">Chris Brown</a>'s record release party when he was stopped and searched by police.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> In November 2011 he was charged with making terrorist threats and disorderly conduct.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx.com_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx.com-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2018, Santana fled <a href="/wiki/Newark_Liberty_International_Airport" title="Newark Liberty International Airport">Newark Liberty International Airport</a>, leaving his passport, identification and luggage behind after <a href="/wiki/Transportation_Security_Administration" title="Transportation Security Administration">Transportation Security Administration</a> Officers examining his carry-on luggage found a loaded .38-caliber handgun inside of his bag, as well as eight <a href="/wiki/Oxycodone" title="Oxycodone">oxycodone</a> pills. After going on the run for three days, Santana turned himself into the custody of the <a href="/wiki/Port_Authority_of_New_York_and_New_Jersey_Police_Department" title="Port Authority of New York and New Jersey Police Department">Port Authority Police Department</a> on March 12.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> On March 26, Santana was denied bail.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Santana was released from custody on April 6, 2018, when his mother posted $500,000 bail after putting her house up.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> He was ordered to remain in the custody of his mother and remained on house arrest at his mother's residence in New Jersey while he awaited a federal trial. He was later allowed to travel and perform with the rest of <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">The Diplomats</a> on their 2018 reunion tour, under the condition that his mother accompanied him at all times.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> On December 18, Santana was sentenced to 27 months in prison followed by one year of supervised release.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Santana began serving his sentence at the medium security <a href="/wiki/Federal_Correctional_Complex,_Petersburg" title="Federal Correctional Complex, Petersburg">FCI Petersburg</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hopewell,_Virginia" title="Hopewell, Virginia">Hopewell, Virginia</a> on March 1, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Santana was released from prison early on August 5, 2020, after serving 19 months of his 27-month sentence.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
