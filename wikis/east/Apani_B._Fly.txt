<p><b>Apani Nicole Smith</b> (born September 17, 1974) known professionally as <b>Apani B. Fly</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">hip hop emcee</a>.
</p><p>In 1996 her first <a href="/wiki/12-inch_single" class="mw-redirect" title="12-inch single">12-inch single</a>, "Estragen", was independently released and shortly followed up by several high-profile guest appearances on projects with <a href="/wiki/Organized_Konfusion" title="Organized Konfusion">Organized Konfusion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mos_Def" title="Mos Def">Mos Def</a>, <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharoahe_Monch" title="Pharoahe Monch">Pharoahe Monch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nujabes" title="Nujabes">Nujabes</a> and <a href="/wiki/MF_Doom" title="MF Doom">MF Doom</a>. Her first full-length album, <i>Rhyme-Related</i> (as a member of <a href="/wiki/Polyrhythm_Addicts" title="Polyrhythm Addicts">Polyrhythm Addicts</a>), was released in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She opened for acts such as <a href="/wiki/Fugees" title="Fugees">Fugees</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brand_Nubian" title="Brand Nubian">Brand Nubian</a>. For the past decade, Apani has remained part of the NYC underground scene with frequent performances at S.O.B.'s, <a href="/wiki/Lyricist_Lounge" title="Lyricist Lounge">Lyricist Lounge</a>, and the Nuyorican Poet's Cafe.
</p>
