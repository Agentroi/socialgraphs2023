<p><b>Brandon Chase Korn</b> (born March 15, 1991), professionally known as <b>Bkorn</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>. Bkorn is best known for producing for <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nav_(rapper)" title="Nav (rapper)">Nav</a>, <a href="/wiki/PnB_Rock" title="PnB Rock">PnB Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Kim" class="mw-redirect" title="Lil Kim">Lil Kim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinx" title="Chinx">Chinx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, and more.  In August 2015, Bkorn signed a publishing deal with <a href="/wiki/Sony/ATV_Music_Publishing" class="mw-redirect" title="Sony/ATV Music Publishing">Sony/ATV Music Publishing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bkorn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bkorn has been playing and writing music since he was 11 years old. As an avid rock and metal music fan, he began play the drums in 5th grade for band class, then self-taught himself how to play both guitar and bass."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, Bkorn started to produce primarily hip hop and R&amp;B music using the Apple software application <a href="/wiki/GarageBand" title="GarageBand">GarageBand</a> typically producing <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">Electronic dance music</a> and cover versions of other popular songs. From there, he became more serious about a career in music production. In 2011, Bkorn graduated from <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hartford" title="University of Hartford">University of Hartford</a> with an Associate's degree in Contemporary Arts, and in 2014 he graduated from <a href="/wiki/Ramapo_College" title="Ramapo College">Ramapo College</a> with a Bachelor's degree in Music Production.<sup id="cite_ref-ramapo.edu_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ramapo.edu-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bkorn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_beginnings">Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bkorn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bkorn continued producing throughout his college career, using <a href="/wiki/Logic_Pro" title="Logic Pro">Logic Pro</a> to make beats. In 2012, Bkorn interned at Puff Daddy's studio, Daddy's House Recording Studio. In 2013, Bkorn landed his first placement on DJ Khaled's 7th studio album <a href="/wiki/Suffering_from_Success" title="Suffering from Success">Suffering from Success</a>. The song was "I'm Still".<sup id="cite_ref-ramapo.edu_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ramapo.edu-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Present">Present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bkorn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since then, Bkorn has co-produced DJ Khaled's 2014 <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> Gold certified <a href="/wiki/Hold_You_Down_(DJ_Khaled_song)" title="Hold You Down (DJ Khaled song)">Hold You Down</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and the 2015 single "How Many Times"; as well as late rapper <a href="/wiki/Chinx" title="Chinx">Chinx</a>'s first album single "On Your Body".
In August 2015, Bkorn signed a publishing deal with <a href="/wiki/Sony/ATV_Music_Publishing" class="mw-redirect" title="Sony/ATV Music Publishing">Sony/ATV Music Publishing</a>. Bkorn has also stated that he is interested in exploring <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a> music genres as well. More recently he worked on song Stargazing in the critically acclaimed album Astroworld by <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a><sup id="cite_ref-LN_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LN-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
