<p><b>Keith Omar Murray</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born September 13, 1974) is an American rapper from New York. He is best known for being a member of the hip hop group <a href="/wiki/Def_Squad" title="Def Squad">Def Squad</a>, which includes fellow rappers <a href="/wiki/Redman_(rapper)" title="Redman (rapper)">Redman</a> and fellow Long Island native <a href="/wiki/Erick_Sermon" title="Erick Sermon">Erick Sermon</a>.
</p><p>Murray debuted his spaced out, complex, multi-syllable rhyming style in 1994, when Murray recorded his debut single, "<a href="/wiki/The_Most_Beautifullest_Thing_in_This_World_(song)" title="The Most Beautifullest Thing in This World (song)">The Most Beautifullest Thing in This World</a>" for <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>, which was quickly followed up by his debut <a href="/wiki/The_Most_Beautifullest_Thing_in_This_World" title="The Most Beautifullest Thing in This World">album of the same name</a>, released to critical and commercial success. Along with Def Squad, he was a part of their debut album, <a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o_(Def_Squad_album)" title="El Niño (Def Squad album)"><i>El Niño</i></a> in 1998.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keith_Murray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1990.E2.80.931995:_Early_years"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1990–1995:_Early_years">1990–1995: Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keith_Murray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1990–1995: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Murray got his start in the music industry under the name MC Do Damage, but later decided to just go by his birth name.
</p><p>In 1994, he was introduced to Erick Sermon of <a href="/wiki/EPMD" title="EPMD">EPMD</a> by his friend <a href="/wiki/K-Solo" title="K-Solo">K-Solo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Sermon included Murray on his album <i><a href="/wiki/No_Pressure_(Erick_Sermon_album)" title="No Pressure (Erick Sermon album)">No Pressure</a></i> on the single "Hostile", with critics praising Murray's lyrical ability. His appearance on Hostile led to Murray being signed to <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>, and work began on his first album. Murray's debut single, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Most_Beautifullest_Thing_in_This_World_(song)" title="The Most Beautifullest Thing in This World (song)">The Most Beautifullest Thing in This World</a></i> was released in 1994 and was a hit.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The song was produced by <a href="/wiki/Erick_Sermon" title="Erick Sermon">Erick Sermon</a> (who also provided background vocals) and peaked at number 50 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> and number 3 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Songs" title="Hot Rap Songs">Hot Rap Singles chart</a>. Murray's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Most_Beautifullest_Thing_in_This_World" title="The Most Beautifullest Thing in This World">The Most Beautifullest Thing in This World</a></i>, was named after the single and released in 1994 to widespread critical acclaim and commercial success,<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> being certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) in August 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-KeithMurrayRIAA_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KeithMurrayRIAA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The album was also given "4 mics" by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Source (magazine)">The Source</a></i>.
</p><p>Murray continued to enjoy a high media-profile by appearing on a <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> advertisement and as a featured guest on <a href="/wiki/Mary_J_Blige" class="mw-redirect" title="Mary J Blige">Mary J Blige</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Be_Happy_(Mary_J._Blige_song)" title="Be Happy (Mary J. Blige song)">Be Happy</a>", <a href="/wiki/Total_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Total (group)">Total</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_You_See_(Total_song)" title="Can&#39;t You See (Total song)">Can't You See</a>", <a href="/wiki/R_Kelly" class="mw-redirect" title="R Kelly">R Kelly</a>'s <a href="/wiki/R._(R._Kelly_album)" title="R. (R. Kelly album)">"Home Alone"</a> and both versions of <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/I_Shot_Ya" title="I Shot Ya">I Shot Ya</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However, his appearances of both versions of "I Shot Ya" led to brief friction with rapper <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>, who believed that the song was a diss towards him being shot and robbed at Quad Recording Studios, regarding the timing, title, and lyrical content.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Sometime in the following year, Tupac Shakur approached Keith Murray at the California <a href="/wiki/House_Of_Blues" class="mw-redirect" title="House Of Blues">House Of Blues</a>, wanting to know if the record was a diss to him. Upon questioning, Murray made it clear that the record was not about Shakur, peacefully resolving the situation. Murray has continued to publicly address this in several interviews over the following years.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="1996.E2.80.931998:_Def_Squad"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1996–1998:_Def_Squad">1996–1998: Def Squad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keith_Murray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1996–1998: Def Squad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Murray's second album <i><a href="/wiki/Enigma_(Keith_Murray_album)" title="Enigma (Keith Murray album)">Enigma</a></i> was released on November 26, 1996, on <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive</a>, once again to critical acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The album peaked at number 39 on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> and reached number 6 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">R&amp;B Albums</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-BllbrdAlbms_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BllbrdAlbms-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Murray also continued to work with other artists, appearing on tracks by <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Underground_Kingz" title="Underground Kingz">UGK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Redman_(rapper)" title="Redman (rapper)">Redman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jamal_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jamal (rapper)">Jamal</a>.
</p><p>In 1998, Murray and The Def Squad released their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o_(Def_Squad_album)" title="El Niño (Def Squad album)">El Niño</a></i>, which peaked at number 2 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> and was certified Gold by the RIAA.
</p><p>Murray's third solo album <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_a_Beautiful_Thing_(album)" title="It&#39;s a Beautiful Thing (album)">It's a Beautiful Thing</a></i> was released in 1998 while Murray was incarcerated for assault.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2001.E2.80.93present:_He.27s_Keith_Murray_and_release_from_Def_Jam"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2001–present:_He's_Keith_Murray_and_release_from_Def_Jam">2001–present: He's Keith Murray and release from Def Jam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keith_Murray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2001–present: He&#039;s Keith Murray and release from Def Jam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After Murray was released from prison, he signed with <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam">Def Jam</a> who released <i><a href="/wiki/He%27s_Keith_Murray" title="He&#39;s Keith Murray">He's Keith Murray</a></i> in 2003, once again to critical and commercial success. The album contained the single "<a href="/wiki/Yeah_Yeah_U_Know_It" title="Yeah Yeah U Know It">Yeah Yeah U Know It</a>.", and featured guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Redman_(rapper)" title="Redman (rapper)">Redman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Foxx" title="Jamie Foxx">Jamie Foxx</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-halfway_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-halfway-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The same year, Murray was featured as a playable character in the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Vendetta" title="Def Jam Vendetta">Def Jam Vendetta</a></i>. Despite the success and acclaim of his first album on Def Jam after this, Murray was dropped from the label just one day after its release after being accused of choking two label employees, although he said the confrontation was limited to words. After being released, Murray announced plans to continue recording music independently.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He released his fifth solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Rap-Murr-Phobia" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap-Murr-Phobia">Rap-Murr-Phobia</a></i> on July 31, 2007. It debuted at number 52 on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The lead single was "Nobody Do It Better" featuring <a href="/wiki/Tyrese_Gibson" title="Tyrese Gibson">Tyrese</a> and Junior.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keith_Murray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May 25, 1995, Murray was arrested after being accused of hitting a man with a barstool in a fight and released on bail. After being sentenced to three years in 1998, Murray, who maintains his innocence, fled from authorities until October 1998, when he turned himself in. Murray filed a lawsuit for <a href="/wiki/Wrongful_conviction" class="mw-redirect" title="Wrongful conviction">wrongful conviction</a>, and later dropped the suit in exchange for his sentence being reduced by three months. Murray served the latter portion of his sentence in the Maple Street Halfway House facility before being released on May 14, 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-halfway_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-halfway-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
