<p><b>Ron Raphael Braunstein</b> (born June 7, 1976), better known by his stage name <b>Necro</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Walking_The_Thin_Line_Between_Sex_And_Death_With_Necro_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walking_The_Thin_Line_Between_Sex_And_Death_With_Necro-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Beneath_The_Street_–_Death_Rap:_Special_Edition_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beneath_The_Street_–_Death_Rap:_Special_Edition-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is the owner of <a href="/wiki/Psycho%2BLogical-Records" title="Psycho+Logical-Records">Psycho+Logical-Records</a> founded November 1999. He is the younger brother of rapper <a href="/wiki/Ill_Bill" title="Ill Bill">Ill Bill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:GlenwoodProjects.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/GlenwoodProjects.jpg/220px-GlenwoodProjects.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/GlenwoodProjects.jpg/330px-GlenwoodProjects.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/GlenwoodProjects.jpg/440px-GlenwoodProjects.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="450" /></a><figcaption>Glenwood Projects</figcaption></figure>
<p>Necro was born in Brooklyn, New York City, and grew up in the <a href="/wiki/Glenwood_Houses" title="Glenwood Houses">Glenwood Houses</a>, where he lived for eight years from about six years old to age 14, when he moved to <a href="/wiki/Canarsie,_Brooklyn" title="Canarsie, Brooklyn">Canarsie, Brooklyn</a>. Born into a Jewish family, he is the son of Israeli immigrants, with ancestry from Israel and Romania.<sup id="cite_ref-Necro_Interview_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Necro_Interview-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Necro's father was a Romanian-born Israeli soldier and his mother an Orthodox <a href="/wiki/Baal_teshuva" title="Baal teshuva">baalat teshuva</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-New_NECRO_INTERVIEW_from_Jew_School_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_NECRO_INTERVIEW_from_Jew_School-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> When he was in high school he was popular among the hip hop fans that went to his school<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> until he got kicked out for fighting in the 10th grade and sold drugs to make money. He constantly read books.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He began his musical career at 11, playing in a <a href="/wiki/Death_metal" title="Death metal">death metal</a> band named Injustice. He made a transition from <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-New_NECRO_INTERVIEW_from_Jew_School_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_NECRO_INTERVIEW_from_Jew_School-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 1988, he started rapping after being influenced by his older brother, rapper <a href="/wiki/Ill_Bill" title="Ill Bill">Ill Bill</a>. Necro derived his stage-name from the <a href="/wiki/Slayer" title="Slayer">Slayer</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Hell_Awaits" title="Hell Awaits">Necrophiliac</a>". Before that he called himself "Mad Mooney", which was a character from a <a href="/wiki/Clive_Barker" title="Clive Barker">Clive Barker</a> book. He made his first demo in about 1990 and won a demo battle contest on the Stretch Armstrong and <a href="/wiki/Bobbito_Garc%C3%ADa" class="mw-redirect" title="Bobbito García">Bobbito</a> show on <a href="/wiki/WKCR-FM" title="WKCR-FM">WKCR-FM</a>. In about 1993, Necro started rapping full-time and made a demo called "Do The Charles Manson". In 1995, he proceeded to rap on the Wildman Steve and DJ Riz show WBAU 90.3FM.<sup id="cite_ref-Necro_–_Artist_Page_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Necro_–_Artist_Page-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Exclusive_Necro_Interview!_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Exclusive_Necro_Interview!-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stylistic_characteristics">Stylistic characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stylistic characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lyrical_style">Lyrical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lyrical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Necro lists <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a> as one of his biggest influences in terms of multi-syllabic rhyme structure and content, as well as <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a>, <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rakim" title="Rakim">Rakim</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Geto_Boys" title="Geto Boys">Geto Boys</a>. Necro is also influenced by <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a>/<a href="/wiki/Death_metal" title="Death metal">death metal</a> lyrics, as well as gangster/horror movies, musicals and popular culture.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Necro raps in a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> sub-genre called <a href="/wiki/Horrorcore" title="Horrorcore">horrorcore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has done <a href="/wiki/Rap_metal" title="Rap metal">rap metal</a> and has combined <a href="/wiki/Rap_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap music">rap</a> with <a href="/wiki/Death_metal" title="Death metal">death metal</a> music.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
Necro refers to his music as "<a href="/wiki/Death_Rap" title="Death Rap">death rap</a>" to describe his style of ultraviolent <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and to distinguish himself from other genre labels created by the media. His music combines death metal beats with explicit raps about violence, death, the occult, drugs and sex.<sup id="cite_ref-Beneath_The_Street_–_Death_Rap:_Special_Edition_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beneath_The_Street_–_Death_Rap:_Special_Edition-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Top_Death-Rap_Artists_–_Trish&#39;s_Picks_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Top_Death-Rap_Artists_–_Trish&#39;s_Picks-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-It&#39;s,_it&#39;s_Horrible_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-It&#39;s,_it&#39;s_Horrible-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Necro_–_Necro,_Secret_Society,_Injustice,_The_Circle_of_Tyrants_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Necro_–_Necro,_Secret_Society,_Injustice,_The_Circle_of_Tyrants-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Necro is a self-taught musician and has been playing instruments since the age of ten. He started producing hip hop beats in 1989 by <a href="/wiki/Music_loop" class="mw-redirect" title="Music loop">looping up records</a> and bought an <a href="/wiki/Ensoniq_EPS" title="Ensoniq EPS">Ensoniq EPS</a> in 1991. In the following years he produced beats for artists like <a href="/wiki/Non_Phixion" title="Non Phixion">Non Phixion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cage_(rapper)" title="Cage (rapper)">Cage</a>, Krist and Missing Linx, who all released independent singles. The first record he produced that received airplay was "No Tomorrow" by <a href="/wiki/Non_Phixion" title="Non Phixion">Non Phixion</a> in 1996, put out on Searchlight Music/FatBeats Distribution.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The first wave of albums released on Necro's label were produced by Necro himself. This includes the debut solo albums of Non-Phixion members Ill Bill, <a href="/wiki/Sabac_Red" title="Sabac Red">Sabac Red</a> and Goretex after he already produced 7 songs for Non Phixion's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Future_Is_Now" title="The Future Is Now">The Future Is Now</a></i> in 2002 and <a href="/wiki/Non_Phixion" title="Non Phixion">Non Phixion</a>'s "The Green CD' in 2004, and two albums for label member Mr Hyde.<sup id="cite_ref-Walking_The_Thin_Line_Between_Sex_And_Death_With_Necro_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walking_The_Thin_Line_Between_Sex_And_Death_With_Necro-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Necro_–_Artist_Page_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Necro_–_Artist_Page-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, a beat Necro produced in 1997 was used by <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a> on his album <i><a href="/wiki/Only_Built_4_Cuban_Linx..._Pt._II" title="Only Built 4 Cuban Linx... Pt. II">Only Built 4 Cuban Linx... Pt. II</a></i> for the song "Gihad", which also featured <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_metal_influence">Death metal influence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Death metal influence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the inception of his rap career Necro has been influenced by the genre of <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a>, and more specifically <a href="/wiki/Death_metal" title="Death metal">death metal</a>, which has altered his path into hip hop. He has been quoted in interviews citing death metal as a key influence for his rhymes, <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Schuldiner" title="Chuck Schuldiner">Chuck Schuldiner</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Death_(metal_band)" title="Death (metal band)">Death</a> in particular.<sup id="cite_ref-brooklynvegan1_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brooklynvegan1-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-horroryearbook1_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-horroryearbook1-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Necro has said that he is heavily influenced by the thrash metal albums <i><a href="/wiki/Kill_%27Em_All" title="Kill &#39;Em All">Kill 'Em All</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Reign_in_Blood" title="Reign in Blood">Reign in Blood</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Slowly_We_Rot" title="Slowly We Rot">Slowly We Rot</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Leprosy_(album)" title="Leprosy (album)">Leprosy</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Master_of_Puppets" title="Master of Puppets">Master of Puppets</a></i>, and he has remarked that those albums take him back to "the '80s when shit was pure".<sup id="cite_ref-Death-Rapper_NECRO_To_Make_European_Live_Debut_In_London_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Death-Rapper_NECRO_To_Make_European_Live_Debut_In_London-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Necro rapped over a death metal <a href="/wiki/Blast_beat" title="Blast beat">blast beat</a> on the track "Suffocated to Death by God's Shadow", with the drums being played by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Smith_(drummer)" title="Mike Smith (drummer)">Mike Smith</a> from the band <a href="/wiki/Suffocation_(band)" title="Suffocation (band)">Suffocation</a> and has also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Scott_Ian" title="Scott Ian">Scott Ian</a> of <a href="/wiki/Anthrax_(American_band)" title="Anthrax (American band)">Anthrax</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dave_Ellefson" class="mw-redirect" title="Dave Ellefson">Dave Ellefson</a> of <a href="/wiki/Megadeth" title="Megadeth">Megadeth</a>, two members of the "Big 4", on the track "Evil Rules".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Necro references heavy metal acts in his rhymes as well. For instance, on "Underground", Necro states he will "penetrate your skull like a riff from <a href="/wiki/Obituary_(band)" title="Obituary (band)">Obituary</a>'s 'Slowly We Rot'" and quotes <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Master_of_Puppets_(song)" title="Master of Puppets (song)">Master of Puppets</a>" by saying "Taste me you will see / more is all you need / dedicated to / how I'm killing you". Over the years, Necro has increased his referencing and collaborations with underground metal acts, as evidenced by his 2004 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pre-Fix_for_Death" title="The Pre-Fix for Death">The Pre-Fix for Death</a></i>, which features many references and collaborations with heavy metal musicians from Obituary, <a href="/wiki/Hatebreed" title="Hatebreed">Hatebreed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slipknot_(band)" title="Slipknot (band)">Slipknot</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Voivod_(band)" title="Voivod (band)">Voivod</a>. In addition, Necro created his own group known as The Circle of Tyrants, named after a song by <a href="/wiki/Celtic_Frost" title="Celtic Frost">Celtic Frost</a>. <i>The Circle of Tyrants</i> album also contains collaborations with artists from <a href="/wiki/Testament_(band)" title="Testament (band)">Testament</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exhumed_(band)" title="Exhumed (band)">Exhumed</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sepultura" title="Sepultura">Sepultura</a> as well as song titles named after '80s thrash metal songs such as <a href="/wiki/Slayer" title="Slayer">Slayer</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/South_of_Heaven" title="South of Heaven">South of Heaven</a>" and Metallica's "<a href="/wiki/The_Four_Horsemen_(Metallica_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Four Horsemen (Metallica song)">The Four Horsemen</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-brooklynvegan1_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brooklynvegan1-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Concepts">Concepts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Concepts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Necro is known for releasing conceptual songs. His multilayered, wide-ranging concepts explore how he beats and murders people who aren't as tough as him <sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and the creative ways in which he has sex with women.  Songs have tackled topics like the Manson murders: "Creepy Crawl",<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> "Cockroaches",<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> "Scalpel",<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> "The 12 King Pimp Commandments",<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> "Dead Body Disposal",<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> "The Human Traffic King",<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> "White Slavery",<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> "I Need Drugs",<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> "Food For Thought", "S.T.D.", picking up women ("86 Measures of Game"), cannibalism ("Human Consumption"), suicide ("You Did It"), bondage and sex games ("EdgePlay"), watersports and micturition ("Piss"), life as a pimp ("Out the Pocket"), a revenge tale ("Some Get Back (Revenge)"), and war narratives ("First Blood").<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Necro is a devoted fan of the 1983 film <i><a href="/wiki/Scarface_(1983_film)" title="Scarface (1983 film)">Scarface</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Necro sampled the main character from <i>Scarface</i>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Montana" title="Tony Montana">Tony Montana</a>, in "Cockroaches". Necro has also rapped, "I live scarface scenes" in "Gory Days",<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> and rapped Montana's infamous quote, "you fuck with me you fucking with the best",<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> whilst sampling the quote simultaneously in "asBESTos". Necro also took the title from "<a href="/wiki/Scarface_(Push_It_to_the_Limit)" title="Scarface (Push It to the Limit)">Push It to the Limit</a>" which appeared in the film's soundtrack and used the title for his own song "Push it to the Limit". <a href="/wiki/Al_Pacino" title="Al Pacino">Al Pacino</a>'s work besides <i>Scarface</i> plays a recurrent theme throughout Necro's work. Necro's new collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a> is called <i><a href="/wiki/The_Godfathers_(Kool_G_Rap_%26_Necro)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Godfathers (Kool G Rap &amp; Necro)">The Godfathers</a></i> as is the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Godfather" title="The Godfather">The Godfather</a></i> that made a star of Al Pacino. In "asBESTos", Necro raps, "I'm completely rehabilitated, reinvigorated, reassimilated"<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> which is taken directly from an Al Pacino quote in <i><a href="/wiki/Carlito%27s_Way" title="Carlito&#39;s Way">Carlito's Way</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Injustice">Injustice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Injustice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Necros' first band was <i>Injustice</i> formed in 1986, with Braunstein playing guitar. With this band, alongside his brother Bill on bass and vocals, he played live shows with bands such as <a href="/wiki/Napalm_Death" title="Napalm Death">Napalm Death</a>, Sepultura and Obituary at the Brooklyn club <i>L'Amours</i>. Injustice recorded their first demo called "The Cursed Earth" in 1989 and their second demo called "Inhuman Conditions" at <a href="/wiki/Josh_Silver" title="Josh Silver">Josh Silver</a>'s house in 1990 before splitting up the same year due to not securing a label contract.<sup id="cite_ref-Necro_–_Necro,_Secret_Society,_Injustice,_The_Circle_of_Tyrants_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Necro_–_Necro,_Secret_Society,_Injustice,_The_Circle_of_Tyrants-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Necro_–_Death_Rap_–_Review_–_Stylus_Magazine_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Necro_–_Death_Rap_–_Review_–_Stylus_Magazine-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-An_Interview_with_Death-Rapper_Necro_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-An_Interview_with_Death-Rapper_Necro-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Tartarean_Desire_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tartarean_Desire-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Psycho.2BLogical-Records"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Psycho+Logical-Records">Psycho+Logical-Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Psycho+Logical-Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Necro founded the independent record label <a href="/wiki/Psycho%2BLogical-Records" title="Psycho+Logical-Records">Psycho+Logical-Records</a><sup id="cite_ref-Walking_The_Thin_Line_Between_Sex_And_Death_With_Necro_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walking_The_Thin_Line_Between_Sex_And_Death_With_Necro-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Beneath_The_Street_–_Death_Rap:_Special_Edition_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beneath_The_Street_–_Death_Rap:_Special_Edition-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> in November 1999. In 15 years, Psycho+Logical-Records has released over 40+ albums all available digitally online on over 300+ outlets &amp; physically in stores on CD &amp; vinyl.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Touring">Touring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Touring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Necro has been performing live since the age of 11. In 2007, Necro played the <a href="/wiki/Download_Festival" title="Download Festival">Download Festival</a> at Donington.<sup id="cite_ref-An_Interview_with_Death-Rapper_Necro_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-An_Interview_with_Death-Rapper_Necro-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Some of the groups he has opened for include <a href="/wiki/Run_DMC" class="mw-redirect" title="Run DMC">Run DMC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beatnuts" class="mw-redirect" title="Beatnuts">Beatnuts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sepultura" title="Sepultura">Sepultura</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_Keith" title="Kool Keith">Kool Keith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Insane_Clown_Posse" title="Insane Clown Posse">Insane Clown Posse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Napalm_Death" title="Napalm Death">Napalm Death</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Biohazard_(band)" title="Biohazard (band)">Biohazard</a>. He has headlined for sold-out shows in some of NYC's mid-sized venues such as Brownie's, Wetlands, <a href="/wiki/The_Knitting_Factory" class="mw-redirect" title="The Knitting Factory">The Knitting Factory</a> &amp; SOB's.<sup id="cite_ref-Necro_–_Artist_Page_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Necro_–_Artist_Page-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
During the summer of 2009, Necro was a featured artist on the <a href="/wiki/Rock_the_Bells" title="Rock the Bells">Rock the Bells</a> tour appearing in Toronto, New York, Chicago, Boston, Los Angeles, Washington, D.C., and San Francisco.<sup id="cite_ref-Rock_The_Bells_–_International_Festival_Series_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rock_The_Bells_–_International_Festival_Series-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On January 1, 2017, <i>Necro</i> was invited to participate in the <i>Juggalo March On Washington</i>. He accepted on January 2, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sounds_of_the_Underground">Sounds of the Underground</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sounds of the Underground">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, Necro toured with Sounds of the Underground. On July 25, 2007, he decided to leave the Sounds of the Underground tour. Necro commented, "I decided to leave the tour collectively with my manager and booking agent (both tour organizers) because we felt the tour's demographic was not receiving hip hop well. I was facing hostile, disrespectful crowds and had lots of glass bottles thrown at me. It was making a lot of people in the tour crew nervous and there was concern someone might get hurt (NOT ME). It was also being suggested that maybe I tone down my live show and not antagonize the already antagonistic crowd. I couldn't roll with that as it's not my style. I was holding back enough as it is but there is a line. All the bands on the tour loved us and everyone behind the scenes knew we displayed major heart and never once got fazed by the hostility. We finished every show according to our 25 minute time slot. I will make one thing clear for the haters: we NEVER got booed OFF the stage. Not once. Yes a ton of emo kids booed us at every show, but we had a twenty-five minute set every night and we stayed on stage for every minute of it, and we represented. Every show had at least 200 NECRO fans in the pit tearing it up, and our meet and greets were incredible! It's just a shame these little kids are so closed-minded. The majority of the hate was coming from mop top emo sixteen-year-olds who have no clue who the originators of metal are, and didn't respect my 'rest in peace' chant to metal legends (<a href="/wiki/Dimebag_Darrell" title="Dimebag Darrell">Dime</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Burton" title="Cliff Burton">Cliff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Schuldiner" title="Chuck Schuldiner">Chuck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denis_D%27Amour" title="Denis D&#39;Amour">Piggy</a>, Sob). As far as the tour organizers and I are concerned, you are my peoples so it's all love and I'm thankful for the opportunity to prove I can represent in a variety of situations. This actually did a lot for me and is just one move in many my team will make to blow NECRO up".<sup id="cite_ref-&#39;Death&#39;_Rapper_NECRO_Leaves_SOUNDS_OF_THE_UNDERGROUND_Tour_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-&#39;Death&#39;_Rapper_NECRO_Leaves_SOUNDS_OF_THE_UNDERGROUND_Tour-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Riot_at_Vancouver_show">Riot at Vancouver show</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Riot at Vancouver show">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On Saturday March 21, 2009, Necro had an appearance scheduled at a Vancouver, Canada nightclub (Richards on Richards). When promoters announced that the show was canceled at the last minute, fans became violent and chaos broke out. People began to throw bottles at the venue's DJ (DJ Sage of the Vancouver hip hop radio show Urban Renewal Project).<sup id="cite_ref-Necro_–_Rapper_Necro&#39;s_No-Show_Sparks_Concert_Riot_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Necro_–_Rapper_Necro&#39;s_No-Show_Sparks_Concert_Riot-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Perth_incident">Perth incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Perth incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 5, 2009, Necro was fined A$3,000 after breaking the jaw of a man in <a href="/wiki/Perth" title="Perth">Perth</a>, Australia.  He was arrested at his hotel later that day, forcing the cancellation of his show at Perth's Hyde Park Hotel. He pleaded guilty to the crime.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ani_Difranco">Ani Difranco</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Ani Difranco">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On May 18, 2010, Necro released <i><a href="/wiki/Die_(album)" title="Die (album)">DIE!</a></i> On July 17, 2010, Necro revealed (through his website's forum) that he is being sued by <a href="/wiki/Ani_Difranco" class="mw-redirect" title="Ani Difranco">Ani Difranco</a> due to sampling her song "Used to You" for the track "The Asshole Anthem". iTunes and Amazon subsequently removed the album from their stores, and Necro re-released the album without this track included.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Inspiration_in_Tori_Stafford.27s_murder"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Inspiration_in_Tori_Stafford's_murder">Inspiration in Tori Stafford's murder</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Inspiration in Tori Stafford&#039;s murder">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 23, 2012, jurors in the <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_Tori_Stafford" title="Murder of Tori Stafford">Tori Stafford murder</a> trial in London, Ontario, were presented with a connection between Necro's music and Tori's admitted killer.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The court in the trial of Michael Rafferty heard testimony from convicted murderer Terri-Lynne McClintic that she listened to Necro the day she abducted Tori. Rafferty's defense attorney also noted similarities between the song "Garbage Bag" and letters McClintic had written when she was in a youth facility in 2007 and 2008; the letters described the hypothetical torture and murder of a person. At 5:12pm on April 8, 2009, the day of the murder, Terri-Lynne McClintic was positively identified as purchasing garbage bags. 8-year-old Tori Stafford had been found dead in a green garbage bag on July 19, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Necro issued a press statement on March 23, 2012, saying he does not support "anything that brings harm to children" and that he believes he has been unfairly blamed for the actions of some of his fans.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Directing_and_Necro_films">Directing and Necro films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Necro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Directing and Necro films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Necro went to Brooklyn College for film for two years where he had access to film making equipment. His movie <i>187 Reasonz Y</i> had an anti-cop theme with a police officer getting shot in the head at the end of the film. The film department was repulsed by Necro's visions and he received no support from them.<sup id="cite_ref-Necro_–_Artist_Page_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Necro_–_Artist_Page-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Necro's film company has already released three direct-to-video features (<i>187 Reasonz Y</i>, <i>The Devil Made Me Do It</i> and the <i>I Need Drugs</i> music video).<sup id="cite_ref-Necro_–_Artist_Page_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Necro_–_Artist_Page-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
