<p><b>Kimetrius Christopher Foose</b> (born 1997 or 1998),<sup id="cite_ref-genius_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-genius-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Lil Skies</b>, is an American rapper and singer from <a href="/wiki/Waynesboro,_Pennsylvania" title="Waynesboro, Pennsylvania">Waynesboro, Pennsylvania</a> signed to All We Got Entertainment through <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>. 
</p><p>His three highest charting tracks on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> are "<a href="/wiki/I_(Lil_Skies_song)" title="I (Lil Skies song)">I</a>" (number 39), "<a href="/wiki/Nowadays" title="Nowadays">Nowadays</a>" (number 55), and "<a href="/wiki/Red_Roses_(song)" title="Red Roses (song)">Red Roses</a>" (number 69). His major label debut mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Life_of_a_Dark_Rose" title="Life of a Dark Rose">Life of a Dark Rose</a></i>, was released in 2018 and peaked at number 10 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart. The mixtape was certified Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He released his debut studio album, <a href="/wiki/Shelby_(album)" title="Shelby (album)"><i>Shelby</i></a>, in 2019. He followed it up by releasing his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Unbothered" title="Unbothered">Unbothered</a></i>, in 2021.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Skies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kimetrius Christopher Foose was born in 1997 or 1998 in <a href="/wiki/Chambersburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Chambersburg, Pennsylvania">Chambersburg, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-genius_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-genius-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Stokey_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stokey-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He began <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyling</a> at age three. He was introduced to music by his father, Michael Burton Jr., a hip hop artist formerly known as Dark Skies, now as BurntMan, and his mother Shelby Foose. Foose's stage name is a play on his father's.<sup id="cite_ref-Elevator_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Elevator-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ShipNC_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ShipNC-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Foose and his family moved to <a href="/wiki/Waynesboro,_Pennsylvania" title="Waynesboro, Pennsylvania">Waynesboro, Pennsylvania</a> when he was in third grade.<sup id="cite_ref-Stokey_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stokey-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> When he was 11, his father was injured in a workplace chemical explosion at the Rust-Oleum plant near <a href="/wiki/Williamsport,_Maryland" title="Williamsport, Maryland">Williamsport, Maryland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Skies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932018:_Career_beginnings_and_Life_of_a_Dark_Rose"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2018:_Career_beginnings_and_Life_of_a_Dark_Rose">2016–2018: Career beginnings and <i>Life of a Dark Rose</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Skies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2016–2018: Career beginnings and Life of a Dark Rose">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Skies claims he used to have to sell marijuana and work "little jobs" to pay for studio time and music videos.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He also worked at a McDonald's and a Japanese restaurant in his hometown.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In the meantime, he would spend the summer at a relative's house; it was at that point in time he worked in collaboration with his dad on his first project released on August 14, entitled, "Birth of Skies Vol. 1". Foose and his father would write an album the following year, <i>Father-Son Talk</i>, discussing the recovery process.<sup id="cite_ref-RH_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RH-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Foose graduated from <a href="/wiki/Waynesboro_Area_Senior_High_School" title="Waynesboro Area Senior High School">Waynesboro Area Senior High School</a> in 2016<sup id="cite_ref-RH2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RH2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and briefly attended <a href="/wiki/Shippensburg_University_of_Pennsylvania" title="Shippensburg University of Pennsylvania">Shippensburg University of Pennsylvania</a> before dropping out to focus on his rap career.<sup id="cite_ref-Genius_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Genius-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> His musical inspirations are <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Offspring" title="The Offspring">The Offspring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punchline_(band)" title="Punchline (band)">Punchline</a>, <a href="/wiki/MxPx" title="MxPx">MxPx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Audiovent" title="Audiovent">Audiovent</a> and <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2016, Foose opened for <a href="/wiki/Fetty_Wap" title="Fetty Wap">Fetty Wap</a> at Shippensburg University<sup id="cite_ref-ShipNC_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ShipNC-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and later released his first mixtape, <i>Alone</i>, in January 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Drill_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Drill-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In July 2017, he released two songs, "Red Roses" featuring Landon Cube<sup id="cite_ref-DrillRoses_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DrillRoses-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and "Off The Goop" featuring Sprite Lee.<sup id="cite_ref-ElevatorSprite_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ElevatorSprite-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He would later release other songs that year, including "Rude" and "Signs of Jealousy."<sup id="cite_ref-Complex_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Foose's repertoire of singles caught the attention of <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a> who partnered with his own label, All We Got.<sup id="cite_ref-Genius_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Genius-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-All_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He released his first major label mixtape, <i>Life of a Dark Rose</i>, on January 10, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape debuted at #23<sup id="cite_ref-BillDebut_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillDebut-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> 200 chart and went on to peak at #10.<sup id="cite_ref-Bill200_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bill200-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The songs "Nowadays" and "Red Roses" (both featuring Landon Cube) debuted on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 list at #85 and #98 respectively<sup id="cite_ref-URDebut_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-URDebut-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and have since peaked at #55 and #69 (also respectively).<sup id="cite_ref-Bill100_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bill100-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He also toured nationally with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert" title="Lil Uzi Vert">Lil Uzi Vert</a> in 2017 and began his "Life of a Dark Rose" tour in 2018, but had to end the tour early due to pneumonia.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On May 4, 2018, the music video for "<a href="/wiki/Lust_(Lil_Skies_song)" title="Lust (Lil Skies song)">Lust</a>" was released, peaking at number 87 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 and being certified platinum by the RIAA.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On May 31, he released "<a href="/wiki/I_Know_You_(Lil_Skies_song)" title="I Know You (Lil Skies song)">I Know You</a>", featuring Yung Pinch, which peaked at 79 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 and is now certified gold by RIAA.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2019:_Shelby">2019: <i>Shelby</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Skies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2019: Shelby">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Shelby_(album)" title="Shelby (album)">Shelby (album)</a></div>
<p>On March 1, 2019, Lil Skies released his album <i>Shelby</i>, named after his mother, while simultaneously debuting the music video for lead single "<a href="/wiki/I_(Lil_Skies_song)" title="I (Lil Skies song)">I</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He made a short documentary for the album on his YouTube channel by interviewing her and surprising her during it by telling her he was going to name it after her.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On May 21, 2019, Lil Skies released a video for "Breathe", a song from his album, <i>Shelby</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Top40-Charts_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Top40-Charts-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On July 14, 2019, Lil Skies collaborated with rapper <a href="/wiki/Machine_Gun_Kelly_(musician)" title="Machine Gun Kelly (musician)">Machine Gun Kelly</a> on the song "Burning Memories".
</p>
<h3><span id="2020.E2.80.93present:_Unbothered"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020–present:_Unbothered">2020–present: <i>Unbothered</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Skies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2020–present: Unbothered">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Unbothered" title="Unbothered">Unbothered</a></div>
<p>On March 4, 2020, Skies released the song "<a href="/wiki/Havin_My_Way" class="mw-redirect" title="Havin My Way">Havin My Way</a>", which features close friend <a href="/wiki/Lil_Durk" title="Lil Durk">Lil Durk</a> and serves as the lead single from his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Unbothered" title="Unbothered">Unbothered</a></i> (2021).<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> It was followed by the second single, "Riot", which was released on May 14, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> "On Sight", the third single, was released on November 6, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On July 10, 2020, he released the track "Red &amp; Yellow" from the <i><a href="/wiki/F9_(film)" title="F9 (film)">Road to Fast 9</a></i> mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On December 16, 2020, Foose announced <i>Unbothered</i> and its complete details along with the single "Ok". The album was released on January 22, 2021.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Skies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Foose resides in Waynesboro, Pennsylvania. In July 2019, Foose and his longtime girlfriend, Jacey Fugate, had a son.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Foose cites his own song "<a href="/wiki/Red_Roses_(song)" title="Red Roses (song)">Red Roses</a>" as his personal favorite song, referring to the musical combination with him and Landon Cube as "epic".<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He cites <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> as his biggest music inspiration.<sup id="cite_ref-highsnobiety.com_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-highsnobiety.com-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Foose tries to avoid drugs as much as possible after seeing it negatively impact the lives of his old friends.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In an interview, Skies claims if he was not rapping, he would like to do some "<a href="/wiki/Bear_Grylls" title="Bear Grylls">Bear Grylls</a> type, outdoor stuff" and that his favorite hobby is fishing.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
