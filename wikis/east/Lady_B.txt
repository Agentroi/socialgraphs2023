					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American rapper</div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; font-size: 125%;">Lady B</th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Wendy Clark</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Origin</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role"><a href="/wiki/Radio_personality" title="Radio personality">Radio DJ</a></td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Wendy Clark</b> (born c. 1962), better known by her <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Lady B</b>, is an American female <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Radio_personality" title="Radio personality">radio DJ</a> from <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>. She is one of the earliest female rappers in <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a>, and one of the first hip hop artists to record a <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>, "To the Beat, Y'all", in 1979.<sup id="cite_ref-anthology_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-anthology-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> She began her career with radio station <a href="/wiki/WHAT_(AM)" title="WHAT (AM)">WHAT</a> in 1979, and recorded her first single later that year, "To the Beat Y'all".<sup id="cite_ref-anthology_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-anthology-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The song, the title of which became a stock rap phrase,<sup id="cite_ref-anthology_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-anthology-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was first released by TEC, a local Philadelphia-based record label, and released again in 1980 by Sylvia Robinson's rap label,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sugar_Hill_Records" title="Sugar Hill Records">Sugar Hill Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_B&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Clark recorded her first single, "To the Beat, Y'all," on the Sugar Hill record label in 1979.<sup id="cite_ref-Coard_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coard-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Clark's rise and longevity (3 decades) in hip-hop earned her the title as Godmother of hip-hop. She is one of the first DJs to play rap records on the radio outside <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> playing artists such as <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soulsonic_Force" title="Soulsonic Force">Soulsonic Force</a> at the start of their careers.<sup id="cite_ref-anthology_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-anthology-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-metro_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-metro-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> She had encountered the New York City rap scene while traveling with World B.Free, ex-player of the Philadelphia 76ers basketball team.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1979, Mary Mason on WRNB 100.3 gave Lady B her own weekend show, which transformed into a success and brought hip hop to the radio in Philly.<sup id="cite_ref-Coard_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coard-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and was a large success.
</p><p>In 1984, Lady B moved to Philadelphia's <a href="/wiki/WUSL" title="WUSL">Power 99 FM</a> and started the program <i>The Street Beat,</i> which blew the radio station's ratings through the roof.<sup id="cite_ref-Coard_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coard-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She ran this program until 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphop_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphop-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She later broadcast for <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Satellite_Radio" title="Sirius Satellite Radio">Sirius Satellite Radio</a> in New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphop_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphop-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She also worked for <a href="/wiki/WRNB" title="WRNB">WRNB 100.3</a> in Philadelphia until she was dismissed in December 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-metro_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-metro-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_recognitions">Awards and recognitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_B&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards and recognitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lady B has received numerous awards throughout her career. In 2002, she received the "Philly Urban Legend Award",<sup id="cite_ref-anthology_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-anthology-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> which acknowledges pioneers in rap music. The World Renowned Entertainment,” Role Model of Excellence Award, two Lifetime Achievement Awards (including the Douglass”Jocko” Henderson award) and is also listed in VIBE magazine's History of Hip-Hop as “maybe the most influential female in hip- hop radio history”.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 13, 2022 the city of Philadelphia renamed the 5700 block of Wyndale Avenue “Lady B Way” in recognition to her accomplishments.
</p>
