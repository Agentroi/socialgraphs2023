<p><b>Katari Terrance Cox</b> (born December 30, 1976)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Kastro</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>. A cousin of <a href="/wiki/2Pac" class="mw-redirect" title="2Pac">2Pac</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-capital_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-capital-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Kastro would perform alongside him in the former's rap group, the <a href="/wiki/Outlawz" title="Outlawz">Outlawz</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and is featured on many of the late rapper's songs such as "<a href="/wiki/Made_Niggaz" class="mw-redirect" title="Made Niggaz">Made Niggaz</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and "<a href="/wiki/Hail_Mary_(2Pac_song)" title="Hail Mary (2Pac song)">Hail Mary</a>". When he was in <a href="/wiki/Elementary_school" class="mw-redirect" title="Elementary school">elementary school</a>, he introduced his friend, and future Outlawz rapper, Malcolm Greenridge, later named <a href="/wiki/E.D.I._Mean" title="E.D.I. Mean">E.D.I. Mean</a>, to 2Pac.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kastro_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kastro began rapping under the name K-Dog, in a rap group with Big Malcolm (<a href="/wiki/E.D.I._Mean" title="E.D.I. Mean">E.D.I. Mean</a>), and Young Hollywood (<a href="/wiki/Yaki_Kadafi" title="Yaki Kadafi">Yaki Kadafi</a>). He later rapped on some of <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>'s songs during the studio sessions for his album <i><a href="/wiki/Strictly_4_My_N.I.G.G.A.Z." class="mw-redirect" title="Strictly 4 My N.I.G.G.A.Z.">Strictly 4 My N.I.G.G.A.Z.</a></i>. Eventually, Mutah (<a href="/wiki/Napoleon_(rapper)" title="Napoleon (rapper)">Napoleon</a>) was added to the group and they became known as Dramacydal.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> K-Dog was featured on the song "Outlaw" from Tupac's <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">platinum</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/Me_Against_The_World" class="mw-redirect" title="Me Against The World">Me Against The World</a></i>, released on March 14, 1995. He was also featured on the songs "Tradin War Stories", "When We Ride", and "Thug Passion" from Tupac's <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">diamond</a> certified double LP <i><a href="/wiki/All_Eyez_On_Me" class="mw-redirect" title="All Eyez On Me">All Eyez On Me</a></i>, released on February 13, 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> During the recording sessions for this  album, Tupac gave K-Dog the name Kastro, naming him after Cuban revolutionary <a href="/wiki/Fidel_Castro" title="Fidel Castro">Fidel Castro</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 4, 1996, Kastro featured on Tupac's "<a href="/wiki/Hit_%27Em_Up" title="Hit &#39;Em Up">Hit 'Em Up</a>", appearing in the music video, alongside the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Outlawz" title="Outlawz">Outlawz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The song was the B-side to Shakur's "<a href="/wiki/How_Do_U_Want_It" title="How Do U Want It">How Do U Want It</a>" single and was a vicious diss song aimed at <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Bad Boy Entertainment">Bad Boy Entertainment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chino_XL" title="Chino XL">Chino XL</a>. The song is widely considered one of the most aggressive hip-hop diss tracks ever recorded.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Kastro's other features include "Hail Mary" and "Life of An Outlaw" from Shakur's <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">multi-platinum</a> album
<i><a href="/wiki/The_Don_Killuminati:_The_7_Day_Theory" title="The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory">The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory</a>,</i> which was recorded over the course of seven days in August 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 7, 1996, Tupac <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_Tupac_Shakur" title="Murder of Tupac Shakur">was fatally wounded by gunshots</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Strip" title="Las Vegas Strip">Las Vegas Strip</a> and was rushed to a hospital at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Las_Vegas" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Las Vegas">University of Las Vegas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He died six days later on September 13, at the age of 25. Kastro was present when the shooting took place. He was in the car directly behind Tupac but could not identify the shooters.<sup id="cite_ref-capital_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-capital-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
