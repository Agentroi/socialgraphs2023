<p><b>David Lawrence Brewster Jr.</b> (born June 3, 1988)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>, better known by his stage name <b>Dave East</b>, is an American rapper. East began his career in 2010, and gained attention in 2014 from his eighth mixtape, <i>Black Rose</i>. Its release garnered the attention of fellow New York rapper <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, who later signed East to his record label, <a href="/wiki/Mass_Appeal_Records" title="Mass Appeal Records">Mass Appeal Records</a>, and later in conjunction with <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>.
</p><p>In 2016, East was chosen as part of the <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)"><i>XXL</i> magazine's</a> 2016 Freshman Class. His tenth mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Kairi_Chanel" title="Kairi Chanel">Kairi Chanel</a></i>, was released in the same year and peaked at number 38 on the <i><a href="/wiki/US_Billboard_200" class="mw-redirect" title="US Billboard 200">US Billboard 200</a></i>. His eleventh mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Paranoia:_A_True_Story" title="Paranoia: A True Story">Paranoia: A True Story</a></i>, was released in 2017 with features from Nas, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a> amongst others and peaked at number 9 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. His debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Survival_(Dave_East_album)" title="Survival (Dave East album)">Survival</a></i> was released on November 8, 2019, and peaked at number 11 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200. He released the mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Karma_3" title="Karma 3">Karma 3</a></i> in 2020, to critical acclaim and peaked at number 36 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.
</p><p>East began his acting career in 2017, where he had a role as himself in <i><a href="/wiki/Being_Mary_Jane" title="Being Mary Jane">Being Mary Jane</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Breaks_(TV_series)" title="The Breaks (TV series)">The Breaks</a></i>. He also had a supporting role in the Netflix film <i><a href="/wiki/Beats_(American_film)" class="mw-redirect" title="Beats (American film)">Beats</a></i> (2019) as "Mister Ford". His breakthrough acting role came in the <a href="/wiki/Hulu" title="Hulu">Hulu</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang:_An_American_Saga" title="Wu-Tang: An American Saga">Wu-Tang: An American Saga</a></i> (2019) where he portrays <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>David Lawrence Brewster Jr. was born in New York City and spent his life between <a href="/wiki/East_Harlem" title="East Harlem">East Harlem</a> where his mother resided, and <a href="/wiki/Queensbridge_Houses" title="Queensbridge Houses">Queensbridge</a> where he lived with his aunt in his late adolescent life. The rapper is of <a href="/wiki/Barbadians" title="Barbadians">Bajan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Creole_people" title="Louisiana Creole people">Louisiana Creole</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/People_of_the_Dominican_Republic" title="People of the Dominican Republic">Dominican</a> descent.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He played basketball, and football at <a href="/wiki/Springbrook_High_School" title="Springbrook High School">Springbrook High School</a> in Silver Spring, MD near Washington, D.C.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, he played in the <a href="/wiki/Amateur_Athletic_Union" title="Amateur Athletic Union">Amateur Athletic Union</a> (AAU) with then-future NBA players <a href="/wiki/Ty_Lawson" title="Ty Lawson">Ty Lawson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greivis_Vasquez" class="mw-redirect" title="Greivis Vasquez">Greivis Vasquez</a> and developed a close friendship with current <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Suns" title="Phoenix Suns">Phoenix Suns</a> all-star <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Durant" title="Kevin Durant">Kevin Durant</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_basketball_career">College basketball career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: College basketball career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Springbrook High School, East attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Richmond" title="University of Richmond">University of Richmond</a>, then transferred to <a href="/wiki/Towson_University" title="Towson University">Towson University</a> where he played basketball.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> While attending Towson, East had ongoing issues with coaching staff which ultimately led to his decision to leave the university. Shortly afterward, he started running into legal trouble in Baltimore, Maryland.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932013:_Beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2013:_Beginnings">2010–2013: Beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010–2013: Beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, he released his first mixtape <i>Change of Plans</i> which gained him a little recognition.<sup id="cite_ref-plans_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-plans-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He later released the mixtapes <i>Insomnia</i>, <i>American Greed</i>, <i>Don't Sleep</i> and <i>No Regrets</i>, solidifying his commitment to music.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, East periodically sold drugs throughout Harlem to support himself and his music.<sup id="cite_ref-villagevoice_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villagevoice-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He then released his sixth mixtape <i>Gemini</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.932016:_Mass_Appeal_signing_and_Kairi_Chanel"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2016:_Mass_Appeal_signing_and_Kairi_Chanel">2014–2016: Mass Appeal signing and <i>Kairi Chanel</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014–2016: Mass Appeal signing and Kairi Chanel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2014, his music reached the ears of rapper, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> who quickly looked for ways to contact East. Unbeknownst to Nas, East was close friends with Nas' younger brother and fellow rapper Jungle, who quickly introduced the two.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> By the summer of 2014, East was the newest signee of Nas' new <a href="/wiki/Mass_Appeal_Records" title="Mass Appeal Records">Mass Appeal Records</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Nehru" title="Bishop Nehru">Bishop Nehru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fashawn" title="Fashawn">Fashawn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boldy_James" title="Boldy James">Boldy James</a> and <a href="/wiki/Run_The_Jewels" class="mw-redirect" title="Run The Jewels">Run The Jewels</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In July 2014, he released his seventh mixtape <i>Black Rose</i> through the label, which received favorable reviews from music critics.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, he released his eighth mixtape <i>Hate Me Now</i> which gained great recognition which allowed him to gain key features from well respected emcees such as, <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, Nas, and two-thirds of <a href="/wiki/The_Lox" title="The Lox">The Lox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2016, <a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)"><i>XXL</i> magazine</a> revealed their annual Freshman Class for 2016, which included East, along with <a href="/wiki/G_Herbo" title="G Herbo">G Herbo</a>, <a href="/wiki/21_Savage" title="21 Savage">21 Savage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kodak_Black" title="Kodak Black">Kodak Black</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert" title="Lil Uzi Vert">Lil Uzi Vert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Yachty" title="Lil Yachty">Lil Yachty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Desiigner" title="Desiigner">Desiigner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Dicky" title="Lil Dicky">Lil Dicky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denzel_Curry" title="Denzel Curry">Denzel Curry</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Anderson_Paak" class="mw-redirect" title="Anderson Paak">Anderson Paak</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> on July 7, 2016, East participated in the freshmen cypher along with Chicago's G Herbo hosted by Philadelphia's very own, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In August 2016, he released a joint <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> titled <i>Born Broke, Die Rich</i> with rapper Kur.<sup id="cite_ref-EP_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EP-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In September 2016, East was featured on the <a href="/wiki/2016_BET_Hip_Hop_Awards" title="2016 BET Hip Hop Awards">BET Hip Hop Awards</a> cypher alongside Young M.A., <a href="/wiki/Ms._Jade" title="Ms. Jade">Ms. Jade</a>, Kur and Sam Black.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On September 29, 2016, he announced that he has signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> with the deal negotiated by Hip-hop executive Steven "Steve-O" Carless <sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and the next day released the mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Kairi_Chanel" title="Kairi Chanel">Kairi Chanel</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-kairi_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kairi-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> which peaked at number 38 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-B200_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-B200-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Kairi Chanel featured a few notable names such as, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">The Diplomats</a>; <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beanie_Sigel" title="Beanie Sigel">Beanie Sigel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2017.E2.80.932018:_Acting_debut_and_Paranoia_2"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–2018:_Acting_debut_and_Paranoia_2">2017–2018: Acting debut and <i>Paranoia 2</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2017–2018: Acting debut and Paranoia 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2017, East made his acting debut, starring in <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Being_Mary_Jane" title="Being Mary Jane">Being Mary Jane</a></i> series.<sup id="cite_ref-Bossip_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bossip-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He later appeared in <a href="/wiki/Puma_(brand)" title="Puma (brand)">Puma</a>'s Tsugi Shinsei commercial and starred in rapper, <a href="/wiki/Trina" title="Trina">Trina</a>'s "It Ain't Me" music video.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In August 2017, East released his the EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Paranoia:_A_True_Story" title="Paranoia: A True Story">Paranoia: A True Story</a></i>, supported by a few tracks such as the single "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Perfect_(Dave_East_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Perfect (Dave East song) (page does not exist)">Perfect</a>" featuring American Singer, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, "Paranoia" along with <a href="/wiki/Jeezy" title="Jeezy">Jeezy</a>, and "Phone Jumpin" with <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>. The EP peaked at number nine on the <i>Billboard</i> 200.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Later in the year East released his 11th mixtape, <i>Karma</i> which was hosted by DJ Holiday. This was the beginning of the <i>Karma</i> series. This tape featured tracks such as "Militant" with Bronx rapper, <a href="/wiki/Don_Q_(rapper)" title="Don Q (rapper)">Don Q</a>. <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a> also reappeared on "Bentley Truck" track which was also featured on the Karma tape. In September 2017, East hosted the first annual Goldie Awards, a DJ and producer battle set taking place in New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>East started the new year by dropping his 12th mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Paranoia_2" title="Paranoia 2">Paranoia 2</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> on January 15, 2018. In February 2018 is when the rapper started to network and reach endorsements that would later further his career. In February, East signed a sponsorship with <a href="/wiki/Belaire_Ros%C3%A9" title="Belaire Rosé">Belaire Rose</a>. The signing of this deal led up to a promotional music video for the brand. The track is titled, "Fresh Prince of Belaire" by Rick Ross which featured East.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> March 2018, East signed an endorsement deal with <a href="/wiki/New_Era_Cap_Company" title="New Era Cap Company">New Era Cap</a> launching his new "lifestyle" 59fifty fitted collection. Shortly after his newly found endorsement, East was featured in New Era's "Claim the Crown" commercial along with some of the top MLB stars such as, <a href="/wiki/Javier_B%C3%A1ez" title="Javier Báez">Javier Baez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Altuve" title="José Altuve">Jose Altuve</a>, and award-winning producer, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Will_Made_It" title="Mike Will Made It">Mike Will Made It</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> As things continued to look up for East, he released his 13th mixtape on July 27, 2018, which was titled <i>Karma 2</i>. This mixtape featured up-and-coming artists such as <a href="/wiki/Lil_Durk" title="Lil Durk">Lil Durk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gunna_(rapper)" title="Gunna (rapper)">Gunna</a> and <a href="/wiki/BlocBoy_JB" title="BlocBoy JB">BlocBoy JB</a>. He also had features from his two lifetime friends <a href="/wiki/Kiing_Shooter" title="Kiing Shooter">Kiing Shooter</a> and Border Buck.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Although this tape did not reach the same success as his first studio album, it still managed to peak at number 41 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> East is known for putting out music more frequently than other artists. In the same calendar year, he released his 15th mixtape, <i>Beloved</i>, which saw him join his favorite MC, <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>. The tape exposed and expressed the gritty street life of New York City.
</p>
<h3><span id="2019.E2.80.932021:_Survival_and_Karma_3"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–2021:_Survival_and_Karma_3">2019–2021: <i>Survival</i> and <i>Karma 3</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2019–2021: Survival and Karma 3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>East made his movie debut  in the Netflix film <i><a href="/wiki/Beats_(American_film)" class="mw-redirect" title="Beats (American film)">Beats</a></i> (2019) in which he had a supporting role as "Mister Ford". His breakthrough acting role came in the Hulu series <i><a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang:_An_American_Saga" title="Wu-Tang: An American Saga">Wu-Tang: An American Saga</a></i> (2019) where he portrays <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a> as a series regular. In September 2019, it was confirmed that East would have a supporting role in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Boogie_(2021_film)" title="Boogie (2021 film)">Boogie</a></i> (2021).<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> After a night in Las Vegas gone wrong, East was pulled from the film. The role of "Monk" was recast and eventually played by <a href="/wiki/Pop_Smoke" title="Pop Smoke">Pop Smoke</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, he was featured on the single "<a href="/wiki/Is_U_Down" class="mw-redirect" title="Is U Down">Is U Down</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Pvrx" title="Pvrx">Pvrx</a> and appeared as a guest vocalist on the single "Taxin" by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Shadow" title="DJ Shadow">DJ Shadow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He released a two part promotional release titled <i>Survival Pacc</i> with the singles "Everyday" featuring <a href="/wiki/Gunna_(rapper)" title="Gunna (rapper)">Gunna</a> on and "Wanna Be a G" featuring <a href="/wiki/Max_B" title="Max B">Max B</a> on August 1, 2019, as a pre-release for his debut album.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
On October 4, 2019, East released the single "Alone" featuring <a href="/wiki/Jacquees" title="Jacquees">Jacquees</a> to support the release of his then upcoming debut album. He released the album, <i><a href="/wiki/Survival_(Dave_East_album)" title="Survival (Dave East album)">Survival</a></i>, on November 8, 2019, through <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mass_Appeal_Records" title="Mass Appeal Records">Mass Appeal Records</a>, and his own label From the Dirt.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> It features <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from <a href="/wiki/Teyana_Taylor" title="Teyana Taylor">Teyana Taylor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/The-Dream" title="The-Dream">The-Dream</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Baby" title="Lil Baby">Lil Baby</a>, amongst others.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> East also started his first world tour in release of the album, titled the Survival Tour which spanned from November 22, 2019 — December 20, 2019.
</p><p>On March 8, 2020, East announced that he was working on the third installment in the <i>Karma</i> series of mixtapes. The mixtape cover featured background images of East with the now late rappers <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pop_Smoke" title="Pop Smoke">Pop Smoke</a>, and East stated that he and Nipsey were making plans to create a collaborative project prior to his death. The album was initially set to be titled <i>Thoughts of a Menace</i>, however it was changed to simply <i><a href="/wiki/Karma_3" title="Karma 3">Karma 3</a></i>. Like his previous album, it was released via Def Jam and Mass Appeal.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> He released the lead single of the mixtape, "Really wit Me", on March 12, 2020. Numerous people, including <a href="/wiki/Korey_Wise" title="Korey Wise">Korey Wise</a> made a cameo in the music video.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> <i>Karma 3</i> was released on August 14, 2020,<sup id="cite_ref-K3_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-K3-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> to critical acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> A deluxe version of the mixtape followed on October 23, 2020, with features from <a href="/wiki/Dej_Loaf" title="Dej Loaf">Dej Loaf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Junior_Reid" title="Junior Reid">Junior Reid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/G_Herbo" title="G Herbo">G Herbo</a>, as well as the "Handsome" remix featuring <a href="/wiki/Jeezy" title="Jeezy">Jeezy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 30, 2021, East and producer <a href="/wiki/Harry_Fraud" title="Harry Fraud">Harry Fraud</a> released a collaborative album titled <i>Hoffa</i>. That same year, he purchased a <a href="/wiki/Storefront" title="Storefront">storefront</a> in his hometown, and plans to open additional businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-BigBiz_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BigBiz-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2022:_Upcoming_releases">2022: Upcoming releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2022: Upcoming releases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In a January 2022 interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Vibe_(magazine)" title="Vibe (magazine)">Vibe</a></i>, East revealed he had been working on various projects, which he intends to release in the year, the first of which will be <i>Beloved 2</i>, a sequel to his 2018 joint-album with&#160;<a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>. He also said his next solo project with Def Jam/Mass Appeal will likely be released in February or March. He also confirmed a follow-up to <i><a href="/wiki/Survival_(Dave_East_album)" title="Survival (Dave East album)">Survival</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-BigBiz_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BigBiz-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>East is known for his raw lyricism and dark storytelling about street life.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> He is influenced by, <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Pun" title="Big Pun">Big Pun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">The Diplomats</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dave_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>East as a teen joined the <a href="/wiki/Rollin%27_30s_Harlem_Crips" title="Rollin&#39; 30s Harlem Crips">Rollin' 30s Harlem Crips</a>, a subset of the larger <a href="/wiki/Crips" title="Crips">Crips</a> gang. East started serving a prison sentence, and during this time he converted to <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> and considered himself a Muslim.<sup id="cite_ref-villagevoice_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villagevoice-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Commenting on his conversion, East stated; "Islam really brought a discipline to my life that I didn't really have before... My old mindset was if they ain't helping me, I ain't helping them, but you can't live life that way."<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> East has two daughters with Millie Colon, born in 2016 and 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
