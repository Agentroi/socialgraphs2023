<p><b>Percy Carey</b> (born June 11, 1970), also known by the <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage names</a> <b>MF Grimm</b>, <b>Grimm Reaper</b>, <b>GM Grimm</b> and originally <b>Build and Destroy</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Underground_hip_hop" title="Underground hip hop">underground</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Music_producer" class="mw-redirect" title="Music producer">music producer</a>, <a href="/wiki/CEO" class="mw-redirect" title="CEO">CEO</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eisner_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Eisner Award">Eisner Award</a>-nominated <a href="/wiki/Comic_book_writer" class="mw-redirect" title="Comic book writer">comic book writer</a> from <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. Grimm has released five solo albums, five collaborative albums and two compilation albums.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MF_Grimm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Percy Carey grew up in the <a href="/wiki/Upper_West_Side" title="Upper West Side">Upper West Side</a> section of Manhattan. His neighbor, <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Freeman" title="Morgan Freeman">Morgan Freeman</a>, helped arrange for him to be cast as a child actor on <a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a>, he first appeared in Season 7, Episode 0806. He appeared on the show from age 5 to around age 9.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> "One episode, I lost my tooth, and me and Big Bird had to go through Sesame Street and try to find it," Carey remembers.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The idea for that episode came about after Carey told <a href="/wiki/Jim_Henson" title="Jim Henson">Jim Henson</a> about a dream he'd had the night before.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During his teenage years Grimm became involved with the hip hop community and decided that he wanted to be an MC at the age of 14. His mother supported this dream and bought him turntables and a microphone.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Carey was expelled from Park West High School in <a href="/wiki/Hell%27s_Kitchen" class="mw-redirect" title="Hell&#39;s Kitchen">Hell's Kitchen</a> after beating up a dean over owed drug money.<sup id="cite_ref-villagevoice.com_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villagevoice.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MF_Grimm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MF Grimm's career began in the late 1980s where he performed in New York City. He later went on tour with <a href="/wiki/MC_Lyte" title="MC Lyte">MC Lyte</a> as well. Into the 1990s Grimm continued to perform at freestyle competitions and performed alongside rappers <a href="/wiki/Lord_Finesse" title="Lord Finesse">Lord Finesse</a>, <a href="/wiki/GZA" title="GZA">GZA</a> and <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a>, amongst others. MF Grimm's most recent releases include a three-part conceptual series of albums with producer Drasar Monumental titled <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_Vietnam_(MF_Grimm_%26_Drasar_Monumental_EP)" class="mw-redirect" title="Good Morning Vietnam (MF Grimm &amp; Drasar Monumental EP)">Good Morning Vietnam</a></i>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MF_Grimm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the early nineties Carey moved to California, where he formed a DJ/rapper group called the Gravediggaz (not to be confused with the <a href="/wiki/Gravediggaz" title="Gravediggaz">Gravediggaz</a>) and through the mid-nineties worked together with <a href="/wiki/Roc_Raida" title="Roc Raida">Roc Raida</a> as his DJ. In 1993, Grimm placed third at the World Supremacy Battle, already known as an experienced <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">battle rapper</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> That year, he released his first 12" single, <i>So Whatcha Want Nigga?</i> (later released on the <i><a href="/wiki/Scars_%26_Memories" title="Scars &amp; Memories">Scars &amp; Memories</a></i> album in 2005) and performed at the Jack The Rapper convention on Execution Night, sharing the stage with <a href="/wiki/Lady_of_Rage" class="mw-redirect" title="Lady of Rage">Lady of Rage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tha_Dogg_Pound" title="Tha Dogg Pound">Tha Dogg Pound</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>. He also collaborated with hip-hop legends like <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurious" title="Kurious">Kurious</a> and <a href="/wiki/KMD" title="KMD">KMD</a>, appearing on albums and singles by them.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1993, MF Grimm had recorded an album's worth of material that was to serve as his debut album. After being shot and hospitalized, a lot of the material was stolen or lost, as it wasn't expected that he would survive his coma. After he woke up from the coma Grimm was able to retrieve some of the lost records and some of this material went on to be released later as singles, and later formed the 2005 compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/Scars_%26_Memories" title="Scars &amp; Memories">Scars &amp; Memories</a></i>.
</p><p>In the mid-late 1990s, MF Grimm went on to release several 12" singles on <a href="/wiki/Bobbito" class="mw-redirect" title="Bobbito">Bobbito</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fondle_%27Em" class="mw-redirect" title="Fondle &#39;Em">Fondle 'Em</a> label and started collaborating extensively with label mate <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Dumile" class="mw-redirect" title="Daniel Dumile">MF Doom</a> whose debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Operation:_Doomsday" title="Operation: Doomsday">Operation: Doomsday</a></i> was recorded in Grimm's basement, and for which Grimm served as an executive producer (financing the project and supplying samples for the album). It was during this time that Grimm founded the <a href="/wiki/Day_By_Day_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Day By Day Entertainment">Day By Day Entertainment</a> label, as well as the hip hop collective <a href="/wiki/Monsta_Island_Czars" title="Monsta Island Czars">Monsta Island Czars</a> with MF Doom.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MF_Grimm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>MF Grimm's first release of the 2000s was the collaborative <i><a href="/wiki/MF_EP" title="MF EP">MF EP</a></i> with MF DOOM on Brick Records. The release included remixes, as well as new material and the instrumental versions to every track.
</p><p>In 2001, MF Grimm recorded his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Downfall_of_Ibliys:_A_Ghetto_Opera" title="The Downfall of Ibliys: A Ghetto Opera">The Downfall of Ibliys: A Ghetto Opera</a></i> while on bail after his prison sentencing.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork.com_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork.com-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The entire album was recorded during a twenty four-hour period, and featured production and appearances from <a href="/wiki/Count_Bass_D" title="Count Bass D">Count Bass D</a> and <a href="/wiki/MF_DOOM" class="mw-redirect" title="MF DOOM">MF DOOM</a>. The album was released on Grimm's <a href="/wiki/Day_By_Day_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Day By Day Entertainment">Day By Day Entertainment</a> label, although he did not hear the album until after his release from prison several years later. Around the time of MF Grimm's release, Day By Day Entertainment released a compilation of MF Grimm and MF DOOM material titled <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_of_MF&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Best of MF (page does not exist)">Best of MF</a></i>. This compilation included features both artists had done, as well as several then-unreleased tracks. Some of the tracks would later be re-released under different titles on various projects.
</p><p>In addition to releasing his own material, Grimm has served as an executive producer on various prolific and now-classic albums from the period, including <i><a href="/wiki/Operation:_Doomsday" title="Operation: Doomsday">Operation: Doomsday</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/KMD" title="KMD">KMD</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Bastards" title="Black Bastards">Black Bastards</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Escape_from_Monsta_Island!" title="Escape from Monsta Island!">Escape from Monsta Island!</a></i>, amongst others.
</p><p>After his release from prison, Grimm completed his second album <i><a href="/wiki/Digital_Tears:_E-mail_from_Purgatory" title="Digital Tears: E-mail from Purgatory">Digital Tears: E-mail from Purgatory</a></i> in 2004 under his <a href="/wiki/Monsta_Island_Czars" title="Monsta Island Czars">Monsta Island Czars</a> moniker Superstar Jet Jaguar. The album's lyrics as well as drum beats were all created while in prison using a portable drum machine that Grimm managed to hide from the prison guards. Following that release he and MF DOOM collaborated once more for his third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Special_Herbs_%2B_Spices_Volume_1" title="Special Herbs + Spices Volume 1">Special Herbs + Spices Volume 1</a></i>, also released in 2004. This album featured MF Grimm freestyling over MF DOOM beats taken from his <i><a href="/wiki/Special_Herbs" class="mw-redirect" title="Special Herbs">Special Herbs</a></i> instrumental album series. Following this release, MF Grimm released a compilation of material recorded and released prior to <i>The Downfall of Ibliys: a ghetto opera</i>. Underground hit singles, as well as some harder-to-find material, were included and the compilation was released in 2005 as <i><a href="/wiki/Scars_%26_Memories" title="Scars &amp; Memories">Scars &amp; Memories</a></i>
</p><p>In 2006, MF Grimm released a 60-track, triple-CD album, <i><a href="/wiki/American_Hunger" title="American Hunger">American Hunger</a></i>, the first ever in hip hop on his Day By Day Entertainment label. The album also featured members of Monsta Island Czars. Each of the three discs was intended as a separate "meal" -- "Breakfast," "Lunch" and "The Last Supper." Included on the album is the track "The Book of Daniel," a scathing diss track recorded in response to MF DOOM's diss against Monsta Island Czars (referring to them as "midgets into crunk").
</p><p>Grimm's life story was retold in a graphic novel autobiography illustrated by Ronald Wimberly, <i><a href="/wiki/Sentences:_The_Life_of_MF_Grimm" title="Sentences: The Life of MF Grimm">Sentences: The Life of MF Grimm</a></i>, which was released by <a href="/wiki/DC_Comics" title="DC Comics">DC Comics</a>' <a href="/wiki/Vertigo_Comics" title="Vertigo Comics">Vertigo</a> imprint in September 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Two months later, MF Grimm appeared on <a href="/wiki/National_Public_Radio" class="mw-redirect" title="National Public Radio">National Public Radio</a> to relate his life story and future plans.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> That year MF Grimm also released the concept album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunt_for_the_Gingerbread_Man" title="The Hunt for the Gingerbread Man">The Hunt for the Gingerbread Man</a></i>, with a mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunt_for_the_Gingerbread_Man#The_Order_of_the_Baker_Mixtape" title="The Hunt for the Gingerbread Man">The Order of the Baker Mixtape</a></i> released as a prequel.
</p><p>In 2008, Grimm, under his real name Percy Carey, was nominated for two Eisner Awards for Sentences, Best Reality Based Work and Best Lettering. He was also nominated for, and won, two Glyph Awards for Best Cover and Story of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Grimm released a short mixtape on the Day By Day Entertainment website entitled <i><a href="/wiki/You_Only_Live_Twice:_The_Audio_Graphic_Novel#Story_Mixtape" title="You Only Live Twice: The Audio Graphic Novel">Story: Substance, Style, Structure and the Principles of Hip Hop</a></i> as a promotional release preceding his work in the new decade.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MF_Grimm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, Grimm released his seventh studio album <i><a href="/wiki/You_Only_Live_Twice:_The_Audio_Graphic_Novel" title="You Only Live Twice: The Audio Graphic Novel">You Only Live Twice: The Audio Graphic Novel</a></i> which was accompanied by a 13-page comic book based on the album by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Mahfood" title="Jim Mahfood">Jim Mahfood</a>.
</p><p>In 2012, he collaborated with underground producer Drasar Monumental and the Vendetta Vinyl label to record a series of releases. The first release by the duo was a mixtape entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_Vietnam_(MF_Grimm_%26_Drasar_Monumental_EP)#Preemptive_Strike_Mixtape" class="mw-redirect" title="Good Morning Vietnam (MF Grimm &amp; Drasar Monumental EP)">Preemptive Strike</a></i> followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_Vietnam_(MF_Grimm_%26_Drasar_Monumental_EP)" class="mw-redirect" title="Good Morning Vietnam (MF Grimm &amp; Drasar Monumental EP)">Good Morning Vietnam</a></i>. The duo followed up their success by releasing a full-length sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_Vietnam_2:_The_Golden_Triangle" title="Good Morning Vietnam 2: The Golden Triangle">Good Morning Vietnam 2: The Golden Triangle</a></i> in November 2013. This release further explored the Vietnam concept, and introduced heroin as a theme as well. The duo released the third and final iteration to the series, <a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_Vietnam_3:_The_Phoenix_Program" title="Good Morning Vietnam 3: The Phoenix Program">Good Morning Vietnam 3: The Phoenix Program</a> November 25, 2014.
</p><p>On February 4, 2015, MF Grimm announced the release of a compilation of his love songs for release on February 14, 2015, titled <i>MF Love Songs</i>. The release is slated to include material from across his entire career.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2020s">2020s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MF_Grimm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2020s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 15, 2021, MF Grimm released <i>The Hunt For The Gingerbread Man 2: Get The Dough</i> produced by Darko the Super,<sup id="cite_ref-MF_Grimm_–_The_Hunt_for_the_Gingerbread_Man_2:_Get_the_Dough_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MF_Grimm_–_The_Hunt_for_the_Gingerbread_Man_2:_Get_the_Dough-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> premiering an animated music video for the track <i>Candy Rain (Falling Down)</i> directed by NutzenTV.<sup id="cite_ref-MF_Grimm_–_Candy_Rain_(Falling_Down)_animated_music_video_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MF_Grimm_–_Candy_Rain_(Falling_Down)_animated_music_video-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aliases">Aliases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MF_Grimm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aliases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MF Grimm has used a number of aliases over the years. He was given the name "Build and Destroy" by King Sun as a teen, but switched to the name "The Grimm Reaper" by the end of the 1980s. "MF Grimm" came about from his time living in California, where he was often told that he had "mad flows." As part of Monsta Island Czars, Grimm has used the names Jet Jaguar and Superstar Jet Jaguar. After his release from prison, Grimm also used the GM Grimm moniker, short for Grandmaster Grimm. Since 2004, Grimm has continued to use the "MF Grimm" moniker, although he sometimes refers to himself as "GM Grimm" or "Jet Jaguar."
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MF_Grimm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1994, Grimm survived an attempted murder where he was shot seven times by rival drug dealers,<sup id="cite_ref-villagevoice.com_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villagevoice.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> following a previous three shots in an earlier murder attempt in 1986. As a result of the 1994 attack, he was deafened, blinded, paralyzed from the waist down, and comatose; doctors predicted that he would have dulled senses and be paralyzed for the rest of his life. MF Grimm's half-brother and manager Jansen Smalls died in the attack. As a result of this attack, all the music labels interested in Grimm's music cut all contact with him. Grimm recovered full vision, hearing, and speech ability, but still uses a <a href="/wiki/Wheelchair" title="Wheelchair">wheelchair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Grimm founded label <a href="/wiki/Day_By_Day_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Day By Day Entertainment">Day By Day Entertainment</a> in the late-1990s. At around the same time, he put together his <a href="/wiki/Godzilla" title="Godzilla">Godzilla</a>-inspired MC collective, the <a href="/wiki/Monsta_Island_Czars" title="Monsta Island Czars">Monsta Island Czars</a>, which at the time also featured MF Doom. However, to pay for the extensive medical bills from his hospitalization, Grimm turned back to drug dealing. This lifestyle eventually caught up with him again when his organization was taken down by the police.
</p><p>Facing narcotics conspiracy charges, Grimm was sentenced to <a href="/wiki/Life_imprisonment" title="Life imprisonment">life imprisonment</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Rockefeller_Drug_Laws" title="Rockefeller Drug Laws">Rockefeller Drug Laws</a> in 2000. Before his sentence began, Grimm recorded his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Downfall_of_Ibliys:_A_Ghetto_Opera" title="The Downfall of Ibliys: A Ghetto Opera">The Downfall of Ibliys: A Ghetto Opera</a></i>. In 2003, his life sentence was reduced and was eventually released.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork.com_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork.com-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_MF_DOOM">Relationship with MF DOOM</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MF_Grimm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Relationship with MF DOOM">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>MF Grimm and <a href="/wiki/MF_DOOM" class="mw-redirect" title="MF DOOM">MF DOOM</a> became friends in the early 1990s, bonding over mutual interests in Hip Hop, comics, and mutual friends. Grimm featured on KMD's 1993 single What a Niggy Know (Remix), credited as "The Grimm Reaper." After the death of DOOM's brother <a href="/wiki/DJ_Subroc" title="DJ Subroc">DJ Subroc</a> and Grimm's paralysis, the two bonded, living together and looking after one another. Although Grimm maintained a presence in Hip Hop, DOOM did not reemerge until the late 1990s. Grimm, in his capacity as executive producer, funded <i><a href="/wiki/Operation:_Doomsday" title="Operation: Doomsday">Operation: Doomsday</a></i> as well as providing the samples which appeared on the album. Grimm also funded the release of KMD's second album <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Bastards" title="Black Bastards">Black Bastards</a></i>, once again serving as an executive producer.
</p><p>In 2000, DOOM and Grimm released the collaborative <i><a href="/wiki/MF_EP" title="MF EP">MF EP</a></i>, featuring both artists splitting the record's material, and including the instrumental versions. Upon Grimm's release from prison in 2003, Day By Day Entertainment released a compilation of MF Grimm and MF DOOM material as <i>Best of MF</i>. In 2004, the duo released a collaborative album of Grimm rapping over Doom's <i><a href="/wiki/Special_Herbs" class="mw-redirect" title="Special Herbs">Special Herbs</a></i> beats called <i><a href="/wiki/Special_Herbs_%2B_Spices_Volume_1" title="Special Herbs + Spices Volume 1">Special Herbs + Spices Volume 1</a></i>.
</p>
