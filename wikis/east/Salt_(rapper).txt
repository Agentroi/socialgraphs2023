<p><b>Cheryl Renee James</b> (born March 28, 1966),<sup id="cite_ref-bio_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> better known by her stage name <b>Salt</b>, is an American rapper. She is best known as a member of the female rap trio <a href="/wiki/Salt-N-Pepa" title="Salt-N-Pepa">Salt-N-Pepa</a>, which also includes Pepa (<a href="/wiki/Sandra_Denton" class="mw-redirect" title="Sandra Denton">Sandra Denton</a>) and Spinderella (<a href="/wiki/Spinderella" class="mw-redirect" title="Spinderella">Deidra "Dee Dee" Roper</a>). James starred in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Salt-N-Pepa_Show" title="The Salt-N-Pepa Show">The Salt-N-Pepa Show</a></i>, a reality TV series focusing on reforming the group; which aired on the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> network in 2008.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salt_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The daughter of a transit worker and Barbara James,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> a bank manager,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> James was born in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. The middle of three children, James grew up in the <a href="/wiki/Bushwick,_Brooklyn" title="Bushwick, Brooklyn">Bushwick</a> section of Brooklyn.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> James attended <a href="/wiki/Grover_Cleveland_High_School_(Queens)" title="Grover Cleveland High School (Queens)">Grover Cleveland High School</a>, graduating in 1983.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> After high school, James enrolled at <a href="/wiki/Queensborough_Community_College" title="Queensborough Community College">Queensborough Community College</a> to study nursing. James met Queens-native Sandra Denton in 1985 while attending college.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salt_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After forming a friendship with Denton, James' then-boyfriend and co-worker <a href="/wiki/Hurby_Azor" title="Hurby Azor">Hurby Azor</a> approached the pair and asked them to record a rap for a  class project. With Azor's guidance as their manager, James and Denton then formed the group<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> "Super Nature" (which eventually changed to "Salt N' Pepa") and  were joined by <a href="/wiki/Latoya_Hanson" class="mw-redirect" title="Latoya Hanson">Latoya Hanson</a> who was replaced by Deidra Roper joined in 1987. They released five studio albums: <i><a href="/wiki/Hot,_Cool_%26_Vicious" title="Hot, Cool &amp; Vicious">Hot, Cool &amp; Vicious</a></i> (1986), <i><a href="/wiki/A_Salt_with_a_Deadly_Pepa" title="A Salt with a Deadly Pepa">A Salt with a Deadly Pepa</a></i> (1988), <i><a href="/wiki/Blacks%27_Magic" title="Blacks&#39; Magic">Blacks' Magic</a></i> (1990), <i><a href="/wiki/Very_Necessary" title="Very Necessary">Very Necessary</a></i> (1993), and <i><a href="/wiki/Brand_New_(Salt-n-Pepa_album)" title="Brand New (Salt-n-Pepa album)">Brand New</a></i> (1997). Salt also co-starred in the 1993 motion picture <i><a href="/wiki/Who%27s_the_Man%3F" title="Who&#39;s the Man?">Who's the Man?</a></i>. In 1997, she recorded the song "Stomp" with <a href="/wiki/Gospel_music" title="Gospel music">gospel</a> artists <a href="/wiki/Kirk_Franklin" title="Kirk Franklin">Kirk Franklin</a> and <a href="/wiki/God%27s_Property" title="God&#39;s Property">God's Property</a> for their album <i><a href="/wiki/God%27s_Property_from_Kirk_Franklin%27s_Nu_Nation" title="God&#39;s Property from Kirk Franklin&#39;s Nu Nation">God's Property</a></i>, one of the bestselling albums in gospel music history. They were the first female rap act to have gold, platinum, and multi-platinum albums, and the first female rap act to win a <a href="/wiki/Grammy" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy">Grammy</a>. The group disbanded in 2002 but reformed in 2008.
</p><p>James appeared on VH1's inaugural <i><a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_Honors" title="Hip Hop Honors">Hip Hop Honors</a></i> program in November 2004, along with Pepa, but they did not perform. Salt, Pepa, and Spinderella, however, did perform on the second Hip Hop Honors on September 22, 2005, performing their hit "<a href="/wiki/Whatta_Man" class="mw-redirect" title="Whatta Man">Whatta Man</a>". This was the trio's first performance as Salt-N-Pepa since 1999. On October 23, 2008, Salt-N-Pepa performed "Shoop", <a href="/wiki/Push_It_(Salt-n-Pepa_song)" title="Push It (Salt-n-Pepa song)">"Push It"</a>, and "Whatta Man" at the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Entertainment Television">BET</a> Hip Hop Awards. In 2009, James was featured in the Generation Gospel Exclusive on 106 &amp; Gospel.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salt_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>James dated Salt-N-Pepa group manager <a href="/wiki/Hurby_Azor" title="Hurby Azor">Hurby Azor</a> during the early start of the group from 1984 until 1989. James married her husband Gavin Wray on Christmas Eve 2000, although they dated for ten years before the marriage. James and Wray have two children.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bio_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>James and her daughter are referenced in Tupac's song "Keep Ya Head Up". In an interview James stated she considers herself a feminist "in a way", emphasizing the need for women to avoid complete emotional and financial dependence on men. Songs like "Tramp" and "<a href="/wiki/Shake_Your_Thang" title="Shake Your Thang">Shake Your Thang</a>" by Salt-N-Pepa express such feminist themes as female autonomy.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> She was baptized into the <a href="/wiki/Seventh-day_Adventist_Church" title="Seventh-day Adventist Church">Seventh-day Adventist Church</a> on a mission trip in <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> with <a href="/wiki/Oakwood_University" title="Oakwood University">Oakwood College</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
