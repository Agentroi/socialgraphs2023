<p><b>Edward Anderson</b> (born November 27,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 1970) is a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip-hop</a> artist from <a href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a>, better known by his stage name <b>Edo G</b>. Edo is the seniormost hip-hop artist from Boston (if not from anywhere in Massachusetts), in the sense that he was the first to gain recognition in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and in the wider <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" class="mw-redirect" title="United States of America">United States of America</a>, and remains one of the best-known Boston rap artist internationally, having collaborated with large numbers of independent rap artists all over the globe. He is additionally known by the name <b>Ed O.G.</b>, which was initially a misspelling by a record label but has endured.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ed_O.G.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Edward Anderson was born in <a href="/wiki/Roxbury,_Boston" title="Roxbury, Boston">Roxbury</a>—a working class, predominantly black neighborhood in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston, Massachusetts</a>. His first <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> was Ed Rock, and started his career in the late 1980s at age 15, in a crew called F.T.I. – Fresh To Impress. They had a song called  "Suzi Q" on the "Boston Goes Def!" vinyl LP compilation, which was released in 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Three years later, with the help of New York's Awesome 2 duo (Teddy Ted and Special K) formed Ed O.G and Da Bulldogs in late 1989. He released his first album in 1991 with his group Da Bulldogs, titled <i>Life of a Kid in the Ghetto</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kellman_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kellman-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The album included the song "Be a Father to Your Child," which received <a href="/wiki/Airplay" title="Airplay">airplay</a> on local Boston radio stations, and "I Got To Have It", which was sampled later by <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a> on the track "<a href="/wiki/Ooh!" title="Ooh!">Ooh!</a>". The group was dropped from <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Records" title="Mercury Records">Mercury</a> Records in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-Conaway_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Conaway-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Edo G. has subsequently gone on to release four solo <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">albums</a> and two <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EPs</a>, toured around the world, and worked with other artists including <a href="/wiki/Pete_Rock" title="Pete Rock">Pete Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>, <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a>, <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>, <a href="/wiki/Common_(rapper)" title="Common (rapper)">Common</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_Thought" title="Black Thought">Black Thought</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">The Roots</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masta_Ace" title="Masta Ace">Masta Ace</a>.
</p><p>On his <a href="/wiki/Solo_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Solo album">solo album</a> <i>The Truth Hurts</i>, he combined with DJ Premier and Pete Rock; "Wishful Thinking" was a <a href="/wiki/Sequel" title="Sequel">sequel</a> of his first solo performance. The connection with Rock turned into a <a href="/wiki/Partnership" title="Partnership">partnership</a> for the album <i>My Own Worst Enemy</i>. One of the album's songs, "Wishing" (featuring Masta Ace), was featured on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boondocks_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Boondocks (TV series)">The Boondocks</a></i> in episode 9, Return of the King.
</p><p>In 2005, Edo G. started a new group called <a href="/wiki/Special_Teamz" title="Special Teamz">Special Teamz</a> with fellow Boston rappers Jaysaun (of Kreators) and <a href="/wiki/Slaine_(rapper)" title="Slaine (rapper)">Slaine</a> (of <a href="/wiki/La_Coka_Nostra" title="La Coka Nostra">La Coka Nostra</a>). They released a <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> on November 11, 2005, entitled <i>The Mixtape</i>. Special Teamz released the album, <i>Stereotypez</i>, on September 25, 2007, which featured production from Pete Rock, DJ Premier, and specialist <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a> <a href="/wiki/Marco_Polo_(producer)" title="Marco Polo (producer)">Marco Polo</a>, who had previously collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Jake_One" title="Jake One">Jake One</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ill_Bill" title="Ill Bill">Ill Bill</a>.  Appearances on the album included <a href="/wiki/Buckshot_(rapper)" title="Buckshot (rapper)">Buckshot</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sean_Price" title="Sean Price">Sean Price</a> of <a href="/wiki/Boot_Camp_Clik" title="Boot Camp Clik">Boot Camp Clik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ill_Bill" title="Ill Bill">Ill Bill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Akrobatik" title="Akrobatik">Akrobatik</a> and <a href="/wiki/Devin_the_Dude" title="Devin the Dude">Devin the Dude</a>.
</p><p>Edo G. proceeded to work on a new album with Masta Ace. The first single, titled "Little Young" was released via <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a>. The album was scheduled for release in October 2009, but the release was delayed by a cease-and-desist order from <a href="/wiki/A%26E_Television_Networks" class="mw-redirect" title="A&amp;E Television Networks">A&amp;E Television Networks</a>, which required a complete revision of the album cover artwork and design, due to copyright issues, as Edo and Masta Ace called their duo "A&amp;E".
</p><p>Edo G. is also a member of the Boston hip hop group 4Peace along with Twice Thou, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wyatt_Jackson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wyatt Jackson (page does not exist)">Wyatt Jackson</a>, and DQuest.
</p><p>In 2013, Edo G and OhNo collaborated on the song "Done Talking".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
