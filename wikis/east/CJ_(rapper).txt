<p><b>Christopher Daniel Soriano Jr.</b><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (born January 4, 1997<sup id="cite_ref-auto_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>), better known by his stage name <b>CJ</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Staten_Island" title="Staten Island">Staten Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He is best known for his 2020 breakout single "<a href="/wiki/Whoopty" title="Whoopty">Whoopty</a>", which peaked at number 3 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a><sup id="cite_ref-offcharts_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-offcharts-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and at number 10 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The song was later included on his debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a>, <i>Loyalty over Royalty</i>, released in February 2021.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Soriano began rapping as a hobby at the age of 9, and began recording and uploading songs to <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> and <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> at the age of 14.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2020, Soriano <a href="/wiki/Independent_music" title="Independent music">self-released</a> his first commercial breakthrough single, titled "<a href="/wiki/Whoopty" title="Whoopty">Whoopty</a>". The song contains a sample from <a href="/wiki/Arijit_Singh" title="Arijit Singh">Arijit Singh</a>'s "Sanam Re", with CJ rapping over the beat that he found on YouTube (later the rights were cleared). The song peaked at number 10 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> in February 2021, and the UK top three.<sup id="cite_ref-spotify_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spotify-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He also reached the top of the <i>Billboard</i> Emerging Artists chart.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He later signed a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Warner_Records" title="Warner Records">Warner Records</a> and his uncle, record executive, James Cruz, on his record label, called Cruz Control Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He stated that various labels reached out to him, however, Warner had the "best situation".<sup id="cite_ref-Bop_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bop-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On January 26, 2021, CJ released the single, titled "Bop", the follow-up to "Whoopty".<sup id="cite_ref-Bop_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bop-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> His debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a>, titled <i>Loyalty Over Royalty</i> was released on February 19, 2021. It was executive produced by <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-EP_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CJ_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His music influences include <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pop_Smoke" title="Pop Smoke">Pop Smoke</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-spotify_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spotify-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
