<p><b>Orlando Jahlil Tucker</b> (born February 8, 1988), better known by his stage name <b>Jahlil Beats</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> producer. He is currently signed to <a href="/wiki/Jay_Z" class="mw-redirect" title="Jay Z">Jay Z</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a>. He has produced for artists such as <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Puff Daddy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hood" title="Ace Hood">Ace Hood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Shmurda" title="Bobby Shmurda">Bobby Shmurda</a>, <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> among others.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated5_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is best known for producing Meek Mill's "<a href="/wiki/Ima_Boss" class="mw-redirect" title="Ima Boss">Ima Boss</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Burn_(Meek_Mill_song)" title="Burn (Meek Mill song)">Burn</a>", and <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>' "Jackpot", which was more famously used by <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Shmurda" title="Bobby Shmurda">Bobby Shmurda</a>'s  "<a href="/wiki/Hot_Nigga" title="Hot Nigga">Hot Nigga</a>".
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jahlil_Beats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jahlil Beats was born in <a href="/wiki/Chester,_Pennsylvania" title="Chester, Pennsylvania">Chester, Pennsylvania</a>. At the age of 12, he started learning audio engineering concepts, and building a passion for music under the guidance of his father (who was in a band) and cousin.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated5_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> By age fifteen he taught his brother, Anthony Tucker <a href="/wiki/The_Beat_Bully" title="The Beat Bully">The Beat Bully</a> how to produce music and they began making beats together. In high school they would give their beats out to friends to rap over.<sup id="cite_ref-Sharp2012_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sharp2012-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Jahlil would then go on to college before losing his financial aid and having to drop out.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jahlil_Beats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After dropping out of college in 2008, Jahlil Beats returned to his city where he would meet upcoming rapper <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>. Shortly after meeting, they collaborated with each other frequently. It is at that point, that Jahlil says was when he started taking music seriously.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He first became notable after producing several tracks with Haydock Beats for <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown_(American_singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chris Brown (American singer)">Chris Brown</a>'s collaboration mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Fan_of_a_Fan" title="Fan of a Fan">Fan of a Fan</a></i>, including "Holla at Me". He then would get what he considers his first big beat placement, which would be "Tonight" off <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>' EP <i><a href="/wiki/There_Is_No_Competition_2:_The_Grieving_Music_EP" title="There Is No Competition 2: The Grieving Music EP">There Is No Competition 2: The Grieving Music EP</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, Jahlil Beats produced Meek Mill's breakout hit "<a href="/wiki/Ima_Boss" class="mw-redirect" title="Ima Boss">Ima Boss</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His production for that song, would later be named the tenth-best instrumental between 2008 and 2013 by <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Later that year following a six-label bidding war, on October 16 he signed a publishing deal with Jay-Z's <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a>, who also manages Meek Mill.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In the following months he collaborated on songs artists such as <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>, <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, Meek Mill, <a href="/wiki/Nelly" title="Nelly">Nelly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>, <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bow_Wow_(rapper)" title="Bow Wow (rapper)">Bow Wow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Willow_Smith" title="Willow Smith">Willow Smith</a>, and <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> for their respective forthcoming albums.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated6_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated6-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He would also start to closely work under <a href="/wiki/No_ID" class="mw-redirect" title="No ID">No ID</a> after signing to Roc Nation. He was even one of the first people to confirm working on Jay-Z's <a href="/wiki/Magna_Carta..._Holy_Grail" class="mw-redirect" title="Magna Carta... Holy Grail">twelfth studio album</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On July 20, 2012, he released a six-track free EP titled <i>Legend Music</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 6, 2012, he released an instrumental mixtape titled <i>Legends Era</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On December 25, 2012, he released an instrumental mixtape titled <i>Crack Music 6</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He followed that with <i>Legends Era II</i> on April 16, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The second installment of <i>Legends Era</i> featured vocal <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearances" class="mw-redirect" title="Guest appearances">guest appearances</a> by <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, French Montana, <a href="/wiki/Young_Jeezy" class="mw-redirect" title="Young Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kid_Ink" title="Kid Ink">Kid Ink</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juelz_Santana" title="Juelz Santana">Juelz Santana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Game (rapper)">Game</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Problem_(rapper)" title="Problem (rapper)">Problem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vado_(rapper)" title="Vado (rapper)">Vado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hood" title="Ace Hood">Ace Hood</a>, and Meek Mill among others.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On August 1 he was scheduled to release his second instrumental mixtape of the year <i>Genius</i>, however its release would be pushed back until September 10, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> It was met with positive reviews by <a href="/wiki/Music_critic" class="mw-redirect" title="Music critic">music critics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On December 25, 2013, Jahlil Beats released the previously announced <i>Legends Era III</i>. The mixtape contained guest appearances by Lil Wayne, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>, Tyga, <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a>, The Game, French Montana, Kid Ink, <a href="/wiki/Kirko_Bangz" title="Kirko Bangz">Kirko Bangz</a>, Meek Mill, <a href="/wiki/The_LOX" class="mw-redirect" title="The LOX">The LOX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ab-Soul" title="Ab-Soul">Ab-Soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Badass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson" title="Action Bronson">Action Bronson</a>, Problem, <a href="/wiki/Logic_(rapper)" title="Logic (rapper)">Logic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gunplay_(rapper)" title="Gunplay (rapper)">Gunplay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dizzy_Wright" title="Dizzy Wright">Dizzy Wright</a>, Vado, Ace Hood, <a href="/wiki/Yo_Gotti" title="Yo Gotti">Yo Gotti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smoke_DZA" title="Smoke DZA">Smoke DZA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trae_tha_Truth" title="Trae tha Truth">Trae tha Truth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Project_Pat" title="Project Pat">Project Pat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trina" title="Trina">Trina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rockie_Fresh" title="Rockie Fresh">Rockie Fresh</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jae_Millz" title="Jae Millz">Jae Millz</a> among others.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On October 31, 2014, he released a mixtape titled <i>Crack Music 7</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_style">Production style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jahlil_Beats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although he used <a href="/wiki/Sample_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sample (music)">samples</a> early in his career, Beats has stated that he is moving away from using samples in his music: "I ain't trying to share my publishing. If you sample they might take 80%, leave you with 20. They getting paid off a record they did 20 years ago. So I like to do a lot of my stuff from scratch because I want all my publishing. I'm stingy with mine."<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In most of his songs, there can be siren heard throughout the beat. His songs also contain a production tag of his then three-year-old niece saying "Jahlil Beats Holla At Me".<sup id="cite_ref-Sharp2012_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sharp2012-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jahlil_Beats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His younger brother is record producer The Beat Bully, who produced the <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>, and <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Stay_Schemin%27" title="Stay Schemin&#39;">Stay Schemin'</a>"; Ross' "<a href="/wiki/So_Sophisticated" title="So Sophisticated">So Sophisticated</a>"; and Meek Mill's "House Party".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated6_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated6-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He has three children, Aaliyah, Amari &amp; Ava Tucker whom he shares with his wife Jacqueline Tucker.
</p>
