<p><b>William Michael Coleman</b> (born July 8, 1992), professionally known as <b>Mike Zombie</b>, is an American record producer and rapper from <a href="/wiki/Willingboro_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Willingboro Township, New Jersey">Willingboro Township, New Jersey</a>. Since 2012, Coleman has been an in-house producer for <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>'s label <a href="/wiki/OVO_Sound" title="OVO Sound">OVO Sound</a>. His biggest production for the label is "<a href="/wiki/Started_from_the_Bottom" title="Started from the Bottom">Started from the Bottom</a>" in 2013. His other major production credit is "<a href="/wiki/They_Don%27t_Love_You_No_More" title="They Don&#39;t Love You No More">They Don't Love You No More</a>" by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>, released a year later.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Zombie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is best known for the production of Drake's single "<a href="/wiki/Started_from_the_Bottom" title="Started from the Bottom">Started from the Bottom</a>". He sampled a track titled "Ambessence piano &amp; drones 1" by Bruno Sanfilippo &amp; Mathias Grassow for the song. "Started from the Bottom" was the winner of "Track of the Year" at the <a href="/wiki/2013_BET_Hip_Hop_Awards" title="2013 BET Hip Hop Awards">2013 BET Hip Hop Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The song was played during <a href="/wiki/NBA_All-Star_Weekend" class="mw-redirect" title="NBA All-Star Weekend">NBA All-Star Weekend</a>, <a href="/wiki/LeBron_James" title="LeBron James">LeBron James</a>' "LeBron Face" <a href="/wiki/Sprite_(soft_drink)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sprite (soft drink)">Sprite</a> commercial", video game <a href="/wiki/NBA_2K14" title="NBA 2K14">NBA 2K14</a>, and video game <a href="/wiki/Assassin%27s_Creed_4" class="mw-redirect" title="Assassin&#39;s Creed 4">Assassin's Creed 4</a>. The song was also nominated for "Best Rap Song" at <a href="/wiki/56th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="56th Annual Grammy Awards">The 56th Annual Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In July 2013, BET named him one of the "top 10 young producers to watch out for".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In October 2013, <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> named him one of the "25 new producers to watch for".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
He previously collaborated with battle rapper <a href="/wiki/Hollow_Da_Don" class="mw-redirect" title="Hollow Da Don">Hollow Da Don</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and Hollow played an important part putting Drake in contact with Mike Zombie.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Zombie released his first mixtape, <i>The End of the Beginning</i>, on December 22, 2013. Recently he produced <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/They_Don%27t_Love_You_No_More" title="They Don&#39;t Love You No More">They Don't Love You No More</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>. In the summer of 2014 he released his first music video from "The End of the Beginning" "616" on MTV Jams. On January 27, 2015 Zombie released a new project called "Rebel Without a Cause". In 2016, he dropped a few singles and two projects: "Humble Genius" (July 1, 2016) and a collaborate album with Benzi Ayo "Ea$tside Story" (October 1, 2016).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2019, Zombie produced a song for <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a> titled "A.I.
