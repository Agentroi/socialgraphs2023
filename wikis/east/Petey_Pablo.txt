<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Petey_Pablo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in Greenville, North Carolina, Barrett spent five years in prison for a 1993 armed robbery and upon his release moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, where he met <a href="/wiki/Black_Rob" title="Black Rob">Black Rob</a> and <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, and, according to rumor, was signed to <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> after the A&amp;R director overheard him rapping in a club bathroom.<sup id="cite_ref-NC_DOC_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NC_DOC-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Shapiro_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shapiro-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Petey_Pablo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span id="2001.E2.80.932002:_First_album"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2001–2002:_First_album">2001–2002: First album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Petey_Pablo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2001–2002: First album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After getting signed to Jive Records, Petey Pablo began working on his debut album. The first single "<a href="/wiki/Raise_Up" title="Raise Up">Raise Up</a>" was released in summer 2001, which was produced by <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a>. "Raise Up" was helped by rotation on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> and heavy airplay on urban radio, and reached number 25 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Pablo's first album <i><a href="/wiki/Diary_of_a_Sinner:_1st_Entry" title="Diary of a Sinner: 1st Entry">Diary of a Sinner: 1st Entry</a></i> peaked at number 13 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and was certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2003.E2.80.932006:_Second_album.2C_and_hiatus"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2003–2006:_Second_album,_and_hiatus">2003–2006: Second album, and hiatus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Petey_Pablo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2003–2006: Second album, and hiatus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In early 2003, <i><a href="/wiki/Diary_of_a_Sinner:_1st_Entry" title="Diary of a Sinner: 1st Entry">Diary of a Sinner: 1st Entry</a></i> received a <a href="/wiki/Grammy" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy">Grammy</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Best_Rap_Album" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Rap Album">Best Rap Album</a>. It lost the award to <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Eminem_Show" title="The Eminem Show">The Eminem Show</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Petey Pablo's success continued with the release of his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Still_Writing_in_My_Diary:_2nd_Entry" title="Still Writing in My Diary: 2nd Entry">Still Writing in My Diary: 2nd Entry</a></i> which he started working on in 2003. The album was a critical and commercial success, peaking at number 4 on the Billboard 200 and was certified Gold by the RIAA. The lead single off the album, "<a href="/wiki/Freek-a-Leek" title="Freek-a-Leek">Freek-a-Leek</a>", produced by <a href="/wiki/Lil_Jon" title="Lil Jon">Lil Jon</a> became Petey Pablo's biggest hit single of his career by reaching number 7 on the Billboard Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Also in 2004, Petey Pablo achieved more mainstream success for his feature on <a href="/wiki/Ciara" title="Ciara">Ciara</a>'s hit single "<a href="/wiki/Goodies_(song)" title="Goodies (song)">Goodies</a>" which topped the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a>. 
</p><p>After the release of his second studio album, Petey Pablo went on a hiatus from music.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2010.E2.80.93present:_Return_to_music_and_third_studio_album"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–present:_Return_to_music_and_third_studio_album">2010–present: Return to music and third studio album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Petey_Pablo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010–present: Return to music and third studio album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After his departure from <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>, Petey Pablo founded his own <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent label</a>, Carolina Music Group.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He released a track in July 2010 entitled "Go", which was produced by Timbaland.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In September 2011, Petey Pablo released a single called "Get Low" on iTunes.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arrest_and_incarceration">Arrest and incarceration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Petey_Pablo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Arrest and incarceration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 11, 2010, Barrett was arrested at <a href="/wiki/Raleigh-Durham_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Raleigh-Durham International Airport">Raleigh-Durham International Airport</a> after trying to carry a stolen 9mm semi-automatic pistol aboard a US Airways flight bound for Los Angeles.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On September 26, 2011, he was sentenced to 35 months in prison,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and entered incarceration at the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Correctional_Institution,_Beckley" title="Federal Correctional Institution, Beckley">Federal Correctional Institution (Beckley)</a> on January 4, 2012. He was held at the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Correctional_Institution,_Fort_Dix" title="Federal Correctional Institution, Fort Dix">Federal Correctional Institution (Fort Dix)</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-contactmusic.com_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contactmusic.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> with a release date of July 17, 2014,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> but he was released early on March 13, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 17, 2012, Petey Pablo released a mixtape called <i>Carolina #1</i> while in prison under the independent newly founded label "Carolina Music Group".<sup id="cite_ref-Welker_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welker-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Pablo had also confirmed working on his third studio album <i>A&amp;R: Anticipated Recordings</i>, which has yet to have a confirmed release date.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx.com_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prison_release_and_later_music">Prison release and later music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Petey_Pablo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Prison release and later music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late 2015 Pablo released a new single titled "Never Imagined".
</p><p>On Sunday, January 17, 2016, after the <a href="/wiki/Carolina_Panthers" title="Carolina Panthers">Carolina Panthers</a>' victory over the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Seahawks" title="Seattle Seahawks">Seattle Seahawks</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_NFL_playoffs" title="2016–17 NFL playoffs">2016 NFC Divisional playoffs</a>, Pablo released a new single via <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">Soundcloud</a> and <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> titled "Carolina Colors". The single was used as a hype song for the 2016 NFC Championship Game against the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Cardinals" title="Arizona Cardinals">Arizona Cardinals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Petey_Pablo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2002, Petey Pablo appeared as himself in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Drumline_(film)" title="Drumline (film)">Drumline</a></i> where he performs "Club Banger", "You Can Find Me", "Raise Up", and "I Told Y'All".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> 
Petey Pablo had a guest appearance in <a href="/wiki/A_Thousand_Deaths_(The_Shield)" class="mw-redirect" title="A Thousand Deaths (The Shield)">"A Thousand Deaths"</a>, a 2005 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Shield" title="The Shield">The Shield</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>  In 2009, Petey Pablo appeared in the film <i>Just Another Day</i> where he played B-Bone. In 2015, Petey Pablo played Clyde in the episode "Without a Country" on the show <i><a href="/wiki/Empire_(2015_TV_series)" title="Empire (2015 TV series)">Empire</a></i>, in which his character performed "Snitch Bitch" with Lucious Lyon (played by <a href="/wiki/Terrence_Howard" title="Terrence Howard">Terrence Howard</a>) in jail.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
