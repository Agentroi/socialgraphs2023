<p><b>Nicholas "Nick" Loftin</b>, also known as <b>Nick Fury</b>, is an American record producer musician. Loftin relocated to <a href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a> and founded New Jeru Entertainment in January 1996 to produce soul, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> music. He has produced for a variety of multi-platinum artists. He co-wrote, arranged and produced his first single "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Custom_Made_(Give_It_To_You)%27&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Custom Made (Give It To You)&#39; (page does not exist)">Custom Made (Give It To You)'</a>" in 2000 for rapper Lil' Kim and followed up co-writing, arranging and producing for the soundtrack to the movie <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Fast_%26_The_Furious&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="The Fast &amp; The Furious (page does not exist)">The Fast &amp; The Furious</a></i> where he collaborated with Murder, Inc. The same year he co-produced for another motion picture, <i><a href="/wiki/Osmosis_Jones" title="Osmosis Jones">Osmosis Jones</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Loftin has worked with Mary J. Blige, T.I., Anthony Hamilton, Nas, Lil' Flip, Wu-Tang Clan, Lil' Kim, Fat Joe, Trina and Nick Cannon, among others. In 2014, he produced the instrumentals to W3P: Willy Wise Workout: 3 Degrees of Power for former world champion boxer, Willy Wise.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
