Herring</b> (born April 13, 1984),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> also known as <b>Hemlock Ernst</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> is an American singer and rapper from <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>, best known for being the frontman of the <a href="/wiki/Synth-pop" title="Synth-pop">synth-pop</a> band <a href="/wiki/Future_Islands" title="Future Islands">Future Islands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-consequenceofsound2011_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-consequenceofsound2011-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was also a member of <a href="/wiki/Art_Lord_%26_the_Self-Portraits" title="Art Lord &amp; the Self-Portraits">Art Lord &amp; the Self-Portraits</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Snails" title="The Snails">The Snails</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and Trouble Knows Me.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_T._Herring&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Carteret_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Carteret County">Carteret County</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, Herring grew up in <a href="/wiki/Newport,_North_Carolina" title="Newport, North Carolina">Newport</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Morehead_City,_North_Carolina" title="Morehead City, North Carolina">Morehead City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-consequenceofsound2011_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-consequenceofsound2011-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He began <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapping</a> at age 14 at <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_battles" class="mw-redirect" title="Freestyle battles">freestyle battles</a> and cyphers.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2002, he enrolled at <a href="/wiki/East_Carolina_University" title="East Carolina University">East Carolina University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Herring is a keen <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">soccer</a> fan and an avid supporter of <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton F.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_T._Herring&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2003, while attending East Carolina University, Herring and other colleagues started the performance art band <a href="/wiki/Art_Lord_%26_the_Self-Portraits" title="Art Lord &amp; the Self-Portraits">Art Lord &amp; the Self-Portraits</a>, which lasted until 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-thedailybeast2014_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thedailybeast2014-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Some of its former members started <a href="/wiki/Future_Islands" title="Future Islands">Future Islands</a> in 2006, and the band relocated to <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a> in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-thedailybeast2014_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thedailybeast2014-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Herring took a break from the band's touring in order to spend more time on his solo rap project, writing more, and doing more shows.<sup id="cite_ref-thedailybeast2014_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thedailybeast2014-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Parallel to <a href="/wiki/Future_Islands" title="Future Islands">Future Islands</a>, Herring continued rapping either solo, under the moniker Hemlock Ernst, or with his brother as Flesh Epic.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Hemlock was his original writing name on an on-line music board when he was in the 9th grade, from a poem he wrote about <a href="/wiki/Socrates" title="Socrates">Socrates</a> taking the <a href="/wiki/Conium_maculatum" title="Conium maculatum">hemlock</a> poison.<sup id="cite_ref-aux2014_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aux2014-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Ernst comes from his character in Art Lord &amp; the Self-Portraits who was named Locke Ernst-Frost: a reference to <a href="/wiki/John_Locke_(poet)" title="John Locke (poet)">John Locke</a> the religious poet, <a href="/wiki/Max_Ernst" title="Max Ernst">Max Ernst</a>, the artist and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Frost" title="Robert Frost">Robert Frost</a>, the American poet.<sup id="cite_ref-aux2014_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aux2014-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, Hemlock Ernst teamed up with producer <a href="/wiki/Madlib" title="Madlib">Madlib</a> for a new rap project called Trouble Knows Me.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-madlib2_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-madlib2-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Entirely produced by Madlib, the self-titled EP was released via <a href="/wiki/Madlib_Invazion_(label)" class="mw-redirect" title="Madlib Invazion (label)">Madlib Invazion</a> on September 1, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> It was pre-sold at Madlib's show at The Mid in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a> on July 17, 2015, as well as Rappcats' website and a Rappcats popup shop.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On March 24, 2016, British musician <a href="/wiki/Four_Tet" title="Four Tet">Four Tet</a> sat in for <a href="/wiki/Benji_B" title="Benji B">Benji B</a> on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a> and played "Rings in the Coffee", an unreleased track by Trouble Knows Me.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 25, 2019, he released a rap album, <i>Back at the House</i>,  via Ruby Yacht under the moniker Hemlock Ernst.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> It was entirely produced by <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Segal" title="Kenny Segal">Kenny Segal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
