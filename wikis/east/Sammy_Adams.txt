<p><b>Samuel Adams Wisner</b> (born August 14, 1987) is an American rapper, singer and songwriter.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sammy_Adams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sammy Adams was born as Samuel Adams Wisner on August 14, 1987, in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>, to Kata Hull and Chuck Wisner. He and his family moved to <a href="/wiki/Wayland,_Massachusetts" title="Wayland, Massachusetts">Wayland, Massachusetts</a>, when he was in high school. Adams then attended <a href="/wiki/Beaver_Country_Day_School" title="Beaver Country Day School">Beaver Country Day School</a> before transferring to <a href="/wiki/Wayland_High_School" title="Wayland High School">Wayland High School</a> during his junior and senior years. Later, he went on to attend Trinity College, where he played soccer. Although he is referred to as a "Boston's Boy," he actually lived in upper-class suburbs for most of his life.
</p><p>Adams has been involved in music since his childhood; he played piano from the age of 7. At 11 years old, he began to improvise while playing on his piano, to create his own beats and melodies. Adams has stated in interviews that his musical influences have not only come from other rappers, but also from the classic rock, rock, classical music, and blues genres of music.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> While seeking to be eclectic, he also stays true to his musical roots so to create a "hip-pop" style in his music.
</p><p>Adams had aspired to be a producer before he had ever aimed at becoming a rapper. He began writing small raps throughout his time at middle and high school. Adams then started recording songs on his <a href="/wiki/MacBook" title="MacBook">MacBook</a> in 2008 in the dorms of his college.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sammy_Adams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2010:_Boston.27s_Boy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010:_Boston's_Boy">2010: <i>Boston's Boy</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sammy_Adams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010: Boston&#039;s Boy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Over the course of the next two years, he recorded a large amount of material. In September 2009, he released "I Hate College (Remix)," a remix of rapper <a href="/wiki/Asher_Roth" title="Asher Roth">Asher Roth</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/I_Love_College" title="I Love College">I Love College</a>," on YouTube. The video has over 10&#160;million views as of September 2015. He also released "Kimber," "Poker Face Remix," "Hard s***," "Opening Day," and "Rollin," which have combined for over 2&#160;million views as of September 2012. Opening day, plus his aboveground debut the Boston's Boy EP. The EP topped iTunes' hip-hop charts, driven by the success of "<a href="/wiki/Driving_Me_Crazy" title="Driving Me Crazy">Driving Me Crazy</a>"<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Adams first EP, <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boston%27s_Boy,&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Boston&#39;s Boy, (page does not exist)">Boston's Boy,</a></i> was released on March 4, 2010, by 1st Round Records and reached #73 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and reached #1 on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>.  The album was received relatively well critically. The album was praised for its dance beats, catchy lyrics, and overall fun feel. Adams credited his loyal college fan base for the success of Boston's Boy. On July 13, 2010, four extra tracks were released by Adams to create a "deluxe" version of Boston's Boy. They were "Still I Rise," "See Me Now," "Fly Jets Over Boston," and "Just Sayin.'" Two of the songs, "Still I Rise" and "Fly Jets Over Boston," featured special guests G. Curtis and <a href="/wiki/Curren$y" class="mw-redirect" title="Curren$y">Curren$y</a>, respectively. G. Curtis was a recent signing to the record label and Curren$y had been working with Adams for a while but had yet to officially collaborate with him on a track.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Adams finished his second mixtape, <i>Party Records Mixtape,</i> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and released it in September 2010. In the same month, Adams was arrested during a show at <a href="/wiki/Kansas_State_University" title="Kansas State University">Kansas State University</a>. After having his noise permit revoked, Adams yelled "Fuck the Police!" and was subsequently handcuffed onstage as he tried to perform his hit "Driving Me Crazy".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2011.E2.80.932014:_Signing_with_RCA_and_debut_EP"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–2014:_Signing_with_RCA_and_debut_EP">2011–2014: Signing with RCA and debut EP</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sammy_Adams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2011–2014: Signing with RCA and debut EP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He performed at the music festival <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> in August 2011. In late 2011, Adams signed with <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a>. His next single, "Blow Up", tells about how he is back on the rise after the signing, following a stagnant period after his explosion onto the scene with Boston's Boy.
</p><p>Adams released the singles "Blow Up"<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and "Summertime", as promotional singles in November 2011 and March 2012, respectively. He made his television debut on the late-night talk show <i>Conan</i> with <a href="/wiki/Conan_O%27Brien" title="Conan O&#39;Brien">Conan O'Brien</a> in January 2012 and performed his single "Blow Up". A third single, "Only One" was released in April 2012. "Only One" was Adams' first track to receive mainstream attention on the radio. Before then, he had been relegated to the college and online scene with few radio plays to speak of. <a href="/wiki/Teen_Vogue" title="Teen Vogue">Teen Vogue</a> quickly named Adams one of five new "Artists To Watch"<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> in 2012 with <a href="/wiki/Artist_Direct" class="mw-redirect" title="Artist Direct">Artist Direct</a> giving the single 4 of 5 stars.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> "Only One" was later included in <a href="/wiki/AT%26T" title="AT&amp;T">AT&amp;T</a>'s <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> commercial featuring footballer <a href="/wiki/Alex_Morgan" title="Alex Morgan">Alex Morgan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 1, 2012, <i>EW</i> reported that Adams was set to appear as himself on <a href="/wiki/The_CW_Television_Network" class="mw-redirect" title="The CW Television Network">CW</a>'s hit teen drama <i><a href="/wiki/90210_(TV_series)" title="90210 (TV series)">90210</a></i>. Adams released a new single titled, "All Night Longer" for digital download on August 21, 2012. In October 2012, he played himself in a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> reality show called Berlin Day'n'Night (Berlin Tag und Nacht). In Fall 2012, Adams went on his "All Night Longer" tour.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In November 2012, he joined the remix version of American singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a>’s song “<a href="/wiki/I_Knew_You_Were_Trouble" title="I Knew You Were Trouble">I Knew You Were Trouble</a>”.
</p><p>During a charity concert in <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> on March 23, 2013, Adams fell sick during his performance and was taken to a nearby ambulance for treatment by <a href="/wiki/Paramedics" class="mw-redirect" title="Paramedics">paramedics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> During April 2013 he went on tour with California rapper <a href="/wiki/T._Mills" class="mw-redirect" title="T. Mills">T. Mills</a>. On May 10, 2013, Adams released a standalone single and its music video. The song is titled "<a href="/wiki/L.A._Story_(song)" title="L.A. Story (song)">L.A. Story</a>" and features American singer <a href="/wiki/Mike_Posner" title="Mike Posner">Mike Posner</a>, and is produced by <a href="/wiki/OneRepublic" title="OneRepublic">OneRepublic</a> frontman <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Tedder" title="Ryan Tedder">Ryan Tedder</a>. The song was released to iTunes on May 28, and to <a href="/wiki/Mainstream_Top_40" class="mw-redirect" title="Mainstream Top 40">Mainstream Top 40</a> radio on June 4, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On September 18, 2013, Sammy took to his personal Twitter page to announce that his debut album, <i>Homecoming</i>, was to be released in October. He later on announced that <i>Homecoming</i> would be released on November 19, 2013, and it was changed to a digital-only EP.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.932016:_1st_Round_and_debut_LP"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–2016:_1st_Round_and_debut_LP">2015–2016: 1st Round and debut LP</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sammy_Adams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015–2016: 1st Round and debut LP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2015, Adams parted with RCA records after 3 years on the label.  He soon announced via Twitter that he would be releasing an album in October 2015 which later postponed to December, as an independent artist.  Soon after, it was announced in an interview that he had rejoined his former independent label, 1st Round Records.
</p><p>On December 3, 2015, Adams released the lead single "Remember" off his debut album called <i>The Long Way</i>, which would be released through 1st Round Records on March 24, 2016.
</p>
<h3><span id="2017.E2.80.93present:_Independent"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–present:_Independent">2017–present: Independent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sammy_Adams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2017–present: Independent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the promotional cycle for <i>The Long Way</i> ended, Adams left 1st Round Records. In August 2017, Adams released his new tropical house single "Shining".
</p><p>On July 29, 2017, Adams headlined the inaugural Briggs Fest hosted in Boston.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 10, 2017, he released new single "Driving" with Telykast and Basko.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sammy_Adams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wisner played varsity soccer during the years that he attended Hobart College. He scored 6 goals and had 4 assists with the Statesmen.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He then transferred to <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Hartford" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity College, Hartford">Trinity College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hartford,_Connecticut" title="Hartford, Connecticut">Hartford, Connecticut</a> where he majored in political science.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Wisner played soccer for Trinity College and was named captain during his senior year.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He featured as number seventeen on the Varsity soccer team.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> While playing for Trinity, Wisner tallied 11 goals and 11 assists. He led the team in points in 2008, starting all 16&#160;games for the Bantams.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>  Wisner was also selected to the All-NESCAC team in 2008, earning First team honors at forward. He is also a fan of English club <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_FC" class="mw-redirect" title="Arsenal FC">Arsenal FC</a>. Wisner married Andie Arthur in 2022. <sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
