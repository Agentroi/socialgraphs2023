</p><p>She started rapping in high school with a group of friends called Primp,<sup id="cite_ref-noisey_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-noisey-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> until graduation at 16.<sup id="cite_ref-villagevoice_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villagevoice-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> After graduating she attended the <a href="/wiki/Fashion_Institute_of_Technology" title="Fashion Institute of Technology">Fashion Institute of Technology</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-villagevoice_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villagevoice-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> until getting a job in retail and starting a <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel as a pastime.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Junglepussy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012, she released her first track as Junglepussy, "Cream Team" on YouTube, followed by "Stitches".<sup id="cite_ref-youtube_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, she performed alongside rapper <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a> at Westway.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2014, <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a> posted "Cream Team" on her <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> and <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-papermag_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-papermag-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-interviewmagazine_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-interviewmagazine-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On June 10, 2014, Junglepussy released her debut mixtape <i>Satisfaction Guaranteed</i>. The self-released 11 track compilation debuted on the <i><a href="/wiki/Vice_(magazine)" title="Vice (magazine)">Vice</a></i> website.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Junglepussy later released videos for the songs "Nah" and "Me" on her official YouTube channel.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 20, 2015, she announced her next album would be titled <i><a href="/wiki/Pregnant_with_Success" title="Pregnant with Success">Pregnant with Success</a></i>, based on a lyric from "You Don't Know".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On the same day, Junglepussy released a video for the assumed first single from the album, "Now or Later".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The album was released on November 17, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It was included on year-end lists by publications such as <i><a href="/wiki/Fact_(UK_magazine)" title="Fact (UK magazine)">Fact</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 11, 2018, she released an album, <i>JP3</i>, which features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Rico_Love" title="Rico Love">Rico Love</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_Boo" title="Gangsta Boo">Gangsta Boo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiki_(rapper)" title="Wiki (rapper)">Wiki</a>, and Quin.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The album was leaked a day before its release.<sup id="cite_ref-thefader_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a weighted average score out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, <i>JP3</i> received an average score of 83, based on 6 reviews, indicating "universal acclaim".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Vibe_(magazine)" title="Vibe (magazine)">Vibe</a></i> included it on the "25 Hip-Hop Albums by Bomb Womxn of 2018" list.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> It was nominated by the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_of_Independent_Music" title="American Association of Independent Music">American Association of Independent Music</a> for its 2019 indie music-celebrating Libera Awards in the category of Best Hip-Hop/Rap Album.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She made her acting debut in the comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Support_the_Girls" title="Support the Girls">Support the Girls</a></i> (2018).<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, Junglepussy released <i>JP4</i>, her fourth studio album, which Guardian described as "a moody blend of <a href="/wiki/Nu-metal" class="mw-redirect" title="Nu-metal">nu-metal</a>, alt-rock and funk".<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a></i> described the record as "stunning", praising McHayle for uniting "a multitude of sounds" and "scoping multiple genres" while still producing a cohesive and enduring record".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The album features appearances from <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_Boo" title="Gangsta Boo">Gangsta Boo</a> and Ian Isiah.
</p><p>In 2022, Junglepussy released the 5 track “JP5000” project to high acclaim from publications such as Pitchfork, Metacritic, and more.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Junglepussy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_influences">Musical style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Junglepussy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Musical style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In a 2014 interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Interview_(magazine)" title="Interview (magazine)">Interview</a></i>, Junglepussy cited she has taken inspiration from musicians such as <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lady_Saw" title="Lady Saw">Lady Saw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kelis" title="Kelis">Kelis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vybz_Kartel" title="Vybz Kartel">Vybz Kartel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mavado_(singer)" title="Mavado (singer)">Mavado</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Veronicas" title="The Veronicas">The Veronicas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metric_(band)" title="Metric (band)">Metric</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gossip_(band)" title="Gossip (band)">Gossip</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Boy" title="Soulja Boy">Soulja Boy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-interviewmagazine_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-interviewmagazine-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_image">Public image</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Junglepussy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Public image">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Junglepussy explained that as a young woman she was mostly influenced by the television show <i><a href="/wiki/Moesha" title="Moesha">Moesha</a></i>, starring singer/actress <a href="/wiki/Brandy_Norwood" title="Brandy Norwood">Brandy</a>. She said in <i><a href="/wiki/Paper_(magazine)" title="Paper (magazine)">Paper</a></i>: "Growing up, I would see Brandy on <i>Moesha</i> and see her keeping in her cornrows and her braids, but still flourish in her art and music, looking fly. I loved <i>Moesha</i> as a child, but now I take away something more special from it. Just because you're a black girl, it doesn't mean you need to only care about hair and makeup. Brandy cared about books, culture and where she was going — you can do both."<sup id="cite_ref-papermag_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-papermag-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
