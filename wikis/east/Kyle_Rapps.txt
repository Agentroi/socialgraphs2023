<p><b>Kyle Sutton</b> (born 1980),<sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphop_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphop-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Kyle Rapps</b>, is an American hip hop artist from <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyle_Rapps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sutton was born in <a href="/wiki/Grand_Rapids,_Michigan" title="Grand Rapids, Michigan">Grand Rapids, Michigan</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphop_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphop-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and raised in <a href="/wiki/Princeton,_New_Jersey" title="Princeton, New Jersey">Princeton, New Jersey</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> primarily by his mother. His father was a minister.<sup id="cite_ref-mhidalgo_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mhidalgo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Sutton attended <a href="/wiki/Rutgers_University" title="Rutgers University">Rutgers University</a>, earning a BA in Spanish.<sup id="cite_ref-cstetler_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cstetler-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, he relocated from <a href="/wiki/Harlem,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Harlem, New York">Harlem, New York</a> to <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City,_Mexico" class="mw-redirect" title="Mexico City, Mexico">Mexico City, Mexico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nvukorepa_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nvukorepa-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyle_Rapps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_Skeptik">As Skeptik</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyle_Rapps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: As Skeptik">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While at Rutgers, under the pseudonym Skeptik, he formed the underground hip hop group Thought Breakers. Their debut EP, <i>Episode 1</i>, was released in 2004 and independently sold over 10,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The group opened for <a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dead_Prez" title="Dead Prez">Dead Prez</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphop_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphop-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mhidalgo_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mhidalgo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-respect072111_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-respect072111-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Around that time, he formed a spoken-word poetry collective, Mayhem Poets.<sup id="cite_ref-mhidalgo_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mhidalgo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cstetler_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cstetler-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, the collective won a <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> small business competition, enabling them to establish the open mic venue Slam Chops in Manhattan, which stayed open for two years.<sup id="cite_ref-cstetler_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cstetler-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Also in 2007, the collective had a two-month <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">off-Broadway</a> run at the <a href="/wiki/New_Victory_Theater" title="New Victory Theater">New Victory Theater</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> After a 2009 trip to <a href="/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberia</a> with Mayhem Poets, Sutton began to go by the name Black Skeptik, and released titles "Rent" and "Frankenstein Saves Hip Hop".<sup id="cite_ref-mhidalgo_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mhidalgo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_Kyle_Rapps">As Kyle Rapps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyle_Rapps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: As Kyle Rapps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, now going by Kyle Rapps, he released the single "Love, Love," featuring <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a> and <a href="/wiki/Homeboy_Sandman" title="Homeboy Sandman">Homeboy Sandman</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-respect072111_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-respect072111-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, his <i>RE-Edutainment</i> mixtape came out, with the title in homage to the 1990 <a href="/wiki/Boogie_Down_Productions" title="Boogie Down Productions">Boogie Down Productions</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/Edutainment_(album)" title="Edutainment (album)">Edutainment</a></i>. KRS-One, <a href="/wiki/Joell_Ortiz" title="Joell Ortiz">Joell Ortiz</a> and <a href="/wiki/U-N-I" title="U-N-I">U-N-I</a> appear on the mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Later in 2011, Rapps released <i>On Air</i>, featuring appearances from Talib Kweli, <a href="/wiki/C-Rayz_Walz" title="C-Rayz Walz">C-Rayz Walz</a>, KRS-One and Homeboy Sandman.<sup id="cite_ref-respect072111_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-respect072111-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The eight-track mixtape is built around samples from French electronic music duo <a href="/wiki/Air_(French_band)" title="Air (French band)">Air</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2013, Mishka NYC Records released <i>SUB</i>, Rapps' first full-length album, produced by Belief and featuring collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson" title="Action Bronson">Action Bronson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mr._Muthafuckin%27_eXquire" title="Mr. Muthafuckin&#39; eXquire">Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Cohen_(rapper)" title="Aaron Cohen (rapper)">Aaron Cohen</a> and Spaceman.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Earmilk" title="Earmilk">Earmilk</a></i> called it "a mesmerizing selection of songs."<sup id="cite_ref-nvukorepa_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nvukorepa-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Rapps put out the single "The Sky's on Fire", produced by <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Shatkin" title="Jesse Shatkin">Belief</a> and featuring vocals by Adrienne Mack-Davis.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Rapps released "Latrell", its title and sampled intro referencing <a href="/wiki/Latrell_Sprewell" title="Latrell Sprewell">Latrell Sprewell</a>, a former professional basketball player who was suspended for attacking his Golden State Warriors coach P.J. Carlesimo in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, Rapps performed virtually over Zoom at Soul Vey, a Black and Jewish community event led by Adam Swig and <a href="/wiki/Kosha_Dillz" title="Kosha Dillz">Kosha Dillz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rapps has collaborated with a large variety of other music artists, including <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a>, <a href="/wiki/Homeboy_Sandman" title="Homeboy Sandman">Homeboy Sandman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diwon" class="mw-redirect" title="Diwon">Diwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson" title="Action Bronson">Action Bronson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mr._Muthafuckin%27_eXquire" title="Mr. Muthafuckin&#39; eXquire">Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a>, Adrienne Mack-Davis, <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Shatkin" title="Jesse Shatkin">Belief</a>, Hefna Gwap, <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chuuwee" title="Chuuwee">Chuuwee</a>, Faruz Feet, <a href="/wiki/Dirt_E._Dutch" title="Dirt E. Dutch">Dirt E. Dutch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joell_Ortiz" title="Joell Ortiz">Joell Ortiz</a>, Sly5thAve, Aaron Cohen, Brody, <a href="/wiki/Y-Love" title="Y-Love">Y-Love</a>, Nathan Sela, Vulkan the Krusader, Sam Siegel, S'natra, DJ JS-1, Breez Brewin, Dru the Monster, Nelson, Little Freckles, Ali Salah Rasé, and Ray Contour.
</p>
