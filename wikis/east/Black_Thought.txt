<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1066479718"></td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Tarik Luqmaan Trotter</b> (born October 3, 1973),<sup id="cite_ref-igpage_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-igpage-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> better known as <b>Black Thought</b>, is an American rapper, singer, actor and the lead <a href="/wiki/Emcee_(hip_hop)" class="mw-redirect" title="Emcee (hip hop)">MC</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">the Roots</a>, which he co-founded with drummer <a href="/wiki/Questlove" title="Questlove">Questlove</a> (Ahmir Thompson). Regarded as "one of the most skilled, incisive, and prolific rappers of his time",<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CWT_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CWT-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> he is widely lauded for his live performance skills, continuous <a href="/wiki/Multisyllabic_rhymes" title="Multisyllabic rhymes">multisyllabic</a> rhyme schemes, complex lyricism, double entendres, and politically aware lyrics. With the Roots, he is a singer and rapper on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Jimmy_Fallon" title="The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a>,</i> frequently playing games with Fallon and his guests.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Thought&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Black Thought was born Tarik Luqmaan Trotter, to Thomas and Cassandra Trotter, both members of the <a href="/wiki/Nation_of_Islam" title="Nation of Islam">Nation of Islam</a>. His father was murdered when Trotter was one year old, and his mother was murdered when he was in high school.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He spent time <a href="/wiki/Tagging_(graffiti)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tagging (graffiti)">tagging</a> "DT" or "Double T" with graffiti around Philadelphia. He sold <a href="/wiki/Crack_cocaine" title="Crack cocaine">crack cocaine</a> briefly, and was sent to live with family in <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a> for a few months in high school.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Trotter attended the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_High_School_for_Creative_and_Performing_Arts" class="mw-redirect" title="Philadelphia High School for Creative and Performing Arts">Philadelphia High School for Creative and Performing Arts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Millersville_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Millersville University">Millersville University</a>, studying <a href="/wiki/Journalism" title="Journalism">journalism</a>. In 1987, he became friends with drummer <a href="/wiki/Questlove" title="Questlove">Ahmir "Questlove" Thompson</a> and formed a drummer/MC duo, performing on the streets of Philadelphia and at talent shows.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_roots_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic_roots-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Trotter subsequently spent some time as one of two MCs in the group the Square Roots; the other was <a href="/wiki/Malik_B." title="Malik B.">Malik B.</a>, whom Tariq met in college.<sup id="cite_ref-Gibson_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gibson-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In high school, Black Thought became interested in the lessons of the <a href="/wiki/Five-Percent_Nation" title="Five-Percent Nation">Nation of Gods and Earths</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (August 2014)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Roots">The Roots</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Thought&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Roots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Square Roots renamed themselves the Roots and released their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Organix_(album)" title="Organix (album)">Organix</a></i> in 1993. The Roots signed to DGC and followed up with <i><a href="/wiki/Do_You_Want_More%3F!!!%3F%3F!" title="Do You Want More?!!!??!">Do You Want More?!!!??!</a></i> in 1995. Recorded without any sampling, the album was more popular among alternative music fans than those of hip hop. Around the release of the album, the Roots performed at the <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a> alternative music festival and <a href="/wiki/Montreux_Jazz_Festival" title="Montreux Jazz Festival">Montreux Jazz Festival</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Illadelph_Halflife" title="Illadelph Halflife">Illadelph Halflife</a></i>, the band's 1996 album, became its first to chart within the top 40 spots on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> because of the successful single "What They Do".<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_roots_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic_roots-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Things_Fall_Apart_(album)" title="Things Fall Apart (album)">Things Fall Apart</a></i> followed in 1999, the year the band played at the <a href="/wiki/Woodstock_99" class="mw-redirect" title="Woodstock 99">Woodstock 99</a> concert.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2000, the Roots won the Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group for "You Got Me", with guest performances by <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eve_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Eve (entertainer)">Eve</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <i>Things Fall Apart</i> was nominated for Best Rap Album.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> For <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>'s acoustic concert for the television program <i><a href="/wiki/MTV_Unplugged" title="MTV Unplugged">MTV Unplugged</a></i>, the Roots provided instrumentals.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Succeeding albums were <i><a href="/wiki/Phrenology_(album)" title="Phrenology (album)">Phrenology</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tipping_Point_(The_Roots_album)" title="The Tipping Point (The Roots album)">The Tipping Point</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Theory_(album)" title="Game Theory (album)">Game Theory</a></i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Rising_Down" title="Rising Down">Rising Down</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/How_I_Got_Over_(album)" title="How I Got Over (album)">How I Got Over</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Undun" title="Undun">Undun</a></i> (2011), and <i><a href="/wiki/%E2%80%A6And_Then_You_Shoot_Your_Cousin" class="mw-redirect" title="…And Then You Shoot Your Cousin">…And Then You Shoot Your Cousin</a></i> (2014).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_music_career">Solo music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Thought&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Black Thought recorded a solo album, titled <i>Masterpiece Theatre</i> and intended for a 2001 release,<sup id="cite_ref-Demby_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Demby-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> but the project was scrapped after he learned that the album would not count toward the Roots' contractual commitments. Most of the songs from the project ultimately appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Phrenology_(album)" title="Phrenology (album)">Phrenology</a></i>. In a June 2008 interview with Brian Kayser of the website HipHopGame, Black Thought spoke of another solo project that was scheduled for release on the Razor and Tie music corporation. He said that Questlove might work on production.<sup id="cite_ref-HHG_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHG-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> However, by September 2014, Black Thought and the solo album were not referenced on the Razor &amp; Tie website.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Black Thought's first release outside of The Roots was the 2011 mixtape <i>The Prestige</i>, which he released alongside 10.Deep and the Money Making Jam Collective, the latter of which included longtime Roots collaborators such as <a href="/wiki/Dice_Raw" title="Dice Raw">Dice Raw</a>, S.T.S., P.O.R.N., and Truck North.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, he stated his intention to follow the mixtape with an album titled <i>Talented Mr. Trotter</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> However, Black Thought's next release would instead be the EP <i><a href="/wiki/Streams_of_Thought,_Vol._1" title="Streams of Thought, Vol. 1">Streams of Thought, Vol. 1</a></i>, a June 2018 collaboration with producer <a href="/wiki/9th_Wonder" title="9th Wonder">9th Wonder</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> That November, Black Thought and <a href="/wiki/Salaam_Remi" title="Salaam Remi">Salaam Remi</a> released a follow-up EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Streams_of_Thought,_Vol._2" title="Streams of Thought, Vol. 2">Streams of Thought, Vol. 2</a></i>. The third installment in the "Streams of Thought" series, <i><a href="/wiki/Streams_of_Thought,_Vol._3:_Cane_%26_Able" title="Streams of Thought, Vol. 3: Cane &amp; Able">Streams of Thought, Vol. 3: Cane &amp; Able</a></i>, was a full studio album released in 2020. The album was preceded by the single "Good Morning", which featured <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Killer_Mike" title="Killer Mike">Killer Mike</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beats" class="mw-redirect" title="Swizz Beats">Swizz Beats</a>; other featured artists on the album include <a href="/wiki/Portugal._The_Man" title="Portugal. The Man">Portugal. The Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy&#160;Q</a> and&#160;<a href="/wiki/The_Last_Artful,_Dodgr" title="The Last Artful, Dodgr">the Last Artful, Dodgr</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Black Thought and <a href="/wiki/Danger_Mouse_(musician)" title="Danger Mouse (musician)">Danger Mouse</a> had begun work on a collaborative album, originally titled <i>Dangerous Thoughts</i>, in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> There was no further news about this album for years,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> until the artists announced in May 2022 that they would be releasing their collaboration that August.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The album, retitled <i><a href="/wiki/Cheat_Codes_(album)" title="Cheat Codes (album)">Cheat Codes</a></i>, was released on August 10, 2022. <i>Cheat Codes</i> included a posthumous appearance from <a href="/wiki/MF_Doom" title="MF Doom">MF Doom</a>; other featured artists included <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kid_Sister" title="Kid Sister">Kid Sister</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Badass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russ_(rapper)" title="Russ (rapper)">Russ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dylan_Cartlidge" title="Dylan Cartlidge">Dylan Cartlidge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Kiwanuka" title="Michael Kiwanuka">Michael Kiwanuka</a>, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">ASAP Rocky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Run_the_Jewels" title="Run the Jewels">Run the Jewels</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Conway_the_Machine" title="Conway the Machine">Conway the Machine</a>.
</p><p>In February 2016, Black Thought joined forces with <a href="/wiki/Fashawn" title="Fashawn">Fashawn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Del_the_Funky_Homosapien" title="Del the Funky Homosapien">Del the Funky Homosapien</a> to record a new track called "Rise Up" for the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Fighter_V" title="Street Fighter V">Street Fighter V</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Capcom" title="Capcom">Capcom</a> released a music video for the song that included appearances by Black Thought and his fellow collaborators.<sup id="cite_ref-early_release_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-early_release-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2017, Black Thought appeared on <a href="/wiki/HOT_97" class="mw-redirect" title="HOT 97">HOT 97</a> with <a href="/wiki/Funkmaster_Flex" title="Funkmaster Flex">Funkmaster Flex</a> and performed a 10-minute freestyle over "The Learning (Burn)" instrumental by <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>. This freestyle went widely viral, trending on Twitter for the next days and hitting millions of views on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Thought&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Black Thought starred in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Bamboozled" title="Bamboozled">Bamboozled</a></i> (2000), and the 2001 films <i><a href="/wiki/Perfume_(2001_film)" title="Perfume (2001 film)">Perfume</a></i>, <i>Love Rome</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Babylon" title="Brooklyn Babylon">Brooklyn Babylon</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Demby_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Demby-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He made his stage debut in January 2022, playing Dr. Junius Crookman in the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Black_No_More" title="Black No More">Black No More</a></i>; he also wrote the lyrics and co-wrote the music for the production.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Black Thought began work on a memoir with journalist and music critic <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Chang_(journalist)" title="Jeff Chang (journalist)">Jeff Chang</a> and filmmaker <a href="/w/index.php?title=Maori_Karmael_Holmes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Maori Karmael Holmes (page does not exist)">Maori Karmael Holmes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> An autobiography entitled <i>The Upcycled Mind</i> is scheduled to be released on November 14, 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Black Thought also executive produced a short film <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.osfashland.org/en/productions/2020-digital/ash-land.aspx">, <i>Ash Land</i></a>  which is featured in the 2023 <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Shakespeare_Festival" title="Oregon Shakespeare Festival">Oregon Shakespeare Festival’s</a> Cyberland Short Films collection, about a woman who loses, then finds herself again while living in the mountains of Ashland, a small town in Oregon during the COVID pandemic, where the festival takes place. Ash Land was an official selection in 2021 for the Academy Award–qualifying Pan African Film Festival, the Brooklyn Film Festival, Bronzelens Film Festival, and Martha Vineyard’s African American Film Festival. The film is directed by South African native <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.osfashland.org/en/artist-biographies/guest-artists/shariffa-ali.aspx">Shariffa Ali</a> and written by <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.osfashland.org/en/artist-biographies/guest-artists/Banna-Desta.aspx">Banna Desta</a> The film ends with a closing fade to black and voice over of Black Thought addressing the “Black Experience” in Oregon with a powerful and final affirmation: “We Black. We in Oregon. Look at us.”
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Thought&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Black Thought is "widely recognized as one of the most skilled, incisive, and prolific rappers of his time", according to <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> critic Andy Kellman.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Henry Adaso of <a href="/wiki/Dotdash" class="mw-redirect" title="Dotdash">About.com</a> praises him as "a surgeon of <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">emceeing</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> and he is highly regarded for his <a href="/wiki/Multisyllabic_rhymes" title="Multisyllabic rhymes">multisyllabic rhymes</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Internal_rhyme" title="Internal rhyme">internal rhymes</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rapping#Flow" title="Rapping">flow</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> breath control,<sup id="cite_ref-howtorap_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-howtorap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> socially conscious lyrics,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> and live performances.<sup id="cite_ref-howtorap_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-howtorap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Questlove stated that Black Thought's clarity and logic also distinguishes him from other emcees,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> and Stephen Kearse of <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork</a></i> notes that his "ability to use words as textures as much as tools has always been a hallmark of his style".<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> In the book <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Rap" title="How to Rap">How to Rap</a></i>, emcee <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a> described seeing the Roots perform his song "Men at Work" at a show; "I never really liked to perform it that much—because your breath control gotta be crazy. But somebody sent me a clip of the Roots ... and [Black Thought] did all three fucking verses and I couldn't believe it—he killed that shit".<sup id="cite_ref-howtorap_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-howtorap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Black Thought has influenced several hip hop artists, including <a href="/wiki/Logic_(rapper)" title="Logic (rapper)">Logic</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Badass</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rapsody" title="Rapsody">Rapsody</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/K-os" title="K-os">k-os</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Shad_(rapper)" title="Shad (rapper)">Shad</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
