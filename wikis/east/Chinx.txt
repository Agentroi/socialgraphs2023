<p><b>Lionel Du Fon Pickens</b> (December 4, 1983 – May 17, 2015),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Chinx</b> (formerly <b>Chinx Drugz</b>), was an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>. He was a member of The Rockaway Riot Squad alongside fellow slain rapper Stack Bundles. Chinx later joined <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Coke_Boys_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Coke Boys Records">Coke Boys Records</a>, gaining recognition for his appearances on the <i>Coke Boys</i> mixtapes and the <i>Cocaine Riot</i> mixtape series. He was killed in a <a href="/wiki/Drive-by_shooting" title="Drive-by shooting">drive-by shooting</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica,_Queens" title="Jamaica, Queens">Jamaica, Queens</a> on May 17, 2015. Two men have since been arrested in the case.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1983.E2.80.931999:_Beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1983–1999:_Beginnings">1983–1999: Beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1983–1999: Beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Chinx was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Far_Rockaway,_Queens" title="Far Rockaway, Queens">Far Rockaway, Queens</a> on December 4, 1983, living in both Redfern Houses and Edgemere Houses, now known as Ocean Bay Apartments. He spent time in <a href="/wiki/North_Amityville" class="mw-redirect" title="North Amityville">North Amityville</a> in his teenage years where he briefly attended high school.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> When speaking of how he started rapping, Chinx said, "I started rapping at the table in junior high school for fun with the homies, and then by 9th grade is when I started taking music seriously. At that point is when I started investing my street money into my studio time and trying to find my sound."<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He said he got the name "Chinx Drugz" since he "used to smoke a lot of <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">weed</a> back in the day, and this older chick used to call me <a href="/wiki/Chink" title="Chink">chinky</a> and it stuck; the Drugz, well, that’s self explanatory — I been slangin’ on these corners."<sup id="cite_ref-urbfeed2268_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urbfeed2268-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2000.E2.80.932010:_Riot_Squad_and_Hurry_Up_.26_Die_series"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000–2010:_Riot_Squad_and_Hurry_Up_&amp;_Die_series">2000–2010: Riot Squad and <i>Hurry Up &amp; Die</i> series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000–2010: Riot Squad and Hurry Up &amp; Die series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Chinx first started rapping with his close friend Stack Bundles while he was in high school, whom he would later form the group Riot Squad with, along with local rappers Bynoe and Cau2Gs. The group started to gain a buzz throughout New York but that ended abruptly in early 2005 when Chinx was sentenced to nearly 5 years in prison. He served his time at <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Kill_Correctional_Facility" title="Arthur Kill Correctional Facility">Arthur Kill Correctional Facility</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag4_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
During this time, Bundles formed a friendship with rapper <a href="/wiki/Max_B" title="Max B">Max B</a>, and together they joined <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a>' group <a href="/wiki/Byrd_Gang" title="Byrd Gang">Byrd Gang</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag4_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While Chinx was incarcerated, on June 11, 2007,  fellow rapper and friend Stack Bundles was shot and killed. In late 2008, Chinx was released from prison and through his affiliation with the now deceased Bundles, he met Max B. The following year, Max B introduced Chinx to fellow New York rapper <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, who was still relatively unknown at the time. Together, Chinx and Montana formed the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_group" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop group">hip hop group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coke_Boys" class="mw-redirect" title="Coke Boys">Coke Boys</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-urbfeed2268_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urbfeed2268-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On April 13, 2009, Drugz released his first solo mixtape, <i>Hurry Up &amp; Die Vol. 1: Get Ya Casket On</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-complex1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He followed that with <i>Hurry Up &amp; Die Vol. 2: From The Cage To The Stage</i> on January 7, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Drugz then released the third mixtape in that series, <i>Hurry Up &amp; Die Vol. 3</i> on April 13, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2011.E2.80.932012:_Coke_Boys_and_Cocaine_Riot_1_.26_2"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–2012:_Coke_Boys_and_Cocaine_Riot_1_&amp;_2">2011–2012: Coke Boys and <i>Cocaine Riot 1 &amp; 2</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011–2012: Coke Boys and Cocaine Riot 1 &amp; 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 15, 2011, Drugz released his fourth official mixtape <i>Flight 2011</i>, which was entirely produced by New York producer <a href="/wiki/Harry_Fraud" title="Harry Fraud">Harry Fraud</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The mixtape featured a sole guest appearance from Coke Boys-cohort French Montana.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On April 19, 2011, he released his fifth solo mixtape <i>Cocaine Riot</i>, featuring guest appearances from the various members of Coke Boys.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 20, 2011, Coke Boys released their first official collaboration mixtape <i>Coke Boys 2</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-rapradar1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rapradar1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape was primarily produced by Harry Fraud, with additional production from <a href="/wiki/Lex_Luger_(record_producer)" title="Lex Luger (record producer)">Lex Luger</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <i>Coke Boys 2</i> featured members Drugz, Montana, Cheeze, Flip, and Charlie Rock.<sup id="cite_ref-rapradar1_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rapradar1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Following the <i>Coke Boys 2</i> release, on September 24, 2011, Drugz and French Montana released a collaboration mixtape named, <i>Coke Boys Run NY</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 13, 2012, Coke Boys released their second collaboration mixtape, <i>Coke Boys 3</i>, which significantly featured Montana and Drugz throughout, along with Flip, gianni maggiacomo, and Charlie Rock.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Additional guest appearances came from <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Red_Cafe" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Cafe">Red Cafe</a> among others.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape was met with positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_critic" class="mw-redirect" title="Music critic">music critics</a>, with the production the focal point of the praise.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag2_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag2-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> credited Montana and Drugz as being the stars of the mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag2_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag2-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag3_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag3-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 3, 2012, Drugz released his sixth solo mixtape, <i>Cocaine Riot 2</i>, to positive critical reception with <a href="/wiki/AllHipHop" title="AllHipHop">AllHipHop</a> calling it a "classic".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <i>Cocaine Riot 2</i> featured guest appearances from rappers <a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson" title="Action Bronson">Action Bronson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chevy_Woods" title="Chevy Woods">Chevy Woods</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> <i>Cocaine Riot 2</i> featured the significant Montana and Drugz collaboration "I'm A Coke Boy".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, he was featured on Wale's "Let a Nigga Know" and <a href="/wiki/Vado_(rapper)" title="Vado (rapper)">Vado</a>'s "You Ain't Good".<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag3_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag3-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013:_Cocaine_Riot_3">2013: <i>Cocaine Riot 3</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013: Cocaine Riot 3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 4, 2013, Drugz released the official remix to "I'm a Coke Boy" featuring rappers Rick Ross and <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a>, along with French Montana.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The remix would end up being ranked number 43 on <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a>'</i>s list of the 50 best songs of 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Then on April 15, 2013, Drugz released his seventh solo mixtape <i>Cocaine Riot 3</i>, which featured the previously released remix of "I'm a Coke Boy".<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> <i>Cocaine Riot 3</i> featured additional guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hood" title="Ace Hood">Ace Hood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yo_Gotti" title="Yo Gotti">Yo Gotti</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roscoe_Dash" title="Roscoe Dash">Roscoe Dash</a> and others.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Drugz told <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> that, "When I was making this mixtape, I was just trying to give the people something a little different from my normal. I basically started messing with a couple of different beats, but I kept it real New York. But I just really wanted to give 'em the other side of Chinx that they ain't been hearing in a while."<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape closes with "Maybe", a tribute to his deceased childhood friend Stack Bundles.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a positive review <i>XXL</i> stated, "For all its signs of promise, <i>Cocaine Riot 3</i> also has some rookie mistakes. There’s also an over-reliance on features here that the mixtape could do without. The heavy guest list prevents <i>Cocaine Riot 3</i> from being a landmark release, and so this mixtape ends up being more of a continuation of what we’ve already heard from Chinx Drug, rather than a step to the next level. Time will tell if he’s up for that challenge."<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag3_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag3-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Less than a week after the release of <i>Cocaine Riot 3</i>, Drugz released the music video for the remix to "I'm a Coke Boy".<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On May 14, 2013, Drugz released his four previous major solo mixtapes, <i>Flight 2011</i>, and the <i>Cocaine Riot</i> series for retail sale on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He was featured on Montana's debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Excuse_My_French_(album)" title="Excuse My French (album)">Excuse My French</a></i> on the deluxe edition track "Throw It in the Bag".
</p><p>On May 22, 2013, "Right There" featuring Juicy J and French Montana was released as the first single from <i>Cocaine Riot 3</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> "Up In Here" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hood" title="Ace Hood">Ace Hood</a> would be released as the mixtape's second single on June 11, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> On August 12, 2013, Drugz released the music video for "Up In Here" featuring Ace Hood from <i>Cocaine Riot 3</i>, which was shot in <a href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami, Florida">Miami, Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> That same day, French Montana announced that Coke Boys were working on their debut studio album as a group, along with a third mixtape to be released prior to the album.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.9315:_I.27ll_Take_It_From_Here.2C_Cocaine_Riot_4_.26_5.2C_and_Welcome_To_JFK"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–15:_I'll_Take_It_From_Here,_Cocaine_Riot_4_&amp;_5,_and_Welcome_To_JFK">2013–15: <i>I'll Take It From Here</i>, <i>Cocaine Riot 4 &amp; 5</i>, and <i>Welcome To JFK</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2013–15: I&#039;ll Take It From Here, Cocaine Riot 4 &amp; 5, and Welcome To JFK">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 4, 2013, Chinx Drugz, now just going by "Chinx", announced he would release an <a href="/wiki/Extended_Play" class="mw-redirect" title="Extended Play">EP</a> via NuSense Music Group and Coke Boys Records, executive produced by Rick Steel<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> titled <i>I'll Take It From Here</i>, in November 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On October 28, 2013, Chinx released "Feelings" featuring French Montana, the first single from the EP.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On November 26, 2013, it was revealed that Coke Boys would release their fourth mixtape in December 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> That same day, Chinx released his debut retail project, the previously announced EP <i>I'll Take It from Here</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag4_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He featured prominently on Coke Boys fourth mixtape, <i>Coke Boys 4</i>, released on January 1, 2014. On February 1, 2014, Chinx released the music video for "Feelings".<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> In April 2014, Chinx revealed that his next mixtape would be titled <i>Cocaine Riot 4</i> and released on May 15, 2014. He released <i>Cocaine Riot 5</i> on December 25, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> He told <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> that his debut studio album would be titled <i>Welcome To JFK</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> He was being considered for the 2015 <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> Freshman Class.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 2, 2015, Chinx's first official single for his posthumous album <i>Welcome to JFK</i> was released through <a href="/wiki/EOne_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="EOne Music">eOne Music</a>. The track is titled "On Your Body".<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinx&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On May 17, 2015, NYPD officers responded to a 911 call in <a href="/wiki/Briarwood,_Queens" title="Briarwood, Queens">Briarwood, Queens</a> around 4:00&#160;a.m. There, they found Chinx and another victim in a car at <a href="/wiki/Queens_Boulevard" title="Queens Boulevard">Queens Boulevard</a> and 84th Drive. Chinx was transported to Jamaica Medical Center in <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a> with gunshot wounds to his torso, where he was pronounced dead, at age 31.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Two men who were already incarcerated on other charges have been re-arrested in connection with the murder.
</p>
