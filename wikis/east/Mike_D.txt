<p><b>Michael Louis Diamond</b> (born November 20, 1965),<sup id="cite_ref-rollingstone_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rollingstone-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known as <b>Mike D</b>, is an American rapper, musician, and music producer.  He is a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV_bio_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV_bio-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Diamond was born in New York City<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> to <a href="/wiki/Harold_Diamond" title="Harold Diamond">Harold Diamond</a>, an art dealer, and <a href="/wiki/Hester_Diamond" title="Hester Diamond">Hester (née Klein) Diamond</a>, an interior designer and art collector.  He grew up on the Upper West Side surrounded by artwork, including pieces by <a href="/wiki/Willem_de_Kooning" title="Willem de Kooning">Willem de Kooning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Rothko" title="Mark Rothko">Mark Rothko</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Barnett_Newman" title="Barnett Newman">Barnett Newman</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_2021_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_2021-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rstone_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rstone-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He attended the arts-oriented <a href="/wiki/Saint_Ann%27s_School_(Brooklyn)" title="Saint Ann&#39;s School (Brooklyn)">Saint Ann's School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walden_School_(New_York_City)" title="Walden School (New York City)">Walden School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rstone_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rstone-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Mike_D_singing_with_the_Beastie_Boys_at_Trans_Musicales_2004_in_Rennes.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="Mike D singing with the Beastie Boys at Trans Musicales 2004 in Rennes" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Mike_D_singing_with_the_Beastie_Boys_at_Trans_Musicales_2004_in_Rennes.jpg/220px-Mike_D_singing_with_the_Beastie_Boys_at_Trans_Musicales_2004_in_Rennes.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Mike_D_singing_with_the_Beastie_Boys_at_Trans_Musicales_2004_in_Rennes.jpg/330px-Mike_D_singing_with_the_Beastie_Boys_at_Trans_Musicales_2004_in_Rennes.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Mike_D_singing_with_the_Beastie_Boys_at_Trans_Musicales_2004_in_Rennes.jpg/440px-Mike_D_singing_with_the_Beastie_Boys_at_Trans_Musicales_2004_in_Rennes.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1544" data-file-height="1024" /></a><figcaption>Mike D singing with the Beastie Boys at Trans Musicales 2004 in Rennes</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 1979, Diamond co-founded the band <a href="/wiki/The_Young_Aborigines" class="mw-redirect" title="The Young Aborigines">The Young Aborigines</a>. In 1981, <a href="/wiki/Adam_Yauch" title="Adam Yauch">Adam Yauch</a>, aka MCA, a friend and follower of the band, became their bass player, and on the suggestion of their then-guitar player, John Berry, the band changed their name to the Beastie Boys.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> By 1983, <a href="/wiki/Ad-Rock" title="Ad-Rock">Adam Horovitz</a> (Ad-Rock) joined, and their sound began to shift away from <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1992, Diamond founded the Beastie Boys' now-defunct record label <a href="/wiki/Grand_Royal_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand Royal Records">Grand Royal Records</a>. He is also interested in interior design, and designed Brooklyn-themed <a href="/wiki/Toile" title="Toile">toile</a> wallpaper; it was used in the renovation of the Marquee nightclub in <a href="/wiki/Chelsea,_Manhattan" title="Chelsea, Manhattan">Chelsea</a>, which reopened in January 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A year after the death of Yauch in 2012, Diamond told <i>Rolling Stone</i> he was "excited about making new stuff again" and released "Humberto Vs the New Reactionaries (Christine and the Queens Remix)" in July 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> A remix of Yoko Ono Plastic Ono Band's "Bad Dancer" by Diamond and Adam Horovitz was streamed online in August 2013.  The pair is credited with "additional beats, programming and other curve balls".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In October 2014, Diamond stated that he had been working in the studio with American rock band <a href="/wiki/Portugal._The_Man" title="Portugal. The Man">Portugal. The Man</a> as a producer.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He has also produced English punk duo <a href="/wiki/Slaves_(UK_band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Slaves (UK band)">SOFT PLAY</a>'s (formerly Slaves) second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Take_Control_(Slaves_album)" title="Take Control (Slaves album)">Take Control</a></i>, which was released on September 30, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-HMV_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HMV-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He hosts the <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music" title="Apple Music">Apple Music</a> podcast The Echo Chamber.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1993, Diamond married film, television and music video director <a href="/wiki/Tamra_Davis" title="Tamra Davis">Tamra Davis</a>; they have since legally separated.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> They have two children, Davis Diamond and Skyler Diamond who formed the group Very Nice Person.<sup id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> They lived in <a href="/wiki/Cobble_Hill,_Brooklyn" title="Cobble Hill, Brooklyn">Cobble Hill, Brooklyn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Earle-Levine_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Earle-Levine-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He grew up on <a href="/wiki/Central_Park_West" class="mw-redirect" title="Central Park West">Central Park West</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_2021_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_2021-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He later lived in Brooklyn and Tribeca,<sup id="cite_ref-Earle-Levine_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Earle-Levine-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and as of 2016 was living in <a href="/wiki/Malibu,_California" title="Malibu, California">Malibu, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
