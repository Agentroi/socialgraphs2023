<p><b>Raymond Leon Scott</b> (born July 18, 1965), better known by his stage name <b>Benzino</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Urban_contemporary" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban contemporary">urban</a> <a href="/wiki/Media_proprietor" title="Media proprietor">media proprietor</a>, television personality, rapper, and record producer. He produced records (as part of the production team Hangmen 3) and co-owned the magazine <i><a href="/wiki/The_Source" title="The Source">The Source</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></i>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benzino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Benzino has said one of the primary inspirations to enter the genre of rap stems from his fondness of the film <i>Wild Style.</i><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Benzino was a founding member of rap groups <a href="/wiki/The_Almighty_RSO" title="The Almighty RSO">the Almighty RSO</a> and <a href="/wiki/Made_Men" title="Made Men">Made Men</a>. He has appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">reality television</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_Atlanta" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: Atlanta">Love &amp; Hip Hop: Atlanta</a></i>, since 2012.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feud_with_Eminem">Feud with Eminem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benzino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Feud with Eminem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Benzino is known for being involved since 2002 in a widely publicized feud with rapper <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>; the feud began after his <i>The Source</i> magazine rated <i><a href="/wiki/The_Eminem_Show" title="The Eminem Show">The Eminem Show</a></i> as four mics out of five. Eminem blamed the magazine and its co-founder Benzino for what he considered an unfair rating. Benzino  even refused to help promote his movie <i><a href="/wiki/8_Mile_(film)" title="8 Mile (film)">8 Mile</a></i> during his performance in Puerto Rico. Benzino later released <a href="/wiki/Diss_track" class="mw-redirect" title="Diss track">diss tracks</a> towards Eminem, titled "Pull Your Skirt Up" (calling him "2003 <a href="/wiki/Vanilla_Ice" title="Vanilla Ice">Vanilla Ice</a>")<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and "Die Another Day", while Eminem released "The Sauce" and "Nail in the Coffin".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> As a continuation of this animosity between the two, Benzino released the diss mixtape <i>Benzino Presents: Die Another Day: Flawless Victory</i>, further disparaging Eminem.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benzino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scott considered Edward DeJesus to be his father figure, especially in the duration of DeJesus's incarceration. Scott's sister Maureen is a medical doctor, and his other sister, Anita Scott-Wilson, has a doctorate degree in <a href="/wiki/Veterinary_medicine" title="Veterinary medicine">veterinary medicine</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Tuskegee_Institute" class="mw-redirect" title="Tuskegee Institute">Tuskegee Institute</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Coi_Leray" title="Coi Leray">Coi Leray</a> is Scott's daughter.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has dated <a href="/wiki/Karlie_Redd" title="Karlie Redd">Karlie Redd</a> and Althea Heart.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Scott and Heart had a son together in November 2015, but they broke off their engagement shortly thereafter. They appeared together on Season 6 of the television show <i><a href="/wiki/Marriage_Boot_Camp" title="Marriage Boot Camp">Marriage Boot Camp</a></i> chronicling their broken relationship.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 29, 2014, Scott was shot after attending his mother's funeral in <a href="/wiki/Duxbury,_Massachusetts" title="Duxbury, Massachusetts">Duxbury, Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC-shot_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-shot-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He suffered non-life-threatening injuries when the bullets hit his shoulder and back. His 36-year-old nephew Gai Scott was later arrested for the shooting.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC-shot_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-shot-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benzino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On April 16, 2019, Scott was stopped in <a href="/wiki/Brookhaven,_Georgia" title="Brookhaven, Georgia">Brookhaven, Georgia</a> and cited for driving an uninsured vehicle. He was not the policy holder responsible for insuring the vehicle.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 22, 2019, Scott was arrested for a <a href="/wiki/Bench_warrant" class="mw-redirect" title="Bench warrant">bench warrant</a> in <a href="/wiki/DeKalb_County,_Georgia" title="DeKalb County, Georgia">DeKalb County, Georgia</a>. Scott was taken to police headquarters for booking and was later jailed. As he was being transported there, he launched a racist tirade against a police officer arresting him, who was of <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Vietnamese American">Vietnamese descent</a>. The tirade involved multiple <a href="/wiki/Racial_slur" class="mw-redirect" title="Racial slur">racial slurs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maternal_insult" class="mw-redirect" title="Maternal insult">maternal insults</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 31, 2020, Scott was arrested and charged with criminal damage to property. He was accused of damaging the <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Silverado" title="Chevrolet Silverado">Chevrolet Silverado</a> pickup truck of a man who was in the company of Scott's ex-fiancé Althea Heart.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 3, 2022, Scott was arrested and charged with one count of second degree criminal damage to property, a felony.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
