<p><b>Ariyan Arslani</b> (born December 2, 1983), professionally known as <b>Action Bronson</b>, is an American rapper, songwriter, chef, wrestler, and television presenter.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Born and raised in Queens, New York, he embarked on his musical career in January 2011, with the <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> <i>Bon Appetit ..... Bitch!!!!!</i>. He independently released his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Dr._Lecter" class="mw-redirect" title="Dr. Lecter">Dr. Lecter</a></i>, in March 2011. In August 2012, Arslani signed his first major-label deal with <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>, but was later moved to the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>-distributed label <a href="/wiki/Vice_Media" title="Vice Media">Vice Records</a>.
</p><p>Arslani went on to create two self-released mixtapes, <i><a href="/wiki/Rare_Chandeliers" title="Rare Chandeliers">Rare Chandeliers</a></i> (2012) with American music producer, Daniel Alan Maman, better known as <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Chips_2" title="Blue Chips 2">Blue Chips 2</a></i> (2013) with longtime producer <a href="/wiki/Party_Supplies" title="Party Supplies">Party Supplies</a>, before releasing his major-label debut, an extended play (EP) titled <i><a href="/wiki/Saaab_Stories" title="Saaab Stories">Saaab Stories</a></i>, with frequent collaborator <a href="/wiki/Harry_Fraud" title="Harry Fraud">Harry Fraud</a>, in 2013. He released his major-label debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Wonderful_(Action_Bronson_album)" title="Mr. Wonderful (Action Bronson album)">Mr. Wonderful</a></i>, in March 2015, debuting at number seven on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, receiving over 48,000 streams that week.
</p><p>Aside from his career in music, Arslani hosted <i><a href="/wiki/The_Untitled_Action_Bronson_Show" title="The Untitled Action Bronson Show">The Untitled Action Bronson Show</a></i>, a talk/variety show, and still hosts his travel program <i><a href="/wiki/Fuck,_That%27s_Delicious" title="Fuck, That&#39;s Delicious">Fuck, That's Delicious</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/Viceland" title="Viceland">Viceland</a>. His frequent collaborators and lifelong friends <a href="/wiki/Meyhem_Lauren" title="Meyhem Lauren">Meyhem Lauren</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Big_Body_Bes" title="Big Body Bes">Big Body Bes</a> are regulars on both of his television series. Renowned chefs such as <a href="/wiki/Mario_Batali" title="Mario Batali">Mario Batali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Zimmern" title="Andrew Zimmern">Andrew Zimmern</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Boulud" title="Daniel Boulud">Daniel Boulud</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Bayless" title="Rick Bayless">Rick Bayless</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grant_Achatz" title="Grant Achatz">Grant Achatz</a>, and others have been guests on his television series. In September 2017, Arslani published his first book, based on his travel show, a cookbook, also entitled <i>Fuck, That's Delicious</i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_Bronson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1983.E2.80.932010:_Early_life_and_career"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1983–2010:_Early_life_and_career">1983–2010: Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_Bronson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1983–2010: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ariyan Arslani<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> was born in <a href="/wiki/Flushing,_Queens" title="Flushing, Queens">Flushing, Queens</a>, New York City, on December 2, 1983,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> to an <a href="/wiki/Albanian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Albanian people">Albanian</a> Muslim father and an American Jewish mother.<sup id="cite_ref-heritage_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-heritage-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Growing up he had been raised in his father's Muslim tradition.<sup id="cite_ref-upbringing_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-upbringing-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Bayside_High_School_(Queens)" title="Bayside High School (Queens)">Bayside High School</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Bayside,_Queens" title="Bayside, Queens">Bayside, Queens</a>, and graduated in 2002. Before embarking on a career as a rapper, which was originally just a hobby, Bronson worked as a cook at his father's Mediterranean restaurant in <a href="/wiki/Forest_Hills,_NY" class="mw-redirect" title="Forest Hills, NY">Forest Hills</a>. He hosted his own online cooking show, <i>Action in the Kitchen</i>. After breaking his leg in the kitchen, Bronson concentrated solely on rapping.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011:_Dr._Lecter_and_Well_Done">2011: <i>Dr. Lecter</i> and <i>Well Done</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_Bronson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011: Dr. Lecter and Well Done">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Action Bronson's debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Dr._Lecter" class="mw-redirect" title="Dr. Lecter">Dr. Lecter</a></i> was independently released by Fine Fabric Delegates on March 15, 2011. The album, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_production" title="Hip hop production">produced</a> entirely by <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Music_producer" class="mw-redirect" title="Music producer">music producer</a> Tommy Mas, was made available for <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital download</a> and also in <a href="/wiki/CD-R" title="CD-R">CD-R</a>, on his official website. In November of that year, Bronson followed up with <i><a href="/wiki/Well-Done_(album)" title="Well-Done (album)">Well-Done</a></i>, a collaborative effort with American hip hop producer <a href="/wiki/Statik_Selektah" title="Statik Selektah">Statik Selektah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On March 12, 2012, he released his second mixtape, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson_discography#Mixtapes" title="Action Bronson discography">Blue Chips</a>,</i> with <a href="/wiki/Party_Supplies" title="Party Supplies">Party Supplies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932013:_Major-label_deal_and_Saaab_Stories"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2013:_Major-label_deal_and_Saaab_Stories">2012–2013: Major-label deal and <i>Saaab Stories</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_Bronson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012–2013: Major-label deal and Saaab Stories">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Action_Bronson.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Action_Bronson.jpg/220px-Action_Bronson.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Action_Bronson.jpg/330px-Action_Bronson.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Action_Bronson.jpg/440px-Action_Bronson.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5184" data-file-height="3456" /></a><figcaption>Action Bronson performing in July 2013</figcaption></figure>
<p>In August 2012, it was announced <a href="/wiki/Paul_Rosenberg_(music_manager)" title="Paul Rosenberg (music manager)">Paul Rosenberg</a> signed Action Bronson to a management deal with <a href="/wiki/Goliath_Artists" title="Goliath Artists">Goliath Artists</a>, which also houses names such as <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blink-182" title="Blink-182">Blink-182</a> and <a href="/wiki/Danny_Brown_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Danny Brown (rapper)">Danny Brown</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In the same month, Bronson was featured on <a href="/wiki/Odd_Future" title="Odd Future">Odd Future</a>-rapper <a href="/wiki/Domo_Genesis" title="Domo Genesis">Domo Genesis</a> and American hip hop producer The Alchemist's collaborative album <i><a href="/wiki/No_Idols_(Domo_Genesis_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="No Idols (Domo Genesis album)">No Idols</a></i>, on tracks "Elimination Chamber" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Earl_Sweatshirt" title="Earl Sweatshirt">Earl Sweatshirt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples" title="Vince Staples">Vince Staples</a>) and "Daily News" (featuring <a href="/wiki/SpaceGhostPurrp" title="SpaceGhostPurrp">SpaceGhostPurrp</a> and Earl Sweatshirt). Later that year Action Bronson joined both <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>, via media company <a href="/wiki/Vice_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Vice Records">VICE</a>, and concert booking agency <a href="/wiki/NUE_Agency" title="NUE Agency">NUE</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On November 15, 2012, Bronson released <i><a href="/wiki/Rare_Chandeliers" title="Rare Chandeliers">Rare Chandeliers</a></i>, a collaborative mixtape with the Alchemist.
</p><p>In March 2013, Bronson performed at the <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival</a><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and later that month was featured on <a href="/wiki/XXL_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="XXL Magazine">XXL Magazine</a>'s 2013 Freshman Class, a list of up-and-coming rappers.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In May 2013, Action Bronson was moved to <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>, and subsequently released an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP) titled <i><a href="/wiki/Saaab_Stories" title="Saaab Stories">Saaab Stories</a></i>, on June 11, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The EP was produced entirely by American music producer <a href="/wiki/Harry_Fraud" title="Harry Fraud">Harry Fraud</a> and features guest appearances from fellow American rappers <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Released as a <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital download</a> only, the EP was preceded by the single, "Strictly 4 My Jeeps".<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In July 2013, he indicated wanting to get fellow <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>-bred rappers, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, on his <a href="/wiki/Major_record_label" class="mw-redirect" title="Major record label">major-label</a> debut album, but unfortunately couldn't get them on.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Also in July, Bronson announced his major-label debut album would be released in early 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 1, 2013, Bronson released <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Chips_2" title="Blue Chips 2">Blue Chips 2</a></i>, the second installment in his <i>Blue Chips</i> series, in promotion of the album.<sup id="cite_ref-complex.com_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex.com-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On October 28, 2013, <a href="/wiki/Funkmaster_Flex" title="Funkmaster Flex">Funkmaster Flex</a> announced he would be hosting an upcoming Action Bronson mixtape. Production was revealed to be coming from <a href="/wiki/Erick_Sermon" title="Erick Sermon">Erick Sermon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Will_Made_It" title="Mike Will Made It">Mike Will Made It</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Mustard" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Mustard">DJ Mustard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jahlil_Beats" title="Jahlil Beats">Jahlil Beats</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex.com_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex.com-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In a November 2013 interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>, Bronson stated that he would have Kool G Rap and <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>, featured on his upcoming album.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.93present:_Mr._Wonderful_and_various_studio_albums"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–present:_Mr._Wonderful_and_various_studio_albums">2014–present: <i>Mr. Wonderful</i> and various studio albums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_Bronson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2014–present: Mr. Wonderful and various studio albums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Action_Bronson_Coachella_2015.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Action_Bronson_Coachella_2015.jpg/220px-Action_Bronson_Coachella_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Action_Bronson_Coachella_2015.jpg/330px-Action_Bronson_Coachella_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Action_Bronson_Coachella_2015.jpg/440px-Action_Bronson_Coachella_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4896" data-file-height="3672" /></a><figcaption>Bronson performing at <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella</a> in 2015</figcaption></figure>
<p>In February 2014, Action Bronson, <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> and <a href="/wiki/360_(rapper)" title="360 (rapper)">360</a> accompanied rapper <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, on a brief tour of Australia, South Africa and New Zealand.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On May 6, 2014, Action Bronson debuted a food-oriented web series, titled <i>Fuck, That's Delicious</i>. The show is a <a href="/wiki/Vice_Media" title="Vice Media">Vice Records</a>-sponsored monthly web series that chronicles the food-obsessed rapper's exploration of all things culinary.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 5, 2014, Bronson released the song "Easy Rider" as the first single from his debut album <a href="/wiki/Mr._Wonderful_(Action_Bronson_album)" title="Mr. Wonderful (Action Bronson album)"><i>Mr. Wonderful</i></a>. The song was produced by Bronson's longtime producer <a href="/wiki/Party_Supplies" title="Party Supplies">Party Supplies</a>. On August 20, 2014, the music video for "Easy Rider", was released. The video for "Easy Rider", which was directed by Tom Gould, pays homage to the <a href="/wiki/Easy_Rider" title="Easy Rider">1969 Peter Fonda and Dennis Hopper film of the same name</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Bronson officially released "Actin Crazy", via digital distribution on January 20. He released the full album on March 23, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2015, Bronson was hospitalized in <a href="/wiki/Anchorage,_Alaska" title="Anchorage, Alaska">Anchorage, Alaska</a>, for emergency surgery after a concert on October 23. He remained in the hospital for several days.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On Monday May 16, 2016, he presented at the <a href="/wiki/Webby_Awards" title="Webby Awards">Webby Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 25, 2017, he released his third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Chips_7000" title="Blue Chips 7000">Blue Chips 7000</a></i>, which serves as a sequel to his 2013 <i>Blue Chips 2</i> mixtape.
</p><p>On October 31, 2018, Bronson announced that he was parting ways with Vice, the media company that served as his record label and television network.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2018, Bronson announced a tour in support of his latest album, <i><a href="/wiki/White_Bronco_(album)" title="White Bronco (album)">White Bronco</a></i>. The tour ran from February 2019 to March 2019. <a href="/wiki/Meyhem_Lauren" title="Meyhem Lauren">Meyhem Lauren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roc_Marciano" title="Roc Marciano">Roc Marciano</a> will be supporting.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 9, 2020, Bronson revealed the title of his new album, <i><a href="/wiki/Only_for_Dolphins" title="Only for Dolphins">Only for Dolphins</a></i>, released on September 25, 2020. The cover art was painted by Bronson himself. Bronson described the album as "ANOTHER BRIGHT THREAD WOVEN INTO THE TEXTURE OF THE COSMOS".<sup id="cite_ref-Dolphins_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dolphins-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bronson released his sixth solo studio album, <i>Cocodrillo Turbo</i>, on April 29, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_and_film">Television and film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_Bronson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Television and film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Action Bronson starred in two television shows which aired on the <a href="/wiki/Viceland" title="Viceland">Viceland</a> channel: the food travel show <i><a href="/wiki/Fuck,_That%27s_Delicious" title="Fuck, That&#39;s Delicious">Fuck, That's Delicious</a></i>, and a nightly food talk show, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Untitled_Action_Bronson_Show" title="The Untitled Action Bronson Show">The Untitled Action Bronson Show</a></i>. He also starred in the now-canceled comedy documentary series <i><a href="/wiki/Traveling_the_Stars:_Action_Bronson_and_Friends_Watch_%27Ancient_Aliens%27" class="mw-redirect" title="Traveling the Stars: Action Bronson and Friends Watch &#39;Ancient Aliens&#39;">Traveling the Stars: Action Bronson and Friends Watch 'Ancient Aliens'</a></i>.
</p><p>He has had cameo roles in the films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Irishman" title="The Irishman">The Irishman</a></i> (2019), playing a coffin salesman,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_King_of_Staten_Island" title="The King of Staten Island">The King of Staten Island</a></i> (2020).<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Bronson also made an appearance for <a href="/wiki/All_Out_(2022)" title="All Out (2022)">AEW's All Out</a> on September 4, 2022, and wrestled his first professional wrestling match at <a href="/wiki/AEW_Grand_Slam_(2022)" title="AEW Grand Slam (2022)">Grand Slam</a>, teaming with <a href="/wiki/Hook_(wrestler)" title="Hook (wrestler)">Hook</a> to defeat <a href="/wiki/Matt_Menard" class="mw-redirect" title="Matt Menard">Matt Menard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angelo_Parker" class="mw-redirect" title="Angelo Parker">Angelo Parker</a> which aired on September 23, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_Bronson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In March 2016, the Program Board of the <a href="/wiki/George_Washington_University" title="George Washington University">George Washington University</a> announced that Bronson would be the headlining performer for the university's yearly spring concert, "Spring Fling". Controversy soon erupted as Bronson's song "Consensual Rape" came to light, as well as statements Bronson made that were considered <a href="/wiki/Homophobic" class="mw-redirect" title="Homophobic">homophobic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transphobic" class="mw-redirect" title="Transphobic">transphobic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Misogynistic" class="mw-redirect" title="Misogynistic">misogynistic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Student activist groups successfully pushed for the university to cancel the performance.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> In April 2016, Bronson was similarly disinvited from the <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College_(Connecticut)" title="Trinity College (Connecticut)">Trinity College</a> Spring Weekend concert.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bronson responded in an open letter, claiming that his songs "depict a story" and "aren't meant to be anything but an artistic expression," condemned all forms of sexual violence, and offered an apology.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_Bronson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_Bronson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Action Bronson cites fellow American rappers <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>, <a href="/wiki/UGK" title="UGK">UGK</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>, as major influences.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Other artists include <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Santana" title="Carlos Santana">Carlos Santana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a> as well as Albanian wedding singers.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rapping_style">Rapping style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_Bronson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Rapping style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bronson frequently raps about food in his songs.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> He is also well known for his frequent, and often obscure, <a href="/wiki/Allusion" title="Allusion">allusions</a> to athletes and sports teams. Bronson's lyrics often make reference to <a href="/wiki/Sports_in_New_York_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Sports in New York City">sports in New York City</a> but also touch on more obscure subjects such as professional wrestlers,<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bodybuilding" title="Bodybuilding">bodybuilders</a>, <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating" title="Figure skating">figure skating</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sports_betting" title="Sports betting">sports betting</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Early in his career, Bronson gained recognition for lyrically and stylistically resembling fellow New York rapper <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a> of Wu-Tang Clan (with whom he collaborated, along with friend and fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Termanology" title="Termanology">Termanology</a>, on a song called "Meteor Hammer", from the 2011 compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/Legendary_Weapons" title="Legendary Weapons">Legendary Weapons</a></i>). Ghostface Killah himself has admitted to confusing Bronson's rapping for his own.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> In a 2011 interview with <a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a>, Bronson was asked about how it was to be compared to the Wu-Tang rapper, and he responded with,
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Yeah, I mean at the end of the day, it's all good because Ghostface Killah is one of the best rappers alive, so if I sound similar to the best rapper alive then that is fine. To me there is no comparison; he is a legend and I am a newcomer. If I would try and emulate with anyone it would be <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, he is the person I look up to the most. I am not upset but at the end of the day I am my own person and no one can take that away from me.<sup id="cite_ref-dxnextaction_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dxnextaction-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Action_Bronson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bronson has two children with a former girlfriend.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Bronson's current girlfriend gave birth to a son in November 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-Men&#39;s_Health_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Men&#39;s_Health-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In mid-2020, prompted by having reached a body weight of 400 pounds (180&#160;kg), and having a myriad of associated health problems, as well as by the birth of his son, Bronson began a regimen of healthier eating as well as <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a> and <a href="/wiki/High-intensity_interval_training" title="High-intensity interval training">high-intensity interval training</a>. As of December 2020 he had lost 127 pounds (58&#160;kg).<sup id="cite_ref-Men&#39;s_Health_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Men&#39;s_Health-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> He took up <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_jiu-jitsu" title="Brazilian jiu-jitsu">Brazilian jiu-jitsu</a> in 2021,<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> training under Ryron Gracie.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
