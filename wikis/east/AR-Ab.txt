<p><b>Abdul Ibrahim West</b> (born September 26, 1982),<sup id="cite_ref-AbdulWest1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AbdulWest1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AbdulWest2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AbdulWest2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>AR-AB</b> (also referred to as <b>King AB</b>), is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>. He is a member of OBH (Original Block Hustlaz), the label and rap group he co-founded with fellow rapper Dark Lo. <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> named him one of "15 Unsigned Rappers Who Should Get a Deal After SXSW" in March 2013, the same year he turned himself in to authorities following legal trouble. He signed with Cash Money Records in May 2016. OBH members have included: Dark Lo, Lik Moss, Shamoney "Da Wolf", p90 smooth, Breeze Begets, Pretty Flock, Skinny me, Newz, Lord Grimey, Kylledge, Kallie Ziltz, NoBrakes Bras, Young Mir, Wiz Lo &amp; others.<sup id="cite_ref-Complex_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5days_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5days-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886046785">.mw-parser-output .toclimit-2 .toclevel-1 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-3 .toclevel-2 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-4 .toclevel-3 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-5 .toclevel-4 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-6 .toclevel-5 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-7 .toclevel-6 ul{display:none}</style><div class="toclimit-3"><meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" /></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AR-Ab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Abdul West was born on September 26, 1982, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. West began his career as a battle rapper and went on to become a member of Cassidy's <a href="/wiki/Larsiny_Family" title="Larsiny Family">Larsiny Family</a>, appearing as a featured artist on his mixtapes throughout 2007, namely <i>07-07-07 (Happy Birthday)</i><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and <i>Put Ya L In The Sky</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He continued working features for artists during this time until the release of his debut mixtape <i>Welcome to Trap Street</i> on May 1, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008, West and Cassidy appeared on the <i>Cocaine City</i> street DVD, where the two collaborated on "City of Dreams" alongside a young <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> This would be Ab's first music video before concentrating on his solo career. He released his second mixtape <i>I See Dead People</i> on January 8, 2009,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and his third mixtape <i>Allegheny AB</i> on May 26, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>West began making waves following the release of his fourth mixtape <i>Who Harder Than Me</i>, released on February 13, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, West began working on his fifth mixtape <i>Who Harder Than Me II</i>, which was released on October 5, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The record included features from fellow Philadelphia rapper <a href="/wiki/Freeway_(rapper)" title="Freeway (rapper)">Freeway</a>, and New York rapper and producer <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a> who had previously worked with Cassidy during the latter's stint with <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders_Entertainment" title="Ruff Ryders Entertainment">Ruff Ryders Entertainment</a>. The collaboration, during which Swizz Beatz referred to Ab as "most important new artist in rap", prompted speculation whether he would be signed to his label.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The following year saw the release of his sixth mixtape <i>MUD Musik</i> on February 28, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> This would be his last release before his latest round of legal trouble which saw West turning himself into authorities and incarcerated in March of that same year.<sup id="cite_ref-5days_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5days-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vladtweet_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vladtweet-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AR-Ab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>West grew up in <a href="/wiki/North_Philadelphia" title="North Philadelphia">North Philadelphia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In late 2010, West and Dark Lo formed their label and rap collective OBH (Original Block Hustlaz), which also includes his brother Lik Moss among other rappers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In 2012, West's grandmother died and his mother died the following month. He describes this as a traumatic moment in his life, having already lost an elder brother prior.<sup id="cite_ref-MotherDeath_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MotherDeath-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> West is a practicing Muslim, who prays 5 times a day.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AR-Ab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>West has been embroiled in a spate of legal issues throughout his career, and prior to his involvement in the music industry, including charges related to drug trafficking, possession, and violent crimes. According to interviews, he has served time in jail on four separate occasions prior to his most-recent 2013 sentencing related to <a href="/wiki/Crack_cocaine" title="Crack cocaine">crack cocaine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-VladJailInterview1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VladJailInterview1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PostJailInterview1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PostJailInterview1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> West, along with eight others, has been indicted in connection with a drug trafficking group in North Philadelphia.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2005_homicide_case">2005 homicide case</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AR-Ab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2005 homicide case">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Cassidy_(rapper)#Murder_case" title="Cassidy (rapper)">Cassidy (rapper) §&#160;Murder case</a></div>
<p>In 2005, West was one of three men wanted by police in connection to an April 15 shooting that left one man dead and two others injured.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Prominent rapper <a href="/wiki/Cassidy_(rapper)" title="Cassidy (rapper)">Cassidy</a> was the main suspect, with Ab and a third unidentified man being wanted as accomplices. In a December 3, 2008, interview with PhillyHeatTV, AR-Ab revealed he was found <a href="/wiki/Acquittal" title="Acquittal">"not guilty"</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> having spent two years fighting the case.<sup id="cite_ref-Vibe_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Cassidy had surrendered to Philadelphia police on the afternoon of June 17, 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The case took a turn when the primary witness withdrew his confession. On January 25, 2006, Cassidy was convicted of involuntary manslaughter, two counts of aggravated assault and possession of an instrument of crime for his involvement in the shooting. He was sentenced to 11 to 23 months in prison plus probation, and was credited with the 7 months he had already served.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He was released after serving 8 months in prison, and went on to relaunch the <a href="/wiki/Larsiny_Family" title="Larsiny Family">Larsiny Family</a> which included then-member AR-Ab.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_shootings">2011 shootings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AR-Ab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011 shootings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During an incident late-September 2011, West survived being shot ten times. A tweet dated September 24, 2011, showed a bandaged West lying in bed recuperating with the caption "10 shots couldn't stop me!!!"<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> A previous Tweet confirmed he was in the hospital receiving treatment.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> He is reported to have suffered two shots to the stomach, two to the hand, five to the <a href="/wiki/Gluteus_maximus_muscle" class="mw-redirect" title="Gluteus maximus muscle">glutes</a> and one shot to the thigh.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_prison_sentence">2013 prison sentence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AR-Ab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013 prison sentence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On April 13, 2013, media outlets reported on a tweet from rapper AR-Ab referring to a past crime which caught up to him.<sup id="cite_ref-vladtweet_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vladtweet-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> "3 years ago I got caught up in a big drug trafficking raid, it just caught up to me. Now I gottta pay da price. I'm doing my final show tonite", claimed the tweet.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Speculations were abound whether he would be going to jail soon.<sup id="cite_ref-vladtweet_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vladtweet-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On April 26, videographer Gil Videos uploaded a video with Ab revealing he will be turning himself in to the authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-5days_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5days-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Following his prison sentence, music videos from AR-Ab and OBH members prominently featured the "Free AR-Ab" slogan, and urged fans to write to Abdul West while he's in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-AbdulWest1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AbdulWest1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AbdulWest2_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AbdulWest2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was released on parole in November 2014, having served 18 months.<sup id="cite_ref-PostJailInterview1_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PostJailInterview1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> According to interviews, Ab was booked for charges related to <a href="/wiki/Crack_cocaine" title="Crack cocaine">crack cocaine</a> and faced up to 3 years, which was hastened to 18 months through his participation in the <a href="/wiki/Boot_camp_(correctional)" title="Boot camp (correctional)">boot camp</a> program.<sup id="cite_ref-PostJailInterview1_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PostJailInterview1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Feud_with_Meek_Mill">Feud with <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AR-Ab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Feud with Meek Mill">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Drake referenced West on his song "Back to Back" where he says "I drove here in the Wraith playin' AR-Ab". West later appeared on VladTV in August 2015 saying that while he hasn't signed on to Drake's <a href="/wiki/OVO_Sound" title="OVO Sound">OVO Sound</a> and sided with him saying he was disappointed in Mill's response.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Mill responded on August 6 during a performance in <a href="/wiki/Camden,_New_Jersey" title="Camden, New Jersey">Camden, New Jersey</a> where he said "Fuck AR-Ab" and questioned his allegiance to an out-of-town rapper. West responded by releasing his own version of "Back to Back" freestyle on August 8 which dissed Mill and also contained violent lyrics where he threatened to unleash his "shooters" upon Mill.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> In response, Meek's cousin Omelly released his own version of "Back to Back" freestyle which dissed AR-Ab and also contained violent lyrics. AR-Ab did another interview on VladTV where he said Mill was too scared to respond himself and Omelly was the "softest one on Dream Chasers". He also told that he won't respond to Omelly's diss because he's a "worker".<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> In January 2016, West revealed that he and Mill had reconciled.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2018_indictment">2018 indictment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AR-Ab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2018 indictment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In October 2018, West, alongside eight others said to be members of a drug trafficking organization, were indicted on counts of distributing drugs in <a href="/wiki/North_Philadelphia" title="North Philadelphia">North Philadelphia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> His charges include a conspiracy to distribute, the possession, and the distribution of cocaine, crack, methamphetamine, and heroin.<sup id="cite_ref-justice.gov_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-justice.gov-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The indictment identified West as the leader of the gang, and that the gang obtained high-end properties in Philadelphia to protect their supply.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On November 19, 2019, West was found guilty of several counts of conspiracy and distribution of crack cocaine, heroin, and methamphetamine in the federal conspiracy case. On April 15, 2021, West was sentenced to 45 years in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
