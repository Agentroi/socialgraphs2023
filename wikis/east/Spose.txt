<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spose&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Peters was born in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Maine" title="Portland, Maine">Portland, Maine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-phoenix_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-phoenix-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Wells,_Maine" title="Wells, Maine">Wells, Maine</a>.
</p><p>Although he began rapping in <a href="/wiki/Eighth_grade" title="Eighth grade">eighth grade</a>, he did not pursue it as a career until after he graduated from <a href="/wiki/Wells_High_School" title="Wells High School">Wells High School</a> in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-pph_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pph-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> As a youth, started a rock band, The Frothy Four.
</p><p>Peters attended <a href="/wiki/University_of_New_Hampshire" title="University of New Hampshire">University of New Hampshire</a> as an English major and later transferred to <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_University" title="Suffolk University">Suffolk University</a>; however, he was offered a record deal when he was two classes from graduating and did not finish.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spose&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Adopted the stage name Spose, he <a href="/wiki/Self-publishing" title="Self-publishing">self-released</a> his first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Preposterously_Dank" title="Preposterously Dank">Preposterously Dank</a></i> in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-pph_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pph-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He continued to work selling lobster at the Barnacle Billy's restaurant in <a href="/wiki/Ogunquit" class="mw-redirect" title="Ogunquit">Ogunquit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Spose released the <i><a href="/wiki/We_Smoked_It_All" title="We Smoked It All">We Smoked It All</a></i> mixtape with rapper <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cam_Groves&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cam Groves (page does not exist)">Cam Groves</a> in 2009.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In January 2010, his single, "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Awesome" title="I&#39;m Awesome">I'm Awesome</a>" (from his <i>We Smoked it All</i> mixtape),<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> received airplay on <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a> radio stations.<sup id="cite_ref-phoenix_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-phoenix-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In February 2010, after the song caught on in other parts of the US, Spose signed a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Republic">Universal Republic Records</a> and announced that he would begin working on a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-phoenix_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-phoenix-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2010, the video was filmed in Wells at the town-owned activity center where Spose had his first concert at the age of 24.<sup id="cite_ref-yccs-2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yccs-2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The video was released on April 14, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> "I'm Awesome" peaked at #37 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-923now_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-923now-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> "I’m Awesome" brought about his comedy hip hop style.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Spose later stated in an interview with that he had left Universal in late 2010 without releasing anything with the label.<sup id="cite_ref-why-downeast_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-why-downeast-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 25, 2010, Spose announced through a series of <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> posts (which were linked to <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>) that he felt that <i>We Smoked It All Vol. 2</i> had too many original songs to be considered a mixtape. Partially because of this, he decided to create a project, <i>Happy Medium</i>, with many original songs that were originally supposed to be on <i>We Smoked It All Vol. 2</i>, in addition to other material.  He also said that new songs from <i>Happy Medium</i> would be released at 4:20 PM Eastern Time every Wednesday up until the release of <i>Happy Medium</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On December 17, 2010, Spose released <i>Happy Medium</i> for <a href="/wiki/Streaming_media" title="Streaming media">streaming</a> on his Facebook page. The physical album was released in <a href="/wiki/Bull_Moose_Music" title="Bull Moose Music">Bull Moose Music</a> stores in late December 2010, and it was released on iTunes in January 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>We Smoked It All Vol. 2</i> was released <a href="/wiki/420_(cannabis_culture)" title="420 (cannabis culture)">intentionally on</a> April 20, 2011, at midnight.
</p><p>Spose has a part in "Red Cup (I Fly Solo)" by <a href="/wiki/Cash_Cash" title="Cash Cash">Cash Cash</a> and "Better Side of Me" by <a href="/wiki/Sparks_the_Rescue" title="Sparks the Rescue">Sparks the Rescue</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Spose announced in an interview with <a href="/wiki/92.3_Now" class="mw-redirect" title="92.3 Now">92.3 Now</a> that he had left <a href="/wiki/Universal_Republic_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Republic Records">Universal Republic Records</a>.
</p><p>In January 2012, "I'm Awesome" became the theme to the comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._D" title="Mr. D">Mr. D</a></i>. Spose released the music video for his single "Pop Song" on February 22, 2012. He released his third album <i>The Audacity!</i>, on April 16, 2012, on iTunes. On October 24, 2012, Spose launched a <a href="/wiki/Crowdfunding" title="Crowdfunding">crowdfunded</a> fundraiser on <a href="/wiki/Kickstarter" title="Kickstarter">Kickstarter</a>.com to raise $25,000 to fund <i>The Yard Sale</i>. It was funded on November 23, 2012, raising $28,147.
</p><p><i>The Peter Sparker Mixtape</i> was released as a free download on June 10, 2013. Deluxe physical copies were sold at bullmoose.com. <i>Dankonia</i>, a mixtape entirely over <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a> instrumentals (with other connections to Outkast, such as the album title, named after <i><a href="/wiki/Stankonia" title="Stankonia">Stankonia</a></i>, which also shares the same release date as <i>Dankonia</i>) was released for free on October 31, 2013.  A physical copy was released in limited numbers on bullmoose.com on November 5, and the <i>Almost Complete Lyrics Book</i> was released on November 29, also on the BullMoose website.
</p><p>Spose released his fourth studio album, <i>Why Am I So Happy</i>, in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-why-downeast_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-why-downeast-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On April 4, 2017, he released his fifth album, <i>Good Luck With Your Life</i>, locked in a mobile game <i>The King Of Maine</i> with an extra song, "I Wanna Keep It."
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spose&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p>
