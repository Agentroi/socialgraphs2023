<p><b>Willie Junior Maxwell II</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born June 7, 1991), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Fetty Wap</b>, is an American rapper and singer. He rose to prominence after his debut single "<a href="/wiki/Trap_Queen" title="Trap Queen">Trap Queen</a>" reached number two on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart in May 2015. This helped Fetty Wap secure a record deal with <a href="/wiki/300_Entertainment" title="300 Entertainment">300 Entertainment</a>. The single was followed up with two <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">top 10</a> hits in the U.S., "<a href="/wiki/679_(song)" title="679 (song)">679</a>" (featuring Remy Boyz) and "<a href="/wiki/My_Way_(Fetty_Wap_song)" title="My Way (Fetty Wap song)">My Way</a>" (remixed featuring <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>). All three songs were featured on Fetty Wap's <a href="/wiki/Fetty_Wap_(album)" title="Fetty Wap (album)">eponymous debut album</a>, released in September 2015, which peaked atop the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fetty_Wap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maxwell was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Paterson,_New_Jersey" title="Paterson, New Jersey">Paterson, New Jersey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Born with <a href="/wiki/Glaucoma" title="Glaucoma">glaucoma</a> in both eyes, Maxwell revealed in a 2015 interview that doctors were unable to save his left eye and instead fitted him with an <a href="/wiki/Ocular_prosthesis" title="Ocular prosthesis">ocular prosthesis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Eastside_High_School_(Paterson,_New_Jersey)" title="Eastside High School (Paterson, New Jersey)">Eastside High School</a> before dropping out in order to pursue his music career.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fetty_Wap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932015:_Beginnings_and_debut_album"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2015:_Beginnings_and_debut_album">2013–2015: Beginnings and debut album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fetty_Wap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2013–2015: Beginnings and debut album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fetty Wap developed an interest in making music in 2013. Initially starting off as a rapper, he later decided to start singing as well because he "wanted to do something different". He was nicknamed "Fetty," and "Wap" was added to the end of the name to perform in tribute to <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>'s alias, Guwop.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fetty Wap's commercial debut single, titled "<a href="/wiki/Trap_Queen" title="Trap Queen">Trap Queen</a>", was released in early 2014. This song appeared in his 2014 mixtape <i>Up Next</i> released in July 2014. He recorded the song in February 2014. However, it did not gain major recognition until mid-November 2014, and since then it has become a <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">platinum record</a> and has over 130 million plays on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>. In November 2014, Fetty Wap represented by Attorney Navarro W. Gray<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> secured a <a href="/wiki/Recording_contract" title="Recording contract">recording contract</a> with <a href="/wiki/300_Entertainment" title="300 Entertainment">300 Entertainment</a>, the imprint was founded by Lyor Cohen, Kevin Liles, Roger Gold and Todd Moscowitz, with distribution through <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The song "Trap Queen" is about a woman dealing and cooking <a href="/wiki/Crack_cocaine" title="Crack cocaine">crack cocaine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> His second mixtape, <i>Fetty Wap: The Mixtape</i> was originally set for release in February 2015, but was delayed because he continued to create new songs.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In June 2015, Fetty Wap was included in <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s 2015 Freshman Class.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 29, 2015, Fetty Wap released his second single "<a href="/wiki/679_(song)" title="679 (song)">679</a>", originally from his <i>Up Next</i> mixtape. The song's accompanying music video premiered on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> in May, before being commercially released. "679" had an original version with Monty only and an added verse by Fetty Wap. The Remy Boyz version was removed from the album.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> His next single, "<a href="/wiki/My_Way_(Fetty_Wap_song)" title="My Way (Fetty Wap song)">My Way</a>", became his second top 10 entry on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. Later, <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a> remixed "My Way" and created the version that was then played on radio.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Afterwards, "679" reached the Hot 100 top 10 peaking at number four.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the week of July 26 to August 1, 2015, the rapper matched <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a> marks by hip-hop prominent artists <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>. He became the first male rapper with three songs occupying the top 20 spots on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 chart since Eminem did so in 2013. He also became the first male rapper in four years to have concurrent singles reach the top 10 of the same chart since Lil Wayne did so in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> With his fourth single "<a href="/wiki/Again_(Fetty_Wap_song)" title="Again (Fetty Wap song)">Again</a>", Fetty Wap became the first act in the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Songs" title="Hot Rap Songs">Hot Rap Songs</a> chart's 26-year history to chart his first four entries in the top 10 simultaneously.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fetty Wap's debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Fetty_Wap_(album)" title="Fetty Wap (album)">Fetty Wap</a></i> was released on September 25, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The album debuted at number one on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, with 129,000 equivalent album units (75,484 in pure album sales).<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Fetty Wap subsequently released two mixtapes for streaming only: <i><a href="/wiki/Coke_Zoo" title="Coke Zoo">Coke Zoo</a></i> in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and <i>ZOO 16: The Mixtape</i> with Zoo Gang.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Fetty Wap received two nominations at the <a href="/wiki/58th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="58th Annual Grammy Awards">58th Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58thgramm_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58thgramm-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932020:_EPs.2C_mixtapes_and_other_releases"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2020:_EPs,_mixtapes_and_other_releases">2016–2020: EPs, mixtapes and other releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fetty_Wap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2016–2020: EPs, mixtapes and other releases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On February 5, 2016, Fetty Wap released a new single titled "<a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Choo_(song)" title="Jimmy Choo (song)">Jimmy Choo</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On April 26, 2016, it was announced that Fetty Wap would be getting his own <a href="/wiki/Mobile_game" title="Mobile game">mobile</a> <a href="/wiki/Racing_video_game" class="mw-redirect" title="Racing video game">racing game</a> available on phone, <a href="/wiki/Tablet_computer" title="Tablet computer">tablet</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Apple_TV" title="Apple TV">Apple TV</a> starting on May 3.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The game is offshoot of mobile game Nitro Nation Stories. A street racing game, it has <a href="/wiki/Multiplayer_video_game" title="Multiplayer video game">multi-player</a>, car customization, and different storylines to choose. It has partnered with automotive brands like <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nissan" title="Nissan">Nissan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cadillac" title="Cadillac">Cadillac</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The Fetty Wap version will include Fetty Wap and Monty into the storyline.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fetty Wap was featured on the <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Harmony" title="Fifth Harmony">Fifth Harmony</a> single "<a href="/wiki/All_in_My_Head_(Flex)" title="All in My Head (Flex)">All in My Head (Flex)</a>". He released the single "<a href="/wiki/Wake_Up_(Fetty_Wap_song)" title="Wake Up (Fetty Wap song)">Wake Up</a>" in April 2016. The official music video for the song was filmed at his alma mater, <a href="/wiki/Eastside_High_School_(Paterson,_New_Jersey)" title="Eastside High School (Paterson, New Jersey)">Eastside High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> His single "Make You Feel Good" was released in August 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 21, 2016, he released a 19 track mixtape titled <i>Zoovier</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His song "Like a Star" features <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a> and was released in December 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 4, 2017, he released the song "Way You Are" featuring Monty,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> and the song "Flip Phone" on February 10, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> He walked in <a href="/wiki/Philipp_Plein" title="Philipp Plein">Philipp Plein</a>'s runway show during <a href="/wiki/New_York_Fashion_Week" title="New York Fashion Week">New York Fashion Week</a> in February 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He released the single "Aye" on May 12, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Aye_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aye-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> He released the mixtape <i>Lucky No. 7</i> on June 7, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Lucky_No_7_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lucky_No_7-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On August 18, 2017, he released the single "There She Go" featuring Monty.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> In October 2017, Fetty Wap featured in <a href="/wiki/Cheat_Codes_(DJs)" title="Cheat Codes (DJs)">Cheat Codes</a>' single "<a href="/wiki/Feels_Great" title="Feels Great">Feels Great</a>" with CVBZ.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 19, 2018, Fetty Wap released the EP <i>For My Fans III: The Final Chapter</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> He released the mixtape <i>Bruce Wayne</i> in June 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-bruce_wayne_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bruce_wayne-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 7, 2019, Fetty Wap's birthday, he released the single "Birthday".<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> On September 27, he released the song "Brand New".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> On February 14, 2020, he released the mixtape <i>Trap &amp; B</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2021.E2.80.93present:_The_Butterfly_Effect"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2021–present:_The_Butterfly_Effect">2021–present: <i>The Butterfly Effect</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fetty_Wap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2021–present: The Butterfly Effect">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 28, 2021, Fetty Wap previewed the song "Out The Hood" in an <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> video with the caption "10/22 T.B.E", giving speculation of a single or mixtape release. Later on October 13, 2021, Fetty Wap posted the official cover art for the album "The Butterfly Effect" announcing it as his second studio album, releasing six years since his debut, however on the intro, he sings "it's been seven years". The Butterfly Effect was released October 22, 2021, through 300 Entertainment &amp; RGF Productions, marking the return of Fetty Wap after several mixtape releases &amp; legal issues. The title derives from his late daughter, Lauren, where he drew inspiration for the album name &amp; some songs.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> In November 2022, Fetty released a single titled "<a href="/wiki/Sweet_Yamz" class="mw-redirect" title="Sweet Yamz">Sweet Yamz</a>", a recreation of the song "Yamz" by <a href="/wiki/Masego_(musician)" title="Masego (musician)">Masego</a> &amp; Devin Morrison. The song went viral on the video platform TikTok and the song received positive reception, with singer <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Wilson_(singer)" title="Charlie Wilson (singer)">Charlie Wilson</a> releasing an unofficial remix through the app, and rapper <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> calling it the "song of the year".<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fetty_Wap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Fetty_Wap_in_concert_.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Fetty_Wap_in_concert_.jpg/220px-Fetty_Wap_in_concert_.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Fetty_Wap_in_concert_.jpg/330px-Fetty_Wap_in_concert_.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Fetty_Wap_in_concert_.jpg/440px-Fetty_Wap_in_concert_.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2448" data-file-height="3264" /></a><figcaption>Fetty Wap at <a href="/wiki/Saratoga_Performing_Arts_Center" title="Saratoga Performing Arts Center">Saratoga Performing Arts Center</a> in 2015</figcaption></figure>
<p>Fetty Wap has referred to his music as "ignorant <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;B_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;B-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> He combines singing and rapping.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fetty Wap usually wears the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Haiti" title="Flag of Haiti">flag of Haiti</a> in honor of his daughter's late grandmother<sup id="cite_ref-CivilTV_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CivilTV-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> and draws influences from <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Haiti" title="Culture of Haiti">Haitian culture</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with CivilTV, he said that he "fell in love with the culture; people don't know what <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> means to me."<sup id="cite_ref-CivilTV_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CivilTV-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> On several occasions, Fetty Wap has also cited <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>-based rapper <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a> as a major influence in his music.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;B_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;B-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fetty_Wap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fetty Wap is the father of six children with five women.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ol><li>Aydin (born May 2011) with Ariel Reese</li>
<li>Zaviera (born March 2015) with Lezhae Zeona</li>
<li>Khari (born March 2016) with <a href="/wiki/Masika_Kalysha" title="Masika Kalysha">Masika Kalysha</a></li>
<li>Amani (born April 2016) with Elaynna Parker</li>
<li>Lauren (2017–2021) with Turquoise Miami</li>
<li>Zy (born 2018) with Lezhae Zeona</li></ol>
<p>In early 2018, model <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Skyy" title="Alexis Skyy">Alexis Skyy</a> named Fetty Wap as the father of her daughter Alaiya. In 2019, Fetty Wap publicly stated that he is not Alaiya's biological father, but is still a father figure to her. In December 2020, Brandon Medford was revealed to be Alaiya's biological father. Their <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_sex_tape" title="Celebrity sex tape">sex tape</a> was leaked in January 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fetty Wap appeared on the third season on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s <i>Love &amp; Hip Hop: Hollywood</i>, which documented his strained relationship with Masika.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> He also appeared on the ninth season of <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_New_York" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York">Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York</a></i> which documented his strained relationship with Alexis Skyy.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2019, Fetty Wap privately married model Leandra K. Gonzalez.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> A year later, Fetty Wap officially divorced from Gonzalez.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2021, Fetty Wap's daughter, Lauren, died at the age of four.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/TMZ" title="TMZ">TMZ</a> reported that her death certificate stated she had died of complications of a congenital heart <a href="/wiki/Arrhythmia" title="Arrhythmia">arrhythmia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> although her mother Turquoise Miami said on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> in August 2021 that Lauren's cause of death had not yet been fully determined.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fetty_Wap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Over the years, Fetty Wap has been on the receiving end of multiple lawsuits including <a href="/wiki/Copyright_infringement" title="Copyright infringement">copyright infringement</a>, <a href="/wiki/Defamation" title="Defamation">defamation</a>, property damage and assault.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At 1:20 AM on November 2, 2017, he was arrested after being pulled over on a <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> highway. He was subsequently charged with <a href="/wiki/Drunk_driving" title="Drunk driving">drunk driving</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reckless_endangerment" class="mw-redirect" title="Reckless endangerment">reckless endangerment</a>, aggravated unlicensed operation of a motor vehicle, illegally changing lanes, and <a href="/wiki/Drag_racing" title="Drag racing">drag racing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 29, 2021, federal prosecutors unsealed an indictment against Fetty Wap, charging him with one count of conspiracy to distribute and possess controlled substances. He was arrested at <a href="/wiki/Citi_Field" title="Citi Field">Citi Field</a> and pleaded not guilty to federal drug charges in a New York court.<sup id="cite_ref-2021_federal_arrest_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2021_federal_arrest-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> Fetty Wap was released on a $500,000 bond on November 5, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup> He was put back in jail again on August 8, 2022, after violating the terms of his pretrial release.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup> On August 22, 2022, he pleaded guilty to his drug charges,<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup> and, on May 24, 2023, Fetty Wap was sentenced to six years in prison in New York.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
