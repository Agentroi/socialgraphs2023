<p><b>Joell Christopher Ortiz</b> (born July 6, 1980) is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a> and a former member of the group <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse_(group)" title="Slaughterhouse (group)">Slaughterhouse</a>. Ortiz grew up in the <a href="/wiki/NYCHA" class="mw-redirect" title="NYCHA">Cooper Park Houses</a> in the <a href="/wiki/East_Williamsburg,_Brooklyn" title="East Williamsburg, Brooklyn">East Williamsburg</a> section of Brooklyn, New York, formerly signed to <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Aftermath_Entertainment" title="Aftermath Entertainment">Aftermath Entertainment</a> record label. He was featured in the Unsigned Hype column of the March 2004 issue of <i>The Source</i> Magazine and was also selected as Chairman's Choice in <i>XXL</i> Magazine. 
</p><p>During the same time Joell also went on to win the 2004 EA Sports Battle which earned his song "Mean Business" a spot on the NBA Live 2005 soundtrack. The same year he was offered a contract to <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a>'s <a href="/wiki/So_So_Def" class="mw-redirect" title="So So Def">So So Def</a> label. He released his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Brick:_Bodega_Chronicles" title="The Brick: Bodega Chronicles">The Brick: Bodega Chronicles</a></i> in 2007. Since then, he has released <i><a href="/wiki/Free_Agent_(album)" title="Free Agent (album)">Free Agent</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/House_Slippers" title="House Slippers">House Slippers</a></i> (2014) and <i>Monday</i> (2019).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He is currently one half of the rap duo Crook and Joell with his former Slaughterhouse bandmate, <a href="/wiki/Crooked_I" title="Crooked I">Crooked I</a>. Together, they released three studio albums.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joell_Ortiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Joell Christopher Ortiz<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> was born to Puerto Rican parents in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a> on July 6, 1980, where he grew up in <a href="/wiki/East_Williamsburg,_Brooklyn" title="East Williamsburg, Brooklyn">East Williamsburg</a>. He was formerly signed to <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Aftermath_Entertainment" title="Aftermath Entertainment">Aftermath Entertainment</a> record label.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>He was featured in the Unsigned Hype column of the March 2004 issue of <i>The Source</i> Magazine and was also selected as Chairman's Choice in <i>XXL</i> Magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> During the same time Joell also went on to win the 2004 EA Sports Battle which earned his song "Mean Business" a spot on the NBA Live 2005 soundtrack.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The same year he was offered a contract to <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a>'s <a href="/wiki/So_So_Def" class="mw-redirect" title="So So Def">So So Def</a> label. The deal quickly went sour which caused Joell to start beef with Jermaine. Joell has since collaborated with <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kool_G._Rap" class="mw-redirect" title="Kool G. Rap">Kool G. Rap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Joell released his street album called <i><a href="/wiki/The_Brick:_Bodega_Chronicles" title="The Brick: Bodega Chronicles">The Brick: Bodega Chronicles</a></i> April 24, 2007, on <a href="/wiki/E1_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="E1 Entertainment">Koch Records</a>. The album was recorded while Joell was trying to get signed and going through some tough times personally.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Although he was signed to Aftermath Entertainment, Dr. Dre allowed him to release the street album on Koch Records.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <i>The Brick</i> features production by Showbiz, <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Alchemist (producer)">The Alchemist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Domingo_(producer)" title="Domingo (producer)">Domingo</a>, Ho Chi from Killahertz Productions, Lil' Fame of <a href="/wiki/M.O.P." title="M.O.P.">M.O.P.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Novel_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Novel (rapper)">Novel</a>, and Moss, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx1_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Guest spots include <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Noyd" title="Big Noyd">Big Noyd</a>, <a href="/wiki/M.O.P" class="mw-redirect" title="M.O.P">M.O.P</a>, <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Immortal_Technique" title="Immortal Technique">Immortal Technique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grafh" title="Grafh">Grafh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ras_Kass" title="Ras Kass">Ras Kass</a>, Stimuli, and Novel.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx1_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ortiz is one quarter of the <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> Slaughterhouse who released their highly anticipated <i><a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse_EP" class="mw-redirect" title="Slaughterhouse EP">Slaughterhouse EP</a></i> on February 8, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The group released their second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Welcome_to:_Our_House" title="Welcome to: Our House">Welcome to: Our House</a></i>, on August 28, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joell_Ortiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was on Kool G Rap's 2002 album "The Giancana Story" on the song "It's Nothing"
</p>
<h3><span id="2007.E2.80.932010:_The_Brick:_Bodega_Chronicles_and_joining_Slaughterhouse"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007–2010:_The_Brick:_Bodega_Chronicles_and_joining_Slaughterhouse">2007–2010: <i>The Brick: Bodega Chronicles</i> and joining Slaughterhouse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joell_Ortiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007–2010: The Brick: Bodega Chronicles and joining Slaughterhouse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Though signed to Aftermath, Ortiz released an album titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Brick:_Bodega_Chronicles" title="The Brick: Bodega Chronicles">The Brick: Bodega Chronicles</a></i> April 24, 2007 on <a href="/wiki/Koch_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Koch Records">Koch Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <i>The Brick</i> features production by <a href="/wiki/Showbiz_and_A.G." title="Showbiz and A.G.">Showbiz</a>, Street Radio, and <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Alchemist (producer)">The Alchemist</a>, among others. Guest artists include rappers <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Noyd" title="Big Noyd">Big Noyd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Immortal_Technique" title="Immortal Technique">Immortal Technique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grafh" title="Grafh">Grafh</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ras_Kass" title="Ras Kass">Ras Kass</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Ortiz parted ways with <a href="/wiki/Aftermath_Entertainment" title="Aftermath Entertainment">Aftermath Entertainment</a> on April 15, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>2009 was a busy year for Ortiz, as he released a number of freestyles and remixes leading up to the <i>Road Kill</i> mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Among these was "Stressful" a song that remixed <a href="/wiki/Drake_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (entertainer)">Drake's</a> "Successful."<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a> reached out to <a href="/wiki/Crooked_I" title="Crooked I">Crooked I</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>, Joell Ortiz, and Nino Bless for a track titled "Slaughterhouse" on his digital release, <i><a href="/wiki/Halfway_House_(album)" title="Halfway House (album)">Halfway House</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag.com_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Based on the reception of the track, they decided to form a super-group, minus Nino Bless, and named it after the first song they made together.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag.com_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> They released numerous songs throughout early 2009, building a buzz for their <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse_(Slaughterhouse_album)" title="Slaughterhouse (Slaughterhouse album)">self-titled album</a> which was released through <a href="/wiki/E1_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="E1 Entertainment">E1</a> on August 11, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The album features production from <a href="/wiki/Alchemist" class="mw-redirect" title="Alchemist">Alchemist</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khalil" title="DJ Khalil">DJ Khalil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mr._Porter" class="mw-redirect" title="Mr. Porter">Mr. Porter</a>, plus guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Pharoahe_Monch" title="Pharoahe Monch">Pharoahe Monch</a>, K-Young, and The New Royales.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In January 2011, the group signed to <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a> and left <a href="/wiki/E1_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="E1 Entertainment">E1 Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932014:_Free_Agent_and_House_Slippers"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2014:_Free_Agent_and_House_Slippers">2010–2014: <i>Free Agent</i> and <i>House Slippers</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joell_Ortiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010–2014: Free Agent and House Slippers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ortiz started to have problems with <a href="/wiki/E1_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="E1 Entertainment">E1 Entertainment</a> and after almost a year, Ortiz left the label on November 5, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In August 2010, Ortiz was in talks, about signing a deal with <a href="/wiki/Steve_Rifkind" title="Steve Rifkind">Steve Rifkind</a>'s label <a href="/wiki/SRC_Records" title="SRC Records">SRC Records</a>. However the deal was never finished.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In an October 31, 2010, interview on Conspiracy Worldwide Radio, Ortiz discussed his relationship with <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> and the flood of record labels that have flocked to sign him after his <i>Free Agent</i> album was released. He also spoke of <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s excitement at working with him.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse_(group)" title="Slaughterhouse (group)">Slaughterhouse</a> 2012 tour, the group stopped in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_Nevada" class="mw-redirect" title="Las Vegas, Nevada">Las Vegas, Nevada</a> at Bootleg Kev's radio show, where they talked about their album <i><a href="/wiki/Welcome_to:_Our_House" title="Welcome to: Our House">Welcome to: Our House</a></i>, and their work with <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>. It was revealed that Ortiz signed with <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a> as a solo act.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, Joell as part of the group Slaughterhouse released their second album <i>Welcome To Our House</i> on August 28, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>On September 16, 2014, Ortiz released his third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/House_Slippers" title="House Slippers">House Slippers</a></i>. The album features <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from, among others,  <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crooked_I" title="Crooked I">Crooked I</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Maino_(rapper)" title="Maino (rapper)">Maino</a>. The album was supported by the singles "House Slippers" and "Music Saved My Life".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2019, before the release of his <i>Monday</i> album, Ortiz was featured in the song "Revenge" (along with fellow <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse_(group)" title="Slaughterhouse (group)">Slaughterhouse</a> member <a href="/wiki/Crooked_I" title="Crooked I">Crooked I</a>) on Cryptik Soul’s album <i>Killer's Blood</i>. Among the other artists on whose songs he has appeared as a featured guest are <a href="/wiki/Brother_Ali" title="Brother Ali">Brother Ali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mega_Ran" class="mw-redirect" title="Mega Ran">Mega Ran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mr._Capone-E" title="Mr. Capone-E">Mr. Capone-E</a>, Playboy Tre and Chan Hays.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joell_Ortiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the release of his 2011 song "<a href="/wiki/Big_Pun" title="Big Pun">Big Pun</a> Back", Ortiz received criticism from Liza Rios (Pun's widow), along with former <a href="/wiki/Terror_Squad_(group)" title="Terror Squad (group)">Terror Squad</a> members <a href="/wiki/Cuban_Link" title="Cuban Link">Cuban Link</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Sunshine" title="Tony Sunshine">Tony Sunshine</a>, claiming that the song was "disrespectful" to the late rapper's memory. Ortiz claims the song was meant to be a tribute, and later, rapper and friend of Big Pun <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a> spoke up in his defense.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
