<p><b>Michael Gene Dionne</b> (born September 4, 1985), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Eyenine</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> recording artist from <a href="/wiki/Dover,_New_Hampshire" title="Dover, New Hampshire">Dover, New Hampshire</a>.
</p><p>Eyenine began writing and recording music at the age of 16, being heavily inspired by acts including <a href="/wiki/Eyedea" title="Eyedea">Eyedea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sage_Francis" title="Sage Francis">Sage Francis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adeem_(hip_hop_artist)" class="mw-redirect" title="Adeem (hip hop artist)">Adeem</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere_(music_group)" title="Atmosphere (music group)">Atmosphere</a>. In his twenties, Eyenine began touring the country with such acts as PT Burnem, Paulie Think, <a href="/wiki/Ecid" title="Ecid">Ecid</a>, Prhym8 and Farout. He helped found an upstart recording label called Flyrock Records which released multiple albums from various New England artists including The If In Life, Rio Bravo, The Nasty and others. After years of freestyling at the weekly Rap Night in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Maine" title="Portland, Maine">Portland, Maine</a> he entered and won two separate Ruckus Cup Classic battle tournaments.<sup id="cite_ref-Ruckus1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ruckus1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ruckus2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ruckus2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He also went on to win two separate Church of Providence battle tournaments hosted by <a href="/wiki/B._Dolan" title="B. Dolan">B. Dolan</a> and refereed by <a href="/wiki/Sage_Francis" title="Sage Francis">Sage Francis</a><sup id="cite_ref-ChurchofProv_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ChurchofProv-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and later came out of battle rap retirement to win the first ever Overcast battle championship in Cincinnati, OH, the birthplace of the Scribble Jam, in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Eyenine is currently under Real Hood Music LLC, a brand of Wu Tang Management and has shared stages with artists such as <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a> and <a href="/wiki/GZA" title="GZA">GZA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-RealHood_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RealHood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eyenine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eyenine began his career in New Hampshire doing local shows around the area. He was one of the founding members of the label Flyrock Records, which released multiple albums and hosted shows in the New England area. Eyenine and Flyrock Records were featured in an episode of <a href="/wiki/Chronicle_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chronicle (U.S. TV series)"><i>Chronicle</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Chronicle_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chronicle-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He was able to work with his biggest influence, <a href="/wiki/Eyedea" title="Eyedea">Eyedea</a> before his untimely passing on his album <i>Afraid to Dream</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Eyedea1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eyedea1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After two full length releases, he began touring the nation and building up a fanbase which eventually caught the attention of the legendary <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Wu_Tang_Clan" class="mw-redirect" title="Wu Tang Clan">Wu Tang Clan</a>. RZA invited Eyenine to join him on the Major Motion Picture Soundtrack tour for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Man_with_the_Iron_Fists_(soundtrack)" title="The Man with the Iron Fists (soundtrack)">The Man with the Iron Fists</a></i> across the country from <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IronFists1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IronFists1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Since then, Eyenine released his third studio album, <i>Dissembler</i> in 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-Dissembler_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dissembler-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> toured with <a href="/wiki/GZA" title="GZA">GZA</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Eyenine_performing_with_GZA_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eyenine_performing_with_GZA-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and has done multiple shows with <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a>, <a href="/wiki/U-God" title="U-God">U-God</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Killah_Priest" title="Killah Priest">Killah Priest</a> and many others.
</p><p>In May 2019, after a five year hiatus, Eyenine teamed up with The Lonely Ghosts to release a full length titled <i>A Reason for Departure</i> which features a marked difference in sound and style from his earlier solo ventures. Eyenine is in the process of recording his fourth solo studio album, titled <i>A Little Above Low Key</i>, being produced by El Shupacabra and features such artists as <a href="/wiki/Ceschi" title="Ceschi">Ceschi</a>, Witness, <a href="/wiki/Adeem_(hip_hop_artist)" class="mw-redirect" title="Adeem (hip hop artist)">Adeem</a> and more. The album is set to be released, after major delays due to COVID-19, in the first quarter of 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-Eyenine_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eyenine-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The date of the event predicted near this tag has passed. (March 2022)">needs update</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Touring">Touring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eyenine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Touring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eyenine has done many tours over the course of his career, ranging from self funded DIY tours to Major Motion Picture Soundtrack tours such as “The Man With the Iron Fists” alongside <a href="/wiki/Rza" class="mw-redirect" title="Rza">Rza</a>, <a href="/wiki/U-God" title="U-God">U-God</a>, <a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(rapper)" title="Supernatural (rapper)">Supernatural</a> and more. He has performed at <a href="/wiki/SXSW" class="mw-redirect" title="SXSW">SXSW</a> multiple years and continues to reach new places each tour. Recently, he has toured the country with fellow acts <a href="/wiki/Ecid" title="Ecid">ECID</a><sup id="cite_ref-EcidTour_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EcidTour-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and Farout multiple times and plans to continue the trend into 2019 in support of his fourth studio album, <i>A Little Above Low Key</i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eyenine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eyenine is an avid fan of anime, video games, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu and playing music of various genres in multiple collaboration projects and bands. He also hosts multiple events around New Hampshire, including various shows of multiple genres and also open mic comedy events.
