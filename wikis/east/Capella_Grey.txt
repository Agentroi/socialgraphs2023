<p><b>Curtis Anthony Jackson II</b> (born August 2, 1995), known professionally as <b>Capella Grey</b>, is an American singer, songwriter, rapper and record producer from the <a href="/wiki/Bronx,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronx, New York">Bronx, New York</a>. He is the founder of Allepac the Family and signed a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol</a>. He released two mixtapes in 2020 and received widespread recognition in 2021 with his breakout single "<a href="/wiki/Gyalis" title="Gyalis">Gyalis</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capella_Grey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Curtis Jackson II was born in <a href="/wiki/New_Rochelle,_New_York" title="New Rochelle, New York">New Rochelle, New York</a>. He grew up in a Jamaican family and started playing instruments at a young age, learning how to play music from church.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was raised in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a> where he went to <a href="/wiki/Harry_S._Truman_High_School_(Bronx)" title="Harry S. Truman High School (Bronx)">Harry S. Truman High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He developed his musical career in <a href="/wiki/Mount_Vernon,_New_York" title="Mount Vernon, New York">Mount Vernon, New York</a> after graduating high school.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capella_Grey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932020:_Career_beginnings_and_early_mixtapes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2020:_Career_beginnings_and_early_mixtapes">2016–2020: Career beginnings and early mixtapes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capella_Grey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2016–2020: Career beginnings and early mixtapes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Capella Grey started his musical career writing and producing for other artists, including "Love You Better" by King Combs featuring <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>. He signed an R&amp;B girl group called Rèign to Allepac Records, and wrote and produced their debut album released in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On January 8, 2020, he released his debut mixtape <i>Yea Nah I'm Out</i>, followed by his second mixtape <i>The QuaranTape Vibe 1.</i><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h3><span id="2021.E2.80.93present:_Vibe_Responsibly_Vol._1"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2021–present:_Vibe_Responsibly_Vol._1">2021–present: <i>Vibe Responsibly Vol. 1</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capella_Grey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2021–present: Vibe Responsibly Vol. 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 4, 2021, he released the single "<a href="/wiki/Gyalis" title="Gyalis">Gyalis</a>", which samples Juvenile's "<a href="/wiki/Back_That_Azz_Up" class="mw-redirect" title="Back That Azz Up">Back That Azz Up</a>". The song went viral in New York and on social media platforms, spawning unofficial remixes from <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kranium" title="Kranium">Kranium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tory_Lanez" title="Tory Lanez">Tory Lanez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angel_(British_musician)" title="Angel (British musician)">Angel</a> and <a href="/wiki/WSTRN" title="WSTRN">Haile</a>, among others. The song reached number 10 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> chart, and was called the "song of the summer" by multiple publications.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The official remix was released on October 1, 2021, featuring <a href="/wiki/Popcaan" title="Popcaan">Popcaan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dancehallmag_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dancehallmag-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He signed with <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol</a> in 2021, saying they are "not trying to change me as an artist to meet the standards or expectations of music now, which is why I went with them."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>On December 17, 2021, Capella Grey released the single "Talk Nice".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On March 18, 2022, he released the single "Confujawn" with <a href="/wiki/Nija_Charles" title="Nija Charles">Nija</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On May 6, he released the music video for "Sas Crise" featuring Alayzha Sky and Ghost, and directed by Mikey Scho.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On June 10, Capella Grey released the single "OT" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>He appeared on Chris Brown's album <i><a href="/wiki/Breezy_(album)" title="Breezy (album)">Breezy</a></i>, on the song "Till The Wheels Fall Off" featuring <a href="/wiki/Lil_Durk" title="Lil Durk">Lil Durk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On July 1, he was featured on <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a>'s single "Forever" featuring <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benny_the_Butcher" title="Benny the Butcher">Benny the Butcher</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Capella Grey was the opening act for <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Badass</a> on a 20-date summer concert tour, the 1999-2000 Tour.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On July 22, he also featured on the song "Welcome Back" with Joey Badass and Chris Brown, on the album <i><a href="/wiki/2000_(Joey_Badass_album)" title="2000 (Joey Badass album)">2000</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capella_Grey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Capella Grey described his musical style as "singing at the tempo of a rapper", and cited <a href="/wiki/Kranium" title="Kranium">Kranium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Wilson_(singer)" title="Charlie Wilson (singer)">Charlie Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jodeci" title="Jodeci">Jodeci</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">Dipset</a> as his musical influences.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Break_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Break-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He calls the genre of his music "RNBOP", which was coined by his frequent collaborator BBearded.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Allepac_the_Family">Allepac the Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capella_Grey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Allepac the Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1066479718"><table class="infobox vcard"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above fn org" style="background-color: LightBlue">Allepac the Family</th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Parent company</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Founded</th><td class="infobox-data">2016</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Founder</th><td class="infobox-data agent">Capella Grey</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Status</th><td class="infobox-data">Active</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Distributor(s)</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genre</th><td class="infobox-data category"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1129693374"><div class="hlist"><ul><li><a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a></li><li><a href="/wiki/Dancehall" title="Dancehall">dancehall</a></li><li><a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a></li></ul></div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Location</th><td class="infobox-data label"><a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, U.S.</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Allepac the Family</b> ("Capella" spelled backwards) is an American record label based in New York City founded by Capella Grey.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Break_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Break-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The label signed a joint venture deal with <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol</a>. Capella Grey talked about the deal saying "they are a great team of record breakers and I am excited to work with them."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
