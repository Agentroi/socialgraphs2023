<p><b>Chad Aaron Bromley</b> (born March 8, 1979), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Apathy</b> (formerly <b>The Alien Tongue</b>), is a former rapper and producer from <a href="/wiki/Willimantic,_Connecticut" title="Willimantic, Connecticut">Willimantic, Connecticut</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His first major release was his debut album; <i><a href="/wiki/Eastern_Philosophy_(album)" title="Eastern Philosophy (album)">Eastern Philosophy</a></i> in March 2006 with guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Celph_Titled" title="Celph Titled">Celph Titled</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryu_(Rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ryu (Rapper)">Ryu</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Blue_Raspberry_(singer)" title="Blue Raspberry (singer)">Blue Raspberry</a>. His second album <i><a href="/wiki/Wanna_Snuggle%3F" class="mw-redirect" title="Wanna Snuggle?">Wanna Snuggle?</a></i> was released in 2009 and his third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Honkey_Kong_(Apathy_album)" title="Honkey Kong (Apathy album)">Honkey Kong</a></i> was released in 2011, both to critical acclaim. His fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Casual" title="Connecticut Casual">Connecticut Casual</a></i> was released on June 3, 2014 and reached #41 in the R&amp;B/Hip Hop Albums charts.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Apathy is known for his frequent collaborations with other MCs and groups such as Demigodz, <a href="/wiki/Celph_Titled" title="Celph Titled">Celph Titled</a>, Doe Rakers, <a href="/wiki/Jedi_Mind_Tricks" title="Jedi Mind Tricks">Jedi Mind Tricks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Army_of_the_Pharaohs" title="Army of the Pharaohs">Army of the Pharaohs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Styles_of_Beyond" title="Styles of Beyond">Styles of Beyond</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Get_Busy_Committee" title="Get Busy Committee">Get Busy Committee</a>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apathy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apathy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001 Bromley joined Demigodz, a local rap collective that included <a href="/wiki/Celph_Titled" title="Celph Titled">Celph Titled</a>, <a href="/wiki/7L_%26_Esoteric" title="7L &amp; Esoteric">7L &amp; Esoteric</a>, EL Fudge, <a href="/wiki/Louis_Logic" title="Louis Logic">Louis Logic</a> and others.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the period 2000 to 2002 he released a number of solo singles that included “Compatible”, “Just Begun”, “School”, and “Ain’t Nuttin’ Nice" released through Bronx Science Records.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Bromley made his album debut when he appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Jedi_Mind_Tricks" title="Jedi Mind Tricks">Jedi Mind Tricks</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Psycho-Social,_Chemical,_Biological_%26_Electro-Magnetic_Manipulation_of_Human_Consciousness" title="The Psycho-Social, Chemical, Biological &amp; Electro-Magnetic Manipulation of Human Consciousness">The Psycho Social</a></i> in 1997. He featured on the tracks "The Three Immortals", "Omnicron" and "The Apostle's Creed".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> By mid-1997, the Demigodz collective fractured and Apathy together with producer Open Mic self-released the <i>Sureshot Affair</i> EP in 1998<i>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He continued to collaborate under the Demigodz moniker and released <i>The Godz Must Be Crazy</i> EP in 2002 on Ill Boogie Records.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The two-disc <i>It's the Bootleg, Muthafuckas! Vol. 1</i> was released in 2003 and collected the earlier Demigodz material,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> while in 2004 <i>Where's Your Album?!!</i> brought together some of the previously released solo releases.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Both appeared on the Demigodz label. After this second EP, Bromley signed on to <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>, but left the label in 2009 due to creative disagreements.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.932011"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–2011">2006–2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apathy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006–2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a solo artist now, Bromley released the album, <i><a href="/wiki/Eastern_Philosophy_(album)" title="Eastern Philosophy (album)">Eastern Philosophy</a></i>, in March 2006 through <a href="/wiki/Babygrande_Records" title="Babygrande Records">Babygrande Records</a>.
</p><p>Apathy also became a member of <a href="/wiki/Army_of_the_Pharaohs" title="Army of the Pharaohs">Army of the Pharaohs</a>, which was originally formed by <a href="/wiki/Vinnie_Paz" title="Vinnie Paz">Vinnie Paz</a> alongside other underground rap artists. He was featured on their debut album; <a href="/wiki/The_Torture_Papers_(album)" title="The Torture Papers (album)">The Torture Papers</a> released in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In January 2007, Apathy released another collection of his most recent works, <i>Baptism by Fire</i>.
</p><p>In 2009, <a href="/wiki/Get_Busy_Committee" title="Get Busy Committee">Get Busy Committee</a> was formed in Los Angeles, California, by longtime friends Ryu (of <a href="/wiki/Styles_of_Beyond" title="Styles of Beyond">Styles of Beyond</a>), Apathy and Scoop DeVille. All production was done by Scoop DeVille and Apathy, while vocals are given by everyone in the group. Their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Uzi_Does_It" title="Uzi Does It">Uzi Does It</a></i>, was released for pre-sale download on October 27, 2009 at www.getbusycommittee.com, and available on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> and other outlets on November 10.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> As of 2012, Get Busy Committee is on hiatus, or "dead" as they refer to it; the group members are focused on their solo careers.
</p><p>He released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Wanna_Snuggle%3F" class="mw-redirect" title="Wanna Snuggle?">Wanna Snuggle?</a></i> in 2009. It was released on Demigodz Records. The album features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Phonte" title="Phonte">Phonte</a> of <a href="/wiki/Little_Brother_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Little Brother (band)">Little Brother</a>, <a href="/wiki/B-Real" title="B-Real">B-Real</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Celph_Titled" title="Celph Titled">Celph Titled</a>, and other members of the Demigodz.
</p><p>His third solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Honkey_Kong_(Apathy_album)" title="Honkey Kong (Apathy album)">Honkey Kong</a></i> was released on August 23, 2011 on Dirty Version Records, along with the <i>Primate Mindstate</i> EP as a bonus disk.  The album features guest contributions by <a href="/wiki/Celph_Titled" title="Celph Titled">Celph Titled</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vinnie_Paz" title="Vinnie Paz">Vinnie Paz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slaine_(rapper)" title="Slaine (rapper)">Slaine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ill_Bill" title="Ill Bill">Ill Bill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a>, Blacastan, <a href="/wiki/Mad_Lion" title="Mad Lion">Mad Lion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steele_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Steele (rapper)">General Steele</a>, <a href="/wiki/7L_%26_Esoteric" title="7L &amp; Esoteric">Esoteric</a>, Crypt the Warchild, Motive, Planetary, and <a href="/wiki/Reef_the_Lost_Cauze" title="Reef the Lost Cauze">Reef the Lost Cauze</a>. Producers for <i>Honkey Kong</i> such as <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vanderslice" title="Vanderslice">Vanderslice</a> contribute to a limited number of beats on this album, though the majority of the production is handled by Apathy himself.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2011.E2.80.932015"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–2015">2011–2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apathy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2011–2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During 2011, Apathy and Celph Titled started producing a second duo album, called <i>Will Sing for Vengeance</i>. They also worked on their solo careers.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In November 2011, Apathy uploaded on to YouTube a new song called "Demigodz Is Back", this song featured Apathy, Ryu and Celph Titled and was produced by Apathy.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> This song marked the return of Demigodz.
</p><p>On May 10, 2012, the pair also announced in an interview with Hiphopauthority.com that they were working on a new Demigodz album, titled <i>Killmatic</i>. Apathy stated, "We got a gang of shit in the works, so we always work on multiple, multiple things but we don’t announce things until we’re absolutely positive and definite that they’re coming out.  So, the Demigodz album should be coming in the early-2013."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Celph said that the album would contain all six members, "Which is: Apathy, Celph Titled, Ryu, Esoteric, Motive, and Blacastan."
</p><p>In 2012, Apathy released the double CD <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_the_Bootleg,_Muthafuckas!_Volume_3:_Fire_Walk_With_Me" class="mw-redirect" title="It&#39;s the Bootleg, Muthafuckas! Volume 3: Fire Walk With Me">It's the Bootleg, Muthafuckas! Volume 3: Fire Walk With Me</a></i> as the third installment to the <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_the_Bootleg,_Muthafuckas!" title="It&#39;s the Bootleg, Muthafuckas!">It's the Bootleg, Muthafuckas!</a></i> series, as well as "<a href="/wiki/The_Alien_Tongue" class="mw-redirect" title="The Alien Tongue">The Alien Tongue</a>," a collection of his earliest demos and recordings.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>  The bootleg double CD included collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5&#39;9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vinnie_Paz" title="Vinnie Paz">Vinnie Paz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ill_Bill" title="Ill Bill">Ill Bill</a>, and <a href="/wiki/B-Real" title="B-Real">B-Real</a>, among others.
</p><p>On January 14, 2013, Apathy confirmed that <i>Killmatic</i> would be released on March 5. Along with that, he confirmed the official album art.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Four days later, Apathy, through his YouTube account, released the first single for <i>Killmatic</i> titled "Demigodz Is Back".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In July 2013, through his Twitter account, Apathy announced he was working on a new EP with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Fraud" title="Harry Fraud">Harry Fraud</a>, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Casual" title="Connecticut Casual">Connecticut Casual</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Apathy later stated on Facebook that the EP would be available for free download.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> It is due to be released April, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On November 30, 2013, <a href="/wiki/Vinnie_Paz" title="Vinnie Paz">Vinnie Paz</a> revealed that two new Army Of The Pharaohs albums would be released in 2014. The first album; <i><a href="/wiki/In_Death_Reborn" title="In Death Reborn">In Death Reborn</a></i> is slated for a release on 22 April and the second LP is expected to drop in November.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Apathy is confirmed to be on both the upcoming albums, new members including Demigodz member Blacastan and <a href="/wiki/Zilla_(rapper)" title="Zilla (rapper)">Zilla</a> from <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a> are said to be joining the group.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 28, 2014, Apathy released "Martha Moxley (Rest in Peace)" featuring <a href="/wiki/Chris_Webby" title="Chris Webby">Chris Webby</a>, Kappa Gamma, Annoyd and Hayze. The song subtly repeats references to the <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_Martha_Moxley" title="Murder of Martha Moxley">Murder of Martha Moxley</a>, naming Moxley and Michael Skakel, both then 15.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 30, 2015 Apathy released two projects: an EP called <i>Weekend At The Cape</i> and the fourth installment of his popular "It's the Bootleg, Muthafuckas!" compilation series called <i>The Black Lodge</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932021"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2021">2016–2021</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apathy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2016–2021">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Albums for 2016 include <i>Handshakes With Snakes</i> (which was originally a mixtape<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>), <i>The Widow's Son</i> and an album with <a href="/wiki/O.C._(rapper)" title="O.C. (rapper)">O.C.</a> as Perestroika.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> However, on February 17, 2016 he told on his <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> page that it wasn't possible to release all three projects in 2016 and that <i>The Widow's Son</i> was delayed until early 2017. March 2017 Apathy said it would be released by the end of the year<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> On July 11, 2017 he announced the release date for Perestroika for September 22, 2017.
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Widow%27s_Son_(album)" title="The Widow&#39;s Son (album)">The Widow's Son</a></i> was released on March 2, 2018, through Dirty Version records. It features guests appearances from <a href="/wiki/Pharoahe_Monch" title="Pharoahe Monch">Pharoahe Monch</a>, <a href="/wiki/M.O.P." title="M.O.P.">M.O.P.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Celph_Titled" title="Celph Titled">Celph Titled</a>, <a href="/wiki/AG_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="AG (rapper)">A.G.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Locksmith_(rapper)" title="Locksmith (rapper)">Locksmith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryu_(Rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ryu (Rapper)">Ryu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diabolic" title="Diabolic">Diabolic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The album contains production work from Apathy himself as well as from <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pete_Rock" title="Pete Rock">Pete Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buckwild_(music_producer)" title="Buckwild (music producer)">Buckwild</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nottz" title="Nottz">Nottz</a>, Stu Bangas, and Chumzilla.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 26, 2020, Apathy posted his planned releases for 2021 to his Instagram page. The projects stated were "Where The River Meets The Sea", An untitled joint project with producer Stu Bangas, "Dive Medicine 2" (a sequel to Apathy's beat tape "Dive Medicine"), an untitled joint project with producer Vanderslice, and an untitled joint project with producer Evidence. Apathy is currently Chief Growth Officer of Greater Property Group.
</p><p><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
