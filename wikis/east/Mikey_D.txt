<p><b>Michael Anthony Deering</b><sup id="cite_ref-auto_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (born November 25,<sup id="cite_ref-twitter.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twitter.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 1967), known by his stage name <b>Mikey D</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>. He is best known for being a lead member of the groups <b>Mikey D &amp; The L.A. Posse</b> and <a href="/wiki/Main_Source" title="Main Source">Main Source</a>. As a member of Main Source, he replaced <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> who parted ways with the group due to business differences. In 1994, he appeared on Main Source's second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Fuck_What_You_Think" title="Fuck What You Think">Fuck What You Think</a></i>, as the lead MC.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mikey_D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mikey D was born on November 25, 1967, in <a href="/wiki/Laurelton,_Queens" title="Laurelton, Queens">Laurelton, Queens</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, New York. Born to Linda Deering and Victor Fisher.<sup id="cite_ref-instagram_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-instagram-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His parents were separated when he was young and was raised by his grandparents, Jerome and Betty Deering in Laurelton Queens, New York. He attended PS 37 in Queens. He then attended JHS 231 in Queens.
</p><p>He attended <a href="/wiki/Springfield_Gardens_High_School" title="Springfield Gardens High School">Springfield Gardens High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bombhiphop_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bombhiphop-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mikey_D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mikey first started rapping at a young age at his junior high school, after hearing music by the <a href="/wiki/Cold_Crush_Brothers" class="mw-redirect" title="Cold Crush Brothers">Cold Crush Brothers</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Clientele_Brothers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Clientele Brothers (page does not exist)">Clientele Brothers</a>, which inspired him to become an MC. He was the youngest member to join the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Clientele_Brothers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Clientele Brothers (page does not exist)">Clientele Brothers</a>, a group of emcees consisting of Eddie O'Jay and Will Seville<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He would be writing rhymes and freestyling in parks and areas around his neighborhood,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> getting training help by O'Jay and Seville, trying to develop his skills and rhymes flows in his adolescence.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While in high school, he met future emcee/actor <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>. Although, him and Mikey didn't go to the same school together. They went to their own separate schools. James went to <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Jackson_High_School_(Queens)" title="Andrew Jackson High School (Queens)">Andrew Jackson High School</a>. They would come over to each of their own schools they were attending and freestyling rhymes with each other. He was known as J-Ski, but Mikey would suggest a new name for his moniker. He would give him a stage name "Ladies Love", but didn't want it in his stage name. He shortened it to just "LL", put it next to the word "Cool" and added "J", which stood for his first name, James.<sup id="cite_ref-bombhiphop_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bombhiphop-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1985, Mikey and his group called the Symbolic Three, got signed to Reality Records.<sup id="cite_ref-bombhiphop_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bombhiphop-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Around that year, he and his friend Johnnie Quest met inspiring upcoming producer named Paul "<a href="/wiki/Paul_C" title="Paul C">Paul C</a>" McKasty, through Cliente Brothers members Eddie O'Jay and Will Seville.<sup id="cite_ref-:18_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:18-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He formed a group with the late <a href="/wiki/Paul_C" title="Paul C">Paul C</a> and childhood friend DJ Johnnie Quest. According to Deering, he stated that his group originally wanted to be called "Boom Bash", but one of his managers didn't think it was a good idea.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The group was called Mikey D &amp; the L.A. Posse. They were signed to Public Records. They had a single which was released in 1987, song called "My Telephone", along with tracks like "Dawn" and "Bust a Rhyme". He followed up with two other singles "I Get Rough" &amp; "Go For It" in the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1988, Mikey D was the winner of the New Music Seminar Battle for World Supremacy where he battled <a href="/wiki/Melle_Mel" title="Melle Mel">Melle Mel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While recording an album with his group, he learned that <a href="/wiki/Paul_C" title="Paul C">Paul C</a> was murdered in his sleep at his home.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> There were disputes between another group who were also named the <a href="/wiki/L.A._Posse" title="L.A. Posse">L.A. Posse</a>, a group from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sleeping_Bag_Records" title="Sleeping Bag Records">Sleeping Bag Records</a>, a label that Mikey was signed to.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Sleeping Bag Records was forced to drop the group's name after being sued for name duplication and Mikey  took a three-year break from the music business after the death of producer Paul "<a href="/wiki/Paul_C" title="Paul C">Paul C</a>" McKasty.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> While spending time in Miami, Florida, his manager was Mike Beasley at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He had a cousin who own a studio in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan,_New_York_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Manhattan, New York City">Manhattan, New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. He would try to find a label that was gonna give Mikey a record deal but he caught the attention of <a href="/wiki/R%26B" class="mw-redirect" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Redd" title="Jeff Redd">Jeff Redd</a> who heard his freestyling raps.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_9-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He told him there was a group that needed a lead rapper for the group <a href="/wiki/Main_Source" title="Main Source">Main Source</a>. He contacted them and freestyled raps to the group and liked what they heard.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_9-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1993, he become an official member of the critically acclaimed Toronto/Queens-based hip hop group <a href="/wiki/Main_Source" title="Main Source">Main Source</a> after <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a> left to pursue a solo career.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Fuck_What_You_Think" title="Fuck What You Think">Fuck What You Think</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Wild_Pitch_Records" title="Wild Pitch Records">Wild Pitch Records</a> in 1994. They released a single called "What You Need", which did well in <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Singles" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Rap Singles">Hot Rap Singles</a>, only peaking at #48.
</p><p>He is featured on a song called "Pump Ya Fist Like This" on <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a>'s 2008's third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Main_Source_(album)" title="Main Source (album)">Main Source</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2020, Mikey released a distribution company called Pass the Torch, where he is the founder and currently operates his own company.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mikey currently hosts his own show on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> called <i>The Real Mikey D History (His-story)</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> a documentary series released in 2021.
</p>
