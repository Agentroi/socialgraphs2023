<p><b>Jin Au-Yeung</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>&#58; <span lang="zh-Hans">欧阳靖</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>&#58; <span lang="zh-Hant">歐陽靖</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>&#58; <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Ōuyáng Jìng</span></i>; born June 4, 1982), known professionally as <b>MC Jin</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>, songwriter, actor and comedian of Chinese descent.<sup id="cite_ref-ajcombio_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ajcombio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Jin is notable for being the first <a href="/wiki/Asian_Americans" title="Asian Americans">Asian American</a> solo rapper to be signed to a <a href="/wiki/Record_label#Major_labels" title="Record label">major record label</a> in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Jin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jin Au-Yeung was born on June 4, 1982, in Miami, Florida, to <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Hong Kong people">Hong Kong</a> immigrants of <a href="/wiki/Hakka_people" title="Hakka people">Hakka</a> descent.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MCJinRebranded_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCJinRebranded-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He was raised in the general Miami area, where his parents owned a Chinese restaurant and Jin attended <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Middle_School_(North_Miami_Beach,_Florida)" class="mw-redirect" title="John F. Kennedy Middle School (North Miami Beach, Florida)">John F. Kennedy Middle School</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Miami_Beach" class="mw-redirect" title="North Miami Beach">North Miami Beach</a>. Jin went on to attend <a href="/wiki/North_Miami_Beach_Senior_High_School" title="North Miami Beach Senior High School">North Miami Beach Senior High School</a>, from which he graduated in 2000. After graduating, Jin decided to forgo college and begin his rap career.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Shortly afterwards, his parents closed the restaurant and the family moved in 2001 to <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimesnov2004_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimesnov2004-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Jin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2001.E2.80.932006:_106_.26_Park_and_Ruff_Ryders"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2001–2006:_106_&amp;_Park_and_Ruff_Ryders">2001–2006: <i>106 &amp; Park</i> and Ruff Ryders</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Jin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2001–2006: 106 &amp; Park and Ruff Ryders">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While in New York, Jin participated in many <a href="/wiki/Battle_rap" title="Battle rap">rap battles</a> with his peers.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimesnov2004_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimesnov2004-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jin's big break came in 2002 when the <a href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Entertainment Television">BET</a> program <i><a href="/wiki/106_%26_Park" title="106 &amp; Park">106 &amp; Park</a></i> invited local rappers to compete in a segment known as <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_Friday" class="mw-redirect" title="Freestyle Friday">Freestyle Friday</a>. Unlike other competitors, Jin occasionally spoke <a href="/wiki/Cantonese" title="Cantonese">Cantonese</a> in his freestyle verses. After winning seven battles in a row, he was inducted into the Freestyle Friday Hall of Fame.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The night of the ceremony, he announced that he had signed a deal with the <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders_Entertainment" title="Ruff Ryders Entertainment">Ruff Ryders Entertainment</a> label.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His first single under Ruff Ryders was titled "<a href="/wiki/Learn_Chinese_(song)" title="Learn Chinese (song)">Learn Chinese</a>" featuring Haitian rapper <a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a>. It contained a sample from song "<a href="/wiki/Black_Caesar_(album)" title="Black Caesar (album)">Blind Man Can See It</a>", by <a href="/wiki/James_Brown" title="James Brown">James Brown</a> (also sampled by <a href="/wiki/Das_EFX" title="Das EFX">Das EFX</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lord_Finesse" title="Lord Finesse">Lord Finesse</a>). The second single for the album was originally supposed to be "I Got a Love", featuring and produced by <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>. The album was originally scheduled to be released in the summer of 2003 but was delayed for over a year by the label. In October 2004, Jin released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rest_Is_History" title="The Rest Is History">The Rest Is History</a></i>, which reached number 54 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> Top 200 albums chart.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The two singles, "Learn Chinese" and "Senorita", were not major mainstream successes and the album only sold 19,000 units in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-MCJinRebranded_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCJinRebranded-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Jin left the label in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 23, 2005, Jin and rapper <a href="/wiki/Serius_Jones" title="Serius Jones">Serius Jones</a> engaged in a rap battle. The battle was featured on Fight Klub DVD.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 18, 2005, Jin revealed that he would be putting his rap career on hold in order to explore other options. To make this clear, he recorded a song titled "I Quit", produced by the Golden Child. The announcement was widely misunderstood to have marked the end of Jin's rap career. However, he later re-emerged under a different alias, The Emcee, and <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyled</a> over such songs as Jay-Z's "Dear Summer." He released a single called "<a href="/wiki/Top_5_(Dead_or_Alive)" class="mw-redirect" title="Top 5 (Dead or Alive)">Top 5 (Dead or Alive)</a>" in which he explored the history of hip-hop's greatest artists, using lyrics. The legendary DJ <a href="/wiki/Kool_Herc" class="mw-redirect" title="Kool Herc">Kool Herc</a>, who is credited as the founder of hip-hop, appears in Jin's music video. With independent label CraftyPlugz/Draft Records, Jin released his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Emcee%27s_Properganda" title="The Emcee&#39;s Properganda">The Emcee's Properganda</a></i>, on October 25, 2005.
</p><p>Jin collaborated in 2005 with Queens-born rapper <a href="/wiki/Creature_(musician)" title="Creature (musician)">Creature</a> on his song "Never Say Die," which also featured <a href="/wiki/Slug_(rapper)" title="Slug (rapper)">Slug</a> and <a href="/wiki/Busdriver" title="Busdriver">Busdriver</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jin was also featured on <a href="/wiki/Taiwanese_people" title="Taiwanese people">Taiwanese</a> pop artist <a href="/wiki/Leehom_Wang" class="mw-redirect" title="Leehom Wang">Leehom Wang</a>'s 2005 album <i><a href="/wiki/Heroes_of_Earth" title="Heroes of Earth">Heroes of Earth</a></i>. Together, Jin and Leehom performed their "Heroes of the Earth" collaboration live in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> on February 16, 2006, at an event arranged by China-resident American <a href="/wiki/A%26R" class="mw-redirect" title="A&amp;R">A&amp;R</a> exec <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Ballen" title="Andrew Ballen">Andrew Ballen</a>. Ballen was also the first promoter to bring Jin to mainland China in 2003 for his "The Rest is History" tour.<sup id="cite_ref-mcjin_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mcjin-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.932007:_Independent_label"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–2007:_Independent_label">2006–2007: Independent label</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Jin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006–2007: Independent label">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jin released two albums in 2006. The first, <i>100 Grand Jin</i>, was a mixtape that was released on August 29, 2006. The single released from the album is "FYI", for which the rapper shot and released a music video. The second album is Jin's third LP, <i><a href="/wiki/I_Promise_(album)" title="I Promise (album)">I Promise</a></i>.
</p><p>On his MySpace, Jin mentioned that he was working on another English album named "Birthdays, Funerals and Things in Between". Jin premiered the song, "Open Letter to Obama," on April 24, 2007, which made him become 1st on then-Presidential nominee <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>'s Top 8 list on <i>MySpace</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 16, 2007, Jin made a tribute song to the victims of the <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Tech_massacre" class="mw-redirect" title="Virginia Tech massacre">Virginia Tech massacre</a> called "Rain, Rain Go Away".<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Later that year, Jin decided to make his November 2006 online album, <i>I Promise</i> available in retail stores; it was given an October 23, 2007, release.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a>/<a href="/wiki/Xbox" title="Xbox">Xbox</a> video game <i><a href="/wiki/NBA_Ballers:_Phenom" title="NBA Ballers: Phenom">NBA Ballers: Phenom</a></i> featured MC Jin as one of the <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> characters along with <a href="/wiki/Luis_Da_Silva_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Luis Da Silva Jr.">Trikz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philip_Champion" title="Philip Champion">Hot Sauce</a>.
</p><p>He released his first all-<a href="/wiki/Cantonese" title="Cantonese">Cantonese</a> language album, <i><a href="/wiki/ABC_(Jin_album)" title="ABC (Jin album)">ABC</a></i>, in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-MCJinRebranded_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCJinRebranded-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.932011:_Success_in_Hong_Kong_and_newfound_Christian_faith"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–2011:_Success_in_Hong_Kong_and_newfound_Christian_faith">2008–2011: Success in Hong Kong and newfound Christian faith</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Jin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2008–2011: Success in Hong Kong and newfound Christian faith">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2008, <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Hong_Kong" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Music Hong Kong">Universal Music Hong Kong</a> flew Jin out to <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> to promote the re-release of his Cantonese album <i>ABC.</i> In an interview, Jin commented, "I think with various artists, <a href="/wiki/Cantonese" title="Cantonese">Cantonese</a> <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip-hop</a> was starting to become more and more widespread."<sup id="cite_ref-:3_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i>ABC</i> debuted at #1 and achieved gold status in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>. He frequently starred in commercials there and made numerous appearances on <a href="/wiki/Television_Broadcasts_Limited" class="mw-redirect" title="Television Broadcasts Limited">TVB</a> like <i>Big Boys Club</i>. He is also seen as an important part of recent Asian hip hop trends<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and was once deemed "the changing face of <a href="/wiki/Cantopop" title="Cantopop">Cantopop</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-:3_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jin did a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysian</a> rapper Point Blanc in a track titled "One Day" (2008).<sup id="cite_ref-sunm_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunm-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He also put the song "Welcome to the Light Club" on his Myspace page.<sup id="cite_ref-rzilla_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rzilla-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Jin is a Christian, stating in his song "Welcome to the Light Club" he was baptized in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-allhc_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allhc-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He has featured in <a href="/wiki/Far_East_Movement" title="Far East Movement">Far East Movement</a>'s "Millionaire". He has done a track over a <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>-produced joint titled "World Premier". He also collaborated with producer Trendsetter (aka Mark Holiday).<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has recently thrown out a Hip-Hop Census in honor of the 2010 Census and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_New_Year" title="Chinese New Year">Chinese New Year</a>, 2010. As said by Jin, this mixtape is open to anybody and will be mixed from the general population along with Jin. He released a mixtape entitled "Say Something", on May 15, 2010. Jin released an album with his friend Hanjin Tan in 2010. On July 10, 2010, Jin collaborated with singer Hanjin Tan (<span lang="zh">陳奐仁</span>) to release another Cantonese album <span title="Chinese-language text"><span lang="zh-Hant-HK">買一送一</span></span> (Buy one get one free).<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Jin also released a music video for his English single "Angels".
</p><p>During his time in Hong Kong, Jin has appeared in films such as <a href="/wiki/Gallants_(film)" title="Gallants (film)"><i>Gallants</i></a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Bruce_Lee,_My_Brother" title="Bruce Lee, My Brother">Bruce Lee, My Brother</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-MCJinRebranded_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCJinRebranded-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December, 2010, Jin released a Christmas music video entitled "Rap Now, 2010" with <a href="/wiki/Chief_Executive_of_Hong_Kong" title="Chief Executive of Hong Kong">Hong Kong Chief Executive</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donald_Tsang" title="Donald Tsang">Donald Tsang</a>. The video, with lyrics penned by Jin, featured a number of lines ending with "<a href="/wiki/Act_Now_(slogan)" class="mw-redirect" title="Act Now (slogan)">Act Now!</a>" (<span title="Chinese-language text"><span lang="zh-Hant-HK">起錨</span></span>), echoing a Hong Kong government slogan. It garnered in excess of nine million views on the CE office's YouTube channel.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:5_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a> report described the video as an "official eyesore" and "a political message thinly veiled as a Christmas card," and further criticized Jin's rapping as resembling "awkward nursery rhymes," in contrast to his earlier performances.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> However, local newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Standard_(Hong_Kong)" title="The Standard (Hong Kong)">The Standard</a></i>, in response to critics, stated in an editorial that "Tsang, like the millions who post videos of themselves or their loved ones, only wants to have fun."<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 7, 2011, Jin released a music video for a song from his upcoming English album entitled "Charlie Sheen," collaborating with artists <a href="/wiki/Dumbfoundead" title="Dumbfoundead">Dumbfoundead</a> and <a href="/wiki/Traphik" class="mw-redirect" title="Traphik">Traphik</a>. Jin released an EP titled <i>Sincerely Yours</i>, a Christian-oriented and self-improvement-oriented EP featuring positive messages about life and self-reflective insights into his own life. After the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a>, Jin participated in the <i><a href="/wiki/Artistes_311_Love_Beyond_Borders" title="Artistes 311 Love Beyond Borders">Artistes 311 Love Beyond Borders</a></i> celebrity charity concert on April 1, 2011, to help raise funds for Japan's disaster recovery effort.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The 3-hour event raised over HK$26 million (USD$3.3 million).<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 16, 2011, Jin released the track "AIYA" featuring label-mate, Toestah. On August 8, 2011, Jin released his second Cantonese album, <span title="Yue Chinese-language text"><span lang="yue">回香靖</span></span> (Homecoming), in Hong Kong.<sup id="cite_ref-MCJinRebranded_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCJinRebranded-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932017:_Return_to_the_United_States"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2017:_Return_to_the_United_States">2012–2017: Return to the United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Jin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2012–2017: Return to the United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, Jin announced that he was moving back to the United States to raise his son with his wife in New York.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2012, Jin released a re-vamped version of his EP, <i>Sincerely Yours 2.0</i>, featuring some of the same lyrics, but many others changed, slightly altering the overall feel of the record. In August 2012, Jin released his English album, "Crazy Love Ridiculous Faith", for free download, an album in which he positively addresses both Christians and non-Christians. In December 2012, Jin released the <a href="/wiki/Brand_New_Me_(EP)" title="Brand New Me (EP)"><i>Brand New Me</i></a> EP.
</p><p>In late 2013, Jin collaborated with independent label, The Great Company. The first track released on the label was called "Hypocrite (The Gold Chapter)." On December 21, 2013, Jin released the first EP under the label called "Hypocrite."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On October 21, 2014, the label released Jin's first album under their label: <i>XIV:LIX</i>, which is "14:59" in <a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">Roman numerals</a> and a reference to the concept of <a href="/wiki/15_minutes_of_fame" title="15 minutes of fame">15 minutes of fame</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The album features Teesa, Hollis, Stacie Bollman, <a href="/wiki/Tim_Be_Told" title="Tim Be Told">Tim Be Told</a>, Bére, and Storm.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The album was not a commercial success.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jin began performing <a href="/wiki/Stand-up_comedy" title="Stand-up comedy">stand-up comedy</a> in New York City in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2017.E2.80.93present:_Success_in_mainland_China"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–present:_Success_in_mainland_China">2017–present: Success in mainland China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Jin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2017–present: Success in mainland China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2017, he competed in the Chinese rap competition show, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rap_of_China" title="The Rap of China">The Rap of China</a></i>, in a masked attire, under the alias of "HipHopMan."<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jin has since released several songs in Mandarin, including "Zero", a promotional song for the 2017 movie <a href="/wiki/The_Foreigner_(2017_film)" title="The Foreigner (2017 film)"><i>The Foreigner</i></a> starring Hong Kong actor <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Chan" title="Jackie Chan">Jackie Chan</a>, who considers Jin to be his friend.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2021, he joined the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Call_Me_by_Fire" title="Call Me by Fire">Call Me by Fire</a></i> as a contestant.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> He was eliminated in episode 5, only to return later in episode 6, following fellow contestant <a href="/wiki/Henry_Huo" title="Henry Huo">Henry Huo Zun</a>'s withdrawal from the show in light of recent controversies.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Jin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jin married his wife Carol on February 12, 2011, in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The couple met at a concert at where Jin was performing.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> They have two sons: Chance (born 2012)<sup id="cite_ref-:3_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and Justus (born 2019).<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jin became a <a href="/wiki/Born_again" title="Born again">born again Christian</a> in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> He was converted by Chinese American pastor <a href="/wiki/Jaeson_Ma" title="Jaeson Ma">Jaeson Ma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MCJinRebranded_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCJinRebranded-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He has since released several <a href="/wiki/Gospel_music" title="Gospel music">gospel</a> themed projects expressing his faith.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/2008_United_States_presidential_election" title="2008 United States presidential election">2008 United States presidential election</a>, Jin expressed his support for <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>. Proceeds from his song "Open Letter to Obama" were donated to Obama's campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In 2019, he announced on Twitter that he was officially endorsing presidential candidate <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Yang" title="Andrew Yang">Andrew Yang</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2020_Democratic_Party_presidential_primaries" title="2020 Democratic Party presidential primaries">2020 Democratic Party presidential primaries</a>. He has released two songs supporting Yang: "Drew Yang Gang, That's Who I Hooked Up Wit'" and "#8MileYang".<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Jin also toured with Andrew Yang's campaign events.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In April 2021, he released “Yang for New York,” a song and video in support of Andrew Yang’s run for mayor of New York City. The three-minute video features such lyrics as “Everyone — white, brown, yellow and black / It’s time to come together, what’s ironic is that / NY forward will bring New York Back.”<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Catchphrases_and_fan_culture">Catchphrases and fan culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MC_Jin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Catchphrases and fan culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jin's catchphrase is "Aiya!"<sup id="cite_ref-:3_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (<span title="Yue Chinese-language text"><span lang="yue">哎吔</span></span>), a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a> interjection roughly equivalent to "Oh my gosh!"<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. Fans pictured with the logo were able to appear in the music video for his song "Aiya!" (featuring Toestah).<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Jin also refers to his fans as his "AIYAfambam".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>After his success and popularity on the Rap of China, Jin gained widespread respect and popularity throughout China, and also with the Chinese people worldwide. A China fanbase is secure with an official account on Weibo "MCJin欧阳靖全国粉丝后援会" <sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> on August 12, 2017. Jin then officially named his Chinese fans "不帥團隊"<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> (Team Bu-shuai), a name which he came up with due to the focus on looks-oriented artists. Bu-Shuai means 'not handsome' in Mandarin, and Jin joked with his fans that although he is not handsome as compared to many artists, he is proud to be so as he is secure in himself as an 'un-handsome'. The official slogan for Team Bu-shuai is "生活愉快、越來不帥", which means to live joyfully and become more and more 'un-handsome'. A series of official Team Bu=shuai merchandise such as tees, hoodies, windbreakers, and caps donning the slogan and team name (designed by Jin) is also available on Taobao.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Jin also released the song "不帥"<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> with an official music video made by him and Team Bu-shuai. The song talks about the special relationship between Jin and his beloved Team Bu-Shuai, promoting the slogan "生活愉快、越來不帥". The fanbase 'Team Bu-shuai' has an official account on Weibo,<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> with over 55,000 followers. Jin actively interacts with its members online, and is known to sometimes arrange to meet them in different cities for movies and coffees offline.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Members of Jin's fan club on Twitter are known as Emojins.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> He referenced them in his 2018 song 'Debut': "Shout out to the emojins, and the emojins only."<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
