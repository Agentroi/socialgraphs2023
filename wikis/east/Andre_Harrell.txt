<p><b>Andre O’Neal Harrell</b> (September 26, 1960&#160;– May 7, 2020) was an American music executive and multimedia <a href="/wiki/Executive_producer" title="Executive producer">producer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 1986, recently a rapper, he formed <a href="/wiki/Uptown_Records" title="Uptown Records">Uptown Records</a>, soon a leader in R&amp;B, rap, and their fusion, "<a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_soul" title="Hip hop soul">hip hop soul</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/New_jack_swing" title="New jack swing">new jack swing</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He gave <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Sean "Diddy" Combs</a> his 1990 start in the industry,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and later was <a href="/wiki/Motown_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Motown Records">Motown Records</a>' CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andre_Harrell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harrell was born in New York City borough the <a href="/wiki/Bronx" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronx">Bronx</a> on September 26, 1960.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ABC_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His father, Bernie, worked at a <a href="/wiki/Greengrocer" title="Greengrocer">produce market</a> in the Bronx's <a href="/wiki/Hunts_Point,_Bronx" title="Hunts Point, Bronx">Hunts Point</a> section; his mother, Hattie, was a nurse's aide.
</p><p>While a teenager, Harrell formed with Alonzo Brown, a friend from high school, a rap duo, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Jeckyll_%26_Mr._Hyde_(group)" title="Dr. Jeckyll &amp; Mr. Hyde (group)">Dr. Jeckyll &amp; Mr. Hyde</a>—Harrell as Jeckyll, Brown as Hyde<sup id="cite_ref-ABC_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>—and reportedly achieved minor hit songs, "Genius Rap" in 1981 and "AM/PM" in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harrell graduated from <a href="/wiki/Bayard_Rustin_Educational_Complex" title="Bayard Rustin Educational Complex">Charles Evans Hughes High School</a> in 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Baruch_College" title="Baruch College">Baruch College</a>, transferred to <a href="/wiki/Lehman_College" title="Lehman College">Lehman College</a>, and, aiming to be a newscaster, majored in communications and business management.<sup id="cite_ref-HistoryMakers_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HistoryMakers-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYT_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Third-year, he withdrew and took work at a local radio station.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HistoryMakers_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HistoryMakers-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andre_Harrell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1983, Harrell met <a href="/wiki/Russell_Simmons" title="Russell Simmons">Russell Simmons</a>, a co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>. He went to work for Def Jam and within two years became vice-president and general manager. After a few years working at Def Jam, Harrell left and founded his own label called <a href="/wiki/Uptown_Records" title="Uptown Records">Uptown Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harrell is credited with having discovered and signed <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Sean "Puffy" Combs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETraugh201023_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETraugh201023-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 1988, <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a> recorded an impromptu cover of <a href="/wiki/Anita_Baker" title="Anita Baker">Anita Baker</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Caught_Up_in_the_Rapture" title="Caught Up in the Rapture">Caught Up in the Rapture</a>" at a recording booth in a local mall.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Her mother's boyfriend at the time later played the cassette for <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Redd" title="Jeff Redd">Jeff Redd</a>, a recording artist and A&amp;R runner for Uptown Records. Redd sent it to Harrell, who met with Blige. In 1989, she was signed to the label and became the company's youngest and first female solo artist.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1988, Harrell was offered a label deal <a href="/wiki/MCA_Records" title="MCA Records">MCA Music Entertainment Group</a>. After he had multiple successful releases, in 1992, MCA offered Harrell a <a href="/wiki/Multimedia" title="Multimedia">multimedia</a> deal, which involved film and television productions. They developed the feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Strictly_Business_(1991_film)" title="Strictly Business (1991 film)">Strictly Business</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">FOX</a>'s hit police drama series, <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Undercover" title="New York Undercover">New York Undercover</a>,</i> which aired from 1994 until 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harrell renamed Uptown Records as Uptown Enterprises, and its records were featured in productions for <a href="/wiki/Universal_Pictures" title="Universal Pictures">Universal Pictures</a> and <a href="/wiki/Universal_Television" title="Universal Television">Universal Television</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 1994, Harrell had a son with Wendy Credle, a music attorney. They named him Gianni Credle-Harrell.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1995, Harrell was appointed CEO of <a href="/wiki/Motown_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Motown Records">Motown Records</a> and remained there until 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He also hosted <i>Champagne &amp; Bubbles</i> on Sunday nights from 6 to 9pm on Emmis Urban AC WRKS (<a href="/wiki/98.7_Kiss_FM" class="mw-redirect" title="98.7 Kiss FM">98.7 Kiss FM</a>)/New York.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Harrell was the CEO of Harrell Records, which is distributed through <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He partnered with budding Atlanta-based production company L7 Entertainment for the release of their new artists Hamilton Park and Netta Brielle.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harrell was the Vice Chairman of Revolt, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a>'s multi-platform music network. On October 17, 2014, he was instrumental in launching the Revolt Music Conference in <a href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami, Florida">Miami, Florida</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Fontainebleau_Miami_Beach" title="Fontainebleau Miami Beach">Fontainebleau Hotel</a>. The event was attended by such entertainment figures as <a href="/wiki/Guy_Oseary" title="Guy Oseary">Guy Oseary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russell_Simmons" title="Russell Simmons">Russell Simmons</a>, and <a href="/wiki/L.A._Reid" title="L.A. Reid">L.A. Reid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andre_Harrell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harrell died on May 7, 2020, at his home in <a href="/wiki/West_Hollywood,_California" title="West Hollywood, California">West Hollywood, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was 59, and news of his death was first announced on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> by <a href="/wiki/D-Nice" title="D-Nice">D-Nice</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ABC_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> According to Wendy Credle, Harrell's ex-partner, he had been suffering from <a href="/wiki/Cardiovascular_disease" title="Cardiovascular disease">heart problems</a> in the time leading up to his death.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> At his funeral, held on May 23, among notable attendees were singers <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> and record producer <a href="/wiki/L.A._Reid" title="L.A. Reid">L.A. Reid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
