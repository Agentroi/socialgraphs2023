					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American rapper</div>
<p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; font-size: 125%;"><div class="">Dai Burger</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Dainene Alexia Baldwin</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, U.S.</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role"><a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">Rapper</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">2009–present</td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Dainene Alexia Baldwin</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (born February 13),<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> best known by her stage name, <b>Dai Burger</b> <a href="/wiki/Pronunciation_respelling_for_English" title="Pronunciation respelling for English">(/deɪ/)</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> is an American rapper. She is from <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. She has released two studio albums to date.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dai_Burger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baldwin was born and raised in Queens, but has also lived in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dai_Burger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baldwin's performing career began while working as a <a href="/wiki/Backup_dancer" title="Backup dancer">backup dancer</a> on tour with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Mama" title="Lil Mama">Lil Mama</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Shorey2_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shorey2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Since that time she has appeared in several music videos, including <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Party_(Beyonc%C3%A9_song)" title="Party (Beyoncé song)">Party</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-Ladygunn_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ladygunn-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and has worked as a stylist for designer <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Field" title="Patricia Field">Patricia Field</a>'s clothing boutique.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She had also toured with <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Boy" title="Soulja Boy">Soulja Boy</a> as a backup dancer.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Baldwin launched her music career with her 2010 project <i>MyMixxxytape</i>, hosted by rapper <a href="/wiki/Junglepussy" title="Junglepussy">Junglepussy</a>. She explains that her <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> came "out of nowhere, but I like to think that I'm stacked, and juicy."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, Burger released her second compilation <i>Raw Burger</i> featuring Junglepussy on the track "Titty Attack".<sup id="cite_ref-RS_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"In Ya' Mouf" was released via <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_Networks" title="Complex Networks">Complex</a></i> Magazine in January 2014. Featuring production by Shane Augustus and Mighty Mark, the mixtape was well received because of Baldwin's clever lyricism and confident representation of girl power.<sup id="cite_ref-Ortiz_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ortiz-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The video for the single "Soufflé" premiered in <i><a href="/wiki/Interview_(magazine)" title="Interview (magazine)">Interview</a></i> magazine<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and was picked as one of the best <a href="/wiki/Feminism" title="Feminism">feminist</a> music videos of 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-BF_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BF-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The release was featured on <a href="/wiki/Solange_Knowles" title="Solange Knowles">Solange Knowles</a>' Saint-Heron<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and contributed to Burger being named "Artist To Watch" by <i>Rolling Stone</i> magazine in April 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-RS_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Gearing up for her next project, Baldwin collaborated with London-based producer <a href="/wiki/Darq_E_Freaker" title="Darq E Freaker">Darq E Freaker</a> on the track "Choppin Necks".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> "Choppin Necks" was hailed by critics as a futuristic dark song, in all the right ways. Burger delivered "jaw-dropping bars", according to James Keith from <i>Complex</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 4, 2015, Dai Burger premiered her 5-track self-titled project "Dai Burger" exclusively with <i><a href="/wiki/Dazed" title="Dazed">Dazed</a></i> magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-dazeddigital.com_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dazeddigital.com-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Baldwin performed at the first ever "Sound &amp; Style" event in New York, hosted by <a href="/wiki/Boss_Lady" title="Boss Lady">Boss Lady</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reebok" title="Reebok">Reebok</a><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and was as an opening act for <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 chainz</a> in Long Beach, NY in the summer of 2014. To date she has collaborated with artists including <a href="/wiki/Kool_Keith" title="Kool Keith">Kool Keith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cakes_da_Killa" title="Cakes da Killa">Cakes da Killa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cazwell" title="Cazwell">Cazwell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jonte%27_Moaning" title="Jonte&#39; Moaning">Jonte' Moaning</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Shorey1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shorey1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Her debut studio album, titled <i>Soft Serve</i>, was released on August 24, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Baldwin released her second studio album, <i>Bite the Burger</i>, in 2019. The album released a score of 6.7 from <i>Pitchfork,</i> with the reviewer commenting, "An artist as bold and honest as Dai should never sound so boring."<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dai Burger is openly gay.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Talking in an interview about her sexuality she stated, "I like <a href="/wiki/Queer" title="Queer">queer</a> because it’s an umbrella, more than it identifies one thing. I don’t have to, you know, choose a letter."<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
