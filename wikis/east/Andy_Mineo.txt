<p><b>Andrew Aaron Mineo</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born April 17, 1988), is an American <a href="/wiki/Christian_hip_hop" title="Christian hip hop">Christian hip hop</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> artist, <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Music_executive" title="Music executive">music executive</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Music_video_director" title="Music video director">video director</a> based in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. He is signed to <a href="/wiki/Reach_Records" title="Reach Records">Reach Records</a> and his creative initiative Miner League. In addition to his solo work, he is a member of Reach Records' hip hop collective <a href="/wiki/116_Clique" class="mw-redirect" title="116 Clique">116 Clique</a>.
</p><p>Originally from <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_New_York" title="Syracuse, New York">Syracuse</a>, Mineo worked as a producer in high school at <a href="/wiki/Henninger_High_School" title="Henninger High School">Henninger High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Upstate_New_York" title="Upstate New York">Upstate New York</a>, and joined the hip-hop group Fat Camp, signed to <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_University" title="Syracuse University">Syracuse University</a>'s Marshall Street Records. After moving to New York City, he realized his weak spiritual condition, re-dedicated his life to Christ and closed down his production studio in order to restart his career. He independently released his first mixtape <i>Sin is Wack</i>  in 2009 and joined a community outreach program created by <a href="/wiki/Nicky_Cruz" title="Nicky Cruz">Nicky Cruz</a> called T.R.U.C.E. where he met long-time friends and collaborators Alex Medina, Wordsplayed, Rich Perez and others. Upon meeting, Andy began collaborating with Alex Medina on a record called "Background" that would later be placed on <a href="/wiki/Lecrae" title="Lecrae">Lecrae</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Rehab_(Lecrae_album)" title="Rehab (Lecrae album)">Rehab</a></i> album and was Andy's introduction to Reach Records.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, Andy released "In My City", a single featuring Efrain from Doubledge, that continued to build momentum and buzz. After rebranding from C-Lite, Andy signed with Reach Records and released, <i><a href="/wiki/Formerly_Known" title="Formerly Known">Formerly Known</a></i>, a full-length studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Heroes_for_Sale_(Andy_Mineo_album)" title="Heroes for Sale (Andy Mineo album)">Heroes for Sale</a></i> on April 16, 2013. On January 28, 2014, he released an EP titled <i><a href="/wiki/Never_Land_(EP)" title="Never Land (EP)">Never Land</a></i>. His sophomore album, <i><a href="/wiki/Uncomfortable_(album)" title="Uncomfortable (album)">Uncomfortable</a></i>, was released on September 18, 2015.
</p><p>Andy began building his own creative initiative, Miner League and signed <a href="/wiki/John_Itiola" class="mw-redirect" title="John Itiola">Wordsplayed</a> along with L.A.-based alternative artist Willow Stephens that featured on his "Strange Motions" track on <i><a href="/wiki/Uncomfortable_(album)" title="Uncomfortable (album)">Uncomfortable</a></i>. In 2016, the first project from the initiative, <i>Clowntown</i>, was released by Wordsplayed<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and followed by Willow Stephens' self-titled EP.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, Miner League and Reach Records partnered to release a collaborative mixtape titled, <i>Andy Mineo &amp; Wordsplayed Present Magic &amp; Bird</i> on August 4, 2017.
</p><p>His video and television work includes "Saturday Morning Car-Tunez", "League Chumps Season 1", and co-direction on numerous of his own music videos. As a producer, his work includes tracks from his self-released "In My City", <i>Formerly Known</i>, <i>Saturday Morning Car-Tunez</i>, and <i>Heroes for Sale</i>. He also produced the single "Destiny" by Sheena Lee, in which he was a featured performer, and, along with rapper <a href="/wiki/Derek_Minor" title="Derek Minor">Derek Minor</a>, aided <a href="/wiki/DJ_Official" title="DJ Official">DJ Official</a> in producing "Power Trip" by <a href="/wiki/Lecrae" title="Lecrae">Lecrae</a>, featuring Andy Mineo, Derek Minor, and <a href="/wiki/Sho_Baraka" title="Sho Baraka">Sho Baraka</a>, from the album <i><a href="/wiki/Gravity_(Lecrae_album)" title="Gravity (Lecrae album)">Gravity</a></i>. <i>Gravity</i> won the <a href="/wiki/Best_Gospel_Album" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Gospel Album">Best Gospel Album</a> category in the <a href="/wiki/2013_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="2013 Grammy Awards">2013 Grammy Awards</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Mineo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mineo was raised in a single-parent home in <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_New_York" title="Syracuse, New York">Syracuse, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-unyc_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unyc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In the summer before entering the 8th grade, Mineo went to a Summer camp at which his sister worked as a counselor and was exposed to the <a href="/wiki/Gospel" title="Gospel">Gospel</a> and his life had changed.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-unyc_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unyc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> However, after his conversion, Mineo went into high school with no Christian support, and he soon drifted away from his faith.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-unyc_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unyc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> While in high school, Mineo acquired recording equipment and started selling studio time out of his home.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He had formed a friendship with Chris "Oxburg" Leonard, and the pair recorded a song together in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Mineo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After graduating from <a href="/wiki/The_City_College_of_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="The City College of New York">The City College of New York</a> (CCNY),<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Mineo, along with Leonard, joined a rap group called Fat Camp, which subsequently won a song contest for <a href="/wiki/WJPZ-FM" title="WJPZ-FM">WJPZ-FM</a> and signed to <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_University" title="Syracuse University">Syracuse University</a>'s Marshall Street Records.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The group opened up for major acts such as <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">The Roots</a> and <a href="/wiki/Common_(rapper)" title="Common (rapper)">Common</a>, as well as members of the underground scene, such as <a href="/wiki/Immortal_Technique" title="Immortal Technique">Immortal Technique</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dead_Prez" title="Dead Prez">Dead Prez</a>, when said artists performed at the University.<sup id="cite_ref-unyc_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unyc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2006, the group recorded and released a studio album entitled <i>The Food</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his first year of college, Mineo met the producer Alex Medina and through him discovered the urban evangelism project T.R.U.C.E.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-unyc_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unyc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After hearing the song "Price Tag" by <a href="/wiki/Da%27_T.R.U.T.H." title="Da&#39; T.R.U.T.H.">Da' T.R.U.T.H.</a>, Mineo decided to close his studio and rededicate his life to Christ.<sup id="cite_ref-introduction_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-introduction-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He also left Fat Camp, which promptly dissolved.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Although he is a Christian, he chooses to not to identify as a "Christian rapper", because he feels the label turns some people off.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the closure of his studio, Mineo went on tour with T.R.U.C.E. He released his first mixtape, <i>Sin is Wack</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Mineo explained in an interview that he came up with the concept of the "Sin is Wack" title through his work with T.R.U.C.E.<sup id="cite_ref-introduction_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-introduction-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Following this release, Mineo sang on the Lecrae song "Background" from <i><a href="/wiki/Rehab_(Lecrae_album)" title="Rehab (Lecrae album)">Rehab</a></i> in 2010 and "Reverse" from <i><a href="/wiki/Blacklight_(Tedashii_album)" title="Blacklight (Tedashii album)">Blacklight</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Tedashii" title="Tedashii">Tedashii</a> in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-introduction_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-introduction-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Mineo explained to <a href="/wiki/Rapzilla" title="Rapzilla">Rapzilla</a> "Background" was his first attempt at singing, and following the success of that song he received subsequent requests.<sup id="cite_ref-introduction_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-introduction-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <i>The Christian Manifesto</i> claimed that Mineo's hook for "Background" made <i>Rehab</i> one of the best Christian albums to date, and stated that Mineo became one of the most sought after artists, particularly for singing hooks.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> When asked about his recognition as a singer instead of an <a href="/wiki/Emcee" class="mw-redirect" title="Emcee">emcee</a>, Mineo stated that "I enjoy making melodies", and said he wasn't too concerned about rapping when he supplied hooks for songs and was content with the opportunity to serve in any capacity.<sup id="cite_ref-introduction_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-introduction-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Mineo contributed to "Put On" from <i>Captured</i> by <a href="/wiki/Flame_(rapper)" title="Flame (rapper)">Flame</a> and "The Reunion Cypha" on <i><a href="/wiki/Stop_the_Funeral" class="mw-redirect" title="Stop the Funeral">Stop the Funeral</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/The_Ambassador_(rapper)" title="The Ambassador (rapper)">The Ambassador</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-introduction_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-introduction-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He released the single "In My City" in 2011 as part of the God in My City Movement <a href="/wiki/Prayer_walk" class="mw-redirect" title="Prayer walk">prayer walk</a> that Mineo was involved in. The song was reported by Rapzilla to become an "anthem for a lot of people."<sup id="cite_ref-introduction_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-introduction-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 28, 2011, at Legacy Conference 2011, Reach Records announced that Mineo had signed with the label.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Upon signing with the label, Mineo abandoned the stage name C-Lite, stating that he "never really liked the name" and that his signing marked a good time to transition to his legal name.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He released his debut mixtape on Reach Records, <i><a href="/wiki/Formerly_Known" title="Formerly Known">Formerly Known</a></i>, on September 29, 2011.
</p><p>In May 2012, Mineo launched a 4-episode <a href="/wiki/Web_series" title="Web series">web series</a> entitled <i>Saturday Morning Car-Tunez</i> which featured him remixing classic hip-hop songs.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The remixed songs were released as a free download.<sup id="cite_ref-saturdaymorningdownload_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-saturdaymorningdownload-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On July 17, Mineo performed alongside Lecrae at the <a href="/wiki/Apple_Store" title="Apple Store">Apple Store</a> in <a href="/wiki/SoHo" class="mw-redirect" title="SoHo">SoHo</a>, New York to promote Lecrae's 6th studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Gravity_(Lecrae_album)" title="Gravity (Lecrae album)">Gravity</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-soho_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-soho-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In August, Mineo performed in the 2012 God Belongs in My City Concert tour with Anthony Shepherd &amp; The Roar and Sheena Lee.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He also headlined the 2012 Kingdom Choice Awards along with <a href="/wiki/Swoope_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Swoope (rapper)">Swoope</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bizzle" title="Bizzle">Bizzle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> From the month of October through November, Mineo toured as a member of the <a href="/wiki/116_Clique" class="mw-redirect" title="116 Clique">116 Clique</a> on the Unashamed 2012: Come Alive tour.<sup id="cite_ref-allhiphop_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allhiphop-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On December 7–8, Mineo was a featured performer at the Kings Dream Conference, a launching of the Kings Dream label by the hip-hop group theBREAX.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 8, 2013, Mineo announced the release date for <i>Heroes for Sale</i>, April 16, 2013, and released the cover.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 1, 2014, Mineo announced season two of <i>Saturday Morning Car-Tunez</i>, which showed the making of Mineo's EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Never_Land_(EP)" title="Never Land (EP)">Never Land</a></i>, which he released on January 28, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-hhdx_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hhdx-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <i>Never Land</i> includes "You Can't Stop Me", "Never Land" and "Paisano's Wylin'".<sup id="cite_ref-hhdx_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hhdx-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 19, 2015, he released a single, "Lay Up", featuring New York native Wordsplayed and produced by Alex Medina and <a href="/wiki/Tyshane" class="mw-redirect" title="Tyshane">Tyshane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In the two weeks before that, he set up a way for fans to text him to receive a private link to the song on SoundCloud.
</p><p>Andy Mineo revealed the art work for his second full-length album with Reach Records, <i><a href="/wiki/Uncomfortable_(album)" title="Uncomfortable (album)">Uncomfortable</a></i>, on July 16, 2015. The album was released on September 18, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, Mineo released a music video for his song "Hear My Heart", which describes his guilt for not learning <a href="/wiki/Sign_language" title="Sign language">sign language</a> to communicate with his <a href="/wiki/Deaf" class="mw-redirect" title="Deaf">deaf</a> sister for 25 years. Mineo said he worked for a year to make translate every sound in the song into a <a href="/wiki/Three-dimensional" class="mw-redirect" title="Three-dimensional">three-dimensional</a> experience so that deaf viewers could experience the rhythm of the music.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mineo founded Miner League, a brand he had explained was going to give him new an outlet to make music and expand his reach. <a href="/wiki/Wordsplayed" title="Wordsplayed">Wordsplayed</a> was the first artists signed to the brand. Mineo had experienced a creative halt while writing his upcoming album. He reached out to Wordsplayed, and together they came up with the concept of <i>Magic and Bird</i>, a five-song <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> of songs from Mineo and Wordsplayed. They spent three weeks in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a> to further develop the project. It grew from a five-song E.P. to a ten-song <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> with one bonus song and two skits. Mineo and Wordsplayed called their song, "Dunk Contest", a summarization of their time in Atlanta.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The pre-release claimed No. 1 on the iTunes' all genres slider. <i>Andy Mineo &amp; Wordsplayed Present Magic &amp; Bird</i> peaked at No. 1 on Top Christian Albums. "The set moved with 11,000 equivalent album units earned in its first week, with 8,000 in traditional sales." said journalist Timothy Yap from Christian music portal Hallels. The mixtape also peaked at No. 3 on Rap Album Sales and No. 23 on Top Rap Albums. "Dunk Contest" entered Spotify's Viral Top 50 in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="The United Kingdom">the United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. On September 21, 2017, Mineo and Wordsplayed launched the Friends and Family Tour.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mineo scored his first RIAA certification when "You Can't Stop Me" went gold on January 17, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The rapper kept the news quiet until he released the remix featuring Messiah on April 11, 2018.
</p><p>On April 27, 2018, Mineo released an extended play called <i>I: the Arrow</i>. Four days before his EP's scheduled release, Mineo said that he was "unsure [of] when [he] began to feel stuck in this cloud or when it will end, but [he knew] there [were] others who feel the same." He featured the electronic group Weathrman his songs, "Family Photo" and "Anxiety". Mineo revealed that the six-track release is the first of four extended plays culminating into his third studio album. The E.P. peaked at No. 2 on <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s Top Christian Albums chart earn 6,000 equivalent album units in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 21, 2018, he released another EP, <i>II: The Sword</i>, which is the second of four EPs.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The EP, peaked at No. 15 on the <i>Billboard</i> Digital Albums Chart for the week of October 6, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Mineo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andy Mineo married Cristina Delgado on August 23, 2014, after their engagement that April.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He lived in Washington Heights, and is close friends with his mentors Alex Medina and Rich Perez, who also are frequent artistic collaborators with Mineo.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Mineo was a pastor at Christ Crucified Fellowship, of which Perez is head pastor, but with the increasing success of his music career took on the role of deacon instead.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Mineo and his wife, Cristina, moved to Atlanta, a location he said is better aligned with his burgeoning music career.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
