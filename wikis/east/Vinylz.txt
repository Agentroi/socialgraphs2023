<p><b>Anderson Hernandez</b> (born September 2, 1989),<sup id="cite_ref-boywonder2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boywonder2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> professionally known as <b>Vinylz</b>, is an American record producer from <a href="/wiki/Washington_Heights,_Manhattan" title="Washington Heights, Manhattan">Washington Heights</a>, New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-Kyles_2013_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kyles_2013-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is known for his work with <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>, <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremih" title="Jeremih">Jeremih</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tinashe" title="Tinashe">Tinashe</a>.
</p><p>Vinylz secured a publishing deal with <a href="/wiki/Sony_ATV" class="mw-redirect" title="Sony ATV">Sony ATV</a> through fellow record producer and mentor, <a href="/wiki/Boi-1da" title="Boi-1da">Boi-1da</a>, with whom he has also worked closely since.<sup id="cite_ref-Complex_Mag1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex_Mag1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vinylz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Anderson Hernandez was born to immigrated parents from the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> on September 2, 1989, in <a href="/wiki/Washington_Heights,_Manhattan" title="Washington Heights, Manhattan">Washington Heights</a>, a neighborhood located in the New York City borough of <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, living across the street from <a href="/wiki/Adult_contemporary" class="mw-redirect" title="Adult contemporary">adult contemporary</a> musician <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Hernandez started producing at age 14.<sup id="cite_ref-Kyles_2013_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kyles_2013-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He received his stage name, Vinylz, courtesy of a friend who noticed both of his interests for clever sampling and his father's collection of vinyl records.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Vinylz began taking music production seriously and it remained his favorite hobby ever since, but he went against his parents' wishes.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> He got an early professional start working with <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a> and decided to drop out of college after the first semester of his first year.<sup id="cite_ref-Fader2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fader2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Setting up shop in Quad Studios, the well-known recording studio located in New York City's <a href="/wiki/Time_Square" class="mw-redirect" title="Time Square">Time Square</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>, Vinylz flourished through his work with up-and-coming or breakout artists like <a href="/wiki/Drag-On" title="Drag-On">Drag-On</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cassidy_(rapper)" title="Cassidy (rapper)">Cassidy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fader2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fader2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> After reaching out to Canadian rapper <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>'s in-house producer, <a href="/wiki/Boi-1da" title="Boi-1da">Boi-1da</a>, via Myspace to retrieve the instrumental for "<a href="/wiki/Best_I_Ever_Had_(Drake_song)" title="Best I Ever Had (Drake song)">Best I Ever Had</a>", Vinylz ended up forming a relationship which would remain long after the request.<sup id="cite_ref-Kyles_2013_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kyles_2013-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Fader2_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fader2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Impressed by his work, Boi-1da became a mentor to the up-and-comer; the two continued on to collaborate from that point on.<sup id="cite_ref-Kyles_2013_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kyles_2013-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Fader2_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fader2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Z" class="mw-redirect" title="Jay Z">Jay Z</a>'s "F***WithMeYouKnowIGotIt" featuring <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/No_New_Friends_(DJ_Khaled_song)" title="No New Friends (DJ Khaled song)">No New Friends</a>" featuring Drake, Rick Ross and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>, both of which produced by Vinylz and Boi-1da, were respectively placed on the Top 40 of the Billboard Hot 100 chart; the latter was certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A variety of hits, alongside Boi-1da, helped Vinylz reach a publishing deal with <a href="/wiki/Sony_ATV" class="mw-redirect" title="Sony ATV">Sony ATV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Complex_Mag1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex_Mag1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Throughout 2015, he produced a high number of tracks for such artists, including <a href="/wiki/Jeremih" title="Jeremih">Jeremih</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Planez" title="Planez">Planez</a>", <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Blessings_(Big_Sean_song)" title="Blessings (Big Sean song)">Blessings</a>", <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/R.I.C.O._(song)" title="R.I.C.O.
