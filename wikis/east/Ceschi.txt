<p><b>Julio Francisco Ramos</b> (born July 24, 1981), better known as <b>Ceschi Ramos</b>, or simply as <b>Ceschi</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/tʃ/: &#39;ch&#39; in &#39;China&#39;">tʃ</span><span title="/ɜː/: r-less &#39;ur&#39; in &#39;nurse&#39;">ɜː</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span><span title="/i/: &#39;y&#39; in &#39;happy&#39;">i</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">CHESS</span>-kee</i></a>),<sup id="cite_ref-newhavenregister_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newhavenregister-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-villagevoice_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villagevoice-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> is an American hip hop musician based in <a href="/wiki/New_Haven,_Connecticut" title="New Haven, Connecticut">New Haven, Connecticut</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-courant2014_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-courant2014-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He is a co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Fake_Four_Inc." title="Fake Four Inc.">Fake Four Inc.</a><sup id="cite_ref-villagevoice_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villagevoice-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He won the Connecticut Music Awards for Best Hip Hop in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-ctnews2014_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ctnews2014-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ceschi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ceschi released his first solo album, <i>Fake Flowers</i>, in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-courant2015_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-courant2015-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <i>They Hate Francisco False</i> followed in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-courant2015_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-courant2015-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008, Ceschi and his brother David Ramos founded the record label <a href="/wiki/Fake_Four_Inc." title="Fake Four Inc.">Fake Four Inc.</a><sup id="cite_ref-villagevoice_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villagevoice-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Ceschi was arrested<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and pleaded guilty to a 100-pound marijuana shipment that he accepted at the home of his grandfather in New Haven.
</p><p>Ceschi released <i><a href="/wiki/The_One_Man_Band_Broke_Up" title="The One Man Band Broke Up">The One Man Band Broke Up</a></i>, a collaborative album with German producer DJ Scientist, in July 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> It was described by <a href="/wiki/Tiny_Mix_Tapes" title="Tiny Mix Tapes">Tiny Mix Tapes</a> as "music for the saddest party in recorded human history."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Ceschi won the Connecticut Music Awards for Best Hip Hop.<sup id="cite_ref-ctnews2014_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ctnews2014-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He released <i>Broken Bone Ballads</i>, a collaborative album with Canadian producer <a href="/wiki/Factor_Chandelier" title="Factor Chandelier">Factor Chandelier</a>, in April 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-browardpalmbeach_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-browardpalmbeach-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  In 2019, Jahan Nostra and Ceschi won Best Music Video at the <a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_Film_Festival" title="Hip Hop Film Festival">Hip Hop Film Festival</a> for their video “El Chapo.”<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
