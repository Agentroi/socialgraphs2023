<p><b>Teyana Me Shay Jacqueli Shumpert</b> (<span title="Name at birth"><a href="/wiki/Birth_name#Maiden_and_married_names" title="Birth name">née</a></span> <b>Taylor</b>; born December 10, 1990)<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> is an American singer, actress, dancer and choreographer.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2005, she signed a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>' <a href="/wiki/Star_Trak_Entertainment" title="Star Trak Entertainment">Star Trak Entertainment</a> imprint. Afterwards, she choreographed the music video for "<a href="/wiki/Ring_the_Alarm" title="Ring the Alarm">Ring the Alarm</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>. Taylor would then appear on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/My_Super_Sweet_16" title="My Super Sweet 16">My Super Sweet 16</a></i>, prior to the 2008 release of her debut single "<a href="/wiki/Google_Me_(Teyana_Taylor_song)" title="Google Me (Teyana Taylor song)">Google Me</a>". Soon after, she landed (uncredited) guest features on the songs "<a href="/wiki/Dark_Fantasy_(song)" title="Dark Fantasy (song)">Dark Fantasy</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Hell_of_a_Life_(song)" title="Hell of a Life (song)">Hell of a Life</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, from his album <i><a href="/wiki/My_Beautiful_Dark_Twisted_Fantasy" title="My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy">My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy</a></i> (2010).
</p><p>In 2011, Taylor starred as Sabrina in the <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Perry" title="Tyler Perry">Tyler Perry</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Madea%27s_Big_Happy_Family_(film)" title="Madea&#39;s Big Happy Family (film)">Madea's Big Happy Family</a></i>. After asking to be released from her recording contract with Star Trak, she signed to <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a> label through <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-Rap-Up.com_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rap-Up.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> she appeared on the GOOD Music compilation album <a href="/wiki/Cruel_Summer_(GOOD_Music_album)" title="Cruel Summer (GOOD Music album)"><i>Cruel Summer</i></a> (2012), which reached number two on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart. In 2014, Taylor released her debut album <a href="/wiki/VII_(Teyana_Taylor_album)" title="VII (Teyana Taylor album)"><i>VII</i></a>, and followed it with the studio albums <i><a href="/wiki/K.T.S.E." title="K.T.S.E.">K.T.S.E.</a></i> (2018) and <a href="/wiki/The_Album_(Teyana_Taylor_album)" title="The Album (Teyana Taylor album)"><i>The Album</i></a> (2020), with the latter charting in the top ten of the <i>Billboard</i> 200.
</p><p>As an actress, Taylor has acted in the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> television series <a href="/wiki/The_Breaks_(TV_series)" title="The Breaks (TV series)"><i>The Breaks</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Daniels" title="Lee Daniels">Lee Daniels</a>' <a href="/wiki/Star_(TV_series)" title="Star (TV series)"><i>Star</i></a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Prime_Video" title="Amazon Prime Video">Amazon Prime</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Coming_2_America" title="Coming 2 America">Coming 2 America</a></i>. Additionally, she starred in the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">reality</a> show <i>Teyana and Iman</i> with her husband, basketball player <a href="/wiki/Iman_Shumpert" title="Iman Shumpert">Iman Shumpert</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Taylor has collaborated with musicians such as <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and has co-written songs for several artists, including <a href="/wiki/Usher_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Usher (entertainer)">Usher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown_(American_entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chris Brown (American entertainer)">Chris Brown</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Omarion" title="Omarion">Omarion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teyana_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Teyana Taylor was born on December 10, 1990, to Nikki Taylor and Tito Smith, in <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a>, New York City. She is of African American and <a href="/wiki/Trinidadian" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinidadian">Trinidadian</a> descent. Taylor is her mother's only child, while her father has two sons and another daughter from a different relationship. Her mother raised her and is currently her manager.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teyana_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.932011:_Early_career"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–2011:_Early_career">2006–2011: Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teyana_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006–2011: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2006, 15-year-old Teyana Taylor was credited as the <a href="/wiki/Choreographer" class="mw-redirect" title="Choreographer">choreographer</a> on the music video for American entertainer <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Hit_single" class="mw-redirect" title="Hit single">hit single</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Ring_the_Alarm" title="Ring the Alarm">Ring the Alarm</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In January 2007, Taylor was offered a contract to join American recording artist and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a> <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>' <a href="/wiki/Star_Trak_Entertainment" title="Star Trak Entertainment">Star Trak Entertainment</a> label, through <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-skopemag_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-skopemag-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In February 2007, Taylor first entered into the public sphere through an episode on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/My_Super_Sweet_Sixteen" class="mw-redirect" title="My Super Sweet Sixteen">My Super Sweet Sixteen</a></i>, a show depicting over-the-top birthday parties for rich—and typically “spoiled” and confrontational—teenagers. In 2007, Taylor <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">appeared</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for Jay-Z's single, "<a href="/wiki/Blue_Magic_(song)" title="Blue Magic (song)">Blue Magic</a>", where she can be seen dancing and <a href="/wiki/Popping" title="Popping">popping</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-skopemag_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-skopemag-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2008, Taylor released her commercial debut single, "<a href="/wiki/Google_Me_(Teyana_Taylor_song)" title="Google Me (Teyana Taylor song)">Google Me</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The song was released as the <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">lead single</a> from her full-length debut <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, titled <i>From a Planet Called Harlem</i>. The mixtape, released August 16, 2009, features production from <a href="/wiki/Jazze_Pha" title="Jazze Pha">Jazze Pha</a>, Pharrell, Mad Scientist, Frost, Shondre and <a href="/wiki/Hit-Boy" title="Hit-Boy">Hit-Boy</a>, among others. Sing365 says that the record’s "…[a] mix of throwback break beats, futuristic boom bap, and melodic renderings is the perfect backdrop for the starlet's musical mayhem."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> "Google Me" debuted at number 90 on US <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;s</span> <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a>. The mixtape was also released for <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">free download</a>, billed as "the mixtape before her debut album".<sup id="cite_ref-FAPCH_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAPCH-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2010, Taylor expanded her career into <a href="/wiki/Acting" title="Acting">acting</a>, appearing in the sequel to <i><a href="/wiki/Stomp_the_Yard" title="Stomp the Yard">Stomp the Yard</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Stomp_the_Yard:_Homecoming" title="Stomp the Yard: Homecoming">Stomp the Yard: Homecoming</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> She also appeared on the debut episode of <i><a href="/wiki/House_of_Glam" title="House of Glam">House of Glam</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Oxygen_(TV_channel)" title="Oxygen (TV channel)">Oxygen</a> in October 2010.
</p><p>In 2010, just hours before <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s fifth album <i><a href="/wiki/My_Beautiful_Dark_Twisted_Fantasy" title="My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy">My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy</a></i> was due to be turned in, West called Taylor into the studio to look over some of her fashion pieces. While in the studio, Taylor was determined to make an appearance on West's album. She purposely hummed along with the tracks he played for her, to get his attention. He eventually took notice, and asked to put her vocals on a few tracks, notably "<a href="/wiki/Dark_Fantasy_(song)" title="Dark Fantasy (song)">Dark Fantasy</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Hell_of_a_Life_(song)" title="Hell of a Life (song)">Hell of a Life</a>". Upon recalling the experience of recording "Dark Fantasy", Taylor stated that "at the time it was pretty empty, just verses." She remembers that West "put [her] in another room by [herself] and said, 'Go'." Taylor came back with the "intro and chorus," and "did all the <a href="/wiki/Scratching" title="Scratching">scratches</a> and the cuts [herself]." She admitted that she was nervous upon letting West hear her contributions to the tracks, trying to cover her nervousness by stating that she "hadn't been in the studio for so long."<sup id="cite_ref-Wete_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wete-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Taylor contributed on one of GOOD Music's <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Fridays" title="GOOD Fridays">GOOD Friday</a> tracks, "<a href="/wiki/Christmas_in_Harlem" title="Christmas in Harlem">Christmas In Harlem</a>". The track was released as a shortened version of the single to the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a> on December 17, 2010, and features <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from American rapper <a href="/wiki/Cyhi_the_Prynce" title="Cyhi the Prynce">Cyhi the Prynce</a> as well as Kanye West.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, Taylor, alongside fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Bow_Wow_(rapper)" title="Bow Wow (rapper)">Bow Wow</a>, were offered roles for <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Perry" title="Tyler Perry">Tyler Perry</a>'s play-turned-film <i><a href="/wiki/Madea%27s_Big_Happy_Family_(film)" title="Madea&#39;s Big Happy Family (film)">Madea's Big Happy Family</a></i>, the fifth installment in Perry's series. The film was released on April 22, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Taylor soon delved back into music, and was featured on the hook of "Party Tonight" alongside fellow rappers Randyn Julius, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Randyn Julius was later enlisted by Junior Reid to do his bars for the <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remix</a> of the track.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932018:_GOOD_Music.2C_debut_and_K.T.S.E."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2018:_GOOD_Music,_debut_and_K.T.S.E.">2012–2018: GOOD Music, debut and <i>K.T.S.E.</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teyana_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012–2018: GOOD Music, debut and K.T.S.E.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2012, it was announced Taylor would be granted her release from the contract with Interscope and Star Trak.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Taylor has stated that she and Star Trak founder Pharrell Williams have remained amicable since the end of their business relationship. She has credited him as a "big brother", and sees her early signing to Star Trak as a "blessing."<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Taylor has stated on many occasions that her release from the label was necessary, as she wanted to have a feeling of independence in the music industry. In an interview with DJ Skee, Taylor mentioned that “when [she] was signed for six years, [she] felt like she couldn't do [anything]”, “couldn't prove to [her] fans that [she] had the talent”, and “couldn't sing from [her] heart.”<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
As an <a href="/wiki/Independent_artist" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent artist">independent artist</a> upon her release, she had begun preparing her second record, a mixtape titled <i>The Misunderstanding of Teyana Taylor</i>. The mixtape drew influences from <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">1990s R&amp;B</a> legends like <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape was released in early 2012, and was preceded by the release of "Make Your Move", featuring rapper <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> and "D.U.I.", featuring <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, which was released on February 25, 2012. More positive reviews of the latter track noted that "The Harlem songstress channels <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">Janet Jackson</a> as she glides over the smooth <a href="/wiki/Hit-Boy" title="Hit-Boy">Hit-Boy</a>-produced beat with her airy vocals, while fellow New Yorkers Fabolous and Jadakiss ride shotgun".<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After taking some time to find herself as an independent artist, Taylor signed a joint-venture deal with Kanye West's label GOOD Music through <a href="/wiki/The_Island_Def_Jam_Music_Group" title="The Island Def Jam Music Group">Island-Def Jam</a> on June 14, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Rap-Up.com_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rap-Up.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> As part of GOOD Music, Taylor appeared on the GOOD Music compilation album <a href="/wiki/Cruel_Summer_(GOOD_Music_album)" title="Cruel Summer (GOOD Music album)"><i>Cruel Summer</i></a>, released September 18, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-alexis1_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alexis1-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> She provides vocals for the opening track "To the World", with Kanye West and <a href="/wiki/R._Kelly" title="R. Kelly">R. Kelly</a>, provides the hook and chorus for "Sin City", which features <a href="/wiki/John_Legend" title="John Legend">John Legend</a>, Cyhi the Prynce, <a href="/wiki/Malik_Yusef" title="Malik Yusef">Malik Yusef</a> and Travis Scott, and also duets with John Legend on a song titled "Bliss".<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Taylor and Legend's duet on the album was highly praised, with Erika Ramirez of <i>Billboard</i> noting that "Taylor's beautifully brash voice ... illuminates in the electric-guitar heavy, <a href="/wiki/Hudson_Mohawke" title="Hudson Mohawke">Hudson Mohawke</a>-produced 'Bliss'."<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to being featured on the <i>Cruel Summer</i> compilation, Taylor soon began the process of recording her <a href="/wiki/Major_record_label" class="mw-redirect" title="Major record label">major-label</a> debut album, to be released eventually through GOOD Music and Island Def Jam.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On July 27, 2014, via <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>, Taylor announced the title of her debut <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio album">studio album</a> to be <i>VII</i>, and revealed it would be released on November 4, through Def Jam and GOOD Music.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The album debuted at number 19 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, selling 16,000 copies in the United States in its first week, while topping the R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums chart.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Taylor was set to be a judge on the <a href="/wiki/America%27s_Best_Dance_Crew_(season_8)" title="America&#39;s Best Dance Crew (season 8)">eighth season</a> of the American <a href="/wiki/Competitive_dance" title="Competitive dance">competitive dance</a> reality show <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Best_Dance_Crew" title="America&#39;s Best Dance Crew">America's Best Dance Crew</a></i>, with its series debut on July 29, 2015 on MTV.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On August 25, Taylor released <i>The Cassette Tape 1994</i>, paying tribute to the iconic <a href="/wiki/1990s_in_music" title="1990s in music">90s sound</a> in music by including many <a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">samples</a> from 90s hits.<sup id="cite_ref-CT_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CT-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 17, 2016, Taylor released her single "Freak On", featuring guest vocals from <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a> and production by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Mustard" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Mustard">DJ Mustard</a>. It samples "<a href="/wiki/Freak_Like_Me" class="mw-redirect" title="Freak Like Me">Freak Like Me</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Adina_Howard" title="Adina Howard">Adina Howard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Taylor announced that it would be the <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> from her upcoming second studio album. On August 28, 2016, at the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="MTV Video Music Awards">MTV Video Music Awards</a>, Kanye West debuted the music video for his new single "<a href="/wiki/Fade_(Kanye_West_song)" title="Fade (Kanye West song)">Fade</a>", which featured a sweat-dripping, athletic Taylor performing a rigorous solo dance routine, in nothing more than short shorts and a simple top; the dynamic and detailed choreography drew considerable praise, and comparisons were made to <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Beals" title="Jennifer Beals">Jennifer Beals</a>’ iconic dance scene from the film <i><a href="/wiki/Flashdance" title="Flashdance">Flashdance</a></i> (1983). One year later, in 2017, Taylor’s dancing in the music video would go on to earn her the “Best Choreography” award at the <a href="/wiki/2017_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2017 MTV Video Music Awards">same ceremony</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> The video, directed by Eli Russell Linnetz, also featured an appearance by Taylor’s husband, Iman Shumpert.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
Taylor's album <i><a href="/wiki/K.T.S.E." title="K.T.S.E.">K.T.S.E.</a></i>, an acronym for "keep that same energy," was the last of G.O.O.D. Music's seven-song album rollout and was released on June 22, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> An updated version of the album was supposed to be released, but Taylor announced that plans were canceled for it.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2019-present:_The_Album_and_other_collaborations">2019-present: <i>The Album and other collaborations</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teyana_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2019-present: The Album and other collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Taylor released a collaboration single with fellow R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/Kehlani" title="Kehlani">Kehlani</a> called "<a href="/wiki/Morning_(Teyana_Taylor_and_Kehlani_song)" title="Morning (Teyana Taylor and Kehlani song)">Morning</a>" in November 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> On December 6, 2019, Taylor released the single "<a href="/wiki/We_Got_Love_(Teyana_Taylor_song)" title="We Got Love (Teyana Taylor song)">We Got Love</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> On May 7, 2020, Taylor announced that her third studio album is titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Album_(Teyana_Taylor_album)" title="The Album (Teyana Taylor album)">The Album</a></i> and would be released sometime in June 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> It was released on June 19, with the 23-track album featuring guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kehlani" title="Kehlani">Kehlani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quavo" title="Quavo">Quavo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> It debuted at number 8 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, earning Taylor her first top 10 album.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 4, 2020, Taylor seemingly announced her retirement from making music via her Instagram page. A few days later, she explained the post was directed at her record label as she continued clarifying her frustration with their lack of support,  "Baby, I gotta do it for my mental health. I have to do it for my emotional health. I have to do it for my kids, so I can stay alive for my kids. Until I'm free, until I can get [Def Jam] to release me, yes I want to retire."<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> She later stated much of the frustration leading to retirement could be traced back to her label undervaluing her, but she would not be retiring for good.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_ventures">Other ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teyana_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013, Taylor signed a deal to design and release two pairs of sneakers with <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a>. The first pair released were the Harlem GLC's which hit stores February 16 the same year. According to Adidas Global Director of Entertainment and Influence Marketing, Jonathan Wexler, Taylor currently holds the record for fastest selling sneakers in <a href="/wiki/Adidas_Originals" title="Adidas Originals">Adidas Originals</a> history.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> She is currently focused on designing and releasing her second pair of sneakers with the brand.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In March 2017, Teyana Taylor launched her "Fade 2 Fit" Workout Program, a ninety-day workout which centers around dance and fitness routines. The inspiration for this program and the incorporated dance elements come from her work in the music video for Kanye West's "Fade". She also released a line of workout apparel, also called Fade2Fit. In May 2019, Taylor featured and directed the music video for <a href="/wiki/Lil_Durk" title="Lil Durk">Lil Durk</a>'s remix of Home Body in his new album Signed to the Streets 3.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2022, Taylor competed in <a href="/wiki/The_Masked_Singer_(American_season_7)" title="The Masked Singer (American season 7)">season seven</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Masked_Singer_(American_TV_series)" title="The Masked Singer (American TV series)">The Masked Singer</a></i> as "Firefly" of Team Good. During the finals, Taylor was declared the winner. At 31 years old, she became the youngest "winner" in the show's history.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teyana_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2015, after she went into early labor, her husband <a href="/wiki/Iman_Shumpert" title="Iman Shumpert">Iman Shumpert</a> assisted in her delivery of their baby girl.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 20, 2016, on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wendy_Williams_Show" title="The Wendy Williams Show">The Wendy Williams Show</a></i>, Taylor revealed that she and Shumpert had "maybe" married secretly, but the couple did not actually wed until October 1, 2016, approximately two weeks after the interview.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>  On September 17, 2023, the couple announced their separation.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 12, 2020, Taylor confirmed her second pregnancy in her music video for her 2020 single "<a href="/wiki/Wake_Up_Love" title="Wake Up Love">Wake Up Love</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
