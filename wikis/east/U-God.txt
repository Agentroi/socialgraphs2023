<p><b>Lamont Jody Hawkins</b> (born November 10, 1970),<sup id="cite_ref-Allmusic_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>U-God</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">rapper</a> and member of the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop culture">hip hop</a> collective <b><a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a></b>.<sup id="cite_ref-Allmusic_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He has been with the group since its inception, and is known for his deep voice and rhythmic flow that can alternate between gruff and smooth.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=U-God&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hawkins was born in <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Brooklyn" title="Brownsville, Brooklyn">Brownsville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He moved to <a href="/wiki/Staten_Island" title="Staten Island">Staten Island</a> as a youth.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was originally a beatboxer for <a href="/wiki/Cappadonna" title="Cappadonna">Cappadonna</a>, and was friends with future members <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inspectah_Deck" title="Inspectah Deck">Inspectah Deck</a> and childhood friend of <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>. Sometime before the members united, U-God was mentored in rap by Cappadonna. He soon became friends with <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a>, and he began rhyming under the alias Golden Arms, based on the Kung-Fu movie <i><a href="/wiki/Kid_with_the_Golden_Arm" title="Kid with the Golden Arm">Kid with the Golden Arm</a></i>. Later on he changed his name to U-God (which is short for his <a href="/wiki/Five-Percent_Nation" title="Five-Percent Nation">Five-Percent Nation</a> righteous name "Universal God Allah").<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=U-God&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Enter_the_Wu-Tang_.2836_Chambers.29_.2F_Wu-Tang_Forever"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Enter_the_Wu-Tang_(36_Chambers)_/_Wu-Tang_Forever"><i>Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers)</i> / <i>Wu-Tang Forever</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=U-God&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers) / Wu-Tang Forever">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>U-God was convicted on the grounds of <a href="/wiki/Illegal_weapons_trade" class="mw-redirect" title="Illegal weapons trade">firearm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drug_possession" class="mw-redirect" title="Drug possession">drug possession</a> charges on <b>April 17, 1992</b>, and was paroled in January 1993. His incarceration prevented him from attending the group's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Enter_the_Wu-Tang_(36_Chambers)" title="Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers)">Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers)</a></i> recording sessions for the most part, his input on the seminal LP consisting of only a short <a href="/wiki/Bridge_(music)" title="Bridge (music)">bridge</a> on the group's debut single "<a href="/wiki/Protect_Ya_Neck" title="Protect Ya Neck">Protect Ya Neck</a>" as well as the fan-favorite opening verse of "Da Mystery of Chessboxin'". Nevertheless, after his release, he quickly became known to fans for his rugged flow and bass-like voice on Wu tracks such as "Winter Warz", "Knuckleheadz", "Investigative Reports", and "Black Jesus". He was featured heavily on the group's second album <i><a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Forever" title="Wu-Tang Forever">Wu-Tang Forever</a></i>, on which he was one of only four of the group to get a solo track – "Black Shampoo" (the others being <a href="/wiki/Inspectah_Deck" title="Inspectah Deck">Inspectah Deck</a> with "The City", <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a> with "Sunshower" and <a href="/wiki/Ol%27_Dirty_Bastard" title="Ol&#39; Dirty Bastard">Ol' Dirty Bastard</a> with "Dog Shit").
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Golden_Arms_Redemption"><i>Golden Arms Redemption</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=U-God&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Golden Arms Redemption">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>U-God was the eighth member of the group to record a solo album, releasing <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Arms_Redemption" title="Golden Arms Redemption">Golden Arms Redemption</a></i> in 1999 on Priority Records, which displayed a wide variety of sounds from gritty <a href="/wiki/Blaxploitation" title="Blaxploitation">blaxploitation</a> <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a> to urgent string sections, and featured guest appearances from several Wu-Tang members and affiliates. It had two singles in "Dat's Gangsta" and "Bizarre". The song "Rumble" was used as the main song for the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang:_Shaolin_Style" title="Wu-Tang: Shaolin Style">Wu-Tang: Shaolin Style</a></i>. Bizarre debuted #7 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Singles" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Rap Singles">Hot Rap Singles</a>, but soon after Priority Records was on the verge of shutting down. Still, the album managed to go Gold in sales. Based on the initial success, U-God was able to open his own label, Suppa Nigga Productions. He released his second album <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Xcitement" title="Mr. Xcitement">Mr. Xcitement</a></i> in 2005.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dopium_and_The_Keynote_Speaker"><i>Dopium</i> and <i>The Keynote Speaker</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=U-God&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Dopium and The Keynote Speaker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:U-God_performing_in_Atlanta.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/U-God_performing_in_Atlanta.jpg/220px-U-God_performing_in_Atlanta.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/U-God_performing_in_Atlanta.jpg/330px-U-God_performing_in_Atlanta.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/U-God_performing_in_Atlanta.jpg/440px-U-God_performing_in_Atlanta.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a><figcaption>U-God performing in Atlanta, 2007</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2009 U-God released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Dopium" title="Dopium">Dopium</a></i> and had the lead single "Wu-Tang" featuring <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>. The album featured guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Sheek_Louch" title="Sheek Louch">Sheek Louch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones" title="Jim Jones">Jim Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a>, <a href="/wiki/GZA" title="GZA">GZA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cappadonna" title="Cappadonna">Cappadonna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Killah_Priest" title="Killah Priest">Killah Priest</a> and Scotty Wotty with production by Bloody Beat Roots, Felix Cartel and <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a>. In 2013 U-God announced a new album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Keynote_Speaker" title="The Keynote Speaker">The Keynote Speaker</a></i> with production by <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a> who also served as the albums executive producer. The album was released on July 23, 2013, by RZA's <a href="/wiki/Soul_Temple_Records" title="Soul Temple Records">Soul Temple Records</a> with guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_Keith" title="Kool Keith">Kool Keith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, RZA, <a href="/wiki/GZA" title="GZA">GZA</a> and <a href="/wiki/Inspectah_Deck" title="Inspectah Deck">Inspectah Deck</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Venom_and_autobiographical_book"><i>Venom</i> and autobiographical book</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=U-God&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Venom and autobiographical book">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 13, 2016, U-God released through Babygrande Records' <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> account a song "Venom" and announced that his new album, also called <i><a href="/wiki/Venom_(U-God_album)" title="Venom (U-God album)">Venom</a></i>, would be released soon.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On September 25, 2017, he added a <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">tweet</a> in which he announced that he had finished his <a href="/wiki/Autobiography" title="Autobiography">autobiography</a> entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Raw:_My_Journey_Into_The_Wu-Tang" class="mw-redirect" title="Raw: My Journey Into The Wu-Tang">Raw: My Journey Into The Wu-Tang</a></i> and it would be released along with his new album in March 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On February 2, 2018, he released a free mixtape <i>Bring Back God II</i> through <a href="/wiki/DatPiff" title="DatPiff">DatPiff</a> platform.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <i>Venom</i> was released on March 30, 2018<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and debuted at #34 on the U.S. Rap Albums Chart.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=U-God&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Around the time of <i>Wu Tang Forever</i>, his two-year-old son Dontae (now a hip-hop artist as iNTeLL) was struck by a stray bullet while on his way to a birthday party, suffering permanent damage to his kidneys and hands,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> an ordeal U-God documented on the Wu-Tang Clan track "A Better Tomorrow".
</p>
