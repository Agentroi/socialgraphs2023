					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American rapper</div>
<p><b>Vanda Bernadeau</b>, professionally known as <b>Dutch ReBelle</b>, is a rapper from <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston, Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_ReBelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ReBelle was born in <a href="/wiki/Hinche" title="Hinche">Hinche</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>. She was raised in <a href="/wiki/Mattapan" title="Mattapan">Mattapan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Milton,_Massachusetts" title="Milton, Massachusetts">Milton</a><a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">.</a> ReBelle began rapping at age 9. In college, she decided to pursue a music career. ReBelle was discovered while <a href="/wiki/Hairdresser" title="Hairdresser">doing</a> <a href="/wiki/Dreadlocks" title="Dreadlocks">locs</a> at the house of a customer whose roommate had a radio show, when she rapped spontaneously <a href="/wiki/Radio_broadcasting" title="Radio broadcasting">on air</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_ReBelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Regarding her stage name, ReBelle has stated, “When I was in high school, it kind of just happened that people started calling me <a href="/wiki/Von_Dutch" title="Von Dutch">Von Dutch</a> after the trucker hat line. Then it got shortened to Dutch in college, and when I started taking music seriously, I added the ReBelle to the end. It was an ode to my country, but it was also from the Lauryn Hill song “I Find It Hard To Say (Rebel)”. ReBelle played her first gig at <a href="/wiki/Boston_University" title="Boston University">Boston University</a>. She then went on to perform at the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Blues" title="House of Blues">House of Blues</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hard_Rock_Cafe" title="Hard Rock Cafe">Hard Rock Café</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, ReBelle performed at the Female Hip-Hop Honors in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, she was named <a href="/wiki/Boston_Music_Awards" title="Boston Music Awards">Boston Music Awards</a>’s Hip-Hop Artist of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, she performed at the first Boston Hip Hop Fest in a decade.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, ReBelle's song, "Rude Boys" was featured in <a href="/wiki/Rock_Band_4" title="Rock Band 4">Rock Band 4</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She's rapped at <a href="/wiki/Isabella_Stewart_Gardner_Museum" title="Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum">Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_Science_(Boston)" title="Museum of Science (Boston)">Museum of Science</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Planetarium" title="Planetarium">planetarium</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and Boston Cannabis Week's music festival.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In February 2020, she opened for <a href="/wiki/Rapsody" title="Rapsody">Rapsody</a> at The Sinclair.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In February 2023, ReBelle attended the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a> for the first time. She is a member of <a href="/wiki/The_Recording_Academy" title="The Recording Academy">The Recording Academy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>KillerBoomBox.com</i> wrote, "If <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn</a> had a baby, it would be Dutch."<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She was named <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_(magazine)" title="Boston (magazine)">Boston</a></i>'s Best Music Artist of 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/WBUR-FM" title="WBUR-FM">WBUR</a></i> dubbed her album, <i>Bang Bang,</i> one of the best local hip-hop albums in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In 2020, <i><a href="/wiki/Bandcamp" title="Bandcamp">Bandcamp</a></i> wrote, "ReBelle lays bars over <a href="/wiki/Trap_music" title="Trap music">trap beats</a>, with a hard edge comparable to <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Tennessee" title="Memphis, Tennessee">Memphis</a>’s <a href="/wiki/Gangsta_Boo" title="Gangsta Boo">Gangsta Boo</a> or Diamond from <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>’s <a href="/wiki/Crime_Mob" title="Crime Mob">Crime Mob</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>ReBelle has stated she is often the only woman in the room in hip-hop spaces and has to create her own space within these environments.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> She has said working in a music industry dominated by men is much easier than surviving other traumas she's experienced.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> She has faced discrimination when trying to book Boston venues as a hip-hop artist.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
