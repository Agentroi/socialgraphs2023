<p><b>Naeem Juwan Hanks</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Spank Rock</b>, is an American rapper and songwriter from <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>. He rose to fame with his 2006 album <i><a href="/wiki/YoYoYoYoYo" title="YoYoYoYoYo">YoYoYoYoYo</a></i>, which was produced by former group member Alex Epton (<a href="/wiki/XXXChange" title="XXXChange">XXXChange</a>). A harbinger of post-millennial alternative rap,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> the duo became known for its mixing of disparate <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Dance_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronic Dance Music">club genres</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_club" title="Baltimore club">Baltimore club</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Miami_bass" title="Miami bass">Miami bass</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-miaminewtimes_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-miaminewtimes-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Electro_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Electro music">electro music</a> and rock.
</p><p>In 2007, Epton left the group to pursue his own production<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> while Juwan went on to release the <i><a href="/wiki/Bangers_%26_Cash" title="Bangers &amp; Cash">Bangers &amp; Cash</a></i> EP (2007) with pop producer <a href="/wiki/Benny_Blanco" title="Benny Blanco">Benny Blanco</a>. After a five-year contract struggle with his label <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Records" title="Downtown Records">Downtown Records</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Juwan released his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Everything_Is_Boring_and_Everyone_Is_a_Fucking_Liar" title="Everything Is Boring and Everyone Is a Fucking Liar">Everything Is Boring and Everyone Is a Fucking Liar</a></i>, with a range of producers including <a href="/wiki/Boys_Noize" title="Boys Noize">Boys Noize</a>, <a href="/wiki/Le1f" title="Le1f">Le1f</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/XXXChange" title="XXXChange">XXXChange</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Squeak_E._Clean" class="mw-redirect" title="Squeak E. Clean">Squeak E. Clean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-discogs_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discogs-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spank_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Juwan grew up in West Baltimore in a row home alongside five sisters and two brothers.<sup id="cite_ref-ResidentAdvisor_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ResidentAdvisor-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> As a teenager, Juwan frequented clubs such as Paradox, where DJs played <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_club" title="Baltimore club">Baltimore club</a> music. In high school, his older sister introduced him to Brooklyn-based producer <a href="/wiki/J.Period" title="J.Period">J. Period</a>, known for producing records by underground rappers <a href="/wiki/Mos_Def" title="Mos Def">Mos Def</a> and <a href="/wiki/Artifacts_(group)" title="Artifacts (group)">Artifacts</a>. J. Period mentored Juwan and helped him improve his skills as a rapper for some time. After moving to Philadelphia and dropping out of college, Juwan met fellow Baltimore native Alex Epton, who had studied composition at the <a href="/wiki/New_England_Conservatory" class="mw-redirect" title="New England Conservatory">New England Conservatory of Music</a> in Boston and learned studio production as an intern at the <a href="/wiki/DFA_Records" title="DFA Records">DFA Records</a> studio in New York.<sup id="cite_ref-ResidentAdvisor_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ResidentAdvisor-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Juwan and Epton started performing under the name Spank Rock together in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phoenixville" class="mw-redirect" title="Phoenixville">Phoenixville</a>, where their reputation quickly increased. They later toured with Hollertronix and <a href="/wiki/M.I.A._(rapper)" title="M.I.A. (rapper)">M.I.A.</a> They also toured with <a href="/wiki/Beck" title="Beck">Beck</a> in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Spank Rock's debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/YoYoYoYoYo" title="YoYoYoYoYo">YoYoYoYoYo</a></i>, was released in 2006 on the <a href="/wiki/Big_Dada" title="Big Dada">Big Dada</a> record label.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It was nominated for the 2006 <a href="/wiki/Shortlist_Music_Prize" title="Shortlist Music Prize">Shortlist Music Prize</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nme_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nme-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, Alex Epton (<a href="/wiki/XXXChange" title="XXXChange">XXXChange</a>) left Spank Rock to pursue his own production while Naeem Juwan signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Records" title="Downtown Records">Downtown Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Spank Rock's first Downtown Records release came in the form of fall 2007's <i><a href="/wiki/Bangers_%26_Cash" title="Bangers &amp; Cash">Bangers &amp; Cash</a></i> EP, a <a href="/wiki/2_Live_Crew" title="2 Live Crew">2 Live Crew</a>-inspired collaborative EP with <a href="/wiki/Benny_Blanco" title="Benny Blanco">Benny Blanco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Spank Rock was featured, along with <a href="/wiki/The_View_(band)" title="The View (band)">The View</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kyle_Falconer" title="Kyle Falconer">Kyle Falconer</a>, on "<a href="/wiki/The_Bike_Song" title="The Bike Song">The Bike Song</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Ronson" title="Mark Ronson">Mark Ronson</a>, which appeared on Ronson's 2010 album <a href="/wiki/Record_Collection_(album)" title="Record Collection (album)">Record Collection</a>. Spank Rock toured with Ronson for the album's 2010 UK tour.
</p><p>The following year, Spank Rock released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Everything_Is_Boring_and_Everyone_Is_a_Fucking_Liar" title="Everything Is Boring and Everyone Is a Fucking Liar">Everything Is Boring and Everyone Is a Fucking Liar</a></i>, on his own label, Bad Blood Records.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork</a></i> premiered the first single from the album, titled "Energy," produced by <a href="/wiki/Boys_Noize" title="Boys Noize">Boys Noize</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-pitchforkpremiere_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchforkpremiere-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and later selected the single "Nasty" as a "Best New Track."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> premiered the album in September 2011 on their website.<sup id="cite_ref-rollingstone_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rollingstone-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The album featured production from Boys Noize, XXXChange, <a href="/wiki/Sam_Spiegel" title="Sam Spiegel">Sam Spiegel</a>, and Le1f, as well as appearances by <a href="/wiki/Santigold" title="Santigold">Santigold</a> and <a href="/wiki/Big_Freedia" title="Big Freedia">Big Freedia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-discogs_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discogs-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pitchforkpremiere_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchforkpremiere-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> After the release of his album, Spank Rock was selected to be one of the faces of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wang_(designer)" title="Alexander Wang (designer)">Alexander Wang</a>'s 2011 T collection, along with Santigold.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Spank Rock released a second EP, <i>E. I. B. A. E. I. A. F. L. Remixes</i>, a collection of various producers' remixes of tracks from <i>Everything Is Boring and Everyone Is a Fucking Liar</i>. He also co-wrote the Boys Noize-produced track "Look at These Hoes" from <a href="/wiki/Santigold" title="Santigold">Santigold</a>'s album, <a href="/wiki/Master_of_My_Make-Believe" title="Master of My Make-Believe">Master of My Make-Believe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-discogs_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discogs-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Look_at_These_Hoes_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Look_at_These_Hoes-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Spank Rock released his third EP, <i>The Upside</i>, in 2014 on Bad Blood Records and Boysnoize Records, with production contributions from Kid Kamillion, Boys Noize, and Damian Taylor. Lead single "Gully" was remixed by Brodinski and <a href="/wiki/Switch_(house_DJ)" class="mw-redirect" title="Switch (house DJ)">Switch</a>, and received extensive play on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Spank Rock began appearing in concerts in 2016 as an <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">MC</a> with <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> electronic music group <a href="/wiki/The_Avalanches" title="The Avalanches">The Avalanches</a>. He subsequently appeared in performances at <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella</a><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Vivid_Sydney" title="Vivid Sydney">Vivid Sydney</a><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> in 2017.
</p><p>Under the Naeem moniker, Juwan released a solo studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Startisha" title="Startisha">Startisha</a></i>, in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> It features contributions from <a href="/wiki/Justin_Vernon" title="Justin Vernon">Justin Vernon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Francis_and_the_Lights" title="Francis and the Lights">Francis and the Lights</a>, Ryan Olson, <a href="/wiki/Swamp_Dogg" title="Swamp Dogg">Swamp Dogg</a>, Velvet Negroni, <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Blank" title="Amanda Blank">Amanda Blank</a>, and Micah James.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
