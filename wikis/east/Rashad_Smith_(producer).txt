<p><b>Burton Rashad "Ringo" Smith</b> (born 
September 28, 1972) is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_and_blues" title="Rhythm and blues">R&amp;B</a> <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>. He was born in England and raised in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, United States, in a Jamaican and Haitian family. Rashad grew up alongside notable hip hop artists such as <a href="/wiki/Mos_Def" title="Mos Def">Mos Def</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Q-Tip_(musician)" title="Q-Tip (musician)">Q-Tip</a>, among others.
</p><p>At the age of 19, he provided beats to <a href="/wiki/Main_Source" title="Main Source">Main Source</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_B_and_Rakim" class="mw-redirect" title="Eric B and Rakim">Eric B and Rakim</a> and <a href="/wiki/A_Tribe_Called_Quest" title="A Tribe Called Quest">A Tribe Called Quest</a>. He proceeded further into the music business by working directly with <a href="/wiki/Andre_Harrell" title="Andre Harrell">Andre Harrell</a> at the newly minted <a href="/wiki/Uptown_Records" title="Uptown Records">Uptown Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> While at Uptown, Rashad met session keyboardist Avon Marshell with whom he formed Tumblin Dice. The duo went on to produce for Uptown's biggest artist's, including <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jodeci" title="Jodeci">Jodeci</a>'s   early albums. Tumblin Dice would go on to become resident producers for the recently formed <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy Records</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Sean "Puffy" Combs</a> requested Rashad join his production team <a href="/wiki/The_Hitmen_(production_team)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Hitmen (production team)">The Hitmen</a>. During this time, Rashad produced chart topping hits. Rashad continued his chart topping success after composing <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a> first hit single, "<a href="/wiki/Woo_Hah!!_Got_You_All_in_Check" class="mw-redirect" title="Woo Hah!! Got You All in Check">Woo Hah!! Got You All in Check</a>". Rashad has composed tracks for <a href="/wiki/Aaliyah" title="Aaliyah">Aaliyah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Lyte" title="MC Lyte">MC Lyte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foxy_Brown_(rapper)" title="Foxy Brown (rapper)">Foxy Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Das_EFX" title="Das EFX">Das EFX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a>, <a href="/wiki/En_Vogue" title="En Vogue">En Vogue</a>, <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>, and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a> and others.
</p><p>He is the cousin of producer and Main Source member <a href="/wiki/K-Cut_(producer)" class="mw-redirect" title="K-Cut (producer)">K-Cut</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-now_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-now-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
