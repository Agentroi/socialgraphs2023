<p><b>Christian Robert Felner</b> (known professionally as <b>Felly</b>) is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer from <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a> who is currently signed to Same Plate Entertainment (a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music" title="Sony Music">Sony Music</a>). He has released numerous EPs, mixtapes, and albums. His most recent album <i>Bad Radio</i> was released on December 9th, 2022.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Felly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Christian Felner<sup id="cite_ref-Bill2015_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bill2015-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was born<sup id="cite_ref-Courant2018_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Courant2018-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Trumbull,_Connecticut" title="Trumbull, Connecticut">Trumbull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLBreak_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBreak-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He graduated high school from <a href="/wiki/Fairfield_College_Preparatory_School" title="Fairfield College Preparatory School">Fairfield College Preparatory School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fairfield,_Connecticut" title="Fairfield, Connecticut">Fairfield, Connecticut</a> in 2013. He began releasing music while still in high school,<sup id="cite_ref-Student_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Student-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and also built a following on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> by making and selling beats.<sup id="cite_ref-Revolt2017_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Revolt2017-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After graduating, he went on to attend the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a> (USC) where he studied music. While in college, Felly founded 2273 Records with fellow USC attendees Jake Standley and Gyyps (with whom Felly frequently collaborates).<sup id="cite_ref-Equinox_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Equinox-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He graduated from USC in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLBreak_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBreak-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Felly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While still attending USC, Felly released several mixtapes and an EP. His first project to receive major attention came in May 2014 with the release of <i>Waking Up To Sirens</i>, which featured the single "Fabrics".<sup id="cite_ref-OKT_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OKT-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> This led to an appearance as an opener for <a href="/wiki/Madlib" title="Madlib">Madlib</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-After_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-After-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He followed that with a second mixtape, <i>Milk &amp; Sugar</i>, which was released in November 2014. That collection featured a guest appearance from <a href="/wiki/Ari_Lennox" title="Ari Lennox">Ari Lennox</a> on the track, "Tell Me".<sup id="cite_ref-Coog_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coog-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> His third mixtape, <i>This Shit Comes in Waves</i>, was released in July 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-XXLBreak_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBreak-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and featured the single "7th King".<sup id="cite_ref-Chiefers_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chiefers-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> That song was the namesake for his 2016 tour (the "7th King Tour") which ran for 21 dates in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-Equinox_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Equinox-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2016, Felly released an EP entitled, <i>Young Fel</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-EDM_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EDM-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and embarked on another tour in support of it.<sup id="cite_ref-Austin_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Austin-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> That collection featured the single "Desert Eagle" (featuring Gyyps), for which Felly filmed a music video.<sup id="cite_ref-UPR_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UPR-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, he would embark on the "Mermaid Gang" tour, which was named after the group formed by Felly, Gyyps, and Trip Carter. All three members performed on stage<sup id="cite_ref-Tonight_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tonight-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> during the 40-date tour.<sup id="cite_ref-Access_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Access-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Throughout 2017, Felly released numerous songs including "Ali",<sup id="cite_ref-XXLAli_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLAli-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> "Baby Boy",<sup id="cite_ref-XXLBaby_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLBaby-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> "Fight the Feeling",<sup id="cite_ref-HNHHFight_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HNHHFight-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> "Delta Deathbus to Zion" (featuring Peter $un),<sup id="cite_ref-SourceZion_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SourceZion-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> "Hotel Room", "Keep The Team Straight", and numerous others.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLHotel_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLHotel-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Some of the tracks were part of a summer series called <i>New Flavors</i> which saw Felly release a new song each Wednesday over that summer.<sup id="cite_ref-HNHHFight_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HNHHFight-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2017, he released his first studio album, <i>Wild Strawberries</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-XXLWild_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLWild-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> as a <a href="/wiki/Cinematic_Music_Group" title="Cinematic Music Group">Cinematic Music Group</a> signee.<sup id="cite_ref-XXLHotel_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLHotel-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The album featured the singles "Baby Boy", "Bag Season" (featuring Trip Carter), and "Wide Angle" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Konshens" title="Konshens">Konshens</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-XXLWild_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLWild-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It also featured other guest appearances from Gyyps and Frex.<sup id="cite_ref-HNHHWild_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HNHHWild-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In the fall of 2017, Felly opened for <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a> for two nights on his "Acoustic Tour".<sup id="cite_ref-XXLPain_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXLPain-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Felly then embarked on his own 20-date "Strawberry Season Tour". In March 2018, he released an EP called, <i>Winters in Brooklyn</i> featuring the single, "BLANCO".<sup id="cite_ref-Access_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Access-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September of that year, it was announced that Felly had been signed to Same Plate Entertainment, a joint venture with Sony Music. He released his first project on that label (<i>Surf Trap</i>) later that month. The lead single off the album was "Pretty Girl"<sup id="cite_ref-Bill2018_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bill2018-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> followed by "Miami".<sup id="cite_ref-HHC_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHC-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In 2019, Felly continued releasing new music with tracks like "At Fault" (with Medasin)<sup id="cite_ref-Fault_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fault-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and "Cheap Cheap" (with <a href="/wiki/Jack_Harlow" title="Jack Harlow">Jack Harlow</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-Cheap_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cheap-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> He was also featured on the <a href="/wiki/Max_Schneider" title="Max Schneider">MAX</a> song, "Acid Dreams", which was released in July 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-BillAcid_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillAcid-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In October of that year, Felly released the single, "Heartstrings", which featured <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Santana" title="Carlos Santana">Carlos Santana</a> on guitar.<sup id="cite_ref-BillHeart_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BillHeart-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
