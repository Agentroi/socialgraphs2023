<p><b>Niatia Jessica Kirkland</b> (born October 4, 1989), better known by her <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Lil Mama</b>, is an American rapper and actress. She experienced top 10 <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> placements at 17 with her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/VYP_(Voice_of_the_Young_People)" title="VYP (Voice of the Young People)">VYP (Voice of the Young People)</a></i> (2008), which debuted at number 25 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. The album spawned her four major singles including her staple song and dance anthem "<a href="/wiki/Lip_Gloss" title="Lip Gloss">Lip Gloss</a>", earning her two <a href="/wiki/Teen_Choice_Awards" title="Teen Choice Awards">Teen Choice Awards</a> and <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award_for_Monster_Single_of_the_Year" title="MTV Video Music Award for Monster Single of the Year">Monster Single of the Year</a> nominations at the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="MTV Video Music Awards">MTV Video Music Awards</a>. Kirkland gained further attention in <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop music</a> after a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Avril_Lavigne" title="Avril Lavigne">Avril Lavigne</a> for the remix of her hit single "<a href="/wiki/Girlfriend_(Avril_Lavigne_song)" title="Girlfriend (Avril Lavigne song)">Girlfriend</a>".
</p><p>Kirkland took a hiatus from music after success of her hit single "Hustler Girl" to stay focused on serving as a judge for total of seven seasons on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Best_Dance_Crew" title="America&#39;s Best Dance Crew">America's Best Dance Crew</a></i> at the age of 19 from 2008 to 2012, which made her one of the youngest judges on any competitive television series in history. Working with producer and fellow judge <a href="/wiki/Randy_Jackson" title="Randy Jackson">Randy Jackson</a>, she served alongside <a href="/wiki/JC_Chasez" title="JC Chasez">JC Chasez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shane_Sparks" title="Shane Sparks">Shane Sparks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mario_Lopez" title="Mario Lopez">Mario Lopez</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Layla_Kayleigh" title="Layla Kayleigh">Layla Kayleigh</a>.
</p><p>She was cast in her <a href="/wiki/Breakthrough_role" title="Breakthrough role">breakout role</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Lopes" title="Lisa Lopes">Lisa "Left Eye" Lopes</a> in the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> biographical film <i><a href="/wiki/CrazySexyCool:_The_TLC_Story" title="CrazySexyCool: The TLC Story">CrazySexyCool: The TLC Story</a></i>, which aired in October 2013 and garnered 4.5 million views in the first night. From the success of the film, Kirkland joined <a href="/wiki/TLC_(group)" title="TLC (group)">TLC</a> on their tour for their last album. She got the opportunity to perform a tribute to Lopes at the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2013" title="American Music Awards of 2013">2013 American Music Awards</a> performing the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Award</a>-winning song "<a href="/wiki/Waterfalls_(TLC_song)" title="Waterfalls (TLC song)">Waterfalls</a>".
</p><p>Kirkland continued to remain in the public eye, returning to music, making new tracks with fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/MC_Lyte" title="MC Lyte">MC Lyte</a> on her single "Ball". She made <i>Billboard</i> history again with her song "Sausage" at the <a href="/wiki/BET_Awards" title="BET Awards">BET Awards</a>. She appeared on multiple magazine covers and was back in television with <i><a href="/wiki/Growing_Up_Hip_Hop:_Atlanta" title="Growing Up Hip Hop: Atlanta">Growing Up Hip Hop: Atlanta</a></i>. In 2019, she starred in the film <i>All In</i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Traci_Braxton" title="Traci Braxton">Traci Braxton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-All_In_Movie_on_www.imdb.com_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All_In_Movie_on_www.imdb.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Mama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Niatia Jessica Kirkland was born on October 4, 1989, in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, and is the eldest daughter of eight children.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She was raised in <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a> from her kindergarten to her eighth-grade year. She moved back to Brooklyn and attended <a href="/wiki/Edward_R._Murrow_High_School" title="Edward R. Murrow High School">Edward R. Murrow High School</a> until her senior year where she continued her education through independent studies. Kirkland is a third generation of West African, Trinidadian, and Jamaican descent and the third born child. Being the oldest daughter, she got her nickname "Lil Mama". She has two older brothers and three younger brothers, one of whom is also a dancer and underground rapper along with her younger sister and last brother. She has one maternal aunt and several paternal uncles.
</p><p>Kirkland faced personal and financial struggles at an early age and experienced even more poverty when her mother, Tara, was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Breast_cancer" title="Breast cancer">breast cancer</a>, which often led Kirkland to struggle in school as she had to care for her siblings and see her mother back and forth in hospitals from procedures. Both her parents had separated when she was a young child, but Kirkland always maintained a close relationship with her father, visiting him consistently. Her family became homeless and their mother had moved her and her siblings into a shelter due to poverty at the time. Kirkland told <a href="/wiki/WWPR-FM" title="WWPR-FM">Power 105.1</a> she stayed in the shelters for seven years from her kindergarten year until the beginning of her eighth-grade year when they managed to get an apartment in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a> and gain more stability. Her younger sibling's father was deceased, so her mother was the matriarch of her family. She contributed to helping her mother financially by taking up small jobs such as baking. Growing up, Kirkland witnessed many hardships in her community including gang and drug violence, teen pregnancy, and domestic violence.
</p><p>After her mother's fight with cancer became terminal, her mother moved in with Kirkland's aunt and Tara's older sister. She stayed there so she could be cared for and help with the care of the younger children in her family.
</p><p>As one of eight children, she began to express herself artistically by writing <a href="/wiki/Poetry" title="Poetry">poetry</a> and music. She trained at dance schools for <a href="/wiki/Ballet" title="Ballet">ballet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz_dance" title="Jazz dance">jazz dance</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tap_dance" title="Tap dance">tap dance</a>, South African dance, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_dance" title="Hip hop dance">hip hop dance</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Street_dance" title="Street dance">street dance</a>; she was in many competitions and recitals making her a professional dancer. She was influenced by <a href="/wiki/MC_Lyte" title="MC Lyte">MC Lyte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Lopes" title="Lisa Lopes">Lisa Lopes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a>, and her parents. Her mother was a talented singer and her father Allen Brunner was a musician and DJ. Kirkland decided to explore her talents further when she took interest in the rap culture, and from then on she continued to perfect her talent in rapping and managed to become an avid free-stylist.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Mama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.932012:_VYP_.28Voice_of_the_Young_People.29_and_turmoil"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–2012:_VYP_(Voice_of_the_Young_People)_and_turmoil">2006–2012: <i>VYP (Voice of the Young People)</i> and turmoil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Mama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006–2012: VYP (Voice of the Young People) and turmoil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Lil_Mama.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Lil_Mama.jpg/220px-Lil_Mama.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Lil_Mama.jpg/330px-Lil_Mama.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Lil_Mama.jpg/440px-Lil_Mama.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="960" /></a><figcaption>Lil Mama performing in 2008</figcaption></figure>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Lil_Mama_Wiki.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Lil_Mama_Wiki.jpg/220px-Lil_Mama_Wiki.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Lil_Mama_Wiki.jpg/330px-Lil_Mama_Wiki.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Lil_Mama_Wiki.jpg/440px-Lil_Mama_Wiki.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2304" data-file-height="3456" /></a><figcaption>Lil Mama at the <a href="/wiki/Black_Girls_Rock!" title="Black Girls Rock!">Black Girls Rock!</a> and Soul Tour in New York City, 2011</figcaption></figure>
<p>Kirkland became known as the "Voice of the Young People" for being a teen sensation and connecting with her younger fans as she continued to present herself as that and embrace it in her music videos and songs. She became the "Queen of Hip-Pop" for mixing her rhymes with pop influences, which started after her collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Avril_Lavigne" title="Avril Lavigne">Avril Lavigne</a>. She first introduced herself as the "Birth of Hip-Pop" at the <a href="/wiki/2007_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2007 MTV Video Music Awards">2007 MTV Video Music Awards</a> sporting a baby-styled look. She later told <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> how she intended to spread and influence more artists to spread the genre of <a href="/wiki/Pop_rap" title="Pop rap">hip-pop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After her 2009 re-release of her single "L.I.F.E.", Kirkland introduced her fans to a whole new side of the darker reality that young people face. The song was followed by a music video that incorporated the themes of the song which included teen pregnancy, drug addiction, child abandonment, domestic violence, and more. This showed Kirkland as a legitimate rapper for even adults to listen to.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Since the release of her first album, Kirkland has been in and out of studios making new music. She released the single "Doughboy" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Mishon" class="mw-redirect" title="Mishon">Mishon</a>). After that release she made appearances on tracks with other artists, including "Turn It Up" by Mishon and "Sexiest" by Yahaira. She also released "On &amp; On &amp; On" and "NY NY LA LA" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The two singles were intended to be a part of her second studio album, but instead they were released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> and digital download but were later removed for unknown reasons. She then released two more singles entitled "Scrawberry" and "Hustler Girl", both singles were followed by music videos. "Scrawberry" introduced a new look for Kirkland, in which she would wear colorful wigs and futuristic attire; this would be her new style for next few years; however, the song was released on her <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">MySpace</a> page and <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, it was unclear if it was removed from streaming services along with her other two singles.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> "Hustler Girl" garnered $1.5 million which topped the income she received from her <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> hits "<a href="/wiki/Shawty_Get_Loose" title="Shawty Get Loose">Shawty Get Loose</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Lip_Gloss" title="Lip Gloss">Lip Gloss</a>", which earned her $1 million and $800,000.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Her next collaboration to follow, "Billionaire" by Prince Malik, would earn her $1.8 million.
</p><p>Kirkland was featured on <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/A_Milli" title="A Milli">A Milli</a>"; she was brought on stage at his concerts to perform the song. Kirkland was  featured in the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Award</a>-nominated album <i><a href="/wiki/First_Love_(Karina_Pasian_album)" title="First Love (Karina Pasian album)">First Love</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Karina_Pasian" title="Karina Pasian">Karina Pasian</a>, a close friend to Kirkland and a veteran music artist. In 2009, she announced the title of her proposed sophomore album as <i>Voice of the Young People: I Am That</i><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> after her single "What It Is (Strike a Pose)" failed to chart. The song was the last single with a music video to be added to her album and was used to commercialize the album. The song failed to chart.
</p><p>On October 7, 2012, <a href="/wiki/RCA_Music_Group" title="RCA Music Group">RCA Music Group</a> announced it was disbanding <a href="/wiki/J_Records" title="J Records">J Records</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>. With the shutdown, Kirkland (and all other artists previously signed to these three labels) would release future material on the <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> brand. However, Kirkland had left Jive before the transition to RCA; she stated in an interview that she was not in the right space to make music and decided to take a hiatus.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <i>Voice of the Young People: I Am That</i> would have had guest appearances including <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Boy" title="Soulja Boy">Soulja Boy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Khalil_(singer)" title="Khalil (singer)">Khalil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angel_Haze" title="Angel Haze">Angel Haze</a>, <a href="/wiki/LoLa_Monroe" title="LoLa Monroe">LoLa Monroe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Teyana_Taylor" title="Teyana Taylor">Teyana Taylor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trina" title="Trina">Trina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keke_Palmer" title="Keke Palmer">Keke Palmer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, among others. Due to her leaving Jive, the album never saw a release and instead it along with the few singles recorded with <a href="/wiki/Zomba_Group_of_Companies" title="Zomba Group of Companies">Zomba Records</a> were shelved, but she managed to release some the songs directly online for her fans which included "Scrawberry", "On &amp; On &amp; On", and "NY NY LA LA". The other singles she planned to record with the featured artist were never recorded and were disbanded. After leaving Jive, Kirkland became an independent artist.
</p><p>On June 13, 2012, <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Best_Dance_Crew" title="America&#39;s Best Dance Crew">America's Best Dance Crew</a></i> came to a series finale. Kirkland had served as a judge for a total of seven seasons. The following year, she released a single titled "Bad as Me" on  <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> and YouTube.
</p>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932017:_CrazySexyCool:_The_TLC_Story.2C_.22Sausage.22.2C_Take_Me_Back.2C_and_When_Love_Kills:_The_Falicia_Blakely_Story"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2017:_CrazySexyCool:_The_TLC_Story,_&quot;Sausage&quot;,_Take_Me_Back,_and_When_Love_Kills:_The_Falicia_Blakely_Story">2013–2017: <i>CrazySexyCool: The TLC Story</i>, "Sausage", <i>Take Me Back</i>, and <i>When Love Kills: The Falicia Blakely Story</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Mama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013–2017: CrazySexyCool: The TLC Story, &quot;Sausage&quot;, Take Me Back, and When Love Kills: The Falicia Blakely Story">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2013, Kirkland starred as <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Lopes" title="Lisa Lopes">Lisa "Left Eye" Lopes</a> in the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> biographical film <i><a href="/wiki/CrazySexyCool:_The_TLC_Story" title="CrazySexyCool: The TLC Story">CrazySexyCool: The TLC Story</a></i>, which aired on October 21, 2013, alongside <a href="/wiki/Keke_Palmer" title="Keke Palmer">Keke Palmer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drew_Sidora" title="Drew Sidora">Drew Sidora</a>, who played <a href="/wiki/Rozonda_Thomas" title="Rozonda Thomas">Rozonda "Chilli" Thomas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tionne_Watkins" title="Tionne Watkins">Tionne "T-Boz" Watkins</a>, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The film generated 4.5 million views in one night. This was her first acting debut and breakout role, in which she received positive feedback from Lopes' fans. Kirkland was the first to be cast and from both the success of the film and the close relationship she formed with Watkins and Thomas. <a href="/wiki/TLC_(group)" title="TLC (group)">TLC</a> took a chance on Kirkland, allowing her to substitute for Lopes. TLC took Kirkland on tour, and Kirkland carried out Lopes' legacy by rapping her verses. Kirkland's journey with TLC came to end after they performed at the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2013" title="American Music Awards of 2013">2013 American Music Awards</a>. Kirkland and TLC dedicated a performance of "<a href="/wiki/Waterfalls_(TLC_song)" title="Waterfalls (TLC song)">Waterfalls</a>" to Lopes. After the tribute, Kirkland and TLC parted ways, and Kirkland went on to return to making music.
</p><p>In November 2014, Kirkland and AV were featured on <a href="/wiki/MC_Lyte" title="MC Lyte">MC Lyte</a>'s song "Ball" from her album <i><a href="/wiki/Legend_(MC_Lyte_album)" title="Legend (MC Lyte album)">Legend</a></i>. The music video was released on November 13, 2014. Kirkland appeared in the video with both artists, providing rap verses, along with some of her signature dance moves.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> That same year, she performed the song live with AV and MC Lyte on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Real_(talk_show)" title="The Real (talk show)">The Real</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In May 2015, Kirkland released the video for her song "Sausage" on <a href="/wiki/WorldStarHipHop" title="WorldStarHipHop">WorldStarHipHop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The song was inspired by the #SausageMovement on <a href="/wiki/Vine_(service)" title="Vine (service)">Vine</a><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> after Kirkland heard two girls on a street corner following the trend. The video incorporates many themes including <i><a href="/wiki/Teenage_Mutant_Ninja_Turtles" title="Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles">Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Vogue_(dance)" title="Vogue (dance)">voguing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a>, Caribbean love, and community fun. It immediately went viral, with more than 3 million views in its first week, topping the <i>Billboard</i> + Twitter Top Tracks charts, passing <a href="/wiki/Maroon_5" title="Maroon 5">Maroon 5</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Sugar_(Maroon_5_song)" title="Sugar (Maroon 5 song)">Sugar</a>" and knocking out <a href="/wiki/Jason_Derulo" title="Jason Derulo">Jason Derulo</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Want_to_Want_Me" title="Want to Want Me">Want to Want Me</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It held itself steady in the top 20 well into June, with numerous dance studio and dance jams featuring choreography and routines on social media danced to the track.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kirkland got a chance to perform the song live during half time of the <a href="/wiki/2015_BET_Hip_Hop_Awards" title="2015 BET Hip Hop Awards">2015 BET Hip Hop Awards</a>. The track was not made available for sale, being absent from both iTunes and <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Music" title="Amazon Music">Amazon Music</a> due to copyright issues;<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> the track was only available on Kirkland's <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> account and her website.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The video also opted out of advertising revenue on YouTube, with no advertisements appearing before the music video.<sup id="cite_ref-youtube.com_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The following year, she released another single "Memes" as a response to memes made of her after her notorious interview on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Breakfast_Club_(radio_show)" title="The Breakfast Club (radio show)">The Breakfast Club</a></i>. Kirkland released <i>Take Me Back</i>, a mixtape which featured "Sausage" and "Memes". The mixtape was later up for sale on her website and SoundCloud account, it featured seven songs including a cover and re-edition of <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Work_(Rihanna_song)" title="Work (Rihanna song)">Work</a>". The song received mixed reviews online both on YouTube and her website. People were not fond of her vocals on the hook, but adored her choreography. The last song to be added was "Too Fly", which caught less attraction after her website was taken down for unknown reasons. The music video was filmed at a club featuring her younger brother Arnstar as one of the dancers. She also produced her singing vocals on the hook. The song was also another copyright issue that couldn't be made for sale and solely released through her mixtape and her SoundCloud account. The music video is a YouTube exclusive.
</p><p>After her acting debut, Kirkland made what <a href="/wiki/SOHH" title="SOHH">SOHH</a> called an "epic comeback". Kirkland was back on red carpets, award shows, talk shows, and magazine covers. In 2016, Kirkland made an appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_Squares" title="Hip Hop Squares">Hip Hop Squares</a></i> as a contestant; the episode was highlighted because of the controversy <a href="/wiki/Tamar_Braxton" title="Tamar Braxton">Tamar Braxton</a> started with the host.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> That same year, Kirkland, alongside <a href="/wiki/Dej_Loaf" title="Dej Loaf">Dej Loaf</a>, gave a tribute to <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a> at VH1's <a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_Honors" title="Hip Hop Honors">Hip Hop Honors</a>, Lil' Kim later thanked Kirkland with a post of the tribute on her <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> account.
</p><p>In June 2015, Kirkland was listed as a featured speaker at an event sponsored by <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a> and the Hip Hop Sisters Network. Titled "Women, Wealth and Relationships", the event also featured guest speakers MC Lyte, <a href="/wiki/Shanice" title="Shanice">Shanice</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Elise_Neal" title="Elise Neal">Elise Neal</a>, as well as main speaker and financial advisor specialist Lynn Richardson.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The event, held on June 27, 2015, focuses on empowering black women both in their lives and financially, providing answers on such tough issues as men, money, and family relationships.
</p><p>On August 28, 2017, Kirkland returned to acting, starring in the television film <i>When Love Kills: The Falicia Blakely Story</i>, based on a true story.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> It premiered to 1.6 million viewers, ranking as <a href="/wiki/TV_One_(American_TV_channel)" title="TV One (American TV channel)">TV One</a>'s #1 original movie premiere of all time among all key demos.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Kirkland, along with director <a href="/wiki/Tasha_Smith" title="Tasha Smith">Tasha Smith</a> and her co-stars <a href="/wiki/Tami_Roman" title="Tami Roman">Tami Roman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Gross" title="Lance Gross">Lance Gross</a>, went on a tour to promote the film and share Falicia Blakely's story.
</p>
<h3><span id="2018.E2.80.93present:_.22Shoe_Game.22_and_Growing_Up_Hip_Hop:_Atlanta"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–present:_&quot;Shoe_Game&quot;_and_Growing_Up_Hip_Hop:_Atlanta">2018–present: "Shoe Game" and <i>Growing Up Hip Hop: Atlanta</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Mama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2018–present: &quot;Shoe Game&quot; and Growing Up Hip Hop: Atlanta">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 17, 2018, Kirkland became competitor on MTV's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Challenge_(TV_series)#The_Challenge:_Champs_vs._Stars" title="The Challenge (TV series)">The Challenge: Champs vs. Stars</a></i>, a competitive television miniseries in which celebrities would team up with pro-athlete champions against each other. There, she met <a href="/wiki/Drake_Bell" title="Drake Bell">Drake Bell</a>, and the two would later go on to collaborate on his track "Call Me When You're Lonely".
</p><p>On May 3, 2018, Kirkland released a single titled "Shoe Game".<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The single was available everywhere online for sale, streaming, and digital download. The single was later followed by a music video, directed by Walu, on May 25, 2018. It became a YouTube exclusive, following a million and twenty thousand views within five months.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Kirkland went on to promote the single on the morning edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Request_Live" title="Total Request Live">Total Request Live</a></i> in late September informing that this will lead to a future album. She was then followed up by <a href="/wiki/Brynn_Elliott" title="Brynn Elliott">Brynn Elliott</a> after discussion of her old album and viewing of her greatest music videos which included "Lip Gloss", "Shawty Get Loose", and "<a href="/wiki/G-Slide_(Tour_Bus)" title="G-Slide (Tour Bus)">G-Slide (Tour Bus)</a>".
</p><p>On September 9, 2018, Kirkland was a presenter for the 2018 BET special <a href="/wiki/Black_Girls_Rock!" title="Black Girls Rock!">Black Girls Rock!</a>; she had the privilege to work with artists such as <a href="/wiki/Queen_Latifah" title="Queen Latifah">Queen Latifah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">Janet Jackson</a>, Mary J. Blige, etc.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> She presented the award to Shanay Thompson for her M.A.D. Girls celebrated by <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On September 25, 2018,<i> <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Tonight" title="Entertainment Tonight">Entertainment Tonight</a></i> announced that Kirkland would join the cast of <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/All_American_(TV_series)" title="All American (TV series)">All American</a></i> as a supporting character; this would be her first acting role since <i>When Love Kills: The Falicia Blakely Story</i>.
</p><p>On October 4, 2018, Kirkland appeared on TV One's morning talk show <i>Sister Circle</i> to discuss her latest single "Shoe Game", and her being cast on <i><a href="/wiki/Growing_Up_Hip_Hop:_Atlanta" title="Growing Up Hip Hop: Atlanta">Growing Up Hip Hop: Atlanta</a></i>. There, she stated that she will be joining the cast as a regular due to her move to <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, to work on her upcoming album that <a href="/wiki/Bow_Wow_(rapper)" title="Bow Wow (rapper)">Bow Wow</a> will be assisting to co-produce and allow the series to document her journey as well as her experiences. She also got to discuss her acting career, as well as her taking vocal lessons from R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Price" title="Kelly Price">Kelly Price</a>; her singing will debut on the intended album.
</p><p>On October 18, 2018, <i>Growing Up Hip Hop: Atlanta</i> premiered for the continuation of its second season. Kirkland made her debut on the first episode alongside Kiyomi Leslie and <a href="/wiki/Masika_Kalysha" title="Masika Kalysha">Masika Kalysha</a> as replacement for cast members who quit. Kirkland stars alongside her mentor and new manager MC Lyte.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On November 14, 2018, Kirkland made her debut appearance on <i>All American</i> in its fifth episode. She introduced her character, Chynna Q, an upcoming hip hop artist.
</p><p>On June 13, 2019, she starred in the film <i>All In</i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Traci_Braxton" title="Traci Braxton">Traci Braxton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-All_In_Movie_on_www.imdb.com_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All_In_Movie_on_www.imdb.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Mama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kirkland's mother, Tara, died on December 15, 2007, following a four-year battle with <a href="/wiki/Breast_cancer" title="Breast cancer">breast cancer</a>. Kirkland's music video for "Shawty Get Loose" was dedicated to her memory; the single won her a <a href="/wiki/Teen_Choice_Awards" title="Teen Choice Awards">Teen Choice Award</a>. Tara also happened to be featured in the intro and closing of Kirkland's music video for her hit single "Lip Gloss". Kirkland had taken over guardianship over her younger siblings along with the help of her family and older brothers. She spoke about this at the red carpet for MTV.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Kirkland has stated she and her mother had a close relationship and her mother relied on her for the help in raising her younger siblings.
</p><p>Kirkland is the older sister of underground rapper and dancer Arnstar, who was a regular on MTV's <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_%27n_Out" title="Wild &#39;n Out">Wild 'n Out</a></i>. He is also dancer in the New York City street dancing crew W.A.F.F.L.E. Their crew garnered more recognition after making their appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">The Ellen DeGeneres Show</a></i> and received a cash reward from DeGeneres herself.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lil_Mama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2009_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2009 MTV Video Music Awards">2009 MTV Video Music Awards</a>, Kirkland came onstage while <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a> performed "<a href="/wiki/Empire_State_of_Mind" title="Empire State of Mind">Empire State of Mind</a>". While Jay-Z was ending his final verse, Kirkland left her seat in the audience, got onto the stage, and started bobbing her head to the beat. Jay-Z was surprised, but continued to perform. Right before this moment, <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a> had tried to hold her from going up. Jay-Z patted Kirkland on the leg to fall back, jokingly telling her, "you <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Paining</a> now," a reference to a similar incident two months earlier when T-Pain jumped on stage with Jay-Z at <a href="/wiki/Summer_Jam_(festival)" title="Summer Jam (festival)">Summer Jam</a> during his performance of "<a href="/wiki/D.O.A._(Death_of_Auto-Tune)" title="D.O.A. (Death of Auto-Tune)">D.O.A. (Death of Auto-Tune)</a>". At the end of the performance, Kirkland came to foreground and posed alongside Jay-Z and Keys. She later clarified that she "would never ... try to disrespect Jay-Z or take a moment that someone has created it and try to relive it. I'm too original for that and I respect him too much for that."<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> In October 2009, she appeared in <a href="/wiki/America%27s_Next_Top_Model_(season_13)" title="America&#39;s Next Top Model (season 13)">cycle 13</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Next_Top_Model" title="America&#39;s Next Top Model">America's Next Top Model</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Benny_Ninja" title="Benny Ninja">Benny Ninja</a>.
</p><p>On August 11, 2011, Kirkland was a guest on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Breakfast_Club_(radio_show)" title="The Breakfast Club (radio show)">The Breakfast Club</a></i>. While on the air, tensions rose between her and fellow DJ <a href="/wiki/Charlamagne_tha_God" title="Charlamagne tha God">Charlamagne tha God</a> as they exchanged jabs, with Charlamagne bringing up her VMAs incident and the controversy between her and <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>. Two more DJs, <a href="/wiki/Angela_Yee" title="Angela Yee">Angela Yee</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Envy" title="DJ Envy">DJ Envy</a>, chimed in, discussing her absence from music at the time. After some time, Kirkland broke down in tears when her mother's passing was brought up.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Kirkland crying spawned numerous memes around the internet.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Five years later, Kirkland returned to <i>The Breakfast Club</i> to explain why she cried, and the DJs and Kirkland exchanged apologies with one another.
</p><p>On March 10, 2016, Kirkland was arrested for driving 38&#160;mph in a 25&#160;mph speed zone with a revoked license. The incident took place in Harlem at around 4 a.m. Officers conducted an investigation and Kirkland's driver's license was revoked. She went on to make a statement apologizing to her family, friends, and fans.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bow Wow set Kirkland on blind date with his friend BT, a radio personality, in December 2018, and caused controversy by allegedly saying that "Y'all gonna be f**king in a week."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> This was aired on their series <i><a href="/wiki/Growing_Up_Hip_Hop:_Atlanta" title="Growing Up Hip Hop: Atlanta">Growing Up Hip Hop: Atlanta</a></i>, and Kirkland posted on Instagram to address the episode, calling out Bow Wow and his girlfriend.
</p>
