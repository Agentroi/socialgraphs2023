<p><b>Heather B. Gardner</b> (born November 13, 1970),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> billed professionally as <b>Heather B.</b>, is an American rapper, reality television personality, and radio host who first gained fame as a member of the hip hop group <a href="/wiki/Boogie_Down_Productions" title="Boogie Down Productions">Boogie Down Productions</a> before becoming a cast member on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Real_World:_New_York" title="The Real World: New York">The Real World: New York</a></i>, the 1992 inaugural season of <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s reality show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Real_World_(TV_series)" title="The Real World (TV series)">The Real World</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heather_B._Gardner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From February 16 to May 18, 1992, Gardner filmed <i><a href="/wiki/The_Real_World:_New_York" title="The Real World: New York">The Real World: New York</a></i>, the first season of MTV's reality television series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Real_World_(TV_series)" title="The Real World (TV series)">The Real World</a></i>, on which she appeared as a cast member.<sup id="cite_ref-RWHouses_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RWHouses-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The series premiered May 21 that year. On the show, she was depicted as a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip-hop</a> artist with the group <a href="/wiki/Boogie_Down_Productions" title="Boogie Down Productions">Boogie Down Productions</a>, on the verge of getting her big career break.<sup id="cite_ref-MTVBio_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTVBio-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> During the season, she is seen recording her first album.<sup id="cite_ref-MTVBio_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTVBio-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> According to MTV's biography for her: "She has a lot of drive and dedication to whatever she is doing. She makes friends quickly and always speaks her mind regardless of the consequences."<sup id="cite_ref-MTVBio_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTVBio-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Gardner was affiliated with the rap group Boogie Down Productions,<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and was signed to <a href="/wiki/Pendulum_Records" title="Pendulum Records">Pendulum Records</a> in 1995. In 1996, she released her first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Takin%27_Mine" title="Takin&#39; Mine">Takin' Mine</a></i>. The single "All Glocks Down", an anti-gun violence anthem, received radio play, as did the follow-up single "If Headz Only Knew."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Gardner scored a minor role in the 1995 film <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Presidents" title="Dead Presidents">Dead Presidents</a></i> as "Peaches". In 1997, Gardner signed with <a href="/wiki/MCA_Records" title="MCA Records">MCA Records</a> and in 1998 she released the single, "Do You," which had a considerable amount of television and radio airplay. In 2002 Gardner produced her second album, <i>Eternal Affairs</i>, with production from Pete Rock and DJ Premier. The album met with positive reviews despite not charting on <i>Billboard</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-AllHipHop_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllHipHop-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Outside of her music career, Gardner has appeared in various projects and television specials related to <i>The Real World</i>. She also appeared in the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wedding_Video_(2003_film)" title="The Wedding Video (2003 film)">The Wedding Video</a></i> directed by <i>Real World</i> alum <a href="/wiki/Norman_Korpi" title="Norman Korpi">Norman Korpi</a>, and a 2005 television commercial for <a href="/wiki/America_Online" class="mw-redirect" title="America Online">America Online</a> anti-virus software.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Gardner's third studio album, <i>Open Bar</i>, which was executive-produced by DJ Premier.<sup id="cite_ref-AllHipHop_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllHipHop-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2012, Gardner is the co-host of the <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Satellite_Radio" title="Sirius Satellite Radio">Sirius Satellite Radio</a> show <i>Sway in the Morning</i> with former <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> reporter <a href="/wiki/Sway_Calloway" title="Sway Calloway">Sway Calloway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2016 the duo appeared in the twelfth episode, "Soliloquy of Chaos,"  of <i><a href="/wiki/Luke_Cage_(TV_series)" title="Luke Cage (TV series)">Luke Cage</a></i>, interviewing <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, about being rescued by Cage during a hold-up of a Harlem bodega.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She appeared as herself in the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Willis" title="Bruce Willis">Bruce Willis</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Wish_(2018_film)" title="Death Wish (2018 film)">Death Wish</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2021, she portrayed the character Aunt Ruth in the <a href="/wiki/Jussie_Smollett" title="Jussie Smollett">Jussie Smollett</a> movie <i>B-Boy Blues</i>, Smollett's adaptation of the novel series written by <a href="/wiki/James_Earl_Hardy" title="James Earl Hardy">James Earl Hardy</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heather_B._Gardner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gardner married fellow hiphop artist born E. Gray, a former member of the group <a href="/wiki/Bravehearts" title="Bravehearts">Bravehearts</a> who goes by the professional name Horse. In a 2010  interview with <i>The Urban Daily</i> Gardner related that she met Gray on a subway train 15 years prior, and that they had been married for nine years, commenting, "It's like everybody else's house and we're a real family. We got a dog and pain-in-the-ass neighbors. He did music and I do music."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
