<p><b>Christopher Francis Ries</b> (born March 9, 1983), known professionally as <b>Young Chris</b>, is an American rapper. He is a member of <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> rap duo <a href="/wiki/Young_Gunz" title="Young Gunz">Young Gunz</a> alongside his childhood friend, rapper Neef Buck, signed to <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-A-Fella Records</a>. The duo saw their greatest success with their 2003 debut single "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Stop,_Won%27t_Stop_(Young_Gunz_song)" title="Can&#39;t Stop, Won&#39;t Stop (Young Gunz song)">Can't Stop, Won't Stop</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-billboard1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The duo released two albums: <i><a href="/wiki/Tough_Luv" title="Tough Luv">Tough Luv</a></i> (2004) and <i><a href="/wiki/Brothers_from_Another" title="Brothers from Another">Brothers from Another</a></i> (2005) and were a part of <a href="/wiki/Beanie_Sigel" title="Beanie Sigel">Beanie Sigel</a>'s <a href="/wiki/State_Property_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="State Property (group)">State Property</a> collective.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Young Chris was signed in December 2010 as a solo artist to <a href="/wiki/Universal_Motown" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Motown">Universal Motown</a> through producer/songwriter <a href="/wiki/Rico_Love" title="Rico Love">Rico Love</a>'s label imprint Division1.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He released two albums through Division1: <i>LIFE: Ladies In For Free</i> and <i>The Revival</i>, both in 2011. In 2014 he released the album <i>Gunna Season</i> through Jaccpot Entertainment. He has also had a large number of self-released mixtapes from 2007 to the present.
</p><p>Young Chris is often confused with the rapper <a href="/wiki/YC_(rapper)" title="YC (rapper)">YC</a>, who used to perform as "Yung Chris", and is thus sometimes incorrectly credited as the performer of the YC song "<a href="/wiki/Racks_(song)" title="Racks (song)">Racks</a>".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Young_Chris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Young_Gunz">Young Gunz</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Young_Chris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Young Gunz">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Young Chris and <a href="/wiki/Neef_Buck" class="mw-redirect" title="Neef Buck">Neef Buck</a> (born Hanif Muhammad) had been friends since they were young boys on the block (C-Ave).<sup id="cite_ref-big_shots_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-big_shots-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  On "<a href="/wiki/Takeover" title="Takeover">Takeover</a>", a track from his 2001 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blueprint" title="The Blueprint">The Blueprint</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> announced the duo as "Chris &amp; Neef".<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
In 2001, the duo performed on the <i><a href="/wiki/State_Property_(soundtrack)" title="State Property (soundtrack)">State Property</a></i> soundtrack album.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Young Chris and Neef starred in the titular movie.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Also during this period of their career, Young Chris appeared throughout Dame Dash's <i>Dream Team</i> compilation, and Beanie Sigel's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Reason_(Beanie_Sigel_album)" title="The Reason (Beanie Sigel album)">The Reason</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The duo performed together on guest appearances for albums like Jay-Z's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blueprint_2" class="mw-redirect" title="The Blueprint 2">The Blueprint 2</a></i>, Freeway's <i><a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Freeway" title="Philadelphia Freeway">Philadelphia Freeway</a></i>, State Property's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chain_Gang_Vol._2" title="The Chain Gang Vol. 2">The Chain Gang Vol. 2</a></i>, and Memphis Bleek's <i><a href="/wiki/M.A.D.E." title="M.A.D.E.">M.A.D.E.</a></i>; all while recording their album.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The Young Gunz scored their first hit with "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Stop,_Won%27t_Stop_(Young_Gunz_song)" title="Can&#39;t Stop, Won&#39;t Stop (Young Gunz song)">Can't Stop, Won't Stop</a>", the lead-off single from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chain_Gang_Vol._2" title="The Chain Gang Vol. 2">The Chain Gang Vol. 2</a></i> album.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Its song and video received major airplay on hip-hop radio, <a href="/wiki/MTV2" title="MTV2">MTV2</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Entertainment Television">BET</a> and reached No. 14 on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart and #6 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> Hot Rap Tracks chart.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard1_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the success of their single, "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Stop,_Won%27t_Stop_(Young_Gunz_song)" title="Can&#39;t Stop, Won&#39;t Stop (Young Gunz song)">Can't Stop, Won't Stop</a>", the label jumped and was ready to release their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Tough_Luv" title="Tough Luv">Tough Luv</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It included the remix to "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Stop,_Won%27t_Stop_(Young_Gunz_song)" title="Can&#39;t Stop, Won&#39;t Stop (Young Gunz song)">Can't Stop, Won't Stop</a>", which featured <a href="/wiki/St._Louis" title="St. Louis">St. Louis</a> rapper <a href="/wiki/Chingy" title="Chingy">Chingy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic1_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Singles included "No Better Love" featuring former <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-A-Fella Records</a> crooner <a href="/wiki/Gerrell_Gaddis" class="mw-redirect" title="Gerrell Gaddis">Rell</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Just_Blaze" title="Just Blaze">Just Blaze</a>-produced "Friday Night".<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic1_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Tough_Luv" title="Tough Luv">Tough Luv</a></i> debuted on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at No. 3 after selling 128,000 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard1_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  That week, the album at No. 2 was labelmate <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s debut album <i><a href="/wiki/The_College_Dropout" title="The College Dropout">The College Dropout</a></i>, which was released just two weeks earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After a brief hiatus, the duo returned with <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a> produced "<a href="/wiki/Set_It_Off_(Young_Gunz_song)" title="Set It Off (Young Gunz song)">Set It Off</a>" from their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Brothers_from_Another" title="Brothers from Another">Brothers from Another</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It was the second release from the "new" <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-A-Fella Records</a>, referencing when <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> became president of <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam Records">Def Jam Records</a>, the first being <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Bleek" title="Memphis Bleek">Memphis Bleek</a>'s <a href="/wiki/534_(album)" title="534 (album)">534</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The album debuted on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at No. 15, and included guest features from <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slim_(singer)" title="Slim (singer)">Slim</a> of <a href="/wiki/112_(band)" title="112 (band)">112</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Legend" title="John Legend">John Legend</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Young_Chris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As early as March 2006, Young Chris thought about recording a solo album. He initially titled it <i>Now or Never</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He stated to <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> magazine that he wanted to release a solo album by the end of 2008 and had been "95 percent done" by June 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-thomas1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thomas1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>  In this article, Young Chris also states that he himself up there with <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Wayne" class="mw-redirect" title="Lil&#39; Wayne">Lil' Wayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juelz_Santana" title="Juelz Santana">Juelz Santana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-thomas1_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thomas1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Since this time, Chris has taken to the internet to solidify himself as a top solo artist, with his YoungChris.com Social Network, hitting the remix circuit, and his much anticipated "The Network" Mixtape series, hosted by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Don_Cannon" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Don Cannon">DJ Don Cannon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2010, Young Chris signed with Division1, a label started by songwriter/producer <a href="/wiki/Rico_Love" title="Rico Love">Rico Love</a>, under the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Motown" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Motown">Universal Motown</a> umbrella.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx2_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Love considers Young Chris his flagship artist, working closely with Young Chris on his debut album and pre-album/mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape, called "The Re-Introduction", was released on November 20, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The aptly titled mixtape gives the spotlight to a newly signed Young Chris, as a solo artist, and also, re-introduces the combination of <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Don_Cannon" title="Don Cannon">Don Cannon</a>, the former "<a href="/wiki/Affiliates" class="mw-redirect" title="Affiliates">Affiliates</a>" partners, hosting their first mixtape together, in several years.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 28, a song called "Philly Shit (Mega Mix)" featuring Young Chris, <a href="/wiki/Eve_(entertainer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Eve (entertainer)">Eve</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_Thought" title="Black Thought">Black Thought</a>, Money Malc, <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fred_the_Godson" title="Fred the Godson">Fred the Godson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diggy_Simmons" title="Diggy Simmons">Diggy Simmons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Game (rapper)">The Game</a> was leaked.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On March 22, 2011, Young Chris' single "A$$ets" was commercially released.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late 2013–2014, Young Chris began releasing a slew of freestyles, the latest being a reworking of Wale's "LoveHate Thing", released on May 6.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He's currently working on a new studio album titled <i>Alive.</i>
</p><p>On May 6, 2014, he released a mixtape on Datpiff called <i>Gunna Seazon</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
