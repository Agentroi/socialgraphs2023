<p><b>George Spivey</b> (born June 21, 1968), known professionally as <b>DJ Scratch</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> DJ and producer from <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York.<sup id="cite_ref-Price_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Price-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>DJ Scratch is the 1988 New Music Seminar Battle For World Supremacy DJ champion, the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Master_of_the_Mix" title="Master of the Mix">Master of the Mix</a> winner and the 2012, 2013 &amp; 2014 Global Spin Awards' "Turntablist of the Year". 19 albums produced by DJ Scratch were <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified Gold</a> in United States, seven of them were certified as Platinum.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Scratch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>DJ Scratch was introduced to <a href="/wiki/EPMD" title="EPMD">EPMD</a> by <a href="/wiki/Jam_Master_Jay" title="Jam Master Jay">Jam Master Jay</a> at the Run's House Tour after DJ K LA Boss left EPMD.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Impressed by his skills, the two designated DJ Scratch as their official <a href="/wiki/D.J." class="mw-redirect" title="D.J.">D.J.</a> by their second album <i><a href="/wiki/Unfinished_Business_(EPMD_album)" title="Unfinished Business (EPMD album)">Unfinished Business</a></i> in 1989. His presence was felt with his powerful cutting and scratching techniques that are heard on various tracks. Aside from scratching, on <i><a href="/wiki/Business_as_Usual_(EPMD_album)" title="Business as Usual (EPMD album)">Business As Usual</a></i>, he produced the track "Funky Piano", "Rampage" and in 1991 worked on a remix for "I'm Mad" off the "Rampage" 12" single.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1992 Scratch produced "Scratch Bring It Back, Pt. 2 (Mic Doc)" and briefly worked with his cousin <a href="/wiki/DJ_Magic_Mike" title="DJ Magic Mike">DJ Magic Mike</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> after EPMD broke up. Soon Scratch made a name for himself as a producer for <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a> and the Flipmode Squad off <a href="/wiki/The_Coming" title="The Coming">Busta's debut album</a> in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> By 2003 DJ Scratch became a well distinguished and revered <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip-hop</a> <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a> after working with <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharoahe_Monch" title="Pharoahe Monch">Pharoahe Monch</a>, <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Roots" title="The Roots">The Roots</a>, <a href="/wiki/Q-Tip_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Q-Tip (rapper)">Q-Tip</a> and other notable acts.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Scratch was the <a href="/wiki/Resident_DJ" title="Resident DJ">resident DJ</a> for three nationally aired TV Shows: <i>Hip Hop Hold Em<span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span></i>, <a href="/wiki/WNYW" title="WNYW">Fox 5</a>'s <i>Uptown Comedy Club</i> and <a href="/wiki/BET_(TV_network)" class="mw-redirect" title="BET (TV network)">B.E.T.</a>'s <i>Rap City: The Basement</i>. Scratch's film credits include <i><a href="/wiki/Juice_(1992_film)" title="Juice (1992 film)">Juice</a></i>, <i>The Original 50 Cent</i>, <i>Backstage</i>, <i>Fly By Night</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rhyme_%26_Reason_(film)" title="Rhyme &amp; Reason (film)">Rhyme &amp; Reason</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a>'s film <i><a href="/wiki/Bamboozled" title="Bamboozled">Bamboozled</a></i>. In addition, <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> commissioned Scratch to produce &amp; feature in their first DJ commercial, "3 DJs".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to Parish Smith, an EPMD member, DJ Scratch left the group in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Scratch posted on his Instagram page words of resentment towards former participants on January 2, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, DJ Scratch helped fill in for <a href="/wiki/A_Tribe_Called_Quest" title="A Tribe Called Quest">A Tribe Called Quest</a>'s DJ <a href="/wiki/Ali_Shaheed_Muhammad" title="Ali Shaheed Muhammad">Ali Shaheed Muhammad</a> on their final album <i><a href="/wiki/We_Got_It_from_Here..._Thank_You_4_Your_Service" title="We Got It from Here... Thank You 4 Your Service">We Got It from Here... Thank You 4 Your Service</a></i> due to Ali producing the soundtrack for the <i><a href="/wiki/Luke_Cage_(TV_series)" title="Luke Cage (TV series)">Luke Cage</a></i> TV series with <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Younge" title="Adrian Younge">Adrian Younge</a> at the time.
</p><p>As of 2022, Scratch hosts The DJ Scratch Show on <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J%27s_Rock_the_Bells_Radio" class="mw-redirect" title="LL Cool J&#39;s Rock the Bells Radio">LL Cool J’s Rock The Bells Radio</a>, which is a station on <a href="/wiki/Sirius_XM" title="Sirius XM">Sirius XM</a>. It is a mix show that features the DJ curating a selection of classic hip-hop.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Scratch calls <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_Flash" title="Grandmaster Flash">Grandmaster Flash</a> his mentor.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
