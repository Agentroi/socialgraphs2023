<p><b>Antoinette Lovell Patterson</b> (born May 4, 1969<sup id="cite_ref-IG_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>), known simply by the <a href="/wiki/Mononym" title="Mononym">mononym</a> <b>Antoinette</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Bronx,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronx, New York">Bronx, New York</a>, who released two albums, during the late 1980s and early 1990s.
</p><p>She is mainly remembered for her beef with <a href="/wiki/MC_Lyte" title="MC Lyte">MC Lyte</a>. She was mentioned in the <i>Vibe Hip-Hop Divas</i> publication<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and her song "I Got An Attitude" (1987) was included in <a href="/wiki/Complex_Networks" title="Complex Networks">Complex</a> list of "The 50 Best Rap Songs by Women".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
