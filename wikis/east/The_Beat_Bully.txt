<p><b>Anthony Joseph Tucker</b>, better known as <b>The Beat Bully</b> or <b>Tone Beats</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a> from <a href="/wiki/Chester,_Pennsylvania" title="Chester, Pennsylvania">Chester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>. His older brother Orlando Tucker, is also a notable record producer under the name, <a href="/wiki/Jahlil_Beats" title="Jahlil Beats">Jahlil Beats</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He is also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Production_team" title="Production team">production team</a> Da Night Rydas. The Beat Bully has produced for rappers such as <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bow_Wow_(rapper)" title="Bow Wow (rapper)">Bow Wow</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a> among others.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He is best known for producing Rick Ross' "<a href="/wiki/Stay_Schemin%27" title="Stay Schemin&#39;">Stay Schemin'</a>" and Meek Mill's "<a href="/wiki/House_Party_(Meek_Mill_song)" title="House Party (Meek Mill song)">House Party</a>".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Beat_Bully&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Beat Bully first begun producing at the age of 13, along with his brother <a href="/wiki/Jahlil_Beats" title="Jahlil Beats">Jahlil Beats</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He originally went by the production alias Tone Beats, but he changed it to The Beat Bully. He told <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i>, "people used to just say I bullied the beats, so I put Beat Bully together."<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmagmill_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmagmill-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The Beat Bully begun taking production seriously in 2009, and he was then connected with rapper <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a> through Jahlil Beats. He then produced "I Want It All", a Lou Williams and Meek Mill song that was his first professionally recorded song. Then the second song he produced for Mill would be "<a href="/wiki/House_Party_(Meek_Mill_song)" title="House Party (Meek Mill song)">House Party</a>"<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmagmill_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmagmill-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> "House Party" would peak at number 45 on the US <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He would end up producing three tracks on Meek Mill's breakout mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Dreamchasers" title="Dreamchasers">Dreamchasers</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmagmill_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmagmill-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Shortly after he formed the <a href="/wiki/Production_team" title="Production team">production team</a> Da Night Rydas with Kenoe, The Beat Bully, Snizzy, and Crack Coke. The team has since produced for <a href="/wiki/2_Chainz" title="2 Chainz">2 Chainz</a>, <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His second major production placement would be on Rick Ross' "<a href="/wiki/Stay_Schemin%27" title="Stay Schemin&#39;">Stay Schemin'</a>", which features <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He originally gave the beat to <a href="/wiki/Bow_Wow_(rapper)" title="Bow Wow (rapper)">Bow Wow</a>, but The Beat Bully would then pass it on to Rick Ross.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In early 2012, The Beat Bully begun being managed by <a href="/wiki/Young_Money_Entertainment" title="Young Money Entertainment">Young Money Entertainment</a> president <a href="/wiki/Mack_Maine" title="Mack Maine">Mack Maine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmagmill_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmagmill-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On July 11, 2012, <a href="/wiki/Birdman_(rapper)" title="Birdman (rapper)">Birdman</a> announced that he had signed The Beat Bully to <a href="/wiki/Cash_Money_Records" title="Cash Money Records">Cash Money Records</a> as a producer.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Over 2012, The Beat Bully produced Rick Ross' "<a href="/wiki/So_Sophisticated" title="So Sophisticated">So Sophisticated</a>" the second single from <i><a href="/wiki/God_Forgives,_I_Don%27t" title="God Forgives, I Don&#39;t">God Forgives, I Don't</a></i>, and contributed production to <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_The_Ring" class="mw-redirect" title="Kiss The Ring">Kiss The Ring</a></i> and Meek Mill's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Dreams_%26_Nightmares" class="mw-redirect" title="Dreams &amp; Nightmares">Dreams &amp; Nightmares</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, The Beat Bully produced the <a href="/wiki/Promotional_single" class="mw-redirect" title="Promotional single">promotional singles</a> "Fuck What Happens Tonight" by <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> and "I Feel Like Pac, I Feel Like Biggie" by DJ Khaled.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_style">Production style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Beat_Bully&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p>
