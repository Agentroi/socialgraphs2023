<p><b>Robert</b> "<b>Bob</b>" <b>Khaleel</b>, better known by his stage name <b>Bronx Style Bob</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> from <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>, New York City.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bronx_Style_Bob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bob was raised in a strict Arab-Jamaican immigrant household and was exposed to the work of poets such as <a href="/wiki/Langston_Hughes" title="Langston Hughes">Langston Hughes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Khalil_Gibran" class="mw-redirect" title="Khalil Gibran">Khalil Gibran</a>.
</p><p>Bob started out in the <a href="/wiki/Bronx" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronx">Bronx</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, at a time when <a href="/wiki/Urban_culture" title="Urban culture">urban culture</a> disciplines such as <a href="/wiki/Graffiti" title="Graffiti">graffiti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Breakdancing" title="Breakdancing">breakin'</a>, electric boogie, <a href="/wiki/DJing" class="mw-redirect" title="DJing">DJing</a>, scratching and cutting the record, and the <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">MCs rhyming</a> were found.
</p><p>He attended <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_High_School_(New_York_City)" title="John F. Kennedy High School (New York City)">John F. Kennedy High School</a> with <a href="/wiki/B-boy" class="mw-redirect" title="B-boy">b-boy</a> superstars <a href="/wiki/Crazy_Legs_(dancer)" title="Crazy Legs (dancer)">Crazy Legs</a> and Mr. Freeze of the <a href="/wiki/Rock_Steady_Crew" title="Rock Steady Crew">Rock Steady Crew</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fastbreak" class="mw-redirect" title="Fastbreak">Fastbreak</a> (from Magnificent Force), DJ <a href="/wiki/Kid_Capri" title="Kid Capri">Kid Capri</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Glidemaster" title="Glidemaster">Glidemaster</a> &amp; Chino (<a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Breakers" title="New York City Breakers">New York City Breakers</a>).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bronx_Style_Bob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1982 to 1985, Bob was the president of the Bronx Style Crew; influenced by growing up around 170 Street/Jerome Avenue, a few blocks from <a href="/wiki/Disco_Fever" title="Disco Fever">Disco Fever</a>, and around the corner from the Ecstasy Garage, two famous nightclubs.
</p><p>In the early '80s, he was a fixture on the <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> scene of clubs and art galleries where <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk rock</a>, hip hop and <a href="/wiki/Graffiti_artist" class="mw-redirect" title="Graffiti artist">graffiti artists</a> came together. From clubs like <a href="/wiki/Danceteria" title="Danceteria">Danceteria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roxy_NYC" class="mw-redirect" title="Roxy NYC">The Roxy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peppermint_Lounge" title="Peppermint Lounge">Peppermint Lounge</a>, Negril, and the Mudd Club to the Fun Gallery. "I ran around with graffiti artists...you would have Andy Warhol Truman Capote Barishnikov in a room with Basqiat Futura 2000 Dondi or Zephyr, with rock steady breakin. Jazzy Jay on the set, and taggers hitting up, blunts flowin', and some graffiti artist gettin' stomped out in the corner by a rival crew...it was like a painting," he said.
</p><p>His first song as a member of <a href="/wiki/Afrika_Bambaataa" title="Afrika Bambaataa">Afrika Bambaataa</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Universal_Zulu_Nation" title="Universal Zulu Nation">Universal Zulu Nation</a> and taking the name "Bronx Style Bob", was in 1985 for the soundtrack to the movie <i>New York Ninja</i>, and in 1986, he embarked on his first tour as a member of the Magnificent Force <a href="/wiki/Breakdance" class="mw-redirect" title="Breakdance">breakdance</a> crew.
</p><p>Upon returning to the US, Bob recorded a series of singles for Spring Posse Records with the group the Zulu Kings, which consisted of <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_Melle_Mel" class="mw-redirect" title="Grandmaster Melle Mel">Grandmaster Melle Mel</a> from <a href="/wiki/The_Furious_Five" class="mw-redirect" title="The Furious Five">the Furious Five</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice-T" title="Ice-T">Ice-T</a>, Grand Master Caz of the <a href="/wiki/Cold_Crush_Brothers" class="mw-redirect" title="Cold Crush Brothers">Cold Crush Brothers</a>, Africa Izlam, Chief Rocker Busy Bee, and Microphone King Donald-D. As a group they would also score the first hip hop animation series <i>Street Frogs</i>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Rifkind" title="Steve Rifkind">Steve Rifkind</a> for S.R.C Records.
</p><p>When he relocated to Paris in 1987, he partnered with French rapper <a href="/wiki/Dee_Nasty" title="Dee Nasty">Dee Nasty</a> on the alternative radio program <a href="/wiki/Radio_Nova_(France)" title="Radio Nova (France)">Radio Nova</a>. He went to record three songs with Dee Nasty for his first major label release <i>Pousse Les Bass</i> on <a href="/wiki/Polydor_Records" title="Polydor Records">Polydor Records</a>.
</p><p>Upon his return to the US in 1989, Bob recorded and tour with Ice-T and the Rhyme Syndicate for much of 1990–91. They embarked on the "Bring Tha Noiz" world tour, which featured <a href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Enemy (group)">Public Enemy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_B._%26_Rakim" title="Eric B. &amp; Rakim">Eric B. &amp; Rakim</a>, <a href="/wiki/EPMD" title="EPMD">EPMD</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a>, <a href="/wiki/N.W.A" title="N.W.A">N.W.A</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Hammer" title="MC Hammer">MC Hammer</a>, Ice-T and The Rhyme Syndicate, <a href="/wiki/Vanilla_Ice" title="Vanilla Ice">Vanilla Ice</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tone_L%C5%8Dc" class="mw-redirect" title="Tone Lōc">Tone Lōc</a>.
</p><p>In 1992, Bob performed on the second stage at the Jones Beach <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a> concert.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Los Angeles, in between tour dates with The Syndicate, he became a founding member of Los Angeles <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a> collective Trulio Disgracias, featuring members of <a href="/wiki/Fishbone" title="Fishbone">Fishbone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Brand_New_Heavies" title="The Brand New Heavies">The Brand New Heavies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parliament_(band)" title="Parliament (band)">Parliament</a>, <a href="/wiki/Funkadelic" title="Funkadelic">Funkadelic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thelonious_Monster" title="Thelonious Monster">Thelonious Monster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suicidal_Tendencies" title="Suicidal Tendencies">Suicidal Tendencies</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Untouchables_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Untouchables (band)">The Untouchables</a>.
</p><p>Bob later signed with <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Records" title="Hollywood Records">Hollywood Records</a> and formed <a href="/wiki/Super_8_(band)" title="Super 8 (band)">Super 8</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a> band.
</p><p>After two years of nonstop touring, Bob began recording with longtime collaborator John O'Brien.
</p><p>Later, Bob created a new professional identity, "Khaleel", and his new album <i>People Watching</i>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Matt_Wallace_(record_producer)" title="Matt Wallace (record producer)">Matt Wallace</a> (<a href="/wiki/The_Replacements_(band)" title="The Replacements (band)">The Replacements</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheryl_Crow" title="Sheryl Crow">Sheryl Crow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maroon_5" title="Maroon 5">Maroon 5</a>, <a href="/wiki/Faith_No_More" title="Faith No More">Faith No More</a>), featured guest artists such as members of Fishbone, <a href="/wiki/Spearhead_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Spearhead (band)">Spearhead</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jellyfish_(band)" title="Jellyfish (band)">Jellyfish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Falkner" title="Jason Falkner">Jason Falkner</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lyle_Workman" title="Lyle Workman">Lyle Workman</a>. "No Mercy", the album's first single, became a staple at pop radio, and Bob toured with <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Mullins" title="Shawn Mullins">Shawn Mullins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Everlast_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Everlast (musician)">Everlast</a>.
</p><p>Bob appeared as a guest vocalist on Everlast's "<a href="/wiki/What_It%27s_Like" title="What It&#39;s Like">What It's Like</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Ends_(song)" title="Ends (song)">Ends</a>" on his 1998 album <i><a href="/wiki/Whitey_Ford_Sings_the_Blues" title="Whitey Ford Sings the Blues">Whitey Ford Sings the Blues</a></i>, as well as on 2000's <i><a href="/wiki/Eat_at_Whitey%27s" title="Eat at Whitey&#39;s">Eat at Whitey's</a></i>. He contributed to the <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a> tribute album <a href="/wiki/Working_Class_Hero:_A_Tribute_to_John_Lennon" title="Working Class Hero: A Tribute to John Lennon"><i>Working Class Hero</i></a>.
</p><p>In 2002, Bob collaborated with songwriter <a href="/wiki/Brendan_Lynch_(music_producer)" title="Brendan Lynch (music producer)">Brendan Lynch</a> to a new band, Contact.
</p>
