<p><b>Daniel Boimah Kenney Knero Lapaé Edwards II</b> (born May 19, 1985), better known as <b>Knero Lapaé</b> or simply <b>Knero</b> (stylized as <b>kNERO</b>; pronounced <i>knee ro</i> <i>la pay</i>), is a Liberian rapper, singer, songwriter, record producer, model, and author.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His music is a mixture of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Afrobeats" title="Afrobeats">Afrobeats</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_family_members">Personal life and family members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knero_Lapa%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life and family members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>kNERO was born in <a href="/wiki/Monrovia" title="Monrovia">Monrovia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberia</a> and raised in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York. His father is <a href="/wiki/Gola_people" title="Gola people">Gola</a> and his mother is from the <a href="/wiki/Kpelle_people" title="Kpelle people">Kpelle</a> tribe. He is one of four children of Sara Léla and Rev. D. Boimah Lapaé Edwards, who was the youngest Liberian to managed an American corporation in Liberia. His father is currently the President of the non-profit organization, The Bomi County Association.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> kNERO was brought up in a predominant political background. He is the nephew of Liberia's 24th president <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Johnson_Sirleaf" title="Ellen Johnson Sirleaf">Ellen Johnson Sirleaf</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He has an uncle who was a judge of the Magistrate Court, a District Commissioner, and County Development Coordinator for the Ministry of Internal Affairs. He is also the first cousin of Richard B. Devine, a former Senator of Liberia.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His family was fortunate enough to escape the <a href="/wiki/First_Liberian_Civil_War" title="First Liberian Civil War">Liberian Civil War</a> as a result of his father's achievements, but still suffers the loss of family and friends.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career_and_performances">Music career and performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knero_Lapa%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career and performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>kNERO released "Not Drunk Enuff" featuring Norwegian singer Adele Erichsen, a former contestant on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice_%E2%80%93_Norges_beste_stemme" title="The Voice – Norges beste stemme">The Voice – Norges beste stemme</a></i>. The single was released under CCAP records. Due to his political ties, he celebrated the <a href="/wiki/Liberian_Declaration_of_Independence" title="Liberian Declaration of Independence">Liberian Declaration of Independence</a> by performing his single "A.F.R.I.C.A" at the Liberian Embassy in Washington, D.C.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He performed at the Hip Hop of Pioneers event held at the <a href="/wiki/Newark_Symphony_Hall" title="Newark Symphony Hall">Newark Symphony Hall</a>; the event featured additional performances from <a href="/wiki/MC_Lyte" title="MC Lyte">MC Lyte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_and_the_Gang" class="mw-redirect" title="Kool and the Gang">Kool and the Gang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dana_Dane" title="Dana Dane">Dana Dane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Between 2006 and 2007, he performed at <a href="/wiki/Fox_Theatre_(Atlanta)" title="Fox Theatre (Atlanta)">The Fox Theatre</a> in Atlanta for <a href="/wiki/Dr._Malachi_Z._York" class="mw-redirect" title="Dr. Malachi Z. York">Dr. Malachi Z. York</a>'s 11th Annual <a href="/wiki/Nuwaupu" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuwaupu">Nuwaupu</a> Ball; his name was announced alongside <a href="/wiki/Erykah_Badu" title="Erykah Badu">Erykah Badu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Cliff" title="Jimmy Cliff">Jimmy Cliff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Musiq_Soulchild" title="Musiq Soulchild">Musiq Soulchild</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> kNERO released his single "My Thang" featuring <a href="/wiki/Jamal_Woolard" title="Jamal Woolard">Jamal "Gravy" Woolard</a> and former <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella" class="mw-redirect" title="Roc-A-Fella">Roc-A-Fella</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Amil_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Amil (rapper)">Amil</a>, which appeared on several urban blog sites.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, kNERO closed a deal to release his next single "King" under Universal Music Group.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Barber_career">Barber career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knero_Lapa%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Barber career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1995, kNERO fell in love with the art of <a href="/wiki/Barbering" class="mw-redirect" title="Barbering">barbering</a>. To avoid street violence and other criminal activities, he learned to master his craft over the years. He then travels from state to state gaining connections in the hair industry, which helped his music career. His limitless experiences, becoming rapper <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a> personal barber. Gaining clients as Comedian Kyle Grooms, Fashion Designer Toure Designs, Humanitarian/Motivational speaker George Green, Celebrity Hairstylist Keith Campbell, Middleweight Boxer <a href="/wiki/Peter_Quillin" title="Peter Quillin">Peter Quillin</a>, and more.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modeling_and_clothing">Modeling and clothing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Knero_Lapa%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Modeling and clothing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, kNERO was a model for photos of The Toure Designs' fall season collection. Later that year, the model in The Toure Designs' Target First Saturdays "Carnival Inspired Fashion" themed at the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Museum" title="Brooklyn Museum">Brooklyn Museum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He continued to walk the runway alongside actress/singer <a href="/wiki/Demetria_McKinney" title="Demetria McKinney">Demetria Mckinney</a>, modeling for Toure Designs' for the 2015 Mercedes Benz Fashion week presented by ICU Network hosted by SB Stunts.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>kNERO Lapaé has made an appearance in the 2013 film <i>Living With No Regrets</i>, with <a href="/wiki/Clifton_Powell" title="Clifton Powell">Clifton Powell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marc_John_Jefferies" title="Marc John Jefferies">Marc John Jefferies</a>, rapper <a href="/wiki/AZ_(rapper)" title="AZ (rapper)">AZ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Baltimore" class="mw-redirect" title="Charlie Baltimore">Charlie Baltimore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Treach" title="Treach">Treach</a> and more.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
