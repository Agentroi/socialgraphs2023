<p><b>Matthew Jefferson</b>, also known as <b>Kemba</b>, formerly known as <b>YC the Cynic</b>, (born November 21, 1990) is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> recording artist from <a href="/wiki/Hunts_Point,_Bronx" title="Hunts Point, Bronx">Hunts Point, Bronx, New York</a>. He was named one of the "Top 5 Underground Hip-Hop Artists in New York" by <i>Deli Magazine</i> (2010) and was ordained "DXNext" by the hip hop website <a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a> (2011). Kemba talks openly about his sense of responsibility to the art form, “It feels like there’s a void in the Bronx, and I don’t necessarily feel like I have to fill it, but I feel like I could."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
He was also a feature on <a href="/wiki/XXXTENTACION" class="mw-redirect" title="XXXTENTACION">XXXTENTACION</a>'s final posthumous album, <a href="/wiki/Bad_Vibes_Forever" title="Bad Vibes Forever">Bad Vibes Forever</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kemba_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>You're Welcome (2010)</b>
</p><p>Released on February 9, 2010, <i>You're Welcome</i> is YC The Cynic's musical debut. Featuring mostly covers of 1990s classics, <i>You're Welcome</i> was an act of homage to those that paved the way for him. Justin Hunte of BrooklynBodega described <i>You're Welcome</i> as being “loaded with personality, slick wordplay, pristine beat selection, and lyricism beyond his years.”<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
Manny Faces of <i>Birthplace Magazine</i> said "lyrically, YC is intelligent and rocks with diction. He enunciates well, emitting just the right amount of emotion for each track, while skillfully staying on message."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  DJBooth.net declared “YC’s heavily-alliterated, elaborately-rhymed flows are undeniably dope.”<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
<i>You're Welcome</i> served as a great look into the talent and potential of the up-and-coming emcee.
</p><p><b>Fall FWD (2011)</b>
</p><p>Released on March 1, 2011, <i>Fall FWD</i> is the second mixtape by YC the Cynic. Containing mostly original production, <i>Fall FWD</i> shows the growth and song-writing ability of the young artist. Derick Fortin of LA2Day said "Fall FWD’s lyrical complexity, aesthetically pleasing flow, diverse subjects, and experimental production is everything true hip-hop fans have been looking for"<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> while JT Langley of Oology says "lyrical content aside, YC shares himself as a patient slow-paced linguist throughout the tape, focusing with precision to weave commentary among clever metaphor, while riding a loose rhyme-scheme that holds the ability to evolve smoothly at the turn of each verse without any obvious signifiers."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the success of <i>Fall FWD</i>, YC the Cynic has performed with the stage with the likes of <a href="/wiki/Bun_B" title="Bun B">Bun B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Currensy" title="Currensy">Curren$y</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joell_Ortiz" title="Joell Ortiz">Joell Ortiz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a> and most notably, a headline show for his most recent release, <i>Fall FWD</i>, at Southpaw in Park Slope, Brooklyn.  He also performed at the closing ceremony of <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.fatbeats.com/">Fat Beats</a> in NYC.  In March 2011, YC the Cynic wrapped a nationwide tour with <a href="/wiki/J-Live" title="J-Live">J.Live</a> that included six performances at the <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">SXSW</a> Festival in Austin, Texas. YC's artist resume includes features in <a href="/wiki/Okayplayer" title="Okayplayer">OkayPlayer</a>, <a href="/wiki/AllHipHop" title="AllHipHop">AllHipHop</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=ACT_LIVE_MUSIC&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ACT LIVE MUSIC (page does not exist)">ACT LIVE MUSIC</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.2dopeboyz.com/2011/03/01/yc-the-cynic-fall-fwd-mixtape/">2dopeboyz</a> and OK-Tho whom named him one of the Top 25 artists of 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><b>GNK (2013)</b>
</p><p>Released on August 26, 2013, <i>GNK</i> is the last project released under the name YC the Cynic. GNK is a collaborative project with Frank Drake (Producer).
</p><p><b>NEGUS (2016)</b>
</p><p>Under his new name, Kemba released the album <i>Negus</i> on June 30, 2016. In December 2016, Kemba was interviewed by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Rosenberg" title="Peter Rosenberg">Peter Rosenberg</a> on Real Late W/ Peter Rosenberg.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Only nine days after the interview was uploaded to <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> invited Kemba on to the stage to rap at Brooklyn's <a href="/wiki/Music_Hall_of_Williamsburg" title="Music Hall of Williamsburg">Music Hall of Williamsburg</a> for an <a href="/wiki/American_Express" title="American Express">American Express</a> sponsored concert that was live-streamed on <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On December 27, 2016, Kemba uploaded a video onto Instagram showing him on the set for <a href="/wiki/Anderson_Paak" class="mw-redirect" title="Anderson Paak">Anderson .Paak</a>'s "Already" music video.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On December 28, 2016, rumors began to surface that Kemba had signed to <a href="/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment" title="Top Dawg Entertainment">Top Dawg Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
In 2019, he was featured on the final posthumous album from the late Floridian rapper <a href="/wiki/XXXTENTACION" class="mw-redirect" title="XXXTENTACION">XXXTENTACION</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bad_Vibes_Forever" title="Bad Vibes Forever">Bad Vibes Forever</a>, with fellow New York native, <a href="/wiki/Joey_Badass" title="Joey Badass">Joey Badass</a>.
</p><p><b>GILDA (2019)</b>
</p><p>In 2019, Kemba released <i>Gilda</i>, his major label debut.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The album is named for, and is a tribute to, Kemba's mother, who passed away two years prior to the album's release.
</p><p>In 2020, Kemba appeared as himself in the film <a href="/wiki/The_40-Year-Old_Version" class="mw-redirect" title="The 40-Year-Old Version">The 40-Year-Old Version</a><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2020, Kemba's music video ''Nobody I Can Trust'' directed by Tomson Tee won the Best Narrative category and placed second for the Best Music Video at the Berlin Music Video Awards.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rebel_Diaz_Arts_Collective">Rebel Diaz Arts Collective</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kemba_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rebel Diaz Arts Collective">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition, YC the Cynic is a founding member of the RDACBX, <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Diaz#Rebel_Diaz_Arts_Collective" title="Rebel Diaz">Rebel Diaz Arts Collective</a>. Created in March 2009 by the hip hop duo <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Diaz" title="Rebel Diaz">Rebel Diaz</a>, the Rebel Diaz Arts Collective (RDACBX) is a hip hop community center that utilizes all five elements to spread their message about the injustices faced by oppressed groups while providing a safe haven for young people to perform and learn.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Some of the RDACBX's resources include a performance space, a multimedia studio, a computer lab, and an art gallery. In "building community through the arts," RDACBX serves as a safe space for young people to learn and perform, hosting workshops in addition to providing artistic space. YC describes his participation in the collective: "We’ve been doing rallies, which are all based on music, but it’s so much deeper. Music is just an acceptable form…it’s inspirational. We use that to better the community and the world at large."<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In June 2010, along with RDACBX, YC the Cynic toured California, and Detroit, for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Social_Forum" title="United States Social Forum">United States Social Forum</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kemba_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>YC The Cynic has openly stated his influences and how they influence him. "I study specific emcees for specific reasons. Like, I've the [sic] importance of enunciation is huge. I look to KRS-One for that." He has also said that he looked to Eminem for multi-syllabic rhythmic patters and "inners," which are rhymes in the beginning or middle of a line. Although KRS-One and Eminem have influenced him, he's also named Mos Def and Andre 3000 as well, with his biggest influence being Cee-Lo Green of Goodie Mob and Gnarls Barkley. "Cee-lo use of melody, song-writing ability, and just the strength and feeling of the words he chooses is pretty invaluable," says the Bronx native.
</p><p>YC the Cynic has been lauded by some of the most notable names in the hip hop industry.  In his "DXNext" feature, the writer described him as being "a standout in the cutthroat New York City underground scene."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>  Brooklyn Bodega described YC as being “loaded with personality, slick wordplay, pristine beat selection, and lyricism beyond his years”<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> while DJBooth.net declares “YC’s heavily-alliterated, elaborately-rhymed flows are undeniably dope.”<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
