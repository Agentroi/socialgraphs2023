<p><b>Jaime Meline</b> (born March 2, 1975), better known by the stage name <b>El-P</b> (shortened from his previous stage name <b>El Producto</b>), is an American rapper, songwriter, and record producer. Starting his career as a member of <a href="/wiki/Company_Flow" title="Company Flow">Company Flow</a>, he has been a driving force in <a href="/wiki/Alternative_hip_hop" title="Alternative hip hop">alternative hip hop</a> since the mid-1990s, producing for rappers including <a href="/wiki/Aesop_Rock" title="Aesop Rock">Aesop Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cage_(rapper)" title="Cage (rapper)">Cage</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mr._Lif" title="Mr. Lif">Mr. Lif</a>. He was a member of <a href="/wiki/The_Weathermen_(hip_hop_group)" title="The Weathermen (hip hop group)">The Weathermen</a> and is the co-founder, owner, and CEO of the <a href="/wiki/Definitive_Jux" title="Definitive Jux">Definitive Jux</a> record label.
</p><p>After releasing four solo studio albums that were critically acclaimed—<i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Damage" title="Fantastic Damage">Fantastic Damage</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/High_Water_(El-P_album)" title="High Water (El-P album)">High Water</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/I%27ll_Sleep_When_You%27re_Dead" title="I&#39;ll Sleep When You&#39;re Dead">I'll Sleep When You're Dead</a></i> (2007), and <i><a href="/wiki/Cancer_4_Cure" title="Cancer 4 Cure">Cancer 4 Cure</a></i> (2012)—El-P began experiencing mainstream success in 2013 when he formed the hip hop duo <a href="/wiki/Run_the_Jewels" title="Run the Jewels">Run the Jewels</a> with fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Killer_Mike" title="Killer Mike">Killer Mike</a>. They have released four studio albums for free, all of which have received widespread acclaim: <i><a href="/wiki/Run_the_Jewels_(album)" title="Run the Jewels (album)">Run the Jewels</a></i> (2013), <i><a href="/wiki/Run_the_Jewels_2" title="Run the Jewels 2">Run the Jewels 2</a></i> (2014), <i><a href="/wiki/Run_the_Jewels_3" title="Run the Jewels 3">Run the Jewels 3</a></i> (2016), and <i><a href="/wiki/RTJ4" title="RTJ4">RTJ4</a></i> (2020).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El-P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>El-P was born Jaime Meline in the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> borough of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> on March 2, 1975, the son of Nan Dillon<sup id="cite_ref-Chamberlin_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chamberlin-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and jazz pianist Harry Meline (who performed under the <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> Harry Keyes).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He is of Cajun, Irish, and Lithuanian descent.<sup id="cite_ref-Chamberlin_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chamberlin-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Although his mother was raised <a href="/wiki/Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholicism">Catholic</a> and his father was raised <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Jewish</a>, they did not emphasize either faith to him during his childhood and he later joked that he "got none of it except a <a href="/wiki/Brit_milah" title="Brit milah">circumcision at birth</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-Chamberlin_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chamberlin-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>El-P's parents divorced when he was seven years old, and he later wrote the song "Last Good Sleep" about once hearing his mother being assaulted by his drunken stepfather in the next room and not realizing what had happened until the next day.<sup id="cite_ref-Chamberlin_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chamberlin-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His mother responded to the beating by immediately ending the relationship, reporting El-P's stepfather to the police, and changing their home's locks; however, El-P had recurring nightmares about the incident for many years, and once chased a stranger he misidentified as his ex-stepfather through the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Subway" title="New York City Subway">New York subway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Chamberlin_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chamberlin-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>El-P was expelled from two high schools for various issues, including his refusal to stop wearing his baseball cap backwards.<sup id="cite_ref-Chamberlin_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chamberlin-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He later got his <a href="/wiki/GED" class="mw-redirect" title="GED">GED</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Chamberlin_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chamberlin-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He went to musical engineering school at the Center for the Media Arts in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After graduating, he enrolled at <a href="/wiki/Hunter_College" title="Hunter College">Hunter College</a>, but dropped out because most of his courses did not relate to the career he wanted.<sup id="cite_ref-Chamberlin_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chamberlin-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El-P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El-P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Getting involved with the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop culture">hip hop culture</a> of New York City at an early age, El-P met <a href="/wiki/Mr._Len" title="Mr. Len">Mr. Len</a> when he hired Len as the DJ for his 17th birthday party. The two became friends and formed <a href="/wiki/Company_Flow" title="Company Flow">Company Flow</a> in 1992. They released their first single, "Juvenile Technique", in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-HuAM_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HuAM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bigg_Jus" title="Bigg Jus">Bigg Jus</a> joined the group and in 1995 the trio released their debut EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Funcrusher_EP" class="mw-redirect" title="Funcrusher EP">Funcrusher</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fcr5_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fcr5-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It sold well for an independently released record, with more than 30,000 copies sold despite only being available on <a href="/wiki/Phonograph_record" title="Phonograph record">vinyl</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HuAM_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HuAM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Subject to a major-label bidding war after the success of the EP, Company Flow waited until it could get a contract on its own terms. The group eventually signed with <a href="/wiki/Rawkus_Records" title="Rawkus Records">Rawkus Records</a>, and released its debut full-length album, <i><a href="/wiki/Funcrusher_Plus" title="Funcrusher Plus">Funcrusher Plus</a></i> (1997).
</p><p>After an <a href="/wiki/Instrumental_hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Instrumental hip hop">instrumental</a> album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Johnny_From_the_Hospitul:_Breaks_%26_Instrumentals_Vol.1" class="mw-redirect" title="Little Johnny From the Hospitul: Breaks &amp; Instrumentals Vol.1">Little Johnny From the Hospitul: Breaks &amp; Instrumentals Vol.1</a></i> (1999), also on <a href="/wiki/Rawkus" class="mw-redirect" title="Rawkus">Rawkus</a>, disagreements between El-P and the label led to Company Flow leaving Rawkus. El-P decided to start his own <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>, <a href="/wiki/Definitive_Jux" title="Definitive Jux">Definitive Jux</a> (known informally as Def Jux, and extra-formally as Definitive Juxtapositions), and because of critically acclaimed albums such as <a href="/wiki/Cannibal_Ox" title="Cannibal Ox">Cannibal Ox</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cold_Vein" title="The Cold Vein">The Cold Vein</a></i> (produced by El-P) and <a href="/wiki/Aesop_Rock" title="Aesop Rock">Aesop Rock</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Labor_Days" title="Labor Days">Labor Days</a></i>, the label rose to prominence.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Company Flow dissolved amicably in 2001 and the following year El-P released his debut <a href="/wiki/Solo_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Solo album">solo album</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Damage" title="Fantastic Damage">Fantastic Damage</a></i>, to critical acclaim. In 2004, El-P collaborated with the Blue Series Continuum for a <a href="/wiki/Jazz-fusion" class="mw-redirect" title="Jazz-fusion">jazz-fusion</a> album titled <i><a href="/wiki/High_Water_(El-P_album)" title="High Water (El-P album)">High Water</a></i>, which received favorable reviews from both hip hop and jazz critics, as well as from more mainstream critics independent of both scenes. 2005 saw the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Collecting_the_Kid" title="Collecting the Kid">Collecting the Kid</a></i>, an odds-and-ends assortment including work from <i>High Water</i> and <i>Bomb the System</i>, in addition to several songs of unknown provenance. El-P's second proper studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/I%27ll_Sleep_When_You%27re_Dead" title="I&#39;ll Sleep When You&#39;re Dead">I'll Sleep When You're Dead</a></i>, was released on March 20, 2007. It generally received very favorable reviews and became El-P's most commercially successful album as a solo artist to date, peaking at No. 78 on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. In an interview about <i>I'll Sleep When You're Dead</i>, he identifies himself as an atheist.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2009, El-P announced that he was working on his third studio album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Cancer_4_Cure" title="Cancer 4 Cure">Cancer 4 Cure</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> El-P was a part of a group called Central Services, which consisted of El-P, <a href="/wiki/Camu_Tao" title="Camu Tao">Camu Tao</a>, and Allysin Baker. In the fall of 2010, the EP <i>Forever Frozen in Television Time</i>, was released exclusively on the Def Jux digital download service. In August 2011, it was announced that El-P signed with Fat Possum Records, which would release <i>Cancer 4 Cure</i>. Later that month, El-P released a single off the upcoming album, via the <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">Adult Swim</a> Singles program, titled "Drones Over BKLYN". On February 22, 2012, El-P announced on his Facebook page that <i>Cancer 4 Cure</i> was complete. The album was released to music retailers on May 22.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Run_the_Jewels">Run the Jewels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El-P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Run the Jewels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Run_the_Jewels" title="Run the Jewels">Run the Jewels</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Run_the_Jewels" title="Run the Jewels">Run the Jewels</a>, a collaboration of El-P with <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>-based rapper <a href="/wiki/Killer_Mike" title="Killer Mike">Killer Mike</a>, was formed in 2013. The two rappers were introduced to each other by <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">Adult Swim</a> executive Jason DeMarco in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-RollingStone_RTJ_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RollingStone_RTJ-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The meeting led to several collaborations, including Killer Mike being featured on the song "Tougher Colder Killer" from <i>Cancer 4 Cure</i> while El-P produced the entirety of <a href="/wiki/Killer_Mike" title="Killer Mike">Killer Mike</a>'s sixth album <i><a href="/wiki/R.A.P._Music" title="R.A.P. Music">R.A.P. Music</a></i>. When <i>R.A.P. Music</i> and <i>Cancer 4 Cure</i> were released within weeks of each other, a joint tour was planned. The success of the tour eventually led to the formation of Run the Jewels.<sup id="cite_ref-RTJ_NPR_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RTJ_NPR-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The duo released <a href="/wiki/Run_the_Jewels_(album)" title="Run the Jewels (album)">their self-titled debut album</a> on June 26, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-RTJ_releasedate_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RTJ_releasedate-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> They released <i><a href="/wiki/Run_the_Jewels_2" title="Run the Jewels 2">Run the Jewels 2</a></i> on October 28, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Run_the_Jewels_3" title="Run the Jewels 3">Run the Jewels 3</a></i> was released on December 24, 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-BB_RTJ3_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB_RTJ3-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> three weeks prior to the previously announced release date of January 13, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> A fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/RTJ4" title="RTJ4">RTJ4</a></i> was released on June 3, 2020. All of their albums to date have been released for free.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_and_featured_appearances">Production and featured appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El-P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Production and featured appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>El-P has contributed productions and guest rhymes to albums by <a href="/wiki/Aesop_Rock" title="Aesop Rock">Aesop Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evil_Nine" title="Evil Nine">Evil Nine</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Krush" title="DJ Krush">DJ Krush</a>, <a href="/wiki/Murs_(rapper)" title="Murs (rapper)">Murs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cage_Kennylz" class="mw-redirect" title="Cage Kennylz">Cage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mr._Lif" title="Mr. Lif">Mr. Lif</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prefuse_73" class="mw-redirect" title="Prefuse 73">Prefuse 73</a>, <a href="/wiki/Del_tha_Funkee_Homosapien" class="mw-redirect" title="Del tha Funkee Homosapien">Del tha Funkee Homosapien</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Ladd" title="Mike Ladd">Mike Ladd</a>, <a href="/wiki/High_and_Mighty_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="High and Mighty (band)">The High and Mighty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jedi_Mind_Tricks" title="Jedi Mind Tricks">Jedi Mind Tricks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aceyalone" title="Aceyalone">Aceyalone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere_(music_group)" title="Atmosphere (music group)">Atmosphere</a>, <a href="/wiki/Techno_Animal" title="Techno Animal">Techno Animal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Das_Racist" title="Das Racist">Das Racist</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Allmusic_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Alec_Empire" title="Alec Empire">Alec Empire</a> on the first <a href="/wiki/Handsome_Boy_Modeling_School" title="Handsome Boy Modeling School">Handsome Boy Modeling School</a> album and with <a href="/wiki/Cage_Kennylz" class="mw-redirect" title="Cage Kennylz">Cage</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chino_Moreno" title="Chino Moreno">Chino Moreno</a> on the second. He was selected, along with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Shadow" title="DJ Shadow">DJ Shadow</a>, to work on <a href="/wiki/Rage_Against_the_Machine" title="Rage Against the Machine">Rage Against the Machine</a> frontman <a href="/wiki/Zack_de_la_Rocha" title="Zack de la Rocha">Zack de la Rocha</a>'s solo album (which never materialized). He has provided remixes for the likes of <a href="/wiki/Beans_(rapper)" title="Beans (rapper)">Beans</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Anti-Pop_Consortium" class="mw-redirect" title="Anti-Pop Consortium">Anti-Pop Consortium</a>), <a href="/wiki/Beck" title="Beck">Beck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackalicious" title="Blackalicious">Blackalicious</a>, <a href="/wiki/Head_Automatica" title="Head Automatica">Head Automatica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hot_Hot_Heat" title="Hot Hot Heat">Hot Hot Heat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dizzee_Rascal" title="Dizzee Rascal">Dizzee Rascal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syd_Matters" title="Syd Matters">Syd Matters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nine_Inch_Nails" title="Nine Inch Nails">Nine Inch Nails</a>, <a href="/wiki/Push_Button_Objects" title="Push Button Objects">Push Button Objects</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rob_Sonic" title="Rob Sonic">Rob Sonic</a>, <a href="/wiki/TV_on_the_Radio" title="TV on the Radio">TV on the Radio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lorde" title="Lorde">Lorde</a>, and Yasushilde, among others. El-P provided the soundtrack for the graffiti film <i><a href="/wiki/Bomb_the_System" title="Bomb the System">Bomb the System</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bio2_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_scoring">Film scoring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El-P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Film scoring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>El-P was the principal composer for the 2005 film <i><a href="/wiki/Bomb_the_System" title="Bomb the System">Bomb the System</a></i>; he was one of the candidates to score a trailer for 2017's <i><a href="/wiki/Blade_Runner_2049" title="Blade Runner 2049">Blade Runner 2049</a></i>, but his score was "rejected or ignored".<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He created the score for the 2020 biopic <i><a href="/wiki/Capone_(2020_film)" title="Capone (2020 film)">Capone</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style">Style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El-P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>El-P's rapping style is characterized by dense, aggressive, and verbose attacks that include heavy use of metaphors, sci-fi fantasy themes, pop culture references, and <a href="/wiki/Association_(psychology)" title="Association (psychology)">associative</a> wordplay. His lyrics often feature themes found in the works of sci-fi author <a href="/wiki/Philip_K._Dick" title="Philip K. Dick">Philip K. Dick</a>, such as paranoia and questions about the nature of reality.<sup id="cite_ref-PF_LP_PKD_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PF_LP_PKD-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Reviewing his 2002 album <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Damage" title="Fantastic Damage">Fantastic Damage</a></i>, critic Steve Huey described El-P as "one of the most technically gifted MCs of his time, spitting out near-impossible phrases and rhythmic variations that simply leave the listener's head spinning".<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>El-P's 2004 album <i><a href="/wiki/High_Water_(El-P_album)" title="High Water (El-P album)">High Water</a></i> was a radical departure, pitting his beats and production against a jazz band under the direction of pianist <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Shipp" title="Matthew Shipp">Matthew Shipp</a>, a fan of El-P's music. In a 2003 interview with Shipp for <i><a href="/wiki/Bomb_(magazine)" title="Bomb (magazine)">Bomb</a></i> magazine, El-P stated, "First and foremost, I wanted to [make a different album] because it scared the shit out of me. And when I get offered the chance to be involved with something that scares me, I usually do it, because I'm trying to learn, I'm trying to understand music as much as I can, to become a better musician in general and work in different capacities."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El-P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>El-P began dating comedian and musician Emily Panic in 2010, and they married in October 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Panic was featured on Killer Mike's 2012 album <i><a href="/wiki/R.A.P._Music" title="R.A.P.
