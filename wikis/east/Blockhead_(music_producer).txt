<p><b>Tony Simon</b> (born October 8, 1976),<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Blockhead</b>, and formerly known as <b>Funky Omelet</b>, is an American hip hop record producer and DJ from <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. Aside from his solo efforts released on the <a href="/wiki/Ninja_Tune" title="Ninja Tune">Ninja Tune</a> label, he is most associated with producing tracks for <a href="/wiki/Aesop_Rock" title="Aesop Rock">Aesop Rock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He has been a member of the groups such as <a href="/wiki/Party_Fun_Action_Committee" title="Party Fun Action Committee">Party Fun Action Committee</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and The Mighty Jones.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blockhead_(music_producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Simon was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Manhattan" class="mw-redirect" title="Downtown Manhattan">Downtown Manhattan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ninjatune_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ninjatune-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He is the son of the late <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Simon" title="Sidney Simon">Sidney Simon</a>, a well-known sculptor in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, and Renee Adriance, a social worker.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He has one brother and five half siblings.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> At a young age, he loved hip hop in both the lyrical and musical aspects.<sup id="cite_ref-ninjatune_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ninjatune-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In his early teens, Simon and his friends formed a group called the Overground, where he started making music.<sup id="cite_ref-ninjatune_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ninjatune-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> As an aspiring rapper, he enrolled in <a href="/wiki/Boston_University" title="Boston University">Boston University</a> and encountered <a href="/wiki/Aesop_Rock" title="Aesop Rock">Aesop Rock</a>, another BU student.<sup id="cite_ref-prefixmag2_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prefixmag2-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> At this point, Simon quit rapping and focused on producing music.<sup id="cite_ref-prefixmag2_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prefixmag2-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> According to him, his stage name comes from the shape of his head: "While it's not square, it's pretty close."<sup id="cite_ref-artistdirect_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-artistdirect-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
