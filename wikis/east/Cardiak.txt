<p><b>Carl McCormick</b> (born June 26, 1988), professionally known as <b>Cardiak</b>, is an American record producer from <a href="/wiki/Willingboro_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Willingboro Township, New Jersey">Willingboro Township, New Jersey</a>. He began producing music in 2008 and is known for his work with closely associated artists such as <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hood" title="Ace Hood">Ace Hood</a>, <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Budden" title="Joe Budden">Joe Budden</a>, <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>/<a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freeway_(rapper)" title="Freeway (rapper)">Freeway</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rick Ross (rapper)">Rick Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trina" title="Trina">Trina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Cafe" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Cafe">Red Cafe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boosie_Badazz" title="Boosie Badazz">Boosie Badazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Lox" title="The Lox">D-Block</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>, among others.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cardiak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cardiak received notoriety for his first major placement in 2010 for his production of <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>' underground hit single "<a href="/wiki/Start_It_Up_(song)" title="Start It Up (song)">Start It Up</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, Ryan Leslie, <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, featured on Banks' third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_for_More_2" class="mw-redirect" title="The Hunger for More 2">The Hunger for More 2</a></i>, accompanied by his two other contributions as producer, "Take Em to War" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Tony_Yayo" title="Tony Yayo">Tony Yayo</a>, Banks' fellow <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a> member) and "Unexplainable" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>). <i>Start It Up</i> peaked at position 52 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Billboard Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> list.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cardiak received his first <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy award</a> nomination in 2013 for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rap_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rap Album">Best Rap Album</a>, because of his production of "Amsterdam" and "<a href="/wiki/Diced_Pineapples" title="Diced Pineapples">Diced Pineapples</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>) for rapper <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rick Ross (rapper)">Rick Ross</a>' fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/God_Forgives,_I_Don%27t" title="God Forgives, I Don&#39;t">God Forgives, I Don't</a></i>, which was released on July 31, 2012, and peaked at number–one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">200</a>. In early 2015, Cardiak won his first Grammy for Best Rap Album for his production of "Groundhog Day" for the deluxe edition of <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s eighth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Marshall_Mathers_LP_2" title="The Marshall Mathers LP 2">The Marshall Mathers LP 2</a></i>, released on November 9, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
