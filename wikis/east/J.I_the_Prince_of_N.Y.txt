<p><b>Justin Irvin Rivera</b> (born September 23, 2001), better known by his stage name <b>J.I the Prince of N.Y</b> or more recently simply <b>J.I.</b>, is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He is best known for his 2019 breakout single "Need Me", which has over 100 million streams on <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J.I_the_Prince_of_N.Y&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rivera grew up in <a href="/wiki/Crown_Heights,_Brooklyn" title="Crown Heights, Brooklyn">Crown Heights, Brooklyn</a> and is of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican</a> descent.<sup id="cite_ref-love_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-love-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nextlevel_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nextlevel-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At a young age he was introduced by his parents to music like <a href="/wiki/Old-school_hip_hop" title="Old-school hip hop">old-school hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reggaeton" title="Reggaeton">reggaeton</a>, and would make rudimentary beats on a miniature keyboard.<sup id="cite_ref-proud_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-proud-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In school, English was his favorite subject and he would write short stories.<sup id="cite_ref-radar_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-radar-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He began writing raps and freestyling with his brothers in middle school, influenced by New York City rappers like <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">Biggie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> and <a href="/wiki/Big_Pun" title="Big Pun">Big Pun</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nextlevel_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nextlevel-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-break_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-break-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J.I_the_Prince_of_N.Y&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1062260506">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:.5em 1.4em .8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:.5em 0 .8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{overflow:hidden;position:relative;margin:.5em auto .8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft span,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright span{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox>blockquote{margin:0;padding:0;border-left:0;font-family:inherit;font-size:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:110%;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote>:first-child{margin-top:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote:last-child>:last-child{margin-bottom:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .quote-title,.mw-parser-output .quotebox .quotebox-quote{display:block}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:640px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{width:100%!important;margin:0 0 .8em!important;float:none!important}}</style><div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:30em; ; background-color: #F2F2F2;">
<blockquote class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">
<p>I got to a point where everything went left, and I had a decision to make: Am I gonna just give up and do nothing for myself? I had already started my career; at the time, I developed a name for myself. Let me try to go hard. I can’t lose anything if I try one more time. So, I tried again.
</p>
</blockquote>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">— J.I. explaining his decision to return to music in 2019 after a period of inactivity.<sup id="cite_ref-proud_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-proud-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>In 2016 he appeared on the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rap_Game" title="The Rap Game">The Rap Game</a></i>, a reality show competition hosted by <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a> on <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-break_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-break-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The then fifteen-year-old secured his place on the show after Dupri found his freestyles on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nextlevel_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nextlevel-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-haute_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-haute-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In one of the challenges, Rivera placed in first, earning a spot on the cover of <i>Hip Hop Weekly</i> magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Although he did not win the overall competition, his impressive performance gained him a substantial following, and he released his debut single "No Static" soon thereafter.<sup id="cite_ref-haute_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-haute-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> By the end of the year he had received more than 500,000 views on his YouTube channel.<sup id="cite_ref-spotify_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spotify-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He released his first project in 2017, a three-track <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> called <i>Barely Famous</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-newtrack_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newtrack-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> After his clout from the show died down, he went through a period where he admits he lost his motivation and stopped focusing on his music.<sup id="cite_ref-proud_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-proud-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He blew all of his money from <i>The Rap Game</i> on <a href="/wiki/Marijuana" class="mw-redirect" title="Marijuana">marijuana</a> and was considering a job in construction.<sup id="cite_ref-247HH_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-247HH-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> After getting kicked out of his mom's house he made the decision to give music one last try and connected with his future manager Gabi Acevedo to restart his career.<sup id="cite_ref-247HH_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-247HH-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2019 he released his mixtape <i>Hood Life Krisis, Vol. 1</i>, which included his breakout single "Need Me", a "melodic, punch-line laden" track sampling the 2000 song "<a href="/wiki/Best_of_Me,_Part_2" title="Best of Me, Part 2">Best of Me, Part 2</a>" by <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BDa" class="mw-redirect" title="Mýa">Mýa</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-love_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-love-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-proud_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-proud-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In November, with the song at over 3 million streams on <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a>, it was announced he had signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> via G*Starr Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-deal_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deal-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He also put out his fifth and final music video for the album, this time for "Love Won't Change".<sup id="cite_ref-deal_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deal-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> As of 2022, "Need Me" remains his most-viewed with over 115 million YouTube views.
</p><p>He released <i>Hood Life Krisis, Vol. 2</i> that December and embarked on his first headlining national tour the following month.<sup id="cite_ref-haute_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-haute-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Holding the final shows in his hometown of New York, he became the first artist in history to sell out the legendary <a href="/wiki/SOB%27s" title="SOB&#39;s">SOB's</a> venue on three consecutive nights.<sup id="cite_ref-spotify_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spotify-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Notably, in April, <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a> paid him homage by playing and singing along to his songs "Used To" and "On Me" during an <a href="/wiki/Instagram_Live" class="mw-redirect" title="Instagram Live">Instagram Live</a> session with OVO Mark.<sup id="cite_ref-love_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-love-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-drake_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-drake-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> A week later he released a new single, "Proud of Me", along with an accompanying music video.<sup id="cite_ref-newtrack_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newtrack-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-drake_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-drake-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> After appearing on <a href="/wiki/Lil_Tjay" title="Lil Tjay">Lil Tjay</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/State_of_Emergency_(Lil_Tjay_EP)" title="State of Emergency (Lil Tjay EP)">State of Emergency</a></i> EP, he further collaborated with him in June on the single "Hood Scars 2", which served as a followup to J.I.'s original song which had by then amassed 15 million streams across all platforms.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2020 he came out with the single "Spanglish" with Puerto Rican rapper <a href="/wiki/Myke_Towers" title="Myke Towers">Myke Towers</a>, with both artists mixing English and Spanish in their verses.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> It served as the lead single to his next EP, <i>Welcome to GStarr Vol. 1</i>, which he released on July 17 and also featured <a href="/wiki/Lil_Durk" title="Lil Durk">Lil Durk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In the first 20 minutes, it garnered 1.3&#160;million streams on Spotify.<sup id="cite_ref-complex_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> A few days prior, Spotify announced a partnership with J.I. as a part of its recently launched RADAR program meant to promote "world-class talent through [their] global marketing and editorial teams" with placement in their official playlists, a mini-documentary, third-party partnerships, a Spotify Singles recording and support on the EP release.<sup id="cite_ref-radar_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-radar-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 30, 2020, his single "Need Me" was certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>, his first career plaque.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 18, 2020, J.I released <i>Hood Life Krisis, Vol. 3</i>, the third installment in his <i>Hood Life Krisis</i> series. The EP includes a sole guest appearance from <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a> on the track "R&amp;B Shit".<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=J.I_the_Prince_of_N.Y&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He has been described as having a "penchant for syrupy melodies."<sup id="cite_ref-love_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-love-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His melodic rap-sung style and distinctive <a href="/wiki/New_York_accent" title="New York accent">New York accent</a> have resulted in comparisons to <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Tjay" title="Lil Tjay">Lil Tjay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-love_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-love-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-break_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-break-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
