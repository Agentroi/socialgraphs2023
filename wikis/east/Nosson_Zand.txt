<p><b>Nathan Isaac Zand</b> (born December 11, 1981), also known as <b>Nosson Zand</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Chasidic" class="mw-redirect" title="Chasidic">Chasidic</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> from <a href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nosson_Zand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Zand was born in <a href="/wiki/Brookline,_Massachusetts" title="Brookline, Massachusetts">Brookline, Massachusetts</a>, on December 11, 1981. He is best known by  his <a href="/wiki/Hebrew" class="mw-redirect" title="Hebrew">Hebrew</a> name Nosson.
</p><p>Growing up in Brookline, he went to <a href="/wiki/Brookline_High_School" title="Brookline High School">Brookline High School</a>, where he took trumpet lessons and was exposed to rap by a friend from the <a href="/wiki/Public_housing" title="Public housing">projects</a>. They spent their school days rapping in the halls, on the street, at parties, talent shows and <a href="/wiki/Open_mic" title="Open mic">open mic</a> events. His first show was at the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Blues" title="House of Blues">House of Blues</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>. He then entered <a href="/wiki/Clark_University" title="Clark University">Clark University</a> where he studied <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French language</a> and U.S. history.
</p><p>He performed on campus with <a href="/wiki/Toots_and_the_Maytals" title="Toots and the Maytals">Toots and the Maytals</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Heather B, <a href="/wiki/The_Last_Poets" title="The Last Poets">The LastPoets</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and multiple solo gigs.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
Upon graduating, he went to live and work in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> where he worked as an English teacher. In <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> he met some <a href="/wiki/Hasidic_Jews" class="mw-redirect" title="Hasidic Jews">Hasidic Jews</a>  from the <a href="/wiki/Chabad" title="Chabad">Chabad</a> movement, and liked their way of life. He then started a new style which he called "Chasidic rap", in which he aimed to present lyrics with a positive message.
He performed at night clubs and other venues throughout <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In 2004 he returned to Boston, where he worked odd jobs and played gigs in Boston, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> and <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.
In a chance encounter in Boston he met <a href="/wiki/Matisyahu" title="Matisyahu">Matisyahu</a> and rapped for him.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Matisyahu then asked Nosson to perform with him on <a href="/wiki/Avalon,_California" title="Avalon, California">Avalon, California</a> and in South Paw in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After further gigs and performance with Matisyahu, in 2007 he went to <a href="/wiki/Hollywood,_Los_Angeles" title="Hollywood, Los Angeles">Hollywood</a> to play the lead role in <i>Song of David</i> a movie about a young <a href="/wiki/Hasid" title="Hasid">Hasid</a> who is studying to become a <a href="/wiki/Rabbi" title="Rabbi">rabbi</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> <a href="/wiki/Yeshiva" title="Yeshiva">yeshiva</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Alienated by the social boundaries that enclose him, he reaches out to the broader world. He becomes obsessed with rap music, wherein he discovers artistic freedom and honest self-expression. He then must choose between diverging worlds. With this film Nosson won the "Best Actor Award" at the <a href="/wiki/Boston_International_Film_Festival" title="Boston International Film Festival">Boston International Film Festival</a>.
</p>

<p>In 2008 he completed his first EP <i>The Return</i> and performed with Matisyahu on a 35 city tour of US and Canada, including at Matisyahu's 8-show "Festival of Light" in New York City, the name of which is a reference to the Jewish <a href="/wiki/Festival_of_Lights_(Judaism)" class="mw-redirect" title="Festival of Lights (Judaism)">Festival of Lights</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Zand released his first full-length album "Believers", which includes a song of the same name featuring <a href="/wiki/Matisyahu" title="Matisyahu">Matisyahu</a>. It is available on Spotify, iTunes and Google Play. He has also made a music video of his song featuring Matisyahu and posted it on YouTube.
</p>
