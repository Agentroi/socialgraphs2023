<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox biography vcard"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="font-size:125%;"><div class="fn" style="display:inline">Jasiri X</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><div style="display:inline" class="nickname">Jasiri Oronde Smith</div><br /><div style="display:inline" class="birthplace"><a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, U.S.</div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role">Rapper, activist</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Organization(s)</th><td class="infobox-data org">1Hood, 1Hood Media</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span style="white-space:nowrap;">Notable work</span></th><td class="infobox-data">"Free the Jena 6"; "What if the Tea Party was Black?"</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Spouse</th><td class="infobox-data">Celeste C. <p><b>Jasiri X</b> (born <b>Jasiri Oronde Smith</b>) is an American rapper and activist<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> who gained attention for his 2007 song "Free the <a href="/wiki/Jena_6" class="mw-redirect" title="Jena 6">Jena 6</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is a recipient of the <a href="/wiki/Rauschenberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Rauschenberg">Rauschenberg</a> Artist as Activist award<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and co-founder of anti-violence group 1Hood.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, he was awarded an <a href="/wiki/Honorary_doctorate" class="mw-redirect" title="Honorary doctorate">honorary doctorate</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Theological_Seminary" title="Chicago Theological Seminary">Chicago Theological Seminary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jasiri_X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jasiri X was born Jasiri Oronde Smith<sup id="cite_ref-:5_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>. In the 1980s he moved with his mother to <a href="/wiki/Monroeville,_Pennsylvania" title="Monroeville, Pennsylvania">Monroeville, Pennsylvania</a> and attended <a href="/wiki/Gateway_High_School_(Pennsylvania)" title="Gateway High School (Pennsylvania)">Gateway High School</a>, graduating at age 16. Intending to become a lawyer, he began college at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland,_College_Park" title="University of Maryland, College Park">University of Maryland</a>, then attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pittsburgh" title="University of Pittsburgh">University of Pittsburgh</a> but did not finish college.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jasiri_X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After leaving school, Jasiri began spoken word,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> performing initially as Jo Smith.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He also became an activist and was introduced to the <a href="/wiki/Nation_of_Islam" title="Nation of Islam">Nation of Islam</a>, taken by an acquaintance to a mosque in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Jasiri met <a href="/wiki/Louis_Farrakhan" title="Louis Farrakhan">Louis Farrakhan</a> a year later when Farrakhan was in Pittsburgh to support a boycott, and Jasiri subsequently registered as a member the Nation of Islam. In 2005, he became the minister of Muhammad Mosque No. 22 in <a href="/wiki/Wilkinsburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Wilkinsburg, Pennsylvania">Wilkinsburg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jasiri X has created music addressing social and political issues, including his 2007 song "Free the <a href="/wiki/Jena_6" class="mw-redirect" title="Jena 6">Jena 6</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> "Trayvon"  following <a href="/wiki/George_Zimmerman" title="George Zimmerman">George Zimmerman</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Killing_of_Trayvon_Martin" title="Killing of Trayvon Martin">killing of Trayvon Martin</a> in February 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> "Do We Need to Start a Riot?"<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and "What if the <a href="/wiki/Tea_Party_movement" title="Tea Party movement">Tea Party</a> was Black?"<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He encouraged other hip-hop artists to engage these concerns as well.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010 he released his first album, <i>American History X</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, he released a second album, called <i>Ascension,</i> with <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a> label Wandering Worx<i>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-:3_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, he released <i>Black Liberation Theology</i>, with features from <a href="/wiki/David_Banner" title="David Banner">David Banner</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tef_Poe" title="Tef Poe">Tef Poe</a> among others.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He has mentioned <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac</a>, the <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">Notorious B.I.G.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Enemy (band)">Public Enemy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/KRS-One" title="KRS-One">KRS-One</a> as among his musical influences.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jasiri X also has a <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>-based show "This Week with Jasiri X"<sup id="cite_ref-:7_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and has been an advocate for <a href="/wiki/Net_neutrality" title="Net neutrality">net neutrality</a><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> particularly as it relates to protecting access for low-income people of color.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, he cofounded the anti-violence group 1Hood,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> which includes a media academy teaching young black boys to analyze media as well as create their own.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_grants">Awards and grants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jasiri_X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards and grants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>2015 "Artist as Activist" fellowship at the <a href="/wiki/Robert_Rauschenberg_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Rauschenberg Foundation">Robert Rauschenberg Foundation</a><sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:7_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2015 USA Fellow, a $50,000 award from <a href="/wiki/United_States_Artists" title="United States Artists">United States Artists</a> for "innovative, accomplished artists...for their fresh perspectives, unique artistic vision, and impact on their field"<sup id="cite_ref-:5_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2015 commission from the <a href="/wiki/Open_Society_Foundations" title="Open Society Foundations">Open Society Foundation</a> to create a film on the effects of the drug war on <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2016 honorary doctorate from the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Theological_Seminary" title="Chicago Theological Seminary">Chicago Theological Seminary</a><sup id="cite_ref-:4_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
