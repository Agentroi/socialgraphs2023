<p><b>Nora Lum</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born June 2, 1988),<sup id="cite_ref-金球奖首个亚裔影后！奥卡菲娜获喜剧电影最佳女主_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-金球奖首个亚裔影后！奥卡菲娜获喜剧电影最佳女主-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Awkwafina</b>, is an American actress, rapper, and comedian. She rose to prominence in 2012 when her rap song "My Vag" became popular on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. She then released her debut album, <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Ranger_(album)" title="Yellow Ranger (album)"><i>Yellow Ranger</i></a> (2014), and appeared on the <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Girl_Code" title="Girl Code">Girl Code</a></i> (2014–2015). She expanded to films with supporting roles in the comedies <i><a href="/wiki/Neighbors_2:_Sorority_Rising" title="Neighbors 2: Sorority Rising">Neighbors 2: Sorority Rising</a></i> (2016), <i><a href="/wiki/Ocean%27s_8" title="Ocean&#39;s 8">Ocean's 8</a></i> (2018), <i><a href="/wiki/Crazy_Rich_Asians_(film)" title="Crazy Rich Asians (film)">Crazy Rich Asians</a></i> (2018), and <i><a href="/wiki/Jumanji:_The_Next_Level" title="Jumanji: The Next Level">Jumanji: The Next Level</a></i> (2019). For her starring role as a grieving young woman in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Farewell_(2019_film)" title="The Farewell (2019 film)">The Farewell</a></i> (2019), Awkwafina won a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Awards" title="Golden Globe Awards">Golden Globe Award</a>.
</p><p>Since 2020, Awkwafina has served as co-creator, writer, and executive producer of the <a href="/wiki/Comedy_Central" title="Comedy Central">Comedy Central</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Awkwafina_Is_Nora_from_Queens" title="Awkwafina Is Nora from Queens">Awkwafina Is Nora from Queens</a></i>, in which she also plays a fictionalized version of herself. In 2021, she portrayed <a href="/wiki/Katy_(Marvel_Cinematic_Universe)" class="mw-redirect" title="Katy (Marvel Cinematic Universe)">Katy</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Cinematic_Universe" title="Marvel Cinematic Universe">Marvel Cinematic Universe</a> superhero film <i><a href="/wiki/Shang-Chi_and_the_Legend_of_the_Ten_Rings" title="Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings">Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> She has also performed voice roles in the animated films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Angry_Birds_Movie_2" title="The Angry Birds Movie 2">The Angry Birds Movie 2</a></i> (2019), <i><a href="/wiki/Raya_and_the_Last_Dragon" title="Raya and the Last Dragon">Raya and the Last Dragon</a></i> (2021), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bad_Guys_(film)" title="The Bad Guys (film)">The Bad Guys</a></i> (2022), and in the musical <i><a href="/wiki/The_Little_Mermaid_(2023_film)" title="The Little Mermaid (2023 film)">The Little Mermaid</a></i> (2023).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Awkwafina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Awkwafina was born in <a href="/wiki/Stony_Brook,_New_York" title="Stony Brook, New York">Stony Brook, New York</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-THR_interview_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-THR_interview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> to Wally Lum, a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Americans" title="Chinese Americans">Chinese American</a>, and Tia Lum, a <a href="/wiki/Korean_Americans" title="Korean Americans">Korean American</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Her father worked in the <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a> field,<sup id="cite_ref-THR_interview_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-THR_interview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and comes from a family of <a href="/wiki/Restaurateur" title="Restaurateur">restaurateurs</a> – his father immigrated to the United States in the 1940s, and opened the <a href="/wiki/Cantonese_restaurant" title="Cantonese restaurant">Cantonese restaurant</a> <i>Lum's</i> in <a href="/wiki/Flushing,_Queens" title="Flushing, Queens">Flushing, Queens</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-usmagazine_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usmagazine-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> one of the neighborhood's first Chinese restaurants.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Her mother was a painter who immigrated with her family to the United States from South Korea in 1972.<sup id="cite_ref-usmagazine_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usmagazine-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She died from <a href="/wiki/Pulmonary_hypertension" title="Pulmonary hypertension">pulmonary hypertension</a> in 1992 when Awkwafina was four, and Awkwafina was subsequently raised by her father and paternal grandparents.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She became especially close to her paternal grandmother, Powah Lum.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:8_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Awkwafina grew up in <a href="/wiki/Forest_Hills,_Queens" title="Forest Hills, Queens">Forest Hills, Queens</a>, and attended <a href="/wiki/Fiorello_H._LaGuardia_High_School" title="Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School">Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School</a>, where she played the <a href="/wiki/Trumpet" title="Trumpet">trumpet</a> and was trained in <a href="/wiki/Classical_music" title="Classical music">classical music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> At age 15, she adopted the stage name Awkwafina, "definitely a person I repressed" and an <a href="/wiki/Alter_ego" title="Alter ego">alter ego</a> to her "quiet and more passive" personality during her college years.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-intothegloss1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intothegloss1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-galoremag1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galoremag1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> From 2006 to 2008, she learned <a href="/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese" title="Mandarin Chinese">Mandarin</a> at <a href="/wiki/Beijing_Language_and_Culture_University" title="Beijing Language and Culture University">Beijing Language and Culture University</a> to communicate with her paternal grandmother without barriers.<sup id="cite_ref-金球奖首个亚裔影后！奥卡菲娜获喜剧电影最佳女主_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-金球奖首个亚裔影后！奥卡菲娜获喜剧电影最佳女主-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> She majored in <a href="/wiki/Journalism" title="Journalism">journalism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_studies" title="Women&#39;s studies">women's studies</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_at_Albany,_State_University_of_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="University at Albany, State University of New York">University at Albany, State University of New York</a> and graduated in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Awkwafina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Awkwafina began rapping at 13.<sup id="cite_ref-galoremag1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galoremag1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> She got her start producing music with <a href="/wiki/GarageBand" title="GarageBand">GarageBand</a> but eventually learned <a href="/wiki/Logic_Pro" title="Logic Pro">Logic Pro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ableton_Live" title="Ableton Live">Ableton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, her song "My Vag" became popular on YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She originally wrote the song in college<sup id="cite_ref-:3_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> as a response to <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Avalon" title="Mickey Avalon">Mickey Avalon</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Mickey_Avalon_(album)" title="Mickey Avalon (album)">My Dick (Tribute to Nate)</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-:4_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> She was fired from her job at a publishing house when her employer recognized her in the video.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Her solo hip-hop album <i><a href="/wiki/Yellow_Ranger_(album)" title="Yellow Ranger (album)">Yellow Ranger</a></i> was released on February 11, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Its 11 tracks include a number of her previous singles released on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, including the title track "Yellow Ranger", "Queef" and "NYC Bitche$". In 2014, Awkwafina appeared in six episodes of the third and fourth seasons of <i><a href="/wiki/Girl_Code" title="Girl Code">Girl Code</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In 2015 she co-hosted its spin-off, <i><a href="/wiki/Girl_Code_Live" class="mw-redirect" title="Girl Code Live">Girl Code Live</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>
In 2016, she collaborated with comedian <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Cho" title="Margaret Cho">Margaret Cho</a> on "Green Tea", a song that pokes fun at Asian stereotypes.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> She was part of the lineup at <a href="/wiki/Tenacious_D" title="Tenacious D">Tenacious D</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Festival_Supreme" title="Festival Supreme">Festival Supreme</a> on October 25, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> She was also a <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a> (DJ) at bars in New York.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> She is profiled in the 2016 documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Bad_Rap_(film)" title="Bad Rap (film)">Bad Rap</a></i>, an official selection at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/Tribeca_Film_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Tribeca Film Festival">Tribeca Film Festival</a>. It puts the spotlight on her and Asian-American rappers such as <a href="/wiki/Dumbfoundead" title="Dumbfoundead">Dumbfoundead</a>, Rekstizzy and Lyricks.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> She released a 7-track EP, <i>In Fina We Trust</i>, on June 8, 2018;<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> it won the 2019 A2IM Libera Award for Best Hip-Hop/Rap Album.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> </p><figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ken_Jeong_%26_Awkwafina.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Ken_Jeong_%26_Awkwafina.png/220px-Ken_Jeong_%26_Awkwafina.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Ken_Jeong_%26_Awkwafina.png/330px-Ken_Jeong_%26_Awkwafina.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Ken_Jeong_%26_Awkwafina.png/440px-Ken_Jeong_%26_Awkwafina.png 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="855" /></a><figcaption>Awkwafina with <a href="/wiki/Ken_Jeong" title="Ken Jeong">Ken Jeong</a> in 2018</figcaption></figure>
<p>Awkwafina hosted the short-form talk show web series <i>Tawk</i> for the digital production company Astronauts Wanted from 2015 to 2017. The first season premiered on YouTube and was picked up for exclusive <a href="/wiki/Streaming_media" title="Streaming media">streaming</a> on <a href="/wiki/Verizon" class="mw-redirect" title="Verizon">Verizon</a>’s <a href="/wiki/Go90" title="Go90">Go90</a> platform.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> It was an Official Honoree at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/Webby_Awards" title="Webby Awards">Webby Awards</a> and was nominated for a 2016 <a href="/wiki/Streamy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Streamy Award">Streamy Award</a> in the News and Culture category.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> In 2016 she played a supporting role as Christine, a member of Kappa Nu in <i><a href="/wiki/Neighbors_2:_Sorority_Rising" title="Neighbors 2: Sorority Rising">Neighbors 2: Sorority Rising</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> and voiced Quail in the animated comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Storks_(film)" title="Storks (film)">Storks</a></i>. In 2018 she starred in the indie comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Dude_(film)" title="Dude (film)">Dude</a></i>, playing Rebecca, one of four best friends. She was among the principal cast in <i><a href="/wiki/Ocean%27s_8" title="Ocean&#39;s 8">Ocean's 8</a>,</i> the all-female spinoff to the <i><a href="/wiki/Ocean%27s_Trilogy" class="mw-redirect" title="Ocean&#39;s Trilogy">Ocean's Trilogy</a></i>. She then co-starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Crazy_Rich_Asians_(film)" title="Crazy Rich Asians (film)">Crazy Rich Asians</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Jon_M._Chu" title="Jon M. Chu">Jon M. Chu</a>, playing <a href="/wiki/Goh_Peik_Lin" class="mw-redirect" title="Goh Peik Lin">Goh Peik Lin</a>, a Singaporean college friend of lead character Rachel Chu (<a href="/wiki/Constance_Wu" title="Constance Wu">Constance Wu</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> She had a recurring role in the <a href="/wiki/Hulu" title="Hulu">Hulu</a> original series <i><a href="/wiki/Future_Man_(TV_series)" title="Future Man (TV series)">Future Man</a></i> in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> She hosted the 2018 <a href="/wiki/IHeartRadio_MMVAs" title="IHeartRadio MMVAs">iHeartRadio MMVAs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Awkwafina hosted the October 6, 2018, episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>, becoming the second East Asian-American woman to host the show (after <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Liu" title="Lucy Liu">Lucy Liu</a>, whose episode Awkwafina cites as her inspiration to one day be famous enough to host <i>SNL</i>). She did an impression of <a href="/wiki/Sandra_Oh" title="Sandra Oh">Sandra Oh</a>, who later in the season became the first East Asian-Canadian woman to host an <i>SNL</i> episode and the third East Asian woman to host overall.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Awkwafina starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Farewell_(2019_film)" title="The Farewell (2019 film)">The Farewell</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Lulu_Wang_(filmmaker)" title="Lulu Wang (filmmaker)">Lulu Wang</a>. She played Billi, a writer who visits her ill grandmother in China.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The film received critical acclaim. Awkwafina received the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Actress_%E2%80%93_Motion_Picture_Comedy_or_Musical" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Comedy or Musical">Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Comedy or Musical</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> becoming the first person of Asian descent to win a Golden Globe Award in any lead actress film category, after being only the sixth woman of Asian descent to be nominated in the lead actress in a musical or comedy category.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> In the same year, she starred as avatar Ming Fleetfoot in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Jumanji:_The_Next_Level" title="Jumanji: The Next Level">Jumanji: The Next Level</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> which was a commercial success.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> In July 2019, Awkwafina was cast as <a href="/wiki/Katy_(Marvel_Cinematic_Universe)" class="mw-redirect" title="Katy (Marvel Cinematic Universe)">Katy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Studios" title="Marvel Studios">Marvel Studios</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Shang-Chi_and_the_Legend_of_the_Ten_Rings" title="Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings">Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings</a></i> alongside actors <a href="/wiki/Simu_Liu" title="Simu Liu">Simu Liu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Leung_Chiu-wai" title="Tony Leung Chiu-wai">Tony Leung Chiu-wai</a>. Directed by <a href="/wiki/Destin_Daniel_Cretton" title="Destin Daniel Cretton">Destin Daniel Cretton</a>, the film was released in theaters on September 3, 2021, earning critical praise and grossing $430.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Shang-Chi_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shang-Chi-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> She won the <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actress">Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actress</a> for her performance in <i>Shang-Chi</i>. In August 2019, Disney announced that Awkwafina would voice <a href="/wiki/Sisu_(Raya_and_the_Last_Dragon)" title="Sisu (Raya and the Last Dragon)">Sisu</a> the dragon in the animated film <i><a href="/wiki/Raya_and_the_Last_Dragon" title="Raya and the Last Dragon">Raya and the Last Dragon</a></i>, which was released on March 5, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Awkwafina improvised much of her dialogue for the film, drawing comparisons to <a href="/wiki/Robin_Williams" title="Robin Williams">Robin Williams</a>' performance as the Genie in <i><a href="/wiki/Aladdin_(1992_Disney_film)" title="Aladdin (1992 Disney film)">Aladdin</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2020, Awkwafina stars in the comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Awkwafina_Is_Nora_from_Queens" title="Awkwafina Is Nora from Queens">Awkwafina Is Nora from Queens</a></i>; she is also a writer and <a href="/wiki/Executive_producer" title="Executive producer">executive producer</a> of that show. As part of the promotional campaign, she recorded new announcements for the <a href="/wiki/7_(New_York_City_Subway_service)" title="7 (New York City Subway service)">7 train</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Nyc_subway" class="mw-redirect" title="Nyc subway">New York City Subway</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-:5_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> making jokes, such as "This is Hunters Point Avenue, a friendly reminder that seats are for people, not your bag" and "This is 46th Street, which is a lucky number, I just learned that on the internet. Also learned that pigeons and doves are the same things, WHAT?!", at every stop. These recordings were used until the series premiered on January 22.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> In a season one episode, Simu Liu made a guest appearance before the release of <i>Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Image_and_artistry">Image and artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Awkwafina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Image and artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Awkwafina_-_Golden_Globes_2020_-_01.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Awkwafina_-_Golden_Globes_2020_-_01.png/220px-Awkwafina_-_Golden_Globes_2020_-_01.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="156" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Awkwafina_-_Golden_Globes_2020_-_01.png/330px-Awkwafina_-_Golden_Globes_2020_-_01.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Awkwafina_-_Golden_Globes_2020_-_01.png/440px-Awkwafina_-_Golden_Globes_2020_-_01.png 2x" data-file-width="971" data-file-height="687" /></a><figcaption>Awkwafina on the red carpet of the <a href="/wiki/77th_Golden_Globe_Awards" title="77th Golden Globe Awards">77th Golden Globe Awards</a> in 2020</figcaption></figure><p>Awkwafina has said that <a href="/wiki/Charles_Bukowski" title="Charles Bukowski">Charles Bukowski</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ana%C3%AFs_Nin" title="Anaïs Nin">Anaïs Nin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joan_Didion" title="Joan Didion">Joan Didion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Waits" title="Tom Waits">Tom Waits</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chet_Baker" title="Chet Baker">Chet Baker</a> were early influences.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before launching her entertainment career, she worked as an intern at the <i><a href="/wiki/Gotham_Gazette" title="Gotham Gazette">Gotham Gazette</a></i> in New York City; as an intern at the <i><a href="/wiki/Times_Union_(Albany)" title="Times Union (Albany)">Times Union</a></i> newspaper in <a href="/wiki/Albany,_New_York" title="Albany, New York">Albany, New York</a>; and as a publicity assistant for publishing house <a href="/wiki/Rodale_Books" class="mw-redirect" title="Rodale Books">Rodale Books</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-intothegloss1_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intothegloss1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> which fired her after they discovered her music videos. She later worked at a vegan bodega.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Awkwafina has expressed support for <a href="/wiki/Time%27s_Up_(organization)" title="Time&#39;s Up (organization)">Time's Up</a>, a movement started by Hollywood celebrities against <a href="/wiki/Sexual_harassment" title="Sexual harassment">sexual harassment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> She has also advocated for more female directors and against the stereotyping of Asians in media.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She was featured in <a href="/wiki/Gap_Inc." title="Gap Inc.">Gap's</a> "Logo Remix" campaign, which featured up-and-coming artists who "are remixing creative culture on their own terms," such as <a href="/wiki/SZA_(singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="SZA (singer)">SZA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sabrina_Claudio" title="Sabrina Claudio">Sabrina Claudio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Naomi_Watanabe" title="Naomi Watanabe">Naomi Watanabe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, she released a guidebook, <i>Awkwafina's NYC</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Awkwafina was honored as Kore Asian Media's Female Breakout of the Year in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 16, 2019, she headlined <a href="/wiki/The_Infatuation" title="The Infatuation">The Infatuation</a>'s annual food festival, <i>EEEEEATSCON</i>. She spoke about her upbringing in Queens, where her family owned a Cantonese restaurant.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Awkwafina has developed a profile as a fashion model, appearing regularly on magazine covers, which she re-posts on her Instagram account. These include <i>Vogue, Allure, Harpers Bazaar, Cosmopolitan, Marie Claire</i> and feminist magazine <i>Bust</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_appropriation">Cultural appropriation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Awkwafina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cultural appropriation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Awkwafina has faced criticism for <a href="/wiki/Cultural_appropriation" title="Cultural appropriation">cultural appropriation</a> of <a href="/wiki/AAVE" class="mw-redirect" title="AAVE">AAVE</a> and mannerisms stereotypic of the African-American community.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Jackson_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jackson-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, she said, "I welcome that conversation because as an Asian-American identity, we're still trying to figure out what that is."<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> On February 5, 2022, Awkwafina tweeted, "My immigrant background allowed me to carve an American identity off the movies and tv shows I watched, the children I went to public school with, and my undying love and respect for hip hop. I think as a group, Asian Americans are still trying to figure out what that journey means for them – what is correct and where they don't belong ... As a non-black POC, I stand by the fact that I will always listen and work tirelessly to understand the history and context of AAVE".<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> Activists criticized this tweet as being several years late and unapologetic.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Awkwafina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_awards_and_nominations_received_by_Awkwafina" title="List of awards and nominations received by Awkwafina">List of awards and nominations received by Awkwafina</a></div>
<p>Awkwafina was honored as Kore Asian Media's Female Breakout of the Year in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> For her performance in the comedy-drama film <i>The Farewell</i>, she received the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Actress_%E2%80%93_Motion_Picture_Comedy_or_Musical" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Comedy or Musical">Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Comedy or Musical</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Satellite_Award_for_Best_Actress_%E2%80%93_Motion_Picture" title="Satellite Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture">Satellite Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Musical or Comedy</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Santa_Barbara_International_Film_Festival" title="Santa Barbara International Film Festival">Santa Barbara International Film Festival Virtuoso Award</a>, among numerous other nominations. Alongside the film's ensemble, she was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award_for_Outstanding_Performance_by_a_Cast_in_a_Motion_Picture" title="Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture">Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture</a>. Awkwafina also received a nomination for the <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Rising_Star_Award" title="BAFTA Rising Star Award">BAFTA Rising Star Award</a>.
</p>
