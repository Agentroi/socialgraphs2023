<p><b>Alfred Schoeninger</b> (born June 16, 1981), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Mopes</b>, and formerly <b>Prolyphic</b>, is an American hip hop musician from <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He is one half of the duo Stick Figures along with Robust and is currently signed to <a href="/wiki/Strange_Famous_Records" title="Strange Famous Records">Strange Famous Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prolyphic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prolyphic first became known in 1999 when he had recorded a demo tape and shopped it around <a href="/wiki/Scribble_Jam" title="Scribble Jam">Scribble Jam</a>, which caught the attention of artists such as <a href="/wiki/Sage_Francis" title="Sage Francis">Sage Francis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sole_(hip_hop_artist)" title="Sole (hip hop artist)">Sole</a> and Scribble Jam founder Kevin Beacham.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Following this, he and Sage Francis continued trading demo tapes and became more familiar with each other through the local <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a> hip hop circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> They continued to stay friends and Sage Francis eventually signed Prolyphic to his label, <a href="/wiki/Strange_Famous_Records" title="Strange Famous Records">Strange Famous Records</a>. He became the second artist signed to the label, after <a href="/wiki/B._Dolan" title="B. Dolan">B. Dolan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, he released Stick Figures' self titled debut album. The same year, he was introduced by Sage Francis to hip hop producer <a href="/wiki/Reanimator_(producer)" title="Reanimator (producer)">Reanimator</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Together, the two released the critically acclaimed album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ugly_Truth_(album)" title="The Ugly Truth (album)">The Ugly Truth</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Strange_Famous_Records" title="Strange Famous Records">Strange Famous Records</a> in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Alias_(musician)" title="Alias (musician)">Alias</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macromantics" class="mw-redirect" title="Macromantics">Macromantics</a> and Sage Francis, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He released <i>Working Man</i>, a collaborative album with London hip hop producer <a href="/wiki/Buddy_Peace" title="Buddy Peace">Buddy Peace</a> in 2013, also on Strange Famous Records.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, he released DNGRFLD, a fully self produced album on Strange Famous, which features <a href="/wiki/Educated_Consumers" title="Educated Consumers">Seez Mics</a> and Cas One.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
