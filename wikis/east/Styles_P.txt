<p><b>David R. Styles</b> (born November 28, 1974), better known by his stage name <b>Styles P</b> (also known <a href="/wiki/Mononymous_person" class="mw-redirect" title="Mononymous person">mononymously</a> as <b>Styles</b>),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an American rapper, best known as a member of hip hop group <a href="/wiki/The_Lox" title="The Lox">the Lox</a>, alongside childhood friends <a href="/wiki/Sheek_Louch" title="Sheek Louch">Sheek Louch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>. Along with the other members of the Lox, he is a founder of <a href="/wiki/D-Block_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="D-Block Records">D-Block Records</a> and was also a part of the <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders_Entertainment" title="Ruff Ryders Entertainment">Ruff Ryders Entertainment</a> collective. In addition, he has released multiple albums and mixtapes as a solo MC. In 2002, he released his debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/A_Gangster_and_a_Gentleman" title="A Gangster and a Gentleman">A Gangster and a Gentleman</a></i>, which contained the hit single "<a href="/wiki/Good_Times_(Styles_P_song)" title="Good Times (Styles P song)">Good Times</a>". The song peaked at number 22 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has gone on to release <i><a href="/wiki/Time_Is_Money_(Styles_P_album)" title="Time Is Money (Styles P album)">Time Is Money</a></i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Gangster_(Extraordinary_Gentleman)" title="Super Gangster (Extraordinary Gentleman)">Super Gangster (Extraordinary Gentleman)</a></i> (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/Master_of_Ceremonies_(Styles_P_album)" title="Master of Ceremonies (Styles P album)">Master of Ceremonies</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/The_World%27s_Most_Hardest_MC_Project" title="The World&#39;s Most Hardest MC Project">The World's Most Hardest MC Project</a></i> (2012), <i><a href="/wiki/Float_(Styles_P_album)" title="Float (Styles P album)">Float</a></i> (2013), <i><a href="/wiki/Phantom_and_the_Ghost" title="Phantom and the Ghost">Phantom and the Ghost</a></i> (2014), <i>A Wise Guy and a Wise Guy</i> (2015), and <i><a href="/wiki/G-Host" title="G-Host">G-Host</a></i> (2018).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Styles_P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>David R. Styles was born November 28, 1974, in <a href="/wiki/Corona,_Queens" title="Corona, Queens">Corona, Queens</a>, New York City, to a South African mother and a Jamaican father from <a href="/wiki/Bed-Stuy" class="mw-redirect" title="Bed-Stuy">Bed-Stuy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After his parents' divorce, Styles and his younger brother Gary moved to <a href="/wiki/Yonkers,_New_York" title="Yonkers, New York">Yonkers</a> with their mother. It was there he met lifelong friends <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sheek_Louch" title="Sheek Louch">Sheek Louch</a>. The trio bonded over their shared passion for <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> and eventually formed a group known as <a href="/wiki/The_Lox" title="The Lox">the Lox</a> in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-complex1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Styles_P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1994.E2.80.932000:_The_Lox"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1994–2000:_The_Lox">1994–2000: The Lox</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Styles_P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1994–2000: The Lox">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Jadakiss_Styles_P.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Jadakiss_Styles_P.jpg/220px-Jadakiss_Styles_P.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Jadakiss_Styles_P.jpg/330px-Jadakiss_Styles_P.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Jadakiss_Styles_P.jpg/440px-Jadakiss_Styles_P.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5184" data-file-height="3456" /></a><figcaption>Styles P (left) performing with <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>As a trio, the Lox began rapping in the mid-1990s and started to build a steady underground following thanks to their performances and street freestyles. In their late teens, the trio met <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a> who was impressed with their lyrical content, and gave their demo to <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Sean "Diddy" Combs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Diddy immediately hired the trio to write for <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy Records</a>. After signing with Bad Boy, the Lox quickly started collaborating on hits with Diddy, <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">the Notorious B.I.G.</a>, Mary J. Blige and <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> which gave them instant notoriety and status within the hip-hop and mainstream charts.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1996 and 1997, the group wrote and performed on a number of Diddy hits, including "<a href="/wiki/It%27s_All_About_the_Benjamins" title="It&#39;s All About the Benjamins">It's All About the Benjamins</a>" and "I Got the Power", <a href="/wiki/Mase" title="Mase">Mase</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/24_Hrs._to_Live" title="24 Hrs. to Live">24 Hrs. to Live</a>", Mariah Carey's "<a href="/wiki/Honey_(Mariah_Carey_song)" title="Honey (Mariah Carey song)">Honey</a>", the Notorious B.I.G.'s "Last Day" and Mary J. Blige's "Can't Get You Off My Mind".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 1997, the Lox received international acclaim when their tribute to the late Biggie Smalls, "We'll Always Love Big Poppa", was picked as the B-side track from Diddy's <i><a href="/wiki/No_Way_Out_(Puff_Daddy_album)" title="No Way Out (Puff Daddy album)">No Way Out</a></i> album (3× Platinum) with the number one hit, "<a href="/wiki/I%27ll_Be_Missing_You" title="I&#39;ll Be Missing You">I'll Be Missing You</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This single was the most played hit in 1997, which opened the door for the Lox's to write more of their own songs and eventually release their first album in January 1998 titled <i><a href="/wiki/Money,_Power_%26_Respect" title="Money, Power &amp; Respect">Money, Power &amp; Respect</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The album went on to be certified platinum, and, although the album was a chart-topping success, the group grew to feel that the glossy image and production style of Bad Boy Records conflicted with their grimier rap aesthetic. After trying and failing to get <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Puff Daddy</a> to grant them a release from their contract, the group confronted him in his office, with Styles P throwing a chair at his own boss. After this, the group was granted their release and left Bad Boy to sign with <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders_Entertainment" title="Ruff Ryders Entertainment">Ruff Ryder Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2000.E2.80.932007:_Ruff_Ryder.27s_and_solo_albums"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000–2007:_Ruff_Ryder's_and_solo_albums">2000–2007: Ruff Ryder's and solo albums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Styles_P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000–2007: Ruff Ryder&#039;s and solo albums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After releasing another Lox album, <i><a href="/wiki/We_Are_the_Streets" title="We Are the Streets">We Are the Streets</a></i> and appearing on albums with other Ruff Ryders artists, Styles released his first solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Gangster_and_a_Gentleman" title="A Gangster and a Gentleman">A Gangster and a Gentleman</a></i>, in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The album was supported by the hit single, <a href="/wiki/Good_Times_(Styles_P_song)" title="Good Times (Styles P song)">Good Times</a>, which peaked at number 22 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a> and number 6 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a>. The song also received widespread airplay on the radio throughout 2002. In 2004, Styles was featured on an alternate version of <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a>'s hit debut single, <a href="/wiki/Locked_Up_(song)" title="Locked Up (song)">Locked Up</a>. Two years later, he released his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Time_Is_Money_(Styles_P_album)" title="Time Is Money (Styles P album)">Time is Money</a></i> on Ruff Ryders which was followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Gangster_(Extraordinary_Gentleman)" title="Super Gangster (Extraordinary Gentleman)">Super Gangster (Extraordinary Gentleman)</a></i> a year later.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic1_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932014:_The_Green_Ghost_and_various_mixtapes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2014:_The_Green_Ghost_and_various_mixtapes">2010–2014: The Green Ghost and various mixtapes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Styles_P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010–2014: The Green Ghost and various mixtapes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Styles_P_2014.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Styles_P_2014.jpg/220px-Styles_P_2014.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Styles_P_2014.jpg/330px-Styles_P_2014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Styles_P_2014.jpg/440px-Styles_P_2014.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5184" data-file-height="3456" /></a><figcaption>Styles P in 2014</figcaption></figure>
<p>After recording many mixtapes Styles returned to the Hip-Hop scene by releasing two retail albums in 2010, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Green_Ghost_Project" title="The Green Ghost Project">The Green Ghost Project</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Green_Lantern" title="DJ Green Lantern">DJ Green Lantern</a> on Invasion Records and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ghost_Dub-Dime" title="The Ghost Dub-Dime">The Ghost Dub-Dime</a></i> a retail mixtape on E1 Records (formally Koch Records).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> This began a string of annual retail releases with his fourth, fifth, sixth, and seventh albums (2011's <i><a href="/wiki/Master_of_Ceremonies_(Styles_P_album)" title="Master of Ceremonies (Styles P album)">Master of Ceremonies</a></i>, 2012's <i><a href="/wiki/The_World%27s_Most_Hardest_MC_Project" title="The World&#39;s Most Hardest MC Project">The World's Most Hardest MC Project</a></i>, 2013's <i><a href="/wiki/Float_(Styles_P_album)" title="Float (Styles P album)">Float</a></i>, and 2014's <i><a href="/wiki/Phantom_and_the_Ghost" title="Phantom and the Ghost">Phantom and the Ghost</a></i>) all being released within a year of each other.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.93present:_The_Lox_reunion"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–present:_The_Lox_reunion">2016–present: The Lox reunion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Styles_P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2016–present: The Lox reunion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2016, the Lox released their first album in 16 years, <i><a href="/wiki/Filthy_America..._It%27s_Beautiful" title="Filthy America... It&#39;s Beautiful">Filthy America... It's Beautiful</a></i>. The album reached number 6 on the <i>Billboard</i> Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums chart. It was followed by the release of the albums, <i><a href="/wiki/G-Host" title="G-Host">G-Host</a></i> (2018), <i>Dime Bag</i> (2018), <i>S.P. the GOAT: Ghost of All Time</i> (2019) and <i>Presence</i> (2019). Styles P and <a href="/wiki/Dave_East" title="Dave East">Dave East</a> also released a collab album titled <i>Beloved</i>, in 2018. Styles P's 13th studio album, <i>Styles David: Ghost Your Enthusiasm</i>, was released on May 22, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Styles_P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Styles is a <a href="/wiki/Vegan" class="mw-redirect" title="Vegan">vegan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BIO_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BIO-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Styles married his wife Adjua on July 31, 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Together, they have two children, Noah (born 1998) and Tai (1995–2015), whom Adjua had from a previous relationship. Styles served as Tai's stepfather for the majority of her life, until she committed suicide in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Styles' younger brother, Gary Quarles Styles, died in a car accident on May 13, 2001. Styles has made many references to Gary in his music, including the tribute song "My Brother", from his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/A_Gangster_and_a_Gentleman" title="A Gangster and a Gentleman">A Gangster and a Gentleman</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2002, Styles was involved in an altercation at a New York nightclub that led to him stabbing a man in the buttocks. Styles surrendered himself to authorities on November 26, 2002, and began serving his eight-month sentence in the Westchester County Jail in <a href="/wiki/Valhalla,_New_York" title="Valhalla, New York">Valhalla, New York</a>, the same day.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Styles was released from prison on August 4, 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Politically, Styles has publicly denounced <a href="/wiki/Black_Lives_Matter" title="Black Lives Matter">Black Lives Matter</a> and endorsed the 2020 re-election campaign of <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_ventures">Business ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Styles_P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Business ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Both Styles and <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a> launched a local juice bar called "Juices for Life" in 2011, there are currently six locations in New York.<sup id="cite_ref-BIO_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BIO-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, Styles and his wife Adjua launched a local health food store called "Farmacy for Life".<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_projects">Other projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Styles_P&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Other projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Styles P released his first novel, <i>Invincible</i>, in 2010<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> through <a href="/wiki/Random_House" title="Random House">Random House</a> Publishing Group.<sup id="cite_ref-barnesandnoble1_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-barnesandnoble1-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In 2011 he opened up a juice bar in the <a href="/wiki/Bronx" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronx">Bronx</a> called Juices for Life with his business partners Nyger Rollocks and Leo Galvez.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He released his EP with <a href="/wiki/Currensy" title="Currensy">Currensy</a> titled, <i>#The1st28</i> on February 28, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Styles P appeared on the sixteenth season of <i><a href="/wiki/Marriage_Boot_Camp" title="Marriage Boot Camp">Marriage Boot Camp</a></i> with his wife, Adjua in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2022, he was cast in the documentary series <i><a href="/wiki/Everything%27s_Gonna_Be_All_White" title="Everything&#39;s Gonna Be All White">Everything's Gonna Be All White</a></i>, airing on <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
