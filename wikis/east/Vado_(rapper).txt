<p><b>Teyon Isiah Winfree</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born March 13, 1985), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Vado</b> (<a href="/wiki/Backronym" title="Backronym">backronym</a> for <b>V</b>iolence <b>A</b>nd <b>D</b>rugs <b>O</b>nly),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> is an American rapper from New York. He signed to <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>'s <a href="/wiki/We_the_Best_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="We the Best Music">We the Best Music</a> in 2013 and was also a member of the duo <a href="/wiki/U.N._(group)" title="U.N. (group)">U.N.</a> with fellow New York rapper <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>.
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886046785">.mw-parser-output .toclimit-2 .toclevel-1 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-3 .toclevel-2 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-4 .toclevel-3 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-5 .toclevel-4 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-6 .toclevel-5 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-7 .toclevel-6 ul{display:none}</style><div class="toclimit-3"><meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" /></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vado_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vado_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Teyon Winfree was born on March 13, 1982. He was raised in Harlem and grew up on 144th and Lenox. His Mother is of Ghanaian, and his father is African American descent. Winfree first started writing at 16 and was influenced by <a href="/wiki/Ma$e" class="mw-redirect" title="Ma$e">Ma$e</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Big_L" title="Big L">Big L</a>. At the age of 19, Vado appeared on a public access television show named "Mad Ciphas."  In 2008, he released tracks with childhood friend, <a href="/wiki/Jae_Millz" title="Jae Millz">Jae Millz</a>. Winfree's original stage name was MOVADO but later was shortened to VADO. Vado is an acronym that stands for "Violence And Drugs Only."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vado_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2009 a mutual friend linked Vado with New York rapper and <a href="/wiki/Dipset" class="mw-redirect" title="Dipset">Dipset</a> member <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a> who invited Vado to come record music with him and after their first session Cam made Vado the new keystone artist of his crew <a href="/wiki/U.N._(group)" title="U.N. (group)">U.N.</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Vado officially released his long-awaited debut mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Slime_Flu" class="mw-redirect" title="Slime Flu">Slime Flu</a></i> on October 12, 2010 under <a href="/wiki/Diplomat_Records" title="Diplomat Records">Diplomat Records</a>/<a href="/wiki/E1_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="E1 Music">E1 Music</a>.
</p><p>Vado is featured on the <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a> track "Future" with rappers <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hood" title="Ace Hood">Ace Hood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wale_(rapper)" title="Wale (rapper)">Wale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Meek Mill (rapper)">Meek Mill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Boi-1da" title="Boi-1da">Boi-1da</a>, from Khaled's fifth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/We_the_Best_Forever" title="We the Best Forever">We the Best Forever</a></i>. In 2010, Vado's mentor and friend <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a> introduced him to <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-A-Fella Records</a> co-founder <a href="/wiki/Dame_Dash" class="mw-redirect" title="Dame Dash">Dame Dash</a>, and Cam'ron and Dame were in talks of <a href="/wiki/The_U.N." class="mw-redirect" title="The U.N.">the U.N.</a> signing to the newly re-launched Roc-A-Fella. Vado signed with Dame's subsidiary record label of Roc-A-Fella under <a href="/wiki/DD172" title="DD172">DD172</a> named <a href="/wiki/BluRoc" class="mw-redirect" title="BluRoc">BluRoc</a>. <a href="/wiki/The_U.N." class="mw-redirect" title="The U.N.">The U.N.</a> would also sign to <a href="/wiki/BluRoc" class="mw-redirect" title="BluRoc">BluRoc</a> instead of Roc-A-Fella.
</p><p>After signing he would release the critically acclaimed mixtape <i>Slime Flu</i> and the <i>Bosses of all Bosses</i> mixtape with Cam'ron.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He would then release the album <i><a href="/wiki/Gunz_n%27_Butta" title="Gunz n&#39; Butta">Gunz n' Butta</a></i> with Cam'ron in April 2011. It would chart on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at #78. On November 25, 2011 he would release the second <i>Slime Flu</i> mixtape.<sup id="cite_ref-datpiff1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datpiff1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2012 after remaining quiet for a while Vado was granted a release from Interscope to return to the independents.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On January 22, 2013 Vado released his third installment of his <i>Slime Flu</i> mixtape series.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On January 30, 2013, Vado confirmed he signed with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>'s <a href="/wiki/We_the_Best_Music_Group" title="We the Best Music Group">We the Best Music Group</a> label.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Vado has appeared on different VH1 shows, like <a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop">Love &amp; Hip Hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Ink_Crew" title="Black Ink Crew">Black Ink Crew</a>. After appearing on <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Dedication_5" title="Dedication 5">Dedication 5</a></i> and DJ Khaled's <i><a href="/wiki/Suffering_from_Success" title="Suffering from Success">Suffering from Success</a></i>, on December 13, 2013, Vado released a new single <i>Look Me In My Eyes</i> featuring <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>. He also announced that he would be releasing a new mixtape titled <i>Sinatra</i>. <i>Sinatra</i> was released February 10, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
