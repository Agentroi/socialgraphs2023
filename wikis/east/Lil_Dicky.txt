<p><b>David Andrew Burd</b> (born March 15, 1988), better known by his stage name <b>Lil Dicky</b>, is an American rapper, comedian, and actor. He came to prominence with the release of the music video for his song "Ex-Boyfriend" in 2013, which became popular with more than one million views on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> in 24 hours. He released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Professional_Rapper_(Lil_Dicky_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Professional Rapper (Lil Dicky album)">Professional Rapper</a></i>, in 2015.
</p><p>In 2018, his song "<a href="/wiki/Freaky_Friday_(song)" title="Freaky Friday (song)">Freaky Friday</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, became a worldwide hit. He also gained attention for his 2019 music video "<a href="/wiki/Earth_(Lil_Dicky_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Earth (Lil Dicky song)">Earth</a>", which featured many singers and actors including <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Grande" title="Ariana Grande">Ariana Grande</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ed_Sheeran" title="Ed Sheeran">Ed Sheeran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Mendes" title="Shawn Mendes">Shawn Mendes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Hart" title="Kevin Hart">Kevin Hart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Puth" title="Charlie Puth">Charlie Puth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_DiCaprio" title="Leonardo DiCaprio">Leonardo DiCaprio</a>. The song was made to raise awareness about the environment, <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>. Burd stated that a portion of the song's proceeds are being donated to a variety of environmental charities.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, Burd and producer <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Schaffer" title="Jeff Schaffer">Jeff Schaffer</a> created a television comedy series based on Burd's life called <i><a href="/wiki/Dave_(TV_series)" title="Dave (TV series)">Dave</a></i>, which premiered on <a href="/wiki/FXX" title="FXX">FXX</a> in March 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The show's second season aired in 2021 and its third season premiered on April 5, 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-season2-renewed_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-season2-renewed-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Burd was born in <a href="/wiki/Cheltenham_Township,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Cheltenham Township, Pennsylvania">Cheltenham Township</a> on the north border of <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> and grew up in an upper-middle-class <a href="/wiki/Jewish" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish">Jewish</a> family in the <a href="/wiki/Elkins_Park,_Pennsylvania" title="Elkins Park, Pennsylvania">Elkins Park</a> neighborhood.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-phillymag_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-phillymag-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He was born with "a tangled <a href="/wiki/Urethra" title="Urethra">urethra</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Hypospadias" title="Hypospadias">hypospadias</a>, in which the urethra does not open from its usual location in the head of the penis, requiring multiple surgeries.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Burd's interest in music started when he was a child, listening to <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a>. He began <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapping</a> in the fifth grade after doing a history report on <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Pushkin" title="Alexander Pushkin">Alexander Pushkin</a> using rap music.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In his youth, the rappers Burd listened to most were <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Burd attended <a href="/wiki/Cheltenham_High_School" title="Cheltenham High School">Cheltenham High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Speaking about his time in high school, Burd said "I was a pussy. I was really awkward looking. I wasn't getting any girls at all, but I was very class-clownish and I got good grades." After graduating from high school, Burd began attending the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Richmond" title="University of Richmond">University of Richmond</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> where fellow 2016 <i>XXL</i> Freshman and rapper <a href="/wiki/Dave_East" title="Dave East">Dave East</a> also attended at the same time.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Burd graduated <a href="/wiki/Summa_cum_laude" class="mw-redirect" title="Summa cum laude">summa cum laude</a> in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After college, Burd relocated to <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> where he worked in account management at the advertising agency <a href="/wiki/Goodby,_Silverstein_%26_Partners" title="Goodby, Silverstein &amp; Partners">Goodby, Silverstein &amp; Partners</a>. After reimagining his monthly progress report as a rap video, the company brought him to work in their creative department, where he <a href="/wiki/Copywriting" title="Copywriting">wrote copy</a> for ads such as the <a href="/wiki/NBA" class="mw-redirect" title="NBA">NBA</a>'s "BIG" campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-phillymag_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-phillymag-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Lil_Dicky_Performing_at_SXSW.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Lil_Dicky_Performing_at_SXSW.jpg/220px-Lil_Dicky_Performing_at_SXSW.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Lil_Dicky_Performing_at_SXSW.jpg/330px-Lil_Dicky_Performing_at_SXSW.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Lil_Dicky_Performing_at_SXSW.jpg/440px-Lil_Dicky_Performing_at_SXSW.jpg 2x" data-file-width="960" data-file-height="651" /></a><figcaption>Lil Dicky performing at <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">SXSW</a> 2014</figcaption></figure>
<p>Burd says he initiated his rap career "simply to get attention comedically, so [he] could write movies, write TV shows and act". However, he "fell in love with rapping" and says he's "not leaving that game until [he's] proved [his] point".<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Burd began working on his debut <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, <i>So Hard</i>, in 2011. The mixtape took Burd over two years to finish<sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> because he still had his <a href="/wiki/Day_job" class="mw-redirect" title="Day job">day job</a> at <a href="/wiki/Goodby,_Silverstein_%26_Partners" title="Goodby, Silverstein &amp; Partners">Goodby, Silverstein &amp; Partners</a> during its development.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Burd recorded a majority of his early material for <i>So Hard</i> on his <a href="/wiki/MacBook_Pro" title="MacBook Pro">MacBook Pro</a> and a $400 microphone; in 2013 he began releasing one song per week for five months straight.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On April 23, 2013, Burd released the music video for his song "Ex-Boyfriend", the mixtape's leading single. The music video received one million views within 24 hours of being posted on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-boston_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boston-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Burd released a new song or music video, in a series titled <i>Hump Days</i>. Following the release of 32 songs and 15 music videos, Burd launched a <a href="/wiki/Kickstarter" title="Kickstarter">Kickstarter</a>, stating, "I've officially run out of money... In a nutshell, you are funding phase two of my rap career." The month-long <a href="/wiki/Crowdfunding" title="Crowdfunding">crowdfunding</a> period began on November 20, 2013, with the goal of raising $70,000 in order to enable Burd to create and produce more music, music videos, and go touring.<sup id="cite_ref-kickstarter_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kickstarter-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-variety_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The Kickstarter well exceeded its target, raising $113,000.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Burd held his first live concert at <a href="/wiki/Theatre_of_Living_Arts" title="Theatre of Living Arts">TLA</a> in Philadelphia on February 19, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Burd signed with CMSN, which also manages <a href="/wiki/Tyga" title="Tyga">Tyga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chiddy_Bang" title="Chiddy Bang">Chiddy Bang</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-variety_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He plans on "having two concurrent careers going on, as a rapper, and as a comedian/actor/writer".<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopdx_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopdx-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Burd released his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Professional_Rapper_(Lil_Dicky_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Professional Rapper (Lil Dicky album)">Professional Rapper</a></i> on July 31, 2015, and features artists <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/T-Pain" title="T-Pain">T-Pain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rich_Homie_Quan" title="Rich Homie Quan">Rich Homie Quan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fetty_Wap" title="Fetty Wap">Fetty Wap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brendon_Urie" title="Brendon Urie">Brendon Urie</a> (<a href="/wiki/Panic!_at_the_Disco" title="Panic! at the Disco">Panic! at the Disco</a>), <a href="/wiki/Two-9" title="Two-9">RetroJace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hannibal_Buress" title="Hannibal Buress">Hannibal Buress</a>. Burd appeared in a 2016 <a href="/wiki/Funny_or_Die" title="Funny or Die">Funny or Die</a> video "Watch Yo Self" with <a href="/wiki/Mystikal" title="Mystikal">Mystikal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_James" title="Trinidad James">Trinidad James</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On June 13, 2016, <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="XXL Magazine">XXL Magazine</a></i> released the 2016 Freshmen line-up. It included Burd, along with <a href="/wiki/Anderson_.Paak" title="Anderson .Paak">Anderson .Paak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kodak_Black" title="Kodak Black">Kodak Black</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Uzi_Vert" title="Lil Uzi Vert">Lil Uzi Vert</a>, <a href="/wiki/21_Savage" title="21 Savage">21 Savage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_East" title="Dave East">Dave East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denzel_Curry" title="Denzel Curry">Denzel Curry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Desiigner" title="Desiigner">Desiigner</a>, <a href="/wiki/G_Herbo" title="G Herbo">G Herbo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lil_Yachty" title="Lil Yachty">Lil Yachty</a>.
</p><p>On April 12, 2017, Burd released a music video for "Pillow Talking". Its special effects made it the 49th <a href="/wiki/List_of_most_expensive_music_videos" title="List of most expensive music videos">most expensive music video ever created</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with <i>XXL</i> in April 2017, Burd mentioned that he was creating a new project and that he was also attempting to pitch a TV show to networks.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> In September 2017, Burd released an EP under his alter ego Brain, <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_Brain" title="I&#39;m Brain">I'm Brain</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On March 15, 2018, Burd released a new single, "<a href="/wiki/Freaky_Friday_(song)" title="Freaky Friday (song)">Freaky Friday</a>", featuring Chris Brown, and the associated music video. By April 9, 2018, the video had reached over 100 million views and topped the charts in New Zealand and the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On April 2, 2018, Burd announced his first Australian tour; he previously studied abroad for two years in Melbourne.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 19, 2019, Burd released a single called "<a href="/wiki/Earth_(Lil_Dicky_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Earth (Lil Dicky song)">Earth</a>", featuring artists such as <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Grande" title="Ariana Grande">Ariana Grande</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ed_Sheeran" title="Ed Sheeran">Ed Sheeran</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Mendes" title="Shawn Mendes">Shawn Mendes</a>. They all voice various animated organisms in the music video. The music video, which was created in partnership with the <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_DiCaprio_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="Leonardo DiCaprio Foundation">Leonardo DiCaprio Foundation</a>, aimed at encouraging better environmental practices worldwide. Upon release Burd referred to the project as "the most important thing [he'll] ever do."<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Burd and Jeff Schafer created a TV series called <i><a href="/wiki/Dave_(TV_series)" title="Dave (TV series)">Dave</a></i>, which portrays a fictionalized version of Burd's life as a rapper. It stars Burd, Taylor Misiak, and <a href="/wiki/Christine_Ko" title="Christine Ko">Christine Ko</a>. The show premiered on March 4, 2020, on FXX. On May 11, 2020, <i>Dave</i> was renewed for a second season, which aired in 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-season2-renewed_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-season2-renewed-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On June 20, 2023, Burd announced that he will release a soundtrack album for <i>Dave</i> later this summer.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_influences">Musical style and influences</span></h2>
<p>Burd's style blends the comical with the relatable. According to <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_(magazine)" title="Boston (magazine)">Boston</a></i> magazine, "Content-wise, Burd comes up with his material from everyday occurrences and everyday experiences. From there, he crafts his videos around those topics to create a visual narrative that accompanies his talent as an emcee. 'It's like a comedian. They are out in the world, and writing things down,' he said. What followed 'Ex-Boyfriend' was a series of other videos that covered similarly average everyday experiences—songs about staying in for the night, songs about being a Jewish kid—he also has a rap battle with <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> in one of his videos."<sup id="cite_ref-boston_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boston-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Burd describes his style as a response to the excessive egotistical nature of rap today: "I really wanted to embody the exact opposite of that, and I think people are appreciating it. There just hasn't been a voice for that normal dude when it comes to rap."<sup id="cite_ref-boston_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boston-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He added, "I think a lot of rap is just escalated to a place that many people can't relate to... My niche is that I'm relatable. I don't rap about going to the club and popping bottles."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> In terms of his rapping skills, Burd is able "to manipulate words at an excessive speed, and weave rhyme patterns together in a way that's funny while also making viewers want to rewind parts of his videos".<sup id="cite_ref-boston_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boston-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Burd says his musical inspirations are <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">A$AP Rocky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Donald_Glover" title="Donald Glover">Childish Gambino</a>, whom he refers to as "a guy with similar aspirations."<sup id="cite_ref-dailytrojan_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailytrojan-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
