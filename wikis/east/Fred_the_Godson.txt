<p><b>Frederick Thomas</b> (February 22, 1985 – April 23, 2020),<sup id="cite_ref-Times_Obit_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obit-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> known as <b>Fred the Godson</b>, was an American DJ and rapper from <a href="/wiki/Bronx" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronx">The Bronx</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_Obit_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_Obit-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NPR_Obit_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPR_Obit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At his death in April 2020, he became one of the most notable New Yorkers to have died of COVID-19 complications early in the pandemic.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fred_the_Godson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frederick Thomas was born on February 22, 1985, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx,_New_York_City" class="mw-redirect" title="The Bronx, New York City">The Bronx, New York City</a>. He gained an early nickname of "Big Bronx".<sup id="cite_ref-Times_Obit_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obit-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He started freestyling in the early 2000s.<sup id="cite_ref-Times_Obit_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obit-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fred_the_Godson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thomas's music was characterized by his husky voice, humorous <a href="/wiki/Wordplay" class="mw-redirect" title="Wordplay">wordplay</a>, and creative rhymes.<sup id="cite_ref-Times_Obit_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obit-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In the early-2010s, he released two mixtapes; his debut was <i>Armageddon</i> in 2010, featuring samples of <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NPR_Obit_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPR_Obit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His second release, <i>City of God</i>, was one of a series for DJ Drama's Gangsta Grillz,<sup id="cite_ref-Times_Obit_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obit-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and featuring <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a> and <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_Obit_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_Obit-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> included him in its annual Freshman class of up-and-comers.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_Obit_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_Obit-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Between 2011 and 2020, Thomas released music and performed regularly, guest DJ-ing on New York radio station based in Hudson <a href="/wiki/Hot_97" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot 97">Hot 97</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-NPR_Obit_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPR_Obit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and collaborated with artists including Pusha T, <a href="/wiki/Jadakiss" title="Jadakiss">Jadakiss</a>, Cam'ron, and Raekwon, as well as releasing the <a href="/wiki/Answer_song" title="Answer song">answer song</a>, "Monique's Room".<sup id="cite_ref-Times_Obit_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obit-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He released two additional mixtapes in 2020, <i>Training Day</i> in January with <a href="/wiki/Jay_Pharoah" title="Jay Pharoah">Jay Pharoah</a>, and <i>Payback</i>, released on March 20, 2020, which would be his last release before his death from COVID-19 one month later.<sup id="cite_ref-NPR_Obit_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPR_Obit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fred_the_Godson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thomas was married to LeeAnn Jemmott; they had two daughters.<sup id="cite_ref-Times_Obit_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obit-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bronx12_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bronx12-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Thomas was reported to be suffering from acute asthma and kidney problems, after contracting <a href="/wiki/COVID-19" title="COVID-19">COVID-19</a> during the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_New_York_(state)" title="COVID-19 pandemic in New York (state)">COVID-19 pandemic in New York (state)</a>, and by April 6, 2020, his fever had abated in hospital, according to his publicist and his <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> account.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_Obit_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_Obit-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Source_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Source-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His wife told media on April 9 that she was concerned he was going to die,<sup id="cite_ref-Bronx12_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bronx12-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> but the next day, said he was "going to make it", and was being weaned from a <a href="/wiki/Ventilator" title="Ventilator">ventilator</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Source_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Source-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-XXL_Update_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL_Update-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Thomas died at <a href="/wiki/Montefiore_Medical_Center" title="Montefiore Medical Center">Montefiore Medical Center</a> on April 23, 2020, due to complications of COVID-19.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He was 35.<sup id="cite_ref-Times_Obit_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Obit-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_Obit_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_Obit-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NPR_Obit_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPR_Obit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fred_the_Godson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 22, 2021, what would've been his 36th birthday, <a href="/wiki/Bronx_Borough_President" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronx Borough President">Bronx Borough President</a> <a href="/wiki/Rub%C3%A9n_D%C3%ADaz_Jr." title="Rubén Díaz Jr.">Rubén Díaz Jr.</a> honored Fred by giving him his own street co-name on the block of his childhood home that he grew up in.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Special appearances were made by <a href="/wiki/Sway_Calloway" title="Sway Calloway">Sway Calloway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Jones_(rapper)" title="Jim Jones (rapper)">Jim Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaquae" class="mw-redirect" title="Jaquae">Jaquae</a>, Mysonne and <a href="/wiki/Justina_Valentine" title="Justina Valentine">Justina Valentine</a>.
</p>
