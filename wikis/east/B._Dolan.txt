<p><b>Bernard Dolan</b> (born March 8, 1981) is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spoken_word" title="Spoken word">spoken word artist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Activist" class="mw-redirect" title="Activist">activist</a>, screenwriter, and composer based in <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence, Rhode Island</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sfrbio_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfrbio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B._Dolan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p>
B. Dolan's first performances took place at New York City's <a href="/wiki/Nuyorican_Poets_Cafe" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuyorican Poets Cafe">Nuyorican Poets Cafe</a>, where he gained respect as part of <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Def_Poetry" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Poetry">Def Poetry</a></i> in 2002 and won numerous slam titles.<sup id="cite_ref-bostonphoenix_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bostonphoenix-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He has since distanced himself from and criticized <a href="/wiki/Slam_poetry" class="mw-redirect" title="Slam poetry">slam poetry</a>. In 2011, he told the <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Phoenix" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston Phoenix">Boston Phoenix</a></i>:</p><blockquote><p>Doing spoken word was the first time I ever tried to present my writing on a stage, and that's what it was good for.  It taught me some basic stage tricks that I still use — like how to change my voice and talk to a crowd. But once you've picked up those chops, you need to get the hell out before you become some asshole who wins the poetry slam for the 10th year in a row.<sup id="cite_ref-bostonphoenix_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bostonphoenix-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>After performing at a Def Jam industry party at the Rush Arts Gallery, B. Dolan realized he wouldn't be able to work within the major label structure. Within months, he dropped out of college, bought a drum machine, and began recording the first album <i>The Failure</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-bostonphoenix_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bostonphoenix-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> During this time, he slept on "park benches and subway trains" while working as a doorman at a building in close proximity to the Twin Towers. It was at this time that the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a> happened, leaving B. Dolan in a "paranoid mental state", which caused him to move back to <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sfrbio_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfrbio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Once back in Rhode Island, B. Dolan tapped into the local arts community, volunteered for at-risk youth programs, created the consumer activist website Knowmore.org, teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Sage_Francis" title="Sage Francis">Sage Francis</a>, signed to <a href="/wiki/Strange_Famous_Records" title="Strange Famous Records">Strange Famous Records</a>, and began to tour internationally since 2005.
</p><p>B. Dolan has also remained prolific as a rapper, releasing albums on Strange Famous Records since 2008 and most notably creating the "Bombzo Way" and "Evel Knievel" characters, which make surprise guest appearances at his concerts.<sup id="cite_ref-sfrbio_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfrbio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bostonphoenix_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bostonphoenix-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, he released his second album <i><a href="/wiki/Fallen_House,_Sunken_City" title="Fallen House, Sunken City">Fallen House, Sunken City</a></i>, the entirety of which is produced by <a href="/wiki/Alias_(artist)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alias (artist)">Alias</a> of <a href="/wiki/Anticon" title="Anticon">Anticon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, B. Dolan created the package show <i>The Church of Love &amp; Ruin</i>, which combined activism and art by bringing openly LGBT and Queer performers into the arena of hip hop, in an attempt to address homophobia in rap.<sup id="cite_ref-bostonphoenix_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bostonphoenix-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 10, 2015, B. Dolan released his third studio album, entitled <i>Kill the Wolf<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></i>, on Speech Development and Strange Famous Records.<sup id="cite_ref-bdolan.net_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bdolan.net-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This was followed with a world tour,<sup id="cite_ref-B.Dolan_World_Tour_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-B.Dolan_World_Tour-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> visiting the US, UK and Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Knowmore.org">Knowmore.org</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B._Dolan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Knowmore.org">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As an activist, B. Dolan is known primarily as a co-founder of Knowmore.org, a wiki devoted to connecting consumers with social responsibility information about corporations.<sup id="cite_ref-knowmore.org_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-knowmore.org-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He is also an author of several articles within the website. He is especially known for an article on <a href="/wiki/American_Apparel" title="American Apparel">American Apparel</a>'s CEO <a href="/wiki/Dov_Charney" title="Dov Charney">Dov Charney</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-knowmore.org_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-knowmore.org-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2008, due to a lack of workers and funds, Dolan decided to take the site down. Recently in 2016, he has started a funding page to redesign the site and get it up and running again.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170217062941/https://www.generosity.com/fundraising/web-s-largest-searchable-corporate-crime-database">[1]</a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_work">Film work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B._Dolan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>B. Dolan co-wrote, appeared briefly in, and composed music for the 2015 independent horror film <i>Almost Mercy</i>, directed and co-written by Tom DeNucci. He subsequently co-wrote and composed the score for the 2019 crime-thriller <i>Vault</i>, also directed and co-written by DeNucci.
</p>
