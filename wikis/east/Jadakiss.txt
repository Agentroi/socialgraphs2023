<p><b>Jason Terrance Phillips</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born May 27, 1975),<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Jadakiss</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Yonkers,_New_York" title="Yonkers, New York">Yonkers, New York</a>. He began his career in the 1990s as a member of the rap trio <a href="/wiki/The_Lox" title="The Lox">The Lox</a>, managed by <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruff Ryders">Ruff Ryders</a> and signed with <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Bad Boy Entertainment">Bad Boy</a>. After leaving the label in 1999, they signed a joint venture deal between <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> and Ruff Ryders. He is currently signed to <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a>, and D-Block.
</p><p>Jadakiss' debut solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_Tha_Game_Goodbye" class="mw-redirect" title="Kiss Tha Game Goodbye">Kiss Tha Game Goodbye</a></i>, was released in 2001. He has released three more solo studio albums, another album with the Lox, and an album with fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>. In 2007, Jadakiss signed with <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella" class="mw-redirect" title="Roc-A-Fella">Roc-A-Fella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam">Def Jam</a>, which, with <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruff Ryders">Ruff Ryders</a>, co-released his 2009 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Kiss_(album)" title="The Last Kiss (album)">The Last Kiss</a></i>. Altogether, he has released five studio albums, most recently <i><a href="/wiki/Ignatius_(album)" title="Ignatius (album)">Ignatius</a></i> in 2020.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jason Terrance Phillips was born on May 27, 1975, in <a href="/wiki/Yonkers,_New_York" title="Yonkers, New York">Yonkers</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, and had an interest in hip hop from an early age. At age 12, he began hustling after his parents asked him to earn his own money. He began <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle rapping</a> for money after watching other people do it on the street corner. While freestyling, he eventually met longtime friends <a href="/wiki/Sheek_Louch" title="Sheek Louch">Sheek Louch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Phillips quickly developed a small underground fanbase and at the age of 12, entered himself into a freestyle competition in Florida where he attracted the attention of the owners of <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruff Ryders">Ruff Ryders</a>. Along with Styles and Louch, he formed a group known as <b>The Warlox</b>, and the trio began rapping together. They eventually met <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a>, who, impressed with their lyrics, handed their demo to <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Puff Daddy</a>, who signed the group to his label <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Bad Boy Entertainment">Bad Boy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopscriptures.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopscriptures.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="1994.E2.80.932000:_The_Lox"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1994–2000:_The_Lox">1994–2000: The Lox</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1994–2000: The Lox">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Upon signing with Bad Boy, Puff Daddy shortened the group's name from The Warloxs to simply The Lox. They made their first appearance on <a href="/wiki/Main_Source" title="Main Source">Main Source</a>'s 1994 LP <i><a href="/wiki/Fuck_What_You_Think" title="Fuck What You Think">Fuck What You Think</a></i> on the track "Set it Off."<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopscriptures.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopscriptures.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> They immediately began writing and performing on hit songs with fellow Bad Boy artists, including Puff Daddy's "It's All About the Benjamins" and "I Got the Power", <a href="/wiki/Mase" title="Mase">Mase's</a> "24 Hrs. to Live", <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey's</a> "Honey", <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige's</a> "Can't Get You Off My Mind" and <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.'s</a> "Last Day". The group developed a close relationship with B.I.G., during which time Jadakiss especially was taken under his wing. The Lox's first hit song was a tribute to The Notorious B.I.G. in the wake of his 1997 death, titled "We'll Always Love Big Poppa". The song was chosen as the <a href="/wiki/B-side" class="mw-redirect" title="B-side">B-side</a> to <a href="/wiki/Puff_Daddy" class="mw-redirect" title="Puff Daddy">Puff Daddy's</a> smash hit B.I.G. tribute song "<a href="/wiki/I%27ll_Be_Missing_You" title="I&#39;ll Be Missing You">I'll Be Missing You</a>", and received widespread commercial success, achieving <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">multi-platinum</a> status from the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA). The success and reception of the song opened the door for them to write more of their own songs, and in 1998, the Lox released their debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Money,_Power_%26_Respect" title="Money, Power &amp; Respect">Money, Power &amp; Respect</a></i>. The album was both a commercial and critical success, peaking at number 3 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a><sup id="cite_ref-billboard.com_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and number 1 on the US <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a><sup id="cite_ref-billboard.com1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> chart, and went on to achieve platinum status. Though the record was successful, The Lox grew unhappy with <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy</a> and Puff Daddy's glossy, radio friendly production, feeling it conflicted with their grimier street aesthetic. Following the release of their debut album, the group left the label to sign with <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruff Ryders">Ruff Ryders</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> They released their second album, We Are The Streets, in 2000, through Ruff Ryders. The album was once again a success, peaking at number 5 on the Billboard 200 and number 2 on the US Top R&amp;B/Hip Hop Albums chart.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard.com_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-billboard.com1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It featured production from <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a>, and spawned one single, "<a href="/wiki/Ryde_or_Die,_Bitch" title="Ryde or Die, Bitch">Ryde or Die, Bitch</a>", featuring fellow Ruff Ryders artist <a href="/wiki/Eve_(rapper)" title="Eve (rapper)">Eve</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="2001.E2.80.932007:_Solo_career"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2001–2007:_Solo_career">2001–2007: Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2001–2007: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Jadakiss_2009.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Jadakiss_2009.jpg/150px-Jadakiss_2009.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="213" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Jadakiss_2009.jpg/225px-Jadakiss_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Jadakiss_2009.jpg/300px-Jadakiss_2009.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1025" data-file-height="1456" /></a><figcaption>Jadakiss in 2009</figcaption></figure>
<p>Jadakiss began embarking on a solo career in 2001 and his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_Tha_Game_Goodbye" class="mw-redirect" title="Kiss Tha Game Goodbye">Kiss Tha Game Goodbye</a></i>, was released in August 2001 on the Ruff Ryders/<a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope</a> label. The album featured popular guests and producers such as <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Alchemist_(musician)" title="The Alchemist (musician)">The Alchemist</a>, <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>, Eve, <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a> and was commercially successful, going on to be certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_Certification" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA Certification">gold</a> by the RIAA and selling over 200,000 copies during its first week of release. Despite commercial success, the album received negative reception from critics who criticised the album for being repetitive and uninspired.  Jadakiss has acknowledged the criticism as valid, saying the record was done less out of inspiration but rather out of contractual obligations to Bad Boy.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopscriptures.com_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopscriptures.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The album produced three singles, "<a href="/wiki/We_Gonna_Make_It" title="We Gonna Make It">We Gonna Make It</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Knock_Yourself_Out_(Jadakiss_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Knock Yourself Out (Jadakiss song)">Knock Yourself Out</a>" and "Put Ya Hands Up". Jadakiss followed this album with <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_of_Death_(Jadakiss_album)" title="Kiss of Death (Jadakiss album)">Kiss of Death</a></i>, released in June 2004. It features guest appearances from Snoop Dogg, <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, The Lox, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Quik" title="DJ Quik">DJ Quik</a> and more. His song <a href="/wiki/Why_(Jadakiss_song)" title="Why (Jadakiss song)">"Why?"</a>, featuring <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Hamilton_(musician)" title="Anthony Hamilton (musician)">Anthony Hamilton</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Havoc_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Havoc (rapper)">Havoc</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>, became one of the year's biggest hits, spawning a remix featuring Styles P, <a href="/wiki/Common_(rapper)" title="Common (rapper)">Common</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>. "Why?" remains Jadakiss' biggest hit, peaking at #11 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. The song also garnered controversy for a line in which Jadakiss claims that he believes <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> planned the <a href="/wiki/9/11_terrorist_attacks" class="mw-redirect" title="9/11 terrorist attacks">9/11 terrorist attacks</a>. Another single from the record, "<a href="/wiki/U_Make_Me_Wanna_(Jadakiss_song)" title="U Make Me Wanna (Jadakiss song)">U Make Me Wanna</a>," featuring <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> peaked at number 21 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a>. The album was both a commercial and critical success, receiving better reviews than his previous album and debuted at number 1 on both the Billboard 200 and the Top R&amp;B/Hip Hop Albums charts. In late 2007, Jadakiss signed to Jay-Z's <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-A-Fella Records</a>, a move Jay had been trying to arrange for a long time.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The move came on the heels of increased D-Block/Roc-a-Fella collaborations; in 2006, Jadakiss and former rival <a href="/wiki/Beanie_Sigel" title="Beanie Sigel">Beanie Sigel</a> collaborated on <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khaled" title="DJ Khaled">DJ Khaled</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Listennn..._the_Album" title="Listennn... the Album">Listennn... the Album</a></i>, and in 2007, on <a href="/wiki/Freeway_(rapper)" title="Freeway (rapper)">Freeway</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Free_at_Last_(Freeway_album)" title="Free at Last (Freeway album)">Free At Last</a></i>. Styles P appeared on a radio freestyle with Freeway and <a href="/wiki/Beanie_Sigel" title="Beanie Sigel">Sigel</a>, and also appeared briefly in the video for Jay-Z's <i><a href="/wiki/American_Gangster_(album)" title="American Gangster (album)">American Gangster</a></i> single "<a href="/wiki/Roc_Boys" class="mw-redirect" title="Roc Boys">Roc Boys</a>," in which he throws up the famous Roc-a-Fella sign, "the Diamond."
</p>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932015:_The_Last_Kiss_and_Top_5_Dead_or_Alive"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2015:_The_Last_Kiss_and_Top_5_Dead_or_Alive">2009–2015: <i>The Last Kiss</i> and <i>Top 5 Dead or Alive</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2009–2015: The Last Kiss and Top 5 Dead or Alive">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Jadakiss released his much-delayed and anticipated third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Kiss_(album)" title="The Last Kiss (album)">The Last Kiss</a></i>. The first single, "<a href="/wiki/By_My_Side_(Jadakiss_song)" title="By My Side (Jadakiss song)">By My Side</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ne-Yo" title="Ne-Yo">Ne-Yo</a> was released in October 2008. Two more singles, "Can't Stop Me" and "Death Wish" featuring Lil Wayne, were released in 2009. One more single, "Who's Real", featuring <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a> and <a href="/wiki/OJ_Da_Juiceman" class="mw-redirect" title="OJ Da Juiceman">OJ Da Juiceman</a> released later that year. In 2010, Jadakiss left Roc-A-Fella records.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, he was featured on two DJ Khaled tracks, one of them titled "It Ain't Over Til It's Over", featuring <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>, and the remix of "<a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_My_Hood" title="Welcome to My Hood">Welcome to My Hood</a>," also featuring <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Twista" title="Twista">Twista</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mavado_(singer)" title="Mavado (singer)">Mavado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birdman_(rapper)" title="Birdman (rapper)">Birdman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hood" title="Ace Hood">Ace Hood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Game_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Game (rapper)">Game</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bun_B" title="Bun B">Bun B</a> and <a href="/wiki/Waka_Flocka_Flame" title="Waka Flocka Flame">Waka Flocka Flame</a>. On April 26, 2012, Jadakiss released his mixtape <i>Consignment</i>, hosted by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 4, 2013, Jadakiss released the first single from his by then upcoming fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Top_5,_Dead_or_Alive" class="mw-redirect" title="Top 5, Dead or Alive">Top 5, Dead or Alive</a></i> titled "Big Boy Dialogue", featuring vocals from <a href="/wiki/The-Dream" title="The-Dream">The-Dream</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The album was scheduled to be released during September or October 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> but after much delay, the album was finally released on November 20, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It features guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Akon" title="Akon">Akon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeezy" title="Jeezy">Jeezy</a>, Lil Wayne, Ne-Yo, <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nipsey_Hussle" title="Nipsey Hussle">Nipsey Hussle</a>, Styles P, Puff Daddy, Sheek Louch, Swizz Beatz, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a>, and was supported by 2 singles, "Jason" featuring Swizz Beatz, and Ain't Nothin' New featuring Ne-Yo and Nipsey Hussle. The album debuted at number 4 on the Billboard 200, and received positive reviews from critics.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HipHopDX_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HipHopDX-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.93present:_The_Lox_reunion.2C_Friday_on_Elm_Street.2C_Ignatius_and_.22Mr._Verzuz.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–present:_The_Lox_reunion,_Friday_on_Elm_Street,_Ignatius_and_&quot;Mr._Verzuz&quot;">2016–present: The Lox reunion, <i>Friday on Elm Street</i>, <i>Ignatius</i> and "Mr. <i>Verzuz</i>"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2016–present: The Lox reunion, Friday on Elm Street, Ignatius and &quot;Mr. Verzuz&quot;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Jadakiss_in_2014.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Jadakiss_in_2014.jpg/220px-Jadakiss_in_2014.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Jadakiss_in_2014.jpg/330px-Jadakiss_in_2014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Jadakiss_in_2014.jpg/440px-Jadakiss_in_2014.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5184" data-file-height="3456" /></a><figcaption>Jadakiss at the Sound Academy in 2014</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2016, The Lox released their first album in 16 years, title "Filthy America…It's Beautiful". The album featured guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Fetty_Wap" title="Fetty Wap">Fetty Wap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>, and peaked at number 42 on the Billboard 200.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 29, 2016, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a> posted a picture on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> with Jadakiss with the caption, "Freddy vs. Jason coming soon", hinting at the release of a joint project with Jadakiss, titled <i>Freddy vs. Jason</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On April 2, 2016, Fabolous and Jadakiss released a freestyle of Future's "<a href="/wiki/Wicked_(Future_song)" title="Wicked (Future song)">Wicked</a>" from <i><a href="/wiki/Purple_Reign" title="Purple Reign">Purple Reign</a></i> that was scheduled appear on <i>Freddy vs. Jason</i>, but did not make the final cut of the album. They also revealed that it would not be a mixtape, but instead a full album.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On October 31, 2017, the album's first and only single, "Stand Up" featuring <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a> was released.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On November 21, 2017, it was announced that the album title was changed to <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_on_Elm_Street" title="Friday on Elm Street">Friday on Elm Street</a></i>. The album was released on November 24, 2017, and features guest appearances from Future, Jeezy, Styles P, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yo_Gotti" title="Yo Gotti">Yo Gotti</a>.
</p><p>On March 6, 2020, Jadakiss's fifth studio album <a href="/wiki/Ignatius_(album)" title="Ignatius (album)">Ignatius</a> was released. On June 29 of the same year, Jadakiss defeated Fabolous in a battle on <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>' <a href="/wiki/Verzuz" title="Verzuz">Verzuz</a> held virtually because of the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>. The following year, Jadakiss returned for another Verzuz battle, this time alongside the Lox against <a href="/wiki/The_Diplomats" title="The Diplomats">The Diplomats (DipSet)</a> live with an audience at the <a href="/wiki/Hulu_Theater" class="mw-redirect" title="Hulu Theater">Hulu Theater</a> at <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a>. The Lox were widely regarded as winning decisively, with decisive moments including Jadakiss calling out the Diplomats for using full songs including their own vocals instead of <a href="/wiki/TV_track" title="TV track">TV tracks</a> and then performing his freestyle over The Notorious B.I.G.'s "<a href="/wiki/Who_Shot_Ya%3F" title="Who Shot Ya?">Who Shot Ya?</a>", originally released on his 2010 mixtape <i>The Champ Is Here, Pt. 3</i>, and answering <a href="/wiki/Cam%27ron" title="Cam&#39;ron">Cam'ron</a>'s remark before performing "<a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_New_York_City" title="Welcome to New York City">Welcome to New York City</a>" that the Lox did not have a New York record that could not beat it  with his verse on "<a href="/wiki/New_York_(Ja_Rule_song)" title="New York (Ja Rule song)">New York</a>" (Jadakiss also performed the verse when Fat Joe – who was in the audience for the Lox-Diplomats Verzuz – and Ja Rule faced each other in the subsequent Verzuz at the same venue).
</p><p>Following their victory, streams of Lox music increased by 215%, including Jadakiss' "Who Shot Ya?" freestyle (both studio and live versions) being added to the iTunes Store and Apple Music 11 years after its initial release.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Jadakiss was also dubbed "Mr. <i>Verzuz</i>" after becoming the first artist to win two battles and was able to restructure his Def Jam contract.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Just over a year after the battle while they were performing at <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>'s Rock The Bells festival at <a href="/wiki/Forest_Hills_Stadium" class="mw-redirect" title="Forest Hills Stadium">Forest Hills Stadium</a> in Queens, Jadakiss presented Styles P and Sheek Louch with Lox championship rings and unveiled his.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_ventures">Business ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Business ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Along with longtime friend and business associate, Jay Jackson and Jadakiss launched an online multimedia creative collective called SoRaspy<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> that also serves as an umbrella company for his business endeavors which include an online publication, record label, apparel, and a YouTube channel.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Reinvesting in their community, in the summer of 2014, Jadakiss and <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a> opened a juice bar in Yonkers, New York, called Juices For Life.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Together, they have gone on to open two more branches in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">The Bronx</a> and one in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2022, Jadakiss launched a family-owned coffee company called Kiss Café with his father and son.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 5, 2004, Jadakiss was arrested in <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> on marijuana and illicit gun possession charges.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24hourhiphop_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24hourhiphop-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Several months later, he reached a plea agreement on misdemeanor charges and was ordered to pay a $900 fine.<sup id="cite_ref-24hourhiphop_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24hourhiphop-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 7, 2006, Jadakiss was once again arrested in his hometown of Yonkers, New York on gun and drug possession charges.<sup id="cite_ref-24hourhiphop_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24hourhiphop-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MTV-Arrested_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-Arrested-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> A Yonkers Police Department spokesperson said Jadakiss was one of four men sitting in a 2006 Toyota Camry that was parked near the intersection of Nepperhan Avenue and Elm Street in Yonkers at around 5 a.m. Saturday. Officers said the vehicle's driver was behaving oddly and said they noticed a strong odor of marijuana while approaching the car. Inside the vehicle, police found a .38 caliber revolver that had been reported stolen. According to The Associated Press, the weapon was loaded.<sup id="cite_ref-24hourhiphop_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24hourhiphop-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MTV-Arrested_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-Arrested-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> All occupants were charged with possession of a stolen firearm; Jadakiss was also charged with possession of marijuana (a small amount was found on his person).<sup id="cite_ref-24hourhiphop_32-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24hourhiphop-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MTV-Arrested_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-Arrested-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The driver, Darnell Frazier, was charged with driving under the influence. The other two men in the vehicle are listed as Benjamin Lockhart and Kristian Smith. All were remanded to Westchester County Jail and were held until at least Tuesday of the following week, when they were due in court.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV-Arrested_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-Arrested-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="House_raid">House raid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: House raid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Police arrested two men on multiple felony charges after they raided Jadakiss' Yonkers, New York apartment and found heroin (5 grams) and marijuana (6.5 pounds).<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Jadakiss was neither present during the investigation, nor wanted by the law enforcement agencies. The incident took place on the morning of August 24, 2009, and started when an investigation revealed the presence of marijuana inside the vehicle driven by two men. Both men were reportedly in police custody and had been criminally charged.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="50_Cent">50 Cent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 50 Cent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, Jadakiss and <a href="/wiki/Fat_Joe" title="Fat Joe">Fat Joe</a> were featured on a song by <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a> titled "<a href="/wiki/New_York_(Ja_Rule_song)" title="New York (Ja Rule song)">New York</a>". In this song, Ja Rule accuses <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> of being a snitch, and also takes shots at <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>. In turn, 50 Cent released a song titled "<a href="/wiki/Piggy_Bank_(song)" title="Piggy Bank (song)">Piggy Bank</a>" in which he disses Fat Joe and Jadakiss among others for performing on the song.  This caused Jadakiss to release numerous 50 Cent diss tracks, including "<a href="/wiki/Checkmate_(Jadakiss_song)" title="Checkmate (Jadakiss song)">Checkmate</a>", "Problem Child", "Shots Fired" and "I'm Sorry Ms. Jackson". 50 Cent along with his G-Unit crew responded with songs such as "I Run New York", "<a href="/wiki/Window_Shopper" title="Window Shopper">Window Shopper</a>", and "Death Wish" by <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Banks" title="Lloyd Banks">Lloyd Banks</a>. Jadakiss challenged 50 Cent to an onstage battle in Madison Square Garden for 1 million dollars, but 50 Cent declined. The feud was later resolved, with Jadakiss and G-Unit performing together at 50 Cent's ThisIs50 Festival on October 3, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Bill_O.27Reilly"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Bill_O'Reilly">Bill O'Reilly</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jadakiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Bill O&#039;Reilly">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, <a href="/wiki/Bill_O%27Reilly_(commentator)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bill O&#39;Reilly (commentator)">Bill O'Reilly</a> called Jadakiss a "smear merchant" due to lyrics in his hit song <a href="/wiki/Why_(Jadakiss_song)" title="Why (Jadakiss song)">"Why?"</a>, in which Jadakiss lyrically implied <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> coordinated the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a>, with the line "Why do <a href="/wiki/Nigga" title="Nigga">niggas</a> push pounds of <a href="/wiki/Cocaine" title="Cocaine">powder</a>? Why did <a href="/wiki/9/11_conspiracy_theories" title="9/11 conspiracy theories">Bush knock down the towers</a>?"<sup id="cite_ref-AP-2004_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP-2004-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Jadakiss said the line was a metaphor for Bush administration not doing enough to stop the attacks.<sup id="cite_ref-AP-2004_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP-2004-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the music video for "Why?", a man holds up a picket sign reading "Buck Fush" (a <a href="/wiki/Spoonerism" title="Spoonerism">spoonerism</a> of "<a href="/wiki/Fuck" title="Fuck">Fuck</a> <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">Bush</a>"). O'Reilly took the position that the President should be allowed to sue Jadakiss for <a href="/wiki/Slander" class="mw-redirect" title="Slander">slander</a>. The track was eventually banned on some radio stations or played with the lyrics in question censored.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AP-2004_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP-2004-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
