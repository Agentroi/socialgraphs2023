<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daddy-O_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1981, Daddy-O began his career as the lead member of the band <a href="/wiki/Stetsasonic" title="Stetsasonic">Stetsasonic</a>, which included <a href="/wiki/Prince_Paul_(producer)" title="Prince Paul (producer)">Prince Paul</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York. He produced and remixed songs for musical acts such as <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Latifah" title="Queen Latifah">Queen Latifah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_B-52%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="The B-52&#39;s">The B-52's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Osborne" title="Jeffrey Osborne">Jeffrey Osborne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chante_Moore" class="mw-redirect" title="Chante Moore">Chante Moore</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1994 to 1998, he was a Senior Director for <a href="/wiki/MCA_Records" title="MCA Records">MCA Records</a>. After leaving MCA, he worked for <a href="/wiki/Motown_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Motown Records">Motown Records</a> and various other entertainment agencies, where he helped to produce and discover talent. Other acts with which he has been associated include <a href="/wiki/Sonic_Youth" title="Sonic Youth">Sonic Youth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sublime_(band)" title="Sublime (band)">Sublime</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barry_White" title="Barry White">Barry White</a>, <a href="/wiki/They_Might_Be_Giants" title="They Might Be Giants">They Might Be Giants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Camron" class="mw-redirect" title="Camron">Camron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pizzicato_5" class="mw-redirect" title="Pizzicato 5">Pizzicato 5</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Third_World_(band)" title="Third World (band)">Third World</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, Daddy-O joined the CloudSpray team as a management and marketing consultant.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, Daddy-O and his partner Lion Lindwedel (founder of True School Entertainment) formed their co-owned label, Odad Truth Records. He released his solo albums on the OTR Label with digital distribution on Chuck D's distribution company SpitDigital.  The first release was <i>#EverybodyButKRS</i>, followed in February 2016 with <i>The Odad, the Gun, &amp; the Children, El Dolor De Las Calles, No Tablecloths in 2018, From My Hood 2 U in 2019, and the most recent solo release of G.O.A.T. Antidote in 2020</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
