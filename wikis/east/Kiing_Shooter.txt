<p><b>William Antonio Daniels</b> (<span style="display:none">(<span class="bday">1992-07-25</span>)</span>July 25, 1992 – <span style="display:none">(<span class="dday deathdate">2020-05-05</span>)</span>May 5, 2020),<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Kiing Shooter</b>, was an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Queensbridge,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Queensbridge, New York">Queensbridge, New York</a>. Kiing Shooter first came into the public eye alongside NYC rapper and longtime friend <a href="/wiki/Dave_East" title="Dave East">Dave East</a>, after releasing numerous freestyles with the rapper in 2017. Kiing Shooter gained recognition with his single "They Say" which was released on June 29, 2018. He subsequently released his debut EP <i>Fucc The Doubters</i> on August 24, 2018, with features from Dave East, <a href="/wiki/Don_Q_(rapper)" title="Don Q (rapper)">Don Q</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mac_(rapper)" title="Mac (rapper)">Mac</a> and it peaked at 148 on the US Billboard 200.
</p><p>His second EP, <i>No Turning Bacc</i> was released on April 20, 2019, and his debut Solo LP <i><a href="/wiki/Still_Outside" class="mw-redirect" title="Still Outside">Still Outside</a></i> dropped on March 27, 2020. Shooter was signed to <a href="/wiki/Nas_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Nas (rapper)">Nas</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mass_Appeal_Records" title="Mass Appeal Records">Mass Appeal</a> record label and Nas's brother <a href="/wiki/Jungle_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jungle (rapper)">Jungle</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Street_Dreams_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Street Dreams Records">Street Dreams</a> record label.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kiing_Shooter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kiing Shooter was a long-time friend of rapper Dave East and has always been around the mainstream artist when touring and performing since 2015, although he never took an interest in taking rapping seriously until 2017. Kiing Shooter made numerous appearances on East's mixtapes, once he started his own label "From The Dirt" in March, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> East was subsequently featured on the single "Eye Witness" by Kiing Shooter on June 9, 2018, and the music video had a guest appearances from rapper <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was featured on the single "Traumatized" by Dave East which peaked at 42 on the <a href="/wiki/US_Billboard_200" class="mw-redirect" title="US Billboard 200">US Billboard 200</a> on August 11, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kiing Shooter released his debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> <i>Fucc The Doubters</i> on August 24, 2018. It featured guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Dave_East" title="Dave East">Dave East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Don_Q_(rapper)" title="Don Q (rapper)">Don Q</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corey_Finesse" class="mw-redirect" title="Corey Finesse">Corey Finesse</a> amongst others. It was released via East's label "From the Dirt" and peaked at 148 on the US Billboard 200.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The album was noted for its authenticity, drive and rise, gaining the attention of New York rap legend, <a href="/wiki/Jungle_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jungle (rapper)">Jungle</a>. Jungle launched his own record label, <i>Street Dream Records</i>, and signed Kiing Shooter in February 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kiing Shooter released his second EP <i>No Turning Bacc</i> on <a href="/wiki/420_(cannabis_culture)" title="420 (cannabis culture)">April 20</a>, 2019. It featured appearances from Corey Finesse, Piif Jones amongst others and was released via "Street Dreams Records". It was supported by the single "If I Could Talk To Them" which dropped on May 9, 2019, in which he reflects on the many losses he has faced on the road to success, including family, friends and collaborators.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Kiing Shooter and Dave East both remixed <a href="/wiki/Pop_Smoke" title="Pop Smoke">Pop Smoke</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_the_Party_(Pop_Smoke_song)" title="Welcome to the Party (Pop Smoke song)">Welcome to the Party</a></i> on August 10, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He was featured on the single "Say Less" by British rapper ASB which was released via <a href="/wiki/GRM_Daily" title="GRM Daily">GRM Daily</a> on August 20, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The pair went on to support Dave East on his <i>Survival Tour</i> which spanned from November 22, 2019 &#8211; December 20, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 13, 2020, Shooter released the single "Losses" featuring <a href="/wiki/Uncle_Murda" title="Uncle Murda">Uncle Murda</a> after teasing an album art for the song on Instagram in January.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The single was to go on and be the lead single of his third project <i>Still Outside</i> which was released on March 27, 2020. The 11 track mixtape also contained features from <a href="/wiki/Corey_Finesse" class="mw-redirect" title="Corey Finesse">Corey Finesse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_East" title="Dave East">Dave East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Dre" title="Mac Dre">Mac Dre</a>, Jazzy Amra, PNV Jay and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Poppa_Da_Don&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Poppa Da Don (page does not exist)">Poppa Da Don</a> via <a href="/wiki/Street_Dream_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Street Dream Records">Street Dream Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mass_Appeal_Records" title="Mass Appeal Records">Mass Appeal Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kiing_Shooter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His artistry has been compared to rappers such as Dave East and Don Q, and he is known for his aggressive style of rap. He is noted for his bars that are setting sights and sounds of East Coast culture in his music.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kiing_Shooter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1062260506">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:.5em 1.4em .8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:.5em 0 .8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{overflow:hidden;position:relative;margin:.5em auto .8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft span,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright span{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox>blockquote{margin:0;padding:0;border-left:0;font-family:inherit;font-size:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:110%;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote>:first-child{margin-top:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote:last-child>:last-child{margin-bottom:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .quote-title,.mw-parser-output .quotebox .quotebox-quote{display:block}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:640px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{width:100%!important;margin:0 0 .8em!important;float:none!important}}</style><div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:350px; ; background-color: #bbccff;">
<blockquote class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">
<p>You made your mark and got your wings<br />On many hearts, you pulled their strings<br />From QB to Heaven, the world in between<br />What a pleasure to have you, in our lives, in this world, in our world<br />We shared our street dreams.

<p><cite class="right-aligned" style="">&#8211; A poem by <a href="/wiki/Nas_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Nas (rapper)">Nas</a> on Kiing Shooter.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></cite>

<p>Daniels was admitted to hospital on April 23, 2020, after showing symptoms of <a href="/wiki/Cirrhosis" title="Cirrhosis">cirrhosis</a>. His condition got worse over time and on May 5, 2020, it was confirmed that he had died of <a href="/wiki/Liver_failure" title="Liver failure">liver failure</a>. It was reported that the liver failure may have been due to complications with <a href="/wiki/COVID-19" title="COVID-19">COVID-19</a> during the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_New_York_City" title="COVID-19 pandemic in New York City">COVID-19 pandemic in New York City</a>, though his friend David Brewster (<a href="/wiki/Dave_East" title="Dave East">Dave East</a>) quickly denounced this on social media. Daniels himself said the cause of his liver problem was due to the excessive consumption of <a href="/wiki/Hennessy" title="Hennessy">Hennessy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Numerous contemporaries including label managers <a href="/wiki/Nas_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Nas (rapper)">Nas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jungle_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jungle (rapper)">Jungle</a> as well as fellow rappers <a href="/wiki/Dave_East" title="Dave East">Dave East</a>, ASB, <a href="/wiki/Uncle_Murda" title="Uncle Murda">Uncle Murda</a> paid tribute to the deceased rapper.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> A vigil was held the following day in Daniels hometown of <a href="/wiki/Queensbridge_Houses" title="Queensbridge Houses">Queensbridge</a>, New York. Due to the current <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_the_United_States" title="COVID-19 pandemic in the United States">COVID-19 pandemic in the United States</a>, three people were arrested after violating social distancing rules in which a minimum of 75 people attended.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
