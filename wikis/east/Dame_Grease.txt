<p><b>Damon J. Blackman</b> (born August 9, 1974), better known by his stage name <b>Dame Grease</b> or simply <b>Grease</b>, is an American record producer from New York City. In 1996, he gained recognition in the hip hop industry through his production work with <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_LOX" class="mw-redirect" title="The LOX">The LOX</a>, while also establishing the sound of <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders_Entertainment" title="Ruff Ryders Entertainment">Ruff Ryders</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dame_Grease&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1996.E2.80.932006:_Career_beginnings_and_Vacant_Lot"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1996–2006:_Career_beginnings_and_Vacant_Lot">1996–2006: Career beginnings and Vacant Lot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dame_Grease&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1996–2006: Career beginnings and Vacant Lot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Grease has produced several artists throughout his career. He has worked extensively with <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast hip hop</a> group, <a href="/wiki/The_LOX" class="mw-redirect" title="The LOX">The LOX</a>. Their use of Grease's sounds helped them acquire the street buzz that landed them their deal on <a href="/wiki/Puff_Daddy" class="mw-redirect" title="Puff Daddy">Puff Daddy</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy Records</a> in 1996. Grease produced several songs on LOX's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Money,_Power_%26_Respect" title="Money, Power &amp; Respect">Money, Power &amp; Respect</a></i>, including the hit <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Poppa" title="Big Poppa">We'll Always Love Big Poppa</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Strickland_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strickland-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Harling_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harling-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was a producer on fellow Bad Boy artist <a href="/wiki/Mase" title="Mase">Mase</a>'s <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">quadruple platinum</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/Harlem_World" title="Harlem World">Harlem World</a></i> (1997). This led to Grease's work as the primary <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">music producer</a> on <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>'s debut <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_Dark_and_Hell_Is_Hot" title="It&#39;s Dark and Hell Is Hot">It's Dark and Hell Is Hot</a></i> (1998),<sup id="cite_ref-Cornish_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cornish-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> producing thirteen of its nineteen tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-Strickland_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strickland-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> With this project Grease helped <a href="/wiki/Ruff_Ryders_Entertainment" title="Ruff Ryders Entertainment">Ruff Ryders Entertainment</a> build its image. During this time, Grease mentored <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">Bronx</a>-bred disc jockey <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a>, who soon became another <a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">sample</a>-free producer.
</p><p>Dame Grease then landed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Priority_Records" title="Priority Records">Priority Records</a> to release music through his own label, Vacant Lot,<sup id="cite_ref-Cornish_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cornish-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> despite offers to be one of Puff Daddy's legendary <a href="/wiki/The_Hitmen_(production_team)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Hitmen (production team)">Hitmen</a><sup id="cite_ref-Cornish_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cornish-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> or an in-house producer on Ruff Ryders.
</p><p>In 1999, Dame Grease produced two tracks on <a href="/wiki/Tricky_(musician)" title="Tricky (musician)">Tricky</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Juxtapose_(album)" title="Juxtapose (album)">Juxtapose</a></i> album.
</p><p>In 2001 Dame Grease began composing music, providing the score for the <a href="/wiki/Steven_Seagal" title="Steven Seagal">Steven Seagal</a>/<a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a> blockbuster <i><a href="/wiki/Exit_Wounds" title="Exit Wounds">Exit Wounds</a></i>, as well as for the 2003 box-office hit, <i><a href="/wiki/Cradle_2_the_Grave_(soundtrack)" title="Cradle 2 the Grave (soundtrack)">Cradle 2 the Grave</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Jet_Li" title="Jet Li">Jet Li</a>, DMX, <a href="/wiki/Gabrielle_Union" title="Gabrielle Union">Gabrielle Union</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Anderson" title="Anthony Anderson">Anthony Anderson</a>, and for <i><a href="/wiki/Never_Die_Alone" title="Never Die Alone">Never Die Alone</a></i> (2004).<sup id="cite_ref-Strickland_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strickland-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Reid_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reid-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dame Grease also teamed up with DMX on the "Ain't No Sunshine" soundtrack, a remake of <a href="/wiki/Bill_Withers" title="Bill Withers">Bill Withers</a> <a href="/wiki/Ain%27t_No_Sunshine" title="Ain&#39;t No Sunshine">1972 classic of the same name</a>.
</p><p>Grease put in work on several music projects throughout 2004 and 2005, including albums from <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kelis" title="Kelis">Kelis</a>, DJ Kay Slay, DJ Envy, <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ol%27_Dirty_Bastard" title="Ol&#39; Dirty Bastard">Ol' Dirty Bastard</a>, and an artist on <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s Aftermath label named Aimee Terrin. Grease also kept the underground bubbling with "Crown Me", a collaboration between <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, Cam'ron, and <a href="/wiki/Juelz_Santana" title="Juelz Santana">Juelz Santana</a>. In 2006, Dame Grease produced "Life Be My Song", a track on DMX's sixth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Year_of_the_Dog..._Again" title="Year of the Dog... Again">Year of the Dog... Again</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2007.E2.80.932013:_After_the_Break"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007–2013:_After_the_Break">2007–2013: After the Break</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dame_Grease&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007–2013: After the Break">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a break in the mid-2000s, Grease reemerged in 2007, securing <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a>'s lead-off "Takin' Pictures" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Young_Jeezy" class="mw-redirect" title="Young Jeezy">Young Jeezy</a>, Rick Ross, Jim Jones, <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Young_Buck" title="Young Buck">Young Buck</a>) and Freeway's single <i>Big Spender</i> (featuring Jay-Z).<sup id="cite_ref-Cornish_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cornish-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Grease also produced several tracks on <a href="/wiki/Hell_Rell" title="Hell Rell">Hell Rell</a>'s debut album <i>For the Hell of It</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Cornish_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cornish-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Dame Grease produced the track "Lamborghini Dreams" for <a href="/wiki/Curren$y" class="mw-redirect" title="Curren$y">Curren$y</a> which was to have been included on Curren$y's <i>This Aint No Mixtape: (Championship Edition)</i> but which was never released.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a 2010 radio interview, Dame discussed projects with the LOX, DMX and his <a href="/wiki/Max_B" title="Max B">Max B</a> project, pairing Max with unreleased verses from Notorious B.I.G. and 2 Pac.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In that year he also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Magix_Music_Maker" title="Magix Music Maker">Magix Music Maker</a>, a software, online services and digital content provider, to create a signature software called the <i>Grease-A-Lizer</i> (MAGIX Music Maker Greasealizer Version), which allows users to create their own tracks using "customized loops and keyboard samples" produced by Dame Grease.<sup id="cite_ref-Cornish_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cornish-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Reid_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reid-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The product was released March 15, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Dame Grease continued producing <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Cornish_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cornish-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Reid_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reid-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Dame also launched Lot Musik Recording Studio dedicated to fostering young talent.<sup id="cite_ref-Strickland_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strickland-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dame released his own album, <i>Goon Musik</i> in 2011. It includes the single "<a href="/wiki/Sour_Diesel_(album)" title="Sour Diesel (album)">Sour Diesel</a>", which features <a href="/wiki/N.O.R.E." title="N.O.R.E.">N.O.R.E.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Styles_P" title="Styles P">Styles P</a>. Meeno, Bigga Threat and Tony Wink provided vocals for the album.<sup id="cite_ref-Strickland_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strickland-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Dame made a production appearance on <a href="/wiki/French_Montana_discography" title="French Montana discography">French Montana's</a> <a href="/wiki/Mister_16:_Casino_Life" class="mw-redirect" title="Mister 16: Casino Life">Mister 16: Casino Life</a> "All Night". Dame was executive producer on DMX's seventh studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Undisputed_(DMX_album)" title="Undisputed (DMX album)">Undisputed</a></i>, which was released in September 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Reid_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reid-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2012, Dame Grease ran into <a href="/wiki/Riff_Raff_(rapper)" title="Riff Raff (rapper)">Riff Raff</a> while working on DMX's <i>Undisputed</i> album at Good Charlotte Studio in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood,_Los_Angeles" title="Hollywood, Los Angeles">Hollywood</a>. A few days later Riff Raff came back to the studio and recorded <i>Hologram Panda</i> in 7 hours. The mixtape version was released December 12, 2012, and the album version was released April 4, 2013, through 101 Distribution.
</p><p>In 2013 Dame Grease started working with the "New Outlaw Order" a Faction of <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">2pac's</a> <a href="/wiki/Outlawz" title="Outlawz">Outlawz</a> (<a href="/wiki/Makaveli_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Makaveli Records">Makaveli Records</a>/ Thug Life 365). In 2014 Dame Grease began working with Outlaw Order members Outlaw Muszamil and outlawed Misfit313, Outlawed misfit313 contract expired on January 1, 2015. He was picked up by Vacant Lot Records (Dame Grease) and followed up with the album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Uprising_(album)" title="The Uprising (album)">The Uprising</a></i> Produced by Dame Grease and Niamson Released on December 25, 2015.
</p>
