<p><b>Concetta Suzanne Kirschner</b> (born February 25, 1971), better known as <b>Princess Superstar</b>, is an American rapper and <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a>. She has had two chart hits in the UK, "<a href="/wiki/Bad_Babysitter" title="Bad Babysitter">Bad Babysitter</a>", which reached number 11 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> in 2002, and "<a href="/wiki/Perfect_(Exceeder)" title="Perfect (Exceeder)">Perfect (Exceeder)</a>", which reached number three in 2007. She has recorded with Moby, The Prodigy, Arthur Baker, Prince Paul, and Grandmaster Flash, amongst others. During the peak of her career, she was acclaimed as Pennsylvania's top female Jewish rapper.
</p><p>Her musical style, as she describes it, is "flip-flop"—a mixture of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electroclash" title="Electroclash">electroclash</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In summer 2014 she debuted her reality television show <i>I Love Princess Superstar</i> on her <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel.<sup id="cite_ref-online.wsj.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-online.wsj.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2018 she released a children's record called <i>These Are the Magic Days</i>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Superstar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kirschner was born in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Harlem" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Harlem">Spanish Harlem</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Jewish" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish">Jewish</a> father with ancestry from <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Sicilian-American" class="mw-redirect" title="Sicilian-American">Sicilian-American</a> mother who converted to <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Judaism</a>. Kirschner identifies herself as a spiritual Jew.<sup id="cite_ref-princesssome_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-princesssome-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
Her parents were <a href="/wiki/Psychologist" title="Psychologist">psychologists</a>. When Kirschner was a child, the family moved to rural <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> and then to the suburbs of <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>. She attended school at the <a href="/wiki/Germantown_Academy" title="Germantown Academy">Germantown Academy</a>, a private high school, where she was a member of the Theatre Club and the Black Student Union. She moved to New York City at the age of 17 and went to NYU, where she got her <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Fine_Arts" title="Bachelor of Fine Arts">BFA</a> in drama. She is an active member of Superstar Machine, and was a member of <a href="/wiki/Mensa_International" title="Mensa International">Mensa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She is married to Luis Miguel Ortega and has a daughter Siren Ortega, and they live in Santa Monica, CA.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Superstar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In New York City she took up the <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-princesssome_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-princesssome-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and her interest in music surpassed her interest in acting. She joined the Gamma Rays, an all-girl power psychedelic band that recorded a 7" single called "Lovely" for <a href="/wiki/TeenBeat_Records" title="TeenBeat Records">TeenBeat Records</a>. Their other recordings appeared on various compilations.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Around the same time, Kirschner was composing hip hop-styled songs on a 4-track recorder.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Princess_Superstar">Princess Superstar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Superstar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Princess Superstar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1994, Kirschner made a demo tape, <i>Mitch Better Get My Bunny</i>, under the name "Princess Superstar". She sent the tape to the <i><a href="/wiki/College_Music_Journal" class="mw-redirect" title="College Music Journal">College Music Journal</a></i> and to the <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a>' <a href="/wiki/Grand_Royal_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand Royal Records">Grand Royal Records</a>. While she got callbacks from CMJ and Grand Royal, she went on to sign with Dark Beloved Cloud Records, who released her song "I'm White" on their <i>Sympathy for Count Pococurante</i> compilation.
</p><p>In 1995, she teamed up with three backing musicians and signed with 5th Beetle Records. They recorded her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Strictly_Platinum" title="Strictly Platinum">Strictly Platinum</a></i>, which gained her some notoriety as being one of very few white female rappers.
</p><p>In 1997, Kirschner went on to launch her own record label, first calling it "A Big Rich Major Label", but then eventually settling on "Corrupt Conglomerate". She recruited a new crew of backing musicians and recorded <i>CEO</i>, a concept album about <a href="/wiki/Organizational_culture" title="Organizational culture">corporate culture</a>.
</p><p>Her next album, 2000's <i>Last of the Great 20th Century Composers</i>, was co-produced by Curtis Curtis and contains the song "Kool Keith's Ass", and a remix of "Do It Like a Robot" by <a href="/wiki/Blues_Explosion" class="mw-redirect" title="Blues Explosion">Blues Explosion</a>.
</p><p>Kirschner went on to collaborate with <a href="/wiki/Kool_Keith" title="Kool Keith">Kool Keith</a> on "Keith and Me" for the 2001 <i>Princess Superstar Is</i> LP released on Ruthless Records/<a href="/wiki/Studio_!K7" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio !K7">Studio !K7</a>. The album, co-produced Curtis Curtis, has guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Beth_Orton" title="Beth Orton">Beth Orton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bahamadia" title="Bahamadia">Bahamadia</a>. The single "Bad Babysitter" was number 11 on the UK Singles Chart.
</p><p>For 2005's <i>My Machine</i>, a futuristic hip-hopera concept double album,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> she collaborated with various producers such as <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Baker_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Arthur Baker (musician)">Arthur Baker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Lu_Cont" class="mw-redirect" title="Jacques Lu Cont">Jacques Lu Cont</a>, <a href="/wiki/Junior_Sanchez" title="Junior Sanchez">Junior Sanchez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armand_Van_Helden" class="mw-redirect" title="Armand Van Helden">Armand Van Helden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Todd_Terry" title="Todd Terry">Todd Terry</a>, and <a href="/wiki/High_and_Mighty_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="High and Mighty (band)">DJ Mighty Mi</a>. This went to number 3 on the UK chart in 2007.
</p><p>In 2008, Kirschner sang on the "Licky" single by long-time associate <a href="/wiki/Larry_Tee" title="Larry Tee">Larry Tee</a> which was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Him_to_the_Greek" title="Get Him to the Greek">Get Him to the Greek</a></i> and other movies and TV shows.
</p><p>In 2009, Kirschner was featured on <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_Flash" title="Grandmaster Flash">Grandmaster Flash</a>'s record <i>The Bridge</i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>.
</p><p>Kirschner produced New York rapper Kalae All Day's debut album, released March 17, 2010.
</p><p>In 2011, Kirschner released "Xmas Swagger" produced by the Rural.
</p><p>Princess Superstar released her sixth studio album, <i>The New Evolution</i>, in 2013. The album was funded by Superstar's fans, using Pledge Music.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Princess Superstar began her Hip Hop for Kids classes at Rough Trade in Brooklyn.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She also released the <i>I'm a Firecracker</i> EP in July with label Instant Records.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> That summer she launched the <i>I Love Princess Superstar</i> reality show on her YouTube channel.<sup id="cite_ref-online.wsj.com_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-online.wsj.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> She also wrote the song "My Booty Is Efficient" with comedian <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Cho" title="Margaret Cho">Margaret Cho</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, she appeared with Margaret Cho on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Late_Show_with_Stephen_Colbert" title="The Late Show with Stephen Colbert">The Late Show with Stephen Colbert</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, she appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Inside_Amy_Schumer" title="Inside Amy Schumer">Inside Amy Schumer</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Comedy_Central" title="Comedy Central">Comedy Central</a>.
</p><p>In 2017, she recorded a children's album called <i>These Are the Magic Days</i> with producers Nacey, Steve Starks, C.Fire and engineered and mastered by Curtis Curtis (released on October 12, 2018).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019 She released a B-sides and Rarities record called "Look What I Found" featuring production by Junior Sanchez and Andrew Wyatt (Miike Snow, Lady Gaga.)
</p><p>In 2020 She released the track 2020 with an accompanying video shot in Santa Monica, CA.
</p><p>In 2021, she turned 50 years old and celebrated it by releasing the single and music video "Gettin' Older (Pussy Still Pop!)" with producer C. Fire.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2023, she released the track "WHO AM I NOW", featuring Stefan Goldmann.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="DJ">DJ</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Superstar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: DJ">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kirschner is also a <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a>. She has released DJ recordings, such as <i>Princess Is a DJ</i> in 2002, <i>Now Is the Winter of Our Discothèque</i> in 2005, and <i>American Gigolo III</i> on <a href="/wiki/International_DeeJay_Gigolo_Records" title="International DeeJay Gigolo Records">International DeeJay Gigolo Records</a> in 2007.
</p>
