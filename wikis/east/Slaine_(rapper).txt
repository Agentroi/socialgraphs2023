<p><b>George Carroll</b>, better known as <b>Slaine,</b> is an American rapper and actor. Born in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, where he spent his childhood and teenage years, Carroll moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in the mid-1990s, where he adopted the moniker of Slaine and started to rap and record hip-hop. In the early 2000s, Carroll became a growing figure in the Boston hip-hop scene, releasing several mixtapes, and the LP <i>Stereotypez</i> (2007) with his group <a href="/wiki/Special_Teamz" title="Special Teamz">Special Teamz</a>. Later, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Brand_You_Can_Trust" title="A Brand You Can Trust">A Brand You Can Trust</a></i> (2009) was released, by the rap <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> <a href="/wiki/La_Coka_Nostra" title="La Coka Nostra">La Coka Nostra</a>, where Carroll is joined by <a href="/wiki/Ill_Bill" title="Ill Bill">Ill Bill</a>, and <a href="/wiki/House_of_Pain" title="House of Pain">House of Pain</a>.&#160;Carroll’s first solo album <i><a href="/wiki/A_World_with_No_Skies" title="A World with No Skies">A World With No Skies 2.0.</a></i> (2011) peaked at 33 on the Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums it, 22 on the Top Rap Albums, 29 on US Independent Albums, and 4 on Top Heatseekers.
</p><p>Carroll’s subsequent albums with La Coka Nostra were <i><a href="/wiki/Masters_of_the_Dark_Arts" title="Masters of the Dark Arts">Masters of the Dark Arts</a></i> (2012) and <i><a href="/wiki/To_Thine_Own_Self_Be_True" title="To Thine Own Self Be True">To Thine Own Self Be True</a></i> (2016). On his own he followed up with <i>The Boston Project</i> (2013), <i><a href="/wiki/The_King_of_Everything_Else" title="The King of Everything Else">The King of Everything Else</a></i> (2014), <i><a href="/wiki/Anti-Hero_(Slaine_and_Termanology_album)" title="Anti-Hero (Slaine and Termanology album)">Anti-Hero</a></i> (2017), <i>One Day</i> (2019), and <i>The Things We Can't Forgive</i> (2021).
</p><p>While rising on the hip-hop scene, Carroll became an actor. For <a href="/wiki/Ben_Affleck" title="Ben Affleck">Ben Affleck</a>'s directorial film debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Gone_Baby_Gone" title="Gone Baby Gone">Gone Baby Gone</a></i> (2007), he thought Carroll had the right look for a part and auditioned him successfully. However before Carroll was completely onboard, Affleck had to do some additional convincing, since the producers were reluctant to hire a first time actor. A few years later, Affleck rehired Carroll for the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Town_(2010_film)" title="The Town (2010 film)">The Town</a></i> (2010). Together with the rest of the cast, they won Best ensemble cast at the 2010 National Board of Review, and Washington DC Area Film Critics Association Awards. Carroll continued acting in motion pictures with roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Killing_Them_Softly" title="Killing Them Softly">Killing Them Softly</a></i> (2012), <i><a href="/wiki/Girl_House" title="Girl House">Girl House</a></i> (2014), <i><a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence" title="Central Intelligence">Central Intelligence</a></i> (2016), <a href="/wiki/Wheelman_(film)" title="Wheelman (film)"><i>Wheelman</i></a> (2017), etc.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slaine_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>George Carroll was born in the <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>. His family often moved between the neighborhoods of <a href="/wiki/South_Boston" title="South Boston">South Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dorchester,_Boston" title="Dorchester, Boston">Dorchester</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Roslindale" title="Roslindale">Roslindale</a>. He started writing rap lyrics in 1986, being influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a>, <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kool_Moe_Dee" title="Kool Moe Dee">Kool Moe Dee</a>. However, his first goal was to become a filmmaker.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Carroll said that he moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> During this time he studied film at the School of Visual Arts in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, but was let go after getting into an altercation with a staff member. Carroll turned his direction toward hip-hop performing his raps at open-mike sessions and street corners.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Eventually while recording a demo in the studio of the rap group <a href="/wiki/Lordz_of_Brooklyn" title="Lordz of Brooklyn">Lordz of Brooklyn</a>, Carroll said that member <a href="/wiki/Kaves" title="Kaves">Kaves</a> nicknamed him Slaine after an Irish comic book character.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Afterward, Carroll lived between Boston and New-York.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slaine_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004_to_2011:_rise_to_prominence_and_breakthrough">2004 to 2011: rise to prominence and breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slaine_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2004 to 2011: rise to prominence and breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late 2004, the creation of the rap <a href="/wiki/Supergroup_(music)" title="Supergroup (music)">supergroup</a> <a href="/wiki/La_Coka_Nostra" title="La Coka Nostra">La Coka Nostra</a> started when hip-hop artist <a href="/wiki/Danny_Boy_(rapper)" title="Danny Boy (rapper)">Danny Boy O'Connor</a> was mentoring two up and coming artists which included Carroll. O'Connor brought them to meet his former DJ Leor Diamant (<a href="/wiki/DJ_Lethal" title="DJ Lethal">DJ Lethal</a>) from his previous group <a href="/wiki/House_of_Pain" title="House of Pain">House of Pain</a>. They decided to make a group, however O'Connor felt that there was a void and asked experienced rapper William Braunstein (<a href="/wiki/Ill_Bill" title="Ill Bill">Ill Bill</a>) to join. The group's name came about, when O'Connor teased other members after they had a night out. The group started releasing music on <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">MySpace</a>, and went viral. Eventually, another former House of Pain member Erik Schrody (<a href="/wiki/Everlast" title="Everlast">Everlast</a>) showed interest to join the group. By 2006, the group consisted of O'Connor, Carroll, Diamant, Braunstein, and Schrody (who left some time after the first album was released).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>During this time, Carroll was getting noticed as a talented rapper in the Boston underground hip-hop scene. He was part of a group named <a href="/wiki/Special_Teamz" title="Special Teamz">Special Teamz</a> alongside artists Jaysaun and <a href="/wiki/Ed_O.G." title="Ed O.G.">Ed O.G.</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In a 2005 <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Globe" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston Globe">Boston Globe</a></i> article, he is described as “one of the city’s most talented hip-hop artist, who exudes mass appeal”.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, he released the mixtape <i>The White Man Is the Devil, Vol. 1.</i><sup id="cite_ref-:5_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, Carroll had his first acting role in <a href="/wiki/Ben_Affleck" title="Ben Affleck">Ben Affleck</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Gone_Baby_Gone" title="Gone Baby Gone">Gone Baby Gone</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Reading a newspaper article about his music triggered Affleck's initial interest in casting him. At 9 am the next day, Affleck started to phone Carroll, who had gone to bed two hours earlier and missed all his calls.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Carroll said "My picture was in the <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Herald" title="Boston Herald">Boston Herald</a></i> talking about what I was doing with my music. I woke up one afternoon, and I had 66 missed phone calls. They called me in to do an audition -- turned out I had to do five auditions."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Originally Carroll was sought after for the role of Cheese, but Affleck changed his mind and wanted him for the bigger part of the gangster Bubba Rogowski. Carroll explained "I thought I wasn't going to get (the role) because they took so long to get back to me, but it turns out Ben was just convincing the studio to take a chance on me despite my lack of acting experience. I was in L.A. recording with La Coka Nostra when I got the call and was obviously ecstatic."<sup id="cite_ref-:6_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Affleck explained his reasoning by saying “it’s really great to hire people with whom an audience isn’t overly familiar, because audiences have developed expectations and kind of anticipate what an actor might do if they’ve seen them a bunch".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Released on October 19, 2007, the film grossed $20.3 million in the U.S. and Canada and $14.3 million in other territories, for a worldwide total of $34.6 million against its $19 million budget.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Also that year, Carroll with <a href="/wiki/Special_Teamz" title="Special Teamz">Special Teamz</a> released the album <i>Stereotypez</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, La Coka Nostra released <i><a href="/wiki/A_Brand_You_Can_Trust" title="A Brand You Can Trust">A Brand You Can Trust</a></i> was released on July 14, 2009, on <a href="/wiki/Suburban_Noize_Records" title="Suburban Noize Records">Suburban Noize Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> It sold over 500,000 units.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> <i><a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a></i> gave four out of five stars.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Andrew Kameka of <i><a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a></i> wrote that "the album is a mostly solid effort and exactly what someone would expect from a supergroup of like-minded members known for high-energy music".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Adam Kennedy of the <a href="/wiki/BBC_News" title="BBC News"><i>BBC</i></a> while praising some the moments of the album said "it’s a tantalising parting taste of potential capabilities".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Steve Juon of <i>RapReviews</i> gave it a seven out of ten saying "there's definitely brutal beauty in the worlds that have collided on La Coka Nostra's "A Brand You Can Trust"".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Thomas Quinlan of <i><a href="/wiki/Exclaim!" title="Exclaim!">Exclaim!</a></i> said "La Coka Nostra are an interesting collection of collaborators that live up to the hype".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Carroll reunited with director Affleck for the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Town_(2010_film)" title="The Town (2010 film)">The Town</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The film is about a gang of bank robbers which includes Carroll, Affleck, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Renner" title="Jeremy Renner">Jeremy Renner</a>, and Owen Burke.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> For their performances they won Best ensemble cast at the 2010 <a href="/wiki/National_Board_of_Review" title="National Board of Review">National Board of Review</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Washington_DC_Area_Film_Critics_Association_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington DC Area Film Critics Association Awards">Washington DC Area Film Critics Association Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The film took #1 at the box office during its opening weekend, taking in $23.8 million.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <i>The Town</i> grossed $92.1 million in the United States and Canada with an additional $61.8 million in other territories for a total of $154 million worldwide on a production budget of $37 million.<sup id="cite_ref-budget_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-budget-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, Carroll released his first solo album <i><a href="/wiki/A_World_with_No_Skies" title="A World with No Skies">A World With No Skies 2.0</a></i>. The addition of the "2.0" is due to the fact that an earlier version of the album had been leaked online.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> it peaked at 33,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> on Top Rap Albums at 22,<sup id="cite_ref-:3_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> on US <a href="/wiki/Independent_Albums" title="Independent Albums">Independent Albums</a> at 29,<sup id="cite_ref-:4_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and on <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers">Top Heatseekers</a> at 4.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> David Jeffries of <i><a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a></i> gave it three and a half stars out of five, saying ''this is a solid outing from the Irish-American MC''.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Steve Juon of <i>Rapreviews</i> gave it eight out of ten and said "It's that self-effacing humor and penchant for punchlines that keep Slaine's album from going so dark".<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Luke Gibson of <i>HipHopDX</i> gave it four out of five stars, praised it to be Carroll best lyrical effort to date, and said ''a national album that portrays him as more than just Boston’s boy, but a voice that resonates with struggle, perseverance and the tender balance between good and evil everywhere''.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> For his music accomplishments, Carroll won "Hip Hop Artist Of The Year" at the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Music_Awards" title="Boston Music Awards">Boston Music Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>That same year on television, on 11 February, Carroll acted in <i>Razer</i> the ninth instalment of the first season of <a href="/wiki/The_Cape_(2011_TV_series)" title="The Cape (2011 TV series)"><i>The Cape</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> On 4 October, he played a part in <i><a href="/wiki/Missing_(Body_of_Proof)" title="Missing (Body of Proof)">Missing</a></i> an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Body_of_Proof" title="Body of Proof">Body of Proof</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_to_present_day:_subsequent_success">2012 to present day: subsequent success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slaine_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012 to present day: subsequent success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, La Coka Nostra released their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Masters_of_the_Dark_Arts" title="Masters of the Dark Arts">Masters of the Dark Arts</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> It reached 176 on <i>Billboard</i> Top Current Albums, 40 on <a href="/wiki/Independent_Albums" title="Independent Albums">Independent Albums</a>, and 31 on Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Adam Fleischer of <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> magazine noted that "La Coka Nostra remain decidedly true to their core with their new album" and "that they are indeed masters of the dark arts". <i>HipHopDX</i> gave the album a positive review and noted that the album was a "sinister, happily violent detour from the pop-centric".<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Peter Marrack of <i>Exclaim!</i> also gave the album a positive review and noted that the album was "more or less a one-way ticket to hell".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Nathan G. O'Brien of <i>Scene Point Blank</i> gave it four out five star and said "with Master of the Dark Arts La Coka Nostra’s pluperfect union of bombastic boom-bap, record scratching, and realism-based hardcore rhyming".<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Also that year, Carroll acted in <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Dominik" title="Andrew Dominik">Andrew Dominik</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Killing_Them_Softly" title="Killing Them Softly">Killing Them Softly</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Carroll released his second solo album <i>The Boston Project</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Where all the songs have featured guest from the Boston hip hop local scenes, including the likes of <a href="/wiki/Esoteric_(rapper)" title="Esoteric (rapper)">Esoteric</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ed_O.G." title="Ed O.G.">Ed O.G.</a>, Millyz, etc. Of this record Carroll explained his intent: “I wanted to create a record that highlights what this city has to offer musically and show how diverse the talent is. This record embodies something much larger than just a Slaine record.”<sup id="cite_ref-:8_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On the Top Rap Albums it peaked at the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> it peaked at 40,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> and on <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers">Top Heatseekers</a> at 10.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Matt Jost of Rapreview gave it a seven point five out of ten saying: "Slaine makes for a formidable tour guide, relying on his own experience but also knowing that, as he puts it at one point, “I speak for more of us.”"<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Andres Tardio of HipHopDx gave it three point five stars out of five and stated that. "<i>The Boston Project</i> is a solid album with versatility that does what Slaine set out to do: represent Boston Hip Hop in a positive light."<sup id="cite_ref-:8_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Martìn Cabellero of <a href="/wiki/The_Boston_Globe" title="The Boston Globe">The Boston Globe</a> liked the album calling it ambitious and a strong showing of his talent.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Carroll released his third album <i><a href="/wiki/The_King_of_Everything_Else" title="The King of Everything Else">The King of Everything Else</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup></i> On the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> it peaked at 189,<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> at 26,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> on Top Rap Albums at 15,<sup id="cite_ref-:3_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> on US <a href="/wiki/Independent_Albums" title="Independent Albums">Independent Albums</a> at 30,<sup id="cite_ref-:4_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and on <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Heatseekers">Top Heatseekers</a> at 4.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Acting credits for that year are the <a href="/wiki/Gangster_film" title="Gangster film">gangster film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/By_the_Gun" title="By the Gun">By the Gun</a>,</i><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Matthews" title="Trevor Matthews">Trevor Matthews</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Girl_House" title="Girl House">Girl House</a></i>, where he plays the main antagonist. The second is described as a good <a href="/wiki/Slasher_film" title="Slasher film">slasher film</a> with good performances.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Mike Wilson of <a href="/wiki/Bloody_Disgusting" title="Bloody Disgusting">Bloody Disgusting</a> said "Slaine hits the right notes when it comes to expressions and emotions, while evoking a bit of sympathy for the sob".<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Scott Hallam of <a href="/wiki/Dread_Central" title="Dread Central">Dread Central</a> praised Carroll "one of the true strengths of the film is Slaine’s portrayal of the killer, Loverboy".<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, La Coka Nostra released <i><a href="/wiki/To_Thine_Own_Self_Be_True" title="To Thine Own Self Be True">To Thine Own Self Be True</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> The album reached 38 on the Billboard Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Steve Juon of <i>RapReviews</i> gave eight point five out of ten and wrote "for a blissful 45 minutes it's an uncut dose of that nostalgia straight through the ear canals to the dopamine centers of my brain".<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Also that year, Carroll acted in <a href="/wiki/Rawson_Marshall_Thurber" title="Rawson Marshall Thurber">Rawson Marshall Thurber</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence" title="Central Intelligence">Central Intelligence</a>,</i><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_Swab" title="John Swab">John Swab</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Let_Me_Make_You_a_Martyr" title="Let Me Make You a Martyr">Let Me Make You a Martyr</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> and <i>Dead Draw</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Carroll and rapper <a href="/wiki/Termanology" title="Termanology">Termanology</a>, as a duo called Slaine Vs Termanology, released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Anti-Hero_(Slaine_and_Termanology_album)" title="Anti-Hero (Slaine and Termanology album)">Anti-Hero</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Also. that year,  he was in the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> movie <i><a href="/wiki/Wheelman_(film)" title="Wheelman (film)">Wheelman</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>

</p><p>In 2021, Carroll acted in the suspense film <i><a href="/wiki/Ida_Red_(film)" title="Ida Red (film)">Ida Red</a></i> by John Swab.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> That year he also released the album <i>The Things We Can't Forgive.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup></i>
</p><p>In 2022, Carroll acted in <i>It Snows All the Time</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2023, Carroll acted in two John Swab's films <i>Little Dixie,</i><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> and <i>One Day as Lion</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal">Personal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slaine_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Carroll is a father with one son.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2022, in addition to his music and acting career, Carroll opened up Charles River Recovery with a group of professionals whose mission is to give treatment to people who suffer from addiction. Carroll found sobriety in March 2014 after a long battle with alcoholism and addiction and became increasingly involved in helping others after losing his best friend to an overdose. Charles River Recovery is a 110 bed inpatient medical detox and clinical stabilization facility in the Boston area.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
