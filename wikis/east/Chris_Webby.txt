<p><b>Christian Walcott Webster</b> (born October 13, 1988), better known by his stage name <b>Chris Webby</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Norwalk,_Connecticut" title="Norwalk, Connecticut">Norwalk, Connecticut</a>. Chris Webby has released many <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a>-hosted <i>Bars On Me</i> (2012) and his <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> <i>There Goes the Neighborhood</i> (2011), which peaked at number 101 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. He has worked with various artists such as <a href="/wiki/Freeway_(rapper)" title="Freeway (rapper)">Freeway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joell_Ortiz" title="Joell Ortiz">Joell Ortiz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_K.R.I.T." title="Big K.R.I.T.">Big K.R.I.T.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apathy_(rapper)" title="Apathy (rapper)">Apathy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bun_B" title="Bun B">Bun B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jarren_Benton" title="Jarren Benton">Jarren Benton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kid_Ink" title="Kid Ink">Kid Ink</a>. In 2013 he and his label, Homegrown Music, signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/E1_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="E1 Music">E1 Music</a>. He then released <i><a href="/wiki/Homegrown_(EP)" title="Homegrown (EP)">Homegrown</a></i>, another EP, in November 2012. Webster released his debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Chemically_Imbalanced_(Chris_Webby_album)" title="Chemically Imbalanced (Chris Webby album)">Chemically Imbalanced</a></i> on October 27, 2014.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chris_Webby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Webby's mother was a middle school teacher at <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Hale_Middle_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Nathan Hale Middle School">Nathan Hale Middle School</a>, his father was a guitarist and he was their only child.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He started listening to rap at a young age admiring <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, he then started writing his own raps in 2000 at age 11.<sup id="cite_ref-thehour1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thehour1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Greens_Farms_Academy" title="Greens Farms Academy">Greens Farms Academy</a>, a private school in Westport, Connecticut.<sup id="cite_ref-ctpost1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ctpost1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chris_Webby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2007.E2.80.932011:_Mixtapes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007–2011:_Mixtapes">2007–2011: Mixtapes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chris_Webby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007–2011: Mixtapes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After graduating from Green Farms Academy in 2007, Chris Webby went to <a href="/wiki/Hofstra_University" title="Hofstra University">Hofstra University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> on scholarship. During this time his rap name was <b>Vindictive</b>, and was a known freestyler through the <a href="/wiki/Frat_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Frat house">frat houses</a>, himself being a member of <a href="/wiki/Sigma_Alpha_Epsilon" title="Sigma Alpha Epsilon">Sigma Alpha Epsilon</a> fraternity (ΣΑΕ).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was arrested on March 27, 2009, during his sophomore year, in connection with a failed robbery attempt of a drug dealer on Hofstra University campus. He would then spend a week in jail and be banned from the school.<sup id="cite_ref-ctpost1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ctpost1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Following getting kicked out he looked at rapping as a career, and focused on releasing music.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 30, 2009, Chris Webby released his debut mixtape <i>The White Noise LP</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape featured his first hit single "<a href="/w/index.php?title=La_La_La_(Chris_Webby_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="La La La (Chris Webby song) (page does not exist)">La La La</a>," first released on April 17, 2009, on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, based on "Around the World" by Beat Ink and samples <a href="/wiki/A_Touch_of_Class_(band)" title="A Touch of Class (band)">ATC</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Around_the_World_(La_La_La_La_La)" title="Around the World (La La La La La)">Around The World</a>". He performed at local Connecticut venues, including Toad's Place and the Heirloom Arts Theatre.<sup id="cite_ref-ctpost1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ctpost1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Then on September 19, 2009, he released his second mixtape <i>Teenage Mutant Ninja Rapper</i>. The two mixtapes included tracks he had recorded since 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 10, 2010, he released his third mixtape <i>Optimus Rhyme</i>, intended to be a prelude to his fourth mixtape <i>The Underclassmen</i>. The project was billed as a "mini-mixtape" and was primarily a compilation of his unreleased tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He teamed up with DJBooth to release his fourth mixtape <i>The Underclassmen</i> on July 14, 2010, which featured a guest appearance by <a href="/wiki/Mac_Miller" title="Mac Miller">Mac Miller</a> and others.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape contains disses towards fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Adams" title="Sammy Adams">Sammy Adams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 21, 2010, Webby released his fifth mixtape <i>Best in the Burbs</i>. The mixtape featured collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Statik_Selektah" title="Statik Selektah">Statik Selektah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ski_Beatz" title="Ski Beatz">Ski Beatz</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Big_K.R.I.T." title="Big K.R.I.T.">Big K.R.I.T.</a> and has been downloaded over 200,000 times on <a href="/wiki/DatPiff" title="DatPiff">DatPiff</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It had even shut down DatPiff's servers due to the high number of downloads upon its release, the first mixtape to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2011.E2.80.932012:_Webster.27s_Laboratory_and_There_Goes_the_Neighborhood"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–2012:_Webster's_Laboratory_and_There_Goes_the_Neighborhood">2011–2012: <i>Webster's Laboratory</i> and <i>There Goes the Neighborhood</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chris_Webby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2011–2012: Webster&#039;s Laboratory and There Goes the Neighborhood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 3, 2011, Webby released his sixth mixtape <i>Webster's Laboratory</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape was hosted by DJ Ill Will and DJ Rockstar, which featured guest appearances from rappers <a href="/wiki/Freeway_(rapper)" title="Freeway (rapper)">Freeway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gorilla_Zoe" title="Gorilla Zoe">Gorilla Zoe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kinetics_(rapper)" title="Kinetics (rapper)">Kinetics</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Kinetics_%26_One_Love" title="Kinetics &amp; One Love">Kinetics &amp; One Love</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Apathy_(rapper)" title="Apathy (rapper)">Apathy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> This mixtape also resulted in DatPiff's server crashing.<sup id="cite_ref-thehour1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thehour1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape was met with positive reviews, receiving a three out of five or "L" from <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i>. Adam Fleischer of <i>XXL</i> said, "<i>Webster's Laboratory</i> ends up being further proof that not only can Chris Webby rap, but his skills are improving with each release. Having said that, his content gets somewhat tired, and <i>Webster's Laboratory</i> is indicative of an artist still trying to find himself and his own sound."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 1, 2011, Webby released his first retail project an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> titled <i>There Goes the Neighborhood</i>. The EP would be released by his record label Homegrown Music, and feature a <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearance</a> by <a href="/wiki/Slaine_(rapper)" title="Slaine (rapper)">Slaine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Upon its release the EP shot to the top of the iTunes hip/hop sales chart.<sup id="cite_ref-thehour1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thehour1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The EP would end up debuting at number 101 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, number 13 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> chart and topping the <a href="/wiki/Heatseekers_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Heatseekers Albums">Heatseekers Albums</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic1_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On February 10, 2012, he began a 40-date North American <a href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">tour</a> in support of the EP.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932013:_Bars_On_Me.2C_signing_to_eOne_Music_and_Homegrown"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2013:_Bars_On_Me,_signing_to_eOne_Music_and_Homegrown">2012–2013: <i>Bars On Me</i>, signing to eOne Music and <i>Homegrown</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chris_Webby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–2013: Bars On Me, signing to eOne Music and Homegrown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Homegrown_(EP)" title="Homegrown (EP)">Homegrown (EP)</a></div>
<p>On September 30, 2012, Webby released his seventh mixtape, <i>Bars On Me</i> hosted by famed mixtape DJ, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Drama" title="DJ Drama">DJ Drama</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape included guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Bun_B" title="Bun B">Bun B</a>, D. Lector, <a href="/wiki/Emilio_Rojas" title="Emilio Rojas">Emilio Rojas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jon_Connor" title="Jon Connor">Jon Connor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kid_Ink" title="Kid Ink">Kid Ink</a>, <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, Nikkiya and <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a>, with notable production credits coming from <a href="/wiki/Cardo" title="Cardo">Cardo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sap_(producer)" title="Sap (producer)">Sap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Don_Cannon" title="Don Cannon">Don Cannon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Omen_(record_producer)" title="Omen (record producer)">Omen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ski_Beatz" title="Ski Beatz">Ski Beatz</a>, and WillPower among others.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Its release was covered by <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>, who interviewed him as he described the title saying, "There's definitely a few meanings that you could use with it, but the main one being lyrical bars. That's what I'm known for, that's what I continue to do, and I think a lot of artists kinda need to step their bars up a little bit."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> It featured all original material and received over 100,000 downloads on DatPiff in less than a week.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During 2013, Webby hinted at signing with a record label, and that he was in talks with a few. However he said, "I wouldn't want to sign under another rapper just because I want to be THE rapper. With me signing, it's not going to be me changing and selling out. It is going to be me getting an opportunity to really formulate my own thing. Homegrown Music will be my <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a>, <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-cbslocal1_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbslocal1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> After remaining independent for his entire career, in mid-2013 he signed a record deal with independent powerhouse <a href="/wiki/E1_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="E1 Music">eOne Music</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Following that he revealed that he was working on an EP and his debut <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio album">studio album</a>, which he said will "highlight his transformation from a rapper to an artist."<sup id="cite_ref-cbslocal1_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbslocal1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-allhiphop1_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allhiphop1-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On August 28, 2013, Webby released the first single, "Down Right" from his first project after signing with eOne Music.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The project was announced to be an EP titled, <i><a href="/wiki/Homegrown_(EP)" title="Homegrown (EP)">Homegrown</a></i>, released on November 12, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932016:_The_Checkup_.E2.80.93_Chemically_Imbalanced_.E2.80.93_Webster.27s_Laboratory_2"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2016:_The_Checkup_–_Chemically_Imbalanced_–_Webster's_Laboratory_2">2013–2016: <i>The Checkup</i> – <i>Chemically Imbalanced</i> – <i>Webster's Laboratory 2</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chris_Webby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013–2016: The Checkup – Chemically Imbalanced – Webster&#039;s Laboratory 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Also in August 2013, Webby indicated working with <a href="/wiki/Scott_Storch" title="Scott Storch">Scott Storch</a> and SupaDupes on his debut album.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> At the red carpet for the 2013 <a href="/wiki/BET_Hip_Hop_Awards" title="BET Hip Hop Awards">BET Hip Hop Awards</a> Chris Webby stated his debut album would be released in February 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> On October 24, 2013, Webby revealed that the EP's release had been pushed back to November 12, 2013, due to sample clearance issues.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Following its release, <i>Homegrown</i> would then peak at number 75 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> In a November 2013 interview with <a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a>, Webby also confirmed <a href="/wiki/Harry_Fraud" title="Harry Fraud">Harry Fraud</a> as a producer on his debut album.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He later revealed the album would be titled <i>Chemically Imbalanced</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-twitter1_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twitter1-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 3, 2014, Chris Webby announced his 8th mixtape, titled <i>The Checkup</i>, to be released on June 17, 2014. Also on June 3, Webby released the first single off <i>The Checkup</i>, named <i>Good Day</i>, featuring fellow Connecticut and Homegrown Music Group rapper, Jitta On The Track. Following the release, on June 10, Webby released <i>Chilly</i>, another song featuring Fat Trel to be on <i>The Checkup.</i> The mixtape was released with ten songs on June 17.
</p><p>On August 19, 2014, Webby digitally released the first single off <i>Chemically Imbalanced</i>, titled "F*ck Off."
</p><p>On September 26, 2014, Webby announced the release date and artwork for <i>Chemically Imbalanced</i>. It was released on October 27, 2014.
</p><p>On July 17, 2015, Webby released his 12th project, "Jamo Neat", a collab EP with producer Sap. The project came together over the course of eight days in the studio together. The EP was supported by the singles "Whatchu Need" featuring Sap &amp; Stacey Michelle, and "Vibe 2 It" featuring Sap.
</p><p>On December 30, 2015,  Webby announced on his personal Twitter account that he had begun working on his 13th project and 9th mixtape called Webster's Laboratory 2 (a sequel to the original from 2011) and began releasing new material in promotion of the project. On May 11, 2016, he announced its release date, and Webster's Laboratory 2 was released on June 1, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chris_Webby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He told <a href="/wiki/AllHipHop" title="AllHipHop">AllHipHop</a> in 2013, that he uses Adderall in the studio, which he is prescribed. He explained that he was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/ADD" class="mw-redirect" title="ADD">ADD</a> at a young age and has been on attention medication throughout his life. He says to this day that he has very bad attention deficit disorder.<sup id="cite_ref-allhiphop1_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allhiphop1-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
