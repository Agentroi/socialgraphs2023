font-size: 125%;"><div class="fn">Lady May</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Rhonda Natasha Robinson</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Also known as</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Mae West, May</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday">1974-03-06</span>) </span>March 6, 1974<span class="noprint ForceAgeToShow"> (age&#160;49)</span></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Origin</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role"><a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">Rapper</a>, actress, <a href="/wiki/Model_(person)" title="Model (person)">model</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">1998–2008</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data">Crazy Cat, <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista</a></td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Rhonda Natasha Robinson</b> (born March 6, 1974), better known by her stage name <b> Lady May</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, singer, songwriter and model from <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>'s suburbs.
</p><p>May is <a href="/wiki/African-American" class="mw-redirect" title="African-American">African-American</a> and partly of <a href="/wiki/Asian_people" title="Asian people">Asian</a> descent.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> She grew up listening to <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>. At the age of fifteen, May stopped attending high school and became a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_dance" title="Hip hop dance">hip hop dancer</a> in music videos for artists such as <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jodeci" title="Jodeci">Jodeci</a>, but was left unsatisfied, eventually turning to <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">rap music</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> She danced with Big Lez and became and accountant for <a href="/wiki/Trackmasters" title="Trackmasters">Trackmasters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She began rapping in the late 1990s under her stage name, Mae West; her rapping skills would eventually gain notice from fellow producer/rapper, Deric "<a href="/wiki/D-Dot" class="mw-redirect" title="D-Dot">D-Dot</a>" Angelettie, who introduced her via his 2000 solo debut album, <i>Tell 'Em Why U Madd</i>, on the track "Shysty Broads" alongside former <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a> protégé, Babe Blue. He later renamed her, Lady May, introduced her to Crazy Cat Productions and she eventually landed a contract deal with <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a> in 2001.
</p><p>May released her debut single in 2002, "<a href="/wiki/Round_Up_(Lady_May_song)" title="Round Up (Lady May song)">Round Up</a>", which featured <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> singer and former label-mate <a href="/wiki/Blu_Cantrell" title="Blu Cantrell">Blu Cantrell</a> it had got slowly rotation on the radio and her and Cantrell performed it together on <a href="/wiki/Soul_Train" title="Soul Train">Soul Train</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Her following planned single was "Dick &amp; Doe"; a music video for the song was shot but in the midst of the pushbacks for <i>May Day</i> (first scheduled for a release in May 2002, then to July 16, 2002, then August 2002), the single and video as well as the album were altogether shelved after the poor reception gained from "Round Up" - the album eventually leaked onto P2P sites. She was also featured on former label-mate Rob Jackson's 2002 single "Boom Boom Boom",<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> which was slated to appear on his debut album, <i>For the People</i>, but was eventually shelved as well. In 2003, she was featured on <a href="/wiki/Willa_Ford" title="Willa Ford">Willa Ford</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/A_Toast_to_Men" title="A Toast to Men">A Toast to Men</a>", also appearing in its video.
</p><p>The same year she appeared on <a href="/wiki/DJ_Kayslay" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Kayslay">DJ Kayslay</a>'s <i>Streetsweeper Vol. 1</i> on the song "Seven Deadly Sins", which also featured <a href="/wiki/Vita_(rapper)" title="Vita (rapper)">Vita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angie_Martinez" title="Angie Martinez">Angie Martinez</a>, Duchess, <a href="/wiki/Amil_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Amil (rapper)">Amil</a>, Sonja Blade, and <a href="/wiki/Remy_Ma" title="Remy Ma">Remy Ma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>May contributed on Jennifer Lopez's 2007 <i><a href="/wiki/Brave_(Jennifer_Lopez_album)" title="Brave (Jennifer Lopez album)">Brave</a></i> album credited as a songwriter on several tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>May Day</i> was ultimately released online on all platforms on August 30, 2023, nearly 21 years after it was ultimately shelved.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It contains a few more interludes that were not present on the tracklist of the initial promotional copies that circulated physically.
</p>
