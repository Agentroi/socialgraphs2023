<p><b>Adam Arnone</b> (born 1978), better known by his stage name <b>Adeem</b> (pronounced "A-D-M" and sometimes styled <b>ADeeM</b>), is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Keene,_New_Hampshire" title="Keene, New Hampshire">Keene, New Hampshire</a>. He is best known for his work in the hip hop group Glue and for winning the <a href="/wiki/Scribble_Jam" title="Scribble Jam">Scribble Jam</a> Emcee Battle in 1998 and 2001. He is also the owner of <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://echofinch.com/">ECHO FINCH</a>, a creative agency and music label.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adeem_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Adeem began rapping at age 15, when he started a group called Mellow Minds with a pair of local rappers in Keene, New Hampshire. The group appeared on <a href="/wiki/Keene_State_College" title="Keene State College">Keene State College</a> Radio from 1994 to 1996, where Adeem began to freestyle. In 1996, Adeem met DJ MF Shalem, and the two began working on routines and choreographing a live set.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adeem_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1998, during a trip to <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Maine" title="Portland, Maine">Portland, Maine</a>, rapper <a href="/wiki/Sole_(artist)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sole (artist)">Sole</a> convinced Adeem to attend <a href="/wiki/Scribble_Jam" title="Scribble Jam">Scribble Jam</a> to perform with his group, The Live Poets. Adeem did not intend to participate in the emcee battle that year, but his friends registered him to compete anyway. After eliminating Atmosphere's <a href="/wiki/Slug_(rapper)" title="Slug (rapper)">Slug</a> in the final round, Adeem won the Emcee Battle— it was his first victory in an organized competition.<sup id="cite_ref-scribblemagazine_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scribblemagazine-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The day after the battle, <a href="/wiki/Mr._Dibbs" title="Mr. Dibbs">Mr. Dibbs</a> expanded the 1200 Hobos DJ Crew to include emcees; Adeem was invited to the crew along with <a href="/wiki/Buck_65" title="Buck 65">Buck 65</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sixtoo" title="Sixtoo">Sixtoo</a>, Slug, <a href="/wiki/Doseone" title="Doseone">Doseone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eyedea" title="Eyedea">Eyedea</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adverse_(hip_hop_artist)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Adverse (hip hop artist) (page does not exist)">Adverse</a>, Sole, and <a href="/wiki/Alias_(musician)" title="Alias (musician)">Alias</a>. Although the crew did not release any projects as a collective, Adeem claimed membership in the group for many years.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late 1998, Adeem met <a href="/wiki/Sage_Francis" title="Sage Francis">Sage Francis</a> at an <a href="/wiki/Open_mic" title="Open mic">open mic</a> event in <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence, Rhode Island</a>. The two artists collaborated and played shows together for a few years afterwards. In 1999, Adeem and Sage flew to <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_Nova_Scotia" title="Halifax, Nova Scotia">Halifax, Nova Scotia</a>, and recorded a 30-minute song with Buck 65 and Sixtoo, which became known as "The Untitled Canadian Recordings". During this time, Adeem and Sage collaborated with Sole and Alias to produce "I'm Afraid", which was released on Sage Francis' <i>Sick of Waiting…</i> in 1999.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Later in 1999 Adeem and DJ MF Shalem recorded their first album, <i>The First Few Inches</i>, on cassette tape. The album, almost exclusively produced by Shalem, was individually dubbed, and all inserts were cut by hand. The album featured a guest verse by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adverse_(hip_hop_artist)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Adverse (hip hop artist) (page does not exist)">Adverse</a>, who joined Adeem and Shalem in 2000 to create a group called The Dorian Three.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the following years, The Dorian Three produced two albums and toured nationally.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>During the summer of 2001, Adeem finished his first solo album, <i>Sweet Talking Your Brain</i>. The album was produced by the Vinyl Monkeys (MF Shalem, The Eclectic, Elektro4, Scott Matelic, Moodswing9, DJ Mayonnaise, Maker) and was self-released in the fall of 2001. The album was later licensed by Syntax Records in the US and by Tri Eight Records in Japan in 2002. Tri Eight subsequently pressed a 12-inch single with the tracks "Sweet Talk", "Forgotten Habit", and "Stargazing".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Adeem entered the emcee battle at Scribble Jam again in 2001. He won by defeating battle heavyweights like <a href="/wiki/Sage_Francis" title="Sage Francis">Sage Francis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blueprint_(rapper)" title="Blueprint (rapper)">Blueprint</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mac_Lethal" title="Mac Lethal">Mac Lethal</a>. At the time, nobody had ever won the emcee battle more than once in the history of the hip-hop festival.<sup id="cite_ref-scribblemagazine_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scribblemagazine-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Because his longtime partner, DJ MF Shalem, was occupied as Sage Francis' tour DJ, Adeem connected with <a href="/wiki/The_Animal_Crackers" title="The Animal Crackers">DJ DQ</a> with the intent to create a touring act. At approximately this time, Maker, a producer from Them Badd Apples in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, sent Adeem a collection of beats over which to rap. After successful projects with both DJ DQ and Maker, the trio decided to visit Maker in <a href="/wiki/Aurora,_Illinois" title="Aurora, Illinois">Aurora, Illinois</a>, to create a touring act. The group, originally called Seconds Away, ultimately became Glue.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2003, Adeem was chosen to compete in an emcee battle on <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Call_with_Carson_Daly" title="Last Call with Carson Daly">Last Call with Carson Daly</a></i>. He finished in second place, behind <a href="/wiki/Breez_Evahflowin" class="mw-redirect" title="Breez Evahflowin">Breez Evahflowin</a>. The final battle lasted two rounds, only one of which was aired by NBC.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2004, Glue released its first album, <i>Seconds Away</i>, to critical success from trade publications and the blogosphere.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> That summer, Glue was asked to play at the <a href="/wiki/Warped_Tour" title="Warped Tour">Warped Tour</a> with a set at every show that summer.
</p><p><i>Catch as Catch Can</i> was ultimately released on September 16, 2006, to critical acclaim;<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> almost 10,000 copies of the album were sold worldwide. Glue supported the album with three coast-to-coast tours between 2006 and 2007, playing with acts like <a href="/wiki/Solillaquists_of_Sound" title="Solillaquists of Sound">Solillaquists of Sound</a>, X:144 &amp; SPS, and <a href="/wiki/Hangar_18_(band)" title="Hangar 18 (band)">Hangar 18</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In January 2008, <a href="/wiki/MTV2" title="MTV2">MTV2</a> featured Glue on its <i>On the Rise</i> program, highlighting the group as an innovative, new hip-hop group from Chicago. The group toured briefly in 2008 but decided to take a break before working on its next album.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p><i>The Volume in the Ground</i>, released in May 2011, featured collaboration with DJ MF Shalem, Maker, Chadeo, and Nobs. Adeem recorded vocals in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando, Florida</a>, with Solillaquists of Sound members Swamburger and Alexandrah.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The album was released, DIY, on Winners Never Sleep Records.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2011, Adeem and DJ MF Shalem began working on a follow-up to their 2004 album. Titled <i>Made in New Hampshire</i>, it was intended for release in October 2011.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The text near this tag is dated. (October 2011)">needs update</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Adam’s follow up album to <i>Volume in the Ground</i> came in the form of a complete redesign of his process. He enlisted the help of musicians Jentri Jollimore, Chris Beam, Mike Chadhina, and Ben Rogers to create a new sound based on the direction he started on <i>Volume in the Ground</i>. These sessions became <i>Move Toward Life</i>. Mostly improvised or written on the spot, Ben Rogers at Loud Sun Studio would capture everything in his underground basement studio. 
The album took the form of a rockabilly/blues/rap three headed monster. Shortly after the album was completed Adam’s mother unexpectedly passed away tragically. While grieving and adjusting to a new reality he set aside the name Adeem and started Adam and the Flood. New beginnings for new days. As a dedication to his mother Adam added the intro Nancy Lives to Move Toward Life. The song was made from recordings found on his mother’s phone. Shortly after the release of the album the Flood expanded to include musicians Josh Blair, Walker Landis, Justin Gregory, and Jon Braught. In 2019 The group would go on to record one ep together named Never Alone at Loud Sun Studios.
</p><p>In 2020 as the pandemic hit, Adam found himself separated from the group and focusing on production. The result of those months in solitude with his family was the album COPE pt 1 and 2. It’s the first album where Adam handled all production and vocals. It was mixed by the legendary Mark “DJ EXIT” Goodchild.
</p><p>In 2021 as a result of the pandemic and his new found love for producing, Adam started ECHO FINCH. A creative agency focusing on licensing music and scoring content. It also serves as a record label. Home to Rain, Modern Fools, Adam and the Flood, and Herr.
</p>
