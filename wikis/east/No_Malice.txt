<p><b>Gene Elliott Thornton Jr.</b> (born August 18, 1973), better known by his stage name <b>No Malice</b> (formerly known as <b>Malicious</b> and later <b>Malice</b>), is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">Virginia Beach, Virginia</a>. He is best known for being one half of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> duo <a href="/wiki/Clipse" title="Clipse">Clipse</a>, alongside his brother and fellow rapper <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>. No Malice released his solo debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Hear_Ye_Him" title="Hear Ye Him">Hear Ye Him</a></i>, in 2013, and his second studio album, <i>Let the Dead Bury the Dead</i>, in 2017.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=No_Malice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1973.E2.80.932009:_Early_life_and_Clipse"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1973–2009:_Early_life_and_Clipse">1973&#8211;2009: Early life and Clipse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=No_Malice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1973–2009: Early life and Clipse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>No Malice was born Gene Elliott Thornton Jr. on August 18, 1973, in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">The Bronx</a> borough of New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He and his family later moved down south to <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach" class="mw-redirect" title="Virginia Beach">Virginia Beach</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He and his brother, <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>, formed the <a href="/wiki/Rap_group" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap group">rap group</a> Clipse in 1992, and later were introduced to fellow Virginian <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, one half of the high-profile production team <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">The Neptunes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Impressed with their lyrical talents, Williams formed a working relationship with the duo.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He eventually helped them secure a recording contract with <a href="/wiki/Elektra_Records" title="Elektra Records">Elektra Records</a>, in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Under Elektra, and with <a href="/wiki/The_Neptunes" title="The Neptunes">The Neptunes</a> handling its production, Clipse recorded their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Exclusive_Audio_Footage" title="Exclusive Audio Footage">Exclusive Audio Footage</a></i>. The group's debut single "The Funeral", helped to generate fan interest in the album, but failed to make a significant commercial impact.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> With "The Funeral" deemed a failure, <i>Exclusive Audio Footage</i> itself was shelved indefinitely. Clipse were subsequently released from their recording contract shortly thereafter.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2001, Williams signed the duo to <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a>, through his recently established <a href="/wiki/Star_Trak_Entertainment" title="Star Trak Entertainment">Star Trak Entertainment</a> imprint.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Clipse released their commercial debut <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_Willin%27" title="Lord Willin&#39;">Lord Willin'</a></i>, on August 20, 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The album debuted at No.&#160;1 on <i>Billboard'</i>s Top R&amp;B/Hip-hop Album chart and No.&#160;4 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, fueled by the strength of the lead singles "<a href="/wiki/Grindin%27" title="Grindin&#39;">Grindin'</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/When_the_Last_Time" title="When the Last Time">When the Last Time</a>", which peaked at No.&#160;30 and No.&#160;19 respectively, on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The album's third single "<a href="/wiki/Ma,_I_Don%27t_Love_Her" title="Ma, I Don&#39;t Love Her">Ma, I Don't Love Her</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Faith_Evans" title="Faith Evans">Faith Evans</a>), was a modest hit, reaching No.&#160;86 on the Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On October 1, 2002—only a month after its release—<i><a href="/wiki/Lord_Willin%27" title="Lord Willin&#39;">Lord Willin'</a></i> was certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA).<sup id="cite_ref-Reference_1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reference_1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late 2003, Clipse began recording material for its second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Hell_Hath_No_Fury_(Clipse_album)" title="Hell Hath No Fury (Clipse album)">Hell Hath No Fury</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, further work on the album ground to a halt in 2004, when <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a>'s urban artists were absorbed into its sister label <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> as part of a larger merger between <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony Music Entertainment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bertelsmann_Music_Group" title="Bertelsmann Music Group">BMG</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Due to contractual requirements, Clipse was forced to stay on Jive, while Star Trak and the rest of its roster moved to a new home at <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> While Clipse resumed work on the album, and eventually finished its recording, the duo became increasingly frustrated with Jive, as the label overlooked it in favor of the more <a href="/wiki/Pop_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pop (music)">pop</a>-oriented acts on its roster, which caused numerous delays in the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Hell_Hath_No_Fury_(Clipse_album)" title="Hell Hath No Fury (Clipse album)">Hell Hath No Fury</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> As delays continued, the group asked for a formal release from its contract. When Jive refused to grant this request, the duo sued the label.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 9, 2006, Clipse finally reached an agreement with <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> to release the album through its own label, <a href="/wiki/Re-Up_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Re-Up Records">Re-Up Records</a>, along with Jive.<sup id="cite_ref-Reference_2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reference_2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <i>Hell Hath No Fury</i> was finally released on November 28, 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It spawned two singles: "<a href="/wiki/Mr._Me_Too" title="Mr. Me Too">Mr. Me Too</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a> and "<a href="/wiki/Wamp_Wamp_(What_It_Do)" title="Wamp Wamp (What It Do)">Wamp Wamp (What It Do)</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Slim_Thug" title="Slim Thug">Slim Thug</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> While the album received a great deal of critical acclaim, its sales were modest, at 78,000 in the first week.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> magazine <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="XXL magazine">XXL</a></i> gave the album a "XXL" rating, marking it as a five-star album. At the time only five albums had previously received that honor.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In a May 19, 2007 interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Eye_Weekly" title="Eye Weekly">Eye Weekly</a></i>, Clipse revealed that the group had been officially released from its recording contract with Jive.<sup id="cite_ref-Reference_3_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reference_3-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After this, the duo began discussions with several record labels, eventually signing with <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> on October 26, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-Reference_5_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reference_5-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The follow-up to <i>Hell Hath No Fury</i>, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Til_the_Casket_Drops_(Clipse_album)" title="Til the Casket Drops (Clipse album)">Til the Casket Drops</a></i>, was released on December 8, 2009 via Columbia Records.<sup id="cite_ref-Release_Date_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Release_Date-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In a departure from the group's previous works, which only featured production from the Neptunes, the album features production from <a href="/wiki/Sean_%22Diddy%22_Combs" class="mw-redirect" title="Sean &quot;Diddy&quot; Combs">Sean "Diddy" Combs</a>' production team <a href="/wiki/The_Hitmen_(production_team)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Hitmen (production team)">The Hitmen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/DJ_Khalil" title="DJ Khalil">DJ Khalil</a> among others. The album did not fare as well commercially as the group's first two albums, peaking at No.&#160;41 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 albums chart.<sup id="cite_ref-pitchfork1_2-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.93present:_Solo_career_and_Hear_Ye_Him"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–present:_Solo_career_and_Hear_Ye_Him">2010&#8211;present: Solo career and <i>Hear Ye Him</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=No_Malice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010–present: Solo career and Hear Ye Him">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2010, No Malice announced that he and Pusha T would release solo albums later in the year,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and that they were no longer signed to Columbia Records.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2011 No Malice published his book <i>Wretched, Pitiful, Poor, Blind &amp; Naked</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> a memoir about his life, including fearing contracting <a href="/wiki/AIDS" class="mw-redirect" title="AIDS">AIDS</a>, as well as his <a href="/wiki/Conversion_to_Christianity" title="Conversion to Christianity">conversion to Christianity</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 6, 2012, he announced that he changed his name from Malice to No Malice via a video posted to <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It depicted No Malice viewing himself in a casket in a funeral parlor and walking away. The video, which was directed by No Malice, opens with multiple quotes from the Bible: first, from <a href="/wiki/Ephesians" class="mw-redirect" title="Ephesians">Ephesians</a> 4:31, "Let all bitterness and wrath and anger and clamor and slander be put away from you, along with all malice;" then, from 1 Peter 2:1, "Wherefore laying aside all malice, and all guile, and hypocrisies, and envies, and all evil speakings;" and Romans 1:29, "Being filled with all unrighteousness, fornication, wickedness, covetousness, maliciousness; full of envy, murder, debate, deceit, malignity; whisperers;" and Colossians 3:8, "But now you must put them all away: anger, wrath, malice, slander, and obscene talk from your mouth."<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, No Malice collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Lecrae" title="Lecrae">Lecrae</a> on his mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Church_Clothes" title="Church Clothes">Church Clothes</a></i>, on the song "Darkest Hour".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On June 19, 2012, No Malice released the first single from <i><a href="/wiki/Hear_Ye_Him" title="Hear Ye Him">Hear Ye Him</a></i> titled "June" featuring Eric David.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On July 24, 2012, the second single from <i>Hear Ye Him</i> titled "Unforgettable" was released.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On January 15, 2013, the third single from <i>Hear Ye Him</i> titled "Smoke &amp; Mirrors" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ab-Liva" class="mw-redirect" title="Ab-Liva">Ab-Liva</a> was released.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On May 21, 2013, it was announced that his debut album <i>Hear Ye Him</i> would be released on July 2, 2013, but the album was later pushed back.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On June 4, 2013, the fourth single from <i>Hear Ye Him</i> titled "Bury That" was released.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 18, 2017, No Malice released his second studio album, <i>Let the Dead Bury the Dead</i>, which contains the track <i>Fake News</i>, where No Malice denounces his earlier work, singing "<i>Take my catalog, I just as soon set a match to it / Money like a side chick, I ain't that attached to it</i>".
</p><p>On July 6, 2018, his single "Give 'Em Game" was released.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> A reviewer on <i>Jam The Hype</i> wrote, "No Malice spits timeless bars that listeners will gain wisdom from."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He and his brother made a guest appearance as <a href="/wiki/Clipse" title="Clipse">Clipse</a> on the track "Use This Gospel" on Kanye West's 2019 album <i>Jesus Is King</i>.
</p><p>In 2022, they have collaborated on <a href="/wiki/Nigo" title="Nigo">Japanese DJ Nigo</a>’s album, titled “<a href="/wiki/I_Know_Nigo!" title="I Know Nigo!">I Know NIGO!</a>” on a song called “Punch Bowl”. The following month, they collaborated on brother <a href="/wiki/Pusha_T" title="Pusha T">Pusha T</a>’s album, titled “<a href="/wiki/It%27s_Almost_Dry" title="It&#39;s Almost Dry">It's Almost Dry</a>”, on a song called “I Pray For You”. He is credited as No Malice on the record, produced by Ye.
</p>
