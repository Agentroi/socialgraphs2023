<p><b>James Harvey Kennedy</b> (born May 25, 1970) is an American actor and comedian. He has played <a href="/wiki/List_of_Scream_(film_series)_characters#Randy_Meeks" title="List of Scream (film series) characters">Randy Meeks</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Scream_(franchise)" title="Scream (franchise)">Scream</a></i> franchise (1996–2000) and a multitude of characters in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jamie_Kennedy_Experiment" title="The Jamie Kennedy Experiment">The Jamie Kennedy Experiment</a></i> (2002–2004) on <a href="/wiki/The_WB" title="The WB">The WB</a>. His other film roles include <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_%2B_Juliet" title="Romeo + Juliet">Romeo + Juliet</a></i> (1996), <i><a href="/wiki/Bowfinger" title="Bowfinger">Bowfinger</a></i> (1999), <i><a href="/wiki/Malibu%27s_Most_Wanted" title="Malibu&#39;s Most Wanted">Malibu's Most Wanted</a></i> (2003), <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Bliss" title="Finding Bliss">Finding Bliss</a></i> (2009), and <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Deeds" title="Good Deeds">Good Deeds</a></i> (2012).
</p><p>He has starred in the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_Whisperer" title="Ghost Whisperer">Ghost Whisperer</a></i> (2005–2010), provided <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting" title="Voice acting">voice-work</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cleveland_Show" title="The Cleveland Show">The Cleveland Show</a></i> (2009–2013) and regularly tours as a stand-up comedian and podcaster.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamie_Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kennedy was born on May 25, 1970, in <a href="/wiki/Upper_Darby_Township,_Pennsylvania" title="Upper Darby Township, Pennsylvania">Upper Darby Township</a>, a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</a>. Kennedy was raised <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He attended and graduated from <a href="/wiki/Monsignor_Bonner_High_School" title="Monsignor Bonner High School">Monsignor Bonner High School</a> in 1988. After high school, Kennedy began his career as a <a href="/wiki/Hollywood,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Hollywood, California">Hollywood</a> extra. Meanwhile, he worked on voice impersonations.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jamie_Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Jamie_Kennedy_by_Gage_Skidmore_3.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Jamie_Kennedy_by_Gage_Skidmore_3.jpg/170px-Jamie_Kennedy_by_Gage_Skidmore_3.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="211" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Jamie_Kennedy_by_Gage_Skidmore_3.jpg/255px-Jamie_Kennedy_by_Gage_Skidmore_3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Jamie_Kennedy_by_Gage_Skidmore_3.jpg/340px-Jamie_Kennedy_by_Gage_Skidmore_3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2120" data-file-height="2632" /></a><figcaption>Jamie Kennedy, 2010</figcaption></figure>
<p>When Kennedy first arrived in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, he became a professional <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_the_United_States" title="Cinema of the United States">Hollywood</a> extra.
</p><p>After a few years of struggling, Kennedy was unable to find an agent and worked as a host at <a href="/wiki/Red_Lobster" title="Red Lobster">Red Lobster</a>. He auditioned for over 80 commercials and could not book one. He then took a job as a <a href="/wiki/Telemarketing" title="Telemarketing">telemarketer</a> and learned that he had a talent for selling things. Kennedy then thought that if he could sell anything, "why not sell myself?", becoming his own agent.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Kennedy created a false persona, screen agent "Marty Power", to attract the attention of real agents and managers over the phone, who would later book his performances. He came to prominence in the late 1990s for playing Randy Meeks in the <a href="/wiki/Scream_(franchise)" title="Scream (franchise)"><i>Scream</i> film series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His lead role as Tim Avery in <i><a href="/wiki/Son_of_the_Mask" title="Son of the Mask">Son of the Mask</a></i> earned him a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Raspberry_Award_for_Worst_Actor" title="Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Actor">Golden Raspberry Award nomination for Worst Actor</a>, he would later voice his experiences with the film on his YouTube channel in the later years after its release.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The film would also go on to inspire Kennedy to host the 2007 documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Heckler_(film)" title="Heckler (film)">Heckler</a></i> after feeling hurt by the film's poor reception in which some reviewers attacked him personally.
</p><p>Kennedy formed a production company called Wannabe Producers, alongside Josh<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> through which he has produced the shows <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jamie_Kennedy_Experiment" title="The Jamie Kennedy Experiment">The Jamie Kennedy Experiment</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Blowin%27_Up" title="Blowin&#39; Up">Blowin' Up</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Starlet" title="The Starlet">The Starlet</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Living_with_Fran" title="Living with Fran">Living with Fran</a></i> created by <a href="/wiki/David_Garrett_(screen_writer)" class="mw-redirect" title="David Garrett (screen writer)">David Garrett</a>. Following <i><a href="/wiki/Malibu%27s_Most_Wanted" title="Malibu&#39;s Most Wanted">Malibu's Most Wanted</a></i>, in which he both wrote and starred, Kennedy co-wrote the <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Blowin%27_Up" title="Blowin&#39; Up">Blowin' Up</a></i> (2006) featuring his friend, <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Stone" title="Stuart Stone">Stu Stone</a>.
</p><p>Kennedy lent his voice to the videogame <i><a href="/wiki/ESPN_NFL_2K5" title="ESPN NFL 2K5">ESPN NFL 2K5</a></i> as a celebrity adversary with his own football team, the Upper Darby <a href="/wiki/Cheesesteak" title="Cheesesteak">Cheesesteaks</a>. He is also unlockable as a free agent tight end in season mode. His stint as <a href="/wiki/Activision" title="Activision">Activision</a>'s emcee at <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Entertainment_Expo" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronic Entertainment Expo">Electronic Entertainment Expo</a> 2007, however, drew much criticism not only for his ignorance of the industry, but also for appearing to perform drunk as he insulted the audience.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In a video uploaded to YouTube on June 14, 2021, Kennedy claimed that Activision had scrapped his script at the last minute and he was ad-libbing his jokes while suffering from <a href="/wiki/Occupational_burnout" title="Occupational burnout">burnout</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While working on his film <i>Malibu's Most Wanted</i>, Kennedy wrote an autobiography titled, <i>Wannabe: A Hollywood Experiment</i>. The book chronicles his life in Hollywood as he attempts to become a star. It gives background on his life and family and quickly dives into his adventures. It tells of such things as Kennedy's living conditions in the Hollywood slums, his dilapidated car, and his kidney issues.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>A performer of <a href="/wiki/Stand-up_comedy" title="Stand-up comedy">stand-up comedy</a>, he is also known for his sketch performances on his television reality show, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jamie_Kennedy_Experiment" title="The Jamie Kennedy Experiment">The Jamie Kennedy Experiment</a></i>, which became the <a href="/wiki/The_WB" title="The WB">WB Network</a>'s highest-ranking new show in 2002, but which was cancelled in April 2004 due to falling viewership.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2006, Jizzy Entertainment released <i><a href="/wiki/Unwashed:_The_Stand-Up_Special" class="mw-redirect" title="Unwashed: The Stand-Up Special">Unwashed: The Stand-Up Special</a>.</i> In 2008, Kennedy released the documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Heckler_(film)" title="Heckler (film)">Heckler</a>,</i> about the plight of stand-up comics versus their often-aggressive audiences.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2007 and 2017, he made two appearances in <i><a href="/wiki/Criminal_Minds" title="Criminal Minds">Criminal Minds</a></i> as a cannibal satanist serial killer.
</p><p>From 2008 to 2010, Kennedy played psychology professor Eli James in the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_Whisperer" title="Ghost Whisperer">Ghost Whisperer</a></i>. From 2009 to 2013, he was in the TV series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cleveland_Show" title="The Cleveland Show">The Cleveland Show</a></i>, playing Roberta Tubbs' boyfriend.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He also stars in <a href="/wiki/Nicktoons" title="Nicktoons">Nicktoons</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Fanboy_%26_Chum_Chum" title="Fanboy &amp; Chum Chum">Fanboy &amp; Chum Chum</a></i> as Kyle, an insecure boy wizard who loathes the fun-loving Fanboy and Chum Chum, but who secretly yearns for their friendship. In April 2010, sources reported that Kennedy would very likely return to the <a href="/wiki/Scream_(franchise)" title="Scream (franchise)"><i>Scream</i> franchise</a> to star in the <a href="/wiki/Scream_4" title="Scream 4">fourth installment</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> however, according to <a href="/wiki/Scream_(franchise)" title="Scream (franchise)"><i>Scream</i></a> screenwriter <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Williamson_(screenwriter)" title="Kevin Williamson (screenwriter)">Kevin Williamson</a>, plans were never made for him to rejoin the franchise, attributing the misinformation to a fabrication by Kennedy.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 31, 2012, Kennedy hosted and produced <i><a href="/wiki/First_Night_2013_with_Jamie_Kennedy" title="First Night 2013 with Jamie Kennedy">First Night 2013 with Jamie Kennedy</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve" title="New Year&#39;s Eve">New Year's Eve</a> television special widely considered <a href="/wiki/List_of_television_shows_considered_the_worst" class="mw-redirect" title="List of television shows considered the worst">one of the worst shows of all time</a>. His film <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_%26_Found_in_Armenia" title="Lost &amp; Found in Armenia">Lost &amp; Found in Armenia</a></i>, in which he plays an American tourist who ends up in an Armenian village, accused of being a Turkish spy, later opened to select theatres on June 7, 2013. His film <i><a href="/wiki/Buddy_Hutchins" title="Buddy Hutchins">Buddy Hutchins</a></i>, in which Kennedy plays a down-on-his-luck recovering alcoholic, was released in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, he portrayed Frump in the Colton Tran adventure mystery film <i>Gloom</i> and Beach Patrolman Alex in Allegra Pictures' horror film <i>The Sand</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, he also starred as Travis Welker in <i><a href="/wiki/Tremors_5:_Bloodlines" title="Tremors 5: Bloodlines">Tremors 5: Bloodlines</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and as Rob in <i>Rivers 9</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy played <a href="/wiki/NARAL_Pro-Choice_America" title="NARAL Pro-Choice America">NARAL Pro-Choice America</a> founder Larry Lader in the 2020 film <a href="/wiki/Roe_v._Wade_(film)" title="Roe v. Wade (film)"><i>Roe v. Wade</i></a>, which stars a predominantly <a href="/wiki/Conservatism_in_the_United_States" title="Conservatism in the United States">conservative</a> <a href="/wiki/Ensemble_cast" title="Ensemble cast">ensemble cast</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wapo_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wapo-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Beast" title="The Daily Beast">The Daily Beast</a></i>, which noted the film depicts Lader as "a shady figure pulling strings from behind the scenes who treats abortions as a money-making operation," Kennedy said he appeared in the film to perform in a dramatic role that he is not normally offered, and personally identifies as <a href="/wiki/Abortion-rights_movements" title="Abortion-rights movements">pro-choice</a>, and that, "it’s also not fair for people to think that because I’m in a project with them that I’m like that, or that I believe in this stuff".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
