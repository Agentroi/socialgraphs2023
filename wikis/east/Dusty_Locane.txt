<p><b>Dusty Locane</b> (often stylized in all uppercase as <b>DUSTY LOCANE</b>; born June 11, 1999) is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a>. He began gaining recognition in 2020 and 2021 when his singles "<a href="/wiki/Rollin_n_Controllin_Freestyle" title="Rollin n Controllin Freestyle">Rollin n Controllin Freestyle</a>" and "Rolando (Caught in the Rain)" began getting popular on various <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> platforms,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> most notably <a href="/wiki/TikTok" title="TikTok">TikTok</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He has been noted by <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> for his "raspy, <a href="/wiki/Drill_music" title="Drill music">drill</a> sound,"<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and by <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork</a></i>, who called "Rolando (Caught in the Rain)" a "must-hear" rap song.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_and_personal_life">Early and personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dusty_Locane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early and personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dusty Locane was raised in "a good home," not "subjected to the harsh realities of living in the hood," in <a href="/wiki/Canarsie,_Brooklyn" title="Canarsie, Brooklyn">Canarsie, Brooklyn</a>, New York.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> His father is Haitian and his mother is <a href="/wiki/Trinidadians_and_Tobagonians" title="Trinidadians and Tobagonians">Trinidadian</a>, which led to him listening to <a href="/wiki/Soca_music" title="Soca music">soca music</a> as a child, in addition to hip hop and R&amp;B.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He attended college for a year before getting expelled and initially wanted to pursue a career in basketball. He then spent some time in jail, and started considering a music career seriously upon being released.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He is affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Rollin%27_60s_Neighborhood_Crips" title="Rollin&#39; 60s Neighborhood Crips">Rollin' 60s Neighborhood Crips</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The rapper has three children, a son born on March 23, 2022, a daughter born June 11, 2022, and another son born in August 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-mixtapez_interview_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mixtapez_interview-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dusty_Locane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of August 2023 he has released two albums, two collaborative albums, one mixtape, two extended plays and 28 singles (including 13 as a featured artist).
</p><p>Dusty Locane released his debut single, "<a href="/wiki/Rollin_n_Controllin_Freestyle" title="Rollin n Controllin Freestyle">Rollin n Controllin Freestyle</a>", on August 7, 2020. On January 8, 2021, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Akademiks" title="DJ Akademiks">DJ Akademiks</a> posted two videos of Locane rapping to his millions of followers on Instagram,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> thus introducing him to new listeners. By December 2021, the song had 200 million streams and was certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dusty Locane announced that his debut album, <i>Untamed</i>, was to be released on November 19, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The album was postponed, however, out of respect for rapper <a href="/wiki/Young_Dolph" title="Young Dolph">Young Dolph</a>, who was murdered on November 17.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It was finally released on December 3, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The album contained 20 songs and was generally received rather positively by critics. The album was supported by 6 singles and featured guest appearances from OMB Jaydee, 3Kizzy, <a href="/wiki/Yung_Bleu" title="Yung Bleu">Yung Bleu</a>, 8ANDITT, <a href="/wiki/Rah_Swish" title="Rah Swish">Rah Swish</a> and KG MORE BREESH.
</p><p>On September 2, 2022 Dusty Locane released a 7 track EP titled <i>Rollin N Controllin</i>. The EP featured the singles "<a href="/wiki/Rollin_n_Controllin_Freestyle" title="Rollin n Controllin Freestyle">Rollin n Controllin Freestyle</a>", "Rolando (Caught in the Rain)", "Rollin n Controllin, Pt. 2 (Picture Me)" and "Rollin N Contollin, Pt.3 (Been Rollin)". The EP featured a sole appearance from Kajun Waters. 
</p><p>On September 23, 2022 Dusty Locane released a collaboration album alongside fellow rappers <a href="/wiki/Ron_Suno" title="Ron Suno">Ron Suno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rah_Swish" title="Rah Swish">Rah Swish</a> and OnPointLikeOP titled <i>Say Dat</i>. The album contained 8 songs.
</p><p>On October 31, 2022 Dusty Locane released an album titled <i>Nightmare On Da Fifth</i>. The album was supported by the single "Cremate (Run Outta Lucc)" which featured Stelly Hundo, OMB Jay Dee and 3Kizzy. The album featured 15 songs and guest appearances from 3Kizzy, Kajun Waters, SFIV5, TaTa, Stelly Hundo and OMB Jay Dee. 3 of the albums songs were released on previous Dusty Locane projects such as "PRESSURE" and "ROLANDO 2 (Catch The Rain)" both coming from <i>Rollin N Controllin</i> and "WAY BACC" coming from <i>Say Dat</i>.
</p><p>On December 5, 2022 Dusty Locane released an EP titled <i>Catch Da Flu</i>. The EP featured 6 songs.
</p>
