<p><b>Al Sherrod Lambert</b> (pronounced <i>æl ʃɛrəd læmbərt</i>; born June 29, 1985), sometimes known as simply <b>A-Rod</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> winning American producer, singer, songwriter, and musician.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomfield.edu_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomfield.edu-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He has won recognition as a gospel, pop, hip hop and R&amp;B producer. He is signed to <a href="/wiki/Kobalt_Music_Group" title="Kobalt Music Group">Kobalt Music Group</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and is a registered <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_Music,_Inc." title="Broadcast Music, Inc.">BMI</a> songwriter.<sup id="cite_ref-bmi.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bmi.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Sherrod_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lambert was born in <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a>. He grew up in nearby <a href="/wiki/Irvington,_New_Jersey" title="Irvington, New Jersey">Irvington</a> and attended <a href="/wiki/Irvington_High_School_(New_Jersey)" title="Irvington High School (New Jersey)">Irvington High School</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Bloomfield_College" title="Bloomfield College">Bloomfield College</a> where he graduated in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomfield.edu_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomfield.edu-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2010 Lambert moved to Los Angeles where he maintains residency with his wife and daughter.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomfield.edu_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomfield.edu-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Sherrod_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Producing_and_songwriting">Producing and songwriting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Sherrod_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Producing and songwriting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lambert started his music career in 2010 when he worked on the songs "Sleeping Pills" and "All Said and Done" on the <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Foxx" title="Jamie Foxx">Jamie Foxx</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/Best_Night_of_My_Life" title="Best Night of My Life">Best Night of My Life</a></i> with fellow New Jersey producer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Eric_Hudson" title="Eric Hudson">Eric Hudson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bmi.com_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bmi.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ratedrnb.com_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ratedrnb.com-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The Following year he wrote "<a href="/wiki/25/8_(song)" title="25/8 (song)">25/8</a>" for <a href="/wiki/Mary_J_Blige" class="mw-redirect" title="Mary J Blige">Mary J Blige</a>  as well as the title track for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Think_Like_a_Man" title="Think Like a Man">Think Like a Man</a></i>. The song was co written by <a href="/wiki/Harmony_Samuels" title="Harmony Samuels">Harmony Samuels</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Bellinger" title="Eric Bellinger">Eric Bellinger</a> and features the vocals of <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Hudson" title="Jennifer Hudson">Jennifer Hudson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ne_Yo" class="mw-redirect" title="Ne Yo">Ne Yo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Lambert wrote several songs for the <a href="/wiki/Fantasia_Barrino" class="mw-redirect" title="Fantasia Barrino">Fantasia Barrino</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/Side_Effects_of_You" title="Side Effects of You">Side Effects of You</a></i>. The album was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Best_Urban_Contemporary_Album" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Urban Contemporary Album">Best Urban Contemporary Album</a> at the 52nd Annual <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a>, as well as a nomination for <a href="/wiki/Best_Traditional_R%26B_Performance" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Traditional R&amp;B Performance">Best Traditional R&amp;B Performance</a> for the single "Get it Right" and a nomination for <a href="/wiki/Best_R%26B_Song" class="mw-redirect" title="Best R&amp;B Song">Best R&amp;B Song</a> for "<a href="/wiki/Without_Me_(Fantasia_Barrino_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Without Me (Fantasia Barrino song)">Without Me</a>". That same year he received two Billboard Pop Awards at the <a href="/wiki/BMI_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="BMI Awards">BMI Awards</a> for his work on the <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Grande" title="Ariana Grande">Ariana Grande</a> singles "<a href="/wiki/The_Way_(Ariana_Grande_song)" title="The Way (Ariana Grande song)">The Way</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Right_There_(Ariana_Grande_song)" title="Right There (Ariana Grande song)">Right There</a>". <a href="/wiki/The_Way_(Ariana_Grande_song)" title="The Way (Ariana Grande song)">"The Way</a>" peaked at #9 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_hot_100" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard hot 100">Billboard hot 100</a> and spent 28 weeks on the charts.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The song is <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> certified double platinum. <a href="/wiki/Right_There_(Ariana_Grande_song)" title="Right There (Ariana Grande song)">"Right There</a>" peaked at #84 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a> and spent 8 weeks on the charts.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The single is <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> certified gold.
</p><p>In 2014 Lamber co-wrote the <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Williams_(singer)" title="Michelle Williams (singer)">Michelle Williams</a> song "Say Yes". The song features backing vocals by former <a href="/wiki/Destiny%27s_Child" title="Destiny&#39;s Child">Destiny's Child</a> members <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Rowland" title="Kelly Rowland">Kelly Rowland</a>  and <a href="/wiki/Beyonce_Knowles" class="mw-redirect" title="Beyonce Knowles">Beyonce Knowles</a>. The song peaked at #1, staying on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_hot_100" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a> chart for 38 weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> It was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Soul_Train_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Soul Train Award">Soul Train Award</a> for best Gospel/Inspirational Song, and received four nominations at the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Stellar_Awards" title="Stellar Awards">Stellar Awards</a> including a win for Music Video of the Year.
</p><p>In 2017 Lambert was a songwriter on the <a href="/wiki/Lalah_Hathaway" title="Lalah Hathaway">Lalah Hathaway</a> album <i>Lalah Hathaway Live</i> which received a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for R&amp;B Album of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-grammy.com_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grammy.com-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The album peaked at #2 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a> and stayed on the charts for 12 weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He also wrote the single "Sexy Body" by <a href="/wiki/Pitbull_(rapper)" title="Pitbull (rapper)">Pitbull</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and the singles "What is Love" and "Good Woman" on the <a href="/wiki/La%27Porsha_Renae" title="La&#39;Porsha Renae">La'Porsha Renae</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/Already_All_Ready" class="mw-redirect" title="Already All Ready">Already All Ready</a></i>. 
Later Lambert would keep his hit songwriting and producitin career moving full steam ahead by co-producing “March 14”’on Drakes Scorpion album, co writing “Made for Now” for the legendary Janet Jackson and co—writing “Borderline” for r and b superstar Brandy in 2020.
In 2021 Lambert would become a main writer for the legendary <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Isley" title="Ronald Isley">Ronald Isley</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Isley_Brothers" title="The Isley Brothers">The Isley Brothers</a> penning three songs for the Rock &amp; Roll hall of famers. 
He now is releasing his on music under his artist name POINTS for his record label Points On The Board.
</p>
