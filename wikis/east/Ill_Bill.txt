<p><b>William Braunstein</b> (born July 14, 1972), known by his stage name <b>Ill Bill</b>, is an American rapper and record producer from <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York City. Having gained fame in the <a href="/wiki/Underground_hip_hop" title="Underground hip hop">underground hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/Non_Phixion" title="Non Phixion">Non Phixion</a>, Ill Bill is known for his diverse lyrics and as the producer, founder and <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a> of Uncle Howie Records. His brother Ron is rapper and producer <a href="/wiki/Necro_(rapper)" title="Necro (rapper)">Necro</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopug.com_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopug.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="Collaboration_work.2Fgroups"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaboration_work/groups">Collaboration work/groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Collaboration work/groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Non_Phixion">Non Phixion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Non Phixion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late 1994, <a href="/wiki/MC_Serch" title="MC Serch">MC Serch</a> took his protégé <a href="/wiki/Sabac_Red" title="Sabac Red">Sabac Red</a> and teamed him up with DJ Eclipse and Ill Bill, thereby creating the group known as Non Phixion.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Within six months Goretex, a childhood friend of Ill Bill, had joined the crew after freestyling for MC Serch. Non Phixion released a studio album titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Future_Is_Now" title="The Future Is Now">The Future is Now</a></i> in 2002, numerous singles and two compilations albums. Much of the production work was done by Necro.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Circle_of_Tyrants">Circle of Tyrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Circle of Tyrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Circle of Tyrants was an underground hip hop group consisting of <a href="/wiki/Necro_(rapper)" title="Necro (rapper)">Necro</a>, Ill Bill, Mr Hyde and Goretex. The group released a self-titled studio album in 2005 on Necro's <a href="/wiki/Psycho%2BLogical-Records" title="Psycho+Logical-Records">Psycho-Logical-Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Necro produced all tracks on the album.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The four members often collaborated on each other's solo projects.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="La_Coka_Nostra">La Coka Nostra</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: La Coka Nostra">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/La_Coka_Nostra" title="La Coka Nostra">La Coka Nostra</a> was founded in 2005 as a loose collective of artists connected through <a href="/wiki/Danny_Boy_(rapper)" title="Danny Boy (rapper)">Danny Boy</a> of <a href="/wiki/House_of_Pain" title="House of Pain">House of Pain</a>. Having taken time off from rap after the break-up of House of Pain, Danny Boy took to working in video production and fashion as well as mentoring other artists. He met <a href="/wiki/Slaine_(rapper)" title="Slaine (rapper)">Slaine</a> in Boston and, impressed with his writing, brought him to L.A. to record some tracks. Eventually it turned into more of a group effort including the former members of House of Pain and Ill Bill. It was announced that Everlast would be leaving La Coka Nostra due to his daughter's medical issues.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Heavy_Metal_Kings_with_Vinnie_Paz">Heavy Metal Kings with Vinnie Paz</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Heavy Metal Kings with Vinnie Paz">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Heavy_Metal_Kings" title="Heavy Metal Kings">Heavy Metal Kings</a> is a group featuring veteran rappers Ill Bill (Non Phixion/La Coka Nostra) and <a href="/wiki/Vinnie_Paz" title="Vinnie Paz">Vinnie Paz</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jedi_Mind_Tricks" title="Jedi Mind Tricks">Jedi Mind Tricks</a>/<a href="/wiki/Army_of_the_Pharaohs" title="Army of the Pharaohs">Army of the Pharaohs</a>). In 2006, Ill Bill was featured on the single "Heavy Metal Kings" by hip hop duo Jedi Mind Tricks, released through <a href="/wiki/Babygrande_Records" title="Babygrande Records">Babygrande Records</a>.
</p><p>Ill Bill and Vinnie Paz have since combined forces to form a group of the same name, "Heavy Metal Kings", and recorded a full self-titled album that was released on April 5, 2011, through Enemy Soil/Uncle Howie.
</p><p><i>Heavy Metal Kings 2</i> was officially confirmed and recording in 2013. It was announced that the album would be in stores in 2014 however as of March 2015. It was released in 2018 with the official name <i>Black God White Devil</i> and has numerous features from Goretex.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Heavy Metal Kings Vs. <a href="/wiki/DJ_Muggs" title="DJ Muggs">DJ Muggs</a> was posted to be in the works by an associated producer<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> on 2011, to this date this album has had no more updates or information released. It is not known if this album is still in the works.
</p>
<h2><span id="Solo_albums.2Falbums_with_brother_Necro"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_albums/albums_with_brother_Necro">Solo albums/albums with brother Necro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Solo albums/albums with brother Necro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="The_Early_Years:_Rare_Demos_.2791.E2.80.93.2794_.282001.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Early_Years:_Rare_Demos_'91–'94_(2001)"><i>The Early Years: Rare Demos '91–'94</i> (2001)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The Early Years: Rare Demos &#039;91–&#039;94 (2001)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Early_Years:_Rare_Demos_%2791%E2%80%93%2794" title="The Early Years: Rare Demos &#39;91–&#39;94">The Early Years: Rare Demos '91–'94</a></i> is the first rarities released on September 16, 2001, under Psycho+Logical-Records. The compilation contains 12 demos, an intro, two skits and one remix. Featured guests on the compilation include Gongo Boy and future <a href="/wiki/Non_Phixion" title="Non Phixion">Non Phixion</a> and Circle of Tyrants member Goretex. These tracks were recorded between 1991 and 1994.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Street_Villains_mixtapes_with_brother_Necro"><i>Street Villains</i> mixtapes with brother Necro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Street Villains mixtapes with brother Necro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ill Bill and his brother <a href="/wiki/Necro_(rapper)" title="Necro (rapper)">Necro</a> released two mixtapes named <i>Street Villains Vol. 1</i> (2003) and <i>Street Villains Vol. 2</i> (2005). <i>Street Villains Vol. 1</i> was released originally to build hype for Ill Bill's at the time upcoming album <i><a href="/wiki/What%27s_Wrong_with_Bill%3F" title="What&#39;s Wrong with Bill?">What's Wrong with Bill?</a></i>
</p>
<h3><span id="Ill_Bill_Is_the_Future_Vol._1.262_.282003.E2.80.932006.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Ill_Bill_Is_the_Future_Vol._1&amp;2_(2003–2006)"><i>Ill Bill Is the Future</i> Vol. 1&amp;2 (2003–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Ill Bill Is the Future Vol. 1&amp;2 (2003–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Ill Bill Is the Future</i> was released in 2003 on Ill Bills label Uncle Howie Records and is a mix of pre-released and new tracks with collaboration tracks with <a href="/wiki/El-P" title="El-P">EI-P</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Beatnuts" title="The Beatnuts">The Beatnuts</a> and Problemz.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Ill Bill Is the Future 2: I'm a Goon</i> was released in 2006 under Uncle Howie Records. The album featured mostly new tracks except for a few preused <a href="/wiki/La_Coka_Nostra" title="La Coka Nostra">La Coka Nostra</a> tracks and featured collaborations from artists such as <a href="/wiki/Q-Unique" title="Q-Unique">Q-Unique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>, Sick Jacken, Block McCloud, <a href="/wiki/MF_Grimm" title="MF Grimm">MF Grimm</a> and <a href="/wiki/B-Real" title="B-Real">B-Real</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Howie_Made_Me_Do_It_mixtapes"><i>Howie Made Me Do It</i> mixtapes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Howie Made Me Do It mixtapes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Recorded from 1999 to 2002, <i>Howie Made Me Do It</i> is a compilation of mostly songs previously released on mixtapes. Howie, the uncle of Ill Bill and Necro, is referenced in lyrics and his voice is sampled in various tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p><i>Howie Made Me Do It 2</i> was released in 2011 with collaborations from various artists such as <a href="/wiki/Vinnie_Paz" title="Vinnie Paz">Vinnie Paz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Q-Unique" title="Q-Unique">Q-Unique</a>. <i>Howie Made Me Do It 3</i> was released late 2013.
</p>
<h3><span id="What.27s_Wrong_with_Bill.3F_.282004.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="What's_Wrong_with_Bill?_(2004)"><i>What's Wrong with Bill?</i> (2004)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: What&#039;s Wrong with Bill? (2004)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>What's Wrong with Bill?</i> is Ill Bill's debut studio album, released on May 4, 2004, by <a href="/wiki/Psycho%2BLogical-Records" title="Psycho+Logical-Records">Psycho+Logical-Records</a>. The album spawned one single, "The Anatomy of a School Shooting", and a music video for "Chasing the Dragon". Ill Bill's brother <a href="/wiki/Necro_(rapper)" title="Necro (rapper)">Necro</a> produced the entire album.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The album never charted but is widely known as an underground classic from Ill Bill by fans.
</p>
<h3><span id="The_Hour_of_Reprisal_.282008.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Hour_of_Reprisal_(2008)"><i>The Hour of Reprisal</i> (2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: The Hour of Reprisal (2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 16, 2008, Ill Bill released his second album on Uncle Howie Records, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hour_of_Reprisal" title="The Hour of Reprisal">The Hour of Reprisal</a></i>. The album includes appearances from <a href="/wiki/Necro_(rapper)" title="Necro (rapper)">Necro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>, <a href="/wiki/B-Real" title="B-Real">B-Real</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cypress_Hill" title="Cypress Hill">Cypress Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Everlast" title="Everlast">Everlast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bad_Brains" title="Bad Brains">Bad Brains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Max_Cavalera" title="Max Cavalera">Max Cavalera</a> of <a href="/wiki/Soulfly" title="Soulfly">Soulfly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vinnie_Paz" title="Vinnie Paz">Vinnie Paz</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jedi_Mind_Tricks" title="Jedi Mind Tricks">Jedi Mind Tricks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Howard_Jones_(American_singer)" title="Howard Jones (American singer)">Howard Jones</a> of <a href="/wiki/Killswitch_Engage" title="Killswitch Engage">Killswitch Engage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Immortal_Technique" title="Immortal Technique">Immortal Technique</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>. It also features production by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Muggs" title="DJ Muggs">DJ Muggs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Todd_Ray" title="Todd Ray">T-Ray</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Lethal" title="DJ Lethal">DJ Lethal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Necro_(rapper)" title="Necro (rapper)">Necro</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a> and Ill Bill himself.
</p><p>During spring 2008, Ill Bill joined <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Wall" title="Paul Wall">Paul Wall</a> on a nationwide tour. <i>The Hour of Reprisal</i> was released on September 16, 2008, by Uncle Howie Records and Fat Beats.
</p>
<h3><span id="Kill_Devil_Hills_.282010.29_with_DJ_Muggs"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Kill_Devil_Hills_(2010)_with_DJ_Muggs"><i>Kill Devil Hills</i> (2010) with DJ Muggs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Kill Devil Hills (2010) with DJ Muggs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>DJ Muggs vs. Ill Bill: <a href="/wiki/Kill_Devil_Hills_(album)" title="Kill Devil Hills (album)">Kill Devil Hills</a></i> is a collaboration studio album by Ill Bill and producer <a href="/wiki/DJ_Muggs" title="DJ Muggs">DJ Muggs</a>. The album was released on August 31, 2010, on Fat Beats Records.<sup id="cite_ref-hiphopug.com_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopug.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> All songs were produced by DJ Muggs.
</p>
<h3><span id="The_Grimy_Awards_.282013.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Grimy_Awards_(2013)"><i>The Grimy Awards</i> (2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: The Grimy Awards (2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His third album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Grimy_Awards" title="The Grimy Awards">The Grimy Awards</a></i>, was released on January 29, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The album includes appearances from <a href="/wiki/H.R." title="H.R.">H.R.</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bad_Brains" title="Bad Brains">Bad Brains</a>, <a href="/wiki/A-Trak" title="A-Trak">A-Trak</a>, <a href="/wiki/El-P" title="El-P">El-P</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jedi_Mind_Tricks" title="Jedi Mind Tricks">Jedi Mind Tricks</a>, <a href="/wiki/O.C._(rapper)" title="O.C. (rapper)">O.C.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cormega" title="Cormega">Cormega</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meyhem_Lauren" title="Meyhem Lauren">Meyhem Lauren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Q-Unique" title="Q-Unique">Q-Unique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shabazz_the_Disciple" title="Shabazz the Disciple">Shabazz the Disciple</a> &amp; Lil Fame of <a href="/wiki/M.O.P." title="M.O.P.">M.O.P.</a> and production by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Premier" title="DJ Premier">DJ Premier</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Muggs" title="DJ Muggs">DJ Muggs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pete_Rock" title="Pete Rock">Pete Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Large_Professor" title="Large Professor">Large Professor</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Beatnuts" title="The Beatnuts">The Beatnuts</a>, <a href="/wiki/El-P" title="El-P">El-P</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ayatollah_(record_producer)" title="Ayatollah (record producer)">Ayatollah</a>, <a href="/wiki/MoSS" title="MoSS">MoSS</a> and Ill Bill himself. The first single, "Severed Heads of State", featuring and produced by El-P, came out on October 29, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="La_Bella_Medusa_.282020.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="La_Bella_Medusa_(2020)"><i>La Bella Medusa</i> (2020)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ill_Bill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: La Bella Medusa (2020)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>La Bella Medusa</i>, Ill Bill's fourth album, was released on October 30, 2020. Bill announced on social media that it had reached <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music" title="Apple Music">Apple Music</a>'s top ten for Hip Hop. The album was placed under releases by <a href="/wiki/Joyner_Lucas" title="Joyner Lucas">Joyner Lucas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trippie_Redd" title="Trippie Redd">Trippie Redd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a> on this chart. It included features from D.V. Alias Khryst, <a href="/wiki/Conway_the_Machine" title="Conway the Machine">Conway the Machine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharoahe_Monch" title="Pharoahe Monch">Pharoahe Monch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>, Rite Hook, Lord Goat, Recognize Ali, Jay Royale, <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slaine_(rapper)" title="Slaine (rapper)">Slaine</a>, Little Vic, Crimeapple, Nems, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Yayo" title="Tony Yayo">Tony Yayo</a> of <a href="/wiki/G-Unit" title="G-Unit">G-Unit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vinnie_Paz" title="Vinnie Paz">Vinnie Paz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sabac_Red" title="Sabac Red">Sabac Red</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
