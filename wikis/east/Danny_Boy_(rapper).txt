<p><b>Daniel O'Connor</b> (born December 12, 1968), better known as <b>Danny Boy</b> or <b>Danny Boy O'Connor</b>, is an American rapper, art director, and the executive director of <a href="/wiki/The_Outsiders_House_Museum" title="The Outsiders House Museum">The Outsiders House Museum</a>. O'Connor spent his childhood in New York, before moving to Los Angeles in the 1980s. In the 1990s, O'Connor co-founded the rap group <a href="/wiki/House_of_Pain" title="House of Pain">House of Pain</a>, with fellow rapper Erik Schrody (<a href="/wiki/Everlast" title="Everlast">Everlast</a>) and DJ Leor Dimant (<a href="/wiki/DJ_Lethal" title="DJ Lethal">DJ Lethal</a>). Based on their cultural heritage they fashioned themselves as rowdy Irish-American hooligans. O'Connor played the role of art director, designing logos, branding, hype man, and co-rapper. In 1992, with the singles "<a href="/wiki/Jump_Around" title="Jump Around">Jump Around</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Shamrocks_and_Shenanigans_(Boom_Shalock_Lock_Boom)" title="Shamrocks and Shenanigans (Boom Shalock Lock Boom)">Shamrocks and Shenanigans</a>", their <a href="/wiki/House_of_Pain_(album)" title="House of Pain (album)">self-titled debut album</a>, also known as <i>Fine Malt Lyrics</i>, went platinum.
</p><p>They followed it up with <i><a href="/wiki/Same_as_It_Ever_Was" title="Same as It Ever Was">Same as It Ever Was</a></i> (1994), which went gold, and <i><a href="/wiki/Truth_Crushed_to_Earth_Shall_Rise_Again" title="Truth Crushed to Earth Shall Rise Again">Truth Crushed to Earth Shall Rise Again</a></i> (1996), before they all went their separate ways. O'Connor took part in several music projects, and continued doing designs as freelance work. In 2006, O'Connor founded the rap group <a href="/wiki/La_Coka_Nostra" title="La Coka Nostra">La Coka Nostra</a> where he was joined by George Carroll (<a href="/wiki/Slaine_(rapper)" title="Slaine (rapper)">Slaine</a>), William Braunstein (<a href="/wiki/Ill_Bill" title="Ill Bill">Ill Bill</a>), Dimant, and Schrody (only for the first album). Together they have three releases: <i><a href="/wiki/A_Brand_You_Can_Trust" title="A Brand You Can Trust">A Brand You Can Trust</a></i> (2009),  <i><a href="/wiki/Masters_of_the_Dark_Arts" title="Masters of the Dark Arts">Masters of the Dark Arts</a></i> (2012) and <i><a href="/wiki/To_Thine_Own_Self_Be_True" title="To Thine Own Self Be True">To Thine Own Self Be True</a></i> (2016).
</p><p>In 2016, O'Connor, who is a lifelong fan of <a href="/wiki/S._E._Hinton" title="S. E. Hinton">S.&#160;E. Hinton</a>'s book <a href="/wiki/The_Outsiders_(novel)" title="The Outsiders (novel)"><i>The Outsiders</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Outsiders_(film)" title="The Outsiders (film)">its film adaptation</a> by <a href="/wiki/Francis_Ford_Coppola" title="Francis Ford Coppola">Francis Ford Coppola</a>, bought the house used in the film located in <a href="/wiki/Tulsa,_Oklahoma" title="Tulsa, Oklahoma">Tulsa, Oklahoma</a>. He turned it into a museum named <a href="/wiki/The_Outsiders_House_Museum" title="The Outsiders House Museum">The Outsiders House Museum</a>, that contains much of the book and film memorabilia. For his effort preserving a cultural landmark he received a key to the city.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Boy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>O'Connor was born in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York City, on December 12, 1968. His father was incarcerated when O’Connor was two months old. O’Connor and his mother moved to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> when he was six. When O’Connor was seventeen his father, a homeless alcoholic, was murdered by someone who poured gasoline on him to set him on fire. During that time O’Connor was in a gang involved in petty crimes, and he was placed on probation.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span id="1990.E2.80.931992:_Founding_House_of_Pain_and_breakthrough"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1990–1992:_Founding_House_of_Pain_and_breakthrough">1990–1992: Founding House of Pain and breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Boy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1990–1992: Founding House of Pain and breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1990, O'Connor got together with fellow rapper Erik Schrody (<a href="/wiki/Everlast" title="Everlast">Everlast</a>) who had just released a rap album called <i><a href="/wiki/Forever_Everlasting" title="Forever Everlasting">Forever Everlasting</a></i> (1990), that didn't have much success. O'Connor knew Schrody when he went to <a href="/wiki/William_Howard_Taft_High_School_(Los_Angeles,_California)" class="mw-redirect" title="William Howard Taft High School (Los Angeles, California)">William Howard Taft High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Woodland_Hills,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Woodland Hills, California">Woodland Hills, California</a> (1984–1986), and various hip hop event they attended in their teens. Both of Irish descent, they decided to make a hip hop group with this identity. While hanging out at O'Connor's home, Schrody noticed a cassette with the title <i>House of Pain</i>, which was a demo of a <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">Punk</a> group O'Connor tried to put together. Schrody really liked the name and felt they should re-use as their name. Schrody brought in his former DJ Leor Diamant (<a href="/wiki/DJ_Lethal" title="DJ Lethal">DJ Lethal</a>), who is actually of Latvian descent, and <a href="/wiki/House_of_Pain" title="House of Pain">House of pain</a> was created.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. The label they chose was <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Boy_Records" title="Tommy Boy Records">Tommy Boy Records</a>, who credits O'Connor's art work to have initially caught their attention.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span id="1997.E2.80.932018:_return_from_obscurity_and_back_to_prominence"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1997–2018:_return_from_obscurity_and_back_to_prominence">1997–2018: return from obscurity and back to prominence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Boy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1997–2018: return from obscurity and back to prominence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After House of Pain disbanded, O'Connor said he struggled with drug abuse, a problem he developed when he became famous, and spent all the money he made from the group's success.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> O'Connor explains that he tool notice of two up and comers George Carroll (<a href="/wiki/Slaine_(rapper)" title="Slaine (rapper)">Slaine</a>) and John Faster (Big Left) and brought them to meet his former DJ from House of Pain, Leor Diamant.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> They decided to make a group with an additional rapper, who didn't stay too long, that Diamant took under his wing, as well as O'Connor becoming its hype man and art director. The group started releasing music on <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">MySpace</a>, and went viral. Eventually, O'Connor felt that there was a void and asked experienced rapper William Braunstein (<a href="/wiki/Ill_Bill" title="Ill Bill">Ill Bill</a>) to join. The group's name came about, when O'Connor teased other members with that nickname, after they had a night out.  Eventually, former House of Pain colleague Erik Schrody, showed interest to join. By 2008, the group consisted of O'Connor, Carroll, Diamant, Braunstein, and Schrody (who left some time after the first album was released).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2005, the documentary film <i><a href="/wiki/Just_for_Kicks_(2005_film)" title="Just for Kicks (2005 film)">Just for Kicks</a></i> about the <a href="/wiki/Athletic_shoe" class="mw-redirect" title="Athletic shoe">sneaker</a> phenomena and history in hip-hop, had its world premiere.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> O'Connor was among the producers. It won "Best Documentary", and "Best Overall Film" at the USVI Film festival. It was part of the official selection at <a href="/wiki/Tribeca_Film_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Tribeca Film Festival">Tribeca Film Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Documentary_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield Documentary Festival">Sheffield Documentary Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/International_Documentary_Film_Festival_Amsterdam" title="International Documentary Film Festival Amsterdam">Amsterdam Documentary Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangkok_International_Film_Festival" title="Bangkok International Film Festival">Bangkok International Film Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/RESFest" title="RESFest">Res Fest</a>, New York Latino Film Festival, San Francisco Black Film Festival, NYC Urban World Festival, Leipzig Documentary Festival, and US Virgin Islands Film Festivals.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, La Coka Nostra released <i><a href="/wiki/A_Brand_You_Can_Trust" title="A Brand You Can Trust">A Brand You Can Trust</a></i> was released on July 14, 2009, on <a href="/wiki/Suburban_Noize_Records" title="Suburban Noize Records">Suburban Noize Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> It sold over 500,000 units.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> <i><a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a></i> gave four out of five stars.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Andrew Kameka of <i><a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a></i> wrote that "the album is a mostly solid effort and exactly what someone would expect from a supergroup of like-minded members known for high-energy music".<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Adam Kennedy of the <a href="/wiki/BBC_News" title="BBC News"><i>BBC</i></a> while praising some the moments of the album said "it’s a tantalising parting taste of potential capabilities, yet until they improve a customer satisfaction hit rate that barely troubles one in three tunes here".<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Steve Juon of <i>RapReviews</i> gave it a seven out of ten.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Thomas Quinlan of <i><a href="/wiki/Exclaim!" title="Exclaim!">Exclaim!</a></i> said "La Coka Nostra are an interesting collection of collaborators that live up to the hype".<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Also in 2009, House of Pain reunited at a private event held by <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Fighting_Championship" title="Ultimate Fighting Championship">UFC</a> president <a href="/wiki/Dana_White" title="Dana White">Dana White</a> in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> on <a href="/wiki/Saint_Patrick%27s_Day" title="Saint Patrick&#39;s Day">St. Patrick's Day</a>. It was officially announced on August 10, 2010, that House of Pain had reunited and performed their first "official" show in a decade at the second annual <a href="/wiki/Epicenter_(music_festival)" title="Epicenter (music festival)">Epicenter music festival</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fontana,_California" title="Fontana, California">Fontana, California</a>, on September 25, 2010. Though Diamant is still a member of the group, he did not join them on their 2011 reunion tour due to prior obligations with <a href="/wiki/Limp_Bizkit" title="Limp Bizkit">Limp Bizkit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> In April–May 2011 House of Pain attended the <a href="/wiki/Groovin%27_the_Moo" title="Groovin&#39; the Moo">Groovin' the Moo</a> touring festival in Australia, and also performed at Scotland's <a href="/wiki/T_in_the_Park" title="T in the Park">T in the Park</a> festival on July 9, 2011, as well as <a href="/wiki/Sonisphere_Festival#UK" title="Sonisphere Festival">Sonisphere UK Festival</a> on July 12 of the same year.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> House of Pain reunited in 2017 for a 25th Anniversary Tour. The tour included shows in DC, Ohio, Pennsylvania, New York, Massachusetts, and California.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Finally that year, while touring, they had a three day layover in <a href="/wiki/Tulsa,_Oklahoma" title="Tulsa, Oklahoma">Tulsa, Oklahoma</a>. O'Connor took advantage of this free time to visit the town and found the location of the house used by the main characters in the film<i><a href="/wiki/The_Outsiders_(film)" title="The Outsiders (film)">The Outsiders</a></i> (1983). He took a picture, posted it on MySpace, and the photo went viral.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> This led O'Connor to found <i>The Delta Bravo Urban Exploration Team.</i><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> With team-members in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, they visit documents notable pop culture landmarks from film, television, music, and true crime.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> One of their speciality is to show a photo of what the location looks like now to compared it to what it looked like when it was part of something that gained notoriety.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Delta Bravo identified locations from movies and television shows including <i><a href="/wiki/Fast_Times_at_Ridgemont_High" title="Fast Times at Ridgemont High">Fast Times at Ridgemont High</a></i> (1982), <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Fever" title="Saturday Night Fever">Saturday Night Fever</a></i> (1977), <a href="/wiki/Valley_Girl_(1983_film)" title="Valley Girl (1983 film)"><i>Valley Girl</i></a> (1983), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bad_News_Bears" title="The Bad News Bears">The Bad News Bears</a></i> (1976), <i><a href="/wiki/All_in_the_Family" title="All in the Family">All in the Family</a></i> (1971 to 1979), <i><a href="/wiki/Gilligan%27s_Island" title="Gilligan&#39;s Island">Gilligan's Island</a></i> (1964 to 1967), etc.<sup id="cite_ref-FromOutside_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FromOutside-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, separately from <i>The Delta Bravo Urban Exploration Team,</i> La Coka Nostra released their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Masters_of_the_Dark_Arts" title="Masters of the Dark Arts">Masters of the Dark Arts</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> It reached 176 on <i>Billboard</i> Top Current Albums, 40 on <a href="/wiki/Independent_Albums" title="Independent Albums">Independent Albums</a>, and 31 on Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Adam Fleischer of <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> magazine noted that "La Coka Nostra remain decidedly true to their core with their new album" and "that they are indeed masters of the dark arts". <i>HipHopDX</i> gave the album a positive review and noted that the album was a "sinister, happily violent detour from the pop-centric".<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Peter Marrack of <i>Exclaim!</i> also gave the album a positive review and noted that the album was "more or less a one-way ticket to hell".<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Nathan G. O'Brien of <i>Scene Point Blank</i> gave it four out five star and said "with Master of the Dark Arts La Coka Nostra’s pluperfect union of bombastic boom-bap, record scratching, and realism-based hardcore rhyming".<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, La Coka Nostra released <i><a href="/wiki/To_Thine_Own_Self_Be_True" title="To Thine Own Self Be True">To Thine Own Self Be True</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> The album reached 38 on the Billboard Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Steve Juon of <i>RapReviews</i> gave eight point five out of ten and wrote "for a blissful 45 minutes it's an uncut dose of that nostalgia straight through the ear canals to the dopamine centers of my brain".<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>That same year, the film <i><a href="/wiki/Let_Me_Make_You_a_Martyr" title="Let Me Make You a Martyr">Let Me Make You a Martyr</a></i> premiered,<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> in which O'Connor plays a role.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span id="2019.E2.80.93present:_The_Outsiders_House_museum_and_current_works"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–present:_The_Outsiders_House_museum_and_current_works">2019–present: The Outsiders House museum and current works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Boy_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2019–present: The Outsiders House museum and current works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>O'Connor, a lifelong fan of <a href="/wiki/S._E._Hinton" title="S. E. Hinton">S. E. Hinton</a>'s book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Outsiders_(novel)" title="The Outsiders (novel)">The Outsiders</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/The_Outsiders_(film)" title="The Outsiders (film)">its film adaptation</a>, opened <a href="/wiki/The_Outsiders_House_Museum" title="The Outsiders House Museum">The Outsiders House Museum</a> in Tulsa, Oklahoma, on August 9, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Since 2009, when discovering the house where the lead characters of the film, the Curtis Brothers, lived in <a href="/wiki/Tulsa,_Oklahoma" title="Tulsa, Oklahoma">Tulsa, Oklahoma</a>. O'Connor always had the thought of buying it, which he eventually did in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-:12_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:12-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> O'Connor said he bought it sight unseen and when he first went inside that it was falling apart. With the help of friends, the Oklahoma Film and Music Office, the City Council, local business and individuals who volunteered the restoration started.<sup id="cite_ref-:22_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:22-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tulsaworld.com_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tulsaworld.com-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> After raising funds the house went through extensive renovations to restore it and maintain its authenticity from the film.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/GoFundMe" title="GoFundMe">GoFundMe</a> was set up for additional funds,<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> notable donors include <a href="/wiki/Jack_White" title="Jack White">Jack White</a> who donated $30,000 and <a href="/wiki/Billy_Idol" title="Billy Idol">Billy Idol</a>. Also to raise funds, screenings of the film were organized that actor <a href="/wiki/C._Thomas_Howell" title="C. Thomas Howell">C. Thomas Howell</a> (one of the film's lead) attended. Also in 2016, the street signs on the corner were changed to "The Outsiders way" and "The Curtis Brothers Lane".<sup id="cite_ref-:12_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:12-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Since its opening, the museum now contains a collection of <i>The Outsiders</i> memorabilia.<sup id="cite_ref-FromOutside_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FromOutside-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On the <a href="/wiki/Quiz_show" class="mw-redirect" title="Quiz show">quiz show</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Jeopardy!" title="Jeopardy!">Jeopardy!</a></i> a question about him and the museum was showcased ''Rapper Danny Boy O'Connor loved the Tulsa house used in the film version of this author's The Outsiders so he restored it''.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> For his efforts on preserving a cultural landmark O'Connor received a key to the city of Tulsa.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 1 of that year, the institution installed a plaque commemorating in a scene <i><a href="/wiki/Rumble_Fish" title="Rumble Fish">Rumble Fish</a></i> (1983), another film directed by Coppola based on a Hinton novel shot in Tulsa, in the alley south of 5th Street between Main Street and Boston Avenue.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2021, separately from <i>The Delta Bravo Urban Exploration Team</i> and The Outsiders House museum, O'Connor acted in the suspense film <i><a href="/wiki/Ida_Red_(film)" title="Ida Red (film)">Ida Red</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> That same year he was a prominent interviewee in <a href="/wiki/Soleil_Moon_Frye" title="Soleil Moon Frye">Soleil Moon Frye</a>'s documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Kid_90" title="Kid 90">Kid 90</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 3, 2022, O'Connor released a photography exhibit named ''We saw the same sunset'' at Mother Road Market. It shows the photos of a thousand sunset taken from the same window on the thirteenth floor of the <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Hotel" title="Mayo Hotel">Mayo Hotel</a>. O'Connor said, “This exhibit is my love letter to Tulsa, when I moved from L.A. to Tulsa five years ago, I was awestruck by Tulsa’s sunsets. I love the idea that we all share the same sunset regardless of our differences, bringing us a step closer to each other.”<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> On June 10, the short documentary <i>Old House New Home</i>, where O'Connor is credited as an executive producer,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> premiered at the <a href="/wiki/DeadCENTER_Film_Festival" title="DeadCENTER Film Festival">DeadCENTER Film Festival</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-:6_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> where it won the best Okie short award.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> The documentary is about O'Connor's effort to preserve the house from <i>The Outsiders.</i><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> At the same festival the thriller film <i>Out for Exile</i> premiered.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_63-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> In it O'Connor plays a role.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2022, O'Connor with The Outsiders House Museum published the book <i>The Outsiders ‘Rare and Unseen’</i>, which contains 148 photos by <a href="/wiki/David_Burnett_(photojournalist)" title="David Burnett (photojournalist)">David Burnett</a> who was the set's photographer. O'Connor said: “We originally got the first lot of photos and then [Burnett] said there may be more. They found the rougher photos, and for me, that’s where the rubber meets the road because they’re unpolished, their guard’s down, they’re not posing".<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2023, O'Connor acted in two John Swab's films <i>Little Dixie,</i><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> and <i>One Day as Lion</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 14 of that year, as part of a 40th anniversary celebration of the release of <i>The Outsiders</i>, O'Connor installed a plaque commemorating the film which was installed at the Admiral Twin drive-In where a scene was shot.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Currently, O'Connor serves as a member of the Arts Commission of City of Tulsa. This came about when the Mayor searched for Tulsans willing willing to volunteer their time to City of Tulsa Authority, Board or Commission. The initiative is meant to have a citizen committees with a range of interests and their advice to develop future policies.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
