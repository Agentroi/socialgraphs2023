<p><b>Khadimou Rassoul Cheikh Fall</b> (born September 10, 1998), known professionally as <b>Sheck Wes</b>, is an  American rapper and singer. He is best known for his 2017 song "<a href="/wiki/Mo_Bamba_(song)" title="Mo Bamba (song)">Mo Bamba</a>", which became popular in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2018, Wes signed signed a joint record deal with <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cactus_Jack_Records" title="Cactus Jack Records">Cactus Jack</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a>, under the aegis of <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Wes has been regarded as a <a href="/wiki/One-hit_wonder" title="One-hit wonder">one-hit wonder</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheck_Wes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Khadimou Rassoul Cheikh Fall was born on September 10, 1998, in the <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> to parents of <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegalese</a> descent. Fall was raised in a Muslim household.<sup id="cite_ref-genius.com_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-genius.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> At age five, he and his mother, Ella Kingsland, moved to <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee" title="Milwaukee">Milwaukee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a>, where he lived for nine years. At 14, he moved back to New York.<sup id="cite_ref-MarcuzFader_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MarcuzFader-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Fall began making music at age 11, to relieve stress due to his troubled childhood as he was raised in a hostile environment. While in high school, basketball became a major extracurricular pursuit, but he attracted interest from a fashion talent scout, leading him to skip a playoff game to participate in the <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a> unveiling of the <a href="/wiki/Yeezy_Season" class="mw-redirect" title="Yeezy Season">Yeezy Season</a> 3 collection.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheck_Wes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2018.E2.80.932019:_Mudboy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–2019:_Mudboy">2018–2019: <i>Mudboy</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheck_Wes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2018–2019: Mudboy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 2, 2018, Wes signed to <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s <a href="/wiki/GOOD_Music" title="GOOD Music">GOOD Music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cactus_Jack_Records" title="Cactus Jack Records">Cactus Jack Records</a> label alongside Interscope.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Wes made a guest appearance on Scott's third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Astroworld_(album)" title="Astroworld (album)">Astroworld</a></i> on the song "<a href="/wiki/No_Bystanders" title="No Bystanders">No Bystanders</a>" alongside American rapper <a href="/wiki/Juice_Wrld" title="Juice Wrld">Juice Wrld</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On October 2, Wes announced his debut studio album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Mudboy_(album)" title="Mudboy (album)">Mudboy</a></i> which was released just three days after.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On March 19, Wes released a new single under the Cactus Jack label called YKTS.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2019.E2.80.932021:_Hell_2_Paradise"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–2021:_Hell_2_Paradise">2019–2021: <i>Hell 2 Paradise</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheck_Wes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2019–2021: Hell 2 Paradise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the release of <i>Mudboy</i>, Wes announced that his second studio album was in the works.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2019, Wes was part of the opening act for <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s concert in <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> alongside fellow American rapper <a href="/wiki/Logic_(rapper)" title="Logic (rapper)">Logic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On July 18, Wes released the singles "Sadio Mane",<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and "Losing My Mind".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The following day on July 19, he appeared on Chase B and <a href="/wiki/Young_Thug" title="Young Thug">Young Thug</a>'s song "Mayday". On October 31, he released the song "YKTS" which was accompanied by a music video. On December 27, Wes made a guest appearance on the Cactus Jack compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/JackBoys" title="JackBoys">JackBoys</a></i> on the song "<a href="/wiki/Gang_Gang_(JackBoys_and_Sheck_Wes_song)" title="Gang Gang (JackBoys and Sheck Wes song)">Gang Gang</a>" also featuring <a href="/wiki/Don_Toliver" title="Don Toliver">Don Toliver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travis_Scott" title="Travis Scott">Travis Scott</a>, and Luxury Tax 50.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 6, 2020, Wes released the single "Rich One Day" and released the single "#BeenBallin" on November 19, 2020.
</p><p>Wes released "GFU" with record label <a href="/wiki/YSL_Records" title="YSL Records">YSL Records</a> and American rapper Yak Gotti, featuring American rapper <a href="/wiki/Yung_Kayo" title="Yung Kayo">Yung Kayo</a>, which serves as the second single from the label's compilation album, <i><a href="/wiki/Slime_Language_2" title="Slime Language 2">Slime Language 2</a></i>, on January 29, 2021.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sheck_Wes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 31, 2019, Ridge Productions claimed Wes had refused to pay for the video for his forthcoming single "Mudboy". Ridge Productions posted the original edit of the video, with substituted audio mocking Wes and his crew for reneging on their agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2019, Wes was caught on video stalking his former partner, singer <a href="/wiki/Justine_Skye" title="Justine Skye">Justine Skye</a>. After Skye came out publicly about Wes abusing her, former classmates of Wes' high school stated that he did abuse other women before at school. In April 2019, he dated social media personality India Love. In June 2019, Love posted and deleted a picture of a black eye that hinted Wes abused her after their breakup. Later on in the year, they rekindled their relationship. In June 2020, they had a public dispute. Love even hinted that Wes abused her after she posted a picture with a black eye with the caption "This is why we are over Sheck, stop telling people I cheated on you". She later took the post down.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Wes was removed from an advertising campaign for <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a> in response to the allegations.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Skye was subsequently reported to have requested a <a href="/wiki/Restraining_order" title="Restraining order">restraining order</a> against Wes.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Wes has denied the allegations and no charges were ever brought forward.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Skye's one-time boyfriend, rapper <a href="/wiki/GoldLink" title="GoldLink">GoldLink</a>, has repeatedly insulted and criticized Wes publicly over the abuse allegations.<sup id="cite_ref-Complex_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 21, 2020, Wes was arrested on drug and gun charges in New York.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He was later released.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
