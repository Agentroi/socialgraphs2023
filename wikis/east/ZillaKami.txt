<p><b>Junius Donald Rogers</b>, professionally known as <b>ZillaKami</b>, is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> duo <a href="/wiki/City_Morgue" title="City Morgue">City Morgue</a> and a former songwriter for <a href="/wiki/6ix9ine" title="6ix9ine">6ix9ine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Darkweb_Insider_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Darkweb_Insider-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has been noted by publications such as <i><a href="/wiki/HotNewHipHop" title="HotNewHipHop">HotNewHipHop</a></i> as a pioneer of <a href="/wiki/Trap_metal" class="mw-redirect" title="Trap metal">trap metal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Rogers is the CEO of Dog Years Skate Club, a <a href="/wiki/Skateboarding" title="Skateboarding">skate</a> apparel and equipment brand.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ZillaKami&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rogers was born in <a href="/wiki/Bay_Shore,_New_York" title="Bay Shore, New York">Bay Shore, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-No_Jumper_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-No_Jumper-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In his teens, Rogers formed a <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk rock</a> band called Scud Got Quayle,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> with some of his friends from school, influenced by the sound of <a href="/wiki/Gorilla_Biscuits" title="Gorilla Biscuits">Gorilla Biscuits</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> His involvement in <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop music</a> began by <a href="/wiki/Ghostwriting" class="mw-redirect" title="Ghostwriting">ghostwriting</a> for fellow New York rapper <a href="/wiki/6ix9ine" title="6ix9ine">6ix9ine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Darkweb_Insider_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Darkweb_Insider-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> During this period, he wrote a song which he intended to feature two vocalists, to which 6ix9ine responded by telling him that he should feature on the track, which would eventually be released under the name "Yokai", and the pair followed up with by releasing another collaborative track called "Hellsing Station".<sup id="cite_ref-No_Jumper_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-No_Jumper-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> However, in August 2017, the pair had a falling out when Rogers posted a photo of 6ix9ine engaging in a sexual act with a girl that he claimed was thirteen,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and bringing to light his <a href="/wiki/6ix9ine#Child_sexual_performance_charges" title="6ix9ine">prior guilty plea to using a child in a sexual performance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Shinners13.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Shinners13.jpg/220px-Shinners13.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="220" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Shinners13.jpg/330px-Shinners13.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Shinners13.jpg/440px-Shinners13.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="500" /></a><figcaption>ZillaKami in 2017</figcaption></figure>
<p>On April 30, 2017, he released his debut EP "LifeIsAHorrorMovie", which he later took down due to him no longer feeling a connection with the music that it contained.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Soon after, ZillaKami got in contact with the son of the owner of the tattoo parlor that his older brother Righteous P worked at, who went by the stage name SosMula. A few days after his release from prison on drug charges, SosMula formed <a href="/wiki/City_Morgue" title="City Morgue">City Morgue</a> with ZillaKami.<sup id="cite_ref-No_Jumper_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-No_Jumper-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In August 2018, after numerous singles scattered throughout the couple years before, they released their debut EP as a group, titled "Be Patient".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 5, 2018, he featured on the track "Vengeance" from <a href="/wiki/Denzel_Curry" title="Denzel Curry">Denzel Curry</a>'s third album <a href="/wiki/Ta13oo" title="Ta13oo">Ta13oo</a>, which also featured <a href="/wiki/JPEGMafia" title="JPEGMafia">JPEGMafia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> which was followed, in the same month, by a feature on Lil Gnar's track "Man Down".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On October 12, 2018, <a href="/wiki/City_Morgue" title="City Morgue">City Morgue</a> released their debut album "<i>City Morgue Vol 1: Hell or High Water</i>",<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and soon after, their headline tour in support of it sold out.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On November 29, 2018, he featured on Powers Pleasant's track "Please Forgive", along with <a href="/wiki/Jay_IDK" class="mw-redirect" title="Jay IDK">Jay IDK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zombie_Juice" title="Zombie Juice">Zombie Juice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Denzel_Curry" title="Denzel Curry">Denzel Curry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> From July 24 to August 23, he joined <a href="/wiki/Suicideboys" title="Suicideboys">Suicideboys</a>' <i>Grey Day</i> tour as a part of City Morgue, along with <a href="/wiki/Turnstile_(band)" title="Turnstile (band)">Turnstile</a>, Denzel Curry, <a href="/wiki/Trash_Talk_(band)" title="Trash Talk (band)">Trash Talk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pouya_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pouya (rapper)">Pouya</a>, Germ, <a href="/wiki/Shoreline_Mafia" title="Shoreline Mafia">Shoreline Mafia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Night_Lovell" title="Night Lovell">Night Lovell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On December 13, 2019, City Morgue released their second album <i>City Morgue Vol 2: As Good as Dead</i>. The deluxe version of said album, featuring several new tracks, was released on May 15, 2020. On July 31, 2020, City Morgue released their mixtape <i>Toxic Boogaloo</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On October 15, 2021, City Morgue released their third studio album <i>City Morgue Volume 3: Bottom of the Barrel</i><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On September 17, 2021, Rogers released his debut solo album <a href="/wiki/DOG_BOY_(ZillaKami_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="DOG BOY (ZillaKami album)"><i>Dog Boy</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_influence">Musical style and influence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ZillaKami&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical style and influence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ZillaKami's music has been described as merging elements of <a href="/wiki/Hardcore_punk" title="Hardcore punk">hardcore punk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> with <a href="/wiki/Trap_music" title="Trap music">trap music</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> by incorporating electric guitars and guttural shouted vocals and aggressive lyrical themes.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-undergroundunderdogs_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-undergroundunderdogs-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> His lyrics often depict themes of extreme violence, death, <a href="/wiki/Sadomasochism" title="Sadomasochism">masochism</a> and drug-use.<sup id="cite_ref-undergroundunderdogs_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-undergroundunderdogs-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Complex_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> In an article by <a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex magazine</a>, writer Jacob Moore described his music as "the most merciless rap music since <a href="/wiki/Necro_(rapper)" title="Necro (rapper)">Necro</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-Complex_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Complex-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He cites his biggest influences as <a href="/wiki/Title_Fight" title="Title Fight">Title Fight</a>'s albums <i><a href="/wiki/Floral_Green" title="Floral Green">Floral Green</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Hyperview_(album)" title="Hyperview (album)">Hyperview</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">Radiohead</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/In_Rainbows" title="In Rainbows">In Rainbows</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(hip_hop_group)" title="Onyx (hip hop group)">Onyx</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-No_Jumper_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-No_Jumper-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
