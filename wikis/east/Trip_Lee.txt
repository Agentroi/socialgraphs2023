<p><b>William Lee Barefield III</b> (born December 17, 1987), better known by his stage name <b>Trip Lee</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Christian_hip_hop" title="Christian hip hop">Christian rapper</a> and singer. Signed to <a href="/wiki/Reach_Records" title="Reach Records">Reach Records</a>, he has recorded both as a solo artist and as a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/116_Clique" class="mw-redirect" title="116 Clique">116 Clique</a>. Originally from <a href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Dallas, Texas">Dallas, Texas</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-hhhdb_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hhhdb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> he serves as a young adult pastor at Concord Church. His third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Between_Two_Worlds_(Trip_Lee_album)" title="Between Two Worlds (Trip Lee album)">Between Two Worlds</a></i> (2010), was nominated for two Dove Awards and won the Stellar Award for Best Hip Hop Album in 2011.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trip_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trip_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>William Lee Barefield III was born on December 17, 1987, in <a href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Dallas, Texas">Dallas, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As a young boy, Lee had dreams of being a professional athlete or a famous rapper, and he began to hone his lyrical skills at the age of 12. "I rapped about random stuff… how hot I was, how many girls I could pull," Lee says.<sup id="cite_ref-reachrecords1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reachrecords1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> But upon giving his life to Christ, at age 14, his focus changed from fortune, fame, and women to serving and ministering the Gospel.
</p><p>Lee slowly recognized that all of his life should be centered around Christ. "One day, I just decided, I'm only doing Christian hip hop. Rappers like <a href="/wiki/The_Ambassador_(rapper)" title="The Ambassador (rapper)">Ambassador</a> and <a href="/wiki/Da%27_T.R.U.T.H." title="Da&#39; T.R.U.T.H.">Da' T.R.U.T.H.</a> helped provide a model for how I would begin to do that," he says.<sup id="cite_ref-reachrecords1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reachrecords1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He also had an immense desire to learn and teach the Word of God, so he became a leader in his youth group where he preached his first sermon at age 17.<sup id="cite_ref-reachrecords1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reachrecords1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2004, Lee met <a href="/wiki/Lecrae" title="Lecrae">Lecrae</a> at a concert; months later, after talking and building with each other, Lecrae began to disciple him. While still in high school, Lee was given the opportunity to pen a few devotionals for the <a href="/wiki/Reach_Records" title="Reach Records">Reach Records</a> website that dealt with theological matters and showcased his spiritual maturity.<sup id="cite_ref-reachrecords1_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reachrecords1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lee attended <a href="/wiki/Cairn_University" title="Cairn University">Cairn University</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Boyce_College" title="Boyce College">Boyce College</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> the undergraduate school of <a href="/wiki/Southern_Baptist_Theological_Seminary" title="Southern Baptist Theological Seminary">Southern Baptist Theological Seminary</a> in <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville, Kentucky</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-christianpost.com_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-christianpost.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trip_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Trip Lee signed with Reach Records, and his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/If_They_Only_Knew_(Trip_Lee_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="If They Only Knew (Trip Lee album)">If They Only Knew</a></i>, was released a few days after his high school graduation.<sup id="cite_ref-reachrecords1_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reachrecords1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His first full-length album introduced hip-hop fans to his distinct southern sound, unique delivery, and gospel-saturated lyrics. That same year, he was also featured on the Dove Award-nominated, <i><a href="/wiki/Jesus_Muzik" title="Jesus Muzik">Jesus Muzik</a></i> with label mate Lecrae.
</p><p>His second album, <i><a href="/wiki/20/20_(Trip_Lee_album)" title="20/20 (Trip Lee album)">20/20</a></i>, was released in 2008 and garnered much success, breaking into the Billboard Top 200 and reaching No. 11 on the Billboard Christian albums chart and No. 4 on the Gospel chart. Back with his third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Between_Two_Worlds_(Trip_Lee_album)" title="Between Two Worlds (Trip Lee album)">Between Two Worlds</a></i>, Lee offers a collection of reflections on life in a fallen world. His hope is that his reflections not only resonate with listeners, but also point to Jesus as the hero.
</p><p>Since his first album release, Lee has traveled the world doing concerts for thousands of listeners, and his last three summers have been spent on national and international tours with his Reach label mates. His Christ-centered hip-hop message has also built a platform for him among churches and other Christian organizations, which has led to numerous opportunities to preach and teach at conferences and other events.
</p><p>Lee released two new singles "Brag on My Lord" and "King Like Mine", the latter featuring Alex Medina, on March 29, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Trip Lee released his fourth full-length studio album, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Life_(Trip_Lee_album)" title="The Good Life (Trip Lee album)">The Good Life</a></i> on April 9, 2012. The album featured various notable Christian artists, such as <a href="/wiki/Sho_Baraka" title="Sho Baraka">Sho Baraka</a> (formerly signed with Reach Records), <a href="/wiki/J.R._(musician)" title="J.R. (musician)">JR</a>., Lecrae, J.Paul, <a href="/wiki/KB_(rapper)" title="KB (rapper)">KB</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andy_Mineo" title="Andy Mineo">Andy "C-Lite" Mineo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Needham" title="Jimmy Needham">Jimmy Needham</a>, <a href="/wiki/V._Rose" title="V. Rose">V. Rose</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Sims" title="Natalie Sims">Suzy Rock</a>, Jai, and Leah Smith.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> As a continuation to the album, Lee has worked with Moody Publishers and has authored a book titled <i>The Good Life</i>, to be released October 1, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 9, 2012, Lee announced he would be "stepping away" from music altogether.<sup id="cite_ref-away_from_music_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-away_from_music-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Unashamed 2012 tour with the Reach Records roster was his final tour before pursuing a career as an author and pastor.<sup id="cite_ref-away_from_music_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-away_from_music-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Primary,_secondary_and_tertiary_sources" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable secondary sources. (April 2020)">non-primary source needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Primary,_secondary_and_tertiary_sources" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable secondary sources. (April 2020)">non-primary source needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> He later clarified that while he would continue to make music, he wouldn't be touring as much.<sup id="cite_ref-bragonmylord.com_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bragonmylord.com-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He is currently training to be a pastor at his local church, while still engaging in public ministry.<sup id="cite_ref-bragonmylord.com_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bragonmylord.com-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 27, 2014, Trip Lee released his fifth studio album, <i>Rise</i>. On January 27, 2015, he released his second book called <i>Rise: Get Up and Live in God's Great Story</i> to accompany the album.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He was part of the Winter Jam Tour 2016 along with KB and <a href="/wiki/Tedashii" title="Tedashii">Tedashii</a> in the central and eastern parts of the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 9, 2016, Trip Lee released a mixtape titled, "The Waiting Room."<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The title was inspired by a line in the song, "Take Me There" from his, "The Good Life" album. He says the album highlights the fact that, as a Christian, his life is much like sitting in a waiting room, looking for what God has ahead.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 4, 2022, Trip Lee released his sixth studio album "The End." his first project in six years (first since 2016). The album released three singles Supernatural, Stone and Right Out The Gate. The album also featured artists WHATUPRG, Wande, Taylor Hill, KB, and Desi Raines.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ministry">Ministry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trip_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ministry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In early 2011, he did the pastoral internship at <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Hill_Baptist_Church" title="Capitol Hill Baptist Church">Capitol Hill Baptist Church</a> in Washington, D.C.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, he became pastoral assistant at <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Hill_Baptist_Church" title="Capitol Hill Baptist Church">Capitol Hill Baptist Church</a> in Washington, D.C.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, he became pastor at Cornerstone Church West End in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, he became young adult pastor at Concord Church <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, Texas.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trip_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

The couple have three children.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
