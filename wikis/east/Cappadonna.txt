<p><b>Darryl Hill</b>, (born September 18, 1968) better known by his stage name <b>Cappadonna</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a>. He is a member of the hip hop collective <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a> and is a member of the hip hop group <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Unit" title="Theodore Unit">Theodore Unit</a> together with <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cappadonna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cappadonna (also known as Cappachino) was known to the future members of the Wu-Tang Clan, and had been <a href="/wiki/U-God" title="U-God">U-God</a>'s mentor.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> However, Cappadonna went to prison and was replaced in the group by <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cappadonna made his recording debut as an affiliate of the Wu-Tang Clan on <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>'s 1995 single "<a href="/wiki/Ice_Cream_(Raekwon_song)" title="Ice Cream (Raekwon song)">Ice Cream</a>" on <i><a href="/wiki/Only_Built_4_Cuban_Linx..." title="Only Built 4 Cuban Linx...">Only Built 4 Cuban Linx...</a></i>. He was also a guest star, along with Raekwon, on <a href="/wiki/Ghostface_Killah" title="Ghostface Killah">Ghostface Killah</a>'s solo debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Ironman_(Ghostface_Killah_album)" title="Ironman (Ghostface Killah album)">Ironman</a></i>. He made his first appearance on a Wu-Tang Clan album in 1997 on <i><a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Forever" title="Wu-Tang Forever">Wu-Tang Forever</a></i> on the single "<a href="/wiki/Triumph_(Wu-Tang_Clan_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Triumph (Wu-Tang Clan song)">Triumph</a>". Following his appearance on <i>Forever</i>, he contributed significantly<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="vague (December 2018)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> to the group's third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_W" title="The W">The W</a></i>, at which point his appearances were no longer marked with "Featuring Cappadonna", as they had been on <i>Forever</i>. After a falling-out with <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a> the next year over royalties, he did not appear at all on the group's subsequent album <i><a href="/wiki/Iron_Flag" title="Iron Flag">Iron Flag</a></i>. He reunited with the group for their concert at 2004's <a href="/wiki/Rock_the_Bells" title="Rock the Bells">Rock the Bells</a>, and appeared on 2007 release <i><a href="/wiki/8_Diagrams" title="8 Diagrams">8 Diagrams</a></i>.
</p><p>Cappadonna's status as a Wu-Tang member or as a featured artist has varied throughout the years, while long referred to by both the group and fans as the "Unofficial Tenth Member". Also, while his vocals on <i>8 Diagrams</i> were not marked with "Feat. Cappadonna", much like <i>The W</i>, he does not appear on the front cover with the original eight living members, and is not featured in the album's booklet with a picture, although he is included in the group's shout-outs sections. In a press conference held October 2, 2014, at Warner Studios for the upcoming release of the Wu-Tang Clan's <i>A Better Tomorrow</i> album, RZA clarified any misunderstanding of Cappadonna's affiliation with Wu-Tang Clan, stating that Cappadonna has been an official member of Wu-Tang Clan since <i>8 Diagrams</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Wu_Tang_Status_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wu_Tang_Status-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cappadonna's first solo effort was 1998's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pillage" title="The Pillage">The Pillage</a></i> which debuted and peaked at number 3 on the charts and certified Gold. His follow-up - 2001's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Yin_and_the_Yang" title="The Yin and the Yang">The Ying and The Yang</a></i> - debuted at number 51 on the charts. Staying vigilant in his support of The Clan, he then featured on over 25 releases including classics from Raekwon, Method Man and Ghostface. 
</p><p>Known for his colorful wardrobe, he frequently refers to fashion designers and his large collection of boutique clothing in his raps.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2019 Cappadonna was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan:_Of_Mics_and_Men" title="Wu-Tang Clan: Of Mics and Men">Wu-Tang Clan: Of Mics and Men</a>,</i> a documentary in honor of the 25th anniversary of Wu-Tang Clan's debut album, <i>Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers).</i><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
