<p><b>Clifford Smith, Jr.</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (born March 2, 1971),<sup id="cite_ref-xxlmag_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlmag-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Method Man</b>, is an American rapper, record producer, and actor. He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_hip_hop" title="East Coast hip hop">East Coast hip hop</a> collective <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>, and is half of the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> duo <a href="/wiki/Method_Man_%26_Redman" title="Method Man &amp; Redman">Method Man &amp; Redman</a>. He took his stage name from the 1979 film <i><a href="/wiki/Method_Man_(film)" class="mw-redirect" title="Method Man (film)">Method Man</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 1996, Method Man won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Best_Rap_Performance_by_a_Duo_or_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group">Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group</a> for "<a href="/wiki/I%27ll_Be_There_for_You/You%27re_All_I_Need_to_Get_By" title="I&#39;ll Be There for You/You&#39;re All I Need to Get By">I'll Be There for You/You're All I Need to Get By</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a>, with whom he currently stars in <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Book_II:_Ghost" title="Power Book II: Ghost">Power Book II: Ghost</a></i>, a spin-off of <i><a href="/wiki/Power_(TV_series)" title="Power (TV series)">Power</a></i>.
</p><p>Method Man has appeared in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/187_(film)" class="mw-redirect" title="187 (film)">187</a></i> (1997),  <i><a href="/wiki/Belly_(film)" title="Belly (film)">Belly</a></i> (1998), <i><a href="/wiki/How_High" title="How High">How High</a></i> (2001), <i><a href="/wiki/Garden_State_(film)" title="Garden State (film)">Garden State</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wackness" title="The Wackness">The Wackness</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/Venom_(2005_film)" title="Venom (2005 film)">Venom</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Tails" title="Red Tails">Red Tails</a></i> (2012),  <i><a href="/wiki/Keanu_(film)" title="Keanu (film)">Keanu</a></i> (2016), and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cobbler_(2014_film)" title="The Cobbler (2014 film)">The Cobbler</a></i> (2014). He and frequent collaborator rapper <a href="/wiki/Redman_(rapper)" title="Redman (rapper)">Redman</a> co-starred on the short-lived <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> television sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Method_%26_Red" title="Method &amp; Red">Method &amp; Red</a></i>. He has also had recurring roles in three <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series, as <a href="/wiki/Tug_Daniels" class="mw-redirect" title="Tug Daniels">Tug Daniels</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Oz_(TV_series)" title="Oz (TV series)">Oz</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Cheese_Wagstaff" class="mw-redirect" title="Cheese Wagstaff">Melvin "Cheese" Wagstaff</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wire" title="The Wire">The Wire</a></i>, and Rodney in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Deuce_(TV_series)" title="The Deuce (TV series)">The Deuce</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Method Man also appeared in the <a href="/wiki/TBS_(American_TV_channel)" title="TBS (American TV channel)">TBS</a> comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_O.G." title="The Last O.G.">The Last O.G.</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born on March 2, 1971, in <a href="/wiki/Hempstead,_Long_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Hempstead, Long Island">Hempstead, Long Island</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Smith divided his childhood between his father's <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a> residence and his mother's home in the Park Hill section of <a href="/wiki/Clifton,_Staten_Island" title="Clifton, Staten Island">Clifton, Staten Island</a>, locally known as Killa Hill.<sup id="cite_ref-Method_Man_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Method_Man-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Growing up in Hempstead, Smith began playing <a href="/wiki/Lacrosse" title="Lacrosse">lacrosse</a> at a young age and continues to be a passionate supporter of the sport. He attended <a href="/wiki/New_Dorp_High_School" title="New Dorp High School">New Dorp High School</a>, where he became friends with <a href="/wiki/Remedy_(rapper)" title="Remedy (rapper)">Remedy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-newvoices2002_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newvoices2002-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He has two sisters, Terri and Missy.<sup id="cite_ref-Method_Man_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Method_Man-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1992.E2.80.931996:_Enter_the_Wu-Tang_.2836_Chambers.29_and_Tical"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1992–1996:_Enter_the_Wu-Tang_(36_Chambers)_and_Tical">1992–1996: <i>Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers)</i> and <i>Tical</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1992–1996: Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers) and Tical">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As Wu-Tang Clan ascended to hip hop stardom, Method Man was always one of the most visible members of the collective. He was one of only two members to get a solo song on the group's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Enter_the_Wu-Tang_(36_Chambers)" title="Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers)">Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers)</a></i> and he was the first to release a solo album under the Clan's unusual contract which allowed its members to release albums under any <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>. Method Man chose to sign with rap label <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a>, although <a href="/wiki/Elektra_Records" title="Elektra Records">Elektra Records</a> A&amp;R man <a href="/wiki/Dante_Ross" title="Dante Ross">Dante Ross</a> initially wanted to sign him around the same time Ross signed fellow group member <a href="/wiki/Ol%27_Dirty_Bastard" title="Ol&#39; Dirty Bastard">Ol' Dirty Bastard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Method Man's solo debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Tical_(album)" title="Tical (album)">Tical</a></i> (1994), was critically acclaimed and well received, entering the American charts at #4 and eventually selling in excess of one million copies. That album featured the hit single "All I Need", later remixed featuring Mary J. Blige, which won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy">Grammy</a> ("<a href="/wiki/You%27re_All_I_Need_to_Get_By" title="You&#39;re All I Need to Get By">I'll Be There for You/You're All I Need</a>"). During this time Method Man also became close friends with fellow New York City-based rapper <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a>, and was the only guest rapper featured on his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Ready_to_Die" title="Ready to Die">Ready to Die</a></i>. He was also featured on <a href="/wiki/Spice_1" title="Spice 1">Spice 1</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/AmeriKKKa%27s_Nightmare" title="AmeriKKKa&#39;s Nightmare">AmeriKKKa's Nightmare</a></i> on the track "Hard 2 Kill". In 1995, he was also featured on "Got the Flava" off <a href="/wiki/Showbiz_and_A.G." title="Showbiz and A.G.">Showbiz and A.G.</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Goodfellas_(Show_and_A.G._album)" title="Goodfellas (Show and A.G. album)">Goodfellas</a></i>. He also appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Batman_Forever_(soundtrack)" title="Batman Forever (soundtrack)">Batman Forever</a> soundtrack. His track, <a href="/wiki/The_Riddler_(song)" title="The Riddler (song)">The Riddler</a>, produced by <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a> included a video with clips from the film. In 1996, Method Man appeared on <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/All_Eyez_on_Me" title="All Eyez on Me">All Eyez on Me</a></i>, on the song "Got My Mind Made Up" alongside his rhyme partner Redman, the Dogg Pound (Daz and Kurupt) and Inspectah Deck, whose verse did not make the released album version (although his nickname "Rebel INS" can be heard as the song fades). He was also featured on Redman's 1996 album <i>Muddy Waters</i> on the track "Do What Ya Feel".
</p>
<h3><span id="1997.E2.80.931998:_Wu-Tang_Forever_and_Tical_2000:_Judgment_Day"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1997–1998:_Wu-Tang_Forever_and_Tical_2000:_Judgment_Day">1997–1998: <i>Wu-Tang Forever</i> and <i>Tical 2000: Judgment Day</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1997–1998: Wu-Tang Forever and Tical 2000: Judgment Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 3, 1997, the Wu-Tang Clan released their Grammy-nominated multiplatinum double CD <i><a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Forever" title="Wu-Tang Forever">Wu-Tang Forever</a></i>, the long-awaited follow up to <i>36 Chambers</i>. The album has sold over 2 million copies to date worldwide.
</p><p>In 1996, the movie, <a href="/wiki/Space_Jam" title="Space Jam">Space Jam</a> was released. Method Man, alongside <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/B-Real" title="B-Real">B-Real</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coolio" title="Coolio">Coolio</a> released a song from the Space Jam Soundtrack called, <a href="/wiki/Hit_%27Em_High_(The_Monstars%27_Anthem)" title="Hit &#39;Em High (The Monstars&#39; Anthem)">Hit 'em High</a>. Method Man would go on to feature on LL Cool J's, <a href="/wiki/4,_3,_2,_1_(LL_Cool_J_song)" title="4, 3, 2, 1 (LL Cool J song)">4,3,2,1</a> the following year.
</p><p>His second solo album was <i><a href="/wiki/Tical_2000:_Judgement_Day" title="Tical 2000: Judgement Day">Tical 2000: Judgement Day</a></i>, released in 1998, which was heavily influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Apocalypse" title="Apocalypse">apocalypse</a> theories surrounding the forthcoming end of the millennium, and which featured myriad guest appearances from his fellow Wu-Tang MCs. The album was certified double platinum. Other guest appearances include <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Lopes" title="Lisa Lopes">Lisa "Left Eye" Lopes</a>, <a href="/wiki/D%27Angelo" title="D&#39;Angelo">D'Angelo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Rock" title="Chris Rock">Chris Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Redman_(rapper)" title="Redman (rapper)">Redman</a>, and brief cameos from <a href="/wiki/Russell_Simmons" title="Russell Simmons">Russell Simmons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Don_%22Magic%22_Juan" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishop Don &quot;Magic&quot; Juan">Bishop Don "Magic" Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">Janet Jackson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The album sold better than his first fueled by the party track "Judgment Day" and the D'Angelo collaboration "Break Ups 2 Make Ups", earning Platinum and Gold certifications in the U.S. and Canada respectively. Reviews for the album were mixed and its long runtime and abundance of intermittent comedy skits were widely criticized. Producers on this album included <a href="/wiki/True_Master" title="True Master">True Master</a>, <a href="/wiki/4th_Disciple" title="4th Disciple">4th Disciple</a> and the <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a>.
</p><p>In 1997, Scottish rock band, <a href="/wiki/Texas_(band)" title="Texas (band)">Texas</a>, had found some success with their single, "<a href="/wiki/Say_What_You_Want" title="Say What You Want">Say What You Want</a>". In 1998, the band collaborated with Method Man and <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a> on a remix of the song titled "Say What You Want (All Day, Every Day)", with RZA producing. It was later included in the Greatest Hits album released by Texas.
</p>
<h3><span id="1999.E2.80.932001:_Blackout.21_.28with_Redman.29_and_WWF_Aggression"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1999–2001:_Blackout!_(with_Redman)_and_WWF_Aggression">1999–2001: <i>Blackout!</i> (with Redman) and WWF Aggression</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1999–2001: Blackout! (with Redman) and WWF Aggression">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Method Man was part of the very successful Hard Knock Life Tour with <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, Redman, <a href="/wiki/Ja_Rule" title="Ja Rule">Ja Rule</a>, and <a href="/wiki/DMX_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="DMX (rapper)">DMX</a>. During this tour, <a href="/wiki/Method_Man_%26_Redman" title="Method Man &amp; Redman">Method Man &amp; Redman</a> recorded <i><a href="/wiki/Blackout!_(Method_Man_%26_Redman_album)" title="Blackout! (Method Man &amp; Redman album)">Blackout!</a></i>, a light-hearted, bass-heavy, profanity-laced, party record with an <a href="/wiki/EPMD" title="EPMD">EPMD</a>-evoking emphasis on funky beats and the mischievous wit and cool flows and good rhythm of the two MCs. The album reached platinum status quickly, both in the U.S. and Canada, fueled by "Da Rockwilder", "Cereal Killa", "1, 2, 1, 2", "Tear It Off" and "Y.O.U." This album also featured three previously released tracks on which the two collaborated.
</p><p>Their success would lead the duo on to star in movies and TV shows, become product spokespersons and household names, but also associated them with marijuana use in the media. The most immediate results of their success was their co-starring roles in the major motion picture film <i><a href="/wiki/How_High" title="How High">How High</a></i>, their endorsement deal for <i><a href="/wiki/Right_Guard" title="Right Guard">Right Guard</a></i>, Redman's starring role in <i><a href="/wiki/Seed_of_Chucky" title="Seed of Chucky">Seed of Chucky</a></i> and a short-lived sitcom on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Fox Television">Fox Television</a> entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Method_%26_Red" title="Method &amp; Red">Method &amp; Red</a></i>.
</p><p>In 1999, Method Man featured on <a href="/wiki/Limp_Bizkit" title="Limp Bizkit">Limp Bizkit</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/N_2_Gether_Now" title="N 2 Gether Now">N 2 Gether Now</a>" from their album <i><a href="/wiki/Significant_Other_(album)" title="Significant Other (album)">Significant Other</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2000, Method Man performed Know Your Role which was included on the album titled, <a href="/wiki/WWF_Aggression" title="WWF Aggression">WWF Aggression</a>. The song is focused on the popularity of professional wrestler at the time <a href="/wiki/Dwayne_Johnson" title="Dwayne Johnson">Dwayne Johnson</a> whose wrestling name was 'The Rock' and the main line in the song is The Rock's most famous caption "Do you smell what The Rock is cookin'?".
</p>
<h3><span id="2001.E2.80.932004:_The_W.2C_Iron_Flag.2C_and_Tical_0:_The_Prequel"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2001–2004:_The_W,_Iron_Flag,_and_Tical_0:_The_Prequel">2001–2004: <i>The W</i>, <i>Iron Flag</i>, and <i>Tical 0: The Prequel</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2001–2004: The W, Iron Flag, and Tical 0: The Prequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Wu-Tang Clan released <i><a href="/wiki/The_W" title="The W">The W</a></i> on November 21, 2000, and <a href="/wiki/Iron_Flag" title="Iron Flag">Iron Flag</a> on December 18, 2001. <i>The W</i> received both critical and commercial success for the group, while <i>Iron Flag</i> did receive some but not to the effect of <i>The W</i>. The efforts earned two more platinum plaques for the Wu-Tang Clan.
</p><p>In 2003, Method Man criticized <a href="/wiki/Oli_%22Power%22_Grant" class="mw-redirect" title="Oli &quot;Power&quot; Grant">Oli "Power" Grant</a> and Mitchell "Divine" Diggs, the managers of the Wu-Tang Clan business.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (June 2015)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> "Number 1 on my shit list right now is Divine from Wu-Tang management.  He took something major from me that he had no intention of giving back."<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aside from the financial issues, Method Man was unhappy with the decision to bring Wu-Tang into the fashion world for a brief period of time with Wu-Wear, despite the brand being a major money-maker for the group. "When Wu-Wear started making shoes and sneakers and pants, it was shoddy material. I never rocked that shit."<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2004, Meth released his third solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Tical_0:_The_Prequel" title="Tical 0: The Prequel">Tical 0: The Prequel</a></i>, which featured the hit party single "<a href="/wiki/What%27s_Happenin%27" class="mw-redirect" title="What&#39;s Happenin&#39;">What's Happenin'</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a> and included guest appearances of pop-rap stars like <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy Elliott</a> and <a href="/wiki/P._Diddy" class="mw-redirect" title="P. Diddy">P. Diddy</a>. The album sold well and was certified <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" class="mw-redirect" title="Music recording sales certification">gold record</a> by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> but did not see the platinum success of his previous solo releases.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>P. Diddy was one of the executive producers for the album, although Meth later voiced his displeasure with the final product. "On the third LP, it was suggested (by Def Jam) to bring in Harve Pierre and P. Diddy. Who am I to argue? Puff knows how to sell some records. But that wasn't the direction to go in, and I know that."<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2006.E2.80.932007:_4:21..._The_Day_After"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2006–2007:_4:21..._The_Day_After">2006–2007: <i>4:21... The Day After</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2006–2007: 4:21... The Day After">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Wu_Tang_Clan_f7858007.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Wu_Tang_Clan_f7858007.jpg/220px-Wu_Tang_Clan_f7858007.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Wu_Tang_Clan_f7858007.jpg/330px-Wu_Tang_Clan_f7858007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Wu_Tang_Clan_f7858007.jpg/440px-Wu_Tang_Clan_f7858007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4368" data-file-height="2912" /></a><figcaption>Method Man at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Eurock%C3%A9ennes" title="Eurockéennes">Eurockéennes</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>Method Man's fourth album, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/4:21" class="mw-redirect" title="4:21">4:21: The Day After</a></i> was released in August 2006 with a star lineup of producers featuring <a href="/wiki/Havoc_(Mobb_Deep)" class="mw-redirect" title="Havoc (Mobb Deep)">Havoc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erick_Sermon" title="Erick Sermon">Erick Sermon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Storch" title="Scott Storch">Scott Storch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Allah_Mathematics" class="mw-redirect" title="Allah Mathematics">Allah Mathematics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mr._Porter" class="mw-redirect" title="Mr. Porter">Mr. Porter</a>, and, most importantly to Meth, RZA. This time around, a more focused Method Man went back to his hip hop roots and both hip hop fans and the media took notice. He did an interview on the ItsHipHop.Tv.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Despite this being one of Meth's strongest solo efforts to date, the album failed to do well commercially due to it having no single or video, which Method Man has held discontent towards his own label for. However, he toured strongly all over the world to promote the album, and appeared onstage with fellow Wu-Tang member <a href="/wiki/Inspectah_Deck" title="Inspectah Deck">Inspectah Deck</a>, as well as New York up and comers <a href="/wiki/Saigon_(rapper)" title="Saigon (rapper)">Saigon</a>, and Gat Murdah. Meth cited various reasons for the problems between him and his label, Def Jam. While he puts most of the blame on personal agendas in the Def Jam offices, Meth did take some blame, himself, for giving in to his record label.
</p><p>In early May 2007, Method Man's camp leaked the street single "New York New York" which became a popular track on the internet.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2007.E2.80.932010:_Blackout.21_2"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007–2010:_Blackout!_2">2007–2010: <i>Blackout! 2</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2007–2010: Blackout! 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 27, 2007, Redman confirmed on BET's <i>Rap City: Tha Bassment</i> that a sequel to <i>How High</i> is currently being written.
</p><p>In an April 10, 2007, <a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">Onion A.V. Club</a> interview,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>  Redman hinted that there would be a second collaborative album with Method Man, with work beginning in midsummer or early September.
</p><p>In early 2008, a remake of the Smooth da Hustler and Trigger tha Gambler classic <i>Broken Language</i> was released to the internet by the duo entitled <i>Broken Language 2008</i>, fueling rumors of a <i>Blackout!</i> sequel coming soon. This rumor was further fueled by the duo while performing in <a href="/wiki/Gainesville,_Florida" title="Gainesville, Florida">Gainesville, Florida</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Florida" title="University of Florida">University of Florida</a>. <i>Blackout! 2</i> was scheduled for a December 9, 2008, release but was pushed back to the first quarter of 2009, with a new release date of May 19, 2009. <a href="/wiki/Bun_B" title="Bun B">Bun B</a> confirmed that he guest starred on <i>Blackout! 2</i> – in April 2009, a single was released titled "City Lights", produced by Nasty Kutt<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Producers such as Erick Sermon, <a href="/wiki/Rockwilder" title="Rockwilder">Rockwilder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pete_Rock" title="Pete Rock">Pete Rock</a> also announced their presence on the album.
</p><p>The duo has finished their <i>Still High</i> tour with Termanalogy, the Alchemist, and Evidence of Dilated Peoples.
</p>
<h3><span id="2011.E2.80.93present:_The_Meth_Lab_and_Crystal_Meth"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–present:_The_Meth_Lab_and_Crystal_Meth">2011–present: <i>The Meth Lab</i> and <i>Crystal Meth</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2011–present: The Meth Lab and Crystal Meth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Wu-Tang_Clan_at_Budapest_Park_-_2015.07.07_(1).JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Wu-Tang_Clan_at_Budapest_Park_-_2015.07.07_%281%29.JPG/220px-Wu-Tang_Clan_at_Budapest_Park_-_2015.07.07_%281%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Wu-Tang_Clan_at_Budapest_Park_-_2015.07.07_%281%29.JPG/330px-Wu-Tang_Clan_at_Budapest_Park_-_2015.07.07_%281%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Wu-Tang_Clan_at_Budapest_Park_-_2015.07.07_%281%29.JPG/440px-Wu-Tang_Clan_at_Budapest_Park_-_2015.07.07_%281%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4320" data-file-height="3240" /></a><figcaption>Method Man at Budapest Park in 2015</figcaption></figure>
<p><i>Crystal Meth</i> is to be Method Man's upcoming fifth and final studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The album has no confirmed release date. The <i>Crystal Meth</i> album was first announced before Method Man began working on <a href="/wiki/Method_Man_and_Redman" class="mw-redirect" title="Method Man and Redman">his and Redman's</a> second collaboration album, <i><a href="/wiki/Blackout!_2" title="Blackout! 2">Blackout! 2</a></i>, during which time it was tentatively titled <i>The Crystal Method</i>. In the liner notes of that album, it was given a scheduled release date for 2009. The album, however, was further postponed until it was later mentioned in an MTV interview it would be released in early 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-MTV_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> At a concert on December 23, 2010, Method Man told the crowd to look for his album in March 2011. However, the album was postponed again.
</p><p>In an April 2011 interview with <i>The Come Up Show</i>, following a recent performance in Canada, Method Man replied to a question regarding his "weed-loving" image: "When you get older and you've got kids and your kids are going to school and you know [their] teachers...and they see how active you are and concerned [you are] with your kids' education or well being, it's hard to sit there and be taken seriously if people are always talking about he's always high...which is totally not the case", he said. "When I first came out, I was young, we were doing our thing, we smoked a lot...and we didn't care if the world knew. Now, I have to use more discretion because of my kids. This is not for me; everything I do is for them now, so I use a bit more discretion and I don't put weed as a forefront any more."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On October 5, 2011, a new single from Method Man, entitled "World Gone Sour (The Lost Kids)", was released on iTunes.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In July 2012, he confirmed that the album will come out in 2013 &amp; will be produced by RZA,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> although he has also stated that RZA would not entirely produce the album, but instead be just one of a few producers which includes RZA, Erick Sermon, Rockwilder, and some new younger producers such as Diggilo.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He also expressed a desire to work with <a href="/wiki/OFWGKTA" class="mw-redirect" title="OFWGKTA">Odd Future</a> frontman <a href="/wiki/Tyler,_The_Creator" class="mw-redirect" title="Tyler, The Creator">Tyler, The Creator</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Throughout 2013, Method Man worked on material for <i>Crystal Meth</i> and significantly worked on the Wu-Tang Clan's sixth studio album, <i>A Better Tomorrow</i>. He also toured with Redman throughout the year.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On January 1, 2014, Method Man announced that a mixtape titled <i>The Meth Lab</i> would be released in March 2014 and that <i>Crystal Meth</i> would be released in August 2014 on <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Boy_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Tommy Boy Entertainment">Tommy Boy Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> However, Method Man released <i><a href="/wiki/The_Meth_Lab" title="The Meth Lab">The Meth Lab</a></i> as a mixtape album<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> through Tommy Boy on August 21, 2015. In 2019 Method Man featured on the record 'Winnebago' with South London rapper Blue Meth. The music video was directed by <a href="/wiki/Nick_Donnelly" title="Nick Donnelly">Nick Donnelly</a><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Crystal Meth</i> was again said to drop in August 2016 but still hasn't been released as of 2023.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the late 1990s, Method Man began a career in acting. He has had recurring roles in critically acclaimed television shows such as <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Oz_(TV_series)" title="Oz (TV series)">Oz</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Tug_Daniels" class="mw-redirect" title="Tug Daniels">Tug Daniels</a>, HBO's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wire" title="The Wire">The Wire</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Prop_Joe" class="mw-redirect" title="Prop Joe">Prop Joe</a>'s nephew <a href="/wiki/Cheese_Wagstaff" class="mw-redirect" title="Cheese Wagstaff">Cheese</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Zone" title="The Twilight Zone">The Twilight Zone</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i>, in which Method Man portrays Drops, a wealthy Las Vegas party promoter who clashes with the CSI team (specifically investigator <a href="/wiki/Nick_Stokes" title="Nick Stokes">Nick Stokes</a>) in their investigations involving Drops' clubs or entourage, in the episodes "Poppin' Tags" (2006), "Big Shots" (2007), and "Drops Out" (2008).
</p><p>Method Man and Redman hosted a pilot on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> called <i>Stung</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Additionally, Method Man has made numerous appearances as himself on TV shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/Mind_of_Mencia" title="Mind of Mencia">Mind of Mencia</a></i><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Chappelle%27s_Show" title="Chappelle&#39;s Show">Chappelle's Show</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>His first prominent role came in with the film <i><a href="/wiki/Belly_(film)" title="Belly (film)">Belly</a></i> (1998), along with fellow rappers <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a> and DMX. Method Man has since added many credits to his name, including roles in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Garden_State_(film)" title="Garden State (film)">Garden State</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/One_Eight_Seven" title="One Eight Seven">One Eight Seven</a></i>, and many others, with starring roles in feature films such as <i>How High</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Soul_Plane" title="Soul Plane">Soul Plane</a></i>. Method Man had a cameo in the horror movie <i><a href="/wiki/Venom_(2005_film)" title="Venom (2005 film)">Venom</a></i> (2005), where he played a deputy who is killed shortly into the movie. On March 27, 2007, Redman confirmed on <a href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Entertainment Television">BET</a>'s show <i><a href="/wiki/Rap_City_(BET_program)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap City (BET program)">Rap City</a></i> that the sequel to <i><a href="/wiki/How_High" title="How High">How High</a></i> was being written; the script for <i><a href="/wiki/How_High_2" title="How High 2">How High 2</a></i> is being written by <a href="/wiki/Dustin_Lee_Abraham" title="Dustin Lee Abraham">Dustin Lee Abraham</a> of <i>CSI</i>, who also wrote the first movie. Method Man also appeared in the 2008 movies <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wackness" title="The Wackness">The Wackness</a></i> (2008) and <i><a href="/wiki/Meet_the_Spartans" title="Meet the Spartans">Meet the Spartans</a></i> (2008).
</p><p>Method Man stars in the <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order_SVU" class="mw-redirect" title="Law &amp; Order SVU">Law &amp; Order SVU</a></i> episode "Snitch" as the main antagonist. The episode was first broadcast December 4, 2007.
</p><p>Method Man appeared in the <i>Def Jam</i> series of video games. In <i><a href="/wiki/Def_Jam:_Fight_for_NY" title="Def Jam: Fight for NY">Fight for NY</a></i> he voiced Blaze, one of the main characters. In <i><a href="/wiki/Def_Jam:_Icon" title="Def Jam: Icon">Icon</a></i>, he voiced Gooch, a major character in the storyline. In <i>Underground</i>, he voiced Meth, one of the major characters.
</p><p>He made a guest appearance in the music video for "<a href="/wiki/If_I_Ain%27t_Got_You" title="If I Ain&#39;t Got You">If I Ain't Got You</a>" (2003) by <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a>, where he played the role of her boyfriend. He also appeared in <a href="/wiki/Beanie_Sigel" title="Beanie Sigel">Beanie Sigel</a>'s music video "Feel It in the Air", where Method Man played an undercover cop leading an operation against Sigel.
</p><p>Method Man played the main antagonist, an arsonist wealthy executive, in an episode of the FOX TV show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Guys_(2010_TV_series)" title="The Good Guys (2010 TV series)">The Good Guys</a></i>.
</p><p>Method Man appears as Valentine, a hip hop business mogul, in the episode <i>Bad Blood</i> in season 2 of <i><a href="/wiki/Burn_Notice" title="Burn Notice">Burn Notice</a></i>.
</p><p>Method Man had a cameo appearance in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Cop_Land" title="Cop Land">Cop Land</a></i> (1997), as a violent criminal who, while fleeing, throws <a href="/wiki/Peter_Berg" title="Peter Berg">Peter Berg</a>'s character off of a New York rooftop. Method Man has also appeared in the TV drama <i><a href="/wiki/Wonderland_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Wonderland (U.S. TV series)">Wonderland</a></i>, as a patient in a mental hospital.
</p><p>Method Man plays a small role in the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sitter" title="The Sitter">The Sitter</a></i> (2011), starring <a href="/wiki/Jonah_Hill" title="Jonah Hill">Jonah Hill</a>. He also played crewman "Sticks" in the <a href="/wiki/George_Lucas" title="George Lucas">George Lucas</a> movie <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Tails" title="Red Tails">Red Tails</a></i> (2012), about the <a href="/wiki/Tuskegee_Airmen" title="Tuskegee Airmen">Tuskegee Airmen</a>.
</p><p>Method Man plays the leads in the films <i>The Mortician</i> (2011) and <i>#Lucky Number</i> (2015).<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, he voiced Phantasm, the primary antagonist on the FX animated comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Chozen" title="Chozen">Chozen</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2016, he played himself in <i><a href="/wiki/Difficult_People" title="Difficult People">Difficult People</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Luke_Cage_(TV_series)" title="Luke Cage (TV series)">Luke Cage</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Paterson_(film)" title="Paterson (film)">Paterson</a></i>. Also that year, he portrayed "Cheddar" in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Keanu_(film)" title="Keanu (film)">Keanu</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2017, he became the host of the TBS series <i><a href="/wiki/Drop_the_Mic" title="Drop the Mic">Drop the Mic</a></i>, based on the popular recurring segment on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Late_Late_Show_with_James_Corden" title="The Late Late Show with James Corden">The Late Late Show with James Corden</a></i>.
</p><p>Method Man played a recurring character in the HBO show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Deuce_(TV_series)" title="The Deuce (TV series)">The Deuce</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Method Man voiced <a href="/wiki/Ben_Urich" title="Ben Urich">Ben Urich</a> in the scripted podcast <i><a href="/wiki/Marvels" title="Marvels">Marvels</a></i>. He also appeared in <a href="/wiki/Shaft_(2019_film)" title="Shaft (2019 film)">Shaft</a> as Freddie P, an acquaintance of John Shaft (<a href="/wiki/Samuel_L._Jackson" title="Samuel L. Jackson">Samuel L. Jackson</a>).
</p><p>In 2020, Method Man appeared as a recurring character in the Netflix original series <i><a href="/wiki/Teenage_Bounty_Hunters" title="Teenage Bounty Hunters">Teenage Bounty Hunters</a></i> as Terrance Coin, Bowser's rival bounty hunter.
</p><p>He also starred in the 2020 film <i><a href="/wiki/Concrete_Cowboy" title="Concrete Cowboy">Concrete Cowboy</a></i>, an adaptation of the book Ghetto Cowboy.
</p><p>In 2020, Method Man also starred as Father Jackson in Netflix film, Vampires vs. the Bronx.
</p><p>He also currently stars in the current <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>-produced series <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Book_II:_Ghost" title="Power Book II: Ghost">Power Book II: Ghost</a></i> as Davis McClean, Tasha's Lawyer.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On May 17, 2007, Method Man was arrested in New York City for marijuana possession. His SUV was pulled over near the Battery Tunnel toll booths for having an expired inspection sticker. An unidentified source said, "It was like something out of <i><a href="/wiki/Cheech_%26_Chong" title="Cheech &amp; Chong">Cheech &amp; Chong</a></i>. He rolls down the window and the smoke would choke a horse."<sup id="cite_ref-nydailynews_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nydailynews-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The arresting officer noticed two <a href="/wiki/Blunt_(cigar)" title="Blunt (cigar)">blunts</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Plastic_bag" title="Plastic bag">plastic bag</a> containing marijuana in plain view. Upon further inspection more marijuana was found under the driver's seat. Method Man was charged with unlawful possession of marijuana, criminal possession of marijuana, <a href="/wiki/DWI" class="mw-redirect" title="DWI">DWI</a>, and driving an uninspected motor vehicle.<sup id="cite_ref-nydailynews_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nydailynews-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> He reached a plea agreement to perform community service including rapping to children about the dangers of drugs.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 5, 2009, Method Man was arrested at his home in Staten Island for income tax violations.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> He was accused of failing to file income tax returns for the state of New York between 2004 and 2007, and owed nearly $33,000 in taxes.  On June 28, 2010,<sup id="cite_ref-klmnb_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-klmnb-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Method Man pleaded guilty to tax evasion and was sentenced to a <a href="/wiki/Discharge_(sentencing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Discharge (sentencing)">conditional discharge</a><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> and paid a $106,000 fine.<sup id="cite_ref-klmnb_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-klmnb-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wendy_Williams">Wendy Williams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Wendy Williams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, Method Man had a personal and publicized conflict with TV talk show host <a href="/wiki/Wendy_Williams" title="Wendy Williams">Wendy Williams</a> on <i>The Breakdown</i>, an Internet show on Onloq.com.<sup id="cite_ref-onloq.com_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-onloq.com-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Williams talked on air about Method Man's wife's having cancer, which was something he wanted to keep private and her own family members had not yet known about. He said that people who lived next door to him did not know, but Williams had dug it up and made it public over the radio. Because of this leaked information, Method Man and his wife had many problems.<sup id="cite_ref-onloq.com_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-onloq.com-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Williams also reported rumors that Method Man had been having an affair with his wife's doctor. Method Man first heard of this while in recording sessions in Los Angeles.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fox_Television">Fox Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Method_Man&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Fox Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, Method Man starred in a Fox sitcom called <i>Method &amp; Red</i>. However, after only a short time on the air, the show was put on hiatus and never returned. Method Man later spoke to the press about Fox's influence on the show's style, saying that "there's been too much compromise on our side and not enough on their side", and criticizing the network's decision to add a laugh track. Before the show premiered, he was telling fans not to bother watching it. He told the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i>:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>This is frustrating for me. I'm trying to keep this show ghetto, and there's a way for it to be both ghetto and intelligent. But it's not going that way.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
