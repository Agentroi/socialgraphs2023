<p><b>Curtis Brown</b> (born April 18, 1960), better known by the stage name <b>Grandmaster Caz</b> or <b>Casanova Fly</b>, is an American rapper, songwriter, and DJ. He was a member of the hip hop group <a href="/wiki/The_Cold_Crush_Brothers" title="The Cold Crush Brothers">The Cold Crush Brothers</a> from 1979 to the mid-1980s. He is best known as the (uncredited) main writer of <a href="/wiki/Big_Bank_Hank" title="Big Bank Hank">Big Bank Hank</a>'s raps on the seminal 1979 <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> single by <a href="/wiki/The_Sugarhill_Gang" title="The Sugarhill Gang">The Sugarhill Gang</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Rapper%27s_Delight" title="Rapper&#39;s Delight">Rapper's Delight</a>".
</p><p>He worked with Debra Harris as a celebrity tour guide for <a href="/wiki/Hush_Hip_Hop_Tours" class="mw-redirect" title="Hush Hip Hop Tours">Hush Hip Hop Tours</a>, a hip-hop cultural sightseeing tour company in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, and is a board member of <a href="/wiki/The_Kennedy_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="The Kennedy Center">The Kennedy Center</a>'s Hip-Hop Council, Hip-Hop Ambassador and board member for Windows of HipHop and CEO of GMC Entertainment Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grandmaster_Caz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Caz first encountered rap in 1974 at a <a href="/wiki/Kool_Herc" class="mw-redirect" title="Kool Herc">Kool Herc</a> block party.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  Shortly after, he teamed with <a href="/wiki/DJ_Disco_Wiz" title="DJ Disco Wiz">DJ Disco Wiz</a> under the name Casanova Fly to form one of the first DJ crews, Mighty Force.  Caz was also the first rapper to perform both DJ (record) and MC (vocal) duties.<sup id="cite_ref-hess_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hess-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the late 1970s, he joined <a href="/wiki/The_Cold_Crush_Brothers" title="The Cold Crush Brothers">The Cold Crush Brothers</a>. Caz admits that he himself stole new equipment during the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_blackout_of_1977" title="New York City blackout of 1977">New York City blackout of 1977</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Slate_podcast_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Slate_podcast-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, Caz was featured on the single "<a href="/wiki/Downtown_(Macklemore_%26_Ryan_Lewis_song)" title="Downtown (Macklemore &amp; Ryan Lewis song)">Downtown</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Macklemore_and_Ryan_Lewis" class="mw-redirect" title="Macklemore and Ryan Lewis">Macklemore and Ryan Lewis</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sugarhill_Gang_controversy">Sugarhill Gang controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grandmaster_Caz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sugarhill Gang controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Grandmaster Caz is generally acknowledged to have been the main writer of the lyrics of <a href="/wiki/Big_Bank_Hank" title="Big Bank Hank">Big Bank Hank</a> (real name Henry Lee Jackson) on the 1979 <a href="/wiki/The_Sugarhill_Gang" title="The Sugarhill Gang">The Sugarhill Gang</a> single "<a href="/wiki/Rapper%27s_Delight" title="Rapper&#39;s Delight">Rapper's Delight</a>", which sold around 5 million copies<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and introduced hip hop music to the mainstream.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Jackson had been Grandmaster Caz's manager, and was working at a pizza parlor when music executive <a href="/wiki/Sylvia_Robinson" title="Sylvia Robinson">Sylvia Robinson</a> overheard him rapping Grandmaster Caz's lyrics, and asked him to be part of the group she was forming, The Sugarhill Gang. Jackson had never rapped before, and asked Caz for lyrics to use. This is most evident in his opening verse, when, instead of introducing himself as Big Bank Hank, he raps, "Check it out, I'm the C-A-S-A-, N-O-V-A, and the rest is F-L-Y". (Casanova Fly is Grandmaster Caz's alternate moniker.)<sup id="cite_ref-wapo_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wapo-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Big Bank Hank's verse in the song about seducing <a href="/wiki/Lois_Lane" title="Lois Lane">Lois Lane</a> away from <a href="/wiki/Superman" title="Superman">Superman</a> was also lifted from Caz.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2000, Caz released the song "MC Delight", a parody of "Rapper's Delight" which told his side of the story.<sup id="cite_ref-wapo_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wapo-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In the song, Caz states about giving away his lyrics, "I gave it to him thinking/ Check books, credit cards, more money/ Than a sucker could ever spend/ But he never gave a nigga a god damn dime/ And was supposed to be my friend".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grandmaster_Caz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He currently hosts Hush Hip Hop Tours, the official sightseeing tour of Harlem and The Bronx.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Caz was a prominent feature in Ice-T's 2012 documentary "<a href="/wiki/Something_from_Nothing:_The_Art_of_Rap" title="Something from Nothing: The Art of Rap">Something from Nothing: The Art of Rap</a>".
</p><p>Caz was interviewed for the 2004 documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Just_to_Get_a_Rep" title="Just to Get a Rep">Just to Get a Rep</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2008, he was one of the participants at the <a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">Cornell University</a> Library conference on Hip Hop.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2021, Grandmaster Caz and female MC <a href="/wiki/Sha-Rock" title="Sha-Rock">Sha-Rock</a> started co-hosting the show <i>That's The Joint</i> on the <a href="/wiki/Sirius_XM" title="Sirius XM">Sirius XM</a> channel Rock The Bells Radio, run by <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>. The show runs Monday through Friday, 10 AM to 1 PM.
</p><p>At present Grandmaster Caz is working with and for A&amp;E, MTV, Paramount, De La Calle, and the History Channel.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_honors">Awards and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grandmaster_Caz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1998, Caz was listed #11 out of Blaze Magazine's Top 50 MCs of all Time. He was also inducted into the Technics DJ Hall of Fame in 1999.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In June 2008, Grandmaster Caz was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Bronx_Walk_of_Fame" title="Bronx Walk of Fame">Bronx Walk of Fame</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> A street plaque bearing his name is now on permanent display on the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Concourse_(Bronx)" title="Grand Concourse (Bronx)">Grand Concourse</a>, the most famous thoroughfare in the Bronx.
</p><p>On April 18, 2022, Grandmaster Caz's birthday, newly-appointed <a href="/wiki/Bronx_Borough_President" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronx Borough President">Bronx Borough President</a> <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Gibson" title="Vanessa Gibson">Vanessa Gibson</a> proclaimed April 18 "Grandmaster Caz Day".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grandmaster_Caz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Artists who cite Grandmaster Caz as an influence include <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rakim" title="Rakim">Rakim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Grandmaster Caz was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Jaleel_White" title="Jaleel White">Jaleel White</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Drunk_History" title="Drunk History">Drunk History</a></i> episode "American Music".
</p><p>In episode 3 of part 2 of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Get_Down" title="The Get Down">The Get Down</a></i>, while listening to "Rapper's Delight" on the radio, The Get Down Brothers' member, Boo, tells his date that Grandmaster Caz is the real writer of the line and says that Caz is the "nicest MC around".
</p>
