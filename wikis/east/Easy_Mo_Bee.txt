<p><b>Osten S. Harvey Jr.</b> (born December 8, 1965), better known by his stage name <b>Easy Mo Bee</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a> and <a href="/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJ</a>, known for his production work for artists such as <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miles_Davis" title="Miles Davis">Miles Davis</a>, as well as his affiliation with <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy Records</a> in its early years, and his production involvement in <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a>'s debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Ready_to_Die" title="Ready to Die">Ready to Die</a></i>.  He also produced two songs on <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">2Pac</a>'s album, <i><a href="/wiki/Me_Against_the_World" title="Me Against the World">Me Against the World</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easy_Mo_Bee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easy_Mo_Bee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Easy Mo Bee was born in <a href="/wiki/Bedford%E2%80%93Stuyvesant,_Brooklyn" title="Bedford–Stuyvesant, Brooklyn">Bedford–Stuyvesant, Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, and raised in the neighborhood's Lafayette Gardens projects.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He began producing after hearing music by <a href="/wiki/Ced_Gee" class="mw-redirect" title="Ced Gee">Ced Gee</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ultramagnetic_MCs" title="Ultramagnetic MCs">Ultramagnetic MCs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marley_Marl" title="Marley Marl">Marley Marl</a>, producer of early hip-hop hits for the <a href="/wiki/Juice_Crew" title="Juice Crew">Juice Crew</a> and <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His first production placement came on Big Daddy Kane's breakthrough album, <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_a_Big_Daddy_Thing" title="It&#39;s a Big Daddy Thing">It's a Big Daddy Thing</a></i>, after which he was approached to work with another <a href="/wiki/Cold_Chillin%27_Records" title="Cold Chillin&#39; Records">Cold Chillin' Records</a> artist, The Genius — an early alias for <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a> co-founder <a href="/wiki/GZA" title="GZA">GZA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Mo Bee produced the majority of the rapper's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Words_from_the_Genius" title="Words from the Genius">Words From the Genius</a></i>, as well as produced "Sexcapades", a track that featured on the B-side of fellow future Wu-Tang co-founder <a href="/wiki/RZA" title="RZA">RZA</a>'s first single, "<a href="/wiki/Ooh_I_Love_You_Rakeem" title="Ooh I Love You Rakeem">Ooh I Love You Rakeem</a>", which the rapper/producer released under the alias Prince Rakeem.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Around that same time, Mo Bee had a group with neighborhood friends A.B. Money and J.R. called <b>Rappin' Is Fundamental</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  The trio released only one album on <a href="/wiki/A%26M_Records" title="A&amp;M Records">A&amp;M Records</a> in 1991: <i>The Doo-Hop Legacy</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Jazz pioneer <a href="/wiki/Miles_Davis" title="Miles Davis">Miles Davis</a> approached the young producer to help fuse jazz and hip-hop. These sessions would become his last studio album, 1992's <i><a href="/wiki/Doo-Bop" title="Doo-Bop">Doo-Bop</a></i>. The project, released posthumously after Davis died during the recording process, leaving the project unfinished, garnered generally mixed reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easy_Mo_Bee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mo Bee first linked up with <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Sean Combs</a>' <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="Bad Boy Entertainment">Bad Boy Entertainment</a> in 1993, when he produced the first single for Combs' up-and-coming artist, the <a href="/wiki/Notorious_B.I.G." class="mw-redirect" title="Notorious B.I.G.">Notorious B.I.G.</a>, "Party and Bullshit".<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Easy also went on to produce much of the label's two flagship releases: <i><a href="/wiki/Project_Funk_Da_World" class="mw-redirect" title="Project Funk Da World">Project: Funk da World</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Craig_Mack" title="Craig Mack">Craig Mack</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Ready_to_Die" title="Ready to Die">Ready to Die</a></i> by B.I.G.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, Mo Bee produced the "Flava in Ya Ear (Remix)," a driving single for both projects, featuring Craig Mack, Biggie, <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, Rampage and <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1994 and 1995, Mo Bee was also associated with <a href="/wiki/2Pac" class="mw-redirect" title="2Pac">2Pac</a>, having produced songs for both, including one called "<a href="/wiki/Runnin%27_(From_tha_Police)" class="mw-redirect" title="Runnin&#39; (From tha Police)">Runnin' From tha Police</a>," featuring both Pac and B.I.G. as well as rapper/producer Stretch and 2Pac's crew <a href="/wiki/Dramacydal" class="mw-redirect" title="Dramacydal">Dramacydal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In addition to featuring 2Pac and B.I.G. on the same record, the song is notable for inventive production techniques he described in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a></i>. "There’s a bassline in the original version. Go back and listen to that record. I played the bassline live all the way through that record from the <a href="/wiki/E-mu_SP-1200" title="E-mu SP-1200">SP-1200</a> through multi-pitch. It was like a bass guitar strumming, and if I messed up, it was like 'Yo bring it back, and plug me in.'"<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mo Bee went on to produce two songs for Pac's 1995 album <i><a href="/wiki/Me_Against_the_World" title="Me Against the World">Me Against the World</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> although the two recorded several other songs that did not make the cut.<sup id="cite_ref-allhiphop_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allhiphop-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> During this time period, he also crafted moderate radio hits for the <a href="/wiki/Lost_Boyz" title="Lost Boyz">Lost Boyz</a> ("Jeeps, Lex Coups, Bimaz &amp; Benz"); <a href="/wiki/Das_EFX" title="Das EFX">Das EFX</a> ("Microphone Master"); and <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a> ("Everything Remains Raw").<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career">Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Easy_Mo_Bee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1997, Mo Bee produced for Biggie's double-disc album, <i><a href="/wiki/Life_After_Death" title="Life After Death">Life After Death</a></i>. The producer crafted two songs, "I Love the Dough" and "<a href="/wiki/Going_Back_to_Cali_(The_Notorious_B.I.G._song)" title="Going Back to Cali (The Notorious B.I.G. song)">Going Back to Cali</a>";<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> these songs would mark the last time Easy would produce for Bad Boy.
</p><p>In 2000, he put out an album called <i>Now or Never: Oddysey 2000</i>, featuring East Coast staples <a href="/wiki/Busta_Rhymes" title="Busta Rhymes">Busta Rhymes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raekwon" title="Raekwon">Raekwon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prodigy_(rapper)" title="Prodigy (rapper)">Prodigy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smif-N-Wessun" title="Smif-N-Wessun">Smif-N-Wessun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sauce_Money" title="Sauce Money">Sauce Money</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Goodie_Mob" title="Goodie Mob">Goodie Mob</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a>. Over the next decade he would craft songs for <a href="/wiki/Big_Daddy_Kane" title="Big Daddy Kane">Big Daddy Kane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ras_Kass" title="Ras Kass">Ras Kass</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mos_Def" title="Mos Def">Mos Def</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_Rob" title="Black Rob">Black Rob</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Price" title="Sean Price">Sean Price</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a> and others, eventually winning a Grammy for his work with <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a> on her album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Diary_of_Alicia_Keys" title="The Diary of Alicia Keys">The Diary of Alicia Keys</a></i> (2003).<sup id="cite_ref-allhiphop_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allhiphop-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
