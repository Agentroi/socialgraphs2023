<p><b>David Brown Williams</b> (born 1983), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Roscoe</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a> rapper.
</p><p>He released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Roscoe_Philaphornia" title="Young Roscoe Philaphornia">Young Roscoe Philaphornia</a></i>, in 2003, with the album debuting at #148 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-200Chart_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-200Chart-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roscoe_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Roscoe was initially introduced to hip hop by his older brother <a href="/wiki/Kurupt" title="Kurupt">Kurupt</a>, who, at that time, was signed to <a href="/wiki/Death_Row_Records" title="Death Row Records">Death Row Records</a> and working on his debut album with <a href="/wiki/Tha_Dogg_Pound" title="Tha Dogg Pound">Tha Dogg Pound</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dogg_Food" title="Dogg Food">Dogg Food</a>. Kurupt flew Roscoe out to California and brought him to Death Row's studios to see him rap. Kurupt was impressed with what he saw and began meeting with his brother in the studio regularly, and eventually began writing his songs. He made his first appearance on his brother's album <a href="/wiki/Tha_Streetz_Iz_a_Mutha" title="Tha Streetz Iz a Mutha">Tha Streetz Iz a Mutha</a>, featuring on the vocals <i>"I Call Shots"</i> and <i>"Girls All Pause"</i> along with <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Daz_Dillinger" title="Daz Dillinger">Daz Dillinger</a> was impressed with Roscoe and helped to produce his debut mixtape, <i>"When The Pain Inflict"</i>, released in 2001. Thanks to the positive reception of his mixtape, Roscoe signed with <a href="/wiki/Priority_Records" title="Priority Records">Priority Records</a> and immediately got to work on his debut album, <a href="/wiki/Young_Roscoe_Philaphornia" title="Young Roscoe Philaphornia">Young Roscoe Philaphornia</a>, released in 2003. The album peaked at #22 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> chart.
</p><p>On October 17, 2013, Roscoe released his most recent mixtape <i>Tha Influence</i>, featuring guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>. The mixtape features <a href="/wiki/Record_production" class="mw-redirect" title="Record production">production</a> by <a href="/wiki/Best_Kept_Secret_(production_team)" title="Best Kept Secret (production team)">Best Kept Secret</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bink!" class="mw-redirect" title="Bink!">Bink!</a>, Charlie Red 3000, Fingazz, <a href="/wiki/Hi-Tek" title="Hi-Tek">Hi-Tek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jahlil_Beats" title="Jahlil Beats">Jahlil Beats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jake_One" title="Jake One">Jake One</a>, <a href="/wiki/Just_Blaze" title="Just Blaze">Just Blaze</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_WiLL_Made_It" class="mw-redirect" title="Mike WiLL Made It">Mike WiLL Made It</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nottz" title="Nottz">Nottz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Statik_Selektah" title="Statik Selektah">Statik Selektah</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tone_Mason" title="Tone Mason">Tone Mason</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On November 11, 2014, he released his third studio album titled <i>Scoe, Tha Influence Album</i>, also the first project to be released under his new label, 3D Shadez Entertainment. The album features Too Short, Kurupt, Kobe, Clyde Carson, Xzibit, KJ Konteh, Yummy Bingham, E-Note, All Star and K Young, with Roscoe taking a more musical approach by using live musicians rather than producers.
</p>
