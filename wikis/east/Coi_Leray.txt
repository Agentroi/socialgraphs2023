<p><b>Coi Leray Collins</b><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;a&#93;</a></sup> (born May 11, 1997) is an American rapper and singer. She began posting music to <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> and released her single "Huddy", along with her debut mixtape <i>Everythingcoz</i> in 2018. After signing a record deal with 1801 Records and <a href="/wiki/Republic_Records" title="Republic Records">Republic Records</a>, Leray released her second mixtape, <i>EC2</i> (2019), and her debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a>, <i>Now or Never</i> (2020). In 2021, the <a href="/wiki/Lil_Durk" title="Lil Durk">Lil Durk</a>-assisted remix of her single, "<a href="/wiki/No_More_Parties" title="No More Parties">No More Parties</a>", reached the <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">top 40</a>  on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, and was certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA).
</p><p>In 2022, Leray released her debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Trendsetter_(Coi_Leray_album)" title="Trendsetter (Coi Leray album)">Trendsetter</a></i>. The album's collaborative single with <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Blick_Blick" title="Blick Blick">Blick Blick</a>", also reached the top 40 of the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. Her 2022 song "<a href="/wiki/Players_(song)" title="Players (song)">Players</a>" peaked in the top 10 of the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and preceded her second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Coi_(album)" title="Coi (album)">Coi</a></i> (2023). Her 2023 single "<a href="/wiki/Baby_Don%27t_Hurt_Me" title="Baby Don&#39;t Hurt Me">Baby Don't Hurt Me</a>" (with <a href="/wiki/David_Guetta" title="David Guetta">David Guetta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anne-Marie" title="Anne-Marie">Anne-Marie</a>), reached the top 20 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coi_Leray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Benzino_Sister_Circle_2018.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Benzino_Sister_Circle_2018.png/175px-Benzino_Sister_Circle_2018.png" decoding="async" width="175" height="225" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Benzino_Sister_Circle_2018.png/263px-Benzino_Sister_Circle_2018.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Benzino_Sister_Circle_2018.png/350px-Benzino_Sister_Circle_2018.png 2x" data-file-width="584" data-file-height="750" /></a><figcaption>Leray is the daughter of media mogul and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Source (magazine)">The Source</a></i> founder <a href="/wiki/Benzino" title="Benzino">Benzino</a> (<i>pictured</i>)</figcaption></figure>
<p>Collins was born on May 11, 1997<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, and raised in <a href="/wiki/Hackensack,_New_Jersey" title="Hackensack, New Jersey">Hackensack</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-office_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-office-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-law_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> She is the daughter of rapper and media mogul <a href="/wiki/Benzino" title="Benzino">Benzino</a>, and has five brothers. Leray's parents divorced after her father left <i><a href="/wiki/The_Source" title="The Source">The Source</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-paperrappers_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paperrappers-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She started making music at age 14.<sup id="cite_ref-betfetty_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-betfetty-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, inspired by her father, she started rapping and released her first songs under the name Coi Leray—"Bow Down", with her brother Taj, and "Rock Back"—via YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-highsnobiety_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-highsnobiety-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphop.com_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphop.com-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> However, both Leray and her brother stopped rapping shortly after.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlbreak_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlbreak-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> At age 16, Leray dropped out of high school and started working in sales.<sup id="cite_ref-law_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coi_Leray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span id="2018.E2.80.932020:_Career_beginnings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–2020:_Career_beginnings">2018–2020: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coi_Leray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2018–2020: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After quitting her job, Leray released her debut single, a response to <a href="/wiki/A_Boogie_wit_da_Hoodie" title="A Boogie wit da Hoodie">A Boogie wit da Hoodie</a>'s song "DTB" titled "G.A.N.", in 2018 through <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-law_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-highsnobiety_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-highsnobiety-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Her breakout single, "Huddy", and her debut mixtape, <i>Everythingcoz</i>, were both released in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-xxlbreak_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xxlbreak-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2018, she was included as a featured artist alongside LouGotCash on the track "Save the Day" by American rapper <a href="/wiki/Ski_Mask_the_Slump_God" title="Ski Mask the Slump God">Ski Mask the Slump God</a> and singer <a href="/wiki/Jacquees" title="Jacquees">Jacquees</a> from <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man:_Into_the_Spider-Verse_(soundtracks)" class="mw-redirect" title="Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (soundtracks)">the soundtrack album</a> for the 2018 film <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man:_Into_the_Spider-Verse" title="Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse">Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse</a></i>. Her second mixtape, <i>EC2</i>, was released on January 18, 2019 through 1801 Records and <a href="/wiki/Republic_Records" title="Republic Records">Republic Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbfame_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbfame-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> She accompanied American rapper <a href="/wiki/Trippie_Redd" title="Trippie Redd">Trippie Redd</a> on his Life's a Trip Tour in early 2019, and released the single "Good Days" in March 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> She appeared as a featured artist on the song "Everything BoZ" by Trippie Redd from his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/!_(Trippie_Redd_album)" title="! (Trippie Redd album)">!</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In April 2020, she released the single "Better Days" with American rapper <a href="/wiki/Fetty_Wap" title="Fetty Wap">Fetty Wap</a>, and was featured on the remix of American actress <a href="/wiki/Keke_Palmer" title="Keke Palmer">Keke Palmer</a>'s single "Sticky" in May 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-betfetty_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-betfetty-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Leray released her debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a>, <i>Now or Never</i>, in August 2020.
</p>

The song benefited from some remixes including a <a href="/wiki/Jersey_club" title="Jersey club">Jersey club</a> remix from DJ Smallz 732.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2023, Leray collaborated on one of the songs of <a href="/wiki/Tomorrow_X_Together" title="Tomorrow X Together">Tomorrow X Together</a>'s 5th mini album, <a href="/wiki/The_Name_Chapter:_Temptation" title="The Name Chapter: Temptation">The Name Chapter: Temptation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Happy_Fools" class="mw-redirect" title="Happy Fools">Happy Fools</a>. </p>
