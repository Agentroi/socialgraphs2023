<p><b>Christopher E. Oroc</b>, <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Melvin Babu</b> (born September 17, 1974), better known as <b>DJ Babu</b> or <b>DJ Babu the Dilated Junkie</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Filipino_Americans" title="Filipino Americans">Filipino-American</a> <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>. He is a member of DJ crew the <a href="/wiki/Beat_Junkies" title="Beat Junkies">Beat Junkies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> trio <a href="/wiki/Dilated_Peoples" title="Dilated Peoples">Dilated Peoples</a> together with <a href="/wiki/Evidence_(musician)" title="Evidence (musician)">Evidence</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rakaa" title="Rakaa">Rakaa Iriscience</a>, and a member of the Likwit Junkies along with rapper Defari. He served as the curator and host of <i>Soundcheck</i> for Beat Junkie Radio on <a href="/wiki/Dash_Radio" title="Dash Radio">Dash Radio</a>. As of 2017, DJ Babu is the Program Director and a lead instructor alongside fellow crew members Mr. Choc, Melo-D, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Rhettmatic" title="DJ Rhettmatic">DJ Rhettmatic</a>, <a href="/wiki/D-Styles" title="D-Styles">D-Styles</a> and J.Rocc at the Beat Junkie Institute of Sound.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Babu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In his early years, Babu lived in <a href="/wiki/Oxnard,_California" title="Oxnard, California">Oxnard, California</a> for one year, and later moved to the neighboring city of <a href="/wiki/Camarillo,_California" title="Camarillo, California">Camarillo</a>; he currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_County,_California" title="Los Angeles County, California">Los Angeles County</a>.
</p><p>Babu started as part of the <a href="/wiki/Beat_Junkies" title="Beat Junkies">Beat Junkies</a> DJ crew, joining in by late 1993. He has won multiple competition titles Including the Vestax World Championships and multiple <a href="/wiki/International_Turntablist_Federation" title="International Turntablist Federation">International Turntablist Federation</a> titles.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> He has appeared in and hosted numerable videos. Babu is famous for redefining the art of <a href="/wiki/Beat_juggling" title="Beat juggling">Beat juggling</a> during the 1997 ITF/Beat juggling category competition, when he first performed his "Blind Alley" routine. Under the name "The Turntablist", produced perhaps the genre's most popular <a href="/wiki/Battle_record" title="Battle record">battle record</a>, 1996's Super Duck Breaks, which sold over 10,000 copies.
</p><p>Along with rapper and record producer <a href="/wiki/Evidence_(musician)" title="Evidence (musician)">Evidence</a> and rapper <a href="/wiki/Rakaa" title="Rakaa">Rakaa</a>, he released the debut <a href="/wiki/Dilated_Peoples" title="Dilated Peoples">Dilated Peoples</a>' single "Third Degree" in 1997 for ABB Records. The trio's dropped their first studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Platform_(album)" title="The Platform (album)">The Platform</a></i>, in 2000. Babu, Evidence and <a href="/wiki/Pharoahe_Monch" title="Pharoahe Monch">Pharoahe Monch</a> are featured in the song "H! Vltg3" from <a href="/wiki/Linkin_Park" title="Linkin Park">Linkin Park</a>'s remix album <i><a href="/wiki/Reanimation_(Linkin_Park_album)" title="Reanimation (Linkin Park album)">Reanimation</a></i>. DJ Babu has also featured on numerous acts alongside groups such as <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_5" title="Jurassic 5">Jurassic 5</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swollen_Members" title="Swollen Members">Swollen Members</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Visionaries_(hip_hop_group)" title="Visionaries (hip hop group)">Visionaries</a>. Individually Babu has been featured in many other groups, including his affiliation with the <a href="/wiki/Likwit_Crew" title="Likwit Crew">Likwit Crew</a>, which led to form the Likwit Junkies duo with Defari. The duo's debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_L.J.%27s" title="The L.J.&#39;s">The L.J.'s</a></i>, was released in March 2005 to generally favorable reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>.
</p><p>Babu's latest endeavors include Dilated Peoples' 2014 release of their fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Directors_of_Photography" title="Directors of Photography">Directors of Photography</a></i> via <a href="/wiki/Rhymesayers_Entertainment" title="Rhymesayers Entertainment">Rhymesayers Entertainment</a> label, which features tracks that were primarily produced by Evidence and Babu, with guest appearances from <a href="/wiki/Vince_Staples" title="Vince Staples">Vince Staples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aloe_Blacc" title="Aloe Blacc">Aloe Blacc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson" title="Action Bronson">Action Bronson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sick_Jacken" class="mw-redirect" title="Sick Jacken">Sick Jacken</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rapsody" title="Rapsody">Rapsody</a> to name a few.
</p><p>In 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Beat_Junkies" title="Beat Junkies">Beat Junkies</a> crew launched their own membership based record pool curated by the members of the crew, including Babu. At the top of 2015, "Beat Junkie Radio" began airing on <a href="/wiki/Dash_Radio" title="Dash Radio">Dash Radio</a>, the station stars all members of the crew, but led by Babu, Mr. Choc, and Rhettmatic. Their live show "Soundcheck' which aires live every 2nd and 4th Tuesday of the month has been ranked as one of the top 5 shows on Dash Radio.
</p><p><i>Duck Season Vol. 4</i> is set to be released in 2018.
</p><p>Aside Babu's musical accomplishments, his hobbies also include photography, with Babu being noted for his "behind the decks" shots of his turntables with Evidence and Rakaa during live shows, as well as landscape shots of his travels around the world with Dilated Peoples.
</p><p>On January 18, 2017, The <a href="/wiki/Beat_Junkies" title="Beat Junkies">Beat Junkies</a> formally announced plans to open their own DJ school (The Beat Junkie Institute of Sound) and Professional training facility. Babu leads in the introductory classes, intermediate/advanced level scratching, creative mixing and beat juggling classes.
</p>
