<p><b>Marc Joseph Predka</b> (born April 21, 1975), better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Tha Trademarc</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">hip hop artist</a>. He first came to prominence when he and his younger first cousin, <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> wrestler <a href="/wiki/John_Cena" title="John Cena">John Cena</a>, collaborated on the 2005 album <i><a href="/wiki/You_Can%27t_See_Me" title="You Can&#39;t See Me">You Can't See Me</a></i>, including Cena's entrance theme "<a href="/wiki/The_Time_Is_Now_(John_Cena_song)" title="The Time Is Now (John Cena song)">The Time Is Now</a>". He also appeared in the music videos of the songs, "Bad Bad Man" and "Right Now" with Cena. Prior to the album, Trademarc appeared on Cena's first WWE DVD <i>Word Life</i>.
</p><p>In August 2007, Trademarc appeared at <a href="/wiki/Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling" class="mw-redirect" title="Total Nonstop Action Wrestling">Total Nonstop Action Wrestling</a>'s (TNA) <a href="/wiki/TNA_Hard_Justice#2007" class="mw-redirect" title="TNA Hard Justice">Hard Justice</a> pay-per-view as the "new boyfriend" of <a href="/wiki/Karen_Angle" class="mw-redirect" title="Karen Angle">Karen Angle</a>, later revealed to be a ruse when Karen and Trademarc helped Karen's "estranged" husband <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Angle" title="Kurt Angle">Kurt Angle</a> win the match.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He appeared again on <i><a href="/wiki/TNA_Impact" class="mw-redirect" title="TNA Impact">iMPACT!</a></i> the next week.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> While working with TNA he also re-recorded Kurt Angle's <a href="/wiki/Music_in_professional_wrestling" title="Music in professional wrestling">entrance music</a>, mixing in a rap he did over a <a href="/wiki/Lunatic_Fringe_(song)" title="Lunatic Fringe (song)">Lunatic Fringe</a> beat with the TNA-composed <a href="/wiki/Dale_Oliver" title="Dale Oliver">"My Quest"</a> to form the hip hop theme "Gold Medal".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In 2008, he released his first solo debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Inferiority_Complex_(album)" title="Inferiority Complex (album)">Inferiority Complex</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="East_Coast_Avengers">East Coast Avengers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tha_Trademarc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: East Coast Avengers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In August 2008, Tha Trademarc collaborated with Massachusetts rappers <a href="/wiki/7L_%26_Esoteric" title="7L &amp; Esoteric">Esoteric</a> and DC The Midi Alien to create the East Coast Avengers. Their group debut album <i>Prison Planet</i> was released in late 2008. The group released their song "Kill Bill O'Reilly" which caused a media controversy due to lyrics that centered on violently killing <a href="/wiki/Bill_O%27Reilly_(political_commentator)" title="Bill O&#39;Reilly (political commentator)">Bill O'Reilly</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Wells_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wells-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Malkin" title="Michelle Malkin">Michelle Malkin</a>'s criticism of the song led to the group recording "Dear Michelle" in response.<sup id="cite_ref-Crawford_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Crawford-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> They have since released another single titled "Hey America!".
</p>
