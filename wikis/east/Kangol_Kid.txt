					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American hip hop musician (1966–2021)</div>
<p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; font-size: 125%;"><div class="">Kangol Kid</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Shaun Shiller Fequiere</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><span style="display:none">(<span class="bday">1966-08-10</span>)</span>August 10, 1966<br /><a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a>, U.S.</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Died</th><td class="infobox-data">December 18, 2021<span style="display:none">(2021-12-18)</span> (aged&#160;55)<br /><a href="/wiki/Manhasset,_New_York" title="Manhasset, New York">Manhasset, New York</a>, U.S.</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">1983–2021</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Select_Records" title="Select Records">Select Records</a><br /><a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a></td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Shaun Shiller Fequiere</b> (August 10, 1966 – December 18, 2021), known by his stage name <b>Kangol Kid</b>, was an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Breakdance" class="mw-redirect" title="Breakdance">break dancer</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Emcee" class="mw-redirect" title="Emcee">emcee</a>.  Kangol is best known as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Old_school_hip_hop" class="mw-redirect" title="Old school hip hop">old school hip hop</a> group <a href="/wiki/UTFO" title="UTFO">UTFO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kangol Kid was the first hip-hop artist to endorse a product through his official sponsorship with <a href="/wiki/Kangol" title="Kangol">Kangol</a> headwear.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AllHipHopTV_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllHipHopTV-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His signature hat has been inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Smithsonian_Institution" title="Smithsonian Institution">Collection of the Smithsonian National Museum of African American History and Culture</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kangol Kid is considered to be one of the "pioneers of hip-hop".<sup id="cite_ref-XS10_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XS10-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kangol_Kid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fequiere was born in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York to <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haitian</a> parents.<sup id="cite_ref-AllHipHopTV_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllHipHopTV-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Fequiere shied away from his Haitian heritage growing up.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He acquired his nickname, The Kangol Kid, from neighborhood friends during the early 1980s, because of the many <a href="/wiki/Kangol" title="Kangol">Kangol</a> hats he owned and wore.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  He was also officially sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Kangol" title="Kangol">Kangol Headwear, Inc.</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>He was signed to his first record label deal at age 17, without having had his own legal representation at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="UTFO_member">UTFO member</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kangol_Kid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: UTFO member">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kangol Kid was originally a <a href="/wiki/Breakdancer" class="mw-redirect" title="Breakdancer">breakdancer</a> along with his dance partner, <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Ice" class="mw-redirect" title="Doctor Ice">Doctor Ice</a>.  In 1984, UTFO became one of the most popular rap and <a href="/wiki/Breakdance" class="mw-redirect" title="Breakdance">breakdance</a> acts in the country on the strength of their breakout single, "<a href="/wiki/Roxanne,_Roxanne" class="mw-redirect" title="Roxanne, Roxanne">Roxanne, Roxanne</a>".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>During the mid-1980s, as "Roxanne, Roxanne" gained popularity and chart position, UTFO was voted best rap group, at the 1988 <a href="/wiki/Urban_Music_Awards" title="Urban Music Awards">Urban Music Awards</a>, had featured spots on <a href="/wiki/Don_Cornelius" title="Don Cornelius">Don Cornelius</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Soul_Train" title="Soul Train">Soul Train</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Phil_Donahue_Show" title="The Phil Donahue Show">The Phil Donahue Show</a></i>, and performed live for actors such as <a href="/wiki/Dustin_Hoffman" title="Dustin Hoffman">Dustin Hoffman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Danza" title="Tony Danza">Tony Danza</a>.  UTFO was the first rap group to perform at <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Theater" title="Apollo Theater">Apollo Theater</a> as they opened for <a href="/wiki/Hall_%26_Oates" title="Hall &amp; Oates">Hall &amp; Oates</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Temptations" class="mw-redirect" title="Temptations">Temptations</a>' <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Kendricks" title="Eddie Kendricks">Eddie Kendricks</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Ruffin" title="David Ruffin">David Ruffin</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 1987, UTFO released their highest-charting album, <i>Lethal</i>, featuring the rock group <a href="/wiki/Anthrax_(American_band)" title="Anthrax (American band)">Anthrax</a> on the title track.
</p>
<h2><span id="Production.2C_management.2C_and_songwriting"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Production,_management,_and_songwriting">Production, management, and songwriting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kangol_Kid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production, management, and songwriting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1985, Fequiere wrote and composed "Private Property" on the <i><a href="/wiki/Lisa_Lisa_%26_Cult_Jam_with_Full_Force" title="Lisa Lisa &amp; Cult Jam with Full Force">Lisa Lisa &amp; Cult Jam with Full Force</a></i> album.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Later that year, he managed and produced the rap group, <a href="/wiki/Whistle_(band)" title="Whistle (band)">Whistle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fequiere worked as a producer, composer, performer, artist manager, and songwriter. He was the President of music production company, Kangol's Kreations, Inc., and CEO of his Public Relations firm, Kreative Media Group.<sup id="cite_ref-XS10_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XS10-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Fequiere lectured at high schools and colleges nationwide on the business of music.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Fequiere became the first rapper to be honored by the <a href="/wiki/American_Cancer_Society" title="American Cancer Society">American Cancer Society</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_death">Personal life and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kangol_Kid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fequiere was an advocate and fundraiser for <a href="/wiki/Breast_cancer_research" class="mw-redirect" title="Breast cancer research">breast cancer research</a> through the Mama Luke Foundation in collaboration with the American Cancer Society. He organized and participated in events throughout the country to raise money for research to fight breast cancer. Fequiere was also a proud father of 4, including 3 sons (T. Shaun, AJ, and Giovanni) and 1 daughter (Amancia) (<a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://www.instagram.com/p/CVxi4hFL-Sd/">https://www.instagram.com/p/CVxi4hFL-Sd/</a> 
</p><p>On December 18, 2021, Fequiere died from <a href="/wiki/Colon_cancer" class="mw-redirect" title="Colon cancer">colon cancer</a> at the age of 55, at a hospital in <a href="/wiki/Manhasset,_New_York" title="Manhasset, New York">Manhasset, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
