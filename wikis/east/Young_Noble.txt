<p><b>Rufus Lee Cooper III</b> (born March 21, 1978), also known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Young Noble</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> who was part of <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>'s rap group <a href="/wiki/Outlawz" title="Outlawz">Outlawz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
Noble joined the Outlawz in early 1996, and was the last member to be added to the group by Tupac himself, before the <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_Tupac_Shakur" title="Murder of Tupac Shakur">late rapper's death in September 1996</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Young_Noble&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Noble was born in <a href="/wiki/Rancho_Cucamonga,_California" title="Rancho Cucamonga, California">Rancho Cucamonga, California</a>. He made his debut on Shakur's album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Don_Killuminati:_The_7_Day_Theory" title="The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory">The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory</a></i> on 4 songs "Bomb First (My Second Reply)", "<a href="/wiki/Hail_Mary_(2Pac_song)" title="Hail Mary (2Pac song)">Hail Mary</a>", "Life of an Outlaw", and "Just Like Daddy".
</p><p>On December 21, 1999, Shakur's album <i><a href="/wiki/Still_I_Rise_(album)" title="Still I Rise (album)">Still I Rise</a></i> was released, which consisted of material recorded with 2Pac and one solo song. Noble replaced <a href="/wiki/Hussein_Fatal" title="Hussein Fatal">Hussein Fatal</a>'s vocals on the songs.
</p><p>Through the Outlawz and Young Noble's Twitter pages, together they announced the release of two volumes of mixtapes titled <a href="/wiki/Outlaw_Rydahz_Vol._1" class="mw-redirect" title="Outlaw Rydahz Vol. 1">Outlaw Rydahz Vol. 1</a> and <i>Outlaw Nation</i> 'Outlaw Rydaz Volume One' was released in March 2012, and <i>Outlaw Nation</i> in October 2012.
He also said it would feature some of his mentors and Tupac Shakur's old affiliates, including Thug Life which was 2Pac's group before he created The Outlawz, <a href="/wiki/Money_B" class="mw-redirect" title="Money B">Money B</a> (who was a member of Shakur's old group <a href="/wiki/Digital_Underground" title="Digital Underground">Digital Underground</a>) and <a href="/wiki/B-Legit" title="B-Legit">B-Legit</a> who featured on Shakur's 1996 album on Death Row Records,
<i><a href="/wiki/All_Eyez_on_Me" title="All Eyez on Me">All Eyez on Me</a></i>, on the track "Aint Hard 2 Find".
</p>
