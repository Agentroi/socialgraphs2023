					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American rapper</div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; font-size: 125%;"><div class="fn">Sweet Tee</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Toi Crystal Jackson<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Also known as</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Sweet Tee</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Origin</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>, United States</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Genres</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Occupation(s)</th><td class="infobox-data role"><a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">Rapper</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Years active</span></th><td class="infobox-data">1985–present</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Labels</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Profile_Records" title="Profile Records">Profile Records</a></td></tr><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-header" style="background-color: #b0c4de"></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Website</th><td class="infobox-data"><span class="url"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://instagram.com/sweetteethemc/">instagram<wbr />.com<wbr />/sweetteethemc<wbr />/</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Musical artist</div>
<p><b>Toi Crystal Jackson</b>, known professionally as <b>Sweet Tee</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">rapper</a> who was signed to <a href="/wiki/Profile_Records" title="Profile Records">Profile Records</a> in the 1980s.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Her first single in 1986 was the hit "It's My Beat" featuring <a href="/wiki/DJ_Jazzy_Joyce" title="DJ Jazzy Joyce">DJ Jazzy Joyce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-whoz_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whoz-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Sweet Tee would be part of the crew of producer <a href="/wiki/Hurby_Azor" title="Hurby Azor">Hurby Azor</a>’s "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Idol_Makers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Idol Makers (page does not exist)">Idol Makers</a>". She scored minor <a href="/wiki/Record_chart" title="Record chart">chart</a> success with her debut <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_Tee_Time" title="It&#39;s Tee Time">It's Tee Time</a></i> in 1988,<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> which peaked at No. 31 on the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">R&amp;B</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She scored four chart <a href="/wiki/Hit_record" class="mw-redirect" title="Hit record">hit</a> <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">singles</a> from her debut album. These included "I Got da Feelin'" (No. 48 US R&amp;B, No. 31 <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>), "On the Smooth Tip" (No. 36 US R&amp;B) and "Why Did It Have to Be Me". In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a>, "It's Like That Y'All" peaked in the Top 40.<sup id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>JMJ Records a subsidiary of Def Jam Recordings signed Sweet Tee and in 1995, Sweet Tee released the single "What's up, Star?" under the moniker Suga. The song appeared on Russell Simmons presents The Show: The Soundtrack.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>UK-based act <a href="/wiki/Tin_Tin_Out" title="Tin Tin Out">Tin Tin Out</a>'s 1994 debut single, "The Feeling", was a piano-based house track that <a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">sampled</a> Sweet Tee's lyrics from "I Got da Feelin'". The song reached No. 32 in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>. It is credited to Tin Tin Out featuring Sweet Tee.<sup id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums_2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums_2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
