<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Justina_Valentine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Valentine was born in <a href="/wiki/Passaic_County,_New_Jersey" title="Passaic County, New Jersey">Passaic County, New Jersey</a>. She is of Italian descent.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She grew up in a family of musicians and did theatre and dance as a child. Valentine always had a love for <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>. In 2006, she started recording her own music.<sup id="cite_ref-stupiddope_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stupiddope-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-socialifestylemag_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-socialifestylemag-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Justina_Valentine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Valentine released her first <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> <i>Route 80</i> in 2012. The singles "Bubble Gum" and "Hip-hop Joan Jett" became popular hits on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. Valentine released the <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> <i>Valentine</i> in 2013, debuting at number 38 on the iTunes R&amp;B charts.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On July 8, 2014, the singer released her second mixtape, <i>Red Velvet</i>. In 2016, she released her studio album, <i>Scarlet Letter</i>. In 2017, Valentine released a third mixtape, <i>Feminem,</i> described as a homage to the rapper <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She released the album <i>Favorite Vibe</i> in 2019, which debuted in the top 30 hip hop charts and gained over a million streams in its first two weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <i>Favorite Vibe</i> was shortly followed by the album <i>Infrared</i> in 2020, which debuted in the top 5 on the iTunes charts.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <i>Infrared</i> is described as "an infusion of classic hip hop and R&amp;B, with a pop sensibility."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, Valentine was added to the cast of season 8 of <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_%27N_Out" class="mw-redirect" title="Wild &#39;N Out">Wild 'N Out</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> (later <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>) and has been a member of the cast for every successive season since.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> As of season 17, she is the longest running female cast member to appear on the show.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Valentine competed on season 1 of the reality series special for <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Challenge_(TV_series)" title="The Challenge (TV series)">The Challenge</a></i> titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Challenge:_Champs_vs._Stars_(season_2)" title="The Challenge: Champs vs. Stars (season 2)">Champs vs. Stars</a>, which premiered on November 21, 2017</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Valentine made it to the finale, finishing as a runner-up alongside fellow "Stars" <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Fighting_Championship" title="Ultimate Fighting Championship">UFC</a> fighter <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Waterson" class="mw-redirect" title="Michelle Waterson">Michelle Waterson</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bachelorette_(American_season_10)" title="The Bachelorette (American season 10)">The Bachelorette Season 10</a></i> winner Josh Murray. In 2019, Valentine hosted the two-part reunion of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Challenge:_War_of_the_Worlds_2" title="The Challenge: War of the Worlds 2">The Challenge: War of the Worlds 2</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2018, Valentine was selected to co-host a relaunched version of the MTV dating game show <i><a href="/wiki/Singled_Out" title="Singled Out">Singled Out</a></i> alongside rapper and fellow <i>Wild 'N Out</i> cast member <a href="/wiki/Conceited_(rapper)" title="Conceited (rapper)">Conceited</a> that aired exclusively on the network's YouTube channel.
</p><p>In August 2019, Valentine and fellow <i>Wild 'N Out</i> cast member Chico Bean stated that <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Maniscalco" title="Sebastian Maniscalco">Sebastian Maniscalco</a> was Valentine's "second freaking cousin".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2021, Valentine began appearing weekly alongside <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon" title="Nick Cannon">Nick Cannon</a> on his syndicated daytime talk show, <i>Nick Cannon</i>, during "The Rap Up" and other segments.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2022, she became the third host of the <a href="/wiki/IHeartRadio" title="IHeartRadio">iHeartRadio</a> and MTV podcast series <i>MTV's Women of Wild 'N Out,</i> alongside co-hosts <a href="/wiki/B._Simone" class="mw-redirect" title="B. Simone">B. Simone</a> and Pretty Vee.
</p><p>Valentine has also gained prominence as a social media personality on <a href="/wiki/TikTok" title="TikTok">TikTok</a> where she actively posts content. As of September 2022, her account (@justinavalentine) has amassed over 10 million followers and over 200 million likes.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_influences">Musical style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Justina_Valentine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Musical style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Valentine's eclectic music style infuses <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> with elements of other genres.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> She has had charting singles and major radio airplay.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
