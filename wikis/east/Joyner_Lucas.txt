<p><b>Gary Maurice</b> "<b>Joyner</b>" <b>Lucas Jr.</b> (born August 17, 1988) is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, actor &amp; entrepreneur. Lucas first received widespread exposure and critical acclaim after the release of his single "Ross Capicchioni" in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VibeRoss_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VibeRoss-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In June 2017, he released his fourth mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/508-507-2209" title="508-507-2209">508-507-2209</a></i>, which was his first on a major label. In November 2017, Lucas released his single "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Not_Racist" title="I&#39;m Not Racist">I'm Not Racist</a>", which quickly went <a href="/wiki/Viral_video" title="Viral video">viral</a>, gaining him further mainstream attention. The video was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Music_Video" title="Grammy Award for Best Music Video">Grammy Award for Best Music Video</a> at the <a href="/wiki/61st_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="61st Grammy Awards">61st Grammy Awards</a>. Since 2016, Lucas has also become known for remixing popular hip hop songs like "<a href="/wiki/Gucci_Gang" title="Gucci Gang">Gucci Gang</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Lil_Pump" title="Lil Pump">Lil Pump</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Bank_Account_(song)" title="Bank Account (song)">Bank Account</a>" by <a href="/wiki/21_Savage" title="21 Savage">21 Savage</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Panda_(song)" title="Panda (song)">Panda</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Desiigner" title="Desiigner">Desiigner</a>, "<a href="/wiki/DNA_(Kendrick_Lamar_song)" title="DNA (Kendrick Lamar song)">DNA</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a> and many others. In October 2018, he announced his debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/ADHD_(Joyner_Lucas_album)" title="ADHD (Joyner Lucas album)">ADHD</a></i>, which was released in 2020.
</p><p>Outside of music, Lucas co-founded the music technology platform <a href="/wiki/Tully_(app)" title="Tully (app)">Tully</a> in 2015, with manager Dhruv Joshi.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joyner_Lucas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gary Maurice Lucas Jr. was born on August 17, 1988, in <a href="/wiki/Worcester,_Massachusetts" title="Worcester, Massachusetts">Worcester, Massachusetts</a>. He began rapping at the age of 10 and attended <a href="/wiki/South_High_Community_School" title="South High Community School">South High Community School</a> in Worcester.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joyner_Lucas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2007.E2.80.932017:_Early_beginnings.2C_record_deal.2C_and_508-507-2209"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007–2017:_Early_beginnings,_record_deal,_and_508-507-2209">2007–2017: Early beginnings, record deal, and <i>508-507-2209</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joyner_Lucas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007–2017: Early beginnings, record deal, and 508-507-2209">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Joyner Lucas first started rapping under the name G-Storm, but by 2007 had changed his stage name to Future Joyner and started working with his cousin Cyrus tha Great, forming a group called Film Skool Rejekts. That year they released <i>Workprint: The Greatest Mixtape of All Time</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Lucas released his first solo mixtape, titled <i>Listen to Me</i>, in 2011.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> After rapper <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a> came to prominence, Lucas dropped the "Future" from his name and continued as Joyner Lucas.<sup id="cite_ref-XXL_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He released his first mixtape under this new moniker in 2013, titled <i>LFO's (Low Frequency Oscillators)</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-lfos_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lfos-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His next mixtape, <i>Along Came Joyner</i>, was released on April 5, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-ACJ_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACJ-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This mixtape contained his critically acclaimed "Ross Capicchioni".<sup id="cite_ref-Stashed_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stashed-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Following the success of the song, Lucas was featured in the <a href="/wiki/2015_BET_Hip_Hop_Awards" title="2015 BET Hip Hop Awards">2015 BET Hip-Hop Awards</a> Cypher. Originally intended to appear in the online-only cypher, he was later promoted to rap as part of the live broadcast cyphers based on the strength of his initial performance.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lucas signed to Atlantic Records on September 21, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Lucas went on to release a project titled <i><a href="/wiki/508-507-2209" title="508-507-2209">508-507-2209</a></i> on June 16, 2017, with Atlantic Records.<sup id="cite_ref-508Joyner_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-508Joyner-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The mixtape charted at #7 on the <a href="/wiki/Heatseekers_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Heatseekers Albums">Heatseekers Albums</a> chart on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> and contained the singles "I'm Sorry", "Ultrasound", "Just Like You" and "Winter Blues".<sup id="cite_ref-HeatseekerssAlbums_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HeatseekerssAlbums-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Ever since 2016, Lucas has also become known for remixing popular hip hop songs.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 28, 2017, Lucas released the single "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Not_Racist" title="I&#39;m Not Racist">I'm Not Racist</a>" via his <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The music video quickly went <a href="/wiki/Viral_video" title="Viral video">viral</a> and gained critical acclaim. The controversial song is about <a href="/wiki/Race_and_society" title="Race and society">race and society</a>, and race relations from the perspectives of a white man and a black man.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The video was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Music_Video" title="Grammy Award for Best Music Video">Grammy Award for Best Music Video</a> at the <a href="/wiki/61st_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="61st Grammy Awards">61st Grammy Awards</a>, but lost to <a href="/wiki/Childish_Gambino" class="mw-redirect" title="Childish Gambino">Childish Gambino</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/This_Is_America_(song)" title="This Is America (song)">This Is America</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-:6_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Lucas and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a> announced a collaborative project titled <i>Angels &amp; Demons</i> on February 25, 2018,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> with the project's first single "<a href="/wiki/Stranger_Things_(Joyner_Lucas_and_Chris_Brown_song)" title="Stranger Things (Joyner Lucas and Chris Brown song)">Stranger Things</a>" releasing the following day.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On April 1, 2018, Lucas released the single "Frozen", a song focused on different forms of reckless driving. The second single from the <i>Angels and Demons</i> collaborative mixtape, "I Don't Die", was released on May 2.
</p><p>On December 24, 2018, it was announced that Lucas departed from Atlantic Records.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2018.E2.80.932020:_ADHD.2C_and_Evolution"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–2020:_ADHD,_and_Evolution">2018–2020: <i>ADHD</i>, and <i>Evolution</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joyner_Lucas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2018–2020: ADHD, and Evolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 31, 2018, Lucas announced that he was forced to cancel appearances in the Australian and European legs of his I'm Kind of a Big Deal tour due to hoarseness and <a href="/wiki/Laryngitis" title="Laryngitis">laryngitis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> A month later, Lucas was featured on "<a href="/wiki/Lucky_You_(Eminem_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lucky You (Eminem song)">Lucky You</a>" from <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s tenth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Kamikaze_(Eminem_album)" title="Kamikaze (Eminem album)">Kamikaze</a></i>. The song debuted at number 6 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, marking Lucas' first top 10 entry on the chart.<sup id="cite_ref-Luck_You_Debut_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Luck_You_Debut-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On October 12, 2018, Lucas announced that he would be releasing his debut studio album entitled <i><a href="/wiki/ADHD_(Joyner_Lucas_album)" title="ADHD (Joyner Lucas album)">ADHD</a></i>. He also announced a single from the album that would be released the following Wednesday.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The song, entitled "<a href="/wiki/I_Love_(Joyner_Lucas_song)" title="I Love (Joyner Lucas song)">I Love</a>", was released as the lead single from <i>ADHD</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> which was released on March 27, 2020. It was preceded by nine singles from October 2018 to March 2020, the time before release. The album featured appearances from <a href="/wiki/Logic_(rapper)" title="Logic (rapper)">Logic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Thug" title="Young Thug">Young Thug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a> and King OSF as well as appearances from <a href="/wiki/Chris_Tucker" title="Chris Tucker">Chris Tucker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Hart" title="Kevin Hart">Kevin Hart</a> who both appeared on skits.
</p><p>On August 17, 2020, Lucas announced the release date of his debut EP, <i>Evolution</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> It was released on October 23, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> It was preceded by two singles, "Fall Slowly" featuring <a href="/wiki/Ashanti_(singer)" title="Ashanti (singer)">Ashanti</a> and "Snitch". The EP featured appearances from <a href="/wiki/The_Game_(rapper)" title="The Game (rapper)">The Game</a>, iyla, Ashanti, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Ross" title="Rick Ross">Rick Ross</a> and Elijah James.
</p>
<h3><span id="2021.E2.80.93present:_.22Ramen_.26_OJ.22.2C_.22Your_Heart.22.2C_movie_shoot.2C_and_Not_Now.2C_I.27m_Busy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2021–present:_&quot;Ramen_&amp;_OJ&quot;,_&quot;Your_Heart&quot;,_movie_shoot,_and_Not_Now,_I'm_Busy">2021–present: "Ramen &amp; OJ", "Your Heart", movie shoot, and <i>Not Now, I'm Busy</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joyner_Lucas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2021–present: &quot;Ramen &amp; OJ&quot;, &quot;Your Heart&quot;, movie shoot, and Not Now, I&#039;m Busy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 30, 2021, Lucas released the single "<a href="/wiki/Ramen_%26_OJ" title="Ramen &amp; OJ">Ramen &amp; OJ</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Lil_Baby" title="Lil Baby">Lil Baby</a>. It was accompanied by a music video. Lucas contributed a song titled "<a href="/wiki/Shoot_My_Shot" class="mw-redirect" title="Shoot My Shot">Shoot My Shot</a>" to the <i><a href="/wiki/Space_Jam:_A_New_Legacy" title="Space Jam: A New Legacy">Space Jam: A New Legacy</a></i> soundtrack.
</p><p>Throughout 2021, Lucas released a number of singles, most notably "<a href="/wiki/Your_Heart" title="Your Heart">Your Heart</a>" with <a href="/wiki/J._Cole" title="J. Cole">J. Cole</a>, which was released on September 24, 2021, and accompanied by a music video. Lucas also collaborated with other notable rappers, including <a href="/wiki/Lil_Durk" title="Lil Durk">Lil Durk</a> on the song "Rambo", released on December 3, 2021, <a href="/wiki/Lil_Tjay" title="Lil Tjay">Lil Tjay</a> on the song "Dreams Unfold", released on August 25, 2021, and <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_$ign" class="mw-redirect" title="Ty Dolla $ign">Ty Dolla $ign</a> on the song "Late to the Party", released on October 8, 2021, and accompanied by a music video. Solo singles "Duck Duck Goose", released on October 29, 2021, and "My Escape", released on December 17, 2021, were also accompanied by music videos.
</p><p>On April 1, 2022, Lucas released a tribute song to <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> entitled "Ye Not Crazy", similar to his earlier tribute song to <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a> entitled "<a href="/wiki/Will_(Joyner_Lucas_song)" title="Will (Joyner Lucas song)">Will</a>". However, due to the backlash of West making antisemitic remarks, Lucas removed it from streaming services months later.
</p><p>On June 4, 2022, Lucas uploaded a photo to social media of him in the studio with the caption 'Working on new album....'. That was interrupted however by the announcement on October 29, 2022, that Lucas would be shooting a movie with good friend and fellow Boston native <a href="/wiki/Mark_Wahlberg" title="Mark Wahlberg">Mark Wahlberg</a>.
</p><p>On March 8, 2023, Lucas uploaded to social media, announcing that the cover art for his upcoming studio album would be released soon with a single released on March 10, 2023, called "Devil's Work 2", the sequel to the original song from his debut album <a href="/wiki/ADHD_(Joyner_Lucas_album)" title="ADHD (Joyner Lucas album)">ADHD</a>. On March 9 the artwork was released, revealing the albums title to be <i>"Not Now, I'm Busy"</i>.
</p><p>One month later on April 3, 2023, Lucas announced another single called "Blackout" featuring <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a> which was released on April 7, 2023. Both singles were accompanied by music videos. In a video posted to social media, and also with <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music" title="Apple Music">Apple Music</a> pre-releasing the album on a single-by-single basis, it was revealed that the album will be released on August 18, 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joyner_Lucas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lucas has cited several artists as influences in his music,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> but he cites Eminem as his biggest musical influence.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> He also revealed he was influenced by <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a> in his song "<a href="/wiki/Will_(Joyner_Lucas_song)" title="Will (Joyner Lucas song)">Will</a>", which he would release a remix of featuring Will Smith himself on May 15, 2020. He cites <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> as one of his influences too.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joyner_Lucas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Joyner Lucas shares a son named Joyner Messiah Lucas (born February 20, 2016) with Carmen Julissa Ayala, with whom he had an off and on romantic relationship. Lucas tells the story of his son's conception in his song "Forever" on his mixtape <i>508-507-2209</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Joyner Messiah Lucas is loved and adored by his father and referenced in many of his songs. He has also made cameos in his father's music videos. Most recently he played Will Smith's son <a href="/wiki/Jaden_Smith" title="Jaden Smith">Jaden Smith</a> in the video of "Will".<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lucas has revealed that the title of his album <i>ADHD</i> comes from the fact that he was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/ADHD" class="mw-redirect" title="ADHD">ADHD</a> as a child. He explained that people surrounding him, including his own parents, made him feel like he was not normal and treated him differently as a result. Eventually, he would act out, bullying other classmates, failing classes, and get sent to an <a href="/wiki/Alternative_school" title="Alternative school">alternative school</a> after expulsion.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Feuds">Feuds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joyner_Lucas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Feuds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Logic">Logic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joyner_Lucas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Logic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to Lucas, when he was on <a href="/wiki/Tech_N9ne" title="Tech N9ne">Tech N9ne</a>'s tour bus, he heard some records from Tech's album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Storm_(Tech_N9ne_album)" title="The Storm (Tech N9ne album)">The Storm</a></i>. Lucas expressed interest on collaborating with <a href="/wiki/Logic_(rapper)" title="Logic (rapper)">Logic</a> on the song "Sriracha" and felt that he could add to the display of fast-flow rapping, and Tech acquiesced.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Logic was originally slated to be the only feature on "Sriracha", but when he spent over six months to deliver his verse, Lucas hopped on the track to offer his own. Joyner stated that when they got the record back, he could tell that Logic was not "too happy" about him being on the track once he heard it.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On his remix of <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Mask_Off" title="Mask Off">Mask Off</a>", Joyner, unhappy by the work Logic turned in after taking nearly half a year to edit his verse, directly addressed the disappointing display. Both he and Tech N9ne were upset with Logic for not emulating the style of quick flow on the song.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Logic's hit song "<a href="/wiki/1-800-273-8255_(song)" title="1-800-273-8255 (song)">1-800-273-8255</a>" peaked at number three on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a></i>, Joyner Lucas immediately took offense and argued that the track was named in order to bring direct competition to his song, "I'm Sorry" from his album <i>508-507-2209</i>. In a later interview, he called Logic "corny"<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> and in another incident, he said that he thinks that Logic "can rap" but feels like "he tries to prove that he's black too much for me."<sup id="cite_ref-:1_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> With both Logic's single and Lucas' mixtape dealing with the issue of mental health representation and resource allocation, Lucas was careful to not slam the track too hard.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Logic dropped his song "Yuck" from his mixtape <i><a href="/wiki/Bobby_Tarantino_II" title="Bobby Tarantino II">Bobby Tarantino II</a></i> in March 2018, the veiled diss inside the song was quickly spotted by Lucas and his fans, despite Logic saying that there was no diss in the song.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Seemingly nonplussed by the insult, Lucas dared Logic to call him out directly on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>. That same month, Joyner took aim at Logic's song "<a href="/wiki/44_More" title="44 More">44 More</a>" and his album <i><a href="/wiki/Everybody_(Logic_album)" title="Everybody (Logic album)">Everybody</a></i> on his remix of <a href="/wiki/BlocBoy_JB" title="BlocBoy JB">BlocBoy JB</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Look_Alive_(BlocBoy_JB_song)" title="Look Alive (BlocBoy JB song)">Look Alive</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-:2_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, the two rappers ended their feud, which was revealed when Logic made a guest appearance on Joyner Lucas' song "<a href="/wiki/ISIS_(Joyner_Lucas_song)" title="ISIS (Joyner Lucas song)">ISIS</a>", the third single from <i>ADHD</i>. Lucas kept the name of the collaborator a secret until he released the song.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hopsin">Hopsin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joyner_Lucas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Hopsin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2018, when a fan asked rapper <a href="/wiki/Hopsin" title="Hopsin">Hopsin</a> on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> who would win in a <a href="/wiki/Rap_battle" class="mw-redirect" title="Rap battle">rap battle</a> between him and Joyner Lucas, Hopsin replied, "That's a dumb question that I'm sure you already know the answer to." Not long after, Lucas commented, "Hold up. @Hopsin wtf is that supposed to mean???"<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> In his response, Hopsin stated that he would "b[r]eak his soul" and also dissed Logic in the comment.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> This dispute would prove to be short-lived, when in less than two days Hopsin shared a screenshot of a <a href="/wiki/FaceTime" title="FaceTime">FaceTime</a> call with Lucas, indicating that they finally made peace, but still wanted people to consider who would beat the other in a rap battle.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tory_Lanez">Tory Lanez</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joyner_Lucas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Tory Lanez">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 19, 2018, Lanez claimed that he was a better rapper than Joyner Lucas on an <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> Live session. Incredulous, Lucas immediately responded by inviting Lanez to a battle rapping challenge and telling him to let the public decide who is better. The next day, Lanez dropped a freestyle over Eminem's "Lucky You", of which Lucas made a guest appearance on. In less than 24 hours, Joyner responded with his freestyle over "<a href="/wiki/Litty" title="Litty">Litty</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Meek_Mill" title="Meek Mill">Meek Mill</a> featuring Tory Lanez, also targeting rapper <a href="/wiki/Trippie_Redd" title="Trippie Redd">Trippie Redd</a>, who joined Tory's Instagram Live and slandered Joyner's name as well. Shortly after, Lanez retaliated with his own freestyle of "Litty", titled "Litty Again", taking shots at Joyner's collaboration with Eminem. Lucas' <a href="/wiki/Answer_song" title="Answer song">answer song</a> was a freestyle of <a href="/wiki/Kodak_Black" title="Kodak Black">Kodak Black</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Zeze_(song)" title="Zeze (song)">Zeze</a>". On the track, he mentions Tory's being accused of plagiarism in 2016, when listeners noticed that the ending of Lanez's song "4am Flex" was similar to <a href="/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar" title="Kendrick Lamar">Kendrick Lamar</a>'s "The Art of Peer Pressure". Joyner also accuses him of copying rapper <a href="/wiki/Don_Q_(rapper)" title="Don Q (rapper)">Don Q</a>'s flow on his 2017 Funkmaster Flex freestyle.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> On November 23, Tory posted on Instagram a video of him declaring victory in the feud and celebrating. Displeased, Lucas reposted the video with his own comment, with a reference to Lanez as a singer.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The end of this rivalry was made public on August 8, 2019, when Joyner Lucas and Tory Lanez collaborated on their remix of "<a href="/wiki/Suge_(song)" title="Suge (song)">Suge</a>" by <a href="/wiki/DaBaby" title="DaBaby">DaBaby</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
