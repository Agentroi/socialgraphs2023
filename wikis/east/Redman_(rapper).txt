<p><b>Reginald Noble</b> (born April 17, 1970), better known by his stage name <b>Redman</b>, is an American rapper, DJ, record producer, and actor. He rose to fame in the early 1990s as an artist on the <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam" class="mw-redirect" title="Def Jam">Def Jam</a> label.
</p><p>He is well known for his collaborations with his close friend <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, as one-half of the rap duo <a href="/wiki/Method_Man_%26_Redman" title="Method Man &amp; Redman">Method Man &amp; Redman</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> including their starring roles in films and sitcoms which include <i>How High</i> and <i>Method and Red</i>.  He was also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Def_Squad" title="Def Squad">Def Squad</a> in the late 1990s.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Raised in <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a>, Redman attended Speedway Avenue School and 13th Avenue School before attending <a href="/wiki/West_Side_High_School_(New_Jersey)" title="West Side High School (New Jersey)">West Side High School</a>, an experience he described as "off the hook".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1987, Redman was expelled from <a href="/wiki/Montclair_State_University" title="Montclair State University">Montclair State University</a> his freshman year due to poor academic performance at age 16. Having no other options, Redman then went back home to live with his mother, Darlene Noble, who eventually kicked him out of her house for selling <a href="/wiki/Cocaine" title="Cocaine">cocaine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Two years later, at age 18, Redman was a young DJ-MC who went by the name "DJ Kut-Killa". He freestyled over funk and hip hop instrumental tracks on vinyl records in various parks and house parties around New York and New Jersey.
</p><p>Redman was eventually discovered by <a href="/wiki/Erick_Sermon" title="Erick Sermon">Erick Sermon</a> of <a href="/wiki/EPMD" title="EPMD">EPMD</a> while he was a DJ for <a href="/wiki/Lords_of_the_Underground" title="Lords of the Underground">Lords of the Underground</a>. Sermon said when he met Redman for the first time, "I knew there was something spectacular about him. Right off the bat. The next day, we talked. And within the next two or three months, he moved to Long Island, to my crib. He moved right into my apartment."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After moving in with Sermon, Redman went out on tour with EPMD. While on tour with the group he did everything from carrying their bags to coming out on stage and doing rap freestyles. In 1990, at an EPMD show in New York, Redman was invited onstage by Erick Sermon where he delivered a rap freestyle that changed his life. He freestyled a song describing himself as a rapper using every letter in the alphabet, from A to Z. After this, Redman was an official rap artist and began production with Erick Sermon on his first major label album, <i><a href="/wiki/Whut%3F_Thee_Album" title="Whut? Thee Album">Whut? Thee Album</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Whut.3F_Thee_Album_.26_Dare_Iz_a_Darkside_.281990.E2.80.931995.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Whut?_Thee_Album_&amp;_Dare_Iz_a_Darkside_(1990–1995)"><i>Whut? Thee Album</i> &amp; <i>Dare Iz a Darkside</i> (1990–1995)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Whut? Thee Album &amp; Dare Iz a Darkside (1990–1995)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1990, Redman made his official debut on <a href="/wiki/EPMD" title="EPMD">EPMD</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Business_as_Usual_(EPMD_album)" title="Business as Usual (EPMD album)">Business as Usual</a></i>, appearing on the tracks "Hardcore" and "Brothers on My Jock".
</p><p>In 1992, Redman released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Whut%3F_Thee_Album" title="Whut? Thee Album">Whut? Thee Album</a></i>, which <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> noted for blending "reggae and funk influences" with a "terse, though fluid rap style".<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The album peaked at number forty-nine on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and was certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">gold</a>. He was named "Rap Artist of the Year" by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Source_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Source (magazine)">The Source</a></i>. Redman followed this up with his 1994 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Dare_Iz_a_Darkside" title="Dare Iz a Darkside">Dare Iz a Darkside</a></i>. The first single, "<a href="/wiki/Rockafella_(song)" title="Rockafella (song)">Rockafella</a>", samples <a href="/wiki/Leon_Haywood" title="Leon Haywood">Leon Haywood</a>'s "I Want'a Do Something Freaky to You" and <a href="/wiki/George_Clinton_(funk_musician)" title="George Clinton (funk musician)">George Clinton</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Flash_Light_(song)" title="Flash Light (song)">Flash Light</a>", two of the most sampled songs in hip hop. Redman's second album was notable for having most of the tracks produced by the artist himself (later on Redman would let other producers make most of his beats). During this time he used former mentor Erick Sermon to produce his records, including "Pick It Up" and "Whateva Man" from <i><a href="/wiki/Muddy_Waters_(album)" title="Muddy Waters (album)">Muddy Waters</a></i>.
</p><p>In 1992, Redman appeared in <a href="/wiki/A_Tribe_Called_Quest" title="A Tribe Called Quest">A Tribe Called Quest</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Scenario_(song)" title="Scenario (song)">Scenario</a>" music video.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Redman released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Dare_Iz_a_Darkside" title="Dare Iz a Darkside">Dare Iz a Darkside</a></i> on November 22, 1994, by Def Jam Recordings. The album debuted at 13 on the US <i>Billboard</i> 200. The album was certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of America. Redman has said that he was on drugs during the recording of the album, and said in 2010 that he had not played the album in recent years because it was made during one of the dark times in his life.
</p>
<h3><span id="Muddy_Waters_.281996.E2.80.931997.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Muddy_Waters_(1996–1997)"><i>Muddy Waters</i> (1996–1997)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Muddy Waters (1996–1997)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 10, 1996, Redman released his third album <i><a href="/wiki/Muddy_Waters_(album)" title="Muddy Waters (album)">Muddy Waters</a></i>, which was highly praised by critics.<sup id="cite_ref-AllmscRvw_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllmscRvw-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SptnkMscRvw_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SptnkMscRvw-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The album featured two of his <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s hit singles: "<a href="/wiki/Whateva_Man" title="Whateva Man">Whateva Man</a>" and "It's Like That (My Big Brother)". The song "Do What You Feel" appeared on the soundtrack to the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto:_Liberty_City_Stories" title="Grand Theft Auto: Liberty City Stories">Grand Theft Auto: Liberty City Stories</a></i>. The album was certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) on February 12, 1997. During this time, with newfound friendships with Method Man and Tupac, they both appeared on Tupac's double album <i>All Eyez on Me</i> on the song "Got my Mind Made Up".
</p><p>In 1999 Redman took part in the Hard Knock Life Tour, including a stop in Toronto at the Air Canada Centre.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Doc.27s_da_Name_2000_.26_Blackout_.281998.E2.80.931999.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Doc's_da_Name_2000_&amp;_Blackout_(1998–1999)"><i>Doc's da Name 2000</i> &amp; <i>Blackout</i> (1998–1999)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Doc&#039;s da Name 2000 &amp; Blackout (1998–1999)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In 1998 Redman released his first collaboration album <i><a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o_(Def_Squad_album)" title="El Niño (Def Squad album)">El Nino</a></i> with group <a href="/wiki/Def_Squad" title="Def Squad">Def Squad</a>. His fourth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Doc%27s_Da_Name_2000" class="mw-redirect" title="Doc&#39;s Da Name 2000">Doc's Da Name 2000</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> was a top seller, shipping <a href="/wiki/Platinum_record" class="mw-redirect" title="Platinum record">platinum</a> in sales and exceeding his previous record of <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" class="mw-redirect" title="Music recording sales certification">gold</a>. It peaked at number 11 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 and had three songs ("I'll Bee Dat", "Da Goodness", "Let Da Monkey Out") that charted.
</p><p>With his labelmate <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>, Redman made a featured appearance on the Doc's Da Name 2000 record on the song "Well All Rite Cha". In 1999, Method Man and Redman released a collaborative album called <i>Blackout!</i>.
</p>
<h3><span id="Malpractice_.282001.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Malpractice_(2001)"><i>Malpractice</i> (2001)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Malpractice (2001)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001 Redman released his fifth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Malpractice_(Redman_album)" title="Malpractice (Redman album)">Malpractice</a></i>. It reached number 4 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 and was certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> on July 21, 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It boasted two singles "<a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Get_Dirty_(I_Can%27t_Get_in_da_Club)" title="Let&#39;s Get Dirty (I Can&#39;t Get in da Club)">Let's Get Dirty (I Can't Get in da Club)</a>" and "Smash Sumthin'".
</p>
<h3><span id="Mixtapes.2C_Collaborations_.26_Production_.282002.E2.80.932006.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Mixtapes,_Collaborations_&amp;_Production_(2002–2006)"><i>Mixtapes, Collaborations &amp; Production</i> (2002–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mixtapes, Collaborations &amp; Production (2002–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the release of Malpractice, he has kept his fans happy by independently releasing the <i>Ill At Will</i> <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtapes</a>, which feature various members of his new record label <i>Gilla House</i> members under Redman's Gilla House imprint include <a href="/wiki/Saukrates" title="Saukrates">Saukrates</a>, Icarus, Ready Roc, E3, Young Heat, and Melanie.
</p><p>Redman had his best-known international hit with <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a>, when he was featured on her 2002 single "<a href="/wiki/Dirrty" title="Dirrty">Dirrty</a>". Around this time he was also featured on a popular remix of <a href="/wiki/Pink_(singer)" title="Pink (singer)">Pink</a>'s track <i>Get This Party Started</i>. He is cited in the song "<a href="/wiki/%27Till_I_Collapse" title="&#39;Till I Collapse">'Till I Collapse</a>" as rapper <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s favorite rapper.
</p><p>Under his birthname, Reggie Noble, Redman has done production for himself, many of his crew members, and even Shaquille O'Neal on his <i><a href="/wiki/Shaq-Fu:_Da_Return" class="mw-redirect" title="Shaq-Fu: Da Return">Shaq-Fu: Da Return</a></i> album.
</p>
<h3><span id="Red_Gone_Wild_.282007.E2.80.932008.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Red_Gone_Wild_(2007–2008)"><i>Red Gone Wild</i> (2007–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Red Gone Wild (2007–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Redman_at_Rock_the_Bells_2008_(cropped).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Redman_at_Rock_the_Bells_2008_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Redman_at_Rock_the_Bells_2008_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Redman_at_Rock_the_Bells_2008_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Redman_at_Rock_the_Bells_2008_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Redman_at_Rock_the_Bells_2008_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Redman_at_Rock_the_Bells_2008_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="393" data-file-height="589" /></a><figcaption>Redman performing in 2008</figcaption></figure>
<p>In March 2007, Redman released <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Gone_Wild" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Gone Wild">Red Gone Wild</a></i>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Pinset" title="Chris Pinset">Chris Pinset.</a> He stated on MySpace that the reason for the long wait to drop the album was the fast-changing pace of hip hop.
On March 27, 2007, Redman confirmed on BET's <i>Rap City: Tha Bassment</i> that the sequel to <i>How High</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/How_High_2#Sequel" title="How High 2">How High 2</a></i>, was currently being written. In an April 10, 2007, <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">Onion A.V. Club</a></i> interview,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Redman hinted that there would be a second collaborative album with Method Man.
</p>
<h3><span id="Blackout.21_2_.26_Reggie_.282009.E2.80.932010.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Blackout!_2_&amp;_Reggie_(2009–2010)"><i>Blackout! 2</i> &amp; <i>Reggie</i> (2009–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Blackout! 2 &amp; Reggie (2009–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Blackout! 2</i> was announced in 2007 by Redman and was originally scheduled for release in 2008, but its release was pushed back a number of times due to numerous reasons. In early 2008, a remake of the Smoothe da Hustler and Trigger tha Gambler classic <i>Broken Language</i> was released on the internet by the duo entitled <i>Broken Language 2008</i>, fueling rumors of a <i>Blackout!</i> sequel coming soon. This rumor was further fueled by the duo while performing in <a href="/wiki/Gainesville,_Florida" title="Gainesville, Florida">Gainesville, Florida</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Florida" title="University of Florida">University of Florida</a>. A <i><a href="/wiki/Blackout!_(Method_Man_%26_Redman_album)" title="Blackout! (Method Man &amp; Redman album)">Blackout!</a></i> LP was scheduled for a December 9, 2008, release but was recently pushed back to the first quarter of 2009. It was finally released on May 19, 2009. It debuted at number 7 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, at number 2 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a>, at number 2 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Rap_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Rap Albums">Top Rap Albums</a> and digital in charted number 7 on the Digital Albums charts selling 63,000 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The album also charted number 10 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Albums_Chart" title="Canadian Albums Chart">Canadian Albums Chart</a>. The album has sold 160,375 copies in the United States by December 12, 2009, according to SoundScan.
</p><p>In May 2009, Redman confirmed his next solo album would come out December 2009. The title for the project is <i>Reggie Noble "0" 9½</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The title of the album was then changed to just <i><a href="/wiki/Redman_Presents..._Reggie" class="mw-redirect" title="Redman Presents... Reggie">Redman Presents... Reggie</a></i>, and three singles ("Coc Back", "Oh My", and "Money on My Mind") were released. The first single "Coc Back" (which features Ready Roc) had music video filmed and released for it. But as it turns out, none of the tracks made the final cut of the album, only being promo singles. During an interview, Redman stated that Reggie Noble (Redman's own birth name), does the album, not Redman. Redman also said that there is going to be more, "poppish", type songs, rather his normal "rugged" and "hardcore" songs.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> After a few push backs, <i>Redman Presents... Reggie</i> was finally released on December 7, 2010. The first official single is "Def Jammable" and a video was released for the single. It has been confirmed that Redman will appear on a remix along with French rapper <a href="/wiki/Soprano" title="Soprano">Soprano</a> of "Tranne Te" by Italian rapper <a href="/wiki/Fabri_Fibra" title="Fabri Fibra">Fabri Fibra</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="Mudface.2C_3Joints_.282011.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Mudface,_3Joints_(2011–present)"><i>Mudface</i>, <i>3Joints</i> (2011–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Mudface, 3Joints (2011–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2013, Redman announced that his next studio album <i>Muddy Waters 2</i> would be released in late 2013<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> via <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_One" title="Entertainment One">Entertainment One</a> and he would release a mixtape to go along with it, which evolved into an EP titled <i>Muddy Waters 2: The Preload</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Redman has also confirmed work on <i>Blackout! 3</i> with Method Man with recording starting summer 2012, however, recording was pushed back to late 2014. <i>Mudface</i> was released on November 13, 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> with <i>Blackout! 3</i> coming out after, and Method Man's long-awaited mixtape and fifth solo album directly following these projects.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Method Man and Redman are also slated to be working on a long-rumored sequel to their original stoner comedy, <i>How High</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In a <a href="/wiki/HipHopDX" title="HipHopDX">HipHopDX</a> interview in February 2017, Matt "M-80" Markoff confirmed the project, revealed that the sequel will be titled <i>Too High</i> and that he is currently looking to acquire finances for the film.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> An EP called '3 Joints' was released on his Gilla House label in 2020, as further work on other albums continues.
</p><p>In 2021, together with producer <a href="/wiki/Chris_Pinset" title="Chris Pinset">Chris Pinset</a>, Redman has released <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=922opSdN-HU">"80 Barz"</a> music video that quickly gained almost 3,000,000 views on YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_ventures">Other ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Other ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Redman_(rapper).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Redman_%28rapper%29.jpg/170px-Redman_%28rapper%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="309" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Redman_%28rapper%29.jpg/255px-Redman_%28rapper%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Redman_%28rapper%29.jpg/340px-Redman_%28rapper%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1269" data-file-height="2308" /></a><figcaption>Redman in 2006</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2001, Redman co-starred with Method Man in the <a href="/wiki/Stoner_film" title="Stoner film">stoner comedy</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/How_High" title="How High">How High</a></i>, which featured the pair playing two marijuana-addled students attempting to survive at Harvard.
</p><p>Redman played a major role in the horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Seed_of_Chucky" title="Seed of Chucky">Seed of Chucky</a></i>.
</p><p>Method Man and Redman also appear in <i><a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Vendetta" title="Def Jam Vendetta">Def Jam Vendetta</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Def_Jam:_Fight_for_NY" title="Def Jam: Fight for NY">Def Jam: Fight for NY</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Def_Jam:_Icon" title="Def Jam: Icon">Def Jam: Icon</a></i> video games, often as tag team partners, and briefly had their own television sitcom, <i><a href="/wiki/Method_%26_Red" title="Method &amp; Red">Method &amp; Red</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> during the 2003–2004 season.
</p><p>In the music video for <a href="/wiki/The_Offspring" title="The Offspring">the Offspring</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Original_Prankster" title="Original Prankster">Original Prankster</a>", Redman plays the part of the "conscience", telling the "Original Prankster" what pranks to pull.
</p><p>In 2004, Redman again co-starred with Method Man in the <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> cartoon, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fairly_OddParents" title="The Fairly OddParents">The Fairly OddParents</a></i>, in <i><a href="/wiki/School%27s_Out!_The_Musical" class="mw-redirect" title="School&#39;s Out! The Musical">School's Out! The Musical</a></i>, rapping the "Pixes Rap".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fashion">Fashion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Fashion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2015, Redman released a collaboration with New York-based streetwear brand Mighty Healthy. The <a href="/wiki/420_(cannabis_culture)" title="420 (cannabis culture)">420</a>-themed collection includes jerseys, T-shirts, hats, and other items with <a href="/wiki/Cannabis" title="Cannabis">cannabis</a>-related graphics.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hosting">Hosting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Hosting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2017, it was announced that Redman would host the first season of <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Scared_Famous_(TV_series)" title="Scared Famous (TV series)">Scared Famous</a></i>, which premiered on October 23, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Redman was born in <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a>, to Darlene and Eddie Noble. His sister Rosalyn Noble is rapper known as Roz. She has appeared on several Redman songs over the years.<sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He is a cousin of rapper <a href="/wiki/Tame_One" title="Tame One">Tame One</a>.
</p><p>In 2001, Redman appeared on an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/MTV_Cribs" title="MTV Cribs">MTV Cribs</a></i> and showcased his <a href="/wiki/Staten_Island" title="Staten Island">Staten Island</a> home which, in contrast to the luxurious homes usually featured in the series, was described by <i><a href="/wiki/XXL_(magazine)" title="XXL (magazine)">XXL</a></i> magazine as "small and grimey". Speaking to <i>Fresh Pair</i>, Redman explained that MTV had already picked out a few houses for him to make his appearance in, but Redman did not want to appear fake, and insisted the episode be aired at his actual residence. The TV crew had to be reduced to two people because that was the only number of people that could fit in his house.<sup id="cite_ref-cribs_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cribs-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The episode, which featured the crew having to pinch two wires together because the house lacked a <a href="/wiki/Doorbell" title="Doorbell">doorbell</a>, and a cameo by Redman's cousin Mr. Cream (formerly known as Sugar Bear) sleeping on the floor in a comforter, has become "the most infamous, most cited" episode of the show according to MTV VP of programming Nina L. Diaz.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> According to a 2017 interview, Redman still lived in the property and was quoted as saying "It's real small. Two bedrooms. [...] I wouldn't know what to do with a big house".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Redman began boarding his four-month-old <a href="/wiki/Pit_bull_terrier" class="mw-redirect" title="Pit bull terrier">pit bull terrier</a> <a href="/wiki/Daddy_(dog)" title="Daddy (dog)">Daddy</a> with dog trainer <a href="/wiki/Cesar_Millan" title="Cesar Millan">Cesar Millan</a>, with Daddy ultimately becoming Millan's "right-hand-man" on his TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Dog_Whisperer" class="mw-redirect" title="Dog Whisperer">The Dog Whisperer</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-memoriam_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-memoriam-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redman_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Redman has been winner for one <a href="/wiki/MOBO_Awards" title="MOBO Awards">MOBO Awards</a>:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/MOBO_Awards" title="MOBO Awards">MOBO Awards</a>
<ul><li>2003, Best Video "Dirrty" w/ <small>Christina Aguilera</small> (winner)</li></ul></li></ul>
<p>Redman has been nominated for two <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammys</a>:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a>
<ul><li>2003, Best Pop Collaboration w/ Vocals "Dirrty" w/ <small>Christina Aguilera</small> (nominated)</li>
<li>2001, Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group "Ooh" w/ <small>De La Soul</small> (nominated)</li></ul></li></ul>
