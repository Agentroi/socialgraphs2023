<p><b>Darold Durard Brown Ferguson Jr.</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (born October 20, 1988), known professionally as <b>ASAP Ferg</b> (stylized as <b>A$AP Ferg</b>), is an American rapper from New York City's <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a> neighborhood. Aside from his solo career, he is a member of the hip hop collective <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Mob" title="ASAP Mob">A$AP Mob</a>, from which he adopted a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Polo_Grounds_Music" title="Polo Grounds Music">Polo Grounds</a> and <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA</a>, the same labels that helped launch A$AP Worldwide. Two years prior, Ferg's A$AP Mob cohorts <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">A$AP Rocky</a> and A$AP Yams, negotiated their own respective deal in 2011. His debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Trap_Lord" title="Trap Lord">Trap Lord</a></i> was released on August 20, 2013, and was met with generally positive reviews. On April 22, 2016, Ferguson released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Always_Strive_and_Prosper" title="Always Strive and Prosper">Always Strive and Prosper</a></i>. On August 18, 2017, Ferguson released his second mixtape, <i><a href="/wiki/Still_Striving" title="Still Striving">Still Striving</a></i>, which was preceded by the single, "<a href="/wiki/Plain_Jane_(song)" title="Plain Jane (song)">Plain Jane</a>" (featuring <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>). In 2019 and 2020, he released <i>Floor Seats</i> and <i>Floor Seats II</i> respectively, the latter featuring the single "<a href="/wiki/Move_Ya_Hips" title="Move Ya Hips">Move Ya Hips</a>" (featuring Nicki Minaj and <a href="/wiki/MadeinTYO" title="MadeinTYO">MadeinTYO</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2021, Ferguson signed an additional management deal with <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Outside of music, Ferguson is the founder of the street fashion brand <a href="/wiki/Traplord" title="Traplord">Traplord</a>, namesake of his debut album.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASAP_Ferg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Darold Durard Brown was born on October 20, 1988, in the <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> to <a href="/wiki/Trinidadians_and_Tobagonians" title="Trinidadians and Tobagonians">Trinidadian</a> parents.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Ferg was raised in Hamilton Heights.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> His father, Darold Ferguson, owned a small Harlem <a href="/wiki/Boutique" title="Boutique">boutique</a> and printed shirts and logos for <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record labels</a>; including <a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_Records" title="Bad Boy Records">Bad Boy Records</a> and performers <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Riley" title="Teddy Riley">Teddy Riley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heavy_D" title="Heavy D">Heavy D</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bell_Biv_DeVoe" title="Bell Biv DeVoe">Bell Biv DeVoe</a>. Before starting his music career, Ferg initially aspired to start his own clothing line.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Inspired by his father, who died of kidney failure, Ferg launched clothing and jewelry lines at a young age and attended art school.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2005, Ferg began launching the "Devoni Clothing", that he designed and distributed high-end belts, which have been worn by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swizz_Beatz" title="Swizz Beatz">Swizz Beatz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diggy_Simmons" title="Diggy Simmons">Diggy Simmons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASAP_Ferg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932012:_Career_beginnings_with_A.24AP_Mob"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2012:_Career_beginnings_with_A$AP_Mob">2009–2012: Career beginnings with A$AP Mob</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASAP_Ferg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009–2012: Career beginnings with A$AP Mob">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By 2009, he started developing an aggressive, <a href="/wiki/Trap_music" title="Trap music">trap</a> style of hip-hop. "Trapping means hustling," he explained. "I went from painting to making clothes to rapping. I always put myself into everything I do one hundred percent. It doesn't matter what I'm working on. I want to perfect everything I do. Success is the only option. They call me 'Trap Lord' because my hustle game is impeccable." Friends since high school, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">A$AP Rocky</a> recognized that Ferg had an "impeccable hustle game" and pushed Ferg to continue <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapping</a> as much as possible. They later joined the hip hop collective A$AP Mob, from which they both adopted their respective <a href="/wiki/Moniker" class="mw-redirect" title="Moniker">monikers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Since 2010, they collaborated on various songs such as "Get High," "Kissin' Pink" and "Ghetto Symphony", the latter being from Rocky's chart-topping <a href="/wiki/Major_record_label" class="mw-redirect" title="Major record label">major label</a> debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Long._Live._ASAP" title="Long. Live. ASAP">Long. Live. A$AP</a></i> (2013).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.932014:_Trap_Lord"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–2014:_Trap_Lord">2012–2014: <i>Trap Lord</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASAP_Ferg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012–2014: Trap Lord">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Ferg's debut single "<a href="/wiki/Work_(ASAP_Ferg_song)" title="Work (ASAP Ferg song)">Work</a>", produced by Chinza and Fly, appeared on the A$AP Mob <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Lords_Never_Worry" title="Lords Never Worry">Lords Never Worry</a></i>, released on August 28, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-datpiff1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datpiff1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The song's music video quickly earned over 2 million online views and was dubbed one of the "50 Best Songs of 2012" by <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex Magazine">Complex Magazine</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On January 10, 2013, he announced <i>Trap Lord</i> would be the title of his debut album.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> When speaking on his debut album <i>Trap Lord</i>, Ferg said: "I hope people take the good and the bad from what I've got to say, I've seen so much shit in my life. There were times I ducked bullets at a basketball game, and then I went to a fashion show. I've experienced depression. I've experienced triumph. I've experienced hate. I've experienced love. I learned how to be independent. I made it out of the hood, and now I want go to the top."<sup id="cite_ref-complex1_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-complex1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 10, 2013, it was announced that he signed a record deal with <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Polo_Grounds_Music" title="Polo Grounds Music">Polo Grounds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On May 14, 2013, the <a href="/wiki/Remix" title="Remix">remix</a> to "Work", featuring fellow American rappers A$AP Rocky, <a href="/wiki/Schoolboy_Q" title="Schoolboy Q">Schoolboy Q</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Montana" title="French Montana">French Montana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_James" title="Trinidad James">Trinidad James</a>, was released to digital retailers.<sup id="cite_ref-apple1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apple1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On June 2, 2013, while revealing <i><a href="/wiki/Trap_Lord" title="Trap Lord">Trap Lord</a></i> would feature <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">guest appearances</a> from hip hop groups <a href="/wiki/Bone_Thugs-n-Harmony" title="Bone Thugs-n-Harmony">Bone Thugs-n-Harmony</a> and <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Onyx (group)">Onyx</a>, Ferg announced that the album would be released on August 20, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The album debuted at number 9 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, with total album sales of 46,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On October 15, he was named "Rookie of the Year" at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/BET_Hip_Hop_Awards" title="BET Hip Hop Awards">BET Hip Hop Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mtv.com_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtv.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> However, he was not there to accept the award as he did not think he was going to win.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, Ferg appeared as a featured artist on the track "Hands on Me" by <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Grande" title="Ariana Grande">Ariana Grande</a>, from her second album <i><a href="/wiki/My_Everything_(Ariana_Grande_album)" title="My Everything (Ariana Grande album)">My Everything</a></i>. The album was released on August 22, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.93present:_Always_Strive_and_Prosper_and_Floor_Seats_II"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–present:_Always_Strive_and_Prosper_and_Floor_Seats_II">2015–present: <i>Always Strive and Prosper</i> and <i>Floor Seats II</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASAP_Ferg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015–present: Always Strive and Prosper and Floor Seats II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:ASAP_Ferg_Veld_festival_2017.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/ASAP_Ferg_Veld_festival_2017.jpg/200px-ASAP_Ferg_Veld_festival_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="300" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/ASAP_Ferg_Veld_festival_2017.jpg/300px-ASAP_Ferg_Veld_festival_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/ASAP_Ferg_Veld_festival_2017.jpg/400px-ASAP_Ferg_Veld_festival_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2583" data-file-height="3870" /></a><figcaption>Ferguson performing at VELD festival in 2017</figcaption></figure>
<p>On February 25, 2015, Ferg released the video for the single "Dope Walk", a track from his <i><a href="/wiki/Ferg_Forever" title="Ferg Forever">Ferg Forever</a></i> (2014) mixtape in which he coined a viral dance of the same name. Composed entirely of iPhone footage shot during New York Fashion Week, the video starred fashion model <a href="/wiki/Cara_Delevingne" title="Cara Delevingne">Cara Delevingne</a> and featured cameos from a host of other celebrities, including <a href="/wiki/Kris_Jenner" title="Kris Jenner">Kris Jenner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wang_(designer)" title="Alexander Wang (designer)">Alexander Wang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russell_Simmons" title="Russell Simmons">Russell Simmons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>, <a href="/wiki/ASAP_Rocky" title="ASAP Rocky">A$AP Rocky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Scott" title="Jeremy Scott">Jeremy Scott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Les_Twins" title="Les Twins">Les Twins</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Haim_(band)" title="Haim (band)">Haim</a>. Ferg told <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>, "With 'Dope Walk' I wanted to bring back kids dancing and having fun again. That's how it used to be in Harlem. I remember everybody Harlem-shaking and 'Chicken Noodle Soup'-ing. Those were some of the most fun and memorable times in my life."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late 2015, Ferg directed the music video for rapper <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>'s "Thought It Was a Drought".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Source" title="The Source">The Source</a></i>, Ferg stated that his "...new album is my best work yet. I put my all into it. I always tell people I really don't know what I'm going to do after this album, because I'm bearing so much truth with this album."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On April 22, 2016, Ferg released his second studio album, which is titled <i>Always Strive And Prosper</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The album's first single, "<a href="/wiki/New_Level" title="New Level">New Level</a>" featuring Future was certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> on August 11, 2016. In 2016, he and <a href="/wiki/Playboi_Carti" title="Playboi Carti">Playboi Carti</a> embarked on the "Turnt &amp; Burnt" tour which had 23 stops.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On June 9, 2017, <a href="/wiki/Lost_Kings" title="Lost Kings">Lost Kings</a> released "<a href="/wiki/Look_At_Us_Now" class="mw-redirect" title="Look At Us Now">Look At Us Now</a>" as a single featuring Ferg and singer <a href="/wiki/Ally_Brooke" title="Ally Brooke">Ally Brooke</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2015, Ferg is accompanied on tour by his dj TJ Mizell,<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> who is the son of the legendary <a href="/wiki/Jam_Master_Jay" title="Jam Master Jay">Jam Master Jay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, Ferg appeared in a video ad for <a href="/wiki/Tiffany%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="Tiffany&#39;s">Tiffany's</a> with <a href="/wiki/Elle_Fanning" title="Elle Fanning">Elle Fanning</a> remixing "<a href="/wiki/Moon_River" title="Moon River">Moon River</a>"<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> and later that year, he also narrated the <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a> World Cup advert 'Creativity is the Answer'.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, Ferg, along with musician <a href="/wiki/Matt_Sweeney" title="Matt Sweeney">Matt Sweeney</a>, he appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Run_the_Jewels" title="Run the Jewels">Run The Jewels</a> track "<i>A Few Words for the Firing Squad (Radiation)</i>" on the latter's fourth studio album, <a href="/wiki/RTJ4" title="RTJ4">RTJ4</a>. He appeared at the end of "<i>A Few Words...</i>" on the hidden track, "Theme Song", providing his voice with the refrain "Yankee and the Brave".<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 1, 2020, it was alleged that Ferg was no longer in and affiliated with A$AP Mob by members of the group.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> This was later redacted by <a href="/wiki/A$AP_Nast" class="mw-redirect" title="A$AP Nast">A$AP Nast</a> on Twitter, with his statements retweeted by the official A$AP Mob Twitter account.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
On September 18, ASAP Ferg's official <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> account was changed to only say Ferg, implying that he has departed A$AP Mob, but this has been unconfirmed.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A$AP Ferg released his fifth album, <i>Floor Seats II</i>, on September 25, 2020. It includes collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Manson" title="Marilyn Manson">Marilyn Manson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Rodman" title="Dennis Rodman">Dennis Rodman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mulatto_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mulatto (rapper)">Mulatto</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
